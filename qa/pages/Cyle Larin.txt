'''Cyle Christopher Larin''' ({{IPAc-en|'|k|ai|l|_|'|l|ae|r|I|n}};<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/how-to-pronounce-chelsea-and-tottenham-stars-names-correctly-athpilicooetah-contay-and-more-a3765901.html|title=How to pronounce Champions League stars' names correctly|first=Standard|last=Sport|date=February 14, 2018|website=Standard.co.uk}}</ref> born 17 April 1995) is a Canadian professional [[Association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[Forward (association football)#Striker|striker]] for [[Segunda División]] club [[Real Valladolid]] and the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada national team]].

After playing [[college soccer]] for the [[UConn Huskies men's soccer|UConn Huskies]], Larin was the first pick of the [[2015 MLS SuperDraft]], joining [[Orlando City SC|Orlando City]]. In his first MLS season, he broke the record for most goals by a rookie, scoring 17 in 27 games and earning the [[MLS Rookie of the Year Award]].<ref name="Rookie of the Year"/> He totaled 89 games and 44 goals for Orlando before moving to Beşiktaş for an undisclosed fee in January 2018.

A full international for Canada since 2014, Larin represented the nation at the [[CONCACAF Gold Cup]] in [[2015 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2015]], [[2017 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2017]], [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2019]] and [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2021]], reaching the semi-finals at the last of those tournaments. He was the top scorer with 13 goals in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONCACAF)|CONCACAF section]] of [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification]], as Canada qualified for the first time since 1986. With 28 goals, he is the team's [[Canada men's national soccer team#Top goalscorers|all-time top goalscorer]].

==Early life==
Larin was born in [[Brampton]], [[Ontario]] in Canada to a [[Jamaica]]n family and attended [[St. Edmund Campion Secondary School]], where he was the school's top goal scorer for the final three years that he attended.<ref name="UConn Bio">{{cite web|title=UConn Bio|url=http://www.uconnhuskies.com/sports/m-soccer/mtt/cyle_larin_855126.html|publisher=University of Connecticut|accessdate=May 23, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150907191612/http://www.uconnhuskies.com/sports/m-soccer/mtt/cyle_larin_855126.html|archive-date=September 7, 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> In 2007, at the age of 11, Larin joined the private soccer academy Sigma FC of the [[Ontario Soccer League]] before graduating in 2013. During his time at Sigma FC, Larin travelled to Europe for training periods with [[Werder Bremen]], [[Hertha Berlin]] and [[VfL Wolfsburg|Wolfsburg]] in Germany, as well as [[Racing Genk]] and [[Club Brugge KV|Club Brugge]] in Belgium twice. About the striker, then-Sigma FC Technical Director [[Bobby Smyrniotis]] said, "Cyle is a rare player to find in Canada...a complete and modern striker able to play with both feet, finish from all areas, dominate his region of the park and provide for his teammates."<ref name="Commit to the University of Connecticut">{{cite web|title=Cyle Larin and Kwame Awuah Commit to the University of Connecticut|url=http://www.sigma-sports.net/newsEvents/CyleLarinAndKwameAwuahCommitToTheUniversityOfConnecticut.html|publisher=Sigma FC|accessdate=May 23, 2014}}</ref>

==College career==
In 2013, Larin committed to the [[University of Connecticut]].  During his first year with the [[UConn Huskies men's soccer|Huskies]], Larin scored 14 goals in 23 appearances, the 6th highest goal total in the entire [[NCAA]] for the season. His performance during his freshman year earned him multiple honours including Freshman of the Year by TopDrawerSoccer.com and being named to the [[American Athletic Conference]] All-Rookie Team.<ref name="UConn Bio"/> In January 2014, Larin was touted as one of the best players available in the [[2014 MLS SuperDraft]]. However, he did not sign a [[Generation Adidas]] deal with [[Major League Soccer]] before the draft and was named the No. 2 best college player available in 2015 if he opted to sign with the league.<ref name="2015 draft">{{cite web|last=Sulat|first=Nate|title=SuperDraft: Who are the top 10 college players ahead of the 2015 draft?|url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/superdraft/2014/news/article/2014/01/22/superdraft-who-are-top-10-college-players-ahead-2015-draft|publisher=Major League Soccer|accessdate=May 23, 2014}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Orlando City===
====2015 season====
In early January 2015, it was announced that Larin had reached a verbal agreement to sign a [[Generation Adidas]] contract with Major League Soccer despite interest from European clubs, including a club from the English [[Premier League]].<ref name="MLS Improves Offer">{{cite web|last1=Tunstall|first1=Brooke|title=MLS Improves Offer, Comes To Terms With Cyle Larin|url=http://americansoccernow.com/articles/mls-improves-offer-signs-uconn-star-cyle-larin|publisher=American Soccer Now|accessdate=January 5, 2015}}</ref> On January 8, 2015, it was officially announced that Larin had signed a Generation Adidas contract with the league, along with four other players.<ref name="Generation adidas class">{{cite web|title=Major League Soccer announces 2015 Generation adidas class|url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/2015/01/08/major-league-soccer-announces-2015-generation-adidas-class|publisher=Major League Soccer|accessdate=January 8, 2015|archive-date=January 22, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150122235423/http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/2015/01/08/major-league-soccer-announces-2015-generation-adidas-class|url-status=dead}}</ref> At the [[2015 MLS SuperDraft]], Larin was selected by Orlando City SC as the first overall pick as expected, becoming the first Canadian ever chosen first overall in the MLS SuperDraft in the process.<ref name="selected first overal">{{cite web|title=MLS SuperDraft: Canadian international Cyle Larin selected first overall by Orlando City SC|url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/superdraft/2015/news/article/2015/01/15/mls-superdraft-canadian-international-cyle-larin-selected-first-overall-orlan|publisher=Major League Soccer|accessdate=January 15, 2015}}</ref>

Larin was first included in an Orlando City match on March 13, 2015, remaining an unused substitute in their 1–0 win at the [[Houston Dynamo]]. Eight days later he made his debut, coming on as a 71st-minute substitute for [[Pedro Ribeiro (Brazilian footballer)|Pedro Ribeiro]] in a 0–1 loss against [[Vancouver Whitecaps FC]] at the [[Citrus Bowl]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/schedule/|title=Schedule & Scores &#124; MLSsoccer.com|website=www.mlssoccer.com}}</ref> He scored his first professional league goal on April 12, opening a 2–0 away win against the [[Portland Timbers]].<ref name="Orlando City SC thinking big">{{cite web|last1=Veness|first1=Simon|title=Orlando City SC thinking big after win at Portland Timbers: "We can be very good in this league"|url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/2015/04/12/orlando-city-sc-thinking-big-after-win-portland-timbers-we-can-be-very-good-|publisher=Major League Soccer|accessdate=April 13, 2015}}</ref> Larin scored in his [[2015 Lamar Hunt U.S. Open Cup|Lamar Hunt U.S. Open Cup]] debut as Orlando was defeated 1–3 by [[Chicago Fire Soccer Club|Chicago Fire]] in the quarterfinals.<ref name="US Open Cup Match Recap">{{cite web|last1=Murray|first1=Shane|title=Chicago Fire 3, Orlando City SC 1 – US Open Cup Match Recap|url=http://matchcenter.mlssoccer.com/matchcenter/2015-07-22-chicago-fire-vs-orlando-city-sc/recap|publisher=Major League Soccer|accessdate=July 28, 2015}}</ref>  On July 18, in a 0–2 home loss to the [[New York Red Bulls]], he was sent off in the first half for a high tackle on [[Sacha Kljestan]]; this red card was eventually overturned.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Edwards|first1=Andy|title=MLS Snapshot: Orlando City SC 0–2 New York Red Bulls (video)|url=http://soccer.nbcsports.com/2015/07/18/mls-snapshot-orlando-city-sc-0-2-new-york-red-bulls-video/|accessdate=October 18, 2015|publisher=NBC|date=July 18, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Independent Review Panel rescinds Orlando City SC forward Cyle Larin's red card|url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2015/07/22/independent-review-panel-rescinds-orlando-city-sc-forward-cyle-larins-red-card|accessdate=October 18, 2015|publisher=Major League Soccer|date=July 22, 2015}}</ref>

Eight days later, he scored his first professional [[hat-trick]] in an MLS fixture against [[New York City FC]]. With the hat-trick, Larin became only the 8th rookie to score three goals in an MLS match and only the third player to do so for an expansion side.<ref name="Hat trick">{{cite web|title=Orlando City SC rookie Cyle Larin enters the history books with hat trick against New York City FC|url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/2015/07/26/orlando-city-sc-rookie-cyle-larin-enters-history-books-hat-trick-against-new|publisher=Major League Soccer|accessdate=July 27, 2015}}</ref> Larin also became the third Canadian to score three goals in a match, along with [[Dwayne De Rosario]] and [[Tesho Akindele]], and the second youngest player to do so behind only [[Kekuta Manneh]], who was 18 at the time of his first MLS hat-trick.<ref name="hat-trick by the numbers">{{cite web|title=Historic? Orlando City SC rookie Cyle Larin's hat-trick, by the numbers|url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/sideline/news/article/2015/07/27/orlando-citys-cyle-larin-joins-likes-sebastien-le-toux-dwayne-de-rosario-aft|publisher=Major League Soccer|accessdate=July 28, 2015}}</ref> He was named MLS Player of the Week for his efforts.<ref name="Larin named MLS Player of the Week">{{cite web|title=Orlando City SC forward Cyle Larin named MLS Player of the Week after hat trick at New York City FC|url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/2015/07/28/orlando-city-sc-forward-cyle-larin-named-mls-player-week-after-hat-trick-new|publisher=Major League Soccer|accessdate=July 28, 2015}}</ref>

In Orlando's next match, Larin scored two more goals as the club defeated [[Columbus Crew SC]] 5–2, tying him with [[Damani Ralph]]'s rookie record of eleven goals.<ref name="Larin ties the rookie record">{{cite web|title=WATCH: Orlando City's Cyle Larin ties the rookie record for goals in a season|url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/2015/08/01/watch-orlando-citys-cyle-larin-ties-rookie-record-goals-season|publisher=Major League Soccer|accessdate=August 2, 2015}}</ref> 
On September 25, against the Red Bulls, he scored his second hat-trick of the season in a 5–2 victory on the road and, in the process, broke the rookie goal-scoring record.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/2015/09/25/orlando-city-forward-cyle-larin-breaks-record-most-goals-scored-season-mls-r|title=Orlando City forward Cyle Larin breaks record for most goals scored in a season by an MLS rookie|author=Alicia Rodriguez|work=MLS}}</ref> In November, having finished his first season with 17 goals in 27 games, Larin won the 2015 [[MLS Rookie of the Year Award]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2015/11/03/benny-feilhaber-sebastian-giovinco-kei-kamara-contend-landon-donovan-mvp-mls|title=Benny Feilhaber, Sebastian Giovinco, Kei Kamara contend for Landon Donovan MVP as MLS announces 2015 Awards finalists|publisher=MLS Soccer|date=November 3, 2015|accessdate=November 3, 2015}}</ref><ref name="Rookie of the Year">{{cite web|title=Orlando City forward Cyle Larin named 2015 AT&T Rookie of the Year|url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2015/11/10/orlando-city-forward-cyle-larin-named-2015-att-rookie-year|publisher=Major League Soccer|accessdate=November 10, 2015}}</ref>

====2016 season====
On March 6, Orlando began the [[2016 Major League Soccer season|new season]] by hosting [[Real Salt Lake]]. Down 2–0 in the fourth minute of added time, Larin scored from [[Brek Shea]]'s pass and then set up [[Adrian Winter]]'s equalizer in the eventual 2–2 draw.<ref>{{cite news|title=Orlando City scores two goals in last minute, ties Real Salt Lake 2–2|url=http://www.orlandosentinel.com/sports/orlando-city-lions/os-orlando-city-ties-real-salt-lake-20160306-story.html|accessdate=March 7, 2016|work=Orlando Sentinel|date=March 6, 2016}}</ref> He scored in each of the team's first three games of the season, the third being the only goal in a win at New York City on March 18.<ref>{{cite news|title=Cyle Larin scores early; Orlando City beats NYCFC 1-0|url=http://www.miamiherald.com/entertainment/celebrities/article67054497.html|accessdate=March 19, 2016|work=Miami Herald|agency=The Associated Press|date=March 18, 2016}}</ref>

Larin was selected for the [[2016 MLS All-Star Game]] in [[San Jose, California]], coming on in place of [[Sebastian Giovinco]] for the final 14 minutes of a 2–1 loss to England's [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]].<ref name="2016 MLS All-Stars">{{cite news|last1=Parker|first1=Graham|title=MLS All-Stars 1-2 Arsenal – as it happened|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2016/jul/28/mls-all-stars-v-arsenal-live|accessdate=July 29, 2016|work=The Guardian|date=July 29, 2016}}</ref>

====2017 season====
Larin scored the first three goals of Orlando's 2017 season, including a brace against Philadelphia. In the early hours of Thursday, June 15, Larin was arrested and charged with DUI.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Abnos|first1=Alexander|title=Orlando City striker Cyle Larin arrested for DUI |url=https://www.si.com/planet-futbol/2017/06/15/cyle-larin-dui-arrest-orlando-city-mls|accessdate=July 11, 2017|work=USA Today|date=June 15, 2017}}</ref> As a result, he missed three games while under assessment by MLS.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.orlandosentinel.com/sports/orlando-city-lions/on-the-pitch/os-sp-orlando-city-striker-cyle-larin-suspended-after-dui-20170627-story.html|title=Orlando City striker Cyle Larin cleared for competition after DUI charge, suspension|last=DelGallo|first=Alicia|work=OrlandoSentinel.com|access-date=July 12, 2017|language=en-US}}</ref> After scoring 12 goals in the 2017 season, Larin indicated a desire to move to Europe in the offseason.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.orlandosentinel.com/sports/orlando-city-lions/on-the-pitch/os-sp-orlando-city-cyle-larin-1026-story.html|title=Orlando City SC's Cyle Larin: Time to go play in Europe|website=Orlando Sentinel|author=Alicia del Gallo|date=October 25, 2017}}</ref> His last match with Orlando City was a friendly match against [[Puerto Rico national football team|Puerto Rico]] to raise money for the devastation caused by [[Hurricane María]] where he scored two goals.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://noticias.elhuracanazulpr.com/2017/11/cae-puerto-rico-6-1-ante-orlando-city-sc.html|title=Cae Puerto Rico 6-1 ante Orlando City SC|first=Publicado por Javier|last=Torres}}</ref>

===Beşiktaş===
Larin was sold to [[Beşiktaş J.K.|Beşiktaş]] in Turkey in January 2018, after refusing to show up to practice with Orlando City, with whom he was still under contract. The transfer fee was undisclosed.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.orlandocitysc.com/post/2018/01/30/orlando-city-sc-comes-terms-be-ikta-jk-transfer-cyle-larin|title=Orlando City SC Comes to Terms with Beşiktaş J.K. for Transfer of Cyle Larin|website=orlandocitysc.com|publisher=MLS|date=January 30, 2018|accessdate=January 31, 2018}}</ref> On April 7, Larin scored on his debut with Beşiktaş in a 5–1 home [[Süper Lig]] win over [[Göztepe S.K.|Göztepe]], minutes after entering as a late substitute.<ref>{{cite news |title=Cyle Larin scores first goal for Besiktas |url=https://sbisoccer.com/2018/04/cyle-larin-scores-first-goal-for-besiktas |accessdate=June 18, 2018 |publisher=SBI |date=April 7, 2018}}</ref>  In the last game of the season on May 19, he scored a hat-trick in a 5–1 win over [[Sivasspor]] also at the [[Vodafone Park]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Henry |first1=Larry Jr. |title=Cyle Larin concludes season with hat-trick for Besiktas |url=https://sbisoccer.com/2018/05/cyle-larin-concludes-season-with-hat-trick-for-besiktas |accessdate=June 18, 2018 |publisher=SBI |date=May 19, 2018}}</ref>

In the second leg of the second qualifying round against the Faroese side [[B36 Torshavn]] in the [[2018–19 UEFA Europa League]], Larin scored three goals in a 6–0 win (8–0 aggregate) on August 2.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Butler |first1=Dylan |title=Ex-Orlando striker Cyle Larin nets hat trick for Besiktas in Europa League |url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2018/08/02/ex-orlando-striker-cyle-larin-nets-hat-trick-besiktas-europa-league |accessdate=August 5, 2018 |publisher=Major League Soccer |date=August 2, 2018}}</ref> This made him the second player after [[Demba Ba]] to bag a hat-trick for the Black Eagles in a European competition.<ref>{{cite news |title=Larin Demba Ba'nın rekoruna ortak oldu |url=http://tr.beinsports.com/haber/larin-demba-banin-rekoruna-ortak-oldu |language=Turkish |accessdate=August 3, 2018 |publisher=beIN Sports TR |date=August 3, 2018}}</ref>

Zulte Waregem|Zulte Waregem]] in July 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.essevee.be/nl/team-essevee/nieuws/officieel-cyle-larin-naar-essevee|title=Officieel: Cyle Larin naar Essevee|date= July 5, 2019|language=Dutch}}</ref> He scored seven times in his season in the [[Belgian First Division A]], including two in a 6–0 win over [[Cercle Brugge]] on October 5.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Ghislain |first1=Pierre |title=Zulte cartonne et revient dans le top 6, Waasland se réveille: très mauvaise affaire pour le Cercle et Eupen|trans-title=Zulte do well and return to the top 6, Waasland wake up: very bad news for Cercle and Eupen|url=https://www.walfoot.be/news/2019-10-05/waasland-beveren-se-donne-de-lair-le-cercle-senfonce-zulte-waregem-dans-le-top-6 |access-date=January 28, 2021 |publisher=WalFoot |date=October 5, 2019 |language=French}}</ref>

After returning to his parent club, on November 29, 2020, Larin was sent off in a 4–3 home win against [[Istanbul]] rivals [[Fenerbahçe S.K. ===Club Brugge===

===Valladolid===
In January 2023, Larin was loaned to [[La Liga]] side [[Real Valladolid|Valladolid]] for the remainder of the season, with an option to buy.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realvalladolid.es/noticia/cyle-larin-polvora-blanquivioleta|date=January 24, 2023|title=CYLE LARIN, PÓLVORA BLANQUIVIOLETA}}</ref> He made his debut on January 29 against [[Valencia CF|Valencia]], scoring the only goal in a 1–0 victory despite coming on as a late substitute.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sportsnet.ca/soccer/article/canadas-cyle-larin-has-dream-debut-in-spain-scores-winner-for-real-valladolid/|date=January 29, 2023|title=Canada's Cyle Larin has dream debut in Spain, scores winner for Real Valladolid|work=[[Sportsnet]]}}</ref> He scored again in a 0–1 victory on 5 February against [[Real Sociedad]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.as.com/soccer/canadian-striker-cyle-larin-is-the-striker-ronaldo-was-looking-for-n/?omnil=resrelrecomv|date=February 5, 2023|title=Canadian striker Cyle Larin is the striker Ronaldo was looking for|work=[[Diario AS]]}}</ref> His loan was made permanent at the end of the season.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realvalladolid.es/noticias/larin-2025|title=Larin 2025|publisher=Real Valladolid|language=es|date=June 26, 2023|accessdate=July 2, 2023}}</ref>

==International career==

After Larin's impressive first-year season at the University of Connecticut, Canada national team manager [[Benito Floro]] called him in for a senior national training camp in [[Florida]] in January 2014 — Larin's first national callup at any level, senior or junior.<ref name="Striker of the Future">{{cite web|last=Squizzato|first=Daniel|title=CanNMT: College sensation Cyle Larin hoping to prove training camp in Austria and friendly matches against [[Bulgaria national football team|Bulgaria]] and [[Moldova national football team|Moldova]] on May 23 and 27, respectively.<ref name="[2014-05] Larin was named in Canada's [[2015 CONCACAF U-20 Championship]] squad on January 5 that year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wakingthered.com/2015/1/5/7493867/canada-names-squad-for-concacaf-u20-championships|title=Canada names squad for CONCACAF U20 Championships|first=Dave|last=Rowaan|date=January 5, 2015}}</ref> Five days later, he made his tournament debut in a 3–1 win over [[Haiti national under-20 football team|Haiti]],<ref name="U20 Debut"/> as Canada exited in the group stage in Jamaica. In June, Larin netted in each leg of Canada's 6–0 aggregate win over [[Dominica national football team|Dominica]] in the [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification – CONCACAF second round|second round of qualification]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]].<ref name="Dominica v. 8, 2015}}</ref>

Larin was not named in manager [[Octavio Zambrano]]'s initial Canada squad for the [[2017 CONCACAF Gold Cup]] due to his recent charge for [[driving under the influence]], but was named in the squad for the knockout stage.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Day |first1=Gavin |title=Cyle Larin aiming to make up for lost time with Canada at Gold Cup |url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2017/07/20/cyle-larin-aiming-make-lost-time-canada-gold-cup |accessdate=June 18, 2018 |publisher=Major League Soccer |date=July 20, 2017}}</ref> He started in the quarter-final against Jamaica at the [[University of Phoenix Stadium]], and was substituted for [[Lucas Cavallini]] after 56 minutes of the 2–1 loss.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Panizo |first1=Franco |title=Jamaica 2, Canada 1 |url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2017/07/20/jamaica-2-canada-1-2017-concacaf-gold-cup-match-recap |accessdate=June 18, 2018 |publisher=Major League Soccer |date=July 20, 2017}}</ref>

Larin was named to the final 23-man squad for the [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup]] on May 30, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.canadasoccer.com/together-we-rise-canada-soccer-announces-squad-for-the-2019-concacaf-gold-cup-p162198|date=May 30, 2019|title=Together We Rise: Canada Soccer announces squad for the 2019 Concacaf Gold Cup|access-date=June 24, 2019|archive-date=July 24, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200724182520/https://www.canadasoccer.com/together-we-rise-canada-soccer-announces-squad-for-the-2019-concacaf-gold-cup-p162198|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On March 25, 2021, Larin scored his first hat-trick with Canada, in a 5–1 win over [[Bermuda national football team|Bermuda]] in the team's first [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – CONCACAF first round|2022 World Cup qualifying]] match.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tsn.ca/cyle-larin-alphonso-davies-combine-to-help-canada-down-bermuda-in-world-cup-qualifier-1.1613675|title=Larin, Davies combine to help Canada down Bermuda in World Cup qualifier|publisher=tsn.ca|date=March 25, 2021}}</ref> He was called up for the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup]] on July 1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.canadasoccer.com/news/canada-soccer-announces-2021-concacaf-gold-cup-roster/|title=CANADA SOCCER ANNOUNCES 2021 CONCACAF GOLD CUP ROSTER|date=July 1, 2021|publisher=Canada Soccer}}</ref> He scored the equalizer in the team's opening 4–1 win over [[Martinique national football team|Martinique]] and two goals in a victory by the same score over [[Haiti national football team|Haiti]], as they reached the semi-finals; the brace in the latter game put him at 10 goals in 8 games in 2021.<ref>{{cite news |last1=D'Amours |first1=Tristan |title=Recap: Canada 4, Martinique 1 |url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/recap-canada-3-martinique-1 |access-date=August 2, 2021 |publisher=Major League Soccer |date=July 12, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Cyle Larin scores twice as Canada handily defeats depleted Haiti at Gold Cup |url=https://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/cyle-larin-scores-twice-canada-handily-defeats-haiti-gold-cup-recap-july-15-1.6100328 |access-date=August 2, 2021 |publisher=CBC |date=July 15, 2021}}</ref>

Larin tied Dwayne De Rosario's record for most goals for Canada (22) on November 16, 2021, scoring twice in a 2–1 home win over [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]] in 2022 World Cup qualifying, a team Canada had not beaten for 21 years.<ref>{{Cite web|date=November 17, 2021|title=Match Analysis: CanMNT 2-1 Mexico -- World Cup Qualifying|url=https://canpl.ca/article/match-analysis-canmnt-2-1-mexico-world-cup-qualifying|access-date=November 17, 2021|website=Canadian Premier League|language=en-CA}}</ref> On January 30, 2022, he scored the opening goal of a 2–0 home win in a qualifier against the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]], surpassing De Rosario's record and became the [[Canada men's national soccer team records and statistics#Team records|all-time top goalscorer]] for Canada.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tsn.ca/cyle-larin-canadian-men-s-team-goals-record-1.1752627|title=Larin becomes Canadian men's team all-time leading scorer
|publisher=tsn.ca|date=January 30, 2022}}</ref> On March 27, he opened the scoring in a 4–0 win over Jamaica as the Canadians made the World Cup for the first time since [[1986 FIFA World Cup|1986]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Canada beat Jamaica to end 36-year World Cup finals drought |url=https://www.reuters.com/lifestyle/sports/canada-beat-jamaica-end-36-year-world-cup-finals-drought-2022-03-27/ |access-date=March 31, 2022 |work=Reuters |date=March 28, 2022}}</ref> He finished the qualification campaign with 13 goals, the highest in the CONCACAF region and the second highest in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification|World Cup Qualification campaign]] overall.<ref>{{cite news |title=How the World Cup draw will work – and who are the teams to beat in Qatar? |url=https://theathletic.com/3219969/2022/03/31/how-the-world-cup-draw-will-work-and-who-are-the-teams-to-beat-in-qatar/ |access-date=March 31, 2022 |work=The Athletic |date=March 31, 2022}}</ref> In November 2022, Larin was confirmed as part of the 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canada-announces-26-man-squad-for-2022-fifa-world-cup-in-qatar|date=November 13, 2022|title=Canada announces 26-man squad for 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]|first=Charlie|last=O'Connor-Clarke}}</ref>

In June 2023 Larin was called-up to the Canadian squad contesting the [[2023 CONCACAF Nations League Finals]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Former CPLers Loturi, Zator called up by Canada for 2023 Concacaf Nations League Finals|date=June 7, 2023|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canada-canmnt-2023-concacaf-nations-league-final-roster|first=Mitchell|last=Tierney|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Larin holds a [[Green card|U.S. green card]], which qualifies him as a domestic player for MLS roster purposes.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/OrlandoCitySC/posts/1315955705129381|title=Orlando City Soccer Club|website=www.facebook.com}}</ref>

In June 2017, Larin was arrested by [[Florida Highway Patrol]] for driving the wrong way on a 4-lane road in [[Orlando, Florida|Orlando]] and given a breathalyzer test that showed that he was above the legal blood alcohol level. He was suspended from MLS until he completed a league-mandated substance abuse assessment, and this ban was lifted by the end of the month.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Davidson |first1=Neil |title=Canadian soccer star Cyle Larin arrested for drunk driving in Florida |url=http://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/mls/canadian-soccer-star-cyle-larin-arrested-drunk-driving-florida-1.4162090 |accessdate=June 18, 2018 |agency=The Canadian Press |publisher=CBC |date=June 15, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Schuller |first1=Rudi |title='I have to gain everyone's trust back' — Orlando striker Larin reflects on DUI arrest |url=http://www.goal.com/en-us/news/i-have-to-gain-everyones-trust-back-orlando-striker-larin/1ut86k720dg2j12ou4wny89wir |accessdate=June 18, 2018 |publisher=Goal.com |date=July 3, 2017}}</ref>

In August 2021, Larin joined Canadian club [[Simcoe County Rovers FC|Simcoe County Rovers]] of [[League1 Ontario]] as a co-owner.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://theathletic.com/2773102/2021/08/16/cyle-larin-simcoe-county-rovers/|title=Cyle Larin becomes part owner of new Canadian club, hopes to develop pathway for young players to reach highest levels|first=Joshua|last=Kloke|work=[[The Athletic]]|date=August 16, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.simcoe.com/news-story/10461748-simcoe-county-rovers-announce-co-owner-cyle-larin/|title=Simcoe County Rovers announce co-owner Cyle Larin|date=August 22, 2021|work=[[Barrie Advance]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.barrietoday.com/local-sports/as-soccer-gains-momentum-in-canada-local-rovers-make-big-moves-4233682|title=As soccer gains momentum in Canada, local Rovers make big moves|date=August 19, 2021|work=Barrie Today|first=Shawn|last=Gibson}}</ref>
