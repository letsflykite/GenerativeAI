'''Kasper Hjulmand''' (born 9 April 1972) is a Danish [[Association football|football]] manager and a former player. He is the head coach for the [[Denmark national football team|Denmark national team]], with whom he reached the semi-finals of [[Euro 2020]].

==Background==
He was born in Gandrup, a small town with 1.500 inhabitants about 15 kilometers east of the city of Aalborg and grew up in the town of Auning, a town with about 3.000 inhabitans on the peninsula of Djursland.
His parents was teachers. 
As a 19-year-old, he started at Vejle Sports Academy, where he had the subjects volleyball, psychology and philosophy.
After his time at the sports academy, he studied sports at the University of Copenhagen, and has a B.Sc in sports with a specialization in coaching. For a short period he also studied Sports Management at the University of North Florida.

==Playing career==
A [[defender (association football)|defender]],<ref>{{Worldfootball.net|kasper-hjulmand}}</ref> Hjulmand began his career with [[Randers Freja]] in 1987 where he played four years, and then signed with [[Herlev IF]] in the winter of 1992. He played with [[Herlev IF]] two seasons and then moved to [[Boldklubben 1893|B.93]] in the winter of 1995.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dbu.dk/page.aspx?id=3777|title=Kasper Hjulmands Stats}}</ref> He also played for the [[North Florida Ospreys men's soccer|North Florida Ospreys]] in 1994, scoring six goals in 18 appearances.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://static.unfospreys.com/custompages/Archives/MSOC/1994.htm|title=Statistics Summary for 1994|website=static.unfospreys.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.jacksonville.com/sports/20190612/unf-grad-kasper-hjulmand-named-denmarks-soccer-coach|title=UNF grad Kasper Hjulmand named Denmark's soccer coach|first=Times-Union and|last=AP|website=The Florida Times-Union}}</ref>
After three seasons with B93 at the age of 26 Hjulmand was forced to retire as a football player due to a knee injury.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.kicker.de/tuchels-nachfolger-ist-da_hjulmand-passt-genau-604529/artikel|title=Tuchels Nachfolger ist da: Hjulmand "passt genau"|website=kicker}}</ref>

==Coaching career==

===Early career===
Hjulmand became head coach of [[Lyngby Boldklub|Lyngby]] from 1 January 2006<ref name="Lyngby BK » Manager history">{{cite web|title=Lyngby BK » Manager history|url=http://www.worldfootball.net/teams/lyngby-bk/9/|publisher=Worldfootball.net|access-date=23 October 2014}}</ref> until 7 July 2008 when he became an assistant coach with [[FC Nordsjælland]].<ref name="Kasper Hjulmand træner i FCN">{{cite news|title=Kasper Hjulmand træner i FCN|url=http://politiken.dk/sport/fodbold/ECE535792/kasper-hjulmand-traener-i-fcn/|access-date=23 October 2014|publisher=politiken.dk|date=7 July 2008|language=da}}</ref> He was named the successor of [[Morten Wieghorst]] at FC Nordsjælland taking over from 1 July 2011<ref name="FC Nordsjælland » Manager history">{{cite web|title=FC Nordsjælland » Manager history|url=http://www.worldfootball.net/teams/fc-nordsjaelland/9/|publisher=Worldfootball.net|access-date=23 October 2014}}</ref> until 18 May 2014<ref name="Hjulmand übernimmt Tuchels Job">{{cite news|title=Hjulmand übernimmt Tuchels Job|url=http://www.sueddeutsche.de/sport/neuer-trainer-bei-mainz-hjulmand-uebernimmt-tuchels-job-1.1964769|access-date=16 May 2014|newspaper=[[Süddeutsche Zeitung]]|date=16 May 2014|language=de}}</ref> when he had his final match as head coach of Nordsjælland; a 2–2 draw against [[Brøndby IF|Brøndby]].<ref name="2013–14 season">{{cite web|title=FC Nordsjaelland|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/intligen/intwettbewerbe/sas-ligaen/2013-14/fc-nordsjlland/vereinstermine.html|website=kicker.de|publisher=kicker|access-date=23 October 2014|language=de}}</ref> Nordsjælland won their first ever Danish championship with Hjulmand as head coach, despite having one of the lowest budgets in the league. He also guided them to the UEFA Champions League group stages. .<ref>{{cite news|title=Nordsjælland claim first Danish title|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=den/news/newsid=1801375.html|access-date=23 October 2014|publisher=UEFA|date=23 May 2012|language=de}}</ref>

===Mainz 05===
On 15 May 2014, Hjulmand was confirmed as [[Thomas Tuchel]]'s successor at [[1. FSV Mainz 05]] for the following season.<ref name="Hjulmand übernimmt Tuchels Job"/> His first two matches in charge was in the [[2014–15 UEFA Europa League qualifying phase and play-off round#Third qualifying round|Third qualifying round]] of the [[2014–15 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]]. In the first leg against [[Asteras Tripoli F.C.|Asteras Tripoli]], Mainz won 1–0,<ref>{{cite news|title=Dank Okazaki: Mainz in der Pole-Position|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/uefa/qualifikation/europa-league-qualifikation/2014-15/3/2505696/spielbericht_1-fsv-mainz-05-30_asteras-tripolis.html|access-date=7 August 2014|publisher=kicker|date=31 July 2014|language=de}}</ref> and in the second leg, Mainz lost 3–1 and they got knocked out of the campaign.<ref>{{cite news|title=Mainz 05 verpasst Playoffs zur Europa League|url=http://www.sueddeutsche.de/sport/fussball-mainz-verpasst-playoffs-zur-europa-league-1.2081872|access-date=7 August 2014|publisher=Süddeutsche Zeitung|date=7 August 2014|language=de}}</ref> Then Mainz were knocked out in the first round of the DFB Cup after losing to [[Chemnitzer FC]] in a shoot–out.<ref>{{cite news|title=10:9! Fink setzt dem Wahnsinn die Krone auf|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/dfbpokal/spielrunde/dfb-pokal/2014-15/1/2397913/spielbericht_chemnitzer-fc-63_1-fsv-mainz-05-30.html|access-date=17 February 2015|publisher=kicker|date=15 August 2014|language=de}}</ref> In his first eight league matches, Mainz were undefeated and in third place.<ref name="Wie erwartet: Aus für FSV-Coach Hjulmand">{{cite news|title=Wie erwartet: Aus für FSV-Coach Hjulmand|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/startseite/621276/artikel_wie-erwartet_aus-fuer-fsv-coach-hjulmand.html|access-date=17 February 2015|publisher=kicker|date=17 February 2015|language=de}}</ref> After this, Mainz had won only one of their 13 matches and dropped down to 14th place.<ref name="Wie erwartet: Aus für FSV-Coach Hjulmand"/> At this point, on 17 February 2015, he was sacked.<ref name="Siebter (!) Kreuzbandriss zwang Schmidt in die Knie">{{cite news|title=Siebter (!) Kreuzbandriss zwang Schmidt in die Knie|url=https://www.welt.de/sport/fussball/bundesliga/1-fsv-mainz/article137546239/Siebter-Kreuzbandriss-zwang-Schmidt-in-die-Knie.html|access-date=17 February 2015|publisher=Die Welt|date=17 February 2015|language=de}}</ref> His final match was a 4–2 loss to [[Borussia Dortmund]].<ref>{{cite web|title=1. FSV Mainz 05|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/vereine/1-bundesliga/2014-15/1-fsv-mainz-05-30/vereinstermine.html|website=kicker.de|publisher=kicker|access-date=17 February 2015|language=de}}</ref>

===Return to Nordsjælland===
On 15 December 2015, it was announced that Hjulmand would return as manager of Nordsjælland as of 1 January 2016.<ref>[http://fcn.dk/2015/12/fc-nordsjaelland-faar-nye-ejere-og-skifter-cheftraener/ FC Nordsjælland får nye ejere og skifter cheftræner], [[FC Nordsjælland]], 15 December 2015</ref> Hjulmand managed to get the team back on track and the club finished 9th in the [[2015–16 Danish Superliga]]. In the following two seasons he led Nordsjælland to a sixth and third place. In January 2019, Hjulmand was linked with [[RSC Anderlecht]], but Nordsjælland wanted a compensation that Anderlecht did not want to pay and instead [[Fred Rutten]] was appointed.<ref>[https://www.tipsbladet.dk/nyhed/superliga/anderlecht-traeners-saerlige-kontrakt-aabner-doeren-paa-klem-hjulmand Anderlecht-træners særlige kontrakt: Åbner dør på klem for Hjulmand], ''[[Tipsbladet]]'', 7 January 2019</ref> Following the failed contract with Anderlecht Hjulmand announced that he would leave Nordsjælland in the summer of 2019 at the end of his contract.<ref>[https://www.tipsbladet.dk/nyhed/superliga/kasper-hjulmand-stopper-i-fcn-til-sommer Kasper Hjulmand stopper i FCN til sommer], ''[[Tipsbladet]]'', 7 January 2019</ref>

On 25 March 2019, Hjulmand left Nordsjælland by mutual consent after having secured a spot in the Championship round of the [[2018–19 Danish Superliga]].<ref>[https://www.bold.dk/fodbold/nyheder/hjulmand-stopper-oejeblikkeligt-i-fcn/ Hjulmand stopper øjeblikkeligt i FCN], bold.dk, 25 March 2019</ref>

===Denmark national team===
In June 2019, it was announced that Hjulmand would replace [[Åge Hareide]] as manager of the [[Denmark national football team]], when Hareide's contract expired after the [[UEFA Euro 2020]] tournament.<ref>[https://www.dr.dk/sporten/fodbold/landsholdet/kasper-hjulmand-bliver-ny-traener-herrelandsholdet-i-fodbold Kasper Hjulmand bliver ny træner for herrelandsholdet i fodbold], ''[[Danmarks Radio]]'', 12 June 2019</ref> Hareide did not lead the team at the Euro 2020, since the tournament was postponed due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>[https://www.bold.dk/fodbold/nyheder/hjulmand-er-startet-som-landstraener-foer-tid/ Hjulmand er startet som landstræner før tid], bold.dk, 18 May 2020</ref> In the Euro 2020, he led Denmark to reach the semi-finals, in which they lost 2–1 after extra-time and a controversial penalty against [[England national football team|England]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/12351311/england-2-1-denmark-kasper-hjulmand-bitter-over-raheem-sterling-penalty-call-in-euro-2020-semi-final |title=England 2-1 Denmark: Kasper Hjulmand bitter over Raheem Sterling penalty call in Euro 2020 semi-final |website=Sky Sports |date=8 July 2021}}</ref> He later led Denmark to finish second in their group in the [[2022–23 UEFA Nations League A]], only one point behind [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]].<ref>{{cite web |author=Will Magee |url=https://www.eurosport.com/football/uefa-nations-league/2022-2023/denmark-v-france-nations-league-live_sto9156688/story.shtml |title=Denmark 2-0 France: Danes ease to win over France, finish second in Nations League group |website=Eurosport |date=25 September 2022 }}</ref> In the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], Denmark finished last in their group with one draw and two defeats, in which they scored only once in the tournament.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.reuters.com/lifestyle/sports/dark-horses-denmark-exit-world-cup-attack-fails-fire-2022-11-30/ |title=Dark horses Denmark exit World Cup as attack fails to fire |website=Reuters |date=30 November 2022 }}</ref>
