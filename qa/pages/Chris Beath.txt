'''Chris James Beath''' (born 17 November 1984) is an Australian [[Association football|football]] [[Referee (association football)|referee]] in the [[A-League]].<ref name="refprofile1">{{cite web|url=http://www.a-league.com.au/default.aspx?s=refereeprofile&pid=1421|title=Profile - Chris Beath|work=Official A-League Website|publisher=Football Federation Australia|access-date=26 September 2009|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101104103812/http://a-league.com.au/default.aspx?s=refereeprofile&pid=1421|archive-date=4 November 2010}}</ref><ref name="abc1">{{cite news|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/stories/2009/11/23/2751355.htm|title=Brink pushes referee over the edge|date=23 November 2009|work=ABC News|publisher=Australian Broadcasting Corporation|agency=[[Australian Associated Press|AAP]]|access-date=28 April 2010}}</ref>

==Refereeing career==
He has refereed at the National Youth Championships in 2003 and in 2004.<ref name="refprofile1" />

Beath was the referee of the tournament at the 2002 Kanga Cup.<ref name="kanga1">{{cite web|url=http://kangacup.ausref.com/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=15&Itemid=32|title=Kanga Cup Referees - Hall of Fame|work=Kanga Cup|publisher=AusRef Football Referees Australia|access-date=28 April 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304092712/http://kangacup.ausref.com/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=15&Itemid=32|archive-date=4 March 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Beath was added to the [[FIFA International Referees List]] in 2011,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.footballaustralia.com.au/2009GameDev/default.aspx?s=ccommunity_referees_news_news_item_new&id=37562|publisher=Football Federation Australia |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101231071930/http://www.footballaustralia.com.au/2009GameDev/default.aspx?s=ccommunity_referees_news_news_item_new&id=37562 |archive-date=31 December 2010 |title=2011 FIFA Match Officials|date=17 December 2010|access-date=16 September 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/associations/association=aus/footballofficials/referees/peoplekind=ref.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070706032557/http://www.fifa.com/associations/association=aus/footballofficials/referees/peoplekind=ref.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=6 July 2007 |title=Referees by Association - Australia |website=FIFA.com |language=en-GB |access-date=16 September 2017}}</ref> serving as a referee for matches including the Kirin Challenge Cup contest between [[Japan national football team|Japan]] and [[Iceland national football team|Iceland]].<ref>Morgan, Michael. [https://www.rsssf.org/tablesi/ijs-intres2012.html "International matches of Iceland 2012 - Details"]. ''[[Rec.Sport.Soccer Statistics Foundation|RSSSF]]'', 7 February 2013. Retrieved on 1 June 2013.</ref>

In 2015, Beath was involved in an exchange program where he refereed in the [[J-League]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.adelaidenow.com.au/sport/football/aleague-chris-beath-to-referee-100th-game-in-sydney-derby-and-says-fulltime-refs-a-matter-of-time/news-story/d3fcfe1e9b0fab655067823e874942a0 |title=A-League: Chris Beath to referee 100th game in Sydney derby and says full-time refs a matter of time |last=Monteverde |first=Marco |date=27 February 2015 |work=The Advertiser|location=Adelaide|access-date=16 February 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.a-league.com.au/news/ffa-jfa-referee-exchange-program-continues |title=FFA-JFA Referee Exchange Program continues|work=A-League.com.au |date=10 March 2016 |access-date=16 February 2018 |language=en}}</ref>

On 4 April 2017, Chris Beath was appointed as one of the inaugural [[video assistant referee]]s (VARs) in the [[Hyundai A-League]], the first top-tier football league in the world to implement the technology.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.a-league.com.au/article/hyundai-a-league-first--to-use-video-assistant-referees/1g3x9yaxih0dp1aaokfqi2uihy |title=Hyundai A-League first  to use Video Assistant Referees |website=Hyundai A-League |publisher=Football Federation Australia |access-date=16 September 2017}}</ref>

In January 2018, Beath was selected as one of the referees for the [[2018 AFC U-23 Championship]] held in China. Beath refereed the opening fixture of this tournament.

On 5 December 2018, Beath was appointed to be a referee at the [[2019 AFC Asian Cup]] in the [[United Arab Emirates]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.the-afc.com/competitions/afc-asian-cup/latest/news/largest-ever-cast-of-match-officials-appointed-for-uae-2019|title=Largest-ever cast of match officials appointed for UAE 2019|website=www.the-afc.com|language=en-GB|access-date=4 July 2019}}</ref>

In August 2021, after the matches Mexico - France (4–1) and the quarter final Brazil - Egypt (1–0), Beath was appointed to the [[Brazil national under-23 football team|Brazil]] vs [[Spain national under-23 football team|Spain]] 2-1 after extra time [[Football at the 2020 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament – Final|Gold Medal Match]] at the [[COVID-19 pandemic|COVID]] delayed [[2020 Summer Olympics]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://olympics.com/tokyo-2020/olympic-games/en/results/football/results-men-fnl-000100-.htm|title=Brazil vs Spain - Gold Medal Match Results|access-date=7 August 2021|archive-date=12 August 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210812012509/https://olympics.com/tokyo-2020/olympic-games/en/results/football/results-men-fnl-000100-.htm|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Beath was also appointed to officiate in the [[2021 FIFA Club World Cup]]. He refereed [[Al Ahly SC]] victory 1–0 over [[C. F. Monterrey]] and also officiated the [[2021 FIFA Club World Cup Final]] between [[Chelsea F.C.]] and [[Palmeiras]]. 

In 2022, Beath was appointed to the [[2022 AFC Cup Final]] as [[Video assistant referee|VAR]].

Beath was selected to officiate at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar,<ref>{{Cite web |title=36 referees, 69 assistant referees and 24 video match officials appointed for FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022™ |url=https://www.fifa.com/tournaments/mens/worldcup/qatar2022/media-releases/origin1904-p.cxm.fifa.com36-referees-69-assistant-referees-and-24-video-match-officials-appointed-for-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022 |access-date=2022-11-17 |website=www.fifa.com |language=en }}{{Dead link|date=March 2023 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> officating the [[2022 FIFA World Cup Group C|Group C]] match between Mexico and Poland. <ref>{{Cite web |title=2022 FIFA World Cup - Mexico vs Poland Match Report |url=https://www.fifa.com/fifaplus/en/match-centre/match/17/255711/285063/400235463}}</ref>

Beath announced his retirement from refereeing on 1 June 2023, shortly after being appointed as the referee for the [[2023 A-League Men Grand Final]]. He holds the record for matches officiated in the A-League, which will be 234 after his final game. Following the grand final, his final appointments will be as a VAR official at the [[2023 FIFA Women's World Cup|2023 Women's World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.footballaustralia.com.au/news/chris-beath-whistles-full-time-decorated-career|title=Chris Beath whistles full-time on decorated career|website=[[Football Australia]]|date=1 June 2023}}</ref>
