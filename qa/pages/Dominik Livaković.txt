'''Dominik Livaković''' ({{IPA-sh|dôminik liʋǎːkoʋitɕ, domǐ-|hr}};<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hjp.znanje.hr/index.php?show=search_by_id&id=f1ZhWhc%3D|title=Dȍminik|quote=Dȍminik (Domìnik)|website=Hrvatski jezični portal|access-date=17 March 2018|language=sh}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://hjp.znanje.hr/index.php?show=search_by_id&id=e19uWxc%3D&keyword=lijevi|title=lijȇvī|quote=Liváković|website=Hrvatski jezični portal|access-date=17 March 2018|language=sh}}</ref> born 9 January 1995) is a Croatian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Croatian First Football League|Prva HNL]] club [[GNK Dinamo Zagreb|Dinamo Zagreb]] and the [[Croatia national football team|Croatia national team]].

==Club career==
Livaković became part of [[NK Zagreb]]'s first team at the beginning of the [[2012–13Croatian First Football League|2012–13 season]]. Initially the club's second-choice goalkeeper, he made his league debut on 31 August 2012, playing the full match in a 1–0 defeat at home to [[HNK Cibalia]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Zagreb vs Cibalia|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2012/08/31/croatia/1-hnl/nk-zagreb/hnk-cibalia-vinkovci/1287280/|publisher=Soccerway|access-date=29 September 2014}}</ref> He soon became the club's number one and made a total of 104 league appearances over four seasons, including 90 in the [[Prva HNL]].

On 30 August 2015, he agreed to join [[GNK Dinamo Zagreb|Dinamo Zagreb]] at the beginning of the [[2016–17 GNK Dinamo Zagreb season|2016–17 season]]. He made his league debut for the club on 2 October 2016 in a goalless draw at home to [[HNK Hajduk Split|Hajduk Split]]. On 18 October 2016, he made his first appearance in the [[UEFA Champions League]] in a 1–0 defeat at home to [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]].

On 30 July 2019, in a [[2019–20 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] [[2019–20 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round#Second qualifying round|qualifying]] 3–0 victory over [[FC Saburtalo Tbilisi|Saburtalo Tbilisi]], Livaković broke [[Dražen Ladić]]'s record for most minutes from the start of Dinamo's season without conceding a goal (413), set in [[1995–96 Croatian First Football League|1995]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=30 July 2019|title=Livaković postao novi klupski rekorder|url=https://gnkdinamo.hr/hr/Novosti/Clanak/livakovic-postao-novi-klupski-rekorder|access-date=6 September 2020|website=GNK Dinamo|language=hr-HR}}</ref> He conceded his first goal of the [[2019–20 GNK Dinamo Zagreb season|season]] three days later, in a 3–1 [[2019–20 Croatian First Football League|league]] victory over [[HNK Gorica|Gorica]], set the new record at 535.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hina|date=2 August 2019|title=Dinamo slomio Goricu: Olmo zabio i asistirao, Lovrić matirao Livakovića|url=https://gol.dnevnik.hr/clanak/rubrika/nogomet/dinamo-slomio-goricu-olmo-zabio-i-asistirao---570406.html|access-date=6 September 2020|website=Gol.hr|language=hr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2 August 2019|title=Dinamo Zagreb 2019-20 - Forum - Sportnet.hr|url=https://sportnet.rtl.hr/sportnetklub/nogomet-1/dinamo-zagreb-2019-20-358600/p5836154/#poruka5836154|access-date=6 September 2020|website=sportnet.rtl.hr|language=hr}}</ref>

==International career==
Livaković received his first call-up to the [[Croatia national football team]] for their friendly against [[Moldova national football team|Moldova]] in May 2016.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.skysports.com/football/croatia-vs-moldova/teams/354998 |title=Croatia v Moldova |publisher=skysports.com|date=27 May 2016 |access-date=10 June 2016}}</ref> He made his debut against [[Chile national football team|Chile]] in the [[2017 China Cup]] where Croatia lost on penalties.

In May 2018 Livaković was named to Croatia's preliminary 32-man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia, and was eventually part of the [[2018 FIFA World Cup squads#Croatia|team]] that finished runners-up after losing the [[2018 FIFA World Cup Final|final]] against [[France national football team|France]].<ref>{{cite web| url = http://www.goal.com/en-gb/amp/news/revealed-every-world-cup-2018-squad-23-man-preliminary-lists/oa0atsduflsv1nsf6oqk576rb| title = Revealed: Every World Cup 2018 squad - Final 23-man lists {{!}} Goal.com}}</ref> He made his competitive debut against [[England national football team|England]] in the [[2018–19 UEFA Nations League A|2018–19 Nations League]] match held on 12 October 2018 at [[Stadion Rujevica]] in [[Rijeka]], which ended in a scoreless draw.

After disappointing performances by [[Lovre Kalinić]], Livaković became Croatia's first-choice goalkeeper for the [[UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying Group E|UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying]] campaign, starting with matchday 3 against [[Wales national football team|Wales]], held on 8 June 2019.

On 5 December 2022, in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] [[2022 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Japan vs Croatia|round of 16]] match against [[Japan national football team|Japan]], Livaković was named man of the match after saving three penalties in the shoot-out to help his side advance to the [[2022 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Croatia vs Brazil|quarter-finals]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Japan-Croatia |url=https://www.fifa.com/fifaplus/en/match-centre/match/17/255711/285073/400128132 |website=FIFA.com |publisher=FIFA.com |access-date=5 December 2022 |date=5 December 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=HNS on Twitter |url=https://twitter.com/HNS_CFF/status/1599827122901557248 |website=Twitter |publisher=HNS |access-date=5 December 2022 |date=5 December 2022}}</ref> He became the third goalkeeper to make three saves in a World Cup shoot-out, after [[Ricardo (footballer, born 1976)|Ricardo]] in [[2006 FIFA World Cup|2006]] and his fellow countryman [[Danijel Subašić]] in 2018. In the aforementioned quarter-finals, he saved another penalty in the shoot-out against [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]], helping Croatia advance to the [[2022 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Argentina vs Croatia|semi-finals]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Livakovic saves three penalties as Croatia advances. == Personal life ==
Livaković hails from a prominent family. His father Zdravko Livaković is a [[Construction engineering|construction engineer]] and a former State Secretary in the [[Ministry of the Sea, Transport and Infrastructure (Croatia)|Ministry of the Sea, Transport and Infrastructure]] during the mandate of [[Božidar Kalmeta]]. His paternal grandfather and grandmother were a [[radiologist]] and an English teacher, respectively.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |last=Mikuličin |first=Ivana |date=29 July 2018 |title=DOMINIK LIVAKOVIĆ 'U mojoj obitelji svi su doktori i inženjeri, no ja sam od malena znao čime ću se baviti...' |url=https://www.jutarnji.hr/scena/domace-zvijezde/dominik-livakovic-u-mojoj-obitelji-svi-su-doktori-i-inzenjeri-no-ja-sam-od-malena-znao-cime-cu-se-baviti-7659474 |access-date=18 June 2022 |website=[[Jutarnji list]] |language=hr-hr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Puljić-Šego |first=Iva |date=4 November 2009 |title=Livaković: Glavna je zvijezda lupeški sindikalac koji ne dolazi na posao |url=https://www.vecernji.hr/vijesti/livakovic-glavna-je-zvijezda-lupeski-sindikalac-koji-ne-dolazi-na-posao-45365 |access-date=18 June 2022 |website=[[Večernji list]] |language=hr}}</ref> His mother Manuela Skoblar is [[Josip Skoblar]]'s first cousin once removed.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Tironi |first=Hrvoje |date=6 March 2019 |title=Moj dida i ja: Svemu me naučio, on je najzaslužniji za sve ovo... |url=https://www.24sata.hr/sport/moj-dida-i-ja-svemu-me-naucio-on-je-najzasluzniji-za-sve-ovo-617610 |access-date=18 June 2022 |website=[[24sata (Croatia)|24sata]] |language=hr}}</ref>

In June 2022, Livaković married his long-time girlfriend Helena Matić in the [[Zadar Cathedral]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=9 November 2022 |title=Helena Matic, Dominik Livakovic's Pretty Wife |url=https://fabwags.com/dominik-livakovic-wife-helena-livakovic/ |access-date=18 June 2022 |website=FabWags.com |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Banjeglav Perković |first=Gorana |date=18 June 2022 |title=Dominik Livaković i Helena Matić izgovorili sudbonosno da!
