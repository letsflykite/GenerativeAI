'''Ethan Kwame Colm Raymond Ampadu''' (born 14 September 2000) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre back]] or [[defensive midfielder]] for {{English football updater|Chelsea}} club [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]], and the [[Wales national football team|Wales national team]].

Ampadu began his career at [[Exeter City F.C.|Exeter City]] after coming through the club’s academy, where he became the youngest player to appear for the first team at age 15. He has also played for German club [[RB Leipzig]] and English club [[Sheffield United F.C.|Sheffield United]] on loan.

Ampadu is a Welsh international footballer. After playing for England at under-16 level, he played for Wales at under-16, under-17 and under-19 level, before making his senior debut for Wales in November 2017. He was part of the Wales squad that reached the round of 16 at [[UEFA Euro 2020]]. He was part of the first Welsh squad to qualify for a [[FIFA World Cup]] since 1958.

==Club career==
===Exeter City===
Born in [[Exeter]], [[Devon]], Ampadu is the son of former professional footballer [[Kwame Ampadu]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/36949680 |title=Exeter City 1–0 Brentford |work=BBC Sport |date=9 August 2016 |access-date=10 August 2016}}</ref> A product of the [[Exeter City F.C.|Exeter City]] youth academy, appearing for the under-18 squad at the age of 14,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/football/news/chelsea-teenager-ethan-ampadu-set-11519170 |title=Chelsea teenager Ethan Ampadu to be handed first Wales start – just a year after Exeter bow at 15|newspaper=Daily Mirror |date=14 November 2017|access-date=11 September 2018}}</ref> he made his senior debut – aged {{Age in years, months and days|2000|9|14|2016|8|9}} – in an [[EFL Cup]] first round match against [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]] at [[St James Park (Exeter)|St James Park]] on 9 August 2016, playing the full 120 minutes of a 1–0 victory.<ref>{{cite news|title=Exeter City 1–0 Brentford|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/36949680|access-date=11 November 2017|work=BBC Sport|date=9 August 2016}}</ref> He became the club's youngest ever player, breaking an 87-year-old record set by [[Cliff Bastin]], and being named man of the match.<ref>{{cite twitter|user=officialecfc|number=763123448759590916|title=15-year-old Ethan Ampadu wins the man-of-the-match award on his full debut for the club. Well done, Ethan!|date=9 August 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.exeterexpressandecho.co.uk/exeter-city-vs-brentford-ethan-ampadu-to-become-exeter-city-s-youngest-ever-player/story-29602487-detail/story.html|title=Exeter City vs Brentford: Ethan Ampadu to become Exeter City's youngest ever player|date=9 August 2016|publisher=ExeterExpressandEcho.co.uk|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160810163205/http://www.exeterexpressandecho.co.uk/exeter-city-vs-brentford-ethan-ampadu-to-become-exeter-city-s-youngest-ever-player/story-29602487-detail/story.html|archive-date=10 August 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.walesonline.co.uk/sport/football/who-ethan-ampadu-wales-face-11729005|title=The 15-year-old wonderkid England want to poach has just become big news|first=Paul|last=Abbandonato|date=10 August 2016|work=Wales Online}}</ref> Ampadu's record was beaten by [[Ben Chrisene]] in August 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49362247|title=Ben Chrisene: Exeter City youngster has 'top level' potential after breaking Ethan Ampadu record|date=15 August 2019|website=BBC Sport}}</ref> A week later, he made his league debut in a 1–0 home defeat against [[Crawley Town F.C.|Crawley Town]] in [[EFL League Two]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Exeter City 0–1 Crawley Town|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/37018095|access-date=11 November 2017|work=BBC Sport|date=16 August 2016}}</ref>

===Chelsea===
[[file:Ethan Ampadu 2017.jpg|thumb|Ampadu with Chelsea in 2017]]
[[File:Chelsea players training before 2019 UEFA Europa League final 06 (cropped).jpg|thumb|Ethan Ampadu training before 2019 UEFA Europa League final]]
On 1 July 2017, Ampadu signed a contract with [[Premier League]] club [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]]. The two clubs had entered negotiations over a compensation fee for the player in an attempt to reach a fee that both found acceptable for the then 16-year-old. Yet, in November 2017, [[Exeter City F.C.|Exeter City]] chairman Julian Tagg said that, whilst there was still a positive relationship between the two clubs, there was also a "massive disparity" in both clubs' valuation of the player. It was set to go to a tribunal.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.devonlive.com/sport/football/football-news/what-know-far-chelsea-exeter-781193|title=What we know so far about the Ethan Ampadu tribunal|last=Byrom|first=David|date=15 November 2017|work=devonlive|access-date=6 January 2018}}</ref>

On 20 September 2017, Ampadu made his debut for Chelsea in an EFL Cup third round match against [[Nottingham Forest F.C.|Nottingham Forest]], coming on in the 55th minute for [[Cesc Fàbregas]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/41249727|title=Chelsea 5–1 Nottingham Forest|work=BBC Sport|date=20 September 2017|access-date=20 September 2017}}</ref> Doing so made him the first player born in the 2000s to play for the Chelsea senior team. At 17 years and six days old, he also became the youngest player to debut for the club in over ten years.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/ethan-ampadu-becomes-youngest-chelsea-player-in-over-a/bc21v7w4akx81wegeigdlwkge |title=Ethan Ampadu becomes youngest Chelsea player in over a decade |date=20 September 2017 |publisher=Goal}}</ref> On 12 December 2017, he made his Premier League debut for Chelsea, coming on as a substitute against [[Huddersfield Town A.F.C.|Huddersfield Town]] in the 80th minute.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/42232614|title=Huddersfield Town 1–3 Chelsea|work=BBC Sport|date=12 December 2017|access-date=21 December 2017}}</ref>

In September 2018 he signed a new five-year contract with Chelsea.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45572179|title=Ethan Ampadu: Chelsea youngster signs new five-year contract|date=19 September 2018|website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

In July 2019, new manager [[Frank Lampard]] confirmed that Ampadu would spend the 2019–20 season out on loan at another club.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49000905|title=Ethan Ampadu: Chelsea defender set to leave on loan|date=16 July 2019|website=BBC Sport}}</ref> In that period, Ampadu joined [[RB Leipzig]] on a season-long loan deal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dierotenbullen.com/de/aktuelles/neuigkeiten/Saison_2019_20/Neuzugang-Ethan-Ampadu.html|title=Transfermeldung! Ethan Ampadu kommt leihweise zu RB Leipzig|language=de|date=22 July 2019|access-date=22 July 2019}}</ref>

On 7 September 2020, Ampadu joined fellow [[Premier League]] club [[Sheffield United F.C.|Sheffield United]] on a season-long [[Loan (sports)|loan]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=United net Wales international|url=https://www.sufc.co.uk/news/2020/september/ethan-ampadu-signs/|access-date=7 September 2020|website=www.sufc.co.uk}}</ref> He played 29 games in all competitions for the Blades, who were relegated at the end of the season.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Boczak |first1=Ben |title=Chelsea fans notice what Ethan Ampadu did to Emerson before red card for Wales vs Italy |url=https://www.football.london/chelsea-fc/news/chelsea-ethan-ampadu-emerson-wales-20861654 |access-date=27 June 2021 |publisher=Football London |date=20 June 2021}}</ref>

On 31 August 2021, Ampadu joined Italian side [[Venezia F.C.|Venezia]] on a season-long loan after signing a new three-year deal with Chelsea.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Ampadu signs new contract and goes on loan |url=https://www.chelseafc.com/en/news/2021/08/31/ampadu-signs-new-contract-and-goes-on-loan |publisher=[[Chelsea F.C.]] |date=31 August 2021 |accessdate=31 August 2021}}</ref> Venezia were relegated at the end of the season.

On 1 September 2022, Ampadu joined [[Spezia Calcio|Spezia]] on a season-long loan.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Official {{!}} Ethan Ampadu is a new Spezia player |url=https://www.acspezia.com/en/news/official-ethan-ampadu-is-a-new-spezia-player.23942.html|publisher=Spezia Calcio |date=1 September 2022 |accessdate=1 September 2022}}</ref> Spezia were relegated at the end of the season.

He scored his first goal for Spezia on the 11 June 2023 while on loan against Hellas Verona.

==International career==
Ampadu plays internationally for the [[Wales national football team]]. He qualifies for Wales through his Welsh mother.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.walesonline.co.uk/sport/football/wales-face-tug-war-england-11729005|title=The 15-year-old wonderkid England want to poach has just become big news|first=Paul|last=Abbandonato|date=10 August 2016|website=walesonline}}</ref> He was previously also eligible to represent England, Republic of Ireland and Ghana.<ref name="walesonline">{{cite web|url=http://www.walesonline.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/manchester-united-fc-target-ethan-11154499|title=Why Man Utd target Ethan Ampadu chose Wales over England|first=Gareth|last=Rogers|date=7 April 2016|work=WalesOnline.co.uk}}</ref>

On 26 May 2017, at the age of 16, Ampadu was called into the [[Wales national football team|senior Wales squad]] ahead of their World Cup qualifier with [[Serbia national football team|Serbia]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Ethan Ampadu: Exeter City youngster included in Wales squad|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40041499|work=[[BBC Sport]]|access-date=26 May 2017}}</ref> He was called up again on 1 November 2017 for friendlies against [[France national football team|France]] and [[Panama national football team|Panama]],<ref>{{cite news|last1=Byrom|first1=David|title=Chelsea starlet Ethan Ampadu included in Wales squad for November friendlies|url=http://www.devonlive.com/sport/football/football-news/chelsea-starlet-ethan-ampadu-included-712273|access-date=11 November 2017|publisher=Devon Live|date=1 November 2017}}</ref> and made his debut aged 17 on 10 November 2017 against France at the [[Stade de France]], coming on as a 63rd-minute substitute for [[Joe Ledley]] in a 2–0 defeat.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41854507 |title=France 2–0 Wales |work=BBC Sport |date=10 November 2017 |access-date=11 November 2017}}</ref>

In May 2021, Ampadu was selected for the Wales squad for the delayed [[UEFA Euro 2020]] tournament.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57301006|title=Uncapped Colwill in Wales Euro 2020 squad|work=BBC Sport }}</ref> On 20 June, in the 55th-minute of Wales' [[UEFA Euro 2020 Group A#Italy vs Wales|1–0 defeat]] to [[Italy national football team|Italy]], he received a straight [[red card (association football)|red card]] for a late challenge on [[Federico Bernardeschi]], becoming the youngest player to receive one at the European Championships.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Italy 1-0 Wales: Matteo Pessina seals win for hosts but Rob Page's side into Euro 2020 last 16 despite Ethan Ampadu red card |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/italy-vs-wales/report/421506 |archive-url=https://archive.ph/20210620215119/https://www.skysports.com/football/italy-vs-wales/report/421506 |date=20 June 2021 |archive-date=20 June 2021 |work=Sky Sports |last=Peter |first=Smith |location=United Kingdom |language=en |access-date=20 June 2021 |url-status=live }}</ref> In November 2022 he was named in the Wales squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63545011 2022 Wales World Cup squad]</ref>
