'''John Stones''' (born 28 May 1994) is an English professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] or [[defensive midfielder]] for {{English football updater|ManchesC}} club [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] and the [[England national football team|England national team]].

Stones began his career with [[Barnsley F.C.|Barnsley]], making his first-team debut in the [[EFL Championship|Championship]] in March 2012 as a 17-year-old. He joined Premier League club [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] for around £3&nbsp;million in January 2013 and amassed 95 appearances over four seasons. In August 2016, he signed for Manchester City for an initial £47.5&nbsp;million with add-ons. With the club, he won five Premier League titles, two [[EFL Cup]]s, two [[FA Cup]]s, and the [[UEFA Champions League]] as part of a historic [[Treble (association football)#Continental trebles|continental treble]] in the [[2022–23 Manchester City F.C. season|2022–23 season]].

Stones made his senior debut for England in May 2014 after previously being [[Cap (association football)|capped]] by [[England national youth football team|England youth teams]] at [[England national under-19 football team|under-19]], [[England national under-20 football team|under-20]] and [[England national under-21 football team|under-21]] levels. He was chosen in [[List of England national football team World Cup and European Championship squads|England's squads]] for the [[UEFA European Championship]] in [[UEFA Euro 2016|2016]] and [[UEFA Euro 2020|2020]], and the [[FIFA World Cup]] in [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022]].

==Early life==
Stones was born in [[Barnsley]], South Yorkshire,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.worldfootball.net/player_summary/john-stones/ |title=John Stones: Profile |website=worldfootball.net |publisher=HEIM:SPIEL |access-date=12 April 2020}}</ref> to parents Janet and Peter Stones.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://lifebogger.com/john-stones-childhood-story-plus-untold-biography-facts/ |title=John Stones Childhood Story Plus Untold Biography Facts |last=Henrico |first=Vanny |date=17 November 2017 |website=Life Bogger |access-date=29 April 2019}}</ref> He grew up in [[Thurlstone]] and attended [[Penistone Grammar School]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.barnsleychronicle.com/article/england-star-cheered-on-at-former-school |title=England star cheered on at former school |date=22 June 2018 |newspaper=Barnsley Chronicle |access-date=29 April 2019}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Barnsley===
Stones came through the [[Barnsley F.C.|Barnsley]] youth academy to sign a professional contract in December 2011.<ref name="Hugman">{{Hugman|25445|access-date=3 April 2020}}</ref> He made his first-team debut in the [[EFL Championship|Championship]] on 17 March 2012, in a 4–0 defeat to [[Reading F.C.|Reading]] at [[Oakwell]], replacing [[Scott Wiseman]] after 52 minutes.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/17323945 |title=Barnsley 0–4 Reading |website=BBC Sport |date=17 March 2012 |access-date=19 March 2012}}</ref> He scored his only goal for Barnsley on his first start, equalising before half time in a [[EFL Cup|League Cup]] first round match away to [[Rochdale A.F.C.|Rochdale]] on 11 August 2012, which resulted in a 4–3 victory after extra time.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/19123950 |title=Rochdale 3–4 Barnsley (aet) |website=BBC Sport |date=11 August 2012 |access-date=12 August 2012}}</ref> A week later, he made his first league start, in a 1–0 victory against [[Middlesbrough F.C.|Middlesbrough]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.skysports.com/football/barnsley-vs-mboro/report/262139 |title=Tykes claim overdue home win |access-date=26 April 2015 |website=Sky Sports |date=18 August 2012}}</ref>

On 3 January 2019, in City's 2–1 victory over [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]], Stones made a crucial goal-line clearance which would end up being the decider in City's victory over Liverpool in the Premier League title race.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11679/11598280/john-stones-makes-remarkable-goal-line-clearance-for-man-city-against-liverpool|title=John Stones makes remarkable goal-line clearance for Man City against Liverpool|publisher=Sky Sports|date=4 January 2019|access-date=14 September 2022}}</ref>

On 6 January 2021, Stones scored the opening goal in a 0–2 away win at Old Trafford against Manchester United in the League Cup semi-final. He was named Man of the Match and was heavily praised by manager Pep Guardiola after an impressive streak of performances alongside [[Rúben Dias|Ruben Dias]] in defence.<ref>{{cite news|title=Man City beat Man Utd to reach EFL Cup final |website=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/55481278|access-date=7 January 2021}}</ref> Just one day later, Stones was announced as the Manchester City Player of the Month for December 2020.<ref>{{cite web|last=Pollard|first=Rob|title=Stones lands Etihad player of the month award|url=https://www.mancity.com/citytv/mens/john-stones-etihad-player-of-the-month-63745616|access-date=7 January 2021|website=www.mancity.com}}</ref> On 17 January 2021, Stones scored his first and second Premier League goals for City, coming in a 4–0 home win over [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Manchester City 4–0 Crystal Palace: Stones scores twice as Man City go second |website=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/55607092|access-date=17 January 2021}}</ref> His performances earned him a place in the [[PFA Team of the Year]] for the [[2020–21 Premier League|2020–21]] season.<ref name="PFA">{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/57356719|title=PFA Premier League Team of the Year: Kevin de Bruyne one of six Manchester City players picked |website=BBC Sport |date=4 June 2021|access-date=4 June 2021}}</ref>

On 10 August 2021, Stones signed a new contract with City, keeping him at the club until 2026.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58157599|title=John Stones: Manchester City and England defender signs new five-year deal with champions|website=BBC Sport|date=10 August 2021}}</ref> On 10 June 2023, Stones was in the starting line-up for the [[2023 UEFA Champions League final]] against [[Inter Milan]] in Istanbul, which Manchester City went on to win 1–0 for their maiden European Cup.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/65872614|title=Manchester City: Treble winners welcomed home by fans after Champions League victory|website=BBC Sport|date=11 June 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:John Stones 2018-06-24 1.jpg|thumb|upright|Stones celebrates scoring for [[England national football team|England]] at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]]]]

Stones was named in manager [[Peter Taylor (footballer, born 1953)|Peter Taylor]]'s [[2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup squads#England|21-man squad]] for the [[2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup]] in May 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thefa.com/england/mens-u20s/News/2013/u20s-world-cup-squad-announcement-280513 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130615071407/http://www.thefa.com/england/mens-u20s/News/2013/u20s-world-cup-squad-announcement-280513 |archive-date=15 June 2013 |title=Final squad named |publisher=The Football Association |date=28 May 2013 |access-date=17 June 2013}}</ref> He made his [[England national under-20 football team|England U20]] debut on 16 June, in a 3–0 win in a warm-up game against [[Uruguay national under-20 football team|Uruguay]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thefa.com/england/mens-u20s/News/2013/uruguay-report-160613 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130624222816/http://www.thefa.com/england/mens-u20s/News/2013/uruguay-report-160613 |archive-date=24 June 2013 |title=Training win for Three Lions |publisher=The Football Association |date=16 June 2013 |access-date=17 June 2013}}</ref> He made his [[England national under-21 football team|under-21s]] debut against [[Scotland national under-21 football team|Scotland]] on 13 August 2013, playing the entire 90 minutes of a 6–0 win for England at [[Bramall Lane]].<ref>{{cite news |title=International friendly: England Under-21s beat Scotland U21s 6–0 |url=http://www.skysports.com/football/england-u21-vs-scotland-u21/292365 |access-date=24 June 2018 |website=Sky Sports |date=13 August 2013}}</ref>

He was named as a standby player for England's [[2014 FIFA World Cup]] squad, but was not included in the [[2014 FIFA World Cup squads#England|final squad]]. Stones made his England debut on 30 May in a 3–0 friendly win over [[Peru national football team|Peru]] at Wembley, replacing Everton teammate [[Leighton Baines]] for the final 15 minutes.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/27383301 |title=England 3–0 Peru |website=BBC Sport |first=Phil |last=McNulty |date=30 May 2014 |access-date=30 May 2014}}</ref> On 3 September 2014, Stones made his first England start in a friendly against [[Norway national football team|Norway]] at [[Wembley Stadium]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://bbc.co.uk/sport/football/28784340 |title=Besiktas 0–0 Arsenal |website=BBC Sport |first=Chris |last=Bevan |date=19 August 2014 |access-date=19 August 2014}}</ref> Five days later, in England's first match of [[UEFA Euro 2016 qualification]], Stones made his competitive debut in a 2–0 victory away to [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]] at [[St. Jakob-Park]], [[Basel]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/29008534 |title=Switzerland 0–2 England |website=BBC Sport |first=Phil |last=McNulty |date=8 September 2014 |access-date=8 September 2014}}</ref>

Stones was selected in [[Roy Hodgson]]'s [[UEFA Euro 2016 squads#England|23-man squad]] for [[UEFA Euro 2016|Euro 2016]] but did not play any of England's four games in the tournament.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thefa.com/news/england/2016/may/england-final-uefa-euro-2016-squad-announcement |title=Roy Hodgson confirms final squad for UEFA EURO 2016 |publisher=The Football Association |date=31 May 2016 |access-date=9 August 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.espnfc.com/club/everton/368/blog/post/2905118/evertons-barkley-and-stones-emerge-from-england-euro-failure-unscathed |title=Everton's Barkley and Stones emerge from England Euro failure unscathed |website=ESPN FC |date=30 June 2016 |access-date=10 August 2016}}</ref>

He was named in the 23-man England [[2018 FIFA World Cup squads#England|squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018 World Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44138600 |title=England World Cup squad: Trent Alexander-Arnold in 23-man squad |website=BBC Sport |date=16 May 2018 |access-date=17 May 2018}}</ref> On 24 June, Stones scored his first two international goals in a 6–1 victory over [[Panama national football team|Panama]] during their second group stage match of the tournament.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cbssports.com/soccer/world-cup/news/england-vs-panama-final-score-recap-harry-kane-hat-trick-john-stones-double-powers-three-lions-into-round-of-16 |title=England vs. Panama final score, recap: Harry Kane hat trick, John Stones double powers Three Lions into round of 16 |website=CBS Sports |access-date=24 June 2018 |date=24 June 2018}}</ref> He became the first Manchester City player to score for England at a [[FIFA World Cup|World Cup finals]] since [[Trevor Francis]] in [[1982 FIFA World Cup|1982]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/john-stones-manchester-city-record-14822456 |title=John Stones equals a Man City record with World Cup goal heroics |last=Brennan |first=Stuart |date=24 June 2018 |newspaper=Manchester Evening News|access-date=28 June 2018}}</ref>

At the [[2019 UEFA Nations League Finals]] in Portugal, Stones made an error in the semi-final against the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]], when he gave away possession to [[Memphis Depay]] in extra time, leading to one of the goals that defeated England 3–1.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Taylor |first1=Daniel |title=England extra-time misery after John Stones' blunder lets in Netherlands |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2019/jun/06/netherlands-england-nations-league-semi-final-match-report |access-date=2 September 2019 |newspaper=The Guardian |date=6 June 2019}}</ref> Manager [[Gareth Southgate]] dropped him from the third-place match against [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]], saying that Stones made his mistake because of fatigue.<ref>{{cite news |last1=O'Toole |first1=Dan |title=Gareth Southgate explains John Stones omission for England game vs Switzerland |url=https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/man-city-john-stones-england-16402690 |access-date=2 September 2019 |newspaper=Manchester Evening News |date=9 June 2019}}</ref>

Stones was named in the England squad for [[UEFA Euro 2020|Euro 2020]].<ref>[https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/england-euro-2020-squad-announcement-live-b1857336.html#post-460820 England Euro 2020 squad] Independent.co.uk. Retrieved 1 June 2021.</ref> Stones started all seven of England's games, with England finishing the tournament as runners-up<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/51198762 |title=Italy 1–1 England |first=Phil |last=McNulty |website=BBC Sport |date=11 July 2021 |access-date=17 July 2021}}</ref> and conceding only two goals, both from set pieces.

Stones was named in England's [[2022 FIFA World Cup squads#England|26-man squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.englandfootball.com/articles/2022/Nov/10/england-mens-senior-squad-named-for-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022-20221011|title=England squad named for FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022|publisher=The Football Association|date=10 November 2022|access-date=10 November 2022}}</ref>

Stones has been a key player for his national team in England's last three major international tournaments, forming a solid defensive partnership with [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] captain [[Harry Maguire]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://theathletic.com/3961026/2022/12/02/maguire-stones-england-partnership/|title=John Stones pays tribute to defensive partner Harry Maguire after questions over pre-tournament form|publisher=The Athletic|date=2 December 2022|access-date=8 February 2023}}</ref> The two are close, having grown up together playing youth football in [[South Yorkshire]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://talksport.com/football/858330/harry-maguire-goal-john-stones-england-world-cup-qualifier-poland/|title=Harry Maguire's classy gesture towards 'good friend' John Stones after making up for mistake in England World Cup qualifying win vs Poland with assist|publisher=talkSPORT|date=1 April 2021|access-date=8 February 2023}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Known for his technical ability and physical presence, Stones is celebrated for being a ball-playing [[centre-back]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.mcfcwatch.com/2017/10/26/city-stat-john-stones-is-european-footballs-pass-master-in-201718 |title=City Stat: John Stones is European football's pass master in 2017/18 |website=City Watch |access-date=18 April 2018 |archive-date=28 August 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180828090656/https://www.mcfcwatch.com/2017/10/26/city-stat-john-stones-is-european-footballs-pass-master-in-201718/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> Teammates and coaches have praised his composure with the ball, defensive skills, range of passing, and versatility and vision.<ref name="goal.com">{{cite web |url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/stones-has-as-much-composure-as-any-defender-in-europe-says/3ggjk0euxk8o1cjjszcmd11vm |title=England: Southgate gushes over Stones, compares him to Pique and Boateng |website=Goal.com |publisher=Perform Group}}</ref><ref name="mcfcwatch.com">{{cite web |url=https://www.mcfcwatch.com/2017/10/26/city-stat-john-stones-is-european-footballs-pass-master-in-201718/ |title=City Stat: John Stones is European football's pass master in 2017/18 |website=City Watch |access-date=18 April 2018 |archive-date=28 August 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180828090656/https://www.mcfcwatch.com/2017/10/26/city-stat-john-stones-is-european-footballs-pass-master-in-201718/ |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name=auto>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/blog/2016/aug/09/john-stones-pep-guardiola-flaws-manchester-city-everton |title=John Stones: a rare talent but value only if Pep Guardiola removes the flaws – Andy Hunter |first=Andy |last=Hunter |newspaper=The Guardian |location=London |date=9 August 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.mancity.com/news/first-team/first-team-news/2016/august/john-stones-in-quotes |title=John Stones: In Quotes |publisher=Manchester City F.C.}}</ref>

[[Joleon Lescott]] said of his ability: "John can do things most defenders can't – a little feign here and there, maybe a turn to get out of trouble when he needs to – and that's what is going to set him apart from others – the ability to do things others can't. He'll do his job and defend well, but there will be moments when he does something special and you'll just go 'ah, that's why he's regarded as the best.' He doesn't seem to get fazed by anything and always seems calm and he never hides".<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.mancity.com/news/first-team/first-team-news/2017/november/joleon-lescott-impressed-with-john-stones |title=Lescott: Stones has the X-Factor |publisher=Manchester City F.C.}}</ref> Manchester City and England teammate [[Kyle Walker]] added: "You don't quite appreciate him until you are playing alongside him. He rarely gets beat, he is great on the ball and he is very calm and level-headed."<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/11679/11130440/man-citys-kyle-walker-hails-unbelievable-john-stones-and-special-pep-guardiola |title=Man City's Kyle Walker hails 'unbelievable' John Stones and 'special' Pep Guardiola |website=Sky Sports}}</ref>

He developed his ball-playing style early in his career at Barnsley with many crediting Manchester City coach [[Pep Guardiola]] for his later development.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/11125793/how-paul-heckingbottom-moulded-careers-of-john-stones-alfie-mawson-and-mason-holgate |title=How Paul Heckingbottom moulded careers of John Stones, Alfie Mawson and Mason Holgate |website=Sky Sports}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/how-pep-guardiolas-love-is-helping-john-stones-fulfil-potential-t39pnmhlw |title=How Pep Guardiola's love is helping John Stones fulfil potential |first=Paul |last=Hirst |date=4 October 2017 |newspaper=The Times}}</ref> During the [[2022–23 Manchester City F.C. season|2022–23 season]], Stones began to play what was described as "a hybrid role: defending in the back four when City don't have the ball, and joining [[Rodri (footballer, born 1996)|Rodri]] as a double pivot in front of City's back three when they are in possession".<ref>{{cite web |url=https://theathletic.com/4577864/2023/06/04/how-john-stones-double-role-brought-city-one-step-closer-to-the-treble |title=How John Stones’ double role brought City one step closer to the treble |website=The Athletic}}</ref>

His range of passing has led to comparisons to [[Rio Ferdinand]], [[Gerard Piqué]], [[Jérôme Boateng]], [[Pep Guardiola]] and [[Franz Beckenbauer]].<ref name="goal.com"/><ref name=auto/><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.espn.co.uk/football/manchester-city/story/2998808/pep-guardiola-compares-john-stones-to-gerard-pique-of-barcelona |title=Stones compares to a young Pique – Pep |date=18 November 2016 |publisher=ESPN}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2017/11/16/john-stones-no-longer-soft-touch-instead-rock-heart-manchester/ |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/archive/20220112/https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2017/11/16/john-stones-no-longer-soft-touch-instead-rock-heart-manchester/ |archive-date=12 January 2022 |url-access=subscription |url-status=live |title=John Stones is no longer a soft touch, but instead a rock at heart of Manchester City's defence – thanks largely to Pep Guardiola's patience |first=James |last=Ducker |date=16 November 2017 |newspaper=The Daily Telegraph}}{{cbignore}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://sport360.com/article/football/english-premier-league/253060/john-stones-on-striving-for-man-city-dominance-comparison-with-franz-beckenbauer-and-coming-up-against-neymar-at-wembley |title=John Stones striving for sustained Manchester City domination in England and Europe |website=Sport 360}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Stones met Millie Savage when both were aged 12. They split up in December 2018, when their daughter was 18 months old.<ref>{{cite news |last1=McShane |first1=Asher |title=John Stones and Millie Savage 'split': 'Family devastated after England star breaks up with childhood sweetheart' |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/news/uk/england-star-john-stones-splits-with-childhood-sweetheart-millie-savage-a4034971.html |access-date=16 May 2020 |newspaper=Evening Standard |date=10 January 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Rodger |first1=James |title=John Stones 'ridiculed by rivals over WhatsApp after replacing childhood sweetheart' |url=https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/john-stones-ridiculed-rivals-over-16401715 |access-date=16 May 2020 |newspaper=Birmingham Mail |date=9 June 2019}}</ref> Teammate [[Bernardo Silva]] has a [[French Bulldog]] named after Stones.<ref>{{cite web|title= FUR REAL? ‘I’ll take it as a compliment’ – John Stones reveals reason why Man City teammate Bernardo Silva named his dog after him |url= https://talksport.com/football/1199366/why-is-bernardo-silvas-dog-named-after-john-stones-manchester-city/ |website=TalkSport|date=21 September 2022|url-status=live}}</ref>
