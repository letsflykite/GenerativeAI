'''Ivan Perišić''' ({{IPA-sh|ǐʋan pěriʃitɕ|hr}};<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hjp.znanje.hr/index.php?show=search_by_id&id=fVhlWRI%3D|title=Ìvan|quote=Ìvan|website=Hrvatski jezični portal|access-date=17 March 2018|language=sh}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://hjp.znanje.hr/index.php?show=search_by_id&id=eV5lXBM%3D&keyword=Pustinjak|title=Pètar|quote=Pèrišić|website=Hrvatski jezični portal|access-date=17 March 2018|language=sh}}</ref> born 2 February 1989) is a Croatian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays for [[Croatian]] club [[Hajduk Split]] and the [[Croatia national football team|Croatia national team]]. Usually deployed as a [[Midfielder#Winger|winger]], he has also featured as an [[Midfielder#Attacking midfielder|attacking midfielder]], [[Forward (association football)#Second striker|second striker]], or [[Wing-back (association football)|wing-back]] known for his ambidexterity<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sportskeeda.com/football/5-best-active-two-footed-players-greenwood-ronaldo/2|title=5 best active two-footed players|website=Sportskeeda|access-date=19 December 2022}}</ref> and versatility.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.premierleague.com/news/2918948|title=The Scout's World Cup review: Perisic shows versatility.|website=Premier League|access-date=19 December 2022}}</ref> 

A product of the [[HNK Hajduk Split|Hajduk Split]] and [[FC Sochaux-Montbéliard|Sochaux]] youth academies, Perišić made a name for himself while playing for [[Club Brugge KV|Club Brugge]], where he was the [[Belgian First Division A top scorers|Belgian Pro League top goalscorer]] and was named [[Belgian professional football awards#Professional Footballer of the Year|Belgian Footballer of the Year]] for 2011. This earned him a transfer to [[Borussia Dortmund]], with whom he won the [[2011–12 Bundesliga]], before signing with [[VfL Wolfsburg]] for €8&nbsp;million in January 2013. He remained there for two and a half seasons, winning the [[2015 DFB-Pokal Final]], before moving to [[Inter Milan]] for €16&nbsp;million. In 2019, he joined [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] on loan, winning the [[Treble (association football)|treble]]. After returning to Inter Milan, he won the [[2020–21 Serie A]] and the [[2021 Supercoppa Italiana]].

Perišić made his debut for the Croatia national team in 2011, and represented his nation at [[UEFA Euro 2012|Euro 2012]], [[UEFA Euro 2016|2016]] and [[UEFA Euro 2020|2020]], as well as the [[2014 FIFA World Cup|2014]], [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]], reaching the [[2018 FIFA World Cup Final|final]] of the 2018 tournament and winning a bronze medal at the 2022 World Cup. Instrumental to [[Croatia national football team#Dalić period (since 2017)|Croatia's second "golden generation"]], Perišić is the nation's player with most goal contributions at the major tournaments (18).<!--- Ten goals and eight assists as of 17 December 2022 ---><ref name="contrib">{{cite news |url=https://gol.dnevnik.hr/clanak/rubrika/sp-2022/ivan-perisic-je-sudjelovao-u-najvise-golova-na-svjetskim-prvenstvima-za-hrvatsku---753882.html |title=Komentator BBC-ja pred milijunima istaknuo bitnu stvar za Perišića, ispisao je povijest Hrvatske |language=hr|website=[[Gol.hr]] |date=28 November 2022 |access-date=28 November 2022}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Early career===
Perišić played in the youth ranks of the club he grew up supporting, [[HNK Hajduk Split|Hajduk Split]].<ref name=guardian2016>{{cite news|title=Ivan Perisic: from chicken farm saviour to key player for Croatia|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/jun/06/ivan-perisic-croatia-chicken-farm|work=The Guardian|date=6 June 2016|access-date=17 July 2017}}</ref> He received interest from a number of clubs, including [[R.S.C. Anderlecht|Anderlecht]], [[PSV Eindhoven|PSV]], [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]], [[Hertha BSC]] and [[Hamburger SV]].<ref name=imsyouth>{{cite web |url=http://www.imscouting.com/global-news-article/-the-new-aljosa-asanovic-/3898/ |title=Player to Watch – Ivan Perisic |publisher=IMScouting |date=26 February 2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100814100812/http://www.imscouting.com/global-news-article/-the-new-aljosa-asanovic-/3898 |archive-date=14 August 2010 }}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Radić|first=Jurica|date=24 August 2020|title=Avion ga je u Splitu čekao dva dana, Hajduk je bio nemoćan: 'Ako ne uspije, svi možemo potrgati diplome'|url=https://sportske.jutarnji.hr/sn/nogomet/nogomet-mix/avion-ga-je-u-splitu-cekao-dva-dana-hajduk-je-bio-nemocan-ako-ne-uspije-svi-mozemo-potrgati-diplome-15015106|access-date=24 August 2020|website=Sportske novosti|language=hr-hr}}</ref> The only match he played for Hajduk was a friendly 3–1 win over [[FK Smederevo 1924|Smederevo]] in [[Murska Sobota]] on 16 July 2006.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|title=PRIJATELJSKE UTAKMICE 2006. GODINE|url=https://hajduk.hr/utakmice/arhiva/prijateljske/2006|access-date=24 August 2020|website=HNK Hajduk Split|language=hr}}</ref> Returning from Murska Sobota to [[Split, Croatia|Split]], Perišić received offers from Anderlecht and [[FC Sochaux-Montbéliard|Sochaux]]. Hajduk offered Perišić a €100,000 contract, which was only €20,000 less than Hajduk's best player [[Niko Kranjčar]]'s contract.<ref name=":0" /> However, the Perišić family opted for French club Sochaux, which paid €360,000 to sign him in the summer of 2006.<ref name=guardian2016/> Sochaux's coach [[Alain Perrin]] personally arrived to Split with a private jet to convince Perišić to sign, waiting two days for his signature.<ref name=":0" /> His physical and technical attributes led to comparisons to former Croatia international [[Aljoša Asanović]] by some journalists.<ref name=imsyouth/> He was a member of the Sochaux youth team, which won the [[Coupe Gambardella]] in 2007.<ref>{{cite news|title=Trois choses à savoir sur Ivan Perisic (Croatie)|trans-title=Three things to know about Ivan Perisic (Croatia)|url=https://www.francefootball.fr/news/Trois-choses-a-savoir-sur-ivan-perisic-croatie/697922|publisher=France Football|language=fr|date=22 June 2016|access-date=17 July 2017}}</ref> During his time at Sochaux, he failed to make a first-team appearance, but did play for the B team.<ref name=guardian2016/> In January 2009, Perišić was sent on loan to [[Belgian First Division A|Belgian top flight]] club [[K.S.V. Roeselare|Roeselare]] for six months.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/dmf02012009_077 |title=Roeselare haalt twee buitenlandse versterkingen binnen |trans-title=Roeselare has two foreign reinforcements|language=nl |publisher=Het Nieuwsblad |date=2 January 2009 |access-date=17 July 2017}}</ref> At the end of the [[2008–09 Belgian First Division|2008–09 season]], there were reports Perišić sought to join Belgian side Anderlecht.<ref>{{cite news|title=Perisic: "Droom van Champions League met Anderlecht"|trans-title=Perisic: "Dream of Champions League with Anderlecht"|url=https://www.voetbalkrant.com/nl/nieuws/lees/2009-05-07/perisic-droom-van-champions-league-met-anderlecht|publisher=voetbalkrant|language=nl|date=7 May 2009|access-date=13 July 2017}}</ref>

===Club Brugge===
On 26 August 2009, Belgian club [[Club Brugge KV|Club Brugge]] acquired Perišić from Sochaux for a €250,000 transfer fee and signed him to a three-year contract.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.clubbrugge.be/nl/nieuws/bericht/4956/Ivan-Perisic-tekent-bij-Club-voor-drie-seizoenen-! |title=Ivan Perisic tekent bij Club voor drie seizoenen ! |language=nl |publisher=Club Brugge |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090828010444/http://www.clubbrugge.be/nl/nieuws/bericht/4956/Ivan-Perisic-tekent-bij-Club-voor-drie-seizoenen-%21 |archive-date=28 August 2009 }}</ref> Prior his move, Perišić was linked with a move to German side Hertha BSC, having gone on trial.<ref>{{cite news |title=Perisic Hertha rounder in Test |trans-title=Allrounder Perisic im Hertha-Test |url=http://www.bz-berlin.de/sport/hertha-bsc/allrounder-perisic-im-hertha-test-article528402.html |publisher=[[B.Z. (newspaper)|B.Z.]] |language=de |date=24 July 2009 |access-date=11 February 2013}}</ref>

In the opening match of the season, Perišić scored his first goal in a 1–1 draw against [[K.R.C. Genk|Genk]], then scored his second in two consecutive games and provided assists in a 4–1 win over [[K.V.C. Westerlo|Westerlo]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2009/09/13/belgium/pro-league/club-brugge-kv/krc-genk/786755/ |title=Club Brugge vs. Genk |publisher=Soccerway |date=13 September 2009}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2009/09/20/belgium/pro-league/kvc-westerlo/club-brugge-kv/786770/ |title=Westerlo vs. Club Brugge |publisher=Soccerway |date=20 September 2009}}</ref> Overall, Perišić scored 9 goals in 33 league appearances, also making eight appearances in Brugge's [[2009–10 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]] campaign, scoring four goals. At the end of the season, Perišić signed a new three-year contract at Brugge, keeping him until 2015.<ref>{{cite news |title=Perisic midfielder revelation of Bruges |trans-title=Perisic, centrocampista rivelazione del Bruges |url=http://www.corrieredellosport.it/talent_scout/2010/09/30-131406/Perisic,+centrocampista+rivelazione+del+Bruges |work=Corriere dello Sport |language=it |date=30 September 2010 |access-date=12 February 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304050413/http://www.corrieredellosport.it/talent_scout/2010/09/30-131406/Perisic,+centrocampista+rivelazione+del+Bruges |archive-date=4 March 2016 }}</ref>

Belgian football critics predicted a bright future for Perišić. In the [[2010–11 Belgian Pro League|2010–11 season]], he was the top scorer of the Belgian Jupiler Pro League after scoring 22 goals for Club Brugge, also being named [[Belgian professional football awards#Professional Footballer of the Year|Player of the Year]] in Belgium.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=bel/news/newsid=1633908.html |title=Perišić named best player in Belgium |publisher=UEFA.com |date=22 May 2011 |access-date=8 December 2012 |first=Berend |last=Scholten }}</ref> During the season, Perišić scored four goals and provided an assist in a 5–0 win against [[R. Charleroi S.C.|Charleroi]] on 29 December 2010.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2010/12/29/belgium/pro-league/club-brugge-kv/royal-charleroi-sporting-club/942818/ |title=Club Brugge vs. Sporting Charleroi |publisher=Soccerway |date=29 December 2010}}</ref>

===Borussia Dortmund===
[[File:Ivan Perisic.jpg|thumb|upright|Perišić at [[Borussia Dortmund]] in 2012]]

On 23 May 2011, Perišić signed a five-year contract to play for German side [[Borussia Dortmund]] after Dortmund paid an estimated €5&nbsp;million transfer fee to Brugge.<ref>{{cite web |title=BVB verpflichtet kroatischen Nationalspieler Ivan Perisic
|trans-title=BVB sign Croatian international Ivan Perisic |language=de |date=30 May 2011 |access-date=30 May 2011 |publisher=[[Borussia Dortmund]] |url=http://www.bvb.de/?%87%ECZ%1B%E7%F4%9C_k%E4%82%9C}}</ref> He made his debut for the club in their 3–1 home victory over Hamburger SV on 5 August, substituting on for [[Chris Löwe]] in the 75th minute.<ref>{{cite news |title=BVB in toller Frühform - Großkreutz, Kagawa, Götze & Co. wirbeln den HSV auseinander |url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/spieltag/1-bundesliga/2011-12/1/1142231/spielanalyse_borussia-dortmund-17_hamburger-sv-12.html |publisher=[[Kicker (sports magazine)|kicker]] |access-date=5 August 2011 |language=de}}</ref> During a [[2011–12 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] match on 13 September 2011, he scored a late equaliser with a volley from 20 yards against [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] after entering as a substitute in the 69th minute.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/newsid=1668949.html | title = Perišić denies Arsenal for Dortmund | website = UEFA.com | date = 13 September 2011 | access-date = 20 January 2020 | first = Chris | last = Burke}}</ref> On 14 October, he scored the first goal in a 2–0 win against [[SV Werder Bremen|Werder Bremen]], a match in which he was later sent off for a second bookable offence.<ref>{{cite news |title=Perisic sorgt für Paukenschläge |url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/spieltag/1-bundesliga/2011-12/9/1143361/spielanalyse_werder-bremen-4_borussia-dortmund-17.html |publisher=[[Kicker (sports magazine)|kicker]] |access-date=14 October 2011 |language=de}}</ref> On 21 April 2012, he scored the important 1–0 goal against [[Borussia Mönchengladbach]] and opened the door to the eighth national championship for Dortmund. The game ended 2–0 (the second was scored by [[Shinji Kagawa]]).<ref>{{cite news |title=Borussia Dortmund clinch back-to-back Bundesliga titles |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2012/apr/21/borussia-dortmund-clinch-bundesliga-title |work=The Guardian |date=21 April 2012}}</ref>

Perišić began the [[2012–13 Bundesliga]] season scoring a brace in a 3–2 loss against Hamburger SV on 22 September 2012.<ref>{{cite news |title=Adler bringt Dortmund zur Verzweiflung |url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/spieltag/1-bundesliga/2012-13/4/1441677/spielanalyse_hamburger-sv-12_borussia-dortmund-17.html |publisher=[[Kicker (sports magazine)|kicker]] |access-date=22 September 2012 |language=de}}</ref> However, Perišić soon found his first team opportunities limited, having played less in the first team and soon told Croatian channel [[Nova TV (Croatia)|Nova TV]] he had received no support from Dortmund manager [[Jürgen Klopp]] and accused him of favouring other players.<ref>{{cite news |title=Borussia Dortmund hold contract extension talks with Marcel Schmelzer |url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/11095/8253377/ |publisher=Sky Sports |date=16 November 2012
|access-date=12 February 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180816111150/http://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/8253377|archive-date=16 August 2018}}</ref> In response, Klopp criticised his actions as childish and Perišić faced a fine due to his comment.<ref>{{cite news |title=Klopp: Perisic should keep his mouth shut |url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/3275/bundesliga/2012/11/17/3534575/klopp-perisic-should-keep-his-mouth-shut |publisher=Goal.com |date=17 November 2012 |access-date=11 February 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210415201424/https://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/3275/bundesliga/2012/11/17/3534575/klopp-perisic-should-keep-his-mouth-shut|archive-date=15 April 2021}}</ref>

===VfL Wolfsburg===
[[File:Krasnodar-Wolfsburg (18).jpg|thumb|upright|Perišić playing for [[VfL Wolfsburg]] in 2014]]

FC Nürnberg]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.whoscored.com/Matches/724028/Live/Germany-Bundesliga-2013-2014-Wolfsburg-Nurnberg |title=Wolfsburg-Nurnberg |publisher=WhoScored.com |date=12 April 2014}}</ref> Perišić finished the season with ten league goals, second-best on the team behind fellow countryman [[Ivica Olić]].{{citation needed|date=June 2021}}

====2015–16 season====
Perišić made his competitive debut for the club three days after signing, starting and playing 85 minutes in a 1–0 win against cross-city rivals [[A.C. Milan|Milan]] in the ''[[Derby della Madonnina]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/34220316|title=Inter Milan 1-0 Milan|publisher=BBC Sport|date=14 September 2015|access-date=27 April 2016}}</ref> He opened his scoring account on 4 October in his fifth league appearance in the 1–1 away draw against [[U.C. Sampdoria|Sampdoria]], profiting from [[Mauro Icardi]]'s assist,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/match/sampdoria-vs-internazionale/2120444/report|title=Sampdoria 1-1 Inter: Perisic leveller earns point for Mancini's men|publisher=Goal.com|author=Stefan Coerts|date=4 October 2015|access-date=27 April 2016}}</ref> which was followed by another goal against [[U.S. Città di Palermo|Palermo]] two weeks later.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/match/palermo-vs-internazionale/2120463/report|title=Palermo 1-1 Inter: Gilardino denies Nerazzurri|publisher=Goal.com|date=24 October 2015|access-date=27 April 2016}}</ref>

On 15 December, Perišić made his [[2015–16 Coppa Italia|Coppa Italia]] debut in the round of 16 match against [[Cagliari Calcio|Cagliari]] at home, appearing as a second-half substitute and scoring the team's third goal of the match in an eventual 3–0 win.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.serpentsofmadonnina.com/2015/12/16/10239774/inter-milan-3-0-cagliari-coppa-italia-round-of-16 | title = Inter Milan 3-0 Cagliari: Simple Enough | language = it | website = serpentsofmadonnina.com | date = 16 December 2015 | access-date = 28 May 2020 | first = Baraka | last = Nasari}}</ref> He began 2016 on 6 January in the match away against [[Empoli F.C.|Empoli]]; his cross from close range was finished home by Icardi for the only goal of the match, which kept Inter top to the table.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en-us/match/empoli-vs-internazionale/2120547/report|title=Inter 1-0 Empoli: Icardi keeps Mancini's men top|publisher=Goal.com|author=Nicholas McGee|date=6 January 2016|access-date=27 April 2016}}</ref> On 7 February, during the match against [[Hellas Verona F.C.|Hellas Verona]], Perišić came on as a 46th-minute substitute to change the fate of the match, providing an assist for Icardi and also scoring for himself to level the result 3–3, rescuing a point for his side.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/feb/07/ivan-perisic-inter-3-3hellas-verona|title=Ivan Perisic rescues point for Inter in 3-3 draw against Hellas Verona|work=The Guardian|date=7 February 2016|access-date=27 April 2016}}</ref>

March was Perišić's best month in personal terms, scoring four goals and providing three assists. On 2 March, in the returning leg of Coppa Italia's semi-final against [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]] at [[San Siro]], Perišić scored the team's second goal of the match to help Inter overturn the 3–0 defeat and equal the aggregate 3–3, which led the match into the penalty shoot-outs. However, Inter lost 5–3 and were eliminated from the competition.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/mar/02/internazionale-juventus-coppa-italia-match-report|title=Inter overturn 3-0 deficit but lose Coppa Italia shootout to Juventus|work=The Guardian|date=2 March 2016|access-date=6 March 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.football-italia.net/80709/coppa-juve-penalties-break-inter-hearts|title=Coppa: Juve penalties break Inter hearts|publisher=Football Italia|date=2 March 2016|access-date=6 March 2016}}</ref> Perišić scored Inter's last goal of [[2015–16 Inter Milan season|2015–16 season]] in a 2–1 home win against Empoli on the final matchday.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.news18.com/news/sports/inter-milan-beat-empoli-2-1-to-secure-4th-spot-in-serie-a-1240188.html|title=Inter Milan Beat Empoli 2-1 to Secure 4th Spot in Serie A|publisher=News18.com|date=8 May 2016|access-date=6 March 2016}}</ref>

Perišić finished his first season with Inter Milan by playing 37 matches, including 34 in league, scoring nine goals, seven of them in league, and Inter Milan finished fourth in [[2015–16 Serie A|Serie A]], returning in European competitions after a one-year absence and was eliminated in the semi-final in the Coppa Italia. He was also Inter Milan's top assist provider with six assists.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/52941/2015-16-stats-icardi-ruthless-perisic-the-top-provider|title=2015/16 STATS: ICARDI RUTHLESS, PERISIC THE TOP PROVIDER|publisher=inter.it|date=21 May 2016|access-date=19 June 2017|archive-date=26 November 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181126134919/https://www.inter.it/en/news/52941/2015-16-stats-icardi-ruthless-perisic-the-top-provider|url-status=dead}}</ref>

====2016–17 season====
Perišić opened [[2016–17 Inter Milan season|his second Inter season]] by playing in the last 30 minutes of the first matchday as fell away at [[A.C. ChievoVerona|Chievo]].<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.serpentsofmadonnina.com/2016/8/21/12577228/chievo-verona-2-0-inter-milan-match-recap-patience-my-young-padawan | title = Chievo Verona 2-0 Inter Milan: Patience my young padawan | language = it | website = serpentsofmadonnina.com | date = 21 August 2016 | access-date = 28 May 2020 | first = Baraka | last = Nasari}}</ref> He then scored his first goal of the new season in the ''[[Derby d'Italia]]'' against Juventus at home, entering in the 69th minute and heading home an Mauro Icardi cross nine minutes later to give Inter second win of the season, also the first in league against Juventus since November 2012.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.football-italia.net/SerieA/match/87859|title=Inter 2 – 1 Juventus|publisher=Football Italia|date=18 September 2016|access-date=18 September 2016}}</ref>

Perišić played his first European match for Inter Milan on 29 September in team's second [[2016–17 UEFA Europa League]] [[2016–17 UEFA Europa League group stage#Group K|group stage]] match against [[AC Sparta Prague|Sparta Prague]], appearing in the last 27 minutes of a 3–1 away defeat.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/54911/inter-beaten-3-1-by-sparta|title=INTER BEATEN 3-1 BY SPARTA|publisher=inter.it|date=29 September 2016|access-date=19 June 2017|archive-date=9 July 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180709010756/https://www.inter.it/en/news/54911/inter-beaten-3-1-by-sparta|url-status=dead}}</ref> On 20 November, in the ''Derby'' against rivals Milan, he first provided the [[Antonio Candreva]]'s long-range strike before scoring himself a last minute equaliser, as Inter took one point in the last moments.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/37972467|title=AC Milan 2-2 Inter Milan|publisher=BBC Sport|date=20 November 2016|access-date=19 June 2017}}</ref>

On 8 January 2017, Inter Milan's first match of the calendar year, Perišić provided a [[Man of the Match]] performance by scoring both goals in a 2–1 away win at [[Udinese Calcio|Udinese]]; it was his first Inter brace which took his tally up to six goals.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/56425/perisic-double-downs-udinese|title=PERISIC DOUBLE DOWNS UDINESE|publisher=inter.it|date=8 January 2017|access-date=19 June 2017|archive-date=9 July 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180709010225/https://www.inter.it/en/news/56425/perisic-double-downs-udinese|url-status=dead}}</ref> This was followed by another splendid individual performance against Chievo six days later as he scored his team's second goal after an individual effort in an eventual 3–1 win.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/match/internazionale-vs-chievo/2305944/report|title=Inter 3-1 Chievo: Three second half goals seal dramatic win for Pioli's men|publisher=Goal.com|date=14 January 2017|access-date=19 June 2017}}</ref>

On 5 February, in the matchday 23 against Juventus at [[Juventus Stadium]], Perišić received his first-ever career [[Penalty card#Red card|red card]] as Inter Milan were defeated 1–0.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/match/juventus-vs-internazionale/2305975/report|title=Juventus 1-0 Inter: Cuadrado stunner gives Old Lady three points|publisher=Goal.com|date=5 February 2017|access-date=19 June 2017}}</ref> He was subsequently banned for two matches by [[Italian Football Federation]] (FIGC) for aggressive confrontation of the referee. After Inter Milan appealed the suspension, it was reduced to one match.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/56999/perisic-ban-reduced-on-appeal|title=PERISIC BAN REDUCED ON APPEAL|publisher=inter.it|date=10 February 2017|access-date=19 June 2017|archive-date=8 July 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180708220947/https://www.inter.it/en/news/56999/perisic-ban-reduced-on-appeal|url-status=dead}}</ref> He returned from suspension on 19 February in the 1–0 win at [[Bologna F.C. 1909|Bologna]],<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.espnfc.us/club/internazionale/110/blog/post/3064908/gabriel-gabigol-barbosa-takes-his-chance-with-winner-for-inter-at-bologna|title=Gabriel 'Gabigol' Barbosa takes his chance with winner for Inter at Bologna|publisher=ESPN FC|author1=Edoardo Dalmonte|date=20 February 2017|access-date=20 February 2017}}</ref> and scored his second brace on 5 March in a 5–1 thrashing of Cagliari at [[Stadio Sant'Elia]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.serpentsofmadonnina.com/2017/3/5/14820288/cagliari-1-5-inter-that-went-well-serie-a-recap-2017|title=Cagliari 1-5 Inter: That went well|publisher=Serpentsofmadonnina.com|date=5 March 2017|access-date=19 June 2017}}</ref>

On 22 April, Perišić reached double-figures for the first time with Inter Milan after scoring in a 5–4 away defeat against [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2017/04/22/italy/serie-a/acf-fiorentina/fc-internazionale-milano/2306077/|title=Fiorentina 5 – 4 Internazionale|publisher=Soccerway|date=22 April 2017|access-date=19 June 2017}}</ref> On the final matchday, he provided a stunning individual performance by providing two assists after individual efforts, also scoring his 11th goal of the season as Inter thrashed Udinese 5–2 at home end the season on a high.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.football-italia.net/SerieA/match/88201|title=Inter 5 - 2 Udinese|publisher=Football Italia|date=21 May 2017|access-date=30 May 2017}}</ref> Perišić finished his second Inter season by making 42 appearances in all competitions, including 36 in league, which 31 were as starter, as Inter finished the [[2016–17 Serie A|Serie A]] in seventh position, once again failing to qualify for the [[2017–18 UEFA Champions League|UEFA Champions League]]. He scored 11 goals, his highest tally since 2010–11 season with Club Brugge; and also provided ten assists, including eight in Serie A, breaking his last season's record.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.inter.it/en/statistiche?season=2016&competition=21|title=STATISTICS|publisher=inter.it|access-date=19 June 2017|archive-date=8 August 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180808140112/https://www.inter.it/en/statistiche?competition=21&season=2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

====2017–18 season====
Perišić started [[2017–18 Inter Milan season|his third Inter season]] on a high, first scoring and assisting in the opening day of [[2017–18 Serie A]] against Fiorentina, then providing two assists in the away match at [[A.S. Roma|Roma]] as Inter won 3–1,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/59379/roma-1-3-inter|title=ROMA 1–3 INTER|publisher=inter.it|date=26 August 2017|access-date=26 August 2017|archive-date=27 June 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180627005235/https://www.inter.it/en/news/59379/roma-1-3-inter|url-status=dead}}</ref> Inter Milan's first league win at the [[Stadio Olimpico]] in nine years.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/59391/roma-inter-all-you-need-to-know|title=ROMA – INTER: ALL YOU NEED TO KNOW|publisher=inter.it|date=27 August 2017|access-date=27 August 2017|archive-date=4 July 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180704063558/https://www.inter.it/en/news/59391/roma-inter-all-you-need-to-know|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On 8 September, Perišić signed a new contract extension with Inter Milan, keeping him at the San Siro until June 2022. After signing, the pitch is the only thing left for me to think about."<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/59547/perisic-its-a-special-day-my-head-is-now-solely-on-the-pitch|title=PERISIC: "IT'S A SPECIAL DAY, MY HEAD IS NOW SOLELY ON THE PITCH"|publisher=inter.it|date=8 September 2017|access-date=8 September 2017|archive-date=23 April 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190423025210/https://www.inter.it/en/news/59547/perisic-its-a-special-day-my-head-is-now-solely-on-the-pitch|url-status=dead}}</ref>

His second goal of the season, a late screamer outside the zone against newly promoted [[S.P.A.L. 2013|SPAL]] two days later, was his 20th career Serie A goal.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.espn.com/soccer/report?gameId=491665 | title = Ivan Perisic scores screamer as Inter Milan ease past SPAL | website = ESPN | date = 10 September 2017 | access-date = 28 May 2020}}</ref> Perišić scored his first [[List of Serie A hat-tricks|Serie A hat-trick]] on 3 December in the 5–0 home win over Chievo.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/42214800 | title = Inter Milan 5-0 Chievo | website = BBC Sport | date = 3 December 2017 | access-date = 20 January 2020}}</ref> His 100th appearance in all competitions for Inter occurred later on 30 December in the goalless draw versus [[S.S. Lazio|Lazio]] on matchday 19.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/61025/inter-0-0-lazio-all-you-need-to-know|title=INTER 0-0 LAZIO: ALL YOU NEED TO KNOW|publisher=inter.it|date=31 December 2017|access-date=31 December 2017|archive-date=12 November 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211112145716/https://www.inter.it/en/news/61025/inter-0-0-lazio-all-you-need-to-know|url-status=dead}}</ref>

====2018–19 season====
Perišić played his first [[2018–19 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] game for Inter on 18 September 2018, in a 2–1 victory over [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ćubel|first=Jelena|date=18 September 2018|title=Inter - Tottenham 2:1, Perišić: Dali smo sve što smo imali! In January 2019, English club Arsenal attempted to sign Perišić.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.planetfootball.com/quick-reads/comparing-ivan-perisics-2018-19-inter-stats-to-arsenals-existing-forwards/|title=Comparing Ivan Perisic's 2018-19 Inter stats to Arsenal's existing forwards|website=planetfootball|date=29 January 2019}}</ref> He agreed to a deal with the club; however, Inter Milan blocked the deal. Perišić featured in 34 matches in [[2018–19 Serie A|Serie A]], only behind [[Samir Handanović]] and [[Matteo Politano]], scoring 8 goals; hence being the second top scorer for Inter after Icardi. Following the appointment of [[Antonio Conte]], Perišić struggled to fit into the new manager's system during [[2019–20 Inter Milan season#Pre-season and friendlies|pre-season]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/international-football/2019/07/20/5d33581e22601d36218b45bd.html|title=Conte frustrated by Perisic: He can't play in the role that I want at Inter|date=20 July 2019|access-date=7 February 2020|publisher=Marca}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Despite being born in [[Split, Croatia|Split]], Perišić grew up in the town of [[Omiš]]. As a child, he worked on his father's poultry farm.<ref name=":0" />

Perišić married Josipa in 2012, having first met her while they were in high school.<ref>{{cite news |date=22 June 2016 |title=Ljubav iz školskih klupa Perišić svoju Josipu voli od dječjih dana! |url=https://dnevnik.hr/showbuzz/celebrity/ivan-perisic-suprugu-upoznao-jos-u-skoli---441073.html |language=hr |work=[[Nova TV (Croatia)|Dnevnik Nove TV]] |access-date=31 October 2017}}</ref> The pair have two children: a son, Leonardo, born on 9 October 2012; and a daughter, Manuela, born on 28 July 2014.<ref>{{cite news |date=9 October 2012 |title=Ivan Perisic ist Papa geworden! |trans-title=Ivan Perisic has become a father! |url=https://plus.google.com/+BorussiaDortmund/posts/YUgoiBfBeyN |language=de |work=[[Google+]] |access-date=31 October 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Dasović |first=Tomislav |title=Ivan Perišić dobio je kćer Manuelu |url=https://www.vecernji.hr/showbiz/ivan-perisic-dobio-je-kcer-manuelu-952919 |language=hr |work=[[Večernji list]] |access-date=31 October 2017}}</ref>
