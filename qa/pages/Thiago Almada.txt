'''Thiago Ezequiel Almada''' (born 26 April 2001) is an Argentine professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as an [[attacking midfielder]] or [[Midfielder#Winger|winger]] for [[Major League Soccer]] club [[Atlanta United FC|Atlanta United]] and the [[Argentina national football team|Argentina national team]]. He was a member of the Argentina team that won the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], and made history being the first active [[Major League Soccer|MLS]] player to win a world title.

== Early life ==
Thiago 'Guayo' Ezequiel Almada was born on 26 April 2001, in [[Ciudadela, Buenos Aires]] in [[Argentina]]. He grew up in the neighbourhood of [[Fuerte Apache]], which is known for high crime rates and prevalent drug use.<ref>{{Cite web|last=SeventhQueen|date=28 August 2019|title=LN – The evil called Fuerte Apache, a neighborhood marked by stigmas|url=https://basi-spanish.com/ln-the-evil-called-fuerte-apache-a-neighbourhood-marked-by-stigmas/|access-date=23 September 2021|website=About Argentina|language=es-AR}}</ref> Almada spent many of his early years selling fruit and vegetables door-to-door to earn extra money. Much time was spent with his grandparents while his parents worked.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Thiago Almada: Argentina's next great playmaker on Man City's radar {{!}} Goal.com|url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/thiago-almada-argentina-playmaker-man-city/1si536etb6tmx1fnyui4xivyom|access-date=23 September 2021|website=www.goal.com}}</ref>

== Club career ==

=== Vélez Sarsfield ===
At the age of four, Almada played football for a local club, Santa Clara, where [[Carlos Tevez]] also began his career. He was picked up by Vélez Sarsfield at age five, where he made his way through the youth academy. In August 2018, just four months shy of his 17th birthday, Almada made his professional debut with the club.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.clarin.com/deportes/futbol/thiago-almada-centennial-fuerte-apache-ilusiona-velez_0_BJpvTo2B7.html|title=Thiago Almada, el centennial de Fuerte Apache que ilusiona a Vélez |date=2 October 2018 }}</ref> In October 2018, he was included in [[The Guardian]]'s "Next Generation 2018".<ref>{{cite web|date=11 October 2018|title=Next Generation 2018: 60 of the best young talents in world football|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/ng-interactive/2018/oct/11/next-generation-2018-60-of-the-best-young-talents-in-world-football|work=The Guardian|accessdate=12 October 2018}}</ref>

Almada began his first-team career as a [[wing forward]] under manager [[Gabriel Heinze]]. He played alongside [[Matías Vargas (footballer, born 1997)|Matías Vargas]], [[Nicolás Domínguez]], [[Lucas Robertone]], and [[Fernando Gago]]. In his first two years with Heinze at the helm, Almada appeared in 46 matches and tallied nine goals.{{Citation needed|date=November 2021}} In 2020, new club manager [[Mauricio Pellegrino]] moved Almada to [[midfielder]], due to the departure of several playersdfield, Almada earned 15 goals in 42 matches.{{Citation needed|date=November 2021}}

=== Atlanta  United ===

==== Signing and 2022 season ====
On 4 December 2021, Vélez Sarsfield announced that it had reached an agreement with [[Major League Soccer]] club [[Atlanta United FC|Atlanta United]] for the pre-transfer of Almada in February 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|date=4 December 2021|title=La noche del 10|trans-title=The night of the 10|url=https://velez.com.ar/futbol/notas/2021/12/04/020350_la-noche-del-10|url-status=live|access-date=6 December 2021|publisher=[[Club Atlético Vélez Sarsfield]]|language=es}}</ref> The transfer, reportedly worth a league-record $16 million, was officially announced by Atlanta on 9 February 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.atlutd.com/news/atlanta-united-acquires-thiago-almada-from-ca-velez-sarsfield-argentina|title=Atlanta United acquires Thiago Almada from CA Vélez Sarsfield &#124; Atlanta United FC}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|title=Thiago Almada to move to Atlanta United for MLS-record $16 million fee, Argentine club says|url=https://theathletic.com/news/thiago-almada-to-move-to-atlanta-united-for-mls-record-16-million-fee-argentine-club-says/AQD2XO3jL8bx/|access-date=9 February 2022|website=The Athletic|language=en}}</ref>

On 10 February 2022, Atlanta United officially announced the transfer of Almada with a series of photos on the club's [[Instagram]] page, showing him with the club's president [[Darren Eales]] at the [[Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport]] in Atlanta. Shortly afterward, Almada was unveiled in the club's kit for the very first time in front of thousands of fans in a virtual welcome ceremony (due to the ongoing [[COVID-19 pandemic]]).<ref>{{Cite web|date=10 February 2022|title=Atlanta United acquires Thiago Almada from CA Velez Sarsfield|url=https://www.atlutd.com/news/atlanta-united-acquires-thiago-almada-from-ca-velez-sarsfield-argentina|url-status=live|access-date=15 February 2022|publisher=[[Atlanta United FC]]|language=en}}</ref>

On 11 February 2022, Almada came under scrutiny from supporters in Atlanta after it was revealed that in his final match for Sarsfield, he had scored a goal and celebrated with a racially insensitive gesture with his eyes, this raised many concerns for the club, and his future, but he had apologized shortly after being asked about the situation.<ref>{{Cite web|date=11 February 2022|title=Atlanta United newcomer Thiago Almada apologizes to Atlanta United supporters|url=https://www.ajc.com/sports/atlanta-united/almada-apologizes-to-atlanta-united-supporters/|url-status=live|access-date=15 February 2022|publisher=[[Atlanta Journal-Constitution]]|language=en}}</ref>

On 14 February 2022, Almada made his official debut for Atlanta wearing the number 8 in a pre-season match versus Mexican outfit [[C.D. Guadalajara]] during Atlanta's pre-season tour of Mexico. Atlanta would end up losing the match 3–0.<ref>{{Cite web|date=14 February 2022|title=Thiago Almada debuts in Atlanta United's 3–0 defeat to Guadalajara, Dom Dwyer starts in 3–0 loss against Tepatitlan FC|url=https://www.dirtysouthsoccer.com/2022/2/13/22931878/atlanta-united-loses-3-0-to-c-d-guadalajara-in-preseason-friendly/|url-status=live|access-date=15 February 2022|publisher=[[Dirty South Soccer]]|language=en}}</ref>

On 15 March 2022, Almada made his MLS debut against expansion team [[Charlotte FC]] as a substitute in Atlanta's win against them, 2–1. Almada had 18 touches in 34 minutes; he said, "I wanted to be here” and “I wanted to get to know everybody. It worked out well in the end. I’m here. I’m ready to fight for everything that we have to fight for.”{{Citation needed|date=February 2023}} Shortly after the Charlotte FC game, on 19 March 2022, Almada scored his first ever [[MLS]] goal in a 3–3 draw vs [[CF Montréal]]. The goal would end up winning the MLS' Goal of The Week contest for Matchday 4.<ref>{{Cite web|date=19 March 2022|title=Thiago Almada's screamer wins MLS AT&T Goal of The Week for Matchday 4|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/thiago-almada-s-smasher-wins-mls-week-4-at-t-goal-of-the-week|url-status=live|access-date=22 April 2022|publisher=[[Major League Soccer]]|language=en}}</ref>

==== 2023 season ====
On 25 February 2023, Almada scored two goals (one of which was a [[Free kick (association football)|free kick]]) in the first game of the [[2023 Major League Soccer season|2023 season]], helping Atlanta gain a 2–1 win over [[San Jose Earthquakes]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Booth |first=Chuck |date=26 February 2023 |title=Free kick excellence from Thiago Almada sees Atlanta United to an opening day victory |url=https://www.cbssports.com/soccer/news/free-kick-excellence-from-thiago-almada-sees-atlanta-united-to-an-opening-day-victory/ |access-date=26 February 2023 |website=CBS Sports |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Schirru |first=Francesco |date=26 February 2023 |title=Almada superstar: dal Mondiale vinto alla doppietta d'oro nel primo match post Qatar |url=https://www.goal.com/it/notizie/almada-argentina-mls-dove-gioca-atlanta/bltaabceacddd632aac |access-date=26 February 2023 |website=Goal.com |language=it-IT}}</ref> As a result, Almada was named MLS Player of the Week,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ajc.com/sports/atlanta-united/atlanta-uniteds-thiago-almada-voted-mls-player-of-the-week/X2PPSTPY35F6HHYXHCN57MDSRY/|title=Atlanta United's Thiago Almada voted MLS player of the week|date=February 28, 2023|last=Robertson|first=Doug|work=[[The Atlanta Journal-Constitution]]|access-date=February 28, 2023}}</ref> as well as being featured in the league's Team of the Matchday.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/team-of-the-matchday-who-shined-brightest-on-mls-is-back-weekend|title=Team of the Matchday: Who shined brightest on MLS is Back weekend?|date=February 28, 2023|last=Sigal|first=Jonathan|work=[[Major League Soccer]]|access-date=February 28, 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
Almada played for the [[Argentina national under-20 football team|Argentina under-20]] squad in 2019, before featuring for the [[Argentina national under-23 football team|under-23]] squad in 2021.{{Citation needed|date=November 2021}}

His debut in the [[Argentina national football team]] came on 23 September 2022, in Miami, during a friendly match against Honduras.{{Citation needed|date=February 2023}}

In November 2022, Almada was officially called up to the Argentinian [[2022 FIFA World Cup|World Cup]] squad to replace injured [[Joaquín Correa]], only five days before Argentina’s first game of the tournament.<ref>{{cite tweet|number=1593352407299153920|user=Argentina|title=El futbolista Thiago Almada se suma a la convocatoria mundialista de Qatar 2022.|author=Argentina national football team|author-link=Argentina national football team|date=17 November 2022|access-date=17 November 2022|language=es|trans-title=Thiago Almada has been added to the squad for Qatar 2022.}}</ref> He was a member of the squad that ultimately won the tournament, becoming the first active MLS player to win a World Cup.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Vertelney |first1=Seth |title=Thiago Almada becomes first active MLS player to win World Cup |url=https://prosoccerwire.usatoday.com/2022/12/18/almada-become-first-active-mls-player-to-win-world-cup/ |website=[[USA Today]] |publisher=[[Gannett]] |access-date=23 January 2023 |date=18 December 2022}}</ref> In Argentina's final group stage game, he made his World Cup 2022 debut against Poland. He came on in the 86th minute as a substitute of [[Alexis Mac Allister]] in which Argentina was victorious by 2-0.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-12-02 |title=Thiago Almada reacts to historic World Cup debut with Argentina |url=https://www.90min.com/posts/thiago-almada-reacts-historic-world-cup-debut-argentina |access-date=2023-03-24 |website=90min.com |language=en-GB}}</ref>

On 23 March 2023, Almada scored his first international goal for Argentina at the senior level during a friendly against Panama in his third appearance for the senior team, becoming the first-ever MLS-based player to score for the national team.<ref>https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/thiago-almada-scores-for-argentina-as-lionel-messi-hits-800-career-goals {{bare URL inline|date=April 2023}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
In February 2021, Almada was identified as a person of interest by local authorities after a party in [[San Isidro, Buenos Aires]] on 4 December 2020. A 28-year-old woman said several people sexually abused her in a house rented by [[Juan Martín Lucero]]. After the investigation became public, his club suspended both him and [[Miguel Brizuela]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=9 February 2021|title=Argentina's Velez suspends players charged with sexual abuse|url=https://apnews.com/article/buenos-aires-argentina-soccer-thiago-sexual-abuse-9dacbd266ed17eb82208f22ab885feeb|access-date=23 September 2021|website=AP NEWS|language=en}}</ref> A week after Almada was suspended, he was reinstated with the club stating, "substantial modifications were generated by virtue of the incorporation of new evidence, expertise and testimonies."<ref>{{Cite web|date=16 February 2021|title=Vélez Sarsfield reinstated Thiago Almada and Miguel Brizuela to the squad|url=https://lapelotita.com/2021/02/16/velez-sarsfield-reinstated-thiago-almada-and-miguel-brizuela-to-the-squad/|access-date=23 September 2021|website=La Pelotita|language=en-US}}</ref>
