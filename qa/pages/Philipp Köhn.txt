'''Philipp François Köhn''' (born 2 April 1998) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Austrian Football Bundesliga|Austrian Bundesliga]] club [[FC Red Bull Salzburg|Red Bull Salzburg]]. Born in Germany, he plays for the [[Switzerland national under-21 football team|Switzerland U21 national team]].<ref>{{Soccerway|philipp-kohn/414682}}</ref>

==Personal life==
He was born in [[Dinslaken]], [[North Rhine-Westphalia]], Germany to a German father and a Swiss mother from [[Lausanne]].

==Club career==

===RB Leipzig===
On 23 February 2017, the then [[RB Leipzig|Leipzig]] sporting director, [[Ralf Rangnick]] announced that Köhn would sign with the club as soon as his [[VfB Stuttgart|Stuttgart]] contract expires in the summer. On 4 June 2017, Köhn signed a four year deal keeping him at the German club till June 2021. He didn't make any appearances for the club.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.apnews.com/ad3504c548774521a0ab8c9455a559c3|title=Leipzig snaps up promising goalkeeper Koehn from Stuttgart|accessdate=31 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://soccer360magazine.com/2017/02/23/rb-leipzig-confirm-first-summer-signing|title=RB LEIPZIG CONFIRM FIRST SUMMER SIGNING|accessdate=31 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.rotowire.com/soccer/player.php?id=24455|title=Philipp Kohn Soccer Stats & News|accessdate=31 August 2019}}</ref>

===Red Bull Salzburg===
On 6 July 2018, [[FC Red Bull Salzburg|Red Bull Salzburg]] announced that they had signed Köhn from [[RB Leipzig|Leipzig]], their unofficial sister club, on a four-year contract. Since [[FC Liefering|Liefering]] is Salzburg's [[Farm team|feeder team]], he is eligible to play for both clubs as a cooperation player.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.redbullsalzburg.at/en/fc-red-bull-salzburg/news/saison_2018_19/welcome-to-salzburg-philipp-koehn.html|title=FC Red Bull Salzburg - Welcome to Salzburg, Philipp Köhn!|accessdate=31 August 2019}}</ref> During the 2019/2020 pre-season friendly, Köhn played in a football match between Salzburg and [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] in the [[Stadion Wals-Siezenheim|Red Bull Arena]], replacing [[Cican Stankovic]] at half time. He conceded two goals from [[Pedro (footballer, born 1987)|Pedro]] and [[Michy Batshuayi|Batshuayi]] as the match ended in a 5–3 loss.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://soccerway.com/matches/2019/07/31/world/club-friendlies/fc-red-bull-salzburg/chelsea-football-club/3024312|title=Salzburg vs. Chelsea - 31 July 2019|date=6 July 2018|accessdate=31 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.chelsea-mad.co.uk/news/tmnw/salzburg_35_chelsea_report_ratings_reaction_as_superb_blues_cruise_past_roten_bu_935440/index.shtml|title=Salzburg 3-5 Chelsea: Report, Ratings & Reaction as Superb Blues Cruise Past Roten Bullen|accessdate=31 August 2019}}</ref> He is yet to make his first team debut for Red Bull Salzburg, but has featured for Liefering twelve times.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.redbullsalzburg.at/en/fc-red-bull-salzburg/team/bullenherde/koehn.html|title=FC Red Bull Salzburg - koehn|date=6 July 2018|accessdate=31 August 2019}}</ref>

=== Wil ===
On 29 July 2020 he went to [[Switzerland|Swiss]] side [[FC Wil|Wil]] on loan.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=29 July 2020|title=Philipp Köhn leihweise in die Schweiz|url=https://www.redbullsalzburg.at/de/fc-red-bull-salzburg/news/saison_2019_20/philipp-koehn-leihweise-in-die-schweiz.html|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=18 August 2020|language=de}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=29 July 2020|title=SCHWEIZER U21-NATIONALTORHÜTER KOMMT|url=https://fcwil.ch/schweizer-u21-nationaltorhueter-kommt/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=18 August 2020|language=de}}</ref>
After 33 matches he went back to Salzburg.

==International career==
Köhn has represented Germany from [[Germany national under-15 football team|under-15]] to [[Germany national under-18 football team|under-18]] age group, before switching his allegiance to Switzerland. He has been playing for Switzerland from [[Switzerland national under-19 football team|under-19]]. He currently plays for the Switzerland [[Switzerland national under-21 football team|under-21]],<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.footballdatabase.eu/en/player/details/311675-philipp-kohn|title=FC Philipp Köhn - Red Bull Salzburg|accessdate=31 August 2019}}</ref> and was named to Switzerland's 2022 World Cup squad.
