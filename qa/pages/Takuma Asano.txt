{{Nihongo|'''Takuma Asano'''|浅野 拓磨|Asano Takuma|born 10 November 1994}} is a Japanese professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)#Winger|winger]] or a [[Forward (association football)#Inside forward|forward]] for [[Bundesliga]] club [[VfL Bochum]] and the [[Japan national football team|Japan national team]].

His younger brother [[Yuya Asano|Yuya]] is also a professional footballer currently playing for [[J1 League]] side [[Hokkaido Consadole Sapporo]].

==Club career==

===Sanfrecce Hiroshima===
After attending Yokkaichi Chuo Technical High School, Asano joined [[Sanfrecce Hiroshima]] at the age of 18 in January 2013. He went on to win the [[J1 League]] in his [[2013 J.League Division 1|debut season]], making one appearance in the process.

Asano went on to win the [[Japanese Super Cup]] in [[2014 Japanese Super Cup|2014]], while starting to break into the squad for the J1 League. He helped the side lift the league title for a second time in [[2015 J1 League|2015]], this time adding nine goals in 34 appearances. Due to his performances, Asano was named the J1 League [[J.League Rookie of the Year|Rookie of the Year]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.arsenal.com/news/news-archive/20160702/arsenal-agree-deal-for-japan-forward | title=Arsenal agree deal for Japan forward | publisher=arsenal.com | date=3 July 2016 | access-date=3 July 2016}}</ref>

===Arsenal===
{{Wikinews|Arsenal signs Japanese Takuma; Chelsea signs Batshuayi}}
On 3 July 2016, Asano was announced as [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]]'s second signing of the season, subject to a medical and international clearance.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/10332294/arsenal-to-sign-takuma-asano-from-japans-sanfrecce-hiroshima | title=Arsenal to sign Takuma Asano from Japan's Sanfrecce Hiroshima | publisher=skysports.com | date=3 July 2016 | access-date=3 July 2016}}</ref> Manager [[Arsène Wenger]] described him as "a talented young striker and very much one for the future".<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/jul/03/arsenal-sign-takuma-asano-j-league-football-transfer-window | title=Arsenal sign Japanese striker Takuma Asano from Sanfrecce Hiroshima | publisher=theguardian.com | date=3 July 2016 | access-date=3 July 2016}}</ref> However, Asano was refused a work permit to play in the [[Premier League]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/11/transfer-zone/2016/08/21/26741872/new-arsenal-signing-asano-refused-work-permit | title=New Arsenal signing Asano refused work permit | publisher=goal.com | date=21 August 2016 | access-date=18 October 2019}} {{dead link|date=December 2022}}</ref>

====Loan to VfB Stuttgart====
On 26 August 2016, Asano was loaned out to [[VfB Stuttgart]] until the end of [[2016–17 VfB Stuttgart season|the season]] with an option for a further year.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.vfb.de/en/vfb/latest/newsarchive/2016/329855-takuma-asano-signs-from-arsenal/ | title=Takuma Asano kommt von Arsenal | publisher=vfb.de | date=26 August 2016 | access-date=26 August 2016}}</ref> He made his debut in a 2–1 home loss to [[1. FC Heidenheim]] two weeks later. On 9 April 2017, Asano scored a brace to give his side a 2–0 win over [[Karlsruher SC]]. He helped the team win the [[2. Bundesliga]] and take promotion to the [[Bundesliga|top flight]].

On 22 June 2017, the loan deal with Stuttgart was extended for [[2017–18 VfB Stuttgart season|another season]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.arsenal.com/news/news-archive/20170622/asano-s-loan-at-stuttgart-is-extended | title=Asano's loan at Stuttgart is extended | publisher=arsenal.com | date=22 June 2017 | access-date=22 June 2017}}</ref>

====Loan to Hannover 96====
On 23 May 2018, Asano was sent on [[2018–19 Hannover 96 season|season-long]] loan to Bundesliga side [[Hannover 96]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.arsenal.com/news/asano-join-hannover-96-loan | title=Asano to join Hannover 96 on loan | publisher=arsenal.com | date=23 May 2018 | access-date=8 June 2018}}</ref>

===Partizan===
On 1 August 2019, Asano completed his move to Serbian club [[FK Partizan|Partizan]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/arsenal/takuma-asano-leaves-arsenal-japan-striker-joins-partizan-belgrade-a4205026.html|title=Takuma Asano leaves Arsenal: Japan striker joins Partizan Belgrade|website=standard.co.uk|publisher=Evening Standard|date=4 August 2019|access-date=1 April 2021}}</ref> He signed a three-year contract and was given the number 11 shirt, while becoming the first Japanese player in club history.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.novosti.rs/vesti/sport.294.html:809982-ASANO-KUVAR-I-SARADNICI-Prvi-Japanac-u-istoriji-Partizana-stigao-sa-brojnom-ekipom | title=ASANO, KUVAR I SARADNICI: Prvi Japanac u istoriji Partizana stigao sa brojnom ekipom | language=Serbian | publisher=novosti.rs | date=3 August 2019 | access-date=3 August 2019}}</ref>

Asano scored on his debut in an eventual 3–1 home win over Turkish club [[Yeni Malatyaspor]] in the first leg of the [[2019–20 UEFA Europa League qualifying phase and play-off round#Third qualifying round|Europa League third qualifying round]]. He entered the field at the beginning of the second half instead of [[Filip Stevanović]] and scored his first goal for the club in the 67th minute.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2020/matches/round=2001148/match=2028050/ | title=Partizan (SRB) 3-1 Yeni Malatyaspor (TUR) | publisher=uefa.com | date=8 August 2019 | access-date=8 August 2019}}</ref> Partizan eliminated Yeni Malatyaspor and then the Norwegian [[Molde FK|Molde]], thus qualifying for the [[2019–20 UEFA Europa League group stage|Group L]] of the Europa League, with [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]], [[AZ Alkmaar]] and [[FC Astana|Astana]] as their opponents. Asano played in all European matches for Partizan this season and was also the scorer in the draw with AZ in [[Alkmaar]] (2–2) and in the victory over Astana (4–1) in [[Belgrade]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2019/11/28/europe/uefa-cup/stichting-az/fk-partizan-beograd/3161481/|title=AZ vs. Partizan|website=Soccerway.com|date=28 November 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2019/12/12/europe/uefa-cup/fk-partizan-beograd/fk-lokomotiv-astana/3161483/|title=Partizan vs. Astana|website=Soccerway.com|date=12 December 2019}}</ref> Partizan finished on second place in the [[2019–20 Serbian SuperLiga|Serbian SuperLiga]], behind [[Red Star Belgrade|Red Star]], and Asano scored four goals in 23 league games. He also appeared in four games in the [[2019–20 Serbian Cup|Serbian Cup]], scoring two goals, both in the quarter-final match against [[FK Radnik Surdulica|Radnik Surdulica]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2020/06/03/serbia/cup/fk-radnik-surdulica/fk-partizan-beograd/3270363/|title=Asano score twice in Cup quarter-final|website=Soccerway.com|date=3 June 2020}}</ref> However, Partizan did not manage to win the trophy in the Cup, as they were defeated by [[FK Vojvodina|Vojvodina]] in the final game after a penalty shoot-out.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2020/06/24/serbia/cup/fk-vojvodina-novi-sad/fk-partizan-beograd/3272515/|title=Vojvodina vs. Partizan 2–2 (PEN 4–2)|website=Soccerway.com|date=24 June 2020}}</ref>

In the 2020/21 season Partizan did not qualify for the group stage of the [[2020–21 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]]. After eliminating the Latvian [[FK RFS|RFS]] and the Romanian [[FC Sfîntul Gheorghe|Sfîntul Gheorghe]], the club was defeated by the Belgian [[R. Charleroi S.C.|Charleroi]] in the third round. Asano appeared in all three European matches (only played one match each due to the pandemic), did not score a goal but recorded an assist to [[Seydouba Soumah]] for the only goal in the 2–1 away defeat against Charleroi after extra time.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2020/09/24/europe/uefa-cup/royal-charleroi-sporting-club/fk-partizan-beograd/3360067/|title=Charleroi vs. Partizan 2–1|website=Soccerway.com|date=24 September 2020}}</ref> Having scored 18 goals in 33 league matches for Partizan in the ongoing championship, Asano announced on 2 May 2021 that he had rescinded his contract with the club.<ref>[https://www.nippon.com/en/news/kd761491963126333440/ Soccer: Asano terminates Partizan contract over unpaid salary] at Nippon.com</ref>

==International career==

===Youth===
At youth level, Asano won a gold medal at the [[2016 AFC U-23 Championship]], scoring a brace against [[South Korea national under-23 football team|South Korea]] to give his side a 3–2 win in the final. He also represented [[Japan national under-23 football team|Japan]] at the [[2016 Summer Olympics]], netting two goals in [[Football at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|the tournament]], as the team exited in the group stage.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://dailycannon.com/2016/08/asanos-japan-knocked-out-of-rio-2016/ | title=Asano's Japan knocked out of Rio 2016 | publisher=dailycannon.com | date=11 August 2016 | access-date=18 October 2019}}</ref>

===Senior===
On 7 May 2015, Asano was invited by [[Japan national football team|Japan]] manager [[Vahid Halilhodžić]] for a two-day training camp.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.nikkansports.com/soccer/japan/news/1473077.html | title=大久保嘉人ら28人日本代表候補合宿メンバー／一覧 | language=Japanese | publisher=nikkansports.com | date=7 May 2015 | access-date=7 May 2015}}</ref> He was subsequently called up to the team for the upcoming [[2015 EAFF East Asian Cup]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.jfa.jp/national_team/news/00006836/ | title=EAFF東アジアカップ2015（8/2～9＠中国／武漢） SAMURAI BLUE（日本代表）メンバー・スケジュール | language=Japanese | publisher=jfa.jp | date=23 July 2015 | access-date=25 July 2015}}</ref> Asano scored his first goal for ''Samurai Blue'' in a 7–2 win over [[Bulgaria national football team|Bulgaria]] at the 2016 [[Kirin Cup]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://sports.yahoo.com/news/japan-7-bulgaria-2-kagawa-130000577.html | title=Japan 7 Bulgaria 2: Kagawa at the double in dominant win | publisher=yahoo.com | date=3 June 2016 | access-date=18 October 2019}}</ref>

In March 2018, manager [[Vahid Halilhodžić]] left out Asano and [[Yosuke Ideguchi]] for [[Exhibition game#Association football|friendly]] matches with [[Mali national football team|Mali]] and [[Ukraine national football team|Ukraine]] in preparations for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018 World Cup]] due to lack of playing time at club level, commenting, "It makes me sad that Asano and Ideguchi are not playing. They were the heroes of our qualifying game against [[Australia national soccer team|Australia]]. They haven't been chosen this time, and if things continue the way they are, there is a chance that they won't be chosen for the World Cup".<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.japantimes.co.jp/sports/2018/03/15/soccer/halilhodzic-reveals-roster-upcoming-friendlies/ | title=Halilhodzic reveals roster for upcoming friendlies | publisher=japantimes.co.jp | date=15 March 2018 | access-date=15 March 2018}}</ref> In May, Asano was named in Japan's preliminary squad for the World Cup, but failed to make the final cut.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://dailycannon.com/2018/05/asano-cut-from-japan-world-cup-squad-despite-making-preliminary-list-of-27/ | title=Asano cut from Japan World Cup squad despite making preliminary list of 27 | publisher=dailycannon.com | date=31 May 2018 | access-date=18 October 2019}}</ref>

In his first match in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]], Asano scored a goal in the 83rd minute to give Japan an upset victory over Germany.<ref>{{Cite news |agency=Associated Press |date=2022-11-23 |title=Japan gets 2 late goals to beat Germany 2-1 at the World Cup |language=en |work=NPR |url=https://www.npr.org/2022/11/23/1138963632/japan-germany-world-cup-upset-qatar-doan-asano |access-date=2022-11-23}}</ref>
