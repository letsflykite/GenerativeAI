'''Héctor Miguel Herrera López''' ({{IPA-es|ˈeɣtoɾ eˈreɾa}}; born 19 April 1990) is a Mexican professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[Major League Soccer]] club [[Houston Dynamo FC|Houston Dynamo]] and the [[Mexico national football team|Mexico national team]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Official: Houston Dynamo sign Mexico, Atletico Madrid midfielder Hector Herrera for summer |url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/sources-houston-dynamo-fc-make-offer-for-mexico-atletico-madrid-midfielder-hecto |publisher=MLS|access-date=2 March 2022}}</ref>

Herrera began his career with [[C.F. Pachuca|Pachuca]] in 2011 and spent three years at the club before joining [[FC Porto]]. He struggled for playing time in his first season in Portugal before gradually cementing his place in the starting eleven. Since the 2015–16 season, Herrera served as club [[Captain (association football)|captain]]. In his six years with Porto, Herrera made 245 appearances across all competitions, winning a [[Primeira Liga]] title and two [[Supertaça Cândido de Oliveira|Supertaça]] cups.

A full international since 2012, Herrera won the Olympic gold medal in [[Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|that year's football tournament]], and was part of Mexico's winning team at the [[2015 CONCACAF Gold Cup]]. He has represented his country at the [[2014 FIFA World Cup|2014]] and [[2018 FIFA World Cup]], the [[2013 FIFA Confederations Cup|2013]] and [[2017 FIFA Confederations Cup]], and the [[Copa América Centenario]].

==Club career==
===Pachuca===
Herrera began playing football for [[C.F. Pachuca]]'s [[C.F. Pachuca Reserves and Academy|youth sides]]. As a youth, he was nicknamed ''Zorrillo'' (skunk), as well as ''Zorro'' (fox).<ref name="ESdH" />

Herrera made his professional debut for Pachuca on 21 July 2010 in a 0–1 defeat to [[Major Soccer League]] side [[Chivas USA]] in a [[2010 North American SuperLiga]] match.<ref>{{cite web |publisher=Soccerway |title=Chivas USA vs. Pachuca 1 – 0 |url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2010/07/22/nc-america/super-liga/club-deportivo-chivas-usa/club-pachuca/941093/ |date=22 July 2010}}</ref> Herrera made his [[Liga MX|league]] debut for Pachuca in a 1–4 home defeat to [[Santos Laguna]] on 23 July 2011.<ref>{{cite web |publisher=Diario del Toluca |title=Mauro Cejas, orgulloso por el Preolímpico de Héctor Herrera |trans-title=Mauro Cejas, proud of the Olympic qualifying tournament of Héctor Herrera |url=http://diarioportal.com/2012/04/03/mauro-cejas-orgulloso-por-el-preolimpico-de-hector-herrera/ |language=es |date=3 April 2012 |access-date=1 June 2012 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120708115150/http://diarioportal.com/2012/04/03/mauro-cejas-orgulloso-por-el-preolimpico-de-hector-herrera/ |archive-date=8 July 2012 |url-status=dead}}</ref> Appearing 14 times for Pachuca in the [[2011–12 Mexican Primera División season#Torneo Apertura|2011 Apertura]], he was a shortlist candidate for best rookie of the tournament.<ref>{{cite web |work=Récord |author=Lindoro, Alex |title=Héctor Herrera, ilusionado con ganar el Balón de Oro |trans-title=Héctor Herrera excited about winning the Golden Bal |url=http://www.record.com.mx/futbol-nacional/2012-01-15/hector-herrera-ilusionado-con-ganar-el-balon-de-oro |language=es |date=15 January 2012}}</ref>

===Porto===
On 28 June 2013, it was announced that Herrera was transferred to Portuguese club [[FC Porto|Porto]] for €8 million.<ref>{{cite web |title=Herrera firmó con el Porto por 8 mde |url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/internacional/noticias/2013/06/28/herrera-firmo-con-el-porto-por-8-mde |website=Medio Tiempo |language=es |access-date=28 June 2013 |date=28 June 2013}}</ref>

Herrera appeared as an unused substitute in his [[2013–14 FC Porto season|first season's]] opening [[Supertaça Cândido de Oliveira|Super Cup]] match against [[Vitória S.C.|Vitória de Guimarães]] on 10 August, ultimately winning 3–0. Eight days later, Herrera made his [[Primeira Liga]] debut, coming on as a substitute for [[Lucho González]] in the 82nd minute in Porto's 3–1 victory over [[Vitória F.C.|Vitória de Setúbal]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Quintero brilló en el triunfo del Porto; Héctor Herrera debutó |url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/internacional/cronicas/2013/08/18/quintero-brillo-en-el-triunfo-del-porto-hector-herrera-debuto_48019 |website=Medio Tiempo |language=es |access-date=18 August 2013 |date=18 August 2013}}</ref> On 6 October, Herrera played his first 90 minutes in Porto's 3–1 away win over [[F.C. Arouca|Arouca]]. On 18 September, Herrera made his [[UEFA Champions League]] debut against [[FK Austria Wien|Austria Wien]] in a 1–0 [[2013–14 UEFA Champions League group stage|group stage]] victory.<ref>{{cite web |title=Debuta en Champions |url=https://www.noroeste.com.mx/publicaciones/view/debuta-en-champions-828617}}</ref> On 22 October, Herrera set a new UEFA Champions League record for the fastest dismissal for two yellow cards when he was sent off in the sixth minute of the group stage match against [[FC Zenit Saint Petersburg|Zenit Saint Petersburg]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://in.reuters.com/article/soccer-champions-porto-zenith-idINDEE99L0DG20131022 |title=Hulk breaks Porto hearts as Zenit win on his return |publisher=Reuters |date=23 October 2013 |access-date=13 May 2014}}</ref> Herrera scored his first league goal with Porto on 20 December in a 4–0 win against [[S.C. ==International career==
===Youth===
In 2012, Herrera was chosen by coach [[Luis Fernando Tena]] to participate in the [[2012 CONCACAF Men's Olympic Qualifying Tournament|2012 CONCACAF Olympic Qualifying Tournament]] held in the [[United States]]. Mexico went on to win the tournament by defeating [[Honduras national under-23 football team|Honduras]] in the final, thus qualifying to the [[2012 Summer Olympics|2012 Olympic Games]] held in [[London]].

Herrera was a vital part in the Mexico squad that won the [[2012 Toulon Tournament]], and was awarded the ''Meilleur Joueur'' (Best Player), the most outstanding player of the tournament.<ref>{{cite web |publisher=Informa Uniradio |title=Héctor Miguel Herrera de Rosarito BC, es elegido mejor jugador en torneo de Toulon |trans-title=Héctor Miguel Herrera of Rosarito BC, is named best player of the Toulon tournament |url=http://uniradioinforma.com/noticias/deportes/articulo121273.html |language=es |date=1 June 2012}}</ref>

Herrera made the final cut for those participating in the 2012 Summer Olympics. Mexico won the gold medal after defeating [[Brazil national under-23 football team|Brazil]] 2–1 in the final.<ref name="ESdH">{{cite web |publisher=El Sol de Hidalgo |title=Miguel Herrera, un Zorro cabal |trans-title=Miguel Herrera, a full Fox |author=Cortés, Constancio |url=http://www.oem.com.mx/elsoldehidalgo/notas/n2498820.htm |language=es |date=10 April 2012 |access-date=29 May 2012 |archive-date=24 September 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924120728/http://www.oem.com.mx/elsoldehidalgo/notas/n2498820.htm |url-status=dead }}</ref>

===Senior===
[[File:Ger-Mex (16).jpg|thumb|250px|upright|Herrera (right) playing against [[Germany national football team|Germany]] at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]]]]
In 2012, Herrera was called up by coach [[José Manuel de la Torre]] to play for [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]] in the [[2014 FIFA World Cup qualification – CONCACAF third round|World Cup qualifiers]] against [[Guyana national football team|Guyana]] on 12 October and [[El Salvador national football team|El Salvador]] on 16 October. He made his senior national team debut against El Salvador, a 2–0 win for Mexico.<ref>{{cite web |title=Héctor Herrera, el 'zorro' que nació para conquistar Europa |url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/mexicanos-mundo/2017/04/19/58f6dbc1ca4741771d8b45f7.html |work=Marca |first=Tania |last=Suárez |date=19 April 2017 |access-date=7 November 2018}}</ref>

On 7 June 2013, Herrera was called up to participate in the [[2013 FIFA Confederations Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Oficializa México lista y numeración para Confederaciones |url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/seleccion-mexicana/noticias/2013/06/07/oficializa-mexico-lista-y-numeracion-para-confederaciones |work=Medio Tiempo |first=Juan Martín |last=Montes |date=7 June 2013 |access-date=8 November 2018}}</ref> He made only one appearance, in a 0–2 loss to host-nation [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]], coming on as a substitute for [[Gerardo Flores Zúñiga|Gerardo Flores]] in the 58th minute of the match.

On 8 May 2014, Herrera was included in the final 23-man roster participating in the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]] by [[Miguel Herrera]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Mexico squad for 2014 World Cup: the 23 chosen by Miguel Herrera |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/jun/05/mexico-squad-2014-world-cup |work=The Guardian |publisher=Guardian News and Media |access-date=5 June 2014}}</ref> He was a starter in all three of Mexico's [[2014 FIFA World Cup#Group A|group stage]] matches, as well as playing in the 1–2 [[2014 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Netherlands vs Mexico|round-of-16 loss]] to the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/27961190 |title=Netherlands 2–1 Mexico |date=29 June 2014 |access-date=30 June 2014 |publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> On 4 September 2015, Herrera netted his first goal with Mexico in a friendly against [[Trinidad and Tobago national football team|Trinidad and Tobago]], scoring off of a corner kick pass from outside of the box, tying the match 3–3.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ricardo Ferretti's debut sees Mexico draw with Trinidad and Tobago |url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/report?gameId=434556 |work=ESPN |date=4 September 2015 |access-date=7 November 2018}}</ref> Four days later, he scored his second goal in a 2–2 draw against [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Argentina salvages draw with Mexico thanks to late Messi, Aguero magic |url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/report?gameId=420728 |work=ESPN |date=8 September 2015 |access-date=7 November 2018}}</ref>

Herrera was included in the roster for the [[2015 CONCACAF Gold Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Chicharito, Vela headline Mexico's 23-man Gold Cup squad |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/mexico/story/2489345/mexico-announce-23-man-gold-cup-squad |work=ESPN |first=Tom |last=Marshall |date=12 June 2015}}</ref> Despite a dip in form following a superb season with Porto,<ref>{{cite web |title=Héctor Herrera, el gran desaparecido de Copa Oro |url=https://www.goal.com/es-mx/news/4773/m%C3%A9xico/2015/07/22/13784902/h%C3%A9ctor-herrera-el-gran-desaparecido-de-copa-oro |work=Goal.com |language=Spanish |date=22 July 2015}}</ref> he appeared in all games except the [[2015 CONCACAF Gold Cup Final|final]] against [[Jamaica national football team|Jamaica]] as Mexico won 3–1.<ref name="Gold Cup">{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2015/jul/26/mexico-rolls-to-seventh-gold-cup-title-with-uncontroversial-win-over-jamaica |title=Gold Cup 2015 final: Mexico roll to seventh title with easy win over Jamaica |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |date=27 July 2015|access-date=27 July 2015}}</ref> He was called up by interim manager [[Ricardo Ferretti]] to participate in the subsequent [[CONCACAF Cup]] – a play-off match to determine CONCACAF's entry into the [[2017 FIFA Confederations Cup]] – against the United States. Mexico won the match 3–2 during overtime.<ref name="CONCACAF Cup">{{cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/confederationscup/news/y=2015/m=10/news=mexico-claim-concacaf-s-spot-at-confederations-cup-2710781.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151012142539/http://www.fifa.com/confederationscup/news/y=2015/m=10/news=mexico-claim-concacaf-s-spot-at-confederations-cup-2710781.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=12 October 2015 |title=Mexico claim CONCACAF's spot at Confederations Cup |publisher=FIFA |date=11 October 2015|access-date=28 November 2016}}</ref>

On 17 May 2016, Herrera was listed on the roster for the [[Copa América Centenario]] by [[Juan Carlos Osorio]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Mexico announces team for Copa America Centenario and it looks scary good |url=https://www.foxsports.com/soccer/story/mexico-announces-team-for-copa-america-centenario-and-it-looks-scary-good-051716 |work=Fox Sports |date=17 May 2016 |access-date=27 June 2018}}</ref> On 5 June, in Mexico's [[Copa América Centenario Group C#Mexico vs Uruguay|first group stage match]] against [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay]], Herrera scored the third goal in the 3–1 victory.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2016/06/05/mexico-3-uruguay-1-copa-america-centenario-match-recap |title=Mexico 3, Uruguay 1 Copa America Centenario match recap |publisher=Major League Soccer |date=5 June 2016 |access-date=10 June 2016}}</ref>

On 8 June 2017, he was listed on the roster for the [[2017 FIFA Confederations Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Rafa Marquez leads Mexico's Confederations Cup squad |url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/mexico/story/3140525/rafa-marquez-leads-mexicos-confederations-cup-squad |work=ESPN |first=Tom |last=Marshall |date=8 June 2017 |access-date=27 June 2018}}</ref> He would go on to appear in all five matches as Mexico lost the [[2017 FIFA Confederations Cup knockout stage#Third place play-off|third place play-off]] against [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]] with a score of 2–1.<ref>{{cite web |title=Hector HERRERA |url=https://www.fifa.com/confederationscup/players/player=329092/statistics.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181108224721/https://www.fifa.com/confederationscup/players/player=329092/statistics.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=8 November 2018 |work=FIFA |access-date=8 November 2018}}</ref> With three assists, Herrera finished as the assist leader of the tournament.<ref name="fifa">{{cite web |title=Hector Herrera – FIFA Player Index |url=https://www.fifa.com/confederationscup/players/player=329092/index.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130619042449/http://www.fifa.com/confederationscup/players/player=329092/index.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=19 June 2013 |publisher=FIFA}}</ref>

On 28 May 2018, Herrera captained the national team in a scoreless draw to [[Wales national football team|Wales]] in preparation for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Mexico captain Hector Herrera leads by example with 8/10 showing vs. Wales |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/club/mexico/203/blog/post/3511978/mexico-captain-hector-herrera-leads-by-example-with-8-10-showing-vs-wales |work=ESPN |first=Cesar |last=Hernandez |date=28 May 2018}}</ref>
On 4 June, Herrera was included in the final 23-man squad for the tournament.<ref>{{cite web |title=Convocatoria de la Selección Nacional de México |url=https://miseleccion.mx/convocatoria-la-seleccion-nacional-mexico-13/ |website=MiSeleccion.mx |language=es |access-date=4 June 2018}}</ref> In the first [[2018 FIFA World Cup#Group F|group stage]] match against Germany, which Mexico won 1–0, Herrera's performance was praised in particular.<ref name="espn">{{cite web |title=Hector Herrera quietly drives Mexico while Hirving Lozano grabs the headlines |url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/club/mexico/203/blog/post/3531262/hector-herrera-quietly-drives-mexico-while-hirving-lozano-grabs-the-headlines |work=ESPN |first=Cesar |last=Hernandez |date=18 June 2018 |access-date=18 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=4 reasons why Mexico beat Germany |url=https://www.sbnation.com/soccer/2018/6/18/17472808/world-cup-2018-mexico-germany-tactics-osorio-herrera |work=SB Nation |first=Conor |last=Dowley |date=18 June 2018 |access-date=18 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Germany 0-1 Mexico player ratings as Mesut Ozil and Joel Kimmich flop, but Hector Herrera and Hirving Lozano star in shock win |url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/football/match-reports/germany-0-1-mexico-player-12728356 |work=Mirror |first=Alex |last=Smith |date=17 June 2018 |access-date=18 October 2018}}</ref> He would appear as a starter in all three group stage matches and the [[2018 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Brazil vs Mexico|round-of-16 match]] against Brazil, where Mexico lost 2–0.<ref>{{cite web |title=Hector HERRERA |url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/players/player/329092/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180623113231/https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/players/player/329092/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=23 June 2018 |work=FIFA |access-date=7 November 2018}}</ref>

In May 2019, Herrera issued a statement via Twitter confirming that he would not form part of the national squad participating at the [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup|CONCACAF Gold Cup]], citing fitness concerns as well as wanting to decide his "professional future" as his contract with Porto was expiring.<ref>{{cite web |title=Héctor Herrera confirma su baja de la Copa Oro |url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/copa-oro/2019/05/13/5cd8aedc22601dc5718b4632.html |website=Marca |date=12 May 2019 |language=es |access-date=13 May 2019}}</ref> Under [[Gerardo Martino]], he reappeared with Mexico in the friendly match against [[United States men's national soccer team|the United States]] on 6 September, and played all 90 minutes in ''El Tri'''s 3–0 victory.<ref>{{cite web |title=Mexico ratings vs. USMNT: 'Chicharito', 'Tecatito', Herrera stellar in win |url=https://www.espn.co.uk/football/mexico/story/3936639/mexico-ratings-vs-usmnt-chicharitotecatitoherrera-stellar-in-win |website=ESPN |date=7 September 2019 |access-date=7 September 2019}}</ref> The following month, Herrera captained Mexico in the [[2019–20 CONCACAF Nations League A|CONCACAF Nations League]] fixtures against [[Bermuda national football team|Bermuda]] and [[Panama national football team|Panama]], scoring off of a free kick layoff in a 5–1 victory against the former.<ref>{{cite web |title=Con golazo ante Bermudas, Herrera cortó racha de dos años sin anotar con el Tri |url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/seleccion-mayor/hector-herrera-corto-racha-2-anos-anotar-seleccion-mexicana |publisher=Mediotiempo.com |date=12 October 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Roberto Alvarado, Rodolfo Pizarro shine in Mexico's easy win over Panama |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/mexico-mex/story/3966299/roberto-alvaradorodolfo-pizarro-shine-in-mexicos-easy-win-over-panama |publisher=ESPN |first=Cesar |last=Hernandez |date=16 October 2019}}</ref>

In June 2021, Herrera participated in the [[2021 CONCACAF Nations League Finals|Concacaf Nations League Finals]],<ref>{{cite web |title=¿Quiénes son los convocados de México para la Concacaf Liga de Naciones 2021? |url=https://www.goal.com/es/noticias/convocados-seleccion-mexicana-liga-de-naciones-concacaf/6gvqzbyti0td1hns6t3v3juou}}</ref> reaching the [[2021 CONCACAF Nations League Final|final]] against the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]], losing 2–3.<ref>{{cite web |title=México pierde la final de la Nations League ante Estados Unidos en un partido de locura |date=7 June 2021 |url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/seleccion-mx/2021/06/07/60bd9fa5e2704e22b88b457f.html}}</ref> He was included in the Best XI of the tournament.<ref>{{cite web |title=Pulisic, Lainez head up CNLF Best XI |date=8 June 2021 |url=https://www.concacaf.com/nations-league/news/pulisic-lainez-head-up-cnlf-best-xi/}}</ref> He participated in the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup|subsequent CONCACAF Gold Cup]],<ref>{{cite web |title=México va a la Copa Oro sin Chicharito Hernández y con Funes Mori |date=July 2021 |url=https://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/selecciones/nota/_/id/8853729/mexico-convocatoria-copa-oro-funes-mori-incluido-chicharito-ausente}}</ref> appearing as captain in the first two group stage matches due to [[Héctor Moreno]] being unfit.<ref>{{cite web |title=Alan Pulido y Héctor Moreno estarían descartados para el debut en Copa Oro |url=https://www.90min.com/es/posts/alan-pulido-y-hector-moreno-estarian-descartados-para-el-debut-en-copa-oro |work=90 Min |date=10 July 2021 |language=Spanish}}</ref> Losing the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup Final|final]] to the United States 0–1,<ref>{{cite web |title=¡Dolor de cabeza del Tata! México pierde Final de Copa Oro ante equipo B de EE.UU. |url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/copa-oro/unidos-vs-mexico-1-0-ee-uu-campeon-copa-oro}}</ref> he was awarded the Golden Ball for best player of the tournament.<ref name="CONCACAF">{{cite web |url=https://www.concacaf.com/gold-cup/article/mexico-mf-hector-herrera-wins-best-player-award/ |title=Mexico MF Hector Herrera wins Best Player Award |publisher=CONCACAF |date=2 August 2021|access-date=2 August 2021}}</ref>

On 5 June 2022, he played his 100th match for Mexico in a friendly game against [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/seleccion-mx/2022/06/06/629d438b268e3e012f8b45d0.html |title=Héctor Herrera cumple 100 partidos con la selección Mexicana |trans-title=Héctor Herrera celebrates 100 games with the Mexican national team |website=Marca |language=es |date=5 June 2022}}</ref>

In October 2022, Herrera was named in Mexico's preliminary 31-man [[Mexico national football team|squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], and in November, he was ultimately included in the final 26-man roster.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lista de convocados de la Selección Mexicana al Mundial de Qatar 2022|url=https://www.tvazteca.com/aztecadeportes/lista-oficial-convocados-seleccion-mexicana-mundial-qatar-2022|work=Tvazteca.com|first=Omar|last=Moro|date=14 November 2022|language=Spanish}}</ref>

==Style of play==
{{Quote box|width=33%|align=upright|quote="Physically [Herrera] is very strong. He seems sluggish, but he is very powerful. Porto have a good eye for signing players of great quality, and Herrera is one of them."|source=—Former [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] manager [[Pep Guardiola]] prior to their Champions League match against Porto in 2015.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.informador.mx/Deportes/Hector-Herrera-agradece-elogios-de-Pep-Guardiola-20150414-0164.html |title=Héctor Herrera agradece elogios de Pep Guardiola |trans-title=Héctor Herrera grateful of praise from Pep Guardiola |language=es |work=Informador.mx |publisher=El Informador |date=14 April 2015 |access-date=14 April 2015}}</ref>}}

Herrera is described as a [[Midfielder#Box-to-box midfielder|box-to-box midfielder]],<ref>{{cite web |title=Eric Gomez: Meet Hector Herrera, Mexico's $16 million midfielder |url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/114/mexico/2012/08/24/3328879/eric-gomez-meet-hector-herrera-mexicos-16-million-midfielder |work=Goal.com |first=Eric |last=Gomez |date=24 August 2012 |access-date=8 November 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Go For It Klopp – Why This 26-Year-Old Porto Midfielder Will Be A Perfect Option For Liverpool? |url=https://soccersouls.com/2016/06/10/why-this-26-year-old-porto-midfielder-will-be-a-perfect-option-for-liverpool/ |first=Harsh |last=Vaidya |work=Soccersouls.com |date=10 June 2016 |access-date=8 November 2018 |archive-date=8 November 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181108224603/https://soccersouls.com/2016/06/10/why-this-26-year-old-porto-midfielder-will-be-a-perfect-option-for-liverpool/ |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name="espn" /> capable of halting opposing advances and able to distribute the ball or shoot the ball on target.<ref>{{cite web |title=In-form Hector Herrera to play a massive role for Mexico in coming months |url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/club/mexico/203/blog/post/3262823/in-form-hector-herrera-needs-to-play-a-decisive-role-for-mexico-in-the-coming-months |work=ESPN |first=Nayib |last=Moran |date=7 November 2017 |access-date=8 November 2018}}</ref> He is also known for his pace and dynamism,<ref>{{cite web |title=AC Milan Given Chance to Sign Porto's Mexico International Midfielder Hector Herrera |url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2018/02/09/ac-milan-given-chance-sign-portos-mexico-international-midfielder-hector-herrera |publisher=Sports Illustrated |access-date=9 February 2018}}</ref> as well as being capable of breaking up opposition attacks with his tough tackling and starting his team's own forays forward with his sharp distribution and tireless running.<ref>{{cite web |title=11 other players Liverpool could realistically sign this summer |url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/11-other-players-liverpool-could-realistically-sign-summer |publisher=Future Publishing |work=FourFourTwo |access-date=7 July 2016 |date=7 July 2016}}</ref>

Herrera has cited [[Juan Román Riquelme]] as an idol and an influence on his playing style.<ref>{{cite web |title=Héctor Herrera: "Desde niño me he fijado en Riquelme" |url=http://www.marca.com/2014/11/10/futbol/futbol_internacional/mexico/1415641977.html |work=Marca |first1=Diego |last1=Acedo |first2=Joseba |last2=Arroyo |date=10 November 2014 |access-date=5 October 2018}}</ref>
