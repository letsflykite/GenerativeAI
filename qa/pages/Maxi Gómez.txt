'''Maximiliano''' "'''Maxi'''" '''Gómez González''' ({{IPA-es|ˈmaksi ˈɣomes}}; born 14 August 1996) is a Uruguayan professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)#Striker|striker]] for [[Süper Lig]] club [[Trabzonspor]] and the [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay national team]].

Having begun his career with [[Defensor Sporting]], he moved to Spain in 2017, where he made 167 [[La Liga]] appearances and scored 51 goals for [[RC Celta de Vigo|Celta]] and [[Valencia CF|Valencia]].

A full international for [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay]] since 2017, Gómez was selected for the [[FIFA World Cup]] in [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022]] and the [[Copa América]] in [[2019 Copa América|2019]] and [[2021 Copa América|2021]].

==Club career==
===Defensor Sporting===
Born in [[Paysandú]], Gómez joined [[Defensor Sporting]]'s youth setup in 2013, from CA Litoral.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ovaciondigital.com.uy/futbol/inconsciente-anos-maxi-gomez-hizo.html|title=Un "inconsciente": con 19 años, "Maxi" Gómez hizo historia violeta|trans-title=An "unconscious": with 19 years, "Maxi" Gómez made history ''violeta''|publisher=Ovación|language=es|date=3 October 2015|access-date=17 June 2017}}</ref> He made his first team debut on 15 September 2015, coming on as a late [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] for [[Héctor Acuña]] in a 1–0 away win against [[Club Universitario de Deportes|Universitario]] for [[2015 Copa Sudamericana|the year]]'s [[Copa Sudamericana]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tenfield.com.uy/universitario-defensor-martes-21-30/|title=Defensor vuela en la Copa, 1:0 en Perú|trans-title=Defensor fly in the cup, 1–0 in Peru|publisher=[[Tenfield]]|language=es|date=16 September 2015|access-date=17 June 2017}}</ref>

Gómez made his [[Uruguayan Primera División|Primera División]] debut on 4 October 2015, but in a 0–4 away loss against [[Club Nacional de Football|Nacional]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tenfield.com.uy/nacional-y-defensor-emocion-en-el-parque/|title=Nacional de gala, goleó 4:0|trans-title=Gala Nacional, thrashed 4–0|publisher=Tenfield|language=es|date=4 October 2015|access-date=17 June 2017}}</ref> His first senior goal came on 16 October, the last in a 2–3 home defeat against [[Montevideo Wanderers F.C.|Montevideo Wanderers]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.teledoce.com/telemundo/deportes/futbol-uruguayo/defensor-y-wanderers-empatan-0-a-0-en-el-franzini/|title=Wanderers derrotó 3 a 2 a Defensor en el Franzini|trans-title=Wanderers defeated Danubio by 3–2 at the Franzini|publisher=[[Teledoce]]|language=es|date=16 October 2015|access-date=17 June 2017}}</ref>

On 31 October 2015, Gómez scored a brace in a 4–1 home routing of [[Juventud de Las Piedras]]; he repeated the feat on 20 November in a 2–2 home draw against [[El Tanque Sisley]], and also in a 3–2 home success over [[Danubio F.C.|Danubio]] the following 28 February. On 22 May 2016, he scored four goals in a 6–5 away win against El Tanque Sisley, and finished [[2015–16 Uruguayan Primera División season|his first season]] with 14 goals in only 21 matches.<ref>{{cite news |title=Maxi Gómez, el jugador de la etapa|trans-title=Maxi Gómez, the player of the season |url=https://www.tenfield.com.uy/maximiliano-gomez-19-anos-el-jugador-de-la-etapa-de-paysandu-a-defensor-sporting-y-sus-cuatro-goles-del-domingo/ |access-date=21 June 2020 |publisher=Tenfield |date=23 May 2016 |language=es}}</ref>

After only four goals in 14 matches in the [[2016 Uruguayan Primera División season|2016 campaign]], Gómez contributed with ten goals in 12 matches in [[2017 Uruguayan Primera División season|2017]]; highlights included a brace in a 2–0 away win against Danubio on 5 March 2017.<ref>{{cite news |title=Defensor Sporting clásico ganador, 2:0 |trans-title=Defensor Sporting winners of the ''Clásico'', 2–0|url=https://www.tenfield.com.uy/danubio-00-defensor-sp/ |access-date=21 June 2020 |publisher=Tenfield |date=5 March 2017 |language=es}}</ref>

===Celta===
[[File:XXIII Memorial Quinocho (RC Celta vs Mainz 05) - 09.jpg|thumb|left|Gómez with [[Celta de Vigo|Celta]] in 2018]]
In May 2017, Gómez signed a five-year deal with [[La Liga]] side [[RC Celta de Vigo|Celta Vigo]], moving abroad for the first time in his career.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.celtavigo.net/es/primer-equipo/noticias/12559-maxi-gomez-imponente-goleador-de-presente-y-futuro-para-el-rc-celta|title=Maxi Gómez, imponente goleador de presente y futuro para el RC Celta|trans-title=Maxi Gómez, imposing goalscorer for the present and future for RC Celta|publisher=Celta Vigo|language=es|date=22 May 2017|access-date=17 June 2017|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170606030825/http://www.celtavigo.net/es/primer-equipo/noticias/12559-maxi-gomez-imponente-goleador-de-presente-y-futuro-para-el-rc-celta|archive-date=6 June 2017}}</ref> He made his debut for the club on 19 August, starting and scoring a brace in a 2–3 home loss against [[Real Sociedad]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marca.com/eventos/marcador/futbol/2017_18/la-liga/jornada_1/cel_rso/|title=La Real Sociedad remonta con penalti polémico|trans-title=Real Sociedad comeback with a controversial penalty|work=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|language=es|date=19 August 2017|access-date=20 August 2017}}</ref> and finished his [[2017–18 La Liga|debut season]] with 17 goals from 36 games, seventh highest in the division.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Joseph |first1=Naveen Ravi |title=LaLiga 2018/19: Top 5 U-21 players to watch out for |url=https://www.sportskeeda.com/football/laliga-top-5-u-21-players-to-watch-out-for-ss/3 |access-date=21 June 2020 |publisher=[[Sportskeeda]] |date=18 August 2018}}</ref>

Gómez contributed 13 goals in [[2018–19 La Liga|2018–19]] as Celta finished one place above relegation. This included one on the final day, as they defeated newly crowned champions [[FC Barcelona]] 2–0 at [[Balaídos]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Celta Vigo 2&ndash;0 Barcelona |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/48153900 |access-date=21 June 2020 |publisher=[[BBC Sport]] |date=4 May 2019}}</ref>

===Valencia===
On 14 July 2019, Gómez signed a contract with [[Valencia CF]] until 2024, with his release clause set at €140 million.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.valenciacf.com/es/article/es-comunicado-oficial-maxi-gomez-2019-07-14|title=Comunicado oficial|trans-title=Official statement|publisher=Valencia CF|date=14 July 2019|access-date=21 June 2020}}</ref> The deal saw Valencia pay €14.5 million with [[Santi Mina]] and [[Jorge Sáenz de Miera Colmeiro|Jorge Sáenz]] moving in the opposite direction, the latter joining Celta on a two-year loan deal.<ref name="BurkeJul19">{{cite web |last1=Burke |first1=Dan |title=Valencia complete Maxi Gómez signing |url=https://en.onefootball.com/🚨-valencia-complete-maxi-gomez-signing/ |website=Onefootball |access-date=16 July 2019 |date=14 July 2019}}</ref> He made his debut on 24 August away to his former club, playing the first hour of a 1–0 loss before being replaced by [[Rodrigo (footballer, born 1991)|Rodrigo]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=López de Vicente |first1=Sergio |title=Celta 1 - Valencia 0: resumen y goles de LaLiga Santander|trans-title=Celta 1–0 Valencia: LaLiga Santander summary and goals |url=https://as.com/futbol/2019/08/24/primera/1566669502_195217.html |access-date=21 June 2020 |work=[[Diario AS]] |date=24 August 2019 |language=es}}</ref>

Gómez scored his first goal for the ''Che'' on 14 September, coming off the bench to net at the end of a 5–2 loss to Barcelona at the [[Camp Nou]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Mann |first1=Mantej |title=Barcelona 5&ndash;2 Valencia |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49702295 |access-date=21 June 2020 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=14 September 2019}}</ref> Against the same opponents on 25 January 2020, he scored both goals in a 2–0 victory at [[Mestalla]], preserving his team's unbeaten home season and inflicting a first defeat of [[Quique Setién]]'s spell in charge of the opponents.<ref>{{cite news |title=Valencia inflict first defeat of Setien reign on Barcelona |url=https://www.shropshirestar.com/sport/uk-sports/2020/01/25/valencia-inflict-first-defeat-of-setien-reign-on-barcelona/ |access-date=21 June 2020 |work=[[Shropshire Star]] |date=25 January 2020}}</ref>

In 2021–22, Gómez was [[sent off]] twice against [[Athletic Bilbao]], once for two [[Penalty card|yellow cards]] in a league match and the latter on 10 February in a [[2021–22 Copa del Rey|Copa del Rey semi-final first leg]] away at the [[San Mamés Stadium (2013)|San Mamés Stadium]]. In the second instance, he was an unused substitute and was given a straight red card at half time for threatening the [[assistant referee]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Álvarez |first1=Fernando |title=Maxi Gómez, expulsado en el descanso en San Mamés|trans-title=Maxi Gómez, sent off at the break in San Mamés |url=https://www.marca.com/futbol/valencia/2022/02/10/62058a6a46163fc4498b45b7.html |access-date=27 October 2022 |work=Marca |date=10 February 2022 |language=Spanish}}</ref> He was issued with a two-match suspension and thereby missed the [[2022 Copa del Rey Final|final]], which his team lost on [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalties]] to [[Real Betis]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Sanchis |first1=Nicolo |last2=Valle |first2=Conrado |title=Maxi Gómez no jugará una hipotética final de Copa |trans-title=Maxi Gómez will not play in a hypothetical Copa final|url=https://as.com/futbol/2022/02/11/copa_del_rey/1644588987_219331.html |access-date=27 October 2022 |work=Diario AS |date=11 February 2022 |language=Spanish}}</ref>

===Trabzonspor===
In the summer of 2022, Valencia sought to sell Gómez due to [[UEFA Financial Fair Play Regulations]] and to free up a spot on their quota of non-European Union players. He was linked to a move to [[Fenerbahçe S.K. (football)|Fenerbahçe SK]], whose [[sporting director]] came to negotiate in Spain,<ref>{{cite news |last1=Torres |first1=David |title=Jorge Jesús habla de Maxi Gómez y el Fernerbahce: ni frío, ni calor|trans-title=Jorge Jesús speaks about Maxi Gómez and Fenerbahçe: not bothered either way|url=https://www.eldesmarque.com/valencia/valencia-cf/365111-jorge-jesus-habla-de-maxi-gomez-y-el-fernerbahce-ni-frio-ni-calor |access-date=27 October 2022 |publisher=El Desmarque |date=19 August 2022 |language=Spanish}}</ref> but signed a three-year deal with the option of a fourth at fellow Turkish [[Süper Lig]] team [[Trabzonspor]] on 31 August. The fee was €3 million and his annual salary was set at €1.75 million.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Torres |first1=David |title=Maxi Gómez se marcha al Trabzonspor: "Con Valencia en su corazón"|trans-title=Maxi Gómez leaves for Trabzonspor: "With Valencia in his heart"|url=https://www.eldesmarque.com/valencia/valencia-cf/367283-maxi-gomez-se-marcha-al-trabzonspor-con-valencia-en-su-corazon |access-date=27 October 2022 |publisher=El Desmarque |date=1 September 2022 |language=Spanish}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Gallego |first1=Daniel |title=El club de fútbol turco Trabzonspor ficha al uruguayo Maxi Gómez |trans-title=Turkish football club Trabzonspor signs Uruguayan Maxi Gómez|url=https://www.aa.com.tr/es/deportes/el-club-de-f%C3%BAtbol-turco-trabzonspor-ficha-al-uruguayo-maxi-g%C3%B3mez/2674836 |access-date=27 October 2022 |publisher=[[Anadolu Agency]] |date=1 September 2022 |language=Spanish}}</ref>

On 6 October 2022, Gómez was sent off after 11 minutes of a 3–1 loss at [[AS Monaco FC]] in the [[UEFA Europa League]] [[2022–23 UEFA Europa League group stage|group stage]], for a high boot into the face of [[Mohamed Camara (footballer, born January 2000)|Mohamed Camara]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Monaco-Trabzonspor: le très vilain geste de Maxi Gomez sur Camara, sanctionné par un carton rouge|trans-title=Monaco-Trabzonspor: Maxi Gómez's highly villainous act towards Camara, punished with a red card |url=https://rmcsport.bfmtv.com/football/europa-league/monaco-trabzonspor-le-tres-vilain-geste-de-maxi-gomez-sur-camara-sanctionne-par-un-carton-rouge_AV-202210060651.html |publisher=[[Radio Monte Carlo]]|access-date=27 October 2022 |date=6 October 2022 |language=French}}</ref>

==International career==
Gómez made his international debut for [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay]] on 10 November 2017 in a goalless friendly away to [[Poland national football team|Poland]], as a 74th-minute substitute for [[Giorgian De Arrascaeta]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Uruguay y Polonia empataron 0-0 en Varsovia|trans-title=Uruguay and Poland drew 0–0 in Warsaw |url=https://www.subrayado.com.uy/uruguay-y-polonia-empataron-0-0-varsovia-n72316 |access-date=21 June 2020 |publisher=Subrayado |date=10 November 2017 |language=es}}</ref> Manager [[Óscar Tabárez]] called him up for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia,<ref>{{cite news |title=World Cup 2018: Uruguay leave out Gaston Ramirez |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44344999 |access-date=21 June 2020 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=2 June 2018}}</ref> where he made his first competitive appearance in the final group game as an added-time replacement in a 3–0 win over [[Russia national football team|the hosts]] in [[Samara]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Jennings |first1=Patrick |title=Uruguay 3&ndash;0 Russia |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44344999 |access-date=21 June 2020 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=25 June 2018}}</ref>

On 26 March 2019, Gómez scored his first international goal to round off a 4–0 victory over [[Thailand national football team|Thailand]] and win the [[China Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Uruguay win 2019 China Cup |url=http://www.chinadaily.com.cn/a/201903/26/WS5c9982f9a3104842260b28b2.html |access-date=21 June 2020 |work=[[China Daily]] |date=26 March 2019}}</ref> He was called up for the [[Copa América]] in [[2019 Copa América|2019]] and [[2021 Copa América|2021]], both in Brazil.<ref>{{cite news |title=Suarez included in Uruguay's final Copa America squad despite injury concerns |url=https://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/suarez-included-in-uruguays-final-copa-america-squad-despite/wqc2zvdj62xe1rlago7wu9px3 |access-date=21 June 2020 |publisher=[[Goal (website)|Goal]] |date=31 May 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Escudero |first1=Simón |title=Uruguay anuncia su lista para la Copa América: Suárez y Valverde|trans-title=Uruguay announce their squad for the Copa América: Suárez and Valverde|url=https://as.com/futbol/2021/06/10/internacional/1623344131_722920.html |access-date=6 September 2021 |work=Diario AS |date=10 June 2021 |language=Spanish}}</ref>

Gómez scored once in [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONMEBOL)|qualification]], coming on with three minutes remaining against [[Chile national football team|Chile]] on 8 October 2020 and scoring the winner in a 2–1 victory.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Polo |first1=Manu |title=Maxi Gómez da la victoria a Uruguay en el descuento ante una correosa Chile|trans-title=Maxi Gómez gives Uruguay victory in added time against a tough Chile |url=https://www.marca.com/futbol/america/2020/10/09/5f7fa69e268e3e545e8b45be.html |access-date=13 December 2022 |work=Marca |date=9 October 2020 |language=Spanish}}</ref> He was called up for the final tournament in [[Qatar]],<ref>{{cite news |last1=Lea |first1=Greg |title=Uruguay World Cup 2022 squad: Diego Alonso {{as written|annou|nces [sic]}} final 26-man squad |url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/uruguay-world-cup-2022-squad-diego-alonso-names-his-latest-squad |access-date=2 December 2022<!--updated page, no original date visible--> |work=[[FourFourTwo]] |date=1 December 2022}}</ref> playing two games as a substitute for [[Darwin Núñez]] in a group stage elimination.<ref>{{cite news |last1=McNulty |first1=Phil |title=Portugal 2&ndash;0 Uruguay |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63698279 |access-date=13 December 2022 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=28 November 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Hafez |first1=Shamoon |title=Ghana 0&ndash;2 Uruguay |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63752147 |access-date=13 December 2022 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=2 December 2022}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Gómez's older brother Richard, also a footballer, played in Uruguay for [[Club Atlético Platense (Uruguay)|Club Atlético Platense]]. He played for [[Galicia (Spain)|Galician]] amateurs [[Mondariz FC]] while Maxi Gómez was at Celta.<ref>{{cite news |title=Richard Gómez, hermano de Maxi, debuta con el Mondariz ante el Pontellas|trans-title=Richard Gómez, Maxi's brother, debuts for Mondariz against Pontellas|url=https://www.lavozdegalicia.es/noticia/gradario/2018/11/01/richard-gomez-hermano-maxi-debuta-mondariz-ante-pontellas/00031541107157462523181.htm |access-date=27 October 2022 |work=[[La Voz de Galicia]] |date=2 November 2018 |language=Spanish}}</ref>
