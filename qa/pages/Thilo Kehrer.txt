'''Jan Thilo Kehrer''' ({{IPA-de|ˈtiːlo ˈkeːʁɐ}}; born 21 September 1996) is a German professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]] for [[Premier League]] club [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]] and the [[Germany national football team|Germany national team]].<ref name="PSG">{{cite web |author1=AFP |title=Schalke's Thilo Kehrer set to join Paris Saint-Germain |url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/11820/11470950/schalkes-thilo-kehrer-set-to-join-paris-saint-germain |website=Sky Sports |accessdate=12 August 2018 |language=en}}</ref> Mainly a [[centre-back]], he can also play in either [[Full-back (association football)|full-back]] position.

==Club career==
=== [[FC Schalke 04]] ===
Kehrer is a graduate of [[FC Schalke 04|Schalke 04]]. He made his Bundesliga debut on 6 February 2016 against [[VfL Wolfsburg]] in a 3–0 home win.<ref name="soccerway2">{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/02/06/germany/bundesliga/fc-schalke-04/vfl-wolfsburg/2054362/|title=Schalke 04 vs. Wolfsburg – 6 February 2015 – Soccerway|publisher=soccerway.com|accessdate=7 February 2016}}</ref> He scored his first goal for the club on 1 April 2017 in the [[Revierderby]] against [[Borussia Dortmund]] at home, producing the equalizer in a 1–1 draw.<ref name="skysports">{{cite web|url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/12040/10821773/bundesliga-round-up-thilo-kehrer-earns-schalke-point-in-revierderby|title=Thilo Kehrer earns Schalke point in Revierderby|publisher=Sky Sports|accessdate=1 April 2017}}</ref>

On 5 May 2018 he scored twice as Schalke secured second place in the Bundesliga for the 2017–18 season and [[UEFA Champions League]] football for the 2018–19 season.<ref>{{cite web |title=Schalke seal second spot and Champions League return with win at Augsburg {{!}} bundesliga.com |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/augsburg-schalke-line-ups-stats-philipp-max-meyer-naldo-tedesco-goretzka-479968.jsp |website=bundesliga.com |publisher=[[Bundesliga]] |language=en}}</ref>

=== [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.]] ===
On 16 August 2018, it was announced that Kehrer had been transferred to [[Ligue 1]] club [[Paris Saint-Germain]] for €37 million.<ref name="PSG" />

On 10 April 2019, Kehrer scored his first goal for Paris Saint-Germain, in a league match against [[RC Strasbourg Alsace|Strasbourg]] that ended 2–2.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Ligue 1 round-up: PSG miss chance to clinch title after draw with Strasbourg|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/psg-vs-strasbourg/report/389990 |access-date=14 July 2020|website=Sky Sports}}</ref> He scored his second goal for the club in a 2–1 league win away to [[FC Nantes|Nantes]] on 4 February 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=European round-up: Paris Saint-Germain win; Borussia Dortmund and RB Leipzig out of DFB-Pokal|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/nantes-vs-psg/report/408588 |access-date=14 July 2020|website=Sky Sports}}</ref> On 24 July 2020, in the [[2020 Coupe de France Final|Coupe de France Final]], Kehrer suffered an injury and was replaced by [[Colin Dagba]]. PSG went on to win the match 1–0.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=24 July 2020|title=Mbappe forced out of Coupe de France final after horror tackle as Champions League looms|url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/mbappe-forced-out-of-coupe-de-france-final-after-horror/17uzcq440u1cs1emy8os5qxb5f |access-date=24 July 2020|website=Goal.com}}</ref>

On 7 November 2020, Kehrer came off injured in a match against [[Stade Rennais F.C.|Rennes]]. He was set to miss three to four months of action due to an adductor injury.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=9 November 2020|title=Thilo Kehrer out for 3–4 months with adductor injury|url=https://www.getfootballnewsfrance.com/2020/thilo-kehrer-out-for-3-4-months-with-adductor-injury |access-date=|website=Get French Football News}}</ref> His recovery proved to be faster than expected, and he made his return to play as a substitute in a 3–1 win against [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] in the [[UEFA Champions League]] [[2020–21 UEFA Champions League|group stage]] on 2 December.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=2 December 2020|title=Manchester United vs. Paris Saint-Germain|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/lineups?gameId=585779 |access-date= 29 March 2023|website=ESPN}}</ref>

=== [[West Ham United F.C.]] ===
[[File:Thilo KehrerWHU.jpeg|thumb|left|upright|Kehrer during training with [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]]]]
On 17 August 2022, Kehrer signed for [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]] on a four-year contract with the option of an additional two years.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.whufc.com/news/west-ham-united-sign-germany-international-thilo-kehrer|title=West Ham United sign Germany international Thilo Kehrer &#124; West Ham United F.C.|website=www.whufc.com}}</ref> The transfer fee was reported to be £10.1m.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://uk-sport-web.prod.oceanusorigin.com/football/news/11095/12674448/thilo-kehrer-west-ham-agree-10-1m-deal-with-paris-saint-germain-for-defender|title=Thilo Kehrer: West Ham agree £10.1m deal with Paris Saint-Germain for defender|website=Sky Sports}}</ref> He made his West Ham debut on 18 August in the [[2022–23 UEFA Europa Conference League|Europa Conference League]] in a 3–1 home win against [[Viborg FF|Viborg]] coming on as a substitute for [[Angelo Ogbonna]].<ref name="BBC Sport Debut">{{cite web | title=Scamacca scores first goal as West Ham beat Viborg | website=BBC Sport | date=17 August 2022 | url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/62575909 | access-date=29 March 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
Kehrer is a [[Germany national youth football team|youth international for Germany]].<ref>{{cite web | url = http://datencenter.dfb.de/profil/298038/spiele_in_wettbewerb/2512/77299#c2512_t77299 | title = Thilo Kehrer | language = de | website = DFB | access-date = 2 July 2017}}</ref> Kehrer won the [[UEFA European Under-21 Championship|U-21 Euros]] in [[2017 UEFA European Under-21 Championship|2017]] and has [[captain (association football)|captain]]ed the [[Germany national under-21 football team|German U-21]]s against [[Kosovo national under-21 football team|Kosovo]], [[Azerbaijan national under-21 football team|Azerbaijan]] and [[Israel national football team|Israel]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Smith |first1=Andy |title=Introducing Thilo Kehrer, Schalke's latest homegrown star {{!}} bundesliga.com |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/thilo-kehrer-schalke-s-latest-homegrown-star-474584.jsp |website=bundesliga.com |publisher=[[Bundesliga]] |language=en}}</ref>

Kehrer was called up to the [[Germany national football team|full Germany squad]] for the first time on 29 August 2018, for Germany's opening [[2018–19 UEFA Nations League]] match against [[France national football team|France]] and the [[exhibition game|friendly]] against [[Peru national football team|Peru]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Joachim Löw unveils first post-World Cup squad|date=29 August 2018|first=Chuck|last=Penfold|work=dw.com|publisher=Deutsche Welle|url=https://www.dw.com/en/joachim-l%C3%B6w-unveils-first-post-world-cup-squad/a-45271908}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Kehrer was born in Germany to a German father and a Burundian mother.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.tagblatt.de/Nachrichten/Zwei-Tuebinger-Schueler-im-Nationaltrikot-155880.html | title = Zwei Tübinger Schüler im Nationaltrikot | language = de | website = [[Schwäbisches Tagblatt]] | date = 28 May 2012 | access-date = 2 July 2017 | first = Hansjörg | last = Lösel}}</ref>

He supports the peace and development organisation ''[http://www.anstoss-zur-hoffnung.de/?L=1 Anstoß zur Hoffnung e.V.] (engl.: Kickoff to hope)''. Through his support, socially disadvantaged people in his mother’s homeland find hope and help.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.anstoss-zur-hoffnung.de/?L=1|title=Profifußballer Thilo Kehrer gibt Anstoß zur Hoffnung für Kinder in Afrika|trans-title=Professional footballer Thilo Kehrer gives hope to children in Africa|website=anstoss-zur-hoffnung.de|publisher=SRS|language=de|date=20 February 2019|accessdate=30 March 2020}}</ref>
