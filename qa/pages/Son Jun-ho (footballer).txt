'''Son Jun-ho''' ({{lang-ko|손준호}}; born 12 May 1992) is a South Korean [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as [[Midfielder (association football)#Central midfielder|central midfielder]] for [[Shandong Taishan F.C.|Shandong Taishan]].

==Club career==
Son Jun-ho would play for [[Youngnam University]], before joining top tier club [[Pohang Steelers]] at the start of the [[2014 K League Classic]] season.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.sportalkorea.com/news/view.php?gisa_uniq=2014010718320113&section_code=10&key=&field=&cp=se&gomb=1 |title=포항, 고교 MVP 이광혁 등 신인 8인 계약 |publisher=Sportal Korea |date=7 January 2014|access-date=31 January 2021|language=ko}}</ref> He would make his professional debut on 26 March 2014 in a league game against [[Jeonbuk Hyundai Motors]] that ended in a 3-1 victory.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2014/03/26/korea-republic/k-league-classic/chonbuk/pohang-steelers/1647504/|title=JEONBUK MOTORS VS. POHANG STEELERS 1 - 3|publisher=soccerway.com|date=26 March 2014|access-date=31 January 2021}}</ref> Three days later he would go on to score his first goal in a league game on 29 March 2014 against [[Sangju Sangmu FC]] in a 4-2 victory.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2014/03/29/korea-republic/k-league-classic/pohang-steelers/gwangju-sangmu-fc/1647509/|title=POHANG STEELERS VS. GIMCHEON SANGMU 4 - 2|publisher=soccerway.com|date=29 March 2014|access-date=31 January 2021}}</ref>

At the beginning of the [[2018 K League Classic]] campaign, Son joined Jeonbuk Hyundai Motors on a four year contract.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.hyundai-motorsfc.com/board/board_view.asp?bgbn=2&seq=6816|title=전북, 도움왕 손준호 영입...4년 계약|publisher=hyundai-motorsfc.com|date=7 January 2018|access-date=31 January 2021|language=ko}}</ref> He would make his debut in a [[AFC Champions League]] game on 13 February 2018 against [[Kashiwa Reysol]] in a match that ended in a 3-2 victory.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2018/02/13/asia/afc-champions-league/chonbuk/kashiwa-reysol/2697979/|title=JEONBUK MOTORS VS. KASHIWA REYSOL 3 - 2|publisher=soccerway.com|date=13 February 2018|access-date=31 January 2021}}</ref> His league debut would come on 1 March 2018 against [[Ulsan Hyundai FC]] in a match that ended in a 2-0 victory.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2018/03/01/korea-republic/k-league-classic/chonbuk/ulsan-horang-i/2725202/|title=JEONBUK MOTORS VS. ULSAN 2 - 0|publisher=soccerway.com|date=1 March 2018|access-date=31 January 2021}}</ref> This was followed by his first goal for the club 8 April 2018 in a league game against his old club Pohang Steelers, in a game that ended in a 2-0 victory.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2018/04/08/korea-republic/k-league-classic/pohang-steelers/chonbuk/2725229/|title=POHANG STEELERS VS. JEONBUK MOTORS 0 - 2|publisher=soccerway.com|date=8 April 2018|access-date=31 January 2021}}</ref> After the game he would start to establish himself as a regular within the team and was part of the squad that went on to win the league title at the end of the season.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.rsssf.org/tabless/skor2018.html|title=South Korea 2018|date=6 June 2019|website=[[RSSSF]]|access-date=31 January 2021}}</ref> This would be followed by another league title at the end of the [[2019 K League 1]] campaign.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.rsssf.org/tabless/skor2019.html|title=South Korea 2019|date=13 May 2020|website=[[RSSSF]]|access-date=31 January 2021}}</ref> In the [[2020 K League 1]] campaign the club would achieve a double in winning the league and [[2020 Korean FA Cup]], while Son would personally win the Most Valuable Player award.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.the-afc.com/news/afcsection/k-league-mvp-son-jun-ho-leaves-jeonbuk-for-shandong-luneng-taishan|title=K League MVP Son Jun-ho leaves Jeonbuk for Shandong Luneng Taishan|publisher=the-afc.com|date=12 January 2021|access-date=31 January 2021}}</ref>

On 13 January 2021, Son joined top tier Chinese football club [[Shandong Taishan F.C.|Shandong Taishan]] for the start of the [[2021 Chinese Super League]] campaign.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.china.org.cn/sports/2021-01/13/content_77109497.htm|title=K League MVP Son Jun-ho joins Shandong Luneng|publisher=china.org.cn|date=13 January 2021|access-date=31 January 2021}}</ref> He made his debut for the club on 20 April 2021 in a league game against [[Chongqing Liangjiang Athletic F.C.|Chongqing Liangjiang Athletic]] that ended in a 2-0 victory.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2021/04/20/china-pr/csl/chongqing-qiche/shandong-luneng/3492297/|title=CHONGQING LIANGJIANG VS. SHANDONG TAISHAN  0 - 2|publisher=Soccerway.com|date=2021-04-20|accessdate=2023-01-31|language=}}</ref> He would immediately establish himself as an integral member of the teams midfield that went on to the win the [[2021 Chinese Super League]] title and [[2021 Chinese FA Cup]].<ref name="CSL 2021">{{Cite web|url=https://k.sina.com.cn/article_7517400647_1c0126e47059025t1f.html|title=山东泰山队获得2021赛季中超联赛冠军|publisher=k.sina.com.cn|date=2021-12-27|access-date=2023-01-31}}</ref> This would be followed up by him winning the [[2022 Chinese FA Cup]] with them the next season.<ref name="CFA 2022">{{Cite web|url=http://ent.people.com.cn/n1/2023/0116/c1012-32607606.html|title=山东泰山队第八次夺足协杯冠军 创足协杯三连冠纪录|publisher=ent.people.com.cn|date=2023-01-16|access-date=2023-01-31}}</ref> [[Fulham FC]] and [[Southampton]] were keen to sign the player during the winter transfer window after a good performance for [[Shandong Taishan F.C.]] but the deal fell through after a fee could not be agreed between the two sides.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.football-asian.com/news/articleView.html?idxno=1853| title=Fulham is paying attention to Son Jun-ho | date=8 March 2022 }}</ref>

On 15 May 2023, Son was reportedly investigated by public security authorities in [[Liaoning]] due to match-fixing allegations involving former Shandong Taishan manager [[Hao Wei]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://en.yna.co.kr/view/AEN20230515004200315|title=China-based S. Korean football player under police investigation: embassy|publisher=ena.yna.co.kr|date=15 May 2023|access-date=16 May 2023}}</ref> The following day, [[Ministry of Foreign Affairs of China|Chinese Foreign Ministry]] spokesperson [[Wang Wenbin]] said that he had been detained for a suspected bribery case.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2023/5/16/china-detains-south-korea-footballer-in-suspected-bribery-case|title=China detains South Korea footballer in suspected bribery case|publisher=aljazeera.com|date=16 May 2023|access-date=16 May 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
Son Jun-ho would make his first senior appearance for [[South Korea national football team|South Korea]] in a friendlies match on 27 January 2018 against [[Moldova national football team|Moldova]], where he came on as a substitute for [[Kim Sung-joon (footballer)|Kim Sung-Joon]] in a 1-0 victory.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2018/01/27/world/friendlies/korea-republic/moldova/2716713/|title=KOREA REPUBLIC VS. MOLDOVA 1 - 0|publisher=soccerway.com|date=27 January 2018|access-date=31 January 2021}}</ref>
