'''Hakim Ziyech''' ({{lang-ar|حكيم زياش}}; {{lang-ber|ⵃⴰⴽⵉⵎ ⵣⵉⵢⴰⵛ}}; {{IPA-nl|haˈkim ziˈjɛʃ}};<ref>{{Cite web |date=1 April 2021 |title=Hakim Ziyech answers to Google questions |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GvzFnXofDxA&t=35s |access-date=2 July 2023 |website=Youtube}}</ref> born 19 March 1993) is a Moroccan professional [[footballer]] who plays as a [[Winger (association football)|right winger]] for [[Premier League]] club [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] and the [[Morocco national football team|Morocco national team]].<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.worldfootball.net/spieler_profil/hakim-ziyech/ | title = Hakim Ziyech | website = WorldFootball.net | access-date = 26 August 2012}}</ref> He is nicknamed "The Wizard",<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ziyech opens up on how he got 'Wizard' nickname at Ajax, settling in at Chelsea|url=https://tribuna.com/en/news/chelsea-2021-02-03-ziyech-opens-up-on-how-he-got-wizard-nickname-at-ajax-settling-in-at-chelsea/|access-date=23 January 2022|website=Tribuna.com|date=3 February 2021 |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=The Wizard of west London: Ziyech is already working his magic at Chelsea {{!}} Goal.com|url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/the-wizard-of-west-london-ziyech-is-already-working-his-magic-at-/r5llxkuem3ii1hqqhebiii0hf|access-date=23 January 2022|website=www.goal.com}}</ref> a title given to him by his former club [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]] supporters.<ref>{{Cite web|last=N|first=Victor|date=3 February 2021|title=Ziyech explains why he's called 'the wizard'|url=https://www.therealchelseafans.com/2021/02/ziyech-explains-why-hes-called-the-wizard/|access-date=23 January 2022|website=THE REAL CHELSEA FANS|language=en-GB}}</ref>

Ziyech started his professional career at Dutch club [[SC Heerenveen|Heerenveen]] in 2012 and signed for fellow Dutch club [[FC Twente|Twente]] two years later. In 2016, he signed for Ajax on a five-year deal, with a reported transfer fee of €11m.<ref name=":3" /><ref name=":4" /> The [[2018–19 Eredivisie|2018–19]] season was the most prolific season in Ziyech's career, as he scored 16 goals and provided 13 assists in the Eredivisie.<ref name=":2" /> He joined Premier League club Chelsea in the [[2020–21 Chelsea F.C. season|2020–21 season]].

Originally eligible to play for the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]] or Morocco at international level, he represented Morocco at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]], [[2019 Africa Cup of Nations]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

Ziyech is known for his finishing, [[Dribbling#Association football|dribbling]], long passes, technique, and ability from [[Direct free kick|free kicks]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Hakim Ziyech |url=http://eredivisielive.nl/eredivisie/speler/1079667/hakim-ziyech/ |access-date=26 August 2012 |publisher=eredivisielive.nl}}</ref> In 2019, he was selected as one of the 20 best players in the [[UEFA Champions League]] for the [[2018–19 UEFA Champions League#Squad of the season|2018–19 season]].<ref name="auto">{{cite web |date=2 June 2019 |title=UEFA Champions League Squad of the Season |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/newsid=2607890.html |work=UEFA.com}}</ref> At the end of the same year, he was ranked 29th in ''[[The Guardian]]''{{'}}s list of "The 100 best footballers in the world".<ref>{{cite news |title=the 100 best footballers in the world 2019 |newspaper=The Guardian |publisher=the guardian.com |url=https://www.theguardian.com/global/ng-interactive/2019/dec/17/the-100-best-male-footballers-in-the-world-2019 |access-date=20 December 2019}}</ref>

Beginning in 2015 when Ziyech joined the national team, he has donated all attained earnings and bonuses from international play to various charities or to the staff of the team.<ref>{{cite news |last1=van den Heever |first1=Megan |title=For country and charity: Morocco player donates 100% of salary |url=https://www.thesouthafrican.com/lifestyle/morocco-vs-france-world-cup-semi-finals-hakim-ziyech-latest-news-14-december-2022/ |access-date=9 January 2023 |work=thesouthafrican.com |date=14 December 2022}}</ref>

Fortunately, he was able to quickly realise what was wrong, having four brothers who went down the wrong path".<ref>{{cite news |title=Broer Ziyech: 'Hakim was de laatste kans voor onze familie' |url=https://www.elfvoetbal.nl/Binnenland/50616/Broer-Ziyech-Hakim-was-de-laatste-kans-voor-onze-familie |access-date=2 February 2023 |work=Elfvoetbal |date=19 August 2014 |language=nl}}</ref> As for Hakim Ziyech, he was demotivated at the age of twelve and decided to put an end to football.

===Chelsea===
====2020–21 season: European champion====
[[File:Hakim Ziyech 2020.jpg|thumb|upright|Ziyech playing for [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] in 2020]]

On 14 February 2020, it was announced that Ajax and [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] had reached an agreement for the transfer of Ziyech for a fee of €40&nbsp;million (potentially increasing to a maximum of €44&nbsp;million, with variable factors). He joined the [[Premier League]] club for the [[2020–21 Chelsea F.C. season|2020–21 season]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://english.ajax.nl/streams/ajax-now/ajax-and-chelsea-reach-agreement-for-ziyech-.htm|title=Ajax and Chelsea reach agreement for Ziyech|website=Ajax English|language=en-US|access-date=14 February 2020}}</ref> Ten days later, Chelsea announced they had agreed personal terms with Ziyech and that he had signed a five-year contract.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Shread |first1=Joe |title=Hakim Ziyech signs five-year Chelsea deal ahead of summer move|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11668/11941864/hakim-ziyech-signs-five-year-chelsea-deal-ahead-of-summer-move |website=Sky Sports|date=24 February 2020|access-date=24 February 2020}}</ref>

Ziyech made his league debut for Chelsea on 17 October 2020, in a 3–3 home draw against [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] after he came off from the bench.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Hafez |first1=Shamoon |title=Chelsea 3–3 Southampton: Blues must cut out individual errors – Frank Lampard|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/54489178 |website=BBC Sport|date=17 October 2020|access-date=17 October 2020}}</ref> On 28 October 2020, he scored his first Chelsea goal in a 4–0 away win over [[FC Krasnodar|Krasnodar]] on matchday two of the [[2020–21 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Steinberg |first1=Jacob |title=Hakim Ziyech stars as Chelsea crush Krasnodar in Champions League|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2020/oct/28/hakim-ziyech-stars-in-chelseas-crushing-victory-over-krasnodar |website=The Guardian|date=28 October 2020|access-date=28 October 2020}}</ref> Three days later, Ziyech started his first league match for Chelsea, contributing with a goal and an assist in a 3–0 away win over [[Burnley F.C.|Burnley]]. In doing so, Ziyech became the first Chelsea player since [[Diego Costa]] to score in his first two starts for Chelsea in all competitions.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/ziyech-becomes-first-chelsea-player-since-costa-to-score-in/7535m0oxxso610lhmh1u5bkpn |title=Ziyech becomes first Chelsea player since Costa to score in first two Blues starts |website=Goal |date=31 October 2020 |access-date=31 October 2020}}</ref> In Chelsea's next league match, Ziyech provided two assists and was named the man of the match as Chelsea defeated [[Sheffield United F.C.|Sheffield United]] by a score of 4–1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11668/12127090/hakim-ziyechs-masterclass-for-chelsea-against-sheffield-united-analysed|title=Hakim Ziyech's masterclass for Chelsea against Sheffield United analysed|website=Sky Sports|date=7 November 2020|access-date=8 November 2020}}</ref>

On 17 April 2021, Ziyech scored the only goal in Chelsea's [[2020–21 FA Cup|FA Cup]] semi-final clash against [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Murray|first=Scott|date=17 April 2021|title=Chelsea 1-0 Manchester City: FA Cup semi-final – as it happened|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2021/apr/17/chelsea-v-manchester-city-fa-cup-semi-final-live|access-date=17 April 2021}}</ref> On 29 May 2021, Ziyech won the [[2021 UEFA Champions League Final]] with Chelsea 1-0 against [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]], but did not come off the bench in the match.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/match/2029498/|title = Man. ====2021–22 season: UEFA Super Cup and FIFA Club World Cup titles====
On 11 August 2021, Ziyech scored against [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]] to give Chelsea their first [[2021 UEFA Super Cup|UEFA Super Cup]] in 23 years, as they won on penalties.<ref name=UEFASC>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2021/aug/11/chelsea-villarreal-super-cup-final-match-report|title=Kepa the hero as Chelsea beat Villarreal on penalties to win UEFA Super Cup|website=The Guardian|date=11 August 2021}}</ref> Ziyech was subbed off shortly before half-time with his arm in a sling after challenging for a header inside his own penalty area and landing badly.<ref name=UEFASC/> On 18 January 2022, Ziyech scored in a 1–1 draw against [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton]].<ref>{{Cite news|title=Brighton further damage Chelsea's title hopes|language=en-GB|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/59947190|access-date=23 January 2022}}</ref> On 23 January, Ziyech scored a wonderful curling shot against [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] in a 2–0 victory.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Chelsea beat Spurs for third time in three weeks|language=en-GB|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/60012767|access-date=23 January 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
===Netherlands===
Born in the Netherlands to a [[Moroccans in the Netherlands|Moroccan]] family, Ziyech was eligible to play for either [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]] or [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]] national team at international level.<ref name="Football Oranje">{{Cite news|url=http://www.football-oranje.com/ziyech-added-to-netherlands-squad/|title=Ziyech added to Netherlands squad – Football Oranje|date=19 May 2015|work=Football Oranje|access-date=3 August 2018}}</ref> He played for Dutch national youth teams, playing for the [[Netherlands national under-19 football team|under-19]], [[Netherlands national under-20 football team|under-20]] and [[Netherlands national under-21 football team|under-21]] squads. However, he opted to play for Morocco at senior level. He received his first call-up to the senior squad in May 2015 for friendly matches against the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]] and [[Latvia national football team|Latvia]].<ref name="Football Oranje"/>

==Style of play==
Ziyech is a right winger who likes to take up a wide starting position and then drive infield with the ball, on to his stronger left foot and into positions where he poses a threat on goal, either with shots, through balls or crosses. He is most dangerous coming into central areas and looking to play killer balls to teammates making diagonal runs towards goal from the left. He is particularly skilled at playing deft balls over a defence, and if his teammates time their runs well, they often very suddenly find themselves with a one-on-one shooting opportunity. His movement off the flank means the right-back can overlap.<ref>{{Cite web|date=22 February 2020|title=Player Analysis: How will Hakim Ziyech fit in at Chelsea?|url=https://thefootballfaithful.com/player-analysis-how-will-hakim-ziyech-fit-in-at-chelsea/|access-date=23 January 2022|language=en-GB}}</ref>

The dip with which Ziyech plays those balls into the area is one of his greatest skills, not only because he so often creates clear-cut chances for his teammate, but also because he teases the opposition goalkeeper with a ball that looks like it might float through to them but drops short into the zone in front of goal. Ziyech can manipulate the ball impressively, and that leads to him creating plenty of chances for his team. He has incredible balance, speed and acceleration, is able to shake off the attention of an opponent with ease and create himself shooting opportunities, rather than going down the line to cross.<ref>{{Cite web|date=12 March 2021|title=Chelsea: Is it good that Hakim Ziyech is changing his style of play?|url=https://theprideoflondon.com/2021/03/12/chelsea-good-hakim-ziyech-changing-style-play/|access-date=23 January 2022|website=The Pride of London|language=en-US}}</ref>

The threat he poses when he moves infield means he draws defenders towards him, creating gaps for others further forwards. That also works the other way around; teammates know that when Ziyech moves towards goal they should make runs that take defenders away and create space for him to shoot. When he does get into a position to shoot from range, he usually looks to bend his foot around the ball and aim for the far corner.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Hakim Ziyech: Premier League Player Watch|url=https://www.coachesvoice.com/cv/hakim-ziyech-chelsea-ajax-tactics/|access-date=23 January 2022|website=The Coaches' Voice|language=en-GB}}</ref>
