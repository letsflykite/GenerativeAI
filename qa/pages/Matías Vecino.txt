'''Matías Vecino Falero''' (born 24 August 1991) is a Uruguayan professional [[footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[Serie A]] club [[S.S. Lazio|Lazio]] and the [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay national team]].

==Club career==
===Early career===
Vecino started his professional career with Central Español in 2010, and switched to Nacional for the 2011–12 season.

===Fiorentina===
In January 2013, Vecino joined [[Serie A]] side [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]] by penning a four-year contract for a reported transfer fee of €2.5 million.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.fiorentina.it/notizia.asp?IDNotizia=124662|title=Vecino è già in Italia: sarà tesserato a luglio ma si allenerà con i Viola|archive-date=28 September 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130928055840/http://www.fiorentina.it/notizia.asp?IDNotizia=124662|trans-title=Vecino is already in Italy: he will be registered in July but will train with the Violas|publisher=fiorentina.it|language=it|date=23 January 2013|access-date=1 August 2017}}</ref> However, the transfer was delayed and became official in the next transfer window in August 2013.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.gazzetta.it/Calciomercato/22-08-2013/ufficiale-fiorentina-vecino-c-transfert-201011211226.shtml|title=Ufficiale Fiorentina-Vecino, c'è il transfert|date=22 August 2013|access-date=26 November 2017|newspaper=La Gazzetta dello Sport|publisher=RCS MediaGroup|location=Milan|language=it}}</ref><ref>{{cite press release|url=http://en.violachannel.tv/breaking-detail/items/matias-vecino-completes-fiorentina-transfer.html|title=Matias Vecino completes Fiorentina transfer|date=22 August 2013|access-date=26 November 2017|work=Viola Channel|publisher=ACF Fiorentina|archive-date=1 December 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171201032641/http://en.violachannel.tv/breaking-detail/items/matias-vecino-completes-fiorentina-transfer.html|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On 3 September 2013, he was not included by manager [[Vincenzo Montella]] in [[2013–14 UEFA Europa League|UEFA Europa League squad]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.firenzepost.it/2013/09/03/fiorentina-ecco-la-lista-uefa-fuori-rebic-e-vecino/|title=FIORENTINA, ECCO LA LISTA UEFA: FUORI REBIC E VECINO|trans-title=FIORENTINA, HERE'S THE UEFA LIST: REBIC AND VECINO OUT|work=Firenze Post|language=it|first=Massimiliano|last=Mugnaini|date=3 September 2013|access-date=1 August 2017}}</ref>

Vecino made his competitive debut as well as his first Serie A one on 26 September 2013 in a 2–1 away defeat against [[Inter Milan]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2013/09/26/italy/serie-a/fc-internazionale-milano/acf-fiorentina/1551920/|title=Internazionale vs. Fiorentina 2 – 1|publisher=Soccerway|date=26 September 2013|access-date=26 September 2013}}</ref> He was used very scarcely during the first part of [[2013–14 ACF Fiorentina season|2013–14 season]], collecting only six appearances, only one as starter, leading the club to send him on loan at fellow Serie A side [[Cagliari Calcio|Cagliari]] until the end of the season, with an option to buy.<ref>{{cite press release|url=http://en.violachannel.tv/breaking-detail/items/vecino-joins-cagliari-on-loan.html|title=Vecino joins Cagliari on loan|work=Viola Channel|publisher=ACF Fiorentina|date=29 January 2014|access-date=26 November 2017|archive-date=1 December 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171201030826/http://en.violachannel.tv/breaking-detail/items/vecino-joins-cagliari-on-loan.html|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite press release|url=https://www.cagliaricalcio.com/news/ultimissime/12024/vecino-al-cagliari|title=Vecino al Cagliari|trans-title=Vecino to Cagliari|date=29 January 2014|access-date=26 November 2017|publisher=Cagliari Calcio|language=it}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.gazzetta.it/Calciomercato/29-01-2014/cagliari-ufficiale-arriva-vecino-fiorentina-202240214370.shtml|title= Cagliari, ufficiale: arriva Vecino dalla Fiorentina |date=29 January 2014|access-date=26 November 2017|newspaper=La Gazzetta dello Sport|publisher=RCS MediaGroup|location=Milan|language=it}}</ref>

Vecino spent the 2014–15 season on loan at [[Empoli F.C.]]<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.gazzetta.it/Calciomercato/13-08-2014/empoli-ufficiali-laxalt-vecino-90127823362.shtml|title= Empoli, ufficiali Laxalt e Vecino |date=13 August 2014|access-date=26 November 2017|newspaper=La Gazzetta dello Sport|publisher=RCS MediaGroup|location=Milan|language=it}}</ref><ref>{{cite press release|url=http://it.violachannel.tv/vc13-dettaglio-breaking/items/data_13-08-2014_vecino-allempoli.html|title=Vecino all'Empoli|date=13 August 2014|access-date=26 November 2017|work=Viola Channel|publisher=ACF Fiorentina|language=it}}</ref>

In mid-2015, Vecino returned to Florence and became a member of the first team. He signed a new 5-year contract in 2016.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.corrieredellosport.it/news/stadio/calciomercato/2016/05/17-11613418/calciomercato_fiorentina_e_ufficiale_vecino_resta_fino_al_2021/|title=Calciomercato Fiorentina, è ufficiale: Vecino resta fino al 2021|date=17 May 2016|access-date=26 November 2017|work=Corriere dello Sport – Stadio |publication-place=Rome|location=Florence|language=it|author=Sullo Stesso Argomento}}</ref><ref>{{cite press release|url=http://en.violachannel.tv/breaking-detail/items/matias-vecino-pens-new-five-year-deal.html|title=Matias Vecino pens new five-year deal|date=17 May 2016|work=Viola Channel|publisher=ACF Fiorentina|access-date=26 November 2017|archive-date=1 December 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171201035046/http://en.violachannel.tv/breaking-detail/items/matias-vecino-pens-new-five-year-deal.html|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===Inter Milan===
On 31 July 2017, Fiorentina authorized Vecino to have a medical with [[Inter Milan]],<ref>{{cite press release|url=http://en.violachannel.tv/breaking-detail/items/fiorentina-authorise-matias-vecino-to-have-inter-medical.html|title=Fiorentina authorise Matias Vecino to have Inter medical|date=31 July 2017|access-date=26 November 2017|work=Viola Channel|publisher=ACF Fiorentina|archive-date=8 February 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180208094139/http://en.violachannel.tv/breaking-detail/items/fiorentina-authorise-matias-vecino-to-have-inter-medical.html|url-status=dead}}</ref> in order to complete a reported €24 million transfer.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.football-italia.net/106961/fiorentina-vecino-inter|title=Fiorentina: Vecino to Inter|work=Football Italia|date=31 July 2017|access-date=1 August 2017|publisher=Tiro Media}}</ref> The transfer was made official on 2 August, with Vecino signing a contract until June 2021.<ref>{{cite press release|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/59155/matias-vecino-is-now-a-nerazzurri-player|title=MATIAS VECINO IS NOW A NERAZZURRI PLAYER!|publisher=Inter Milan|date=2 August 2017|access-date=2 August 2017|archive-date=2 August 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170802125041/http://www.inter.it/en/news/59155/matias-vecino-is-now-a-nerazzurri-player|url-status=dead}}</ref>

He was given squad number 11, and made his competitive debut on 20 August in the opening [[2017–18 Serie A|Serie A]] matchday against his former side Fiorentina, featuring full-90 minutes as Inter won 3–0.<ref>{{cite press release|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/59291/inter-3-0-fiorentina|title=INTER 3-0 FIORENTINA|publisher=Inter Milan|date=20 August 2017|access-date=20 August 2017|archive-date=3 September 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190903111434/https://www.inter.it/en/news/59291/inter-3-0-fiorentina|url-status=dead}}</ref> Vecino scored his maiden Inter goal six days later in the next match against [[A.S. Roma|Roma]], netting the third goal in an eventual 1–3 away win, the first win at [[Stadio Olimpico]] over Roma after nine years.<ref>{{cite press release|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/59379/roma-1-3-inter|title=ROMA 1-3 INTER|publisher=Inter Milan|date=26 August 2017|access-date=26 August 2017|archive-date=27 June 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180627005235/https://www.inter.it/en/news/59379/roma-1-3-inter|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite press release|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/59391/roma-inter-all-you-need-to-know|title=ROMA - INTER: ALL YOU NEED TO KNOW|publisher=Inter Milan|date=27 August 2017|access-date=27 August 2017|archive-date=4 July 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180704063558/https://www.inter.it/en/news/59391/roma-inter-all-you-need-to-know|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On 20 May 2018, Vecino scored the winning goal vs [[S.S. Lazio|Lazio]], in Inter's 2–3 win, a victory which allowed the club to return to the [[UEFA Champions League]] after a six year absence.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44192082|title=Inter Milan will play in the Champions League for the first time since 2012 after a dramatic win at Lazio|work=BBC|date=20 May 2018}}</ref> On 18 September 2018, he scored an injury-time winning goal vs [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]], in Inter's 2–1 win in the UEFA Champions League.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2018/sep/18/internazionale-tottenham-champions-league-match-report|title=Spurs stunned by Matías Vecino's injury-time winner for Inter|work=The Guardian|date=18 September 2018}}</ref>

===Lazio===
On 1 August 2022, Vecino joined Lazio on a free transfer, signing a three-year contract.<ref>{{Cite web |last= |date=1 August 2022 |title=Comunicato 01.08.2022 |url=https://www.sslazio.it/it/news/press-release-2/67680-comunicato-01-08-2022 |access-date=1 August 2022 |website=www.sslazio.it |language=it}}</ref>

==International career==
Vecino was born in Canelones, Uruguay, to a family of Italian ancestry, with roots in the city of [[Campobasso]], and was eligible for both national teams.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fiorentina.it/notizia.asp?IDNotizia=122346|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130413031557/http://www.fiorentina.it/notizia.asp?IDNotizia=122346|url-status=dead|title=Fiorentina|date=13 April 2013|archive-date=13 April 2013}}</ref> He has been capped by the [[Uruguay national under-20 football team]] for the [[2011 South American Youth Championship]] and for the [[2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup]].<ref>FIFA. [https://web.archive.org/web/20111122081458/http://www.fifa.com/worldfootball/statisticsandrecords/players/player=337196/index.html "FIFA Player Statistics: Matias VECINO"]. Retrieved on 14 May 2013.</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.auf.org.uy/15-mat-iacute-as-vecino/|title=15 - Matías Vecino|date=1 June 2018|access-date=5 April 2021}}</ref> He scored the goal to qualify Uruguay for the [[Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|2012 Olympic Games]] in [[2012 Summer Olympics|London]].<ref>FIFA. [https://web.archive.org/web/20110401020930/http://www.fifa.com/mensolympic/news/newsid=1380450/index.html "Uruguay end 84-year drought"]. 10 February 2011. Retrieved on 14 May 2013.</ref> Vecino's debut for the [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay senior team]] came on 25 March 2016 against [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/03/26/south-america/wc-qualifying-south-america/brazil/uruguay/2161444/|title=Brazil vs. Uruguay 2 – 2|publisher=Soccerway|date=25 March 2016|access-date=26 November 2017}}</ref>

In May 2018, he was named in Uruguay's provisional 26 man [[Uruguay national football team|squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/amp/news/revealed-every-world-cup-2018-squad-23-man-preliminary-lists/oa0atsduflsv1nsf6oqk576rb|title=Revealed: Every World Cup 2018 squad - 23-man & preliminary lists & when will they be announced? - Goal.com}}</ref>
