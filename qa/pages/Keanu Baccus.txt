'''Keanu Kole Baccus''' (born 7 June 1998) is a South African-Australian professional [[association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[Midfielder (association football)|attacking midfielder]] for [[Scottish Premiership]] club [[St Mirren F.C.|St Mirren]]. Born in South Africa, he is an international with the [[Australia national soccer team|Australia national team]], where he made his full international debut in September 2022.

==Early life==
Baccus was born in [[Durban]], [[South Africa]] but moved to [[Australia]] before his first birthday where his family settled in western [[Sydney]].<ref>{{Cite web|title="It's Keanu, not Keano!" — why St Mirren's new general is putting his foot down|url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/its-keanu-not-keano-why-st-mirrens-new-general-is-putting-his-foot-down-hvlt0597p|date=9 April 2022|website=thetimes.co.uk}}</ref> Baccus attended Kings Langley Public School where he was inspired by Socceroo Mark Schwarzer  to participate in the sport.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Home - Kings Langley Public School|url=https://kingslangl-p.schools.nsw.gov.au/|access-date=27 July 2021|website=kingslangl-p.schools.nsw.gov.au|language=en-AU}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Mark Schwarzer Profile, News & Stats {{!}} Premier League|url=https://www.premierleague.com/players/1218/Mark-Schwarzer/overview|access-date=27 July 2021|website=www.premierleague.com|language=en}}</ref> He is the younger brother of [[Macarthur FC]] player [[Kearyn Baccus]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.snl24.com/kickoff/sanews/sa-players-abroad/durban-born-keanu-baccus-joins-st-mirren-20220630|title=Durban-born Keanu Baccus joins St. Mirren|author=Staff reporter|website=Kickoff}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Western Sydney Wanderers===
After rising from the Wanderers Academy to serve as co-captain of the youth team, Baccus signed a two-year senior contract in May 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wswanderersfc.com.au/article/keanu-baccus-earns-first-team-contract/kr6tv9l19mxh11adwfidoxffd|title=Keanu Baccus earns first team contract|publisher=[[Western Sydney Wanderers FC|Western Sydney Wanderers]]|date=5 May 2017}}</ref>

===St Mirren===
In April 2022, [[St Mirren F.C.|St. Mirren]] boss [[Stephen Robinson (footballer)|Stephen Robinson]] announced that Baccus was joining the [[Scottish Premiership]] club following the conclusion of the 2022 A League campaign.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Burns |first=Scott |date=25 April 2022 |title=Western Sydney Wanderers star Keanu Baccus agrees St Mirren pre contract |url=https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/keanu-baccus-pens-st-mirren-26795812 |access-date=5 May 2022 |website=Daily Record |language=en}}</ref> A few months later, the signing was confirmed by the club as a two-year deal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stmirren.com/all-news/4478-keanu-baccus-agrees-two-year-deal|title=Keanu Baccus agrees two-year deal|website=[[St Mirren F.C.|St Mirren]]|date=27 June 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
Baccus qualified for the [[2020 Summer Olympics|Tokyo 2020 Olympics]] as part of the [[Australia national under-23 soccer team|Australia under-23]] team. The team beat Argentina in their first group match but were unable to win another match. They were therefore not in medal contention.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Australian Olympic Team for Tokyo 2021|url=https://www.theroar.com.au/olympics/australian-olympic-team/|access-date=25 February 2022|website=The Roar|language=en-US}}</ref>

In September 2022, Baccus debuted for the [[Australia men's national soccer team|senior team]] as a second-half substitute in a friendly against [[New Zealand national football team|New Zealand]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=22 June 2022 |title=New Zealand vs Australia, International Friendlies, Round 1, 25th Sep 2022 |url=https://www.socceroos.com.au/match/new-zealand-v-australia-friendlies-25-09-2022/2289966 |access-date=25 September 2022 |website=Socceroos |language=en}}</ref> On 8 November 2022, Baccus was named in [[Australia men's national soccer team|Australia's]] [[FIFA World Cup|World Cup]] squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Socceroos Squad Announced for FIFA 2022 World Cup |url=https://www.socceroos.com.au/news/socceroos-squad-announced-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022tm|accessdate=8 November 2022|date=8 November 2022}}</ref> Baccus was used as a substitute in Australia's first three games in the tournament, before making his first senior international start in Australia's loss in the round of sixteen to [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.socceroos.com.au/news/dream-come-true-baccus-over-moon-after-socceroos-starting-debut|website=[[Socceroos]]|first=Euan|last=James|date=7 December 2022|access-date=7 December 2022|title='Dream come true': Baccus over the moon after Socceroos starting debut}}</ref>
