'''Mamadou Loum N'Diaye''' (born 30 December 1996) is a Senegalese professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[defensive midfielder]] for [[Reading F.C.|Reading]], on loan from [[FC Porto|Porto]], and the [[Senegal national football team|Senegal national team]].

==Club career==
Loum made his professional debut in the [[Segunda Liga]] for [[S.C. Braga B]] on 15 August 2015 in a game against [[Gil Vicente F.C.]], as a 70th-minute substitute for [[Carlos Fortes (footballer, born 1994)|Carlos Fortes]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=Soccerway|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2015/08/15/portugal/liga-de-honra/sporting-braga-b/gil-vicente-fc/2068649/|title=Game Report by Soccerway|date=15 August 2015}}</ref> He was called up once for [[S.C. Braga]] for their [[Primeira Liga]] game away to [[F.C. Paços de Ferreira]] on 23 April 2017, remaining unused in a 3–1 loss.<ref>{{cite news |title=Paulinho, Loum e Stojiljkovic chamados para deslocação a Paços de Ferreira|trans-title=Paulinho, Loum and Stojiljkovic called up for trip to Paços de Ferreira |url=https://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-nos/sp--braga/detalhe/paulinho-loum-e-stojiljkovic-chamados-para-deslocacao-a-pacos-de-ferreira |accessdate=10 May 2020 |work=[[Record (newspaper)|Record]] |date=22 April 2017 |language=Portuguese}}</ref> A day before the anniversary of that game, he was sent off at the end of a 2–2 home draw with [[Académico de Viseu F.C.|Académico de Viseu FC]].<ref>{{cite news |title=COMENTÁRIO: Sporting de Braga B e Académico de Viseu empatam em jogo intenso|trans-title=COMMENTARY: Sporting de Braga B and Académico de Viseu draw in intense game|url=https://www.dn.pt/lusa/comentario-sporting-de-braga-b-e-academico-de-viseu-empatam-em-jogo-intenso-9278590.html |accessdate=13 July 2020 |work=[[Diário de Notícias]] |date=22 April 2018 |language=Portuguese}}</ref>

On 30 July 2018, Loum was loaned to top-flight team [[Moreirense F.C.]] for the season.<ref>{{cite news |title=Mamadou Loum chega por empréstimo do Braga|trans-title=Mamadou Loum arrives on loan from Braga|url=https://www.ojogo.pt/futebol/1a-liga/moreirense/noticias/mamadou-loum-chega-por-emprestimo-do-braga-9656191.html |accessdate=10 May 2020 |work=[[O Jogo]] |date=30 July 2018 |language=Portuguese}}</ref> He scored his first goal on 2 November in a 3–1 win at eventual champions [[S.L. Benfica]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Moreirense gela a Luz e Benfica já vai em três derrotas seguidas|trans-title=Moreirense freeze the Luz and Benfica now on three consecutive defeats|url=https://www.ojogo.pt/futebol/1a-liga/noticias/moreirense-gela-a-luz-e-benfica-ja-vai-em-tres-derrotas-seguidas-10125278.html |accessdate=10 May 2020 |work=O Jogo |date=2 November 2018 |language=Portuguese}}</ref> 

In January 2019, Loum moved to [[FC Porto]] on a loan deal for the rest of the season with an option to turn the deal permanent.<ref>{{cite news |title=Oficial: Loum é reforço do FC Porto |trans-title=Official: Loum is addition to FC Porto|url=https://www.ojogo.pt/futebol/1a-liga/porto/noticias/oficial-loum-e-reforco-do-fc-porto-10518852.html |accessdate=13 July 2020 |work=O Jogo |date=31 January 2019 |language=Portuguese}}</ref> He played three games over the rest of the season and then the club paid €7.75 million for 75% of his economic rights.<ref>{{cite news |title=FC Porto: Loum custou 7,75 milhões de euros, Manafá três milhões|trans-title=FC Porto: Loum cost 7.75 million euros, Manafá three million|url=https://tvi24.iol.pt/liga/relatorio-e-contas/fc-porto-loum-custou-7-75-milhoes-de-euros |accessdate=13 July 2020 |publisher=[[TVI 24]] |date=11 October 2019 |language=Portuguese}}</ref> On 2 December, he started a 2–0 home win over Paços de Ferreira and scored the opening goal.<ref>{{cite news |title=Loum abriu, Zé Luís fixou de bicicleta. FC Porto vence Paços|trans-title=Loum opened, Zé Luís sealed it with bicycle kick. FC Porto defeat Paços|url=https://www.tsf.pt/desporto/direto-sergio-conceicao-faz-duas-alteracoes-na-rececao-ao-pacos-11576244.html |accessdate=13 July 2020 |publisher=[[TSF (radio station)|TSF]] |date=2 December 2019 |language=Portuguese}}</ref>

On 23 July 2021, after featuring rarely for Porto, Loum was loaned to [[La Liga]] side [[Deportivo Alavés]] for the [[2021–22 La Liga|2021–22 campaign]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.deportivoalaves.com/noticia/mamadou-loum-refuerza-el-centro-del-campo-del-deportivo-alaves|title=Mamadou Loum refuerza el centro del campo del Deportivo Alavés|trans-title=Mamadou Loum bolsters the centre of midfield of Deportivo Alavés|publisher=Deportivo Alavés|language=es|date=23 July 2021|accessdate=30 July 2021}}</ref>

On 30 July 2022, Loum joined [[EFL Championship]] club [[Reading F.C.|Reading]] on a season-long loan.<ref>{{Cite web |last=FC |first=Reading |title=Mamadou Loum secures season-long loan move from FC Porto |url=https://readingfc.co.uk//news/2022/july/29/mamadou-loum-secures-season-long-loan-move-from-fc-porto/ |access-date=30 July 2022 |website=Reading FC}}</ref>

==International career==
Loum represented [[Senegal national under-20 football team|Senegal]] at the [[2015 FIFA U-20 World Cup]]. He made his debut for the [[Senegal national football team|senior team]] on 26 March 2019 in a friendly against [[Mali national football team|Mali]], playing the full 90 minutes of a 2–1 win in [[Dakar]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[ESPN]]|url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/match?gameId=538421|title=Senegal v Mali game report|date=26 March 2019}}</ref> He was called up for the [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations]], which the team won in Cameroon at the start of the following year; his one appearance was a goalless draw with neighbours [[Guinea national football team|Guinea]] in the second group game.<ref>{{cite news |title=Senegal 0&ndash;0 Guinea |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/59903966 |access-date=7 February 2022 |publisher=[[BBC Sport]] |date=14 January 2022}}</ref>

He was appointed a [[National Order of the Lion|Grand Officer of the National Order of the Lion]] by [[President of Senegal]] [[Macky Sall]] following the nation's victory at the 2021 Africa Cup of Nations.<ref name= "reuters">{{cite web |title=Cash prizes, real estate, and highest honours as Senegal celebrates Nations Cup heroes |url=https://www.reuters.com/lifestyle/sports/cash-prizes-real-estate-highest-honours-senegal-celebrates-nations-cup-heroes-2022-02-08/ |website=[[Reuters]] |access-date=28 March 2023 |date=8 February 2022}}</ref>
