{{nihongo|'''Ritsu Dōan'''|堂安 律 |Dōan Ritsu|born 16 June 1998}} is a Japanese professional [[footballer]] who plays as a [[Midfielder (association football)#Winger|winger]] or an [[Midfielder (association football)#Attacking midfielder|attacking midfielder]] for [[Bundesliga]] club [[SC Freiburg]] and the [[Japan national football team|Japan national team]].<ref name="sw">{{cite web | url=https://int.soccerway.com/players/ritsu-doan/396704/ | title=R. Doan | website=Soccerway | access-date=21 June 2015}}</ref>

==Club career==

=== Gamba Osaka ===
Coming through the youth system, Dōan joined [[J1 League]] club [[Gamba Osaka]] in 2015.{{Citation needed|date=June 2021}} On 27 May, he debuted against [[FC Seoul]] in the [[2015 AFC Champions League]].{{Citation needed|date=June 2021}} In 2016, he mainly played for their Gamba Osaka's newly-established reserve team [[Gamba Osaka U-23]] in the [[J3 League]].<ref>{{J.League player|15846}}</ref>

=== Groningen ===
In June 2017, Dōan moved to [[Eredivisie]] club [[FC Groningen]] on loan with an option to purchase on a three-year contract.{{Citation needed|date=June 2021}} The option was exercised on 23 April 2018, and Dōan transferred to Groningen on a permanent basis on 1 July 2018.<ref>{{cite web|title=FC Groningen licht optie en contracteert Ritsu Doan – FC Groningen|date=23 April 2018 |url=https://www.fcgroningen.nl/nieuws/fc-groningen-licht-optie-en-contracteert-ritsu-doan/2|language=nl}}</ref>

On 15 April 2018, against [[Roda JC Kerkrade|Roda]], Dōan scored his eighth goal of the season, equaling the total of goals [[Arjen Robben]] scored as a teenager for Groningen.<ref>{{cite web|title=Roda terug op aarde na bevrijdende zege FC Groningen|url=https://www.vi.nl/nieuws/roda-terug-op-aarde-na-bevrijdende-zege-fc-groningen-#|website=www.vi.nl|language=nl}}</ref>

===PSV Eindhoven===
Dōan joined [[PSV Eindhoven]] on a five-year contract in 2019.<ref name="bielefeld">{{cite news |title=Football: Japan winger Ritsu Dōan joins Bielefeld on loan from PSV |url=https://english.kyodonews.net/news/2020/09/f1836f09bd88-soccer-japan-winger-doan-joins-bielefeld-on-loan-from-psv.html |access-date=5 September 2020 |work=Kyodo News |date=5 September 2020}}</ref>

====Loan to Arminia Bielefeld====
In September 2020, he moved on loan to [[Bundesliga]] side [[Arminia Bielefeld]] for the [[2020–21 Arminia Bielefeld season|2020–21 season]].<ref name="bielefeld" /> Dōan made his debut in the opening game of the season, a 1–1 draw against [[Eintracht Frankfurt]].{{Citation needed|date=June 2021}} Dōan scored his first goal for Bielefeld in a 4–1 home defeat against [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]].{{Citation needed|date=June 2021}}

===SC Freiburg===
On 5 July 2022, [[SC Freiburg]] announced that they had signed Dōan ahead of the [[2022–23 Bundesliga|2022–23]] season.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.scfreiburg.com/en/latest/news/first-team/news/20212022/freiburg-sign-ritsu-doan/|title=FREIBURG SIGN RITSU DOAN|website=scfreiburg.com|date=5 July 2022|access-date=14 July 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
===Youth===
In May 2017, Dōan was elected [[Japan national under-20 football team|Japan national under-20 team]] for the [[2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup|2017 U-20 World Cup]]. At this tournament, he played full-time in all four matches and scored three goals.<ref>{{FIFA player|391646}}</ref>

===Senior===
On 30 August 2018, Dōan received his first international call-up from the [[Japan national football team|Japan national team]] for the [[Kirin Cup|Kirin Challenge Cup]] 2018.<ref>{{cite web|title=SAMURAI BLUE (Japan National Team) Squad, Schedule - KIRIN CHALLENGE CUP 2018【9/7＠Hokkaido vs Chile】【9/11＠Osaka vs Costa Rica】|url=http://www.jfa.jp/eng/samuraiblue/news/00018623/|date=30 August 2018|work=jfa.jp|access-date=30 August 2018}}</ref> He started his debut appearance in a [[Exhibition game|friendly match]] against [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica]] on 11 September 2018, and scored his debut goal in a friendly match against [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay]] on 16 October 2018.{{Citation needed|date=November 2022}}

He was called-up by [[Hajime Moriyasu]] to represent Japan in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]]. He featured in Japan's first match of the competition against [[Germany national football team|Germany]] on 23 November 2022, being subbed on in the second half while Japan was 1–0 down. Dōan scored the [[Equaliser (sports)|equalizer]] four minutes after coming on as his side later went on to win the game 2–1.<ref>{{cite web|title=World Cup 2022 - Germany 1-2 Japan: Late Ritsu Doan and Takuma Asano goals earn shock victory|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/germany-vs-japan/report/462968|date=23 November 2022|access-date=23 November 2022}}</ref> In an almost identical turn of events, he was subbed on in the second half of Japan's final group stage match against [[Spain national football team|Spain]] on 1 December 2022 while Japan was 1–0 down, and scored the equalizer two minutes after coming on as his side later went on to win 2–1. The win ultimately qualified Japan for the tournament's [[2022 FIFA World Cup knockout stage|knockout stages]] as group winners.<ref>{{cite web|title=World Cup 2022 - Japan 2-1 Spain: Ao Tanaka's controversial goal stuns Luis Enrique's side but both sides advance|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/12757077/world-cup-2022-japan-2-1-spain-ao-tanakas-controversial-goal-stuns-luis-enriques-side-but-both-sides-advance|date=1 December 2022|access-date=1 December 2022}}</ref>
