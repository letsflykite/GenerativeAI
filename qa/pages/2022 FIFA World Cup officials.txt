The following is a list of match officials (referees, assistant referees, and video assistant referees) who officiated at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{Cite news |date=11 November 2022 |title=Meet the referees taking charge of World Cup 2022 |work=aljazeera.com |publisher=[[Al Jazeera]] |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/sports/2022/11/11/meet-the-referees-heading-to-the-world-cup-2022 |access-date=18 November 2022}}</ref>

FIFA appoints ten officials for each match of the tournament. This includes the on-site team of the [[Referee (association football)|referee]], two [[Assistant referee (association football)|assistant referees]], the [[Assistant referee (association football)#Fourth official|fourth official]] and the [[Assistant referee (association football)#Reserve assistant referee|reserve assistant referee]]. At a centralised video operation room in [[Doha]], the team of video match officials operate the [[video assistant referee]] (VAR) system.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/technical/football-technology/football-technologies-and-innovations-at-the-fifa-world-cup-2022/video-assistant-referee-var |title=Video Assistant Referee (VAR) |publisher=[[FIFA]] |access-date=2 December 2022}}</ref> This team consists of the lead video assistant referee and three assistants, designated as the assistant VAR (AVAR1), the offside VAR (AVAR2) and the support VAR (AVAR3). Finally, a stand-by assistant VAR official is appointed for each match, used in case of a loss of connection between the stadium and the centralised video operation room.<ref>{{cite tweet |author=FIFA |author-link=FIFA |user=fifamedia |number=1594337494648193029 |date=20 November 2022 |title=The stand-by AVAR is a new role within the match officials' team at FIFA World Cup 2022 matches. In case of a loss of connection between the stadium and the centralized VAR Room, a back-up VAR room located at each stadium will be activated. |access-date=2 December 2022}}</ref> In such a scenario, a backup VAR room located in each stadium will be activated, with the fourth official serving as the video assistant referee (and the reserve assistant referee taking his place), and the stand-by assistant VAR serving as the assistant VAR.<ref>{{cite tweet |author=FIFA |author-link=FIFA |user=fifamedia |number=1594337631009398786 |date=20 November 2022 |title=While the fourth official will operate as VAR, with the reserve assistant referee replacing him, the stand-by VAR will assist him as AVAR and the VAR system will work with only two VMO. All this happens within minutes and without match restart being delayed. |access-date=2 December 2022}}</ref>

For the first time in the history of the FIFA World Cup, the FIFA Referees Committee has also appointed three female referees and three female assistant referees.<ref name="referee 2022"/>

==Referees and assistant referees==
On 19 May 2022, FIFA announced the list of 36 [[referee (association football)|referees]] and 69 [[assistant referee (association football)|assistant referees]] from all six confederations for the 2022 FIFA World Cup.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/1da4b811328add8f/original/MEDIA-Alphabetical-order-List-of-Match-Officials-FWC-2022-Qatar.pdf |title=FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022: List of match officials |website=FIFA.com |publisher=[[Fédération Internationale de Football Association|FIFA]] |format=PDF |date=19 May 2022 |access-date=18 November 2022}}</ref> These were selected from an extended list of over 50 trios.<ref name="referee 2022">{{Cite news |date=19 May 2022 |title=36 referees and 63 assistant referees and 24 video match officials appointed for FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022 |work=FIFA.com |publisher=[[Fédération Internationale de Football Association|FIFA]] |url=https://www.fifa.com/tournaments/mens/worldcup/qatar2022/media-releases/36-referees-69-assistant-referees-and-24-video-match-officials-appointed-for-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022 |access-date=18 November 2022}}</ref>

On 15 December 2022, FIFA announced that [[Polish Football Association|Polish]] referee [[Szymon Marciniak]] would adjudicate the final.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/fifamedia/status/1603447958703423507|title=FIFA Media}}</ref>
