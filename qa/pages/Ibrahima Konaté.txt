'''Ibrahima Konaté''' (born 25 May 1999) is a French professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] for [[Premier League]] club [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] and the [[France national football team|France national team]].

Starting off with [[FC Sochaux-Montbéliard|Sochaux]], Konaté moved to [[RB Leipzig]] in 2017. After four years with the club, Liverpool signed him in 2021 for a fee of £36 million. He won the [[EFL Cup]] and [[FA Cup]] in his first season.

==Early and personal life==
Ibrahima Konaté<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fdp.fifa.org/assetspublic/ce44/pdf/SquadLists-English.pdf |title=Squad List: FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022: France (FRA) |publisher=FIFA |page=12 |date=18 December 2022 |access-date=1 March 2023}}</ref> was born on 25 May 1999<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.espn.co.uk/football/player/_/id/251634/ibrahima-konate |title=Ibrahima Konaté: Overview |publisher=ESPN |access-date=7 September 2021}}</ref> in [[Paris]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Ibrahima Konaté|language=fr|newspaper=L'Équipe|location=Paris|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/FootballFicheJoueur62329.html|access-date=7 September 2021}}</ref>  He grew up on a council estate in the [[11th arrondissement of Paris]] and is the second youngest of eight children born to parents from [[Mali]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/may/26/ibrahima-konate-liverpool-final-champions-league-final-paris|title=Ibrahima Konaté: 'The final in Paris, my home. I couldn't have dreamed it'|date=26 May 2022|work=The Guardian|access-date=26 May 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Touré|first=Noyine|date=7 February 2019|title=Ibrahima Konaté affole l'Europe|url=https://www.afriquesports.net/football/ibrahima-konate-affole-leurope/}}</ref>
He is a [[Muslims|Muslim]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Hendrix |first=Hale |date=5 April 2020 |title=Ibrahima Konate Childhood Story Plus Untold Biography Facts |url=https://lifebogger.com/ibrahima-konate-childhood-story-plus-untold-biography-facts/ |access-date=17 April 2022 |website=LifeBogger}}</ref> Outside of football, Konaté is a fan of ''[[Attack on Titan]]'' as his favourite series.<ref>{{cite news |last=Northcroft |first=Jonathan |date=27 May 2022 |title=Ibrahima Konaté: I asked Jürgen Klopp, 'If I was your son, what would you advise? |url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/ibrahima-konate-i-asked-juergen-klopp-if-i-was-your-son-what-would-you-advise-f6xjg5vxj |access-date=17 April 2022 |newspaper=The Times}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Early career===
Konaté grew up in the [[11th arrondissement of Paris]] and played as a teen for [[Paris FC]] youth teams.<ref name="espn_may2021">{{cite news|author1=Julien Laurens|date=28 May 2021|title=Ibrahima Konate's move to Liverpool is just reward for the hard-working French U-21 star|publisher=ESPN|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/english-premier-league/story/4396833/ibrahima-konates-move-to-liverpool-is-just-reward-for-the-hard-working-french-u-21-star}}</ref> When he was 14, he left for Sochaux and joined their boarding academy at the age of 15.<ref>{{cite news|date=28 May 2021|title=Who is Ibrahima Konate?|publisher=Bundesliga|url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/ibrahima-konate-who-is-the-rb-leipzig-france-defender-upamecano-liverpool-10647|access-date=2 June 2021}}</ref> He started his career as a striker before moving to the defence.<ref name="espn_may2021"/>

===Sochaux===
Konaté made his professional debut for [[FC Sochaux-Montbéliard|Sochaux]] in a 1–0 [[Ligue 2]] loss to [[AJ Auxerre|Auxerre]] on 7 February 2017, at the age of 16.<ref>{{cite web|title=LFP.fr - Ligue de Football Professionnel - Domino's Ligue 2 - Saison 2016/2017 - 24ème journée - FC Sochaux-Montbéliard / AJ Auxerre|url=http://www.lfp.fr/ligue2/feuille_match/82410|website=www.lfp.fr}}</ref>

=== RB Leipzig ===
After a successful debut season, with 12 games and 1 goal in half a season, Konaté joined [[RB Leipzig]] in the [[Bundesliga]] on 12 June 2017 on a five-year contract by free transfer.<ref>{{cite web|title=RB Leipzig land French talent Ibrahima Konate - bundesliga.com|url=http://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/rb-leipzig-sign-french-defender-ibrahima-konate-sochaux-446529.jsp|website=bundesliga.com - the official Bundesliga website}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=RasenBallsport Leipzig - Neuzugang-Ibrahima-Konate|url=http://www.dierotenbullen.com/neuigkeiten/Saison_2016_17/Neuzugang-Ibrahima-Konate.html|website=redbulls}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|date=28 May 2021|title=Who is Ibrahima Konate?|publisher=Bundesliga|url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/ibrahima-konate-who-is-the-rb-leipzig-france-defender-upamecano-liverpool-10647|access-date=2 June 2021}}</ref> Konaté scored his first Leipzig goal in a 4–0 win against [[Fortuna Düsseldorf]].<ref>{{cite news|date=27 January 2019|title=Fortuna Dusseldorf v RB Leipzig Live Commentary & Result, 27/01/2019, Bundesliga|website=Goal.com|url=https://www.goal.com/en-gb/match/fortuna-d%C3%BCsseldorf-v-rb-leipzig/1ev3ryv0jlqkw2mcivtxtsz8q|access-date=2 June 2021}}</ref>

===Liverpool===
On 28 May 2021, [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] announced that they had reached an agreement with RB Leipzig to acquire Konaté on 1 July, pending international clearance and a work permit being granted.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/57242659 |date=28 May 2021|title=Liverpool agree deal for Konaté |website=BBC Sport |access-date=28 May 2021}}</ref> The club had already agreed personal terms with the player in April,<ref>{{cite news|title=Liverpool seal £36m deal for RB Leipzig defender Ibrahima Konaté|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2021/may/28/liverpool-seal-36m-deal-for-rb-leipzig-defender-ibrahima-konate|access-date=29 May 2021}}</ref> and triggered his release clause of approximately £36 million in May 2021.<ref>{{cite news |last=Reddy |first=Melissa |date=28 May 2021 |title=Ibrahima Konate: Liverpool close to sealing transfer after triggering RB Leipzig defender's release clause |newspaper=The Independent |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/liverpool-ibrahima-konate-transfer-leipzig-b1855680.html |access-date=29 May 2021}}</ref> On 18 September, Konaté made his Premier League debut, starting alongside [[Virgil van Dijk]], and kept a clean sheet in a 3–0 victory against [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Report: Mane, Salah and Keita goals see off Palace at Anfield.|url=https://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/first-team/443819-match-report-crystal-palace-premier-league|access-date=23 September 2021|publisher=Liverpool F.C.}}</ref> He made his second start of the season partnering Virgil van Dijk in the first [[Liverpool F.C.–Manchester United F.C. rivalry|North-West Derby]] of the season, in which Liverpool beat Manchester United 5–0. It was the heaviest defeat inflicted on United by Liverpool since 1895 and the heaviest defeat United had suffered without scoring in since 1955.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Jackson |first1=Jamie |title=Manchester United's Ole Gunnar Solskjær calls 5-0 thrashing 'darkest day' |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2021/oct/24/manchester-uniteds-ole-gunnar-solskjr-calls-5-0-thrashing-darkest-day |website=The Guardian |date=24 October 2021 |publisher=Guardian News & Media Limited |access-date=25 October 2021}}</ref> Konaté won plaudits from fans for how he handled star players like [[Cristiano Ronaldo]] and [[Bruno Fernandes]] and also featured in [[Garth Crooks]]' team of the week, with Crooks saying, "No frills or skills but he does use his power and strength to maximum effect. This was shown in match against Manchester United, [[Mason Greenwood]], Cristiano Ronaldo and [[Marcus Rashford]] hardly had a kick."<ref>{{cite news |last1=Crooks |first1=Garth |title=Garth Crooks' team of the week: Alisson, Keita, Mount, Salah, Foden |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/59031790 |access-date=25 October 2021}}</ref>

On 5 April 2022, Konaté scored his first goal for Liverpool, a header in a 3–1 away win against [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]] in the [[2021–22 UEFA Champions League knockout phase|UEFA Champions League]] quarter-final first leg.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/apr/05/benfica-liverpool-champions-league-quarter-final-first-leg-match-report|title=Luis Díaz returns to Portugal to haunt Benfica and seal Liverpool's first-leg win|last=Hunter|first=Andy|date=5 April 2022|work=The Guardian|access-date=6 April 2022}}</ref>

Konaté missed the beginning of the [[2022–23 Liverpool F.C. season|2022–23 season]] through injury.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Gorst |first=Paul |date=31 July 2022 |title=Ibrahima Konate injury explained as Keita and Jones absences addressed |url=https://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/ibrahima-konate-injury-explained-naby-24636392 |access-date=4 November 2022 |website=Liverpool Echo |language=en}}</ref>

==International career==
Konaté received his first call-up to the senior [[France national football team|France national team]] on 4 June 2022 for the [[2022–23 UEFA Nations League|UEFA Nations League]], replacing [[Raphaël Varane]] due to injury.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Liverpool FC - Ibrahima Konate called up by France |url=https://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/ibrahima-konate-called-france |access-date=4 June 2022 |website=www.liverpoolfc.com |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Husband |first=Ben |date=4 June 2022 |title=Konate handed first France call-up as Varane's suffers more injury woe |url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/football/news/raphael-varane-injury-ibrahima-konate-27146129 |access-date=4 June 2022 |website=mirror |language=en}}</ref> He made his full international debut on 10 June in a 1–1 draw against [[Austria national football team|Austria]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Austria 1 France 1 |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/61766082 |access-date=12 June 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Ibrahima Konate's season keeps getting better with senior debut for France |url=https://www.thisisanfield.com/2022/06/ibrahima-konates-season-keeps-getting-better-with-senior-debut-for-france/ |website=This Is Anfield |date=11 June 2022 |access-date=12 June 2022}}</ref> On 9 November, Konaté was included in [[Didier Deschamps]]'s squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], where he helped France reach the [[2022 FIFA World Cup Final|final]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Sears |first=Jack |date=9 November 2022 |title=Ibrahima Konate named in France squad for World Cup |url=https://www.thisisanfield.com/2022/11/ibrahima-konate-named-in-france-squad-for-world-cup/ |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=This Is Anfield}}</ref>

==Style of play==
[[File:2019-03-30 Fußball, Männer, 1. Bundesliga, RB Leipzig - Hertha BSC StP 3845 LR10 by Stepro.jpg|thumb|right|Konaté playing for RB Leipzig in 2019]]
Konaté has been compared to [[Virgil van Dijk]] due to his positional play, tackling, pace and strength.<ref>{{cite news|date=28 May 2021|title=Who is Ibrahima Konate?|publisher=Bundesliga|url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/ibrahima-konate-who-is-the-rb-leipzig-france-defender-upamecano-liverpool-10647|access-date=2 June 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|author=Alex Young|date=29 March 2021|title=Who is Ibrahima Konaté: Liverpool target is 'the new Virgil van Dijk' but has worrying injury record|newspaper=Evening Standard|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/who-is-ibrahima-konate-liverpool-fc-transfer-injury-record-stats-style-b926837.html|access-date=2 June 2021}}</ref> Guido Schafer said that: "he is tall, fast, good technique and he is a little bit of Virgil van Dijk. He has very high-class potential. He is a great athlete, he has good speed and there are no silly tackles - he is an intelligent player."<ref>{{cite news|author=Alex Young|date=29 March 2021|title=Who is Ibrahima Konaté: Liverpool target is 'the new Virgil van Dijk' but has worrying injury record|newspaper=Evening Standard|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/who-is-ibrahima-konate-liverpool-fc-transfer-injury-record-stats-style-b926837.html|access-date=2 June 2021}}</ref> Konaté is also well known for his passing and strength in the air.<ref>{{cite news|author=Alex Young|date=29 March 2021|title=Who is Ibrahima Konaté: Liverpool target is 'the new Virgil van Dijk' but has worrying injury record|newspaper=Evening Standard|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/who-is-ibrahima-konate-liverpool-fc-transfer-injury-record-stats-style-b926837.html|access-date=2 June 2021}}</ref>
