'''James Daniel Maddison''' (born 23 November 1996) is an English professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as an [[attacking midfielder]] for [[Premier League]] club [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] and the [[England national football team|England national team]].

Maddison began his career with [[Coventry City F.C.|Coventry City]] before joining [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]] in 2016. He spent the 2016–17 season on [[Loan (association football)|loan]] at [[Scottish Premiership]] club [[Aberdeen F.C.|Aberdeen]]. In his first season back at Norwich, Maddison was named in the Championship [[PFA Team of the Year]] and [[EFL Awards|EFL Team of the Season]]. He joined [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] in 2018.

Maddison was capped by [[England national under-21 football team|England at under-21 level]] from 2017 to 2019, and made his debut for the senior team in 2019. He was part of the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] squad.

==Early life==
Maddison was born and raised in [[Coventry]], West Midlands.<ref>{{cite web |title=James Maddison |url=http://www.thefa.com/england/mens-seniors/squad/james-maddison |publisher=The Football Association |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref> He has Irish ancestry through a grandparent.<ref>{{cite news |title=Leicester star James Maddison considering international switch to Republic of Ireland |url=https://www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/sport/football/international/leicester-star-james-maddison-considering-international-switch-to-republic-of-ireland-38432443.html |newspaper=Belfast Telegraph |date=24 August 2019 |access-date=11 March 2020}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Coventry City===
Maddison joined [[Coventry City F.C.|Coventry City]]'s youth team, and was included in the first-team squad in the [[2013–14 Coventry City F.C. season|2013–14 season]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.ccfc.co.uk/news/2013/october/pressley-stays-unchanged-for-stevenage/ |title=Pressley stays unchanged for Stevenage |first=Kieran |last=Crowley |publisher=Coventry City F.C. |date=5 October 2013 |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref> though did not make an appearance. He made his debut in August 2014, coming on as a [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] in a 2–1 [[EFL Cup|League Cup]] defeat to [[Cardiff City F.C.|Cardiff City]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.ccfc.co.uk/news/2014/august/match-report-coventry-city-1-2-cardiff-city/ |title=Match report: Coventry City 1–2 Cardiff City |first=Nick |last=Connoll |publisher=Coventry City F.C. |date=13 August 2014 |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref> Maddison made his league debut, again as a substitute, in a 3–1 home loss against [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]] and his first league start in the following game, against [[Oldham Athletic A.F.C.|Oldham Athletic]]. He scored his first league goal in the game, with a [[Free kick (association football)|free kick]] in the first half, as the Sky Blues lost the game 4–1.{{Citation needed|date=October 2018}}

In November 2014, Maddison signed his first professional contract, lasting three-and-a-half years,<ref>{{cite web |last=Joyce |first=Matt |title=Coventry City midfielder James Maddison signs a three-and-a-half-year deal with the club |url=https://www.ccfc.co.uk/news/2014/november/coventry-city-midfielder-james-maddison-signs-a-three-and-a-half-year-deal-with-the-club-/ |publisher=Coventry City F.C. |date=24 November 2014 |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref> but missed much of the rest of the [[2014–15 Coventry City F.C. season|2014–15 season]] after being sent off during a Boxing Day defeat to [[Doncaster Rovers F.C.|Doncaster Rovers]] and then picking up a back injury.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/30543582 |title=Doncaster Rovers 2–0 Coventry City |website=BBC Sport |date=26 December 2014 |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Turner |first=Andy |title=Sky Blues starlet James Maddison struggling to be fit for FA Youth Cup game at Manchester City |url=https://www.coventrytelegraph.net/sport/football/football-news/sky-blues-starlet-james-maddison-8440501 |newspaper=Coventry Telegraph |date=14 January 2015 |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref>

===Norwich City===
Maddison signed for [[Premier League]] club [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]] on 1 February 2016 on a three-and-a-half-year contract for an undisclosed fee, but was immediately [[Loan (association football)|loaned]] back to Coventry City for the remainder of the [[2015–16 Coventry City F.C. season|2015–16 season]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.canaries.co.uk/News/2016/february/transfer-news-city-sign-coventrys-james-maddison/ |title=Transfer news: City sign Coventry's James Maddison |publisher=Norwich City F.C. |date=1 February 2016 |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref>

Maddison made his first team debut for Norwich in an [[EFL Cup]] tie against his former club Coventry on 23 August 2016.<ref>{{cite news |title=Norwich 6–1 Coventry |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/37091247 |website=BBC Sport |date=23 August 2016 |access-date=29 March 2022}}</ref> On 31 August 2016, Maddison was loaned to [[Scottish Premiership]] club [[Aberdeen F.C.|Aberdeen]] for the first part of the [[2016–17 Aberdeen F.C. season|2016–17 season]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/37237269 |title=Norwich City midfielder James Maddison joins Aberdeen on loan |website=BBC Sport |date=31 August 2016 |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref> He made his debut as a substitute against [[Inverness Caledonian Thistle F.C.|Inverness Caledonian Thistle]], then started and scored in the next match in a 3–1 win against [[Dundee F.C.|Dundee]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Young |first=Chick |title=Dundee 1–3 Aberdeen |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/37331437 |website=BBC Sport |date=18 September 2016 |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref> He followed this with a last-minute winner against [[Rangers F.C.|Rangers]] on 25 September.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/sport/football/football-match-reports/aberdeen-2-rangers-1-james-8911476 |title=Aberdeen 2 Rangers 1: James Maddison hits perfect free-kick to win it for Dons – three things we learned |first=Gary |last=Ralston |newspaper=Daily Record |location=Glasgow |date=25 September 2016 |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref>

After returning from his loan spell at Aberdeen, Maddison made his long-awaited league debut for Norwich on 17 April 2017, coming on as a substitute in the second half and scoring in a 3–1 win against [[Preston North End F.C.|Preston North End]] at Deepdale. Maddison signed a new four-year contract in June.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.canaries.co.uk/News/2017/june/james-maddison-new-deal/ |title=James Maddison signs new deal with the Canaries |publisher=Norwich City F.C. |access-date=19 February 2019}}</ref> With the arrival of new coach [[Daniel Farke]], Maddison was given regular starts in the team during the [[2017–18 Norwich City F.C. season|2017–18 season]] and was named Norwich City's [[Norwich City F.C. Player of the Season|Player of the Season]] at the end of the campaign,<ref name=NorwichPOTY>{{cite web |url=https://www.canaries.co.uk/News/2018/april/madders-leeds/ |title=James Maddison: It's one of the best awards you can get |publisher=Norwich City F.C. |date=28 April 2016 |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref> as well as receiving a nomination for the EFL Championship Young Player of the Season award.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/43641225 |title=EFL awards: Tom Cairney, Ruben Neves & Ryan Sessgenon on Championship shortlist |website=BBC Sport |date=4 April 2018 |access-date=4 April 2018}}</ref>

===Leicester City===
[[File:James Maddison.jpg|thumb|upright|Maddison playing for [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] in 2019]]

Maddison signed for Premier League club [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] on 20 June 2018, on a five-year contract for an undisclosed fee thought to be around £20&nbsp;million.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44553234 |title=Leicester sign midfielder James Maddison from Norwich on five-year deal |website=BBC Sport |date=20 June 2018 |access-date=2 July 2018}}</ref> He scored his first goal in the Premier League on 18 August in a 2–0 win against [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45153216 |title=Leicester City 2–0 Wolverhampton Wanderers |first=Harry |last=Poole |website=BBC Sport |date=18 August 2018 |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref> He later followed up his impressive start for the club with back-to-back Premier League goals, from set-pieces, against [[AFC Bournemouth]] and [[Huddersfield Town A.F.C.|Huddersfield Town]], respectively.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45530720 |title=Leicester City 3–1 Huddersfield Town |first=Mantej |last=Mann |date=22 September 2018 |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref> On 29 July 2020, he agreed a new four-year contract with Leicester.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/53580180 |title=Maddison agrees new Leicester deal |work=BBC Sport }}</ref>

On 27 September 2020, Maddison scored a curler outside the box in a 5–2 win against [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] in the league which was later voted as [[Premier League Goal of the Month]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Man City 2–5 Leicester: Jamie Vardy scores hat-trick in stunning Foxes victory over Man City |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11661/12083580/man-city-2-5-leicester-jamie-vardy-scores-hat-trick-in-stunning-foxes-victory-over-man-city|access-date=5 March 2021 |website=Sky Sports}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Maddison strike voted September 2020's Budweiser Goal of the Month |url=http://www.premierleague.com/news/1857311|access-date=5 March 2021 |publisher=Premier League}}</ref>

On 11 April 2021, Maddison was one of three players dropped from Leicester's squad for the game against [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]] after breaching [[COVID-19]] protocols.<ref>{{cite news |title=James Maddison among Leicester players dropped for Covid-19 breach |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/56710648 |access-date=11 April 2021 |website=BBC Sport |date=11 April 2021}}</ref>

===Tottenham Hotspur===

On 28 June 2023, Maddison joined [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] for undisclosed fee, signing a five-year contract.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Hotspur |first1=Tottenham |title=Maddison signs from Leicester |url=https://www.tottenhamhotspur.com/news/2023/june/maddison-signs-from-leicester/ |website=tottenhamhotspur.com |access-date=28 June 2023}}</ref> The transfer fee was reported to be £40m.<ref>{{Cite news |date=28 June 2023 |title=Tottenham complete £40m Maddison transfer |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/66049249 |access-date=2023-06-28}}</ref>

==International career==
Maddison was included in an [[England national under-21 football team|England under-21]] squad in March 2016,<ref name=U21>{{cite web |url=http://www.thefa.com/news/2017/nov/02/england-u21s-squad-named-for-ukraine-021117 |title=The England U21s squad to face Ukraine in Kiev has been named by boss Aidy Boothroyd |first=Nicholas |last=Veevers |publisher=The Football Association |date=2 November 2017 |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref> but was unable to play due to injury.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thefa.com/news/2017/nov/07/james-maddison-on-england-u21-call-up-honour-071117 |title=England U21s newboy James Maddison gives us his story after first call-up to squad |first=James |last=Maddison |publisher=The Football Association |date=7 November 2017 |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref> He received his first call-up to the England under-21 team in November 2017,<ref name=U21/> and made his debut against [[Ukraine national under-21 football team|Ukraine]] in a [[2019 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification|2019 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualifier]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41884793 |title=Ukraine U21 0–2 England U21 |website=BBC Sport |date=10 November 2017 |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref> He received his first call-up to the [[England national football team|senior team]] in October 2018 for the [[UEFA Nations League]] matches against [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]] and [[Spain national football team|Spain]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45744731 |title=James Maddison, Jadon Sancho and Mason Mount named in England squad |website=BBC Sport |date=4 October 2018 |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref>

On 27 May 2019, Maddison was included in England's 23-man squad for the [[2019 UEFA European Under-21 Championship]]<ref>{{cite news |title=ENGLAND U21S SQUAD NAMED BY AIDY BOOTHROYD FOR THIS SUMMER'S EURO FINALS IN ITALY |url=http://www.thefa.com/news/2019/may/26/england-u21s-squad-named-for-euros-270519 |publisher=The Football Association |date=27 May 2019|access-date=19 June 2019}}</ref> and scored his first goal for his country during a 3–3 draw with [[Croatia national under-21 football team|Croatia]] at the [[San Marino Stadium]] on 24 June.<ref>{{cite news |title=England U21 3–3 Croatia U21 |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/48752768 |website=BBC Sport |date=24 June 2019|access-date=25 June 2019}}</ref>

Maddison withdrew from the senior squad in October 2019 due to illness.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49999097 |title=James Maddison: England and Leicester midfielder withdraws from Euro 2020 qualifiers squad |date=10 October 2019 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> He was later seen visiting a casino, although his conduct was defended by club manager [[Brendan Rodgers]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/50087592 |title=James Maddison: Leicester boss Brendan Rodgers backs midfielder after casino 'mistake' |date=17 October 2019 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> Maddison made his debut for England on 14 November when he came on for [[Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain]] in the 56th minute of a 7–0 win over [[Montenegro national football team|Montenegro]] in [[UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying]].<ref name=EnglandDebut>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/50327100 |title=England 7–0 Montenegro |first=Phil |last=McNulty |website=BBC Sport |date=14 November 2019 |access-date=14 November 2019}}</ref>

After a three-year absence, Maddison was named in the 26-man England squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63585869 |title=Maddison, Rashford and Wilson make England squad |work=BBC Sport }}</ref> However, he did not make an appearance in the tournament.<ref>{{Cite web |title=England Men Stats, FIFA World Cup |url=https://fbref.com/en/squads/1862c019/2022/s1711/England-Men-Stats |access-date=2023-06-28 |website=FBref.com |language=en}}</ref> He made his first start for England on 26 March 2023 in a [[UEFA Euro 2024 qualifying|UEFA Euro 2024 qualifier]] against [[Ukraine National Football Team|Ukraine]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.englandfootball.com/england/mens-senior-team/fixtures-results/2023/england-mens-ukraine-uefa-euro-qualifier-wembley-sunday-26-march-2023 |title=England v Ukraine - UEFA European Qualifier match centre|website=England Football|date=26 March 2023}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Maddison and his partner, Kennedy Alexa, have a son who was born in July 2021.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Footballer James Maddison cradles his newborn son Leo in adorable snap |url=https://dailyuknews.com/entertainment/footballer-james-maddison-cradles-his-newborn-son-leo-in-adorable-snap/|access-date=26 November 2021 |website=DUK News|date=18 August 2021 }}</ref> The pair also have a set of twins, born on 2 July 2023.
