'''Aleksandar Mitrović''' ({{lang-sr-Cyrl|Александар Митровић}}, {{IPA-sr|aleksǎːndar mǐtroʋitɕ|pron}}{{refn|group=note|The name Aleksandar in Serbian can also be pronounced {{IPA-sr|alěksaːndar|}}, but the pronunciation {{IPA-sr|aleksǎːndar|}} is the one most commonly used in Serbia.}} ({{Audio-nohelp|SR - Aleksandar Mitrovic.oga|listen}}); born 16 September 1994) is a Serbian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Striker (association football)|striker]] for {{English football updater|Fulham}} club [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]] and the [[Serbia national football team|Serbia national team]].

A youth product at [[FK Partizan|Partizan]], he turned professional after a loan at [[FK Teleoptik|Teleoptik]], and was a regular as they won the [[Serbian SuperLiga]] in his first season. At the age of 18, Mitrović was named among the top 10 talents under the age of 19 in Europe by a selection of [[UEFA]] reporters.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.uefa.com/under19/news/newsid=1978673.html |title=Ten Under-19 talents to watch |publisher=UEFA |date=2 August 2013 |access-date=5 August 2013}}</ref> He then joined [[R.S.C. Anderlecht|Anderlecht]] for a club record €5 million, and scored 44 goals in 90 games across all competitions in a two-season spell. He won the [[Belgian Pro League]] in his first campaign at the club, and was the [[Belgian Pro League top scorers|league's top scorer]] in his second. In 2015, he moved to [[Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United]] for £13 million. In 2018, he was loaned to [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]], and joined them permanently after helping them achieve promotion to the Premier League.

Mitrović helped [[Serbia national under-19 football team|Serbia]] win the [[2013 UEFA European Under-19 Championship|2013 European Under-19 Championship]], being voted the best player of the tournament. That same year, he played his first game for the senior national team and has since earned over 80 caps, representing Serbia at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup]]. He is Serbia's [[Serbia national football team#Top goalscorers|all-time record goalscorer]].<ref name="Irish Times ref">{{cite news |url=https://www.irishtimes.com/sport/soccer/mitrovic-sets-record-as-serbia-earn-draw-with-portugal-1.4522119#:~:text=Serbia%20striker%20Aleksandar%20Mitrovic%20became,Group%20A%20qualifier%20on%20Saturday. |author=The Irish Times |author-link=The Irish Times |title=Mitrovic sets record as Serbia earn draw with Portugal |newspaper=[[The Irish Times]] |access-date=3 April 2021 |date=27 March 2021}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Early years===
Born in [[Smederevo]], Mitrović came to [[FK Partizan|Partizan]] and went through the youth system of the club. Before being promoted to the first team, he made his senior debut with their affiliated side [[FK Teleoptik|Teleoptik]] in the [[2011–12 Serbian First League|2011–12 season]], scoring seven goals in 25 league matches.<ref name=Soccerway/>

===Partizan===
On 27 June 2012, Mitrović signed his first professional contract with Partizan, on a four-year contract.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.partizan.co.rs/35805-mlade-snage-nadolaze/ |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130416121703/http://www.partizan.co.rs/35805-mlade-snage-nadolaze/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=16 April 2013 |title=Mlade snage nadolaze |language=sr |publisher=FK Partizan |date=27 June 2012 |access-date=30 August 2012}}</ref> He made his official debut for the club in a [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] [[2012–13 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round#Second qualifying round|qualifier]] against Maltese side [[Valletta F.C.|Valletta]], scoring a goal nine minutes after coming on as a substitute.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2013/matches/round=2000344/match=2009436/index.html |title=Partizan ease past Valletta |publisher=UEFA |date=24 July 2012 |access-date=30 August 2012}}</ref> On 23 August 2012, Mitrović scored a header against [[Tromsø IL|Tromsø]] in the [[UEFA Europa League|Europa League]] [[2012–13 UEFA Europa League qualifying phase and play-off round#Play-off round|play-off round]]. Three days later, he scored his first [[Serbian SuperLiga|league]] goal in a home fixture against [[FK Jagodina|Jagodina]]. On 17 November 2012, Mitrović scored the opening goal in his first ever [[Eternal derby (Serbia)|Eternal derby]], which Partizan eventually lost 3–2. Five days later, he also scored in a 1–1 away draw with Azerbaijani side [[Neftçi PFK|Neftchi Baku]] during the Europa League [[2012–13 UEFA Europa League group stage#Group H|group stage]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2013/matches/round=2000356/match=2010002/index.html |title=Neftçi and Partizan inseparable again |publisher=UEFA |date=22 November 2012 |access-date=15 November 2013}}</ref> By the end of his debut season, Mitrović was Partizan's top scorer in all competitions with 15 goals in 36 appearances even though he was one of the youngest players of the team.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.mozzartsport.com/vesti/mitrovic-nadam-se-ostanku-u-partizanu-vecoj-minutazi-i-trofeju-najboljeg-strelca |title=Mitrović: Nadam se ostanku u Partizanu, većoj minutaži i trofeju najboljeg strelca |language=sr |website=Mozzart Sport |date=27 May 2013 |access-date=31 May 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130609065115/http://www.mozzartsport.com/vesti/mitrovic-nadam-se-ostanku-u-partizanu-vecoj-minutazi-i-trofeju-najboljeg-strelca |archive-date=9 June 2013 |url-status=dead}}</ref> Due to his displays, he earned a place in Jelen SuperLiga's Team of the Season selection.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.mozzartsport.com/vesti/idealnih-11-superlige-partizan-pet-vosa-tri-zvezda-hajduk-i-srem-po-jednog-predstavnika |title=Idealnih 11 Superlige: Partizan pet, Voša tri, Zvezda, Hajduk i Srem po jednog predstavnika! ===Anderlecht===
[[File:Aleksandar Mitrović'14.JPG|thumb|upright|Mitrović playing for [[R.S.C. Anderlecht|Anderlecht]] in 2014]]

On 12 August 2013, after much speculation, it was announced that Mitrović was sold to [[R.S.C. Anderlecht|Anderlecht]] and that he would join the Belgian club on 30 August at the request of the player and his family.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sr.partizan.rs/69455-69455/ |title=Saopštenje FK Partizan |language=sr |publisher=FK Partizan |date=12 August 2013 |access-date=15 November 2013 |archive-date=8 December 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141208190430/http://www.sr.partizan.rs/69455-69455/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> The transfer fee was €5&nbsp;million,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sr.partizan.rs/69504-69504/ |title=Saopštenje FK Partizan |language=sr |publisher=FK Partizan |date=13 August 2013 |access-date=17 August 2013 |archive-date=16 August 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130816135803/http://www.sr.partizan.rs/69504-69504 |url-status=dead }}</ref> which is Anderlecht's record signing fee. On 1 September, Mitrović provided two assists on his debut for the club after coming on as a substitute at the beginning of the second half in a league match against [[S.V. Zulte Waregem|Zulte Waregem]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.goal.com/en-gb/match/zulte-waregem-v-anderlecht/1umvcr1usr426j02xonhva31n|title=Zulte Waregem 4–3 Anderlecht|website=Goal.com |date=1 September 2013 |access-date=29 September 2021}}</ref>

On 10 December 2013, in the last round of the [[2013–14 UEFA Champions League group stage#Group C|Champions League Group C]] against [[Olympiacos F.C.|Olympiacos]], Mitrović replaced goalkeeper [[Silvio Proto]] who had been sent off,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/match/olympiakos-piraeus-vs-anderlecht/1584542/report |title=Olympiakos 3–1 Anderlecht: Saviola double sends Greek champions through |website=Goal.com |date=10 December 2013 |access-date=15 May 2016}}</ref> but failed to save a penalty from [[Alejandro Domínguez (footballer, born 1981)|Alejandro Domínguez]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2014/matches/round=2000479/match=2011852/postmatch/report/ |title=Saviola the hero as Olympiacos squeeze through |publisher=UEFA |date=10 December 2013 |access-date=15 May 2015}}</ref> Mitrović ended his debut season in Belgium with 16 league goals as Anderlecht won their [[List of Belgian football champions|33rd league title]].<ref name=sky>{{cite news |url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/11678/9916019/aleksandar-mitrovic-we-profile-the-newcastle-and-chelsea-transfer-target |title=Aleksandar Mitrovic: We profile the striker after he joins Newcastle |website=Sky Sports |date=21 July 2015}}</ref>

Mitrović began the [[2014–15 Belgian Pro League|2014–15 season]] by scoring in Anderlecht's 2–1 [[2014 Belgian Super Cup|Super Cup]] defeat of [[K.S.C. Lokeren Oost-Vlaanderen|K.S.C. Lokeren]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.rtbf.be/sport/football/belgique/jupilerproleague/detail_anderlecht-lokeren-supercoupe-le-resume-video-en-direct-live?id=8318869 |title=Anderlecht soulève sa douzième Supercoupe face à Lokeren |language=fr |publisher=RTBF |date=20 July 2014}}</ref> On 5 November 2014, in the [[2014–15 UEFA Champions League group stage#Group D|Champions League Group D]] against [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]], he scored the equaliser in the 90th minute, completing Anderlecht's comeback from 3–0 to 3–3.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/nov/04/arsenal-anderlecht-champions-league-match-report |title=Arsenal throw away three-goal lead against Anderlecht |newspaper=The Guardian |date=5 November 2014 |access-date=5 November 2014}}</ref> Overall, he scored 20 goals in the Pro League, making him the competition's top scorer, and 28 in all competitions.<ref name=sky/> On 22 March 2015, he scored the team's only goal in the 2–1 [[2015 Belgian Cup Final|Belgian Cup Final]] loss to [[Club Brugge KV|Club Brugge]] in Brussels.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://fr.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/news/newsid=2226748.html |title=Bruges arrache la Coupe de Belgique |language=fr |date=22 March 2015 |publisher=UEFA}}</ref>

===Fulham===
On 1 February 2018, Mitrović joined [[EFL Championship|Championship]] club [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]] on loan until the end of the season.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/42898336 |title=Fulham: Middlesbrough's Cyrus Christie and Newcastle's Aleksandar Mitrovic join |website=BBC Sport |date=1 February 2018 |access-date=11 February 2018}}</ref> In the build up to deadline day, Mitrović was poised to join [[FC Girondins de Bordeaux|Bordeaux]] and then former club Anderlecht, but both moves broke down, and after a discussion with their manager, the fellow Serb [[Slaviša Jokanović]] on [[Snapchat]], he decided to join Fulham.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/no-food-four-clubs-and-two-jets-mission-impossible-on-deadline-day-for-aleksandar-mitrovic-0hbkfv9cc |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180208225020/https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/no-food-four-clubs-and-two-jets-mission-impossible-on-deadline-day-for-aleksandar-mitrovic-0hbkfv9cc |url-status=dead |archive-date=8 February 2018 |title=No food, four clubs and two jets...mission impossible on deadline day for Aleksandar Mitrovic |first=Alyson |last=Rudd |author-link=Alyson Rudd |newspaper=The Times |date=8 February 2018 |access-date=11 February 2018 |url-access=subscription }}</ref> On 3 February, Mitrović made his debut for the West London club in a 2–0 win over [[Nottingham Forest F.C.|Nottingham Forest]], and almost scored, but his headed effort was cleared off the line by [[Joe Worrall (footballer)|Joe Worrall]]. On 21 February, Mitrović scored his first goal for the club in a 1–1 draw with [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/43053718 |title=Bristol 1–1 Fulham |website=BBC Sport |date=21 February 2018 |access-date=24 June 2018}}</ref> kickstarting a run of form which saw him score six goals in four matches. In the six matches played by Fulham in April, Mitrović scored five times, including winning goals against [[Sheffield Wednesday F.C.|Sheffield Wednesday]] and [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/43602110 |title=Sheffield Wednesday 0–1 Fulham |website=BBC Sport |date=7 April 2018 |access-date=24 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/43833102 |title=Fulham 2–1 Sunderland |website=BBC Sport |date=27 April 2018 |access-date=27 April 2018}}</ref> Due to his prolific run of form in March and April, he was the back-to-back Championship Player of the Month.<ref name="potm3">{{cite news |url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/11315527/cardiff-boss-neil-warnock-and-fulham-striker-aleksandar-mitrovic-win-sky-bet-championship-march-awards |title=Cardiff boss Neil Warnock and Fulham striker Aleksandar Mitrovic win Sky Bet Championship March awards |website=Sky Sports |date=6 April 2018 |access-date=6 April 2018}}</ref><ref name="potm4">{{cite web |url=https://www.efl.com/news/2018/may/player-of-the-month-aleksandar-mitrovic-fulham/ |title=Player of the Month: Aleksandar Mitrovic – Fulham |publisher=English Football League |date=4 May 2018}}</ref>

Mitrović finished the season with 12 goals, four behind top scorer [[Ryan Sessegnon]], as Fulham missed out on automatic promotion on the final day, losing 3–1 to Birmingham City; their first league loss of the calendar year.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/43938848 |title=Birmingham City 3–1 Fulham |website=BBC Sport |date=6 May 2018 |access-date=24 June 2018}}</ref> The goal against Sunderland would prove to be his last for the club in his loan spell, as he did not score in the play-offs, but he did start in the [[2018 EFL Championship play-off Final|play-off final]], with Fulham beating Aston Villa 1–0.<ref name="playoff final">{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44178427 |title=Aston Villa 0–1 Fulham |first=Phil |last=Cartwright |website=BBC Sport |date=26 May 2018 |access-date=26 May 2018}}</ref>

In a 7–0 win against [[Luton Town F.C.|Luton Town]] on 2 May, Mitrović scored two goals to equal and then break the record of [[Guy Whittingham]] for the highest scoring English league season in the current 46 game format by scoring 43 goals in a season. ==International career==
===Youth===
With four goals, Mitrović was the top scorer of the [[Serbia national under-19 football team|Serbian national under-19 team]] in their successful qualifying campaign for the [[2012 UEFA European Under-19 Championship]]. On 3 July 2012, the opening day of the final tournament, he was sent off during a match against [[France national under-19 football team|France U19]], which caused him to miss the rest of the competition due to suspension. On 26 March 2013, Mitrović scored two goals in a friendly for [[Serbia national under-21 football team|Serbia's U21 team]] against [[Bulgaria national under-21 football team|Bulgaria U21]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://sport.blic.rs/Fudbal/Evropski-fudbal/230473/Dva-gola-Mitrovica-za-pobedu-orlica-protiv-Bugarske |title=Dva gola Mitrovića za pobedu "orlića" protiv Bugarske |language=sr |newspaper=Blic |date=26 March 2013 |access-date=5 June 2013}}</ref>
