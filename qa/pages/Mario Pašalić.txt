'''Mario Pašalić''' ({{IPA-sh|mâːrio pǎʃalitɕ|hr}};<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hjp.znanje.hr/index.php?show=search_by_id&id=e1xhXRU%3D|title=Màrija|quote=Mȃrio|website=Hrvatski jezični portal|access-date=17 March 2018|language=sh}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://hjp.znanje.hr/index.php?show=search_by_id&id=eV9nXhA%3D&keyword=pa%C5%A1a|title=pȁša<sup>2</sup>|quote=Pašàlīć|website=Hrvatski jezični portal|access-date=17 March 2018|language=sh}}</ref> born 9 February 1995) is a Croatian professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for {{Italian football updater|Atalanta}} side [[Atalanta B.C.|Atalanta]] and the [[Croatia national football team|Croatia national team]].

He began his career at [[HNK Hajduk Split|Hajduk Split]] before moving to [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] for a reported £3&nbsp;million in 2014. Chelsea loaned him to [[Elche CF|Elche]], [[AS Monaco FC|Monaco]], [[A.C. Milan|Milan]], [[FC Spartak Moscow|Spartak Moscow]] and then Atalanta. Following two seasons at the [[Bergamo|Bergamasque]] club between [[2018–19 Atalanta B.C. season|2018]] and [[2019–20 Atalanta B.C. season|2020]], he was signed permanently ahead of the [[2020–21 Atalanta B.C. season|2020–21]] season.

Pašalić made his senior international debut for [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]] in 2014, representing the side at [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Club career==

===Hajduk Split===
Pašalić started playing football at county club NK GOŠK Kaštel Gomilica before joining the [[HNK Hajduk Split|Hajduk Split]]'s youth academy in 2006. A youth international, he first gained wider recognition in the 2011–12 season, scoring 17 goals for the under-17s, despite being a midfielder, and scoring the winner for the under-19s in a 3–2 success against [[NK Hrvatski Dragovoljac|Hrvatski Dragovoljac]], which secured the club's championship trophy.

During the pre-season, however, Pašalić was diagnosed with a [[staphylococcus]] infection, being sidelined for the first half of the [[2012–13 HNK Hajduk Split season|2012–13 campaign]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.dalmacijanews.com/Hajduk/View/tabid/88/ID/108416/Novi-pocetak-Junak-titule-Hajdukovih-juniora-Mario-Pasalic-vraca-se-nakon-teske-bolesti.aspx|title=Hajdukovih juniora Mario Pašalić vraća se nakon teške bolesti|last=Duplančić|first=Blaž|date=5 January 2013|work=Dalmacija News|language=hr|access-date=21 April 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130108105325/http://www.dalmacijanews.com/Hajduk/View/tabid/88/ID/108416/Novi-pocetak-Junak-titule-Hajdukovih-juniora-Mario-Pasalic-vraca-se-nakon-teske-bolesti.aspx|archive-date=8 January 2013}}</ref> He played his first match as a professional on 14 April 2013 [[Substitute (association football)|replacing]] [[Ivan Vuković]] in the 90th minute of a 2–1 home success against [[HNK Cibalia|Cibalia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sportcom.hr/sport/nogomet/domaci-nogomet/maxtv-prva-liga/maxtv-prva-liga-hajduk-pobijedio-pogotkom-iz-zale%C4%91a-lokomotiva-postignula-pet-pogodaka-u-nastavku.html|title=Hajduk pobijedio pogotkom iz zaleđa, petarda lokosa|language=hr|publisher=SportCom|date=14 April 2013|access-date=28 July 2014}}</ref>

In summer 2013, Pašalić signed a four-year professional contract with Hajduk and went on to make 36 appearances in all competitions during the [[2013–14 HNK Hajduk Split season|2013–14 season]], scoring 11 goals and assisting a further five. On 14 September 2013, he scored two goals in a [[Eternal derby (Croatia)|derby]] 2–0 victory against [[GNK Dinamo Zagreb|Dinamo Zagreb]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sportnet.hr/vijesti/466158/nogomet-maxtv-prva-liga/video-hajduk-pobijedio-dinamo-na-poljudu-mario-pasalic-junak-derbija/|title=Video: Hajduk pobijedio Dinamo na Poljudu, Mario Pašalić junak derbija|language=hr|publisher=Sportnet|date=14 September 2013|access-date=28 July 2014}}</ref> and repeated the same fate on 8 February the following year, in a 2–1 win at city rivals [[RNK Split]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hrsport.net/Vijest.aspx?ID=470477|title=Video: Mario Pašalić s dva pogotka u samoj završnici donio preokret Hajduku protiv Splita|language=hr|publisher=HRSport|date=8 February 2014|access-date=28 July 2014}}</ref>

===Chelsea===
On 9 July 2014, [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] announced the signing of Pašalić for an undisclosed fee.<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/28232501 | title=Chelsea sign Croatian teenager Pasalic | work=BBC Sport }}</ref> Upon signing, Pašalić said, "I am very happy because I am now a Chelsea player."<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.chelseafc.com/news/latest-news/2014/07/pasalic-signs.html|title=Pasalic signs|date=9 July 2014|work=Chelsea Official site|access-date=9 July 2014}}</ref>

====Loan to Elche====
On 22 July 2014, Pašalić joined [[Elche CF|Elche]] on a season-long loan deal.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.chelseafc.com/news/latest-news/2014/07/spanish-loan-for-pasalic.html|title=Spanish loan for Pasalic|date=22 July 2014|work=Chelsea Official site|access-date=23 July 2014}}</ref> He made his [[La Liga]] debut on 25 August, starting and being [[Penalty card#Yellow card|booked]] in a 0–3 away loss against [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.espnfc.com/spanish-primera-division/match/402623/barcelona-elche/report|title=Enrique off to a winning start|publisher=[[ESPN FC]]|date=24 August 2014|access-date=30 April 2015}}</ref> On 12 April 2015, Pašalić scored his first goal in the [[Spanish football league system|top flight of Spanish football]], netting the last in a 2–0 win over [[Córdoba CF|Córdoba]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.espnfc.com/spanish-primera-division/match/402324/cordoba-elche/report|title=Cordoba 0–2 Elche|publisher=ESPN FC|date=12 April 2015|access-date=30 April 2015}}</ref> On 29 April 2015, Pašalić scored in a 4–0 victory over [[Deportivo de La Coruña]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2015/04/29/spain/primera-division/elche-club-de-futbol/real-club-deportivo-de-la-coruna/1821995/|title=Elche 4–0 Deportivo de La Coruña|publisher=Soccerway|date=29 April 2015|access-date=6 July 2015}}</ref> On 3 May 2015, he scored a late goal which turned out to be the winner in the match against [[Málaga CF|Málaga]] in a 2–1 victory.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2015/05/03/spain/primera-division/malaga-club-de-futbol/elche-club-de-futbol/1822015/|title=Málaga 1–2 Elche|publisher=Soccerway|date=3 May 2015|access-date=6 July 2015}}</ref>

So far he has featured in to pre-season games for Monaco F.C.|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/33384795 |publisher=BBC Sport (British Broadcasting Corporation)|date=3 July 2015|access-date=3 July 2015}}</ref> During an interview about his loan move to Monaco, Pašalić stated that staying at Chelsea this season was never an option and that he "needs to improve, grow and then hope to get a chance with Chelsea".<ref>{{cite news|title=Pasalic: Monaco move can boost my Chelsea chances|url=http://www.goal.com/en-us/news/69/transfer-zone/2015/07/03/13276822/pasalic-monaco-move-can-boost-my-chelsea-chances|publisher=Goal|date=3 July 2015|access-date=6 July 2015}}</ref>

On 28 July, Pašalić made his Monaco debut in a 3–1 victory against Swiss side [[BSC Young Boys|Young Boys]] in the [[2015–16 UEFA Champions League|UEFA Champions League]] [[2015–16 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round#Third qualifying round|third qualifying round]], in which he scored the final goal to make it 3–1.<ref>{{Cite news|title=UEFA Champions League 2016 - Young Boys-Monaco — UEFA.com|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2016/matches/round=2000632/match=2015626/index.html|publisher=UEFA|date=28 July 2015}}</ref> On 8 August, Pašalić made his [[Ligue 1]] debut after playing 25 minutes in a 2–1 win over [[OGC Nice|Nice]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Chelsea here Chelsea there|url=https://www.chelseafc.com/news/latest-news/2015/08/chelsea-here-chelsea-there0.html|work=Chelsea Official site|access-date=10 August 2015}}</ref> On 19 August, Pašalić scored against [[Valencia CF|Valencia]], but it was not enough to stop Monaco from losing 3–1 in the first leg of the [[2015–16 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round#Play-off round|Champions League play-off]].<ref>{{Cite news|title=Valencia 3–1 Monaco: Rodrigo & Co. pick apart wasteful visitors|url=http://www.goal.com/en/match/valencia-vs-monaco/2134112/reportl|publisher=Goal|date=19 August 2015}}</ref> On 17 September, he made his [[2015–16 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]] debut in a 1–1 away draw with [[R.S.C. ====Loan to Milan====

On 2 April, Pašalić scored the equalizer in Milan's 1–1 away draw with Pescara.

====Loan to Spartak Moscow====

Lazio|Lazio]], in which he scored Atalanta's only goal.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Gabelić|first=Tomislav|date=17 December 2020|title=Pašalić operiran: Pritiskalo mi je živac i baš mi stvaralo bol...|url=https://www.24sata.hr/sport/pasalic-operiran-na-teren-bi-se-trebao-vratiti-za-mjesec-dana-734894|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=1 February 2021|website=24sata|language=hr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Hina|date=31 January 2021|title=Video: Jurić i Kalinić poraženi u Rimu, pogledajte pogodak kojim se Pašalić vratio nakon duže pauze|url=https://sportske.jutarnji.hr/sn/nogomet/serie-a/video-juric-i-kalinic-porazeni-u-rimu-pogledajte-pogodak-kojim-se-pasalic-vratio-nakon-duze-pauze-15047002|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=1 February 2021|website=Sportske novosti|language=hr-hr}}</ref>

==International career==
On 14 May 2014, Pašalić was on [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]] manager [[Niko Kovač]]'s 30-man list that was named to participate in the [[2014 FIFA World Cup|2014 World Cup]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/jul/09/mario-pasalic-signs-chelsea-hajduk-split|title = Chelsea sign Croatian teenage midfielder Mario Pasalic|website = [[TheGuardian.com]]|date = 9 July 2014}}</ref> He was not, however, selected for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup squads#Croatia|final squad]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Pasalic loan move to Monaco|url=http://www.chelseafc.com/news/latest-news/2015/07/pasalic-loan-to-monaco.html|work=Chelsea Official site}}</ref> He made his debut for Croatia on 4 September 2014 when he replaced [[Mario Mandžukić]] in a friendly home victory over [[Cyprus national football team|Cyprus]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Mario Pašalić Profile|url=http://www.chelseafc.com/teams/on-loan-players/mario-pasalic.html|work=Chelsea Official site}}</ref>

In May 2018, he was named in [[Zlatko Dalić]]'s preliminary 32-man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia but did not make the [[2018 FIFA World Cup squads#Croatia|final 23]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://hns-cff.hr/en/news/18052/head-coach-dalic-presents-24-man-croatia-squad/ |title=Head coach Dalić presents 24-man Croatia squad |website=hns-cff.hr |publisher=Croatian Football Federation |date=21 May 2018 |access-date=21 May 2018}}</ref>

He scored his debut goal for the national team on 7 October 2020 in a friendly 2–1 victory over [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Olivari|first=Davorin|date=7 October 2020|title=Hrvatska bez pola prve momčadi srušila Švicarsku! Zablistao 'Dalićev eksperiment', na kraju ušao i Modrić|url=https://sportske.jutarnji.hr/sn/nogomet/reprezentacija/hrvatska-bez-pola-prve-momcadi-srusila-svicarsku-zablistao-dalicev-eksperiment-na-kraju-usao-i-modric-15023796|website=Sportske novosti|language=hr-hr}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Pašalić was born in [[Mainz]], Germany, but grew up in [[Kaštel Gomilica]], Croatia. His father Ivan is a [[Croats of Bosnia and Herzegovina|Bosnian Croat]] from [[Sarajlije]] near [[Tomislavgrad]]. His mother Slavica hails from [[Vrlika]]. They both moved to Germany due to the [[Yugoslav Wars]]. He is a cousin of fellow footballer [[Ivan Krstanović]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=PSD|date=16 September 2013|title=Mario Pašalić: Kad se samo sjetim da sam prije godinu dana ležao u bolnici...|url=https://slobodnadalmacija.hr/sport/hajduk/mario-pasalic-kad-se-samo-sjetim-da-sam-prije-godinu-dana-lezao-u-bolnici-212347|url-status=live|access-date=26 February 2021|website=[[Slobodna Dalmacija]]|language=hr-hr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=11 July 2020|title=Mario Pašalić dobio mural u Tomislavgradu|url=https://www.bljesak.info/sport/nogomet/mario-pasalic-dobio-mural-u-tomislavgradu/317539|url-status=live|access-date=26 February 2021|website=Bljesak.info|language=hr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=|date=15 September 2013|title=Mario Pašalić, Hajdukova 8, porijeklom je s Duvanjskog polja! Zar ste sumnjali?|url=https://www.tomislavnews.com/mario-pasalic-hajdukova-8-porijeklom-je-s-duvanjskog-polja-zar-ste-sumnjali/|url-status=live|access-date=26 February 2021|website=Tomislav News|language=hr}}</ref>

In June 2019, Pašalić married his longtime girlfriend Marija Grbeša on the [[List of islands of Croatia|island]] of [[Čiovo]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=I. M.|date=17 June 2019|title=Glamurozno vjenčanje na domaćem terenu: Hrvatski reprezentativac oženio se dugogodišnjom ljubavi!|url=https://dnevnik.hr/showbuzz/celebrity/mario-pasalic-je-ozenio-djevojku-mariju-grbesu---564989.html|url-status=live|access-date=26 February 2021|website=[[Dnevnik Nove TV|Dnevnik.hr]]|language=hr}}</ref> In February 2022, Pašalić and Grbeša became parents of a baby boy, whom they named Luka.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://gol.dnevnik.hr/clanak/rubrika/sp-2022/pasalic-otkrio-o-cemu-je-pricao-s-vlasicem-oko-izvodjenja-11-eraca-obojica-smo-se-slozili---756218.html |title=Pašalić otkrio o čemu je pričao s Vlašićem oko izvođenja 11-eraca: "Obojica smo se složili" |website=[[Dnevnik Nove TV|Gol.hr]] |date=12 December 2022 |access-date=12 December 2022 |lang=hr |author=HINA |author-link=Croatian News Agency}}</ref>
