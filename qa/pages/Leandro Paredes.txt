'''Leandro Daniel Paredes''' ({{IPA-es|leˈandɾo paˈɾeðes}}; born 29 June 1994) is an Argentine professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[defensive midfielder]] for [[Ligue 1]] club [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] and the [[Argentina national football team|Argentina national team]]. He was part of the squad that won the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

== Club career ==
=== Boca Juniors ===
Paredes made his league debut for [[Boca Juniors]] in a 2–0 away defeat to [[Argentinos Juniors]] on 6 November 2010. On 29 January 2014, he joined Italian [[Serie A]] outfit [[A.C. Chievo Verona|Chievo Verona]]  for the rest of the 2013–14 season.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chievoverona.it/en/news/leandro-paredes-coming-chievoverona|title=Leandro Paredes coming to ChievoVerona|publisher=A.C. ChievoVerona|access-date=10 February 2014|date=29 January 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140222032503/http://www.chievoverona.it/en/news/leandro-paredes-coming-chievoverona|archive-date=22 February 2014}}</ref>

=== Roma ===
It was revealed that [[A.S. Roma|Roma]] agreed to sign Paredes from [[Boca Juniors]] on loan with a reported €4.5&nbsp;million pre-set price, however Roma ran out of non-EU registration quota for international transfer. He was signed by Chievo instead.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.larena.it/stories/556_chievo/644803_paredes_il_boca_esperienza_bellissima/|title= Paredes "Il Boca? Esperienza bellissima" |date=12 February 2014|access-date=21 July 2014|work=L'Arena|language=it}}</ref>
[[File:Leandro Paredes in 2014 (cropped).jpg|left|thumb|Paredes with [[AS Roma|Roma]] in 2014]]
On 19 July 2014, Paredes joined Roma in a temporary deal, with an option to buy outright from Boca Juniors.<ref>{{cite news|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140729031636/http://www.asroma.it/pdf/corporate/operazioni_di_mercato/2014-07-19-Paredes.pdf|archive-date=29 July 2014|url=http://www.asroma.it/pdf/corporate/operazioni_di_mercato/2014-07-19-Paredes.pdf|title=OPERAZIONI DI MERCATO LEANDRO DANIEL PAREDES|date=19 July 2014|access-date=21 July 2014|publisher=AS Roma|language=it}}</ref> On 27 September 2014, Paredes made his Roma debut, as a second-half substitute in the 2–0 win against [[Hellas Verona F.C.|Hellas Verona]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2014/09/27/italy/serie-a/as-roma/hellas-verona-fc/1836191/|title=ROMA VS. HELLAS VERONA 2 - 0
|date=27 September 2014|access-date=8 February 2015|publisher=Soccerway}}</ref> On 8 February 2015, Paredes scored his first Roma goal in the club's 2–1 victory against [[Cagliari Calcio|Cagliari]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2015/02/08/italy/serie-a/cagliari-calcio/as-roma/1836531/|title=CAGLIARI VS. ROMA 1 - 2
|date=8 February 2015|access-date=8 February 2015|publisher=Soccerway}}</ref> In June 2015, Roma signed Paredes for €6.067 million.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://res.cloudinary.com/as-roma-turbine-sandbox/image/upload/v1448016420/asroma-uat/yj1gskyc3yxchsj469wr.pdf|title=Relazione Finanzaria Annuale Al 30 Giugno 2015 |date=27 October 2015|access-date=25 March 2016|publisher=A.S. Roma|language=it}}</ref>

=== Zenit Saint Petersburg ===
On 1 July 2017, [[FC Zenit Saint Petersburg|Zenit]] announced the signing of Paredes on a four-year contract. The fee was €23 million with a possible €4 million in add-ons.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.fc-zenit.ru/news/2017-07-01-leandro-paredes-prodolzhit-kareru-v-zenite.htm|title=Leandro Paredes joins Zenit|date=1 July 2017|access-date=1 July 2017|publisher=Zenit}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.asroma.com/it/notizie/2017/06/paredes-ceduto-a-titolo-definitivo-al-football-club-zenit|title=Paredes sold outright to FC Zenit|date=1 July 2017|access-date=1 July 2017|publisher=AS Roma|language=it}}</ref>

=== Paris Saint-Germain ===
On 29 January 2019, [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] announced the signing of Paredes on a four and-a-half year contract,<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]]|url=https://www.psg.fr/equipes/equipe-premiere/content/leandro-paredes-au-paris-saint-germain|title=Leandro Paredes au Paris Saint-Germain|trans-title=Leandro Paredes at Paris Saint-Germain|date=29 January 2019|language=fr}}</ref> for an initial €40 million, potentially rising to €47 million.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/soccer-transfers/story/3762006/paris-saint-germain-confirm-leandro-paredes-signing-from-zenit-saint-petersburg|title=PSG confirm Paredes signing from Zenit|date=29 January 2019|website=ESPN.com|language=en|access-date=29 January 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://tech2.org/russia/zenit-sells-leandro-paredes-for-40-million-euros-what-is-known-about-a-possible-agreement-with-psg-or-chelsea-russian-championship-2018-2019-football/|title=Zenit sells Leandro Paredes for 40 million euros. What is known about a possible agreement with PSG or Chelsea|website=tech2.org|publisher=Tech2|date=24 January 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190425040928/https://tech2.org/russia/zenit-sells-leandro-paredes-for-40-million-euros-what-is-known-about-a-possible-agreement-with-psg-or-chelsea-russian-championship-2018-2019-football/|archive-date=25 April 2019}}</ref> He scored his first goal for the club in a 2–0 victory against [[Pau FC|Pau]] in the [[Coupe de France]] on 29 January 2020, exactly a year after signing for PSG, while wearing the [[Captain (association football)|captain]]'s armband.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Nemer|first=Roy|date=29 January 2020|title=Leandro Paredes scores, Mauro Icardi assist in PSG win|url=https://mundoalbiceleste.com/2020/01/29/leandro-paredes-scores-mauro-icardi-assist-in-psg-win/|access-date=1 September 2020|website=mundoalbiceleste.com}}</ref>

On 10 April 2021, Paredes scored his first [[Ligue 1]] goal, a free-kick in a 4–1 win over [[RC Strasbourg Alsace|Strasbourg]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|date=10 April 2021|title=Video: Paredes Scores Incredible Free Kick From Long Distance Against Strasbourg|url=https://psgtalk.com/2021/04/video-paredes-scores-incredible-free-kick-from-long-distance-against-strasbourg/|url-status=live|access-date=10 April 2021|website=PSG Talk}}</ref>

==== Loan to Juventus ====

On 31 August 2022, Paredes joined Serie A club [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]] on a season-long loan with an option to buy.<ref>{{cite web |date=31 August 2022 |title=Leandro Paredes joins Juventus |url=https://en.psg.fr/teams/first-team/content/leandro-paredes-joins-juventus-mercato-psg |access-date=31 August 2022 |publisher=Paris Saint-Germain F.C.}}</ref>

==International career==
On 19 May 2017, Paredes received his first senior call-up by newly appointed coach [[Jorge Sampaoli]] for [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]]'s friendlies against [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]] and [[Singapore national football team|Singapore]] in June.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/8/main/2017/05/19/35665212/icardis-argentina-exile-over-but-aguero-misses-out|title=Icardi's Argentina exile over, but Aguero misses out|publisher=Goal.com|author=Jamie Smith|date=19 May 2017|access-date=7 June 2017}}</ref> He made his senior international debut in the match against Singapore on 13 June, helping Argentina to a 6–0 away win, and also marked his debut by scoring his first international goal.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/sg/news/singapore-0-argentina-6-dream-debut-gomez-sampaolis-bold-experiment|title=Singapore 0 Argentina 6: Dream debut for Gomez in Sampaoli's bold experiment|work=FourFourTwo|language=en-SG|date=13 June 2017|access-date=13 June 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170613155549/https://www.fourfourtwo.com/sg/news/singapore-0-argentina-6-dream-debut-gomez-sampaolis-bold-experiment|archive-date=13 June 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In May 2018, he was named in Argentina’s preliminary 35-man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia<ref>{{cite web |title=Brazil, Argentina, and Mexico unveil preliminary squads |url=https://www.fifa.com/tournaments/mens/worldcup/2018russia/news/brazil-argentina-and-mexico-unveil-preliminary-squads |website=FIFA |access-date=29 October 2022 |date=14 May 2018}}</ref> but did not make the final 23.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/icardi-cut-from-argentinas-23-man-world-cup-squad/lo0f6fig55ih1jh7w9v4enau6 |title=Icardi cut from Argentina's 23-man World Cup squad |website=[[Goal (website)|Goal]] |publisher=[[Perform Group]] |date=21 May 2018 |access-date=21 May 2018}}</ref> On 21 May 2019, Paredes was included in [[Lionel Scaloni]]'s final 23-man Argentina squad for the [[2019 Copa América]].<ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2019/05/21/argentina-copa-america-squad-roster-messi-aguero-dybala-scaloni |title=Messi Leads Argentina's Copa America Squad; Icardi Omitted |magazine=Sports Illustrated |first=Avi |last=Creditor |date=21 May 2019 |access-date=21 May 2019}}</ref> Following Argentina's third-place achievement, Paredes was listed among the "Best XI" team of the tournament.<ref>{{cite news |author=SNS Web |title=Copa America 2019 Best XI: Lionel Messi Left Out, Five Brazil Players Selected |url=https://www.thestatesman.com/sports/copa-america-2019-best-xi-lionel-messi-left-out-five-brazil-players-selected-1502775848.html/amp |newspaper=[[The Statesman (India)|The Statesman]] |access-date=4 June 2021}}</ref>

In June 2021, he was included in [[Lionel Scaloni]]'s final Argentina 28-man squad for the [[2021 Copa América]].<ref>{{cite web |url = https://en.as.com/en/2021/06/14/football/1623671691_620875.html |title = Argentina National Team Squad 2021 |work = Diario AS |date = 15 June 2021 }}</ref> In the semi-finals of the competition, Argentina played against [[Colombia national football team|Colombia]] and the match eventually went into a penalty-shootout during which Paredes successfully converted his spot-kick to help Argentina progress to the [[2021 Copa América final|final]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Copa America 2021: Argentina get past Colombia on penalties to set up final against Brazil |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/57745944 |access-date=8 May 2022 |work=BBC Sport |date=7 July 2021}}</ref>

On 11 November 2022, Paredes was named to Argentina's final 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.afa.com.ar/es/posts/lista-de-convocados-de-la-seleccion-argentina-para-qatar-2022ok|title=Lista de convocados de la Selección Argentina para Qatar 2022|publisher=Asociación del Fútbol Argentino|date=11 November 2022|language=es|access-date=10 December 2022}}</ref> During Argentina's quarterfinal match against the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]], Paredes fouled [[Nathan Aké]] and deliberately smashed the ball into the Dutch bench,<ref name="NOS">{{Cite web |url=https://nos.nl/artikel/2455824-gemoederen-liepen-hoog-op-dumfries-krijgt-na-afloop-rood-messi-bekvecht-met-weghorst |title=Gemoederen liepen hoog op: Dumfries krijgt na afloop rood, Messi bekvecht met Weghorst |publisher=NOS |date=10 December 2022 |language=nl |access-date=10 December 2022}}</ref> prompting a brawl. Paredes scored in the penalty-shootout against the Netherlands winning 4–3 for Argentina after the game was tied 2–2 (a.e.t). Paredes scored again in the penalty-shootout against France in the World Cup final. Winning the penalty-shootout 4–2, after the game was tied 3–3 (a.e.t).

== Personal life ==
Paredes is of [[Paraguay|Paraguayan]] descent through his mother and speaks fluent [[Guarani language|Guaraní]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://d10.ultimahora.com/leandro-paredes-un-campeon-america-sangre-guarani-n2951787.html|title=Leandro Paredes, un campeón de América con sangre guaraní|website=d10.ultimahora.com}}</ref> Paredes' cousin, [[Franco Paredes|Franco]], is also a professional footballer.<ref name="OLE1">{{cite news|title=En River debutó el primo de un ex Boca|url=https://www.ole.com.ar/river-plate/debut-paredes-river-libertadores_0_J6f8ENLP.html|accessdate=5 November 2020|work=Olé|date=4 March 2020}}</ref>
