'''Jules Olivier Ntcham''' (born 9 February 1996) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for {{English football updater|SwanseaC}} club [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]]. Born in France, he plays for the [[Cameroon national football team|Cameroon national team]].

Ntcham was born in [[Longjumeau]], a southern suburb of [[Paris]], and played for numerous youth clubs in and around Paris before joining youth academies at [[Paris FC]] and [[Le Havre AC|Le Havre]]. In 2012, at the age of 16, Ntcham joined [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]]'s [[Manchester City F.C. EDS and Academy|Elite Development Squad]] for £730,000.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Newman |first1=Blair |title=Olivier Ntcham: Man City's long-term answer to Toure who's thriving in Serie A |url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/olivier-ntcham-man-citys-long-term-answer-toure-whos-thriving-serie |website=Four Four Two |date=28 September 2015 |publisher=Four Four Two |access-date=29 January 2021}}</ref> Developing under City's Football Development Executive [[Patrick Vieira]], Ntcham's potential was noticed by club manager [[Manuel Pellegrini]], who included him on City's pre-season tour of [[Australia]] in 2015.

After signing a professional contract with City, Ntcham was loaned out for a two season spell at [[Genoa C.F.C.|Genoa]] in Italy's [[Serie A]], with an option to buy later.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Jackson |first1=Jamie |last2=Romano |first2=Fabrizio |title=Do Manchester City already have a new Paul Pogba in Olivier Ntcham? |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/blog/2015/sep/23/manchester-city-olivier-ntcham-loan-genoa-serie-a |website=[[The Guardian]] |date=23 September 2015 |access-date=29 January 2021}}</ref> In July 2017, Ntcham signed for [[Scottish Premiership]] side [[Celtic F.C.|Celtic]] on a four-year permanent deal. He signed a contract extension with the [[Glasgow]] club in November 2018, extending his stay until 2022.<ref>{{cite web |title=Olivier Ntcham signs contract extension with Celtic |url=https://www.heraldscotland.com/sport/17213248.olivier-ntcham-signs-contract-extension-celtic/ |website=www.heraldscotland.com |publisher=Herald Scotland |access-date=29 January 2021}}</ref> In February 2021, Ntcham was loaned out to [[France|French]] club Marseille.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Celtic transfer news: Olivier Ntcham joins Marseille on loan|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/12206068/celtic-transfer-news-olivier-ntcham-joins-marseille-on-loan|access-date=2 February 2021|website=Sky Sports|language=en}}</ref> Ntcham joined Swansea City in September 2021.

Internationally, Ntcham has represented France at numerous levels, most recently the [[France national under-21 football team]] at the [[2019 UEFA European Under-21 Championship]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Celtic star Olivier Ntcham called up for France U21s Euro squad along with Moussa Dembele |url=https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/celtic-star-olivier-ntcham-called-16183625 |website=www.dailyrecord.co.uk |date=22 May 2019 |publisher=Daily Record |access-date=29 January 2021}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Manchester City===
Ntcham signed for [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] in 2012, joining from French club [[Le Havre AC|Le Havre]] for a fee in the region of €1m, after refusing to sign a professional contract at the club.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/blog/2015/sep/23/manchester-city-olivier-ntcham-loan-genoa-serie-a|title=Do Manchester City already have a new Paul Pogba in Olivier Ntcham?|author=Jamie Jackson|work=the Guardian|date=23 September 2015 }}</ref> In July 2015, he signed a five-year deal with Manchester City, before joining Italian side [[Genoa C.F.C.|Genoa]] on a two-year loan.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.sportsmole.co.uk/football/man-city/transfer-talk/news/olivier-ntcham-joins-genoa-on-loan_236526.html|title=Olivier Ntcham joins Genoa on loan|last=Meyers|first=Alex|date=31 July 2015|publisher=Sports Mole|access-date=22 August 2017}}</ref>

====Loan to Genoa====
On 28 August 2015, [[Serie A]] side Genoa signed Ntcham on a two-year [[loan (sports)|loan]] with an option to buy.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/sport/man-city-watching-impressive-olivier-10005753|title=Man City watching impressive Olivier Ntcham's progress at Genoa carefully|author=Stuart Brennan|date=8 September 2015|work=men}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title = Genoa complete deal for Man City midfielder Olivier Ntcham – Tribal Football|url = http://www.tribalfootball.com/articles/genoa-complete-deal-for-man-city-midfielder-olivier-ntcham-4088964|access-date = 31 August 2015}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title = Manchester City confirm loan moves for Stevan Jovetic, Olivier Ntcham and Enes Unal| date=August 2015 |url = http://www.ibtimes.co.uk/manchester-city-confirm-loan-moves-stevan-jovetic-olivier-ntcham-enes-unal-1513647|access-date = 31 August 2015}}</ref> He made his debut for the club on 23 August 2015, in a 1–0 defeat to [[U.S. Città di Palermo|Palermo]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2015/08/23/italy/serie-a/us-citta-di-palermo/genoa-cfc/2120383/|title=Palermo vs. Genoa – 23 August 2015 – Soccerway|work=soccerway.com}}</ref> His second match was marked with a 2–0 win against [[Hellas Verona F.C.|Hellas Verona]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2015/08/30/italy/serie-a/genoa-cfc/hellas-verona-fc/2120390/|title=Genoa vs. Hellas Verona – 30 August 2015 – Soccerway|work=soccerway.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title = Result: Genoa too strong for Hellas Verona|url = http://www.sportsmole.co.uk/football/genoa/result/result-genoa-too-strong-for-hellas-verona_242909.html|access-date = 31 August 2015}}</ref>

Ntcham's first goal for Genoa came in the opening game of his second [[2016-17 Genoa C.F.C. season|season]], during a 3–1 win over [[Cagliari Calcio|Cagliari]] on 21 August 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.espnfcasia.com/report?gameId=461290|title= Genoa 3–1 Cagliari report|publisher= ESPN FC|access-date= 23 August 2016}}</ref> He scored his second goal on 26 February 2017, in stoppage time, rescuing a 1–1 draw with [[Bologna F.C. 1909|Bologna]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://english.ahram.org.eg/NewsContent/6/55/258962/Sports/World/Five-things-we-learned-in-Italy-Serie-A.aspx|title=Five things we learned in Italy Serie A|date=27 February 2017|work=Ahram Online|access-date=2 March 2017}}</ref>

===Celtic===
On 12 July 2017, Ntcham signed a four-year contract with [[Celtic F.C.|Celtic]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.celticfc.net/news/12893 | title=Celtic delighted to sign highly-rated Olivier Ntcham on 4-year deal | publisher=[[Celtic F.C.|Celtic]] | date=12 July 2017 | access-date=12 July 2017}}</ref> He scored his first goal for Celtic in a 1–0 win against [[Partick Thistle]] on 11 August 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40822499 |title=Partick 0–1 Celtic
|publisher=BBC |date=11 August 2017 |access-date=14 August 2017}}</ref> On 9 November 2018, at the age of 22, he signed a new four-year contract to keep him at Celtic until the year 2022.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.celticfc.net/news/15239 |title=Celtic delighted as Olivier Ntcham signs new four-year deal |publisher=celtic |date=9 November 2018 |access-date= 11 November 2018}}</ref>

Ntcham scored the winning goal against [[S.S. Lazio|Lazio]] in the [[Europa League]] on 7 November 2019, giving Celtic a historic 2–1 victory as their first ever win in Italy.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2019/nov/07/lazio-celtic-europa-league-match-report|title=Olivier Ntcham's late show at Lazio fires Celtic into Europa League last 32|last=Media|first=P. A.|date=7 November 2019|work=The Guardian|access-date=7 November 2019|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref>

====Loan to Marseille====
On 1 February 2021, Ntcham joined French side [[Olympique de Marseille|Marseille]] on loan for the remainder of the 2020–21 season.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.om.fr/en/news/4803/1st-team/46947-olivier-ntcham-olympian |title=Olivier Ntcham is an Olympian |publisher=Olympique de Marseille |date=1 February 2021 |access-date=1 February 2021}}</ref> Manager [[André Villas-Boas]], who had informed the Marseille board that he didn't want the player, offered his resignation the following day in response to Ntcham joining and was suspended as manager a few hours later for publicly criticising the club's board.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2021/feb/02/andre-villas-boas-offers-to-resign-as-marseille-coach-over-signing-of-ntcham |title=André Villas-Boas sacked by Marseille after Ntcham signing criticism |date=2 February 2021 |access-date=2 February 2021 |work=The Guardian}}</ref> On 10 February 2021, he made his debut for Marseille as a substitute for [[Valentin Rongier]] in a 2–0 away win over [[AJ Auxerre]] in the [[Coupe de France]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/match?gameId=592254 |title=AJ Auxerre 0-2 Marseille |website=[[ESPN]] |date=10 February 2021 |accessdate=15 February 2021}}</ref> On 10 February 2021, he made his league debut as a substitute for [[Michaël Cuisance]] in a 0–0 away draw against [[FC Girondins de Bordeaux|Bordeaux]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/match?gameId=573026 |title=Bordeaux 0-0 Marseille |website=[[ESPN]] |date=14 February 2021 |accessdate=15 February 2021}}</ref>

===Swansea City===
On 1 September 2021, Ntcham joined [[EFL Championship|Championship]] club [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]] on a free transfer.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.swanseacity.com/news/olivier-ntcham-joins-swansea-city|title=OLIVIER NTCHAM JOINS SWANSEA CITY|date=1 September 2021|website=swanseacity.com|access-date=1 September 2021}}</ref> He scored his first goal for Swansea in a 3–3 draw with [[Luton Town F.C.|Luton Town]] on 18 September 2021.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58525101|title=Luton 3-3 Swansea|publisher=[[BBC]]|date=18 September 2021|access-date=20 September 2021}}</ref>

==International career==
Ntcham was born in France to parents of Cameroonian descent.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.leparisien.fr/sports/iledefrance/des-cites-a-city-le-conte-de-fees-ntcham-29-11-2012-2365463.php|title=FOOT : le conte de fées de Olivier Ntcham|date=29 November 2012}}</ref> He was a youth international for France at various levels.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/celtic-pair-moussa-dembele-olivier-11294548|title=Celtic pair Moussa Dembele and Olivier Ntcham star in France Under-21 win|date=5 October 2017|work=[[Daily Record (Scotland)|Daily Record]]|access-date=6 October 2017}}</ref>

In November 2019, he was placed on standby for [[Cameroon national football team|Cameroon]]'s [[Africa Cup of Nations]] qualifiers against [[Cape Verde national football team|Cape Verde]] and [[Rwanda national football team|Rwanda]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://stv.tv/amp/1442149-celtic-s-ntcham-set-to-switch-international-allegiance/|title=Celtic's Ntcham set to switch international allegiance|date=5 November 2019|website=stv.tv|access-date=13 November 2019}}</ref> He debuted with the Cameroon national team in a friendly 2–0 loss to [[Uzbekistan national football team|Uzbekistan]] on 23 September 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vavel.com/en-us/soccer/2022/09/22/1124007-cameroon-vs-uzbekistan-live-stream-how-to-watch-on-tv-and-score-updates-friendly-match.html|title=Highlights and goals: Cameroon 0-2 Uzbekistan in international friendly &#124; 09/23/2022|date=23 September 2022|website=VAVEL}}</ref>
