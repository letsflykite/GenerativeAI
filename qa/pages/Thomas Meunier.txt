'''Thomas André A. Meunier''' (born 12 September 1991) is a Belgian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Full-back (association football)|right-back]] for {{German football updater|BoDortmu}} club [[Borussia Dortmund]] and the [[Belgium national football team|Belgium national team]].

==Club career==
{{Overly detailed|section|date=August 2021}}

===Virton===
Born in [[Sainte-Ode]], Belgium, Meunier began playing for various youth teams, starting with RUS Saint-Ode, RUS Givry and [[Standard Liège]]. He spent two seasons with Liège before being released due to injuries.<ref>{{cite news|title=J'en suis là… grâce à mon renvoi du Standard Liege !|url=http://www.dhnet.be/sports/football/j-en-suis-la-grace-a-mon-renvoi-du-standard-527dbd323570ea593db72bb7|publisher=DHnet|language=nl|date=9 November 2013|access-date=17 November 2017}}</ref><ref name="Meunier La Meuse Interview">{{cite news|title=Anderlecht? Je n'ai pas encore le niveau|url=http://www.lameuse.be/archive/d-20130101-Z1735W|publisher=La Meuse|language=fr|date=11 December 2010|access-date=6 August 2018}}</ref> Meunier revealed that his release affected him and at one point he considered quitting football before being persuaded by his mother to continue. During a trial at [[R.E. Virton|Virton]],<ref name="Meunier Nieuwblad Interview 2018">{{cite news|date=12 June 2018|title=Rode Duivel Thomas Meunier was bijna gestopt met voetballen: "Mijn mama en Youtube hebben mij gered"|language=nl|publisher=Nieuwsblad|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/dmf20180612_03556960|access-date=6 August 2018}}</ref> Meunier impressed the club's management and was subsequently signed by the club.<ref name="Meunier Nieuwblad Interview 2018"/><ref>{{cite news|date=5 November 2011|title=Revelatie Thomas Meunier verkoos Brugge boven Luik|language=nl|publisher=Nieuwsblad|url=http://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/g0t3hve4q|access-date=17 November 2017}}</ref>

Meunier started to play professionally for Virton's first team in the first half of 2009, making his debut for the club in a 1–1 draw against [[K.F.C. Vigor Wuitens Hamme|K.F.C. V.W. Hamme]] on 31 January 2009.<ref>{{cite news|title=Football - Division 2 - Virton-Hamme (ce soir, 20 heures) >P.32 L'Excelsior ne peut pas perdre sous peine d'être encore plus englué dans la lutte pour le maintien : Virton: 10 joueurs formés au club dans les 16!|url=http://www.lameuse.be/archive/d-20090130-Y1ANPW?|publisher=La Meuse|language=fr|date=30 January 2009|access-date=6 August 2018}}</ref> He went on to make five appearances, as Virton were relegated from the Second Division.

After becoming a first team regular in the 2009–10 season, making 16 appearances and scoring 5 times in all competitions, the 2010–11 season proved to be his breakthrough.<ref>{{cite news|title=Division 3B - Excelsior Virton-FC Bleid (3-1) : Meunier: un œil a suffi|url=http://www.lameuse.be/archive/d-20130119-Z1YDTR?&noCookies=1|publisher=La Meuse|language=fr|date=15 March 2010|access-date=6 August 2018}}<br>{{cite news|title=Foot " Meunier file vers la D1 " dit Arnaud André|url=http://www.lameuse.be/archive/d-20130107-Z1FXY1?&noCookies=1|publisher=La Meuse|language=fr|date=13 September 2010|access-date=6 August 2018}}<br>{{cite news|title=L'avis de ... Arnaud André ex-attaquant virtonais : " Pour moi, Thomas file vers la Division 1"|url=http://www.lameuse.be/archive/d-20130107-Z1FY27?&noCookies=1|publisher=La Meuse|language=fr|date=14 September 2010|access-date=6 August 2018}}</ref> His first goal of the 2010–11 season came on 22 August 2010 when he scored in a 3–0 win over [[C.S. Visé]].<ref>{{cite news|title=COFIDIS CUP - 5E JOURNÉE Visé a lourdement chuté à domicile : Plus de Liégeois en Coupe de Belgique|url=http://www.lameuse.be/archive/d-20130109-Z1HJ35?|publisher=La Meuse|language=fr|date=23 August 2010|access-date=6 August 2018}}</ref> He then scored twice on 2 October 2010, in a 4–0 win over [[RFC Liège]], followed up by scoring another brace seven days later on 9 October 2010, in a 2–1 win over [[Sporting Hasselt]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Division 3B - Fc Liège-Virton (0-4) : "Le but de l'année? Charleroi Couillet Fleurus]] and KSKL Ternat.<ref>{{cite news|title=Football (D3B): le RFC Liège battu par Huy (1-2)|url=http://www.lameuse.be/archive/d-20121229-Z12KJZ?|publisher=La Meuse|language=fr|date=30 January 2011|access-date=6 August 2018}}<br>{{cite news|title=Mouscron écrase tous ses adversaires|url=http://www.dhnet.be/regions/tournai-ath-mouscron/mouscron-ecrase-tous-ses-adversaires-51b788f3e4b0de6db9805ac4|publisher=DHnet.be|language=fr|date=6 February 2011|access-date=6 August 2018}}</ref> Throughout the 2010–11 season, Meunier quickly made an impression for the side, as he made 29 appearances and scoring 11 times in all competitions.<ref>{{cite news|title=l'équipe de la décennie Chaque jour, une personnalité de la province nous donnera son équipe type de la décennie. ==International career==
===Youth career===
After appearing for the [[Belgium national youth football team|Belgium U15]] team, Meunier was called up for the [[Belgium national under-21 football team|Belgium U21]] team for the first time in February 2011, but did not play.<ref>{{cite news|title=Thomas Meunier vervangt Fileccia in beloftenselectie|url=http://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/dmf20110204_134|publisher=Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=4 February 2011|access-date=17 November 2017}}</ref>

Meunier was called up for the second time in August 2011<ref>{{cite news|title=Selectie nationale beloften voor oefeninterland tegen Turkije|url=http://www.standaard.be/cnt/dmf20110804_140|publisher=De Standaard|language=nl|date=4 August 2011|access-date=17 November 2017}}</ref> and finally made his Belgium U21 debut on 9 August 2011, in a 3–2 friendly loss against [[Turkey national under-21 football team|Turkey U21]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Football - diables rouges espoirs (belgique -Turquie: 2-3) Le coach Franky Dury juge le Brugeois, Guillaume François et Anthony Moris (blessé) : Meunier a fêté sa 1ère titularisation|url=http://www.lameuse.be/archive/d-20121218-Z0HDFD|publisher=La Meuse|language=fr|date=9 August 2011|access-date=6 August 2018}}</ref> On 6 September 2011, he then scored his first U21 goal, in the [[2013 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship qualification]] 4–1 home win over [[Azerbaijan national under-21 football team|Azerbaijan U21]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://sporza.be/cm/sporza/voetbal/1.1104546|title=U21 beat Azerbaijan in EC qualification|language=nl|date=6 September 2011|publisher=sporza.be}}</ref> He made seven appearances (five of those were in 2013 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship qualification matches) and scored once for the Belgium U21 team.

===Senior career===
[[File:Thomas Meunier 2018.jpg|thumb|left|180px|Meunier playing for [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]] at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|FIFA World Cup]] in Russia]]

After being called up to the senior side for the first time on 14 November 2013, Meunier made his debut for the [[Belgium national football team|Belgium national team]] in a home friendly match against [[Colombian national football team|Colombia]], which was preparation for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]] in Brazil.<ref>{{cite news|title=Marc Wilmots roept Meunier op voor Colombia en Japan|url=http://sporza.be/cm/sporza/voetbal/Rode_Duivels/1.1773026|publisher=Sporza.be|language=nl|date=7 November 2013|access-date=17 November 2017}}<br>{{cite news|title=Colombia speelt de Belgen van hun wolk|url=http://sporza.be/cm/sporza/matchcenter/mc_voetbal/oefeninterlands/1.1777849|publisher=Sporza.be|language=nl|date=14 November 2013|access-date=17 November 2017}}</ref> He also appeared as a substitute in two further friendlies, against [[Japan national football team|Japan]] (2–3 loss) and [[Iceland national football team|Iceland]] (3–1 win), but was not included in coach [[Marc Wilmots]]' final [[2014 FIFA World Cup squads#Belgium|23-man squad]] for the tournament.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/jun/06/belgium-world-cup-squad-2014|title=Belgium squad for 2014 World Cup: the 23 chosen by Marc Wilmots|date=6 June 2014|work=The Guardian}}</ref>

After missing out in the World Cup, Meunier was called up in November 2014 and made his first appearance in almost a year, in a 3–1 win over [[Iceland national football team|Iceland]] on 12 November 2014.<ref>{{cite news|title=Meunier en De Bock sluiten aan bij Duivels|url=http://www.standaard.be/cnt/dmf20141110_01368840|publisher=De Standaard|language=nl|date=10 November 2014|access-date=17 November 2017}}<br>{{cite news|title=Les Diables ne manquent pas leur répétition|url=http://www.7sur7.be/7s7/fr/1508/Sports/article/detail/2118789/2014/11/12/Les-Diables-ne-manquent-pas-leur-repetition.dhtml|publisher=7SUR7.be|language=fr|date=12 November 2014|access-date=17 November 2017}}</ref>

After being called up to the squad for the [[UEFA Euro 2016|Euro 2016]], Meunier played the whole 90 minutes at right back in the UEFA [[UEFA Euro 2016 Group E|Euro 2016 group stage]] and then set up a goal for [[Axel Witsel]], in a 3–0 win over [[Republic of Ireland national football team|Republic of Ireland]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Ook Lombaerts out voor EK, twijfels over Boyata, Kabasele erbij|url=http://sporza.be/cm/sporza/voetbal/Rode_Duivels/1.2670009|publisher=Sporza.be|language=nl|date=30 May 2016|access-date=17 November 2017}}<br>{{cite news|title=Belgium get going as Romelu Lukaku punishes Republic of Ireland|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/jun/18/belgium-republic-of-ireland-euro-2016-match-report|work=The Guardian|date=18 June 2016|access-date=17 November 2017}}</ref> He also aided his team in keeping another clean sheet in 90 minutes at right back in another victory over [[Sweden national football team|Sweden]] four days later.<ref>{{cite news|title=Sweden 0 – 1 Belgium|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/36535313|publisher=BBC Sport|date=22 June 2016|access-date=17 November 2017}}</ref> He also started in Belgium's 4–0 [[UEFA Euro 2016 knockout phase|round of 16]] win against [[Hungary national football team|Hungary]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Hungary 0 – 4 Belgium|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/36569531|publisher=BBC Sport|date=26 June 2016|access-date=17 November 2017}}<br>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro/season=2016/teams/player=250029318/index.html|title=Thomas Meunier|language=en|access-date=26 June 2016|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> Meunier finished the UEFA Euro 2016 campaign with five appearances for the side.

On 13 November 2016, Meunier scored in the 8th minute in Belgium's 8–1 [[2018 FIFA World Cup Qualification]] win over [[Estonia national football team|Estonia]] at the [[King Baudouin Stadium]] to register his first ever senior international goal.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/37965298|title=Belgium 8–1 Estonia|date=13 November 2016|access-date=14 November 2016|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> On 31 August 2017, Meunier recorded his first career senior international hat-trick in Belgium's 9–0 defeat of [[Gibraltar national football team|Gibraltar]], a match in which he also recorded four assists.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://nieuws.vtm.be/stadion/stadion/meunier-eerste-hattrick-ooit|title=MEUNIER: "EERSTE HATTRICK OOIT"|date=31 August 2017|work=Vlaamse Televisie Maatschappij|language=nl}}</ref> On 7 October 2017, he scored the opening goal of Belgium's 4–3 defeat of [[Bosnia and Herzegovina national football team|Bosnia and Herzegovina]] to end the 2018 World Cup qualifying campaign with five goals and seven assists from eight matches.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://en.psg.fr/pro/article/meunier-scores-and-assists-with-belgium|title=Meunier scores and assists with Belgium|date=7 October 2017|work=Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171117092354/https://en.psg.fr/pro/article/meunier-scores-and-assists-with-belgium|archive-date=17 November 2017|url-status=live}}</ref>

Meunier was selected in Belgium's [[Belgium national football team#Current squad|final 23-man squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{Cite news|title=Roberto Martinez annonce sa liste des 23 Diables Rouges|url=https://fr.metrotime.be/2018/06/04/actualite/diables-rouges-roberto-martinez-annonce-liste-23/|publisher=Metro|language=fr|date=6 August 2018}}</ref> He was Belgium's first-choice right midfielder and played his first World Cup match, in a 3–0 win over [[Panama national football team|Panama]] in their opening group game at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite news|title=World Cup 2018: Belgium best debutants Panama 3-0 in Sochi|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44434275|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=6 August 2018}}</ref> In a next match against  [[Tunisia national football team|Tunisia]] on 23 June 2018, Meunier set up [[Romelu Lukaku|Romelu Lukaku's]] second goal of the game, in a 5–2 win to qualify for the Round of 16.<ref>{{Cite news|title=World Cup 2018: Belgium v Tunisia|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44439152|publisher=BBC Sport|date=23 June 2018|access-date=6 August 2018}}</ref> He also set up [[Nacer Chadli]] to score in injury time in a 3–2 victory over [[Japan national football team|Japan]] in the round of 16 of the 2018 FIFA World Cup.<ref>{{cite news|title=De perfecte counter van de kwalificatie ontleed: in tien seconden naar uitbundige extase|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/dmf20180702_03595072|publisher=Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=2 July 2018|access-date=6 August 2018}}</ref> However, after a 2–1 win over [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]] on 6 July 2018, he received another booking, which caused him to miss the semi–finals.<ref>{{cite news|title=World Cup 2018: Belgium produce masterclass to knock out Brazil|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44652178|publisher=BBC Sport|date=6 July 2018|access-date=6 August 2018}}<br>{{cite news|title=Geschorste Meunier vervangen tegen Frankrijk? Er zijn twee opties|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/dmf20180709_03604191|publisher=Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=9 July 2018|access-date=6 August 2018}}</ref> On 14 July 2018, Meunier returned from suspension, scoring the opening goal of the match for Belgium in a 2–0 victory against [[England national football team|England]] in their [[2018 FIFA World Cup#Third place play-off|third-place play-off]], his nation's best-ever World Cup finish.<ref>{{cite news|title=World Cup 2018: England finish fourth after Belgium defeat|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44748604|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=6 August 2018}}</ref>

On 12 June 2021, Meunier came on as a substitute for the injured [[Timothy Castagne]] after just 27 minutes and scored Belgium's second goal in the 34th minute during a 3–0 win in their first [[UEFA Euro 2020 Group B|Euro 2020 group stage]] match against [[Russia national football team|Russia]]. In doing so, Meunier became the first ever player to score a first half goal after coming on as a substitute in the [[UEFA European Championship]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro-2020/match/2024450--belgium-vs-russia/postmatch/report/?iv=true|title=Belgium 3-0 Russia: Lukaku scores twice in opening win|work=[[UEFA]]|date=12 June 2021|accessdate=15 June 2021}}</ref>
