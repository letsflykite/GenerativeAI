'''Mathías Olivera Miramontes''' (born 31 October 1997) is a Uruguayan professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[left-back]] for {{Italian football updater|Napoli}} club [[S.S.C. Napoli|Napoli]] and the [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay national team]].<ref name="soccerway">{{Soccerway|406775}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=5 – Mathías Olivera – AUF|url=https://www.auf.org.uy/5-math-iacute-as-olivera-2017/|access-date=13 June 2020}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Uruguay===
Born in [[Montevideo]], Olivera is a [[Club Nacional de Football|Nacional]] youth graduate. After progressing through the youth setup, he made his professional debut on 13 February 2016, starting and being [[Penalty card#Yellow card|booked]] in a 3–0 away win against [[Club Atlético River Plate (Montevideo)|River Plate]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tenfield.com.uy/river-00-nacional/|title=Nacional letal y contundente; 3:0|trans-title=Lethal and overwhelming Nacional, 3–0|publisher=[[Tenfield]]|language=es|date=13 February 2016|accessdate=14 August 2017}}</ref>

Olivera appeared in only one further match for the club, a 0–2 loss at [[Club Plaza Colonia de Deportes|Plaza Colonia]] on 29 February 2016. In December of that year, he was bought outright by his agent [[Daniel Fonseca]] and assigned to [[Atenas de San Carlos]] in [[Uruguayan Segunda División|Segunda División]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.referi.uy/fonseca-se-lleva-al-juvenil-olivera-n1013120|title=Fonseca se lleva al juvenil Olivera|trans-title=Fonseca takes the youth player Olivera|publisher=Referi|language=es|date=24 December 2016|accessdate=14 August 2017}}</ref>

In July 2017, Oliveira passed a medical at [[Galatasaray S.K. (football)|Galatasaray]], but eventually did not sign a contract and subsequently returned to Uruguay.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.referi.uy/la-sombra-fonseca-el-misterioso-pase-mathias-olivera-n1101221|title=La sombra de Fonseca en el misterioso pase de Mathías Olivera|trans-title=The shadow of Fonseca in the mysterious rights of Mathías Olivera|publisher=Referi|language=es|date=24 July 2017|accessdate=14 August 2017}}</ref>

===Getafe===
On 14 August 2017, Olivera signed a six-year deal with [[La Liga]] club [[Getafe CF|Getafe]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.getafecf.com/Noticia/tabid/137/Article/4617/Mathias-Oivera-nuevo-refuerzo-azulon.aspx|title=Mathías Oivera, nuevo refuerzo azulón|trans-title=Mathías Olivera, new ''azulón'' addition|publisher=Getafe CF|language=es|date=14 August 2017|accessdate=14 August 2017|archive-date=9 August 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210809213634/http://www.getafecf.com/Noticia/tabid/137/Article/4617/Mathias-Oivera-nuevo-refuerzo-azulon.aspx|url-status=dead}}</ref> He scored his first professional goal on 21 April 2018, netting the game's only in a 1–0 win against [[SD Eibar|Eibar]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Eibar vs. Getafe – 21 April 2018|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2018/04/21/spain/primera-division/sociedad-deportiva-eibar/getafe-club-de-futbol/2529573/|access-date=13 June 2020}}</ref>

===Albacete (loan)===
On 4 July 2018, [[Segunda División]] side [[Albacete Balompié|Albacete]] announced the signing of Olivera on a season long loan deal. He played 15 matches and scored a goal for the club before his loan was cut short by Getafe on 25 January 2019.<ref>{{cite web|title=Gracias, Mathías|url=https://www.albacetebalompie.es/noticia/gracias-mathias|access-date=13 June 2020}}</ref>

===Napoli===
On 26 May 2022, Italian club [[S.S.C. Napoli|Napoli]] announced the signing of Olivera on a three-year deal.<ref name="napoli">{{cite web|publisher=S.S.C. Napoli|title=Il Napoli ufficializza l'acquisto di Mathias Olivera|url=https://www.sscnapoli.it/static/news/Il-Napoli-ufficializza-l-acquisto-di-Mathias-Olivera-25182.aspx|date=26 May 2022|access-date=27 May 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
Olivera is a former Uruguay youth international. He was part of [[Uruguay national under-20 football team|Uruguay under-20 team]] which won [[2017 South American U-20 Championship]] and finished fourth at [[2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup]].<ref>{{cite tweet|number=856899785424596994|user=Uruguay|title=#Sub20 | Plantel de @Uruguay para el...|date=25 April 2017}}</ref>

On 7 January 2022, Olivera was named in Uruguay's 50-man preliminary squad for [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONMEBOL)|FIFA World Cup qualifying]] matches against [[Paraguay national football team|Paraguay]] and [[Venezuela national football team|Venezuela]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.auf.org.uy/reservados-vs-paraguay-venezuela/|title=Reservados vs. Paraguay y Venezuela|publisher=[[Uruguayan Football Association]]|date=7 January 2022|access-date=18 January 2022}}</ref> He made his senior team debut on 27 January 2022 in a 1–0 win against Paraguay.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.getafecf.com/Noticia/tabid/137/Article/8032/Damian-Suarez-y-Olivera-debutan-con-Uruguay-con-victoria.aspx|title=Damián Suárez y Olivera debutan con Uruguay con victoria|publisher=[[Getafe CF|Getafe]]|date=28 January 2022|access-date=29 January 2022}}</ref>
