'''Thomas Jok Deng''' (born 20 March 1997) is an Australian professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[central defender]] for [[J1 League]] club [[Albirex Niigata]]. Born a South Sudanese refugee in Kenya, he has represented the [[Australia national soccer team|Australia national team]].

==Personal life==
Thomas Jok Deng was born on 20 March 1997<ref name=soccerway/> into a family of [[South Sudan]]ese refugees in [[Nairobi]], [[Kenya]]. He, along with his family, were fleeing from the conflict in South Sudan and eventually resettled in [[Adelaide]], [[South Australia]],<ref name = CornerFlag/> in 2003, when Deng was six years old.<ref name=td>{{cite web | title=Thomas Deng | website=Perth African Nations Sports Association | date=19 September 2020 | url=https://www.pansa.org.au/thomas-deng/ | access-date=15 June 2022}}</ref>

Deng has four older siblings, as well as a half-brother who lives in Uganda.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Davutovic |first1=David |title=Victory star Thomas Deng revisits fork in the road on visit to youth justice centre |url=https://www.heraldsun.com.au/sport/football/a-league/teams/victory/victory-star-thomas-deng-revisits-fork-in-the-road-on-visit-to-youth-justice-centre/news-story/699f41ffdcaebe224cf9da551f2022d9 |website=Herald Sun |publisher=News Corp |accessdate=16 December 2019}}</ref> His older brother, [[Peter Deng]], has represented [[South Sudan national football team|South Sudan]] at senior level.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cornerflag.com.au/peter-dengs-journey-to-south-sudanese-debut/|title=Peter Deng's journey to South Sudanese debut – The Corner Flag|website=cornerflag.com.au|language=en-US|access-date=27 April 2018}}</ref>

==Club career==
Deng's first club, along with his good friend and fellow refugee and Socceroo [[Awer Mabil]], was the [[Adelaide Blue Eagles]].<ref name=td/>

After the family had moved to [[Melbourne]] in 2011,<ref name=td/> Deng made his senior football debut as a 16 year old in 2013 at [[Western Eagles F.C.]] under then-manager Budala Barešić-Nikić in the [[Victorian State League Division 3]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://theworldgame.sbs.com.au/video/604722755961/Deng-on-how-he-made-it-to-Olyroos-level|title = Deng on how he made it to Olyroos level}}</ref><ref>John Punshon "http://www.ozfootball.net/ark/States/VIC/2013SD4WR.html"</ref>
The following season, the youngster moved to nearby [[Green Gully SC]], where manager Bob Stojcevski recruited Deng to play for the club's u20 side. He was eventually promoted to the senior team and made 13 first team appearances in the [[National Premier Leagues Victoria|NPL Victoria]] in 2014.<ref name = CornerFlag>{{cite web|last1=Kulas|first1=Damir|title=The rise and rise of Thomas Deng|url=http://www.cornerflag.com.au/the-rise-and-rise-of-thomas-deng/|website=The Corner Flag|accessdate=30 November 2015}}</ref>

Following his impressive performances at Gully, Melbourne Victory National Youth League coach Darren Davies rewarded Deng with a youth contract. Deng debuted for the senior team for [[Melbourne Victory FC|Melbourne Victory]] against [[Balmain Tigers]] in the [[2015 FFA Cup]]. He made his [[A-League]] debut on 9 October 2015 against [[Adelaide United]], as a substitute replacing [[Fahid Ben Khalfallah]] after a [[Penalty card#Red card|red card]] was shown to [[Leigh Broxham]]. Thomas made his starting debut in the [[A-League]] for Melbourne Victory on 17 October 2015 in round 2 against [[Melbourne Derby (A-League)|town rivals]], [[Melbourne City FC|Melbourne City]], where he was selected as Man of the Match by supporters and social media.

In June 2016, Deng moved to [[Jong PSV]] on a one-year loan deal.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.psv.nl/psv/nieuws/artikel/psv-huurt-australische-verdediger-deng-19.htm|publisher=[[PSV Eindhoven]]|accessdate=28 June 2016|date=27 June 2016|title=PSV huurt Australische verdediger Deng (19)|language=Dutch}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.melbournevictory.com.au/article/thomas-deng-joins-psv-eindhoven-on-loan/1srxg725j0c5g151vzmwk40vx7|title=Thomas Deng joins PSV Eindhoven on loan|publisher=[[Melbourne Victory FC|Melbourne Victory]]|date=28 June 2016|access-date=28 June 2016|archive-date=3 October 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161003094608/http://www.melbournevictory.com.au/article/thomas-deng-joins-psv-eindhoven-on-loan/1srxg725j0c5g151vzmwk40vx7|url-status=dead}}</ref> After making five appearances, he returned to Melbourne Victory at the end of the season.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com.au/news/deng-could-stengthen-victorys-defence-461792|title=Deng could strengthen Victory's defence...|first=Clement|last=Tito|newspaper=[[FourFourTwo]]|date=15 May 2017|access-date=15 May 2017|archive-date=21 June 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180621073358/https://www.fourfourtwo.com.au/news/deng-could-stengthen-victorys-defence-461792|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In January 2020, Deng left Melbourne Victory to join Japanese club [[Urawa Red Diamonds]] following his performance at the [[2020 AFC U-23 Championship]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.smh.com.au/sport/soccer/deng-quits-victory-to-join-j-league-giants-urawa-20200128-p53vik.html|title=Deng quits Victory to join J-League giants Urawa|first=Michael|last=Lynch|newspaper=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|date=28 January 2020}}</ref>

==International career==
He was part of the main squad of the [[Australia national under-23 soccer team|Olyroos]] at the [[2016 AFC U-23 Championship]] and put on an extremely impressive display throughout the tournament, garnering high praise from respected Australian pundits: Geoff Fullgrabe, Alan Vucenik, and [[Craig Foster]].

Due to being born in Kenya and having [[South Sudanese]] heritage, he is eligible to play for [[Australia men's national soccer team|Australia]], [[Kenya national football team|Kenya]] and [[South Sudan national football team|South Sudan]].

In October 2018, Deng was named in the Socceroos squad for their training camp in the [[United Arab Emirates|UAE]] and their friendly against [[Kuwait national football team|Kuwait]]. In the friendly he made his debut, together with fellow South Sudanese refugee and boyhood friend [[Awer Mabil]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2018/oct/16/a-dream-come-true-refugees-deng-and-mabil-make-socceroos-debuts|title='A dream come true': refugees Deng and Mabil make Socceroos debuts together|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|date=16 October 2018}}</ref>

On 22 July 2021, Deng captained Australia in a historic 2–0 win over Argentina in the first group game of the [[2020 Summer Olympics]] in Tokyo, also winning the Player of the Match award.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theaustralian.com.au/sport/olympics/olympics-2021-all-the-ollyroos-action-from-tokyo-match-report/news-story/8be222cd3739497c76a9b21a817a33bd|title='Olympics 2021: All the Olyroos action from Tokyo; match report'|newspaper=[[The Australian]]|date=23 July 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.socceroos.com.au/news/south-sudan-starring-sapporo-skipper-thomas-dengs-inspirational-journey|title='From South Sudan to starring in Sapporo: Skipper Thomas Deng's inspirational journey'|newspaper=[[Socceroos]]|date=24 July 2021}}</ref>

He was included into Australian squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], but played no game in the tournament as Australia reached the round of sixteen.
