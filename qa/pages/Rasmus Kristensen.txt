'''Rasmus Nissen Kristensen''' ({{IPA-da|ˈne̝sn̩ ˈkʰʁestn̩sn̩}}; born 11 July 1997) is a Danish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[right-back]] for {{English football updater|LeedsUni}} club [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]] and the [[Denmark national football team|Denmark national team]].

==Youth career==
Kristensen was born to a footballing family and began playing football in Brande IF when he was six years old. He played in the club for six years, before joining [[Herning Fremad]] where he played for two years. In 2012, Kristensen signed a youth contract with [[FC Midtjylland]].<ref>{{ cite web | title =Fokus på: Ramus Nissen Kristensen – Født ind i titlernes årgang | url = https://www.dbold.dk/2015/12/fokus-paa-ramus-nissen-kristensen-foedt-ind-i-titlernes-aargang/ | publisher = dbold.dk | date = 15 December 2015}}</ref>

==Club career==

===FC Midtjylland===
Kristensen was promoted to the first team squad in summer 2016 at the age of 18 and signed a five-year professional contract.<ref>{{ cite web | title = FCM giver backkomet fem år | url = http://www.bold.dk/fodbold/nyheder/fcm-giver-backkomet-fem-aar/ | publisher = bold.dk | date = 22 June 2016}}</ref>

At the age of 18, Kristensen got his official debut for FCM on 7 March 2016, in a [[Superligaen|Superliga]] match against [[FC Nordsjælland]].<ref>{{ cite web | title = NORDSJÆLLAND VS. MIDTJYLLAND 2 - 1 | url = https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/03/07/denmark/superliga/fc-nordsjalland/fc-midtjylland/2048580/ | publisher = soccerway.com | date = 7 March 2016}}</ref> He started on the bench, but replaced [[Václav Kadlec]] in the 39th minute in a match, which FCM lost 1–2. He turned into a key player for the team in the 2016–17 season.

===Ajax===
On 23 January 2018, Kristensen joined [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]] on a four-and-a-half-year contract.<ref>{{ cite web | title = Ajax en FC Midtjylland akkoord over Rasmus Nissen Kristensen | url = http://www.ajax.nl/streams/actueel/ajax-en-fc-midtjylland-akkoord-over-rasmus-nissen-kristensen.htm | publisher = ajax.nl | date = 23 January 2018}}</ref>

===RB Salzburg===
After one and a half years with Ajax, Kristensen moved to Austria and joined [[Austrian Football Bundesliga]] club [[FC Red Bull Salzburg]] on a five-year contract.<ref>[https://www.redbullsalzburg.at/en/fc-red-bull-salzburg/news/saison_2019_20/welcome-to-salzburg-rasmus-kristensen.html FROM AJAX AMSTERDAM TO FC RED BULL SALZBURG], redbullsalzburg.at, 19 July 2019</ref>

===Leeds United===
On 8 June 2022, Kristensen joined [[Premier League]] club [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]] on a five year contract for an undisclosed fee in the region of £10 million.<ref name=Leedssalz>{{cite news |title=Leeds United Agree Deal For Rasmus Kristensen |url=https://www.leedsunited.com/news/team-news/29969/leeds-united-agree-deal-for-rasmus-kristensen |access-date=8 June 2022 |publisher=Leeds United FC |date=8 June 2022}}</ref> He will play for the team from 1 July 2022,<ref>{{cite web | title=Leeds agree deal for defender Kristensen | publisher=[[BBC Sport]] | date=8 June 2022 | url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/61736577 | access-date=8 June 2022 }}</ref> and became Leeds's second confirmed arrival of the summer transfer window, reuniting with former Salzburg teammate [[Brenden Aaronson]].<ref name=Leedssalz /> He made his senior league debut for Leeds as part of the starting eleven in their season opener on 6 August with a 2–1 home win over [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/aug/06/leeds-wolves-premier-league-match-report|title= Brenden Aaronson inspires Leeds to comeback victory over Wolves|last=Unwin|first=Will|date=6 August 2022|access-date=7 August 2022|website=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref> On 18 March 2023, he scored seconds after coming on as a substitute in Leeds’ 4–2 win at [[Molineux Stadium|Molineux]] against [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=Leeds out of bottom three with victory at Wolves |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/64923722 |access-date=2023-03-18}}</ref>

==International career==
In November 2020, he was called up to [[Kasper Hjulmand]]'s senior squad due to several cancellations from, among others, the Danish national team players playing in England, due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in England|COVID-19 restrictions]], as well as a case of [[Coronavirus disease 2019|COVID-19]] in the squad, which had put several national team players in quarantine.<ref>{{cite news |title=Wheelman pulls Salzburg-back into national team squad|url=https://jyllands-posten.dk/sport/fodbold/ECE12557329/hjulmand-hiver-salzburgback-ind-i-landsholdstruppen/|publisher=jyllands-posten.dk |accessdate=12 November 2020}}</ref>

He made his debut for the [[Denmark national football team]] on 4 September 2021 in a [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group F|World Cup qualifier]] against the [[Faroe Islands national football team|Faroe Islands]], a 1–0 away victory. He started the game and was substituted at half-time.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[FIFA]]|url=https://www.fifa.com/tournaments/mens/worldcup/qatar2022/qualifiers/uefa/match-center/400155657|title=Faroe Islands v Denmark game report|date=4 September 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Kristensen is the nephew of the former [[SK Sturm Graz|Sturm Graz]] player Sigurd Kristensen and is the cousin of [[Leon Jessen]].
