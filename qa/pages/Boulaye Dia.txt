'''Boulaye Dia''' (born 16 November 1996) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]] for Italian {{Italian football updater|Salernitana}} club [[US Salernitana 1919|Salernitana]]. Born in France, he plays for the [[Senegal national football team|Senegal national team]].

==Club career==

===Reims===
Dia signed a professional contract with [[Stade de Reims]] on 16 July 2018 after a successful debut season with [[Jura Sud Foot]] where he scored 15 goals in 21 games.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://stade-de-reims.com/boulaye-dia-renfort-offensif-groupe-pro-2/|title=Boulaye Dia, renfort offensif du groupe Pro 2 - Stade de Reims|date=16 July 2018|publisher=}}</ref> He made his professional debut with Reims in a 1–1 [[Ligue 1]] tie with [[Angers SCO|Angers]] on 20 October 2018.<ref name="lfp">{{cite web|url=https://www.lfp.fr/ligue1/feuille_match/84707|title=Stade de Reims - Angers SCO (1-1) - Saison 2018/2019 - Ligue 1 Conforama|website=lfp.fr|accessdate=21 October 2018}}</ref>

On 25 October 2020, Dia scored a [[hat-trick]] in a 4–0 win against [[Montpellier HSC|Montpellier]], thereby becoming the first Reims player to score one in the top flight since [[Santiago Santamaría]] in 1978. The win was the club's first in the league in the [[2020–21 Stade de Reims season|2020–21 season]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Reims stun Montpellier, Briand scores 100th goal |url=https://www.ligue1.com/Articles/MATCH-REPORTS/2020/10/25/reims-stun-montpellier-briand-scores-100th-goal |website=Ligue 1 |accessdate=26 October 2020 |date=25 October 2020}}</ref>

===Villarreal===
On 13 July 2021, [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]] announced the signing of Dia on a five-year deal from [[Stade de Reims|Stade Reims]]. On his first official debut for Villarreal which was against [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] in the [[2021 UEFA Super Cup]], he received lots of praise for his fine display.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|title=Chelsea win Super Cup on penalties|language=en-GB|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58157867|access-date=30 August 2021}}</ref>

====Loan move to Salernitana====
On 18 August 2022, Dia went to [[Serie A]] club [[U.S. Salernitana 1919|U.S. Salernitana]] on loan for one year, with a buyout clause.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ussalernitana1919.it/boulaye-dia-e-un-giocatore-della-salernitana/|title=Boulaye Dia è un giocatore della Salernitana|trans-title=Boulaye Dia is a Salernitana player|publisher=US Salernitana 1919|language=it|date=18 August 2022|accessdate=20 August 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
Born in France, Dia holds French and Senegalese nationalities. He was called up to represent the [[Senegal national football team|Senegal national team]] on 1 October 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.afrik-foot.com/senegal-boulaye-dia-dans-la-liste-pas-mbaye-niang|title=Sénégal : 1ère pour Boulaye Dia, Mbaye Niang zappé !|first=Romain|last=Lantheaume|date=1 October 2020}}</ref> He first played for Senegal in a friendly 3–1 loss to [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]] on 9 October 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2020/10/09/world/friendlies/morocco/senegal/3369317/|title=Morocco vs. Senegal - 9 October 2020 - Soccerway|website=Soccerway}}</ref>

He was part of [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations squads#Senegal|Senegal's squad]] for the [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations]]; the Lions of Teranga went on to win the tournament for the first time in their history.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cafonline.com/total-africa-cup-of-nations/news/cisse-unveils-senegal-squad-hopes-for-elusive-totalenergies-afcon-title|title=Cisse unveils Senegal squad, hopes for elusive TotalEnergies AFCON title|publisher=[[Confederation of African Football]]|date=25 December 2021|access-date=28 March 2023|archive-date=9 January 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220109145849/https://www.cafonline.com/total-africa-cup-of-nations/news/cisse-unveils-senegal-squad-hopes-for-elusive-totalenergies-afcon-title|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Senegal 0-0 Egypt |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/60186276 |website=[[BBC Sport]] |access-date=28 March 2023 |date=7 February 2022}}</ref>

Dia was appointed a [[National Order of the Lion|Grand Officer of the National Order of the Lion]] by [[President of Senegal]] [[Macky Sall]] following the nation's victory at the tournament.<ref name= "reuters">{{cite web |title=Cash prizes, real estate, and highest honours as Senegal celebrates Nations Cup heroes |url=https://www.reuters.com/lifestyle/sports/cash-prizes-real-estate-highest-honours-senegal-celebrates-nations-cup-heroes-2022-02-08/ |website=[[Reuters]] |access-date=28 March 2023 |date=8 February 2022}}</ref>
