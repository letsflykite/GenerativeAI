'''Thomas Joseph Delaney''' (born 3 September 1991) is a Danish professional [[Association football|footballer]], who plays as a [[defensive midfielder]] for [[La Liga]] club [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]] and the [[Denmark national football team|Denmark national team]].

Delaney began his career at [[FC Copenhagen|Copenhagen]], making 249 total appearances and scoring 24 goals, while winning the [[Danish Superliga]] five times and the [[Danish Cup]] on four occasions. He then played in Germany for [[SV Werder Bremen|Werder Bremen]] and [[Borussia Dortmund]], making 107 total [[Bundesliga]] appearances before a move to Sevilla in 2021.

Delaney made his international debut for [[Denmark national football team|Denmark]] in 2013. He was part of their squads at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]], [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]], reaching the semi-finals of the 2020 tournament.

==Club career==
===Copenhagen===
[[file:Dnepr-Kopengagen (5).jpg|thumb|upright|left|Delaney with Copenhagen in 2014]]
Delaney started playing youth football at [[Kjøbenhavns Boldklub]], the reserve team of Danish club [[F.C. Copenhagen]].<ref name="FCKcontract" />

Delaney made his debut for Copenhagen in an unofficial friendly in August 2008. After playing the first half of the [[2008-09 in Danish football|2008–09 season]] in the KB youth team, he was a part of the FCK squad in the [[Danish Cup 2008-09|Danish Cup]] that campaign. He made his official debut in the first leg of the semi-finals against [[Nordvest FC]] in April, where he substituted [[Hjalte Nørregaard]] in the middle of second half.<ref name="FCKcontract" /> In the return leg two weeks later Delaney was among the starting eleven playing the first 60 minutes before he was substituted with [[Şaban Özdoğan]].

In the summer break 2009, he was promoted to the first team squad together with Özdoğan. He scored on his continental debut on 22 July 2009, playing the full second leg in Copenhagen's [[2009–10 UEFA Champions League#Second qualifying round|Champions League]] second qualifying round victory over [[FK Mogren]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/newsid=846084.html|title=Misery for Mogren in Copenhagen|publisher=UEFA|date=15 July 2009|access-date=2 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/mogren-vs-fc-cophagen/104658|title=Mogren 0-6 Copenhagen|publisher=Sky Sports|date=22 July 2009|access-date=2 August 2019}}</ref> His first [[Danish Superliga|Superliga]] match was on 9 August 2009 against [[SønderjyskE Fodbold|SønderjyskE]] replacing [[Atiba Hutchinson]] near the end of the match.<ref name="FCKnewcontract" /> On 29 December 2011, signed a contract extension keeping him at Copenhagen until the end of 2014.<ref name="FCKcontract">{{cite web|url=https://www.fck.dk/en/news/contract-extension-delaney|title=Contract Extension for Delaney|publisher=FCK|date=29 December 2011|access-date=2 August 2019}}</ref> At the time, Delaney had the distinction of being the longest serving current player at the club, despite being only 20.<ref name="FCKcontract" />

On 6 July 2013, Delaney extended his contract with the club to 2015. At the same time, he got jersey number 8.<ref>[http://www.fck.dk/nyhed/2013/07/06/thomas-delaney-forlaenger-med-fc-koebenhavn Thomas Delaney forlænger til 2015], ''[[F.C. Copenhagen]]'', 6 July 2013</ref> In the following season, he became a regular starter in his favorite position, including [[UEFA Champions League]] matches against [[Juventus F.C.]], [[Real Madrid]] and [[Galatasaray S.K. (football)|Galatasaray]]. On 23 January 2014, he again extended his contract, until 2017.<ref name="FCKnewcontract">{{cite web|url=https://www.fck.dk/en/news/delaney-signs-new-contract|title=Delaney signs new contract|publisher=FCK|date=24 January 2014|access-date=2 August 2019}}</ref> At the same time, he was officially named vice-captain of the team.<ref name="FCKnewcontract" /> Half a year later, Delaney was promoted to club captain.

On 14 May 2015 he was named Man of the Match as his side won the [[2014–15 Danish Cup#Final|Danish Cup]] final 3–2 after extra-time against fellow Superliga side [[FC Vestsjælland]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fck.dk/kamp/14-05-15/fc-vestsjaelland-fc-kobenhavn|title=FC Vestsjaelland - FC Kobenhavn|publisher=FCK|date=14 May 2015|access-date=2 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/news/newsid=2247585.html|title=FCK beat Vestsjælland to win Danish Cup|publisher=UEFA|date=14 May 2015|access-date=2 August 2019}}</ref>

On 27 September 2016, Delaney contributed to a comfortable 4–0 victory for his side with a left-footed half-volley from 30 yards against Belgian side [[Club Brugge KV]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.beinsports.com/en/football/news/delaney-stunner-helps-danish-champions-canter/343220|title=Delaney stunner helps Danish champions canter to victory|website=beIN SPORTS}}</ref> in the [[UEFA Champions League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2017/matches/round=2000783/match=2019302/postmatch/report/|title=UEFA Champions League 2016/17 - History - København-Club Brugge – UEFA.com|last=uefa.com|date=27 September 2016}}</ref>
The goal came third in the vote for goal of the season in the UEFA Champions League.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/newsid=2476065.html|title=Observers' choice: goals of the #UCL season|last=UEFA.com|date=5 June 2017 }}</ref>

===Werder Bremen===
On 17 August 2016, [[SV Werder Bremen|Werder Bremen]] announced Delaney would be joining the club in January 2017.<ref>{{cite web|title=Thomas Delaney to move to Werder in the winter|url=http://www.werder.de/en/news/news/werder-bremen-news/20162017/thomas-delaney-17082016/|website=Werder Bremen|access-date=17 August 2016|date=17 August 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160818025246/http://www.werder.de/en/news/news/werder-bremen-news/20162017/thomas-delaney-17082016/|archive-date=18 August 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Walsh|first1=Jonathan|title=Delaney delight at Werder move|url=http://www.vavel.com/en/international-football/germany-bundesliga/683218-delaney-delight-at-werder-move.html|website=VAVEL|access-date=17 August 2016|date=17 August 2016}}</ref> On 18 February 2017, he scored his first goal for the club, a free kick, in Werder Bremen's 2–0 win away to [[1. FSV Mainz 05|Mainz 05]] before being stretchered off the pitch with a suspected concussion. It was the club's first win of the new year following four straight defeats.<ref>{{cite news|title=Bremen bounce back at Mainz|url=http://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/blmd21n-mainz-0-2-bremen-report-433459.jsp|access-date=18 February 2017|work=Bundesliga|date=18 February 2017}}</ref>

On 1 April 2017, Delaney scored a [[hat-trick]], the first of his career, in Werder Bremen's 5–2 win away against [[SC Freiburg]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Thomas Delaney treble boosts Werder Bremen|url=http://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/blmd26n-sc-freiburg-2-5-sv-werder-bremen-report-goalmania-438319.jsp|website=Bundesliga|access-date=1 April 2017|date=1 April 2017}}</ref>

===Borussia Dortmund===
On 7 June 2018, Delaney signed for [[Borussia Dortmund]] on a four-year contract. The transfer fee paid to Werder Bremen was reported as €20 million.<ref>{{cite news |title=Delaney wechselt für 20 Millionen Euro zum BVB |url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/startseite/725249/artikel_delaney-wechselt-fuer-20-millionen-euro-zum-bvb.html#omrss |access-date=7 June 2018 |work=kicker Online |date=7 June 2018 |language=de}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.bvb.de/News/Uebersicht/Borussia-Dortmund-verpflichtet-Thomas-Delaney |title= Borussia Dortmund verpflichtet Thomas Delaney |language= de |date= 7 June 2018 |access-date=7 June 2018}}</ref>

On 13 May 2021, Delaney was a second-half substitute for Dortmund in their 4–1 victory over [[RB Leipzig]] in the [[2021 DFB-Pokal Final]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://apnews.com/article/bundesliga-borussia-dortmund-dortmund-europe-leipzig-57d576ad46190c226251b8f92669dbf8|title=Borussia Dortmund beats Leipzig 4-1 in German Cup final|website=AP|date=13 May 2021}}</ref>

===Sevilla===
On 25 August 2021, Delaney moved to Spain to join [[La Liga]] side [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]] on a four-year deal for a fee reported by ''[[Diario de Sevilla]]'' as €7 million.<ref>{{cite news |title=El Sevilla cierra a Delaney por 7 millones de euros |url=https://www.diariodesevilla.es/sevillafc/Sevilla-cierra-Delaney-millones-euros_0_1604540970.html |access-date=27 January 2022 |work=Diario de Sevilla |date=24 August 2021 |language=Spanish}}</ref>

====Loan to Hoffenheim====
On 30 January 2023, Delaney returned to Germany and joined [[TSG 1899 Hoffenheim|1899 Hoffenheim]] on loan until the end of the 2022–23 season.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=TSG 1899 Hoffenheim|url=https://www.tsg-hoffenheim.de/en/news/overview/2023/01/tsg-hoffenheim-sign-thomas-delaney-on-loan/|title=TSG Hoffenheim sign Thomas Delaney on loan|date=30 January 2023|access-date=30 January 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
Delaney made his debut for the Denmark under-18 team in October 2008, and went on to play three matches in total. He played 11 games for the [[Denmark national under-19 football team|Denmark U-19 team]]. On 11 August 2010, he made his debut for the [[Denmark national under-21 football team|Denmark U-21 team]] in a friendly against the [[Italy national under-21 football team|Italy U-21 team]].

Delaney made his debut for the [[Denmark national football team|senior national team]] on 15 October 2013 in a [[2014 FIFA World Cup qualification]] match against [[Malta national football team|Malta]]. He played the full 90 minutes of the 6–0 home win.<ref>{{cite news |title=Delaney, Larsen og Duncan med fra start |url=https://www.berlingske.dk/sport/delaney-larsen-og-duncan-med-fra-start |access-date=27 January 2022 |work=Berlingske |date=15 October 2013 |language=Danish}}</ref> His second cap did not come until 25 March 2015, as a half-time substitute in a 3–2 friendly win over the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]] in [[Aarhus]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Hattrick af Bendtner sikrer 3-2 sejr |url=https://www.dbu.dk/nyheder/2015/marts/hattrick-af-bendtner-sikrer-3-2-sejr/ |access-date=27 January 2022 |publisher=Danish Football Association |date=25 March 2015 |language=Danish}}</ref>

Delaney scored his first international goal on 1 September 2017 to open a 4–0 home win over Poland in [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification|qualification]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Poland thrashed 4-0 by Denmark in World Cup qualifying |url=https://apnews.com/article/3cc4f01a44dd453fac3fc4e773b7f2f0 |access-date=27 January 2022 |publisher=Associated Press |date=1 September 2017}}</ref> Three days later, he scored a hat-trick as the team came from behind to win 4–1 away to [[Armenia national football team|Armenia]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Thomas Delaney hat-trick helps Denmark to victory over Armenia |url=https://www.shropshirestar.com/sport/uk-sports/2017/09/04/thomas-delaney-hat-trick-helps-denmark-to-victory-over-armenia/ |access-date=27 January 2022 |work=Shropshire Star |date=4 September 2017}}</ref>

In June 2018, he was named in Denmark's squad for the World Cup in Russia.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/revealed-every-world-cup-2018-squad-23-man-preliminary-lists/oa0atsduflsv1nsf6oqk576rb|title=Revealed: Every World Cup 2018 squad - Final 23-man lists|website=Goal.com}}</ref> He was also named in the 26-man squad for [[UEFA Euro 2020]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=13 June 2021|last=Pope|first=Conor|title=Euro 2020 squads: Every confirmed team for the 2021 tournament|url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/euro-2020-squad-every-country-team-players-list-line-ups|access-date=3 July 2021|website=FourFourTwo}}</ref> He scored the first goal in the 2–1 quarter-final game against the [[Czech Republic national football team|Czech Republic]].<ref>{{Cite news|title=Denmark beat Czechs to reach last four|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/51198684|access-date=3 July 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Delaney was born in Denmark and spent his youth there. His father was also born in Denmark but has American citizenship due to his own father (Thomas Delaney's paternal grandfather) being born in the United States.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.yanks-abroad.com/content.php?mode=show&id=9004|title=DELANEY DISCUSSES AMERICAN ROOTS|publisher=Yanks Abroad|publication-date=11 April 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150313143225/http://www.yanks-abroad.com/content.php?mode=show&id=9004|archive-date=13 March 2015|url-status=usurped}}</ref> Delaney also has Irish roots through his paternal great-grandfather who moved from Ireland to the United States in the 19th century during the [[Great Famine (Ireland)|Great Famine]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Croke|first=Ruaidhrí|title=Thomas Delaney: Dane with Irish name has eyes only for Russia|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/sport/soccer/international/thomas-delaney-dane-with-irish-name-has-eyes-only-for-russia-1.3285646|date=10 November 2017|access-date=10 June 2020|newspaper=The Irish Times|language=en}}</ref>

Delaney is red–green [[Color blindness|colour blind]].<ref>{{cite web|title=World Cup 2018: Why millions of fans see the football like this|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-44535687/|website=BBC.com|access-date=11 July 2018|date=10 July 2018}}</ref>
