'''Alexis Mac Allister''' (born 24 December 1998) is an Argentine professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for {{English football updater|Liverpoo}} club [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] and the [[Argentina national football team|Argentina national team]].

Born to a footballing family, Mac Allister started his senior career with [[Argentinos Juniors]] in 2016, winning the [[Primera Nacional|Argentine second division]] in his debut season. He joined [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]] in 2019 but was immediately [[Loan (association football)|loaned]] back to Juniors until the end of the season. During his second year at Brighton, he was again loaned to Argentina to [[Boca Juniors]], where he won the [[2019–20 Argentine Primera División|2019–20 Primera División]]. Returning from the loan the [[2020–21 Brighton & Hove Albion F.C. season|following season]], Mac Allister had his breakthrough year for Brighton, becoming a key player for the team. He was their top scorer in the [[2022–23 Brighton & Hove Albion F.C. season|2022–23 season]].

Mac Allister represented Argentina at [[Argentina national under-23 football team|under-23 level]], winning the [[2020 CONMEBOL Pre-Olympic Tournament]] and playing at the [[2020 Summer Olympics]]. He made his senior international debut in 2019 and was part of the Argentina squad that won the [[2022 Finalissima]] with a 3–0 victory against [[Italy national football team|Italy]]. He was part of the Argentina team that won the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] and he [[Assist (association football)|assisted]] a goal in [[2022 FIFA World Cup final|the final]] against [[France national football team|France]].

== International career ==

=== Youth career, senior debut, and 2020 Summer Olympics ===
Soon after debuting for Argentinos, Mac Allister received a call-up from [[Claudio Úbeda]] for the [[Argentina national under-20 football team|Argentina under-20s]].<ref name="Arg U20s">{{Cite web |date=13 November 2017 |title=El semillero de los Mac Allister |trans-title=The hotbed of the Mac Allisters |url=http://www.afa.org.ar/5916/el-semillero-de-los-mac-allister |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171113222928/http://www.afa.org.ar/5916/el-semillero-de-los-mac-allister |archive-date=13 November 2017 |access-date=13 November 2017 |publisher=Argentine Football Association |language=es}}</ref> He was selected for the [[Argentina national football team|senior team]] for the first time in August 2019, ahead of friendlies in the United States in September versus [[Chile national football team|Chile]] and [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]].<ref name="AFA1">{{Cite news |date=18 August 2019 |title=Convocados fecha FIFA |trans-title=Summoned FIFA date |publisher=Argentine Football Association |url=https://www.afa.com.ar/es/posts/convocados-fecha-fifa |access-date=18 August 2019}}</ref> His international bow arrived in the match with Chile at the [[Los Angeles Memorial Coliseum|Los Angeles Coliseum]] on 5 September.<ref name="Soccerway"/>

On 1 July 2021, Mac Allister was named in the [[Argentina national under-23 football team|Argentinean Olympic]] squad for the [[2020 Summer Olympics]] in Tokyo, taking place in 2021 due to the previous year's postponement as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic.<ref>{{Cite news |date=8 July 2021 |title=Brighton's Alexis Mac Allister heads to Tokyo Olympics |newspaper=The Argus |location=Brighton |url=https://www.theargus.co.uk/sport/19428636.brightons-alexis-mac-allister-heads-tokyo-olympics/ |access-date=24 July 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |date=21 July 2021 |title=Olympics: Tokyo 2020 chief does not rule out cancelling Games |website=BBC News |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-asia-57911122 |access-date=24 July 2021}}</ref> He played in Argentina's opening game against [[Australia men's national under-23 soccer team|Australia]] starting the match, playing 78 minutes of a 2–0 defeat at the [[Sapporo Dome]] in Sapporo on 22 July.<ref>{{Cite news |date=22 July 2021 |title=Mac Allister and Argentina shocked by Australia in Olympics opener |website=WeAreBrighton.com |url=http://www.wearebrighton.com/newsopinion/mac-allister-and-argentina-shocked-by-australia-in-olympics-opener/ |access-date=24 July 2021}}</ref> He started the next two [[Football at the 2020 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament – Group C|group games]], a 1–0 victory over [[Egypt national under-23 football team|Egypt]] on 25 July again being played in the Sapporo Dome and a 1–1 draw with [[Spain national under-23 football team|Spain]] on 28 July at the [[Saitama Stadium 2002|Saitman Stadium]] in [[Midori-ku, Saitama]]. Argentina were eliminated finishing third in their group on goal difference.<ref>{{Cite web |date=25 July 2021 |title=Football – Egypt vs Argentina – Group C results – Tokyo 2020 |url=https://olympics.com/tokyo-2020/olympic-games/en/results/football/results-men-gpc-000300-.htm |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210810093457/https://olympics.com/tokyo-2020/olympic-games/en/results/football/results-men-gpc-000300-.htm |archive-date=10 August 2021 |access-date=11 August 2021 |publisher=Olympics}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=28 July 2021 |title=Spain vs Argentina – Group C results – Tokyo 2020 |url=https://olympics.com/tokyo-2020/olympic-games/en/results/football/results-men-gpc-000600-.htm |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210810101736/https://olympics.com/tokyo-2020/olympic-games/en/results/football/results-men-gpc-000600-.htm |archive-date=10 August 2021 |access-date=11 August 2021 |publisher=Olympics}}</ref>

=== Recall into senior squad and 2022 FIFA World Cup victory ===
[[File:Alexis Mac Allister WC 2022.jpg|thumb|upright|Mac Allister playing for Argentina at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]]]]

Mac Allister was recalled to the Argentina national team's senior squad in January 2022 after two-and-a-half years since his first two caps. However, he tested positive for [[COVID-19]] and missed Argentina's [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification|2022 FIFA World Cup qualifier]] against Chile.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Howard |first=Derren |date=27 January 2022 |title=Brighton and Aston Villa international stars ruled-out with Covid-19 |url=https://www.brightonandhoveindependent.co.uk/sport/football/brighton-and-hove-albion/brighton-and-aston-villa-international-stars-ruled-out-with-covid-19-3543841 |access-date=28 January 2022 |website=Brighton and Hove Independent}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=28 January 2022 |title=Chile suffer World Cup qualifier loss to Argentina |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/60166226 |access-date=28 January 2022 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> He eventually made his first appearance for the national team for almost three years where he started in the 3–0 home win over [[Venezuela national football team|Venezuela]] on 25 March.<ref>{{Cite web |date=25 March 2022 |title=Argentina 3–0 Venezuela – FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022 |url=https://www.ffa.com/tournaments/mens/worldcup/qatar2022/qualifiers/conmebol/match-center/400104626 |access-date=1 June 2022 |publisher=FIFA}}</ref> Five days later, in the draw with [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador]] he was forced off with after being a victim of a knee-high challenge. Due to the subsequent injury, he was returned to Brighton ahead of their next fixture.<ref>{{Cite news |date=31 March 2022 |title=Alexis Mac Allister in injury scare with Argentina |url=https://www.theargus.co.uk/sport/20033114.alexis-mac-allister-injury-scare-argentina/ |newspaper=The Argus |location=Brighton |access-date=1 June 2022}}</ref> On 1 June, Mac Allister was an unused substitute as Argentina beat [[Italy national football team|Italy]] in the [[2022 Finalissima]] held at [[Wembley Stadium]].<ref name="Finalissima">{{Cite web |date=1 June 2022 |title=Italy 0–3 Argentina: Messi and Di Maria shine in impressive Finalissima win |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/61653337 |access-date=1 June 2022 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

On 11 November, Mac Allister was named in Argentina's 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=11 November 2022 |title=Lista de la Selección Argentina para el Mundial de Qatar 2022 |language=es |publisher=TyC Sports |url=https://www.tycsports.com/seleccion-argentina/lista-seleccion-argentina-mundial-de-qatar-2022-id476596.html |access-date=11 November 2022}}</ref> In the last [[2022 FIFA World Cup Group C|group game]] against [[Poland national football team|Poland]] on 30 November, he scored his first international goal opening the score line in the eventual 2–0 win, seeing Argentina through to the round of 16 as group champions.<ref>{{Cite news |date=30 November 2022 |title=World Cup 2022: Poland 0–2 Argentina – South Americans top group after dramatic night |website=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63725317 |access-date=30 November 2022}}</ref> After Argentina made [[2022 FIFA World Cup final|the final]], Mac Allister started the match against defending champions [[France national football team|France]] where he assisted [[Ángel Di María]]'s goal to make the score 2–0. In the second half of extra time, Mac Allister was substituted before Argentina won the competition following a penalty shootout.<ref>{{Cite web |date=18 December 2022 |title=2022 FIFA World Cup final report |url=https://www.fifa.com/fifaplus/en/match-centre/match/17/255711/285077/400128145 |url-status=live |access-date=19 December 2022 |publisher=FIFA}}</ref>
