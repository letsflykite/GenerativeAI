'''Kevin Christian Trapp''' ({{IPA-de|ˈkɛvɪn ˈtʁap}};<ref>{{cite book|last1=Krech|first1=Eva-Maria|last2=Stock|first2=Eberhard|last3=Hirschfeld|first3=Ursula|last4=Anders|first4=Lutz Christian|title=Deutsches Aussprachewörterbuch|trans-title=German Pronunciation Dictionary|language=de|year=2009|publisher=Walter de Gruyter|location=Berlin|isbn=978-3-11-018202-6|pages=648, 993}}</ref> born 8 July 1990) is a German professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Bundesliga]] club [[Eintracht Frankfurt]] and the [[Germany national football team|Germany national team]].

He began his professional career at [[1. FC Kaiserslautern]], where he took part in two [[Bundesliga]] seasons. In 2012, he signed for Eintracht Frankfurt for a fee of €1.5 million and played regularly over three years, also competing in the [[UEFA Europa League]]. He joined [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] for an estimated €10 million in 2015, where he won several domestic honours. In 2018, he re-joined Eintracht Frankfurt on loan, before signing permanently the next season.

Trapp won 21 caps for Germany's youth teams, including 11 for the [[Germany national under-21 football team|under-21s]], and made his full debut in June 2017. He was part of their squad that won the [[2017 FIFA Confederations Cup]] and also competed at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]], [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]].

Trapp was a member of several German youth national teams, and was first called up to the [[Germany national youth football team|Germany U17]] team, appearing as an unused substitute throughout the national side's [[2007 FIFA U-17 World Cup|FIFA U-17 World Cup]] tournament, as they finished third place.<ref>{{cite news|title=HEIKO HERRLICH BENENNT DFB-AUFGEBOT FÜR DIE U 17-WM|url=https://www.dfb.de/news/detail/heiko-herrlich-benennt-dfb-aufgebot-fuer-die-u-17-wm-11663/|publisher=German Football Association|language=de|date=24 July 2007|access-date=3 July 2020}}<br>{{cite news|title=DFB-TRAINER HEIKO HERRLICH STELLT U 17-WM-AUFGEBOT VOR|url=https://www.dfb.de/news/detail/dfb-trainer-heiko-herrlich-stellt-u-17-wm-aufgebot-vor-11807/|publisher=German Football Association|language=de|date=24 July 2007|access-date=3 July 2020}}</ref>

On 4 June 2018, Trapp was selected in Germany's final 23-man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dw.com/en/germany-announce-final-world-cup-squad-with-manuel-neuer-without-leroy-san%C3%A9/a-44064011|title=Germany announce final World Cup squad, with Manuel Neuer, without Leroy Sané|publisher=Deutsche Welle|access-date=4 June 2018}}</ref> He was unused in a group-stage exit for the defending champions.<ref>{{cite news|title=TRAPP: "WIR BRAUCHEN EINEN PERFEKTEN TAG"|url=https://www.dfb.de/news/detail/trapp-wir-brauchen-einen-perfekten-tag-216279/|publisher=German Football Association|language=de|date=10 June 2020|access-date=3 July 2020}}</ref> On 19 May 2021, he was selected to the squad for [[UEFA Euro 2020]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.kicker.de/em-kader-offiziell-loew-beruft-mueller-hummels-und-volland-804642/artikel|title=EM-Kader offiziell: Löw beruft Müller, Hummels und Volland|date=19 May 2021|website=kicker.de|language=de|access-date=19 May 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Trapp was born in [[Merzig]], Saarland and stayed in the city until 2004.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.worldfootball.net/player_summary/kevin-trapp/ |title=Kevin Trapp: Profile |website=worldfootball.net |publisher=HEIM:SPIEL |access-date=27 June 2020}}</ref><ref name="Trapp Trial"/> Trapp said in an interview that he idolised [[Oliver Kahn]].<ref name="Trapp Idol"/> Trapp attended Peter-Wust-Gymnasium.<ref>{{cite news|title=KEVIN TRAPPS EHEMALIGES GYMNASIUM KOOPERIERT MIT DEM SFV|url=https://www.dfb.de/news/detail/kevin-trapps-ehemaliges-gymnasium-kooperiert-mit-dem-sfv-161325/|publisher=German Football Association|language=de|date=11 February 2017|access-date=3 July 2020}}</ref>

He became engaged to Brazilian model [[Izabel Goulart]] in July 2018.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Szathmary |first1=Zoe |title=Izabel Goulart, Kevin Trapp reveal engagement on Instagram |url=http://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/2018/07/05/izabel-goulart-kevin-trapp-reveal-engagement-on-instagram.html |access-date=5 August 2018 |publisher=Fox News |date=6 July 2018}}</ref> In addition to speaking German, Trapp speaks six languages, including Portuguese.<ref name="Trapp Trial"/><ref>{{cite news|title=KEVIN TRAPP IN DFB@HOME: "ICH WOLLTE FRÜHER WIE OLLI KAHN WERDEN"|url=https://www.dfb.de/news/detail/kevin-trapp-in-dfbhome-ich-wollte-frueher-wie-olli-kahn-werden-214873/|publisher=German Football Association|language=de|date=14 April 2020|access-date=3 July 2020}}<br>{{cite news|title=Große Bühne, leere Bühne: Eintracht nach 1666 Tagen zurück|url=https://www.fr.de/sport/fussball/grosse-buehne-leere-buehne-eintracht-nach-1666-tagen-zurueck-11086735.html|publisher=Frankfurter Rundschau|language=de|date=20 September 2018|access-date=3 July 2020}}</ref><ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2we973erg3c "Ich dachte, ich bin in Frankfurt" I PK nach FC Barcelona - Eintracht Frankfurt], Eintracht Frankfurt, Trapp speaking spanish after Frankfurt beat Barcelona, 15 April 2022.</ref>
