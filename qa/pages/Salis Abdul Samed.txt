'''Salis Abdul Samed''' (born 26 March 2000) is a Ghanaian professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[defensive midfielder]] for {{French football updater|Lens}} club [[RC Lens|Lens]] and the [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national team]].

==Club career==

=== Clermont ===
Abdul Samed played for [[JMG Academy]] originally in Accra, before later moving to the academy in [[Djékanou]], Ivory Coast, after the Accra academy closed.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://jmgmanagement.pro/en/salis-abdul-samed-jmg-academician-profesionnal-player/ |title=Salis Abdul Samed JMG academician professional player |publisher=[[JMG Academy]] |date=8 December 2017 |access-date=19 May 2023}}</ref> On 24 July 2019, he joined [[Clermont Foot|Clermont]] on a two-year loan deal from the JMG Academy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://jmgmanagement.pro/2019/07/24/c-s-clermont-sign-blankson-anoff-and-salis-abdul-samed/|title=C.S. Clermont sign Blankson Anoff and Salis Abdul Samed, &#124;|date=24 July 2019}}</ref> He made his debut with the club in a 2–2 (5–4 on penalties) [[Coupe de la Ligue]] loss to [[RC Lens|Lens]] on 27 August 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2019/08/27/france/coupe-de-la-ligue/racing-club-de-lens/clermont-foot-auvergne/3082728/|title=Lens vs. Clermont - 27 August 2019 - Soccerway|website=Soccerway}}</ref> Samed was part of the Clermont squad that earned promotion into the Ligue 1 after placing second in the [[2019–20 Ligue 2]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Abayomi |first=Ogunniyi |date=28 February 2023 |title=Salis Abdul Samed: The Ghanaian Midfielder Making Strides at Lens |url=https://breakingthelines.com/player-analysis/salis-abdul-samed-the-ghanaian-midfielder-making-strides-at-lens/ |access-date=24 June 2023 |website=Breaking The Lines |language=}}</ref> On 12 July 2021, Abdul Samed moved to Clermont on a permanent basis and signed a four-year contract.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[Clermont Foot|Clermont]]|url=https://www.clermontfoot.com/salis-abdul-samed-prolonge-jusquen-2025/|title=SALIS ABDUL SAMED PROLONGE JUSQU'EN 2025|date=12 July 2021|language=fr}}</ref>

== International career ==
Prior to the 2022 FIFA World Cup, he received his first call-up to the Ghana national team in November 2022.<ref>{{Cite web |last= |first= |date=14 November 2022 |title=Otto Addo announces squad for World Cup finals |url=https://www.ghanafa.org/otto-addo-announces-squad-for-world-cup-finals |access-date=24 June 2023 |website=Ghana Football Association |language=}}</ref> He made his debut against Switzerland in a pre-tournament friendly, starting and playing the full 90 minutes as Ghana won 2–0.<ref>{{Cite web |date=17 November 2022 |title=RC Lens midfielder Salis Abdul Samed proud after Ghana debut in win over Switzerland |url=https://ghanasoccernet.com/rc-lens-midfielder-salis-abdul-samed-proud-after-ghana-debut-in-win-over-switzerland |access-date=24 June 2023 |website=Ghanasoccernet}}</ref> In November 2022, Samed participated at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] with the [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national team]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=Ghana must build on World Cup positives despite elimination - Salis Abdul Samed |url=https://www.modernghana.com/sports/1199808/ghana-must-build-on-world-cup-positives-despite.html |access-date=21 December 2022 |website=Modern Ghana |language=en}}</ref>
