'''Mouez Hassen''' ({{lang-ar|معز حسن}}; born 5 March 1995) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Tunisian Ligue Professionnelle 1]] side [[Club Africain]]. A dual French-Tunisian national, he plays for the [[Tunisia national football team|Tunisia national team]].

==Early life==
Hassen was born in [[Fréjus]], Var.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/FootballFicheJoueur45795.html |title=Mouez Hassen |newspaper=L'Équipe |location=Paris |access-date=2 April 2021 |language=fr}}</ref>

==Career==
===Nice===
Hassen made his [[Ligue 1]] debut on 25 September 2013 in a 2–0 away defeat against [[FC Nantes]].{{citation needed|date=April 2021}}

===Southampton===
On 31 January 2017, Hassen signed a five-month loan deal with [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]], where he was re-united with his former manager, [[Claude Puel]],<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/38813450|title=Mouez Hassen: Southampton sign French keeper on loan from Nice|website=BBC Sport|date=31 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://southamptonfc.com/news/2017-01-31/announcement-mouez-hassen-southampton-fc-signing|title=Saints complete Mouez Hassen signing|publisher=Southampton F.C.|date=31 January 2017}}</ref> although he made no appearances for the club.{{citation needed|date=April 2021}}

===Châteauroux===
Hassen joined [[LB Châteauroux|Châteauroux]] on loan on 9 July 2017.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Mouez-hassen-nice-prete-a-chateauroux/817056|title=Mouez Hassen (Nice) prêté à Châteauroux|newspaper=L'Équipe|location=Paris|date=9 July 2017 |language=French}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://sport24.lefigaro.fr/football/transferts/fil-info/mouez-hassen-prete-a-chateauroux-officiel-867627|title=Mouez Hassen Prêté à Châteauroux (Officiel)|newspaper=Le Figaro |location=Paris |date=9 July 2017 |language=French}}</ref>

===Cercle Brugge===
On 1 November 2019, Hassen signed a one-season contract with a one-season extension option with [[Belgian First Division A]] club [[Cercle Brugge K.S.V.|Cercle Brugge]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cerclebrugge.be/nl/news/welkom-bij-cercle-brugge-mouez-hassen|accessdate=1 November 2019|date=1 November 2019|publisher=Cercle Brugge K.S.V.|language=Dutch|title=New Branding &#124; Leve Cercle }}</ref>

===Club Africain===
On 9 September 2021 he joined Tunisian club [[Club Africain]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[Club Africain]]|url=https://www.facebook.com/watch/?v=4281154008598525|title=After the successful medical examination, international goalkeeper Moaz Hassan spent two seasons contract.|date=9 September 2021|language=ar}}</ref>

==International career==
Hassen made his debut for [[Tunisia national football team|Tunisia]] keeping a clean sheet in an international friendly against [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica]] on 27 March 2018 at the [[Allianz Riviera]] in Nice.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2018/mar/27/tunisia-costa-rica-international-friendly-match-report|title=Wahbi Khazri gives warning to England in Tunisia victory over Costa Rica|newspaper=The Guardian|location=London|date=27 March 2018}}</ref>

In June 2018, it was revealed that Hassen had faked being injured during two international [[Exhibition match|friendly]] matches against [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]] and [[Turkey national football team|Turkey]] to allow teammates to eat food mid-game and break their fast during the month of [[Ramadan]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2018/06/tunisia-keeper-fakes-injury-players-break-ramadan-fast-180603174439967.html|title=Tunisia keeper 'fakes' injury to help players break Ramadan fast|publisher=Al Jazeera|date=4 June 2018}}</ref>

In June 2018, he was named in Tunisia's 23-man [[Tunisia national football team|squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Okeleji |first1=Oluwashina |title=Tunisia World Cup squad: Leicester City's Benalouane in 23-man squad |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/amp/football/44342860 |website=BBC Sport |accessdate=10 June 2018 |date=2 June 2018}}</ref>
