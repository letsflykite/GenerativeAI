'''Wout François Maria Weghorst''' (born 7 August 1992) is a Dutch professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Striker (association football)|striker]] for {{English football updater|Burnley}} club [[Burnley F.C.|Burnley]] and the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands national team]].

Weghorst began his professional career in the [[Eerste Divisie|second tier]] of Dutch football with [[FC Emmen|Emmen]]. He then played in the [[Eredivisie]] with [[Heracles Almelo]] and [[AZ Alkmaar|AZ]], before joining [[VfL Wolfsburg]] in 2018. After scoring 70 goals in 144 games for Wolfsburg, he was signed by Burnley in January 2022 for a fee of £12 million. After the club were relegated from the Premier League, Weghorst had loan spells at Turkish [[Süper Lig]] club [[Beşiktaş J.K.|Beşiktaş]] and Premier League club [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]].

Weghorst made one appearance for the [[Netherlands national under-21 football team|Netherlands under-21 team]] in 2014, before making his senior international debut in March 2018. He represented the Netherlands at [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], where he scored two goals in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Netherlands vs Argentina|quarter-final match]] against [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]].

==Club career==
===Early career===
Born in [[Borne, Overijssel|Borne]], Overijssel, Weghorst started his career at local clubs RKSV NEO and [[DETO Twenterand]],<ref>{{cite news |title="Willen is kunnen" – Wout Weghorst |trans-title="To want is to be able" – Wout Weghorst |url=https://www.rksvneo.nl/wout-weghorst-willen-is-kunnen/|access-date=9 March 2018 |work=RKSVNEO.nl |date=14 April 2013 |language=nl}}</ref> before joining [[Eredivisie]] club [[Willem II (football club)|Willem II]] in 2011.<ref name="willem">{{cite news |title=Transfer Wout Weghorst levert Willem II 75.000 euro op |url=https://www.bd.nl/willem-ii/transfer-wout-weghorst-levert-willem-ii-75-000-euro-op~ad1122d8/ |website=bd.nl |date=27 June 2018 |access-date=8 January 2023 |language=nl}}</ref> Despite having the opportunity to make his way into the first-team, he never broke through, and only featured for the reserve team.<ref name="willem"/> He signed for [[Eerste Divisie]] club [[FC Emmen|Emmen]] in 2012.<ref>{{cite news |title=De lange weg van Wout Weghorst: van de derde klasse naar de Bundesliga |url=https://www.elfvoetbal.nl/Binnenland/272852/De-lange-weg-van-Wout-Weghorst-van-de-derde-klasse-naar-de-Bundesliga |website=elfvoetbal.nl |date=26 June 2018 |access-date=8 January 2023 |language=nl}}</ref> He made his debut on 10 August 2012, in a match against [[FC Dordrecht|Dordrecht]] which ended 1–1. Weghorst would go on the score his first goal in professional football a month later, in the derby against [[SC Veendam|Veendam]], a match that ended in a 2–1 win for Emmen. He managed to play in 28 league matches in his debut season, scoring 8 goals. After his spell with Emmen, Weghorst signed a contract with Eredivisie club [[Heracles Almelo]] on a free transfer.<ref>{{cite news |title=Wout Weghorst de nieuwe Bas Dost van Heracles Almelo |trans-title=Wout Weghorst the new Bas Dost for Heracles Almelo |url=http://www.rtvoost.nl/voetbal/betaald-voetbal/194362/wout-weghorst-de-nieuwe-bas-dost-van-heracles-almelo#|access-date=9 March 2018 |work=RTVOost.nl |date=20 July 2014 |language=nl}}</ref>

On 9 August, Weghorst made his Eredivisie debut for Heracles Almelo, in a 0–3 home loss against [[AZ Alkmaar|AZ]] in the [[Polman Stadion]]. He was a regular starter in his two years in Almelo. In the first season the club battled relegation from the Eredivisie, but his second season was more successful with Heracles ending in the sixth spot and, through the subsequent [[2015-16 Eredivisie|play-offs]], the club qualified for the [[2016-17 UEFA Europa League#Third qualifying round|Europa League qualifying round]] – the first time in Heracles Almelo history that the club qualified for an international competition.<ref>{{cite news |title=Heracles gaat Europa in! Dat is geen wonder, dat is echt |trans-title=Heracles goes to Europe! It is not a dream, it is real |url=https://www.tubantia.nl/heracles/heracles-gaat-europa-in-dat-is-geen-wonder-dat-is-echt~a9c9f5c1/|access-date=9 March 2018 |work=Tubantia.nl |date=22 May 2016 |language=nl}}</ref>

===VfL Wolfsburg===
On 26 June 2018, Weghorst signed a four-year contract with [[Bundesliga]] club [[VfL Wolfsburg]].<ref>[https://www.vfl-wolfsburg.de/en/info/news/detailspage/artikel/weghorst-joins-wolfsburg-from-alkmaar-50478.html Weghorst joins Wolfsburg from Alkmaar]. VfL Wolfsburg. 26 June 2018.</ref> On 16 March 2019, he scored his first hat-trick for Wolfsburg, and first by any player for the club since [[Mario Gómez]] in April 2017, in a 5–2 league win over [[Fortuna Düsseldorf]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/wolfsburg-fortuna-dusseldorf-live-line-ups-stats-john-brooks-kownacki-weghorst-3699-3005 |title=Wolfsburg hat-trick hero Wout Weghorst blows away Fortuna Düsseldorf |publisher=Bundesliga |date=16 March 2019|access-date=17 March 2019}}</ref> He finished his first season in Germany with 17 goals, ranking joint-third in the Bundesliga goalscoring charts for the [[2018-19 Bundesliga|2018–19]] campaign.<ref>{{cite web |title=Bundesliga 2018–19 Top Scorer |url=https://www.worldfootball.net/goalgetter/bundesliga-2018-2019/ |website=Worldfootball.net |publisher=Heim:Spiel |access-date=5 February 2021}}</ref>

In the [[2019–20 Bundesliga|2019–20]] season, Weghorst produced another impressive goal return, scoring 16 times in the league as Wolfsburg finished 6th.{{citation needed|date=January 2023}}

Weghorst scored twice in the [[2020–21 Europa League]] qualifying rounds, but Wolfsburg ultimately failed to qualify for the group stage, losing to Greek team [[AEK Athens]] in the playoff round.<ref>{{cite web |title=Last minute founder in Athens ends Wolfsburg's European dream |url=https://bulinews.com/news/6245/last-minute-founder-athens-ends-wolfsburgs-european-dream |website=Bulinews |access-date=5 February 2021}}</ref>

==Style of play==
At {{convert|1.97|m}}, Weghorst commonly plays as a [[Striker (association football)|striker]], in particular a 'target man' role, due to his ability to hold up the play.<ref>{{cite news |title=Why Burnley signed Wout Weghorst: The 6ft 6in ultra-competitive 'upgrade' on Chris Wood |url=https://theathletic.com/3100034/2022/01/31/why-burnley-signed-wout-weghorst-the-6ft-6in-ultra-competitive-upgrade-on-chris-wood/ |website=theathletic.com |date=31 January 2022 |access-date=8 January 2023}} {{subscription required}} </ref> He is renowned as a [[Glossary of association football terms#G|goal poacher]], with good reactive finishing inside the penalty area,<ref>{{cite news |title=Wout Weghorst: Wolfsburg's Dutch dynamo in attack |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/wout-weghorst-wolfsburg-dutch-striker-dynamo-in-attack-509688.jsp |website=bundesliga.com |access-date=8 January 2023}}</ref> but also regularly drops deep to help link play.<ref>{{cite news |title=Who is Netherlands striker Wout Weghorst?
