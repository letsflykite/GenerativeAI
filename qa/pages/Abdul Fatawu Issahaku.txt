'''Abdul Fatawu Issahaku''' (born 8 March 2004) is a [[Ghana]]ian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as an [[attacking midfielder]] or a [[midfielder#Winger|winger]] for [[Sporting CP]] and the [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national team]].

Issahaku has been described by British news outlet, [[The Guardian]] as arguably the greatest African prospect of his generation and was named in the outlet's "Next Generation 2021.

== Club career ==

=== Steadfast ===
In 2019, at the age of fifteen, Issahaku started his career with [[Tamale, Ghana|Tamale]]-based club [[Steadfast FC]], in the Zone One of the [[Division One League, Ghana|Division One League]], the Ghanaian second tier. During his debut season, he immediately established himself as one of the most talented players in the league, scoring eight goals and assisting five in 13 matches before the league was halted due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Ghana|COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hafiz|first=Mohammed|date=26 March 2020|title=Meet 16 years Division One Goal Sniper Issahaku Abdul Fataw Of Steadfast FC And Former U17 Star.|url=https://ghanasoccernet.com/meet-16-years-division-one-goal-sniper-issahaku-abdul-fataw-of-steadfast-fc-and-former-u17-star|url-status=live|access-date=19 March 2021|website=GhanaSoccernet|language=en|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200327172519/https://ghanasoccernet.com/meet-16-years-division-one-goal-sniper-issahaku-abdul-fataw-of-steadfast-fc-and-former-u17-star |archive-date=27 March 2020 }}</ref>

In his second season, he scored 12 goals and made 12 assists in 14 league matches and picked up 8 [[man of the match]] awards.<ref>{{Cite web|date=3 August 2021|title=Rauf Salifu, Stephen Diyuo and Ernest Kusi lead nominees for the Division One League Updates-Ghana Player of the Season prize.|url=https://www.facebook.com/DivisionOneLeagueUpdateGH/photos/a.601763560205425/1416821942032912/|url-status=live|access-date=14 September 2021|website=Division One League Updates-Ghana}}</ref> In 2021, after the [[2021 Africa U-20 Cup of Nations|Africa U-20 Cup of Nations]], Fatawu was linked with several European teams, being some of them [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]], [[Bayer 04 Leverkusen|Bayer 04 Lervekusen]], and [[Sporting CP]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Osei |first=Bernard Esar Ebo |date=7 April 2022 |title=#Donkomi: Abdul Fatawu Issahaku signs five-year deal with Sporting Club |url=https://citisportsonline.com/2022/04/06/donkomi-abdul-fatawu-issahaku-signs-five-year-deal-with-sporting-club/ |access-date=9 April 2022 |website=Citi Sports Online |language=}}</ref><ref name=":4">{{Cite web |last=Dawes |first=Olly |date=7 April 2022 |title=Wonderkid Abdul Fatawu Issahku won't be signing for Liverpool after all |url=https://www.hitc.com/en-gb/2022/04/07/liverpool-sporting-abdul-fatal-issahaku/ |access-date=10 April 2022 |website=HITC |language=}}</ref> In October 2021, Issahaku joined [[Dreams F.C. (Ghana)|Dreams]] on a loan until 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|date=14 October 2021|title=Ghanaian youngster Abdul Fatawu Issahaku joins Dreams FC on loan- Reports|url=https://ghanasoccernet.com/ghanaian-youngster-abdul-fatawu-issahaku-joins-dreams-fc-on-loan-reports|access-date=4 January 2022|website=GhanaSoccernet|language=en}}</ref> In the same month, he was included in [[The Guardian]]'s "Next Generation 2021", and was described by them as "arguably the best African prospect of his generation".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/ng-interactive/2021/oct/07/next-generation-2021-60-of-the-best-young-talents-in-world-football|title=Next Generation 2021 by The Guardian|website=[[TheGuardian.com]] |access-date=27 March 2022|language=en}}</ref>

=== Sporting CP ===
In April 2022, he was signed by [[Sporting CP]] on a five-year contract with a release clause of 60 million euros.<ref>{{Cite news |date=7 April 2022 |title=Sporting Lisbon sign Ghana teenager Issahaku |language= |work=[[BBC Sport]] |publisher=[[British Broadcasting Corporation]] |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/61023569 |access-date=8 April 2022}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=6 April 2022 |title=Abdul Fatawu Issahaku é Leão |url=https://www.sporting.pt/pt/noticias/futebol/2022-04-06/abdul-fatawu-issahaku-e-leao |access-date=17 April 2022 |website=www.sporting.pt |language=pt-pt}}</ref> This brought finality to rumors of him joining Sporting CP on loan from Liverpool and other transfer saga rumour.<ref name=":4" /> Prior to signing officially Issahaku had been at the club since February and had been training with the U-23 side. As he was an under age player at the time, and with the season already ongoing, he couldn't be registered with the first-team until the 2022–23 season, then, he began to train with the [[Sporting CP Youth Academy|B team]] until available to play for the professional team.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-bwin/sporting/detalhe/prodigio-ganes-contratado-pelo-sporting-ja-trabalha-na-academia|title=Prodígio ganês contratado pelo Sporting já trabalha na Academia|language=pt|access-date=27 March 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-bwin/sporting/detalhe/issahaku-atinge-maioridade|title=Issahaku atinge maioridade|access-date=27 March 2022|language=pt}}</ref>

Ahead of the 2022–23 season, Issahaku was named in the squad for pre-season training and tour for the first team.<ref>{{Cite web |date=17 May 2022 |title='Outstanding talent' Abdul Fatawu Issahaku included in Sporting CP's plans for pre-season |url=https://ghanasoccernet.com/outstanding-talent-abdul-fatawu-issahaku-included-in-sporting-cps-plans-for-pre-season |access-date=14 August 2022 |website=GhanaSoccernet |language=en}}</ref> He was involved in the team's preparations prior to the start of the season playing his first pre-season game against Belgium side [[Royale Union Saint-Gilloise|Royale Union]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=14 July 2022 |title=Sporting CP deploy Fatawu Issahaku in an unfamiliar role during pre-season friendly - MyJoyOnline.com |url=https://www.myjoyonline.com/sporting-cp-deploy-fatawu-issahaku-in-an-unfamiliar-role-during-pre-season-friendly/ |access-date=14 August 2022 |website=www.myjoyonline.com |language=en-US}}</ref> On 7 August 2022, he was named on the bench for the first match of the season. The following weekend he made his debut after coming on in the 80th minute for [[Francisco Trincão]] in Sporting CP's 3–0 victory over [[Rio Ave F.C.|Rio Ave]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=14 August 2022 |title=Sporting CP win first home game of 2022/2023 |url=https://www.sporting.pt/en/news/football/main-team/2022-08-14/sporting-cp-win-first-home-game-of-2022/2023 |access-date=14 August 2022 |website=www.sporting.pt |language=en}}</ref>
