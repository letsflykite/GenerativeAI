'''Yannick Ferreira Carrasco''' (born 4 September 1993) is a Belgian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Midfielder#Winger|winger]] and [[Defender (association football)#Wing-back|wing-back]] for [[La Liga]] club [[Atlético Madrid]] and the [[Belgium national football team|Belgium national team]].

He began his career with [[AS Monaco|Monaco]], where he scored 20 goals in 105 professional games, winning [[Ligue 2]] in his first season and finishing runner-up in [[Ligue 1]] in the second. In 2015, he joined [[La Liga]] side [[Atlético Madrid]] for a reported €20 million, scoring in the [[2016 UEFA Champions League Final|final]] as they were runners-up in the [[UEFA Champions League]]. After a two-year spell with [[Dalian Professional F.C.|Dalian Professional]] in the [[Chinese Super League]], Carrasco returned to Atlético in early 2020 on a loan basis. In September 2020 it was announced that Carrasco would officially transfer to Atletico on a permanent basis and would sign a contract for four years.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://sporza.be/nl/2020/09/08/carrasco-maakt-definitief-overstap-naar-atletico-madrid-voor-4/ |title=Carrasco heeft voldoende indruk gemaakt voor 4-jarig contract bij Atletico |date=8 September 2020 |website=sporza.be}}</ref>

Carrasco made his international debut for Belgium in April 2015. He played at the [[UEFA European Championship]] in [[UEFA Euro 2016|2016]] and [[UEFA Euro 2020|2020]], and was part of their squad that came third at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]].

==Personal life==
Carrasco was born in [[Vilvoorde]] to a Portuguese father and Spanish mother.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hln.be/hln/nl/950/Buitenlands-Voetbal/article/detail/2254012/2015/03/17/Op-bezoek-bij-Ferreira-Carrasco-Graag-wat-meer-erkenning-in-Belgie.dhtml |title=Op bezoek bij Ferreira Carrasco: "Graag wat meer erkenning in België" |publisher=Het Laatste Nieuws |date=17 March 2015 |access-date=17 March 2015}}</ref> His father left the family when Yannick was still a child, leaving his mother, Carmen, to raise him and his brother Mylan.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://as.com/diarioas/2015/10/27/english/1445976534_611051.html |title=Carrasco's troubled path: "I owe it all to my mother" {{!}} English {{!}} AS.com|work=AS.com|access-date=27 May 2018}}</ref> He has two younger half-siblings, Hugo and Celia. While initially known as "Ferreira Carrasco" at the time of his professional debut, he later chose to drop the paternal part of his [[Spanish naming customs|Spanish-style]] double surname.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.intothecalderon.com/2018/3/28/17071696/yannick-carrasco-tantalizing-unfulfilling-spell-atletico-madrid-real-madrid-champions-league-la-liga |title=We'll Always Have Milan: Yannick Carrasco's tantalizing, unfulfilling spell at Atlético Madrid |work=Into the Calderon|access-date=27 May 2018}}</ref> Both his maternal grandparents are from the region of [[Andalusia]]; his maternal grandmother being from [[Seville]], while his maternal grandfather being from [[Córdoba, Spain|Córdoba]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://as.com/futbol/2021/06/25/eurocopa/1624642125_455938.html |title=Yannick Carrasco, en el nombre de la madre ante Portugal |date=26 June 2021}}</ref>

In 2017, Carrasco married former [[Miss Belgium]] [[Noémie Happart]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.nrj.be/articles/noemie-happart-et-yannick-carrasco-se-sont-maries.html |title=Noémie Happart et Yannick Carrasco se sont mariés !}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Monaco===
Carrasco joined [[AS Monaco FC|Monaco]] from Belgian club [[K.R.C. Genk|Genk]] in 2010. He made his professional debut on 30 July 2012 in the opening game of the [[Ligue 2]] [[2012–13 Ligue 2|season]] against [[Tours FC|Tours]], opening a 4–0 victory on the stage 
at the [[Stade Louis II]] via a free kick.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.asm-fc.com/en/player/yannick-ferreira-carrasco-2225.html |title=Yannick Ferreira Carrasco |publisher=AS Monaco |access-date=20 October 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140726161346/http://www.asm-fc.com/en/player/yannick-ferreira-carrasco-2225.html |archive-date=26 July 2014 |url-status=dead}}</ref> On 13 April 2013, he scored both goals of a 2–0 league victory over [[AJ Auxerre|Auxerre]]. In his debut campaign with the club, he appeared in 27 games and scored 6 goals as Monaco won promotion back to [[Ligue 1]].<ref name=L22013>{{cite web |url=http://eurosport.yahoo.com/news/football-monaco-promoted-ligue-1-late-winner-154431768.html |title=Ligue 1 – Monaco promoted to top flight after late winner |publisher=Yahoo Sport |date=11 May 2013 |access-date=20 October 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131024162741/http://eurosport.yahoo.com/news/football-monaco-promoted-ligue-1-late-winner-154431768.html |archive-date=24 October 2013 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

His first top-flight goal came on 5 October 2013 against [[AS Saint-Étienne|Saint-Étienne]], converting a [[James Rodríguez]] cross and helping Monaco to a 2–1 victory. He scored twice in the opening 10 minutes fifteen days later as Monaco drew 2–2 away to [[FC Sochaux-Montbéliard|Sochaux]];<ref>{{cite news |title=Sochaux 2–2 Monaco |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/24601921|access-date=4 June 2016 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=20 October 2013}}</ref> the team finished their first season back at the top as runners up to [[Paris Saint-Germain]].

On 25 February 2015, he scored the last goal of Monaco's 3–1 away win at [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] in the last 16 first leg of the [[2014–15 UEFA Champions League knockout phase|2014–15 UEFA Champions League]], after replacing [[Dimitar Berbatov]] in the 75th minute.<ref>{{cite news |last1=McNulty |first1=Phil |title=Arsenal 1–3 Monaco |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/31608951|access-date=26 February 2015 |work=BBC Sport |date=25 February 2015}}</ref>

===Atlético Madrid===
[[File:Carrasco2016.jpg|180px|thumb|left|Carrasco in 2016]]

On 10 July 2015, [[Atlético Madrid]] announced the signing of Carrasco on a five-year deal for a reported fee of €20 million.<ref>{{cite news |title=Atletico Madrid sign Belgium international Yannick Carrasco from Monaco |url=http://www.foxsports.com/soccer/story/atletico-madrid-sign-belgium-international-yannick-ferreira-carrasco-from-monaco-071015|access-date=12 July 2015 |work=FOX Sports |date=10 July 2015}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.espnfc.com/story/2518900/atletico-madrid-sign-yannick-ferreira-carrasco-from-monaco |title=Atletico Madrid sign Yannick Ferreira-Carrasco from Monaco |website=ESPNFC.com|access-date=17 March 2016}}</ref> On 18 October, he scored his first goal for Atletico in a 2–0 away victory over [[Real Sociedad]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/34549410 |title=Real Sociedad 0-2 Atletico Madrid |work=BBC Sport|access-date=17 March 2016}}</ref>

On 28 May 2016, as a half-time replacement for [[Augusto Fernández]] in the [[2016 UEFA Champions League Final]] at the [[San Siro]], Carrasco scored Atlético's 79th-minute equaliser against [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]]; his team lost in a [[penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shoot-out]]. He was the first Belgian to score in a European Cup final.<ref name=uefa>{{cite news |title=Spot-on Real Madrid defeat Atlético in final again |url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2016/matches/round=2000638/match=2015789/postmatch/report/|access-date=3 June 2016 |publisher=UEFA |date=28 May 2016}}</ref>

On 15 October 2016, he scored his first professional hat-trick in a 7–1 rout of [[Granada CF]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.espnfc.com/atletico-madrid/story/2973719/yannick-carrascos-hard-work-paid-off-with-hat-trick-diego-simeone |title=Yannick Carrasco's hard work paid off with hat trick - Diego Simeone |date=15 October 2016 |publisher=[[ESPN FC]] |access-date=16 October 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.waygoal.net/simeone-carrasco-can-have-a-big-growth/ |title=Carrasco can have a big growth - Diego Simeone |publisher=WAY GOAL |access-date=16 October 2016}}</ref>

===Dalian Professional===
On 26 February 2018, along with teammate [[Nicolás Gaitán]], Carrasco moved to [[Chinese Super League]] newcomers Dalian Yifang (later rebranded as [[Dalian Professional F.C.|Dalian Professional]]),<ref>{{cite press release |date=26 February 2018 |title=Yannick Carrasco to join Dalian Yifang |url=http://en.atleticodemadrid.com/noticias/yannick-carrasco-to-join-dalian-yifang |publisher=Atlético Madrid |access-date=26 February 2018}}</ref> a club owned by ''Atléti's'' partial owner [[Dalian Wanda Group]].{{citation needed|date=March 2019}} He made his debut on 3 March in an 8–0 loss to [[Shanghai SIPG F.C.|Shanghai SIPG]],<ref>{{cite news |title=Chinese Super League {{!}} Oscar awards hat trick as Gaitán and Carrasco feel 8-0 drubbing on CSL debut |url=https://en.as.com/en/2018/03/03/football/1520094916_391994.html |access-date=31 August 2020 |work=AS |date=4 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Shread |first1=Joe |title=Jose Fonte, Yannick Carrasco and Nico Gaitain beaten 8-0 on Dalian Yifang debut |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/11274622/jose-fonte-yannick-carrasco-and-nico-gaitain-beaten-8-0-on-dalian-yifang-debut |website=Sky Sports |access-date=31 August 2020 |date=3 March 2018}}</ref> and scored his first goal in his fourth match for the club on 31 March, in a 1–1 away draw with [[Henan Jianye F.C.|Henan Jianye]], ending his club's season-opening three-game losing streak.<ref>{{cite news |date=31 March 2018 |title=Henan Jianye 1-1 Dalian Yifang |url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2018/03/31/china-pr/csl/henan-jianye/dalian-aerbin-fc/2738497/ |website=soccerway |access-date=2 April 2018}}</ref>

===Return to Atlético Madrid ===
On 31 January 2020, Carrasco returned to Atlético Madrid on loan until the end of the [[2019–20 Atlético Madrid season|season]].<ref>{{cite press release |date=31 January 2020 |title=Agreement with Dalian Professional FC for the loan of Yannick Carrasco |url=https://en.atleticodemadrid.com/noticias/agreement-with-dalian-professional-fc-for-the-loan-of-yannick-carrasco |publisher=Atlético Madrid |access-date=31 January 2020}}</ref> On 8 September 2020, he rejoined the club on a permanent basis, signing a four-year contract.<ref>{{cite press release |date=8 September 2020 |title=Agreement with Dalian Professional FC over the transfer of Yannick Carrasco |url=https://en.atleticodemadrid.com/noticias/agreement-with-dalian-professional-fc-over-the-transfer-of-yannick-carrasco |publisher=Atlético Madrid |access-date=8 September 2020}}</ref> On 21 November 2020, he scored the only goal in a 1–0 victory over [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2020/nov/21/atletico-madrid-barcelona-la-liga-match-report |title=Atlético Madrid's Carrasco punishes Ter Stegen to increase Barcelona woe |website=The Guardian |date=21 November 2020}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:2017 RUS v BEL exhibition - Yannick Ferreira Carrasco.jpg|150px|thumb|right|Exhibition match against [[Russia national football team|Russia]] in [[Sochi]] (2017)]]
Carrasco made his senior international debut in March 2015, as a 69th-minute substitute for [[Marouane Fellaini]] in a 5–0 win over [[Cyprus national football team|Cyprus]] in [[UEFA Euro 2016]] [[UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying|qualification]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Burton |first1=Chris |title=Carrasco returns to Europe to re-sign for Atletico Madrid on short-term loan |url=https://www.goal.com/en-sa/news/carrasco-returns-to-europe-to-re-sign-for-atletico-madrid-on/1ezj3k26ilprp1lgrpanthsqbm |website=goal.com |access-date=31 August 2020 |date=31 January 2020}}</ref> He was named in manager [[Marc Wilmots]]' squad for the final tournament.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Scholten |first1=Berend |title=UEFA EURO reporter's view: Belgium |url=http://www.uefa.com/news/newsid=2370390.html|access-date=4 June 2016 |publisher=UEFA |date=1 June 2016}}</ref> On 26 June, in the last 16 in [[Toulouse]], he scored his first international goal to conclude a 4–0 win over [[Hungary national football team|Hungary]], after replacing [[Dries Mertens]] in the second half.<ref>{{cite news |title=Hungary 0-4 Belgium |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/36569531|access-date=26 June 2016 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=27 June 2016}}</ref>

Carrasco was included in the Belgian squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] by manager [[Roberto Martínez]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Fifa World Cup 2018: Chinese Super League stars on show but Ryan Giggs highlights struggles for players moving to mainland |url=http://www.scmp.com/sport/china/article/2152134/fifa-world-cup-2018-chinese-super-league-stars-show-ryan-giggs |publisher=South China Morning Post |access-date=23 June 2018 |date=23 June 2018}}</ref> He made his debut in the opening group stage victory over [[Panama national football team|Panama]] and was deployed as an attacking [[Defender (association football)#Wing-back|left wing back]] in a 3–4–3 formation.<ref>{{cite web |title=World Cup 2018: Five things we learned from Belgium's opening win over debutants Panama |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/world-cup/world-cup-2018-belgium-panama-lukaku-goals-dries-mertens-a8405106.html |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/archive/20220507/https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/world-cup/world-cup-2018-belgium-panama-lukaku-goals-dries-mertens-a8405106.html |archive-date=7 May 2022 |url-access=subscription |url-status=live |work=The Independent |access-date=23 June 2018 |date=19 June 2018}}{{cbignore}}</ref>
