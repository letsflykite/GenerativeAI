'''Matías Nicolás Viña ''' (born 9 November 1997) is an Uruguayan professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[left-back]] for [[Serie A]] club [[A.S. Roma|Roma]] and the [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay national team]].<ref>{{Soccerway|matias-nicolas-vina-susperreguy/471082}}</ref>

==Club career==
Viña was signed by [[Club Nacional de Football|Nacional]] in 2015 when he was 17 years old. He joined the Fourth Division and had the opportunity to practice with the professional team.<ref>{{cite web|first=Sebastián|last=Amaya|url=http://www.referi.uy/una-nueva-generacion-cracks-n1024304|title=¿Una nueva generación de cracks?|website=Referí|date=2 February 2017|access-date=4 February 2017|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.tenfield.com.uy/nacional-de-pena-en-el-clasico-de-las-amarillas/|title=Nacional: clásico en la mira con De Pena|website=Tenfield|date=27 April 2015|language=es}}</ref> One year later, Viña achieved his first minutes with the club's reserve team, having played eight games in the Third Division.<ref>{{cite web March 2021}}</ref> On 31 January 2020, Viña left the club to join [[Campeonato Brasileiro Série A|Série A]] side [[Palmeiras]], where he won the [[Treble (association football)|treble]]<ref>{{Cite On 30 January 2023, Viña joined [[Premier League]] club [[AFC Bournemouth]] on loan until the end of the season, with an option to buy for €15 million.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-01-30 |title=Viña makes Cherries move |url=https://www.afcb.co.uk/news/first-team/viña-makes-cherries-move/ |access-date=2023-06-24 |website=AFCB}}</ref> On 15 April, he scored his first goal for the club following his first ever start, coming in a 3–2 away win at [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Tottenham 2-3 Bournemouth: Dango Ouattara's stoppage-time winner gives Cherries shock dramatic win at Spurs |url=https://www.skysports.com/amp/football/news/11095/12855772/tottenham-2-3-bournemouth-dango-ouattaras-stoppage-time-winner-gives-cherries-shock-dramatic-win-at-spurs |access-date=2023-06-24 |website=Sky Sports |language=en}}</ref>

==International career==
Making his debut in 2016 for the [[Uruguay national under-20 football team|Uruguay under-20 team]] in a friendly against [[Chile national under-20 football team|Chile]], Viña played in both the [[2017 South American U-20 Championship]], coming first, and in the 2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup, finishing in fourth place. He played 27 games for the youth selection, scoring four times.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.auf.org.uy/matias-vina-en-su-primera-experiencia-con-la-seleccion-mayor/|title=Entrevista a Matías Viña - AUF|website=www.auf.org.uy|access-date=17 January 2020}}</ref>

Viña made his senior international debut for [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay]] on 6 September 2019, coming on from the bench in a [[Exhibition game|friendly]] against [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ovaciondigital.com.uy/futbol/dejaron-debut-brian-rodriguez-matias-vina-uruguay.html|title=Qué dejaron los debut de Brian Rodríguez y Matías Viña en Uruguay|last=ElPais|website=Diario EL PAIS Uruguay|language=spanish|access-date=17 January 2020}}</ref> His debut as a starter came four days later, in a friendly against the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]]; Viña played the whole 90 minutes in a 1–1 draw.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.elobservador.com.uy/nota/matias-vina-rindio-examen-y-aprobo-es-una-opcion-para-el-lateral-izquierdo-20199110252|title=Matías Viña rindió examen y aprobó: es una opción para el lateral izquierdo|last=Observador|first=El|website=El Observador|access-date=18 January 2020}}</ref>
