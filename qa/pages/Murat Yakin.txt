'''Murat Yakin''' ({{lang-tr|Yakƒ±n}}; born 15 September 1974) is a Swiss [[association football|football]] coach and former player. He is the manager of the [[Switzerland national football team]].

== Playing career ==
Yakin spent the longest spell of his career playing for his hometown club [[FC Basel]], where he was the defensive linchpin, captain and libero of a team which enjoyed domestic and relative European success. He won the [[Swiss Super League]] on five occasions (1995, 1996, 2002, 2004, 2005), and the [[Swiss Cup]] three times (1994, 2002, 2003). He recalls the [[2002‚Äì03 UEFA Champions League#Third qualifying round|2002‚Äì03 Champions League second leg qualifying match]] on 28 August 2002 against [[Celtic F.C.|Celtic]] in [[St. Jakob-Park]] as the "match of his life". Basel won the game 2‚Äì0, with Yakin scoring the second goal in the 22-minute as Basel qualified 3‚Äì3 on the [[away goals rule]] for the [[2002‚Äì03 UEFA Champions League]] [[2002‚Äì03 UEFA Champions League group stage#Group B|group stage]].<ref name="Das Spiel meines Lebens">{{Cite book  | last1 = Widmer | first1 = Benedikt | last2 = Mugglin | first2 = David | title = Das Spiel meines Lebens | publisher = rotweiss Verlag GmbH, Basel | year = 2011 | isbn = 978-3-7245-1785-6|language=de}}</ref>

In 2003, he played in the [[FIFA]] "Match against Poverty" in Basel, on [[Ronaldo (Brazilian footballer)|Ronaldo's]] team which won 4‚Äì3 against [[Zinedine Zidane]]'s.<ref>[https://www.fifa.com/classicfootball/awards/gala/news/newsid=90283.html] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091226020238/http://www.fifa.com/classicfootball/awards/gala/news/newsid%3D90283.html |date=26 December 2009 }}</ref>

Yakin was [[cap (sport)|capped]] 49 times for the [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland national team]], representing his country at [[UEFA Euro 2004]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.worldfootball.net/teams/schweiz-team/em-2004-in-portugal/2/|title=Switzerland ¬ª Squad EURO 2004 Portugal|publisher=World Football|access-date=29 July 2019}}</ref>

==Coaching career==
===Early career===
Yakin was appointed coach of [[FC Thun]] in 2009 after a time as a youth coach at [[Grasshopper Club Z√ºrich|Grasshoppers]] and head coach of [[FC Frauenfeld]].<ref>{{WorldFootball.net|murat-yakin}}</ref> Yakin led Thun to promotion in his first year as manager there, winning the [[Swiss Challenge League]] in 2010.<ref name="Thun">{{cite web|url=http://liberoguide.com/fc-thun/|title=FC Thun|publisher=Libero Guide|access-date=29 July 2019}}</ref> In his second season with the club, Thun managed to finish 5th in the [[2010‚Äì11 Swiss Super League|Swiss Super League]] and earned a spot in the [[2011‚Äì12 UEFA Europa League#Second qualifying round|second qualifying round]] of the following season's UEFA Europa League.<ref name="Thun" />

In May 2011, he joined [[FC Luzern]] for a reported 200,000 Swiss francs.{{Citation needed|date=October 2011}} He took over from [[Christian Brand]], who was made caretaker after the sacking of [[Rolf Fringer]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://justcantbeatthat.com/2011/fc-yakin-the-club-formerly-known-as-fc-luzern/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161127023436/http://justcantbeatthat.com/2011/fc-yakin-the-club-formerly-known-as-fc-luzern/|url-status=dead|archive-date=27 November 2016|title=justcantbeatthat.com}}</ref>

===Basel===
On 15 October 2012, Yakin was appointed as the new manager of FC Basel.<ref name="Vogel fliegt ‚Äì Yakin √ºbernimmt">{{Cite news |url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/intligen/startseite/576290/artikel_vogel-fliegt---yakin-uebernimmt.html |title=Vogel fliegt ‚Äì Yakin √ºbernimmt |publisher=Kicker |date=15 October 2012 |access-date=15 October 2012 |language=de}}</ref> Under his management, Basel won twice, home and away, against [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] in the [[2013‚Äì14 UEFA Champions League|2013‚Äì14 Champions League]] [[2013‚Äì14 UEFA Champions League group stage|group stage]]. He praised the supporters in the stadium, saying that they pushed the team to win through the 90 minutes. He was also delighted to have received praise from then Chelsea manager [[Jos√© Mourinho]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sportsmole.co.uk/football/chelsea/champions-league/news/yakin-delighted-with-mourinho-praise_120517.html|title=Yakin delighted with Mourinho praise|website=Sports Mole}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.theguardian.com/football/2013/nov/26/basel-chelsea-champions-league-match-report|title=Basel 1-0 Chelsea &#124; Champions League Group E match report|date=26 November 2013|website=the Guardian}}</ref> On 17 May 2014, FC Basel announced Yakin was no longer with the club after he guided them to two domestic titles in as many years.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.fcb.ch/news/show/byItemID/de-de/10044/32243/16/7 |title=fcb.ch |access-date=17 May 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140521001113/http://www.fcb.ch/news/show/byItemID/de-de/10044/32243/16/7 |archive-date=21 May 2014 |url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="Spartak" />

=== Spartak Moscow ===
On 16 June 2014, Yakin was appointed as manager of [[Russian Premier League]] side [[FC Spartak Moscow|Spartak Moscow]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[FC Spartak Moscow]]|url=http://spartak.com/main/news/55905/|script-title=ru:–ú–£–†–ê–¢ –Ø–ö–ò–ù ‚Äì –ù–û–í–´–ô –ì–õ–ê–í–ù–´–ô –¢–†–ï–ù–ï–† ¬´–°–ü–ê–†–¢–ê–ö–ê¬ª|date=16 June 2014|language=ru}}</ref> According to Russian media, the deal was a long-term contract with Yakin earning an annual salary of 1.6 million euros ($2.18 million).<ref name="Spartak" />

On 30 May 2015, after only one season with the club, his contract with the club was terminated after a mutual agreement.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[FC Spartak Moscow]]|url=http://spartak.com/main/news/newsautoupdate/|script-title=ru:¬´–°–ü–ê–†–¢–ê–ö¬ª –ò –Ø–ö–ò–ù –†–ê–°–¢–û–†–ì–ê–Æ–¢ –û–¢–ù–û–®–ï–ù–ò–Ø –ü–û –û–ë–û–Æ–î–ù–û–ú–£ –°–û–ì–õ–ê–°–ò–Æ|date=30 May 2015|language=ru}}</ref>

=== Return to FC Schaffhausen ===
On 17 June 2019, it was confirmed, that Yakin had returned to [[FC Schaffhausen]] as their new manager having left [[FC Sion]] previously that year.<ref>[https://www.nzz.ch/sport/murat-yakin-ist-wieder-trainer-beim-fc-schaffhausen-ld.1489444 Murat Yakin ist wieder Trainer beim FC Schaffhausen], nzz.ch, 17 June 2019</ref>

=== Switzerland national team ===
On 9 August 2021, Yakin was appointed as the manager of the [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland national team]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=swissinfo.ch/ug|title=New manager for Swiss national football team appointed|url=https://www.swissinfo.ch/eng/society/new-coach-for-swiss-national-football-team-appointed/46853490|access-date=9 August 2021|website=SWI swissinfo.ch}}</ref> In the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)|2022 World Cup qualification]], [[Northern Ireland national football team|Northern Ireland]] held [[UEFA Euro 2020|reigning European champions]] [[Italy national football team|Italy]] to a goalless draw, thereby ensuring that Switzerland qualified for the tournament and Italy had to play in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification ‚Äì UEFA second round|playoffs]]. In gratitude, he sent 9.3 kg of [[Swiss chocolate]] to the [[Irish Football Association]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/nati_sfv_asf/status/1463537899744419846| title= We kept our word, @NorthernIreland üòâ A sweet Thank You is on its way! #sweetcaroline #sweetchocolate|publisher=Twitter|date=24 November 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Fullerton |first1=Gareth |title=Switzerland boss Murat Yakin sends thank-you chocolates to Northern Ireland |url=https://www.belfastlive.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/switzerland-boss-murat-yakin-sends-22273217 |access-date=19 June 2022 |work=Belfast Live |date=25 November 2021}}</ref> In the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], he led Switzerland to finish second in their group, in order to qualify to the round of 16, where they lost 6‚Äì1 to [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63789753|title=Portugal 6‚Äì1 Switzerland|website=BBC Sport|date=6 December 2022}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Yakin is the elder brother of [[Hakan Yakin]], who was also a professional footballer with successful stints at [[Grasshopper Club Z√ºrich|Grasshoppers]], [[FC Basel|Basel]], and [[BSC Young Boys]] and represented [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]] at international level also.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/sport/a-family-affair-in-need-of-high-maintenance-1.381867|title=A family affair in need of high maintenance|newspaper=The Irish Times|date=8 October 2003|access-date=29 July 2019}}</ref> Yakin is of [[Turks in Switzerland|Turkish]] descent.
