'''Robert Lynch Sánchez''' (born 18 November 1997) is a Spanish professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Premier League]] club [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]] and the [[Spain national football team|Spain national team]]. Sánchez came through Albion's [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C. Reserves and Academy|youth academy]].

==Club career==
===Early life and career===
Sánchez was born in [[Cartagena, Spain|Cartagena]], [[Region of Murcia]].<ref name="Soccerway"/> He was born to a Jamaican-English father and Spanish mother.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8NPQtvt0dlc|title=Robert Sanchez - What is the deal?|via=www.youtube.com}}</ref> He spent his early career playing locally with Escuela de Fútbol de Santa Ana, [[Cartagena FC]] and Ciudad Jardín<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.laverdad.es/deportes/futbol/cartagenero-robert-sanchez-20201102004342-ntvo.html |title=El cartagenero Robert Sánchez debuta en la Premier League inglesa |trans-title=Cartagena's Robert Sánchez debuts in the English Premier League |first=Rubén |last=Serrano |newspaper=La Verdad |date=2 November 2020 |access-date=28 March 2022 |language=es}}</ref> before joining [[Levante UD|Levante]] in 2010.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://eldesmarque.com/valencia/levante-ud/288379-robert-sanchez-de-las-categorias-inferiores-del-levante-a-la-seleccion |title=Robert Sánchez, de las categorías inferiores del Levante a la selección |date=15 March 2021 |website=ElDesmarque Valencia}}</ref>

===Brighton & Hove Albion===
Sánchez moved to England to sign for [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]] at the age of 15, before signing his first professional contract in June 2015.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/teams/mens-team/players/14538/robert-sanchez |title=Profile|access-date=1 November 2020 |publisher=Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.}}</ref><ref name = "revealed">{{cite web |url=http://www.theargus.co.uk/sport/15404319.Revealed__The_teenager_on_tour_with_Albion_in_Austria/ |title=Revealed: Everything you need to know about Brighton and Hove Albion's new kid on the block Robert Sanchez |date=12 July 2017|access-date=18 June 2018 |author=Andy Naylor |newspaper=The Argus}}</ref> He signed a new three-year contract with the club in April 2018.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.theargus.co.uk/sport/16174000.New_deal_for_Albion_keeper_Sanchez/ |title=Albion keeper Robert Sanchez signs new contract |date=20 April 2018|access-date=18 June 2018 |author=Andy Naylor |newspaper=The Argus}}</ref>

====Loan spells====
In June 2018, Sánchez moved on [[Loan (association football)|loan]] to [[Forest Green Rovers F.C.|Forest Green Rovers]] for the 2018–19 season.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44502343 |title=Robert Sanchez: Forest Green Rovers sign Brighton goalkeeper on loan |date=15 June 2018|access-date=18 June 2018 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> He played in the opening game of the season making his debut away at [[Grimsby Town F.C.|Grimsby]] where Rovers won 4–1.<ref name="sb1819"/> Sánchez was recalled by parent club Brighton in January 2019 to provide cover as Albion's [[Mathew Ryan]] was called up for [[Australia men's national soccer team|Australia]] for the [[2019 AFC Asian Cup|Asian Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fgr.co.uk/news/january-2019/robert-sanchez-returns-to-brighton |title=Robert Sánchez returns to Brighton |publisher=Forest Green Rovers F.C. |date=4 January 2019 |access-date=5 August 2019}}</ref> He made 17 appearances for Forest Green that season, all coming in the league.<ref name="sb1819"/>

On 24 July 2019, Sánchez joined [[Rochdale A.F.C.|Rochdale]] on loan for the [[2019–20 Rochdale A.F.C. season|2019–20 season]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/49103384 |title=Robert Sanchez: Rochdale sign Brighton goalkeeper on loan |date=24 July 2019 |website=BBC Sport|access-date=24 July 2019}}</ref> He made his debut for the club in a 3–2 away win over [[Tranmere Rovers F.C.|Tranmere Rovers]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49135859 |title=Tranmere 2–3 Rochdale |work=BBC Sport |access-date=13 August 2019}}</ref>

====Return to Brighton====
On 1 November 2020, Sánchez made his Brighton debut, playing in a 2–1 defeat in a [[Premier League]] match against [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54679345 |title=Tottenham Hotspur 2–1 Brighton & Hove Albion: Gareth Bale scores his first goal since re–joining Spurs |website=BBC Sport |date=1 November 2020 |access-date=1 November 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.theargus.co.uk/sport/18838938.robert-sanchez-talks-premier-league-debut-albion/ |title=Sanchez says he only had 'good nerves' before Premier League debut |newspaper=The Argus}}</ref> On 23 February 2021, Sánchez signed a new four-and-a-half-year contract with Brighton, running until June 2025.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11741/12226924/robert-sanchez-brighton-goalkeeper-signs-new-deal-until-june-2025 |title=Sanchez signs new Brighton deal until June 2025 |website=Sky Sports |date=23 February 2021|access-date=26 February 2021}}</ref>

Sánchez was sent off in the 1–1 home draw against [[Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United]] on 6 November, in Brighton's eleventh match of the [[2021–22 Brighton & Hove Albion F.C. season|2021–22 season]], for fouling [[Callum Wilson (footballer, born 1992)|Callum Wilson]] who was within a goalscoring opportunity.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/59099963 |title=Brighton & Hove Albion 1–1 Newcastle United: Magpies fight back to draw as Eddie Howe watches from the stands |website=BBC Sport |date=6 November 2021 |access-date=6 November 2021}}</ref> He kept a clean sheet in the 4–0 win over [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] on 7 May 2022, helping Brighton earn their record top flight victory, with his long-range pass providing a crucial role in [[Pascal Groß]]'s goal.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/61281254|title=Brighton 4-0 Manchester United: Big defeat ends United's Champions League hopes |website= BBC Sport|date=7 May 2022|access-date=7 May 2022}}</ref>

In the second half of the [[2022–23 Brighton & Hove Albion F.C. season|2022–23 season]] Sánchez lost his number one spot to long standing back-up keeper [[Jason Steele (footballer)|Jason Steele]], with Brighton manager [[Roberto De Zerbi]] saying that "he is sad for Robert" and that he has "one of the best relationships" with the player.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.90min.com/posts/jason-steele-sunderland-till-i-die-brighton-charge-europe|title=Jason Steele: From Sunderland Till I Die to Brighton's charge for Europe|publisher=90 min|date=7 March 2023|access-date=15 March 2023}}</ref> He was selected to play instead of Steele on 19 March, keeping a clean sheet in the 5–0 [[2022–23 FA Cup|FA Cup quarter-final]] win over [[EFL League Two|League Two]] side [[Grimsby Town F.C.|Grimsby]] at Falmer Stadium.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/3116523/wembley-next-as-albion-cruise-into-fa-cup-semi-finals|title=Wembley next as Albion cruise into FA Cup semi-finals|website=Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|date=19 March 2023|access-date=15 April 2023}}</ref> On 15 April, with Steele injured, Sánchez played against [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] in a 2–1 away win.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/3151576/albions-super-subs-get-european-push-back-on-track|title=Albion's super subs get European push back on track|publisher=Brighton & Hove Albion|date=15 April 2023|access-date=15 April 2023}}</ref> Eight days later he retained his place, keeping a clean sheet against [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] in 120 minutes of play in the FA Cup semi-final at [[Wembley stadium|Wembley]], but failing to save a penalty in the eventual shootout loss.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/3231077/its-wembley-heartbreak-as-albion-are-beaten-on-penalties|title=It's Wembley heartbreak as Albion are beaten on penalties|website=Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|date=23 April 2023|access-date=24 April 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
Sánchez was born in Spain to an English father and Spanish mother.<ref name="lavozdigital 2021">{{cite web |title=Cádiz CF: Robert Sánchez, el flamante portero que empezó de la mano de Jorge Cordero |website=La Voz Digital |date=24 March 2021 |url=https://www.lavozdigital.es/canal-amarillo/noticias-cadiz-cf/lvdi-cadiz-robert-sanchez-flamante-portero-empezo-mano-jorge-cordero-202103241203_noticia.html |language=es | access-date=26 March 2021}}</ref> Sánchez received his first call-up to the [[Spain national football team|Spanish national team]] in March 2021, for [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification]] matches against [[Greece national football team|Greece]], [[Georgia national football team|Georgia]] and [[Kosovo national football team|Kosovo]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/2068680/sanchez-named-in-spain-squad |title=Sanchez named in Spain squad |publisher=Brighton & Hove Albion F.C. |date=15 March 2021|access-date=15 March 2021}}</ref>

He was selected to the Spain squad for the delayed [[UEFA Euro 2020]] tournament in May 2021.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/sergio-ramos-euro-2020-spain-squad-b1852746.html |title=Sergio Ramos left out of Spain's Euro 2020 squad |date=24 May 2021 |newspaper=The Independent}}</ref> Sánchez and [[David de Gea]] remained as understudies to [[Unai Simón]] and did not make an appearance as Spain were knocked out on penalties against [[Italy national football team|Italy]] in the semi-final at [[Wembley Stadium]] on 6 July.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/51198738 |title=Euro 2020: Italy beat Spain on penalties to reach final – BBC Sport |date=7 July 2021 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

He made his international debut on 5 September 2021, replacing Simón in the second half of a World Cup qualifier 4–0 win against Georgia in [[Badajoz]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.mundodeportivo.com/seleccion-espanola/20210905/1001685087/robert-sanchez-debuta-espana.html |newspaper=Mundo Deportivo |title=Robert Sánchez debuta con España |date=5 September 2021}}</ref>

Sánchez was part of Spain's [[2021 UEFA Nations League Finals]] squad in October.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/2284275/albion-players-in-international-action/ |title=Albion players in international action |publisher=Brighton & Hove Albion F.C. |date=6 October 2021 |access-date=10 October 2021}}</ref> He remained as back-up and did not make an appearance in either of the semi-final victory over Italy or the final loss against [[France national football team|France]] as Spain finished as runners-up.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58820411 |title=Italy 1–2 Spain: La Roja end Azzurri's long unbeaten run to reach Nations League final |website=BBC Sport |date=6 October 2021 |access-date=10 October 2021}}</ref><ref name="auto">{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58859225 |title=France beat Spain to become second Nations League winners |website=BBC Sport |date=10 October 2021 |access-date=10 October 2021}}</ref>

On 11 November 2022, Sánchez was named in Spain's 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63598679|title=World Cup 2022: Laporte and Rodri in squad but Thiago and De Gea miss out |website=BBC Sport |date=11 November 2022 |access-date=11 November 2022}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Sánchez has said that he modelled himself on Spanish goalkeepers [[Iker Casillas]] and [[David de Gea]].<ref name = "revealed"/>
