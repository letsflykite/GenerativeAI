{{nihongo|'''Yuto Nagatomo'''|長友 佑都|Nagatomo Yūto|born 12 September 1986}} is a Japanese professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|full back]] for Japanese club [[FC Tokyo]] and the [[Japan national football team|Japan national team]].<ref name="Soccerway"/>

A graduate from the [[Meiji University]], Nagatomo started his professional career with [[F.C. Tokyo|FC Tokyo]] in 2007,<ref name=":0" /> quickly establishing himself as a first choice both within the team and the Japanese national team. He would go on to play in [[UEFA|Europe]] for over a decade, first joining [[A.C. Cesena|Cesena]] in 2010, and then enjoying stints with [[Inter Milan]] (where he played for seven years, making more than 200 total appearances and even [[Captain (association football)|captaining]] the team for a brief period of time),<ref>{{cite web|title=Il filo di Roma-Inter: Yuto Nagatomo|url=https://www.vocegiallorossa.it/il-filo-di/il-filo-di-roma-inter-yuto-nagatomo-106218|access-date=29 December 2021|website=Voce Giallorossa|language=it}}</ref> [[Galatasaray S.K. (football)|Galatasaray]] and [[Olympique Marseille]]. He would then go back to his boyhood club [[F.C. Tokyo|FC Tokyo]] in September 2021.<ref>{{cite news|date=12 September 2021|title=ニュース｜FC東京オフィシャルホームページ|url=http://www.fctokyo.co.jp/news/12771|access-date=29 December 2021|publisher=FC Tokyo|language=ja}}</ref>

During his club career, he won a [[J.League Cup]] in 2009 (with Tokyo), a [[Coppa Italia]] in 2011 (with Inter Milan), two back-to-back [[Süper Lig|Turkish titles]] in 2018 and 2019, both a [[Turkish Cup]] and a [[Turkish Super Cup]] in 2019 (all with Galatasaray). He also won an individual award as [[Asian Football Confederation|AFC]]'s [[Asian Footballer of the Year#AFC Asian International Player of the Year|Asian International Player of the Year]] in 2013.<ref>{{Cite web|date=26 November 2013|title=AFC Asian International Player of the Year: Yuto Nagatomo|url=https://www.the-afc.com/en/about_afc/afc_annual_awards/news/afc_asian_international_player_of_the_yearyuto_nagatomo.html|access-date=29 December 2021|website=AFC}}</ref>

With 142 caps (as well as four goals), Nagatomo is [[Japan national football team|Japan]]'s second-most capped player of all time, behind only [[Yasuhito Endō]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Japan - Record International Players|url=https://www.rsssf.org/miscellaneous/jap-recintlp.html|access-date=29 December 2021|website=RSSSF}}</ref> Having won his first cap for the ''Samurai Blue'' in 2008, just some months after making his professional debut with FC Tokyo, the full-back has represented Japan at one edition of the [[Olympic Games]] (in [[2008 Summer Olympics|2008]]), four consecutive [[FIFA World Cup|World Cups]] (in [[2010 FIFA World Cup|2010]], [[2014 FIFA World Cup|2014]], [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022]]), three consecutive [[AFC Asian Cup|Asian Cups]] (in [[2011 AFC Asian Cup|2011]], [[2015 AFC Asian Cup|2015]] and [[2019 AFC Asian Cup|2019]]) and one [[FIFA Confederations Cup|Confederations Cup]] (in [[2013 FIFA Confederations Cup|2013]]). He was part both of the squad that won the Asian Cup in [[2011 AFC Asian Cup|2011]] and the one that finished as runners-up in the same competition in [[2019 AFC Asian Cup|2019]].

==Club career==
===FC Tokyo===
Nagatomo officially signed with [[F.C. Tokyo|FC Tokyo]] in 2008,<ref name=":0">{{cite news|url=http://www.fctokyo.co.jp/home/index.phtml?cont=item&item=5642|script-title=ja:長友佑都選手(明治大学) 来季新加入内定のお知らせ
|trans-title=Nagatomo Yuto player (Meiji University) (is) next season's new signing|date=19 December 2007|access-date=31 January 2011|work=FC Tokyo|language= ja}}</ref> while at Meiji. But he made an appearance at [[J.League Cup]] as Special Designated Youth Player in 2007. Nagatomo made 84 appearances in his four years at FC Tokyo.

===Cesena===
In July 2010, Nagatomo was signed on loan by the newly promoted [[Serie A]] side [[A.C. Cesena|Cesena]], where he played the first half of the season as Cesena's starting left back.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.cesenacalcio.it/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=3527:il-difensore-giapponese-yuto-nagatomo-al-cesena&catid=4:comunicato-stampa&Itemid=121&lang=it|title=Il difensore giapponese Yuto Nagatomo al Cesena|date=14 July 2010|access-date=16 July 2010|work=AC Cesena|language=it}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.fctokyo.co.jp/home/index.phtml?cont=item&item=9761|script-title=ja:長友佑都選手 チェゼーナへ期限付き移籍決定のお知らせ|trans-title=Player Nagatomo Yuto to Cesena in period confirmed|date=14 July 2010|access-date=31 January 2011|work=FC Tokyo|language=ja}}</ref> Before moving to Italy, Nagatomo gave a speech to 25,000 supporters just after the match involving [[F.C. Tokyo]] and [[Vissel Kobe]] on 17 July 2010. After being signed on a permanent deal, Nagatomo was then sold to Internazionale.

===Internazionale===
[[File:Yuto Nagatomo 2011.jpg|160px|thumb|Nagatomo with Inter in 2011]]

Due to his impressive performances for both Cesena and [[Japan national football team|Japan]] in the [[2011 AFC Asian Cup|2011 Asian Cup]], he was fully signed by Cesena in January 2011.<ref>{{cite news |title=Cesena, Nagatomo è tutto bianconero |url=http://www.tuttomercatoweb.com/?action=read&id=250851 |author=Emanuele Melfi |work=tuttomercatoweb.com |date=28 January 2011 |access-date=31 January 2011 |language=it}}</ref> But before making any league appearances he was sent straight out on loan to [[Inter Milan|Internazionale]] in exchange for [[Davide Santon]] also on loan, becoming the first East Asian player to sign with Internazionale.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.cesenacalcio.it/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=4434:davide-santon-e-del-cesena-nagatomo-allinter&catid=4:comunicato-stampa&Itemid=121&lang=it|title=Davide Santon è del Cesena, Nagatomo all'Inter|trans-title=Davide Santon to Cesena, Nagatomo to Inter|date=31 January 2011|access-date=31 January 2011|work=AC Cesena|language=it|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171214071746/http://www.cesenacalcio.it/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=4434:davide-santon-e-del-cesena-nagatomo-allinter&catid=4:comunicato-stampa&Itemid=121&lang=it|archive-date=14 December 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.inter.it/aas/news/reader?N=35718&L=en|title=Yuto Nagatomo joins Inter |date=31 January 2011|access-date=31 January 2011|publisher=F.C. Internazionale Milano}}</ref>

Nagatomo, wearing number 55, made his debut for ''Nerazzurri'' on 6 February 2011, replacing [[Wesley Sneijder]] in the Serie A match against [[A.S. Roma|Roma]], won by Inter 5–3.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/match/internazionale-vs-roma/990188/report|title=Inter 5-3 Roma: Samuel Eto'o And The Nerazzurri Hold Off A Spirited Roman Comeback|publisher=Goal.com|author=Andrew McLean|date=6 February 2011|access-date=6 February 2011}}</ref> In the following game, Nagatomo came on as a late substitute against [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]], before starting in both of the following games against [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]] and [[Cagliari Calcio|Cagliari]]. Nagatomo showed his versatility by playing right back against [[U.C. Sampdoria|Sampdoria]] on 27 February, and finishing the whole 90 minutes.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.whoscored.com/Matches/443732/Live/Italy-Serie-A-2010-2011-Sampdoria-Inter|title=Nagatomo playing at right full back|publisher=WhoScored.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/35939|title=Sampdoria 0-2 Inter: Sneijder and Eto'o|publisher=inter.it|date=27 February 2011|access-date=3 September 2016}}</ref> On 6 March 2011, Nagatomo scored his first goal for Inter in the 5–2 trashing of [[Genoa C.F.C.|Genoa]] on a [[Houssine Kharja|Kharja]] assist with a swivel and a finish into the roof of the net.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2011/03/06/italy/serie-a/fc-internazionale-milano/genoa-cfc/990227/|title=Internazionale vs. Genoa 2 – 0|work=Soccerway|date=6 March 2011|access-date=3 September 2016}}</ref>

On 15 March, to show solidarity with his home country in light of the [[2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami|catastrophic earthquake and tsunami]] which took place the previous week he paraded the [[Japanese flag]] (with the writing "[[You'll Never Walk Alone]]", a chant of his former team in Tokyo) around the pitch during a [[2010–11 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] match against [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://jp.reuters.com/news/pictures/articleslideshow?articleId=JPJAPAN-20069620110316&channelName=jp_quake#a=1lang=Ja|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110715175315/http://jp.reuters.com/news/pictures/articleslideshow?articleId=JPJAPAN-20069620110316&channelName=jp_quake#a=1lang=Ja|url-status=dead|archive-date=15 July 2011|title=長友が日の丸に「心は一つ」、欧州CLで被災地へメッセージ|date=16 March 2011|access-date=16 March 2011|work=Reuters|language=ja}}</ref>
Inter had drawn German club side [[FC Schalke 04|Schalke 04]] in the quarter-finals of Champions League. Nagatomo, along with international teammate [[Atsuto Uchida]], who played for Schalke, became the second and third Japanese footballer to play in the quarter-finals of the UEFA Champions league. ([[Keisuke Honda]] was the first, playing for [[PFC CSKA Moscow|CSKA Moscow]] during [[2009–10 UEFA Champions League|2009–10 season]]).

After the [[Serie A]] season was over, Internazionale made an agreement with [[A.C. Cesena|Cesena]] of making Yuto's move permanent in a cash plus player exchange deal. Nagatomo was valued at €10.95 million,<ref name=Inter2011bilancio>FC Internazionale Milano Report and Accounts on 30 June 2011 {{in lang|it}}</ref> however part of the fee was paid via half of the registration rights of [[Luca Garritano]] (€700,000)<ref name=Inter2011bilancio/> and [[Luca Caldirola]] (€2.5 million).

Nagatomo enjoyed his most productive season under [[Walter Mazzarri]], scoring five goals, along with six assists during the 2013–14 season.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.whoscored.com/Players/37188/History/Yuto-Nagatomo|title=Yuto Nagatomo Career Statistics|publisher=whoscored.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.legaseriea.it/it/giocatori/yuto-nagatomo/NGTYT|title=Nagatomo Career Statistics|publisher=LegaSerieA.it}}</ref>

Nagatomo lost his place in the starting lineup in the [[2014–15 Inter Milan season|2014–15 season]], making only 14 league appearances. He captained Inter for the first time on 28 September 2014 in the [[2014–15 Serie A]] matchday 5 versus [[Cagliari Calcio|Cagliari]], leaving the field in the 27th minutes after receiving two yellow cards in 120 seconds as Inter was defeated 1–4 at [[San Siro]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thenational.ae/sport/yuto-nagatomo-sent-off-and-inter-milan-crushed-4-1-by-lowly-cagliari-1.237676?videoId=5645785441001|title=Yuto Nagatomo sent off and Inter Milan crushed 4-1 by lowly Cagliari|publisher=The National|date=29 September 2014|access-date=13 December 2017}}</ref> Nagatomo said that he had turned down a January 2016 offer from [[Premier League]] outfit [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]], stating that his intentions were to renew with Inter.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11860/10163923/yuto-nagatomo-claims-he-turned-down-manchester-united|title=Yuto Nagatomo claims he turned down Manchester United}}</ref>

On 8 April 2016, Nagatomo agreed a contract extension with the club, signing until June 2019.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.espnfc.com/internazionale/story/2846135/yuto-nagatomo-signs-new-inter-milan-contract-until-2019|title=Yuto Nagatomo signs new Inter Milan contract until 2019|publisher=ESPN FC|date=8 April 2016|access-date=3 September 2016}}</ref> During the 2016–17 season, he played 20 matches in all competitions, including 16 in league, 11 of them as starter, as Inter finished 7th in championship, was eliminated in the quarter-final of [[2016–17 Coppa Italia|Coppa Italia]] by [[S.S. Lazio|Lazio]], and finished bottom in [[2016–17 UEFA Europa League group stage#Group K|Europa League Group K]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2017/standings/round=2000792/group=2005001/index.html|title=Group stage|publisher=UEFA|access-date=13 December 2017}}</ref>

Nagatomo improved his game at the start of [[2017–18 Inter Milan season|2017–18 season]] under new manager [[Luciano Spalletti]], winning his place once again.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thesportsman.com/articles/yuto-nagatomo-looks-a-different-player-at-inter-milan-under-luciano-spalletti|title=Yuto Nagatomo Looks A Different Player At Inter Milan Under Luciano Spalletti|publisher=The Sportsman|author=Richard Hall|date=29 October 2017|access-date=29 October 2017}}</ref> His 200th official appearance for Inter occurred on 16 September 2017 in the matchday 3 match at  [[F.C. Crotone|Crotone]], winning a free kick that lead to the first goal in an eventual 0–2 win.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.inter.it/ba/news/2017/09/17/crotone-0-2-inter-semua-yang-perlu-kamu-ketahui.html|title = Crotone 0-2 Inter: Semua yang perlu kamu ketahui}}</ref> Later on 12 December, Nagatomo played 120 minutes in the [[2017–18 Coppa Italia#Round of 16|2017–18 Coppa Italia]] round of 16 fixture against underdogs of [[Pordenone Calcio|Pordenone]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.football-italia.net/114192/coppa-inter-scrape-past-heroic-pordenone|title=Coppa: Inter scrape past heroic Pordenone|publisher=Football Italia|date=12 December 2017|access-date=13 December 2017}}</ref> The match went to penalty shootouts where he netted the winning penalty in the 5–4 penalty shootout that led Internazionale to the victory and progression to quarter-finals.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.pulse.com.gh/sports/football/coppa-italia-nagatomo-rescues-inter-in-italian-cup-id7719677.html|title=Nagatomo rescues Inter in Italian Cup|publisher=Pulse.com|work=Pulse News Agency International|date=12 December 2017|access-date=13 December 2017}}</ref>

===Marseille===
On 31 August 2020, Nagatomo signed with [[Ligue 1]] club [[Olympique de Marseille|Marseille]] on a free transfer.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Transferts-yuto-nagatomo-a-l-om-c-est-officiel/1166658|title=Transferts : Yuto Nagatomo à l'OM, c'est officiel|publisher=[[L'Équipe]]|date=31 August 2020|access-date=31 August 2020}}</ref> He made his first appearance for the club in a 1–1 league draw against [[Lille OSC|Lille]] on 20 September 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Rault|first=Mathieu|date=20 September 2020|title=OM-LOSC : les compositions officielles !|url=https://www.footmercato.net/a7253499304223629794-om-losc-les-compositions-officielles|access-date=21 September 2020|website=Foot Mercato|language=fr}}</ref>

==International career==
Nagatomo earned his first international cap for [[Japan national football team|Japan]] on 24 May 2008 in a friendly against [[Ivory Coast national football team|Ivory Coast]]. His first international goal was scored in a friendly against [[Syria national football team|Syria]] held on 13 November 2008. Nagatomo was also a member of the Japan U-21 team for the [[2008 Summer Olympics]].
[[File:Flickr - tpower1978 - International friendly match cropped.jpg|thumb|right|Nagatomo playing for Japan in 2009|150px]]

===2010 FIFA World Cup===
He was called up for the [[2010 FIFA World Cup]] for Japan.

===2011 AFC Asian Cup===
Nagatomo was selected as part of the Japan squad for the [[2011 AFC Asian Cup]] by coach [[Alberto Zaccheroni]]. He featured in every game Japan played in the competition and helped them defeat [[Australia national football team|Australia]] in the final, thus earning his first international honour.

===2013 FIFA Confederations Cup===
He was included in [[Alberto Zaccheroni]]'s 23-man Japan squad for the [[2013 FIFA Confederations Cup]] where he featured in the three games before they were eliminated in the group stage.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/42945|title = Nagatomo in Japan squad for Confederations Cup}}</ref>

===2014 FIFA World Cup and later===
[[File:JPN-TUR 20190109 Asian Cup 1.jpg|thumb|Nagatomo and Turkmen player [[Mihail Titow]] at [[2019 Asian Cup]]]]
Nagatomo was part of Japan's national squad to play in the 2014 World Cup in Brazil. He started in all three group matches but Japan was eliminated in the group stage.

Nagatomo earned his [[List of men's footballers with 100 or more international caps|100th international cap]] on 10 November 2017 by captaining his side in the 1–3 friendly loss to [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]] at [[Stade Pierre-Mauroy]], [[Lille]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/60393/nagatomo-makes-100th-international-appearance|title=NAGATOMO MAKES 100TH INTERNATIONAL APPEARANCE|publisher=inter.it|date=11 November 2017|access-date=13 December 2017}}</ref> This made his only the 7th Japan player to achieve the feat.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rsssf.org/tablesj/jap-intres.html|title=Japan International Match – List of Full International Matches|author=Yoon Hyung-Jin|website=[[RSSSF]]|access-date=1 July 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rsssf.org/miscellaneous/jap-recintlp.html|title=Japan – Record International Players|author=Mamrud, Roberto|website=[[RSSSF]]|access-date=2 July 2012}}</ref>

[[File:Yūto Nagatomo in Iran - Japan, AFC Asian Cup 2019 20 (cropped).jpg|thumb|Nagatomo in action against [[Iran national football team|Iran]] at the [[2019 AFC Asian Cup]]]]
He was later selected for the [[2018 World Cup]], [[2019 Asian Cup]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

Currently with 142 caps, he is [[Japan national football team|Japan's]] second highest capped player behind [[Yasuhito Endo]] who holds 152 caps for [[Japan national football team|Japan]].

The couple had maintained a long-distance relationship for several years as Taira was based in Japan.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.tokyohive.com/article/2017/01/taira-airi-nagatomo-yuto-register-their-marriage|title=Taira Airi & Nagatomo Yuto register their marriage |date=30 January 2017|access-date=3 February 2021|work= tokyohive|publisher= 6Theory Media, LLC.}}</ref> Nagatomo proposed to her on the [[San Siro]] pitch in February 2016<ref>{{cite news|title=Nagatomo, actress Taira confirm wedding plans|url=http://www.japantimes.co.jp/sports/2016/12/24/soccer/nagatomo-actress-taira-confirm-wedding-plans/|work=[[The Japan Times]]|date=24 December 2016}}</ref> and they registered their marriage in January 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2085199/full/|script-title=ja:長友佑都＆平愛梨がゴールイン 結婚報告「家族となりました！」|publisher=Oricon News|date=29 January 2017|access-date=16 February 2017|language=ja}}</ref> They have 3 sons. He pursued a degree in [[Political economy|Political Economy]] at the [[Meiji University]], where he graduated in 2007.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Gozzini|first=Alessandra|date=12 December 2012|title=I laureati della Serie A|url=https://www.gazzetta.it/premium/plus/Calcio/12-12-2012/i-laureati-serie-chiellini-top-cinque-913482519997.shtml|access-date=29 December 2021|website=Gazzetta dello Sport|language=it}}</ref>
