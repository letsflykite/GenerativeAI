'''Alireza Jahanbakhsh Jirendeh''' ({{lang-fa|علیرضا جهانبخش جیرنده}}, {{IPA-fa|æliːɾezɑː dʒæhɑːnˈbæxʃ|pron}}; born 11 August 1993) is an Iranian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Midfielder#Winger|right winger]] and [[Midfielder|attacking midfielder]] for [[Eredivisie]] club [[Feyenoord]] and the [[Iran national football team|Iran national team]]. He represented Iran at the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]], [[2015 AFC Asian Cup]], [[2018 FIFA World Cup]], [[2019 AFC Asian Cup]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]]. Jahanbakhsh also represented Iran at the [[Iran national under-20 football team|U20]] and [[Iran national under-23 football team|U23]] levels.

In 2014 Jahanbakhsh was voted the second greatest young talent of the 2013–14 [[Eredivisie]] season.<ref>{{cite web|title=Grootste talent 2013-2014|url=https://www.voetbalhubb.nl/ranking/grootste-talent-eredivisie-2013-2014|website=www.voetbalhubb.nl|language=nl|access-date=12 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180513011831/https://www.voetbalhubb.nl/ranking/grootste-talent-eredivisie-2013-2014|archive-date=13 May 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> In the [[2017–18 Eredivisie]] season, Jahanbakhsh scored 21 league goals, making him the first Asian player to become top scorer in a major European league.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ifpnews.com/exclusive/jahanbakhsh-becomes-first-asian-top-scorer-of-major-european-leagues/|title=Jahanbakhsh Becomes First Asian Top Scorer of Major European Leagues|date=6 May 2018}}</ref><ref name="foxsportsasia.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.foxsportsasia.com/football/asian-football/878070/irans-alireza-jahanbakhsh-finishes-eredivisie-top-scorer/|title=Iran's Alireza Jahanbakhsh finishes as Eredivisie top scorer|date=6 May 2018|publisher=FOX Sports Asia}}</ref>

==Early life==
Jahanbakhsh was born on 11 August 1993 in [[Jirandeh]], a small city located in [[Rudbar County]], [[Gilan Province]]. He grew up in [[Qazvin]] to [[Tat people (Iran)|Tats]]  parents.<ref name="navad"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://jamejamonline.ir/sport/1941252663027394694/%D8%A2%D8%B1%D8%B2%D9%88%D9%87%D8%A7%DB%8C%20%D8%A8%D8%B2%D8%B1%DA%AF%20%D8%B9%D9%84%DB%8C%D8%B1%D8%B6%D8%A7%20%D8%AC%D9%87%D8%A7%D9%86%D8%A8%D8%AE%D8%B4|title=آرزوهای بزرگ علیرضا جهانبخش|last=جم|first=Jamejam, جام|date=14 May 2015|website=Jamejam Online|language=fa|access-date=22 January 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.irna.ir/news/82910353/جهانبخش-از-فوتبال-قزوين-رشد-كرد|title=جهانبخش از فوتبال قزوين رشد كرد|date=9 May 2018|website=ایرنا|language=fa|access-date=22 January 2020}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Damash===
Jahanbakhsh played most of his youth in [[Rasht]] and Persian [[Qazvin]] before joining the youth academy of [[Damash Tehran]] in 2008. Jahanbaksh started his career in [[Tehran]] with [[Parseh Tehran F.C.|Damash Tehran]], an affiliate of Damash Gilan in the [[Iran Football's 2nd Division|2nd Division]]. In 2011, Jahanbakhsh returned to Gilan and at the age of 17. In 2011, Jahanbakhsh played his first game for [[Damash Gilan]] in the [[Persian Gulf Pro League|Iran Pro League]] against [[Sanat Mes Kerman F.C.|Mes]] as one of Damash's youngest ever players.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iplstats.com/website11-12/Week06/DGL_MES_06.htm|title=Iranian Premier League Stats: 2011-2012 Season|access-date=12 August 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |first=Saleh |last=Doosti |url=http://www.anglechronicle.com/alireza-jahanbakhsh-thrilled-after-being-called-into-iran-squad/240 |title=Alireza Jahanbakhsh Thrilled After Being Called Into Iran Squad &#124; Angle Chronicle - Part 240 |work=Angle Chronicle |date=25 February 2014 |access-date=12 August 2014 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20140508004925/http://www.anglechronicle.com/alireza-jahanbakhsh-thrilled-after-being-called-into-iran-squad/240 |archive-date=8 May 2014 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> Jahanbakhsh scored his first goal on 15 January 2012 in a 1–0 victory against [[Fajr Sepasi Shiraz F.C.|Fajr]]. In 42 league appearances with Damash, he scored 10 goals.

He made his debut as a substitute in a league match against [[FC Groningen|Groningen]] on 3 August 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espn.co.uk/football/match?gameId=367133|title=NEC Nijmegen vs. FC Groningen - Football Match Report |publisher=ESPN |date=3 August 2013 |access-date=25 July 2018}}</ref> He scored his first goal in an 8–0 win over [[Harkemase Boys]] in the [[2013–14 KNVB Cup|KNVB Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.persianfootball.com/news/2013/09/24/jahanbakhsh-scores-maiden-goal-in-the-netherlands/ |title=Jahanbakhsh scores maiden goal in the Netherlands [VIDEO&#93; |publisher=Persianfootball.com |access-date=12 August 2014}}</ref> On 1 December 2013, Jahanbakhsh scored two goals and assisted the other in a 3–2 league match win over [[AZ Alkmaar]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.persianfootball.com/news/2013/12/01/alireza-jahanbakhsh-scores-a-brace-assists-one/ |title=Alireza Jahanbakhsh scores a brace, assists one [VIDEO&#93; |publisher=Persianfootball.com |access-date=12 August 2014}}</ref> On 22 December, he scored and assisted at [[FC Groningen]] while providing a secondary assist on 15 February 2014 against RKC Waalwijk. ===AZ===
====2015–16 season====
On 3 August 2015, Jahanbakhsh signed a five–year contract with Dutch club [[AZ Alkmaar]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://m.voetbalzone.nl/doc.asp?uid=252227|title=AZ haalt Jahanbakhsh voor vijf jaar naar Alkmaar}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=AZ akkoord over Jahanbakhsh - AZ|url=https://www.az.nl/nieuws/az-akkoord-over-jahanbakhsh/1?catid=|website=www.az.nl|language=nl}}</ref> He was given number nine as his jersey number, and made his [[Eredivisie]] debut for AZ on 12 September 2015 in a 3–1 win over [[De Graafschap]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.az.nl/en/news/az-secure-first-eredivisie-win-at-de-vijverberg/7?catid=1834|title=AZ secure first Eredivisie win at De Vijverberg - AZ|website=az.nl}}</ref> Jahanbakhsh made his [[UEFA Europa League]] debut in a 2–1 [[2015–16 UEFA Europa League#Group L|group stage]] win over [[Athletic Bilbao]] on 1 October 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2016/matches/round=2000658/match=2016970/index.html|title=UEL - Matches|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> Jahanbakhsh recorded his first assist for AZ on 21 November 2015 in a 3–1 victory against [[SC Heerenveen|Heerenveen]]. Jahanbakhsh scored his first goal for AZ on 30 January 2016 in a match against his former club [[NEC Nijmegen|NEC]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.azfanpage.nl/wedstrijden/12512/ |title=AZ heeft na winst op NEC subtop in het vizier • AZFanpage |access-date=6 February 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160207115947/http://www.azfanpage.nl/wedstrijden/12512/ |archive-date=7 February 2016 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> He scored his second goal of the season and also recorded his fourth assist on 13 February 2016 in a 6–3 victory over [[Heracles Almelo]].

====2016–17 season====
In June 2016 Jahanbakhsh switched from number 9 to number 7 for the upcoming 2016–17 season.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.varzesh3.com/news/1336243/شماره-7-به-علیرضا-جهانبخش-رسید|script-title=fa:شماره 7 به علیرضا جهانبخش رسید|trans-title=Number 7 will be given to Jahanbakhsh.|publisher=Varzesh 3|language=fa|access-date=15 July 2018}}</ref> On 2 July 2016, in AZ's first preseason match, Jahanbakhsh recorded a goal and an assist in his team's 5–1 victory. On 28 August he scored his first goal of the season against [[FC Utrecht]]. The match was ended 2-1 which Jahanbakhsh scored the winning goal.

On 13 January 2017, Jahanbakhsh scored a brace and recorded an assist, as well as being named man of the match in a 3–1 victory against [[Go Ahead Eagles]]. On 21 August 2016, he scored his first goal of the season in a 2–1 win over [[FC Utrecht|Utrecht]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/match?gameId=451135|title=FC Utrecht 1–2 AZ Alkmaar |date=21 August 2016 |publisher=ESPN}}</ref> On 16 February 2017, he scored his first ever [[UEFA|European]] goal in a 4–1 loss to [[Olympique Lyonnais|Lyon]] in Round of 32 of the [[2016–17 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/history/season=2017/matches/round=2000793/match=2019655/index.html |title=AZ v Lyon|date=16 February 2017|publisher=UEFA|access-date=15 July 2018}}</ref>

Jahanbakhsh was named the best winger of the 2016–17 season in the Dutch League.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.foxsports.nl/nieuws/artikel/1886458/top-beste-vleugelaanvallers|title=Top 5: De beste buitenspelers|website=foxsports.nl}}</ref>

====2017–18 season====
Jahanbakhsh scored a hat trick and recorded an assist on 18 April 2018 against Vitesse in the [[2017–18 Eredivisie]].<ref>{{cite web|title=AZ Alkmaar 4-3 Vitesse - Eredivisie 2017/2018 Live|url=https://www.whoscored.com/Matches/1189309/Live/Netherlands-Eredivisie-2017-2018-AZ-Alkmaar-Vitesse|language=en}}</ref> Jahanbakhsh scored another hat trick on the last match day to become the leading goal scorer of the Eredivisie season with 21 goals, and also finished joint third with 12 assists.<ref name="foxsportsasia.com"/> He became the first Asian player ever to become the top goalscorer in a top level European league.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tasnimnews.com/en/news/2018/05/07/1719089/alireza-jahanbakhsh-wins-eredivisie-golden-boot|title=Alireza Jahanbakhsh Wins Eredivisie Golden Boot|publisher=[[Tasnim News Agency]]|date=7 May 2018|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref>

====2018–19 season====
[[File:BHA v FC Nantes pre season 03 08 2018-287 (43135243974).jpg|thumb|Jahanbakhsh in a pre-match warmup with Brighton in August 2018]]
On 25 July 2018, Jahanbakhsh transferred to [[Premier League]] side [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]] for an undisclosed club record fee, signing a five-year contract.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/2018/july/albion-sign-iran-international-for-club-record-fee/|title=ALBION SIGN IRAN INTERNATIONAL FOR CLUB RECORD FEE|publisher=Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|access-date=25 July 2018}}</ref> He announced on his [[Instagram]] that he will wear number 16, the same number he used to wear in his first season at [[N.E.C. (football club)|NEC]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.tarafdari.com/node/1052191|script-title=fa:علیرضا جهانبخش پیراهن شماره 16 برایتون را بر تن خواهد کرد|trans-title=Alireza Jahanbakhsh will wear number 16 at Brighton|date=7 August 2018|access-date=12 August 2018|publisher=Tarafdari|language=fa}}</ref>

On 11 August 2018, Jahanbakhsh made his debut for Brighton as a substitute in the club's opening match of the [[Premier League]] season against [[Watford F.C.|Watford]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45067369|title=Watford 2–0 Brighton|publisher=BBC Spor|date=11 August 2018|access-date=12 August 2018}}</ref> Even though he played 25 matches in the season due to injuries and poor performance he amounted no goals and no assists.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.varzesh3.com/news/1658608/|title=واکنش عجیب جهانبخش بعد از گل تماشایی!|website=www.varzesh3.com|access-date=28 December 2019}}</ref>

====2019–20 season====
With the managerial change at Brighton with [[Graham Potter]] replacing [[Chris Hughton]], Jahanbakhsh rarely found himself in the match squad until 8 December 2019 when he came in as a substitute in a match against [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton]]. On 28 December 2019, he scored his first goal for the club on his first start against [[A.F.C. Bournemouth|Bournemouth]]. Jahanbakhsh was overcome with emotion and was seen crying after scoring his first ever goal for his club.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/50875693|title=Brighton 2–0 Bournemouth|publisher=BBC Sport|date=28 December 2019|access-date=28 December 2019}}</ref> On 1 January 2020, he scored an equalising bicycle kick goal for Brighton against [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]]. The goal against Chelsea was later voted by people as the [[Premier League Goal of the Month]] for January, in which Jahanbakhsh described it as the best goal he has ever scored.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Jahanbakhsh strike voted January 2020 Budweiser Goal of the Month|url=http://www.premierleague.com/news/1603305|access-date=5 March 2021|website=www.premierleague.com|language=en}}</ref>

====2020–21 season====

Jahanbakhsh came on as a sub in the [[2020–21 Brighton & Hove Albion F.C. season|Brighton's opening day of the season]] in a 3–1 home loss against Chelsea in the league.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54151530 | publisher=BBC Sport | title=Premier League: Brighton 1–3 Chelsea | access-date=17 September 2020}}</ref> He scored a cracker in the next game, a 4–0 home thrashing over [[Portsmouth F.C.|Portsmouth]] in the [[2020–21 EFL Cup|EFL Cup]] on 17 September.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54097200| publisher=BBC Sport |title= Brighton fringe players impress in win - BBC Sport | access-date=18 September 2020}}</ref> He scored again 6 days later again coming in the EFL Cup, this time a 2–0 away win over [[Preston North End F.C.|Preston]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54190605|title=Preston North End 0-2 Brighton & Hove Albion: Seagulls set up Man Utd tie – BBC Sport|publisher=BBC Sport |date=23 September 2020|access-date=23 September 2020}}</ref> Jahanbakhsh started in Brighton's 3–2 home victory over champions [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] on 18 May 2021, with fans returning to football. Brighton came back from 2–0 down to secure their first win over ''The Sky Blues'' since 1989.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57066733|title=Brighton 3-2 Man City: Albion come back from two down to beat 10-man champions - BBC Sport|publisher=BBC Sport |date=18 May 2021|access-date=18 May 2021}}</ref>

===Feyenoord===
On 17 July 2021, [[Feyenoord]] announced that Jahanbakhsh had joined the club on a 3-year contract with an option for a fourth year.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.feyenoord.nl/nieuws/nieuwsoverzicht/feyenoord-legt-jahanbakhsh-voor-drie-seizoenen-vast---170721|title=Feyenoord legt Jahanbakhsh voor drie seizoenen vast|language=Dutch|publisher=Feyenoord|date=17 July 2021|access-date=17 July 2021}}</ref> He made his debut for Feyenoord on 29 July in the second leg of the [[2021–22 UEFA Europa Conference League|UEFA Europa Conference League]] tie against [[FC Drita]] where he started the match but later substituted in the 3–2 home victory, with Feyenoord also going through 3–2 on aggregate.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaconferenceleague/match/2032895/|title=Feyenoord-Drita - UEFA Europa Conference League|publisher=Feyenoord|date=22 August 2021|access-date=22 August 2021}}</ref> He scored his first two goals for the club on 12 August, scoring Feyenoord's first two goals in a 3–0 win against [[FC Luzern]] in the [[2021–22 UEFA Europa Conference League#Third qualifying round|third qualifying round of the 2021–22 UEFA Europa Conference League]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.vi.nl/nieuws/jahanbakhsh-moet-strijd-staken-na-eerste-twee-feyenoord-goals|title=Jahanbakhsh moet strijd staken na eerste twee Feyenoord-goals|language=Dutch|publisher=Feyenoord|date=12 August 2021|access-date=12 August 2021}}</ref> Jahanbakhsh made his league debut for the [[Rotterdam]] side on 22 August starting in the 2–0 home victory over [[Go Ahead Eagles]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://matchcenter.feyenoord.nl/feyenoord-go-ahead-eagles-2021-2022|title=Liveblog - Matchcenter - Feyenoord 2-0 Go Ahead Eagles|publisher=Feyenoord|date=22 August 2021|access-date=22 August 2021}}</ref>

===Youth===
Jahanbakhsh was part of the squad that played at the [[2010 AFC U-19 Championship]] in China, playing in two games. Being only 16 at the start of the championships in 2010, he continued at the same age group, captaining the [[Iran national under-20 football team|national under-20 team]] for their successful [[2012 AFC U-19 Championship qualification]] by scoring 5 goals in four appearances. At the [[2012 AFC U-19 Championship|championships]], he added two more to his tally against the [[United Arab Emirates national football team|United Arab Emirates]] and [[South Korea national football team|South Korea]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/11065/9289258/world-cup-scout |title=World Cup Scout |work=Sky Sports |access-date=12 August 2014}}</ref>

Jahanbakhsh was called into [[Iran national under-23 football team|Iran's]] [[Football at the 2016 Summer Olympics|Olympic]] [[2016 AFC U-23 Championship qualification|qualifiers]] in March 2015 and was named team [[Captain (association football)|captain]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.persianfootball.com/news/2015/03/14/azmoun-and-jahanbakhsh-invited-to-olympic-football-team/|title=Azmoun and Jahanbakhsh Invited to Olympic Football Team|website=persianfootball.com}}</ref> With the presence of Jahanbakhsh the under-23 team qualified to the [[2016 AFC U-23 Championship]] in [[Qatar]]. As the [[2016 AFC U-23 Championship]] was not held during the [[FIFA International Match Calendar]], Jahanbakhsh was not released by AZ Alkmaar.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.the-afc.com/ir-iran-afc-u-23-championship-2016/blow-for-iran-as-nourollahi-ruled-out-of-afc-u23-championship|title=The AFC.com - The Asian Football Confederation|website=the-afc.com}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Jahanbakhsh has been compared to Iranian legend [[Mehdi Mahdavikia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/sg/features/alireza-jahanbakhsh-eye-catching-iranian-name-and-style|title=Alireza Jahanbakhsh: The eye-catching Iranian, in name and style|date=26 April 2016|access-date=24 May 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180509220721/https://www.fourfourtwo.com/sg/features/alireza-jahanbakhsh-eye-catching-iranian-name-and-style|archive-date=9 May 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> In Holland, Jahanbakhsh established himself as a dangerous and pacy player.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gelderlander.nl/vitesse/toptip-voor-de-finale-ali-en-rickygoal~aab4e34c/|title=Cookies op gelderlander.nl - gelderlander.nl|website=gelderlander.nl}}</ref>
In several interviews Jahanbakhsh revealed his life time dream is to play in [[Bundesliga]], although there is huge interest in him from Italian and English sides declaring that as a kid he grew up watching his idols [[Ali Daei]], [[Ali Karimi (footballer, born 1978)|Ali Karimi]] and [[Vahid Hashemian]] play for [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] and Mehdi Mahdavikia for [[Hamburger SV]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tehrantimes.com/news/421461/It-s-my-dream-to-play-in-Bundesliga-Alireza-Jahanbakhsh/|title=It's my dream to play in Bundesliga: Alireza Jahanbakhsh|date=21 February 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tasnimnews.com/en/news/2018/02/22/1663777/iran-s-alireza-jahanbakhsh-on-napoli-s-radar-report/|title=Iran's Alireza Jahanbakhsh on Napoli's Radar: Report - Tasnim News Agency}}</ref>
