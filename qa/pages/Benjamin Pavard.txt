'''Benjamin Jacques Marcel Pavard''' (born 28 March 1996) is a French professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]]  for {{German football updater|BaMunich}} club [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] and the [[France national football team|France national team]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Benjamin Pavard Ranking History |url=https://www.footballcritic.com/benjamin-pavard/ranking-history/5049 |access-date=2023-02-28 |website=FootballCritic |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Benjamin Pavard: 10 things on Bayern Munich's French World Cup winner |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/benjamin-pavard-10-things-on-bayern-munich-france-world-cup-winner-1440 |access-date=2023-02-28 |website=bundesliga.com - the official Bundesliga website |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Benjamin Pavard: “I am one of the best defenders in the Bundesliga.” – Get French Football News |url=https://www.getfootballnewsfrance.com/2019/benjamin-pavard-i-am-one-of-the-best-defenders-in-the-bundesliga/ |access-date=2023-02-28 |language=en-US}}</ref> Although usually deployed as a right-back, he is also capable of playing as a [[centre-back]].

He began his career at [[Lille OSC|Lille]] in [[Ligue 1]] and transferred to [[VfB Stuttgart]] in 2016, where he won the [[2. Bundesliga]] in his [[2016–17 2. Bundesliga|first season]]. In January 2019, he agreed a move to Bayern Munich, which was completed after Stuttgart's relegation at the end of the season. In 2020, he completed an historic sextuple by winning the [[2019–20 Bundesliga|Bundesliga]], [[2019–20 DFB-Pokal|DFB-Pokal]], [[2019–20 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]], [[2020 DFL-Supercup|DFL-Supercup]], [[2020 UEFA Super Cup|UEFA Super Cup]] and [[2020 FIFA Club World Cup|FIFA Club World Cup]].

Pavard made his international debut for France in November 2017, and won the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] and the [[2020–21 UEFA Nations League]], also featuring at [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]].

==Early life==
Benjamin Jacques Marcel Pavard<ref name="Legion"/> was born on 28 March 1996<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fifadata.com/documents/FWC/2018/pdf/FWC_2018_SQUADLISTS.PDF |title=FIFA World Cup Russia 2018: List of Players: France |publisher=FIFA |page=11 |date=15 July 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190611000407/https://www.fifadata.com/documents/FWC/2018/pdf/FWC_2018_SQUADLISTS.PDF |archive-date=11 June 2019}}</ref> in [[Maubeuge]], Nord.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/FootballFicheJoueur54718.html |title=Benjamin Pavard |newspaper=L'Équipe |location=Paris |access-date=18 December 2022 |language=fr}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Early career===
Pavard first played with his hometown club in [[Jeumont]], where former France international striker [[Jean-Pierre Papin]] also began his career.<ref name=ten>{{cite web |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/benjamin-pavard-10-things-france-world-cup-winner-vfb-stuttgart-510108.jsp |title=Benjamin Pavard: 10 things on VfB Stuttgart's French World Cup winner |publisher=Bundesliga |access-date=9 August 2018}}</ref>

Spotted by [[Jean-Michel Vandamme]], Pavard joined [[Lille OSC|Lille]]'s academy at the age of nine.<ref name=ten/> He made his [[Ligue 1]] debut on 31 January 2015 against [[FC Nantes]], playing the full game in a 1–1 away draw.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2015/01/31/france/ligue-1/fc-nantes/lille-olympique-sporting-club-metropole/1687153/ |title=Nantes vs. Lille – 31 January 2015 |website=Soccerway |publisher=Perform Group |access-date=15 March 2015}}</ref> Pavard made 21 league appearances across two seasons for Lille, before leaving the club in 2016.<ref name=ten/>

===VfB Stuttgart===
[[File:Benjamin pavard.jpg|thumb|upright|Pavard training with [[VfB Stuttgart]] in 2016]]

On 30 August 2016, Pavard moved to [[VfB Stuttgart]], signing a four-year contract.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.vfb.de/en/vfb/latest/newsarchive/2016/329807-vfb-sign-benjamin-pavard/ |title=VfB sign Benjamin Pavard |publisher=VfB Stuttgart |date=30 August 2016|access-date=30 August 2016}}</ref> He made his debut in the [[2. Bundesliga]] on 3 October, scoring in a 4–0 home win over [[SpVgg Greuther Fürth]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Mit dieser Startelf spielt der VfB |url=https://www.stuttgarter-zeitung.de/inhalt.vfb-stuttgart-gegen-spvgg-greuther-fuerth-mit-diesem-kader-spielt-der-vfb.b8896e67-356b-487e-b885-78ff13513e9b.html |access-date=1 July 2018 |work=Stuttgarter Zeitung |date=3 October 2016 |language=de}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Hier finden Sie alle Informationen zum achten Spieltag |url=https://www.stuttgarter-nachrichten.de/inhalt.vfb-stuttgart-gegen-spvgg-greuther-fuerth-hier-finden-sie-alle-informationen-zum-achten-spieltag.1b2d54f4-b078-4a33-b641-0afebad51c43.html |access-date=1 July 2018 |work=Stuttgarter Nachrichten |date=3 October 2016 |language=de}}</ref> He played 21 games<ref name=next/> and his team ended the [[2016–17 2. Bundesliga|season]] as league champions.<ref name=bundesliga>{{cite news |title=Stuttgart wins German 2nd division to return to Bundesliga |url=https://sports.abs-cbn.com/football/news/2017/05/22/stuttgart-wins-german-2nd-division-return-bundesliga-26324 |access-date=1 July 2018 |publisher=ABS-CBN Sports |date=22 May 2017 |archive-date=12 June 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180612142608/https://sports.abs-cbn.com/football/news/2017/05/22/stuttgart-wins-german-2nd-division-return-bundesliga-26324 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

Pavard made his [[Bundesliga]] debut on 19 August in a 2–0 loss at [[Hertha BSC]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Leckie double helps Hertha beat Stuttgart |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/hertha-berlin-stuttgart-line-ups-and-stats-holger-badstuber-green-kalou-456780.jsp |access-date=1 July 2018 |publisher=Bundesliga |date=19 August 2017}}</ref> He scored his first goal in the Bundesliga with a delightful back header on 29 October in a 3–0 win over [[SC Freiburg]] at the [[Mercedes-Benz Arena (Stuttgart)|Mercedes-Benz Arena]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Tillenburg |first1=Robin |title=Stuttgart siegt in Überzahl gegen Freiburg |url=https://www.sportschau.de/fussball/bundesliga/spielbericht-vfb-stuttgart-sc-freiburg-100.html |access-date=1 July 2018 |date=29 October 2017}}</ref><ref name="ten"/> He extended his contract with Stuttgart on 20 December 2017 until 30 June 2021.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.vfb.de/en/vfb/latest/news/professionals/2017/benjamin-pavard-vertragsverlaengerung-/ |title=VfB extend contract with Benjamin Pavard |publisher=VfB Stuttgart |date=20 December 2017|access-date=20 December 2017}}</ref>

He was one of only four players to feature in every single second of the [[2017–18 Bundesliga]] season.<ref name=next>{{cite news |title=VfB Stuttgart and France defender Benjamin Pavard: The next Lilian Thuram? |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/benjamin-pavard-vfb-stuttgart-france-defender-in-demand-world-cup-thuram-507154.jsp |access-date=1 July 2018 |publisher=Bundesliga |date=1 July 2018}}</ref> He played in several positions, namely [[right-back]], [[centre-back]], [[defensive midfielder]] and [[Right winger|right wing]], but after the appointment of [[Tayfun Korkut]] as manager in January 2018 he was exclusively a centre back; the team conceded 10 goals in their last 14 games as they went from near the relegation places to finish 7th, only just missing out on a [[UEFA Europa League]] place.<ref name=next/>

In [[2018–19 Bundesliga|2018–19]], Pavard was again a regular as Stuttgart fought relegation all season, which was confirmed with defeat to [[1. FC Union Berlin]] in a playoff.<ref>{{cite news |title=Union Berlin hold on against VfB Stuttgart to earn historic promotion |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/union-berlin-vfb-stuttgart-live-play-off-second-leg-line-ups-stats-mario-gomez-4796 |access-date=17 July 2019 |publisher=Bundesliga |date=24 May 2019}}</ref>

===Bayern Munich===
On 9 January 2019, [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] confirmed Pavard would join the club for the 2019–20 season, signing a five-year contract lasting until 30 June 2024.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2019/01/fc-bayern-sign-benjamin-pavard |title=FC Bayern sign world champion Benjamin Pavard |publisher=FC Bayern Munich |date=9 January 2019|access-date=9 January 2019}}</ref>

He made his competitive debut on 3 August in a 2–0 defeat in the [[2019 DFL-Supercup]] against [[Borussia Dortmund]], as an 80th-minute substitute for [[Thiago Alcântara]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fcbayern.com/en/matches/profis/supercup/2019-2020/borussia-dortmund-fc-bayern-muenchen-03-08-2019-matchreport |title=Off-colour Red fall to ruthless Dortmund |publisher=FC Bayern Munich | access-date = 18 August 2019}}</ref> On 31 August, he scored his first goal for the club to equalise in a 6–1 home win over [[1. FSV Mainz 05|Mainz 05]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Bayern Munich 6–1 Mainz |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/49537130 |access-date=2 September 2019 |website=BBC Sport |date=31 August 2019}}</ref> On 11 February 2021, Pavard scored Bayern's only goal in a 1–0 win against [[Tigres UANL]] in the [[2020 FIFA Club World Cup Final]], as the club won its sixth trophy in a year.<ref name="FIFA 2020"/>

On 26 October 2022, he scored his first [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] goal in a 3–0 away win against [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/match/2035710--barcelona-vs-bayern/ |title=Barcelona 0–3 Bayern |publisher=UEFA |date=26 October 2022}}</ref> A week later, he scored a goal in a 2–0 victory against [[Inter Milan]], in which Bayern Munich finished top of their [[2022–23 UEFA Champions League group stage#Group C|Group C]] with six wins out of six matches for the second consecutive season.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://sport.sky.de/fussball/artikel/fc-bayern-benjamin-pavard-setzt-mit-tor-gegen-inter-statement-in-eigener-sache/12736315/33896 |title=Tor-Balsam gegen den Frust: Pavard stellt Nagelsmann vor Probleme |website=Sky Sport |language=de |date=2 November 2022}}</ref> On 11 March 2023, he scored a first-half [[Hat-trick#Association football|brace]] in a 5–3 home win over [[FC Augsburg]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/64928867|website=[[BBC Sport]]|title=Bayern Munich 5–3 FC Augsburg|language=en|date=11 March 2023|access-date=13 March 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
On 6 November 2017, Pavard was selected by [[France national football team|France]]'s head coach [[Didier Deschamps]] for the friendly matches against [[Wales national football team|Wales]] and [[Germany national football team|Germany]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.foxsports.com/soccer/story/benjamin-who-pavard-is-surprise-inclusion-in-france-squad-110717 |title=Benjamin who? Pavard is surprise inclusion in France squad |work=Fox Sports |date=7 November 2017|access-date=10 November 2017}}</ref> He made his debut against the Welsh on 10 November in a 2–0 win at the [[Stade de France]], replacing [[Christophe Jallet]] at half time.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Pritchard |first1=Dafydd |title=France 2–0 Wales |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41854507 |access-date=1 July 2018 |website=BBC Sport |date=10 November 2017}}</ref>

victory over [[Australia men's national soccer team|Australia]].<ref>{{cite web July 2018|access-date=25 July 2018 |publisher=FIFA}}</ref> and nominated for the [[Puskas Award]] of the year.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.fifa.com/the-best-fifa-football-awards/videos/fifa-puskas-award-2018-the-nominees|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191106200435/https://www.fifa.com/the-best-fifa-football-awards/videos/fifa-puskas-award-2018-the-nominees|url-status=dead|archive-date=6 At [[UEFA Euro 2020]], Pavard suffered a head injury in France's opening 1–0 win over [[Germany national football team|Germany]], but continued playing. He said after the game that he felt "a little knocked out for 10 to 15 seconds". This appeared to break UEFA's protocol that a player showing signs of concussion should be withdrawn from the match, whether or not he or the manager agreed with it. After analysing reports by the French team's doctors, UEFA concluded that Pavard never lost consciousness and it was right for him to play on.<ref>{{cite news |last1=MacInnes |first1=Paul |title=Uefa 'satisfied' France treated Benjamin Pavard correctly after Gosens collision |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2021/jun/16/sickening-handling-of-benjamin-pavard-head-blow-criticised-by-brain-injury-charity-headway-euro-2020 |access-date=30 June 2021 |newspaper=The Guardian |date=16 June 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Speight |first1=Janek |title=Euro 2020: UEFA concussion protocol under question after 'sickening' Pavard incident |url=https://www.dw.com/en/euro-2020-uefa-concussion-protocol-under-question-after-sickening-pavard-incident/a-57932198 |access-date=30 June 2021 |publisher=Deutsche Welle |date=17 June 2021}}</ref>

On 9 November 2022, he was called up to the 25-man French squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{Cite web |title=France World Cup squad: William Saliba and Ibrahima Konate picked by Didier Deschamps in 25-man squad |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/12742737/france-world-cup-squad-william-saliba-and-ibrahima-konate-picked-by-didier-deschamps-in-25-man-squad |website=Sky Sports |date=9 November 2022}}</ref>
