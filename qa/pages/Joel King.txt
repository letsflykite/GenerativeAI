'''Joel Bruce King''' (born 30 October 2000) is an Australian professional [[association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|left back]] for [[Sydney FC]].

==Club career==
===Sydney FC===
King made his professional debut for [[Sydney FC]] in a Round 27 clash against [[Newcastle Jets FC|Newcastle Jets]], playing the full game in a 2–0 loss at [[Newcastle International Sports Centre|McDonald Jones Stadium]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.newcastlejets.com.au/news/jets-sink-sydney-finish-season |title=Jets sink Sydney to finish season |date=27 April 2019}}</ref> On 9 May 2019, he signed his first professional contract with the club, penning a one-year deal for the 2019–2020 season.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.sydneyfc.com/news/sydney-fc-youngster-joel-king-re-signs |title=Sydney FC Youngster Joel King Re-Signs |date=9 May 2019}}</ref> King cemented his spot in the Sydney team, and started in every game for Sydney during the [[2020–21 A-League|2020-21]] season with regular fullback [[Michael Zullo]] missing the majority of the season with a recurring calf injury. His consistent performances during the season resulted in him winning the [[A-League Young Footballer of the Year|2020-21 Young Footballer of the year award]] at the end of season awards, becoming the first Sydney FC player to do so.<ref>{{cite web |title=Dolan Warren Awards: Joel King named Young Footballer of the Year |url=https://www.a-league.com.au/news/dolan-warren-awards-joel-king-named-young-footballer-year |website=a-league.com.au |date=23 June 2021 |publisher=A-League |access-date=22 July 2021}}</ref>

===Odense Boldklub===
On 29 January 2022, it was announced that King had signed with Danish club [[Odense Boldklub|OB]] after triggering a six figure release clause.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.smh.com.au/sport/soccer/king-s-ransom-new-socceroo-sold-to-danish-club-as-overhaul-begins-at-sydney-fc-20220129-p59s7y.html |title=King's ransom: Sydney Socceroo sold to Danish club as Wanderers' slump worsens |first=Vince |last=Rugari |newspaper=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]] |date=29 January 2022}}</ref>

===Return to Sydney FC===
In February 2023, King was loaned back to former club [[Sydney FC]] until the end of the [[2022–23 A-League Men]] season.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sydneyfc.com/news/the-king-returns|title=The King Returns|website=[[Sydney FC]]|date=1 February 2023}}</ref> King made 13 appearances in his loan spell, including starts in an Elimination Final against [[Sydney Derby (A-League Men)|derby]] rivals [[Western Sydney Wanderers FC|Western Sydney Wanderers]] and in both leagues of the Semi Final tie against [[Melbourne City FC|Melbourne City]].

King then re-signed on a permanent basis with Sydney FC at the end of that season, in June 2023, on a three-year deal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sydneyfc.com/news/socceroos-full-back-joel-king-makes-permanent-switch-home|title=Socceroos full back Joel King makes permanent switch home|website=[[Sydney FC]]|date=23 June 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
===Australian under-23s===
King was called up to the [[Australia national under-23 soccer team|Australian under-23]] squad for the delayed [[Football at the 2020 Summer Olympics|2020 Tokyo Olympics]] by manager [[Graham Arnold]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Joel King Earns Olympic Selection |url=https://www.sydneyfc.com/news/joel-king-earn-olympic-selection |website=Sydney FC |date=29 June 2021 |access-date=22 July 2021}}</ref> He made his debut in a friendly warm up game for the Olympics against [[New Zealand national under-23 football team|New Zealand]] in a 0–2 loss. 
<ref>{{cite web |last1=Curulli |first1=Chris |title=International Friendly Report: Australia defeated by New Zealand on rainy evening in Ichihara |date=12 July 2021 |url=https://www.socceroos.com.au/news/live-updates-australia-u-23s-v-new-zealand |access-date=22 July 2021}}</ref> 
King provided an assist for Australia's first goal of the tournament, scored by [[Lachlan Wales]] against [[Argentina national under-23 football team|Argentina]] in which Australia ran out 2-0 winners.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Rayson |first1=Zac |title=ROO BEAUTY: Aussies 'shock the world' as Arnie 'masterclass' stuns global powerhouse |url=https://www.foxsports.com.au/tokyo-olympics-2021/live-olyroos-ready-to-shock-the-world-after-13year-olympic-exile/news-story/6a323a384569c377b1ac832774f61b63 |website=Fox Sports Australia |date=23 July 2021 |access-date=23 July 2021}}</ref>

===Australia===
King was called up to the [[Australia national football team|Socceroos]] squad by manager [[Graham Arnold]] for their [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – AFC third round|2022 FIFA World Cup qualification]] match against [[Vietnam national football team|Vietnam]] as regular left back [[Aziz Behich]] was unable to take part. King started the match and put in a good performance with Australia winning 4-0.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Monteverde |first1=Marco |title=Star defender Trent Sainsbury impressed with the Socceroos debut of Joel King |url=https://www.news.com.au/sport/football/star-defender-trent-sainsbury-impressed-with-the-socceroos-debut-of-joel-king/news-story/c05820d52029cc9cf979bb916d25e15b |publisher=news.com.au |access-date=12 April 2023}}</ref>
