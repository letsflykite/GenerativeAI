'''Pape Alassane Gueye''' (born 24 January 1999) is a Senegalese professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[defensive midfielder]] for [[La Liga]] club [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]], on loan from [[Ligue 1]] club [[Olympique de Marseille|Marseille]]. Born in France, he plays for the [[Senegal national football team|Senegal national team]].

==Club career==
===Le Havre===
Gueye made his professional debut for [[Le Havre AC|Le Havre]] in a 0–0 [[Ligue 2]] tie with [[Chamois Niortais F.C.|Chamois Niortais]] on 5 May 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lfp.fr/ligue2/feuille_match/82529|title=LFP.fr - Ligue de Football Professionnel - Domino's Ligue 2 - Saison 2016/2017 - 36ème journée - Havre AC / Chamois Niortais|website=www.lfp.fr}}</ref> He signed his first professional contract on 20 June 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://maligue2.fr/2017/06/20/havre-premier-contrat-pro-pape-gueye/|title=Le Havre : premier contrat pro pour Pape Gueye - MaLigue2|date=20 June 2017|publisher=}}</ref>

===Watford===
On 29 April 2020, it was revealed that Gueye had agreed to join Premier League team Watford when his contract with Le Havre expired on 1 July 2020. Gueye signed a five-year contract with Watford and wore the club's jersey, according to promotional material and a press release made by the club.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.watfordfc.com/news/club/official-hornets-sign-gueye|title=Official: Hornets Sign Gueye|publisher=Watford FC|date=29 April 2020|accessdate=30 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|agency=Reuters|title=Marseille receives one-year transfer ban over Gueye dispute - Watford|url=https://sportstar.thehindu.com/football/marseille-one-year-transfer-ban-over-pape-gueye-dispute-watford-senegal-fifa/article38285617.ece|access-date=27 January 2022|website=Sportstar|date=18 January 2022 |language=en}}</ref> Twenty-four hours later, Watford agreed a deal for Gueye's transfer to [[Ligue 1]] club [[Olympique de Marseille|Marseille]], which included a sell-on clause for Watford. Gueye claimed his agent had given “bad advice”, citing his Watford contract contained a salary of £45,000 per month, not the £45,000 per week, that Gueye was originally quoted by his agent.<ref>{{cite news |first=Adam |last=Leventhal |title=Exclusive: Pape Gueye pictured 'signing' for Watford — but now unlikely to join |url=https://theathletic.com/1899937/2020/06/30/exclusive-pape-gueye-watford-marseille-transfer/ |work=The Athletic |publisher=The Athletic Media Company |date=30 June 2020 |accessdate=1 July 2020 |url-access=subscription }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Marseille handed transfer ban in Pape Gueye dispute|url=https://sports.yahoo.com/marseille-handed-transfer-ban-pape-122835324.html|access-date=27 January 2022|website=sports.yahoo.com|language=en-US}}</ref>

===Marseille===
On 1 July 2020, Gueye signed a four year deal with [[Ligue 1]] side [[Olympique de Marseille|Marseille]],<ref>{{cite press release|title=Pape Gueye est Olympien|access-date=14 September 2020|publisher=Olympique de Marseille|date=1 July 2020|url=https://www.om.fr/fr/video/4803/equipe-pro/36196-pape-gueye-est-olympien|language=fr}}</ref> for a reported fee of €3 million (£2.7 million).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Transferts-pape-gueye-a-l-om-officiel/1148755|title=Transferts : Pape Gueye à l'OM (officiel)|date=1 July 2020|website=[[L'Équipe]]|access-date=1 July 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Walters|first=Mike|date=2 July 2020|title=Watford sell Pape Gueye to Marseille just 17 hours after finalising arrival|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/football/transfer-news/watford-sell-pape-gueye-marseille-22287414|publisher=[[Daily Mirror]]}}</ref>

====Loan to Sevilla====
On 30 January 2023, Gueye was loaned out to [[La Liga]] club [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]] for the remainder of the season.<ref>{{cite press release|title=Pape Gueye prêté au FC Séville|access-date=30 January 2023|publisher=Olympique de Marseille|date=30 January 2023|url=https://www.om.fr/fr/actualites/4803/equipe-pro/72613-pape-gueye-prete-au-fc-seville|language=fr}}</ref>

==International career==
Gueye was born in France and is of Senegalese descent.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dakaractu.com/Le-Havre-Pape-Gueye-signe-son-premier-contrat-professionnel_a133567.html|title=Le Havre Pape Gueye sign son premier contrat professionnel - Dakar Actu|date=20 June 2017|publisher=}}</ref> He represented both the [[France national under-18 football team]] and the [[France national under-19 football team|France U19s]]. However, he decided to represent Senegal on senior level. He debuted with the [[Senegal national football team|Senegal national team]] in a 2–0 [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification]] win over [[Congo national football team|Congo]] on 14 November 2021.<ref name="Global Sports Archive 2021">{{cite web | title=Match Report of Senegal vs Congo - 2021-11-14 - WC Qualification | website=Global Sports Archive | date=14 November 2021 | url=https://globalsportsarchive.com/match/soccer/2021-11-14/senegal-vs-congo/2358612/ | access-date=15 November 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Pape Gueye Stats|url=https://fbref.com/en/players/7155e3e1/Pape-Gueye|access-date=27 January 2022|website=FBref.com|language=en}}</ref>

He was part of [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations squads#Senegal|Senegal's squad]] for the [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations]]; the Lions of Teranga went on to win the tournament for the first time in their history.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cafonline.com/total-africa-cup-of-nations/news/cisse-unveils-senegal-squad-hopes-for-elusive-totalenergies-afcon-title|title=Cisse unveils Senegal squad, hopes for elusive TotalEnergies AFCON title|publisher=[[Confederation of African Football]]|date=25 December 2021|access-date=28 March 2023|archive-date=9 January 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220109145849/https://www.cafonline.com/total-africa-cup-of-nations/news/cisse-unveils-senegal-squad-hopes-for-elusive-totalenergies-afcon-title|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Senegal 0-0 Egypt |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/60186276 |website=[[BBC Sport]] |access-date=28 March 2023 |date=7 February 2022}}</ref>

Gueye was appointed a [[National Order of the Lion|Grand Officer of the National Order of the Lion]] by [[President of Senegal]] [[Macky Sall]] following the nation's victory at the tournament.<ref name= "reuters">{{cite web |title=Cash prizes, real estate, and highest honours as Senegal celebrates Nations Cup heroes |url=https://www.reuters.com/lifestyle/sports/cash-prizes-real-estate-highest-honours-senegal-celebrates-nations-cup-heroes-2022-02-08/ |website=[[Reuters]] |access-date=28 March 2023 |date=8 February 2022}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Gueye is a [[Midfielder#Box-to-box midfielder|box-to-box midfielder]], noted for his aggressive and highly competitive style of play. He also has a good game viewing and physical strength.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.numero-diez.com/milan-udinese-pape-gueye-calciomercato/|title=ESCLUSIVA - Milan e Udinese si muovono per un talento della Ligue 2|last=|first=|date=7 February 2020|website=Numero Diez|language=it|url-status=|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=3 March 2020}}</ref>
