'''Tariq Kwame Nii-Lante Lamptey''' (born 30 September 2000) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[wing-back (association football)|right wing-back]] or [[right-back]] for {{English football updater|Brigh&HA}} club [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]] and the [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national team]].

Previously a youth international for England, Lamptey switched allegiances to represent Ghana at senior international level and made his debut in 2022.

==Club career==
===Chelsea===
[[File:Tariq Lamptey 2019.jpg|thumb|upright|Lamptey playing for [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] 2019]]
Lamptey was born in [[Hillingdon]], Greater London.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.worldfootball.net/player_summary/tariq-lamptey/ |title=Tariq Lamptey: Profile |website=worldfootball.net |publisher=HEIM:SPIEL |access-date=15 November 2022}}</ref> He was playing grassroots football for his local team, Larkspur Rovers,<ref>{{Cite news |title=Tariq Lamptey interview: On leaving Chelsea, loving Dani Alves and more |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/12040/12122376/tariq-lamptey-interview-on-leaving-chelsea-loving-dani-alves-and-more |website=Sky Sports |date=3 November 2020 |access-date=4 November 2020}}</ref> before joining the [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] academy at the age of eight.<ref name="auto">{{cite web |url=https://weaintgotnohistory.sbnation.com/2019/12/30/21042127/lampard-unveils-latest-homegrown-chelsea-academy-talent-as-tariq-lamptey-becomes-7th-debutant |title=Lampard unveils latest Chelsea Academy talent as Tariq Lamptey becomes 7th debutant of season – We Ain't Got No History |website=SB Nation |date=30 December 2019|access-date=9 July 2020}}</ref>

On 29 December 2019, Lamptey made his first-team debut away against [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] in the [[Premier League]], replacing [[Fikayo Tomori]] in the 59th minute where he was a lively presence helping Chelsea come back from 1–0 down to win 2–1.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/50882583 |title=Arsenal 1–2 Chelsea |website=BBC Sport |date=29 December 2019}}</ref> Speaking to Chelsea TV after the game, Lamptey described how nervous he was making his debut: "My heart was racing, this is the moment me and my family have been waiting for."<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.chelseafc.com/en/videos/v/2019/12/29/tariq-lamptey-----my-heart-was-racing--this-is-the-moment-me-and-5xb3F5aTE681Z2Oufy8t9sXExTvdX5C0 |title=Tariq Lamptey {{!}} ''My heart was racing, this is the moment me and my family have been waiting for'' |publisher=Chelsea F.C. |access-date=29 December 2019}}</ref> Lamptey became the seventh academy graduate to make a first-team appearance during [[Frank Lampard]]'s managerial tenure at Chelsea, following in the footsteps of [[Mason Mount]], [[Billy Gilmour]], [[Reece James (footballer, born 1999)|Reece James]], [[Marc Guéhi]], [[Tino Anjorin]] and [[Ian Maatsen]].<ref name="auto"/> Lamptey made two more appearances for Chelsea, both coming in the [[2019–20 FA Cup|FA Cup]] wins over [[Nottingham Forest F.C.|Nottingham Forest]] at home and [[Hull City A.F.C.|Hull City]] away before leaving the club.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/50976149 |title=Chelsea 2–0 Nottingham Forest, FA Cup third round |website=BBC Sport |date=5 January 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/51208507 |title=Hull City 1–2 Chelsea: Blues edge Tigers to reach fifth round |website=BBC Sport |date=25 January 2020}}</ref>

===Brighton & Hove Albion===
====First months, first full season and injury: 2020–21 season====
On 31 January 2020, Lamptey signed for Premier League club [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]] on a three-and-a-half-year contract.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.chelseafc.com/en/news/2020/01/31/lamptey-signs-for-brighton |title=Tariq Lamptey has today completed a permanent transfer to Brighton & Hove Albion |publisher=Chelsea F.C. |access-date=31 January 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/51333836 |title=Tariq Lamptey: Brighton sign 19-year-old defender from Chelsea |website=BBC Sport |date=31 January 2020 |access-date=1 February 2020}}</ref> He made an impressive debut for Brighton in his first Premier League start, a 0–0 away draw against [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] on 23 June – Brighton's second match back from the Premier League's temporary break due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom|COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/51893553 |title=Leicester 0–0 Brighton: Kasper Schmeichel saves penalty as Foxes draw again |website=BBC Sport |date=23 June 2020 |access-date=9 July 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.eastbourneherald.co.uk/sport/football/brighton-and-hove-albion/tariq-lamptey-signed-chelsea-future-making-huge-impact-today-2904224 |title=Tariq Lamptey: Signed from Chelsea for the future but making huge impact today |newspaper=Eastbourne Herald |date=4 July 2020 |access-date=9 July 2020}}</ref>

Lamptey started in Brighton's opening match of the [[2020–21 Brighton & Hove Albion F.C. season|2020–21 season]] against his former club Chelsea in which he set up [[Leandro Trossard]]'s goal in a 3–1 home defeat.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54151530 |title=Premier League: Brighton 1–3 Chelsea |work=BBC Sport |access-date=17 September 2020}}</ref> He scored his first goal on 1 November in a 2–1 Premier League defeat away to [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54679345 |title=Tottenham Hotspur 2–1 in his career on 21 November after receiving two yellow cards in the 2–1 away victory against [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54941198 |title=Aston Villa 1–2 Brighton & Hove Albion: Seagulls secure first ever win at Villa Park |website=BBC Sport |date=21 November 2020 |access-date=21 November 2020}}</ref> On 17 January 2021, Lamptey signed a new three-and-a-half-year contract with Brighton.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11741/12190464/tariq-lamptey-brighton-defender-signs-new-deal |title=Tariq Lamptey signs new long-term deal with Brighton |website=Sky Sports |date=17 January 2021|access-date=17 January 2021}}</ref> On 12 March, after being sidelined since picking up a hamstring injury in a 0–0 away draw against [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]] on 16 December 2020, it was announced that Lamptey would miss the remainder of the season due to the injury.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/55241019 |title=Fulham 0–0 Brighton & Hove Albion: Hosts out of bottom three with point |website=BBC Sport |date=16 December 2020 |access-date=20 March Lamptey made his return to a matchday squad for the first time in nine months on 19 September 2021, remaining as an unused [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] in Brighton's 2–1 home win over Leicester.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58533950 |title=Brighton & Hove Albion 2–1 Leicester City: Seagulls hold off late pressure to go third |date=19 September 2021 |access-date=19 September 2021 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> Three days later, he made his first competitive appearance since December 2020, starting and playing the first half of the 2–0 victory at home to [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]] in the [[2021–22 EFL Cup|EFL Cup third round]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58566907 |title=Brighton & Hove Albion 2–0 Swansea City: Aaron Connolly double sends Seagulls through |date=22 September 2021 |access-date=22 September 2021 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> Lamptey made his first league appearance in 10 months on 16 October 2021, when he came on as a 59th-minute substitute in a 0–0 draw away to [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58833912 |title=Norwich City 0–0 Brighton & Hove Albion: Canaries earn second point of season |date=16 October 2021 |access-date=16 October 2021 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> He made his first Premier League start since returning from injury in a 1–1 home draw against [[Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United]] on 6 November, playing 75 minutes.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/59099963 |title=Brighton & Hove Albion 1–1 Newcastle United: Magpies fight back to draw as Eddie Howe watches from the stands |website=BBC Sport |date=6 November 2021 |access-date=6 November 2021}}</ref> On 7 May, he came on as a Lamptey scored his second goal for Brighton with the team's third in a 3–1 victory away to Arsenal on 9 November in the [[2022–23 EFL Cup|EFL Cup third round]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63481003 |title=Carabao Cup: Arsenal 1–3 Brighton – Seagulls move into last 16 with win at Emirates Stadium |website=BBC Sport |date=9 November 2022 |access-date=10 November 2022}}</ref> He did not start a Premier League match in the [[2022–23 Brighton & Hove Albion F.C. season|2022–23 season]] until his 13th appearance, when he played the whole match in a 4–2 home loss to Arsenal on 31 December.<ref>{{cite web |title=Brave Albion beaten by the leaders |url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/3002690/brave-albion-beaten-by-the-leaders |publisher=Brighton & Hove Albion F.C. |date=31 December 2022 |access-date=31 December 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
Lamptey was born in England to Ghanaian parents. He has been a youth international for England, having represented the Young Lions from [[England national under-18 football team|under-18]] to [[England national under-21 football team|under-21]] level.{{citation needed|date=November 2022}}

In July 2020, he was contacted by the [[Ghana Football Association]] about potentially switching allegiances to represent Ghana.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://ghanasoccernet.com/photos-ghanaian-youngster-tariq-lamptey-joined-by-family-as-he-signs-professional-chelsea-contract |title=PHOTOS: Ghanaian youngster Tariq Lamptey joined by family as he signs professional Chelsea contract |website=GhanaSoccernet}}</ref> However, on 8 September, Lamptey made his England under-21 debut, starting in a 2–1 away victory over [[Austria national under-21 football team|Austria]] in a [[2021 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification|2021 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualifier]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54002977 |title=Eddie Nketiah scores again in England U21 win |work=BBC Sport |access-date=17 September 2020}}</ref> After 18 months out of the under-21 team due to injury, he then made his return to the side in March 2022, taking part in the [[2023 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification|2023 European Under-21 Championship qualification]] matches against [[Andorra national under-21 football team|Andorra]] and [[Albania national under-21 football team|Albania]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/2540443/albion-trio-get-under-21-call-up |publisher=Brighton & Hove Albion F.C. |title=Albion trio get under-21 call-up |access-date=20 March 2022}}</ref>

In May 2022, it was announced that Lamptey was considering switching allegiances to Ghana, with England under-21 manager [[Lee Carsley]] saying that Lamptey had "asked to be left out of the squad for a bit of head space."<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/61581832 |website=BBC Sport |title=Brighton: Tariq Lamptey considers representing Ghana instead of England |date=25 March 2022 |access-date=25 March 2022}}</ref> After months of further speculation, in July the president of the Ghana FA, [[Kurt Okraku]], announced that Lamptey was one of the few players that were officially made eligible to represent Ghana.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Eleto |first=Clinton |date=5 July 2022 |title=Breaking News: Tariq Lamptey and four other new players complete Ghana Black Stars switch |url=https://www.saharafootball.net/ghanaswitch/ |access-date=6 July 2022 |website=Sahara Football}}</ref> Lamptey made his debut on 23 September, coming on as a 72nd-minute substitute, replacing [[Denis Odoi]] in a 3–0 [[Friendly (association football)|friendly]] loss against [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]] at the [[Stade Océane]] in [[Le Havre]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=23 September 2022 |title=Brazil 3–0 Ghana: Tottenham forward Richarlison scores twice |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63016559 |access-date=26 September 2022 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

On 14 November, Lamptey was named in Ghana's 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=Brighton's Tariq Lamptey confirmed in Ghana World Cup squad |url=https://www.theargus.co.uk/sport/23122932.brightons-tariq-lamptey-confirmed-ghana-world-cup-squad/ |newspaper=The Argus |location=Brighton |date=14 November 2022 |access-date=14 November 2022}}</ref>
