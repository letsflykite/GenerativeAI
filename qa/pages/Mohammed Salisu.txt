'''Mohammed Salisu Abdul Karim''' (born 17 April 1999) is a Ghanaian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Centre-back|centre-back]] for [[EFL Championship]] club [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] and the [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national team]].

Salisu started his senior career with [[Real Valladolid|Valladolid]], featuring for the [[Real Valladolid B|reserve side]] before making his first team debut in 2019. He made 34 appearances for the club before joining Southampton for a £10.9&nbsp;million fee in 2020.

==Club career==
===Early career===
Born in [[Kumasi]], Salisu started his career at his local youth club Kumasi Barcelona Babies before joining [[West African Football Academy]] in 2013.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Afadzinu|first=Nene|date=11 August 2020|title=Mohammed Salisu: Ghana's next defensive stalwart?|url=https://3news.com/mohammed-salisu-ghanas-next-defensive-stalwart/|url-status=live|access-date=20 May 2021|website=3news}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=12 August 2020|title=Mohammed Salisu: Ghana's next defensive stalwart?|url=https://www.ghanaweb.com/GhanaHomePage/SportsArchive/Mohammed-Salisu-Ghana-s-next-defensive-stalwart-1031827|access-date=20 May 2021|website=GhanaWeb}}</ref> He left the latter for personal reasons in 2015,<ref>{{cite web|last=Ampofo|first=Owuraku|date=12 August 2020|title=5 things you didn't know about Southampton's new signing Mohammed Salisu|url=https://www.myjoyonline.com/5-things-you-didnt-know-about-southamptons-new-signing-mohammed-salisu/|url-status=live|access-date=25 August 2020|website=Joy Sports}}</ref> and then remained over a year without a club before impressing on a trial at the newly formed [[Nsawam]] branch of the African Talent Football Academy in March 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://theathletic.co.uk/1941621/2020/08/11/mohammed-salisu-southampton-transfer/ |title=Bootless in Kumasi, a star in Spain: Mohammed Salisu's rise to Southampton |website=[[The Athletic]] |first=Carl |last=Anka |date=11 August 2020 |access-date=25 August 2020}}</ref> In October 2017, he joined [[Real Valladolid]]'s youth setup.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ghanasoccernet.com/feature-nsawam-based-african-talent-football-academy-launches-operations |title=Feature: Nsawam-based African Talent Football Academy launches operations |website=Ghana Soccernet |first=Nuhu |last=Adams |date=29 November 2017 |access-date=16 July 2018}}</ref>

===Valladolid===
====2017–18 season====
Salisu made his senior debut with [[Real Valladolid B|the reserves]] on 28 January 2018, starting in a 4–2 [[Segunda División B]] home loss against [[Coruxo FC|Coruxo]]. On 1 March, he extended his contract until 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realvalladolid.es/noticias/actualidad/01032018/Salisu-amplia-su-contrato-hasta-2021/ |title=Salisú amplía su contrato hasta 2021 |trans-title=Salisú extends his contract until 2021 |publisher=Real Valladolid CF |language=es |date=1 March 2018 |access-date=16 July 2018 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20190924174911/http://www.realvalladolid.es/noticias/actualidad/01032018/Salisu-amplia-su-contrato-hasta-2021/ |archivedate=24 September 2019}}</ref> He scored his first senior goal on 29 April, netting the equaliser in the 82nd minute of a 2–2 away draw against [[Racing de Ferrol|Racing Ferrol]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/match/racing-ferrol-v-real-valladolid-ii/commentary-result/eamu14jtfqvc2jhks0ajtal4a |title=Racing Ferrol 2–2 Real Valladolid II |website=[[Goal (website)|Goal]] |publisher=[[DAZN Group]] |location=Leeds |date=29 April 2018 |access-date=26 August 2020}}</ref>

====2018–19 season====
On 16 July 2018, Salisu was promoted to the senior Valladolid squad.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realvalladolid.es/noticias/actualidad/16072018/Salisu-da-el-salto/|title=Salisú da el salto |trans-title=Salisú makes the jump |publisher=Real Valladolid CF |language=es |date=16 July 2018 |access-date=16 July 2018}}</ref> He made his professional debut on 9 January 2019, starting in a 1–0 away loss against [[Getafe CF|Getafe]] in [[2018–19 Copa del Rey|that season]]'s [[Copa del Rey]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.marca.com/futbol/copa-rey/cronica/2019/01/09/5c362eb9e5fdea24308b4614.html |title=Ángel sale al rescate |trans-title=Ángel comes to the rescue |newspaper=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]] |language=es |first=José |last=Pérez |date=9 January 2019 |access-date=14 January 2019}}</ref> On 22 May 2019, Salisu extended his contract to keep him at the club until 2022.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realvalladolid.es/noticias/actualidad/22052019/Salisu-amplia-contrato-hasta-2022/ |title=Salisu amplía contrato hasta 2022 |trans-title=Salisu extends contract until 2022 |publisher=Real Valladolid CF |language=es |date=22 May 2019 |access-date=4 July 2019 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20190704222933/http://www.realvalladolid.es/noticias/actualidad/22052019/Salisu-amplia-contrato-hasta-2022/ |archivedate=4 July 2019}}</ref>

====2019–20 season====
He made his [[La Liga]] debut on 18 August, starting in a 2–1 away victory over [[Real Betis]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ghanasportsonline.com/2019/08/spanish-la-liga-side-real-valladolid-commends-mohammed-salisu-for-making-senior-debut/ |title=Spanish La Liga side Real Valladolid commends Mohammed Salisu for making senior debut |website=Ghana Sports Online |first=Frank |last=Darkwah |date=19 August 2019 |access-date=4 November 2019}}</ref> After the departure of [[Fernando Calero]] to [[RCD Espanyol|Espanyol]], Salisu became a starter for the [[Castile and León]] side, partnering [[Kiko Olivas]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://as.com/futbol/2019/08/26/primera/1566818107_271239.html |title=Salisu pisa fuerte en su debut en la máxima categoría |trans-title=Salisu comes out strong in his debut in the top tier |newspaper=[[Diario AS]] |language=es |first=Carlos Raúl |last=Martínez |date=26 August 2019 |access-date=4 November 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://elpais.com/deportes/2019/10/18/actualidad/1571394280_576245.html |title=Salisu, el muro del Valladolid |trans-title=Salisu, the wall of Valladolid |newspaper=[[El País]] |language=es |first=Alessandro |last=Leone |date=18 October 2019 |access-date=4 November 2019}}</ref> On 26 October 2019, he scored his first professional goal by netting the second goal of a 2–0 home win against [[SD Eibar|Eibar]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/en-ug/news/salisu-nets-first-ever-valladolid-goal-in-2-0-win-over-eibar/1s8gpx1mug1ib18oiakfh0xxiu|title=Salisu nets first ever Valladolid goal in 2–0 win over Eibar |website=Goal |location=Leeds |publisher=DAZN Group |first=Kunle |last=Fayiga |date=26 October 2019|access-date=4 November 2019}}</ref>

===Southampton===
====2020–21 season====
On 12 August 2020, Salisu joined [[Premier League]] club [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] for a fee worth £10.9&nbsp;million. The English club had triggered a buyout clause in the defender's contract with Salisu putting pen to paper on a four-year deal.<ref name=saintssignsalisu>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11700/12047984/mohammed-salisu-southampton-sign-defender-from-real-valladolid |title=Mohammed Salisu: Southampton sign defender from Real Valladolid |website=[[Sky Sports]] |date=12 August 2020 |access-date=13 August 2020}}</ref> Manager [[Ralph Hasenhüttl]] hailed his new signing, saying he was a future prospect who had all the qualities to come to the Premier League and help Southampton.<ref name="saintssignsalisu"/>

Salisu had yet to feature for Southampton three months after he had signed with Hasenhüttl providing an update in November 2020, stating:<blockquote><nowiki>''</nowiki>There is still a way to go I think for being match fit, but I am very happy he's here. He's a long-term project and I am sure that he will play a lot of games for us.<nowiki>''</nowiki><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.dailyecho.co.uk/sport/18887908.salisu-still-a-way-go-match-fitness-says-hasenhuttl/ |title=Salisu still has 'a way to go' before match fitness, says Hasenhuttl |newspaper=[[Southern Daily Echo]] |publisher=[[Newsquest]] |location=Southampton |first=Dan |last=Rose |date=20 November 2020 |access-date=21 November 2020}}</ref></blockquote> Salisu made his Southampton debut on 11 February 2021, six months after signing for the club, playing the full 90 minutes and keeping a [[Shutout|clean sheet]] in a 2–0 [[FA Cup]] away victory over [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]] as [[Danny Ings]] and [[Stuart Armstrong]] scored a goal each to send Southampton through to the quarter-finals of the competition.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mohammed Salisu: Ghanaian centre-back makes Southampton debut after six months |url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/mohammed-salisu-ghanaian-centre-back-makes-southampton-debut/62a4hixbyvcv16p5cmp5ywgb5 |website=Goal |location=Leeds |publisher=DAZN Group |first=Prince |last=Narkortu Teye |date=11 February 2021 |access-date=11 February 2021 |url-status=live |archive-url= |archive-date=}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Wolverhampton Wanderers 0–2 Southampton |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/wolverhampton-wanderers-vs-southampton/report/441174 |first=Richard |last=Morgan |date=11 February 2021 |access-date=11 February 2021 |website=Sky Sports}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|title=Mohammed Salisu makes Southampton debut in FA Cup win over Wolverhampton |url=https://www.modernghana.com/sports/1061090/mohammed-salisu-makes-southampton-debut-in-fa.html |first=Samuel Ekow |last=Amoasi Appiah |date=11 February 2021 |access-date=11 February 2021 |website=Modern Ghana}}</ref>

He made his Premier League debut three days later when he came on in the 72nd minute to replace the injured [[Kyle Walker-Peters]], also against Wolverhampton Wanderers ending with a 2–1 loss at home.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/55967801 |title=Southampton 1–2 Wolverhampton Wanderers |website=[[BBC Sport]] |first=Saj |last=Chowdhury |date=14 February 2021 |access-date=14 February 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.dailyecho.co.uk/sport/19089889.saints-dealt-fresh-kyle-walker-peters-injury-blow/ |title=Saints dealt fresh Kyle Walker-Peters injury blow |newspaper=Southern Daily Echo |publisher=Newsquest |location=Southampton |first=Tom |last=Crocker |date=14 February 2021 |access-date=14 February 2021}}</ref> Salisu made his first Premier League start the following week in a 1–1 draw with [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]], putting in an impressive performance.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Smyth|first=Rob|date=20 February 2021 |title=Southampton 1–1 Chelsea |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2021/feb/20/southampton-v-chelsea-premier-league-live |url-status=live |access-date=20 February 2021 |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |location=London}}</ref>

==== 2021–22 season ====
On 21 September 2021, Salisu scored his first professional goal for Southampton against [[Sheffield United F.C.|Sheffield United]] in the [[EFL Cup]] which ended 2–2 at full time before Southampton advanced 4–2 on penalties.<ref>{{Cite web|date=21 September 2021|title=Sheffield United 2–2 Southampton|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58553816|url-status=live|access-date=22 September 2021|website=BBC Sport}}</ref> On 28 December 2021, Salisu gave away a penalty and got a [[Fouls and misconduct (association football)|red card]] after receiving his second booking for a foul on [[Son Heung-min|Son Heung-Min]], with the game against [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham]] ending in a 1–1 draw.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Reddy|first=Luke|date=28 December 2021|title=Southampton 1–1 Tottenham: 10-man Saints hold Spurs at St Mary's|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/59736885|url-status=live|access-date=29 December 2021|website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

== International career ==
In November 2019, Salisu received his first call-up to [[Ghana national football team|Ghana's senior squad]], after being named by [[James Kwesi Appiah|Kwesi Appiah]] in the squad for the 2021 African Cup of Nations qualifiers against [[South Africa national football team|South Africa]] and [[São Tomé and Príncipe national football team|São Tomé and Príncipe]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Teye|first=Prince Narkortu|date=17 November 2019|title=Afcon 2021 qualifying: Ghana call up Real Valladolid prodigy Salisu in 23-man squad|url=https://www.goal.com/en-cm/news/afcon-2021-qualifying-ghana-calls-up-real-valladolid-prodigy/1jb55f7cl2b2l1mtwoir57d6z6|url-status=live|access-date=20 May 2021|website=Goal.com}}</ref> He withdrew from the squad citing injury problems.<ref name="Tahiru">{{Cite web|last=Tahiru|first=Fentuo|date=26 April 2021|title="I will play for Ghana but I'm not ready yet" – Mohammed Salisu|url=https://citisportsonline.com/2021/04/26/i-will-play-for-ghana-but-im-not-ready-yet-mohammed-salisu/|url-status=live|access-date=20 May 2021|website=Citi Sports Online}}</ref> In 2020, after [[Charles Akonnor|C.K. Akonnor]] was appointed as manager of the national team, according to media speculation he made attempts to reach him but his efforts proved futile as seemed Salisu was not interested in playing for the team.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=25 September 2020|title=C.K Akonnor justifies the omission of Mohammed Salisu in 23-man squad for Mali friendly|url=https://citisportsonline.com/2020/09/25/c-k-akonnor-justifies-the-omission-of-mohammed-salisu-in-23-man-squad/|access-date=20 May 2021|website=Citi Sports Online}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|date=30 July 2020|title=The inside story of why Mohammed Salisu is allegedly unwilling to play for Ghana|url=https://www.ghanaweb.com/GhanaHomePage/SportsArchive/The-inside-story-of-why-Mohammed-Salisu-is-allegedly-unwilling-to-play-for-Ghana-1022131|access-date=20 May 2021|website=GhanaWeb}}</ref> His family later came out with a statement to refute all the information within the public space that suggested his refusal to play for the national team. The statement read that <nowiki>'' We further wish to state unequivocally that our son is a proud Ghanaian and very ready to represent Ghana at every level and time''</nowiki>.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /> His elder brother later granted an interview on local radio station Angel FM talking about his brother's call up snub into the ''Black Starlets'', the [[Ghana national under-17 football team|Ghana U-17 team]] whilst he was playing in Ghana, due his inability to pay to be in the team and not because his performance was substandard.<ref name=":1" />

In April 2021, during an interview with MozzartSport Kenya, Salisu stated that he would be proud to play for the national team, but also that he felt like it was not the right time for him to play, as he wanted to focus on his club career, instead.<ref name="Tahiru"/><ref>{{Cite web|last=Teye|first=Prince Narkortu|date=26 April 2021|title=Mohammed Salisu: Southampton defender gives latest update on playing for Ghana {{!}} Goal.com|url=https://www.goal.com/en-gh/news/mohammed-salisu-southampton-defender-gives-latest-update-on/wp4q4vlwnjz4147lo05u7o7ua|url-status=live|access-date=20 May 2021|website=www.goal.com}}</ref> In July 2022, the president of the [[Ghana Football Association|Ghana FA]], [[Kurt Okraku]], officially announced that Salisu had finally agreed to represent the Black Stars.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Eleto |first=Clinton |date=5 July 2022 |title=Breaking News: Tariq Lamptey and four other new players complete Ghana Black Stars switch |url=https://www.saharafootball.net/ghanaswitch/ |access-date=6 July 2022 |website=Sahara Football |language=en}}</ref>

Salisu made his debut for [[Ghana national football team|Ghana]] on 23 September 2022, as a second-half substitute in a 3–0 friendly defeat against [[Brazil national football team |Brazil]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Brazil vs.Ghana |url=https://uk.soccerway.com/matches/2022/09/23/world/friendlies/brazil/ghana/3935288/ |website=Soccerway.com |access-date=24 September 2022 |date=23 September 2022}}</ref> On 17 November 2022, Salisu scored his first goal in a friendly against [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]], which Ghana won 2–0.<ref name="Soccerway171122" />
