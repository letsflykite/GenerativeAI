'''Juan Marcos Foyth''' (born 12 January 1998) is an Argentine professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as [[Defender (association football)#Right-back|right-back or centre-back]] for [[La Liga]] club [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]] and the [[Argentina national football team|Argentina national team]].

Having begun his professional career at his hometown club [[Estudiantes de La Plata|Estudiantes]], he went on to play in the [[Premier League]] for [[Tottenham Hotspur]] and in [[La Liga]] for Villarreal. He won the [[UEFA Europa League]] with the latter club in [[2021 UEFA Europa League Final|2021]].

Foyth made his senior international debut for Argentina in 2018 and was part of their squad that came third at the [[2019 Copa América]] and won the [[2022 Finalissima]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

===Tottenham Hotspur===
Foyth joined [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] for a reported £8 million on 30 August 2017, on a five-year contract.<ref>{{cite press release |url=http://www.tottenhamhotspur.com/news/juan-foyth-completes-move-300817/ |title=Foyth completes move |date=30 August 2017|access-date=30 August 2017 |publisher=[[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41100066 |title=Juan Foyth: Tottenham sign Estudiantes centre-back on five-year deal |date=20 August 2017 |work=[[BBC Sport]]}}</ref> He made his debut on 19 September in an [[2017–18 EFL Cup|EFL Cup]] tie against [[Barnsley F.C.|Barnsley]] that Tottenham won 1–0.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/tottenham-1-barnsley-0-maurico-pochettino-lauds-juan-foyth-after-impressive-wembley-debut-a3638816.html |title=Tottenham 1 Barnsley 0: Maurico Pochettino lauds Juan Foyth after impressive Wembley debut |first=Jack |last=Rosser |work=[[Evening Standard]] |date=19 September 2017}}</ref> 

Foyth made his [[Premier League]] debut on 3 November 2018 in a 3–2 away win against [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]], where he conceded two [[Penalty kick (association football)|penalties]] which were both converted.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/46000540 |title=Spurs survive Wolves fightback to go fourth |work=BBC Sport |date=3 November 2018}}</ref> In his next league appearance, away to [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]], he scored his first career goal, handing Tottenham a 1–0 win.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2018/nov/10/crystal-palace-tottenham-premier-league-report |title=Juan Foyth scores first career goal to give Tottenham win at Crystal Palace |first=Paul |last=MacInnes |date=10 November 2018 |work=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref> On 4 May 2019, he came on at half time for [[Toby Alderweireld]] and was [[Ejection (sports)|sent off]] three minutes later for a foul on [[Jack Simpson (footballer)|Jack Simpson]] in a 1–0 loss at [[AFC Bournemouth]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Hytner |first1=David |title=Nine-man Tottenham undone at the last as Aké snatches win for Bournemouth |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2019/may/04/bournemouth-tottenham-hotspur-premier-league-match-report |access-date=4 May 2022 |work=[[The Observer]] |date=4 May 2019}}</ref>

===Villarreal===
In October 2020, Foyth signed a new deal with Tottenham until 2024, and was then loaned to Spanish club [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54406776 |title=Tottenham: Juan Foyth signs new deal before loan move to Villarreal |work=BBC Sport |date=4 October 2020 |access-date=4 October 2020 }}</ref> He made his debut on 22 October, scoring in a 5–3 home win over Turkey's [[Sivasspor]] in the group stage of the [[UEFA Europa League]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Europa League: Juan Foyth tuvo un gran debut con Villarreal en la victoria por 5-3 sobre Sivasspor|trans-title=Europa League: Juan Foyth had a great debut for Villarreal in the 5-3 victory over Sivasspor|url=https://tntsports.com.ar/internacional/Europa-League-Juan-Foyth-tuvo-un-gran-debut-con-Villarreal-en-la-victoria-por-5-3-sobre-Sivasspor-20201022-0023.html |access-date=4 May 2022 |publisher=[[TNT Sports (Argentina)|TNT Sports]] |date=22 October 2020 |language=Spanish}}</ref> He made 11 more appearances in the tournament as his team won it, including a start in the [[2021 UEFA Europa League Final|final victory]] over [[Manchester United]].<ref name=europa/>

At the end of his loan season, Foyth signed for Villarreal in June 2021 on a five-year deal for a fee of €15 million.<ref>{{cite news |title=Villarreal ejerce opción de compra sobre argentino Foyth, quien firma contrato por 5 temporadas|trans-title=Villarreal activate buying option on Argentine Foyth, who signs a contract for 5 seasons|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/futbol-villarreal-foyth-idARL2N2NT19I |access-date=4 May 2022 |work=[[Reuters]] |date=11 June 2021 |language=Spanish}}</ref> On 16 August, he was sent off in a goalless draw at [[Granada CF]] in the opening game of the season.<ref>{{cite news |title=Juan Foyth dismissed as Villarreal start LaLiga campaign with goalless home draw |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/juan-foyth-athletic-bilbao-villarreal-granada-europa-league-b1903674.html |access-date=20 December 2022 |work=[[The Independent]] |date=16 August 2021}}</ref> He became a key player at right back for manager [[Unai Emery]], replacing veteran [[Mario Gaspar]] and covering for the injured [[Serge Aurier]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Mateu |first1=Ismael |title=Los comodines de Emery frente a las adversidades |trans-title=Emery's wildcards in the face of adversity|url=https://www.superdeporte.es/villarreal-cf/2022/01/28/comodines-emery-frente-adversidades-62059595.html |access-date=4 May 2022 |work=[[Super Deporte]]|date=28 January 2022 |language=Spanish}}</ref> On 12 May 2022, he scored his first La Liga goal, to restore the lead in a 5–1 win at [[Rayo Vallecano]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Oludare |first1=Shina |title=Dia bags assist as Chukwueze's Villarreal decimate Rayo Vallecano |url=https://www.goal.com/en-my/news/dia-bags-assist-as-chukwueze-s-villarreal-decimate-rayo-vallecano/bltb81e40aa9380f8e9 |access-date=20 December 2022 |publisher=[[Goal (website)|Goal]] |date=12 May 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
Foyth played for the [[Argentina national under-20 football team|Argentina national under-20 team]] in 2017. He made 12 appearances in total for the national youth team: nine at the [[2017 South American Youth Football Championship]] in Ecuador, and another three during the [[2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.planetfootball.com/quick-reads/spurs-target-juan-foyth-good-football-manager/ |title=Who is Spurs target Juan Foyth and is he any good on Football Manager?|work=Planet Football|date=11 July 2017 }}</ref>

Foyth was called up for the first time to the senior [[Argentina national football team|Argentina national team]] by manager [[Lionel Scaloni]] for a number of friendly games to be played in October 2018.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://cartilagefreecaptain.sbnation.com/2018/9/28/17913872/tottenham-hotspur-juan-foyth-international-argentina-first-call-up-2018 |title=Tottenham's Juan Foyth named to Argentina squad for upcoming friendlies |first=Dustin |last=Menno |date=28 September 2018 |work=Cartilage Free Captain}}</ref> He made his debut on 16 November in a friendly against [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]] where he helped Argentina to a 2–0 win, being named [[man of the match]] and receiving wide praise for his performance in this match.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.hitc.com/en-gb/2018/11/19/argentine-press-rave-about-tottenham-hotspur-star-juan-foyth/ |title=Argentine press rave about Tottenham Hotspur star Juan Foyth |first=Dan|last= Coombs|work=HITC |date=19 November 2018 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.101greatgoals.com/news/tottenham-hotspurs-juan-foyth-lauded-following-argentina-2-0-mexico/ |title=Tottenham Hotspur's Juan Foyth lauded following Argentina 2-0 Mexico |first=Jamie |last=Brown |date=17 November 2018 |work=101 Great Goals |access-date=19 November 2018 |archive-date=27 March 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190327090128/https://www.101greatgoals.com/news/tottenham-hotspurs-juan-foyth-lauded-following-argentina-2-0-mexico/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> 

He was included in the final 23-man squad for the [[2019 Copa América]], and made his first start in the competition in the match against [[Qatar national football team|Qatar]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.hitc.com/en-gb/2019/06/24/argentina-fans-react-to-tottenham-hotspur-ace-juan-foyths-displa/ | title=Argentina fans react to Tottenham Hotspur ace Juan Foyth's display|date=24 June 2019|first=John |last=Verrall|work=HITC }}</ref> He started as a right-back in three further games, including the semi-final they lost to [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]] 2–0,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://talksport.com/football/567599/brazil-2-0-argentina-juan-foyth-tottenham-fans/ |title='ENDING CAREERS' Brazil 2-0 Argentina: Juan Foyth produces sensational piece of skill to leave Philippe Coutinho for dead and Tottenham delighted |author=Oliver Dawnay|date=3 July 2019|work=[[Talksport]]}}</ref> and the [[2019 Copa América knockout stage#Third place play-off|third-place play-off]] match against [[Chile national football team|Chile]], which they won 2–1.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/live/football/48870186/page/3 |title=Messi sent off, Aguero scores & Sanchez injured in thrilling Copa America tie |date=6 July 2019 |work=BBC Sport }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.hackneygazette.co.uk/sport/football/tottenham-hotspur/juan-foyth-tottenham-international-round-up-1-6147666 |title=Foyth helps Argentina clinch third spot at Copa America |date=8 July 2019 |work=[[Hackney Gazette]] |access-date=8 July 2019 |archive-date=5 August 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200805023045/https://www.hackneygazette.co.uk/sport/football/tottenham-hotspur/juan-foyth-tottenham-international-round-up-1-6147666 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

On 1 June 2022, Foyth remained as an unused substitute as Argentina won 3–0 against reigning [[UEFA Euro 2020|European Champions]] [[Italy national football team|Italy]] at [[Wembley Stadium]] in the [[2022 Finalissima]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/finalissima/news/0276-1549514538ab-f782a73cf733-1000--argentina-outclass-italy-in-finalissima/ |title=Italy 0-3 Argentina: South American champions cruise to Finalissima glory |publisher=[[UEFA]] |date=1 June 2022 |access-date=19 December 2022 }}</ref>

He was named in Argentina's final 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]] by Scaloni. He replaced [[Nahuel Molina]] in the final moments as Argentina defeated [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]] 3–0 in the semi-final. He was an unused substitute as Argentina won the World Cup by defeating France 4–2 in a penalty shoot-out to win [[2022 FIFA World Cup Final|the final]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/blogs/fwc-2022-live-blog-vi-en#mage-greater-than-fwc-2022-greater-than-kylian-mbappe-of-france-celebrates|title=Argentina are World Champions!|publisher=[[FIFA]]|date=18 December 2022|accessdate=19 December 2022}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Foyth, who was born in Argentina, is of Polish descent and holds a [[Polish passport]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.getfootballnewsfrance.com/2017/juan-foyth-set-to-complete-move-to-psg/|title=Juan Foyth set to complete move to PSG|website=Get Football News France|date=24 July 2017 }}</ref> He also holds [[Spanish nationality]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.mundodeportivo.com/futbol/fc-barcelona/20220817/1001852165/foyth-apellido-inventado-origen-polaco.html | title=Foyth, un apellido 'inventado' y de origen polaco | date=17 August 2022 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.sport.es/es/noticias/barca/foyth-central-moldeado-pochettino-reconvertido-14292825 | title=Foyth, el central moldeado por Pochettino y reconvertido a lateral por Unai | date=16 August 2022 }}</ref>

His grandparents' surname was spelled ''Fojt'', which was changed on arrival in Argentina.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Torres |first1=Diego |title=Juan Foyth: "Con Pochettino y Kane aprendí a ser agresivo"|trans-title=Juan Foyth: "With Pochettino and Kane I learned to be aggressive"|url=https://elpais.com/deportes/2021-09-29/juan-foyth-con-pochettino-y-kane-aprendi-a-ser-agresivo.html |access-date=4 May 2022 |work=[[El País]] |date=29 September 2021 |language=Spanish}}</ref>

Foyth married his longtime girlfriend Ariana Alonso in [[La Plata]], Argentina, in July 2019.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.pronto.com.ar/articulo/famosos/se-caso-juan-foyth/20190715130822330246.html|title=¡Se casó Juan Foyth, el joven defensor de la Selección Argentina!|trans-title=Juan Foyth, young defender of the Argentine national team, got married! |date=15 July 2019 |work=Pronto|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190720153407/https://www.pronto.com.ar/articulo/famosos/se-caso-juan-foyth/20190715130822330246.html |archive-date=20 July 2019|language=Spanish }}</ref>
