'''Kamal Anthony Miller''' (born May 16, 1997) is a Canadian professional [[association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Centre-back|centre-back]] for [[Major League Soccer]] club [[Inter Miami CF]] and the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada national team]].

==Early life==
Born in the [[Toronto]] suburb of [[Scarborough, Ontario|Scarborough]], Miller is of Jamaican and Trinadadian descent.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sportsnet.ca/soccer/canadian-kamal-miller-takes-talents-kitchen-pandemic/|title=Canadian Kamal Miller takes talents to kitchen during pandemic - Sportsnet.ca|website=www.sportsnet.ca}}</ref> He began playing youth soccer at age four with Malvern SC.<ref>{{Canada Soccer player|id=4035}}</ref> Afterward, he played with North Scarborough SC and [[Vaughan Azzurri]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://canadasoccer.com/news/canada-soccer-announce-squad-for-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022/|title=Canada Soccer announces squad for FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022|date=November 12, 2022|work=[[Canadian Soccer Association]]}}</ref>

==College career==
Miller played four years of [[college soccer]] at [[Syracuse University]] between 2015 and 2018. He scored his first collegiate goal on September 22, 2015, against [[Binghamton Bearcats men's soccer|Binghamton]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://cuse.com/news/2015/9/22/MSOC_0922151345.aspx|title=Miller's First Career Goal Leads Orange By Binghamton|website=Syracuse University Athletics}}</ref> In 2018, he was named to the All-[[Atlantic Coast Conference|ACC]] third team<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://cuse.com/news/2018/11/7/mens-soccer-orange-trio-earn-all-acc-honors.aspx|title=Orange Trio Earn All-ACC Honors|website=Syracuse University Athletics}}</ref> and was named to the [[United Soccer Coaches]] All-South Region third team.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://cuse.com/news/2018/12/5/mens-soccer-buchanan-miller-earn-all-region-honors.aspx|title=Buchanan, Miller Earn All-Region Honors|website=Syracuse University Athletics}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Early career===
While at college, Miller also played for [[League1 Ontario]] club [[Vaughan Azzurri]]<ref>{{cite web|date=February 11, 2015|title=Success Stories Keep Coming for League1 Ontario|url=https://www.ontariosoccer.net/news_article/show/744891-success-stories-keep-coming-for-league1-ontario|work=[[Ontario Soccer Association]]}}</ref> and [[USL PDL]] sides [[K-W United FC]]<ref>{{cite web|title=K-W United FC – 2016 Regular Season – Roster – # – Kamal Miller -|url=https://www.uslleaguetwo.com/roster_players/12562556|access-date=March 3, 2019|website=www.uslleaguetwo.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=K-W United FC – 2017 Regular Season – Roster – # – Kamal Miller -|url=https://www.uslleaguetwo.com/roster_players/18332078|access-date=March 3, 2019|website=www.uslleaguetwo.com}}</ref> and [[Reading United AC]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Reading United AC – 2018 Regular Season – Roster – # – Kamal Miller – D|url=https://www.readingunitedac.com/roster_players/24450492?mirror_id=3923379&subseason=482928|access-date=March 3, 2019|website=www.readingunitedac.com}}</ref>

===Orlando City===
On January 11, 2019, Miller was selected 27th overall in the [[2019 MLS SuperDraft]] by [[Orlando City SC|Orlando City]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://dailyorange.com/2019/01/former-syracuse-centerback-kamal-miller-drafted-27th-overall-pick-mls-superdraft/|title=Former Syracuse centerback Kamal Miller drafted 27th overall in MLS SuperDraft|date=January 11, 2019|website=The Daily Orange – The Independent Student Newspaper of Syracuse, New York|access-date=March 3, 2019}}</ref> He officially signed with the club on March 1, 2019<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.orlandocitysc.com/news/orlando-city-sc-signs-2019-mls-superdraft-pick-kamal-miller|title=Orlando City SC Signs 2019 MLS SuperDraft Pick Kamal Miller &#124; Orlando City|website=orlandocitysc}}</ref> and made his professional debut appearance the following day, starting in Orlando's season opener, a 2–2 draw with [[New York City FC]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/matchcenter/orlvsnyc-03-02-2019/|title=ORLvsNYC 03-02-2019 &#124; MLSsoccer.com|website=mlssoccer}}</ref> As part of Orlando's end-of-season roster decisions it was announced Miller had his contract option for the 2020 season exercised.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.orlandocitysc.com/post/2019/11/21/orlando-city-sc-announces-2019-end-season-roster-decisions|title=Orlando City SC Announces 2019 End-of-Season Roster Decisions|website=Orlando City SC|date=November 21, 2019}}</ref>

=== CF Montréal ===
Miller was selected by [[Austin FC]] in the [[2020 MLS Expansion Draft]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=2020 Expansion Draft results: Austin FC makes picks|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2020/12/15/2020-expansion-draft-results-austin-fc-makes-picks|access-date=December 15, 2020|website=[[MLSsoccer.com]]}}</ref> and immediately traded to the [[CF Montréal|Montreal Impact]] in exchange for $225,000 in General Allocation Money and a first round pick (11th) in the [[2021 MLS SuperDraft]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Impact acquires centre back Kamal Miller from Austin FC |url=https://www.impactmontreal.com/en/post/2020/12/15/impact-acquires-centre-back-kamal-miller-austin-fc |website=www.impactmontreal.com |date=December 15, 2020}}</ref> He made his debut for Montreal on April 17 against [[Toronto FC]]. After a strong start with Montreal, the club announced on July 12 a contract extension until 2023.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cfmontreal.com/en/post/2021/07/12/contract-extension-defender-kamal-miller|date=July 12, 2021|title=Contract extension for defender Kamal Miller}}</ref> Miller scored his first goal for his new club on November 3, scoring the second in a 2–0 win over the [[Houston Dynamo]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/mls/mls-cf-montreal-houston-dynamo-recap-nov-3-1.6236528|title=CF Montreal blanks Houston to set up must-win season finale|date=November 3, 2021}}</ref>

As a result of Miller's strong play for Montreal during the [[2022 Major League Soccer season|2022 MLS season]], in July 2022 the league announced him as one of the commissioner's picks for the [[2022 MLS All-Star Game]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.cfmontreal.com/news/kamal-miller-selected-for-the-mls-all-star-game|title=Kamal Miller selected for the MLS All-Star Game|date=July 12, 2022|work=[[CF Montréal]]}}</ref>

=== Inter Miami CF ===
On April 12, 2023 Miller was traded by [[CF Montréal]] to [[Inter Miami CF]] along with $1.3 million dollars in General Allocation Money in exchange for [[Bryce Duke]] and winger [[Ariel Lassiter]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tsn.ca/cf-montreal-trades-kamal-miller-to-inter-miami-for-bryce-duke-and-ariel-lassiter-canada-canmnt-1.1944757|title=CF Montreal sends CanMNT's Miller to Inter Miami|date=April 12, 2023}}</ref> He made his debut on April 22 against the [[Houston Dynamo FC|Houston Dynamo]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canadiansoccerdaily.com/2023/04/24/canadians-abroad-roundup-how-davies-injury-impacts-canadas-summer-plans/|first=Peter|last=Galindo|date=April 24, 2023|title=Canadians Abroad Roundup: How Alphonso Davies’ injury impacts Canada’s summer plans|work=Canadian Soccer Daily}}</ref>

==International career==
===Youth===
Miller was capped at youth level for [[Canada men's national under-20 soccer team|Canada U20s]], competing at the [[2017 CONCACAF U-20 Championship#Group A|2017 CONCACAF U20 Championship]]. Miller was named to the [[Canada men's national under-23 soccer team|Canadian U-23]] provisional roster for the [[2020 CONCACAF Men's Olympic Qualifying Championship]] on February 26, 2020.<ref>{{cite web |title=Canada Soccer announces Provisional List for Concacaf Men's Olympic Qualifying |url=https://www.canadasoccer.com/canada-soccer-announces-provisional-list-for-concacaf-men-s-olympic-qualifying-p162594-preview-1 |publisher=Canada Soccer |access-date=February 26, 2020 |date=February 26, 2020 |archive-date=March 9, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200309191149/https://www.canadasoccer.com/canada-soccer-announces-provisional-list-for-concacaf-men-s-olympic-qualifying-p162594-preview-1 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

===Senior===
On March 18, 2019, he received his first senior call-up for [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada]] for their final [[2019–20 CONCACAF Nations League qualifying|CONCACAF Nations League qualifying]] game against [[French Guiana national football team|French Guiana]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Culver |first1=Jordan |title=Orlando City centerback Kamal Miller called up to Canadian men's national team |url=https://www.prosoccerusa.com/mls/orlando-city-sc/orlando-city-kamal-miller-canada-mnt/ |website=Pro Soccer USA }}{{Dead link|date=March 2023 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> In June 2019, Miller was selected in Canada's [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup]] squad and made his senior international debut on June 23, entering as a 61st minute substitute in Canada's final group stage match, a 7–0 win over [[Cuba national football team|Cuba]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Canada v Cuba Match Report |url=https://www.goldcup.org/en/gold-cup/game-detail/476779#tab_match=match-details |website=www.goldcup.org |access-date=June 23, 2019 |archive-date=November 10, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191110134306/https://www.goldcup.org/en/gold-cup/game-detail/476779#tab_match=match-details |url-status=dead }}</ref> In July 2021 Miller was called up to represent Canada at the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.canadasoccer.com/news/canada-soccer-announces-2021-concacaf-gold-cup-roster/|title=CANADA SOCCER ANNOUNCES 2021 CONCACAF GOLD CUP ROSTER|date=July 1, 2021}}</ref>

In November 2022, Miller was named to Canada's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canada-announces-26-man-squad-for-2022-fifa-world-cup-in-qatar|date=November 13, 2022|title=Canada announces 26-man squad for 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]|first=Charlie|last=O'Connor-Clarke}}</ref> He played every minute of the team's run in [[Qatar]], as they were eliminated in the group stage.<ref>{{cite web|first=Armen|last=Bedakian|url=https://onesoccer.ca/a/watch-canmnt-star-kamal-miller-reflects-on-world-cup-journey-money-buys-happiness-pod-canada|title=WATCH: CanMNT star Kamal Miller reflects on World Cup journey (Money Buys Happiness)|date=January 16, 2023|work=[[OneSoccer]]}}</ref>

In June 2023 Miller was named to Canada's 23-man squad for the [[2023 CONCACAF Nations League Finals]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Former CPLers Loturi, Zator called up by Canada for 2023 Concacaf Nations League Finals|date=June 7, 2023|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canada-canmnt-2023-concacaf-nations-league-final-roster|first=Mitchell|last=Tierney|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]}}</ref> On June 19 he was called-up to the [[2023 CONCACAF Gold Cup]] squad.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canmnt-announces-final-23-man-squad-for-concacaf-gold-cup|date=June 19, 2023|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]|title=CanMNT announces final 23-man squad for Concacaf Gold Cup|first=Charlie|last=O'Connor-Clarke}}</ref>
