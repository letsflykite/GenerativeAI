'''Danilo Luís Hélio Pereira''' {{post-nominals|post-noms=[[Order of Prince Henry|CvIH]] [[Order of Merit (Portugal)|ComM]]}} (born 9 September 1991), known as '''Danilo Pereira''' ({{IPA-pt|dɐˈnilu pɨˈɾɐjɾɐ}}) or simply '''Danilo''', is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[defensive midfielder]] or [[centre-back]] for [[Ligue 1]] club [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] and the [[Portugal national football team|Portugal national team]].

He came through [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]]'s youth academy, before signing with Italian [[Serie A]] side [[Parma Calcio 1913|Parma]] in 2010. He was consecutively loaned to various clubs, before being signed by [[C.S. Marítimo|Marítimo]] in 2013. In July 2015, he joined [[FC Porto|Porto]] for a reported fee of €4.5 million, winning two [[Primeira Liga]] titles and one [[Taça de Portugal]] while being part of the squad that won a [[Double (association football)|domestic double]] in 2020, making 202 official appearances for them. In October 2020, he moved to French side Paris Saint-Germain on an initial one-year loan deal; it was made permanent at the end of the season.

Born in Guinea-Bissau, Pereira represented [[Portugal national youth football team|Portugal at youth level]] at [[Portugal national under-18 football team|under-18]], [[Portugal national under-19 football team|under-19]], [[Portugal national under-20 football team|under-20]] and [[Portugal national under-21 football team|under-21 levels]], reaching the final of the [[2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup]]. He made his senior international debut in 2015, and was part of the squads that won [[UEFA Euro 2016]] and the [[2019 UEFA Nations League Finals]] on home soil.

==Club career==
===Early career===
Born in [[Bissau]], Guinea-Bissau as the oldest child of Quinta Djata, a nurse, Pereira and his family moved to Portugal when he was five. He played his youth football with three clubs in the [[Lisbon]] area, finishing his development with [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.publico.pt/2010/08/22/desporto/noticia/danilo-pereira-um-miudo-timido-em-italia-1452382|title=Danilo Pereira, um miúdo tímido em Itália|trans-title=Danilo Pereira, a shy kid in Italy|newspaper=[[Público (Portugal)|Público]]|first=Marco|last=Vaza|language=pt|date=22 August 2010|access-date=15 March 2018}}</ref>

Benfica subsequently decided against signing Pereira to a professional contract, and Italian side [[Parma F.C.|Parma]] acquired him on a [[Free transfer (association football)|free transfer]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-nos/benfica/detalhe/danilo-pereira-no-parma-457977.html|title=Danilo Pereira no Parma|trans-title=Danilo Pereira to Parma|newspaper=[[Record (newspaper)|Record]]|language=pt|date=18 August 2010|access-date=15 March 2018}}</ref> He spent the second half of the 2010–11 season on loan to [[Aris Thessaloniki F.C.|Aris]] in Greece and, upon his return to the [[Stadio Ennio Tardini]], made his first appearance in [[Serie A]] on 21 December 2011, coming on as a late [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] in a 3–3 home draw against [[Catania Calcio|Catania]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.siciliatoday.net/quotidiano/calcio/Calcio-Parma-Catania-3-3_27812.shtml|title=Calcio, Parma-Catania 3–3|trans-title=Football, Parma-Catania 3–3|publisher=Sicilia Today|first=Salvo|last=Emanuele|language=it|date=21 December 2011|access-date=2 February 2016}}</ref> 

Pereira was loaned again for [[2012–13 Eredivisie|2012–13]], spending the campaign with [[Roda JC Kerkrade|Roda]] in the [[Eredivisie]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rodajc.nl/Nieuws/616/1/roda-jc-kerkrade-huurt-danilo-van-parma|title=Roda JC Kerkrade huurt Danilo van Parma|trans-title=Roda JC Kerkrade get Danilo from Parma|publisher=Roda JC|language=nl|date=29 June 2012|access-date=26 June 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130816232401/http://www.rodajc.nl/Nieuws/616/1/roda-jc-kerkrade-huurt-danilo-van-parma|archive-date=16 August 2013}}</ref> and being first choice to help his team narrowly avoid relegation.

===Porto===
On 2 July 2015, Pereira agreed to a four-year contract with [[FC Porto|Porto]] for a transfer fee of €4.5 million, the highest sale in Marítimo's history, with a [[buyout clause]] of €40 million.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.publico.pt/desporto/noticia/fc-porto-anuncia-danilo-pereira-1700892|title=FC Porto anuncia Danilo Pereira|trans-title=FC Porto announce Danilo Pereira|newspaper=Público|language=pt|date=2 July 2015|access-date=2 July 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fcporto.pt/en/noticias/Pages/Danilo-Pereira-por-quatro-epocas.aspx|title=Danilo Pereira signs for four seasons|publisher=FC Porto|date=3 July 2015|access-date=6 August 2015}}</ref> He scored a career-best six goals – from 33 appearances – in [[2015–16 Primeira Liga|his first year]], in a runner-up finish.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bolanarede.pt/nacional/porto/danilo-pereira-o-dragao-do-ano/|title=Danilo Pereira, o dragão do ano|trans-title=Danilo Pereira, dragon of the year|publisher=Bola Na Rede|first=Joana|last=Quintas|language=pt|date=31 December 2017|access-date=15 March 2018}}</ref> In October 2016, he was named Porto's footballer of the year after winning the "Dragão de Ouro" award.<ref name=RR>{{cite web|url=https://rr.sapo.pt/noticia/66832/danilo_e_o_futebolista_do_ano_no_fc_porto|title=Danilo é o Futebolista do Ano no FC Porto|trans-title=Danilo is FC Porto's Footballer of the Year|publisher=[[Rádio Renascença]]|language=pt|date=24 October 2016|access-date=23 July 2018}}</ref>

In [[2019 Taça de Portugal Final|the final]] of the [[Taça de Portugal]], on 25 May 2019 against Sporting, following a 2–2 draw after [[Overtime (sports)|extra time]] and despite Pereira converting his attempt, Porto lost the [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shootout]] 5–4.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tsf.pt/desporto/direto-quem-leva-a-taca-sporting-0-0-fc-porto-10940801.html|title=Do legado de Dost à frieza de Luiz Phellype.
