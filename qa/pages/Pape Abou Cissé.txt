'''Pape Abou Cissé''' (born 14 September 1995) is a Senegalese professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Center-back|centre back]] for Greek [[Super League Greece|Super League]] club [[Olympiacos F.C.|Olympiacos]] and the [[Senegal national football team|Senegal national team]]. He began his career in Senegal with [[AS Pikine]] and then played for French [[Ligue 2]] club [[AC Ajaccio|Ajaccio]].

==Club career==
A youth international for Senegal, he is a youth product of [[AS Pikine]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ndamli.sn/index.php/component/ndamligue/player/1-ligue-1-senegalaise/7-as-pikine/181-pape-abou-cisse|title=Informations sur le joueur – Pape Abou Cisse|website=ndamli.sn|access-date=26 December 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130601041301/http://www.ndamli.sn/index.php/component/ndamligue/player/1-ligue-1-senegalaise/7-as-pikine/181-pape-abou-cisse|archive-date=1 June 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> After a trial, Cissé joined [[AC Ajaccio|Ajaccio]] in early 2015 as a trainee professional.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.ac-ajaccio.com/Brevi/Pape_Cisse_stagiaire_pro-06136|title=Pape Cissé stagiaire pro|website=AC Ajaccio|language=fr|date=5 February 2015|access-date=25 April 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160313200557/http://www.ac-ajaccio.corsica/Brevi/Pape_Cisse_stagiaire_pro-06136|archive-date=13 March 2016}}</ref> He made his professional debut in a [[Ligue 2]] draw against [[Valenciennes FC|Valenciennes]] in April 2015, playing the entire game.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.lfp.fr/ligue2/feuille_match/79459|title=Valenciennes FC vs. AC Ajaccio|website=Ligue de Football Professionnel|language=fr|date=3 April 2015|access-date=25 April 2015}}</ref> He went on to make 71 appearances in the French second tier.<ref name=Soccerway/>

In 2017, he joined [[Olympiacos F.C.|Olympiacos]] as a pre-contract signing.{{citation needed|date=August 2019}} On 14 October 2017, he scored his first goal with Olympiacos, after an assist from [[Kostas Fortounis]] in a 4–3 away win against [[Panionios F.C.|Panionios]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gazzetta.gr/football/superleague/article/1148805/panionios-olympiakos-3-4 |title= Πανιώνιος–Ολυμπιακός 3–4  |website=gazzetta.gr |language=el|date= 14 October 2017|access-date=29 August 2019}}</ref> Benfica were reportedly interested in signing the player in the January 2018 transfer window.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://dz.besoccer.com/info/un-renfort-senegalais-pour-benfica-326759 |title=Un renfort sénégalais pour Benfica |first=Aymeric|last=Butot |website=dz.besoccer.com |language=fr|date=9 November 2017|access-date=29 August 2019}}</ref>

Cissé scored an injury-time equaliser for Olympiacos against [[Panathinaikos F.C.|Panathiniakos]] in the first derby of the [[2018–19 Superleague Greece]] season.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.sport24.gr/football/ellada/SuperLeague/olympiakos_vs_panathinaikos/olympiakos-panathhnaikos-1-1-o-sise-isofarise-thn-gkolara-toy-katse.5360786.html |title=Ολυμπιακός – Παναθηναϊκός 1–1: Ο Σισέ ισοφάρισε την γκολάρα του Κάτσε |website=sport24.gr |language=el|date=11 November 2018}}</ref> He was included in [[UEFA]]'s Europa League Breakthrough Team for 2018: the citation described him as having a "towering presence in the Olympiacos back line, allied to deceptive pace and a great awareness [and] also a threat from set pieces."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/news/newsid=2589211.html |title=Europa League breakthrough team of 2018 |website=uefa.com|date=2 January 2019|access-date=29 August 2019}}</ref> On 12 February 2020, Pape Abou Cisse scored a quick-fire double to quash [[Lamia F.C.|Lamia]]’s stubborn challenge and secure Olympiacos’ qualification for the [[Greek Cup|Greek Cup semi-finals]], in a thrilling 3–2 home win game.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.onsports.gr/podosfairo/story/665097/olympiakos-lamia-3-2-ta-highlights-tis-erythroleykis-prokrisis-photos-video |title=Ολυμπιακός-Λαμία 3-2 |website=www.onsports.gr|date=12 February 2020|access-date=12 February 2020}}</ref> On 27 February 2020, scored with a stooping header from a corner-kick, when French international [[Mathieu Valbuena]] curling over a dangerous delivery into the heart of the [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]]'s penalty area in a glorious 2–1 away win in London for the [[Europa League|Europa League Round of 32, 2nd leg]]. He has been included in the [[UEFA Europa League|UEFA Europa League Team of the Week]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sport24.gr/football/EuropaLeague/arsenal-olympiakos-1-2-o-thrulos-ths-eyrwphs.5687358.html |title= Άρσεναλ - Ολυμπιακός 1-2: Ο Θρύλος της Ευρώπης |website=www.sport24.gr|date=27 February 2020|access-date=28 February 2020}}</ref>

On 30 January 2021, Senegalese international defender Pape Cissé will be loaned by Olympiakos to [[AS Saint-Étienne|Saint-Étienne]] with an option to buy. The former player of AS Pikine has the opportunity to bounce back in a historic French club which can keep him against €13 million. <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.today24.news/en/2021/01/pape-abou-cisse-is-loaned-to-the-greens-official.html |title= Pape Abou Cissé is loaned to the Greens |website=www.today24.news|date=30 January 2021}}</ref> 

On 19 August 2021, he scored after [[Aguibou Camara]] did not manage to beat the goalkeeper of Slovan, sealing a thriumphic 3-0 home win [[2021–22 UEFA Europa League|2021–22 UEFA Europa League playoffs 1st round]] game against [[ŠK Slovan Bratislava]]. <ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.sport24.gr/football/olympiakos-slovan-3-0-ekane-kratisi-stoys-omiloys-toy-europa-league-me-triara.9328439.html |title=Ολυμπιακός - Σλόβαν 3-0: Έκανε κράτηση στους ομίλους του Europa League με τριάρα |publisher=www.sport24.gr |date=19 August 2021}}</ref> On 4 December 2021, with two goals by Cisse (24 ', 49') Olympiakos defeated [[OFI Crete F.C.|OFI]] in [[Theodoros Vardinogiannis Stadium]], sealing a vital away win in his club effort to win the championship. <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.novasports.gr/sport/podosfairo/event/super-league-1/article/12214123/ofi-olympiakos-1-3-diplo-me-sise-nter-for-vids/ |title= ΟΦΗ – Ολυμπιακός 1-3: Διπλό με… Σισέ-ντερ φορ! |website=www.novasports.gr|date=4 December 2021}}</ref> On 12 December 2021, he was the only scorer after [[Rony Lopes]]'s assist in a 1-0 home win game against [[Aris F.C.]]  <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sport-fm.gr/article/podosfairo/superleague1/117551 |title= ΟΛΥΜΠΙΑΚΟΣ- ΑΡΗΣ 1-0 |website=www.sport-fm.gr|date=12 December 2021}}</ref>

==International career==
Cissé represented the [[Senegal national under-20 football team|Senegal U20s]] at the [[2015 African U-20 Championship]] in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/32934023|title=Under-20 World Cup: African sides ready for challenge|date=29 May 2015|website=bbc.com|access-date=29 August 2019|first=Oluwashina|last=Okeleji}}</ref>

On 24 August 2018, he received his first senior call-up, for the [[2019 Africa Cup of Nations qualification Group A|2019 Africa Cup of Nations qualifier]] match against [[Madagascar national football team|Madagascar]] on 9 September 2018.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.afrik-foot.com/can-2019-le-senegal-avec-5-nouveaux-contre-madagascar|title= CAN 2019: le Sénégal avec 5 nouveaux contre Madagascar|website=afrik-foot.com |language=fr|date=24 August 2018|access-date=29 August 2019}}</ref> He made his senior debut on 13 October 2018, in another qualifier at home to [[Sudan national football team|Sudan]], and scored the only goal of the match.<ref name=NFT/> Cissé's performances in the [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations]], after replacing Napoli star [[Kalidou Koulibaly]] who had tested positive for coronavirus, in the Lions of Teranga's first two Afcon matches against [[Zimbabwe national football team|Zimbabwe]] and [[Guinea national football team|Guinea]] attracted the interest of many European clubs.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/12521739/africa-cup-of-nations-who-made-whoscored-coms-team-of-the-group-stages|title= Africa Cup of Nations: Who made WhoScored.com's team of the group stages?|website=www.skysports.com |language=en|date=21 January 2022}}</ref>

He was appointed a [[National Order of the Lion|Grand Officer of the National Order of the Lion]] by [[President of Senegal]] [[Macky Sall]] following the nation's victory at the 2021 Africa Cup of Nations.<ref name= "reuters">{{cite web |title=Cash prizes, real estate, and highest honours as Senegal celebrates Nations Cup heroes |url=https://www.reuters.com/lifestyle/sports/cash-prizes-real-estate-highest-honours-senegal-celebrates-nations-cup-heroes-2022-02-08/ |website=[[Reuters]] |access-date=28 March 2023 |date=8 February 2022}}</ref>
