'''Osman Bukari''' (born 13 December 1998) is a Ghanaian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[winger (association football)|winger]] for Serbian club [[Red Star Belgrade]] and the [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national team]].

==Club career==
===AS Trenčín===
First European club in Osman Bukari's professional career was Slovakian side [[AS Trenčín|AS Trenčin]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://ghanasoccernet.com/exclusive-talented-youngster-osman-bukari-joins-slovakian-outfit-as-trencin-from-anderlecht |title=Bukari joins Trenčin|publisher=Ghana soccer |access-date=12 July 2018}}</ref> Bukari joined them in July 2018 from [[Accra Lions FC|Accra Lions]] in his homeland. In his two years in [[Slovakia]]n football, Bukari was one of the best players in his team, playing 66 matches and recording 16 goals and 25 assists. In the [[2019–20 Slovak First Football League|2019–20 season]], he was officially Trenčin's best player and was inducted in the league's best XI.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fortunaliga.sk/clanok/2104-v-jedenastke-sezony-az-sedem-futbalistov-majstra-najlepsim-hracom-greif |title=Best XI of Slovak league |publisher=Fortunaliga |access-date=12 July 2020}}</ref> He was also on the three-name shortlist for the best player of the season.

===Gent===
After his eye-catching performances in Slovakia, Belgian side [[K.A.A. Gent|KAA Gent]] signed Bukari<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.kaagent.be/nl/nieuws/04-09-2020/welkom-osman |title=Bukari joins Gent |publisher=KAA Gent|access-date=4 September 2020}}</ref> on 4 September 2020. After signing a three-year contract with Gent, Bukari quickly established himself as one of the first-team regulars. He played in the [[UEFA Europa League]] as Gent faced [[TSG 1899 Hoffenheim|Hoffenheim]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/match/2029739--gent-vs-hoffenheim/ |title=UEFA Europa League vs. Hoffenheim|publisher=UEFA Europa League|access-date=29 October 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/match/2029847--hoffenheim-vs-gent/ |title=UEFA Europa League vs. Hoffenheim|publisher=UEFA Europa League|access-date=10 December 2020}}</ref> [[Red Star Belgrade]]<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/match/2029763--crvena-zvezda-vs-gent/ |title=UEFA Europa League vs. Red Star|publisher=UEFA Europa League|access-date=5 November 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/match/2029799--gent-vs-crvena-zvezda/ |title=UEFA Europa League vs. Red Star|publisher=UEFA Europa League|access-date=26 November 2020}}</ref> and [[FC Slovan Liberec|Slovan Liberec]]<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/match/2029728--liberec-vs-gent/ |title=UEFA Europa League vs. Liberec|publisher=UEFA Europa League|access-date=22 October 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/match/2029812--gent-vs-liberec/ |title=UEFA Europa League vs. Liberec|publisher=UEFA Europa League|access-date=3 December 2020}}</ref> in the group stage. He scored four goals and made six assists in 35 appearances for Gent.

====Loan to Nantes====
On 12 August 2021, French [[Ligue 1]] outfit [[FC Nantes]] signed Bukari <ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fcnantes.com/articles/article2809.php?num=37573 |title=FC OSMAN BUKARI PRÊTÉ AU FC NANTES!|publisher=autor |access-date=12 August 2021}}</ref> on a season-long loan. The Ghanaian was a part of the team that won [[Coupe de France]], the fourth in club's history, and secured European qualification after a 21-year-long wait.

===Red Star Belgrade===
Serbian champions [[Red Star Belgrade]] signed Bukari<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.crvenazvezdafk.com/en/vest/13366/osman-bukari-predstavljen-na-marakani |title=Bukari joins Red Star |publisher=Red Star Belgrade|access-date=21 June 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/osman-bukari-fk-crvena-zvezda-seal-signing-of-ghana-winger-from-kaa-gent/bltc35a12ee32a1dd62 |title=Bukari joins Red Star |publisher=GOAL|access-date=21 June 2022}}</ref> on 21 June 2022 in a deal worth €3 million. He made his club debut against [[FK Radnički Niš|Radnički Niš]]<ref>{{cite web |url=https://superliga.rs/sezone/2022-23/match/3376 |title=Bukari makes his Red Star debut |publisher=Serbian SuperLiga|access-date=10 July 2022}}</ref> and got on the scoresheet in the same match. He was [[Player of the match|man of the match]] with three goals in an assist in Red Star Belgrade's [[UEFA Champions League]] qualifying match against [[FC Pyunik|Pyunik]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/match/2035379--crvena-zvezda-vs-pyunik/ |title=Hat-trick against Pyunik |publisher=UEFA Champions League|access-date=3 August 2022}}</ref> in which the Serbian team won 5–0. Bukari scored one of the best goals seen in [[Eternal derby (Serbia)|Eternal derby]] when he scored with a fine left-footed volley in Red Star Belgrade's 1–1 draw at rivals [[FK Partizan|Partizan]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fqdcAJQ6IcA |title=Goal vs. Partizan |publisher=Arena sport|access-date=31 August 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
Bukari made his [[Ghana national football team|Ghana]] bow on 25 March 2021 against [[South Africa national soccer team|South Africa]]<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cafonline.com/total-africa-cup-of-nations/2021/matches/2032528/ |title=Bukari makes his Ghana debut|publisher=CAF |access-date=25 March 2021}}</ref> in an [[Africa Cup of Nations]] qualifying match. Good performances on the club level for FC Nantes and Red Star Belgrade earned him a Black Stars recall and he's been a regular for the national team since March 2022. He scored his first goal for his country on 1 June 2022 in an AFCON qualifier against [[Madagascar national football team|Madagascar]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cafonline.com/africa-cup-of-nations/2023-qualifiers/matches/2285038/ |title=First goal for Ghana|publisher=CAF |access-date=1 June 2022}}</ref> Bukari has been called up by national coach [[Otto Addo]] to represent the Black Stars in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022]] [[FIFA World Cup|World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.ghanafa.org/otto-addo-announces-squad-for-world-cup-finals |title=Ghana World Cup 2022 squad |publisher=Football Association of Ghana|access-date=14 November 2022}}</ref> In his World Cup debut, Bukari scored in the 90th minute in a 3–2 loss to [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]], and performed the renowned celebration of their captain [[Cristiano Ronaldo]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/africa/63755476 |title=World Cup 2022: Bukari on copying Cristiano Ronaldo goal celebration |website=BBC Sport |date=25 November 2022 }}</ref>
