The '''2022 FIFA World Cup qualification UEFA Group I''' was one of the ten [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)|UEFA groups]] in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification|World Cup qualification]] tournament to decide which teams would qualify for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] tournament in Qatar.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/58b8109657d40e3d/original/ijoruwm3ly5kgz9wbu6j-pdf.pdf |title=UEFA preliminary competition for the FIFA World Cup 2022: Draw procedures |work=[[FIFA]] |format=PDF |date=22 October 2020 |access-date=22 October 2020}}</ref> Group I consisted of six teams: [[Albania national football team|Albania]], [[Andorra national football team|Andorra]], [[England national football team|England]], [[Hungary national football team|Hungary]], [[Poland national football team|Poland]] and [[San Marino national football team|San Marino]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://editorial.uefa.com/resources/0264-1108c702e548-758110ebe57c-1000/draw_result_sheet.pdf |title=FIFA World Cup Preliminary Competition – Draw Results |work=UEFA |format=PDF |date=7 December 2020 |access-date=7 December 2020}}</ref> The teams played against each other home-and-away in a [[round-robin tournament|round-robin format]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/271202c2bf80db17/original/adjlwts3o6c57g0skqor-pdf.pdf |title=UEFA preliminary competition format for the FIFA World Cup 2022 |work=FIFA |format=PDF |date=4 December 2020 |access-date=7 December 2020}}</ref>

The group winners, England, qualified directly for the World Cup finals, while the runners-up, Poland, advanced to the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA second round|second round (play-offs)]].

* Receiving a red card (red card suspensions could be extended for serious offences)
* Receiving two yellow cards in two different matches (yellow card suspensions were carried forward to the play-offs, but not the finals or any other future international matches)
