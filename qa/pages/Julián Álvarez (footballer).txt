'''Julián Álvarez''' ({{IPA-es|xuˈljan ˈalβaɾes}}; born 31 January 2000) is an Argentine professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]] for [[Premier League]] club [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] and the [[Argentina national football team|Argentina national team]].

Álvarez began his career at [[Club Atlético River Plate|River Plate]] in his native country. He was a key member of the Argentina team that won the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], as well as the Manchester City side that won the [[Treble (association football)|continental treble]] in the [[2022–23 Manchester City F.C. season|2022–23 season]], becoming the first ever player to win a treble and a World Cup in the same season.

Álvarez appeared late on during the second leg of the [[2018 Copa Libertadores finals]], with River beating rivals Boca Juniors.<ref name="Soccerway"/> Álvarez scored the first goal of his senior career on 17 March 2019, netting in a 3–0 league win over [[Club Atlético Independiente|Independiente]].<ref name="Soccerway"/> During the succeeding December, he scored in the [[2019 Copa Argentina Final]] against [[Central Córdoba de Santiago del Estero|Central Córdoba]] as River won 3–0 to secure the trophy. In 2020, Álvarez netted five goals in six [[Copa Libertadores]] group stage encounters.

On 25 May 2022, Álvarez scored six goals for River Plate in an 8–1 win over [[Club Alianza Lima|Alianza Lima]] in the [[2022 Copa Libertadores|Copa Libertadores]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://mscfootball.com/2022/05/future-manchester-city-player-julian-alvarez-scores-six-goals-for-river-plate-in-8-1-win/ | title=Future Manchester City Player Julian Alvarez Scores Six Goals for River Plate in 8-1 Win | date=26 May 2022 | access-date=26 May 2022 | archive-date=26 May 2022 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220526052540/https://mscfootball.com/2022/05/future-manchester-city-player-julian-alvarez-scores-six-goals-for-river-plate-in-8-1-win/ | url-status=live }}</ref>

===Manchester City===
On 31 January 2022, his 22nd birthday, it was confirmed that Álvarez had signed for [[Premier League]] champions [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] on a five-and-a-half-year contract, but with the player remaining at River Plate on loan until July.<ref>{{cite news|title=City sign Julian Alvarez from River Plate|url=https://www.mancity.com/news/mens/manchester-city-complete-signing-of-julian-alvarez-63779231|access-date=31 January 2022|publisher=Manchester City F.C.|date=31 January 2022|archive-date=23 October 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221023090319/https://www.mancity.com/news/mens/manchester-city-complete-signing-of-julian-alvarez-63779231|url-status=live}}</ref> He scored on his competitive debut on 30 July, where City lost 3–1 to [[Liverpool F.C.–Manchester City F.C. rivalry|rivals]] [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] in the [[2022 FA Community Shield]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=Hafez |first=Shamoon |date=29 July 2022 |title=Community Shield: Liverpool 3-1 Man City - Darwin Nunez seals victory |website=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/live/football/62275194 |access-date=30 July 2022 }}{{Dead link|date=April 2023 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> On 7 August, Álvarez made his Premier League debut after coming on as a substitute for [[Erling Haaland]] in a 2–0 away win over [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]].<ref>{{cite news|last=McNulty|first=Phil|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/62366384|title=West Ham 0-2 Man City: Erling Haaland scores twice as Man City turn on style|publisher=BBC Sport|date=7 August 2022|access-date=7 August 2022|archive-date=7 August 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220807020635/https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/62366384|url-status=live}}</ref> On 31 August, Álvarez scored his first two Premier League goals in a 6–0 win against [[Nottingham Forest F.C.|Nottingham Forest]] at the [[City of Manchester Stadium|Etihad Stadium]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/62655348 |title=Man City 6-0 Nottingham Forest: Erling Haaland scores another hat-trick in easy victory |website=BBC Sport |date=31 August 2022 |access-date=31 August 2022 |archive-date=1 November 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221101161811/https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/62655348 |url-status=live }}</ref> On 5 October, he scored his first [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] goal in a 5–0 win over [[F.C. Copenhagen|Copenhagen]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/manchester-city-julian-alvarez-pep-guardiola-norwegian-erling-haaland-b2196822.html |title=Pep Guardiola talks up ‘weapon’ Julian Alvarez after latest Man City goal |publisher=The Independent |date=6 October 2022 }}</ref>

On 17 May 2023, he scored the last goal in a 4–0 win over [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]] in the Champions League semi-final second leg, which cemented his club's qualification to the [[2023 UEFA Champions League final|final]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.mancity.com/news/mens/manchester-city-real-madrid-champions-league-semifinal-second-leg-63819943 |title=Super City secure UCL final spot with stunning win over Real Madrid |publisher=Manchester City |date=17 May 2023 }}</ref> Four days later, he scored the only goal in a 1–0 victory over [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] to win his first Premier League title, and third in a row for his club.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2023/may/21/manchester-city-chelsea-premier-league-match-report |title=Manchester City cap title triumph with win over Chelsea thanks to Álvarez |publisher=The Guardian |date=21 May 2023 }}</ref> On 10 June, he won the [[2023 UEFA Champions League final|Champions League final]] with his club, despite being a non-playing substitute, to become among the players who won the FIFA World Cup and Champions League in the same season, in addition to achieving both the latter and the Copa Libertadores.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://mundoalbiceleste.com/2023/06/10/julian-alvarez-and-maximo-perrone-win-champions-league-with-manchester-city/ |title=Julián Álvarez and Máximo Perrone win Champions League with Manchester City |publisher=Mundo Albiceleste |date=10 June 2023 }}</ref>

Álvarez made his debut for the [[Argentina national football team|Argentina senior team]] on 3 June 2021, in a [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONMEBOL)|World Cup qualifier]] against [[Chile national football team|Chile]].
