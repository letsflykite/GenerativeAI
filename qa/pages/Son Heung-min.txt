'''Son Heung-min''' ({{lang-ko|손흥민}}; {{IPA-ko|son.ɣɯŋ.min}}; born 8 July 1992) is a South Korean professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]] for [[Premier League]] club [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] and [[Captain (association football)|captains]] the [[South Korea national football team|South Korea national team]].<ref name="Position">{{cite web |url=https://www.whoscored.com/Teams/30/Archive/England-Tottenham?stageId=16368# |title=Premier League – 2018/2019 – Tottenham Formations Summary |website=Whoscored.com |access-date=2 June 2020 |archive-date=7 June 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200607131458/https://www.whoscored.com/Teams/30/Archive/England-Tottenham?stageId=16368 |url-status=live }}</ref> Considered one of the best players in the world and widely regarded as one of the greatest Asian footballers of all time, he is known for his explosive speed, finishing, two-footedness and ability to link play.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Top 50 players at World Cup 2022, No. 14: Son Heung-min |url=https://www.foxsports.com/stories/soccer/top-50-players-at-world-cup-2022-no-14-son-heung-min |access-date=2023-05-01 |website=FOX Sports |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=The 100 best male footballers in the world 2022 |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/ng-interactive/2023/jan/24/the-100-best-male-footballers-in-the-world-2022 |access-date=2023-05-01 |website=The Guardian |language=en}}</ref>

Born in [[Chuncheon]], [[Gangwon Province, South Korea|Gangwon Province]], Son relocated to Germany to join [[Hamburger SV]] at 16, making his debut in the [[Bundesliga]] in 2010. In 2013, he moved to [[Bayer 04 Leverkusen|Bayer Leverkusen]] for a club record €10 million before signing for Tottenham for £22 million two years later, becoming the most expensive Asian player in history.<ref name=AsianTransferRecord /> While at Tottenham, Son became the top Asian goalscorer in both Premier League and [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] history,<ref>{{cite A full international since 2010, Son has represented South Korea at the [[2014 FIFA World Cup|2014]], [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022]] [[FIFA World Cup]]s and is his country's joint top scorer at World Cups, tied with Park Ji-sung and [[Ahn Jung-hwan]] on three goals. Son has also represented South Korea at the [[2018 Asian Games]], where the team won gold, and the [[2011 AFC Asian Cup|2011]], [[2015 AFC Asian Cup|2015]], and [[2019 AFC Asian Cup|2019]] editions of the [[AFC Asian Cup]]. Korea was the runner-up in 2015.

==Club career==
===Hamburger SV===
[[File:Son Heung-Min.jpg|left|thumb|upright|Son warming up for [[Hamburger SV]] in 2011]]
In August 2008, Son dropped out of Dongbuk High School's football club (formerly [[FC Seoul Reserves and Academy|FC Seoul under-18 team]])<ref>{{cite news |url=http://sports.news.naver.com/sports/index.nhn?category=worldfootball&ctg=news&mod=read&office_id=076&article_id=0002318350 |script-title=ko:손흥민 10대1 인터뷰①"하트브레이커 춤? 내가 워낙 몸치라" |publisher=The Sports Chosun |language=ko |date=22 March 2013 |access-date=28 August 2015 |archive-date=31 January 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160131234130/http://sports.news.naver.com/sports/index.nhn?category=worldfootball&ctg=news&mod=read&office_id=076&article_id=0002318350 |url-status=live }}</ref> and joined [[Hamburger SV]]'s youth academy at age 16 through {{ill|Korean FA Youth Project|ko|대한축구협회 우수선수 해외유학 프로젝트}}.<ref>{{cite news |script-title=ko:KFA 우수선수 해외유학 6기생, 29일 독일로 출국 |url=http://www.kfa.or.kr/layer_popup/popup_live.php?act=news_tv_detail&idx=2500&div_code=news&check_url=bGF5ZXI=&lang=KOR |language=ko |publisher=Korea Football Association |date=28 July 2008 |access-date=20 January 2019 |archive-date=20 January 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190120194535/http://www.kfa.or.kr/layer_popup/popup_live.php?act=news_tv_detail&idx=2500&div_code=news&check_url=bGF5ZXI=&lang=KOR |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.abendblatt.de/sport/amateure/article566773/Abenteuer-in-einer-anderen-Welt.html |title=Abenteuer in einer anderen Welt |language=de |publisher=Hamburger Abendblatt |access-date=21 January 2011 |date=6 October 2008 |first=Andreas |last=Pröpping |archive-date=19 July 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100719002824/http://www.abendblatt.de/sport/amateure/article566773/Abenteuer-in-einer-anderen-Welt.html |url-status=live }}</ref> A year later, he returned to South Korea. After participating in the [[2009 FIFA U-17 World Cup|FIFA U-17 World Cup]], he formally joined [[Hamburger SV]]'s youth academy in November 2009<ref>{{cite news |script-title=ko:손흥민"독일 함부르크 갑니다" |url=https://news.naver.com/main/read.nhn?mode=LSD&mid=sec&sid1=102&oid=087&aid=0000232331 |language=ko |website=Kangwon Ilbo |date=11 November 2009 |access-date=20 January 2019 |archive-date=8 December 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191208110401/https://news.naver.com/main/read.nhn?mode=LSD&mid=sec&sid1=102&oid=087&aid=0000232331 |url-status=live }}</ref>

He was impressive in the 2010–11 pre-season, leading the team with nine goals, and signed his first professional contract on his 18th birthday.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://archiv.mopo.de/archiv/2010/20100711/sport/hsv/son_ist_vehs_sturmjuwel.html |title=Son ist Vehs Sturmjuwel |language=de |newspaper=Hamburger Morgenpost |location=Hamburg |access-date=21 January 2011 |date=11 July 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100717142419/http://archiv.mopo.de/archiv/2010/20100711/sport/hsv/son_ist_vehs_sturmjuwel.html |archive-date=17 July 2010}}</ref> After scoring against [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] in August, he was out for two months due to a foot injury. He returned on 30 October 2010 to score his first league goal, against [[1. FC Köln]] in the 24th minute. The goal made Son the youngest [[List of Hamburger SV players|Hamburg player]] to score a goal in the Bundesliga at 18, breaking the record held by [[Manfred Kaltz]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://kr.news.yahoo.com/sports/uefa/view?aid=2010110600100559719 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110718134033/http://kr.sports.yahoo.com/news/uefa/view?aid=2010110600100559719 |archive-date=18 July 2011 |script-title=ko:손흥민 놓칠라 ... 함부르크 감독 부랴부랴 "계약 늘리자" |language=ko |website=Joongang Ilbo |publisher=Yahoo! |date=6 November 2010 |access-date=6 November 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://news.sportsseoul.com/read/soccer/891447.htm |script-title=ko:123년 함부르크 역사상 '최연소골' 손흥민 "이제 시작" |language=ko |publisher=Sports Seoul |access-date=6 November 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101104050213/http://news.sportsseoul.com/read/soccer/891447.htm |archive-date=4 November 2010}}</ref>

===Bayer Leverkusen===

====2020–21: PFA Team of the Year====
[[File:Heung-Min Son 2021.png|thumb|226x226px|Son in 2021 with Tottenham Hotspur]]
In the second Premier League match of the [[2020–21 Tottenham Hotspur F.C. season|2020–21 season]], Son scored four goals, all assisted by [[Harry Kane]], in a 5–2 win against [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] and the first League win and away win of the season. This was the first time in Premier League history in which a single player scored four goals in a match while being assisted by the same teammate.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54135440 |title=Southampton 2: 5 Tottenham Hotspur |first=Gary |last=Rose |work=BBC Sport |date=20 September 2020 |access-date=20 September 2020 |archive-date=20 September 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200920142611/https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54135440 |url-status=live }}</ref>  In the Premier League game on 4 October, he scored a brace against Manchester United to help Tottenham win 6–1, which is the biggest win for Tottenham at [[Old Trafford]] and their best result against United since a home win in 1932.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54313274 |title= Manchester United 1 : 6 Tottenham Hotspur|work=BBC Sport}}</ref> Based on his performances, Son received his third Premier League Player of the Month award on 13 November 2020.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Son Heung-min Named Premier League Player of the Month for October |date=13 November 2020 |url=https://www.90min.com/posts/son-heung-min-named-premier-league-player-of-the-month-for-october |access-date=13 November 2020 |publisher=90min |language=en-GB}}</ref>

On 2 January 2021, Son scored his 100th goal for Tottenham in the 3–0 win against [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/55450391 |title= Tottenham Hotspur 0–3 Leeds United|work=BBC Sport |date=2 January 2021|first= Steve|last= Sutcliffe}}</ref> On 10 February 2021, he provided three assists, but Tottenham lost 5–4 to [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] in the [[2020–21 FA Cup|FA Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/55953180 |title=Wed 10 Feb 2021, The FA Cup – {{subst:lc:Fifth}} round: Everton 5–4 Tottenham Hotspur |publisher=BBC Sport |date=10 February 2021 |access-date=11 February 2021}}</ref> On 7 March, Son provided an assist to Harry Kane in a 4–1 win against Crystal Palace, and this, their 14th combined goal effort whereby one assisted another, set a record for the most goal combinations in a single Premier League season.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.espn.co.uk/football/tottenham-hotspur/story/4257853/tottenhams-kane-and-son-break-goalscoring-record-in-premier-league |title=Tottenham's Kane and Son break goalscoring record in Premier League |date=7 March 2021 |work=ESPN |access-date=8 March 2021 }}</ref> After the end of the season, he and Kane were selected for the [[PFA Team of the Year (2020s)#Premier League 2|PFA Premier League Team of the Year]], although Tottenham finished in seventh place.<ref name="PFAteam"/>

In doing so, Son had the distinction of scoring the first goal under each of Tottenham's last three permanent managers.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/OptaJoe/status/1456355986667708426|title=Opta Joe: 3 – Son Heung-min has now scored Tottenham's first goal under each of their last three permanent managers (Jose Mourinho, Nuno Espirito Santo and Antonio Conte). ====2022–23: 100th Premier League goal ====
After winning the Golden Boot the previous season, Son had a poor start to the [[2022–23 Tottenham Hotspur F.C. season|2022–23 season]], failing to score any goal in the first eight games and proving only one assist.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://theathletic.com/3593125/2022/09/15/son-heung-min-spurs-poor-form/ |title=Son Heung-min's Spurs form: A blip or long-term worry? |work=The Athletic |first1=Charlie|last1= Eccleshare |first2=Mark |last2=Carey|date=15 September 2022 }}</ref> He ended his goal drought in the game against [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] on 17 September, when he came off the bench to score a hat-trick in 13 minutes to help Tottenham win 6–2.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/sep/17/tottenham-leicester-premier-league-match-report |title=Son's rapid treble lifts Tottenham and exposes Leicester's fragility |first=Same |last=Dalling |work=The Observer |date=17 September 2022}}</ref> On 1 November, Son collided with [[Olympique de Marseille|Marseille]]'s [[Chancel Mbemba]] during the final matchday of the UEFA Champions League group stage.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Why is South Korea's Heung-min Son wearing a mask at the World Cup? {{!}} Goal.com |url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/why-south-koreas-heung-min-son-wearing-mask-world-cup/blt06977fc1d7dda1ec |access-date=2022-12-03 |website=www.goal.com}}</ref> This collision caused Son to suffer an [[Facial trauma|orbital fracture]] in his left eye. Son managed to recover to the point that he would be healthy enough to play in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]] for South Korea, after he declared himself fit to play.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Son in South Korea World Cup squad despite injury |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63607340 |access-date=2022-12-03}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Son is a versatile player who can play in any [[Forward (association football)|forward]] position,<ref name="Position"/><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.spurs-web.com/spurs-news/stats-reveal-how-son-heung-mins-positioning-and-role-have-changed-dramatically-this-season/ |title=Stats reveal how Son Heung-min's positioning and role have changed dramatically this season |publisher=SpursWeb |date=29 November 2020 |access-date=1 December 2020}}</ref> ([[Forward (association football)#Winger|winger]], [[Forward (association football)#Second striker|second striker]], [[Forward (association football)#Striker|striker]]) and can even be deployed as an [[Midfielder#Attacking midfielder|attacking midfielder]] or [[Defender (association football)#Wing-back|wing-back]] if necessary. He himself has confirmed this, saying, "I don't care where I play. The main thing is I'm in the game. I can play as a second striker or behind. Whatever the coach says, I'll do. I don't have a favorite position. I'll be anywhere and always on the throttle."<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.mopo.de/hsv/hsv-stuermer-heung-min-son-im-interview-wie-viele-tore-schiessen-sie-heute--herr-son-,5067038,8728222.html |title=Wie viele Tore schießen Sie heute, Herr Son? |trans-title=How many goals will you score today, Mr. Son? |language=de |newspaper=Hamburger Morgenpost |location=Hamburg |access-date=16 September 2011 |date=30 July 2011 |first=Florian |last=Rebien |archive-date=22 March 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120322221121/http://www.mopo.de/hsv/hsv-stuermer-heung-min-son-im-interview-wie-viele-tore-schiessen-sie-heute--herr-son-,5067038,8728222.html |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="best players in Premier League">{{cite news |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/european/tottenham-vs-dortumnd-result-final-score-highlights-goals-jan-vertonghen-son-heungmin-a8779851.html |title=Tottenham vs Dortmund: Jan Vertonghen says Son Heung-Min is one of the best players in Premier League |newspaper=The Independent |last1=Delaney |first1=Miguel |date=14 February 2019 |access-date=6 October 2019 |archive-date=6 October 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191006143315/https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/european/tottenham-vs-dortumnd-result-final-score-highlights-goals-jan-vertonghen-son-heungmin-a8779851.html |url-status=live }}</ref>

Son is known for his two-footed ability, explosive pace, positional sense, movement, close control and clinical finishing which make him especially effective on the counter-attack.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2018/02/10/son-heung-min-spurs-not-so-secret-weapon/ |title=Why Son Heung-min is Tottenham's not-so-secret weapon |first=JJ |last=Bull |date=10 February 2018 |newspaper=The Daily Telegraph |location=London |access-date=10 March 2018 |archive-date=4 March 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180304114808/https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2018/02/10/son-heung-min-spurs-not-so-secret-weapon/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Moreover, he has drawn praise from teammates and in the media for his selfless work-rate and defensive contribution, and is capable of providing assists for teammates, in addition to scoring goals himself.<ref name="best players in Premier League"/><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.foxsportsasia.com/football/1089771/ballon-dor-2019-5-favourites-to-win-the-award-lionel-messi/ |title=Ballon d'Or 2019: 5 favourites to win the award |publisher=FOX Sports Asia |last1=Rangarajan |first1=Aditya |date=1 May 2019 |access-date=6 October 2019 |archive-date=6 October 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191006134927/https://www.foxsportsasia.com/football/1089771/ballon-dor-2019-5-favourites-to-win-the-award-lionel-messi/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Personal life==

Son has dated the [[K-pop]] singers [[Bang Min-ah]] and [[Yoo So-young]], and is currently single.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2019/mar/08/son-heung-min-tottenham-spurs-interview-marriage-wait|title=Son Heung-min: 'My father says I shouldn't marry until I retire and I agree'|first=David|last=Hytner|newspaper=The Guardian |date=8 March 2019|via=www.theguardian.com}}</ref>

Son used the suspension of football during the [[COVID-19 pandemic]] to complete [[Conscription in South Korea|South Korean mandatory military service]]. He was exempted from military service with a gold medal at the [[Football at the 2018 Asian Games|Asian Games]], but he had to do basic military training regardless of exemption. He completed basic training in the [[Republic of Korea Marine Corps]] in [[Jeju Province|Jeju]] in April–May 2020, finishing in the top five of 157 trainees.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sportbible.com/football/football-news-son-heung-min-explains-why-he-wont-get-married-during-football-career-20200802|title=Son-Heung Min Explains Why He Won't Get Married During Football Career|website=www.sportbible.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://onefootball.com/en/news/tottenham-star-awarded-pilsung-prize-set-to-return-to-the-club-next-week-29881496|title=Tottenham star awarded 'Pilsung' prize, set to return to the club next week|website=OneFootball}}</ref>
