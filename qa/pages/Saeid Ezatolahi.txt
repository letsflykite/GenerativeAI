'''Saeid Ezatolahi Afagh''' ({{lang-fa| سعید عزت‌اللهی آفاق}}; born 1 October 1996) is an Iranian professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[defensive midfielder]] for Danish club [[Vejle Boldklub|Vejle]] and the [[Iran national football team|Iran national team]].

Ezatolahi came up through [[Malavan F.C.|Malavan's]] [[Youth system|youth academy]] and impressed from an early age, making his first team debut in 2012 and being the [[Iran Pro League]]'s youngest ever player at the age of 16. Ezatolahi has earned several accolades, including the Iranian federation's [[Iran football award winners#Young player of the year 2|young player of the year]] award. In 2014, he was named one of the most talented football players according to Wonderkids.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fmscout.com/a-football-manager-2014-wonderkids.html|title=Football Manager 2014 Wonderkids|author=Stam|work=FM Scout}}</ref>

Ezatolahi became the youngest [[Attacker|goalscorer]] in the history of the [[Iran national football team]] at the age of 19 years and 42 days after scoring in a 3–1 victory against [[Turkmenistan national football team|Turkmenistan]] on 12 November 2015.<ref name="youngest">{{cite web|url=http://www.persianfootball.com/news/2015/11/15/irans-journey-to-russia-fans-pop-up-for-the-surf-turf/|title=Iran's Journey to Russia… Fans Pop Up for the Surf & Turf!|publisher=Persianfootball.com|date=15 November 2015}}</ref> This record was broken by 17-year-old [[Allahyar Sayyadmanesh]] on 6 June 2019.<ref>[https://90tv.ir/news/%D9%84%DA%98%DB%8C%D9%88%D9%86%D8%B1-%D8%A7%D8%B3%D8%AA%D9%82%D9%84%D8%A7%D9%84-%D8%AC%D9%88%D8%A7%D9%86-%D8%AA%D8%B1%DB%8C%D9%86-%DA%AF%D9%84%D8%B2%D9%86-%D8%AA%D8%A7%D8%B1%DB%8C%D8%AE-%D8%AA%DB%8C%D9%85-%D9%85%D9%84%DB%8C-%D8%B4%D8%AF لژیونرِ استقلال جوان‌ترین گلزن تاریخ تیم ملی شد]</ref>

==Club career==
===Early years===
Ezatolahi's father, Nader, and uncle Ghader Ezatolahi played for Malavan in the [[Takht Jamshid Cup]]. Saeid started his career with [[Shahrdari Bandar Anzali F.C.|Shahrdari Bandar Anzali]] in 2008 as a youth player.<ref>{{cite web|script-title=fa:گفتگو با سعید عزت اللهی عضو تیم ملی زیر 10 سال ایران|url=http://malavanbandar.persianblog.ir/post/134/|language=Persian|website=Malavan Bandar|accessdate=1 September 2014}}</ref>

===Malavan===
He moved to the [[Malavan F.C.|Malavan]] youth academy in the summer of 2010 and was promoted to the first team in July 2012.

====2012–13====
Ezatolahi made his debut against [[Rah Ahan F.C.|Rah Ahan]] on 26 October 2012 as a substitute just 25 days after his 16th birthday, making him the youngest ever player in the history of the [[Iran Pro League]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Holders Mexico begin title defence|url=https://www.fifa.com/u17worldcup/news/newsid=2198164/index.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131018045110/http://www.fifa.com/u17worldcup/news/newsid=2198164/index.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=18 October 2013|publisher=FIFA.com|accessdate=1 September 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Iranian Premier League Stats: 2012-2013 Season|url=http://www.iplstats.com/website12-13/Week13/RAH_MLV_13.htm|website=iplstats.com|publisher=IPL Stats|accessdate=1 September 2014}}</ref> At the end of the season, [[Tehran]] giants [[Esteghlal F.C.|Esteghlal]] reportedly made an offer for him.<ref>{{cite web|script-title=fa:باشگاه استقلال خواستار جذب سعید عزت‌اللهی شد/ درخواست 2 میلیاردی ملوان|url=http://www.farsnews.ir/newstext.php?nn=13920324000903|publisher=Fars News|language=Persian|accessdate=1 September 2014| date=14 June 2013 }}</ref> He was also awarded the prize for [[Iran football award winners#Young player of the year 2|young player of the year]].<ref>{{cite web|script-title=fa:بهترین‌های لیگ دوازدهم معرفی شدند|url=http://fararu.com/fa/news/155716/%D8%A8%D9%87%D8%AA%D8%B1%DB%8C%D9%86%E2%80%8C%D9%87%D8%A7%DB%8C-%D9%84%DB%8C%DA%AF-%D8%AF%D9%88%D8%A7%D8%B2%D8%AF%D9%87%D9%85-%D9%85%D8%B9%D8%B1%D9%81%DB%8C-%D8%B4%D8%AF%D9%86%D8%AF|website=fararu.com/|language=Persian|publisher=Fararu|accessdate=1 September 2014}}</ref>

====2013–14====
He made his season debut as a regular starter against [[Saba Qom F.C.|Saba Qom]].<ref>{{cite web|script-title=fa:جزئیات مسابقه صباي قم - ملوان بندر انزلي|url=http://iranleague.ir/MatchDetails/5723|language=Persian|website=iranleague.ir|publisher=Iran League|accessdate=1 September 2014}}</ref> After giving an impressive performance at the [[2013 FIFA U-17 World Cup]] in October 2013, European clubs like [[Besiktas J.K.|Besiktas]], [[FC Lugano|Lugano]] and [[FC Lausanne Sport|Lausanne Sport]] were reported to follow the youngster closely. In December 2013, Ezatolahi seemed to be on his way to Brazilian Serie A club [[Atlético Paranaense]], which was rapidly denied by his management. In the first week of December 2013, [[FC Rubin Kazan|Rubin Kazan]] showed concrete interest in Ezatolahi by sending an invitation letter.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ezatollahi has received an invitation letter from Rubin Kazan|url=http://www.persianleague.com/the-news/1-latest-news/8478-ezatollahi-has-received-an-invitation-letter-from-rubin-kazan.html|website=PersianLeague.com|publisher=Persian League|accessdate=1 September 2014}}</ref>

====2014–15====
Ezatolahi was enrolled for Malavan's [[2014–15 Iran Pro League|2014–15]] squad, but he did not play any matches due to finalizing his contract with Atlético.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ffiri.ir/club/%D8%A8%D8%A7%D8%B4%DA%AF%D8%A7%D9%87-%D9%87%D8%A7%DB%8C-%D9%84%DB%8C%DA%AF-%D8%A8%D8%B1%D8%AA%D8%B1/101/%D9%85%D9%84%D9%88%D8%A7%D9%86-%D8%A8%D9%86%D8%AF%D8%B1-%D8%A7%D9%86%D8%B2%D9%84%DB%8C/10011|title=باشگاه ملوان بندر انزلی|work=ffiri.ir}}</ref>

===Rostov===
====2015–16====
[[File:Saeid Ezatolahi 2016.jpg|thumb|Ezatolahi before a Rostov match.]]
At the end of the 2014–15 season, Ezatolahi chose not to sign with [[Atlético Madrid B]], and on 21 July 2015, Ezatolahi joined international teammate [[Sardar Azmoun]] at [[Russian Premier League]] club [[FC Rostov|Rostov]] with a four-year contract.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.persianfootball.com/news/2015/07/17/ezatollahi-closes-in-on-rostov-deal/|title=Ezatollahi closes in on Rostov deal|work=persianfootball.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eurofotbal.cz/premier-liga/|title=Premier Liga - Fotbal Rusko - Premier Liga: zprávy, výsledky, statistiky|work=eurofotbal.cz}}</ref> Due to [[Visa (document)|Visa]] issues, he could not play for Rostov until 2016, and in January 2016, Ezatolahi's Visa issues were resolved.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://90tv.ir/news/198536/%D8%AD%D8%B1%D9%81-%D9%87%D8%A7%DB%8C-%D8%AC%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%A8-%D8%B3%D8%AA%D8%A7%D8%B1%D9%87-%D8%AA%DB%8C%D9%85-%DA%A9%DB%8C-%D8%B1%D9%88%D8%B4-%DA%A9%D9%87-%D8%AE%D8%A7%DA%A9%D9%BE%D9%88%D8%B1-%D9%88-%D9%85%D8%A7%DB%8C%D9%84%DB%8C-%DA%A9%D9%87%D9%86-%D8%A7%D9%88-%D8%B1%D8%A7-%D8%AD%D8%AA%DB%8C-%D8%AF%D8%B9%D9%88%D8%AA-%D9%87%D9%85-%D9%86%DA%A9%D8%B1%D8%AF%D9%86%D8%AF|title=90tv.ir - وبسایت رسمی برنامه ۹۰ - حرف های جالب ستاره تیم کی روش که خاکپور و مایلی کهن او را حتی دعوت هم نکردند|publisher=}}</ref> He made his debut for the club on 2 February 2016 playing in a 3–0 friendly win against Chinese side [[Jiangsu Sainty F.C.|Jiangsu Sainty]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fc-rostov.ru/season/games/2513|title=Матчи - Футбольный клуб "Ростов"|publisher=}}</ref> Ezatolahi scored his first goal for Rostov on 27 February 2016 in a 2–0 friendly win against Norwegian side [[Stabæk Fotball|Stabæk]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.budstikka.no/sport/fotball/stabak-fotball/0-2-tap-for-femte-gang/s/5-55-274945|title=0-2-tap for femte gang|date=27 February 2016|publisher=}}</ref>

Ezatolahi made his first team debut for Rostov on 12 May 2016 in a 3–1 victory against [[FC Dynamo Moscow]].

====2016–17====
On 26 July 2016, Ezatolahi made his first start for Rostov in an official game in the first leg of the [[2016–17 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round|2016–17 UEFA Champions League third qualifying round]] against [[R.S.C. Anderlecht]] and scored a goal in the 16th minute, equalizing the score to 1–1. In the second half of the game, another shot by him from outside of the box was denied by the crossbar, and the game ended with a score of 2–2.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[UEFA]]|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2017/matches/round=2000781/match=2020350/index.html|title=Rostov-Anderlecht game summary|date=26 July 2016}}</ref> He is the first scorer of the club in a European match (not counting the [[UEFA Intertoto Cup]]) and also the youngest ever Iranian player to score at the Champions League.<ref>[http://www.varzesh3.com/news/1344481/عزت-اللهی-تاریخ-ساز-روستوف-شد عزت اللهی تاریخ ساز روستوف شد]</ref> He scored his first [[Russian Premier League]] goal for Rostov on 20 August 2016 in a 3–0 victory over [[FC Tom Tomsk]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[Russian Premier League]]|url=http://rfpl.org/match/match_11439.html|title=Rostov-Tom Tomsk game report|language=Russian|date=20 August 2016}}</ref>

His contract with [[FC Rostov]] expired in 2016. His agent Mehdi Hagitali said his new team would be a team where he would consistently play.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[RIA Novosti]]|url=http://rsport.ru/football/20161220/1114015041.html|script-title=ru:Футболист "Ростова" Эзатолахи продолжит карьеру там, где будет играть - агент|date=20 December 2016|language=Russian}}</ref> FC Rostov removed him from their [[Russian Premier League]] roster on 31 December 2016.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[Russian Premier League]]|url=http://rfpl.org/players/Ezatolahi-Afag-Said|title=Ezatolahi Russian Premier League player page|accessdate=1 January 2017|language=Russian}}</ref>

====Anzhi Makhachkala (loan)====
In February 2017, Ezatolahi was loaned to [[Russian Premier League]] side [[FC Anzhi Makhachkala|Anzhi Makhachkala]] for six months. Ezatolahi made his first appearance for the club on 8 February 2017, coming on as a second-half substitute in a friendly against [[FK Dukla Prague|Dukla Prague]]. On 24 February 2017, he officially signed a new contract with [[FC Rostov]] and finalized the loan deal with Anzhi.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[FC Rostov]]|url=http://www.fc-rostov.ru/press/news/10780|script-title=ru:Саид Эзатолахи продлил контракт с «Ростовом»|date=25 February 2017|language=Russian}}</ref> In his first league game for Anzhi against [[FC Rubin Kazan]] on 6 March 2017, he was sent-off in the 77th minute after receiving two yellow cards, with his team conceding a goal in an 0–1 loss shortly after.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[Russian Premier League]]|url=http://rfpl.org/match/match_11648.html|title=Anzhi-Rubin game report|date=6 March 2017|language=Russian}}</ref> During his loan, he started in only three league games out of 13 that Anzhi played in that span, making additional seven appearances as a substitute, with Anzhi achieving its objective of avoiding relegation from the Russian Premier League.

====Amkar Perm (loan)====
Upon returning from the Anzhi loan, he didn't make any appearances for FC Rostov in the first eight games of the season, and on 31 August 2017, he joined [[FC Amkar Perm]] on loan until the end of the 2017–18 season.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[FC Amkar Perm]]|url=http://www.fc-amkar.org/news/32350/|script-title=ru:ЭЗЗАТОЛЛАХИ – ИГРОК «АМКАРА»|date=31 August 2017|language=Russian}}</ref> He made his first appearance for Amkar on 21 September 2017 in a [[Russian Cup (football)|Russian Cup]] match.

The loan contract is reportedly worth €1.3 million.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tehrantimes.com/news/426341/Standard-Liege-set-sight-on-Saeid-Ezatolahi-report|title=Standard Liege set sight on Saeid Ezatolahi: Report|date=10 August 2018}}</ref>

==== Reading (loan) ====
On 31 August 2018, [[Reading F.C.|Reading]] announced the signing of Ezatolahi on a season-long loan deal from [[FC Rostov|Rostov]].<ref name="Saeid Ezatolahi">{{cite web |title=Royals sign Saeid Ezatolahi on season-long loan |url=https://www.readingfc.co.uk/news/2018/august/royals-sign-saeid-ezatolahi-on-season-long-loan/ |website=readingfc.co.uk |publisher=Reading F.C. |accessdate=31 August 2018 |date=31 August 2018}}</ref> He had a couple of man of the match performances in his first few games before an unfortunate season-ending injury.

==== Eupen (loan) ====
On 28 August 2019, he moved on loan to the Belgian club [[K.A.S. Eupen|Eupen]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[FC Rostov]]|url=http://www.fc-rostov.ru/press/news/12839|title=Саид Эззатоллахи перешел в бельгийский ФК "Эйпен" на правах аренды|trans-title=Saeid Ezatolahi joined Belgian club Eupen on loan|date=28 August 2019|language=Russian}}</ref>

In June 2020, he returned to Rostov and became a free agent when his contract expired later that month.<ref>{{cite web|title=An Uncertain Future ahead of Saeid Ezatolahi|url=https://www.tasnimnews.com/en/news/2020/06/13/2285512/an-uncertain-future-ahead-of-saeid-ezatolahi|accessdate = 5 July 2020}}</ref>

==== Vejle ====
On 20 August 2020, Ezatolahi signed a three-year deal with newly promoted [[Danish Superliga]] club [[Vejle Boldklub]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=20 August 2020|title=VEJLE BOLDKLUB HENTER IRANSK LANDSHOLDSSPILLER|url=https://vejle-boldklub.dk/vejle-boldklub-henter-iransk-landsholdsspiller/|access-date=|website=}}</ref>

On 19 January 2022, he was sent on a six-month loan to [[Qatar Stars League]] club [[Al-Gharafa SC|Al-Gharafa]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Vejle Boldklub sender midtbaneprofil til Qatar |url=https://www.berlingske.dk/sport/vejle-boldklub-sender-midtbaneprofil-til-qatar |work=Berlingske.dk |date=19 January 2022 |language=da}}.</ref>

==International career==
[[File:Iran and Spain match at the FIFA World Cup (11).jpg|thumb|Ezatolahi playing for [[Iran national football team|Iran]] against [[Andrés Iniesta]]'s [[Spain national football team|Spain]] in [[2018 FIFA World Cup]]]]

===Youth===
Saeid Ezatolahi participated with the [[Iran national under-17 football team|Iran U17 national team]] in all group matches as well as the round of 16 in the [[2013 FIFA U-17 World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Saeid EZATOLAHI|url=https://www.fifa.com/worldfootball/statisticsandrecords/players/player=371736/index.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131021033242/http://www.fifa.com/worldfootball/statisticsandrecords/players/player=371736/index.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=21 October 2013|publisher=FIFA.com|accessdate=1 September 2014}}</ref>

He was invited to the [[Iran national under-20 football team|Iran U20]] by [[Ali Doustimehr]]. He scored his first goal against [[United Arab Emirates national under-20 football team|the United Arab Emirates]].<ref>{{cite web|script-title=fa:پیروزی تیم فوتبال جوانان برابر امارات با گلزنی عزت‌اللهی|url=http://ffiri.ir/news/detail/%D9%BE%DB%8C%D8%B1%D9%88%D8%B2%DB%8C-%D8%AA%DB%8C%D9%85-%D9%81%D9%88%D8%AA%D8%A8%D8%A7%D9%84-%D8%AC%D9%88%D8%A7%D9%86%D8%A7%D9%86-%D8%A8%D8%B1%D8%A7%D8%A8%D8%B1-%D8%A7%D9%85%D8%A7%D8%B1%D8%A7%D8%AA-%D8%A8%D8%A7-%DA%AF%D9%84%D8%B2%D9%86%DB%8C-%D8%B9%D8%B2%D8%AA-%D8%A7%D9%84%D9%84%D9%87%DB%8C/115063/news.aspx|language=Persian|website=FFIRI.ir|publisher=FFIRI|accessdate=1 September 2014}}</ref> Ezatolahi played two matches for Iran during the [[2014 AFC U-19 Championship]].

As the [[2016 AFC U-23 Championship]] was not held during the [[FIFA International Match Calendar]], Ezatolahi was not released by Rostov.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.the-afc.com/ir-iran-afc-u-23-championship-2016/blow-for-iran-as-nourollahi-ruled-out-of-afc-u23-championship|title=Blow for Iran as Nourollahi ruled out of AFC U23 Championship - AFC|publisher=}}</ref>

===Senior===
He was invited to the [[Iran national football team|Iran senior national team]] camp by [[Carlos Queiroz]] on 16 May 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.persianfootball.com/news/2015/05/16/queiroz-names-extended-team-melli-squad/|title=Queiroz names extended Team Melli squad|work=persianfootball.com}}</ref> Ezatolahi was included in the final list for the [[Exhibition game|friendly]] match against [[Uzbekistan national football team|Uzbekistan]] and the [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification (AFC)|2018 FIFA World Cup qualifier]] against [[Turkmenistan national football team|Turkmenistan]]. He debuted as a starter against Uzbekistan on 11 June 2015 in a 1–0 win.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://theworldgame.sbs.com.au/match/2033055/uzbekistan-vs-iran/live|title=Uzbekistan vs Iran - Live Match - Football: The World Game on SBS|work=The World Game}}</ref> He scored his first senior national team goal during his fourth cap on 12 November 2015 in a 3–1 World Cup qualifying win over Turkmenistan.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2015/11/12/asia/wc-qualifying-asia/iran/turkmenistan/2028661/|title=Iran vs. Turkmenistan - 12 November 2015 - Soccerway|publisher=}}</ref> He became the youngest goal scorer for the Iranian national team at the age of 19 years and 42 days.<ref name="youngest"/> In May 2018, he was named in Iran's preliminary squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite web|title= World Cup 2018: All the confirmed squads for this summer's finals in Russia|url= https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/440833652018}} {{Dead link|date=June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44357568|title=World Cup 2018: Iran head coach Carlos Queiroz names final squad|date=4 June 2018|work=BBC Sport|access-date=9 June 2018|language=en-GB}}</ref> He missed Iran's first group match against [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]] because of a red card in qualification but played other two matches against [[Spain national football team|Spain]] and [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]]. He was ruled out of Iran's squad for [[2019 AFC Asian Cup]] because of injury.

==Personal life==
[[File:Media conference of Ferrari (Iranian film)-15.jpg|thumb|Ezatollahi at ''Ferari'' film press conference during 2017 [[Fajr Film Festival]]]]
Ezatollahi is son of Nader Ezatolahi, an Iranian football coach in [[Malavan F.C.|Malavan Academy]] who is also his agent.

In 2019, Ezatollah published a rap song supporting the Iranian team at [[2019 AFC Asian Cup]] since he got injured playing for Reading before the cup and could not be with the team.
