'''Aaron Christopher Ramsdale''' (born 14 May 1998) is an English professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for {{English football updater|Arsenal}} club [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] and the [[England national football team|England national team]].

Ramsdale began his senior club career playing for [[Sheffield United F.C.|Sheffield United]] and signed for [[AFC Bournemouth]] in 2017. Following successive loans to [[Chesterfield F.C.|Chesterfield]] and [[AFC Wimbledon]], Ramsdale played a season with Bournemouth and re-joined Sheffield United in a transfer worth an initial £18 million. In 2021, Ramsdale signed for Arsenal in a club record transfer worth up to £30 million, becoming their most expensive goalkeeper.

Ramsdale has represented England at all levels from [[England national under-18 football team|under-18]] to the [[England national football team|senior team]], and won the [[UEFA European Under-19 Championship]] in [[2017 UEFA European Under-19 Championship|2017]], and was in the squad that finished as runners-up at [[UEFA Euro 2020]].

==Early life==
Ramsdale was born in [[Stoke-on-Trent]], Staffordshire and was raised in the nearby village of [[Chesterton, Staffordshire|Chesterton]].<ref name="SUFC Signing">{{Cite web|title=Aaron returns as Blades sign exciting stopper|url=https://www.sufc.co.uk/news/2020/august/ramsdale-signs/|access-date=20 August 2020|publisher=Sheffield United F.C.}}</ref> Ramsdale began his career at [[Newcastle-under-Lyme]]-based club Marsh Town. Goalkeeping coach [[Fred Barber]] took him up to [[Bolton Wanderers F.C.|Bolton Wanderers]] for a trial and they ended up signing him.<ref name="The Sentinel 2019">{{cite news |title=Premier League debut for 21-year-old North Staffs starlet - and ex-Stoke City midfielder's nephew |url=https://www.stokesentinel.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/bournemouth-aaron-ramsdale-chris-hemming-3199059 |website=stokesentinel.co.uk |publisher=The Sentinel |first=Peter |last=Smith |date=11 August 2019 |access-date=18 November 2021}}</ref> In 2014, Ramsdale was identified by ''[[The Sentinel (Staffordshire)|The Sentinel]]'' as a future prospect after helping his school, [[Sir Thomas Boughey Academy|Sir Thomas Boughey High School]], to reach the [[English Schools' Football Association|English Schools' FA Cup]] semi-final.<ref name="The Sentinel 2019"/>

===AFC Bournemouth===
On 31 January 2017, Ramsdale was signed by [[Premier League]] club [[AFC Bournemouth]] for an undisclosed fee believed to be in the region of £800,000.<ref>{{cite news|title=AFC Bournemouth sign young 'keeper Aaron Ramsdale from Sheffield United|url=http://www.afcb.co.uk/news/article/2016-17/afc-bournemouth-keeper-aaron-ramsdale-from-sheffield-united-3547589.aspx|date=31 January 2017|access-date=11 February 2017|publisher=AFC Bournemouth|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170201214616/http://www.afcb.co.uk/news/article/2016-17/afc-bournemouth-keeper-aaron-ramsdale-from-sheffield-united-3547589.aspx|archive-date=1 February 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Payne |first=Ned |date=2 February 2017 |title=AFC Bournemouth: Youngster Ramsdale thanks Blades after joining Cherries |url=https://www.bournemouthecho.co.uk/sport/15065190.afc-bournemouth-youngster-aaron-ramsdale-thanks-blades-after-joining-cherries/ |access-date=2023-02-26 |website=Bournemouth Echo |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last= |first= |date=2020-08-15 |title=Sheffield United to sign Aaron Ramsdale from Bournemouth for £18.5m |language=en-GB |work=The Guardian |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2020/aug/15/sheffield-united-to-sign-aaron-ramsdale-from-bournemouth-transfer |access-date=2023-02-26 |issn=0261-3077}}</ref> He was on the bench for the final game of the [[2016–17 AFC Bournemouth season|2016–17 season]] against [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Leicester City 1–1 AFC Bournemouth|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/39912024|website=BBC Sport|date=21 May 2017|access-date=1 February 2018}}</ref>

Ramsdale made both his Premier League debut and his full debut for Bournemouth on the opening day of the [[2019–20 AFC Bournemouth season|2019–20 season]], in a 1–1 draw against former team Sheffield United.<ref>{{cite news|title=Bournemouth 1–1 Sheffield United|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49215986|first=Michael|last=Emons|website=BBC Sport|date=10 August 2019|access-date=21 November 2019}}</ref> Ramsdale established himself as Bournemouth's first choice keeper in the first few months of the 2019–20 season, starting every Premier League game for the club until 12 January, when he missed the match against [[Watford F.C.|Watford]] due to injury.<ref>{{cite news|title=Bournemouth 0–3 Watford|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/50996713|first=Thomas|last=Mallows|website=BBC Sport|date=12 January 2020|access-date=3 March 2020}}</ref> He returned to the team in the next Premier League game, a 1–0 loss to [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Norwich City 1–0 AFC Bournemouth|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/51073182|first=Matthew|last=Howarth|website=BBC Sport|date=18 January 2020|access-date=3 March 2020}}</ref>

He signed a new, long-term contract with the club in October 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/50124484|title=Aaron Ramsdale: Bournemouth keeper signs new long-term deal|date=21 October 2019|website=BBC Sport|access-date=21 November 2019}}</ref> That same month, Ramsdale's form saw him win the club's Player of the Month award for October, keeping clean sheets in the games against Norwich City and Watford.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.afcb.co.uk/news/first-team/ramsdale-voted-october-player-of-the-month/|title=Ramsdale voted October Player of the Month|date=31 October 2019|publisher=AFC Bournemouth|access-date=3 March 2020}}</ref> His form continued in the next few months, as he again picked up the club's Player of the Month award in January for another string of excellent performances, including a Man of the Match winning display against [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.afcb.co.uk/news/first-team/ramsdale-wins-player-of-the-month|title=Ramsdale wins Player of the Month|date=30 January 2020|publisher=AFC Bournemouth|access-date=3 March 2020}}</ref> On 10 August, Ramsdale was named Player of the Year by the club's supporters.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.afcb.co.uk/news/club-news/ramsdale-your-player-of-the-year/|title=RAMSDALE YOUR PLAYER OF THE YEAR|publisher=AFC Bournemouth|date=10 August 2020|access-date=15 August 2020}}</ref>

====Loans to Chesterfield and Wimbledon====
In the January 2018 transfer window, Ramsdale was loaned out to [[Chesterfield F.C.|Chesterfield]] for the rest of the [[2017–18 Chesterfield F.C. season|2017–18 season]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.chesterfield-fc.co.uk/news/2018/january/20180105-fourth-new-recruit-joins-spireites/|title=Fourth New Recruit Joins Spireites|publisher=Chesterfield F.C.|date=5 January 2018|access-date=1 February 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180201212800/https://www.chesterfield-fc.co.uk/news/2018/january/20180105-fourth-new-recruit-joins-spireites/|archive-date=1 February 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> Ramsdale made his Chesterfield debut against [[Accrington Stanley F.C.|Accrington Stanley]] on 6 January 2018, but scored an [[Own goal (association football)|own goal]] after he "allowed a seemingly harmless [[Kayden Jackson|Jackson]] strike from the right to squirm into the net", in a 4–0 loss.<ref>{{cite news|title=Accrington Stanley 4–0 Chesterfield|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/42519872|website=BBC Sport|date=6 January 2018|access-date=1 February 2018}}</ref> A week later, against [[Luton Town F.C.|Luton Town]], he kept a clean sheet in a 2–0 win.<ref>{{cite news|title=Chesterfield 2–0 Luton Town|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/42587767|website=BBC Sport|date=13 January 2018|access-date=1 February 2018}}</ref> In the 2017–18 season, Ramsdale made 19 appearances in total for the club in [[EFL League Two|League Two]], but was ultimately unable to help prevent the club from suffering relegation to the [[National League (division)|National League]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Chesterfield relegated from League Two after Morecambe draw|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/43885346|website=BBC Sport|date=24 April 2018|access-date=3 March 2020}}</ref>

On 4 January 2019, Ramsdale joined [[AFC Wimbledon]] on loan until the end of the [[2018–19 AFC Wimbledon season|2018–19 season]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.afcwimbledon.co.uk/news/2019/january/dons-sign-goalkeeper-on-loan-from-bournemouth/|title=Dons sign goalkeeper on loan from Bournemouth|publisher=AFC Wimbledon|date=4 January 2019|access-date=21 November 2019}}</ref> The next day, Ramsdale made his debut for the Dons in a 3–2 [[2018–19 FA Cup|FA Cup]] third round victory over [[Fleetwood Town F.C.|Fleetwood Town]] at the [[Highbury Stadium (Fleetwood)|Highbury Stadium]] in Fleetwood.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.afcwimbledon.co.uk/news/2019/january/dons-march-into-fourth-round-with-a-dramatic-winner/|title=Dons march into fourth round with a dramatic winner|publisher=AFC Wimbledon|date=5 January 2019|access-date=21 November 2019}}</ref> His league debut came a week later in a 1–1 draw with [[Coventry City F.C.|Coventry City]] at the [[Ricoh Arena]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.afcwimbledon.co.uk/fixtures/first-team/201819/january/coventry-city-vs-afc-wimbledon-on-12-jan-19/|title=Dons earn a creditable draw at Coventry|publisher=AFC Wimbledon|date=12 January 2019|access-date=21 November 2019}}</ref> He helped the club defeat [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham]] in the FA Cup. In the 2018–19 season, Ramsdale made 20 appearances in total for the club in [[EFL League One|League One]], and in doing so he won the club's Young Player of the Season Award for his performances in goal,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.afcwimbledon.co.uk/news/2019/april/player-of-the-year-will-quick-to-focus-on-completing-survival-bid/|title=Player of the Year Will quick to focus on completing survival bid|publisher=AFC Wimbledon|date=28 April 2019|access-date=21 November 2019}}</ref> as Wimbledon achieved survival in League One despite being 10 points from safety in February.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/bradford-vs-wimbledon/393636|title=Bradford 0–0 AFC Wimbledon: Wally Downes' Dons secure League One status|website=Sky Sports|date=4 May 2019|access-date=21 November 2019}}</ref>

==International career==
===Youth===
Ramsdale has represented England at [[England national under-18 football team|Under 18]], [[England national under-19 football team|Under 19]], [[England national under-20 football team|Under 20]] and [[England national under-21 football team|Under 21]] level.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefa.com/england/all-teams/players?p=357107|title=Profile – Aaron Ramdale|publisher=The Football Association |access-date=13 March 2017}}</ref>

He was first called up by England U18 in March 2016 and made his debut for the team in a 4–1 win over [[Republic of Ireland national football team|Republic of Ireland U18]] on 27 March 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sufc.co.uk/news/2016/march/call-up-for-young-keeper/|title=Call-up for young keeper|publisher=Sheffield United F.C.|date=16 March 2016|access-date=1 February 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180201202427/https://www.sufc.co.uk/news/2016/march/call-up-for-young-keeper/|archive-date=1 February 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> He went on to make two appearances for the England U18 team.{{citation needed|date=November 2022}}

In August 2016, Ramsdale was called up by England U19, where he made his debut on 4 September 2016, in a 1–0 loss against [[Belgium national under-19 football team|Belgium U19]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sufc.co.uk/news/2016/august/ramsdale-called-up/|title=Ramsdale called-up|publisher=Sheffield United F.C.|date=24 August 2016|access-date=1 February 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180201203304/https://www.sufc.co.uk/news/2016/august/ramsdale-called-up/|archive-date=1 February 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> Ramsdale started all five games for the [[England national under-19 football team|England under-19 team]] that won the [[2017 UEFA European Under-19 Championship]]<ref name="U19 Champion">{{cite web|title=European Under-19 Championship: England beat Portugal in final|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40619862|website=BBC Sport|date=15 July 2017|access-date=9 August 2017}}<br />{{cite news|title=Chelsea crucial to England winning U19 European Championship – Ramsdale|url=http://www.espn.co.uk/football/england-u19/story/3158799/chelsea-crucial-to-england-winning-u19-european-championship-aaron-ramsdale|publisher=ESPN|date=16 July 2017|access-date=1 February 2018}}</ref> and kept three clean sheets in five matches during the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.afcb.co.uk/news/first-team/ramsdales-england-u19s-make-euros-final|title=RAMSDALE'S ENGLAND U19S MAKE EUROS FINAL|publisher=AFC Bournemouth|date=12 July 2017|access-date=1 February 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180201205538/https://www.afcb.co.uk/news/first-team/ramsdales-england-u19s-make-euros-final|archive-date=1 February 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

After winning the UEFA European U19-Championship, Ramsdale was called up by England U20 in August 2017, where he made his debut on 4 September 2017, in a 0–0 draw against [[Switzerland national under-20 football team|Switzerland U20]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.afcb.co.uk/news/club-news/players-set-for-international-action|title=PLAYERS SET FOR INTERNATIONAL ACTION|publisher=AFC Bournemouth|date=29 August 2017|access-date=1 February 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180201205844/https://www.afcb.co.uk/news/club-news/players-set-for-international-action|archive-date=1 February 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 18 May 2018, Ramsdale received a call up to [[England national under-21 football team|England U21]]s for the first time by Manager [[Aidy Boothroyd]], for the [[Toulon Tournament]].<ref>{{cite news|publisher=The Football Association|date=18 May 2018|access-date=18 May 2018|title=ENGLAND U21S HEAD TO MAURICE REVELLO TOURNAMENT IN TOULON WITH A 20-MAN SQUAD |url=http://www.thefa.com/news/2018/may/18/england-u21s-squad-named-for-toulon-180518}}</ref> Ramsdale received his first cap for the England U21 team on 1 June 2018, playing the full 90 minutes in a 4–0 win against [[Qatar national under-20 football team|Qatar]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bournemouthecho.co.uk/sport/16266791.cherries-keeper-aaron-ramsdale-makes-england-under-21s-debut/|title=Cherries keeper Aaron Ramsdale makes England under-21s debut|date=3 June 2018|newspaper=Bournemouth Daily Echo|access-date=13 May 2021}}</ref> Ramsdale was first choice goalkeeper during qualifiers for the [[2021 UEFA European Under-21 Championship]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/16261/12134095/england-u21-rhian-brewster-eberechi-eze-and-marc-guehi-withdraw-from-squad|title=England U21: Rhian Brewster, Eberechi Eze and Marc Guehi withdraw from squad|date=16 November 2020|website=Sky Sports|access-date=13 May 2021}}</ref> and also started all three games for England as they were eliminated at the group stage of that tournament.<ref>{{cite news|title=Aidy Boothroyd: Aaron Ramsdale throws support behind under-fire England U21s boss|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/international/aidy-boothroyd-england-u21s-ramsdale-b1825956.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210402092222/https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/international/aidy-boothroyd-england-u21s-ramsdale-b1825956.html |archive-date=2 April 2021 |url-access=limited |url-status=live|first=Nick|last=Mashiter|newspaper=The Independent|date=2 April 2021|access-date=13 May 2021}}</ref>

===Senior team===

was called up to the squad one game into the tournament after Henderson withdrew with an injury.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57480270 |title=Euro 2020: Aaron Ramsdale |newspaper=The Guardian |date=15 November 2021 |access-date=16 November 2021}}</ref> In November 2022, Ramsdale was named in Gareth Southgate's [[2022 FIFA World Cup squads|26-man squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] held in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.englandfootball.com/articles/2022/Nov/10/england-mens-senior-squad-named-for-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022-20221011|title=England ==Personal life==
Ramsdale is a [[West Bromwich Albion F.C.|West Bromwich Albion]] fan, and cited an admiration for their former keeper [[Ben Foster (footballer)|Ben Foster]] when he played against him in 2019.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bournemouthecho.co.uk/sport/17996908.it-great-share-pitch-him---ramsdales-respect-watford-stopper-foster|title='It was great to share the pitch with him' - Ramsdale's respect for Watford stopper Foster |website=Bournemouth Echo |date=27 October 2019 |access-date=9 July 2021}}</ref>

In August 2022, Ramsdale announced his engagement to his girlfriend, Georgina Irwin.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Mayowa |first=Sofoluwe |date=2022-08-19 |title=Aaron Ramsdale of Arsenal is Engaged to His Girlfriend, Georgina Irwin |url=https://futballnews.com/aaron-ramsdale-of-arsenal-is-engaged-to-his-girlfriend-georgina-irwin/ |access-date=2023-05-13 |website=Futball News |language=en-US}}</ref> In June 2023, the couple announced that they were expecting their first child together and married.<ref>{{Citation |title=Arsenal goalkeeper Aaron Ramsdale reveals fiancee is pregnant with first child |date=2023-06-04 |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/tv/sport/arsenal-aaron-ramsdale-pregnant-football-goalkeeper-b2351178.html |access-date=2023-06-17 |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Banerjee |first=Apratim |date=2023-06-21 |title=IN PHOTOS: Arsenal star Aaron Ramsdale enjoys intimate wedding with long-term partner Georgina Irwin |url=https://www.sportskeeda.com/football/news-in-photos-arsenal-star-aaron-ramsdale-enjoys-intimate-wedding-long-term-partner-georgina-irwin |access-date=2023-07-01 |website=www.sportskeeda.com |language=en-gb}}</ref>
