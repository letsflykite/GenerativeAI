'''Gerónimo Rulli''' ({{IPA-es|xeˈɾonimo ˈruli}}; born 20 May 1992) is an Argentine [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Eredivisie]] club [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]] and the [[Argentina national football team|Argentina national team]].

Though he did not play any minutes, Rulli was a member of the Argentina squad that won the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Club career==
===Estudiantes===
Born in [[La Plata]], [[Buenos Aires Province]], Rulli graduated from local [[Estudiantes de La Plata|Estudiantes]]' youth setup, being promoted to the first-team squad in 2012, being initially assigned as a backup to [[Justo Villar]] and [[Agustín Silva]]. On 8 April 2013, after profiting from Villar's departure to [[Club Nacional de Football|Nacional]] and Silva's injury, he played his first match as a professional, starting in a 0–1 loss at [[Arsenal de Sarandí]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://442.perfil.com/2013-04-08-207615-estudiantes-quiere-cortar-la-racha/|title=Arsenal lo ganó sobre el final|trans-title=Arsenal wins it in the end|language=es|publisher=Cuatro Cuatro Dos|date=8 April 2013|access-date=6 August 2014}}</ref>

Rulli appeared in further 11 matches during [[2012–13 Argentine Primera División season|the campaign]], and established a record of 588 minutes without suffering a goal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marca.com/2014/06/03/futbol/equipos/atletico/1401776261.html|title=Rulli, ¿el sustituto de Courtois?|trans-title=Rulli, Courtois' replacement?|language=es|publisher=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|date=3 June 2014|access-date=6 August 2014}}</ref> He appeared in all league matches in [[2013–14 Argentine Primera División season|2013–14]], overtaking Silva in the pecking order.

===Real Sociedad and loan to Montpellier===
On 24 July 2014 Rulli was loaned to [[La Liga]]'s [[Real Sociedad]] for one year from [[Deportivo Maldonado]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realsociedad.com/document/view/spa/374/176978/geronimo-rulli-joins-on-a-one-year-loan|title=Gerónimo Rulli joins on a one-year loan|publisher=Real Sociedad's official website|date=24 July 2014|access-date=6 August 2014}}</ref> which bought the player's rights a month earlier.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tn.com.ar/deportes/millones/triangulo-por-rulli-de-estudiantes-a-real-sociedad-via-maldonado_519937|title=Triángulo por Rulli: de Estudiantes a Real Sociedad, vía Maldonado|trans-title=Triangle for Rulli: from Estudiantes to Real Sociedad, via Maldonado|language=es|publisher=Toda Pasion|date=24 July 2014|access-date=6 August 2014}}</ref> He made his debut for the club on 28 August, starting and being replaced in the 85th minute of a 0–3 away loss against [[FC Krasnodar|Krasnodar]] in the [[2014–15 UEFA Europa League]] due to an [[ankle]] injury.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marca.com/eventos/marcador/futbol/2014_15/europa_league/fase_previa/vuelta/krs_rso/|title=La Real Sociedad se olvida del fútbol y de Europa|trans-title=Real Sociedad forgets their football and Europe|publisher=Marca|language=es|date=28 August 2014|access-date=27 January 2015}}</ref>

Rulli returned to action in November, and made his debut in [[Spanish football league system|the main category of Spanish football]] on 20 December 2014, starting in a 1–1 away draw against [[Levante UD|Levante]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.com/spanish-primera-division/match/402476/levante-real-sociedad/report|title=Moyes denied at the death|publisher=[[ESPN FC]]|date=20 December 2014|access-date=27 January 2015}}</ref> On 4 January of the following year, he made several key saves which granted the ''Txuri-urdin'' a 1–0 home win against [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.us/spanish-primera-division/match/402461/real-sociedad-barcelona/report|title=Barcelona lose away to Real Sociedad for third straight time|publisher=ESPN FC|date=4 January 2015|access-date=27 January 2015}}</ref>

On 4 July 2015, Rulli's loan was extended for a further year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realsociedad.com//document/view/spa//188257/official-statement--agreement-reached-on-geronimo-rulli-loan-|title=Official Statement: Agreement reached on Gerónimo Rulli loan|publisher=Real Sociedad's official website|date=4 July 2015|access-date=9 July 2015}}</ref>

On 19 July 2016, Rulli was signed by [[Premier League]] side [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] for £4 million.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/jul/19/manchester-city-geronimo-rulli-deal|title=Manchester City agree £4m deal for goalkeeper Gerónimo Rulli|last=Aarons|first=Ed|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=19 July 2016|access-date=11 January 2017}}</ref> He was shortly afterwards loaned back to Real Sociedad, before moving permanently in January 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realsociedad.com/document/view/spa/374/204219/gero-rulli-is-now-officially-a-real-sociedad-player|title=Gero Rulli is now officially a Real Sociedad player|publisher=Real Sociedad|date=9 August 2016|access-date=11 January 2017}}</ref>

On 14 August 2019, Rulli joined [[Ligue 1]] club [[Montpellier HSC|Montpellier]] on a season-long loan with the option to make the transfer permanent.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mhscfoot.com/articles/2019-2020/equipe-pro/le-mhsc-tient-son-nouveau-gardien-geronimo-rulli|title=Le MHSC tient son nouveau gardien: Gerónimo Rulli|trans-title=''MHSC'' have their new keeper: Gerónimo Rulli|publisher=Montpellier HSC|language=fr|date=14 August 2019|access-date=14 August 2019}}</ref>

=== Villarreal ===
On 4 September 2020, after his loan ended with Montpellier, Rulli agreed to a four-year deal with [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://villarrealcf.es/en/news/item/28443-gero-rulli-is-a-yellow|title=Gero Rulli is a Yellow!|publisher=Villarreal CF|date=4 September 2020|access-date=5 September 2020}}</ref> In his first season, manager [[Unai Emery]] preferred to play [[Sergio Asenjo]] in goal [[2020–21 La Liga|in the league]], and Rulli in the Europa League. He made his league debut for the club on 21 April in a 2–1 loss at [[Deportivo Alavés]], having been chosen to prepare for the European semi-final against [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Escario |first1=Sergio |title=¡Sorpresón! Rulli, titular; Asenjo al banquillo en Mendizorroza|trans-title=Big surprise! Rulli, starter; Asenjo to the bench at Mendizorroza|url=https://www.mundodeportivo.com/futbol/laliga/20210421/493374473266/sorpreson-rulli-titular-asenjo-al-banquillo-en-mendizorroza.html |access-date=11 June 2021 |work=Mundo Deportivo |date=21 April 2021 |language=Spanish}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Pérez |first1=José I. |title=Camino a la salvación|trans-title=On the road to survival|url=https://www.marca.com/futbol/primera-division/alaves-vs-villarreal/cronica/2021/04/21/6080538ce2704e55b18b4600.html |access-date=11 June 2021 |work=Marca |date=21 April 2021 |language=Spanish}}</ref>

On 26 May 2021, Rulli scored the winning penalty kick and then saved [[David de Gea]]'s penalty in his side's 11–10 [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shoot-out]] victory over [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] in the [[2021 UEFA Europa League Final|final]] of the [[2020–21 UEFA Europa League]] after the game finished 1–1 at [[Overtime (sports)#Association football|extra-time]] to win the first European title for Villarreal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/match/2029909--villarreal-vs-man-united/postmatch/report/|title=Villarreal 1-1 Manchester United (aet, 11-10 pens): Spanish side win Europa League in marathon shoot-out|website=[[UEFA]]|date=26 May 2021|access-date=26 May 2021}}</ref> In the second leg of the [[2021–22 UEFA Champions League]] semi-finals, Villarreal was leading 2–0 against [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] in the first half to level the tie 2–2 on aggregate. In the second half, he made goalkeeping errors and conceded three goals, in which his team eventually lost 3–2 in the [[Estadio de la Cerámica]], and was knocked out of the competition.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/watch-villarreal-stun-liverpool-with-two-first-half-goals-to/blt6521b817213e81a0 |title=Watch: Rulli howlers help Liverpool overcome first-half shocker at Villarreal to book place in Champions League final |website=Goal.com |date=3 May 2022 }}</ref>

===Ajax===
On 6 January 2023, [[Eredivisie]] side [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]] announced they had agreed the transfer of Rulli with Villarreal, signing the player on a three-and-a-half-year contract.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://english.ajax.nl/articles/ajax-sign-geronimo-rulli/|title=Ajax sign Gerónimo Rulli|publisher=Villarreal CF|date=6 January 2023|access-date=6 January 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:Gerónimo Rulli - Rio 2016.jpg|thumb|Rulli at the [[Football at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|2016 Olympics]]]]
On 20 March 2015, Rulli was called up to the [[Argentina national football team|Argentina national team]] for the matches against [[El Salvador national football team|El Salvador]] and [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realsociedad.com//document/view/spa//184974/rulli-y-finnbogason--convocados-con-sus-selecciones|title=Rulli y Finnbogason, convocados con sus selecciones|trans-title=Rulli and Finnbogason, called up for their national teams|publisher=Real Sociedad's official website|language=es|date=20 March 2015|access-date=22 March 2015}}</ref> but he did not play in either fixture. In 2016, Rulli was included in the preliminary squad for [[Copa América Centenario]] but was eventually excluded.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.goal.com/en-us/news/3297/argentina/2016/05/04/23145612/argentina-recalls-tevez-for-initial-copa-america-squad|title=Argentina recalls Tevez for initial Copa America squad|publisher=goal.com |date=4 May 2016 |access-date=3 July 2016}}</ref>

Rulli was chosen as the [[List of overage players in Olympic football|overage player]] for the [[Argentina national under-23 football team|Argentina under-23 squad]] at the [[Football at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|2016 Olympic tournament]] in Brazil.<ref>{{cite news |title=Cuesta y Rulli serán los capitanes de la selección que jugará en Río 2016 |trans-title=Cuesta and Rulli will be the captains of the national team that will play at Río 2016|url=https://www.eltribuno.com/salta/nota/2016-7-15-18-26-0-cuesta-y-rulli-seran-los-capitanes-de-la-seleccion-que-jugara-en-rio-2016 |access-date=11 June 2021 |work=[[El Tribuno]] |date=15 July 2016 |language=Spanish}}</ref> He played all three games in a [[Football at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament – Group D|group stage elimination]], and filled in for the suspended [[Víctor Cuesta]] as captain in the last of those, a 1–1 draw with [[Honduras national under-23 football team|Honduras]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Argentina no pudo con Honduras y el fútbol olímpico se despidió de Rio|trans-title=Argentina could not deal with Honduras and the Olympic football team said goodbye to Rio|url=https://www.diariocol.com/2016/08/10/argentina-no-pudo-honduras-futbol-olimpico-se-despidio-rio/ |access-date=11 June 2021 |work=Diario Col |date=10 August 2016 |language=Spanish}}</ref>

After [[Jorge Sampaoli]] took charge in May 2017, Rulli was consistently called up to Argentina's senior squad, but did not play.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/es-ar/noticias/la-primera-lista-de-convocados-de-sampaoli-en-argentina/hcrwdrub3qid1u7597thqrui8|title=La primera lista de convocados de Sampaoli en Argentina|trans-title=Sampaoli's first Argentina squad|publisher=Goal}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lanacion.com.ar/2057197-seleccion-argentina-pinola-bustos-benedetto-y-acosta-los-cuatro-convocados-por-sampaoli|title=Eliminatorias: Pinola, Bustos, Benedetto y Acosta, convocados del fútbol local|trans-title=Qualifiers: Pinola, Bustos, Benedetto and Acosta, call-ups from domestic football|date=27 August 2017|website=La Nación}}</ref> Under manager [[Lionel Scaloni]], he made his first team debut against [[Guatemala national football team|Guatemala]] on 8 September 2018, in a 3–0 friendly win in [[Los Angeles]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Así formará la selección ante Guatemala: qué jugadores tendrán su debut en la Argentina |trans-title=How the national team will line up against Guatemala: which players will have their debuts for Argentina|url=https://www.lanacion.com.ar/deportes/asi-formara-seleccion-guatemala-que-jugadores-tendran-nid2169808/ |access-date=11 June 2021 |work=La Nación |date=7 September 2018 |language=Spanish}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Argentina 3 Guatemala 0: No Messi, no worries as Simeone scores on debut |url=https://in.news.yahoo.com/argentina-3-guatemala-0-no-053807388.html |access-date=11 June 2021 |publisher=Yahoo! |date=8 September 2018}}</ref>

On 1 June 2022, Rulli remained as an unused substitute as Argentina won 3–0 against reigning [[UEFA Euro 2020|European Champions]] [[Italy national football team|Italy]] at [[Wembley Stadium]] in the [[2022 Finalissima]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/finalissima/news/0276-1549514538ab-f782a73cf733-1000--argentina-outclass-italy-in-finalissima/ |title=Italy 0-3 Argentina: South American champions cruise to Finalissima glory |publisher=[[UEFA]] |date=1 June 2022 |access-date=19 December 2022 }}</ref>

He was named in Argentina's final 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]] by Scaloni. He did not play a single minute in the tournament as Argentina won the World Cup by defeating France 4–2 in a penalty shoot-out to win [[2022 FIFA World Cup Final|the final]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/blogs/fwc-2022-live-blog-vi-en#mage-greater-than-fwc-2022-greater-than-kylian-mbappe-of-france-celebrates|title=Argentina are World Champions!|publisher=[[FIFA]]|date=18 December 2022|accessdate=19 December 2022}}</ref>
