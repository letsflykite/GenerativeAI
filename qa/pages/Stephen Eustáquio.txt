'''Stephen Antunes Eustáquio''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|s|t|ɛ|f|ən}} {{respell|STEF|ən}}; born 21 December 1996) is a Canadian professional [[Association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[Primeira Liga]] club [[FC Porto|Porto]] and the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada national team]].

After starting out at Nazarenos, he has spent most of his club career in Portugal with [[S.C.U. Torreense|Torreense]], [[Leixões S.C.|Leixões]], [[G.D. Chaves|Chaves]], [[F.C. Paços de Ferreira|Paços de Ferreira]] and Porto. He also played briefly in Mexico with [[Cruz Azul]].

Born in Canada, Eustáquio represented [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]] at youth level. However, in 2019 he committed to play for [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada]] and debuted for them in November of that year. He was part of their squad that reached the semi-finals of the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup]], also being selected for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]].

==Club career==
===Early career===
Born in [[Leamington, Ontario]], to [[Portuguese people|Portuguese]] parents, Eustáquio first played soccer with Leamington Minor Soccer,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canadasoccer.com/news/canada-soccer-announce-squad-for-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022/|title=Canada Soccer announces squad for FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022|publisher=[[Canadian Soccer Association]]|date=12 November 2022|access-date=13 November 2022}}</ref> moving to Portugal at age 7. After beginning with amateurs Nazarenos, he spent two seasons in the [[Campeonato de Portugal (league)|third division]] with [[S.C.U. Torreense|Torreense]].<ref name=CAN>{{Canada Soccer player|id=5005}}</ref><ref name=FUTEBOL>{{cite web|url=http://futeboldistritaldeleiria.pt/2015/02/mauro-eustaquio-o-nazareno-que-sonha-com-selecao-canada/|title=Mauro Eustáquio, o nazareno que sonha com a seleção do Canadá|trans-title=Mauro Eustáquio, the Nazaré boy who dreams of Canada national team|publisher=Futebol Distrital de Leiria|language=pt|date=21 February 2015|access-date=23 November 2017}}</ref>

===Leixões===
On 7 June 2017, Eustáquio signed with [[Leixões S.C.|Leixões]] for an undisclosed fee.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.ojogo.pt/futebol/2a-liga/noticias/interior/steohen-8543286.html|title=Stephen Eustáquio já assinou pelo Leixões|trans-title=Stephen Eustáquio has already signed for Leixões|newspaper=[[O Jogo]]|language=pt|date=7 June 2017|access-date=23 November 2017}}</ref> On 23 July he appeared in his first match as a professional in a 2–0 home win against [[Académico de Viseu F.C.|Académico de Viseu]] in [[2017–18 Taça da Liga|the first round]] of the [[Taça da Liga]] where he played the full 90 minutes,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.dn.pt/lusa/interior/comentario-leixoes-derrota-academico-de-viseu-a-salta-para-a-3a-fase-da-taca-da-liga-8657843.html|title=COMENTÁRIO: Leixões derrota Académico de Viseu a salta para a 3.ª fase da Taça da Liga|trans-title=COMMENT: Leixões defeat Académico de Viseu and make it to 3rd stage of League Cup|newspaper=[[Diário de Notícias]]|language=pt|date=23 July 2017|access-date=23 November 2017}}</ref> and his debut in the [[Segunda Liga]] was on 6 August in a 4–1 away loss to [[Real S.C.|Real Massamá]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.dn.pt/lusa/interior/comentario-hat-trick-de-vinicius-ao-leixoes-oferece-estreia-de-sonho-ao-real-massama-8688580.html|title=COMENTÁRIO: 'Hat trick' de Vinícius ao Leixões oferece estreia de sonho ao Real Massamá|trans-title=COMMENT: Vinícius hat trick to Leixões offers dream debut to Real Massamá|newspaper=Diário de Notícias|language=pt|date=6 August 2017|access-date=23 November 2017}}</ref>

===Chaves===
Eustáquio joined [[G.D. Chaves|Chaves]] on 31 January 2018, on a five-and-a-half-year deal after his [[buyout clause]] of €500,000 was paid.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-nos/chaves/detalhe/stephen-chega-do-leixoes-e-assina-por-cinco-epocas.html|title=Stephen chega do Leixões e assina por cinco épocas|trans-title=Stephen arrives from Leixões and signs for five seasons|newspaper=[[Record (newspaper)|Record]]|first1=Joaquim|last1=Paulo|first2=Ricardo|last2=Vasconcelos|first3=André|last3=Monteiro|language=pt|date=31 January 2018|access-date=4 February 2018}}</ref> He made his [[Primeira Liga]] debut four days later, playing the entire 2–1 away victory over [[C.D. Feirense|Feirense]];<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.maisfutebol.iol.pt/liga/04-02-2018/feirense-desp-chaves-1-2-cronica|title=Feirense-Desp. Chaves, 1–2 (crónica)|trans-title=Feirense-Desp. Chaves, 1–2 (match report)|publisher=Mais Futebol|first=Vítor|last=Maia|language=pt|date=4 February 2018|access-date=4 February 2018}}</ref> he scored his first top-flight goal on 14 April, helping his team to come from behind to draw 3–3 at [[Boavista F.C.|Boavista]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tsf.pt/desporto/golo-tardio-de-platiny-da-empate-ao-desportivo-de-chaves-com-o-bavista-9260084.html|title=Chaves empata com Boavista no último minuto|trans-title=Chaves draw with Boavista in the last minute|publisher=[[TSF (radio station)|TSF]]|language=pt|date=14 April 2018|access-date=10 October 2022}}</ref>

On 14 September 2018, in a [[2018–19 Taça da Liga|Portuguese League Cup]] match at the [[Estádio do Dragão]], Eustáquio scored a late equalizer which helped Chaves draw 1–1 and record their first ever point at [[FC Porto|Porto]]'s home ground.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-nos/chaves/detalhe/eustaquio-repete-dose-e-faz-historia-pelos-flavienses?ref=Chaves_DestaquesPrincipais|title=Eustáquio repete dose e faz história pelos flavienses|trans-title=Eustáquio repeats feat and makes history at the ''flavienses''|newspaper=Record|language=pt|date=15 September 2018|access-date=14 October 2018}}</ref>

===Cruz Azul===
Eustáquio moved to [[Cruz Azul]] of the Mexican [[Liga MX]] on 15 January 2019.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/liga-mx/2019/01/15/5c3dee82268e3eb8118b458d.html|title=Stephen Eustáquio llega a Cruz Azul|trans-title=Stephen Eustáquio arrives at Cruz Azul|newspaper=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|first=Javier|last=Cardoso|language=es|date=15 January 2019|access-date=17 January 2019}}</ref> Vítor Severino, who worked as assistant to manager [[Luís Castro (footballer, born 1961)|Luís Castro]] at Chaves, described him as "like the typical player from [[La Masia]], from [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]]".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/liga-mx/2019/01/15/5c3df838e5fdeaa5438b4667.html|title=Liga MX Clausura 2019: Eustáquio, el brillante asistente con 'perfil Barça' para sustituir a Marcone|trans-title=Liga MX Clausura 2019: Eustáquio, the brilliant passer 'made in Barça' to replace Marcone|newspaper=Marca|first=Pedro|last=Barata|language=es|date=15 January 2019|access-date=17 January 2019}}</ref> On his debut, against [[Club Tijuana|Tijuana]], he was [[Ejection (sports)|sent off]] mere minutes after taking the field as a [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]], but eventually the [[video assistant referee]] cautioned him with only a [[Penalty card#Yellow card|yellow card]] upon review; shortly after, however, he was stretchered off with an injury.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.publimetro.com.mx/mx/publisport/2019/01/26/var-salva-eustaquio-sale-lesionado.html|title=VAR salva a Eustaquio, pero sale lesionado|trans-title=VAR saves Eustaquio, but he is stretchered off|newspaper=Publímetro|first=Israel|last=González|language=es|date=26 January 2019|access-date=29 January 2019}}</ref>

After being sidelined for eight months, Eustáquio returned to the pitch on 22 September 2019, playing for the under-20 team in preparation for his full return.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/liga-mx/cruz-azul-stephen-eustaquio-volvio-actividad-meses|title=Ocho meses después, Stephen Eustaquio vuelve a las canchas|trans-title=Eight months later, Stephen Eustaquio returns to the pitches|publisher=Medio Tiempo|first=Abigail|last=Parra|language=es|date=22 September 2019|access-date=24 September 2019}}</ref>

==International career==
===Youth===
Eligible to represent [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada]] and [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]], Eustáquio appeared for [[Canada men's national under-17 soccer team|Canada's under-17 side]] at the 2012 AGS Cup.<ref name=Commit>{{cite web|url=https://www.sportsnet.ca/soccer/former-portugal-midfielder-stephen-eustaquio-commits-play-canada/|title=Former Portugal midfielder Stephen Eustaquio commits to play for Canada|publisher=[[Sportsnet]]|date=22 February 2019|access-date=22 February 2019}}</ref>

In November 2017, [[Portugal national under-21 football team|Portugal under-21]] manager [[Rui Jorge]] selected him for [[2019 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification|2019 UEFA European Championship qualifiers]] against [[Romania national under-21 football team|Romania]] and [[Switzerland national under-21 football team|Switzerland]] to be held early in that month.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.ojogo.pt/internacional/selecao/noticias/interior/rui-jorge-justifica-convocatoria-da-novidade-stephen-eustaquio-8892346.html|title=Rui Jorge justifica convocatória da novidade Stephen Eustaquio|trans-title=Rui Jorge justifies novelty selection Stephen Eustaquio|newspaper=O Jogo|language=pt|date=3 November 2017|access-date=21 November 2017}}</ref> He won his first [[Cap (sport)|cap]] against the former, playing 90 minutes and being booked in the 1–1 draw in [[Constanța]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.maisfutebol.iol.pt/qualificacao/selecao-sub21/sub21-romenia-portugal-1-1-cronica|title=Sub21: Roménia-Portugal, 1–1 (crónica)|trans-title=Under21: Romania-Portugal, 1–1 (match report)|publisher=Mais Futebol|first=Pedro Jorge|last=Da Cunha|language=pt|date=10 November 2017|access-date=21 November 2017}}</ref>

===Senior===
In February 2019, Eustáquio committed to play for Canada at senior level.<ref name=Commit/> Upon his return from his knee injury, he received his first call-up to the team on 1 October 2019 for a [[2019–20 CONCACAF Nations League|CONCACAF Nations League]] fixture against the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.canadasoccer.com/canada-s-top-players-to-feature-in-toronto-for-rivalry-match-against-usa-in-concacaf-nations-league--p162392|title=Canada's top players to feature in Toronto for Rivalry match against USA in Concacaf Nations League|publisher=Canadian Soccer Association|date=1 October 2019|access-date=1 October 2019|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191001153359/https://www.canadasoccer.com/canada-s-top-players-to-feature-in-toronto-for-rivalry-match-against-usa-in-concacaf-nations-league--p162392|archive-date=1 October 2019}}</ref> He made his debut on 15 November, coming from the bench for [[Mark-Anthony Kaye]] in the second half of the 4–1 away defeat.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://northerntribune.ca/concacaf-nations-league-usa-canada/|title=USA routs Canada 4–1 in CONCACAF Nations League bout|newspaper=Northern Tribune|first=John|last=Jacques|date=15 November 2019|access-date=20 April 2020}}</ref>
