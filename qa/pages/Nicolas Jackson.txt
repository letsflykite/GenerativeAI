'''Nicolas Jackson''' (born 19 June 2001) is a professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]] for [[Premier League]] club [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]]. Born in [[The Gambia]], he plays for the [[Senegal national football team|Senegal national team]].

==Club career==
===Early career===
Born in [[Banjul]] and with Senegalese ancestry, Jackson moved to [[Ziguinchor]], Senegal at the age of 16.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elperiodicomediterraneo.com/villarreal-cf/2023/05/05/leon-jackson-ruge-villarreal-86889342.html|title=El ‘León’ Jackson ya ruge en el Villarreal|trans-title=The 'Lion' Jackson already roars at Villarreal|publisher=El Periódico Mediterráneo|language=es|date=5 May 2023|accessdate=24 May 2023}} {{subscription required}}</ref><ref name=SNA>{{cite web|url=https://sportnewsafrica.com/en/at-a-glance/nicholas-jackson-the-rise-from-casasport-to-chelsea/|title=Nicholas Jackson: The rise from Casa Sport to Chelsea|publisher=Sport News Africa|date=30 June 2023|accessdate=30 June 2023}}</ref> He began playing for local side ASC Tilene before joining [[Casa Sports]],<ref name=SNA/> where he was a part of the first team squad during the [[2018–19 Senegal Premier League|2018–19 season]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://lsfp.sn/player/nicolas-jackson/|title=Nicolas Jackson|publisher=Ligue Sénégalaise de Football Professionnel|language=fr|accessdate=7 October 2020}}</ref> He was also named [[man of the match]] in a 1–1 draw against [[AS Pikine]] on 16 November 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://lsfp.sn/as-pikine-vs-casa-sports-1-1-les-pikinois-tenus-en-echec-a-domicile/|title=AS Pikine vs Casa Sports (1–1): les Pikinois tenus en échec à domicile|trans-title=AS Pikine vs Casa Sports (1–1): the ''Pikinois'' held in check at home|publisher=Ligue Sénégalaise de Football Professionnel|language=fr|date=23 November 2018|accessdate=7 October 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://africafoot.com/des-verts-de-la-casamance-aux-blues-londoniens-la-fulgurante-ascension-de-nicolas-jackson/|title=Des verts de la Casamance aux Blues londoniens, la fulgurante ascension de Nicolas Jackson|trans-title=From the greens of Casamance to the London Blues, the meteoric rise of Nicolas Jackson|publisher=Africa Foot|language=fr|date=25 June 2023|accessdate=30 June 2023}}</ref>

===Villarreal===
In September 2019, Jackson agreed to a contract with [[La Liga]] side [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.galsenfoot.com/mercato-diomansy-camara-fait-signer-un-joueur-du-casa-sport-a-villarreal/|title=Mercato: Diomansy Camara fait signer un joueur du Casa Sport à Villarreal|trans-title=Transfer market: Diomansy Camara signs a Casa Sport player at Villarreal|publisher=Galsenfoot|language=fr|date=4 September 2019|accessdate=7 October 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.castelloninformacion.com/nicolas-jackson-reforzara-filiales-villarreal-delantero-senegales/|title=El Villarreal firma al joven delantero Nicolás Jackson el 'Neymar' senegalés para uno de sus dos filiales|trans-title=Villarreal sign young forward Nicolás Jackson the Senegalese 'Neymar' to one of their reserve teams|publisher=Castellón Información|language=es|date=3 September 2019|accessdate=7 October 2020}}</ref> being assigned to the ''Juvenil A'' squad.

====Loan to Mirandés====
On 5 October 2020, after finishing his formation, Jackson was loaned to [[Segunda División]] side [[CD Mirandés|Mirandés]] for the [[2020–21 Segunda División|2020–21 season]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://villarrealcf.es/en/news/item/28694-jackson-will-play-on-loan-at-mirandes|title=Jackson will play on loan at Mirandés|publisher=Villarreal CF|date=5 October 2020|accessdate=7 October 2020}}</ref> He made his professional debut thirteen days later, coming on as a second-half [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] for [[Antonio Caballero (footballer)|Antonio Caballero]] in a 0–0 home draw against [[RCD Mallorca|Mallorca]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.marca.com/futbol/segunda-division/mirandes-vs-mallorca/cronica/2020/10/18/5f8c7994268e3e07368b45ed.html|title=Un cerocerismo injusto por lo visto en Anduva|trans-title=An unfair 0–0 for what it was seen in Anduva|publisher=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|language=es|date=18 October 2020|accessdate=18 October 2020}}</ref> 

Jackson scored his first professional goal on 28 November 2020, netting the opener in a 1–1 home draw against [[CD Castellón]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.marca.com/futbol/segunda-division/mirandes-vs-castellon/cronica/2020/11/28/5fc2ad0d46163f31b98b459a.html|title=El Castellón se crece y araña un buen punto de Anduva|trans-title=Castellón grow big and scratch a good point out of Anduva|publisher=Marca|language=es|date=28 November 2020|accessdate=30 November 2020}}</ref> It was his only goal of [[2020–21 Segunda División|the season]], as the ''Jabatos'' finished in the 10th position.

====Breakthrough====
Upon returning, Jackson played for Villarreal's [[Villarreal CF B|B-team]] in [[Primera División RFEF]], scoring seven goals as the side [[2021–22 Primera División RFEF|achieved promotion to the second division]]. He made his first team – and [[La Liga]] – debut on 3 October 2021, replacing [[Arnaut Danjuma]] late into a 2–0 home win over [[Real Betis]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://as.com/futbol/2021/10/11/primera/1633964587_987485.html|title=Nicolas Jackson, la nueva perla amarilla que ya perfila Emery|trans-title=Nicolas Jackson, the new yellow pearl who Emery already shapes up|publisher=[[Diario AS]]|language=es|date=11 October 2021|accessdate=24 May 2023}}</ref>

Jackson scored his first goal in the top tier on 13 August 2022, netting the opener in a 3–0 away success over [[Real Valladolid]]. Thirteen days later, he and teammate [[Álex Baena]] were promoted to the main squad.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://villarrealcf.es/en/news/item/35264-baena-and-jackson-officially-become-first-team-players|title=Baena and Jackson officially become first-team players|publisher=Villarreal CF|date=26 August 2022|accessdate=26 August 2022}}</ref>

In January 2023, Villarreal agreed a £22.5 million deal from [[AFC Bournemouth]] for the signing of Jackson but he failed his medical due to [[hamstring]] issues.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dailyecho.co.uk/sport/23282482.bournemouths-swoop-southampton-target-jackson-fails-medical/|title=Bournemouth's swoop for Southampton target Jackson fails after medical|publisher=[[Southern Daily Echo]]|date=27 January 2023|accessdate=24 May 2023}}</ref> After returning to action in March, he scored nine goals within the months of April and May, which included braces against [[RC Celta de Vigo|Celta de Vigo]], [[Athletic Bilbao]] and [[Cádiz CF|Cádiz]].

===Chelsea===
On 30 June 2023, Jackson signed for [[Premier League]] club [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] on an eight-year deal lasting until 2031.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Nicolas Jackson joins the Blues |url=https://www.chelseafc.com/en/news/article/nicolas-jackson-joins-the-blues |access-date=2023-07-01 |website=www.chelseafc.com |language=en-gb}}</ref>

==International career==
Born in the Gambia, Jackson was raised in Senegal and is a dual citizen.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.seneweb.com/news/Sport/decouverte-nicolas-jackson-premiere-imme_n_388150.html|title=Découverte : Nicolas Jackson, première immersion pour le « sous-marin jaune »|date=15 November 2022|website=Seneweb.com|language=fr}}</ref> He represented [[Senegal national football team|Senegal]] at [[Senegal national under-20 football team|under-20 level]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://lsfp.sn/matches-amicaux-les-u-20-invites-en-arabie-saoudite/|title=Matches amicaux – Les U 20 invités en Arabie saoudite|trans-title=Friendlies – U-20 invited to Saudi Arabia|publisher=Ligue Sénégalaise de Football Professionnel|language=fr|date=28 December 2018|accessdate=7 October 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://lsfp.sn/preparation-de-matches-amicaux-lequipe-nationale-juniors-en-stage-a-partir-de-lundi/|title=Préparation de matches amicaux : L'équipe nationale juniors en stage à partir de lundi|trans-title=Preparation for friendly matches: The national junior team in training camp from Monday|publisher=Ligue Sénégalaise de Football Professionnel|language=fr|date=4 January 2019|accessdate=7 October 2020}}</ref> receiving his first call-up in November 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.seneplus.com/sports/premiere-selection-pour-nicolas-jackson-du-casa-sports|title=Première sélection pour Nicolas Jackson du Casa-Sports|trans-title=First call-up of Nicolas Jackson from Casa Sports|publisher=SenePlus|language=fr|date=15 November 2018|accessdate=7 October 2020}}</ref>

After being called up to the full side by manager [[Aliou Cissé]] for two friendlies in September 2022, Jackson was included in the 26-man list for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] on 11 November 2022.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fsfoot.sn/aliou-cisse-devoile-les-26-lions-pour-qatar/ |title=Aliou Cisse dévoile les 26 Lions pour Qatar |trans-title=Aliou Cissé unveils 26 Lions for Qatar |publisher=[[Senegalese Football Federation]] |date=11 November 2022 |access-date=11 November 2022 |language=fr}}</ref> He made his full international debut in Senegal's opening match of the competition, replacing [[Krépin Diatta]] in a 2–0 loss against the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://castellonplaza.com/jackson-debuta-con-senegal-en-el-mundial-y-cae-ante-paises-bajos|title=Jackson debuta con Senegal en el Mundial y cae ante Países Bajos|trans-title=Jackson debuts with Senegal in the World Cup and suffer defeat against the Netherlands|publisher=Castellón Plaza|language=es|date=21 November 2022|accessdate=24 May 2023}}</ref>
