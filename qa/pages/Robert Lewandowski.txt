'''Robert Lewandowski''' ({{IPA-pl|ˈrɔbɛrt lɛvanˈdɔfskʲi|-|Pl-Robert Lewandowski.ogg}}; born 21 August 1988) is a Polish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)#Striker|striker]] for [[La Liga]] club [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] and [[Captain (association football)|captains]] the [[Poland national football team|Poland national team]]. Recognised for his positioning, technique and finishing, Lewandowski is regarded as one of the best strikers of all time, as well as one of the most successful players in [[Bundesliga]] and [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] history. He has scored over [[List of footballers with 500 or more goals|600 senior career goals]] for club and country.

After being the top scorer in the third and second tiers of Polish football with [[Znicz Pruszków]], Lewandowski moved to top-flight [[Lech Poznań]], helping the team win the [[2009–10 Ekstraklasa]]. In 2010, he transferred to [[Borussia Dortmund]], where he won honors including two consecutive [[Meisterschale|Bundesliga titles]] and the [[2013–14 Bundesliga#Top scorers|league's top goalscorer]] award. In 2013, he also featured with Dortmund in the [[2013 UEFA Champions League Final]]. Prior to the start of the [[2014–15 Bundesliga|2014–15 season]], Lewandowski agreed to join Dortmund's domestic rivals, [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]], on a [[Free transfer (association football)|free transfer]]. In Munich, he won the Bundesliga title in every one of his eight seasons. Lewandowski was integral in Bayern's [[UEFA Champions League]] win in [[2019–20 UEFA Champions League|2019–20]] as part of a [[Treble (association football)|treble]]. He is one of only two players, alongside [[Johan Cruyff]], to achieve the European treble while being the highest goalscorer in all three competitions, and the first to do it as the sole top scorer.<ref>{{Cite web |date=24 August 2020 |title=Lewandowski claims his own personal treble |url=https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2020/08/champions-league-2020/lewandowski-claims-his-own-personal-treble |access-date=2023-05-10 |website=FC Bayern Munich}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Kappel |first=David |date=24 August 2020 |title=Robert Lewandowski First To Win Treble & End Up As Top Goalscorers In All Competitions |url=https://www.snl24.com/soccerladuma/international/messi-ronaldo-neymar-mbappe-watch/robert-lewandowski-first-to-win-treble-end-up-as-top-goalscorers-in-all-competitions-20200824 |access-date=2023-05-10 |website=Soccer Laduma}}</ref> In 2022, he was signed by [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]], where he won the [[2022–23 Supercopa de España|Supercopa de España]], the [[2022–23 La Liga|La Liga]] title and the [[Pichichi Trophy]] in his debut season.

A full international for Poland since 2008, Lewandowski has earned 142 caps and was a member of their team at the [[UEFA European Championship]] in [[UEFA Euro 2012|2012]], [[UEFA Euro 2016|2016]], and [[UEFA Euro 2020|2020]], and the [[FIFA World Cup]] in [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022]]. With 79 international goals, Lewandowski is the all-time top scorer for Poland and the [[List of men's footballers with 50 or more international goals|third overall men's international goalscorer in Europe]], only behind [[Ferenc Puskás]] (84) and [[Cristiano Ronaldo]] (123).<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro/history/news/0253-0d81f47dfe05-e92503acfb7d-1000--europe-s-top-international-scorers/ |title=Europe's top international scorers: Cristiano Ronaldo out in front |website=www.uefa.com |date=13 June 2022 |access-date=14 July 2022 }}</ref> He won [[IFFHS World's Best International Goal Scorer|IFFHS World's Best International Goal Scorer Award]] in 2015 and 2021, [[International Federation of Football History & Statistics#The World's Best Top Goal Scorer|IFFHS World's Best Top Goal Scorer Award]] in 2020 and 2021, and [[IFFHS World's Best Top Division Goal Scorer|IFFHS World's Best Top Division Goal Scorer Award]] in 2021. He also won the [[IFFHS World's Best Player]] in 2020 and 2021 and the [[European Golden Shoe]] for the 2020–21 and 2021–22 seasons. Lewandowski has been named the [[Piłka nożna magazine plebiscite|Polish Footballer of the Year]] a record eleven times and the [[Polish Sports Personality of the Year]] three times. Moreover, he has won the [[Gerd Müller Trophy]] twice, in 2021 and 2022.

In 2020, Lewandowski won the [[The Best FIFA Men's Player|Best FIFA Men's Player Award]] (retained in 2021) and the [[UEFA Men's Player of the Year Award]]. He has been named to the [[UEFA Team of the Year]] twice. He is the [[List of UEFA Champions League top scorers#All-time top scorers|third-highest goalscorer]] in the history of the [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]]. Lewandowski has been named the [[Vereinigung der Vertragsfußballspieler|VDV Bundesliga Player of the Season]] a record five times. He has scored over 300 goals in the Bundesliga ([[List of Bundesliga top scorers|second-highest goalscorer of all time in Bundesliga]], only behind [[Gerd Müller]]'s 365 Bundesliga goals), having reached the century mark quicker than any other foreign player, and is the league's [[Bundesliga records and statistics#Goals 2|all-time leading foreign goalscorer]]. In 2015, while playing for Bayern, he scored five goals in less than nine minutes against [[VfL Wolfsburg]], the fastest by any player in Bundesliga history as well as any [[Big Five (association football)|major European football league]] for which he was awarded four [[Guinness World Records]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=1 December 2015 |title=Robert Lewandowski receives awards for five-goal feat |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/34979052 |access-date=12 July 2022 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> Moreover, he has won the [[List of Bundesliga top scorers by season|Bundesliga Top Scorer Award]] in seven seasons, most prominently in the [[2020–21 Bundesliga]] where he scored 41 goals in a single campaign, breaking Gerd Müller's previous Bundesliga record of 40 goals, set in [[1971–72 Bundesliga|1971–72]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=How Robert Lewandowski broke Gerd Müller's 40-goal Bundesliga record|url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/can-robert-lewandowski-break-gerd-muller-s-40-goal-record-bayern-munich-7491|website=Bundesliga|access-date=19 June 2021}}</ref> On 30 November 2021, he finished second in the [[2021 Ballon d'Or|Ballon d'Or]], just 33 points behind the winner [[Lionel Messi]].

==Club career==

===Early career===

Lewandowski was born in [[Warsaw]]<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.eurosport.co.uk/football/robert-lewandowski_prs141648/person.shtml |title=Robert Lewandowski |website=[[Eurosport]] |access-date=21 March 2020}}</ref> and grew up in [[Leszno, Warsaw West County|Leszno]], [[Warsaw West County]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Gontarczyk |first=Karolina |date=26 April 2019 |title=Otwarte spotkanie z Robertem Lewandowskim w Lesznie |trans-title=Open meeting with Robert Lewandowski in Leszno |url=http://zachodniemazowsze.info/2019/04/otwarte-spotkanie-robertem-lewandowskim-lesznie/ |work=Zachodnie Mazowsze |access-date=21 March 2020}}</ref> He took his first steps in football as an unregistered player for the local club, Partyzant Leszno.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Hurkowski |first1=Rafał |last2=Murawski |first2=Robert |date=25 August 2015 |title=Śladami Lewandowskiego: Partyzant Leszno |trans-title=Lewandowski's Steps: Partyzant Leszno |url=https://www.polsatsport.pl/wiadomosc/2015-08-25/sladami-lewandowskiego-partyzant-leszno/ |language=Polish |work=[[Polsat Sport]] |location=Warsaw |publisher=[[Cyfrowy Polsat]] |access-date=5 June 2019}}</ref> In 1997, he joined MKS Varsovia Warsaw, where as a teen he played for seven years.<ref name="bajka">{{cite news |last=Cegliński |first=Łukasz |date=11 September 2008 |title=Bajka o Robercie Lewandowskim |trans-title=The Tale of Robert Lewandowski |url=http://www.sport.pl/celebrities/1,96810,5686861,Bajka_o_Robercie_Lewandowskim.html |language=Polish |work=[[Agora (company)#Gazeta.pl|Sport.pl]] |location=Warsaw |publisher=[[Agora (company)|Agora]] |access-date=1 May 2012}}</ref> The following year he moved to [[III liga|4th tier]] side Delta Warsaw, where he finally managed to play in the first team, scoring four goals at the end of the season.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Kuna |first=Tomasz |title=.: liga polska |url=http://www.90minut.pl/strzelcy.php?id=1357 |access-date=19 November 2012 |publisher=90minut.pl |language=pl}}</ref>

In {{ill|2006–07 III liga|lt=2006–07|pl|III liga polska w piłce nożnej (2006/2007)}}, Lewandowski was the [[II liga|Polish third division]]'s top goalscorer with 15 goals, helping [[Znicz Pruszków]] win the promotion.<ref name="krol">{{Cite news |last=Leśniowski |first=Piotr |date=26 May 2008 |title="Kolejorz" walczy o króla |language=Polish |work=[[Gazeta Wyborcza]] |url=http://www.archiwum.wyborcza.pl/Archiwum/1,0,5158528,20080527PO-DLO_D,Kolejorz_walczy_o_krola,.html |access-date=1 December 2013}}</ref> The [[2007–08 II liga|next season]], he was the [[2007–08 II liga#Top goalscorers|top scorer]] in the [[I liga|Polish second highest division]] with 21 goals.<ref name=krol/>

===Lech Poznań===
[[File:LewandowskiR.jpg|thumb|upright|left|Lewandowski playing for [[Lech Poznań]] in 2009]]
In June 2008, [[Lech Poznań]] signed Lewandowski from Znicz for 1.5&nbsp;million [[Polish złoty|PLN]].<ref name="bajka" /><ref name="official">{{Cite news |date=18 June 2008 |title=Robert Lewandowski piłkarzem Kolejorza |language=pl |work=[[Lech Poznań]] |url=http://www.lechpoznan.pl/art,9498,robert_lewandowski_pilkarzem_kolejorza_.htm |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080917233558/http://www.lechpoznan.pl/art%2C9498%2Crobert_lewandowski_pilkarzem_kolejorza_.htm |archive-date=17 September 2008}}</ref> Earlier that month, Lewandowski's agent [[Cezary Kucharski]] offered him to his former team [[Sporting Gijón]], which [[2008–09 La Liga|had been promoted]] to the [[La Liga]], Spain's first division, after ten years in the [[Segunda División]]. However, Gijón rejected him.<ref>{{Cite web |date=25 April 2013 |title=Lewandowski was offered to Preciado's Sporting |url=http://www.resultados-futbol.com/noticia/lewandowski-fue-ofrecido-al-sporting-preciado |access-date=14 September 2013 |website=[[Terra Networks|Terra]]}}</ref>

He debuted for Lech on 17 July 2008 as a substitute in the [[2008–09 UEFA Cup qualifying rounds#First leg|first qualifying first leg match]] of the [[2008–09 UEFA Cup|UEFA Cup]] versus [[Khazar Lankaran FK|Khazar Lankaran]] from [[Azerbaijan]], in which he scored the only goal of the evening in the 75th minute at the [[Tofiq Bahramov Republican Stadium]]. During his [[Ekstraklasa]] debut in the first game of the season, in a match against [[GKS Bełchatów]], he scored a heel flick goal just four minutes after coming into the game late second half. In [[2008–09 Ekstraklasa|his first season]] in the Polish top division, he was second in the goal-scoring charts. Lewandowski finished the season with 18 goals in 42 matches.<ref name="ClubMatches">{{Cite web |title=Robert Lewandowski » Club matches |url=http://www.worldfootball.net/player_summary/robert-lewandowski/2/ |access-date=6 February 2018 |publisher=World Football}}</ref> He also scored in a 1–1 away draw against [[Wisła Kraków]] in the [[2009 Polish Super Cup]] on 27 July, and converted his attempt in the won [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shoot-out]]. The next season, he became the [[2009–10 Ekstraklasa#Top goalscorers|top scorer]] with 18 goals and helped his team win the [[2009–10 Ekstraklasa|2009–10 championship]].<ref name="ClubMatches"/>

English coach, [[Sam Allardyce]], said that Lewandowski was about to join [[Premier League]] club [[Blackburn Rovers F.C.|Blackburn Rovers]] in 2010, but the volcanic ash clouds caused by the [[2010 eruptions of Eyjafjallajökull]] which suspended all flights in and out of the UK, in addition to other financial worries, prevented the potential transfer.<ref>{{Cite web |date=26 April 2013 |title=Robert Lewandowski: Sam Allardyce regrets missed signing |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/22305776 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> Moreover, Lewandowski was also about to join Italian club [[Genoa C.F.C.|Genoa]], before president [[Enrico Preziosi]] decided to cancel the transfer.<ref>{{Cite web |date=14 April 2020 |title=Lewandowski 'deal was done' for Genoa before president 'pulled the plug' |url=https://www.goal.com/en-om/news/lewandowski-deal-was-done-for-genoa/10mwwby95gq521hrayoljtafbm |website=Goal.com}}</ref>

===Borussia Dortmund===

====2010–2012: League and cup double====
[[File:Robert Lewandowski 2010.jpg|thumb|upright|right|Lewandowski at [[Borussia Dortmund]] in 2010]]
Following press speculation that Lewandowski might move to one of a number of clubs,<ref>{{Cite news |last=Adamoli |first=Gessi |date=14 April 2010 |title=Accordo con Lewandowski è arrivato il bomber polacco |language=it |work=[[la Repubblica]] |url=http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/2010/04/14/accordo-con-lewandowski-arrivato-il-bomber-polacco.html |access-date=19 May 2010}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=17 April 2010 |title=Blackburn target Lewandowski cancels trip to UK |url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/news/story?id=772358&cc=5901 |access-date=19 May 2010 |website=[[ESPN FC]]}}</ref> he joined [[Bundesliga]] club [[Borussia Dortmund]] in June 2010, signing a four-year contract with the German club<ref>{{Cite web |date=11 June 2010 |title=Dortmund macht Lewandowski-Transfer perfekt |url=http://www.focus.de/sport/fussball/bundesliga1/bundesliga-dortmund-macht-lewandowski-transfer-perfekt_aid_518537.html |access-date=11 June 2010 |website=Focus |language=de}}</ref> for a fee reported to be worth around [[Euro|€]]4.5&nbsp;million.<ref>{{Cite web |date=18 November 2010 |title=Borussia Dortmund striker Robert Lewandowski hits double as Poland beat Ivory Coast (video) |url=http://www.imscouting.com/global-news-article/Borussia-Dortmund-striker-Robert-Lewandowski-hits-double-as-Poland-beat-Ivory-Coast-/12139/ |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120330155229/http://www.imscouting.com/global-news-article/Borussia-Dortmund-striker-Robert-Lewandowski-hits-double-as-Poland-beat-Ivory-Coast-/12139/ |archive-date=30 March 2012 |access-date=1 May 2012 |publisher=imscouting.com}}</ref> On 19 September, he scored his first goal in the Bundesliga to make it 3–0 in the [[Revierderby]] against [[FC Schalke 04|Schalke 04]]; the game ended 3–1.<ref>{{Cite web |date=19 September 2010 |title=Schalke 04 1–3 Borussia Dortmund |url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/match?id=297229&cc=5901 |access-date=1 December 2013 |publisher=ESPN FC}}</ref>

In the [[2011–12 Bundesliga|2011–12 Bundesliga campaign]], Lewandowski profited from an injury to [[Lucas Barrios]] and he was elevated to an ever-present position in the starting XI until the winter break. The striker responded by finding the net two times in Dortmund's 3–0 [[2011–12 DFB-Pokal#First round|DFB-Pokal first round]] victory over [[SV Sandhausen|Sandhausen]].<ref>2011–12 DFB-Pokal</ref> Lewandowski opened his league account in a 2–0 win over [[1. FC Nürnberg|Nürnberg]] on 20 August 2011 by providing the finishing touch from a [[Mario Götze]] cross.<ref>{{Cite web |date=20 August 2011 |title=Borussia Dortmund 2 – 0 Nurnberg |url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/report?id=321020&cc=5901 |access-date=17 May 2012 |publisher=ESPN FC}}</ref> On 1 October, Lewandowski netted a [[hat-trick]] and provided an [[Assist (association football)|assist]] in the club's 4–0 victory over [[FC Augsburg|Augsburg]], following a disappointing 0–3 loss to [[Olympique de Marseille|Marseille]] in the [[2011–12 UEFA Champions League group stage#Group F|UEFA Champions League group stage]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=1 October 2011 |title=Borussia Dortmund 4 – 0 FC Augsburg |url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/report?id=320971&cc=5901 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120714091732/http://soccernet.espn.go.com/report?id=320971&cc=5901 |archive-date=14 July 2012 |access-date=17 May 2012 |publisher=ESPN FC}}</ref> He later scored his first Champions League goal in a 1–3 [[Karaiskakis Stadium|away]] defeat to [[Olympiacos F.C.|Olympiacos]] on 19 October.<ref>{{Cite web |date=1 February 2019 |title=All of Robert Lewandowski's #UCL goals |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/history/video/024e-0e68e5fafae8-e63ef51fb17d-1000--all-of-robert-lewandowski-s-ucl-goals/ |publisher=UEFA}}</ref> Dortmund climbed into second place in the Bundesliga with a comfortable 5–0 victory over [[1. FC Köln|Köln]] on 22 October, with Lewandowski finding the net either side of half-time.<ref>{{Cite web |date=22 October 2011 |title=Borussia Dortmund 5–0 Koln: Five-star champions move second in Bundesliga table |url=http://www.goal.com/en-us/match/60749/borussia-dortmund-vs-fc-k%C3%B6ln/report |access-date=17 May 2012 |website=Goal |publisher=Perform Group |location=Leeds}}</ref> Dortmund travelled to [[SC Freiburg|Freiburg]] on 17 December and Lewandowski struck twice and provided an assist for [[Kevin Großkreutz]], as Dortmund eased to a 4–1 triumph, scoring his first hat-trick in Bundesliga.<ref>{{Cite web |date=17 December 2011 |title=Report: SC Freiburg 1 – 4 Borussia Dortmund |url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/report?id=320891&cc=5901 |access-date=17 May 2012 |publisher=ESPN FC}}</ref> Due to his strong performances, he was named [[Piłka nożna magazine plebiscite|Footballer of the Year in Poland]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=12 December 2011 |title=Lewandowski ist Polens Bester |url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/intligen/startseite/561944/artikel_lewandowski-ist-polens-bester.html |access-date=10 November 2012 |website=kicker |language=de}}</ref>

Following the winter break, on 22 January 2012, Dortmund thrashed [[Hamburger SV|Hamburg]] 5–1 to move level on points with leaders [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]]; Lewandowski netted twice and added an assist for [[Jakub Błaszczykowski]] in the rout.<ref>{{Cite web |date=22 January 2012 |title=Report: Hamburg SV 1 – 5 Borussia Dortmund |url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/report?id=320883&cc=5901 |access-date=17 May 2012 |publisher=ESPN FC}}</ref> He scored in a 1–0 home win over Bayern Munich on 11 April.<ref>{{Cite web |date=11 April 2012 |title=Borussia Dortmund see off Bayern Munich to close on successive title |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2012/apr/11/borussia-dortmund-bayern-munich-match-report |access-date=17 May 2012 |website=The Guardian}}</ref> The result gave Dortmund a six-point cushion over their title rivals with only four games left to play.<ref>{{Cite web |date=11 April 2012 |title=Borussia Dortmund 1 – 0 Bayern Munich |url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/report?id=320773&cc=5901 |access-date=17 May 2012 |publisher=ESPN FC}}</ref> On 21 April, Lewandowski provided the assist for [[Shinji Kagawa]]'s 59th-minute goal as Dortmund won 2–0 over [[Borussia Mönchengladbach]] to seal their second straight title.<ref>{{Cite web |date=21 April 2012 |title=Borussia Dortmund 2–0 Borussia Monchengladbach: Perisic and Kagawa trigger celebrations as Jurgen Klopp's men retain Bundesliga crown |url=http://www.goal.com/en-us/match/61119/borussia-dortmund-vs-m%C3%B6nchengladbach/report |access-date=17 May 2012 |website=Goal |publisher=Perform Group |location=Leeds}}</ref> In the final Bundesliga game of the campaign, Lewandowski scored two first-half goals as Dortmund beat Freiburg 4–0 and celebrated lifting the title.<ref>{{Cite web |date=5 May 2012 |title=Borussia Dortmund 4 – 0 SC Freiburg |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/match_facts/0,,11065_3412576,00.html |access-date=17 May 2012 |publisher=Sky Sports}}</ref>

Lewandowski finished the year as the third top goal scorer with 22 goals, none from the penalty spot, and six assists.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Goals & Assists |url=http://www.bundesliga.de/en/liga/scorer/ |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120508204855/http://www.bundesliga.de/en/liga/scorer/ |archive-date=8 May 2012 |access-date=12 May 2012 |publisher=bundesliga.de}}</ref>

On 12 May, in the final game of the [[2011–12 Borussia Dortmund season|season for Dortmund]], he scored a hat-trick in the [[2012 DFB-Pokal Final|DFB-Pokal Final]], a 5–2 win over Bayern Munich, to earn the club its first domestic [[Double (association football)|double]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=12 May 2012 |title=Dortmund complete double |url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/12040/7752618/Dortmund-complete-double |access-date=17 May 2012 |publisher=Sky Sports}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=12 May 2012 |title=Borussia Dortmund rout Bayern Munich to claim double |url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/news/story/_/id/1069150/borussia-dortmund-rout-bayern-munich-to-claim-historic-double?cc=5901 |access-date=17 May 2012 |publisher=ESPN FC}}</ref> Lewandowski finished as the [[2011–12 DFB-Pokal#Top goalscorers|DFB-Pokal's top goalscorer]], with seven goals from six games.<ref>{{Cite web |title=DFB-Pokal – Torjäger |trans-title=DFB Cup – Goalscorer |url=https://www.kicker.de/dfb-pokal/torjaeger/2011-12/6 |access-date=17 May 2012 |website=kicker |language=de}}</ref>

====2012–2014: Champions League runner-up and league top goalscorer====
[[File:Robert Lewandowski 2013 in Wilhelmshaven.jpeg|thumb|left|upright|Lewandowski playing for [[Borussia Dortmund]] in 2013]]

On 12 August 2012, Lewandowski began the [[2012-13 Borussia Dortmund season|2012–13 season]] by scoring in the 1–2 [[2012 DFL-Supercup]] defeat to Bayern Munich.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Daniel |first=Chris Punnakkattu |date=12 August 2012 |title=DFL Supercup 2012: FC Bayern Munich 2–1 Borussia Dortmund |url=https://blog.cpdfootball.de/2012/08/12/dfl-supercup-2012-fc-bayern-munich-v-borussia-dortmund/ |access-date=29 April 2021 |publisher=CPD Football}}</ref> He made his first appearance of the [[2012–13 Bundesliga]] campaign in Dortmund's 2–1 victory over [[SV Werder Bremen|Werder Bremen]] on the opening day of the season.<ref>{{Cite web |date=24 August 2012 |title=Borussia Dortmund 2–1 Werder Bremen |url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/match?id=346403&cc=5901 |access-date=24 November 2012 |publisher=ESPN FC}}</ref>

He netted his first goal in a 3–0 victory over [[Bayer 04 Leverkusen|Bayer Leverkusen]] on 15 September 2012, extending Dortmund's run to 31 games unbeaten and moved the club into third in the Bundesliga.<ref>{{Cite web |date=15 September 2012 |title=Dortmund 3–0 Leverkusen |url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/report?id=346378&cc=5901 |access-date=24 November 2012 |publisher=ESPN FC}}</ref> Three days later, in the club's first [[2012–13 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] game of the season, Lewandowski scored an 87th-minute winner to defeat [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]], 1–0.<ref>{{Cite web |last=James |first=Andy |date=18 September 2012 |title=Dortmund leave it late to defeat Ajax |url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2013/matches/round=2000347/match=2009502/postmatch/report/index.html |access-date=25 April 2013 |publisher=UEFA}}</ref> He set club's new record of the longest scoring streak, having scored in 12 consecutive league games, surpassing [[Friedhelm Konietzka]]'s record from [[1964–65 Bundesliga|1964–65 season]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=17 March 2013 |title=BVB-Torjäger Lewandowski überholt Konietzka |trans-title=BVB striker Lewandowski overtakes Konietzka |url=https://www.ruhrnachrichten.de/bvb/bvb-torjaeger-lewandowski-ueberholt-konietzka-538280.html |access-date=28 April 2013 |publisher=ruhrnachrichten.de |language=de}}</ref> On 9 February 2013, he opened the scoring in a home match against Hamburg, but was sent off in the 31st minute for a foul on [[Per Ciljan Skjelbred]] and Dortmund lost 4–1.{{citation needed|date=July 2022}}

According to Borussia Dortmund director [[Michael Zorc]], speaking in February 2013, Lewandowski would not be renewing his contract with the club, and would leave either in the summer of 2013 or after the 2013–14 season.<ref>{{Cite news |date=28 February 2013 |title=Borussia Dortmund say Robert Lewandowski will not sign new contract |publisher=Sky Sports |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11899/8529191/Borussia-Dortmund-say-Robert-Lewandowski-will-not-sign-new-contract |access-date=6 March 2013}}</ref> He finished season with 24 league goals, one goal short of the Bundesliga's top scorer, Bayer Leverkusen's [[Stefan Kießling]].{{citation needed|date=July 2022}}

On 27 February 2013, Lewandowski played in his side's 1–0 defeat to Bayern Munich in the [[2012–13 DFB-Pokal#Quarter-finals|2012–13 DFB-Pokal quarter final]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=27 February 2013 |title=Robben nutzt Schmelzers Tiefschlaf zum Sieg |url=https://www.kicker.de/bayern-gegen-dortmund-2013-dfb-pokal-1877174/analyse |access-date=29 April 2021 |website=kicker.de |language=de}}</ref> On 24 April, Lewandowski became the first player to score [[European Cup and UEFA Champions League records and statistics#Four goals in a match|four goals in a Champions League semi-final]] as Borussia Dortmund defeated Spanish champions [[Real Madrid]] 4–1 in the [[2012–13 UEFA Champions League knockout phase#First leg 3|first leg]] at Dortmund's [[Westfalenstadion|Signal Iduna Park]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=James |first=Andy |date=24 April 2013 |title=Four-goal Lewandowski leaves Madrid reeling |url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2013/matches/round=2000350/match=2009608/postmatch/report/index.html#four+goal+lewandowski+shines |access-date=25 April 2013 |publisher=UEFA}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last=Hallam |first=Mark |date=25 April 2013 |title=Dortmund hero Lewandowski linked more loudly with move |publisher=Deutsche Welle |url=http://www.dw.de/dortmund-hero-lewandowski-linked-more-loudly-with-move/a-16772839 |access-date=6 January 2014}}</ref> On 25 May, he played in the [[2013 UEFA Champions League Final]] in which Borussia Dortmund were defeated 1–2 by Bayern Munich.<ref>{{Cite web |date=25 May 2013 |title=Borussia Dortmund 1–2 Bayern Munich |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/22540468 |access-date=11 May 2014 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

On 27 July 2013, Lewandowski won the [[2013 DFL-Supercup]] with Dortmund, 4–2, against Bayern Munich.<ref name="2013 German Super Cup">{{Cite web |date=27 July 2013 |title=Dortmund prevail over Bayern in Supercup thriller |url=http://www.bundesliga.com/en/tournaments/news/2013/0000262446.php |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131106163106/http://www.bundesliga.com/en/tournaments/news/2013/0000262446.php |archive-date=6 November 2013 |access-date=5 November 2013 |publisher=bundesliga.com}}</ref> He scored his first goal of the season in Dortmund's 4–0 win over Augsburg in the club's opening Bundesliga match on 10 August.<ref>{{Cite web |date=10 August 2013 |title=Aubameyang hat-trick has Dortmund flying |url=http://www.bundesliga.com/en/liga/news/2013/0000264271.php |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130813011953/http://www.bundesliga.com/en/liga/news/2013/0000264271.php |archive-date=13 August 2013 |access-date=1 May 2014 |publisher=Bundesliga}}</ref> On 1 November, he scored his only hat-trick of the season in a 6–1 Bundesliga win against [[VfB Stuttgart]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=1 November 2013 |title=Lewandowski shines as Stuttgart hit for six |publisher=bundesliga.com |url=http://www.bundesliga.com/en/liga/news/2013/0000276334.php |url-status=dead |access-date=5 November 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131105001353/http://www.bundesliga.com/en/liga/news/2013/0000276334.php |archive-date=5 November 2013}}</ref>

On 25 February 2014, Lewandowski scored twice in the [[2013–14 UEFA Champions League knockout phase#Round of 16|Champions League round of 16]] first leg against [[FC Zenit Saint Petersburg|Zenit Saint Petersburg]], becoming Dortmund's overall top scorer in European competition, surpassing [[Stéphane Chapuisat]]'s 16 goals.<ref>{{Cite web |date=25 February 2014 |title=Lewandowski najlepszym strzelcem BVB w Europie! |url=http://sport.wp.pl/kat,1752,title,Lewandowski-najlepszym-strzelcem-BVB-w-Europie,wid,16433212,wiadomosc.html?ticaid=112450 |access-date=25 February 2014 |publisher=sport.wp.pl |language=pl }}</ref> He scored his 100th goal for the club on his 182nd appearance, as Dortmund defeated [[VfL Wolfsburg]] 2–0 in the [[2013–14 DFB-Pokal#Semi-finals|semi-finals]] of the [[2013–14 DFB-Pokal]] on 16 April, and revealed a shirt with the number 100 during celebration.<ref>{{Cite news |date=16 April 2014 |title=Lewandowski scores 100th goal for Borussia Dortmund |publisher=[[Polskie Radio]] |url=http://www.thenews.pl/1/5/Artykul/168448,Lewandowski-scores-100th-goal-for-Borussia-Dortmund |access-date=24 October 2014}}</ref>

Lewandowski ended the [[2013–14 Borussia Dortmund season|2013–14 season]] as the top goalscorer in the [[2013–14 Bundesliga|Bundesliga]] with 20 goals, which earned him the {{ill|Torjägerkanone|de}}.<ref>{{Cite web |date=10 May 2014 |title=Lewandowski top of the charts as Dortmund thrash Hertha |url=http://www.bundesliga.com/en/liga/news/2013/0000293681.php |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140511003549/http://www.bundesliga.com/en/liga/news/2013/0000293681.php |archive-date=11 May 2014 |access-date=11 May 2014 |publisher=Bundesliga}}</ref> He also scored six goals in the Champions League, as Dortmund reached the quarter-finals.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Robert Lewandowski |publisher=UEFA |url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2014/clubs/player=250002096/index.html |access-date=11 May 2014}}</ref> During the second leg of the round of 16 match between Borussia Dortmund and Zenit, Lewandowski received a second yellow card, which resulted in his suspension for the [[2013–14 UEFA Champions League knockout phase#First leg 2|first leg of the quarter-final]] against Real Madrid.{{citation needed|date=July 2022}}

Lewandowski played his final match for Dortmund in the [[2014 DFB-Pokal Final]] against Bayern Munich on 17 May. Head coach [[Jürgen Klopp]] had excused him from some training ahead of the final due to injury concerns; although Lewandowski played all 120 minutes of the final, Dortmund lost, 0–2.<ref>{{Cite web |date=16 May 2014 |title=Bayern Munich and Borussia Dortmund set for German Cup final |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/27446201 |access-date=16 May 2014 |website=[[BBC Sport]]}}</ref> He finished the season with 28 goals in 48 matches.<ref name="2013 German Super Cup" /><ref>{{Cite web |title=Robert Lewandowski |url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/vereine/2013-14/51570/vereinsspieler_robert-lewandowski.html |access-date=6 February 2018 |website=kicker |language=de}}</ref>

===Bayern Munich===
In November 2013, Lewandowski confirmed he would sign a pre-contractual agreement for Borussia Dortmund's rivals Bayern Munich,<ref>{{Cite news |last=Smith |first=Paul |date=10 November 2013 |title=Bayern Munich win the race to sign Borussia Dortmund striker Robert Lewandowski |work=Daily Mirror |url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/football/news/robert-lewandowski-bayern-munich-win-2715710 |access-date=5 January 2014}}</ref> which officially happened on 3 January 2014, when he signed a five-year contract beginning at the start of the [[2014–15 FC Bayern Munich season|2014–15 season]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=4 January 2014 |title=Robert Lewandowski to join Bayern Munich from Borussia Dortmund |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/25605262 |access-date=20 July 2017 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> Lewandowski was officially presented as a Bayern Munich player on 9 July 2014.<ref name="Unveiled">{{Cite news |last=Badger |first=Jake |date=9 July 2014 |title=In pictures: Bayern Munich introduce Robert Lewandowski |work=The National |url=https://www.thenational.ae/sport/in-pictures-bayern-munich-introduce-robert-lewandowski-1.477970 |access-date=7 June 2020}}</ref>

====2015–2017: Domestic success, Torjägerkanone, and 100 Bayern goals====
[[File:Robert Lewandowski Training FC Bayern München-2.jpg|thumb|upright|Lewandowski training with [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] in 2015]]

Lewandowski's [[2015–16 FC Bayern Munich season|second season]] began with the [[2015 DFL-Supercup]] on 1 August, with Bayern losing in a penalty shootout away to VfL Wolfsburg; he had been substituted in the 72nd minute for [[Rafinha (footballer, born 1985)|Rafinha]].<ref name="supercup">{{Cite news |last=Dunbar |first=Ross |date=1 August 2015 |title=Bendtner leads Wolfsburg over Bayern Munich on penalties in German Super Cup clash |work=Fox Sports |url=http://www.foxsports.com/soccer/story/bendtner-leads-wolfsburg-over-bayern-munich-on-penalties-in-german-super-cup-clash-080115?vid=null |access-date=3 August 2015}}</ref> Eight days later in the [[2015–16 DFB-Pokal#FC Nöttingen v Bayern Munich|DFB-Pokal first round match]], he scored the last goal in a 3–1 win against [[Oberliga Baden-Württemberg]] club [[FC Nöttingen|Nöttingen]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=9 August 2015 |title=Nottingen 1–3 Bayern Munich: Vidal off the mark in cup win |url=http://www.goal.com/en/match/n%C3%B6ttingen-vs-bayern-m%C3%BCnchen/2038931/report?ICID=MP_MS_5 |access-date=14 August 2015 |website=Goal |publisher=Perform Group |location=Leeds}}</ref> On 14 August, in the opening match of the [[2015–16 Bundesliga|new Bundesliga season]], he scored the second goal of a 5–0 win over Hamburg.<ref>{{Cite web |date=14 August 2015 |title=Bayern Munich 5–0 Hamburg: Bavarians begin Bundesliga campaign in ominous fashion |url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/match/bayern-m%C3%BCnchen-vs-hamburger-sv/2054191?ICID=HP_HN_HP_RI_1_1 |access-date=14 August 2015 |website=Goal |publisher=Perform Group |location=Leeds}}</ref>

On 22 September 2015, Lewandowski set a [[Bundesliga records and statistics#Goals 2|Bundesliga record]] by coming on as a substitute with Bayern trailing 0–1 to Wolfsburg and scoring five goals in 8 minutes and 59 seconds, the fastest by any player in Bundesliga history, to take a 5–1 lead. He also set Bundesliga records for the fastest hat-trick (three goals in four minutes), and most goals scored by a substitute (five).<ref>{{Cite web |date=22 September 2015 |title=Bayern striker Robert Lewandowski scores 5 goals in 9 minutes |url=http://www.espnfc.com/bayern-munich/story/2625497/bayern-robert-lewandowski-scores-five-in-nine-minutes |access-date=22 September 2015 |publisher=[[ESPN FC]]}}</ref> Lewandowski's five goals in nine minutes was also the fastest in any [[Big Five (association football)|major European football league]] since [[Opta Sports|Opta]] began keeping records, and it ended Wolfsburg's 14-match unbeaten run.<ref>{{Cite web |date=22 September 2015 |title=Robert Lewandowski: More goals in nine minutes than Liverpool |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/34332408 |access-date=22 September 2015 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> He was awarded four certificates by [[Guinness World Records]] for this feat.<ref>{{Cite web |date=1 December 2015 |title=Robert Lewandowski receives awards for five-goal feat |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/34979052 |access-date=1 December 2015 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

Four days later, he scored twice in a 3–0 win at [[1. FSV Mainz 05|Mainz]], the first goal being his 100th Bundesliga goal on his 168th appearance, a league record for a foreign player. He also reached 10 goals in the opening 7 matches with this brace, a feat only achieved before by [[Gerd Müller]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=26 September 2015 |title=1. FSV Mainz 05 0–3 Bayern Munich |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/34351078 |access-date=26 September 2015 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> On 29 September, he scored a [[List of UEFA Champions League hat-tricks|Champions League hat-trick]] in a 5–0 win over [[Dinamo Zagreb]], putting him on ten goals in three games in a week.<ref>{{Cite news |date=29 September 2015 |title=Bayern Munich 5–0 Dinamo Zagreb |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/34275527 |access-date=30 September 2015}}</ref> He added two in a 5–1 rout of Dortmund five days later, to total 12 goals in his last four appearances.<ref>{{Cite news |date=4 October 2015 |title=Bayern Munich 5–1 Borussia Dortmund |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/34381544 |access-date=5 October 2015}}</ref> On 24 October, Lewandowski scored in a 4–0 home win over 1. FC Köln, a result which made Bayern the first Bundesliga team ever to win all 10 of their opening games of a season.<ref>{{Cite news |date=24 October 2015 |title=Bayern Munich 4–0 1. FC Köln |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/34619451 |access-date=24 October 2015}}</ref> The victory in Cologne was also Bayern's 1,000th win in the Bundesliga.<ref>{{Cite web |date=24 October 2015 |title=Erfolg gegen Köln: Bayern feiert 1000. ====2017–2019: Consecutive Golden Boots and all-time foreign Bundesliga goalscorer====
[[File:Robert Lewandowski Training 2017-03 FC Bayern Muenchen-3.jpg|thumb|upright|Lewandowski in training with [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] in 2017]]

The [[2017–18 FC Bayern Munich season|season]] began with Bayern Munich winning the [[2017 DFL-Supercup]] against Borussia Dortmund, in which Lewandowski scored the opening goal for the Bavarians by controlling a low cross from [[Joshua Kimmich]] to cancel out [[Christian Pulisic]]'s opener. The match ended 2–2 after extra time. Lewandowski, again, scored the first penalty of the shootout as Bayern eventually won 5–4.<ref>{{Cite web |date=6 August 2017 |title=Bayern Munich beat Dortmund on penalties to retain Supercup |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/borussia-dortmund-bayern-munich-supercup-line-ups-and-statistics-456360.jsp |access-date=7 June 2020 |publisher=Bundesliga}}</ref>

Lewandowski started from where he left in the last season and once again was the top scorer in the early stages of the [[2017–18 Bundesliga]]. On 13 December, in the league fixture against Köln, he scored the only goal of the game, to reach Bundesliga's top ten goalscorers of all time.<ref>{{Cite web |date=13 December 2017 |title=Robert Lewandowski moves into the top 10 on the Bundesliga's all-time goalscoring list |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/robert-lewandowski-into-top-10-on-the-bundesliga-s-all-time-goalscoring-list-464867.jsp |access-date=15 December 2017 |publisher=Bundesliga}}</ref> A couple of months later, on Matchday 22, Lewandowski again found the back of the net against Schalke 04 at the [[Allianz Arena]] to equal the record of scoring in 11 successive home games in a single season, a record also held by then Bayern manager [[Jupp Heynckes]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=10 February 2018 |title=Robert Lewandowski equals record of scoring in 11 successive home games |work=Marca |location=Spain |url=https://amp.marca.com/en/football/international-football/2018/02/10/5a7f3d72e2704eee108b459f.html |access-date=11 February 2018}}</ref> He continued his goal scoring form by netting a hat-trick against Hamburg as the runaway leaders won 6–0, while he also missed a kick from the spot which would have been his fourth goal of the day. This was his first penalty missed for Bayern in the Bundesliga, nevertheless he scored the second spot kick to complete his hat-trick.<ref>{{Cite news |date=10 March 2018 |title=Bundesliga round-up: Robert Lewandowski's hat-trick edges Bayern Munich closer to the title |work=Sky sports |url=https://www.skysports.com/amp/football/news/11881/11285045/bundesliga-round-up-robert-lewandowski's-hat-trick-edges-bayern-munich-closer-to-the-title |access-date=11 March 2018}}</ref>

On 11 February 2018, he was voted Poland's Footballer of the Year for the seventh time in a row.<ref>{{Cite web |date=11 February 2018 |title=Lewandowski Footballer of the Year again |url=https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2018/02/lewandowski-named-polish-footballer-of-the-year-again |access-date=4 March 2018 |publisher=FC Bayern Munich}}</ref> On 22 February, he fired his long-time agent, Cezary Kucharski. Lewandowski hired renowned dealmaker [[Pini Zahavi]] as his new agent; the hiring of Zahavi was rumoured to be the start of Lewandowski trying to seal a summer move to Real Madrid.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Uersfeld |first=Stephen |date=22 February 2018 |title=Robert Lewandowski splits from agent amid Real Madrid links – sources |url=https://global.espn.com/football/bayern-munich/story/3393508/robert-lewandowski-splits-from-agent-amid-real-madrid-links-sources |access-date=4 March 2018 |publisher=[[ESPN]]}}</ref> On 24 February, he played his 250th Bundesliga game against [[Hertha BSC]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=24 February 2018 |title=Coman sustains capsular injury |url=https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2018/02/news-in-brief-240218 |access-date=4 March 2018 |publisher=FC Bayern Munich}}</ref> On 19 May, Lewandowski scored Bayern's only goal in a 3–1 defeat in the [[2018 DFB-Pokal Final|DFB-Pokal Final]] against Eintracht Frankfurt.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Match Report |url=https://fcbayern.com/en/matches/profis/dfb-pokal/2017-2018/fc-bayern-muenchen-eintracht-frankfurt-19-05-2018-matchreport |access-date=12 August 2018 |publisher=FC Bayern Munich}}</ref>

Lewandowski finished the league as the Bundesliga's top goalscorer with 29 goals. This was the third time he won the Torjägerkanone award.<ref>{{Cite web |date=14 May 2018 |title=Robert Lewandowski, Thomas Müller and the Bundesliga's 2017/18 chart-toppers |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/lewandowski-thomas-muller-2017-18-most-goals-most-assists-top-stats-481046.jsp |access-date=20 May 2018 |publisher=Bundesliga}}</ref> He finished the season with 41 goals in 48 matches in all competitions.<ref>{{Cite web |title=The 10 top goalscorers of 2017/18 in all competitions |url=https://www.besoccer.com/new/the-10-top-goalscorers-of-2017-18-in-all-competitions-436024 |access-date=30 May 2018 |website=BeSoccer}}</ref>

On 1 August, after a summer of transfer speculation, Bayern CEO [[Karl-Heinz Rummenigge]], confirmed in an interview that Lewandowski would not be allowed to leave Bayern at any price, saying "the top quality we have at Bayern Munich will stay here. Lewandowski ended the [[2018–19 Bundesliga|league campaign]] as the Bundesliga's top goalscorer with 22 goals for the fourth time.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Bayern Munich's Robert Lewandowski claims Bundesliga topscorer prize for fourth time |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/bayern-munich-s-robert-lewandowski-claims-bundesliga-topscorer-prize-for-fourth-time-4597 |access-date=18 May 2019 |publisher=Bundesliga}}</ref> On 25 May, he scored a brace as Bayern won against [[RB Leipzig|Leipzig]] 3–0 in the [[2019 DFB-Pokal Final]]. Lewandowski began his international career with Poland [[Poland national under-19 football team|under-19]] in 2007.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Kumari |first=Payal |date=25 February 2022 |title=Everything You Need To Know About Robert Lewandowski: Debut, Teams, Goals, Records And Net Worth |url=https://sportsaxle.com/everything-you-need-to-know-about-robert-lewandowski-debut-teams-goals-records-and-net-worth/ |access-date=20 July 2022 |website=Sports Axle |language=en-US}}</ref> He would also make three appearances for [[Poland national under-21 football team|Poland's U21 team]], in friendly matches against [[England national under-21 football team|England]], [[Belarus national under-21 football team|Belarus]] and [[Finland national under-21 football team|Finland]].{{citation needed|date=July 2022}}

==Style of play==
[[File:20190428 DFL 1. Bundesliga FCN - FCB 850 0565.jpg|left|thumb|Lewandowski playing against [[FC Nürnberg]] in 2019]]
Lewandowski is widely regarded as one of the best strikers in the world,<ref>{{Cite news |date=22 October 2019 |title=Robert Lewandowski: Is the Bayern Munich player the world's best striker right now? |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/50145108 |access-date=20 August 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Veth |first=Manuel |title=Lewandowski Underlines Status As World's Best Striker In Win Over Chelsea |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/manuelveth/2020/08/08/lewandowski-underlines-status-as-worlds-best-striker-in-win-over-chelsea/ |access-date=20 August 2020 |website=Forbes}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last=Gheerbrant |first=James |title=Robert Lewandowski – the planet's best striker – deserves European glory with Bayern Munich |work=The Times |url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/robert-lewandowski-the-planet-s-best-striker-deserves-european-glory-with-bayern-munich-7bqrhtxlw |access-date=20 August 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Bayern Munich's Robert Lewandowski the world's best striker? |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/bayern-munich-s-robert-lewandowski-world-s-best-striker-456853.jsp |access-date=20 August 2020 |website=bundesliga.com}}{{Dead link|date=March 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=27 November 2019 |title=Ranked! - Player Biography and Profile |url=https://www.sports-king.com/nicknames.php?q=lewangoalski |access-date=30 May 2022 |website=www.sports-king.com}}</ref> A well-rounded [[Forward (association football)|forward]], he is said to possess almost all the necessary qualities of a traditional [[Squad number (association football)|number nine]]: height, strength, balance, pace, intelligent movement and proficiency with both feet.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Haugstad |first=Thore |date=10 October 2015 |title=How Robert Lewandowski became this season's deadliest striker |url=http://www.espnfc.co.uk/blog/tactics-and-analysis/67/post/2653540/how-robert-lewandowski-became-this-seasons-deadliest-striker |access-date=16 July 2016 |website=ESPN FC}}</ref> Although he primarily operates as a goal-poacher in the penalty area, due to his positional sense, ability to shoot first time, strength in the air, and powerful shot with either foot, his excellent technical skills, quick feet, proficient [[Dribbling#Association football|dribbling]], vision, and physique also enable him to hold up the ball with his back to goal and either bring his teammates into play, or win fouls for his team in useful positions; despite often functioning as a lone-[[centre forward]] or as an out-and-out [[Striker (association football)|striker]].

He has also stood out for his work-rate and defensive contribution off the ball, and is capable of dropping into deeper roles on the pitch, in order to create space for teammates with his movement, or surprise defenders by making late and sudden attacking runs into the area. Lewandowski is an accurate [[Penalty kick (association football)|penalty]] taker and has repeatedly shown coolness and composure on the spot; he is also capable of scoring from long range, and has been known to take [[Free kick (association football)|free kicks]]. ==Outside football==

===Personal life===
Lewandowski's father gave him the name Robert to make it easier for him when moving abroad as a professional footballer.<ref name="Fortitude"/> Lewandowski's father, Krzysztof (died in 2005),<ref>{{Cite web |last=Michalak |first=Przemysław |date=16 June 2013 |title=Lewandowski: Ojciec zmarł w 2005 roku. Wszystkie gole dedykuję jemu |url=http://www.2x45.info/aktualnosci/17751/lewandowski-ojciec-zmarl-w-2005-roku-wszystkie-gole-dedykuje-jemu/ |publisher=2x45 Info}}</ref> was a Polish [[judo]] champion, and also played football for [[Hutnik Warsaw]] in the second division.<ref name="background">{{Cite news |last1=Schuth |first1=Joachim |last2=Weiler |first2=Jörg |date=7 July 2010 |title=Ricken und Sammer sind meine Idole |language=de |work=[[Bild]] |url=http://www.bild.de/BILD/sport/fussball/bundesliga/vereine/dortmund/2010/07/08/bvb-star-robert-lewandowski/lars-ricken-und-matthias-sammer-sind-meine-idole.html |access-date=13 July 2010}}</ref> His mother, Iwona, is a former [[volleyball]] player for [[AZS Politechnika Warsaw|AZS Warsaw]] and later vice-president of Partyzant Leszno.<ref name=background/> His sister, Milena, also plays volleyball and has represented the U21 national team.<ref name=background/>

His wife, [[Anna Lewandowska]], won the bronze medal at the 2009 [[Karate]] World Cup.<ref name=background/> They married on 22 June 2013 in the [[Serock#Church of the Annunciation of the Blessed Virgin Mary|Church of the Annunciation of the Blessed Virgin Mary]] in [[Serock]]. They have two daughters.<ref>{{Cite web |date=4 May 2017 |title=Stolzer Papa von Klara |trans-title=Proud Dad of Klara |url=http://www.spiegel.de/panorama/leute/fc-bayern-muenchen-robert-lewandowski-ist-vater-geworden-a-1146126.html |access-date=4 May 2017 |website=Der Spiegel |language=de}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=6 May 2020 |title=Robert Lewandowski: Second daughter born – 'Welcome to the world, Laura' |url=https://www.en24.news/en/2020/05/robert-lewandowski-second-daughter-born-welcome-to-the-world-laurahtml |access-date=6 May 2020 |website=En24 News}}</ref>

Lewandowski is a practising [[Catholic Church in Poland|Catholic]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=12 June 2018 |title=Famous football player Robert Lewandowski is not ashamed of Jesus. Video in 8 languages |work=Polonia Christiana |publisher=Father Piotr Skarga Association for Christian Culture |location=Kraków |url=https://www.eng.pch24.pl/famous-football-player-robert-lewandowski-is-not-ashamed-of-jesus--video-in-8-languages-,60911,i.html |access-date=10 April 2019}}</ref> He met [[Pope Francis]] in October 2014, when Bayern Munich visited [[Vatican City]] following a 7–1 win over [[A.S. Roma]] in the [[2014–15 UEFA Champions League group stage#Group E|UEFA Champions League]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=22 October 2014 |title=Bayern Munich: Pope praises German club after win over Roma |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/29729737 |access-date=24 October 2014 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

In October 2017, the day after scoring to help Poland qualify for the 2018 World Cup, Lewandowski finished his [[Bachelor of Physical Education]] (BPhEd) with coaching and management at the Academy of Sport Education in Warsaw, concluding a decade of studies.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Burton |first=Chris |date=10 October 2017 |title=Bayern striker Lewandowski gets a degree... after writing about himself |work=Goal |publisher=Perform Group |location=Leeds |url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/bayern-striker-lewandowski-gets-a-bachelors-degree-after-writing-/jl5t25q4k2bk10hcjfkh6r0fc |access-date=5 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |date=10 October 2017 |title=School's out for Robert Lewandowski as Bayern Munich striker completes decade-long university studies |publisher=Bundesliga |location=Frankfurt |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/bayern-munich-s-robert-lewandowski-completes-university-course-461260.jsp |access-date=7 September 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=8 October 2017 |title=Lewandowski: Wczoraj awansował do mundialu, dziś obronił pracę licencjacką |url=https://www.przegladsportowy.pl/pilka-nozna/reprezentacja-polski/lewandowski-dzis-awans-jutro-licencjat/1ph2exn |website=Przegląd Sportowy}}</ref>

In addition to his native [[Polish language|Polish]], Lewandowski also speaks English and German.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Leadership League: Robert Lewandowski {{!}} Leadership League |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eep--Dny8oc |access-date=19 July 2020 |website=CNBC International TV |via=YouTube}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=FC Bayern Press Conference w/ Robert Lewandowski {{!}} ReLive |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Sp_Yk5d8I1Y |access-date=19 July 2020 |website=FC Bayern Munich |via=YouTube}}</ref>
