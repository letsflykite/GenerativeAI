'''Azzedine Ounahi''' ({{Lang-ar|عز الدين أوناحي}}, {{IPA-ar|ʕiz ʔad.di:n u:na:ħi:|pron}}; born 19 April 2000) is a Moroccan professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for{{French football updater|Marseille}} club [[Olympique de Marseille|Marseille]] and the [[Morocco national football team|Morocco national team]].<ref name="soccerway">{{Soccerway|564548}}</ref>

==Club career==

=== Early career ===
A former player of [[Raja CA]] and [[Mohammed VI Football Academy|Mohammed VI Academy]], Ounahi joined [[RC Strasbourg Alsace|Strasbourg]] in 2018. In August 2020, he moved to [[Championnat National]] club [[US Avranches|Avranches]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Azzeddine Ounahi (ex-Strasbourg) signe à Avranches|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Transferts-azzeddine-ounahi-ex-strasbourg-signe-a-avranches/1157767|date=3 August 2020|access-date=15 August 2021}}</ref>

=== Angers ===
On 14 July 2021, [[Ligue 1]] club [[Angers SCO|Angers]] announced the signing of Ounahi on a four-year deal.<ref>{{cite web|title=Angers recrute Azzedine Ounahi|url=https://www.footmercato.net/amp3453273236795645825-angers-recrute-azzedine-ounahi|date=14 July 2021|access-date=15 August 2021}}</ref> He made his professional debut on 15 August 2021 by scoring a goal in club's 3–0 league win against [[Olympique Lyonnais|Lyon]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Angers vs. Lyon - 15 August 2021|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2021/08/15/france/ligue-1/angers-sporting-club-de-louest/olympique-lyonnais/3525912/|access-date=15 August 2021}}</ref>

=== Marseille ===
After attracting the interest of several top-tier clubs across Europe, thanks to his breakthrough performances for [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]] at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]],<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web |date=23 December 2022 |title=Azzedine Ounahi : "Garder les pieds sur terres, continuer de travailler et surtout préparer la prochaine compétition" |url=https://www.angers-sco.fr/azzedine-ounahi-garder-les-pieds-sur-terres-continuer-de-travailler-et-surtout-preparer-la-prochaine-competition/ |access-date=29 January 2023 |website=Angers SCO |language=fr-FR}}</ref> on 29 January 2023 Ounahi officially joined fellow Ligue 1 side [[Olympique de Marseille|Marseille]] for an estimated fee of 8 million [[Euro|euros]] plus add-ons, signing a contract until June 2027.<ref>{{Cite web |date=29 January 2023 |title=Azzedine Ounahi est Olympien ! |url=https://www.om.fr/fr/actualites/4803/equipe-pro/72562-azzedine-ounahi-est-olympien |access-date=29 January 2023 |website=Olympique de Marseille |language=fr-FR}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Béal |first=Xavier |date=29 January 2023 |title=Montant du transfert, durée du contrat, salaire : toutes les infos sur la signature d'Ounahi à l'OM |url=https://www.goal.com/fr/news/montant-transfert-duree-contrat-salaire-toutes-infos-signature-ounahi-om/bltafa4abc43373d5e2 |access-date=29 January 2023 |website=Goal.com |language=fr-FR}}</ref> On 1 February 2023, he scored his first goal for Marseille on his debut against [[FC Nantes|Nantes]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=PLAYER RATINGS {{!}} Nantes 0-2 Marseille: Azzedine Ounahi scores on debut as OM put pressure on PSG |url=https://onefootball.com/fr/news/player-ratings-nantes-0-2-marseille-azzedine-ounahi-scores-on-debut-as-om-put-pressure-on-psg-36715725 |access-date=2023-02-02 |website=OneFootball |language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-02-02 |title=Azzedine Ounahi debuts for Marseille with flying colors |url=https://en.hespress.com/57968-azzedine-ounahi-debuts-for-marseille-with-flying-colors.html |access-date=2023-02-03 |website=HESPRESS English - Morocco News |language=en-US}}</ref>

==International career==
===Youth===
Ounahi was called up for [[Morocco national under-20 football team|Morocco U20]] to participate in the [[Football at the 2018 Mediterranean Games|2018 Mediterranean Games]]. On 22 June 2018, he scored a goal at the 68th minute in a tie against [[Italy national under-18 football team|Italy]]. He later went on to win the bronze medal with the team after a victory in the [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shoot-out]] against [[Greece national under-19 football team|Greece]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Pluie de médailles pour le Maroc aux Jeux méditerranéens de Tarragone|url=https://www.huffpostmaghreb.com/entry/pluies-de-medailles-pour-les-athletes-marocains-aux-jeux-mediterraneens-de-tarragone_mg_5b3773e9e4b08c3a8f6abaa4|date=30 August 2018|access-date=13 January 2022|language=es|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180726143546/https://www.huffpostmaghreb.com/entry/pluies-de-medailles-pour-les-athletes-marocains-aux-jeux-mediterraneens-de-tarragone_mg_5b3773e9e4b08c3a8f6abaa4|archive-date=26 July 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===Senior===
On 23 December 2021, [[Vahid Halilhodžić]] gave Ounahi his first call-up to the [[Morocco national football team|Moroccan senior national team]] for the [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=23 December 2021|title=Vahid Halilhodzic reveals final list of players for CAN 2022|url=https://en.hespress.com/32767-vahid-halilhodzic-reveals-final-list-of-players-for-can-2022.html|access-date=13 January 2022|website=HESPRESS English - Morocco’s leading digital media|language=en-US}}</ref> Ounahi made his professional debut for Morocco in the [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations|2021 African Cup of Nations]] in a 1–0 victory against [[Ghana national football team|Ghana]] on 10 January 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Morocco vs. Ghana -
