'''Carlos Uriel Antuna Romero''' (born 21 August 1997) is a Mexican professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Midfielder#Winger|winger]] for [[Liga MX]] club [[Cruz Azul]] and the [[Mexico national football team|Mexico national team]].

==Club career==
===Early career===
In 2012, Antuna joined the [[Santos Laguna]] youth academy, successfully going through the U-15, U-17 and U-20 teams.

In 2017, [[Santos Laguna|first-team]] manager [[José Manuel de la Torre]] promoted Antuna. On 5 March, Antuna made his professional debut in the [[Liga MX]] entering as a substitute in a 2–1 loss to [[Club Universidad Nacional|Pumas UNAM]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://concacaf.com/es/article/mexico-u20-estrella-antuna-hace-liga-mx-debut-con-santos|title=Estrella mexicana del Sub 20 Antuna, debutó con Santos en Liga MX|publisher=Concacaf.com|language=es|access-date=7 September 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170914173001/http://www.concacaf.com/es/article/mexico-u20-estrella-antuna-hace-liga-mx-debut-con-santos|archive-date=14 September 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===FC Groningen===
On 12 July 2017, English club [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]], via [[City Football Group]], announced they had signed Antuna on a four-year contract.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.mcfcwatch.com/2017/07/13/city-football-group-sign-mexico-u20-midfielder-uriel-antuna-from-santos-laguna/ |title=NEWS City Football Group sign Mexico U20 midfielder Uriel Antuna from Santos Laguna |publisher= City Watch |website= mcfcwatch.com |access-date= 12 July 2017}}</ref> On 9 August, he joined [[Eredivisie]] club [[FC Groningen]] on a two-year loan deal.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.mcfcwatch.com/2017/08/08/dutch-side-fc-groningen-sign-manchester-city-youngster-on-two-year-loan-deal/ |title=Dutch side FC Groningen sign Manchester City youngster on two-year loan deal |publisher= City Watch |website= mcfcwatch.com |access-date= 9 August 2017}}</ref>

On 10 September, Antuna made his league debut against [[VVV-Venlo]]; he entered the match as a substitute in a 1–1 draw.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.informador.com.mx/deportes/2017/738667/6/mexicano-uriel-antuna-debuta-en-la-liga-de-holanda.htm|title= Mexicano Uriel Antuna debuta en la Liga de Holanda|publisher= Informador |language= es |access-date=10 September 2017}}</ref>

===LA Galaxy===
On 29 January 2019, Antuna joined [[Major League Soccer|MLS]] side [[LA Galaxy]] on loan for their 2019 season. He scored his first goal for the team in their 2–1 victory over [[Real Salt Lake]] on 28 April.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lagalaxy.com/post/2019/01/29/la-galaxy-acquire-midfielder-uriel-antuna|title = LA Galaxy acquire midfielder Uriel Antuna &#124; LA Galaxy}}</ref> In October, he extended his contract with Manchester City, keeping him through 2022.<ref>{{cite web|title=Report: LA Galaxy's on-loan winger Uriel Antuna extends Manchester City contract|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2019/10/03/report-la-galaxys-loan-winger-uriel-antuna-extends-manchester-city-contract|publisher=Mlssoccer.com|first=Tom|last=Bogert|date=3 October 2019}}</ref> At the end of his time with LA Galaxy, he made 31 league appearances with 6 goals and 5 assists; he also received interest from Portuguese club [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Report: LA Galaxy's Uriel Antuna drawing interest from Benfica|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2019/10/25/report-la-galaxys-uriel-antuna-drawing-interest-benfica|work=MLSSoccer.com|first=Alicia|last=Rodriguez|date=25 October 2019}}</ref>

===Guadalajara===
On 28 November 2019, Antuna joined [[Liga MX]] side [[C.D. Guadalajara|Guadalajara]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Conoce a fondo a Uriel Antuna, primer refuerzo de Chivas. |url=https://www.chivasdecorazon.com.mx/es//noticias/32541/Conoce-a-fondo-a-Uriel-Antuna-primer-refuerzo-de-Chivas |language=es|access-date=28 November 2019 |website=chivasdecorazon.com.mx}}</ref> On 11 January 2020, he would make his debut with Guadalajara, coming on as a substitute for [[Isaác Brizuela]] in a 2–0 victory over [[FC Juárez|Juárez]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Chivas defeat Juarez convincingly 2-0|url=https://vivaligamx.com/2020/01/12/chivas-defeat-juarez-convincingly-2-0/|work=VivaLigaMX.com|first=Nestor|last=Esquivel|date=12 January 2020}}</ref> On 5 September, Antuna scored his first goal with ''Chivas'' against [[Tigres UANL]] in a 3–1 victory.<ref>{{cite web|title=Uriel Antuna marca su primer gol con Chivas en la Liga MX|date=6 September 2020|url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/liga-mx/2020/09/06/5f544cfb268e3e781a8b4582.html}}</ref>

==International career==
===Youth===
Antuna was called up to the [[Mexico national under-20 football team|under-20]] team camp in preparations for the [[2017 CONCACAF U-20 Championship]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Convocatoria de la Selección Nacional de México Sub 20 |url=http://miseleccion.mx/convocatoria-de-la-seleccion-nacional-de-mexico-sub-20/ |website=MiSeleccion.com.mx |language=es}}</ref> He was subsequently called up for the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|title=Convocatoria de la Selección Nacional de México Sub-20|url=https://miseleccion.mx/convocatoria-la-seleccion-nacional-mexico-sub-20/|website=Miseleccion.mx|publisher=FMF|access-date=30 October 2018}}</ref> During a [[2017 CONCACAF U-20 Championship#Classification stage|classification stage]] match against [[El Salvador national under-20 football team|El Salvador]] he would score a [[hat-trick]], with Mexico winning the match 6–1.<ref>{{cite web|title=Antuna hat-trick propels Mexico past El Salvador at CU20|url=https://www.concacaf.com/en/under-20s-men/article/antuna-hat-trick-propels-mexico-past-el-salvador-at-cu-20|website=Concacaf.com|publisher=CONCACAF|access-date=30 October 2018}}</ref> He was included in the best XI of the tournament.<ref name="Best XI">{{cite web|url=http://www.concacaf.com/article/tsg-announces-cu20-best-xi-awards-2|title=TSG announces CU20 Best XI, Awards|publisher=CONCACAF.com|date=8 March 2017|access-date=10 March 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170312051624/http://www.concacaf.com/article/tsg-announces-cu20-best-xi-awards-2|archive-date=12 March 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> Antuna was called up for the [[2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Convocatoria de la SNM Sub-20 para la Copa del Mundo de la FIFA Corea 2017|url=https://miseleccion.mx/convocatoria-la-snm-sub-20-la-copa-del-mundo-la-fifa-corea-2017/|website=miseleccion.mx|publisher=FMF|access-date=30 October 2018}}</ref> He would appear in all five matches until they were eliminated by [[England national under-20 football team|England]] in the [[2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup#Quarter-finals|quarter-finals]].

Antuna was included in the final roster that participated at the [[2018 Toulon Tournament]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Revelan convocatoria del Tri Sub-21 para el torneo Esperanzas de Toulon|url=http://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/mundial/nota/_/id/4333245/revelan-convocatoria-del-tri-sub-21-para-el-torneo-esperanzas-de-toulon|work=ESPN|language=es|date=22 May 2018|access-date=26 October 2018}}</ref> He would appear in all five matches as Mexico went on to face [[England national under-21 football team|England]] in the final, losing 2–1.<ref>{{cite web|title=England Beat Mexico to Win 2018 Toulon Tournament Final, 3rd Straight Title|url=https://bleacherreport.com/articles/2780312-england-beat-mexico-to-win-2018-toulon-tournament-final-3rd-straight-title|work=Bleacher Report|first=Christopher|last=Simpson|access-date=21 August 2018}}</ref>

Antuna participated at the [[2020 CONCACAF Men's Olympic Qualifying Championship|2020 CONCACAF Olympic Qualifying Championship]], scoring three goals in five appearances, where Mexico won the competition. He was subsequently included in the tournament's Best XI.<ref name=CONCACAF>{{cite web|title=Ellos son los 4 mexicanos que encabezan el 11 ideal de Concacaf del Preolímpico 2021|url=https://soyreferee.com/futbolinternacional/Ellos-son-los-4-mexicanos-que-encabezan-el-11-ideal-de-Concacaf-del-Preolimpico-2021-20210331-0016.html}}</ref> He was subsequently called up to participate in the [[2020 Summer Olympics]]. Antuna won the [[bronze medal]] with the Olympic team.<ref>{{cite web|title=Revelan lista oficial de la Selección Sub-23 que acudirá a Tokio 2020|date=5 July 2021|url=https://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/selecciones/nota/_/id/8854207/mexico-sub23-convocatoria-jaime-lozano-tokio-2020}}</ref>

===Senior===
On 3 November 2017, [[Juan Carlos Osorio]] named Antuna to the [[Mexico national football team|senior national team]] for the friendly matches against [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]] and [[Poland national football team|Poland]] on 10 and 13 November but did not appear in either match.<ref>{{cite web |title=Uriel Antuna, Omar Govea get first Mexico call-ups for friendlies |url=http://www.espnfc.com/mexico/story/3257343/uriel-antuna-and-omar-govea-get-first-mexico-call-ups-for-november-friendlies |website=ESPN FC |date=3 November 2017 |access-date=3 November 2017}}</ref>

In May 2019, Antuna was included in [[Gerardo Martino]]'s preliminary list for the [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup|CONCACAF Gold Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Marco Fabián vuelve y no está Tecatito; anuncian lista previa de México para Copa Oro|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/seleccion-mayor/seleccion-mexicana-prelista-copa-oro-2019-tata-martino|publisher=Mediotiempo.com|first=Enrique|last=Martínez Villar|date=14 May 2019}}</ref> He made his debut on 5 June in a friendly against [[Venezuela national football team|Venezuela]], appearing as a 68th minute substitute for [[Roberto Alvarado]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[ESPN]]|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/match?gameId=536881|title=Mexico v Venezuela game report|date=5 June 2019}}</ref> After originally being left out of the definite list for the Gold Cup, he was included after [[Jorge Sánchez (footballer, born 1997)|Jorge Sánchez]] picked up an injury in a friendly match against [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Jorge Sánchez se pierde la Copa Oro; a Moreno lo esperarán|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/seleccion-mayor/copa-oro-2019-jorge-sanchez-baja-uriel-antuna-reemplazara|publisher=Mediotiempo.com|first=Enrique|last=Martínez Villar|date=14 June 2019}}</ref> In Mexico's opening match against [[Cuba national football team|Cuba]], Antuna scored a hat-trick and provided an assist in the team's 7–0 rout;<ref>{{cite web|publisher=Goal|url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/antuna-hat-trick-shows-mexico-still-deep-enough-to-roll-in/p1q821co0jfr1rqm6d2jbt39v|title=Uriel Antuna's Hat trick|date=15 June 2019}}</ref> his first goal at the 2nd minute of the match is the fastest goal scored by Mexico in a [[CONCACAF Gold Cup|Gold Cup tournament]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Gol de Uriel Antuna impone récord para México|url=https://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/copa-oro/nota/_/id/5736280/gol-de-uriel-antuna-impone-r%C3%A9cord-para-m%C3%A9xico|work=ESPN Deportes|language=es|date=15 June 2019}}</ref> He appeared in every match as Mexico would go on to win the tournament.<ref name="sports">{{cite web|title=Mexico Turns Tide, Wins Gold Cup Title Again vs. Wasteful USMNT|url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2019/07/07/mexico-usa-gold-cup-title-dos-santos-pizarro-pulisic-berhalter|work=[[Sports Illustrated]]|first=Brian|last=Straus|date=7 July 2019}}</ref>

On 19 November, in Mexico's final [[2019–20 CONCACAF Nations League A|Nations League]] group stage match against [[Bermuda national football team|Bermuda]], Antuna scored the winning goal in injury time to secure a 2–1 win, maintaining Mexico's perfect group-stage record.<ref>{{cite web|title=Uriel Antuna's Injury-Time Winner Saves Mexico vs Bermuda in 2019 Nations League|url=https://bleacherreport.com/articles/2863449-uriel-antunas-injury-time-winner-saves-mexico-vs-bermuda-in-2019-nations-league|first=Joseph|last=Zucker|work=Bleacher Report|date=19 November 2019}}</ref>

In October 2022, Antuna was named in Mexico's preliminary 31-man [[Mexico national football team|squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], and in November, he was ultimately included in the final 26-man roster.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lista de convocados de la Selección Mexicana al Mundial de Qatar 2022|url=https://www.tvazteca.com/aztecadeportes/lista-oficial-convocados-seleccion-mexicana-mundial-qatar-2022|work=Tvazteca.com|first=Omar|last=Moro|date=14 November 2022|language=Spanish}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Antuna is an agile player with sheer acceleration and speed who can [[Dribbling#Association football|dribble]] past opponents,<ref>{{cite web|title=LA Galaxy winger Uriel Antuna added to Mexico Gold Cup roster|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2019/06/15/la-galaxy-winger-uriel-antuna-added-mexico-gold-cup-roster|publisher=Mlssoccer.com|date=14 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Another LA Galaxy player to perform in the Gold Cup|url=https://en.as.com/en/2019/06/16/football/1560641899_504531.html|publisher=En.as.com|first=Ana Paulina|last=Maupomé|date=16 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Uriel Antuna's meteoric rise that almost didn't happen, Guardado's growing legacy and more|url=https://www.fmfstateofmind.com/2019/6/20/18693386/uriel-antuna-andres-guardado-gerardo-tata-martino-rodolfo-pizarro-hector-moreno-mexico-tri-gold-cup|publisher=FMF State of Mind|first=Antonio|last=Tinajero|date=20 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Who Is LA Galaxy's Uriel Antuna?|url=https://www.lafbnetwork.com/who-is-la-galaxys-uriel-antuna/|first=Chanie|last=Franco|publisher=Lafbnetwork.com|date=22 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title='Charly' Rodriguez, 'JJ' Macias emerge as Mexico stars for 2020|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/mexico-mex/story/4019781/charly-rodriguez-and-jj-macias-emerge-as-mexico-stars-for-2020|publisher=ESPN|first=Tom|last=Marshall|date=1 January 2020}}</ref> with [[ESPN]] describing his [[work rate]] as "intriguing" yet "unrefined."<ref>{{cite web|title=Dos Santos, Guardado 8/10 as Mexico keeps rolling|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/club/mexico/203/blog/post/3872527/dos-santosguardado-8-10-as-mexico-keeps-rolling|publisher=ESPN|first=Cesar|last=Hernandez|date=24 October 2019}}</ref> Described as a player that likes to hold on to the ball and cross, he is fouled on often.<ref>{{cite web|title=Uriel Antuna Football Statistics|url=https://www.whoscored.com/Players/344696/Show/Uriel-Antuna|website=Whoscored.com}}</ref>

During a [[Guardianes 2020 Liga MX final phase|Guardianes 2020 quarter-finals]] match against [[Club América]], his speed throughout the encounter was recorded as one of the fastest footballers in the world, second to [[Kylian Mbappe]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Uriel Antuna, muy cerca de Kylian Mbappe como el jugador más rápido del mundo|url=https://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/mexico/nota/_/id/7806996/antuna-cerca-de-mbappe-jugador-mas-rapido|publisher=ESPN Deportes|language=es|date=26 November 2020}}</ref>
