'''Antoine Serlom Semenyo''' (born 7 January 2000) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|striker]] for [[Premier League]] side [[AFC Bournemouth]] and the [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national team]].

==Club career==
===Bristol City===
In 2017, Semenyo signed for [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]], after impressing for [[South Gloucestershire and Stroud College]]'s football academy.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.sgscol.ac.uk/news/CitysignSGSstudentAntoineSemenyo |title=City sign SGS student Antoine Semenyo |publisher=South Gloucestershire and Stroud College  |accessdate=24 November 2022 }}</ref> Semenyo made his Bristol City debut on the final day of the [[2017‚Äì18 Bristol City F.C. season|2017‚Äì18]] season coming on as a second-half substitute for [[Lloyd Kelly]] in the 3‚Äì2 loss to [[Sheffield United F.C.|Sheffield United]] at [[Ashton Gate Stadium|Ashton Gate]].<ref name="BBC Football">{{cite web |title=Bristol City 2‚Äì3 Sheffield United|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/43938863|publisher=BBC Football|date=6 May 2018|accessdate=10 May 2018}}</ref> Semenyo was allocated shirt number 18 for Bristol City and he was named as a substitute for the Championship away match against [[Blackburn Rovers F.C.|Blackburn Rovers]] on 9 February 2019. On 27 April 2019, Semenyo controversially received his first career red card against [[Derby County F.C.|Derby County]] for a challenge on [[Tom Huddlestone]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/47994358|title=Bristol City 0‚Äì2 Derby County: Lawrence and Bogle boost Rams' play-off hopes|date=27 April 2019|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> In June 2019, he signed a new four-year contract with the club.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/48756475|title=Antoine Semenyo: Bristol City forward signs new four-year deal|date=25 June 2019|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> On 5 September 2020 he scored his first goal for Bristol City in an [[EFL Cup]] tie against [[Exeter City]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/53955628|title=Bristol City 2‚Äì0 Exeter |publisher=BBC|date=5 September 2020 |accessdate=5 September 2020}}</ref> 
Semenyo played consistently for [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]] in the [[2020‚Äì21 Bristol City F.C. season|2020‚Äì21]] season playing fifty competitive games also scoring five and assisting seven goals.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://footballleagueworld.co.uk/what-is-the-current-situation-with-antoine-semenyo-at-bristol-city-we-take-a-look/|title=What is the current situation with Antoine Semenyo at Bristol City? We take a look|website=footballleagueworld.co.uk|first=Ned|last=Holmes|date=22 July 2021|access-date=9 October 2021}}</ref>

Semenyo won the [[EFL Championship Player of the Month]] award for January 2022 after scoring three goals and getting three assists.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.bcfc.co.uk/news/viva-semenyo-player-of-the-month/#:~:text=Antoine%20Semenyo%20has%20been%20voted,Rangers%20attacking%20midfielder%20Chris%20Willock. | title=üèÜ Viva Semenyo ‚Äì Player of the Month }}</ref> He finished the season with 8 goals and 12 assists in 32 games in all competitions, the best season of his career so far.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.skysports.com/football/story-telling/11095/12651420/championship-tyrese-campbell-daryl-dike-among-25-of-the-most-exciting-talents-under-the-age-of-25|title=Championship: 25 under 25|publisher= Sky Sports}}</ref>

====Newport County (loan)====
On 18 July 2018, Semenyo joined [[Newport County A.F.C.|Newport County]] on loan until the end of the 2018‚Äì19 season.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44874548 |title=Newport County sign Semenyo on loan from Bristol City |publisher=BBC Sport |date=18 July 2018 |accessdate=18 July 2018}}</ref> He made his Newport debut in a 3‚Äì0 defeat against [[Mansfield Town F.C.|Mansfield Town]] on 4 August 2018 as a second-half substitute.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44989421 |title=Mansfield Town 3‚Äì0 Newport County |publisher=BBC Sport |date=4 August 2018 |accessdate=4 August 2018}}</ref> On 14 August 2018, he scored his first goal for Newport in a 4‚Äì1 win over [[Cambridge United F.C.|Cambridge United]] in the [[EFL Cup]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45092718|title=Carabao Cup: Amond double helps Newport hammer Cambridge 4‚Äì1|date=14 August 2018|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref>

He was recalled by Bristol City on 28 January 2019.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/47024019 |title=Bristol City recall Antoine Semenyo from Newport County loan |publisher=BBC Sport |date=28 January 2019 |accessdate=28 January 2019 }}</ref>

====Sunderland (loan)====
On January deadline day 2020, Semenyo joined [[EFL League One|League One]] side [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]] on a sixth-month loan deal until the end of the 2019‚Äì20 season.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.sunderlandecho.com/sport/football/sunderland-afc/phil-parkinson-and-antoine-semenyo-react-youngster-joins-sunderland-and-goes-straight-squad-portsmouth-clash-1382156 |title=Phil Parkinson and Antoine Semenyo react as youngster joins Sunderland and goes straight into squad for Portsmouth clash |last=Smith |first=Phil |publisher=[[Sunderland Echo]] |date=31 January 2020 |accessdate=1 February 2020}}</ref>

===AFC Bournemouth===
On 27 January 2023, [[AFC Bournemouth]] completed a deal worth ¬£10 million for Semenyo on a four-and-a-half-year deal.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/64432637|title=Antoine Semenyo: Bournemouth sign Ghana forward from Bristol City|website=BBC Sport|date=27 January 2023}}</ref> He scored his first league goal on 30 April, 2023 in a 4-1 home win over [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=Fisher |first=Ben |date=2023-04-30 |title=Leeds woes deepen as Jefferson Lerma takes Bournemouth away from danger |language=en-GB |work=The Guardian |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2023/apr/30/bournemouth-leeds-premier-league-match-report |access-date=2023-04-30 |issn=0261-3077}}</ref>

==International career==
Born in England, Semenyo is of Ghanaian descent.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ghanasportsonline.com/2019/08/performance-of-ghanaian-players-abroad-yartey-sparkles-ache-strikes-as-antwi-hits-brace-as-20-players-score-23-goals-abroad/|title=Performance of Ghanaian Players Abroad: Yartey sparkles, Ache strikes as Antwi hits brace as 20 players score 23 goals abroad ‚Äì Ghana Sports Online|date=12 August 2019}}</ref> He debuted with the [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national team]] in a 3‚Äì0 [[2023 Africa Cup of Nations qualification]] win over [[Madagascar national football team|Madagascar]] on 1 June 2022.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2022/06/01/africa/africa-cup-of-nations-qualification/ghana/madagascar/3783008/ | title=Ghana vs. Madagascar ‚Äì 1 June 2022 ‚Äì Soccerway }}</ref> On 14 November 2022, Semenyo was included in Ghana's 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/africa/63576436| title=World Cup 2022: Jojo Wollacott ruled out of Ghana squad after finger injury| work=BBC Sport}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Semenyo's father, Larry, is a former footballer, having played as a midfielder for [[Okwawu United S.C.|Okwawu United]] in the [[Ghana Premier League]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://footy-ghana.com/2022/05/photos-antoine-semenyo-models-in-ghana-kit-to-mark-debut-black-stars-call-up/ |title=PHOTOS: Antoine Semenyo models in Ghana kit to mark debut Black Stars call-up |publisher=Footy Ghana |date=26 May 2022 |accessdate=24 November 2022 }}</ref>
