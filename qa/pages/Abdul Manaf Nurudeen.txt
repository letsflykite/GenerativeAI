'''Abdul Manaf Nurudeen''' (born 8 February 1999) is a Ghanaian professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Belgian Pro League]] club [[K.A.S. Eupen|Eupen]] and the [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national team]].

==Club career==
As a youth player, Manaf Nurudeen joined the Senegalese [[Aspire Academy]].

In 2017, he signed for [[K.A.S. Eupen|Eupen]] in Belgium. On 7 November 2020, he debuted for Eupen during a 1–1 draw with [[Waasland-Beveren]].<ref>{{Soccerway|489059}}</ref>

Manaf Nurudeen wears a [[Helmet|goalkeeper helmet.]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lavenir.net/cnt/dmf20170721_01032931/mon-casque-ce-n-est-pas-pour-imiter-petr-cech-explique-abdul-manaf-nurudeen-as-eupen|title="Mon casque, ce n'est pas pour imiter Petr Cech…" explique Abdul Manaf Nurudeen (AS Eupen)|publisher=lavenir.net}}</ref>

==International career==
Manaf Nurudeen represented Ghana at the [[2019 Africa U-20 Cup of Nations]].

He was first called up to the [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national team]] in September 2021 for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – CAF second round|World Cup qualifiers]] against Ethiopia and South Africa, and remained on the bench in these games.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[FIFA]]|url=https://www.fifa.com/tournaments/mens/worldcup/qatar2022/qualifiers/caf/match-center/400198013|title=Ghana v Ethiopia game report|date=3 September 2021}}</ref>

Nurudeen was part of the Ghanaian team in the [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations]] that was eliminated at the group stage of the competition.<ref>{{Cite web|date=30 December 2021|title=AFCON 2021: Edmund Addo, Manaf Nurudeen join Black Stars camp in Doha|url=https://citisportsonline.com/2021/12/30/afcon-2021-edmund-addo-manaf-nurudeen-join-black-stars-camp-in-doha/|access-date=26 January 2022|website=Citi Sports Online|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|title=Ghana dumped out of Afcon by Comoros|language=en-GB|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/59947164|access-date=26 January 2022}}</ref> He debuted with Ghana in a 3–0 friendly loss to [[Algeria national football team|Algeria]] on 5 January 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ca.soccerway.com/matches/2022/01/05/world/friendlies/algeria/ghana/3707457/|title=Algeria vs. Ghana - 5 January 2022 - Soccerway|website=ca.soccerway.com}}</ref>
