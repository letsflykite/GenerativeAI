'''Kalvin Mark Phillips''' (born 2 December 1995) is an English professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for {{English football updater|ManchesC}} club [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] and the [[England national football team|England national team]].

A [[Leeds United F.C. Reserves and Youth Team|Leeds United academy]] graduate, Phillips made his first-team debut in 2015. In the [[2019–20 Leeds United F.C. season|2019–20 season]], he was a member of the Leeds team coached by [[Marcelo Bielsa]] that won the [[EFL Championship|Championship]]. Phillips signed for Manchester City in 2022 and won a continental treble of the [[2022–23 Premier League]], the [[2022–23 FA Cup]], and the [[2022–23 UEFA Champions League]] in his first season with the club.

Phillips made his international debut for the England national team in 2020, and was part of the England team that finished as runners-up at [[UEFA Euro 2020]], and played at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Early life==
Phillips was born in [[Leeds]], West Yorkshire.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.worldfootball.net/player_summary/kalvin-phillips/ |title=Kalvin Phillips: Profile |website=worldfootball.net |publisher=HEIM:SPIEL |access-date=10 September 2020}}</ref> He attended [[the Farnley Academy]] during his secondary school years.<ref>{{Cite web|date=8 September 2020|title=Leeds United's Kalvin Phillips nets proud message from former school after England debut|url=http://westleedsdispatch.com/leeds-uniteds-kalvin-phillips-nets-proud-message-from-former-school-after-england-debut/|access-date=23 February 2021|website=West Leeds Dispatch}}</ref> Phillips was one of four children and was raised in poverty while his mother worked two jobs and his father was in prison.<ref>{{cite news |title=Leeds and England star Kalvin Phillips reveals father's jail time and pays tribute to mother who would often go starving at night as midfielder gets match-winning assist in Euro 2020 opener |url=https://talksport.com/football/789373/leeds-england-kalvin-phillips-father-mum-influence/ |access-date=16 June 2021 |publisher=Talksport |date=20 March 2023}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Leeds United===
====Early career====
[[File:Kalvin Phillips Leeds United.jpg|thumb|upright|Phillips with [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]] in 2015]]

Phillips played for local club Wortley from 2003 to 2010 before joining [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]]'s academy aged 14.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://westleedsdispatch.com/from-wortley-fc-to-england-leeds-uniteds-kalvin-phillips-nets-england-call-up/ |title=From Wortley FC to England – Leeds United's Kalvin Phillips nets England call-up |website=West Leeds Dispatch |date=25 August 2020 |access-date=14 September 2020}}<br />{{cite news |url=https://www.yorkshireeveningpost.co.uk/sport/grandma-buzzing-family-integral-kalvin-phillips-leeds-united-success-story-823551 |title=Grandma 'buzzing' with family integral to Kalvin Phillips' Leeds United success story |first=Lee |last=Sobot |newspaper=Yorkshire Evening Post |location=Leeds |date=13 September 2019 |access-date=14 September 2020}}</ref> He progressed through the club's youth ranks and signed his first professional deal with the club in the summer of 2014, retaining him for the [[2014–15 Leeds United F.C. season|2014–15]] season.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.leedsunited.com/news/team-news/2418/united-youngsters-sign-up |title=United youngsters sign up |publisher=Leeds United F.C. |date=9 July 2014 |access-date=20 April 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.yorkshireeveningpost.co.uk/sport/football/leeds-united/leeds-united-rising-star-to-commit-to-whites-1-7289683 |title=Leeds United: Rising star to commit to Whites |newspaper=Yorkshire Evening Post |date=3 June 2015 |access-date=20 April 2018}}</ref>

During the 2014–15 season, Phillips became the captain of the development and under-18 teams. Phillips joined the first team for the trip to [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]] in the [[2014–15 FA Cup|FA Cup]] third round on 4 January 2015; he was unused substitute in a 1–0 defeat. On 30 March, academy coach [[Jason Blunt]] said he felt Phillips would become a future fans favourite.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.yorkshireeveningpost.co.uk/sport/leeds-united/latest-whites-news/leeds-united-phillips-can-become-a-real-fans-favourite-1-7182469 |title=Leeds United: Phillips can become a real fans' favourite |newspaper=Yorkshire Evening Post |date=30 March 2015 |access-date=30 March 2015}}</ref> Phillips made further appearances on the bench in league matches over the following months, and made his professional debut against [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]] in 4–3 defeat on 6 April.<ref>{{cite news |title=Wolverhampton Wanderers v Leeds United |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/32111079 |website=BBC Sport |date=6 April 2015}}</ref> Along with [[Sam Byram]], [[Charlie Taylor (footballer, born 1993)|Charlie Taylor]], [[Alex Mowatt]] and [[Lewis Cook (footballer, born 1997)|Lewis Cook]], Phillips was the latest graduate of a promising group of academy players to regularly feature under former youth manager [[Neil Redfearn]] in the [[2014–15 Leeds United F.C. season|2014–15 season]]. On 11 April, he retained his position in the Leeds starting 11, making his home debut against [[Cardiff City F.C.|Cardiff City]] at [[Elland Road]], and marked the match with his first senior goal in only his second appearance, although the match finished in a 2–1 defeat.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.yorkshireeveningpost.co.uk/sport/leeds-united/latest-whites-news/leeds-united-1-cardiff-city-2-phillips-goal-a-rare-highlight-in-defeat-1-7205759 |title=Leeds United 1 Cardiff City 2: Phillips goal a rare highlight in defeat |newspaper=Yorkshire Evening Post |date=12 April 2015 |access-date=12 April 2015}}</ref>

At the end of the season, he signed a new two-year contract.<ref name="2015offer" /><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/33021402 |title=Kalvin Philips: Leeds United midfielder signs new deal |website=BBC Sport |date=5 June 2015 |access-date=20 April 2018}}</ref> On 31 July 2015, Phillips was given the number 27 shirt for the upcoming [[2015–16 Leeds United F.C. season|2015–16]] season.<ref>{{cite web |publisher=Leeds United F.C. |date=31 July 2015 |access-date=31 July 2015 |title=2015/16 squad numbers announced |url=http://www.leedsunited.com/news/article/13qz1j2snco7s1ip1xc6j5mk2b/title/201516-squad-numbers-announced}}</ref> However, with Redfearn replaced as head coach during the summer, Phillips made just 10 league appearances over the course of the season under head coaches [[Uwe Rösler]] and then [[Steve Evans (footballer, born 1962)|Steve Evans]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.soccerbase.com/players/player.sd?player_id=80307&season_id=145 |title=Games played by Kalvin Phillips in 2015/16 |publisher=Soccerbase |accessdate=2 April 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/34570202#:~:text=Steve%20Evans%20guided%20Rotherham%20to%20successive%20promotions%20from,head%20coach%2C%20replacing%20the%20sacked%20Uwe%20Rosler.%20Evans%2C |title=Steve Evans: Leeds appoint new boss as Massimo Cellino banned |publisher=BBC Sport |date=19 October 2015 |accessdate=2 April 2021}}</ref>

On 8 February 2021, Phillips made his 200th appearance<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/55882704|title=Leeds United 2 Crystal Palace 0|website=BBC Sport|access-date=17 February 2021}}</ref> for Leeds in a 2–0 win over [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]], joining a select group of just 70 players<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.lufctalk.com/stats/club-200/|title=Leeds United Club 200|website=LUFCTALK STATS & HISTORY|access-date=17 February 2021}}</ref> that have made over 200 appearances for the club, including then current teammates [[Stuart Dallas]] and [[Liam Cooper]] who also hit the milestone that season.

In the last game of the season on 23 May 2021, Phillips scored his first Premier League goal, a free kick in Leeds' 3–1 win over West Bromwich Albion.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/57132105|title=Leeds United 3–1 West Bromwich Albion: Hosts end season with fourth straight win|website=BBC Sport|access-date=23 May 2021}}</ref>

===Manchester City===
Phillips signed for Premier League champions [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] on 4 July 2022 on a six-year contract, for a reported initial fee of £42 million, potentially rising to £45&nbsp;million in add-ons.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11661/12640261/kalvin-phillips-man-city-complete-transfer-of-leeds-united-midfielder |title=Kalvin Phillips: Man City complete transfer of Leeds United midfielder |website=Sky Sports |date=4 July 2022 |access-date=4 July 2022}}</ref> On 7 August, Phillips made his league debut after coming on as a substitute for [[Rodri (footballer, born 1996)|Rodri]] in a 2–0 away win over [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]].<ref>{{cite news|last=McNulty|first=Phil|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/62366384|title=West Ham 0–2 Man City: Erling Haaland scores twice as Man City turn on style|publisher=BBC Sport|date=7 August 2022|access-date=8 August 2022}}</ref> Phillips though suffered from injury problems and underwent surgery on his shoulder in September.<ref>{{Cite magazine |last=Burton |first=Harri |title=Surgery Update On Manchester City's Kalvin Phillips |url=https://www.si.com/soccer/manchestercity/news/surgery-update-on-manchester-citys-kalvin-phillips |access-date=2022-12-24 |magazine=Sports Illustrated|date=27 September 2022 }}</ref> When he returned from the World Cup in mid December 2022, manager [[Pep Guardiola]] commented that Phillips was overweight.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Phillips 'overweight' after World Cup – Guardiola |website=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/64081762 |access-date=2022-12-24}}</ref>

On 11 January 2023, Phillips started his first match for Manchester City, a 2–0 loss at [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] in the [[2022-23 EFL Cup|EFL Cup]] quarter-final.<ref>{{Cite news|title= Mara and Djenepo strike early to take Southampton past Manchester City|website=The Guardian|url= https://www.theguardian.com/football/2023/jan/11/southampton-manchester-city-carabao-cup-quarter-final-match-report|access-date=2023-05-22}}</ref> He made his first Premier League start in City’s final home game of the season, a 1–0 win over [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] on 21 May. <ref>{{Cite news|title= Phillips 'enjoyed every moment' moment of City win|website=Manchester City F.C.|url= https://www.mancity.com/news/mens/phillips-post-chelsea-reaction-pl-21-may-63820287 |access-date=2023-05-22}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:Kalvin Phillips 2022-11-21 1.jpg|thumb|upright|Phillips with [[England national football team|England]] at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]]]]

Phillips was eligible to play for his country of birth, [[England national football team|England]], as well as [[Jamaica national football team|Jamaica]] and the [[Republic of Ireland national football team|Republic of Ireland]] through his heritage.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://rjrnewsonline.com/sports/england-born-players-could-represent-reggae-boyz-despite-english-fa-interest |title=England-born Players Could Represent Reggae Boyz Despite English FA Interest |website=Jamaican News Online}}</ref> He ultimately chose to represent England and was called by [[Gareth Southgate]] for the first time in August 2020, before receiving his first cap on 8 September versus [[Denmark national football team|Denmark]] in the [[2020–21 UEFA Nations League A#Group 2|UEFA Nations League]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/53892235|title=England call-ups for Harry Maguire, Phil Foden, Mason Greenwood and Kalvin Phillips|date=25 August 2019|website=BBC Sport|access-date=25 August 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/53980518 |title=Denmark 0–0 England |website=BBC Sport |date=8 September 2020}}</ref> He was only the third player in the 21st century to represent England prior to making an appearance in the top division of a country, after [[Jack Butland]] and [[Wilfried Zaha]]. In an interview after being called up, Phillips spoke of his intention to give his first England shirt to Marcelo Bielsa after the Leeds United manager handed him a playing shirt from his old club [[Newell's Old Boys]], along with a message to him and his family to commemorate his first international call-up.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://talksport.com/football/755327/leeds-phillips-bielsa-gesture-england/ |title=CLASSY: Leeds ace Kalvin Phillips reveals plan to make incredible Marcelo Bielsa gesture after first England call-up |website=Talksport |date=6 September 2020}}</ref>

the world's press for his combative, dynamic displays.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kalvin Phillips earns huge praise from supporters after Germany showing|url=https://www.yorkshireeveningpost.co.uk/sport/football/leeds-united/utter-quality-leeds-united-and-england-fans-pile-huge-praise-on-kalvin-phillips-after-germany-showing-3290860|access-date=7 July 2021|website=www.yorkshireeveningpost.co.uk}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last=Smith |first=Rory |date=5 July 2021|title=No Semifinalist Is an Island|newspaper=The New York ==Style of play==
After starting his career as a [[Box-to-box midfielder|box-to-box]] or [[central midfielder]],<ref name="leeds-live.co.uk">{{cite web |url=https://www.leeds-live.co.uk/sport/leeds-united/kalvin-phillips-marcelo-bielsa-leeds-15508583 |title=The evolution of Kalvin Phillips under Marcelo Bielsa – in their own words |date=6 December 2018 |access-date=2 July 2019 |website=Leeds Live}}</ref> Phillips was converted into a defensive midfielder by Marcelo Bielsa before the start of the 2018–19 season.<ref name="leeds-live.co.uk"/> He is known for his range of short sharp passing, long ball distribution and ability to start attacks whilst covering his defensive duties, with him described as a 'lynchpin'.<ref name="JOE URQUHART"/>

Marcelo Bielsa described his style "He's very good at getting the ball and putting it into another space, a better space. He's very good when he has to cover the team when our full-backs go in attack. And when we are outnumbered, he is very good with his defending."<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.yorkshireeveningpost.co.uk/sport/football/leeds-united/leeds-united-latest/leeds-united-midfielder-phillips-is-lynchpin-of-the-bielsa-revolution-1-9306238 |title=Leeds United: Midfielder Phillips is lynchpin of the Bielsa revolution |date=17 August 2018 |access-date=2 July 2019 |newspaper=Yorkshire Evening Post}}</ref> Phillips was also converted into playing as a [[Defender (association football)#Centre-back|centre back]] under Bielsa.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.yorkshireeveningpost.co.uk/sport/football/leeds-united/leeds-united-latest/kalvin-phillips-can-add-a-new-dimension-to-leeds-united-s-defence-1-9278531 |title=Kalvin Phillips can add a new dimension to Leeds United's defence |date=1 August 2018 |access-date=2 July 2019 |newspaper=Yorkshire Evening Post}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.yorkshireeveningpost.co.uk/sport/football/leeds-united/leeds-united-latest/pontus-jansson-picture-puts-leeds-united-centre-back-situation-back-under-microscope-1-9864863 |title=Pontus Jansson picture puts Leeds United centre-back situation back under microscope |date=8 July 2019 |newspaper=Yorkshire Evening Post |author=Lee Sobot |access-date=8 July 2019}}</ref>
