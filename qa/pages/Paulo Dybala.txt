'''Paulo Bruno Exequiel Dybala''' (born 15 November 1993) is an Argentine professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as an [[Attacking Midfielder|attacking midfielder]] or [[Forward (association football)|winger]] for [[Serie A]] club [[A.S. Roma|Roma]] and the [[Argentina national football team|Argentina national team]]. He is known for his exceptional dribbling ability, quick feet, and an eye for goal.<ref>{{cite web |title=13 fun facts about Paulo Dybala|url=https://www.goal.com/en-in/lists/13-fun-facts-about-paulo-dybala/bltf33d6634a8ef98ca}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=The top 15 best dribblers in the world of football right now 2023|url=https://urfootball.com/the-best-dribblers-in-the-world/}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=FIFA Rating|url=https://www.fifacm.com/player/211110/paulo-dybala#:~:text=Paulo%20Dybala%20Rating%20is%2086,Italy%20Serie%20A%20(1).}}</ref>

Nicknamed "''La Joya''" ("The Jewel") in his prime,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lmcordoba.com.ar/nota.php?ni=71388|title=Los maestros de la 'joya'|trans-title=The "Jewel's" teachers|publisher=La Mañana Cordoba|author1=Marcos Villalobo|language=es|date=10 October 2011|access-date=1 June 2015|archive-url=https://archive.today/20121230031329/http://www.lmcordoba.com.ar/nota.php?ni=71388|archive-date=30 December 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> Dybala began his senior club career in 2011 playing for [[Instituto Atlético Central Córdoba|Instituto de Córdoba]], before signing for [[S.S.D. Palermo|Palermo]] in 2012, at age 18, where he won a [[Serie B]] title. In 2015, Dybala signed for [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]] and won five league titles and four [[Coppa Italia|Coppas Italia]]. He was also voted [[Serie A Awards|Serie A Most Valuable Player]] once, was also included in the [[Serie A Team of the Year]] four times, and ranks as Juventus' [[List of Juventus F.C. records and statistics#All-time top 10 goalscorers|ninth-highest all-time goalscorer]]. In 2022, he joined Roma.

Dybala made his senior international debut for Argentina in 2015 at age 21, and has since earned over 30 caps, including appearing at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]], [[2019 Copa América]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]], winning the latter.

==Club career==
===Instituto de Córdoba===
Nicknamed "''La Joya''"<ref name="dybala_lajoya">{{cite news |url=http://www.ole.com.ar/instituto/Joya-bien_0_674932859.html |title=La Joya está bien |publisher=Olè |language=es |date=2 April 2012 |access-date=1 July 2012}}</ref> or "''El pibe de la pensión''",<ref name="dybala_elpibedelapension">{{cite news |url=http://www.lmcordoba.com.ar/nota.php?ni=64071 |publisher=I'm Cordoba |language=es |title=El pibe de la pensión |date=1 August 2012 |access-date=23 October 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402181712/http://www.lmcordoba.com.ar/nota.php?ni=64071 |archive-date=2 April 2015 }}</ref> Dybala made his professional debut in the [[Primera B Nacional]] (Argentine second division) with his hometown club [[Instituto Atlético Central Córdoba]] at age 17. In total, he played 40 matches with the club, scoring 17 goals.<ref name=SW/><ref name="dybala_player2">{{cite news |url=http://www.institutoatleticocentralcordoba.com/index.php?option=com_joomleague&func=showPlayer&p=2&pid=59&Itemid= |publisher=Instituto Atletico Central Cordoba |language=es |title=Paulo Dybala player 2 |date=1 August 2012 |access-date=23 October 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110915051053/http://www.institutoatleticocentralcordoba.com/index.php?option=com_joomleague&func=showPlayer&p=2&pid=59&Itemid= |archive-date=15 September 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He was the youngest to score a goal, beating the record of [[Mario Kempes]]. Dybala was also the first to play 38 consecutive matches in a professional league in the country (again edging Kempes), and was also the first to score two hat-tricks in a season. Dybala also scored in six consecutive games, surpassing the previous record of four matches.<ref name="dybala_record">{{cite news |url=http://mundod.lavoz.com.ar/futbol/dybala-pibe-records |title=Dybala, el pibe de los récords |trans-title=Dybala, the kid of Records |publisher=MundoD |language=es |date=27 March 2012 |access-date=27 March 2012 }}</ref>

===Palermo===
[[File:Paulo Dybala - 2015 - US Città di Palermo (corner kick).jpg|thumb|Dybala taking a corner for [[U.S. Città di Palermo|Palermo]] in 2015]]

On 29 April 2012, [[U.S. Città di Palermo]] president [[Maurizio Zamparini]] announced the signing of Dybala stating, "We have got Paulo Dybala – the new [[Sergio Agüero]]."<ref name="dybala_aguero">{{cite news |url=http://www.corrieredellosport.it/serie_a/palermo/2012/04/28-236116/Zamparini%3A+%C2%ABPreso+Dybala.+%C3%88+il+nuovo+Aguero%C2%BB |title=Zamparini: "Preso il nuovo Aguero" |publisher=Corriere dello Sport |language=it |date=28 April 2012 |access-date=1 June 2016 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304114849/http://www.corrieredellosport.it/serie_a/palermo/2012/04/28-236116/Zamparini:+%C2%ABPreso+Dybala.+%C3%88+il+nuovo+Aguero%C2%BB |archive-date=4 March 2016 }}</ref> Later the same day, however, Instituto's general secretary José Teaux stated that the man who had completed the negotiations with Palermo did not have the mandate to sell Dybala. Nonetheless, on 20 July 2012 Palermo released a press announcement confirming the signing of Dybala, who signed a four-year deal with the Sicilian club.<ref name="dybala_signed">{{cite news |url=http://palermocalcio.it/it/1213/news/scheda.php?id=26948 |publisher=US Città di Palermo |language=it |title=DYBALA HA FIRMATO |trans-title=DYBALA SIGNED |date=20 July 2012 |access-date=20 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120722191623/http://palermocalcio.it/it/1213/news/scheda.php?id=26948 |archive-date=22 July 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> According to the financial filing of the club, the transfer fee was €8.64&nbsp;million.<ref>U.S. Città di Palermo S.p.A. ''bilancio'' (financial report and accounts) on 30 June 2013, PDF purchased from Italian [[Camera di Commercio, Industria, Agricoltura e Artigianato|C.C.I.A.A.]] (in Italian)</ref>

Dybala made his debut for the club in a [[Serie A]] match against [[S.S. Lazio|Lazio]]. He scored his first and second goal in Italy on 11 November 2012 when Palermo defeated [[U.C. Sampdoria|Sampdoria]] at home, 2–0.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.legaseriea.it/en/serie-a-tim/match-report/-/match-report/Palermo-Sampdoria/47894 |title=Palermo 2 - 0 Sampdoria - legaseriea.it |website=www.legaseriea.it |access-date=17 January 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130205162720/http://www.legaseriea.it/en/serie-a-tim/match-report/-/match-report/Palermo-Sampdoria/47894 |archive-date=5 February 2013 |url-status=dead}}</ref> Dybala had his breakthrough season in the [[2014–15 Serie A]] where he scored ten goals in the first half of the season, forming a successful striking partnership with fellow Argentine–Italian [[Franco Vázquez]] and being linked with several top European clubs.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://eurosport.yahoo.com/football/serie-a/top-goalscorers.html|title=Football - Serie A - Standing - Top Scorers - 2014-2015 - - Yahoo! Eurosport|work=yahoo.com|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141228193641/https://eurosport.yahoo.com/football/serie-a/top-goalscorers.html|archive-date=28 December 2014}}</ref> He finished the season with 13 goals and 10 assists, which made him one of the top assist providers in the league.<ref name="espnfc.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.com/italian-serie-a/12/statistics/assists?season=2014|title=Serie A 2014-15 Statistics: Assists|publisher=ESPN FC|access-date=1 June 2015}}</ref>

===Juventus===
====2015–2018: Transfer and three domestic doubles====

====2018–2022: Struggles and return to form====
[[File:Juventus vs. FC Zenit Saint Petersburg, 2 November 2021 34.jpg|thumb|Dybala in action for Juventus in 2021]]
On 1 September 2018, Dybala made his 100th Serie A appearance for Juventus, coming on as a late second-half substitute in a 2–1 away win over [[Parma F. C.|Parma]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://sport.sky.it/calcio/serie-a/2018/08/30/serie-a-calendario-orari-come-vedere-terza-giornata.html |title=Calendario Serie A, la terza giornata: orari, programma partite e come vederle |publisher=Sky.it |language=it |date=30 August 2018 |access-date=2 September 2018 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.gazzetta.it/Calcio/Serie-A/01-09-2018/parma-juventus-1-2-gol-mandzukic-gervinho-matuidi-290650448057.shtml |title=Parma-Juventus 1-2: gol di Mandzukic, Gervinho e Matuidi |publisher=La Gazzetta dello Sport |language=it |author1=Luca Bianchin |date=1 September 2018 |access-date=2 September 2018 }}</ref> On 2 October, Dybala scored a [[hat-trick]] in a 3–0 home win over [[BSC Young Boys|Young Boys]] in the [[2018–19 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/128798/dybala-i-wanted-game|title=Dybala: 'I wanted a game like this'|publisher=Football Italia|date=2 October 2018}}</ref> In Juventus's following Champions League group match on 23 October, Dybala scored the only goal of the game in a 1–0 away win over [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/man-utd-vs-juventus/401859|title=Manchester United 0–1 Juventus|publisher=skysports.com|author=Pete Hall|date=24 October 2018|access-date=14 December 2018}}</ref> With the arrival of [[Cristiano Ronaldo]], Dybala was often deployed out of position in a deeper role by manager Allegri during the 2018–19 season; as a result of this positional switch, as well as struggles with injuries, and difficulties with his manager, Dybala suffered a loss of form, and his goalscoring output decreased significantly, as he managed to score only five goals in 30 league matches, and 10 goals across 42 appearances in all competitions. However, Juventus managed to retain the Serie A title.<ref name="swap">{{cite web |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11667/11774327/paulo-dybala-to-man-united-how-juventus-forward-would-fit-in-at-old-trafford |title=Paulo Dybala to Man United: How Juventus forward would fit in at Old Trafford |publisher=Sky Sports |last1=Smith |first1=Peter |date=1 August 2019 |access-date=24 November 2019 }}</ref><ref name="plug">{{cite web |url=https://www.thescore.com/chlg/news/1808823 |title=Report: Juve pulled plug on Dybala's move to Tottenham |publisher=The Score |last1=Nesci |first1=Gianluca |date=8 August 2019 |access-date=24 November 2019 }}</ref>

Following a disappointing 2018–19 campaign, Dybala was initially linked with moves to [[Premier League]] sides [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] and [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham]], although he ultimately remained with Juventus for the 2019–20 season.<ref name="swap"/><ref name="plug"/><ref name="reason">{{cite web |url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/news/paulo-dybala-juventus-tottenham-man-utd-reason-he-did-not-want-join |title=Paulo Dybala reveals the reason he didn't want to join Manchester United or Tottenham this summer |publisher=FourFourTwo |date=9 October 2019 |access-date=24 November 2019 }}</ref> Although he was initially not expected to start under the club's new manager [[Maurizio Sarri]], he eventually broke into the first XI and scored his first goal of the season on 6 October 2019, in a 2–1 away win over [[Derby d'Italia|rivals]] [[Inter Milan|Inter]], to help his side overtake the ''Nerazzurri'' at the top of the Serie A table.<ref name="reason"/><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/49939525 |title=Inter Milan 1–2 Juventus |publisher=BBC Sport |date=6 October 2019 |access-date=24 November 2019 }}</ref> On 7 December, he made his 200th appearance for Juventus in a 3–1 away defeat to Lazio in Serie A.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://espanol.eurosport.com/futbol/dybala-disputa-su-200-partido-con-el-juventus_sto7568113/story.shtml |title=Dybala disputa su 200 partido con el Juventus |publisher=Eurosport |language=es |date=8 December 2019 |access-date=9 December 2019 }}</ref>

In the [[2020 Coppa Italia Final]] against [[S.S.C. Napoli|Napoli]] on 17 June, following a 0–0 draw after regulation time, Dybala missed Juventus's first spot-kick in the resulting shoot-out, with his shot being saved by [[Alex Meret]]; Napoli ultimately won the match 4–2 on penalties.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/154624/coppa-napoli-beat-juventus-penalties|title=Coppa: Napoli beat Juventus on penalties|publisher=Football Italia|date=17 June 2020}}</ref> At the end of the season, Dybala was awarded the [[Serie A Awards|Serie A MVP award]] for the [[2019–20 Serie A|2019–20 season]].<ref name="Serie A Awards 2019–20">{{cite web|date=4 August 2020|title=GLI MVP DELLA STAGIONE 2019/2020|url=http://www.legaseriea.it/uploads/default/attachments/comunicati/comunicati_m/8418/files/allegati/8514/cs_65_-_mvp_stagione_2019-2020.pdf|access-date=4 August 2020|publisher=Lega Serie A|language=it}}</ref> He finished the campaign with 11 goals and 6 assists, helping Juventus win their 9th consecutive title.<ref>{{Cite web|date=13 August 2020|title=MVP: Paulo Dybala|url=https://www.ultimouomo.com/mvp-paulo-dybala/|access-date=16 October 2020|website=L'Ultimo Uomo|language=it-IT}}</ref>

On 20 October 2020, Dybala made his first appearance of the new season in Juventus's opening [[2020–21 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] game, coming on as a second–half substitute in a 2–0 away win over [[FC Dynamo Kyiv|Dynamo Kyiv]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.sportmediaset.mediaset.it/calcio/championsleague/champions-dinamo-kiev-juve-0-2-decide-tutto-una-doppietta-di-morata_24432064-202002a.shtml |title=Champions, Dinamo Kiev-Juve 0-2: decide tutto una doppietta di Morata |publisher=Sportmediaset.it |language=it |date=20 October 2020 |access-date=20 October 2020 }}</ref> Dybala scored his first goal of the season on 4 November against [[Ferencvárosi TC|Ferencváros]] in a 4–1 win during a Champions League group stage match.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/match/2029410--ferencvaros-vs-juventus/|title=Ferencváros-Juventus &#124; UEFA Champions League|website=UEFA.com}}</ref> On 21 March 2022, Juventus chief executive officer [[Maurizio Arrivabene]] announced that the club had decided not to renew Dybala's contract which would expire in the summer, citing changes in the club's project following the arrival of striker [[Dušan Vlahović]] earlier that year.<ref>{{cite news |title=Paulo Dybala To Leave Juventus In Summer After Contract Not Renewed |url=https://sports.ndtv.com/football/paulo-dybala-to-leave-juventus-in-summer-after-contract-not-renewed-2835473 |access-date=17 May 2022 |agency=Agence France-Presse |via=NDTV |date=21 March 2022}}</ref> After being sidelined for months due to injury,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/164630/injury-updates-mckennie-and-dybala|title=Injury updates on McKennie and Dybala|publisher=Football Italia|date=7 April 2021}}</ref> Dybala made his return on 7 April 2021, scoring the winning goal of a 2–1 home win over Napoli.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/SerieA/match/158610|title=Juventus 2 - ==Personal life==
Dybala obtained Italian citizenship on 13 August 2012,<ref name="Dybala è cittadino italiano">{{cite web|author1=Alessio Alaimo|date=14 August 2012|title=ESCLUSIVA TMW - Palermo, Dybala è cittadino italiano|url=http://www.tuttomercatoweb.com/serie-a/esclusiva-tmw-palermo-dybala-e-cittadino-italiano-376629|access-date=12 January 2017|publisher=TuttoMercatoWeb.com|language=it}}</ref> in order to facilitate his move to [[Palermo F.C.|Palermo]],<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|last=Wilson|first=Jonathan|title=Paulo Dybala a striking new option for Argentina|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/sport/soccer/champions-league/paulo-dybala-a-striking-new-option-for-argentina-1.3069073|access-date=28 August 2020|newspaper=The Irish Times|language=en}}</ref> and circumvent [[Italian Football Federation|FIGC]] regulations that permit only a maximum of two non-[[Citizenship of the European Union|EU]] players in any [[Serie A]] team.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Foreign player cap for Serie A|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/6243648/foreign-player-cap-for-serie-a|access-date=28 August 2020|website=Sky Sports|language=en}}</ref> Dybala initially attempted to obtain Polish citizenship through ancestry from his grandfather, but the bureaucracy proved too complex.<ref name=":0" />

Dybala has several tattoos on his body: two stripes on his left arm, an Arabic tattoo as well as a crowned football on his leg.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tattooino.com/sportsmen/paulo-dybalas-tattoos/|title=Paulo Dybala's Tattoos|work=Tattooino|language=en-US|date=11 June 2018|access-date=14 January 2020}}</ref>
