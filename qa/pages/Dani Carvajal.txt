'''Daniel''' "'''Dani'''" '''Carvajal Ramos''' ({{IPA-es|daˈnjel kaɾβaˈxal ˈramos}}; born 11 January 1992) is a Spanish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[right back]] for [[La Liga]] club [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]] and the [[Spain national football team|Spain national team]].
 
Having risen through the [[Real Madrid CF (youth)|Real Madrid youth]] ranks, he spent a season with [[Bayer 04 Leverkusen|Bayer Leverkusen]] before returning to Real Madrid. He broke into the first team in 2013, and has subsequently gone on to win 20 major trophies at Real Madrid including five [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] trophies, three [[La Liga]] titles and a [[Copa del Rey]].

At youth international level, Carvajal won the [[2011 UEFA European Under-19 Championship|2011 European Championship]] with the [[Spain national under-19 football team|under-19 team]] and the [[2013 UEFA European Under-21 Championship|2013 edition]] with the [[Spain national under-21 football team|under-21 side]]. He made his [[Spain national football team|senior]] debut in 2014, representing the nation at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup]]. At the [[2023 UEFA Nations League Finals]], he scored the winning penalty which led Spain to their first UEFA Nations League title.

==Club career==
===Real Madrid Castilla===
Carvajal was born in [[Leganés]], a suburb of [[Madrid]]. He joined [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]]'s youth system as a 10-year-old, and continued climbing through the ranks until reaching [[Real Madrid Castilla]] in 2010.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.marca.com/2013/05/24/futbol/equipos/real_madrid/1369401797.html|title=Dani Carvajal: "Nací siendo del Real Madrid"|trans-title=Dani Carvajal: "I was born a Real Madrid fan"|newspaper=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|language=es|date=24 May 2013|access-date=24 October 2012}}</ref>

In [[2010–11 Segunda División B|his first season]] as a senior Carvajal was immediately cast in the [[reserve team]]'s starting XI, also being the team's [[Captain (association football)|captain]]. In [[2011–12 Segunda División B|the following campaign]], he fared even better (38 games and two goals, [[2012 Segunda División B play-offs|playoffs]] included), as the B's returned to [[Segunda División]] after a five-year absence.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://deportes.elpais.com/deportes/2012/07/10/actualidad/1341929494_839108.html|title=Carvajal, el lateral que no pudo debutar con el Madrid|trans-title=Carvajal, the fullback who could not make Madrid debut|newspaper=[[El País]]|language=es|date=11 July 2012|access-date=24 October 2012}}</ref>

===Bayer Leverkusen===
On 11 July 2012, after failing to make a single appearance for Real's first team,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.elconfidencial.com/deportes/liga-bbva/2012/07/06/la-marcha-de-carvajal-ratifica-la-poca-confianza-de-mourinho-en-la-cantera-blanca-101442/|title=La marcha de Carvajal ratifica la poca confianza de Mourinho en la cantera blanca|trans-title=Carvajal departure confirms Mourinho's little confidence in white youth system|newspaper=[[El Confidencial]]|language=es|date=6 July 2012|access-date=24 October 2012}}</ref> Carvajal signed a five-year contract with Germany's [[Bayer 04 Leverkusen]] for a transfer fee of [[Euro|€]]5&nbsp;million. The Spaniards added on a buy-back clause in the region of €6.5&nbsp;million if they wanted to re-sign the player after one season, €7&nbsp;million after two and approximately €8.5&nbsp;million after three.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.abc.es/20120711/deportes-futbol/abci-bayer-carvajal-201207111155.html|title=El Bayer Leverkusen confirma el fichaje de Carvajal|trans-title=Bayer Leverkusen confirm signing of Carvajal|newspaper=[[ABC (newspaper)|ABC]]|language=es|date=11 July 2012|access-date=24 October 2012 }}</ref>

Carvajal made his [[Bundesliga]] debut on 1 September 2012 in a 2–0 home win against [[SC Freiburg]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://espnfc.com/uk/en/report/346393/report.html?soccernet=true&cc=5739|title=Bayer Leverkusen 2–0 SC Freiburg|publisher=[[ESPN FC]]|date=1 September 2012|access-date=24 October 2012}}</ref> being subsequently named to the Team of the Week.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/spieltag/1-bundesliga/2012-13/2/elf-des-tages-am-spieltag.html|title=1. Bundesliga&nbsp;– Elf des Tages|trans-title=1. Bundesliga&nbsp;– Team of the Week|publisher=[[Kicker (sports magazine)|kicker]]|language=de|access-date=3 September 2012}}</ref> He scored his first goal for his new club on 25 November, netting the second in an eventual 2–1 success at [[TSG 1899 Hoffenheim]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://espnfc.com/uk/en/report/346287/report.html?soccernet=true&cc=5739|title=TSG Hoffenheim 1–2 Bayer Leverkusen|publisher=ESPN FC|date=25 November 2012|access-date=26 November 2012}}</ref>

Carvajal was selected as one of the three best right backs at the end of [[2012–13 Bundesliga|his first and only season]], behind [[FC Bayern Munich]]'s [[Phillip Lahm]] and [[FC Schalke 04]]'s [[Atsuto Uchida]]. He received 16% of the total vote from the fans.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bundesliga.com/en/fanzone/team-of-the-season/|title=Team of the season 2012/13|publisher=[[Bundesliga]]|date=25 May 2013|access-date=1 June 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140412230941/http://www.bundesliga.com/en/fanzone/team-of-the-season/|archive-date=12 April 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===Real Madrid===
On 3 June 2013, Real Madrid exercised its buy-back option on Carvajal, who became the team's first signing for [[2013–14 La Liga|2013–14]] for a fee believed to be in the region of €6.5&nbsp;million. This was confirmed by Bayer's [[director of football]] [[Rudi Völler]], who stated: "Real Madrid noticed the outstanding performances by Dani this season, and it was only a matter of time before they exercised their buy-back option."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bayer04.de/B04-ENG/en/_site_index.aspx|title=Real Madrid recall Carvajal|publisher=Bayer Leverkusen|date=3 June 2013|access-date=3 June 2013}}</ref> Shortly after the move, he spoke of his happiness to return, thanking the president, the fans and the club at a press conference.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/2892/transfer-zone/2013/07/05/4096427/carvajal-revels-in-real-madrid-return?ICID=OP|title=Carvajal revels in Real Madrid return|publisher=[[Goal (website)|Goal]]|date=5 July 2013|access-date=13 July 2013}}</ref>

Carvajal made his [[La Liga]] debut on 18 August 2013, in a 2–1 home win against [[Real Betis]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://espnfc.com/uk/en/report/373215/report.html?soccernet=true&cc=5739|title=Isco an instant hero|publisher=ESPN FC|date=18 August 2013|access-date=29 November 2013}}</ref> One month later he played his first match in the [[UEFA Champions League]], again featuring the full 90 minutes in a 6–1 away victory of [[Galatasaray S.K. (football)|Galatasaray SK]] in [[2013–14 UEFA Champions League|the group stage]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2014/matches/round=2000479/match=2011769/index.html|title=Six-goal Madrid overwhelm Galatasaray|publisher=UEFA|date=17 September 2013|access-date=29 November 2013}}</ref>

Carvajal appeared in 45 matches in [[2013–14 Real Madrid CF season|his first season]] and scored twice, in the league against [[Rayo Vallecano]]<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.marca.com/eventos/marcador/futbol/2013_14/primera/jornada_31/rma_ray/|title=Goles para aguantar el chaparrón|trans-title=Goals to stem storm|newspaper=Marca|location=Spain|language=es|date=29 March 2014|access-date=2 June 2014}}</ref> and [[CA Osasuna]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.marca.com/eventos/marcador/futbol/2013_14/primera/jornada_35/rma_osa/|title=Calma antes de la tempestad|trans-title=Calm before the storm|newspaper=Marca|location=Spain|language=es|date=26 April 2014|access-date=2 June 2014}}</ref> He added 120 minutes in [[2014 UEFA Champions League Final|the Champions League final]], a 4–1 triumph over [[Atlético Madrid]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/27383593|title=Real Madrid 4–1 Atlético Madrid|work=[[BBC Sport]]|date=24 May 2014|access-date=25 May 2014}}</ref>

Carvajal started in Real Madrid's triumphs in the [[2014 UEFA Super Cup]] over compatriots [[Sevilla FC]],<!--rem deprecated source (dailymail)--> and in [[2014 FIFA Club World Cup|that year]]'s [[FIFA Club World Cup]] [[2014 FIFA Club World Cup Final|final]] against Argentina's [[San Lorenzo de Almagro]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/clubworldcup/matches/round=259701/match=300298898/match-report.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141220234228/http://www.fifa.com/clubworldcup/matches/round=259701/match=300298898/match-report.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=20 December 2014|title=Real Madrid coast to Morocco 2014 title|publisher=FIFA|date=20 December 2014|access-date=6 May 2015}}</ref> In the first leg of [[2014–15 UEFA Champions League|the Champions League's semi-finals]], on 5 May 2015, he conceded a [[Penalty kick (association football)|penalty]] by fouling [[Carlos Tevez]], with the [[Juventus F.C.]] forward converting the spot-kick to defeat the Spaniards 2–1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/32582191|title=Juventus 2–1 Real Madrid|work=BBC Sport|last1=Henson|first1=Mike|date=5 May 2015|access-date=6 May 2015}}</ref>

On 8 July 2015, Carvajal signed a new deal with the ''Merengues'' until 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/07/carvajal-at-real-madrid-until-2020|title=Carvajal at Real Madrid until 2020|publisher=Real Madrid CF|date=8 July 2014|access-date=8 July 2014}}</ref> He contributed with eight appearances and one goal<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2016/matches/round=2000634/match=2015738/index.html|title=Ronaldo-inspired Madrid hold off Shakhtar|publisher=UEFA|date=25 November 2015|access-date=30 May 2016}}</ref> in [[2015–16 UEFA Champions League|the campaign's Champions League]], as the tournament ended in win; in [[2016 UEFA Champions League Final|the final]] against Atlético Madrid, he was subbed off injured early into the second half of a 1–1 draw ([[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shootout]] triumph).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2016/matches/round=2000638/match=2015789/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Spot-on Real Madrid defeat Atlético in final again|publisher=UEFA|date=28 May 2016|access-date=29 May 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.sport.es/es/noticias/champions/carvajal-lesion-lagrimas-5165730|title=Carvajal: Lesión y lágrimas|trans-title=Carvajal: Injury and tears|newspaper=[[Sport (Spanish newspaper)|Sport]]|language=es|date=28 May 2016|access-date=30 May 2016}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:UEFA EURO qualifiers Sweden vs Spain 20191015 Dani Carvajal 10.jpg|thumb|upright|Carvajal playing for Spain in 2019]]

Carvajal acted as backup to [[FC Barcelona]]'s [[Martín Montoya]] at the [[2013 UEFA European Under-21 Championship]], playing in the 3–0 group stage win against [[Netherlands national under-21 football team|the Netherlands]] as [[Spain national under-21 football team|Spain]] went on to win the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/under21/season=2013/matches/round=2000188/match=2010955/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Spain beat Netherlands to top Group B|publisher=UEFA|date=12 June 2013|access-date=2 June 2014}}</ref> He was selected by [[Spain national football team|full side]] manager [[Vicente del Bosque]] for his 30-man provisional squad for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]], being excused alongside all other Real Madrid and Atlético Madrid players from the warm-up game against [[Bolivia national football team|Bolivia]] on 30 May due to the Champions League final,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/y=2014/m=5/news=del-bosque-waits-to-name-23-2343915.html|title=Del Bosque waits to name 23|publisher=FIFA|date=25 May 2014|access-date=2 June 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140528001519/http://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/y%3D2014/m%3D5/news%3Ddel-bosque-waits-to-name-23-2343915.html|archive-date=28 May 2014}}</ref> and being omitted from the final list the following day.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/27647635|title=World Cup 2014: Spain drop Alvaro Negredo and Jesus Navas|work=BBC Sport|date=31 May 2014|access-date=31 May 2014}}</ref>

On 29 August 2014, Carvajal was called up to the squad for the first time, for matches against [[France national football team|France]] and [[Macedonia national football team|Macedonia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sefutbol.com/en/these-are-players-called-matches-against-france-and-macedonia|title=Vicente del Bosque has announced the 23-man squad for the matches against France (September 4th) and Macedonia (September 8th)|publisher=[[Royal Spanish Football Federation]]|date=29 August 2014|access-date=29 August 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140831194953/http://www.sefutbol.com/en/these-are-players-called-matches-against-france-and-macedonia|archive-date=31 August 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> He made his debut on 4 September, in a 0–1 [[Exhibition game|friendly]] defeat to the former in which he played the full 90 minutes;<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/29047046|title=France 1–0 Spain|work=BBC Sport|date=4 September 2014|access-date=6 September 2014}}</ref> he was also selected for the [[UEFA Euro 2016]] tournament,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/36312769|title=Euro 2016: Diego Costa, Juan Mata & Fernando Torres not in Spain squad|work=BBC Sport|date=17 May 2016|access-date=17 May 2016}}</ref> being dropped after an injury contracted whilst with his club.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://deportes.elpais.com/deportes/2016/05/30/actualidad/1464604756_004662.html|title=Dani Carvajal se pierde la Eurocopa 2016|trans-title=Dani Carvajal misses 2016 European Championship|newspaper=El País|language=es|date=30 May 2016|access-date=30 May 2016}}</ref>

Carvajal was named in Spain's final [[2018 FIFA World Cup squads|squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/morata-misses-out-on-spain-world-cup-squad/1ub1qz7jod09i1lfvdcy84ea2l|title=Morata misses out on Spain's 23-man World Cup squad|publisher=Goal|date=21 May 2018|access-date=21 May 2018}}</ref> After sitting out the first game against [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]] as he was not fully fit,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/world-cup/2018/06/15/portugal-vs-spain-world-cup-2018-live-score-latest-updates/|title=Cristiano Ronaldo scores sensational hat-trick as Portugal hold Spain in instant World Cup classic|newspaper=[[The Daily Telegraph]]|date=15 June 2018|access-date=20 June 2018}}</ref> he made his first appearance in [[FIFA World Cup|the competition]] on 20 June and played the entire 1–0 win over [[Iran national football team|Iran]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/aponline/2018/06/20/sports/soccer/ap-soc-wcup-iran-spain.html|title=Diego Costa scores again, Spain beats Iran 1–0 at World Cup|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|date=20 June 2018|access-date=20 June 2018}}</ref>

Carvajal played for Spain in the [[2023 UEFA Nations League Finals]], scoring the winning penalty via [[Panenka (penalty kick)|panenka]] in the [[2023 UEFA Nations League final|final]] against Croatia.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Spain win Nations League final on penalties |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/65944659 |access-date=21 June 2023}}</ref>
