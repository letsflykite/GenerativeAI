'''Ismaël Kenneth Jordan Koné''' (born June 16, 2002) is a Canadian professional [[Association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[EFL Championship]] club [[Watford F.C.|Watford]]. Born in Ivory Coast, he represents the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada national team]].

== Club career ==
===CF Montréal===
After spending a few months training with the [[CF Montréal]] first team, initially unable to sign a contract due to “some technicalities in MLS rules,”<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://nationalpost.com/pmn/sports-pmn/cf-montreal-sign-19-year-old-m-ismael-kone|title=CF Montreal sign 19-year-old M Ismael Kone|date=August 13, 2021|work=[[National Post]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.985fm.ca/nouvelles/sports/422063/soccer-ismael-kone-un-des-plus-beaux-espoirs-au-quebec|title=Ismaël Koné, " un des plus beaux espoirs au Québec "|first=Jeremy|last=Filosa|language=fr|trans-title=Ismaël Koné, "one of the most beautiful hopes in Quebec"|date=September 7, 2021|work=[[CHMP-FM|CHMP-FM 98.5 FM]]}}</ref> Koné officially signed a two-year contract with two option years on August 13, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://en.cfmontreal.com/news/ismael-kone-signs-with-first-team|title=Ismaël Kone signs with first team|work=[[CF Montreal]]|date=August 13, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.journaldemontreal.com/2021/09/07/retenez-le-nom-dismael-kone|first=Dave|last=Lévesque|language=fr|date=September 7, 2021|trans-title=Remember the name of Ismaël Koné|title=Retenez le nom d'Ismaël Koné|work=[[Le Journal de Montréal]]}}</ref> However, soon after signing his contract he suffered a knee injury, which limited his participation in training.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://lookcharms.com/ismael-kone-is-ready-to-come-and-lend-a-hand-to-cf-montreal/|title=Ismaël Koné is ready to come and lend a hand to CF Montreal|work=Look Charms|date=September 8, 2021}}</ref> After the 2021 season, he went to train with Montreal's sister club, Italian [[Serie A]] club [[Bologna F.C. 1909|Bologna]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://destroyandprogress.substack.com/p/who-is-ismael-kone-exploring-more?s=r|title=Who is Ismael Koné? A deep dive into the rapidly improving 19-year-old CF Montreal midfielder set to earn first CanMNT call-up this March window|date=March 18, 2022|first=Alexandre|last=Gangué-Ruzic|work=Destroy and Progress}}</ref> Koné made his professional debut and scored his first goal with Montreal in a 3–0 win against [[Santos Laguna]] on February 23, 2022, in the [[2022 CONCACAF Champions League]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://btsvancity.com/2022/02/23/im-happy-with-the-result-cf-montreal-erases-santos-laguna-heartbreak-with-commanding-3-0-win-to-advance-to-champions-league-quarter-finals/|title="I'm happy with the result" CF Montreal erases Santos Laguna heartbreak with commanding 3–0 win to advance to Champions League quarter-finals|first=Alexandre|last=Gangué-Ruzic|date=February 23, 2022|work=BTS Van City}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tvasports.ca/2022/02/26/scenario-reve-pour-ismael-kone|title=Scénario rêvé pour Ismaël Koné|language=fr|first=Dave|last=Lévesque|work=[[TVA Sports]]|trans-title=Dream scenario for Ismaël Koné}}</ref> During the 2022 summer transfer window, [[English Championship]] clubs [[Sheffield United]] and [[Norwich City]] were reported to have approached Montréal over Koné's services.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/cf-montreal-wunderkind-ismael-kone-seeks-great-leap-in-mls-cup-playoffs|title=CF Montréal wunderkind Ismaël Koné seeks great leap in MLS Cup Playoffs|first=Charles|last=Boehm|website=[[Major League Soccer]]|date=October 22, 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://journalmetro.com/local/saint-laurent/2897101/cf-montreal-ismael-kone-demeure-club/|title=CF Montréal: Ismaël Koné demeure avec le club|trans-title=CF Montreal: Ismaël Koné remains with the club|language=fr|date=September 2, 2022|first=Clément|last=Gaboury|work=[[Métro (Montreal newspaper)|Journal Métro]]}}</ref>

===Watford===
In December 2022, English [[EFL Championship|Championship]] club [[Watford F.C.|Watford]] announced they had agreed to sign Koné on a transfer from CF Montréal, with the midfielder signing a four-and-a-half year deal.<ref>{{cite web|title=Official: Hornets Sign Koné|date=December 5, 2022|url=https://www.watfordfc.com/news/official-hornets-sign-kone|work=[[Watford F.C.|Watford Football Club]]}}</ref> He made his debut on January 7, 2023, starting an [[FA Cup]] match against [[Reading F.C.|Reading]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.watfordobserver.co.uk/sport/23235429.watford-fa-cup-new-injury-concern/|title=Watford out of the FA Cup, but new injury is of more concern|date=January 7, 2022|work=[[Watford Observer]]|first=Andrew|last=French}}</ref>

==International career==
Koné was eligible to play for [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada]] and the [[Ivory Coast national football team|Ivory Coast]]. In March 2022, Koné was called up to Canada for their final window of [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – CONCACAF third round|2022 FIFA World Cup qualification]] matches.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.journaldequebec.com/2022/03/20/ismael-kone-deja-appele-en-equipe-nationale-1|title=Ismael Koné déjà appelé en équipe nationale|language=fr|trans-title=Ismael Koné already called up for the national team|date=March 20, 2022|work=[[Le Journal de Québec]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://onesoccer.ca/a/ismael-kone-dayne-st-clair-earn-canada-call-ups-cf-montreal-minnesota-united|title='If you're good enough, you're old enough': CF Montreal's Ismael Kone earns CanMNT call|date=March 20, 2022|first=Armen|last=Bedakian|work=[[OneSoccer]]}}</ref> He made his debut on March 24 against [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica]], as a substitute for [[Jonathan Osorio]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://northerntribune.ca/world-cup-costa-rica-canada/|first=John|last=Jacques|title=Cautions Cost Canada In Cagey Costa Rica Clash|date=March 24, 2022|work=Northern Tribune}}</ref> Koné scored his first international goal for Canada in a friendly against [[Bahrain national football team|Bahrain]] on November 11, 2022.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/ismael-kone-s-stock-is-rising-canada-draw-bahrain-in-world-cup-tune-up|title=Ismaël Koné's stock is rising: Canada draw Bahrain in World Cup tune-up|first=Peter|last=Galindo|work=[[Major League Soccer]]|date=November 11, 2022}}</ref> Two days later on November 13, he was named to Canada's 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canada-announces-26-man-squad-for-2022-fifa-world-cup-in-qatar|date=November 13, 2022|title=Canada announces 26-man squad for 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]|first=Charlie|last=O'Connor-Clarke}}</ref> He appeared in all three of the team's matches at the World Cup.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/au-revoir-montreal-watford-bound-ismael-kone-seeks-biggest-leagues|title=Au revoir, Montréal: Watford-bound Ismaël Koné takes the next step|date=December 8, 2022|work=[[Major League Soccer]]|first=Tom|last=Bogert}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/kone-grateful-cf-montreal-mls-1.6679542|title=Canadian international Koné 'grateful' to CF Montreal for giving him his start|date=December 8, 2022|work=[[CBC Sports]]|first=Elias|last=Grigoriadis}}</ref> At the end of the year, Koné was named the men's 2022 [[Canada Soccer Young Player of the Year]].<ref name=young>{{Cite web|url=https://canadasoccer.com/news/simi-awujo-and-ismael-kone-named-canada-soccer-young-players-of-the-year/|title=Simi Awujo and Ismaël Koné named Canada Soccer Young Players of the Year|date=December 20, 2022|work=[[Canadian Soccer Association]]}}</ref>

In June 2023, Koné was called-up to Canada ahead of the [[2023 CONCACAF Nations League Finals]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Former CPLers Loturi, Zator called up by Canada for 2023 Concacaf Nations League Finals|date=June 7, 2023|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canada-canmnt-2023-concacaf-nations-league-final-roster|first=Mitchell|last=Tierney|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]}}</ref>
