'''Aymeric Jean Louis Gérard Alphonse Laporte''' (born 27 May 1994) is a French-Spanish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] for {{English football updater|ManchesC}} club [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] and the [[Spain national football team|Spain national team]]. He previously played for [[CD Basconia|Basconia]] and [[Athletic Bilbao]].

When he joined Athletic Bilbao in 2010 at the age of 16, he became only the second player born in France—after [[Bixente Lizarazu]]—to play for them,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://msn.foxsports.com/foxsoccer/europaleague/story/apoel-kiryat-shmona-0-2-athletic-bilbao-europa-league-match-report-112812h|title=Athletic Bilbao makes easy work of Hapoel IKS|website=[[Fox Sports]]|date=29 November 2012|access-date=8 December 2012}}</ref> going on to make 222 competitive appearances for the club. In January 2018, he signed for Manchester City.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.abc.es/deportes/futbol/abci-laporte-posa-como-jugador-5722453230001-20180130015032_video.html|title=Laporte ya posa como jugador del Manchester City|trans-title=Laporte already posing as a Manchester City player|newspaper=[[ABC (newspaper)|ABC]]|location=Madrid|language=es|date=30 January 2018|access-date=30 January 2018}}</ref> He was part of the side which won the first [[Treble (association football)#Domestic trebles|domestic treble]] in English men's football in [[2018–19 Manchester City F.C. season|2019]], and the country's second-ever [[Treble (association football)#Continental trebles|continental treble]] in [[2022–23 Manchester City F.C. season|2023]].

French by birth, Laporte won 51 [[Cap (sport)|caps]] for the country at [[France national youth football team|youth international levels]], and was called up to the [[France national football team|senior team]] twice but remained uncapped. In 2021, after receiving [[Spanish citizenship]] and approval from [[FIFA]] to switch national teams, Laporte was named in [[Spain national football team|Spain]]'s squad for [[UEFA Euro 2020]]. He would then go on to represent the nation at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

===Manchester City===
Near the end of the winter transfer window of January 2018, Laporte signed for [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] for a reported fee of £57&nbsp;million (his contractual [[release clause]] amount and a record-breaking figure for both clubs). He was given the number 14 shirt,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/42873612 |title=Aymeric Laporte: Manchester City sign French defender for club-record £57m |website=[[BBC Sport]] |date=30 January 2018 |access-date=30 January 2018}}</ref> while Bilbao immediately spent around half of the fee on [[Iñigo Martínez]] as a replacement.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/athletic-bilbao-sign-martinez-from-real-sociedad/g2qcqw6nfa8t1fj74qrh862fb |title=Athletic Bilbao sign Inigo Martinez to replace Aymeric Laporte |website=[[Goal (website)|Goal.com]] |publisher=[[DAZN Group]] |location=Leeds |first=Nicholas |last=McGee |date=30 January 2018 |access-date=30 January 2018}}</ref>

Just one day after signing, Laporte made his Manchester City and [[Premier League]] debut against [[West Bromwich Albion F.C.|West Bromwich Albion]]. City came out victorious winning 3–0 as they kept up their title charge.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Clayton|first=David |title=Blues extend lead at top with comfortable win |url=https://www.mancity.com/news/first-team/match-report/2018/january/man-city-v-west-brom-match-report |access-date=11 February 2021 |publisher=Manchester City F.C.}}</ref> He went on to make 13 appearances throughout the season for City, helping them accumulate an unprecedented 100 points in a Premier League season. He made a total of nine league appearances meaning he was eligible for a winner's medal.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Aymeric Laporte |url=https://www.premierleague.com/players/11044/Aymeric-Laporte/overview |access-date=11 February 2021 |publisher=Premier League}}</ref>

Laporte scored his first goal for City on 25 August 2018, equalising against [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]] in a 1–1 away draw.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45230636 |title=Wolverhampton Wanderers 1–1 Manchester City |website=BBC Sport |first=Phil |last=McNulty |date=25 August 2018 |access-date=25 August 2018}}</ref> He made 51 appearances during the 2018–19 season as the club completed the first domestic [[Treble (association football)|treble]] in English football history ([[2018–19 Premier League|Premier League]], [[2018–19 FA Cup|FA Cup]], [[2018–19 EFL Cup|League Cup]], plus the [[2018 FA Community Shield|Community Shield]]).<ref name="facup19"/> He scored five goals throughout the season, including an important goal on the final day of the Premier League campaign as City romped to victory over [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton and Hove Albion]] securing back-to-back league titles and the second of Laporte's career. Overall he played a total of 4,352 minutes.<ref name="sb1819"/>

On 31 August 2019, Laporte sustained an injury to his right knee, causing him to be carried off on a stretcher.<ref name="knee19">{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.co.uk/football/manchester-city/story/3932410/city-fear-laporte-out-for-a-while-with-knee-injury |title=City fear Laporte out 'for a while' with knee injury |website=[[ESPN]] |first=Rob |last=Dawson |date=31 August 2019}}</ref> He underwent surgery in September 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49576729 |title=Aymeric Laporte: Manchester City defender has surgery on right knee |date=4 September 2019 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

Laporte had been an automatic starter for Manchester City after his return from injury during the 2019–20 season and through the start of the 2020–21 season until a poor display in a 2–0 defeat to [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] on 21 November 2020.<ref name=Stones /> [[John Stones]] replaced Laporte in the centre of defence and formed a formidable partnership with new signing [[Rúben Dias]] in Laporte's absence from the starting lineup.<ref name=Stones>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2021/jan/19/pep-guardiola-aymeric-laporte-decide-own-future-manchester-city|title=Pep Guardiola says Aymeric Laporte can decide own future at Manchester City|website=The Guardian|date=19 January 2021|accessdate=26 April 2021}}</ref> On 25 April 2021, Laporte headed in the only goal of the game as City beat Tottenham to win their fourth straight [[2021 EFL Cup Final|EFL Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2021/04/25/manchester-city-vs-tottenham-league-cup-final-2021-live-score/ |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/archive/20220112/https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2021/04/25/manchester-city-vs-tottenham-league-cup-final-2021-live-score/ |archive-date=12 January 2022 |url-access=subscription |url-status=live|title=Aymeric Laporte makes it four Carabao Cups in a row for dominant Man City against outclassed Spurs|website=Telegraph|date=25 April 2021|accessdate=26 April 2021}}{{cbignore}}</ref>

==International career==
===France===
Laporte represented [[France national football team|France]] at [[France national under-17 football team|under-17]], [[France national under-18 football team|under-18]], [[France national under-19 football team|under-19]] and [[France national under-21 football team|under-21 levels]],<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mundodeportivo.com/20140227/futbol/laporte-convocado-para-la-sub-21-por-francia_54402647025.html|title=Laporte, convocado para la sub 21 por Francia|trans-title=Laporte, called up to France under-21|newspaper=Mundo Deportivo|location=Barcelona|language=es|date=27 February 2014|access-date=22 April 2014}}</ref> [[Captain (association football)|captaining]] each side.<ref name=Spain>{{cite news|url=http://www.espnfc.co.uk/european-championship-qualifying/story/2652467/athletic-bilbao-aymeric-laporte-would-consider-spain-call|title=Athletic Bilbao's Aymeric Laporte would consider Spain call|website=ESPN FC|date=7 October 2015|access-date=11 April 2016}}</ref> He was part of the team that came runners-up to [[Serbia national under-19 football team|Serbia]] in the [[2013 UEFA European Under-19 Championship]] in Lithuania, and was selected in the Team of the Tournament.<ref name=UEFA>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/under19/season=2013/technical-report/tr-under19-2013.pdf|title=Technical report|publisher=UEFA|pages=18–19|access-date=11 April 2016}}</ref>

On 24 March 2016, in a [[2017 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification Group 3|2017 European Under-21 Championship qualifier]] against [[Scotland national under-21 football team|Scotland]] in [[Angers]], Laporte was stretchered off with a fracture and dislocation to his right [[fibula]] and ankle, concluding his season prematurely.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/transfers/aymeric-laporte-injury-manchester-city-transfer-plans-dealt-a-blow-as-top-target-suffers-serious-a6951786.html|title=Aymeric Laporte injury: Manchester City transfer plans dealt a blow as top target suffers fractured ankle|newspaper=[[The Independent]]|location=London|last=De Menezes|first=Jack|date=25 March 2016|access-date=11 April 2016}}</ref> Previously, in October 2015, he stated that he would consider representing [[Spain national football team|Spain]] if rejected by France for [[UEFA Euro 2016]].<ref name=Spain/>

In August 2016, at the behest of new national manager [[Julen Lopetegui]], Laporte began the process of making himself eligible for Spain.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.marca.com/futbol/seleccion/2016/08/30/57c489e146163f72308b4591.html|title=Lopetegui 'ficha' a Laporte|trans-title=Lopetegui 'signs' Laporte|newspaper=Marca|first=Enrique|last=Ortego|location=Madrid|language=es|date=30 August 2016|access-date=18 September 2016}}</ref> A month later, he was called up to the senior France squad by [[Didier Deschamps]] for [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group A|2018 World Cup qualifiers]] against [[Bulgaria national football team|Bulgaria]] and the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]] in October,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://sport24.lefigaro.fr/football/euro-2012/equipe-de-france/actualites/deschamps-appelle-laporte-et-laisse-giroud-a-la-maison-826710|title=Équipe de France: Laporte arrive, Fekir revient|trans-title=France team: Laporte arrives, Fekir returns|newspaper=[[Le Figaro]]|location=Paris|language=fr|date=29 September 2016|access-date=30 September 2016}}</ref> and stated he had decided not to apply for [[Spanish nationality law|Spanish nationality]] with the continued aim of playing for France.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://rmcsport.bfmtv.com/football/aymeric-laporte-je-pouvais-prendre-la-nationalite-espagnole-je-ne-l-ai-pas-fait-1043930.html |title=Aymeric Laporte: "Je pouvais prendre la nationalité espagnole, je ne l'ai pas fait"|trans-title=Aymeric Laporte: "I could take Spanish nationality, I did not do so"|publisher=[[BFM TV]]|language=fr|date=3 October 2016|access-date=16 June 2017}}</ref> He did not take part in either match.

In August 2019, he was called up for France's [[UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying Group H|UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying]] fixtures against [[Albania national football team|Albania]] and [[Andorra national football team|Andorra]];<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sports.yahoo.com/laporte-included-france-squad-euro-2020-qualifiers-130529339--sow.html|title=Laporte included in France squad for Euro 2020 qualifiers|website=Yahoo Sports|date=29 August 2019|access-date=29 August 2019}}</ref> however just two days later he was injured playing for his club and ruled out of any involvement.<ref name=knee19/>

===Spain===
In May 2021, the [[Council of Ministers (Spain)|Spanish Council of Ministers]] granted Spanish nationality to Laporte following a process initiated on his behalf by the [[Royal Spanish Football Federation]] (RFEF); [[FIFA]] approved the change of nationality later that week, allowing him to play for Spain at the upcoming [[UEFA Euro 2020]] tournament.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thescore.com/euro_fed/news/2170835/report-laporte-to-play-for-spain-at-euro-2020-after-nationality-approved|title=Report: Laporte can play for Spain at Euro 2020 after nationality approved|agency=[[Agence France-Presse]]|date=11 May 2021}}</ref> Laporte had previously claimed that "playing for Spain is out of the question" and that he would not apply for dual citizenship.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://bleacherreport.com/articles/2811048-aymeric-laporte-says-playing-for-spain-out-of-the-question-amid-france-absence|title=Aymeric Laporte Says Playing for Spain 'Out of the Question' Amid France Absence|first=Matt|last=Jones|date=16 December 2018|website=Bleacher Report}}</ref>

On 24 May 2021, Laporte was included in Spain's [[UEFA Euro 2020 squads#Spain|24-man squad]] for Euro 2020.<ref>{{cite news |title=Spain leave Sergio Ramos out of Euro 2020 squad and call up Aymeric Laporte |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2021/may/24/spain-leave-sergio-ramos-out-of-euro-2020-squad-and-call-up-aymeric-laporte |access-date=24 May 2021 |newspaper=The Guardian |date=24 May 2021}}</ref> On 4 June, he made his Spain debut when he started in a friendly goalless draw with [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Jabbar|first=Nasir|date=4 June 2021|title=Aymeric Laporte Produces Sublime Piece Of Defending On Spain Debut|url=https://www.sportbible.com/football/news-aymeric-laporte-produces-sublime-piece-of-defending-on-spain-debut-20210604|access-date=4 June 2021|website=SportBible|language=en}}</ref> He scored his debut goal for the national team on 23 June, in the [[UEFA Euro 2020 Group E|group stage]] 5–0 routing of [[Slovakia national football team|Slovakia]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=O'Brien|first=Sean|date=23 June 2021|title='Terrible' Slovakia gift Spain biggest Euros win as Laporte justifies switch|url=https://talksport.com/football/900831/slovakia-spain-euro-2020-man-city-aymeric-laporte-france-ferran-torres/|access-date=23 June 2021|website=talkSPORT|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Spanish football journalist [[Guillem Balagué]] described Laporte as a "centre-back who can play from the back and is strong", adding that "he is certainly a powerful defender".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/12037/11220803/man-city-likely-to-sign-aymeric-laporte-for-60m-says-guillem-balague|title=Man City likely to sign Aymeric Laporte for £60m, says Guillem Balagué|website=[[Sky Sports]]|first=Guillem|last=Balagué|date=24 January 2018|access-date=22 February 2018}}</ref> He is also known for his passing ability.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/the-game-dissected-why-aymeric-laporte-is-the-key-man-in-manchester-citys-premier-league-title-charge-m835r9dzb |title=The Game Dissected: why Aymeric Laporte is the key to Manchester City's hopes |work=The Times |last1=Clarke |first1=Tom |date=15 April 2019 |access-date=14 July 2021 }}</ref>
