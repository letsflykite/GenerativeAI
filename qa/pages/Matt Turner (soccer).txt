'''Matthew Charles Turner''' (born June 24, 1994) is an American professional [[Association football|soccer]] <!--do not change to footballer. He is American so we use American English--> player who plays as a [[goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for {{English football updater|Arsenal}} club [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] and the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States national team]].<ref name="Arsenal Signing">{{Cite web |title=Matt Turner joins from New England Revolution |url=https://www.arsenal.com/news/matt-turner-joins-new-england-revolution |publisher=Arsenal F.C. |date=June 27, 2022 |access-date=June 27, 2022}}</ref>

With the United States he won the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup]] and [[2023 CONCACAF Nations League Finals|2023 CONCACAF Nations League]], winning the best goalkeeper award for both competitions.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-06-18 |title=Pulisic, Turner and Lake take home CNL honors |url=https://www.concacaf.com/nations-league/news/pulisic-turner-and-lake-take-home-cnl-honors/ |access-date=2023-07-10 |website=Concacaf |language=en}}</ref>

==Early career==
A native of [[Park Ridge, New Jersey]], Turner attended [[Saint Joseph Regional High School]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.northjersey.com/story/sports/soccer/2016/03/05/soccer-former-st-joseph-star-matt-turner-signs-with-mls-team/94532368/|title=Soccer: Former St. Joseph star Matt Turner signs with MLS team|website=North Jersey Media Group}}</ref> Turner didn't play soccer until he was 14 years old, initially taking up the sport to stay in shape for his primary sports of basketball and baseball. He started playing in goal when the only other goalkeeper for his freshman team was injured at tryouts.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web |last=Stejskal |first=Sam |date=May 31, 2022 |title=As his USMNT stock rose and Arsenal hovered, Matt Turner met profound heartbreak |url=https://theathletic.com/3342477/2022/05/31/matt-turner-usmnt-arsenal/ |access-date=June 4, 2022 |website=The Athletic |language=en}}</ref>

Turner spent his entire collegiate career at [[Fairfield University]]. He made a total of 39 appearances for the Stags and finished with 21 shutouts. He was named to the All-[[Metro Atlantic Athletic Conference]] second team during his senior season.<ref name="fairfield_stags">{{cite web|title=Matt Turner Signs With New England Revolution|url=http://www.fairfieldstags.com/ViewArticle.dbml?SPSID=810598&SPID=137707&DB_LANG=C&DB_OEM_ID=32500&ATCLID=210772220|website=FairfieldStags.com|publisher=Fairfield Stags|accessdate=May 8, 2016|date=March 4, 2016}}</ref>

He also played in the [[Premier Development League]] for [[Jersey Express S.C.|Jersey Express]], leading the team to the PDL national semifinals in 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.uslleaguetwo.com/news_article/show/1248356|title=How Matt Turner’s USL League Two experience put him on a path to the World Cup|date=November 19, 2022|work=[[USL League Two]]|first=Nicholas|last=Murray}}</ref>

==Club career==
===New England Revolution===
Turner was not selected in the [[2016 MLS SuperDraft]], but he signed a professional contract with [[Major League Soccer]] (MLS) club [[New England Revolution]] on March 3, 2016, after a successful preseason trial.<ref name="fairfield_stags"/><ref>{{cite web|title=Revolution sign forward Michael Gamble and goalkeeper Matt Turner|url=http://www.revolutionsoccer.net/post/2016/03/03/revolution-sign-forward-michael-gamble-and-goalkeeper-matt-turner|website=RevolutionSoccer.net|publisher=New England Revolution|accessdate=May 8, 2016|date=March 3, 2016}}</ref>

====2016–17 seasons====
[[File:CINvRIC 2017-07-09 - Matt Turner (40074313270) (cropped).jpg|thumb|left|Turner playing for [[Richmond Kickers]] against [[FC Cincinnati (2016–18)|FC Cincinnati]] in 2017]]
On April 29, he joined [[United Soccer League (2010)|United Soccer League]] club [[Richmond Kickers]] on loan and made his professional debut a day later in a 1–0 victory over [[Toronto FC II]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Campos|first1=Anthony|title=Match Recap: TFC II 0–1 Richmond Kickers|url=http://www.torontofc.ca/post/2016/04/30/match-recap-tfc-ii-0-1-richmond-kickers|website=TorontoFC.ca|accessdate=May 8, 2016|date=April 30, 2016}}</ref> During his two loan stints with the Kickers, Turner recorded 7 clean sheets in 27 starts.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.revolutionsoccer.net/news/recap-revs-drop-season-opener-philadelphia-2-0 |title=Recap: Revs drop season opener in Philadelphia, 2–0 |date=March 3, 2018 |accessdate=July 8, 2022}}</ref>

====2018 season====
[[File:DC United (50120929083).jpg|thumb|right|Turner (right) during a Revolution match against [[D.C. United]] in 2018]]
Turner earned the starting job for the Revolution entering the [[2018 New England Revolution season|2018 season]], beating out [[Brad Knighton]] and [[Cody Cropper]] for the job.{{citation needed|date=August 2022}} He made his MLS debut on March 3, 2018, in the Revolution's season opener against [[Philadelphia Union]], making four saves in a 2–0 loss.<ref>{{cite web|last1=McCarthy|first1=Kyle|title=Revs sent off for openers|url=http://www.bostonherald.com/sports/soccer/2018/03/revs_sent_off_for_openers|website=BostonHerald.com|accessdate=March 5, 2018|date=March 4, 2018}}</ref> Turner started all four of New England's matches to open the season, recording a 1.25 goals against average and 16 saves throughout that period. On March 31, 2018, he made six saves and recorded his first MLS clean sheet in the club's 2–0 win at [[PNC Stadium|BBVA Compass Stadium]] against [[Houston Dynamo FC]]. It was the first clean sheet a Revolution keeper had recorded since August 24, 2016. He recorded four more clean sheets over the 2018 season.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.revolutionsoccer.net/news/recap-revs-stretch-unbeaten-run-three-games-2-0-win-over-dynamo |title=Recap: Revs stretch unbeaten run to three games with 2–0 win over Dynamo |date=April 1, 2018 |accessdate=July 8, 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.revolutionsoccer.net/news/turning-the-page-the-remarkable-rise-of-matt-turner-from-new-england-to-arsenal |title=Turning the Page. The remarkable rise of Matt Turner, from New England to Arsenal |date=June 16, 2022 |accessdate=July 8, 2022}}</ref>

====2019 season====
After a difficult start to the 2019 season which saw Turner drop to third string behind Cropper and Knighton, he re-emerged as the starter for the club, ultimately starting 20 matches and recording five clean sheets.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/players/matt-turner/stats/ |title= Matt Turner Stats |accessdate=July 8, 2022}}</ref> Turner would lead the Revolution to its first playoff match since 2015. It was also Turner's first playoff match. He started in the Revolution's 1–0 away loss to [[Atlanta United FC]] in the first round of the [[2019 MLS Cup Playoffs]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/atlanta-united-1-new-england-revolution-0-2019-mls-cup-playoffs-match-recap |title=Atlanta United 1, New England Revolution 0. 2019 MLS Cup Playoffs Match Recap |date=October 19, 2019 |accessdate=July 8, 2022}}</ref> Turner finished as an MLS goalkeeper of the year finalist, ranking 5th in voting behind winner [[Vito Mannone]], [[D.C. United|D.C. United's]] [[Bill Hamid]], [[New York City FC]]'s [[Sean Johnson (soccer)|Sean Johnson]], and Atlanta's [[Brad Guzan]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/minnesota-united-fc/story/3972498/mls-goalkeeper-of-the-year-minnesotas-mannone-beats-out-dc-uniteds-hamid |title=MLS Goalkeeper of the Year: Minnesota's Mannone beats out D.C. United's Hamid |date=October 24, 2019 |accessdate=July 8, 2022}}</ref>

====2020 season====
Turner had a stand-out season in [[2020 New England Revolution season|2020]] and was subsequently named as a finalist for the [[MLS Goalkeeper of the Year Award]]. Over the course of the season, Turner started 22 of the team's 23 matches, recording an 8–7–8 record, setting a new club record for goals against per game (1.08), recording six shut-outs, and leading his team to a second consecutive trip to the [[2020 MLS Cup Playoffs|MLS Cup Playoffs]]. At the conclusion of the season, Turner ranked second in Revs history in saves percentage and goals against average (1.36). He additionally finished as the leader ranked among the top-10 MLS goalkeepers in saves percentage, goals against average, and saves. His save percentage was the highest of any MLS keeper since the start of the 2019 season.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.revolutionsoccer.net/news/matt-turner-named-finalist-allstate-mls-goalkeeper-year |title=Matt Turner named finalist for Allstate MLS Goalkeeper of the Year |date=November 11, 2020 |accessdate=July 8, 2022}}</ref> He ultimately finished the season second in voting for the MLS goalkeeper of the year, behind [[Andre Blake]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/philadelphia-unions-andre-blake-named-2020-allstate-mls-goalkeeper-year |title=Philadelphia Union's Andre Blake named 2020 Allstate MLS Goalkeeper of the Year |date=November 16, 2020 |accessdate=July 8, 2022}}</ref>

In round one of the [[2020 MLS Cup Playoffs]], Turner recorded a clean sheet against the [[Supporters' Shield]]-holding [[Philadelphia Union]] in the Revolution's 2–0 win.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.inquirer.com/soccer/philadelphia-union-vs-new-england-revolution-mls-playoffs-20201124.html |title=Union's season ends with 2–0 playoff loss to New England Revolution
 |date=November 24, 2020 |accessdate=July 8, 2022}}</ref> Turner then saved a [[Nani (footballer)|Nani]] penalty kick in the Revolution's Eastern Conference semifinal 3–1 victory over [[Orlando City SC]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/matchcenter/orlvsne-11-29-2020/ |title=Orlando 1–3 New England |date=November 29, 2020 |accessdate=July 8, 2022}}</ref>

In 2020, New England Revolution fans voted Turner the Revolution team MVP, his teammates voted him the Revolution Player of the Year,<ref>{{cite web|title=New England Revolution announce 2020 team awards|url=https://www.revolutionsoccer.net/post/2020/11/12/new-england-revolution-announce-2020-team-awards|website=revolutionsoccer.net/|publisher=New England Revolution|accessdate=November 14, 2020|date=November 13, 2020}}</ref> and Revolution [[Supporters' groups|supporters group]] the [[Midnight Riders (MLS supporters association)|Midnight Riders]] voted him the Midnight Riders Man of the Year.<ref>{{Cite web|date=March 7, 2012|title=Man of the Year Award|url=https://www.midnightriders.com/events-awards/man-of-the-year-award/|access-date=December 7, 2020|website=The Midnight Riders|language=en-US}}</ref>

====2021 season====
Turner continued to play well over the course of the [[2021 New England Revolution season|2021 season]], recording a 1.25 goals-against average, a 73.2 save percentage, and five shutouts over 28 starts. He was named the 2021 MLS Goalkeeper of the Year. He was the first keeper in Revolution history to earn the honor. During the 2021 season, Turner was named [[2021 MLS All-Star Game]] MVP after making two penalty saves against the [[Liga MX]] All stars on August 25, 2021. He also set a club record with 17 regular season wins, which was tied for the most in the league.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wcvb.com/article/revolution-matt-turner-2021-mls-goalkeeper-of-the-year/38324067# |title=New England Revolution's Matt Turner named 2021 MLS Goalkeeper of the Year |date=November 22, 2021 |accessdate=July 8, 2022}}</ref>

===Arsenal===
On February 11, 2022, it was announced that Turner and [[Premier League]] club [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] had agreed to a deal that would see him move to the London club in the summer of 2022. He was Arsenal's first summer signing of the window.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/60354447|title=Arsenal to sign US goalkeeper Turner|work=BBC Sport }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.revolutionsoccer.net/news/matt-turner-set-to-join-arsenal-in-summer-transfer|title=Matt Turner set to join Arsenal in summer transfer &#124; New England Revolution|website=revolutionsoccer.net}}</ref> Arsenal reportedly paid a transfer fee of $6 million that could potentially rise to $10 million.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/official-new-england-revolution-transfer-matt-turner-to-arsenal-for-summer|title=Official: New England Revolution transfer Matt Turner to Arsenal for summer &#124; MLSSoccer.com|website=mlssoccer}}</ref> On June 27, 2022, Arsenal announced Turner as the new player on a long-term contract. Turner was given the number 30 shirt.<ref name="Arsenal Signing"/>

Turner started Arsenal's July 8, 2022, friendly at [[1. FC Nürnberg]]. Arsenal won the match 5–3.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/report?gameId=636882 |title=Gabriel Jesus scores twice as Arsenal fight back to claim preseason win over Nurnberg |date=July 8, 2022 |accessdate=July 8, 2022}}</ref>

On September 8, 2022, Turner started against [[FC Zürich]] during the [[2022–23 UEFA Europa League]] group stage, which Arsenal won 2–1.<ref>{{cite web|date=September 8, 2022|title=FC Zürich vs. Arsenal-Football Match Report-September 8, 2022|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/report?gameId=652684|access-date=September 8, 2022|publisher=ESPN}}</ref> On October 6, Turner recorded his first clean sheet for the club in a 3–0 win against [[FK Bodø/Glimt|Bodø/Glimt]] at [[Emirates Stadium|the Emirates]] in the subsequent Europa League fixture.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Arsenal ease to victory over Bodo/Glimt |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63151667 |access-date=October 20, 2022}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=mlssoccer |title=Matt Turner earns first clean sheet with Arsenal in Europa League win {{!}} MLSSoccer.com |url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/matt-turner-earns-first-clean-sheet-with-arsenal-in-europa-league-win |access-date=October 20, 2022 |website=mlssoccer |language=en}}</ref> Following this, Turner recorded back-to-back clean sheets in further Europa League wins against Bodø/Glimt again and [[PSV Eindhoven]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=Saka scores as Arsenal near Europa League knockouts |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63236405 |access-date=October 20, 2022}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |title=Xhaka goal edges Arsenal closer to last 16 |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63319350 |access-date=October 20, 2022}}</ref> The former of the two performances, a 1–0 away win at the [[Aspmyra Stadion]] on October 13, drew praise from Arsenal manager [[Mikel Arteta]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Verri |first=Matt |date=October 13, 2022 |title=Arteta hails 'phenomenal' display from unlikely Arsenal star |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/mikel-arteta-matt-turner-arsenal-bodo-glimt-b1032584.html |access-date=October 20, 2022 |website=Evening Standard |language=en}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:2022 FIFA World Cup United States 1–1 Wales - (38).jpg|thumb|right|Turner (in orange) with [[United States men's national soccer team|USMNT]] in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar]]
Turner made his international debut on January 31, 2021, for the United States men's national team, starting in goal against [[Trinidad and Tobago national football team|Trinidad and Tobago]] in a friendly match.<ref>{{cite web|title=Revolution goalkeeper Matt Turner backstops US men's team in shutout of Trinidad & Tobago|url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/2021/02/01/sports/revolution-goalkeeper-matt-turner-backstops-us-mens-team-shutout-trinidad-tobago|publisher=The Boston Globe|accessdate=February 1, 2021|date=January 31, 2021}}</ref> Turner recorded a clean sheet, saving a penalty kick from [[Alvin Jones (footballer)|Alvin Jones]].

Turner started in all six games for the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]] in the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup]]. He recorded five clean sheets, including one in a 1–0 win against [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]] in the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup Final|final]]. For his performance, Turner was awarded "Best Goalkeeper" of the tournament.

In November 2022, Turner was selected for the [[United States men's national soccer team|USMNT]] [[2022 FIFA World Cup squads#United States|squad]] that competed in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2022/11/berhalter-names-26-player-usmnt-roster-for-2022-fifa-world-cup |title=Berhalter Names 26 Player USMNT Roster For 2022 FIFA World Cup |date=November 9, 2022 |publisher=[[United States Soccer Federation]] |access-date=November 10, 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.sportingnews.com/us/soccer/news/usa-world-cup-2022-roster-usmnt-squad-starting-xi-qatar/uqwz7kdurshiubk1nethvccj |title=USA World Cup 2022 roster: USMNT final squad and possible starting XI for Qatar |last=Bonn |first=Kyle |date=November 9, 2022 |publisher=[[Sporting News]] |access-date=November 10, 2022}}</ref> where he played as the primary goalkeeper and was one of three players to feature in all 360 minutes of the tournament for the American squad.<ref name="Turner nominated U.S. Soccer Player of the Year 2022">{{cite web |url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2022/12/making-the-case-matt-turner-for-biosteel-us-soccer-male-player-of-the-year |title=Making the Case: Matt Turner for BioSteel U.S. Soccer Male Player of the Year |date=December 20, 2022 |publisher=United States Soccer Federation |access-date=December 21, 2022}}</ref> Turner kept two [[clean sheet]]s in the tournament, becoming the first US goalkeeper to record multiple clean sheets in one edition of the FIFA World Cup since 1930.<ref name="Turner nominated U.S. Soccer Player of the Year 2022"/><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.arsenal.com/news/turner-creates-history-usa-reach-knockout-stage |title=Turner creates history as USA reach knockout stage |date=November 29, 2022 |publisher=Arsenal F.C. |access-date=November 30, 2022}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Turner is Jewish.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.jta.org/2022/11/18/sports/all-the-jewish-players-and-storylines-to-watch-in-the-2022-world-cup|title=All the Jewish players and storylines to watch in the 2022 World Cup|first=Jacob|last=Gurvis|date=November 18, 2022}}</ref> Turner and his father both obtained Lithuanian passports in 2020; Turner's paternal great-grandmother fled from religious persecution during [[World War II]], and as Jews emigrated from [[History of the Jews in Lithuania|Lithuania]].<ref name="mlssoccer">{{Cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/new-england-revolution-goalkeeper-matt-turner-explains-his-lithuanian-passport-a|title=New England Revolution goalkeeper Matt Turner explains his Lithuanian passport and European aspirations|last=Butler|first=Dylan|date=September 2, 2020|work=MLSSoccer.com}}</ref><ref name=applepodcasts /> His father's family is [[Jewish]], and they [[Anglicized]] their surname from "Turnovski" to ″Turner″ at [[Ellis Island]] when immigrating,<ref name=applepodcasts>{{Cite web|url=https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/goalkeeper-matt-turner-of-usmnt-new-england-revolution/id1513071118?i=1000528945436|title=Goalkeeper Matt Turner of USMNT & New England Revolution; |website=Fútbol with Grant Wahl Podcast | accessdate=October 26, 2021}}</ref> whereas his mother's family is [[Catholic]].<ref name=mlssoccer /><ref name=applepodcasts />

He married ex-NFL cheerleader Ashley Herron in 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://newyorkfolk.com/sport/meet-new-arsenal-star-matt-turners-stunning-ex-nfl-cheerleader-wag-ashley/|title=Meet new Arsenal star Matt Turner's stunning ex-NFL cheerleader Wag Ashley|first=New|last=Yorker|date=February 12, 2022}}</ref><ref name="auto"/> Their son Easton was born on June 29, 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/Cfj2uq-oPiz/|title=Matt Turner on Instagram|website=Instagram}}</ref> In March 2023, the couple announced they were expecting a daughter.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/USMNT/status/1640529868742963200|title=U.S. Men's National Soccer Team on Twitter|website=Twitter}}</ref>
