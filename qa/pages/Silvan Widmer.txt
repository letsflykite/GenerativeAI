'''Silvan Dominic Widmer''' (born 5 March 1993) is a Swiss professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[right-back]] for [[Bundesliga]] club [[1. FSV Mainz 05|Mainz 05]] and the [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland national team]].

==Club career==
Widmer began his playing career at SV Würenlos and [[FC Baden]] before he moved on to [[FC Aarau]] where he rose through their youth ranks, soon playing regularly for Aarau's reserve team. He eventually made his league debut on 23 July 2011 against [[FC Winterthur]], starting the match.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.football.ch/sfl/777811/de/Teamaufgebot.aspx |title=FC Winterthur v. FC Aarau |work=Swiss Football League |publisher=Schweizerischer Fussballverband |access-date=15 April 2012 }}</ref> He scored his first [[Swiss Challenge League]] goal in a 2–2 home draw against [[FC St. Gallen]] on 21 November 2011.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.football.ch/sfl/777811/de/Teamaufgebot.aspx |title=FC Aarau v. FC St. Gallen |work=Swiss Football League |publisher=Schweizerischer Fussballverband |access-date=15 April 2012 }}</ref>

He signed with [[Udinese Calcio|Udinese]] in the summer of 2012, but remained at Aarau for the 2012–13 season.

On 12 July 2018, Widmer signed with [[FC Basel|Basel]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://m.corrieredellosport.it/news/calcio/calcio-mercato/2018/07/12-45288555/calciomercato_udinese_ufficiale_ceduto_widmer_al_basilea/|title=Widmer signed with Basel|access-date=12 July 2018}}</ref> for a club record €5.5 million.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[Kicker (sports magazine)|Kicker]]|page=209|title=Europas Top-Ligen 2018/2019}}</ref>

Under trainer [[Marcel Koller]] Basel won the [[2018–19 Swiss Cup|Swiss Cup in the 2018–19 season]]. In the first round Basel beat FC Montlingen 3–0, in the second round [[FC Echallens|Echallens Région]] 7–2 and in the round of 16 [[FC Winterthur|Winterthur]] 1–0. In the quarter-finals [[FC Sion|Sion]] were defeated 4–2 after extra time and in the semi-finals [[FC Zürich|Zürich]] were defeated 3–1. All these games were played away from home. The final was held on 19 May 2019 in the [[Stade de Suisse|Stade de Suisse Wankdorf Bern]] against [[FC Thun|Thun]]. Striker [[Albian Ajeti]] scored the first goal, [[Fabian Frei]] the second for Basel, then [[Dejan Sorgić]] netted a goal for Thun, but the end result was 2–1 for Basel.<ref name="fcb-achiv-fcb-thun-cupfinal-2018–19">{{cite web | last = Verein "Basler Fussballarchiv” | url = https://www.fcb-archiv.ch/saison/spiele?command=detail&id=31396 | title = FC Basel - FC Thun 2:1 (1:0) | publisher = Verein "Basler Fussballarchiv” | access-date = 16 November 2019}}</ref> Widmer played in five cup games and scored one goal, this being the winning goal in the match against Winterthur.<ref name="fcb-achiv-winterthur-fcb-2018–19">{{cite web | last = Verein "Basler Fussballarchiv” | url = https://www.fcb-archiv.ch/saison/spiele?command=detail&id=31384 | title = FC Winterthur - FC Basel 0:1 (0:0) | publisher = Verein "Basler Fussballarchiv” | access-date = 16 November 2019}}</ref>

Widmer moved to [[Bundesliga]] club [[1. FSV Mainz 05]] in July 2021, having agreed a three-year contract.<ref>{{cite web |title=Mainz 05 holt EM-Fahrer Silvan Widmer vom FC Basel |url=https://www.kicker.de/mainz-holt-em-fahrer-widmer-der-bringt-alles-mit-865018/artikel |website=kicker |access-date=9 July 2021 |language=de |date=9 July 2021}}</ref>

==International career==
Widmer was a Switzerland youth international having played both at [[Switzerland national under-19 football team|under-19]] and [[Switzerland national under-21 football team|under-21]] level.

He made his debut for the [[Switzerland national football team|senior national squad]] on 12 October 2014 in a [[UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying Group E]] game against [[San Marino national football team|San Marino]] as a 59th-minute substitute for [[Stephan Lichtsteiner]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[UEFA]]|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro/season=2016/matches/round=2000446/match=2013885/index.html|title=San Marino v Switzerland game report|date=14 October 2014}}</ref>

Widmer scored his first international goal for Switzerland on 6 September 2020, in a 1–1 [[UEFA Nations League]] draw against [[Germany national football team|Germany]].

In 2021 he was called up to the national team for the [[UEFA European Championship|2020 UEFA European Championship]].<ref>https://www.uefa.com / uefaeuro-2020 / match / 2024485 - switzerland-vs-spain / lineups /? iv = true</ref>
