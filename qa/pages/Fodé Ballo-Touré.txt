'''Fodé Ballo-Touré''' (born 3 January 1997) is a Senegalese professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[left-back]] for {{Italian football updater|Milan}} club [[A.C. Milan|AC Milan]]. Born in France, he plays for the [[Senegal national football team|Senegal national team]].<ref name="soccerway">{{Soccerway|fode-ballo-toure/320252}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Lille===
Ballo-Touré developed through the [[Paris Saint-Germain Academy|Paris Saint-Germain]] academy. On 1 July 2017, his contract with PSG expired and he signed with [[Lille OSC]] managed by Head Coach [[Marcelo Bielsa]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Transferts-fode-ballo-toure-psg-a-lille/815016 |title=Transferts : Fodé Ballo-Touré (ex-PSG) à Lille |publisher=[[L'Équipe]] |accessdate=6 August 2017 |language=French}}</ref> He made his [[Ligue 1]] debut on 6 August 2017 in a 3–0 home win against [[FC Nantes|Nantes]]. He started the match and was replaced by [[Rominigue Kouamé]] at halftime.<ref name="soccerway2">{{cite web |url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2017/08/06/france/ligue-1/lille-olympique-sporting-club-metropole/fc-nantes/2464906/ |title=Lille vs. Nantes - 6 August 2017 - Soccerway |publisher=soccerway.com |accessdate=6 August 2017}}</ref>

===Monaco===
On 10 January 2019, Ballo-Touré signed with [[AS Monaco FC|Monaco]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Fode-ballo-toure-a-monaco-jusqu-en-2023-officiel/976140 |title=Fodé Ballo-Touré à Monaco jusqu'en 2023 (officiel) |publisher=[[L'Équipe]] |accessdate=10 January 2019 |language=French}}</ref>

===AC Milan===
On 18 July 2021, Ballo-Touré signed a contract with [[A.C. Milan|AC Milan]] until June 2025.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.acmilan.com/en/news/articles/media/2021-07-18/official-statement-fode-ballo-toure |title=Official Statement: Fodé Ballo-Touré |website=A.C. Milan |date=18 July 2021 }}</ref>

==International career==
Ballo-Touré was born in France to Malian father and Senegalese mother. He was a youth international for France.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.footmali.com/malifoot/fod%C3%A9-ballo-tour%C3%A9-un-autre-talent-dorigine-malienne-se-r%C3%A9v%C3%A8le-%C3%A0-l%E2%80%99hexagone+-+footmali.com |title=Fodé Ballo Touré : Un autre talent d'origine malienne se révèle à l'Hexagone |date=13 October 2016 |publisher=}}</ref> However, he decided to represent Senegal on senior level, over France and Mali. He made his debut for [[Senegal national football team|Senegal]] in a 0–0 draw against [[Congo national football team|Congo]] on 26 March 2021.<ref>{{Cite web |date=26 March 2021 |title=Congo vs Sénégal : Les compositions officielles - Equipe nationale Football, Football |url=https://wiwsport.com/2021/03/26/congo-vs-senegal-les-compositions-officielles/ |access-date=26 March 2021 |website=wiwsport |language=fr-FR}}</ref>

He was part of [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations squads#Senegal|Senegal's squad]] for the [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations]]; the Lions of Teranga went on to win the tournament for the first time in their history.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cafonline.com/total-africa-cup-of-nations/news/cisse-unveils-senegal-squad-hopes-for-elusive-totalenergies-afcon-title|title=Cisse unveils Senegal squad, hopes for elusive TotalEnergies AFCON title|publisher=[[Confederation of African Football]]|date=25 December 2021|access-date=28 March 2023|archive-date=9 January 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220109145849/https://www.cafonline.com/total-africa-cup-of-nations/news/cisse-unveils-senegal-squad-hopes-for-elusive-totalenergies-afcon-title|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Senegal 0-0 Egypt |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/60186276 |website=[[BBC Sport]] |access-date=28 March 2023 |date=7 February 2022}}</ref>

Ballo-Touré was appointed a [[National Order of the Lion|Grand Officer of the National Order of the Lion]] by [[President of Senegal]] [[Macky Sall]] following the nation's victory at the tournament.<ref name= "reuters">{{cite web |title=Cash prizes, real estate, and highest honours as Senegal celebrates Nations Cup heroes |url=https://www.reuters.com/lifestyle/sports/cash-prizes-real-estate-highest-honours-senegal-celebrates-nations-cup-heroes-2022-02-08/ |website=[[Reuters]] |access-date=28 March 2023 |date=8 February 2022}}</ref>
