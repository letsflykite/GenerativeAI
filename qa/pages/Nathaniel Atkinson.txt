'''Nathaniel Caleb Atkinson''' (born 13 June 1999) is an Australian professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[defender (association football)|right back]] for [[Scottish Premiership]] side [[Heart of Midlothian F.C.|Heart of Midlothian]].

==Club career==
===Early career===
Born in [[Launceston, Tasmania|Launceston]], [[Tasmania]], Atkinson was part of the youth setup at [[Riverside Olympic FC|Riverside Olympic]] before joining the NTC program at [[Football Tasmania]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Former Launceston Player to Trial for Melbourne City|url=https://ntjsa.org.au/former-launceston-player-to-trial-for-melbourne-city/|accessdate=1 January 2018|work=NTJSA|date=20 July 2015|archive-date=2 January 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180102073235/https://ntjsa.org.au/former-launceston-player-to-trial-for-melbourne-city/|url-status=dead}}</ref> In 2016, Atkinson joined the [[Melbourne City FC|Melbourne City]] academy after a successful trial with the club.<ref name="Teenager">{{cite news|title=Talented teenager motivated by soccer-mad family|url=http://www.examiner.com.au/story/3663149/talented-teenager-motivated-by-soccer-mad-family/|accessdate=1 January 2018|work=Examiner|date=13 January 2016}}</ref> He played with the [[Melbourne City FC Youth|Melbourne City NPL]] side in the [[National Premier Leagues Victoria|NPL Victoria]] from 2016 to 2017.<ref name="Teenager"/> Towards the end of the [[2016–17 Melbourne City FC season|2016–17 season]], Atkinson began training with the Melbourne City first team.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Cooling|first1=Andrew|title=Atkinson Trains With Melbourne City First Team|url=http://www.sliceofcheese.net/atkinson-trains-with-melbourne-city-first-team/|accessdate=1 January 2018|work=Slice of Cheese|date=16 March 2017}}</ref>

===Melbourne City===
Going into the [[2017–18 Melbourne City FC season|2017–18 season]], Atkinson was given the 37 shirt.<ref>{{cite web|title=Melbourne City Squad 2017–18|url=http://www.worldfootball.net/teams/melbourne-city-fc/2018/2/|website=worldfootball.net}}</ref> He made his competitive debut for the first team in a [[FFA Cup]] match against [[Peninsula Power FC|Peninsula Power]] at [[Dolphin Oval]] on 1 August 2017. He came on as a late stoppage time substitute for [[Bruno Fornaroli]] as Melbourne City won the match 2–0.<ref>{{cite web|title=Peninsula Power 0–2 Melbourne City|url=https://us.soccerway.com/matches/2017/08/01/australia/ffa-cup/peninsula-power/melbourne-heart-fc/2479827/|website=Soccerway}}</ref> On 10 December 2017, due to the suspension of Melbourne City's regular right-back [[Manny Muscat]], Atkinson made his [[A-League]] debut for the club against the [[Central Coast Mariners FC|Central Coast Mariners]]. Despite being named as a right back before the match, Atkinson played the match as a right winger as Melbourne City managed to win 1–0.<ref name="Debut">{{cite news|last1=Windley|first1=Matt|title=Young gun Nathaniel Atkinson praised by coach Warren Joyce after starring in Melbourne City win|url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/sport/football/a-league/young-gun-nathaniel-atkinson-praised-by-coach-warren-joyce-after-starring-in-melbourne-city-win/news-story/494a5ad6a333f3b020f4de577184c9a8|accessdate=1 January 2018|work=Herald Sun|date=10 December 2017}}</ref> After the match, Melbourne City head coach [[Warren Joyce]] praised Atkinson, saying "I thought he took his opportunity well. He’s one that caught my eye as soon as I came to the club."<ref name="Debut"/>

On 12 January 2018, after a promising start, Atkinson signed a two-year senior contract with Melbourne City.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.smh.com.au/sport/soccer/nathaniel-atkinson-gets-twoyear-deal-with-melbourne-city-20180112-h0h8yj.html|title=Nathaniel Atkinson gets two-year deal with Melbourne City|first=Michael|last=Lynch|newspaper=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|date=12 January 2018}}</ref>

In September 2020, Atkinson signed a two-year contract with [[Perth Glory FC|Perth Glory]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://theworldgame.sbs.com.au/glory-sign-nabbout-on-two-year-deal|title=Glory sign Nabbout on two-year deal|date=1 September 2020|accessdate=1 September 2020|website=[[The World Game]]}}</ref> However, three weeks later, his contract was terminated due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]] situation,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.perthglory.com.au/news/squad-update|title=Squad Update|first=Gareth|last=Morgan|website=[[Perth Glory FC|Perth Glory]]|date=23 September 2020}}</ref> allowing him to sign another two-year contract with Melbourne City.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.melbournecityfc.com.au/news/melbourne-city-re-signs-atkinson-berenguer-and-griffiths|title=Melbourne City re-signs Atkinson, Berenguer and Griffiths|website=[[Melbourne City FC|Melbourne City]]|date=23 September 2020}}</ref>

===Heart of Midlothian===

On 24 December 2021, Atkinson signed a three-and-a-half year contract with [[Scottish Premiership]] club [[Heart of Midlothian F.C.|Heart of Midlothian]], with the deal going through on 10 January 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Match made in heaven for Nathaniel {{!}} Heart Of Midlothian Football Club|url=https://www.heartsfc.co.uk/news/article/match-made-in-heaven-for-nathaniel|access-date=10 January 2022|website=www.heartsfc.co.uk}}</ref>

==International career==
In January 2017, Atkinson was selected to train with the [[Australia men's national under-20 soccer team|Australia U20]] side.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Shaw|first1=Rob|title=Riverside Olympic's Nathaniel Atkinson called up by Young Socceroos|url=http://www.examiner.com.au/story/4437984/national-call-up-rewards-atkinson/|accessdate=1 January 2018|work=Examiner|date=31 January 2017}}</ref>

In November 2019 he was one of four players suspended by the Australia U23 due to "unprofessional conduct".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/50471012|title=Four Australia Under-23 players banned after complaint from woman|work=BBC Sport |date=19 November 2019}}</ref>

Atkinson qualified for the [[2020 Summer Olympics|Tokyo 2020 Olympics.]] He was part of the [[Olyroos]] Olympic squad. The team beat Argentina in their first group match but were unable to win another match. They were therefore not in medal contention.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Australian Olympic Team for Tokyo 2021|url=https://www.theroar.com.au/olympics/australian-olympic-team/|access-date=22 February 2022|website=The Roar|language=en-US}}</ref>

He was called up to the senior [[Australia men's national soccer team|Australia]] squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – AFC third round|2022 FIFA World Cup qualifiers]] on 24 and 29 March 2022.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.socceroos.com.au/news/march-squad-announcement|title=CONFIRMED: Fresh faces & experienced Socceroos named as Australia gets set for Japan and Jeddah|publisher=[[Football Australia]]|website=Socceroos|date=17 March 2022}}</ref>

Atkinson was named in Australia's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in November 2022.<ref>{{Cite web |date=8 November 2022 |title=SOCCEROOS SQUAD ANNOUNCED: FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022™ |url=https://www.socceroos.com.au/news/socceroos-squad-announced-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022tm |access-date=9 November 2022 |website=Socceroos |language=en}}</ref>
