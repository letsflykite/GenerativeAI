'''José María Giménez de Vargas''' ({{IPA-es|xoˈse maˈɾi.a xiˈmenes ðe ˈβaɾɣas}};{{efn-lg|In isolation, ''Vargas'' is pronounced {{IPA-es|ˈbaɾɣas|}}.}} born 20 January 1995) is a Uruguayan professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Centre-back|central defender]] for [[La Liga]] club  [[Atlético Madrid]] and the [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay national team]].

After starting out his professional career with [[Danubio FC]], Giménez moved to Spain, signing for [[Atlético Madrid]] ahead of the [[2013–14 Atlético Madrid season|2013–14 season]].<ref name="Sky Sports News">{{cite web|title=Atlético Madrid complete signing of teenage defender Jose María Giménez|url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/11838/8670571/Atletico-Madrid-complete-signing-of-teenage-defender-Jose-Maria-Gimenez|work=Sky Sports News|access-date=27 April 2013}}</ref> A solid, powerful and fast moving defender, Giménez settled quickly with the club, winning four major titles with the latter, including the [[La Liga]] title in his debut season.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sport24.co.za/Soccer/International/Atletico-Madrid-win-La-Liga-20140517|title=Atletico Madrid win La Liga|publisher=Sport 24|date=17 May 2014|access-date=29 January 2015}}</ref>

At international level, Giménez made his Uruguay debut in 2013, and has since represented the nation at three [[FIFA World Cup]]s and four editions of the [[Copa América]].

==Club career==
===Danubio===
Born in [[Toledo, Uruguay|Toledo]], Giménez made his professional debut for [[Danubio F.C.]] in the [[Uruguayan Primera División]] on 17 November 2012 (when the manager was [[Juan Ramón Carrasco]]) against [[Club Atlético River Plate (Montevideo)|River Plate]] in which he started and played the full 90 minutes as Danubio lost the match 2–0.<ref>{{cite web|title=DANUBIO VS. RIVER PLATE 0 - 2|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2012/11/17/uruguay/primera-division/danubio-futbol-club/ca-river-plate/1380964/|work=Soccerway|access-date=27 April 2013}}</ref>

===Atlético Madrid===
[[File:Loko-AM2018 (2).jpg|thumb|Giménez with Atlético in 2018]]
On 25 April 2013, it was confirmed that Giménez had signed for €900,000 with Spanish side [[Atlético Madrid]], and would join the club in the pre-season of the [[2013–14 La Liga|2013–14 season]].<ref name="Sky Sports News"/> On 14 September, Giménez made his ''Atleti'' – and [[La Liga]] – debut, starting in a 4–2 home win over [[UD Almería]].<ref>[http://espnfc.com/en/report/373177/report.html?soccernet=true&cc=3888 Atleti maintain perfect start] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131023103025/http://espnfc.com/en/report/373177/report.html?soccernet=true&cc=3888 |date=23 October 2013 }}; [[ESPN FC]], 14 September 2013</ref>

Giménez scored his first goal for the club on 6 December 2014, opening a 2–0 win away to [[Elche CF]] to move Atlético into second place.<ref>{{cite news|title=Elche 0-2 Atl Madrid|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/30330862|access-date=6 December 2014|work=[[BBC Sport]]|date=6 December 2014}}</ref> He also scored a header against their biggest rival [[Real Madrid C.F.|Real Madrid]] in Atlético's 2–0 win in the [[Copa del Rey]] in January 2015.

On 20 February 2019, Giménez scored the opener in 2-0 home win against Italian champions [[Juventus]] to give Atlético the advantage in the first leg of the [[2018-19 UEFA Champions League knockout phase|Round of 16]] fixture in the [[UEFA Champions League]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Giménez and Godín put Atlético Madrid in charge against Juventus|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2019/feb/20/atletico-madrid-juventus-champions-league-match-report|date=20 February 2019|work=The Guardian}}</ref>

Ahead of the 2019–20 season, Giménez was made third captain of the team, behind [[Koke (footballer, born 1992)|Koke]] and [[Jan Oblak]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://en.atleticodemadrid.com/noticias/koke-oblak-gimenez-saul-to-captain-atleti-in-2019-20-season|title=Koke, Oblak, Giménez, Saúl to captain Atleti in 2019/20 season|date=16 August 2019|publisher=Atlético Madrid|access-date=22 August 2019}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:Go_Uruguay!_-_Arriba_Uruguay!_-_140619-6398-jikatu_(14307280607).jpg|thumb|Giménez (upper row, second from the right) lining up for a team photo during the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]]]]
Giménez participated for [[Uruguay national under-20 football team|Uruguay]] at the [[2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup]] as the team finished as runner-up to [[France national under-20 football team|France]].<ref name=fifa>{{cite news|url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/players/player=368655/profile-detail.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140911131829/http://www.fifa.com/worldcup/players/player=368655/profile-detail.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=11 September 2014|title=Jose GIMENEZ|access-date=22 June 2014|work=FIFA.com}}</ref>

He debuted for the [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay senior team]] in a [[2014 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONMEBOL)|2014 FIFA World Cup qualifier]] against [[Colombia national football team|Colombia]] on 10 September 2013.<ref name=fifa/>

On 2 June 2014, Giménez was named in Uruguay's squad for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]] finals.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/football/teams/uruguay/10810104/Uruguay-World-Cup-2014-squad.html|title=Uruguay World Cup 2014 squad|work=[[The Daily Telegraph]]|date=2 June 2014|access-date=18 June 2014}}</ref> The 19-year-old defender made his tournament debut against [[England national football team|England]] in the team's second group match, deputising for the injured captain [[Diego Lugano]] in a 2–1 victory for ''La Celeste''.
<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.gazzetta.it/Calcio/Mondiali/21-06-2014/mondiali-uruguay-lugano-ancora-out-contro-italia-ritocca-baby-gimenez-80999714413.shtml|title=Mondiali, Uruguay, Lugano ancora out: contro l'Italia (ri)tocca a baby Gimenez|trans-title=World Cup, Uruguay, Lugano still out: against Italy they will turn to baby Gimenez|language=it|date=21 June 2014|access-date=22 June 2014|work=[[La Gazzetta dello Sport]]}}</ref> He went on to start in the final group match – a 1–0 win over [[Italy national football team|Italy]] – and the 2–0 [[2014 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Round of 16|round of 16]] loss to [[Colombia national football team|Colombia]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.fifa.com/worldfootball/statisticsandrecords/players/player=368655/index.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130619080516/http://www.fifa.com/worldfootball/statisticsandrecords/players/player=368655/index.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=19 June 2013|title=José María Giménez|access-date=13 June 2015|work=FIFA}}</ref>

Giménez scored his first international goal in a 1–0 friendly win against [[South Korea national football team|South Korea]] on 8 September 2014.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.espnfc.com.au/blog/espn-fc-united-blog/68/post/2142706/tim-vickery-four-up-and-coming-prospects-from-brazil-bolivia-peru-and-uruguay|title=Stakes are high in South American friendlies as players look to impress|date=12 November 2014|work=ESPN}}</ref> In May 2015, he was named in [[2015 Copa América squads#Uruguay|Uruguay's squad]] for the [[2015 Copa América]] by coach [[Óscar Tabárez]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Uruguay confirm Copa América squad numbers as Rolán lands Suárez's No.9 |url=http://www.ca2015.com/en/news/uruguay-confirm-copa-america-squad-numbers-with-rolan-taking-suarezs-no9/1g4mw7am9khxi1dcpc7jxfh8f4 |access-date=9 June 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150612033334/http://www.ca2015.com/en/news/uruguay-confirm-copa-america-squad-numbers-with-rolan-taking-suarezs-no9/1g4mw7am9khxi1dcpc7jxfh8f4 |archive-date=12 June 2015 }}</ref> On 20 June, he scored Uruguay's goal in a 1–1 draw with [[Paraguay national football team|Paraguay]] which saw both teams progress to the [[2015 Copa América knockout stage|knockout stage]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/33085636|title=Uruguay 1-1 Paraguay|date=20 June 2015|work=BBC}}</ref>

In May 2018 he was named in Uruguay's provisional 26-man [[Uruguay national football team|squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.worldsoccer.com/world-cup/uruguay-world-cup-fixtures-399702 |title=Uruguay World Cup Fixtures, Squad, Group, Guide |publisher=World Soccer |first1 =Sam |last1=Tremlett |date=15 June 2018 |access-date=15 June 2018 }}</ref> On 15 June, with the score tied at 0–0, he scored the winning goal in the final minute of Uruguay's opening game of the World Cup against [[Egypt national football team|Egypt]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Emons |first1=Michael |title=Egypt 1–0 Uruguay |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44408321 |publisher=BBC Sport |access-date=15 June 2018 |date=15 June 2018}}</ref>

In March 2019, Tabárez included Giménez in the final 23-man Uruguay squad for the [[2019 Copa América]] in Brazil.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://copaamerica2019.live/uruguay-team-squads/ |title=Uruguay Team Squads Copa America 2019 |work=copaamerica2019.live |date=7 March 2019 |access-date=13 June 2019 |archive-date=16 April 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210416112817/https://copaamerica2019.live/uruguay-team-squads/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> He headed the equaliser in the 2–2 group draw with [[Japan national football team|Japan]] in [[Porto Alegre]],<ref>{{cite news |title=Gimenez Header Helps Uruguay Draw Japan in Copa America |url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2019/06/21/jose-gimenez-header-sees-uruguay-draw-japan-copa-america |access-date=21 June 2020 |work=[[Sports Illustrated]] |date=20 June 2019}}</ref> and was the only Uruguayan in the Team of the Tournament despite a quarter-final exit.<ref name=team/>

==Personal life==
José is married to Regina Iafolla, who he had been dating since they were teenagers. They have two children together, Lautaro and Luciano.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ohmyfootball.com/news/623/gimenez-wife-regina-iafolla|date=12 December 2020|title=Jose Gimenez Wife - Regina Iafolla }}</ref>
 
On 22 September 2020, Giménez tested positive for the [[COVID-19|coronavirus]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.atleticodemadrid.com/noticias/jose-maria-gimenez-tests-positive-for-covid-19|title=José María Giménez tests positive for Covid-19|date=22 September 2020|access-date=24 June 2020|publisher=Atlético Madrid}}</ref>
