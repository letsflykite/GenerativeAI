'''Janny Sikazwe''' (born 1979) is a former [[Zambia]]n [[association football|international football]] [[referee (association football)|referee]].
He got his big break in 2008 at [[COSAFA U-20 Challenge Cup]] in [[South Africa]] when he was called to replace another referee who had failed a fitness test.{{r|espn}}

On 7 January 2023 he officiated his last career match between Napsa Stars and Red Arrows.

He was one of the referees for the [[2015 Africa Cup of Nations]]. He refereed the [[2016 FIFA Club World Cup Final]] When in Japan, and the [[2017 Africa Cup of Nations Final]] in Gabon.<ref name="espn">{{Cite news |title='Sikazwe could referee the World Cup final' |work=ESPN.com |url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/fifa-world-cup/story/3518743/world-cup-zambian-janny-sikazwe-could-referee-the-final |access-date=10 June 2018}}</ref> He was selected to officiate at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia, and made his debut in the Belgium vs Panama Group G game on 18 June. The match also saw him going into the record books as he became the first Zambian referee to officiate a game at the FIFA World Cup finals.<ref>{{Cite web |date=19 June 2018 |title=Reverential salute for referee Janny Sikazwe |url=https://zambianfootball.co.zm/referee-sikazwe-culminates-reverential-pantheon/ |access-date=12 January 2022 |website=ZamFoot}}</ref>

Sikazwe also refereed at the [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations]], where he came under scrutiny for incorrectly calling for full-time twice in the Group F game between [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations Group F|Mali and Tunisia]] on 12 January 2022: firstly at the 86th minute, and again before the 91st minute by about 17 seconds.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Darwin |first=Stephen |date=12 January 2022 |title=Crazy scenes at Afcon as referee Janny Sikazwe twice blows too early for full-time in Mali's 1-0 win over Tunisia |url=https://www.goal.com/en-om/news/crazy-scenes-afcon-referee-janny-sikazwe-mali-1-0-tunisia/blt31c8770f3e0f5a3f |access-date=12 January 2022 |website=[[Goal (website)|Goal]]}}</ref> He announced the return of the match after 25 minutes of stopping to complete three minutes, but the Tunisian team refused to complete it. A forensic report who also from his country tried to protect him with his stated that Sikazwe suffered [[heat stroke]], which contributed to his mishandling of the match.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Lawless |first=Josh |date=13 January 2022 |title=AFCON Official Explains Why Referee From Tunisia Vs Mali Game Blew Early For Full-Time TWICE |url=https://www.sportbible.com/football/official-explains-why-referee-from-tunisia-vs-mali-game-blew-early-20220113 |access-date=25 January 2022 |website=[[SPORTbible]] |publisher=The LADbible Group Ltd}}</ref>
