'''Mason Tony Mount''' (born 10 January 1999) is an English professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as an [[Attacking midfielder|attacking]] or [[central midfielder]] for {{English football updater|ManchesU}} club [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] and the [[England national football team|England national team]]. 

Mount began his senior club career with [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]], and joined [[SBV Vitesse|Vitesse]] and [[Derby County F.C.|Derby County]] on consecutive [[Loan (association football)|loan]]s between 2017 and 2019. He established himself as an integral player for Chelsea in the following years, and won the [[UEFA Champions League]], the [[UEFA Super Cup]], and the [[2021 FIFA Club World Cup|FIFA Club World Cup]] in 2021. He was named [[Chelsea F.C. Player of the Year|Chelsea Player of the Year]] in the 2020–21 and 2021–22 seasons. Mount was signed by Manchester United in July 2023 for an initial transfer fee of £55 million.

Mount won the [[UEFA European Under-19 Championship]] with the [[England national under-19 football team|England national under-19 team]] in 2017. He made his senior international debut in 2019, at age 20, and was part of the England team at [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

===Manchester United===

On 5 July 2023, Mount signed for [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] on an initial five-year contract until June 2028, with the option to extend for a further year.<ref>{{cite web |title=United sign Mason Mount |url=https://www.manutd.com/en/news/detail/official-man-utd-sign-mason-mount-from-chelsea |website=www.manutd.com |date=5 July 2023 |access-date=5 July 2023 |language=en}}</ref> United paid Chelsea a guaranteed transfer fee of £55 million, with the deal containing potential add-ons of £5 million, and Mount took on the prestigious number seven jersey, which was last worn by [[Cristiano Ronaldo]] prior to his departure in the previous season.<ref>{{cite news |title=Mason Mount: Erik ten Hag's Man Utd plans were key in £55m transfer from Chelsea |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/66063196 |website=BBC Sport |date=5 July 2023 |access-date=5 July 2023}}</ref>

Mount played youth international football for England at [[England national under-16 football team|under-16]], [[England national under-17 football team|under-17]], [[England national under-18 football team|under-18]], [[England national under-19 football team|under-19]] and [[England national under-21 football team|under-21]] levels.<ref name="englandyouthstats">{{cite web |title=Mason Mount's matches |url=https://www.besoccer.com/player/matches/m-mount-307334 |website=besoccer.com |access-date=1 June 2023}}</ref> Mount represented the under-17s at the [[2016 UEFA European Under-17 Championship]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Veevers |first=Nicholas |date=4 May 2016 |title=England under-17s squad named for Euro Championship |url=http://www.thefa.com/news/2016/may/04/euro-finals-squad-announced-040516 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190721235740/http://www.thefa.com/news/2016/may/04/euro-finals-squad-announced-040516 |archive-date=21 July 2019 |access-date=10 October 2018 |publisher=The Football Association}}</ref>

Mount was included in the under-19 team for the [[2017 UEFA European Under-19 Championship]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=26 June 2017 |title=Squad for U19 Euro |url=http://www.thefa.com/news/2017/jun/26/england-u19-squad-for-euros-260617 |url-status=live |publisher=The Football Association}}</ref> He provided the assist for [[Lukas Nmecha]] to score the winning goal against [[Portugal national under-19 football team|Portugal]] in the final.<ref name="U19">{{Cite web |last=Walker |first=Andy |date=15 July 2017 |title=Portugal 1–2 England U19s |access-date=10 October 2018 |publisher=The Football Association}}</ref> He was subsequently named [[UEFA European Under-19 Championship#Golden Player Award|Golden Player]] of the tournament.<ref ===Senior===

Following his impressive season with Vitesse, Mount was invited by manager Gareth Southgate to train with the [[England national football team|senior team]] for a week ahead of the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=Rooney |first=Will |date=18 May 2018 |title=Portsmouth-born Mount to train with Southgate's England squad |work=The News |location=Portsmouth October 2018}}</ref> He was called up to the senior team for the [[UEFA Nations League]] matches against [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]] and [[Spain national football team|Spain]] in October 2018.<ref>{{Cite news |date=4 October 2018 |title=James Maddison, Jadon Sancho and Mason Mount named in England squad |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45744731 ==Personal life==
Mount has been best friends with fellow England international [[Declan Rice]] since childhood.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Steinberg |first=Jacob |date=29 November 2019 |title=Mason Mount and Declan Rice: 'We're always together. They call us Dumb and Dumber' |newspaper=The Guardian |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2019/nov/29/mason-mount-declan-rice-chelsea-west-ham-england-childhood-friends |url-status=live |access-date=30 June 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210204070509/https://www.theguardian.com/football/2019/nov/29/mason-mount-declan-rice-chelsea-west-ham-england-childhood-friends |archive-date=4 February 2021}}</ref> Mount supports Portsmouth, citing former Portsmouth players such as [[Peter Crouch]], [[Jermain Defoe]] and [[Nwankwo Kanu]] as his footballing heroes whilst growing up.<ref name="GrowingUpFA" /> He attended [[Purbrook Park School]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.chelseafc.com/en/videos/v/2022/01/10/mason-mount-surprises-his-old-school-----free-kick-challenge-wit-hVf6LDi1 |title=Mason Mount surprises his old school! Free-kick challenge with the kids!|publisher=Chelsea F.C. |access-date=10 January 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.purbrookparkschool.co.uk/news/?pid=3&nid=1&storyid=84 |title=Mason Mount Visit |publisher=purbrookparkschool.co.uk |access-date=10 January 2022}}</ref> In 2021, Mount became a patron of the charity [[Together for Short Lives]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.togetherforshortlives.org.uk/mason-mount-chelsea-and-england-midfielder-named-as-patron-for-together-for-short-lives/ |title=Mason Mount, Chelsea and England midfielder, named as Patron for Together for Short Lives |publisher=togetherforshortlives.org.uk |access-date=15 June 2022}}</ref>

Mount was the victim of a campaign of harassment by [[TikToker]] Orla Melissa Sloan between 2021 and 2023, along with former team-mate [[Billy Gilmour]]. Sloan was convicted of stalking the pair in June 2023, as well as of harassment against [[Ben Chilwell]].<ref>{{cite web |title=TikToker who stalked Chelsea star Mason Mount sentenced |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-london-65967497 |publisher=[[BBC News]] |access-date=28 June 2023 |date=20 June 2023}}</ref>
