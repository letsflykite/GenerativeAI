'''Nico Cedric Schlotterbeck''' (born 1 December 1999) is a German professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] for [[Bundesliga]] club [[Borussia Dortmund]] and the [[Germany national football team|Germany national team]].<ref name="worldfootball">{{cite web |url=https://www.worldfootball.net/player_summary/nico-schlotterbeck/ |title=Nico Schlotterbeck |website=WorldFootball.net |publisher=HEIM:SPIEL |access-date=16 March 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title= "Mein Bruder hat mir einiges berichtet": Union holt Nico Schlotterbeck | url = https://www.kicker.de/780842/artikel/_mein_bruder_hat_mir_einiges_berichtet_union_holt_nico_schlotterbeck | date = 31 July 2020 |access-date= 26 March 2022|website=kicker|language=de}}</ref>

==Club career==

=== SC Freiburg ===
Schlotterbeck debuted for [[SC Freiburg]] on 9 March 2019, coming on as a half-time substitute for [[Philipp Lienhart]] in the 2–1 home win against [[Hertha BSC]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/spieltag/1-bundesliga/2018-19/25/4243487/spielanalyse_sc-freiburg-7_hertha-bsc-29.html |title=Herthas Unglücksrabe heißt Ibisevic |trans-title=Hertha's misfortune is called Ibišević |website=kicker.de |publisher=[[Kicker (sports magazine)|kicker-sportmagazin]] |date=9 March 2019 |access-date=16 March 2019 |language=German}}</ref> In his final match for Freiburg, the club lost the [[2022 DFB-Pokal Final|DFB-Pokal Final]] on penalties but Schlotterbeck was awarded the man of the match.<ref>{{Cite web |last=GmbH |first=Perform Media Deutschland |date=22 May 2022 |title=SC Freiburg - RB Leipzig, Stimmen zum DFB-Pokalfinale: Man darf den alten Trainer nicht schlecht machen |url=https://www.spox.com/de/sport/fussball/dfbpokal/saison-2021-2022/finale/sc-freiburg-rb-leipzig/finale-stimmen-zum-leipziger-pokalsieg-tedesco-lobt-marsch.html |access-date=31 May 2022 |website=www.spox.com |language=de}}</ref>

==== Loan to Union Berlin ====
On 31 July 2020, Schlotterbeck joined [[1. FC Union Berlin|Union Berlin]] on a one-year loan.<ref>{{Cite web |date=31 July 2020|title=N ico Schlotterbeck Joins 1.FC Union Berlin on Loan|url= https://lastwordonfootball.com/2020/07/31/nico-schlotterbeck-joins-1-fc-union-berlin-on-loan/ |access-date=31 July 2020|website=Last Word On Football}}</ref>

=== Borussia Dortmund ===
On 2 May 2022, [[Borussia Dortmund]] announced the signing of Schlotterbeck on a five-year deal, starting from the 2022–23 season.<ref>{{cite web | title = BVB signs Nico Schlottbeck | url = https://www.bvb.de/eng/News/Overview/BVB-sign-Nico-Schlotterbeck| publisher=Borussia Dortmund | date=2 May 2022 | access-date=2 May 2022}} </ref> He joined for a reported €25 million transfer fee.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Johnson |first=Paul |date=3 May 2022 |title=Borussia Dortmund Sign Nico Schlotterbeck from Freiburg for €25m |url=https://www.fearthewall.com/2022/5/3/23054928/borussia-dortmund-sign-nico-schlotterbeck-from-freiburg-for-25m-niklas-sule-bayern-munich-bundesliga |access-date=3 May 2022 |website=[[SB Nation]]}}</ref>

==International career==
Schlotterbeck got his debut call-up to the [[Germany national football team|Germany national team]] under coach [[Hansi Flick]] for [[FIFA World Cup qualification]] in September 2021.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.dfb.de/news/detail/bundestrainer-flick-beruft-drei-neulinge-und-fuenf-rueckkehrer-231201/
|title=Bundestrainer Flick beruft drei Neulinge und fünf Rückkehrer |publisher=dfb.de |date=27 August 2021 |access-date=6 September 2021}}</ref> He made his debut against Israel in a friendly on 27 March 2022.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Flick on Schlotterbeck's Germany debut: "Hopefully, it will be a good lesson for him" |url=https://bulinews.com/news/12042/flick-schlotterbecks-germany-debut-hopefully-will-a-good-lesson-for-him |access-date=31 May 2022 |website=Bulinews |language=en}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Schlotterbeck is the nephew of former professional footballer [[Niels Schlotterbeck]], who played for Freiburg.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.scfreiburg.com/squad/keven-schlotterbeck |title=Keven Schlotterbeck |website=scfreiburg.com |publisher=[[SC Freiburg]] |access-date=3 February 2019 |language=German |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180924190702/https://www.scfreiburg.com/squad/keven-schlotterbeck |archive-date=24 September 2018}}</ref> His older brother, [[Keven Schlotterbeck|Keven]], is a professional footballer who played the 2019–20 season at Union Berlin on loan from SC Freiburg, to which he returned for the 2020–21 season. <ref>{{cite news |last=Frahm |first=Daniela |url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/startseite/744575/artikel_sc-mit-einem-schlotterbeck---oder-auch-mit-zwei.html |title=SC mit einem Schlotterbeck – oder auch mit zwei |trans-title=SC with a Schlotterbeck – or with two |website=kicker.de |publisher=[[Kicker (sports magazine)|kicker-sportmagazin]] |date=14 March 2019 |access-date=16 March 2019 |language=German}}</ref> Schlotterbeck's cousin, Sandrine, appeared on the seventh series of the German edition of [[Love Island (German TV series)|''Love Island'']] in 2022.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Möller |first=Domescu |title="Love Island"-Kandidatin Sandrine ist gleich mit zwei Ex-Unionern verwandt – raten Sie mal mit wem! |url=https://www.berliner-kurier.de/show/love-island/love-island-diese-reality-tv-kandidatin-ist-gleich-mit-zwei-ex-unionern-verwandt-li.217335 |access-date=31 May 2022 |website=Berliner Kurier |language=de-DE}}</ref>
