'''Teun Koopmeiners''' ({{IPA-nl|ˈtøːŋ ˈkoːpmɛi̯nərs}}; born 28 February 1998) is a Dutch professional [[footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for {{Italian football updater|Atalanta}} club [[Atalanta B.C.|Atalanta]] and the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands national team]].

Having represented the Netherlands at various youth levels, Koopmeiners was called up to the senior team's preliminary [[UEFA Nations League]] squad in August 2020.<ref name="KNVB Media">{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2020/sep/19/leeds-fulham-premier-league-match-report|title=PRELIMINARY ORANGE SELECTION CONSISTS OF 32 PLAYERS|date=19 August 2020|access-date=25 September 2020|work=KNVB Media}}</ref> He was later part of their squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Club career==
===AZ===
Koopmeiners grew up in [[Castricum]], [[North Holland]], and took his first steps in football as a youth player for the local club, Vitesse '22.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.vitesse22.nl/nieuwsbericht/teun-koopmeiners-maakt-competitiedebuut-bij-az/ |title=Teun Koopmeiners maakt competitiedebuut bij AZ |date=3 October 2017 |newspaper=vitesse22.nl |language=Dutch |access-date=22 October 2019}}</ref> In 2009, he joined the [[AZ Alkmaar|AZ]] youth academy at under-12 level.<ref name="AZprofile">{{cite web|title=Spelerspagina van AZ|url=https://www.az.nl/nl/teams/spelers/az/teun-koopmeiners|website=az.nl|publisher=[[AZ Alkmaar]]|access-date=22 October 2019}}</ref> He eventually progressed through the ranks, and as part of the club's reserve team, [[Jong AZ]], he became champion of the Dutch third division in the [[2016–17 Tweede Divisie|2016–17 season]], reaching promotion to the [[Eerste Divisie]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.nu.nl/voetbal/4587372/jong-az-promoveert-jupiler-league-dankzij-titel-in-tweede-divisie.html |title=Jong AZ promoveert naar Jupiler League dankzij titel in Tweede Divisie |date=1 April 2017 |newspaper=nu.nl |language=Dutch |access-date=22 October 2019}}</ref>

On 18 August 2017, Koopmeiners made his professional debut for Jong AZ in a 3&ndash;1 away win over [[FC Den Bosch]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/match/_/gameId/481825|title=FC Den Bosch vs. Jong AZ – Football Match Report |publisher=ESPN |date=18 August 2017 |access-date=21 October 2019}}</ref> He made his first team-debut on 1 October, coming on as a substitute for [[Alireza Jahanbakhsh]] for the final 30 minutes of a 4&ndash;0 home loss to [[Feyenoord]].<ref>{{cite web|title=AZ vs. Feyenoord – 1 October 2017|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2017/10/01/netherlands/eredivisie/stichting-az/feyenoord-rotterdam-nv/2462397/|website=Soccerway}}</ref> At the end of the [[2017–18 Eredivisie|2017–18 season]], AZ ended in third place, and thereby qualified for [[2018–19 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]]. In his first professional season, he made 26 league appearances in which he scored one goal.<ref name="SW">{{cite web|title=T. Koopmeiners|url=https://int.soccerway.com/players/teun-koopmeiners/399769/|website=Soccerway}}</ref>

On 1 March 2020, Koopmeiners played a 70-metre pass to set up [[Oussama Idrissi]] for AZ's second goal in a 2–0 win against [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]] which put his side level on points with Ajax.<ref>{{cite web |title=Dank 70-Meter-Pass: Alkmaar holt Ajax in der Eredivisie ein |url=https://www.kicker.de/771114/artikel |website=kicker |access-date=2 March 2020 |language=German |date=1 March 2020}}</ref> During the [[2019–20 AZ Alkmaar season]] Koopmeiners scored 16 goals in all competitions in 42 appearances,<ref>{{cite web |title=Games played by Teun Koopmeiners in 2019/2020 |url=https://www.soccerbase.com/players/player.sd?player_id=106199&season_id=152 |website=Soccerbase |access-date=25 September 2020 |date=25 September 2020}}</ref> helping his side finish second in the [[Eredivisie]] on goal difference behind the Champions [[AFC Ajax]].<ref>{{cite web |title=AZ Alkmaar's title chase is over but is this Europe's best academy?|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11912/11978634/az-alkmaars-title-chase-is-over-but-is-this-europes-best-academy |website=Sky Sports |access-date=26 August 2020 |date=25 September 2020}}</ref>

He scored his first goal of the [[2020–21 AZ Alkmaar season|2020–21]] season against [[Viktoria Plzen]] on 26 August 2020 in the [[UEFA Champions League]] qualifier 3–1 victory.<ref>{{cite web |title=Champions League qualifiers round-up: AZ Alkmaar survive scare to progress |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11945/12057421/champions-league-qualifiers-round-up-az-alkmaar-survive-scare-to-progress |website=Sky Sports |access-date=26 August 2020 |date=25 September 2020}}</ref> On 14 January 2021, Koopmeiners scored two goals, including an audacious backheel flick, to help his side clinch an 3–1 away victory against [[PSV Eindhoven]].<ref>{{cite web |title=AZ verslaat PSV in Eindhoven: 1-3 |url=https://www.rtlnieuws.nl/sport/voetbal/artikel/5208494/az-verslaat-psv-eindhoven-1-3 |publisher=RTL Nieuws |access-date=4 February 2021}}</ref>

===Atalanta===
On 30 August 2021, Koopmeiners signed for [[Serie A]] club [[Atalanta B.C.|Atalanta]] for a reported fee of €12&nbsp;million.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://football-italia.net/official-koopmeiners-arrive-at-atalanta/ |title=Official: Koopmeiners arrive at Atalanta |last=Mork |first=Martin |date=30 August 2021 |publisher=Football Italia |access-date=3 September 2021}}</ref> He made his debut on 11 September in the home match against [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]], as a late substitute for [[Matteo Pessina]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Con la Fiorentina esordio di Koopmeiners |url=https://www.atalanta.it/news/con-la-fiorentina-esordio-di-koopmeiners/ |website=Atalanta |language=it-IT |date=12 September 2021}}</ref>

On 1 September 2022, Koopmeiners scored a [[hat-trick#Association football|hat-trick]] in a 3–1 Serie A victory over [[Torino F.C.|Torino]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://football-italia.net/serie-a-atalanta-3-1-torino-koopmeiners-hat-trick-takes-top-spot/ |title=Atalanta 3–1 Torino: Koopmeiners hat-trick takes top spot |last=Campanale |first=Susy |date=1 September 2022 |work=Football Italia}}</ref>

==International career==
Koopmeiners made four appearances for the [[Netherlands national under-17 football team|Netherlands national under-17 team]],<ref name="OnsOranje">{{cite web|title=Koopmeiners – Profiel – OnsOranje|url=https://www.onsoranje.nl/teams/184965/speler/298|website=onsoranje.nl}}</ref> as well as two appearances for the [[Netherlands national under-18 football team|national under-18 team]]. As part of the [[Netherlands national under-19 football team|under-19s]], he played at the [[2017 UEFA European Under-19 Championship]], reaching the semi-finals and only losing to [[Portugal national under-19 football team|Portugal]]. At under-19 level, Koopmeiners made 13 appearances.<ref name="OnsOranje"/> After five appearances for the [[Netherlands national under-20 football team|Netherlands Under-20 team]],<ref name="OnsOranje"/> he made his debut for the [[Netherlands national under-21 football team|national under-21 team]] on 22 March 2018 in a 4&ndash;1 loss in [[Doetinchem]] to the [[Belgium national under-21 football team|Belgium U21]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Matchdata – OnsOranje|url=https://www.onsoranje.nl/teams/185185/uitslagen/wedstrijd/30267|website=onsoranje.nl}}</ref>

On 19 August 2020, Koopmeiners was called up to the senior [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands national team]] by caretaker manager [[Dwight Lodeweges]] in the preliminary squad for the [[UEFA Nations League]] fixtures against [[Poland national football team|Poland]] and [[Italy national football team|Italy]].<ref name="KNVB Media"/> In October, he made his debut in a friendly match against [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Mexico spoil De Boer debut with deserved win |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/soccer-friendly-nld-mex-report-idUKL8N2GY67P|accessdate=7 October 2020 |work=Reuters |date=7 October 2020}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Koopmeiners can play as a [[defensive midfielder]] or [[central midfielder]]. He can also play as a [[centre-back]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.football-oranje.com/the-50-eredivisie-u21-stars-to-watch-2018-19-teun-koopmeiners/|title=The 50 Eredivisie U21 Stars To Watch 2018/19: Teun Koopmeiners|date=5 August 2018|access-date=25 September 2020|work=KNVB Media}}</ref> He is known for his leadership qualities, and captained [[AZ Alkmaar]] despite his young age.<ref>{{cite web |title=Teun Koopmeiners, de geboren leider van AZ |url=https://www.volkskrant.nl/sport/teun-koopmeiners-de-geboren-leider-van-az~b6bae6f2/ |website=De Volkskrant |access-date=5 February 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
His younger brother [[Peer Koopmeiners|Peer]] is also a professional footballer; Peer still plays for [[AZ Alkmaar|AZ]].<ref>[https://www.vi.nl/nieuws/koopmeiners-grapt-over-broertje-door-jou-heb-ik-straks-een-voorletter-op-mn-rug Koopmeiners grapt over broertje: 'Door jou heb ik straks een voorletter op m'n rug!'] - Voetbal International {{in lang|nl}}</ref>
