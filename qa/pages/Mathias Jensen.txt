'''Mathias Jensen''' (born 1 January 1996) is a Danish professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for {{English football updater|Brentfor}} club [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]] and the [[Denmark national football team|Denmark national team]].

Jensen is a product of the [[FC Nordsjælland|Nordsjælland]] [[Football academy|academy]] and began his senior career with the club in 2016. Following two seasons as a regular member of the team, he transferred to [[RC Celta de Vigo|Celta Vigo]] in 2018. After an injury-hit [[2018–19 Celta de Vigo season|2018–19]] season, he transferred to Brentford and was a part of the squad which was promoted to the Premier League in 2021. A former Denmark youth international, Jensen made his full international debut in 2020.

A [[midfielder]], Jensen progressed through the [[Football academy|academy]] at [[FC Nordsjælland|Nordsjælland]] and signed a three-year professional contract in January 2015.<ref>{{Cite web |date=15 first team squad for the [[2015–16 Danish Superliga|2015–16]] season and made five appearances during the campaign, scoring one goal, which came in a 2–2 draw with [[Randers FC|Randers]] on 1 May 2016.<ref name="Soccerway" /> Jensen was a regular in the [[2016–17 Danish Superliga|2016–17]] season, making 22 appearances,<ref name="Soccerbase1617" /> before [[2017–18 Danish Superliga|2017–18]] proved to be his breakthrough season.<ref name="Soccerway" /> He made 36 appearances, scored 12 goals, was awarded the [[Captain (association football)|captaincy]] and his achievements were recognised Nordsjælland Player of the Year awards respectively.<ref name="Talent">{{Cite web |date=2 March 2018 |title=Mathias Jensen snuppede årets talent ===Celta Vigo===
On 8 August 2018, Jensen moved to Spain joining [[La Liga]] club [[RC Celta de Vigo|Celta Vigo]] on a five-year contract.<ref name="JoinsCelta">{{Cite web |date=8 August 2018 |title=El Celta refuerza su centro del campo con el danés Mathias Jensen |url=http://www.europapress.es/deportes/futbol-00162/noticia-celta-refuerza-centro-campo-danes-mathias-jensen-20180808190302.html |publisher=Europa Press |language=ES}}</ref> During an injury-affected [[2018–19 Celta de Vigo season|2018–19]] season, he made just six appearances and left the club in July 2019.<ref name="Soccerbase1819" /><ref name="SignsBrentford" />

===Brentford===

==== 2019–2021 ====
On 10 July 2019, Jensen moved to England signing with [[EFL Championship|Championship]] club [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]] on a four-year contract, with the option of an additional year, for an undisclosed fee,<ref name="SignsBrentford">{{Cite web |title=Brentford sign Mathias Jensen from Celta Vigo |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2019/july/mathias-jensen-joins-brentford-from-celta-vigo/ |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220701133859/https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2019/july/mathias-jensen-joins-brentford-from-celta-vigo/ |archive-date=1 July 2022 |access-date=11 July 2019 |website=www.brentfordfc.com |language=en-gb}}</ref> reported to be £3.5 million.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Frank feeling good about Brentford's fortunes |url=https://www.ealingtimes.co.uk/sport/17771108.frank-feeling-good-brentford-s-fortunes/ |access-date=16 July 2019 |website=Ealing Times |language=en}}</ref> He made 43 appearances and scored one goal during the [[2019–20 Brentford F.C. season|2019–20]] season,<ref name="Soccerbase1920" /> which ended with defeat in the [[2020 EFL Championship play-off Final|2020 Championship play-off Final]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Brentford 1 Fulham 2 |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2020/august/match-report-for-brentford-vs-fulham-on-04-aug-20/ |access-date=4 August 2020 |website=www.brentfordfc.com |language=en-gb}}</ref> Jensen made a career high 53 appearances during the [[2020–21 Brentford F.C. season|2020–21]] season and celebrated promotion to the [[Premier League]] with victory in the [[2021 EFL Championship play-off Final|2021 Championship play-off Final]].<ref name="Soccerbase2021" /><ref name="Vincent" />

==== 2021–22 ====
As a result of suffering a "badly gashed" foot while away on international duty, Jensen missed Brentford's entire [[2021–22 Brentford F.C. season|2021–22]] [[pre-season]] and the first three matches of the regular season.<ref>{{Cite web |last=McCullough |first=Ian |date=11 August 2021 |title=Jensen and Dasilva to miss Arsenal game but Toney is fit |url=https://www.westlondonsport.com/brentford/bees-duo-ruled-out-of-arsenal-clash-but-toney-is-fit |access-date=24 August 2021 |website=West London Sport |language=en-GB}}</ref><ref name="Soccerbase2122" /> Restricted to a mix of starting and substitute roles,<ref>{{Cite web |last=Harris |first=Jay |title=Mathias Jensen has not been at his best – do Brentford need Saman Ghoddos to kick-start season? |url=https://theathletic.com/2951705/2021/11/12/mathias-jensen-has-not-been-at-his-best-do-brentford-need-saman-ghoddos-to-help-kickstart-season/ |url-access=subscription |access-date=22 May 2022 |website=The Athletic |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Vegas |first=Paul |title=Mathias Jensen open to staying with Brentford |url=http://www.tribalfootball.com/articles/mathias-jensen-open-to-staying-with-brentford-4409727 |access-date=22 May 2022 |website=Tribal Football}}</ref> particularly after the January 2022 signing of [[Christian Eriksen]],<ref name="Harris">{{Cite web |last=Harris |first=Jay |title=Mathias Jensen made replacing Eriksen look so effortless that nobody talks about it anymore |url=https://theathletic.com/4541834/2023/05/24/brentfords-player-of-the-season-mathias-jensen/ |url-access=subscription |access-date=24 May 2023 |website=The Athletic |language=en}}</ref> Jensen made 35 appearances during a "stop-start" season.<ref name="Soccerbase2122" /><ref name="NewContract">{{Cite web |title=Jensen signs new contract |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/en/news/article/mathias-jensen-new-brentford-contract |access-date=18 January 2023 |website=www.brentfordfc.com |language=en-gb}}</ref> Despite being affected by [[COVID-19 pandemic|COVID-19]] and another minor injury, Jensen won praise from head coach [[Thomas Frank (football manager)|Thomas Frank]] for training "like an animal" while sidelined.<ref name="Harris" />

==== 2022–23 ====
Entering the final year of his contract, Jensen hired his own [[personal trainer]] to work with him daily during the off-season in [[Denmark]] and he returned for the [[2022–23 Brentford F.C. season|2022–23]] pre-season "in incredible shape".<ref name="Harris" /><ref name="Goodwin">{{Cite web |last=Goodwin |first=Adam |title=Mathias Jensen: Confidence is Key |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/en/news/article/the-long-read-mathias-jensen-brentford-interview-premier-league |access-date=24 February 2023 |website=www.brentfordfc.com |language=en-gb}}</ref> He was challenged by head coach Thomas Frank to be the replacement for the departed Christian Eriksen.<ref name="Harris" /> Jensen began the regular season in a starting role and as a regular [[Set piece (football)|set piece]] taker.<ref name="Soccerway" /><ref name="Goodwin" /> Coaching from specialist [[throw-in]] coach Thomas Gronnemark helped length his throws.<ref name="Goodwin" /> Jensen's performance and first Premier League goal in a 4–0 win over [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] on 13 August 2022 was recognised with the [[man of the match]] award.<ref name="Soccerbase2223" /><ref>{{Cite tweet |number=1558777911636893698 |user=BrentfordFC |title=🙌 Mathias Jensen 🙌 A well-deserved Man of the Match award for Mathias! #BrentfordFC {{!}} #BREMUN {{!}} @UtilitaFootball}}</ref> On 18 January 2022, Jensen signed a new {{frac|3|1|2}}-year contract, with the option of a further year.<ref name="NewContract" /> Head coach Thomas Frank commended Jensen's "[[Pressing (association football tactic)|pressing]] abilities", "his defensive mindset" and stated that "he has been one of our best players so far this season".<ref name="NewContract" /> In the process of winning his 23rd cap for [[Denmark national football team|Denmark]] in a [[UEFA Euro 2024 qualifying Group H|Euro 2024 qualifier]] versus [[Finland national football team|Finland]] on 23 March 2023, Jensen became Brentford's most-capped international player.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Jensen becomes club's most-capped player |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/en/news/article/international-mathias-jensen-denmark-v-finland-most-capped-player-record |access-date=24 March 2023 |website=www.brentfordfc.com |language=en-gb}}</ref> Until missing the final match of the season,<ref>{{Cite web |title=Brentford 1–0 Man City: Thomas Frank's Bees beat champions through Ethan Pinnock's goal but miss out on Europe |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11661/12886959/brentford-1-0-man-city-thomas-franks-bees-beat-champions-through-ethan-pinnocks-goal-but-miss-out-on-europe |access-date=28 May 2023 |website=Sky Sports |language=en}}</ref> Jensen was an ever-present starter in league matches and he improved his goal tally to five.<ref name="Soccerway" />

==International career==
Jensen made 30 appearances and scored 3 goals for Denmark between [[Denmark national under-18 football team|U18]] and [[Denmark national under-21 football team|U21]] level.<ref name="DBU">{{DBU player|id=7377}}</ref> He was a part of Denmark's squads at the [[2017 UEFA European Under-21 Championship|2017]] and [[2019 UEFA European Under-21 Championship|2019 European U21 Championships]].<ref name="DBU" /> Jensen was named in the senior team's preliminary 35-man squad for the [[2018 World Cup]],<ref>{{Cite web |title=Revealed: Every World Cup 2018 squad – Final 23-man lists |url=https://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/revealed-every-world-cup-2018-squad-23-man-preliminary-lists/oa0atsduflsv1nsf6oqk576rb |website=Goal.com}}</ref> but he was not included in the final squad.<ref name="SignsBrentford" /> In October 2020, Jensen won a late call-up to the full Denmark squad for a series of three matches, as a replacement for injured Brentford teammate [[Christian Nørgaard]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=16 players building-up to international action this week |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2020/october/international-preview-october-2020/ |access-date=7 October 2020 |website=www.brentfordfc.com |language=en-gb}}</ref> He made his full international debut with a start in a 4–0 [[Friendly match|friendly]] win over the [[Faroe Islands national football team|Faroe Islands]] on 7 October and scored his first full international goal on his fifth cap, in a 8–0 [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group F|2022 World Cup qualifying]] win over [[Moldova national football team|Moldova]] on 28 March 2021.<ref name="DBU" />

Jensen was named in the Denmark squad for [[UEFA Euro 2020|Euro 2020]] and he appeared as a substitute in each of the six matches during the Danes' run to the semi-final.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Christian Nørgaard and Mathias Jensen named in Denmark Euro 2020 squad |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2021/may/denmark-euro-2020-squad-named/ |access-date=26 May 2021 |website=www.brentfordfc.com |language=en-gb}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Christian and Mathias' Euro adventure ends in Semi-Final |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2021/july/international-round-up-20210708/ |access-date=8 July 2021 |website=www.brentfordfc.com |language=en-gb}}</ref> Jensen was named in Denmark's [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]] squad and made two appearances prior to the team's group stage exit.<ref name="DBU" /><ref>{{Cite web |title=De første spillere til VM-truppen er fundet |url=https://dbu.dk/nyheder/2022/november/de-foerste-spillere-til-vm-truppen-er-fundet/ |access-date=7 November 2022 |website=DBU |language=DA}}</ref>

== Style of play ==
Jensen has been described as a "player who can dictate the game", "has a great passing ability" and "a great football brain".<ref name="SignsBrentford" /> Midway through the [[2022–23 in English football|2022–23]] season, he remarked that while he has "always been a technical player who doesn't really enjoy the big [[Tackle (association football)|tackles]]", he had developed his "aggression" and "adapted to the tempo of the Premier League".<ref name="NewContract" />

== Personal life ==
Jensen is a Manchester United and [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] supporter.<ref>{{Cite interview |last=Gillett |first=Stephen |title=The Mighty Interview – Mathias Jensen |url=http://www.playmakerstats.com/files/TMD/TMD3_DIGITAL_SPREADS.pdf |access-date=6 May 2021 |work=The Mighty Delta |pages=13–16}}</ref>
