The '''[[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (AFC)|AFC]] second round''' of '''[[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification]]''', which also served as the second round of '''[[2023 AFC Asian Cup qualification]]''', was played from 5 September 2019 to 15 June 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.the-afc.com/afc/documents/PdfFiles/afc-competitions-calendar-2019|publisher=AFC|title=AFC Competitions Calendar 2019|format=PDF|date=21 March 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190715163628/www.the-afc.com/afc/documents/PdfFiles/afc-competitions-calendar-2019|archive-date=15 July 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.the-afc.com/afc/documents/PdfFiles/afc-competitions-calendar-2020|publisher=AFC|title=AFC Competitions Calendar 2020|format=PDF|date=25 September 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201004141026/https://www.the-afc.com/afc/documents/PdfFiles/afc-competitions-calendar-2020|archive-date=4 October 2020|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name=Asian>{{cite web|url=https://olympics.com/en/news/afc-asian-qualifiers-2022-fifa-world-cup-dates-june-2021-march-2022|title=2022 FIFA World Cup: Asian qualifiers to be completed by March 2022 |publisher=International Olympic Committee|date=12 November 2020}}</ref>

== Format ==

A total of forty teams were drawn into eight groups of five to play home-and-away [[round-robin tournament|round-robin]] matches. They included the 34 teams (teams ranked 1–34 in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (AFC)#Entrants|AFC entrant list]]) which received byes to this round, and the six winners from the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – AFC first round|first round]].

Seven group winners (excluded Qatar, who had already qualified to the World Cup as tournament host) and the five best runners-up advanced to the third round.

Matches in this round were also part of the [[2023 AFC Asian Cup qualification|2023 AFC Asian Cup qualifying]] campaign.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://newsin.asia/asias-2022-football-world-cup-qualifiers-drawn-features-continents-lowest-ranked-national-teams/|title=Asia's 2022 Football World Cup qualifiers drawn, features continent's lowest ranked national teams|date=20 April 2019|publisher=NewsIn.Asia}}</ref> The twelve teams which advanced to the third round of the FIFA World Cup qualification and Qatar, as group winner, automatically qualified for the [[2023 AFC Asian Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://assets.the-afc.com/migration/a/f/AFC%20Asian%20Cup%20China%202023%20Competition%20Regulations.pdf|publisher=AFC|title=Competition Regulations AFC Asian Cup China 2023|format=PDF|date=11 September 2020}}</ref> Twenty-four teams (22 which advanced directly and two which will advance from an additional play-off round)<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.the-afc.com/competitions/afc-asian-cup/news/line-up-for-afc-asian-cup-china-2023tm-qualifiers-final-round-unveiled |title=Line-up for AFC Asian Cup China 2023 Qualifiers Final Round unveiled |date=16 June 2021 |publisher=AFC |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210627203248/https://www.the-afc.com/competitions/afc-asian-cup/news/line-up-for-afc-asian-cup-china-2023tm-qualifiers-final-round-unveiled |archive-date=27 June 2021 |url-status=dead}}</ref> will play in the third round of the AFC Asian Cup qualification to decide the remaining eleven teams. In total, the 2023 AFC Asian Cup features 24 teams.

==Seeding==
The draw for the second round was held on 17 July 2019 at 17:00 [[Time in Malaysia|MST]] ([[UTC+08:00|UTC+8]]), at the AFC House in [[Kuala Lumpur]], Malaysia.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/asian-hopefuls-begin-mammoth-campaign-for-qatar-2022|title=Asian hopefuls begin mammoth campaign for Qatar 2022|publisher=FIFA|date=17 July 2019}}</ref>

The seeding was based on the [[FIFA World Rankings]] of June 2019 (shown in parentheses below).<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/fifa-world-ranking/ranking-table/men/rank/id12582/#AFC |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190616182645/https://www.fifa.com/fifa-world-ranking/ranking-table/men/rank/id12582/#AFC |url-status=dead |archive-date=June 16, 2019 |title=FIFA Men's Ranking – June 2019 (AFC) |publisher=FIFA |date=14 June 2019}}</ref>

==Schedule==
The schedule of each matchday was as follows.

On 5 March 2020, FIFA announced that it would be monitoring the health situation in the region for possible rescheduling of matchdays 7 through 10 due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/statement-on-upcoming-asian-fifa-world-cup-qualifiers |title=Statement on upcoming Asian FIFA World Cup qualifiers |website=FIFA.com |language=en-GB |access-date=8 March 2020}}</ref> Later on 9 March, FIFA and AFC jointly announced that the matches on matchdays 7–10 due to take place in March and June 2020 were postponed, with the new dates to be confirmed. However, subject to approval by FIFA and AFC, and agreement of both member associations, the matches may be played as scheduled provided that the safety of all individuals involved meets the required standards.<ref name="covid19fifa">{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/update-on-upcoming-fifa-world-cup-qualifiers|title=Update on upcoming FIFA World Cup ===Centralised venues===
On 12 March 2021, AFC confirmed the hosts for the group stage scheduled to take place from 31 May to 15 June.<ref name="AFC 0312">{{cite web|url=https://www.the-afc.com/competitions/fifa-world-cup/latest/news/centralised-venues-for-asian-qualifiers-confirmed|title=Centralised venues for Asian qualifiers confirmed|publisher=AFC|date=12 March 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210622060030/https://www.the-afc.com/competitions/fifa-world-cup/latest/news/centralised-venues-for-asian-qualifiers-confirmed|archive-date=22 June 2021|url-status=dead}}</ref>
*Group A: United Arab Emirates (China hosted Guam on 28 May.)
*Group B: Kuwait
*Group C: Bahrain
*Group D: Saudi Arabia
*Group E: Qatar
*Group F: Japan
*Group G: United Arab Emirates
*Group H: South Korea

In general these hosts were the seeded (Pot 1) teams from each group.  The exceptions were Group A (where United Arab Emirates took over hosting duties after China could not host due to COVID-19 restrictions), Group B (where Pot 4 team Kuwait hosted rather than Australia) and Group C (where Pot 3 team Bahrain were chosen rather than Iran).
