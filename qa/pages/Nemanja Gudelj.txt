'''Nemanja Gudelj''' ({{lang-sr-Cyrl|Немања Гудељ}}; born 16 November 1991) is a Serbian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[La Liga]] side [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]] and the [[Serbia national football team|Serbia national team]]. He is the son of former player [[Nebojša Gudelj]] and the older brother of [[Dragiša Gudelj]].

==Club career==

===NAC Breda===
Gudelj signed his first professional contract with [[NAC Breda]] in July 2009 but failed to make an official appearance in his debut season despite being named on the bench several times.<ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.bndestem.nl/sport/sportalgemeen/5248519/NAC-geeft-jeugdspeler-Nemanja-Gudelj-contract.ece | title=NAC geeft jeugdspeler Nemanja Gudelj contract | language=nl | publisher=bndestem.nl | date=13 July 2009 | access-date=9 September 2010}}</ref>

===AZ===
[[File:Nemanja Gudelj.jpg|thumb|150px|Gudelj playing for AZ in 2014]]
In the summer of 2013 Gudelj transferred to [[AZ Alkmaar]] for a fee of around €3 million, sold by his father who was manager at [[NAC Breda|NAC]], signing a four-year contract.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vi.nl/nieuws/242302/Gewilde-Gudelj-verlaat-NAC-Breda-voor-contract-bij-AZ.htm|title=Voetbal International - Gewilde Gudelj verlaat NAC Breda voor contract bij AZ|work=Voetbal International}}</ref>

He received interest from Portuguese club [[FC Porto|Porto]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rtvnh.nl/sport/147976/Gudelj+niet+naar+FC+Porto|title=Gudelj niet naar FC Porto|work=rtvnh.nl}}</ref>

===Ajax===
On 6 May 2015, it was announced that Gudelj would join [[AFC Ajax]] starting from the 2015–16 season. Along with him, his younger brother Dragiša would move to [[Jong Ajax|Ajax youth team]] and his father Nebojša would become a scout for the club focusing primarily on scouting players from [[Balkans|the Balkans]] and Serbia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/sport/ajax-sign-serbian-brother/1830180.html|title=Ajax sign Serbian brothers and give dad a job in package deal|work=Channel NewsAsia}}</ref> On 5 November 2016, Gudelj was removed from the first team selection after stating he couldn't be motivated when not starting.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.telegraaf.nl/telesport/26963841/__Ajax_zet_ook_Gudelj_uit_selectie__.html|title=Ajax zet ook Gudelj uit selectie|access-date=8 January 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170417153000/http://www.telegraaf.nl/telesport/26963841/__Ajax_zet_ook_Gudelj_uit_selectie__.html|archive-date=17 April 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===Tianjin TEDA===
On 5 January 2017, Ajax announced Gudelj would transfer to [[Tianjin Teda F.C.|Tianjin TEDA]] on 25 January 2017 for a sum rumoured to be around €5.5 million.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ajax.nl/streams/ajax-actueel/nemanja-gudelj-vertrekt-naar-china.htm|title=Nemanja Gudelj vertrekt naar China}}</ref>

===Guangzhou Evergrande Taobao===
On 28 January 2018, [[Guangzhou Evergrande Taobao F.C.|Guangzhou Evergrande Taobao]] announced that Gudelj was transferred from [[Tianjin Teda F.C.|Tianjin TEDA]] and signed a 2-year contract. The transfer fee was not announced.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gzevergrandefc.com/news.aspx?fid=138&ftid=45670|title=内马尼亚·古德利(Nemanja Gudelj)正式加盟}}</ref> He made his debut for Guangzhou on 21 February 2018 in a 0–0 away draw against [[Cerezo Osaka]] in the second group stage match of [[2018 AFC Champions League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sports.sina.com.cn/china/afccl/2018-02-21/doc-ifyrvspi0484517.shtml|title=古德利：第一场满意自身表现 0-0这结果能接受|date=21 February 2018 }}</ref> On 14 March, he scored his first goal for the club in the fourth group stage match of the AFC Champions League against [[Jeju United FC|Jeju United]], which ensured Guangzhou Evergrande's 2–0 away win.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sports.sina.com.cn/china/afccl/2018-03-15/doc-ifyscsmv8305414.shtml|title=两场零失球!恒大已走上正轨 古德利正向暴力鸟看齐|date=15 March 2018 }}</ref> Gudelj was excluded from first team squad in July 2018 due to the limitation of the number of foreign players.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sports.sina.com.cn/china/j/2018-07-16/doc-ihfkffak2911679.shtml|title=恒大外援7选4决定放弃这3人 暴力鸟未必马上登场|date=16 July 2018 }}</ref>

====Sporting CP (loan)====
On 23 August 2018, Gudelj was loaned to [[Primeira Liga]] side [[Sporting CP]] for the 2018–19 season. 
The contract at will extend for four years as a Sporting CP player. The actual loan contract expires in the end of the season so he would move as a free player .<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gzevergrandefc.com/news.aspx?fid=138&ftid=46038|title=关于外援古德利租借至葡萄牙体育俱乐部的公告|work=[[Guangzhou Evergrande Taobao F.C.]]|date=23 August 2018}}</ref>

===Sevilla===
[[File:Nemanja Gudelj 2020.jpg|140px|thumb|Gudelj playing for Sevilla in 2020]]
On 23 July 2019, Gudelj signed with Spanish side [[Sevilla FC]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Transferts-nemanja-gudelj-s-engage-avec-le-seville-fc/1042780|title=Transferts : Nemanja Gudelj s'engage avec le Séville FC|work=[[L'Équipe]]|date=23 July 2019}}</ref> In July 2020 he tested positive for [[Coronavirus disease 2019|COVID-19]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=29 July 2020|title=Nemanja Gudelj, el positivo de COVID-19 del Sevilla|url=https://www.marca.com/futbol/sevilla/2020/07/29/5f216bd846163f48188b464f.html|access-date=6 August 2020|publisher=Marca|language=es}}</ref>

==International career==
After being capped at the U19 and U21 levels, Gudelj earned his first cap for [[Serbia national football team|Serbia]] on 5 March 2014, in a 2–1 away win over [[Republic of Ireland national football team|Ireland]] in a [[friendly match]] after coming on as a last minute substitute for [[Antonio Rukavina]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=flashscore.com|title=Ireland vs. Serbia 1:2|url=http://www.flashscore.com/match/nTIzstpQ/#lineups;1|access-date=11 March 2014}}</ref> In 2014, Gudelj played for the national team a further four times, playing against [[Jamaica national football team|Jamaica]] twice, [[Panama national football team|Panama]] and [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]].

In July 2014 his former coach at AZ, [[Dick Advocaat]], was named as [[Serbia national football team|Serbia's national team]] manager for the [[UEFA Euro 2016|Euro 2016 campaign]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/28531229|title=BBC Sport - Serbia appoint Dick Advocaat as coach for Euro 2016 qualifiers|work=BBC Sport}}</ref> He scored his first goal on 18 November 2014 in a 2–0 away friendly win against [[Greece national football team|Greece]].

In November 2022, he was selected in Serbia's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fss.rs/en/head-coach-dragan-stojkovic-announces-the-list-of-players-for-the-fifa-world-cup-2022-in-qatar/ |title=Head-coach Dragan Stojković announces the list of players for the FIFA World Cup 2022 in Qatar |publisher=[[Football Association of Serbia]] |date=11 November 2022 |access-date=11 November 2022}}</ref> He played in group stage matches against [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]]<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/sports/world-cup-2022/richarlison-brings-brazil-to-life-in-the-second-half-defeats-serbia-2-0/3971175/ |title=Richarlison Brings Brazil to Life in the Second Half, Defeats Serbia 2-0 |first=Kristen |last=Conti |newspaper=NBCNew York |location=New York |date=24 November 2022 |access-date=7 December 2022}}</ref> and [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]].<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/dec/02/serbia-switzerland-world-cup-group-g-match-report |title=Switzerland advance and Serbia go out as Freuler finish settles stormy contest |first=Nick |last=Ames |newspaper=The Guardian|location=London |date=2 December 2022 |access-date=7 December 2022}}</ref> Serbia finished fourth in the group.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://soccer.nbcsports.com/2022/12/02/world-cup-2022-group-g-brazil-serbia-cameroon-switzerland-rankings-table-standings/ |title=World Cup 2022 Group G: Brazil, Serbia, Cameroon, Switzerland schedule, fixtures, rankings |first=Nicholas |last=Mendola |newspaper=NBC Sports|location=Lusail |date=2 December 2022 |access-date=7 December 2022}}</ref>
