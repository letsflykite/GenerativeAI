'''Abdou-Lakhad Diallo''' (born 4 May 1996), known as '''Abdou Diallo''', is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] for [[Ligue 1]] club [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]]. Born in France, he plays for the [[Senegal national football team|Senegal national team]].

==Club career==

===Monaco===
Born in [[Tours]], Diallo joined the youth academy of [[AS Monaco FC|Monaco]] at the age of 15.<ref name="Abdou Diallo: Mainz's natural-born defensive leader with an eye for goal">{{cite web |title=Abdou Diallo: Mainz's natural-born defensive leader with an eye for goal |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/mainz-s-jurgen-klopp-heir-abdou-diallo-natural-leader-467746.jsp |publisher=Bundesliga |access-date=12 September 2018 |date=27 May 2018}}</ref> On 28 March 2014, he signed his first professional contract with the club. Vice-president of the club, [[Vadim Vasilyev (businessman)|Vadim Vasilyev]] commented that Diallo "fits perfectly into our sports project. He has a lot of talent and we hope that he will continue to progress alongside the great players of AS Monaco".<ref>{{cite web |first=Franck |last=Orocoti |title=Monaco: Abdou Diallo signe pro |trans-title=Monaco: Abdou Diallo signs pro |url=https://www.africatopsports.com/2014/03/29/monaco-abdou-diallo-signe-pro/ |publisher=Africa Top Sports |access-date=12 September 2018 |language=fr |date=29 March 2014}}</ref> On 14 December, he made his first team debut, coming on as a substitute for [[Bernardo Silva]] in the extra time of a 1–0 league victory against [[Olympique de Marseille|Marseille]].<ref>{{cite web |title=MATCH REPORT: AS Monaco 1-0 AS Monaco |url=http://www.asmonaco.com/en/article/match-report-as-monaco-1-0-as-monaco-66702.html |publisher=AS Monaco FC |access-date=12 September 2018 |date=14 December 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180912131102/http://www.asmonaco.com/en/article/match-report-as-monaco-1-0-as-monaco-66702.html |archive-date=12 September 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In June 2015, Diallo was loaned out to Belgian club [[S.V. Zulte Waregem|Zulte Waregem]] for the 2015–16 season.<ref>{{cite web|title=Abdou Diallo joins Zulte Waregem|url=http://www.getfootballnewsfrance.com/2015/abdou-diallo-joins-zulte-waregem/|publisher=Get French Football News|access-date=29 July 2015}}</ref> During his stint at the club, he was deployed at an attacking role, scoring three goals in 33 league matches.<ref name="Abdou Diallo: Mainz's natural-born defensive leader with an eye for goal"/> In December 2016, media reports emerged that Spanish club [[Real Betis]] was interested in signing him.<ref>{{cite web |first=Miguel A. |last=Moran |title=Abdou Diallo, central zurdo del Mónaco, otra opción para la defensa del Betis |trans-title=Abdou Diallo, left-back at Monaco, another option for Betis defense |url=http://www.marca.com/futbol/betis/2016/12/31/5867f078e2704e500c8b4638.html |publisher=Marca |access-date=12 September 2018 |language=es |date=31 December 2016}}</ref> Nevertheless, he made five league appearances during the [[2016–17 Ligue 1|2016–17]] season, with his side winning the league.<ref name="Abdou Diallo: Mainz's natural-born defensive leader with an eye for goal"/>

===Mainz 05===
On 14 July 2017, Diallo moved to [[Bundesliga]] club [[1. FSV Mainz 05|Mainz 05]], and signed a five-year contract.<ref>{{cite web |title=Mainz sign France Under-21 captain Abdou Diallo |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/mainz-sign-france-under-21-captain-abdou-diallo-monaco-455150.jsp |publisher=Bundesliga |access-date=12 September 2018 |date=14 July 2017}}</ref> On 9 September, he scored his first goal for the club in a 3–1 league victory against [[Bayer 04 Leverkusen|Bayer Leverkusen]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Mainz off the mark with 3-1 victory over winless Leverkusen |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/mainz-bayer-leverkusen-line-ups-stats-alario-aranguiz-bailey-brandt-havertz-458320.jsp |publisher=Bundesliga |access-date=12 September 2018 |date=9 September 2017}}</ref> During the season, he was deployed at both back three and back four; and had an 80% pass success rate. He started in 27 league matches during the season.<ref name="Abdou Diallo: Mainz's natural-born defensive leader with an eye for goal"/>

===Borussia Dortmund===
On 26 June 2018, Diallo joined [[Borussia Dortmund]] on a five-year deal for a fee of €28 million.<ref name=Kicker.de>{{cite news|title=Wechsel perfekt: Diallo der zweitteuerste BVB-Neuzugang|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/startseite/726350/artikel_wechsel-perfekt-diallo-der-zweitteuerste-bvb-neuzugang.html|access-date=26 June 2018|date=26 June 2018|language=de}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Borussia Dortmund sign Abdou Diallo, France under-21 defender, from Mainz |url=http://www.espn.in/football/soccer-transfers/story/3545338/borussia-dortmund-sign-abdou-diallo-a-france-under-21-defender-from-mainz |publisher=ESPN |access-date=16 September 2018 |date=26 June 2018}}</ref> In an interview, he said that [[Ousmane Dembélé]] advised him to join the club.<ref>{{cite web |title=Wolf, Diallo, Delaney, Hakimi and Hitz: meet the Borussia Dortmund new boys |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/borussia-dortmund-transfers-hakimi-witsel-delaney-diallo-wolf-hitz-510298.jsp |publisher=Bundesliga |access-date=16 September 2018 |date=4 August 2018}}</ref> On 15 September, he scored his first goal for the club in a 3–1 league victory against [[Eintracht Frankfurt]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Dortmund overpower Frankfurt 3-1 in German Bundesliga - Xinhua {{!}} English.news.cn |url=http://www.xinhuanet.com/english/2018-09/15/c_137468632.htm |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180915053505/http://www.xinhuanet.com/english/2018-09/15/c_137468632.htm |url-status=dead |archive-date=15 September 2018 |website=www.xinhuanet.com |access-date=16 September 2018 |date=15 September 2018}}</ref>

===Paris Saint-Germain===
On 16 July 2019, Diallo signed for [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] (PSG) until June 2024.<ref name="PSG">{{cite web |title=Abdou Diallo signs for Paris Saint-Germain |url=https://en.psg.fr/teams/first-team/content/abdou-diallo-signs-for-paris-saint-germain |access-date=16 July 2019 |website=Paris Saint-Germain F.C.}}</ref> The transfer fee of his deal to the French club was of €32&nbsp;million.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.90min.com/posts/6412663-paris-saint-germain-confirm-signing-of-abdou-diallo-from-borussia-dortmund-on-five-year-deal|title=Paris Saint-Germain Confirm Signing of Abdou Diallo from Borussia Dortmund on Five-Year Deal|website=90Min|date=16 July 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Dortmund confirm €32M Diallo deal with PSG|url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/dortmund-confirm-32m-diallo-deal-with-psg/axklpi0mwdbw1pok9xucgngja|website=Goal.com|access-date=12 May 2020}}</ref>

On 3 August 2019, Diallo made his debut for Paris Saint-Germain in the [[Trophée des Champions]], which ended as a 2–1 win for PSG over [[Stade Rennais F.C.|Rennes]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=French Trophy of Champions: Paris Saint-Germain vs Rennes|url=http://www.xinhuanet.com/english/2019-08/03/c_138281549_13.htm|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200604013315/http://www.xinhuanet.com/english/2019-08/03/c_138281549_13.htm|url-status=dead|archive-date=4 June 2020|website=Xinhuanet|access-date=12 May 2020}}</ref> He made his league debut against [[Nîmes Olympique|Nîmes]], in a 3–0 home win on 11 August.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Starting Lineups - PSG vs Nimes|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/psg-vs-nimes/teams/408371|website=Sky Sports|access-date=12 May 2020}}</ref> Diallo's [[UEFA Champions League]] debut came on 18 September, in a 3–0 win against [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Paris-Real Madrid |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/match/2027025--paris-vs-real-madrid/lineups/|website=UEFA.com|access-date=12 May 2020}}</ref> At the end of the [[2019–20 Ligue 1|2019–20 season]], Diallo received a [[Ligue 1]] winners medal for his contributions that season, which was ended early (on 30 April 2020) due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Grading PSG: Decent Debut For Diallo in Paris|url=https://psgtalk.com/2020/05/grading-psg-decent-debut-for-diallo-in-paris/|website=PSG Talk|date=5 May 2020|access-date=12 May 2020}}</ref>

==== Loan to RB Leipzig ====
On 1 September 2022, Diallo completed a return to Germany, signing for Bundesliga club [[RB Leipzig]] on a season-long loan with an option-to-buy for a reported fee of €25 million.<ref>{{Cite web |date=1 September 2022 |title=Abdou Diallo loaned to RB Leipzig |url=https://en.psg.fr/teams/first-team/content/abdou-diallo-loaned-to-rb-leipzig-mercato-psg |access-date=1 September 2022 |publisher=[[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.]] |lang=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=1 September 2022 |title=Transferts : le PSG officialise les départs d'Abdou Diallo à Leipzig et de Layvin Kurzawa à Fulham |url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Transferts-le-psg-officialise-les-departs-d-abdou-diallo-a-leipzig-et-de-layvin-kurzawa-a-fulham/1351513 |access-date=1 September 2022 |website=[[L'Équipe]] |lang=fr}}</ref>

==International career==
Diallo was born in France and is of Senegalese descent.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.seneplus.com/football/abdou-diallo-le-retour-du-prince|title=ABDOU DIALLO, LE RETOUR DU "PRINCE" !|date=30 June 2016}}</ref> He played for many youth teams of France, and even captained [[France national under-21 football team|France's under-21s]].<ref name="Abdou Diallo: Mainz's natural-born defensive leader with an eye for goal"/>

On 17 March 2021, Diallo was called up to the [[Senegal national football team|Senegal national team]] for the first time.<ref>{{Cite web|title=CAN 2021 : LE SÉNÉGAL AVEC ABDOU DIALLO, BALLO-TOURÉ ET NAMPALYS MENDY|url=https://www.football365.fr/can-2021-senegal-abdou-diallo-ballo-toure-nampalys-mendy-9957305.html|url-status=live|access-date=17 March 2021|website=foot365|date=17 March 2021|language=fr|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210317152100/https://www.football365.fr/can-2021-senegal-abdou-diallo-ballo-toure-nampalys-mendy-9957305.html |archive-date=17 March 2021 }}</ref> He made his debut in a 0–0 draw against [[Congo national football team|Congo]] on 26 March.<ref>{{Cite web|date=26 March 2021|title=Congo vs Sénégal : Les compositions officielles - Equipe nationale Football, Football|url=https://wiwsport.com/2021/03/26/congo-vs-senegal-les-compositions-officielles/|access-date=26 March 2021|website=wiwsport|language=fr-FR}}</ref>

He was part of [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations squads#Senegal|Senegal's squad]] for the [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations]]; the Lions of Teranga went on to win the tournament for the first time in their history.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cafonline.com/total-africa-cup-of-nations/news/cisse-unveils-senegal-squad-hopes-for-elusive-totalenergies-afcon-title|title=Cisse unveils Senegal squad, hopes for elusive TotalEnergies AFCON title|publisher=[[Confederation of African Football]]|date=25 December 2021|access-date=28 March 2023|archive-date=9 January 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220109145849/https://www.cafonline.com/total-africa-cup-of-nations/news/cisse-unveils-senegal-squad-hopes-for-elusive-totalenergies-afcon-title|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Senegal 0-0 Egypt |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/60186276 |website=[[BBC Sport]] |access-date=28 March 2023 |date=7 February 2022}}</ref>

Diallo was appointed a [[National Order of the Lion|Grand Officer of the National Order of the Lion]] by [[President of Senegal]] [[Macky Sall]] following the nation's victory at the tournament.<ref name= "reuters">{{cite web |title=Cash prizes, real estate, and highest honours as Senegal celebrates Nations Cup heroes |url=https://www.reuters.com/lifestyle/sports/cash-prizes-real-estate-highest-honours-senegal-celebrates-nations-cup-heroes-2022-02-08/ |website=[[Reuters]] |access-date=28 March 2023 |date=8 February 2022}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Diallo plays as a [[Defender (association football)|central defender]]. [[Rouven Schröder]] (sporting director of 1. FSV Mainz) has said that Diallo is "strong in the air and shrewd in the challenge".<ref name="Abdou Diallo: Mainz's natural-born defensive leader with an eye for goal" /> Michael Zorc, the sporting director of Borussia Dortmund, described Diallo as "a modern, strong central defender who is very intelligent. He can play a wider defensive role too or even be deployed in a defensive midfield role".<ref>{{cite web|date=26 June 2018|title=Abdou Diallo quits Mainz 05 for Borussia Dortmund|url=http://www.newindianexpress.com/sport/football/2018/jun/26/abdou-diallo-quits-mainz-05-for-borussia-dortmund-1834028.html|access-date=12 September 2019|publisher=The New Indian Express}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Diallo's younger brother, [[Ibrahima Diallo (footballer, born 1999)|Ibrahima]], is also a footballer for [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sport24.lefigaro.fr/football/transferts/fil-info/monaco-prete-le-jeune-ibrahima-diallo-a-brest-921146|title=Monaco prête le jeune Ibrahima Diallo à Brest|date=13 August 2018}}</ref>
