'''Yussuf Yurary Poulsen''' ({{IPA-da|ˈjusuf ˈpʰʌwlsn̩}}; born 15 June 1994) is a Danish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Striker (association football)|striker]] for [[Bundesliga]] club [[RB Leipzig]] and the [[Denmark national football team|Denmark national team]]. He sometimes uses ''Yurary'' as his shirt name.

After starting his professional career with [[Lyngby Boldklub|Lyngby]], he transferred to RB Leipzig in 2013, helping them from the [[3. Liga]] to the [[Bundesliga]] in the space of three seasons. He holds the RB Leipzig record for most appearances with over 300, and has scored over 80 goals for the club. He played in the [[DFB-Pokal]] final in [[2019 DFB-Pokal Final|2019]] and [[2021 DFB-Pokal Final|2021]].

Poulsen made his international debut for Denmark in 2014 and has earned over sixty caps. He was part of their squad at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] and [[UEFA Euro 2020]], reaching the semi-finals of the latter.

==Early life==
Poulsen was born to a [[Tanzania]]n father and a Danish mother.<ref>[http://www.lvz-online.de/gestaltete-specials/knipser/rb-leipzig/unterschrieben-yussuf-poulsen-verstaerkt-rb-leipzig-erste-einsaetze-am-Wochenende/r-rb-Leipzig-a-195965.html Unterschrieben: Yussuf Poulsen verstärkt RB Leipzig – erste Einsätze am Wochenende], www.lvz-online.de vom 4. Juli 2013</ref> His father worked on a container ship, oscillating between [[Tanga, Tanzania|Tanga]] and Denmark before he settled in Copenhagen.<ref name="dierotenbullenyussufyurarypoulsen"/> He died of cancer when Poulsen was six years old.

==Club career==
===Early career===
Poulsen began playing football at [[BK Skjold]]. At first he was a defensive player and in [[Kenneth Zohore]] he had a teammate, with whom he was to play later in the Danish youth national teams. After Zohoré joined [[FC Copenhagen]], Poulsen was moved to a position as a striker.<ref name="dierotenbullenyussufyurarypoulsen">{{Cite web|url=http://www.dierotenbullen.com/neuigkeiten/Saison_2013_14/Reportage-Yussuf-Poulsen.html|title=AUS DEM KLUBMAGAZIN: Yussuf Yurary Poulsen UND SEIN WEG ZU RB LEIPZIG!|author=RasenBallsport Leipzig GmbH (publisher)|work=DIE ROTEN BULLEN (Vereinswebsite von RB Leipzig)|date=2013|access-date=25 July 2015|language=de}}</ref>

===Lyngby BK===
At the age of 14 he joined the youth ranks of [[Lyngby BK]]. He played his first match as a senior on 4 December 2011, when he came on the pitch against AC Horsens after 84 minutes, as a substitute for Mathias Tauber. Poulsen did not immediately establish himself in the starting eleven, and he was at the end of the season only on five appearances. His club, Lyngby were also relegated from Superligaen. 5 August 2012 he scored his first goal at senior level, as well as for his club, when he in the second match in the 1. Division (second Danish league) scored the 1-0 winning goal against [[AB Gladsaxe]]. He was a regular player in the starting 11, and played 32 matches, scoring eleven goals.<ref name="Soccerway statistics">{{cite web |title=Y. Poulsen |url=https://int.soccerway.com/players/yussuf-poulsen/155071/ |website=Soccerway |publisher=Perform Group |access-date=28 August 2018}}</ref>

===RB Leipzig===
His achievements attracted the interest of foreign and domestic clubs, and on 3 July 2013, he signed a contract with the newly promoted [[3. Liga]] German side [[RB Leipzig]].<ref name="dierotenbullenyussufyurarypoulsen" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/3liga/startseite/588827/artikel_poulsen-wird-ein-roter-bulle.html|title=Poulsen wird ein roter Bulle|author=Olympia Verlag GmbH (publisher)|work=kicker online|date=4 July 2013|access-date=31 October 2013|language=de}}</ref> He made his club debut on 19 July 2013 in a 1–0 win at [[Hallescher FC]],<ref>{{cite news |title=Ausschreitungen nach RB-Leipzig-Gastspiel in Halle |url=https://www.t-online.de/sport/fussball/id_64584724/3-liga-krawalle-nach-rb-leipzig-gastspiel-in-halle.html |access-date=27 January 2022 |publisher=T-Online |date=20 July 2013 |language=German}}</ref> and scored his first goals in a 2–0 home win over [[Rot-Weiss Essen]] on 24 August,<ref>{{cite news |title=Poulsen rechtfertigt Zornigers Vertrauen |url=https://www.kicker.de/leipzig-gegen-erfurt-2013-liga-1897136/analyse |access-date=27 January 2022 |work=Kicker |date=24 August 2013 |language=German}}</ref> and netted two more in the final game on 10 May 2014 to win 3–1 at [[Stuttgarter Kickers]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Roth |first1=Matthias |title=Röttger schenkt RB Leipzig zum Abschied ein Tor |url=https://www.sportbuzzer.de/artikel/rottger-schenkt-rb-leipzig-zum-abschied-ein-tor/ |access-date=27 January 2022 |publisher=Sport Buzzer |date=10 May 2014 |language=German}}</ref> He finished the [[2013–14 RB Leipzig season|2013–14 season]] with 10 goals in 36 appearances as his team were promoted as runners-up.<ref name="2013–14 season">{{cite web |title=Yussuf Poulsen |url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/vereine/2013-14/76446/vereinsspieler_poulsen-yussuf.html |website=kicker.de |publisher=kicker |access-date=28 August 2018 |language=de}}</ref>

In his first season in the [[2. Bundesliga]], Poulsen scored 12 goals in 32 appearances during the [[2014–15 RB Leipzig season|2014–15 season]] as his team came fifth.<ref name="2014–15 season">{{cite web |title=Yussuf Poulsen |url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/vereine/2014-15/76446/vereinsspieler_poulsen-yussuf.html |website=kicker.de |publisher=kicker |access-date=28 August 2018 |language=de}}</ref> He was sent off on 23 February in a 1–1 draw at [[Eintracht Braunschweig]].<ref>{{cite news |title=1:1 in Braunschweig: Leipzig kann Aufstieg fast abschreiben |url=https://www.mannheim24.de/sport/fussball/unentschieden-gegen-braunschweig-leipzig-kann-aufstieg-abschreiben-zr-4760775.html?itm_source=story_detail&itm_medium=interaction_bar&itm_campagin=share |access-date=27 January 2022 |publisher=Mannheim 24 |date=23 February 2015 |language=German}}</ref>

[[File:2019-10-23 Fußball, Männer, UEFA Champions League, RB Leipzig - FC Zenit St. Petersburg 1DX 2759 by Stepro.jpg|thumb|left|Poulsen in action for Leipzig against [[FC Zenit Saint Petersburg|Zenit]] in October 2019]]
Poulsen seven goals in 32 appearances during the [[2015–16 RB Leipzig season|2015–16 season]] as RB Leipzig won promotion as runners-up to [[SC Freiburg]].<ref name="2015–16 season">{{cite web |title=Yussuf Poulsen |url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/vereine/2015-16/76446/vereinsspieler_poulsen-yussuf.html |website=kicker.de |publisher=kicker |access-date=28 August 2018 |language=de}}</ref> On 28 August 2016, he played in RB Leipzig's first Bundesliga match,<ref>{{cite news |title=Bundesliga debutants Leipzig earn draw at Hoffenheim |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/blmd1n-tsg-1899-hoffenheim-2-2-rb-leipzig-report.jsp |access-date=27 January 2022 |publisher=Bundesliga |date=28 August 2016}}</ref> and in the sixth match of the [[2016–17 Bundesliga|2016–17 season]], he scored his first Bundesliga goal in a 2–1 victory at home against [[FC Augsburg]] on 30 September.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dw.com/en/record-setting-leipzig-up-to-third-after-beating-augsburg/a-35937394|title=Record-setting Leipzig up to third after beating Augsburg|website=DW.COM|access-date=30 September 2016}}</ref><ref name="2016–17 season">{{cite web |title=Yussuf Poulsen |url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/vereine/2016-17/76446/vereinsspieler_poulsen-yussuf.html |website=kicker.de |publisher=kicker |access-date=28 August 2018 |language=de}}</ref> He finished the [[2016–17 RB Leipzig season|2016–17 season]] with five goals in 30 appearances.<ref name="2016–17 season"/>

Poulsen finished the [[2017–18 RB Leipzig season|2017–18 season]] five goals in 41 appearances.<ref name="2017–18 season">{{cite web |title=Yussuf Poulsen |url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/vereine/2017-18/76446/vereinsspieler_poulsen-yussuf.html |website=kicker.de |publisher=kicker |access-date=28 August 2018 |language=de}}</ref> This included one on 14 October 2017 in a 3–2 win at [[Borussia Dortmund]], the hosts' first home defeat in 41 games.<ref>{{cite news |title=Leipzig get revenge on Dortmund in clash of pretenders to Bayern throne |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/blog/2017/oct/16/leipzig-get-revenge-on-dortmund-in-clash-of-pretenders-to-bayern-throne |access-date=27 January 2022 |work=The Guardian |date=16 October 2017}}</ref>

On 30 March 2019, during a 5–0 win over [[Hertha BSC]], he became the first-ever Leipzig player to score a hat-trick in the Bundesliga and in the process helped the club record its 50th ever win in the competition. His treble also took him to 15 league goals for the campaign which meant he became only the third Danish player, after [[Ebbe Sand]] and [[Allan Simonsen]] to score more than 14 Bundesliga goals in a single season.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/rb-leipzig-hertha-berlin-live-line-ups-stats-timo-werner-tyler-adams-kalou-3270|title=Yussuf Poulsen hits hat-trick as five-star RB Leipzig rout Hertha Berlin|publisher=Bundesliga|date=30 March 2019|access-date=31 March 2019}}</ref> In the same month, he extended his contract by a year to 2022.<ref>{{cite news |title=Yussuf Poulsen renews RB Leipzig deal to 2022 |url=https://www.beinsports.com/au/bundesliga/news/yussuf-poulsen-renews-rb-leipzig-deal-to-2022/1139610 |access-date=27 January 2022 |publisher=beIN Sports |date=14 March 2019}}</ref> His team were runners-up in the [[2019 DFB-Pokal Final]]; he scored in 3–1 wins in the first round at [[FC Viktoria Köln]]<ref>{{cite news |title=Poulsen and Forsberg rescue tired RB Leipzig in German Cup |url=https://www.besoccer.com/new/poulsen-and-forsberg-rescue-tired-rb-leipzig-in-german-cup-487259 |access-date=27 January 2022 |publisher=Be Soccer |date=19 August 2018}}</ref> and the semi-final at [[Hamburger SV]].<ref>{{cite news |title=RB Leipzig book place in DFB Cup final for the first time with victory in Hamburg |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/hamburg-rb-leipzig-live-dfb-cup-semi-final-timo-werner-tyler-adams-poulsen-3916 |access-date=27 January 2022 |publisher=Bundesliga |date=23 April 2018}}</ref>

Poulsen became the first Leipzig player to 250 appearances on 24 May 2020, when he scored in a 5–0 win at [[1. FSV Mainz 05]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Timo Werner nets hat-trick as RB Leipzig hammer Mainz |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/mainz-rb-leipzig-live-line-ups-stats-werner-nagelsmann-upamecano-sabitzer-11177 |access-date=24 August 2020 |publisher=Bundesliga |date=24 May 2020}}</ref> He was then ruled out for the rest of the season with ankle ligament damage.<ref>{{cite news |title=Yussuf Poulsen: RB Leipzig captain suffers ankle ligament injury |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/european/rb-leipzig-yussuf-poulsen-injury-update-latest-bundesliga-a9532881.html |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/archive/20220507/https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/european/rb-leipzig-yussuf-poulsen-injury-update-latest-bundesliga-a9532881.html |archive-date=7 May 2022 |url-access=subscription |url-status=live |access-date=27 January 2022 |work=The Independent |date=26 May 2020}}{{cbignore}}</ref>

He scored five times on the way to the [[2021 DFB-Pokal Final|2021 DFB-Pokal final]], including two in a 4–0 home win over [[VfL Bochum]] in the last 16.<ref>{{cite news |title=Yussuf Poulsen double helps RB Leipzig past Bochum and into DFB Cup quarter-finals |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/rb-leipzig-bochum-dfb-cup-live-line-ups-stats-upamecano-adams-angelino-14583 |access-date=27 January 2022 |publisher=Bundesliga |date=3 February 2021}}</ref> On 28 October 2021, he reached 300 matches in a 2–1 home loss to [[Club Brugge KV]] in the Champions League group stage.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Schäfer |first1=Guido |title=Zwischen Anspruch und Wirklichkeit klafft bei RB Leipzig der Grand Canyon |url=https://www.sportbuzzer.de/artikel/analyse-zu-rb-leipzig-zwischen-anspruch-und-wirklichkeit-klafft-der-grand-canyon/ |access-date=27 January 2022 |publisher=Sport Buzzer |date=29 September 2021 |language=German}}</ref>

==International career==
Poulsen was eligible to play for Tanzania but did not receive any offers from the [[Tanzania Football Federation|Tanzanian federation]]; as a result, he chose to represent his native Denmark.<ref>[http://www.bt.dk/fodbold/venner-fra-barnsben-yussuf-poulsen-hjaelper-hoejbjerg-igennem-sorgen Venner fra barnsben: Yussuf Yurary Poulsen hjælper Højbjerg igennem sorgen], http://www.bt.dk 10 October 2015</ref><ref>[http://www.bt.dk/fodbold/derfor-broed-pierre-emile-sammen-i-graad-poulsen-og-hoejbjerg-har-en-faelles-forti Derfor brød Pierre-Emile sammen i gråd: Poulsen og Højbjerg har en fælles fortid], http://www.bt.dk 14 June 2015</ref>

He made his debut for the [[Denmark national football team|Danish national team]] on 30 January 2013 against [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Mexico vs. Denmark - 31 January 2013|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2013/01/31/world/friendlies/mexico/denmark/1416606/|website=Soccerway}}</ref> On 13 June 2015, he scored his first goal for his country, against [[Serbia national football team|Serbia]] in a 2–0 win. Poulsen was selected in Denmark's squad for the [[2016 Summer Olympics]], however he rejected the call-up in order to cement his place at Leipzig.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.worldfootball.net/news/_n2340447_/olympics-dane-turns-down-rio-to-prepare-for-bundesliga/|title=Olympics: Dane turns down Rio to prepare for Bundesliga|newspaper=worldfootball.net|language=en-GB|access-date=31 January 2017}}</ref>

In May 2018, he was named in Denmark's 23-man [[Denmark national football team|squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/amp/news/revealed-every-world-cup-2018-squad-23-man-preliminary-lists/oa0atsduflsv1nsf6oqk576rb|title=Revealed: Every World Cup 2018 squad - Final 23-man lists|publisher=Goal.com}}</ref> On 16 June, he scored the lone goal in their opening game against [[Peru national football team|Peru]], and was named [[Player of the match|man of the match]]. Earlier in the game he conceded a penalty with a foul on [[Christian Cueva]], which the Peruvian missed.<ref>{{cite news |title=Relieved 'bad guy' Poulsen ruins Peru's World Cup return |url=https://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/sport/relieved-bad-guy-poulsen-ruins-peru-s-world-cup-return-10439502 |access-date=13 April 2020 |publisher=Channel News Asia |date=17 June 2018}}</ref>

Poulsen was part of the Danish squad that reached the semi-finals of [[UEFA Euro 2020]] in the summer of 2021. He scored his first goal in the [[UEFA European Championship|European Championship]] in a 2–1 defeat to [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]] on 17 June,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro-2020/match/2024459--denmark-vs-belgium/ |title=Denmark 1–2 Belgium |website=UEFA |date=17 June 2021 }}</ref> adding another four days later in a 4–1 win over [[Russia national football team|Russia]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Flood |first1=George |title=Russia 1-4 Denmark: Danes to face Wales in Euros last 16 after thumping win in Copenhagen |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/russia-vs-denmark-result-euro-2021-copenhagen-damsgaard-christensen-goals-b941850.html |access-date=27 January 2022 |work=Evening Standard |date=22 June 2021}}</ref>
