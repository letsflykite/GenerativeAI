'''Nikola Milenković''' ({{lang-sr-cyr|Никола Миленковић}}, {{IPA-sh|nǐkola milěːŋkoʋitɕ|pron}}; born 12 October 1997) is a Serbian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Centre-back|centre-back]] and [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|right-back]] for [[Serie A]] club [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]] and the [[Serbia national football team|Serbia national team]].

==Club career==
===Partizan===
====2015–16 season====
Passing the youth categories of [[FK Partizan|Partizan]], Milenković was loaned to [[FK Teleoptik|Teleoptik]] for the 2015–16 season.<ref>{{cite web [[FK Mladost Lučani|Mladost Lučani]] played on 10 April 2016.<ref>{{cite web ====2016–17 season====
Milenković started new season pairing with [[Cédric Gogoua]] for the [[2016–17 UEFA Europa League#Second qualifying round|second qualifying round]] of the 2016–17 UEFA Europa League against [[Zagłębie Lubin]]. He also started [[Serbian SuperLiga|SuperLiga]] competition in the first fixture match against [[OFK Bačka]] before spending the following two rounds on the bench. After several bad results and some injuries of the players in defense, Milenković started playing continuously from the fourth fixture against [[FK Javor Ivanjica|Javor Ivanjica]] onwards. He scored his first season goal in a match against [[FK Rad|Rad]], played on 27 August 2016.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sr.partizan.rs/145028-partizan-cukaricki-10-10|title=Partizan – Rad 4:0 (2:0)|website=FK Partizan official website|language=sr|date=27 August 2016|access-date=28 August 2016}}</ref> He also made his [[Serbian Cup]] debut in the first round match, against [[FK Napredak Kruševac|Napredak Kruševac]] on 21 September 2016.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sr.partizan.rs/146449-partizan-napredak-3|title=Partizan – Napredak 3:1 (3:0)|website=FK Partizan official website|language=sr|date=21 September 2016|access-date=23 September 2016}}</ref> After [[Miroslav Vulićević]]'s injury in a [[Serbian Cup|cup]] match against [[FK Žarkovo|Žarkovo]] on 25 October 2016, Milenković wore the captain's armbrand.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sr.partizan.rs/148183-zarkovo-partizan|title=Žarkovo – Partizan 0:2 (0:0)|website=FK Partizan official website|language=sr|date=25 October 2016|access-date=25 October 2016}}</ref> In February 2017, he was listed as one of the 30 best youth footballers in the world by the Italian newspaper [[La Gazzetta dello Sport]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.mozzartsport.com/vesti/oni-ce-pokoriti-svet-spisak-30-najboljih-mladih-fudbalera-planete-to-je-i-jedan-iz-partizana/160088|title=Oni će pokoriti svet: Spisak 30 najboljih mladih fudbalera planete, tu je i jedan iz Partizana|website=mozzartsport.com|language=sr|date=22 February 2017|access-date=22 February 2017}}</ref> he scored his third goal for [[FK Partizan|Partizan]] as a senior in 27 fixture match of the season against [[FK Voždovac|Voždovac]], played on 18 March 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sr.partizan.rs/156284-partizan-vozdovac-3|title=Partizan – Voždovac 4:0 (2:0)|website=FK Partizan official website|language=sr|date=18 March 2017|access-date=18 March 2017}}</ref>

On 27 May, in his last match for Partizan, Milenković scored a header against Partizan's biggest rival [[Red Star Belgrade|Red Star]] which won the game and clinched Partizan's double of the season, winning both the league and cup competitions.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.b92.net/sport/fudbal/vesti.php?yyyy=2017&mm=05&dd=27&nav_id=1265289|title=Milenković doneo duplu krunu Partizanu!|website=[[B92]]|language=sr|date=27 May 2017|access-date=27 May 2017}}</ref>

===Fiorentina===
On 24 May 2017, Partizan president [[Milorad Vučelić]] confirmed that Milenković would be joining [[Serie A]] side [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]] in the summer.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://sport.blic.rs/fudbal/domaci-fudbal/nada-se-ligi-sampiona-vucelic-posle-titule-ostaju-djurdevic-i-leonardo/ybz4n84|title=NADA SE LIGI ŠAMPIONA Vučelić posle titule: Ostaju Đurđević i Leonardo|website=[[Blic]]|language=sr|date=24 May 2017|access-date=27 May 2017}}</ref> The transfer fee was reported as €5.1 million.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.football-italia.net/99714/%E2%80%98milenkovic-will-join-fiorentina%E2%80%99|title=Milenkovic will join Fiorentina|website=football-italia.net|language=en|date=15 March 2017|access-date=27 May 2017}}</ref> Milenković made his debut for Fiorentina in 1–0 away victory over [[Cagliari Calcio|Cagliari]] on 22 December 2017, having played 84 minutes of the game.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://sport.sky.it/calcio/serie-a/2017/12/22/cagliari-fiorentina-risultato-gol.html|title=Cagliari-Fiorentina 0-1: Decide Babacar. GOL E HIGHLIGHTS|website=[[Sky Sports]]|language=it|date=22 December 2017|access-date=5 February 2018}}</ref> During the first few months, Nikola was getting used to his new environment and that's why Viola coach [[Stefano Pioli]] didn't often include him in the starting line-up. However, the tragic death of the team captain [[Davide Astori]] made Pioli change his mind and include Nikola in the starting eleven more often.

At the start of 2018–19 Serie A season matchday 2,  Milenković scored his first goal at [[Stadio Artemio Franchi]] in 6–1 win over [[A.C. ChievoVerona|ChievoVerona]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2018/08/26/italy/serie-a/acf-fiorentina/ac-chievo-verona/2863768/|title=Milenković first goal for Fiorentina|website=Soccerway.com|date=26 August 2018}}</ref> On 20 April 2019 Milenković scored 6th minute opener in 2–1 away defeat against rivals [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://us.soccerway.com/matches/2019/04/20/italy/serie-a/juventus-fc/acf-fiorentina/2864079/|title=JUVENTUS VS. FIORENTINA 2 - 1
|website=Soccerway.com|date=20 April 2019}}</ref>

In the first round of the 2019–20 season against [[SSC Napoli|Napoli]], Milenković scored the second goal for his team as a 7-goal thriller ends in Napoli's favor.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2019/08/24/italy/serie-a/acf-fiorentina/ssc-napoli/3111701/|title=FIORENTINA VS. NAPOLI  3 - 4

|website=Soccerway.com|date=6 October 2019}}</ref> On October 30, Nikola scored his third goal of the season and thus helped Fiorentina come from behind to claim a 2–1 win against [[US Sassuolo Calcio|Sassuolo]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2019/10/30/italy/serie-a/us-sassuolo-calcio/acf-fiorentina/3111798/|title=SASSUOLO VS. ==International career==
Milenković was a member of the [[Serbia national under-19 football team|Serbia under-19]] team in early 2016.<ref name="soccerway"/> Later the same year, he was called up to the [[Serbia national under-20 football team|Serbia U20]] national squad by coach [[Nenad Lalatović]], and made his debut for the team in a match against [[Montenegro national under-21 football team|Montenegro]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.fss.rs/index.php?id=3159&aid=11464|script-title=sr:Лалатовићева Србија (селекција до 20 година) боља од Црне Горе у Подгорици – 2:1(0:0)|website=[[Football Association of Serbia]]|language=sr|date=13 November 2016|access-date=25 November 2016}}</ref> Milenković was called up to the [[Serbia national under-21 football team|U21]] squad by Lalatović in March 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.mozzartsport.com/vesti/zasluzeno-milenkovic-u-reprezentaciji/162136|title=Zasluženo: Milenković u reprezentaciji!|website=mozzartsport.com|language=sr|date=17 March 2017|access-date=18 March 2017}}</ref> He made his first appearance at [[Serbia national under-21 football team|U21]] level on 28 March 2017, in a friendly match against [[Slovakia national under-21 football team|Slovakia]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.fss.rs/index.php?id=2021&aid=12271|script-title=sr:Млада репрезентација са промешаним картама у Словачкој – 1:2 (0:0)|website=[[Football Association of Serbia]]|language=sr|date=28 March 2017|access-date=31 March 2017}}</ref>

Milenković made his debut for the [[Serbia national football team|senior national team]] in a friendly 3–0 loss to [[Qatar national football team|Qatar]] on 29 September 2016.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.eurosport.com/football/international-friendlies/2016/live-qatar-serbia_mtc910197/live.shtml|title=Qatar - Serbia match report|website=eurosport.com|date=29 September 2016|access-date=8 October 2016}}</ref>
 
In June 2018 Serbian head coach [[Mladen Krstajić]] included Milenković in the final 23-man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018 World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44083365 | title = World Cup 2018: All the confirmed squads for this summer's finals in Russia | publisher = BBC Sport | date = 4 June 2018 | access-date = 25 June 2018}}</ref> He played in all three group stage matches.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44427970 | title = Costa Rica 0–1 Serbia | publisher = BBC Sport | author = Timothy Abraham | date = 17 June 2018 | access-date = 25 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44439145 | title = Serbia 1–2 Switzerland | publisher = BBC Sport | author = Matthew Henry | date = 23 June 2018 | access-date = 25 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/06/27/sports/world-cup/brazil-vs-serbia.html|title=World Cup: Brazil cruises into next round with easy victory|newspaper=The New York Times|author=Andrew Das|date=27 June 2018|access-date=27 June 2018}}</ref> He is widely considered as the best performer in the World Cup 2018 squad, with 34 successful tackles (the most in the group stage), and some great positional play.

In November 2022, he was selected in Serbia's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fss.rs/en/head-coach-dragan-stojkovic-announces-the-list-of-players-for-the-fifa-world-cup-2022-in-qatar/ |title=Head-coach Dragan Stojković announces the list of players for the FIFA World Cup 2022 in Qatar |publisher=[[Football Association of Serbia]] |date=11 November 2022 |access-date=11 November 2022}}</ref> He played in all three group stage matches, against [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]],<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/sports/world-cup-2022/richarlison-brings-brazil-to-life-in-the-second-half-defeats-serbia-2-0/3971175/ |title=Richarlison Brings Brazil to Life in the Second Half, Defeats Serbia 2-0 |first=Kristen |last=Conti |newspaper=NBCNew York |location=New York |date=24 November 2022 |access-date=7 December 2022}}</ref> [[Cameroon national football team|Cameroon]],<ref>{{Cite news |last=Sky Sports |date=2022-11-28 |title=World Cup 2022 - Cameroon 3-3 Serbia: Vincent Aboubakar and Eric Maxim Choupo-Moting goals complete stunning comeback |language=en |work=Sky Sports |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/cameroon-vs-serbia/report/462988#:~:text=World%20Cup%202022%20%2D%20Cameroon%203,Moting%20goals%20complete%20stunning%20comeback&text=Cameroon%20kept%20alive%20their%20faint,with%20Serbia%20in%20Al%2DWakrah. |access-date=2022-11-28}}</ref> and [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]].<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/dec/02/serbia-switzerland-world-cup-group-g-match-report |title=Switzerland advance and Serbia go out as Freuler finish settles stormy contest |first=Nick |last=Ames |newspaper=The Guardian|location=London |date=2 December 2022 |access-date=7 December 2022}}</ref> Serbia finished fourth in the group.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://soccer.nbcsports.com/2022/12/02/world-cup-2022-group-g-brazil-serbia-cameroon-switzerland-rankings-table-standings/ |title=World Cup 2022 Group G: Brazil, Serbia, Cameroon, Switzerland schedule, fixtures, rankings |first=Nicholas |last=Mendola |newspaper=NBC Sports|location=Lusail |date=2 December 2022 |access-date=7 December 2022}}</ref>
