'''Sean Everet Johnson''' (born May 31, 1989) is an American professional [[association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Major League Soccer]] club [[Toronto FC]] and the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States national team]].

==Career==

===College and amateur===
[[Image:Sean johnson (soccer player).jpg|thumb|Sean Johnson in 2011]]
Johnson grew up in [[Lilburn, Georgia]] where he attended [[Brookwood High School (Snellville, Georgia)|Brookwood High School]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sbisoccer.com/2017/02/sean-johnson-looking-to-lift-nycfc-following-offseason-trade-from-fire|title = Sean Johnson looking to lift NYCFC following offseason trade from Fire|date = February 8, 2017}}</ref> For a brief period he attended school in California where he played for his high school in Los Angeles County. He would graduate from there in 2005.

Johnson moved to [[Florida]] to play [[college soccer]] at the [[University of Central Florida]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://ucfathletics.cstv.com/sports/m-soccer/mtt/johnson_sean00.html |title=Player Bio: Sean Johnson - UCF OFFICIAL ATHLETIC SITE |access-date=March 13, 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100104180455/http://ucfathletics.cstv.com/sports/m-soccer/mtt/johnson_sean00.html |archive-date=January 4, 2010 }} [[CBS Sports Network]]</ref> where he earned Member of the C-USA Commissioner's Honor Roll and was named Team MVP in 2008. He left college early to enter the [[2010 MLS SuperDraft]] as a [[Generation Adidas]] player.

During his college years Johnson also played one season with [[Atlanta Blackhawks]] in the [[USL Premier Development League]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.uslsoccer.com/teams/2009/13598759.html#STATS |title= United Soccer Leagues (USL)|website=www.uslsoccer.com |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121004045236/http://www.uslsoccer.com/teams/2009/13598759.html |archive-date=October 4, 2012}}</ref>

===Professional===
Sean Johnson was drafted in the fourth round (51st overall) of the [[2010 MLS SuperDraft]] by [[Chicago Fire Soccer Club|Chicago Fire]].<ref>[http://web.mlsnet.com/mls/events/superdraft/2010/draft_tracker.jsp 2010 MLS SuperDraft] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100117173019/http://web.mlsnet.com/mls/events/superdraft/2010/draft_tracker.jsp |date=January 17, 2010 }}</ref> He was the last Generation Adidas player selected in that draft. He made his MLS debut on August 1, 2010, during a 3–2 victory over [[Los Angeles Galaxy]] after earning the starting spot over [[Andrew Dykstra]]. In his rookie season, he earned back to back "Save of the Week" honors. In 2013, he was named the Chicago Fire's Defender of the Year.  He had an 11–9–7 record with the Fire, including with 6 clean sheets.

On December 11, 2016, [[Atlanta United FC|Atlanta United]] acquired Johnson from the [[Chicago Fire Soccer Club|Chicago Fire]] for general allocation money. Later that day [[New York City FC]] acquired Johnson from Atlanta United in exchange for general allocation money and target allocation money.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.atlutd.com/post/2016/12/11/atlanta-united-make-roster-moves-during-active-mls-half-day-trade-window|title=Atlanta United make roster moves during active MLS half-day trade window|date=December 11, 2016}}</ref> He made 206 total appearances for NYCFC and won the [[MLS Cup 2021|2021 MLS Cup]] with the club as their starting goalkeeper.

On January 27, 2023, Johnson signed a two-year contract with [[Toronto FC]].<ref>https://www.torontofc.ca/news/toronto-fc-sign-u-s-international-goalkeeper-sean-johnson {{bare URL inline|date=April 2023}}</ref>

===International===
Johnson holds United States and Jamaican citizenships.<ref name="ives">{{cite web|url=http://www.soccerbyives.net/2013/07/johnson-outstanding-start.html|title=Johnson outstanding in goal in first Gold Cup start for USMNT|date=July 17, 2013}}</ref>  He had brief tryouts with the Jamaican [[Jamaica national under-17 football team|under-17]] team in 2005 and [[Jamaica national under-20 football team|under-20]] team in 2008.

Johnson was selected to the [[United States men's national under-20 soccer team|United States men's national under-20 team]] in 2009.  He appeared in one match in the [[2009 CONCACAF U-20 Championship]] and later took part in three friendly matches. He also participated in the [[2009 FIFA U-20 World Cup]] as an unused substitute.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/tournaments/archive/u20worldcup/egypt2009/matches/round=253217/match=300090224/report.html|title=FIFA: USA vs Germany, 26 September 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130120075459/https://www.fifa.com/tournaments/archive/u20worldcup/egypt2009/matches/round=253217/match=300090224/report.html |archive-date=January 20, 2013}}</ref>

Johnson was a member of the [[United States men's national under-23 soccer team|under-23 national team]] that participated in the [[2012 CONCACAF Men's Olympic Qualifying Championship|2012 Olympic Games qualifying tournament]].  After an injury to [[Bill Hamid]], Johnson entered the game on the 39th minute of the last group match against [[El Salvador national under-23 football team|El Salvador]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://concacaf.globalsportsmedia.com/page.php?sport=soccer&language_id=us&page=tournament&view=match&match_id=1234093 |title=CONCACAF Olympic qualifying 2012: USA vs El Salvador, 26 March 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120401025150/http://concacaf.globalsportsmedia.com/page.php?sport=soccer&language_id=us&page=tournament&view=match&match_id=1234093 |archive-date=April 1, 2012 }}</ref>

Johnson earned his first senior cap for the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States national team]] entering as a halftime substitute in a friendly match against [[Chile national football team|Chile]] on January 22, 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ucfathletics.cstv.com/sports/m-soccer/spec-rel/012511aaa.html|title=Former UCF Goalkeeper Sean Johnson Makes Debut with U.S. Men's National Team|date=January 25, 2011}}</ref>

Johnson was named one of three goalkeepers assigned to the United States's [[2013 CONCACAF Gold Cup]] roster by coach [[Jürgen Klinsmann|Jurgen Klinsmann]]. He served as second choice keeper to [[Nick Rimando]] during a pre-tournament friendly against [[Guatemala national football team|Guatemala]] and for the duration of the tournament. Johnson earned his first senior national team start on July 17, 2013, in the final match of the group stage, recording a clean sheet in a 1–0 victory against rival [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica]]. During that game he made a key save in the 80th minute which led to the US's winning goal. The start officially cap-tied the dual-national Johnson to the United States.<ref name="ives" />

==Personal life==
His parents, Everet and Joy Johnson, are of [[Jamaicans|Jamaican]] and [[African Americans|African-American]] descent.
