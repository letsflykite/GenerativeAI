The '''2022 FIFA World Cup qualification UEFA Group A''' was one of the ten [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)|UEFA groups]] in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification|World Cup qualification]] tournament to decide which teams would qualify for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] finals tournament in Qatar.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/58b8109657d40e3d/original/ijoruwm3ly5kgz9wbu6j-pdf.pdf |title=UEFA preliminary competition for the FIFA World Cup 2022: Draw procedures |work=[[FIFA]] |format=PDF |date=22 October 2020 |access-date=22 October 2020 |archive-date=29 October 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201029082100/https://resources.fifa.com/image/upload/draw-procedures-for-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022tm-preliminary-draw-uefa.pdf?cloudid=ijoruwm3ly5kgz9wbu6j |url-status=live }}</ref> Group A consisted of five teams: [[Azerbaijan national football team|Azerbaijan]], [[Luxembourg national football team|Luxembourg]], [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]], the [[Republic of Ireland national football team|Republic of Ireland]] and [[Serbia national football team|Serbia]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://editorial.uefa.com/resources/0264-1108c702e548-758110ebe57c-1000/draw_result_sheet.pdf |title=FIFA World Cup Preliminary Competition – Draw Results |work=UEFA |format=PDF |date=7 December 2020 |access-date=7 December 2020 |archive-date=31 December 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201231182830/https://editorial.uefa.com/resources/0264-1108c702e548-758110ebe57c-1000/draw_result_sheet.pdf |url-status=live }}</ref> The teams played against each other home-and-away in a [[round-robin tournament|round-robin format]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/271202c2bf80db17/original/adjlwts3o6c57g0skqor-pdf.pdf |title=UEFA preliminary competition format for the FIFA World Cup 2022 |work=FIFA |format=PDF |date=4 December 2020 |access-date=7 December 2020 |archive-date=15 December 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201215061006/https://img.fifa.com/image/upload/adjlwts3o6c57g0skqor.pdf |url-status=live }}</ref>

The group winners, Serbia, qualified directly for the World Cup finals, while the runners-up, Portugal, advanced to the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA second round|second round (play-offs)]].

[[Qatar national football team|Qatar]] were partnered with the five-team Group A, which enabled the 2022 FIFA World Cup hosts to play centralised [[Exhibition game|friendlies]] against these countries on their "spare" match dates. However, these friendlies did not count in the qualifying group standings. Qatar played their "home" matches in Europe in order to allow short travel times for their opponents.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/european-qualifiers/news/0264-11103d6ec600-5258c3966355-1000/ |title=Group A matches with Qatar |work=UEFA |date=11 December 2020 |access-date=12 December 2020 |archive-date=31 December 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201231182744/https://www.uefa.com/european-qualifiers/news/0264-11103d6ec600-5258c3966355-1000/ |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://editorial.uefa.com/resources/0264-110ad9b5b487-e3521001b47e-1000/group_a_-_fixtures_list_with_qatar.pdf |title=World Cup Qualifying Calendar – Group A with Qatar |work=UEFA |date=8 December 2020 |access-date=8 December 2020}}</ref>
