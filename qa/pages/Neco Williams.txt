'''Neco Shay Williams''' (born 13 April 2001) is a Welsh professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Full-back (association football)|full-back]] for {{English football updater|NottingF}} club [[Nottingham Forest F.C.|Nottingham Forest]] and the [[Wales national football team|Wales national team]].

Williams was a member of the [[Wales national football team|Wales]] squad that qualified for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], becoming the first to do so since 1958.

==Club career==
===Liverpool===
[[File:Neco Williams LFCU19 2019.jpg|thumb|upright|Williams playing for [[Liverpool F.C. Reserves and Academy|Liverpool U19]] in 2019]]

Williams joined the academy of [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] at the age of six and progressed through the youth ranks, representing the club at U18, U19 and U23 level, as well as in the [[UEFA Youth League]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.goal.com/en-ae/news/neco-williams-set-to-make-liverpool-debut-in-carabao-cup-tie/ty89ttsmu31d1lfhzq6yi19kk |title=Neco Williams set to make Liverpool debut in Carabao Cup tie against Arsenal |website=Goal.com |last=Jones |first=Neil |date=30 October 2019 |access-date=30 October 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/50436341 |title=Neco Williams: Wales youngster aims to seize Liverpool chance |website=BBC Sport |last=Vincet |first=Gareth |date=15 November 2019 |access-date=29 November 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.soccerladuma.co.za/news/articles/international/categories/english-premier-league/who-is-neco-williams-liverpool-s-latest-academy-graduate/666416?next=true |title=Who Is Neco Williams, Liverpool's Latest Academy Graduate? |website=Soccer Laduma |date=31 October 2019 |access-date=29 November 2019 |last=Bekker |first=Liam}}</ref> He suffered a serious back injury which ruled him out for most of the 2017–18 season, but recovered his place to feature in the club's victorious [[FA Youth Cup]] campaign two years later.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/meet-neco-williams-liverpool-youngster-17157988 |title=Meet Neco Williams – Liverpool youngster described by Steven Gerrard as 'magnificent' in contention for Arsenal clash |newspaper=Liverpool Echo |last=Batte |first=Kathryn |date=28 October 2019 |access-date=30 October 2019}}</ref> 

Williams' form at youth level was rewarded when, on 30 October 2019, he made his debut for the senior side, starting in a 5–5 (5–4) [[penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shoot-out]] win over [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] in the [[EFL Cup]]. He featured prominently in the match and recorded a last-minute [[Assist (association football)|assist]] for [[Divock Origi]]'s goal to send the match to penalties, where Liverpool emerged victorious.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/liverpool-vs-arsenal-result-carabao-cup-liverpool-win-penalty-shootout-anfield-a4274851.html |title=Liverpool 5–5 Arsenal: Liverpool win penalty shootout after 10-goal thriller at Anfield |newspaper=London Evening Standard |last=Krishnan |first=Joe |date=30 October 2019 |access-date=30 October 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/first-team/371335-neco-williams-liverpool-arsenal-debut-reaction |title=Neco Williams: Anfield debut a dream come true |publisher=Liverpool F.C. |last=Williams |first=Sam |date=31 October 2019 |access-date=31 October 2019}}</ref> In December, he was included in Liverpool's squad which won the [[2019 FIFA Club World Cup]], although he was an unused substitute in both of the club's matches in the competition.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/50869633 |title=Flamengo 0–1: Roberto Firmino's extra-time strike delivers first Club World Cup for Liverpool |website=BBC Sport |date=21 December 2019 |access-date=22 December 2019 |last=Poole |first=Harry}}</ref>

round, and [[Shrewsbury Town F.C.|Shrewsbury Town]] in the 4th round replay, forcing an [[own goal]] from [[Ro-Shaun Williams]] in the latter fixture to secure his side's progression in the the youngest in Liverpool's history, with an average age of 19 years and two days.<ref>{{cite news ===Nottingham Forest===
Williams signed for newly promoted Premier League club [[Nottingham Forest F.C.|Nottingham Forest]] on 10 July 2022 on a four-year contract for a fee reported to be around £17&nbsp;million.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nottinghamforest.co.uk/news/2022/july/10/forest-secure-wales-international-williams/ |title=Forest secure Wales international Williams |publisher=Nottingham Forest F.C. |date=10 July 2022 |access-date=7 August 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://theathletic.com/news/nottingham-forest-neco-williams-liverpool-transfer/wSi5kNICpcCp/ |title=Forest confirm £17m Williams signing from Liverpool |first=Amitai |last=Winehouse |website=The Athletic |date=13 July 2022 |access-date=7 August 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
Williams was born in [[Wrexham]] and has represented Wales at Under 19 level.<ref name=worldfootball>{{cite web |url=https://www.worldfootball.net/player_summary/neco-williams/ |title=Neco Williams: Profile |website=worldfootball.net |publisher=HEIM:SPIEL |access-date=24 July 2020}}</ref> In August 2020 he was called into the senior Wales squad for the first time.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/53903132|title=Wales call up Liverpool's Williams|website=BBC Sport }}</ref>  Williams made his [[Wales national football team|Wales]] debut in a 1–0 [[2020–21 UEFA Nations League B|UEFA Nations League]] win over [[Finland national football team|Finland]] on 3 September 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://uk.soccerway.com/matches/2020/09/03/europe/uefa-nations-league/finland/wales/3243448/|title=Finland vs. Wales – 3 September 2020 |website=Soccerway}}</ref> He scored his first goal for the senior team on 6 September 2020 against [[Bulgaria national football team|Bulgaria]] in the UEFA Nations League, scoring the winning goal in a 1–0 victory for Wales.<ref name=Bulgaria>{{Cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/matchstats?gameId=570767|title=Wales vs. Bulgaria – Football Match Stats – September 6, 2020 |publisher=ESPN|date=6 September 2020|access-date=6 September 2020}}</ref> In May 2021 he was selected for the Wales squad for the delayed [[UEFA Euro 2020]] tournament.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57301006|title=Uncapped Colwill in Wales Euro 2020 squad|website=BBC Sport }}</ref>

Williams helped Wales qualify for the [[FIFA World Cup]] in 2022 for the first time since 1958.<ref>{{Cite web |last=UEFA.com |title=Wales-Ukraine {{!}} European Qualifiers 2022 |url=https://www.uefa.com/european-qualifiers/match/2033893--wales-vs-ukraine/ |access-date=14 June 2022 |publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In November 2022 he was named in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup squads#Wales|Wales squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63545011 2022 Wales World Cup squad]</ref>

==Personal life==
Williams has a younger brother, Keelan Williams, who plays for [[Burnley F.C.|Burnley]]'s [[Burnley F.C. Under-23s and Academy|Under 18 side]].<ref>{{cite web |title=5-goal Liverpool U18s thrash Burnley in final game of 2021 |url=https://www.thisisanfield.com/2021/12/5-goal-liverpool-u18s-thrash-burnley-in-final-game-of-2021/ |website=This Is Anfield |date=18 December 2021 |access-date=24 December 2021}}</ref>

Williams' grandfather, Kelvin Jones, died in November 2022, the day before Williams started in Wales' opening game of the World Cup; he revealed the loss after game, dedicating his performance to Jones.<ref>{{cite news |title=World Cup 2022: Neco Williams played after suffering family bereavement |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63711673 |access-date=23 November 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Liverpool players rally as Neco Williams reveals heartache behind World Cup debut |url=https://www.thisisanfield.com/2022/11/liverpool-players-rally-as-neco-williams-reveals-heartache-behind-world-cup-debut/ |website=This is Anfield |date=22 November 2022 |access-date=23 November 2022}}</ref>
