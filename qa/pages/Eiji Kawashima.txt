{{Nihongo|'''Eiji Kawashima'''|川島 永嗣|Kawashima Eiji|born 20 March 1983}} is a Japanese professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Ligue 1]] club [[RC Strasbourg Alsace|Strasbourg]].

Kawashima played in Japan for [[Omiya Ardija]], [[Nagoya Grampus|Nagoya Grampus Eight]] and [[Kawasaki Frontale]] before joining [[Lierse S.K.]] in Belgium in 2010. He then played for another Belgian club, [[Standard Liège]], from 2012 to 2015, and [[Dundee United F.C.|Dundee United]] in Scotland from 2015 to 2016. He represented [[Japan national football team|Japan]] at the [[2010 FIFA World Cup|2010]], [[2014 FIFA World Cup|2014]] and [[2018 FIFA World Cup]]s, earning over 90 caps, and was also named in the final 26-player squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]].

==Early life==
Born in [[Yono, Saitama]], Japan, Kawashima began playing football and started to playing in the goalkeeper after he idolised [[Sergio Goycochea]] and saw a video of his performance, which touched him and since played in the goalkeeper position.<ref name="Kawashima Goalkeeper">{{cite news|title=川島永嗣が感じた日本と欧州の違い…GKへの思いとヨーロッパでプレーする意義|url=http://www.goal.com/jp/ニュース/川島永嗣が感じた日本と欧州の違いgkへの思いとヨーロッパでプレーする意義/1viqo1i75lhvh12b5jsb45jr8n|publisher=Goal.com|language=ja|date=3 February 2018|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref> He then joined Yononishi Junior High School and Urawa Higashi High School.<ref>{{cite news|title=駒大の大型ボランチ・菊地が川崎F入りへ|url=https://web.gekisaka.jp/news/detail/?30380-11021-fl|publisher=Web Gekisaka|language=ja|date=24 November 2007|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref>

Kawashima has an older brother and older sister.<ref>{{cite news|script-title=ja:川島永嗣|url=http://www.frontale.co.jp/profile/2010/mem_01.html|publisher=Kawasaki Frontale|language=ja|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref>

==Club career==

===Early career in Japan===
Kawashima started his professional career at [[Omiya Ardija]] in [[J2 League]] after graduating Urawa Higashi High School.<ref name="Kawashima 2001"/><ref name="Kawashima Fashion"/> On 6 April 2002, he made his Omiya Ardija debut.<ref>{{cite news|title=Eiji Kawashima – Profile|url=https://www.sanspo.com/soccer/japanese/kawashima/profile.html|publisher=Sanspo|language=ja|access-date=23 August 2018|archive-date=5 June 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210605170512/https://www.sanspo.com/soccer/japanese/kawashima/profile.html|url-status=dead}}</ref> He went on to make 41 appearances for the side during his three-year spell. During his time at Omiya Ardija, Kawashima went on trial at [[Serie A]] side [[Parma Calcio 1913|Parma]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sponichi.co.jp/soccer/flash/KFullFlash20100531010.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100603025147/http://www.sponichi.co.jp/soccer/flash/KFullFlash20100531010.html |archive-date=3 June 2010|url-status=dead|title=評価の低さ払しょく 川島「日本のＧＫは欧州でもできる」（サッカー） — スポニチ Sponichi Annex ニュース |language=ja}}</ref><ref name="Kawashima Goalkeeper"/>

In 2004, Kawashima joined [[Nagoya Grampus|Nagoya Grampus Eight]] in Japan's [[J1 League]].<ref name="Kawashima Nagoya Grampus">{{cite news|title=楢﨑正剛と川島永嗣「2人のGKの数奇な巡り合わせ」|url=https://www.soccer-king.jp/sk_column/article/88629.html|publisher=Soccer King|language=ja|date=7 January 2013|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref> Over the three seasons, he competed with veteran goalkeeper [[Seigo Narazaki]] and served as the club's second choice goalkeeper. At times, Kawashima was given a chance of first team appearances, due to Narazaki suffered an injury. However, Kawashima himself suffered an injury concern after injuring his hand towards the end of the season and was sidelined for four weeks.<ref>{{cite news|script-title=ja:名古屋の川島、骨折|url=https://web.gekisaka.jp/news/detail/?21425-2321-fl|publisher=Web Gekisaka|language=ja|date=6 November 2006|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref> He went on to make 17 appearances for the side.

===Lierse===
After participating in the [[2010 FIFA World Cup|2010 World Cup]], Kawashima completed a move to Europe by joining [[Lierse S.K.]] in the [[Belgian Pro League]].<ref name=fifa20100706>{{cite news|title=Kawashima seals Lierse switch|url=https://www.fifa.com/worldfootball/clubfootball/news/newsid=1268784.html|agency=PA|publisher=[[FIFA]]|date=6 July 2010|access-date=11 February 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100603025147/http://www.fifa.com/worldfootball/clubfootball/news/newsid%3D1268784.html|archive-date = 3 June 2010|url-status=dead}}<br>{{cite news|title=GK川島、今季からベルギーでプレー|url=http://jp.uefa.com/memberassociations/news/newsid=1503830.html|publisher=UEFA.com|language=ja|date=8 July 2010|access-date=21 May 2012}}</ref> Upon joining the club, he signed a two–year contract with an option to extend.<ref>{{cite news|title=De eerste samoerai van Lierse|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/gbr2sk7hh|publisher=Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=8 July 2010|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref> About the move Kawashima said, "After I experienced the World Cup, I thought that putting myself in a more competitive environment is the only way I can get better."<ref name=fifa20100706/>

Kawashima made his Lierse debut in a 1–0 loss against [[Sint-Truidense V.V.|Sint-Truidense]] in the opening game of the season.<ref>{{cite news|title=Kawashima loses in Belgian debut|url=https://www.japantimes.co.jp/sports/2010/08/02/soccer/kawashima-loses-in-belgian-debut/|publisher=The Japan Times|date=2 August 2010|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref> After making his Lierse debut, Kawashima quickly became the club's first choice goalkeeper.<ref>{{cite news|title=Eiji Kawashima: 'Er is altijd concurrentie'|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/8s323n1h|publisher=Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=12 November 2010|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref> However, the club struggled at the start of the season and suffered five defeats in the first five matches, but Kawashima impressed the side with saves.<ref>{{cite news|title=VOETBAL 1STE KLASSE Antonio Ghomsi houdt voeten bij KV Mechelen op de grond, maar toont tegelijkertijd ook ambitie|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/l82u3p8g|publisher=Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=17 August 2010|access-date=23 August 2018}}<br>{{cite news|title=VOETBAL 1ste KLASSE CHARLEROI - LIERSE Tot grote tevredenheid van Eric Van Meir houden Kawashima en de verdediging van Lierse de nul|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/ao3006mi|publisher=Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=28 September 2010|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref> On 25 September 2010, he kept his first clean in a 1–0 win over [[R. Charleroi S.C.|Charleroi]], the side's first win in the league of the season.<ref>{{cite news|title=Lierse wint degradatietopper op Charleroi|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/dmf20100925_084|publisher=Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=25 September 2010|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref> In mid–October, Kawashima suffered a groin injury that kept him out throughout the month.<ref>{{cite news|title=VOETBAL 1ste KLASSE G. ====2011 Lierse shouting incident====
On 19 August 2011, during a match between [[Lierse S.K.|Lierse]] and [[Beerschot AC|Germinal Beerschot]], which Lierse were leading 1–0, Kawashima was subjected to taunts and insults by Beerschot supporters, who chanted "Kawashima-Fukushima!" in reference to the [[Fukushima Daiichi nuclear disaster]]. After being confronted by Kawashima, the referee halted the match for several minutes until order was restored. After the match had ended in a 1–1 draw, Kawashima left the [[Association football pitch|pitch]] in tears, visibly upset at the insults. Kawashima said, "I can pass on many things, but not that. This is not funny. Using the drama of Fukushima in this manner is not at all funny."<ref>{{cite news|title=Puike Stijnen pakt puntje bij debuut, Beerschotfans bekogelen Kawashima|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/dmf20110819_200|publisher=Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=19 August 2011|access-date=23 August 2018}}<br>{{cite news|title=Kawashima: 'Onvergeeflijk van de Beerschotfans'|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/dmf20110820_052|publisher=Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=20 August 2011|access-date=23 August 2018}}<br>{{cite web|url=http://www.101greatgoals.com/blog/japanese-goalie-eiji-kawashima-subjected-to-unsavoury-fukushima-disaster-chants-lierse-germinal-beerschot/|title=Japanese goalie Eiji Kawashima subjected to unsavoury Fukushima disaster chants (Lierse – Germinal Beerschot)|date=21 August 2011|access-date=8 May 2013|work=101greatgoals.com|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100603025147/http://www.101greatgoals.com/blog/japanese-goalie-eiji-kawashima-subjected-to-unsavoury-fukushima-disaster-chants-lierse-germinal-beerschot/|archive-date=3 June 2010 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

After the match, Beerschot released a club statement on the incident: "Our fans have crossed a thin line where a bit of fun turns into something serious. The chants aimed at Lierse goalkeeper Kawashima were offensive and completely out of order".<ref>{{cite news|title=Beerschot: 'Kawashima hitste onze fans op'|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/dmf20110820_083|publisher=Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=20 August 2011|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref> However, Beerschot said that Kawashima was partly to blame: "The Lierse shot-stopper also took part in this as he provoked the Beerschot fans with offensive gestures and facial expressions. Our own goalkeeper [[Stijn Stijnen]] on the other hand never reacted to abusive chants and insults from the Lierse faithful." Beerschot further emphasized that it was completely unacceptable to assert insults of this nature.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Somerford|first1=Ben|last2=Coerts|first2=Stan (translation)|title=Beerschot call for calm after insulting chants but claim Lierse's Eiji Kawashima wasn't blameless|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/14/asia/2011/08/24/2633592/beerschot-call-for-calm-after-insulting-chants-but-claim-lierses-|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121016185148/http://www.goal.com/en/news/14/asia/2011/08/24/2633592/beerschot-call-for-calm-after-insulting-chants-but-claim-lierses-|url-status=dead|archive-date=16 October 2012|date=24 August 2011|access-date=25 August 2011}}</ref>

==Style of play==
At Lierse, Kawashima received praise from the club. CEO Neil De Kewler said of him: "The movement within the goal is quick, the processing of the ball on the line is skillful, furthermore, the player with the leadership that can lead the defense line", while technical director Root Kelzer said: "I can not think of any bad points about him, I will not have anyone who dislikes him, He is a very professional athlete, seriously and disciplinedly open for everyone, every time he looks up, he asks for a handshake with a friendly smile, not only as a player but also as an individual."<ref>{{cite news|script-title=ja:川島永嗣のプロフェッショナリズム（前編）|url=https://www.footballchannel.jp/2013/06/03/post5026/2/|publisher=Football Channel|language=ja|date=3 June 2013|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref>

Prior to signing for Dundee United, Manager Mixu Paatelainen said about him: "As for Eiji, he is quality. He is very agile and quick. He commands and is a leader behind the defence. He talks a lot and sometime demands better action from the defenders."<ref>{{cite news|title=Manager Mixu Paatelainen: New keeper Eiji Kawashima has the character and ability to rally my struggling Dundee United side|url=https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/manager-mixu-paatelainen-new-keeper-7013587|publisher=Daily Record|date=15 December 2015|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref>

It worked when he joined Lierse two years later after signing for Tetsuro Kiyooka.<ref>{{cite news|title=川崎F・川島が海外移籍も視野に代理人契約|url=https://web.gekisaka.jp/news/detail/?46790-27758-fl|publisher=Web Gekisaka|language=ja|date=11 December 2008|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref> In September 2014, he revealed on his blog that he was married.<ref>{{cite news|script-title=ja:「皆様にご報告」|url=https://ameblo.jp/eiji-kawashima/entry-11922918356.html|publisher=Ameblo|language=ja|date=10 September 2015|access-date=23 August 2018}}<br>{{cite news|title=日本代表GK川島が入籍!!ブログで喜び報告|url=https://web.gekisaka.jp/news/detail/?146898-146898-fl|publisher=Web Gekisaka|language=ja|date=10 September 2014|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref> He said he met his wife four years ago and began dating three years later before getting married.<ref>{{cite news|script-title=ja:ＧＫ川島が結婚発表「家族守らなければ」|url=https://www.nikkansports.com/soccer/japan/news/f-sc-tp2-20140911-1364776.html|publisher=Nikkan Sports|language=ja|date=11 September 2014|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref> In December 2015,  Kawashima revealed on his blog that he was father for the first time when his wife gave birth to a son named Kensei.<ref>{{cite news|script-title=ja:川島永嗣、Ｗの喜び！第１子長男誕生＆ダンディーＵと本契約へ|url=https://www.sponichi.co.jp/soccer/news/2015/12/03/kiji/K20151203011620730.html|publisher=Sponichi Annex|language=ja|date=3 December 2015|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref>

In October 2010, Kawashima signed a sponsorship with [[Samantha Thavasa]].<ref>{{cite news|script-title=ja:ＧＫ川島がサマンサタバサと契約|url=https://www.nikkansports.com/soccer/japan/news/p-sc-tp2-20101005-686820.html|publisher=Nikkan Sports|language=ja|date=5 October 2010|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref> He was featured in June 2012 cover of [[Ermenegildo Zegna]] magazine.<ref>{{cite news|script-title=ja:おっしゃれ～川島ファッション誌でビシッ|url=https://www.nikkansports.com/soccer/japan/news/p-sc-tp2-20120606-962861.html|publisher=Nikkan Sports|language=ja|date=6 June 2012|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref> Outside of football, Kawashima is considered very popular in his native country Japan.<ref>{{cite news|title=Keeper van Lierse 'vangt ze' in Japanse reclame|url=http://www.standaard.be/cnt/blbca_20111221_001|publisher=De Standaard|language=nl|date=21 December 2011|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref> During a 1–1 draw against Genk on 18 December 2010, at least 500 Japanese Kawashima's supporters visited [[Herman Vanderpoortenstadion]] to watch him play.<ref>{{cite news|title=Lierse: 'Meer drukte rond Kawashima dan Lukaku'|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/gnj33ffcv|publisher=Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=9 December 2010|access-date=23 August 2018}}<br>{{cite news|title=Élk Japans meisje wil trouwen met Lierse-doelman|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/gmv33v7rr|publisher=Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=19 December 2010|access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref>
