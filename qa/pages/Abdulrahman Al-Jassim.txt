'''Abdulrahman Ibrahim Al-Jassim''' ({{lang-ar|عبد الرحمن الجاسم}}; born 14 October 1987) is a Qatari [[Referee (association football)|football referee]] who has been a full international referee for [[FIFA]] since 2013.

== Career ==
He was one of the referees for the [[2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup]] in South Korea.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/news/y=2017/m=4/news=match-officials-for-the-fifa-u-20-world-cup-korea-republic-2017-appoin-2878553.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170404181400/http://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/news/y=2017/m=4/news=match-officials-for-the-fifa-u-20-world-cup-korea-republic-2017-appoin-2878553.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=4 April 2017|title=Match officials for the FIFA U-20 World Cup Korea Republic 2017 appointed|publisher=FIFA.com|date=4 April 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://resources.fifa.com/mm/document/tournament/competition/02/87/85/54/listofappointedmatchofficialsfifau-20wckorearep2017_neutral.pdf|title=List of Appointed Match Officials FIFA U-20 World Cup Korea Rep. 2017|publisher=FIFA.com|access-date=25 May 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170404221407/http://resources.fifa.com/mm/document/tournament/competition/02/87/85/54/listofappointedmatchofficialsfifau-20wckorearep2017_neutral.pdf|archive-date=4 April 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> Al-Jassim was appointed to be a [[Video assistant referee|Video Assistant Referee]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/36-referees-and-63-assistant-referees-appointed-as-russia-2018-match-officials|title=2018 FIFA World Cup™ - News - 36 referees and 63 assistant referees appointed as Russia 2018 Match Officials|website=www.fifa.com|language=en-GB|access-date=4 July 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Match officials for the 2018 FIFA World Cup|url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/6437a6096b00d2e8/original/w7qdlidnrq4lah2pvvc3-pdf.pdf|access-date=4 July 2021|website=FIFA}}</ref> Al-Jassim was also appointed to be a referee at the [[2019 AFC Asian Cup]] in the United Arab Emirates.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.the-afc.com/competitions/afc-asian-cup/latest/news/largest-ever-cast-of-match-officials-appointed-for-uae-2019|title=Largest-ever cast of match officials appointed for UAE 2019|website=www.the-afc.com|language=en-GB|access-date=4 July 2019}}</ref> He was also the [[2020 AFC Champions League Final]] referee. In June 2019 it was announced that Al-Jassim would officiate in the [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup]] as part of a referee exchange program between [[CONCACAF]] and the [[Asian Football Confederation|AFC]], which had previously seen Mexican referee [[César Arturo Ramos]] in the 2019 AFC Asian Cup.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.goldcup.org/en/article/match-officials-appointed-for-2019-concacaf-gold-cup|title=Match Officials Appointed for 2019 Concacaf Gold Cup|website=www.goldcup.org|language=en|access-date=4 July 2019}}</ref> The FIFA Referees Committee appointed Abdulrahman for [[2019 FIFA Club World Cup]] in Qatar on 14 November as part of an all-Qatari trio of officials.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://fifa.pressfire.net/media/newsletter/List-of-Appointed-Match-Officials-FIFA-CWC-Qatar-2019.pdf|title=List of Appointed Match Officials|publisher=FIFA}}</ref> He officiated the final between [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] and [[Clube de Regatas do Flamengo|Flamengo]] at the [[Khalifa International Stadium]] in Doha.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://menafn.com/1099455585/FIFA-Club-World-Cup-finals-to-have-all-Qatari-referee-trio|title=FIFA Club World Cup finals to have all-Qatari referee trio|website=menafn.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://menafn.com/1099455834/Qatars-Al-Jassim-to-blow-the-whistle-at-title-decider|title=Qatar's Al Jassim to blow the whistle at title decider|website=menafn.com}}</ref> He was appointed to the referee team for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], in his native Qatar.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thepeninsulaqatar.com/article/01/11/2022/fifa-referee-al-jassim-relishing-home-world-cup-challenge|title=FIFA referee Al Jassim relishing home World Cup challenge|work=The Peninsula|date=November 1, 2022|first=Fawad|last=Hussain}}</ref>
