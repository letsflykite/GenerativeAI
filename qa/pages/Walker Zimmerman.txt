'''Walker Dwain Zimmerman''' (born May 19, 1993) is an American professional [[Association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[Defender (association football)|center-back]] for [[Major League Soccer]] club [[Nashville SC]] and the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States national team]].

A native of [[Lawrenceville, Georgia|Lawrenceville]], [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]], Zimmerman played two seasons in [[college soccer|college]] with the [[Furman Paladins men's soccer|Furman Paladins]] before signing a [[Generation Adidas]] deal with [[Major League Soccer]]. He was then selected in the [[2013 MLS SuperDraft]] by [[FC Dallas]] as the 7th overall pick. Initially struggling for playtime due to injuries, Zimmerman established himself as a regular starter in [[2016 FC Dallas season|2016]]. He started in 38 matches as FC Dallas completed the double, winning the [[Supporters' Shield]] and the [[2016 U.S. Open Cup Final|U.S. Open Cup]]. In December 2017, Zimmerman was traded to expansion side [[Los Angeles FC]], helping the club win the Supporters' Shield in [[2019 Major League Soccer season|2019]]. Zimmerman was also selected for the [[2019 MLS All-Star Game|MLS All-Star]] team and [[2019 Major League Soccer season#MLS Best XI|MLS Best XI]]. Zimmerman was then traded to another expansion side, [[Nashville SC]], in February 2020. With Nashville SC, he won back-to-back [[MLS Defender of the Year Award|Defender of the Year]] awards in [[2020 Major League Soccer season#End-of-season awards|2020]] and [[2020 Major League Soccer season#End-of-season awards|2021]].

Zimmerman made his debut for the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]] in January 2017 against [[Jamaica national football team|Jamaica]]. In 2019, he was selected into the squad that finished runners-up in the [[CONCACAF Gold Cup]]. He was also a member of the squad that won the tournament in [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2021]], starting all three group stage matches before having to leave the tournament due to a hamstring injury.

==Club career==
===Youth and college===
Zimmerman came through the youth academy at Gwinnett Soccer Association in [[Lilburn, Georgia]], where he was mentored by former [[Atlanta Silverbacks]] and [[Charleston Battery]] manager Nuno Piteira. Zimmerman also went to [[Brookwood High School (Georgia)|Brookwood High School]] and played soccer there. He was coached by Daniel Klinect.

Zimmerman played [[college soccer]] at [[Furman University]] between 2011 and 2012. He was named First Team All-Conference 2011, Southern Conference Freshman of the Year 2011, and National Soccer Coaches Association of America NCAA Division I Men's All-America Second Team in 2012.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://walkerzimmerman.com/category/career/ |title={title} |access-date=May 13, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130123114210/http://walkerzimmerman.com/category/career/ |archive-date=January 23, 2013 |url-status=dead  }}</ref>

===Professional===
[[FC Dallas]] selected Zimmerman in the first round (No. 7 overall) of the [[2013 MLS SuperDraft]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/video/2013/01/17/superdraft-13-fc-dallas-snag-walker-zimmerman-7th-pick|title=SuperDraft '13 – FC Dallas snag Walker Zimmerman with the 7th pick|work=MLSsoccer.com|access-date=May 13, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130531045306/http://www.mlssoccer.com/video/2013/01/17/superdraft-13-fc-dallas-snag-walker-zimmerman-7th-pick|archive-date=May 31, 2013|url-status=live}}</ref> He made his professional debut on May 11, 2013, as a late substitute in a 2–1 victory against [[D.C. United]].

On December 10, 2017, Zimmerman was traded by FC Dallas to new expansion side [[Los Angeles FC]] in exchange for $250,000 in General Allocation Money and $250,000 in Targeted Allocation Money. The two clubs also swapped allocation rankings with FC Dallas moving to #1 and Los Angeles FC moving to #11.<ref>{{cite news|title=LAFC ACQUIRES WALKER ZIMMERMAN FROM FC DALLAS|url=https://www.lafc.com/walker-zimmerman|access-date=December 10, 2017|work=Los Angeles FC|date=December 10, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171211053441/https://www.lafc.com/walker-zimmerman|archive-date=December 11, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> During the 2019 season, Zimmerman was also an assistant coach for the [[UCLA Bruins men's soccer]] program.

On February 11, 2020, Zimmerman was traded to Major League Soccer expansion club [[Nashville SC]] in exchange for up to $1.25 million in General Allocation Money and a 2020 international roster spot.<ref>{{cite news |title=LAFC Acquires Up To $1.25 Million In Allocation Money From Nashville SC In Exchange For Walker Zimmerman |url=https://www.lafc.com/post/2020/02/11/lafc-acquires-125-million-allocation-money-nashville-sc-exchange-walker-zimmerman |access-date=February 11, 2020 |work=Los Angeles FC |date=February 11, 2020}}</ref>

On February 29, 2020, Zimmerman scored the first goal in Nashville SC history.

On April 29, 2022, Nashville announced they had signed Zimmerman to a contract extension through 2025, and that it would also make him a [[Designated Player]] on their roster.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nashvillesc.com/news/nashville-soccer-club-extends-contracts-of-mls-best-xi-hany-mukhtar-and-walker-z|title=Nashville Soccer Club Extends Contracts of MLS Best XI Hany Mukhtar and Walker Zimmerman|date=April 29, 2022|work=[[Nashville SC]]}}</ref> The new contract made Zimmerman only the fourth American defender to be signed to a DP deal in league history.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://theathletic.com/3281638/2022/04/29/walker-zimmerman-nashville-sc-contract/|title=How Nashville SC made Walker Zimmerman a designated player: 'He was our Tom Brady'|date=April 29, 2022|work=[[The Athletic]]|first=Paul|last=Tenorio}}</ref>

==International career ==
On January 6, 2017, Zimmerman was called up for the first time to the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States national team]] by coach [[Bruce Arena]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2017/01/05/16/31/20170105-mnt-release-head-coach-bruce-arena-calls-32-players-for-january-camp |title=Arena Calls 32 Players for MNT January Camp |publisher=U.S. Soccer |date=January 5, 2017 |access-date=June 19, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170106010928/http://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2017/01/05/16/31/20170105-mnt-release-head-coach-bruce-arena-calls-32-players-for-january-camp |archive-date=January 6, 2017 |url-status=live  }}</ref> Zimmerman earned his first cap and start against [[Jamaica national football team|Jamaica]] in a January Camp friendly on February 3, 2017. He was named player of the match.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fcdallas.com/post/2017/02/03/fc-dallas-defender-walker-zimmerman-makes-usmnt-debut-named-player-match|title=FC Dallas Defender Walker Zimmerman Makes USMNT Debut, Named Player of the Match|date=February 3, 2017|access-date=February 4, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170204171043/http://www.fcdallas.com/post/2017/02/03/fc-dallas-defender-walker-zimmerman-makes-usmnt-debut-named-player-match|archive-date=February 4, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> He scored his first goal (a header) on May 28, 2018, against [[Bolivia national football team|Bolivia]]. In a [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]] match against [[Wales]], Zimmerman tackled [[Gareth Bale]], the Welsh attacker for [[Penalty kick (association football)|penalty]] in the 82nd minute. [[Gareth Bale|Bale]] scored the penalty and tied the game. The game ended 1–1. According to [[ESPN]], it was "a clumsy and unnecessary challenge".<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-11-21 |title=On brink of win, USMNT can't close out Wales |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/fifa-world-cup/story/4806871/on-brink-of-winusmnt-cant-close-out-wales |access-date=2022-11-23 |website=ESPN.com |language=en}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Zimmerman was born in [[Lawrenceville, Georgia]], on May 19, 1993, to Becky and David Zimmerman. He has two older siblings.

Zimmerman is a [[Christianity|Christian]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Doering |first1=Joshua |title=Walker Zimmerman leans on faith as he leads Nashville SC, USMNT |url=https://sportsspectrum.com/sport/soccer/2021/06/17/walker-zimmerman-faith-nashville-sc-usmnt/ |website=Sports Spectrum |date=June 17, 2021 |access-date=June 18, 2021}}</ref> Zimmerman is married to Sally Zimmerman. They have one child together.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Doering |first1=Joshua |title=Walker Zimmerman leads Nashville SC into MLS playoffs, grateful for 'how sacrificial God's love is' |url=https://sportsspectrum.com/sport/soccer/2021/11/23/walker-zimmerman-nashville-fc-gods-love/ |website=Sports Spectrum |date=November 23, 2021 |access-date=November 23, 2021}}</ref>
