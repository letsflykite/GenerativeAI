'''Stefan de Vrij''' (born 5 February 1992) is a Dutch professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre back]] for {{Italian football updater|Internazionale}} club [[Inter Milan]] and the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands national team]].

He began his career at his local club VV Spirit, and debuted as a professional for [[Feyenoord]] aged 17. He spent five seasons at the [[Eredivisie]] club, serving briefly as captain, before joining Lazio in July 2014.

A full international since 2012, De Vrij has earned over 50 caps for the Netherlands. He was part of the Dutch team that finished third at the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]], and was selected for the [[Castrol Performance Index]] Team of the Tournament. He also played at [[UEFA Euro 2020]].

==Early life==
De Vrij grew up in [[Ouderkerk aan den IJssel]]. He is the youngest son of Jan de Vrij, who played for local club VV Spirit, where Stefan de Vrij started his career. His older brothers Niels and Eric played football in their youth and continued to different career paths.<ref name="ad">{{cite news |title=Dromen van doorbraak: Stefan de Vrij (16) ontwikkelt zich bij Feyenoord en Oranje sterk |url=http://www.ad.nl/ad/nl/1038/Rotterdam/article/detail/249911/2009/02/03/Dromen-van-doorbraak-Stefan-de-Vrij-16-ontwikkelt-zich-bij-Feyenoord-en-Oranje-sterk.dhtml |newspaper=AD |language=nl |trans-title=Dreaming of breakthrough: Stefan de Vrij (16) develops strongly at Feyenoord and Oranje |date=3 February 2009 |access-date=30 September 2009}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Feyenoord===
[[File:Stefan de Vrij.jpg|thumb|De Vrij training with [[Feyenoord]] in 2012|alt=|left|256x256px]]

De Vrij played for local [[Ouderkerk aan den IJssel]] club VV Spirit for five seasons. At the age of 10, he attended a [[Feyenoord]] talent day. After various training sessions and a friendly match against ARC, De Vrij was officially asked to join Feyenoord's youth division.<ref name="ad"/> At VV Spirit, De Vrij often played as a central midfielder, but at Feyenoord he got turned into a defender. The youngster developed himself rapidly. After playing for Feyenoord U15, De Vrij was one of the players to skip the U16 team to play in the U17 immediately.<ref name="ad"/>

On 17 July 2009, De Vrij signed his first professional contract with Feyenoord until summer 2012.<ref>{{cite news |title=De Vrij tekent eerste profcontract |url-status=dead}}</ref> De Vrij made his official debut for Feyenoord's first team on 24 September 2009, when he replaced [[Kelvin Leerdam]] in the 58th minute in the [[KNVB Cup]] away match against [[Harkemase Boys]] (0–5).<ref>{{cite news |title=Feyenoord boekt onberispelijke zege in Friesland ===Lazio===
On 30 July 2014, De Vrij joined [[Serie A]] club [[S.S. Lazio|Lazio]] for an undisclosed fee. He said "Lazio really proved that they wanted me and I am very happy I have made this move. I hope to become a more complete defender in Italy." De Vrij had been widely expected to follow his international manager [[Louis van Gaal]] to [[Manchester United]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Stefan de Vrij: Netherlands defender signs for Lazio |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/28573394|access-date=30 July 2014 |work=[[BBC Sport]] |date=30 July 2014}}</ref>

He made his debut on 24 August in the third round of the [[Coppa Italia]], and scored the third goal of a 7–0 home win over third-tier [[Bassano Virtus]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Coppa Italia, Lazio a valanga. Udinese, Di Natale show|trans-title=Coppa Italia, Lazio avalanche. Udinese, the Di Natale show |url=http://sport.sky.it/sport/calcio_italiano/2014/08/24/coppa_italia_terzo_turno_risultati_genoa_verona_lazio_cesena_empoli_sampdoria_udinese.html|access-date=25 August 2014 |work=[[Sky Italia]] |date=24 August 2014 |language=it|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140826120711/http://sport.sky.it/sport/calcio_italiano/2014/08/24/coppa_italia_terzo_turno_risultati_genoa_verona_lazio_cesena_empoli_sampdoria_udinese.html|archive-date=26 August 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> A week later, he played his first Serie A match as Lazio began the season with a 3–1 defeat at [[A.C. Milan]].<ref>{{cite news |title=AC Milan 3–1 Lazio: Inzaghi era starts with a bang |url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/match/milan-vs-lazio/1836107?ICID=HP_FM_6|access-date=31 August 2014 |work=Goal.com |date=31 August 2014}}</ref> On 21 September, he was sent off in the 85th minute of a game away to [[Genoa CFC|Genoa]] which Lazio had been dominating, and two minutes later [[Mauricio Pinilla]] scored the only goal to give Genoa victory.<ref>{{cite news |title=Match report from Genoa v Lazio |url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/live/match/323239/report|access-date=9 November 2014 |work=Sky Sports |date=21 September 2014}}</ref> De Vrij featured in the [[2015 Coppa Italia Final]] on 20 May against [[Juventus]], being substituted for [[Keita Baldé Diao|Keita]] at half time in extra time in an eventual 2–1 defeat.<ref>{{cite news |title=Juventus 2–1 Lazio |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/32783652|access-date=21 May 2015 |work=BBC Sport |date=20 May 2015}}</ref>

His second season began with the [[2015 Supercoppa Italiana]], playing the full 90 minutes of a 2–0 loss to [[Juventus]] in [[Shanghai]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.vavel.com/en/international-football/2015/08/08/italy-calcio/523784.html |title=Goals and Highlights: Lazio 0-2 Juventus in Serie a 2021 &#124; 11/20/2021 |date=20 November 2021}}</ref> In September, he picked up a knee injury on international duty but was passed fit to play a further match for the Netherlands, infuriating Lazio's medical staff.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Gladwell |first1=Ben |title=Lazio hit out at Netherlands doctors over Stefan De Vrij injury |url=http://www.espnfc.co.uk/lazio/story/2603218/lazio-angry-over-stefan-de-vrij-injury-for-dutch|access-date=8 November 2015 |publisher=ESPN |date=9 September 2015}}</ref> He did not make a sufficient recovery, and was subjected to an operation the following month.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Amako |first1=Uche |title=Manchester United rocked as defensive target needs surgery on knee injury |url=http://www.express.co.uk/sport/football/615947/Manchester-United-Stefan-De-Vrij-transfer-target-surgery-knee-injury|access-date=8 November 2015 |work=Daily Express |date=30 October 2015}}</ref> In November, it was confirmed that he would miss the next six months due to the injury.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Vickers |first1=Craig |title=De Vrij sidelined for six months |url=https://www.vavel.com/en/international-football/2015/11/04/italy-calcio/562603-de-vrij-sidelined-for-six-months.html |website=Varel |access-date=21 January 2021 |date=4 November 2015}}</ref>

He scored his first goal for the club in Serie A on 11 September 2016 in a 1–1 away draw to [[A.C. ChievoVerona|Chievo]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.legaseriea.it/it/serie-a-tim/match-report/2016-17/UNICO/UNI/3/CHILAZ |title=Calendario e Risultati - Stagione 2016-17 - 3^ Giornata - Lega Serie A |website=www.legaseriea.it}}</ref>

In March 2018, Lazio's sporting director Igli Tare announced that De Vrij would be leaving the club on a free transfer in the summer.<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://thelaziali.com/index.php/2018/03/29/tare-talks-de-vrij-savic-lucas/ |title=Tare Talks: De Vrij, Milinkovic-Savic and Lucas Leiva |date=29 March 2018 |work=The Laziali|access-date=29 March 2018 |language=en-GB}}</ref>

===Inter Milan===
Following months of speculation, on 28 May 2018, De Vrij announced that he was going to join [[Inter Milan]] starting on 1 July.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.ad.nl/buitenlands-voetbal/ik-denk-me-bij-inter-nog-verder-te-kunnen-ontwikkelen~aabf2d61/ |title='Ik denk me bij Inter nog verder te kunnen ontwikkelen' |date=28 May 2018|access-date=28 May 2018 |publisher=[[Algemeen Dagblad]] |language=nl}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.football-italia.net/122012/de-vrij-‘i’ve-signed-inter’ |title=De Vrij: 'I've signed for Inter' |publisher=football-italia.net |date=28 May 2018|access-date=28 May 2018}}</ref> On 11 July, he was officially presented as an Inter player, signing a contract until June 2023.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.inter.it/en/news/63223/stefan-de-vrij-is-an-inter-player |title=Stefan De Vrij is an Inter player! |publisher=Inter Milan |date=2 July 2018|access-date=19 March 2019}}</ref> De Vrij received squad number 6, and made his competitive debut on 19 August in the opening championship week against [[U.S. Sassuolo Calcio|Sassuolo]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.inter.it/en/news/63733/sassuolo-1-0-inter-berardi-penalty-proves-decisive |title=Sassuolo 1-0 Inter: Berardi penalty proves decisive |publisher=Inter Milan |date=19 August 2018|access-date=19 March 2019}}</ref>

He scored his first goal for ''Nerazzurri'' one week later, putting his side two goals up against [[Torino F.C.|Torino]] at home, in an eventual 2–2 draw.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.inter.it/en/news/63819/inter-2-2-torino-nerazzurri-held-during-first-game-at-san-siro |title=Inter 2-2 Torino: Nerazzurri held during first game at San Siro |publisher=Inter Milan |date=26 August 2018|access-date=19 March 2019}}</ref> In September 2018, De Vrij played his 100th match in [[Serie A]] in the 2–1 home win over Fiorentina.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.inter.it/en/news/64263/inter-2-1-fiorentina-all-you-need-to-know |title=Inter 2-1 Fiorentina, all you need to know |publisher=Inter Milan |date=26 September 2018|access-date=19 March 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.inter.it/en/news/64257/inter-keep-up-momentum-with-2-1-win-over-fiorentina |title=Inter keep up momentum with 2-1 win over Fiorentina |publisher=Inter Milan |date=25 September 2018|access-date=19 March 2019}}</ref> He also made his first [[UEFA Champions League]] appearance in season opener against [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]], assisting [[Matías Vecino]]'s injury-time winner for a 2–1 home win.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.inter.it/en/news/64141/match-review-inter-2-1-tottenham-vecino-is-there |title=Match Review, Inter 2-1 Tottenham: Vecino is there! |publisher=Inter Milan |date=19 September 2018|access-date=19 March 2019}}</ref> De Vrij eventually played 5 out of 6 matches in the competition's [[2018–19 UEFA Champions League group stage#Group B|group stage]], which saw en early elimination of Inter, instead continuing their European adventure in [[2018–19 UEFA Europa League knockout phase|knockout phase]] of [[2018–19 UEFA Europa League|UEFA Europa League]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.inter.it/en/news/65451/match-review-inter-1-1-psv-eindhoven |title=Match Review, Inter 1-1 PSV Eindhoven |publisher=Inter Milan |date=12 December 2018|access-date=19 March 2019}}</ref>

Later on 14 March 2019, in the second leg of Europa League round of 16, De Vrij's wrong ball control resulted fatal for Inter who conceded inside 5 minutes, and eventually lost the game to [[Eintracht Frankfurt]] 1–0 at [[San Siro]], crashing out of competition 1–0 on aggregate.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.inter.it/en/news/66867/inter-0-1-eintracht-the-nerazzurri-out-of-the-europa-league |title=Inter 0-1 Eintracht: The Nerazzurri out of the Europa League |publisher=Inter Milan |date=14 March 2019|access-date=19 March 2019}}</ref> Three days later, De Vrij scored in the [[Derby della Madonnina]] match against rivals [[A.C. Milan|Milan]], helping his team to win 3–2,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.inter.it/en/news/66927/milano-is-still-nerazzurro-milan-2-3-inter |title=Milano is still Nerazzurro: Milan 2-3 Inter! |publisher=Inter Milan |date=17 March 2019|access-date=19 March 2019}}</ref> taking back the third place in championship,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.euronews.com/2019/03/17/inter-edge-milan-in-five-goal-derby-thriller-to-go-third |title=Inter edge Milan in five-goal derby thriller to go third |publisher=Euronews |agency=Reuters |date=17 March 2019|access-date=19 March 2019}}</ref> and completing the first league double over them since 2011–12 season.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.inter.it/en/news/66951/inter-starting-to-dominate-the-derbymilano |title=Inter starting to dominate the #DerbyMilano |publisher=Inter Milan |date=18 March 2019|access-date=19 March 2019}}</ref>

On 9 November 2019, in the [[2019–20 Inter Milan season|2019–20 season]], De Vrij made his 50th appearance in all competitions for Inter in a 2–1 league win over [[Hellas Verona F.C.|Verona]] at San Siro.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.inter.it/en/news/2019/11/10/stats-inter-hellas-verona.html |title=Inter 2-1 Hellas Verona, all you need to know |publisher=Inter Milan |date=10 November 2019|access-date=10 November 2019}}</ref> De Vrij’s contract with Inter Milan is set to expire in June 2023 <ref>{{cite web|date=24 April 2023|title =Contract extension discussions with the former Lazio defender

==International career==
===Youth teams===
De Vrij's strong development at Feyenoord did not pass by unnoticed, as he quickly received his first invitation for a national youth team.<ref name="ad"/> On 21 November 2007, the Netherlands U16 won the friendly match against Ukraine U16 (1–0), with De Vrij's first appearance in the starting line-up.<ref>{{cite news |title=Onder 16 wint eerste oefenduel |url=http://www.knvb.nl/archief/oranje/knvb.nl_oranje_nieuws/onder_16_wint_eerste_oefenduel |newspaper=KNVB |language=nl |trans-title=Under 16 wins first friendly |date=24 September 2009 |access-date=26 September 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090619183701/http://www.knvb.nl/archief/oranje/knvb.nl_oranje_nieuws/onder_16_wint_eerste_oefenduel |archive-date=19 June 2009}}</ref>

De Vrij was active on the [[2009 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship]] in Germany. The Dutch team, aided by a strong contingent of seven Feyenoord players in the squad, finished second after losing the final to Germany in extra-time (2–1).<ref>{{cite news |title=Germany secure maiden U17 crown |url=http://www.uefa.com/competitions/under17/fixturesresults/round=15210/match=2001173/report=rp.html |newspaper=UEFA |date=18 May 2009 |access-date=26 September 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090814043850/http://www.uefa.com/competitions/under17/fixturesresults/round=15210/match=2001173/report=rp.html |archive-date=14 August 2009}}</ref> De Vrij was paired up with [[Dico Koppers]] in central defence<ref>{{cite news |title=Captain's guide: Netherlands |url=http://www.uefa.com/competitions/under17/news/kind=1/newsid=830487.html |newspaper=UEFA |date=17 May 2009 |access-date=30 September 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090520152510/http://www.uefa.com/competitions/under17/news/kind=1/newsid=830487.html |archive-date=20 May 2009}}</ref> and participated in every match the ''Oranje'' played without getting substituted, making a notable contribution.<ref name="ad"/> With a second place on the European Championship, the Netherlands U17 qualified for the [[2009 FIFA U-17 World Cup]] in Nigeria.

===Senior team===
On 7 May 2012, De Vrij was named in the provisional list of 36 players for the [[UEFA Euro 2012]] tournament, one of nine uncapped players to be chosen by [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]] manager [[Bert van Marwijk]] as part of the preliminary squad. He did not make the final cut. On 15 August 2012, De Vrij made his debut under new manager [[Louis van Gaal]] in the 4–2 loss against [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]] in a [[Exhibition game|friendly match]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.sbnation.com/2012/8/15/3245385/netherlands-vs-belgium-2012-final-score-result-match-report |title=Belgium Vs. Netherlands 2012: Final Score, Dries Mertens Dominates In 4-2 Win |publisher=SBNation.com |author=Kim McCauley |date=15 August 2012|access-date=21 March 2019}}</ref>

He was one of four Feyenoord defenders named in [[Louis van Gaal]]'s squad for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |title=World Cup 2014: Netherlands announce final World Cup squad |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/27402627|access-date=4 June 2014 |work=[[BBC Sport]] |date=31 May 2014}}</ref> In the Netherlands' first game of the tournament, against [[Spain national football team|Spain]] on 13 June, the referee gave a questionable penalty kick after 26 minutes after [[Diego Costa]] stood on De Vrij's foot and fell.<ref>{{cite news |title=Spain earns first goal after questionable penalty call |url=http://ftw.usatoday.com/2014/06/spain-penalty-call |work=USA TODAY SPORTS |date=13 June 2014}}</ref> [[Xabi Alonso]] converted this penalty to put Spain 1–0 up. The Dutch fought back, and De Vrij scored their third goal, the first of his international career, as they eventually won 5–1.<ref>{{cite news |title=Spain 1–5 Netherlands |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/25285043|access-date=13 June 2014 |work=[[BBC Sport]] |date=13 June 2014}}</ref> De Vrij played in all of the Netherlands' matches as they eventually finished in third place. He and [[Arjen Robben]] were the two Dutch players selected to the Team of the Tournament.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/statistics/castrol-index/top11.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140621231333/http://www.fifa.com/worldcup/statistics/castrol-index/top11.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=21 June 2014 |title=World Cup 2014: Castrol Index Top 11 |publisher=FIFA.com |access-date=14 July 2014}}</ref>

In the Netherlands' first game of [[UEFA Euro 2016 qualification]] away to the [[Czech Republic national football team|Czech Republic]] on 9 September 2014, De Vrij equalised with a header from [[Daley Blind]]'s cross but the Dutch lost 2–1.<ref>{{cite news |title=Czech Rep. 2–1 Netherlands |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/29131242|access-date=9 September 2014 |work=[[BBC Sport]] |date=9 September 2014}}</ref> He headed the team into the lead after 13 minutes in an eventual 2–0 win over Spain in a friendly at the [[Johan Cruyff Arena|Amsterdam ArenA]] on 31 March 2015, also wearing the captain's armband after [[Wesley Sneijder]]'s substitution.<ref>{{cite news |title=Netherlands 2–0 Spain: Rejuvenated Dutch ease pressure on Hiddink |url=http://www.goal.com/en/match/netherlands-vs-spain/1973973/report?ICID=MP_MS_5|access-date=31 March 2015 |work=Goal.com |date=31 March 2015}}</ref>
