'''Abdul Rahman Baba''' (born 2 July 1994), also known as '''Baba Rahman''', is a Ghanaian professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[left-back]] for [[PAOK FC|PAOK]], and the [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national team]].

Beginning his career at [[Dreams F.C. (Ghana)|Dreams FC]], he played in the [[Ghanaian Premier League]] with [[Asante Kotoko]]. In 2012, he signed for [[Bundesliga]] club [[Greuther Fürth]], where he spent two seasons. He then played for [[FC Augsburg]] before joining Chelsea in 2015 for a fee of £14 million, potentially rising to £22 million.

Rahman made his international debut in 2014 and was part of their squad which were runners-up at the [[2015 Africa Cup of Nations]].

==Club career==
===Ghana===
Rahman began his career at [[Dreams F.C. (Ghana)|Dreams FC]] of the [[Ghana Football Leagues|Ghana Division Two]]. After impressive performances, he was then transferred on loan to [[Asante Kotoko SC|Asante Kotoko]] of the [[Ghanaian Premier League]] for one season.<ref name="German club Greuther Furth beats Arsenal to sign Rahman" />

For the 2012 season, Rahman was a finalist for the league's Discovery of the Year Award, eventually losing to Joshua Oninku.<ref>{{cite web|title=Daniel Nii Adjei wins Player of the Season Award|date = 30 November 2001|url=http://www.ghanaweb.com/GhanaHomePage/NewsArchive/artikel.php?ID=242181|publisher=GhanaWeb|access-date=20 June 2012}}</ref> After becoming a sought after player during his time with the [[Kumasi]]-based club, serious enquiries were made about the player by [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] and [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] of the [[Premier League]] and [[Parma F.C.|Parma]] of [[Serie A]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Arsenal, Man City deals loom for Kotoko left-back Abdul-Rahman|date=4 March 2012|url=http://www.ghanasoccernet.com/arsenal-man-city-deals-loom-for-kotoko-left-back-abdul-rahman/|publisher=GhanaSoccerNet|access-date=20 June 2012}}</ref>

===Germany===
[[file:12AbdulRahmanBaba.jpg|thumb|upright|left|Rahman with Greuther Fürth in 2012]]
However, Rahman eventually signed with newly promoted [[SpVgg Greuther Fürth|Greuther Fürth]] in the [[Bundesliga]] on 12 June 2012. Rahman stated that his decision to join Fürth was because the club offered, "the best conditions in which to develop my career."<ref name="German club Greuther Furth beats Arsenal to sign Rahman">{{cite web|title=German club Greuther Furth beats Arsenal to sign Rahman|url=http://www.mtnfootball.com/africa/ghana/news/2012/june/13-german-club-greuther-furth-beats-arsenal-to-sign-rahman.html|publisher=MTN Football|access-date=20 June 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120707170745/http://www.mtnfootball.com/africa/ghana/news/2012/june/13-german-club-greuther-furth-beats-arsenal-to-sign-rahman.html|archive-date=7 July 2012}}</ref> In the derby against [[1. FC Nürnberg]] on 11 August 2014 he scored his first two goals for Fürth in a 5–1 home victory.<ref>{{cite web|title=Doppelpacker Baba lässt das Kleeblatt tanzen|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/2bundesliga/spieltag/2-bundesliga/2014-15/2/2407543/spielanalyse_spvgg-greuther-fuerth-82_1-fc-nuernberg-81.html|publisher=kicker.de|access-date=15 August 2014}}</ref> The next day he signed with [[FC Augsburg]] of the same division.<ref>{{cite web|title=Baba-Wechsel zum FCA perfekt|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/2bundesliga/vereine/609563/artikel_baba-wechsel-zum-fca-perfekt.html|publisher=kicker.de|access-date=15 August 2014}}</ref>

In Rahman's 2014–15 season with Augsburg, he made 108 tackles, more than any other player in the Bundesliga that season. Using aspects of his strength and pace, he was able to win 90 of those tackles, coming out to an 83% successful tackle rate. That season, Rahman also had 83 interceptions and won 80 contested aerial balls.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Chelsea transfer news: Who is Baba Rahman?|url = https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/football/teams/chelsea/11795039/Chelsea-transfer-news-Who-is-Baba-Rahman.html|website = Telegraph.co.uk|access-date = 20 February 2016}}</ref>

===Chelsea===

====Schalke 04 (loan)====
[[File:2018-08-17 1. FC Schweinfurt 05 vs. FC Schalke 04 (DFB-Pokal) by Sandro Halank–128.jpg|thumb|Baba playing for Schalke 04 in 2018]]
On 2 August 2016, [[FC Schalke 04]] confirmed that Rahman had moved on a season-long loan to the club after failing to impress the new Chelsea manager [[Antonio Conte]] during pre-season. Rahman was given the number 14 jersey for the upcoming season. He was signed by his former Augsburg manager, [[Markus Weinzierl]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.chelseafc.com/news/latest-news/2016/08/baba-makes-schalke-switch.html|title=Baba makes Schalke switch|date=2 August 2016|website=www.chelseafc.com|publisher=Chelsea F.C.|access-date=2 August 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.schalke04.de/en/news/160802_baba/page/2362--86-86-.html|title=Abdul Rahman Baba joins S04 on loan|date=2 August 2016|website=www.schalke04.de|publisher=FC Schalke 04|access-date=2 August 2016}}</ref> Rahman told the local newspaper ''[[Ruhr Nachrichten]]'' that Conte advised him to leave on loan because he preferred more defensive players than him.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.us/english-premier-league/story/2922205/baba-rahman-too-attacking-for-chelsea-manager-antonio-conte|title=Baba Rahman 'too attacking' for Chelsea manager Antonio Conte|last=Buczko|first=Stefan|publisher=ESPN|date=3 August 2016}}</ref>

Rahman made his competitive debut for the [[Gelsenkirchen]] team on 20 August, as a winger instead of a defender in a 4–1 victory against [[FC 08 Villingen]] in the first round of the [[2016–17 DFB-Pokal|DFB-Pokal]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/08/20/germany/dfb-pokal/fc-08-villingen/fc-schalke-04/2244383/|title=Villingen 1-4 Schalke 04|date=20 August 2016|publisher=Soccerway}}</ref> He made his league debut on 27 August, coming on for [[Sead Kolašinac]] in the 62nd minute of an eventual 1–0 loss at [[Eintracht Frankfurt]] on the first day of the season.<ref>{{cite news|title=Frankfurt hold on to beat Schalke|url=http://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/blmd1n-eintracht-frankfurt-1-0-fc-schalke-04-report.jsp|access-date=15 September 2016|publisher=Bundesliga|date=27 August 2016}}</ref> Rahman scored his first goal for Schalke on 15 September, the game's only in a Europa League victory at [[OGC Nice]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/noblmd3-0sg-nice-0-1-fc-schalke-04-report.jsp|title=OGC Nice 0-1 FC Schalke 04 &#124; UEFA Europa League &#124; Report|website=bundesliga.com - the official Bundesliga website}}</ref>

In January 2018, Rahman joined Schalke 04 on loan for a second time agreeing an 18-month stay until summer 2019.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Cann|first1=James|title=Chelsea left-back Baba Rahman joins Schalke on 18-month loan|url=https://www.independent.ie/sport/soccer/chelsea-leftback-baba-rahman-joins-schalke-on-18month-loan-36543246.html|access-date=29 January 2018|work=Irish Independent|date=29 January 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Bis 2019: Schalke leiht Baba erneut aus|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/startseite/715626/artikel_bis-2019_schalke-leiht-baba-erneut-aus.html|access-date=29 January 2018|work=kicker Online|date=29 January 2018|language=de}}</ref>

====Reims (loan)====
In January 2019, he returned early to Chelsea and was immediately loaned to [[Stade de Reims]] until the end of the season.<ref>{{cite news|title=Baba Rahman loaned to Reims|url=https://www.chelseafc.com/en/news/2019/1/28/baba-rahman-loaned-to-reims/|work=Chelsea FC|date=29 January 2019}}</ref>

==== Mallorca (loan) ====
On 2 September 2019, the last day of transfer window, Rahman was again loaned out in a season-long deal, this time to newly promoted [[La Liga]] side [[RCD Mallorca|Mallorca]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.rcdmallorca.es/en/en/content/news/news/abdul-rahman-baba-joins-rcd-mallorca-on-loan-from-chelsea|title=Abdul Rahman Baba Joins RCD Mallorca On Loan From Chelsea|publisher=RCD Mallorca|language=en|access-date=2 September 2019}}{{Dead link|date=June 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

==== PAOK (loan) ====
On 30 January 2021, Rahman was loaned out to [[PAOK FC|PAOK]] for the remainder of the [[2020–21 PAOK FC season|2020–21 season]].<ref name=":1" /> He made his debut for the Greek side against [[PAS Lamia 1964|Lamia]] on 20 February scoring the first goal in a 4–0 victory to send his side second in the [[2020–21 Super League Greece|league]] table.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ghanafootballnews.com/baba-rahman-scores-on-debut-for-paok-heavy-victory-against-lamia-in-greece/|title=Baba Rahman Scores On Debut For PAOK Heavy Victory Against Lamia In Greece|date=21 February 2021|website=Ghana Football News}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=21 February 2021|title=Baba Rahman Scores On Debut For PAOK Heavy Victory Against Lamia In Greece|url=https://ghanafootballnews.com/baba-rahman-scores-on-debut-for-paok-heavy-victory-against-lamia-in-greece/|access-date=21 February 2021|website=Ghana Football News}}</ref>

==== Reading (loan) ====
On 27 August 2021, Rahman joined [[Reading F.C.|Reading]] on loan.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.readingfc.co.uk/news/2021/august/Rahman-joins-the-Royals/|title=Rahman joins the Royals|date=27 August 2021|website=readingfc.co.uk}}</ref> He made his debut for the club against [[Queens Park Rangers F.C.|Queens Park Rangers]] on 10 September. On 31 August 2022, Rahman rejoined Reading on another season-long-loan.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.readingfc.co.uk/news/2022/august/31/confirmed---baba-is-back-/ | title=Confirmed &#124; Baba is back! }}</ref>

===PAOK===
On 10 July 2023, Rahman signed a two-year contract with a one-year extension option to [[Super League Greece]] club [[PAOK FC|PAOK]].

==International career==
Rahman played every minute of [[Ghana national football team|Ghana]]'s campaign at the [[2015 Africa Cup of Nations]] in [[Equatorial Guinea]], providing the cross from which [[André Ayew]] headed in the winner against [[South Africa national football team|South Africa]] to win [[2015 Africa Cup of Nations Group C|Group C]].<ref>{{cite news|title=South Africa 1-2 Ghana|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/30891882|access-date=17 August 2015|publisher=BBC Sport|date=27 January 2015}}</ref> In the [[2015 Africa Cup of Nations Final|final]] against the [[Ivory Coast national football team|Ivory Coast]], Rahman scored in the [[penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shootout]] in which his team lost 9–8.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Smith|first1=Alan|title=Ivory Coast win the Africa Cup of Nations on penalties – as it happened|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2015/feb/08/africa-cup-of-nations-final-ivory-coast-v-ghana-live|access-date=17 August 2015|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=8 February 2015}}</ref>  

Baba Rahman was selected for Ghana's final squad at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], and provided an assist during his side's opening match against [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]]; he produced the cross which was headed into goal by [[Osman Bukari]].<ref>{{cite web |date=25 November 2022 |title=Otto Addo defends Baba Rahman over 'unlucky' role in Portugal's second goal |url=https://citisportsonline.com/2022/11/25/otto-addo-defends-baba-rahman-over-unlucky-role-in-portugals-second-goal/ |access-date=4 December 2022 |website=Citi Sports Online}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Rahman has "Baba" on the back of his jersey, the name of his Chelsea-supporting father.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Perrin|first1=Charles|title=Revealed: Chelsea new boy Baba Rahman explains decision behind choosing shirt name|url=http://www.express.co.uk/sport/football/599506/Baba-Rahman-Chelsea-shirt-name-revelation-transfer-window|access-date=23 September 2015|work=[[Daily Express]]|date=19 August 2015}}</ref> In May 2016, Rahman married his longtime childhood girlfriend, Selma, in their hometown of Tamale in northern Ghana.<ref>{{cite news|title=Baba Rahman Marries Childhood Girlfriend Salma|url=http://www.ghanastar.com/sports/baba-rahman-ties-knot-with-girlfriend-salma/|access-date=29 May 2016|work=[[GhanaStar]]|date=29 May 2016}}</ref> Rahman started doing philanthropic work in his hometown, Tamale whilst playing in Europe. In 2021, he donated football materials and equipment to his former school and to the Tamale Juvenile Unit to help upcoming sportsmen within the northern part of Ghana.<ref name=":3" /><ref name=":4" />
