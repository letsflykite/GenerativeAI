'''Diego Nicolás de la Cruz Arcosa''' (born 1 June 1997) is a Uruguayan professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as an [[midfielder|attacking midfielder]] for [[Argentine Primera División]] club [[Club Atlético River Plate|River Plate]] and the [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay national team]].<ref name="soccerway">{{Soccerway|418994}}</ref>

==International career==
De la Cruz began his international career for Uruguay with [[Uruguay national under-17 football team|under-17 team]].<ref name="auf1">{{cite web|title=11 - Nicolás De La Cruz - AUF|url=https://www.auf.org.uy/11-nicol-aacute-s-de-la-cruz-2017/|accessdate=10 October 2020}}</ref> He was captain of [[Uruguay national under-20 football team|Uruguay under-20 team]] which won [[2017 South American U-20 Championship]] and reached semi-finals of [[2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup]].<ref name="auf2">{{cite web|title=Sudamericano Sub-20 2017|url=https://auf.org.uy/sudamericano-sub-20-2017/|accessdate=10 October 2020}}</ref>

On 15 August 2017, de la Cruz joined Argentinian club [[Club Atlético River Plate|River Plate]], signing a 4-year contract.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Plate |first=Club Atletico River |date=15 August 2017 |title=Nicolás de la Cruz se sumó a River Plate |url=http://www.cariverplate.com.ar/nicolas-de-la-cruz-se-sumo-a-river-plate |access-date=2023-04-20 |website=caRiverPlate.com.ar |language=es}}</ref>

On 18 September 2020, de la Cruz was included in Uruguay's 26-men preliminary squad for [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONMEBOL)|World Cup qualifying]] matches against [[Chile national football team|Chile]] and [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador]].<ref name="auf3">{{cite web|title=26 futbolistas reservados del exterior para el debut en las Eliminatorias|url=https://www.auf.org.uy/26-futbolistas-reservados-del-exterior-para-el-debut-en-las-eliminatorias/|accessdate=10 October 2020}}</ref> He was later included in the final squad and made his senior debut on 8 October 2020 in Uruguay's 2–1 win against Chile. He started the match and played 55 minutes before getting replaced by [[Nahitan Nández]].<ref name="auf4">{{cite web|title=Uruguay venció 2-1 a Chile en el estadio Centenario|url=https://www.auf.org.uy/detalles-uruguay-chile-fecha-1-eliminatorias-2022/|accessdate=10 October 2020}}</ref>

==Personal life==
De la Cruz is the half-brother of former Uruguayan international footballer [[Carlos Sánchez (Uruguayan footballer)|Carlos Sánchez]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.efe.com/efe/america/deportes/el-uruguayo-nicolas-de-la-cruz-hermano-carlos-sanchez-ficha-por-river/20000010-3352831|title=El uruguayo Nicolás de la Cruz, hermano de Carlos Sánchez, ficha por River|trans-title=Uruguayan Nicolás de la Cruz, brother of Carlos Sánchez, signs for River|publisher=EFE|language=es|date=16 August 2017|access-date=10 October 2020}}</ref> Their nephew [[Facundo Trinidad]] is also a professional footballer. All three of them are youth academy graduates of [[Liverpool F.C. (Montevideo)|Liverpool Montevideo]].<ref name="sobrino">{{cite web|title=Facundo Trinidad firmó contrato|url=http://www.liverpoolfc.com.uy/noticias/facundo-trinidad-firmo-contrato|accessdate=10 October 2020}}</ref>

In 2022 he obtained Argentine citizenship.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.tycsports.com/river-plate/river-nicolas-de-la-cruz-nacionalidad-cupo-extranjero-id456090.html|work=[[TyC Sports]]|title=Festeja River: Nicolás de la Cruz se nacionalizó argentino y libera un cupo de extranjero|date=19 August 2022|access-date=30 December 2022|language=es}}</ref>
