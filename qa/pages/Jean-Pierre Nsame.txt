'''Jean-Pierre Junior Nsame''' (born 1 May 1993) is a Cameroonian professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]] for [[Swiss Super League]] club [[BSC Young Boys|Young Boys]] and the [[Cameroon national football team|Cameroon national team]].

==Club career==
===France===
Nsame made his professional debut in April 2012 for [[Angers SCO|Angers]] in a 1–1 [[Ligue 2]] draw against [[FC Metz|Metz]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.lfp.fr/ligue2/feuille_match/75084|title=FC Metz vs. Angers SCO|publisher=[[Ligue de Football Professionnel|lfp.fr]]|language=fr|date=27 April 2012|access-date=20 June 2015}}</ref> He made 23 appearances for Angers over four seasons with the club and also enjoyed loan spells with [[USJA Carquefou|Carquefou]] and [[Amiens SC|Amiens]] in the [[Championnat National]].

At the end of the 2015–16 season, having failed to break into the first team on a regular basis, Nsame was released by the club. In July 2016, Nsame went on trial at English [[EFL League One|League One]] side [[Walsall F.C.|Walsall]] and played in a 2–1 friendly defeat to [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]]. However, after negative details emerged about his past, he was released by the club.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bescotbanter.net/2016/07/trialist-jean-pierre-nsame-in-walsall.html|title=Trialist Jean-Pierre Nsame in Walsall squad to take on Norwich City|publisher=Bescot Banter|date=9 July 2016|access-date=18 July 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.expressandstar.com/sport/football/walsall-fc/2016/07/12/walsall-end-interest-in-french-striker-jean-pierre-nsame/|title=Walsall end interest in Cameroon striker Jean-Pierre Nsame|publisher=Express & Star|date=12 July 2016|access-date=18 July 2020}}</ref>

===Switzerland===

==== Servette ====
On 23 August 2016, Nsame signed for [[Swiss Challenge League]] side [[Servette FC|Servette]]. He finished as the league's top goalscorer with 23 goals, helping the club to an impressive [[2016–17 Swiss Challenge League|third place finish]] after their promotion the previous season.

==== Young Boys ====
His goals attracted interest from other clubs and in July 2017, after only one year in [[Geneva]], he joined [[BSC Young Boys|Young Boys]] of the [[Swiss Super League]]. In his first season, he was part of the Young Boys squad that won the Super League title for the first time in 32 years.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://thesefootballtimes.co/2018/09/17/how-young-boys-shook-their-nearly-men-tag-to-lift-the-title-and-make-the-champions-league/|title=How Young Boys shook their nearly-men tag to lift the title and make the Champions League|publisher=These Football Times|date=17 September 2018|access-date=18 July 2020}}</ref> They clinched the title with a 2–1 win over [[FC Luzern|Luzern]] on 28 April 2018, with Nsame scoring an 89th minute winner.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/uk-soccer-swiss/soccer-young-boys-end-32-year-wait-for-swiss-title-and-end-basel-dominance-idUKKBN1HZ0YS|title=Young Boys end 32-year wait for Swiss title and end Basel dominance|publisher=Reuters|date=28 April 2018|access-date=23 April 2019}}</ref> He helped the club retain their title the following [[2018–19 Swiss Super League|campaign]], scoring 15 goals in 31 league appearances.

On 14 September 2019, Nsame netted his first hat-trick for the club in an 11–2 victory over fifth division side [[FC Freienbach]] in the second round of the [[2019–20 Swiss Cup|Swiss Cup]]. Five days later, he scored his first goal in [[UEFA|European competition]] in a 2–1 defeat to Portuguese side [[FC Porto]] in the group stage of the [[2019–20 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/fc-porto-vs-young-boys/420447|title=FC Porto 2-1 BSC Young Boys Bern|website=Sky Sports|date=19 September 2019|access-date=14 January 2020}}</ref> On 24 November, he scored a hat-trick in a 4–3 win over [[FC Sion]] in the [[2019–20 Swiss Super League|Swiss Super League]]; the match also saw [[Pajtim Kasami]] score a hat-trick for Sion.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/sion-vs-young-boys/411111|title=FC Sion 3-4 BSC Young Boys Bern|website=Sky Sports|date=24 November 2019|access-date=14 January 2020}}</ref>

In the 2019–20 Swiss Super league season Nsame finished the season as the league's top goalscorer with 32 goals in 32 matches<ref>{{Cite web|title=Top Scorers Swiss Super League 2019-2020 - Football|url=https://www.eurosport.com/football/swiss-super-league/2019-2020/standingperson.shtml|access-date=19 October 2020|website=Eurosport|language=en}}</ref> and powered the Young Boys to winning the Swiss double, by winning the Swiss League for the 3rd straight time<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=Young Boys seal third straight Swiss title|url=https://www.beinsports.com/en/football/news/young-boys-seal-third-straight-swiss-title/1515371|access-date=19 October 2020|website=beIN SPORTS|language=en}}</ref> and the Swiss Cup.<ref name=":0" />

====Loan to Venezia====
On 31 January 2022, Nsame joined Italian club [[Venezia F.C.|Venezia]] on loan until the end of the 2021–22 season, with an option to buy.<ref>{{cite press release|publisher=[[Venezia F.C.|Venezia]]|url=https://www.veneziafc.it/en/news/jean-pierre-nsame-joins-venezia-fc|title=JEAN-PIERRE NSAME JOINS VENEZIA FC|date=31 January 2022|access-date=31 January 2022|language=it}}</ref>

==International career==
Nsame was called up to the [[Cameroon national football team|Cameroon]] squad for the [[2017 Confederations Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Mazibuko |first1=Sandile |title=Christian Bassogog tops Cameroon Confederation Cup squad |url=https://www.goal.com/en-za/news/broos-includes-seven-new-faces-in-cameroons-confed-cup-squad/1dr2n0l819d3r1a74bmep6y3cj |website=Goal |access-date=23 April 2019}}</ref>

He earned his first cap for Cameroon when he started their match against [[Nigeria national football team|Nigeria]] on 4 September 2017 in a qualifying match for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018 World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |title=2018 World Cup: Cameroon fail to qualify after Nigeria draw |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/41154779 |website=BBC Sport |access-date=23 April 2019 |date=4 September 2014}}</ref>

On 9 November 2022, he was named in the final squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].<ref>{{cite tweet |author=Cameroon national football team |author-link=Cameroon national football team |user=LIndomptables |number=1590446431575638016 |date=9 November 2022 |title=Liste des 26 Lions Indomptables qui iront défendre les couleurs du Cameroun à la Coupe du Monde FIFA Qatar 2022. |trans-title=List of the 26 Indomitable Lions who will defend the colours of Cameroon at the FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022. |language=fr |access-date=12 November 2022}}</ref>
