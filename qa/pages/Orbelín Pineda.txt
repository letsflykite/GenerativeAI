'''Orbelín Pineda Alvarado''' (born 24 March 1996) is a Mexican professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[Super League Greece|Greek Super League]] club [[AEK Athens F.C.|AEK Athens]] and the [[Mexico national football team|Mexico national team]].

==Club career==
===Querétaro===
Pineda was first called up to the [[Querétaro F.C.|Querétaro]] senior team on 21 January 2014, for a [[Copa MX]] match against [[Ascenso MX]] side [[Club Celaya|Celaya]] and made his debut as a second-half substitute in a 1–0 loss.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2014/01/22/mexico/copa-mexico/queretaro-fc/celaya/1618012/ |title=Querétaro vs. Celaya - 22 January 2014 - Soccerway |website=Int.soccerway.com |date=22 January 2014 |access-date=28 May 2017}}</ref> Pineda made his [[Liga MX]] debut on 1 August 2014, in a 2–0 victory against [[C.F. Pachuca|Pachuca]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/jugador/orbelin-pineda&id_liga=1&id_torneo=544|title=Orbelín Pineda|publisher=mediotiempo|access-date=22 April 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150530002725/http://www.mediotiempo.com/jugador/orbelin-pineda%26id_liga%3D1%26id_torneo%3D544|archive-date=30 May 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On 24 January 2015, he scored his first goal in a league match against Pachuca in a 1–2 loss.<ref>{{cite web|title=Los 5 goles de Orbelín Pineda con Querétaro en la Liga MX|url=https://somosinvictos.com/2015/04/18/los-5-goles-de-orbelin-pineda-con-queretaro-en-la-liga-mx/|publisher=Somos Invictos|date=18 April 2015}}</ref> He was considered a pivotal part of the team as Querétaro reached the Clausura championship finals, only to succumb to [[Santos Laguna]] following an aggregate score of 5–3.<ref name="OotB"/>

===Guadalajara===
On 2 December 2015, [[C.D. Guadalajara|Guadalajara]] announced they had signed Pineda for an undisclosed price on a five-year contract.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chivasdecorazon.com.mx/noticias/26291/chivas-y-gallos-llegan-a-un-acuerdo-por-orbeln-pineda |title=Chivas y Gallos llegan a un acuerdo por Orbelín Pineda |website=Chivasdecorazon.com.mx |access-date=28 May 2017}}</ref> Pineda made his official debut as a starter on 10 January 2016, against [[C.D. Veracruz|Veracruz]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Matias Almeyda's new-look Chivas slip up in Liga MX Clausura opener|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/mexican-liga-mx/22/blog/post/2784384/marshall-chivas-slip-up-against-veracruz|work=ESPN|first=Tom|last=Marshall|date=10 January 2016}}</ref> He scored his first goal for the club on 16 January 2016, against [[Cruz Azul]] in a 1–1 tie.<ref>{{cite web|title=Cruz Azul y Chivas ligaron empates en el Clausura 2016|url=https://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/cronica?juegoId=439411|work=ESPN Deportes|date=16 January 2016|language=es}}</ref>

On 10 July, he would score the first goal in the [[2016 Supercopa MX]] against [[C.D. Veracruz|Veracruz]] which finished 2–0.<ref>{{cite web|title=Chivas secure statement win over Veracruz in the Supercopa MX|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/mexican-liga-mx/22/blog/post/2911232/chivas-statement-win-over-veracruz-supercopa-mx|publisher=ESPN|first=Tom|last=Marshall|date=11 July 2016}}</ref>

In April 2017, Guadalajara won the [[Clausura 2017 Copa MX|Clausura Copa MX]] [[Clausura 2017 Copa MX Final|final]] against Morelia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chivasdecorazon.com.mx/noticias/28506/Hace-historia-en-la-Copa-|title=Hace historia en la Copa|publisher=chivasdecorazon.com.mx|date=19 April 2017|access-date=19 April 2017|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Chivas beat Monarcas Morelia on penalties to win Copa MX|url=https://www.fmfstateofmind.com/2017/4/19/15367364/chivas-beat-monarcas-morelia-on-penalties-to-win-copa-mx|work=FMFStateOfMind.com|first=Rafael|last=Diaz|date=19 April 2017}}</ref> The following month, they disputed [[Clausura 2017 Liga MX championship stage|Clausura championship]] against [[Tigres UANL]], as Guadalajara would go on to win the title, finishing an eleven-year-long drought of league titles.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.chivasdecorazon.com.mx/es/noticias/28711/Regres-el-Gigante|title=Regresó el Gigante|publisher=chivasdecorazon.com.mx|date=28 May 2017|access-date=28 May 2017|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Chivas Win 12th Liga MX Championship After Beating Tigres 4-3 on Aggregate|url=https://bleacherreport.com/articles/2712373-chivas-win-12th-liga-mx-championship-after-beating-tigres-4-3-on-aggregate|work=Bleacher Report|date=29 May 2017|first=Tom|last=Sunderland}}</ref> As a result, Guadalajara won their first [[Double (association football)|Double]] since the [[1969–70 Mexican Primera División season|1969–70 season]].<ref>{{cite web|title=¡Doblete Sagrado! Chivas conquista Copa y Liga después de 37 años|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/liga-mx/doblete-sagrado-chivas-conquista-copa-liga-37-anos|work=MedioTiempo.com|date=29 May 2019|language=es}}</ref>

In April 2018, Guadalajara went on to win the [[2018 CONCACAF Champions League Finals|CONCACAF Champions League Finals]] against [[Major League Soccer]] team [[Toronto FC]] following a [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shoot-out]] victory of 4–2.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chivasdecorazon.com.mx/noticias/30259/CAMPEONES-DE-CONCACAF-|title=¡CAMPEONES DE CONCACAF! |publisher=chivasdecorazon.com.mx|date=25 April 2018|access-date=25 April 2018|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Chivas Win CONCACAF Champions League|url=https://www.beinsports.com/us/liga-mx/news/chivas-win-concacaf-champions-league/855746|work=BeinSport.com|date=25 April 2018}}</ref>

===Cruz Azul===
In December 2018, it was announced [[Cruz Azul]] signed Pineda for $12 million.<ref>{{cite web|title=¡Oficial! Cruz Azul contrata a Orbelín Pineda|url=https://www.elsoldemexico.com.mx/deportes/futbol/oficial-cruz-azul-contrata-a-orbelin-pineda-2818306.html|work=El Sol de México|date=18 December 2018|language=es}}</ref> He made his debut with the team on 4 January 2019, in 1–1 draw against [[Club Puebla|Puebla]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Orbelín, titular en debut con Cruz Azul ante Puebla; Cabecita y Yotún a la banca|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/orbelin-titular-debut-cruz-azul-puebla-cabecita-yotun-banca|work=MedioTiempo.com|first=José Andrés|last=Díaz López|date=4 January 2019|language=es}}</ref>

In July 2019, he would win the [[2019 Supercopa MX|Supercopa MX]] after defeating [[Club Necaxa|Necaxa]] 4–0.<ref>{{cite web|title=Cruz Azul es campeón de la Supercopa MX|url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/supercopa-mx/2019/07/15/5d2bbe2346163fc8808b4666.html|publisher=Marca|first=Alan|last=Osornio|date=14 July 2019|language=es}}</ref> In September, he would also go on to win the inaugural [[2019 Leagues Cup|Leagues Cup]] after defeating [[Tigres UANL]] 2–1.<ref>{{cite web|title=Leagues Cup triumph huge for Cruz Azul, but tournament still a work in progress|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/concacaf-leagues-cup/story/3946604/leagues-cup-triumph-huge-for-cruz-azulbut-tournament-still-a-work-in-progress|publisher=ESPN|first=Jeff|last=Carlisle|date=19 September 2019}}</ref>

Pineda played an integral part in Cruz Azul's championship-winning [[2020–21 Liga MX season#Torneo Guardianes 2021|Guardianes 2021]] campaign.

===Celta de Vigo===
On 7 January 2022, Pineda signed a five-year contract with Spanish club [[RC Celta de Vigo|Celta de Vigo]].<ref>{{cite news|title=La magia de Orbelín Pineda se suma al ataque del RC Celta|url=https://rccelta.es/equipo/actualidad/la-magia-de-orbelin-pineda-se-suma-al-ataque-del-rc-celta/|language=es|publisher=RC Celta|date=7 January 2022}}</ref>

==== Loan to AEK Athens ====
On 15 July, Pineda joined Greek club [[AEK Athens F.C.|AEK Athens]] on a season-long loan, rejoining former Guadalajara manager [[Matías Almeyda]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=15 July 2022 |title=ORBELÍN PINEDA JUGARÁ ESTA TEMPORADA CEDIDO EN EL AEK DE ATENAS |url=https://rccelta.es/equipo/actualidad/orbelin-pineda-aek/ |access-date=15 July 2022 |website=RC Celta |language=es}}</ref> Pineda made his debut on 20 August in a [[Super League Greece|league]] match against [[PAS Lamia 1964|Lamia]], as a starter, he scored a goal and contributed an assist in a 3–0 victory.<ref>{{cite web|title=Video: ¡Debut soñado! Orbelín da asistencia y hace golazo en su debut de la Superliga|url=https://juanfutbol.com/articulo/hectorgomez-2/video-debut-sonado-orbelin-participa-en-el-primer-gol-del-aek-de-la-temporada}}</ref>

=== AEK Athens ===
On 1 July 2023, AEK Athens permanently signed Pineda from Celta de Vigo for a fee of €6.5 million, while the player penned a four-year deal until 2027. The club officially announced, on social media, the transfer as 'the biggest in the club's history'.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Η ΑΕΚ ανακοίνωσε την μεγαλύτερη μεταγραφή της ιστορίας της με την αγορά του Πινέδα, υπέγραψε έως το καλοκαίρι του 2027 |url=https://www.sport24.gr/football/aek-metagrafes-anakoinose-tin-agora-toy-pineda-ypegrapse-eos-to-kalokairi-toy-2027.10099992.html |access-date=2023-07-01 |website=www.sport24.gr |language=el}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=El atacante Orbelín Pineda inicia una nueva etapa en el AEK de Atenas|url=https://rccelta.es/equipo/actualidad/orbelinpineda-aek/|language=es|publisher=RC Celta|date=1 July 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
===Youth===
Pineda was called up to the [[Mexico national under-20 football team|under-20]] side by manager [[Sergio Almaguer]] to take part in the [[2015 FIFA U-20 World Cup]] in New Zealand. He made his debut in Mexico's opening match as a half-time substitution for [[José David Ramírez]]. Mexico went on to lose the game 2–0 to [[Mali national under-20 football team|Mali]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/matches/round=271602/match=300269547/index.html#statistics#nosticky |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150528073506/http://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/matches/round=271602/match=300269547/index.html#statistics#nosticky |url-status=dead |archive-date=28 May 2015 |title=FIFA U-20 World Cup New Zealand 2015 - Matches - Mexico-Mali |website=FIFA.com |date=15 May 2017 |access-date=28 May 2017}}</ref> Pineda started the next two matches for Mexico, but the team finished in 4th place in their group and failed to qualify to the next round.

===Senior===
Pineda was included in the provisional roster for the [[2015 CONCACAF Gold Cup]] by [[Miguel Herrera]] but was cut from the final list, remaining in the Provisional List if someone else got injured.<ref>{{cite web|title=CONCACAF Gold Cup 2015 Provisional 35-Player Rosters Announced|url=http://www.concacaf.com/article/concacaf-gold-cup-2015-provisional-35-player-rosters-announced|website=CONCACAF|accessdate=10 August 2016|date=10 June 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150611090755/http://www.concacaf.com/article/concacaf-gold-cup-2015-provisional-35-player-rosters-announced|archive-date=11 June 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> He was called up again, this time by [[Juan Carlos Osorio]], to be included in Mexico's provisional squad for the [[Copa América Centenario]] but was cut from the final squad.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.goal.com/en-us/news/114/mexico/2016/05/02/23053752/mexico-has-mix-of-experience-youth-on-provisional-copa?ICID=HP_BN_4|title=Mexico has mix of experience, youth on provisional Copa America Centenario roster|publisher=goal.com |date=2 May 2016 |access-date=3 July 2016}}</ref> Pineda would make his [[Mexico national football team|senior national team]] debut on 6 September 2016 against [[Honduras national football team|Honduras]] during a [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONCACAF)|2018 FIFA World Cup qualification]] match, coming in as a substitute for [[Jesús Dueñas]] at the 71st minute, finishing in a scoreless draw.<ref>{{cite web|title=Orbelín Pineda debutó con la Selección Nacional de México|url=https://miseleccion.mx/orbelin-pineda-debuto-la-seleccion-nacional-mexico/|website=Miseleccion.mx|access-date=5 October 2018}}</ref>

He was included in the finalized roster that would participate at the [[2017 CONCACAF Gold Cup]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Lista la convocatoria de México para la Copa Oro|url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/copa-oro/2017/06/29/59542cb0ca4741ab508b4609.html|work=Marca|date=28 June 2017|language=es}}</ref> where on 9 July he would score his first goal with the senior national team during a group stage match against [[El Salvador national football team|El Salvador]], scoring the final goal of a 3–1 win.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mexico 3 El Salvador 1: Defending Gold Cup champions win opener|url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/us/news/mexico-3-el-salvador-1-defending-gold-cup-champions-win-opener|work=Four Four Two|date=10 July 2017|access-date=5 October 2018}}</ref>

In June 2019, Pineda was included in [[Gerardo Martino]]'s [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup]] roster.<ref>{{cite web|title=Selección Mexicana anuncia lista definitiva para Copa Oro 2019|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/copa-oro/seleccion-mexicana-anuncia-lista-definitiva-copa-oro-2019|date=6 June 2019|work=MedioTiempo.com}}</ref> Mexico went on to win the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mexico Turns Tide, Wins Gold Cup Title Again vs. Wasteful USMNT|url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2019/07/07/mexico-usa-gold-cup-title-dos-santos-pizarro-pulisic-berhalter|publisher=[[Sports Illustrated]]|first=Brian|last=Straus|date=7 July 2019}}</ref>

Pineda participated at the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2021 Gold Cup]],<ref>{{cite web|title=México va a la Copa Oro sin Chicharito Hernández y con Funes Mori|date=July 2021 |url=https://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/selecciones/nota/_/id/8853729/mexico-convocatoria-copa-oro-funes-mori-incluido-chicharito-ausente}}</ref> scoring a total of 3 times and contributing one assist.<ref>{{cite web|title=CONCACAF Gold Cup Scoring Stats - 2021-22|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/stats/_/league/concacaf.gold}}</ref> Mexico finished runner-up after losing the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup Final|final]] to the United States 0–1.<ref>{{cite web|title=¡Dolor de cabeza del Tata! México pierde Final de Copa Oro ante equipo B de EE.UU.|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/copa-oro/unidos-vs-mexico-1-0-ee-uu-campeon-copa-oro}}</ref>

In October 2022, Pineda was named in Mexico's preliminary 31-man [[Mexico national football team|squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], and in November, he was ultimately included in the final 26-man roster.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lista de convocados de la Selección Mexicana al Mundial de Qatar 2022|url=https://www.tvazteca.com/aztecadeportes/lista-oficial-convocados-seleccion-mexicana-mundial-qatar-2022|work=Tvazteca.com|first=Omar|last=Moro|date=14 November 2022|language=Spanish}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Pineda is able to play as a [[Midfielder#Deep-lying playmaker (Strolling 10)|deep-lying]], roaming or more [[Midfielder#Advanced playmaker|advanced playmaker]], he has regularly played as the more advanced of the two in a 4-2-3-1 formation. A fantastic reader of the game, Pineda can spot incisive through balls and is able to find space on the pitch by moving into the channels between defenders.<ref name="OotB">{{cite web |title= Scout Report: Orbelín Pineda {{!}} Chivas' intelligent midfielder |url= http://outsideoftheboot.com/2016/03/23/scout-report-orbelin-pineda-chivas-intelligent-midfielder |work=Outside of the Boot |first= Thomas |last=Harrison |access-date=27 July 2018}}</ref> Former [[C.D. Guadalajara|Guadalajara]] manager [[Matías Almeyda]] described Pineda as "one of those players that has to end up in a great European club. He has the talent and the attributes." He is technically sharp, skillful, and with a turn of pace able to burst past opponents but at his short stature, he isn't the strongest player, although his development may have been held up by the fact he's been moved from an attacking midfielder to the holding role and even on the wing since he debuted in the first division in late 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.espnfc.us/mexican-liga-mx/22/blog/post/3028049/hirving-lozano-orbelin-pineda-cesar-montes-rodolfo-pizarro-and-erick-aguirre-are-el-tris-ones-to-watch |title= Hirving Lozano Orbelin Pineda Cesar Montes Rodolfo Pizarro and Erick Aguirre are El Tri's ones to watch |website= Espnfc.us |date=30 December 2016 |access-date= 28 May 2017}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Pineda's older brother, [[Onay Pineda|Onay]], was also a professional footballer who played as a [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|right-back]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://suracapulco.mx/11/orbelin-pineda-ayer-cuidaba-chivos-y-hoy-busca-un-lugar-en-el-tri-olimpico/|title=Orbelín Pineda ayer cuidaba chivos y hoy busca un lugar en el Tri Olímpico|author=Édgar Contreras|date=5 July 2016|access-date=2 February 2017|publisher=El Sur: Periodico de Guerreo, Deportes}}{{dead link|date=March 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>
