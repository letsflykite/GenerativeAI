'''Noussair Mazraoui''' ({{lang-ar|نُصَيْر مَزرَاوِيّ}}; born 14 November 1997) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|full-back, central-midfielder or winger]] for [[Bundesliga]] club [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]]. Born in the Netherlands, he represents [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]] at international level.

==Club career==
===Ajax===
Mazraoui grew up in [[Alphen aan de Rijn]] and started playing for local club AVV Alphen at age 4.<ref name="alphen">{{cite web |last1=van der Hansz |first1=Silvester |title=Alphenaar Mazraoui leeft zijn droom tussen de sterren bij Ajax |url=https://www.ad.nl/alphen/alphenaar-mazraoui-leeft-zijn-droom-tussen-de-sterren-bij-ajax~a370278c8/ |publisher=[[Algemeen Dagblad]] |access-date=5 October 2020 |language=nl |date=30 July 2017}}</ref> He moved to [[Alphense Boys]] three years later, where he only played for a year before being invited to play in the youth academy of [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]].<ref name="alphen" /> He made his professional debut with Jong Ajax on 12 August 2016 in an [[Eerste Divisie]] game against [[Almere City FC|Almere City]], replacing [[Richairo Živković]] after 86 minutes.<ref name="soccerway2">{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/08/12/netherlands/eerste-divisie/fc-omniworld/jong-ajax/2240581/|title=Almere City vs. Ajax II – 12 August 2016 – Soccerway|publisher=soccerway.com|access-date=24 August 2016}}</ref> On 25 October 2020, Mazraoui scored his first professional goal in a 3–1 victory against [[FC Midtjylland]] in the [[2020–21 UEFA Champions League]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=Ajax 3–1 FC Midtjylland {{!}} Football |language=en-GB |work=The Guardian |url=https://www.theguardian.com//football/match/2020/nov/25/ajax-v-midtjylland |access-date=7 March 2022 |issn=0261-3077}}</ref> He scored his first league goal in a 5–0 win against [[N.E.C. (football club)|N.E.C.]].

===Bayern Munich===
On 24 May 2022, Mazraoui signed a four-year deal with [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]], starting from 1 July.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2022/05/mazraoui-signing |title=FC Bayern sign Noussair Mazraoui |website=Bayern Munich |date=24 May 2022 }}</ref> In January 2023, he had an inflammation in his [[pericardium]] after suffering from [[Covid-19]], which would rule him out for six to eight weeks.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/africa/64187674 |title=Noussair Mazraoui: Bayern Munich defender out with heart inflammation |website=BBC Sport |date=6 January 2023 }}</ref> On 13 May 2023, Mazraoui scored his first goal for the club in a 6–0 win against [[FC Schalke 04]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=Bayern put six past Schalke to move closer to title |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/65584582 |access-date=2023-05-14}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Bayern Munich make title statement with dominant win over Schalke |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/bayern-munich-schalke-live-team-news-match-stats-mane-sane-tuchel-23457 |access-date=2023-05-14 |website=bundesliga.com - the official Bundesliga website |language=en}}</ref>

==International career==
Mazraoui was a youth international for [[Morocco]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.skysports.com/football/italy-u21-vs-morocco-u21/teams/385286|title=Football (Sky Sports)|website=SkySports}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fff.fr/equipes-de-france/4/u20/matchs/2563/2017-11-08/maroc-france|title=Match – Maroc – France – FFF}}</ref> He first played for the Moroccan national team in 2018.

On 10 November 2021, Mazraoui revealed that the relations between him and Moroccan national team coach [[Vahid Halilhodžić]] fell.<ref>{{Cite web |date=11 November 2021 |title=Vahid and Mazraoui quarreled because of… a bottle! |url=https://www.archysport.com/2021/11/vahid-and-mazraoui-quarreled-because-of-a-bottle/ |access-date=7 March 2022 |website=Archysport |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=10 November 2021 |title=Maroc : Noussair Mazraoui revient sur son clash avec Vahid Halilhodzic {{!}} Africa Foot United |url=https://africafootunited.com/maroc-noussair-mazraoui-revient-sur-son-clash-avec-vahid-halilhodzic/ |access-date=7 March 2022 |website=africafootunited.com |language=fr-FR |archive-date=5 June 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220605072443/https://africafootunited.com/maroc-noussair-mazraoui-revient-sur-son-clash-avec-vahid-halilhodzic/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> On 13 March 2022, Mazraoui and his Moroccan teammate, [[Hakim Ziyech]], both rejected Halilhodžić's invitation to represent Morocco in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – CAF third round]] against [[DR Congo national football team|DR Congo]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=13 March 2022 |title=زياش ومزراوي يغيبان عن المنتخب |url=https://www.hespress.com/مزراوي-وزياش-يغيبان-عن-المنتخب-957565.html |access-date=16 March 2022 |website=Hespress – هسبريس جريدة إلكترونية مغربية |language=ar}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=مزراوي يرفض دعوة الإنضمام لمنتخب المغرب |url=https://www.yalla-sport.com/news/348097 |access-date=16 March 2022 |website=www.yalla-sport.com}}</ref> He later accepted to join the national team and represented Morocco in the 2022 FIFA World Cup after Halilhodžić was replaced by [[Walid Regragui]].

On 10 November 2022, he was named in Morocco's 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Morocco World Cup 2022 squad: Who's in and who's out? {{!}} Goal.com |url=https://www.goal.com/en-bh/lists/morocco-2022-world-cup-squad-hakimi-ziyech-mazraoui-qatar/blta36257fc4feeb66a |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=www.goal.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=10 November 2022 |title=Moroccan coach unveils list of 26 Atlas Lions in 2022 World Cup |url=https://en.hespress.com/52697-moroccan-coach-unveils-list-of-26-atlas-lions-in-2022-world-cup.html |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=HESPRESS English - Morocco News |language=en-US}}</ref> He played a role in Morocco's charge to the semi-finals, helping them become the first African side to reach the final 4 stage at the World Cup.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/morrocans-2022-world-cup-achraf-hakimi-noussair-mazraoui-22055 |title=Moroccans in the Bundesliga, including 2022 World Cup bronze medal challengers Achraf Hakimi and Noussair Mazraoui |website=Bundesliga |date=16 December 2022 }}</ref>
