'''Romain Ghanem Paul Saïss''' ({{lang-ar|رومان غانم بول سايس}}; born 26 March 1990) is a <!-- Note that the nationality is omitted on purpose; this is per consensus at WikiProject Football for dual-nationals born in one country and representing the other at international level. Nationality is instead explained further down in the lede, as done in this article. --> professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] for [[Süper Lig]] club [[Beşiktaş J.K.|Beşiktaş]] and [[Captain (association football)|captains]] the [[Morocco national football team|Morocco national team]].

Saïss began his career with [[Olympique de Valence|Valence]], then played for [[Le Havre AC|Le Havre]] and [[Clermont Foot|Clermont]] of [[Ligue 2]], and [[Angers SCO|Angers]] of [[Ligue 1]] before joining [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]] in 2016. He made 206 appearances and scored 15 goals, before moving to Beşiktaş in 2021.

Born in France to a Moroccan father and French mother, Saïss represents Morocco at international level. A full international since 2012, he has earned over 70 caps for Morocco, becoming the team's captain in 2019. He has represented the nation at three [[Africa Cup of Nations]] tournaments and two [[FIFA World Cup]]s, helping the team come fourth at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 edition]] of the latter.

==Club career==
===Early career===
Saïss began his senior career with [[Olympique de Valence|Valence]] in the [[Championnat de France Amateur 2]] (fifth-tier), and supplemented his €500 monthly salary with washing up at his parents' restaurant. At 21, he signed his first professional contract with [[Clermont Foot|Clermont]] in [[Ligue 2]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Masi |first1=Joseph |title=Big Interview: Wolves' Romain Saiss washed pots, now he is dreaming of lifting the FA Cup |url=https://www.expressandstar.com/sport/football/wolverhampton-wanderers-fc/2019/04/07/big-interview-wolves-romain-saiss-washed-pots-now-he-is-dreaming-of-lifting-the-fa-cup/ |access-date=12 May 2020 |work=Express & Star |date=7 April 2019}}</ref>

In June 2013, Saïss moved across Ligue 2 to [[Le Havre AC|Le Havre]] on a two year deal.<ref name=havre>{{cite news |title=Romain Saïss au HAC pour 2 ans|trans-title=Romain Saïss to HAC for 2 years|url=http://www.hac-foot.com/actualite/romain-saiss-au-hac-pour-2-ans.html |access-date=12 May 2020 |publisher=Le Havre AC |date=27 June 2013 |language=fr}}</ref> When it ended, he joined [[Ligue 1]] side [[Angers SCO|Angers]] on a two-year contract.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Romain-saiss-signe-deux-ans-a-angers/571491|title=Romain Saïss signe deux ans à Angers|trans-title=Romain Saïss signs for two years at Angers|date=3 July 2015|access-date=23 August 2016|work=L'Equipe|language=fr}}</ref>

===Wolverhampton Wanderers===
[[File:Romain Saïss playing for Wolverhampton Wanderers (24.09.2016).png|left|150px|Romain Saïss playing for Wolverhampton Wanderers in 2016.]]On 30 August 2016, Saïss moved to England, joining [[EFL Championship]] club [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]] for an undisclosed fee on a four-year deal.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/37221689|title=Romain Saiss: Wolves sign Angers midfielder for undisclosed fee|date=30 August 2016|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> He made his debut on 17 September in a 2–0 win at [[Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/37324949|title=Newcastle 0–2 Wolves|date=17 September 2016|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> In the game, he was subjected to racial abuse by opponent [[Jonjo Shelvey]], who was given a five-match ban and £100,000 fine by [[The FA]] in December.<ref>{{cite news |title=FA reveals reasons for racial abuse ban for Newcastle's Jonjo Shelvey |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/dec/23/fa-reveal-reasons-banning-newcastles-jonjo-shelvey-five-games |access-date=12 May 2020 |work=The Guardian |agency=Press Association |date=23 December 2016}}</ref>

He scored his first goal for the club on 30 September 2017 in a 4–0 win against [[Burton Albion F.C.|Burton Albion]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/41369653|title=Burton Albion 0–4 Wolverhampton Wanderers|publisher=BBC Sport|date=30 September 2017}}</ref>

Following Wolves' promotion to the [[Premier League]] in 2018, Saïss made his debut Premier League appearance as a substitute in a 1–1 draw with [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] at [[Old Trafford]] on 22 September 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45530717|title=Manchester United 1–1 Wolves: Joao Moutinho earns visitors draw at Old Trafford|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|first=Alex|last=Bysouth|date=22 September 2018|access-date=22 February 2019}}</ref> His first start in the Premier League came in a 2–1 defeat away to [[Cardiff City F.C.|Cardiff City]] at the [[Cardiff City Stadium]] on 30 November.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/38217655|title=Cardiff City 2–1 Wolverhampton Wanderers|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|first=Dafydd |last=Pritchard|date=30 November 2018|access-date=22 February 2019}}</ref> He scored his first Premier League goal in a 1–1 draw with [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]] at [[Craven Cottage]] on 26 December 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/46615162|title=Fulham 1–1 Wolves: Aleksandar Mitrovic 'wants to cry' after missing chances in draw|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|first=Jack|last=Skelton|date=26 December 2018}}</ref> On 21 February 2019 he agreed a new contract until the summer of 2021.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.expressandstar.com/sport/football/wolverhampton-wanderers-fc/2019/02/21/romain-saiss-signs-new-wolves-deal/|title=Romain Saiss signs new Wolves deal|work=[[Express & Star]]|first=Luke|last=Hatfield|date=21 February 2019|access-date=22 February 2019}}</ref>

On 25 July 2019 Saïss made his debut appearance in UEFA European club football competition as a late substitute in Wolves's 2–0 home win against [[Crusaders F.C.|Crusaders]] in the [[2019–20 UEFA Europa League]] Second Qualifying Round<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49098383|title=Europa League: Wolves beat gritty Crusaders 2–0 on European return|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|date=25 July 2019|access-date=25 October 2019}}</ref> and on 24 October he scored his first goal in such a competition when he scored the opening goal of a 2–1 away win at [[ŠK Slovan Bratislava|Slovan Bratislava]] in the [[2019–20 UEFA Europa League group stage]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/50119135|title=Slovan Bratislava 1–2 Wolves: Raul Jimenez hits winner in Europa League|publisher=BBC Sport|first=Neil|last=Johnston|date=24 October 2019|access-date=25 October 2019}}</ref>

Saïss triggered an automatic one-year extension to his contract on 18 March 2021, by making 20 starts in the Premier League during the [[2020–21 Premier League|2020–21]] season, tying him to the club until June 2022.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.expressandstar.com/sport/football/wolverhampton-wanderers-fc/2021/03/18/exclusive-wolves-romain-saiss-earns-one-year-contract-extension/|title=Exclusive: Wolves' Romain Saiss earns one-year contract extension|publisher=[[Express & Star]]|first=Joe|last=Edwards|date=18 March 2021|access-date=3 May 2021}}</ref> He was described as the Moroccan [[Paolo Maldini|Maldini]] by head coach [[Bruno Lage]] on 15 December that year, after his display in the away Premier League tie at [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]], where he scored the only goal in a much longed-for win (Wolves's first-ever win at Brighton in the top-flight in seven attempts dating back to 1979).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/59573772|title=Brighton 0–1 Wolves: Romain Saiss goal takes visitors up to eighth in Premier League|publisher=BBC Sport|first=Michael|last=Emons|date=15 December 2021|access-date=5 March 2022}}</ref> He made his 100th appearance in the Premier League in 2–0 win away to [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] on 13 February 2022.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/60276484|title=Tottenham Hotspur 0–2 Wolverhampton Wanderers:Conte's side beaten at home|publisher=BBC Sport|first=Alex|last=Bysouth|date=13 February 2022}}</ref> His 200th competitive appearance in all competitions for Wolves was on 5 March 2022 in a home fixture against [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/60531471|title=Wolves 0–2 Crystal Palace: Jean-Philippe Mateta and Wilfried Zaha seal win for Eagles|publisher=BBC Sport|first=Tom|last=Mallows|date=5 March 2022}}</ref>

On 31 May 2022, Saïss announced that he would leave Wolves at the end of his contract on 1 July, having played 206 games for the club over six seasons, and scoring 15 goals, nine of them in the Premier League.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wolves.co.uk/news/features/20220531-thank-you-romain/|title=Thank you, Romain|website=www.wolves.co.uk|date=31 May 2022|accessdate=17 June 2022}}</ref>

===Beşiktaş===
On 14 June 2022, Saïss moved to Turkey, joining [[Süper Lig]] club [[Beşiktaş J.K.|Beşiktaş]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.moroccoworldnews.com/2022/06/349722/moroccan-defender-romain-saiss-passes-besiktas-jk-medical-check|title=Moroccan Defender Romain Saiss Passes Besiktas JK Medical Check|work=Morocco World News|date=15 June 2022|accessdate=31 May 2022}}</ref> He made his debut on 6 August as the [[2022–23 Süper Lig|season]] began with a 1–0 home win over [[Kayserispor]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Beşiktaş - Kayserispor maçı sonrası Romain Saiss konuştu! "İlk maçı kazanmak çok önemliydi"|trans-title=After the Beşiktaş - Kayserispor match, Romain Saïss spoke! “Winning the first game was very important”|url=https://www.sabah.com.tr/spor/futbol/2022/08/06/besiktas-kayserispor-maci-sonrasi-romain-saiss-konustu-ilk-maci-kazanmak-cok-onemliydi |access-date=6 December 2022 |work=Sabah |date=7 August 2022 |language=Turkish}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:Spain vs Morocco (43).jpg|thumb|Saïss (in white) defending against [[Spain national football team|Spain]] at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]]]]
Saïss, who was born and raised in France to a Moroccan father and a French mother, chose to represent the [[Morocco national football team|Morocco national team]]. He made his debut in a 1–0 [[friendly (association football)|friendly]] loss to [[Togo national football team|Togo]] in November 2012.<ref name=havre/>

Saïss was in the Moroccan squad that reached the quarter-finals of the [[2017 Africa Cup of Nations]] in Gabon. He scored his first international goal in their 3–1 group win over Togo.<ref>{{cite news |title=Morocco fight back to beat Togo and keep Africa Cup of Nations hopes alive |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2017/jan/20/togo-morocco-africa-cup-of-nations-match-report |access-date=12 May 2020 |work=The Guardian |agency=Reuters |date=20 January 2017}}</ref> In June 2018, he was named in Morocco's 23-man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia,<ref>{{cite web |title=Morocco announce 23-man World Cup squad|url=https://africanfootball.com/news/750416/Morocco-announce-23-man-World-Cup-squad|publisher=African Football|date=4 June 2018|access-date=22 February 2019}}</ref> and featured in group games against [[Iran national football team|Iran]] and [[Spain national football team|Spain]].{{Citation needed|date=December 2022}} After the [[2019 Africa Cup of Nations]], in which Morocco made the last 16, Saïss became the captain of the ''Atlas Lions''.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Boyle |first1=Paul |title=Romain Saïss: 'Nuno does a fantastic job. It's normal other teams want him' |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2019/dec/27/romain-saiss-interview-wolves |access-date=13 May 2020 |work=The Guardian |date=27 December 2019}}</ref>

On 10 November 2022, Saïss was named in Morocco's 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Morocco World Cup 2022 squad: Who's in and who's out|url=https://www.goal.com/en-bh/lists/morocco-2022-world-cup-squad-hakimi-ziyech-mazraoui-qatar/blta36257fc4feeb66a |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=Goal}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=10 November 2022 |title=Moroccan coach unveils list of 26 Atlas Lions in 2022 World Cup |url=https://en.hespress.com/52697-moroccan-coach-unveils-list-of-26-atlas-lions-in-2022-world-cup.html |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=Hespress}}</ref> He scored the opening goal of a 2–0 group stage victory over [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]] on 27 November, Morocco's first goal of the tournament. The goal had previously been awarded to [[Abdelhamid Sabiri]], but Saïss was later credited for the goal by [[FIFA]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Aamari |first=Oussama |date=28 November 2022 |title=Morocco-Belgium: FIFA Credits Romain Saiss with First Goal |url=https://www.moroccoworldnews.com/2022/11/352671/morocco-belgium-fifa-credits-romain-saiss-with-first-goal |access-date=14 December 2022 |website=[[Morocco World News]]}}</ref> In the semi-final against France – the first ever played by an African nation – he was substituted after 21 minutes due to injury as Morocco lost 2–0.<ref>{{cite news |last1=McNulty |first1=Phil |title=France 2&ndash;0 Morocco |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63882918 |access-date=17 December 2022 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=14 December 2022}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Saïss's father is Moroccan and his mother is French, both restaurant owners.<ref>{{Cite web |date=14 December 2017 |title=Wolves' Romain Saïss: 'Busquets is an inspiration – his positioning, his simplicity'|first=Paul|last=Doyle |url=http://www.theguardian.com/football/2017/dec/14/wolves-romain-saiss-busquets-morocco-world-cup |access-date=6 December 2022 |website=The Guardian}}</ref> He is a [[Muslims|Muslim]] and observes fasting in the month of [[Ramadan]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Edwards |first1=Joe |title=Hard to be home alone admits Wolves' Romain Saiss |url=https://www.expressandstar.com/sport/football/wolverhampton-wanderers-fc/2020/04/24/hard-to-be-home-alone-admits-wolves-romain-saiss/ |access-date=13 May 2020 |work=Express & Star |date=24 April 2020}}</ref>
