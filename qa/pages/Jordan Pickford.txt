'''Jordan Lee Pickford''' (''{{ne}}'' '''Logan'''; born 7 March 1994) is an English professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for {{English football updater|Everton}} club [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] and the [[England national football team|England national team]].

Pickford has previously played for Sunderland's academy, reserve, and senior teams in addition to [[Loan (association football)|loan]] spells at [[Darlington F.C.|Darlington]], [[Alfreton Town F.C.|Alfreton Town]], [[Burton Albion F.C.|Burton Albion]], [[Carlisle United F.C.|Carlisle United]], [[Bradford City A.F.C.|Bradford City]] and [[Preston North End F.C.|Preston North End]]. He signed for Everton for a fee of £30 million in June 2017.

Pickford has represented England at [[England national under-16 football team|under-16]], [[England national under-17 football team|under-17]], [[England national under-18 football team|under-18]], [[England national under-19 football team|under-19]], [[England national under-20 football team|under-20]] and [[England national under-21 football team|under-21]] levels. He made his debut for the full [[England national football team|England]] national team in November 2017 in a friendly against [[Germany national football team|Germany]]. He was the starting goalkeeper in England's [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] fourth-place finish, in which the squad reached its first semi-final appearance since 1990. He also started in England's [[Euro 2020]] runners-up finish, in which England reached their first ever Euro final and first major tournament final since 1966. Starting every game in the group stage, Pickford conceded no goals.

====Loan to Bradford City====
On 21 July 2014, Pickford joined [[EFL League One|League One]] club Bradford City on a season-long loan for the 2014–15 season.<ref>{{cite web |title=Pickford on the move |url=http://www.safc.com/news/team-news/2014/july/pickford-on-the-move |publisher=Sunderland A.F.C. |access-date=21 July 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140721121410/http://www.safc.com/news/team-news/2014/july/pickford-on-the-move |archive-date=21 July 2014}}</ref> He made his debut on 9 August in a 3–2 win against [[Coventry City F.C.|Coventry City]] at [[Valley Parade]],<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/28646631 |title=Bradford City 3–2 Coventry City |website=BBC Sport |date=9 August 2014 |access-date=11 October 2016}}</ref> and kept his first clean sheet in the following match, a 0–0 draw away to [[Walsall F.C.|Walsall]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/28716713 |title=Walsall 0–0 Bradford City |website=BBC Sport |date=16 August 2014 |access-date=11 October 2016}}</ref> Pickford then received his first ever red card after he was sent off in the 11th minute of a 2–1 loss to [[Rochdale A.F.C.|Rochdale]] on 10 January 2015.<ref>{{cite news |title=Bradford City 1–2 Rochdale |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/30663535 |website=BBC Sport |date=10 January 2015 |access-date=17 October 2016}}</ref> He served a one-match suspension which his club did not appeal.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bradfordcityfc.co.uk/news/article/city-decide-against-pickford-appeal-2197948.aspx |title=City decide against Pickford appeal |publisher=Bradford City A.F.C. |date=12 January 2015 |access-date=17 October 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161017202706/http://www.bradfordcityfc.co.uk/news/article/city-decide-against-pickford-appeal-2197948.aspx |archive-date=17 October 2016 |url-status=live}}</ref>

====Return to Sunderland====
On 1 January 2016, Pickford was recalled by Sunderland, with the side struggling in 19th place in the [[2015–16 Premier League|Premier League]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Man Utd's Sam Johnstone off on loan to Preston North End |url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/11697/10115470/man-utds-sam-johnstone-off-on-loan-to-preston-north-end |website=Sky Sports |access-date=31 December 2015}}</ref> He made his first-team debut on 9 January 2016, in a 3–1 [[2015–16 FA Cup|FA Cup]] loss to [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Arsenal 3 Sunderland 1: Gunners fight back to knock Cats out of FA Cup |url=http://www.shieldsgazette.com/sport/sunderland-afc/latest-safc-news/arsenal-3-sunderland-1-gunners-fight-back-to-knock-cats-out-of-fa-cup-1-7665597 |newspaper=Shields Gazette |access-date=9 January 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160113014103/http://www.shieldsgazette.com/sport/sunderland-afc/latest-safc-news/arsenal-3-sunderland-1-gunners-fight-back-to-knock-cats-out-of-fa-cup-1-7665597 |archive-date=13 January 2016}}</ref> He made his Premier League debut against [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] at [[White Hart Lane]] on 16 January 2016 losing 4–1, meaning he had played in the top five leagues of English football by the age of 21.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.premierleague.com/news/60834 |title=Pickford promise fulfilled for club and country |publisher=Premier League |date=16 July 2016 |access-date=12 November 2017}}</ref> On 26 January, Pickford signed a new contract committing his future to Sunderland until 2020.<ref name="skysports.com"/>

Pickford began the [[2016–17 Sunderland A.F.C. season|2016–17]] season as understudy to Vito Mannone. However, after Mannone suffered a serious arm injury in training, Pickford started Sunderland's third league match of the season, away to [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]]. He produced several saves during the match, but in the 85th minute he made an error that allowed [[Jay Rodriguez]] to equalise for the hosts in a 1–1 draw.<ref name="Echo_October2016"/> On 13 April 2017, Pickford was named as one of six players shortlisted for the [[PFA Young Player of the Year]] award.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.thepfa.com/news/2017/4/13/pfa-mens-young-player-of-the-year |title=Men's PFA Young Player of the Year 2017 |date=13 April 2017 |publisher=Professional Footballers' Association}}</ref>

===Everton===
====2017–18 season====
On 15 June 2017, Pickford signed for Premier League club [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] on a five-year contract. The initial fee of £25&nbsp;million, with the possibility of rising to £30&nbsp;million in add-ons, made him [[Goalkeeper (association football)#Highest fees|the third most expensive goalkeeper in history]] at the time, the most expensive British goalkeeper of all time and will make him Everton's second most expensive signing, pending add-on payments,<ref>{{cite news |title=Jordan Pickford: Everton confirm £25m, rising to £30m, deal with Sunderland |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40258852 |website=BBC Sport |date=15 June 2017 |access-date=5 September 2017}}</ref> as [[Gylfi Sigurðsson]] is currently the club's most expensive signing with £40&nbsp;million plus add-on payments.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40848343 |title=Gylfi Sigurdsson: Everton sign £45m deal for Swansea midfielder |website=BBC Sport |date=16 August 2017 |access-date=9 September 2017}}</ref> Pickford made his league debut for Everton on 12 August 2017, when his club beat [[Stoke City F.C.|Stoke City]] 1–0 at Goodison Park.<ref name=sb1718/> At the conclusion of his first season, he was named Everton's Player of the Season, along with numerous other club accolades.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/11671/11355385/jordan-pickford-honoured-after-dominating-everton-awards-night |title=Jordan Pickford 'honoured' after dominating Everton awards night |website=Sky Sports |date=1 May 2017 |access-date=4 July 2018}}</ref>

====2018–19 season====
On 2 December 2018, against [[Merseyside derby|local rivals]] [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]], Pickford made a last-minute mistake after he mishandled a volley from [[Virgil van Dijk]], allowing [[Divock Origi]] to score the winning goal for Liverpool from a close-range header in the 96th minute.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/46333097 |title=Liverpool 1–0 Everton: Jordan Pickford error gifts Divock Origi late winner |website=BBC Sport |date=2 December 2018 |access-date=12 December 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/premier-league/liverpool-everton-jordan-pickford-mistake-goal-error-divock-origi-a8664181.html |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/archive/20220614/https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/premier-league/liverpool-everton-jordan-pickford-mistake-goal-error-divock-origi-a8664181.html |archive-date=14 June 2022 |url-access=subscription |url-status=live |title= Liverpool vs Everton: Jordan Pickford explains what he was trying to do in 'freak' mistake |newspaper=The Independent |date=2 December 2018 |access-date=12 December 2018}}</ref>

On 10 March 2019, against [[Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United]], Pickford misjudged a cross from [[Matt Ritchie]] and then [[Tackle (football move)#Rugby union|rugby tackle]]d [[Salomón Rondón]], conceding a penalty. He quickly redeemed himself by saving Ritchie's penalty with his legs, and Everton doubled their lead a minute later.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2019/03/09/villain-hero-scapegoat-jordan-pickford-spotlight-newcastle/ |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/archive/20220112/https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2019/03/09/villain-hero-scapegoat-jordan-pickford-spotlight-newcastle/ |archive-date=12 January 2022 |url-access=subscription |url-status=live |title=From villain, to hero, to scapegoat – Jordan Pickford in the spotlight as Newcastle fight back to stun Everton|newspaper=The Daily Telegraph |date=9 March 2019 |access-date=21 March 2019}}{{cbignore}}</ref> In the second half, when an effort by Rondón went wide of the post, Pickford was seen smiling at the Newcastle fans in the Gallowgate end. However, he conceded three times after that, including parrying a shot by [[Miguel Almirón]] into the path of [[Ayoze Pérez]]. On ''[[Match of the Day]]'', [[Ian Wright]] suggested that Pickford should have focused on his game more, and given he was a former Sunderland player, not let his emotions affect him.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/47424848|title=Newcastle United 3–2 Everton|website=BBC Sport|date=10 March 2019|access-date=22 March 2019}}</ref> In April 2019 Pickford was allegedly filmed involved in a fracas; police said they would investigate.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/47779510|title=Jordan Pickford: Police investigate alleged incident involving England goalkeeper|date=2 April 2019|website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

====2019–20 season====
Pickford remained Everton's first choice goalkeeper, making 38 appearances in the [[Premier League]] and keeping 13 clean sheets.<ref>{{cite web |title=Jordan Pickford statistics 2019/20 |url=https://www.premierleague.com/players/4640/Jordan-Pickford/stats?co=1&se=274 |website=PremierLeague.com |publisher=Premier League |access-date=15 November 2020}}</ref>

However, he struggled with form and again made several errors. His record of four errors leading to goals was the highest of any keeper in the Premier League.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Lucas |first1=Katherine |title=Jordan Pickford keeps making errors – but will Everton and England stick by the goalkeeper? |url=https://inews.co.uk/sport/football/jordan-pickford-errors-everton-england-ancelotti-southgate-659590 |website=iNews |date=25 September 2020 |publisher=Daily Mail and General Trust plc |access-date=15 November 2020}}</ref> When it came to saving shots, only [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]]'s [[Kepa Arrizabalaga]] saved fewer than Pickford.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Swan |first1=Rob |title=Kepa Arrizabalaga has the worst save percentage of any goalkeeper in the Premier League |url=https://www.givemesport.com/1539468-kepa-arrizabalaga-has-the-worst-save-percentage-of-any-goalkeeper-in-the-premier-league |website=Give Me Sport |date=19 January 2020 |publisher=Sports New Media Limited |access-date=15 November 2020}}</ref> Despite this, [[Carlo Ancelotti]] remained steadfast in his support for Pickford, saying "Sometimes unfortunately you can have a mistake but after that you need to have the personality to move on quickly and he did."<ref>{{cite web |title=Jordan Pickford has personality to brush off critics, says Carlo Ancelotti |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11671/11940145/jordan-pickford-has-personality-to-brush-off-critics-says-carlo-ancelotti |website=Sky Sports |publisher=Sky |access-date=15 November 2020}}</ref>

However, he performed much better in the [[EFL Cup]], being part of the team that made the Quarter-Finals of the competition, including saving a penalty from [[Leicester City]]'s [[James Maddison]]. It was however not enough and Leicester progressed through after the [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shoot-out]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Rose |first1=Gary |title=Everton 2–2 Leicester City (2–4 on Penalties): Foxes survive comeback to progress on penalties |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/50738363 |access-date=15 November 2020}}</ref>

====2020–21 season====
Pickford again continued to be Everton's first choice goalkeeper, being part of the team that won its opening four games for the first time since the 1969–70 season.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Dawkes |first1=Phil |title=Everton 4 – 2 Brighton: Calvert-Lewin scores again as Toffees go top |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54305845 |access-date=14 November 2020}}</ref>

In the first [[Merseyside derby]] of the season, Pickford was criticised<ref>{{cite web |last1=Blackham |first1=Daniel |title=Alan Shearer delivers VAR rant over Jordan Pickford's tackle on Virgil van Dijk |url=https://www.express.co.uk/sport/football/1349114/Alan-Shearer-VAR-rant-Jordan-Pickford-Virgil-van-Dijk |website=Express.co.uk |date=18 October 2020 |publisher=Daily Express |access-date=14 November 2020 |quote=Everton goalkeeper Jordan Pickford should have been sent off for his “awful challenge” on Virgil van Dijk which left the Liverpool defender facing eight months out with a potential ACL injury. The derby was one of the most heated in recent years, with [[Richarlison]] being sent off for a late challenge on [[Thiago Alcântara|Thiago Alcantara]] and captain [[Jordan Henderson]] having a game-winning goal ruled out for offside by [[Video assistant referee|VAR]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Hunter |first1=Andy |title=Liverpool denied by Everton and VAR in wild Merseyside derby draw |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2020/oct/17/everton-liverpool-premier-league-match-report |website=Observer Sport |date=17 October 2020 |publisher=The Guardian |access-date=14 November 2020}}</ref>

====2021–22 season====

On 25 May 2022, Pickford claimed the Player of the Season award from Everton following a personally outstanding campaign.<ref>{{cite news |title=Pickford and Gordon scoop Everton awards|url= https://www.evertonfc.com/news/2634469/pickford-and-gordon-scoop-everton-awards |website=Everton F.C. |date=25 May 2022 |access-date=5 June 2022}}</ref>On 28 May 2022, Pickford claimed the 2021–22 Castrol Save of the Season award for his amazing goal line stop against Chelsea. After Mason Mount's shot had hit both posts, Pickford raced across his line and then dived in the opposite direction to keep out Azpilicueta's rebound as Everton beat Chelsea 1–0 on 1 May 2022.<ref>{{cite web |last1=League |first1=Premier |title= Pickford wins Castrol Save of the Season award |url= https://www.premierleague.com/news/2635571 |website=Premier League |date=28 May 2022 |publisher=Premier League|access-date=5 June 2022}}</ref>

====2022–23 season====

Pickford kept his first clean sheet of the [[2022–23 Everton F.C. season|2022–23 season]] on 3 September 2022, in the Merseyside derby draw at [[Goodison Park]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/62697702|website=BBC Sport|first=Phil|last=McNulty|title=Everton and Liverpool in eventful goalless draw|date=3 September 2022|access-date=25 February 2023}}</ref> He was praised for his performance in the match by [[BBC Sport]]'s chief football writer [[Phil McNulty]], who said that "Pickford's status as England's first choice goalkeeper is unquestioned and fully deserved" ahead of the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/62781637|website=BBC Sport|first=Phil|last=McNulty|title=Jordan Pickford: England keeper shows class as Everton begin to take shape|date=3 September 2022|access-date=25 February 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
===Youth===

Pickford has represented the England international team at every level from under-16s to under-21s.<ref name="Echo_October2016">{{cite news |title=10 things you may not know about England new boy Jordan Pickford |url=http://www.sunderlandecho.com/sport/football/sunderland-afc/10-things-you-may-not-know-about-england-new-boy-jordan-pickford-1-8173261 |newspaper=Sunderland Echo |access-date=13 June 2017}}</ref> In October 2009, he made his [[England national under-16 football team|England U16]] debut against [[Wales national under-16 football team|Wales U16]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Bruce chasing Zenden |url=http://www.thenorthernecho.co.uk/sport/4686565.Bruce_chasing_Zenden |newspaper=The Northern Echo |location=Darlington |date=16 October 2009 |access-date=17 October 2016}}</ref> In 2011, Pickford represented England at the [[2011 FIFA U-17 World Cup]]. In a game versus Canada, Pickford allowed an 86th-minute goal, from a downfield kick by Canadian goalkeeper [[Quillan Roberts]] which tied the game in a 2–2 draw,<ref>{{Cite news |title=Canadian goalkeeper makes history with goal at u17 World Cup |url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/sports/soccer/canadian-goalkeeper-makes-history-with-goal-at-u-17-world-cup/article584267/ |newspaper=The Globe and Mail |access-date=5 April 2014}}</ref> which remains as the only goal scored by a goalkeeper in a FIFA finals to date from open play.<ref>{{cite web |title=Uruguay advance as records fell |url=https://www.fifa.com/u17worldcup/news/newsid=1458456/index.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110626121039/http://www.fifa.com/u17worldcup/news/newsid=1458456/index.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=26 June 2011 |publisher=FIFA |access-date=14 July 2018}}</ref>

On 25 August 2015, he was called up to the [[England national under-21 football team|England under-21]] squad for the first time.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.sunderlandecho.com/sport/sunderland-afc/latest-safc-news/safc-match-analysis/sunderland-s-duncan-watmore-and-jordan-pickford-selected-for-england-u21s-1-7427777 |title=Sunderland's Duncan Watmore and Jordan Pickford selected for England U21s |newspaper=Sunderland Echo |access-date=11 October 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924123418/http://www.sunderlandecho.com/sport/sunderland-afc/latest-safc-news/safc-match-analysis/sunderland-s-duncan-watmore-and-jordan-pickford-selected-for-england-u21s-1-7427777 |archive-date=24 September 2015}}</ref> Pickford made his debut for the under-21s in a [[Exhibition game#Association football|friendly]] against the [[United States men's national under-23 soccer team|United States under-23s]] at Deepdale on 3 September 2015.<ref name="Echo_October2016"/>

On 14 October, Pickford assisted [[Nathan Redmond]]'s goal in the England U21s' 3–0 victory over [[Kazakhstan national under-21 football team|Kazakhstan]] at the [[Ricoh Arena]] with a long range pass from his own penalty area.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.sunderlandecho.com/sport/sunderland-afc/latest-safc-news/safc-match-analysis/watch-sunderland-s-ace-goalkeeper-jordan-pickford-hailed-after-england-assist-1-7513763 |title=Watch: Sunderland's ace goalkeeper Jordan Pickford hailed after England assist |newspaper=Sunderland Echo |access-date=11 October 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304081137/http://www.sunderlandecho.com/sport/sunderland-afc/latest-safc-news/safc-match-analysis/watch-sunderland-s-ace-goalkeeper-jordan-pickford-hailed-after-england-assist-1-7513763 |archive-date=4 March 2016}}</ref> His team won the [[2016 Toulon Tournament]], their first in 22 years.<ref name=toulon>{{cite news |title=England Under-21s win Toulon Tournament for first time in 22 years |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/36410296 |access-date=11 October 2016 |website=BBC Sport |date=29 May 2016}}</ref>

Pickford led the England under-21 to the semi-final of the [[2017 UEFA European Under-21 Championship]] in June 2017 with a string of strong performances.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/8/main/2017/06/30/36749952/saul-pickford-star-in-euro-under-21-team-of-the-tournament |title=Saul & Pickford star in Euro Under-21 Team of the Tournament |website=Goal.com |date=30 June 2017}}</ref>

===Senior team===
On 24 August 2017, Pickford was called up to the England squad by manager [[Gareth Southgate]] for two 2018 FIFA World Cup qualifiers against [[Malta national football team|Malta]] and [[Slovakia national football team|Slovakia]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2017/aug/24/england-squad-maguire-chalobah-slovakia-malta |title=Harry Maguire and Nathaniel Chalobah called up to England squad for first time |newspaper=The Guardian |location=London |date=24 August 2017 |access-date=12 November 2017}}</ref> However, he later withdrew from the squad due to injury.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2017/aug/29/jordan-pickford-out-england-squad-world-cup-qualifiers |title=Jordan Pickford ruled out of England's squad for World Cup qualifiers |newspaper=The Guardian |location=London |date=29 August 2017 |access-date=5 September 2017}}</ref> Pickford made his debut in a friendly on 10 November 2017, starting as England drew 0–0 with [[Germany national football team|Germany]] at [[Wembley Stadium]].<ref>{{cite news |first=Phil |last=McNulty |title=England 0–0 Germany |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41854505 |website=BBC Sport |date=10 November 2017 |access-date=11 November 2017}}</ref>

====2018 FIFA World Cup====
[[File:Jordan Pickford 2018 (cropped).jpg|thumb|upright|Pickford playing for [[England national football team|England]] at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]]]]

Pickford was named in the 23-man [[England national football team|England national team]] squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44138600 |title=England World Cup squad: Trent Alexander-Arnold in 23-man squad |website=BBC Sport |date=16 May 2018 |access-date=17 May 2018}}</ref> He started the opening match against [[Tunisia national football team|Tunisia]], which England won 2–1.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44434276 |title=Tunisia 1–2 England |website=BBC Sport |date=19 June 2018 |access-date=19 June 2018}}</ref> In their round of 16 match on 3 July, Pickford helped England beat [[Colombia national football team|Colombia]] 4–3 on penalties after a 1–1 draw, saving from [[Carlos Bacca]], to claim his nation's first World Cup penalty shoot-out victory and a place in the quarter-final.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44610244 |title=Colombia 1–1 England |first=Phil |last=McNulty |website=BBC Sport |date=3 July 2018 |access-date=3 July 2018}}</ref>

On 7 July 2018, Pickford kept his first clean sheet of the World Cup and was named man of the match, as England knocked out [[Sweden national football team|Sweden]] with a 2–0 win in the quarter-finals. The result earned England a place in the semi-finals of the World Cup for the first time since [[1990 FIFA World Cup|1990]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44665445 |title=World Cup 2018: England beat Sweden 2–0 to reach semi-finals |first=Phil |last=McNulty |website=BBC Sport |date=7 July 2018 |access-date=7 July 2018}}</ref>

====2019 UEFA Nations League====
In June 2019, Pickford scored and then saved the decisive penalty as England beat [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]] to finish third at the inaugural [[2019 UEFA Nations League Finals|UEFA Nations League Finals]]. It was the second penalty shoot-out in a row that England had won, with Pickford saving penalties in both, and he became the first goalkeeper to take (and score) a penalty in a competitive shoot-out for England.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/48191915 |title=Switzerland 0 0 England |first=Phil |last=McNulty |website=BBC Sport |date=9 June 2019 |access-date=16 June 2019}}</ref>

====Euro 2020====
On 1 June 2021, Pickford was named in the 26-man squad for the rescheduled UEFA Euro 2020. At the tournament, Pickford became the first goalkeeper in history to keep clean sheets in the first five games of a European Championship.<ref>{{cite web | url= https://theworldgame.sbs.com.au/pickford-makes-euro-history-as-england-s-clean-sheet-run-rolls-on | title= Pickford makes Euro history as England's clean sheet run rolls on | work=The World Game | access-date=4 July 2021}}</ref> On 7 July 2021, Pickford set an all-time England record for most consecutive scoreless minutes posted by a goalkeeper. He set the mark of 721 minutes midway through the first half [[UEFA Euro 2020|Euro 2020]] semi-final against [[Denmark national football team|Denmark]], passing a record previously held by [[Gordon Banks]] (720 minutes).<ref>{{Cite web|title=Pickford passes Banks to set all-time England record for scoreless minutes {{!}} Goal.com|url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/pickford-passes-banks-to-set-all-time-england-record-for/i81umdcuiv7qz6mjyw2s2p7z|access-date=7 July 2021|website=www.goal.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Meade|first=Samuel|date=7 July 2021|title=Pickford breaks 55-year record moments before conceding first Euro 2020 goal|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/football/news/jordan-pickford-england-record-euros-24484292|access-date=7 July 2021|website=mirror}}</ref> On 11 July 2021, Pickford saved two Italy penalties during the shoot-out in the [[UEFA Euro 2020 Final|final]], after the match finished 1–1 after extra time. However, England lost 3–2 after missing their last three penalties.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Euro 2020 final: England beaten by Italy on penalties|first=Phil|last=McNulty|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/51198762|date=11 July 2021|access-date=30 July 2021}}</ref>

====2022 FIFA World Cup====
Pickford was selected for England's 26-man squad on 10 November 2022 for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63585869|website=BBC Sport|title=World Cup 2022:England squad features Maddison, Rashford and Wilson|date=10 November 2022|access-date=24 February 2023}}</ref> He played in every minute of England's campaign and kept three clean sheets. The first two were in the group stage games against the [[United States men's national soccer team|USA]] (0–0)<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63603425|website=BBC Sport|title=England labour to goalless draw against USA|first=Phil|last=McNulty|date=25 November 2022|access-date=24 February 2023}}</ref> and [[Wales national football team|Wales]] (3–0),<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63603442|website=BBC Sport|title=Rashford double sees England top group as Wales exit|first=Phil|last=McNulty|date=29 November 2022|access-date=24 February 2023}}</ref> as England topped [[2022 FIFA World Cup Group B|Group B]]. He also kept out [[Senegal national football team|Senegal]] in England's 3–0 [[2022 FIFA World Cup knockout stage|Round of 16]] win.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63771033|website=BBC Sport|title=England beat Senegal to set up France quarter-final|first=Phil|last=McNulty|date=4 December 2022|access-date=24 February 2023}}</ref> Pickford won his 50th [[Cap (sport)|cap]] in England's quarter-final defeat to [[France national football team|France]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.evertonfc.com/news/2980985/pickford-collects-50th-cap-but-england-exit-world-cup|website=Everton F.C.|title=Pickford collects 50th cap but England exit World Cup|date=10 December 2022|access-date=25 February 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63843792|website=BBC Sport|title=Kane misses penalty as England exit World Cup|first=Phil|last=McNulty|date=10 December 2022|access-date=24 February 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63585869|website=englandstats.com|title=Jordan Pickford - 50 Caps (0 Goals)}}</ref>
