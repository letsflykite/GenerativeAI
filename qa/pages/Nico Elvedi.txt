'''Nico Elvedi''' (born 30 September 1996) is a Swiss professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Centre back|centre back]] for [[Bundesliga]] club [[Borussia Mönchengladbach]] and the [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland national team]].

==Club career==
Born in [[Zürich]], Elvedi was a youth player for [[FC Zürich]]. He made his [[Swiss Super League]] debut on 15 May 2014 in a 1–0 away win against [[FC Lausanne-Sport]] playing the full match.<ref name="soccerway2">{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2014/05/15/switzerland/super-league/fc-lausanne-sport/fc-zurich/1481356/|title=Lausanne Sport vs. FC Zürich - 15 May 2014 - Soccerway|publisher=soccerway.com|access-date=2 November 2014}}</ref>

Elvedi joined [[Borussia Mönchengladbach]] in 2015 for a €4 million transfer fee. He scored his first goal in the [[Bundesliga]] during the derby against [[1. FC Köln]]. in 2017. The match saw Borussia Mönchengladbach win 1–0.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Elvedi strike gives Gladbach Rhine derby win over Cologne |url=https://www.goal.com/en-za/match/borussia-mgladbach-v-köln/4prworbnbe0agq2a0b6uy87l6 |access-date=2022-12-09 |website=bundesliga.com - the official Bundesliga website |language=en}}</ref>

==International career==
Elvedi played for various Swiss youth teams, and made his debut for the senior [[Switzerland national football team]] in a friendly 1–2 defeat to [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]] on 28 May 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Switzerland vs. Belgium - Football Match Summary - May 28, 2016 - ESPN|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/match/_/gameId/441676|access-date=1 June 2021|website=ESPN.com|language=en}}</ref>

He was included in the [[Switzerland national football team]] 23-man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Switzerland opts for experience in World Cup squad |url=https://www.apnews.com/b886bdc694bf4942a35f1dcfd01c2697 |access-date=1 August 2019 |work=Associated Press News |date=4 June 2018}}</ref>

In May 2019, he played in [[2019 UEFA Nations League Finals]], where his team finished 4th.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/48191915|title=Pickford the hero in England shootout win|work=BBC Sport}}</ref>

In 2021, he was called up to the national team for the [[UEFA European Championship|2020 UEFA European Championship]], where the team upset favorites [[France national football team|France]] en route to the quarter-finals, where they lost to [[Spain national football team|Spain]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-06-28 |title=France 3-3 Switzerland (aet; pens 4-5): Euro 2020 last 16 – as it happened |url=http://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2021/jun/28/france-v-switzerland-euro-2020-last-16-score-updates-live |access-date=2022-12-09 |website=the Guardian |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Switzerland vs. Spain - Football Match Summary - July 2, 2021 - ESPN |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/match/_/gameId/560300 |access-date=2022-12-09 |website=ESPN.com |language=en}}</ref>

In 2022, Elvedi was part of the Switzerland national football team's [[2022 FIFA World Cup squads#Switzerland|26-man squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], where Switzerland were knocked out in the round-of-16 by [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]], 6-1.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Das |first=Andrew |date=2022-12-06 |title=Ronaldo's Replacement Scores a Hat Trick in Portugal's Romp Over Switzerland |language=en-US |work=The New York Times |url=https://www.nytimes.com/live/2022/12/06/sports/portugal-switzerland-world-cup-score |access-date=2022-12-09 |issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Elvedi's twin brother, [[Jan Elvedi|Jan]], is also a professional footballer who plays for [[2. Bundesliga]] club [[SSV Jahn Regensburg|Jahn Regensburg]].<ref>{{FootballDatabase.eu}}</ref><ref>{{kicker|jan-elvedi}}</ref>
