'''Hans Vanaken''' (born 24 August 1992) is a Belgian professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as an [[attacking midfielder]] for [[Belgian Pro League]] club [[Club Brugge KV|Club Brugge]] and the [[Belgium national football team|Belgium national team]]. Vanaken is well known in Belgium for his prolific goal scoring ability despite playing in midfield.

==Club career==
===Early career===
Vanaken began his career at the youth team of [[Lommel United]] before leaving the club to join the [[PSV Eindhoven]] academy in 2002 when the former declared bankruptcy.<ref name="ClubBruggeProfile" /> He returned to a revamped Lommel in 2008 and made his senior debut for the club in the [[Belgian Second Division]] in 2010.<ref name="ClubBruggeProfile" /> He caught the eye of a number of top division clubs after a string of standout performances for Lommel and joined [[K.S.C. Lokeren Oost-Vlaanderen|Lokeren]] in 2013.<ref name="ClubBruggeProfile" />

He made top flight debut for Lokeren on 28 July 2013, when he scored two goals in a 3–2 win against defending champions [[R.S.C. Anderlecht|Anderlecht]].<ref name="ClubBruggeProfile" /> Vanaken was instrumental in Lokeren's run to the [[2014 Belgian Cup Final|final]] of the [[2013–14 Belgian Cup]], playing in six of their seven matches and scoring one goal as his side edged out [[S.V. Zulte Waregem|Zulte Waregem]] for the title.<ref name="soccerway" /> Vanaken further excelled in his second season in the top flight, scoring eight league goals and finishing third in the voting for the Belgian Golden Shoe award.<ref name="Medical" />

===Club Brugge===
It was announced in May 2015 that Lokeren had reached an agreement with Club Brugge over the transfer of Vanaken to the latter with the player signing a five-year deal subject to a medical.<ref name="Medical">{{cite web|url=https://www.clubbrugge.be/en/news/club-brugge-reached-agreement-principle-land-hans-vanaken|title=Club Brugge reached an agreement in principle to land Hans Vanaken|website=Club Brugge|date=29 May 2015|access-date=27 January 2020}}</ref><ref name="NewContract">{{cite web|url=https://www.clubbrugge.be/en/news/prolonged-contract-hans-vanaken|title=Prolonged contract for Hans Vanaken!|website=Club Brugge|date=9 August 2019|access-date=26 January 2020}}</ref> His maiden season at Club Brugge came with a spot in the starting line-up, where he proved instrumental in helping the club lift their 14th league championship.<ref name="ClubBruggeProfile">{{cite web|url=https://www.clubbrugge.be/en/hans-vanaken#bio|title=Hans Vanaken Profile|website=Club Brugge|date=10 July 2017|access-date=26 January 2020}}</ref>

In 2018, Vanaken scored 21 goals and provided a further 21 assists as he helped his team to its 15th league title and the Belgian Supercup. On 16 January 2019, Vanaken won the [[Belgian Golden Shoe]] award after having won the Belgian Professional Footballer of the Year award in May 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.clubbrugge.be/en/news/golden-boot-goes-hans-vanaken|title=Golden Boot goes to Hans Vanaken!|website=Club Brugge|date=16 January 2019|access-date=26 January 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.clubbrugge.be/en/news/club-plunders-player-year-awards|title=Club plunders Player of the Year Awards|website=Club Brugge|date=14 May 2018|access-date=26 January 2020}}</ref>

Vanaken signed a new contract with Brugge on 9 August 2019, tying him to the club until 2024.<ref name="NewContract" />

On 11 December 2019, Vanaken scored Brugge's equalizing goal in their [[2019–20 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] home match against Spanish club [[Real Madrid]]; but Brugge would eventually fall to a 3–1 defeat and be parachuted into the [[2019–20 UEFA Europa League knockout phase|UEFA Europa League]] Round of 32.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://bleacherreport.com/articles/2866545-rodrygo-real-madrid-beat-club-brugge-3-1-in-champions-league|title=Rodrygo, Real Madrid Beat Club Brugge 3-1 in Champions League|website=Bleacher Report|date=11 December 2019|access-date=26 January 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/50749103|title=Real Madrid's two Brazilian teenagers scored as they beat Club Bruges in a Champions League dead rubber match.|website=BBC|date=11 December 2019|access-date=26 January 2020}}</ref>

On 15 January 2020, Vanaken became the first player since [[Jan Ceulemans]] in 1986 to win back-to-back Belgian Golden Shoe awards. Vanaken won the award on the back of 20 goals and 7 assists during 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.clubbrugge.be/en/news/second-golden-boot-hans-vanaken|title=A second Golden Boot for Hans Vanaken!|website=Club Brugge|date=15 January 2020|access-date=26 January 2020}}</ref>

On 20 May 2021, Vanaken scored twice as Brugge drew 3–3 with [[R.S.C. Anderlecht–Club Brugge KV rivalry|rivals]] [[R.S.C. Anderlecht|Anderlecht]] to win the [[2020–21 Belgian First Division A|Belgian First Division A]] title for the fourth time in six years and 17th time overall.<ref name=clinch /> It was the first time since 1973 that Club Brugge had been crowned champions at Anderlecht's ground and the first time since 1976–77 and 1977–78 that Brugge had won back-to-back league titles.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/soccer/2021/05/21/club-brugge-wins-back-to-back-belgian-titles/44220377/|title=Club Brugge wins back-to-back Belgian titles|website=USA Today|date=21 May 2021|accessdate=20 August 2021}}</ref>

==International career==
Vanaken made his debut for the [[Belgium national football team]] on 7 September 2018, replacing [[Eden Hazard]] in a 4–0 victory against [[Scotland national football team|Scotland]] at [[Hampden Park]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/scotland-vs-belgium/report/388918|title=Scotland 0-4 Belgium: Romelu Lukaku and Eden Hazard score|website=Sky Sports|date=7 September 2018|access-date=26 January 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/29908/11494219/timothy-castagne-hans-vanaken-leandro-trossard-birger-verstraete-who-are-belgiums-newcomers|title=Timothy Castagne, Hans Vanaken, Leandro Trossard, Birger Verstraete: Who are Belgium's newcomers?|website=Sky Sports|date=9 September 2018|access-date=26 January 2020}}</ref>

He scored his first two goals for Belgium on 30 March 2021, coming in an 8–0 win over [[Belarus national football team|Belarus]] in [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group E|qualifying]] for the 2022 FIFA World Cup.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/report?gameId=590381|title=Belgium rout of Belarus puts reserves in the spotlight|website=ESPN|date=30 March 2021}}</ref>

On 17 May 2021, Vanaken was one of two Belgium-based players to be selected for the final 26-man squad for the [[UEFA Euro 2020]], along with club teammate [[Simon Mignolet]].<ref name="Red Devils Squad">{{cite news |url=https://www.rbfa.be/en/news/selection-red-devils-euro-2020-has-been-announced |title=Selection of the Red Devils for EURO 2020 has been announced |work=[[Royal Belgian Football Association]] |date=17 May 2021 |access-date=17 May 2021}}</ref>
