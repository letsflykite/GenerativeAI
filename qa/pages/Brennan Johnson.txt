'''Brennan Price Johnson''' (born 23 May 2001) is a professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]] for {{English football updater|NottingF}} club [[Nottingham Forest F.C.|Nottingham Forest]] and the [[Wales national football team|Wales national team]].

==Early life==
Johnson was born in [[Nottingham]], Nottinghamshire.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://uk.soccerway.com/players/brennan-johnson/529972/ |title=B. Johnson: Summary |website=Soccerway |publisher=Perform Group |access-date=5 March 2013}}</ref> He is the son of former footballer [[David Johnson (footballer, born 1976)|David Johnson]].<ref name="auto"/><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2017/03/23/evertonin-talks-nottingham-forest-sign-teenager-brennan-johnson/ |title=Everton in talks with Nottingham Forest to sign teenager Brennan Johnson as search for country's leading young talents continues |first=John |last=Percy |date=23 March 2017 |newspaper=The Daily Telegraph |location=London |access-date=28 August 2019}}</ref>

==Club career==
After joining the [[Nottingham Forest F.C.|Nottingham Forest]] academy from [[Dunkirk F.C.|Dunkirk]] at eight years old,<ref name="auto">{{cite news |url=https://www.nottinghampost.com/sport/football/football-news/brennan-johnson-hoping-maintain-family-2897521 |title=Brennan Johnson hoping to maintain the family business by pulling on Nottingham Forest's Garibaldi red |first=Paul |last=Taylor |date=22 May 2019 |newspaper=Nottingham Post |access-date=28 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://theathletic.com/3310425/2022/05/14/brennan-johnson-made-in-nottingham-from-the-banks-of-the-trent-to-powering-forests-promotion-push/ |title=Brennan Johnson – made in Nottingham |first=Paul Taylor and Nick |last=Miller |website=The Athletic}}</ref> Johnson made his [[First team (association football)|first-team]] debut aged 18, appearing on 3 August 2019 as an 88th-minute [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] in a 2–1 loss against [[West Bromwich Albion F.C.|West Bromwich Albion]] on the opening day of the season.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nottinghamforest.co.uk/news/2019/august/match-report-forest-vs-west-bromwich-albion/ |title=Forest 1–2 West Bromwich Albion |publisher=Nottingham Forest F.C. |date=3 August 2019 |access-date=28 August 2019}}</ref>

On 25 September 2020, Johnson joined [[EFL League One|League One]] club [[Lincoln City F.C.|Lincoln City]] on a season-long [[Loan (association football)|loan]].<ref>{{cite web |title=The Imps sign exciting Forest attacker on loan |url=https://www.weareimps.com/news/2020/september/200925-brennansigns/ |publisher=Lincoln City F.C. |access-date=25 September 2020}}</ref> He made his debut two days later, coming off the bench against [[Charlton Athletic F.C.|Charlton Athletic]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Report: The Imps v Charlton Athletic |url=https://www.weareimps.com/news/2020/september/200927-reportcharlton/ |publisher=Lincoln City F.C. |access-date=27 September 2020}}</ref> He scored his first career goal against [[Plymouth Argyle F.C.|Plymouth Argyle]], heading in from close range.<ref>{{cite web |title=Report: The Imps V Plymouth Argyle |url=https://www.weareimps.com/news/2020/october/201020-plymouthreport/ |publisher=Lincoln City F.C. |access-date=20 October 2020}}</ref> In April 2021, Johnson scored his first career [[Hat-trick (association football)|hat-trick]] in a match against [[Milton Keynes Dons F.C.|Milton Keynes Dons]], taking Johnson just 11 minutes to accomplish this feat.<ref>{{cite web |title=Brennan Johnson hits 11-minute hat-trick at LNER Stadium |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/lincoln-city-vs-milton-keynes-dons/431271 |website=Sky Sports |access-date=13 April 2021}}</ref>

Johnson's first professional goal for Nottingham Forest came in a 1–1 draw against local rivals [[Derby County F.C.|Derby County]] on 28 August 2021.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58289221 |title=Derby County 1–1 Nottingham Forest |website=BBC Sport |date=28 August 2021 |access-date=21 October 2021}}</ref> Forest experienced an upturn of form after the appointment of [[Steve Cooper (football manager)|Steve Cooper]], replacing [[Chris Hughton]], and after scoring one goal and providing two assists in a month where Forest had three wins, Johnson was awarded the [[EFL Young Player of the Month]] award for September 2021.<ref name="EFL YPOTM Sep21"> {{cite web |url=https://www.nottinghamforest.co.uk/news/2021/october/16/brennan-named-efl-young-player-of-the-month/ |title=Brennan named EFL Young Player of the Month |publisher=Nottingham Forest F.C. |date=16 October 2021 |access-date=21 October 2021}}</ref> Johnson was awarded the [[EFL Championship Player of the Month]] award for April 2022 after his four goals and four assists fired Forest into the play-offs, narrowly missing out on automatic promotion.<ref name="Apr22 POTM">{{cite web |url=https://www.efl.com/news/2022/may/sky-bet-efl-manager-and-player-of-the-month-april-winners/ |title=Sky Bet EFL Manager and Player of the Month April winners! |publisher=English Football League |date=13 May 2022 |access-date=13 May 2022}}</ref> Johnson ended the season as Forest's top scorer with 19 goals in all competitions, including in both semi-final legs of their successful play-off campaign. He was named the EFL Championship Young Player of the Season.<ref name="EFLChampYoung">{{cite web |url=https://www.efl.com/news/2022/april/aleksandar-mitrovic-named-sky-bet-championship-player-of-the-season/ |title=Aleksandar Mitrovic named Sky Bet Championship Player of the Season |publisher=English Football League |date=24 April 2022 |access-date=5 March 2023}}</ref>

On 1 July 2022, Johnson signed a new four-year contract with Nottingham Forest, keeping him at the club until 2026.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.nottinghamforest.co.uk/news/2022/july/01/johnson-signs-new-long-term-deal/ |title=Johnson signs new long-term deal |publisher=Nottingham Forest F.C. |date=1 July 2022 |access-date=23 August 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
Johnson was eligible to represent [[England national football team|England]], [[Jamaica national football team|Jamaica]] and [[Wales national football team|Wales]] at international level. He played for England under-17s before switching to Wales in 2018.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dai-sport.com/brennans-double-strike-wales-polish-success/ |title=Brennan's double strike for Wales in Polish success |first=Terry |last=Phillips |website=Dai Sport |date=16 October 2018 |access-date=28 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.nottinghampost.com/sport/football/football-news/nottingham-forest-youngster-brennan-johnson-1993730 |title=Brennan Johnson starts as Wales beaten by Republic of Ireland U19 |first=Matt |last=Davies |date=11 September 2018 |newspaper=Nottingham Post |access-date=28 August 2019}}</ref> In September 2020, Johnson was called up to the senior Wales squad for the first time.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/53968852 |title=Wales suffer quadruple player blow |website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

Johnson made his Wales debut in a 0–0 draw against the [[United States men's national soccer team|USA]] on 12 November 2020.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Wales and USA play out goalless draw |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54819952 |last=Pritchard |first=Dafydd |website=BBC Sport |date=12 November 2020}}</ref> He made his first start for Wales against Finland on 1 September 2021, in which he won a [[Penalty kick (association football)|penalty]].{{fact|date=March 2022}} Johnson scored his first goal for Wales on 11 June 2022 in a 1–1 [[2022–23 UEFA Nations League A|UEFA Nations League]] draw against [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=Johnson strikes late as Wales draw with Belgium |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/61687944 |last=Marsh |first=Philip |website=BBC Sport |date=11 June 2022}}</ref> In November 2022 he was named in the Wales squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63545011 2022 Wales World Cup squad]</ref>
