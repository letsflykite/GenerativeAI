'''Milad Mohammadi''' ({{lang-fa|میلاد محمدی }}; born 29 September 1993) is an Iranian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays for [[Super League Greece|Greek Super League]] club [[AEK Athens F.C.|AEK Athens]] and the [[Iran national football team|Iranian national team]]. Known for his speed and versatility, Milad plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|left back]] and [[Midfielder#Winger|winger]]. His twin brother [[Mehrdad Mohammadi|Mehrdad]] plays for [[Al-Arabi SC (Qatar)|Al-Arabi]] in the [[Qatar Stars League]].

==Club career==

===Rah Ahan===
Milad Mohammadi joined [[Rah Ahan Tehran F.C.|Rah Ahan]] in the summer of 2014 with a five–year contract<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.farsnews.com/newstext.php?nn=13930425000606 |title=دوقلوها و طالب‌لو با راه‌آهن ثبت کردند |work=خبرگزاری فارس}}</ref> and made his debut for them in the first fixture of the [[2014–15 Iran Pro League]] against [[Esteghlal F.C.|Esteghlal]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://iranleague.ir/MatchDetails/6259 |title=لیگ برتر : راه آهن یزدان - استقلال تهران |work=وب سایت رسمی سازمان لیگ فوتبال ایران,لیگ برتر فوتبال ایران,فوتبال برتر|access-date=16 August 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140819084430/http://iranleague.ir/MatchDetails/6259|archive-date=19 August 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In July 2015 Mohammadi went on trial with [[Austrian Football Bundesliga|Austrian Bundesliga]] club [[SK Sturm Graz|Sturm Graz]].<ref>{{cite AV media |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=28uffIKcAPc |title=Spielaufzeichnung: SK Sturm Graz 1:0 RFK Terek Grozny (0:0) |date=8 July 2015 |via=YouTube}}</ref> He reportedly had two offers from European sides but Rah Ahan coach [[Farhad Kazemi]] refused to let him leave the club.

===Akhmat Grozny===
On 6 February 2016 Mohammadi signed a contract with [[Russian Premier League]] club [[FC Terek Grozny|Terek Grozny]] until 2019.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.persianfootball.com/news/2016/02/06/milad-mohammadi-joins-russian-premier-league-club-terek-grozny/ |title=Milad Mohammadi joins Russian Premier League club Terek Grozny}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://fc-terek.ru/newslist/#6745 |title=РФК "Терек" Грозный им. А. А. Кадырова}}</ref> Milad made his debut for Terek Grozny as a substitute on 2 April 2016 in a 3–2 win over [[FC Anzhi Makhachkala|Anzhi Makhachkala]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/04/02/russia/premier-league/terek-groznyi/anzhi-makhachkala/2050276/ |title=Terek Grozny vs. Anzhi - 2 April 2016 - Soccerway}}</ref> Mohammadi scored his first goal for Terek and his first ever professional league goal on 29 April 2017 in a 5–2 victory against [[FC Ural Yekaterinburg|Ural Yekaterinburg]]. After the 2016–17 season, he was named as one of the top 50 U23 Asian talents to watch for.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://mitchellprocter.wordpress.com/2017/06/25/50-u23-players-to-watch-out-for/ |title=50 U23 Players to Watch Out for |date=25 June 2017}}</ref> 

On 27 May 2019, Akhmat confirmed that Mohammadi left the club as a free agent upon the expiration of his contract.<ref name="BalajMohammadi">{{cite web |publisher=[[FC Akhmat Grozny]] |url=http://fc-akhmat.ru/page/10643 |title=Милад Мохаммади и Беким Балай покидают "Ахмат". Спасибо за все, друзья!|trans-title=Milad Mohammadi and Bekim Balaj leave Akhmat. Thank you for everything, friends! |date=27 May 2019 |language=ru}}</ref>

===Gent===
In July 2019 Mohammadi signed with [[Belgium|Belgian]] club [[K.A.A. Gent|Gent]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.tasnimnews.com/en/news/2019/07/18/2056879/iran-s-milad-mohammadi-signs-for-kaa-gent |title=Iran's Milad Mohammadi Signs for KAA Gent |date=18 July 2019}}</ref>

===AEK Athens===
On 17 September 2021, Mohammadi signed a three-year contract with [[Super League Greece|Superleague Greece]] side [[AEK Athens F.C.|AEK Athens]] after getting released from [[K.A.A. Gent|Gent]] on 25 August.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sport24.gr/football/aek-episima-stin-enosi-o-mochamanti.9357576.html |title=ΑΕΚ: Επίσημα στην Ένωση ο Μοχαμάντι για τρία χρόνια |publisher=www.sport24.gr |date=17 September 2021 |language=el}}</ref>

==International career==
Mohammadi scored a goal during the [[2016 AFC U-23 Championship]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Milad Mohammadi Career Statistics |url=https://www.goal.com/en-my/player/milad-mohammadi/career/cz3jt5t4fy7zj3xsz239wtxw5 |website=Goal.com}}</ref>

[[Carlos Queiroz]] invited Mohammadi to a national team camp in June 2015, making his debut against [[Uzbekistan national football team|Uzbekistan]] in a friendly match on 11 June 2015 and was on the bench on 16 June 2015 in a [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification (AFC)|World Cup qualifier]] against [[Turkmenistan national football team|Turkmenistan]].{{fact|date=September 2021}}

He was named in Iran's [[Iran national football team|squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia,<ref>{{cite web |title=World Cup 2018: All the confirmed squads for this summer's finals in Russia |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/440833652018}} {{Dead link|date=June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44357568 |title=World Cup 2018: Iran head coach Carlos Queiroz names final squad |date=4 June 2018 |publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=9 June 2018}}</ref> where he came to prominence when he tried and failed at an extravagant [[throw-in]], in the 93rd minute while Iran were trailing 1–0 to [[Spain national football team|Spain]].<ref>{{cite web | access-date=20 January 2020 |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44555796 |title=World Cup 2018: Iran 0-1 Spain - the throw-in everyone is talking about |work=BBC Sport |date=20 June 2018}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Milad Mohammadi was born south of Tehran in Fallah district, however he has said that he is a native of Qazvin.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.aspress.ir/News/111190.html |script-title=fa:میلاد محمدی: از کارگری به تیم امید رسیدم |publisher=aspress |language=fa |date=Jan 2015|access-date=31 May 2016|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160701120108/http://www.aspress.ir/News/111190.html|archive-date=1 July 2016}}</ref> His family is originally from [[Qazvin Province]], Iran. Football fans in Iran nicknamed him "Mig-Mig" that refers to a cartoon character named Roadrunner in [[Wile E. Coyote and the Road Runner]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.varzesh3.com/news/1276241/%D9%85%DB%8C%DA%AF-%D9%85%DB%8C%DA%AF-%D9%84%D9%82%D8%A8-%D8%AF%D9%88%D9%82%D9%84%D9%88%D9%87%D8%A7%DB%8C-%D9%81%D9%88%D8%AA%D8%A8%D8%A7%D9%84-%D8%A7%DB%8C%D8%B1%D8%A7%D9%86 |title=میلاد محمدی: میگ میگ لقب دوقلوهای فوتبال ایران |publisher=Varzesh3 |date=Oct 2015 |access-date=15 November 2017}}</ref> He is the twin brother of [[Mehrdad Mohammadi]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.aspress.ir/News/111190.html |script-title=fa:میلاد محمدی: از کارگری به تیم امید رسیدم |publisher=aspress |date=Jan 2015|access-date=31 May 2016 |language=fa|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160701120108/http://www.aspress.ir/News/111190.html|archive-date=1 July 2016}}</ref>
