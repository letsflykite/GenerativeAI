'''Ruben Estephan Vargas Martinez''' (born 5 August 1998) is a Swiss professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)#Winger|winger]] for [[Bundesliga]] club [[FC Augsburg]] and the [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland national team]].

==Club career==
Vargas made his professional debut for [[FC Luzern]] in a 1–1 tie with [[FC Zürich]] on 27 August 2017.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.sfl.ch/superleague/matchcenter/match/201718/luz-fcz-13019/ | title=LUZ - FCZ &#124; Super League 2017/2018 &#124; Match }}</ref>

He signed a 5 year contract with [[FC Augsburg|Augsburg]] in the summer of 2019,<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://fcaugsburg.de/wns/ruben-vargas-joins-fc-augsburg/?lang=en|title=Ruben Vargas joins FC Augsburg|date=18 June 2019|website=FC Augsburg|language=en-US|access-date=18 November 2019}}</ref> becoming the first Bundesliga player of Dominican descent. He scored his first goal in his second match, against [[1. FC Union Berlin|Union Berlin]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/who-is-ruben-vargas-augsburg-shaqiri-lichsteiner-protege-7076|title=Ruben Vargas: Lichtsteiner's protege, the Bundesliga's top-scoring new player and Augsburg's answer to Shaqiri|website=bundesliga.com - the official Bundesliga website|language=en|access-date=18 November 2019}}</ref>

==International career==
Vargas made his [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland senior team]] debut on 8 September 2019, in a [[UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying Group D|Euro 2020 qualifier]] against [[Gibraltar national football team|Gibraltar]]. He replaced [[Granit Xhaka]] in the 74th minute.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[UEFA]]|url=https://www.uefa.com/european-qualifiers/season=2020/matches/round=2001086/match=2026193/index.html|title=Switzerland v Gibraltar game report|date=8 September 2019}}</ref>

=== Euro 2020 ===
Vargas scored a crucial penalty for Switzerland, during [[UEFA Euro 2020]], in a penalty shootout against [[France national football team|France]] on 28 June in the [[UEFA_Euro_2020#Round_of_16|Round of 16]] knockout stage, qualifying Switzerland for the quarter-final.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2021/jun/28/france-v-switzerland-euro-2020-last-16-score-updates-live|title=France 3-3 Switzerland (Aet; pens 4-5): Euro 2020 last 16 – as it happened|newspaper=The Guardian|date=28 June 2021|last1=Murray|first1=Scott}}</ref>
Vargas, however, later missed a penalty for Switzerland, during the penalty shootout against [[Spain national football team|Spain]] on 2 July in the quarter-final.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[UEFA]]|url= https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro-2020/match/2024485--switzerland-vs-spain/|title=Switzerland v Spain game report|date=2 July 2021}}</ref> Switzerland would later lose on penalties.

==Personal life==
Vargas was born in [[Adligenswil]], Switzerland, to a [[List of people from the Dominican Republic|Dominican]] father and a [[Swiss people|Swiss]] mother, and holds citizenship of both nations.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.luzernerzeitung.ch/sport/fussball/fcl/vargas-ich-will-einen-stammplatz;art9653,1182028|title=FCL-Talent Ruben Vargas: "Ich will einen Stammplatz"|first=Luzerner Zeitung AG 6006|last=Luzern|publisher=}}</ref> Vargas shares a hometown with his former Augsburg teammate and ex [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]] captain [[Stephan Lichtsteiner]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://fcaugsburg.de/wns/vargas-i-want-to-take-it-step-by-step-here/?lang=en|title=VARGAS: "I WANT TO TAKE IT STEP BY STEP HERE"|website=Augsburg|date=27 August 2019|accessdate=13 June 2020}}</ref>
