'''Conor John Gallagher''' (born 6 February 2000) is an English professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[Premier League]] club [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] and the [[England national football team|England national team]].

Beginning his career with Chelsea, Gallagher spent time on [[Loan (association football)|loan]] at [[EFL Championship]] clubs [[Charlton Athletic F.C.|Charlton Athletic]], [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]], and [[Premier League]] clubs [[West Bromwich Albion F.C.|West Bromwich Albion]] and [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]], between 2019 and 2022. At Crystal Palace, Gallagher was named the club's [[Crystal Palace F.C. Player of the Year|Player of the Year]] for the [[2021–22 Crystal Palace F.C. season|2021–22 season]]. He subsequently made his first-team debut at Chelsea during the [[2022–23 Chelsea F.C. season|2022–23 season]].

After representing England at various youth levels, Gallagher made his debut for the [[England national football team|senior national team]] in November 2021.

==Early and personal life==
Gallagher was born in [[Epsom]], Surrey to Lee and Samantha Gallagher and is the youngest of their four sons. He grew up in [[Great Bookham]]<ref>{{cite news |last=Madeley |first=Steve |date=30 November 2020 |title=Meet the Gallaghers: 'Even now we still have a play fight and beat Conor up' |url=https://theathletic.com/2224929/2020/11/29/meet-the-gallaghers-west-brom/ |website=The Athletic |access-date= 13 December 2021}}</ref> and attended [[Howard of Effingham School]].<ref name=u17>{{cite news |last=Seckington |first=Kaylee |title=Bookham-based Chelsea midfielder called up for England U17s' World Cup squad |url=https://www.getsurrey.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/bookham-based-chelsea-midfielder-called-13687443 |website=Get Surrey |date=28 September 2017 |access-date=13 December 2021}}</ref> His older brothers, Jake, Josh and [[Dan Gallagher (footballer)|Dan]],<ref>{{Cite news |last=Seckington |first=Kaylee |title="Even I didn't realise how good he is," Aldershot Town midfielder admits after facing Chelsea starlet brother |url=https://www.getsurrey.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/even-didnt-realise-how-good-13377425 |website=Get Surrey |date=27 July 2017 |access-date=14 May 2019}}</ref> are footballers at non-league level.<ref name="auto1">{{Cite web |url=https://www.chelseafc.com/en/teams/academy/conor-gallagher |title=Conor Gallagher |publisher=Chelsea F.C.}}</ref><ref name="auto2">{{Cite web |url=https://www.londonnewsonline.co.uk/exclusive-interview-with-chelsea-midfield-prospect-conor-gallagher-his-thoughts-on-charlton-loan-move-chances-of-breaking-into-the-plans-of-blues-boss-frank-lampard-and-undergoing-heart-surgery/ |title=Exclusive interview with Chelsea midfield prospect Conor Gallagher and his thoughts on Charlton loan move |first=Richard |last=Cawley |website=London News Online |date=20 September 2019}}</ref> Gallagher's family are [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] fans, and he lived a ten-minute drive from [[Cobham Training Centre|their training ground]] in [[Stoke d'Abernon]].<ref name="auto2"/>

==Club career==
===Early career===
[[File:Conor Gallagher December 2018.jpg|thumb|upright|Gallagher playing for [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] in 2018]]

After playing for Epsom Eagles,<ref name="auto2"/> Gallagher joined Chelsea at the age of eight.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49206860 |title=Conor Gallagher: Charlton Athletic sign Chelsea midfielder on loan |date=2 August 2019 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> In October 2018, he signed a new contract with Chelsea, contracting him to the club until June 2021.<ref name="auto1"/> He had minor heart surgery that year.<ref name="auto2"/> In May 2019, he was an unused [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] in the [[2019 UEFA Europa League final]], collecting a winner's medal.<ref name="auto2"/><ref name="auto3">{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49762680 |title=Conor Gallagher: Chelsea youngster on 'idol' Frank Lampard and Charlton Athletic loan |first=Rob |last=Stevens |website=BBC Sport |date=20 September 2019 |access-date=6 December 2019}}</ref> He was awarded Chelsea's Academy Player of the Year for the [[2018–19 Chelsea F.C. season|2018–19 season]].<ref name="auto3"/>

===2019–20 season: Loans to Charlton Athletic and Swansea City===
In August 2019, Gallagher signed a new three-year contract with Chelsea and moved on [[Loan (association football)|loan]] to [[EFL Championship|Championship]] club [[Charlton Athletic F.C.|Charlton Athletic]].<ref name="auto"/> After his first month with Charlton, in which he scored three goals in six matches, he won the [[EFL Young Player of the Month]] award for August.<ref name="auto3"/><ref name="auto4">{{Cite web |url=https://www.cafc.co.uk/news/view/5d84ad7327487/conor-gallagher-wins-efl-young-player-of-the-month-award-for-august |title=Conor Gallagher wins EFL Young Player of the Month award for August |publisher=Charlton Athletic F.C. |date=20 September 2019}}</ref> On 14 January 2020, Gallagher was recalled by Chelsea.<ref>{{cite news |title=Conor Gallagher trains with Chelsea after recall from Charlton loan |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11668/11908401/conor-gallagher-trains-with-chelsea-after-recall-from-charlton-loan |website=Sky Sports |date=14 January 2020 |access-date=14 January 2020}}</ref> 

On 15 January 2020, the day after leaving Charlton, Gallagher joined Championship club [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]] on loan for the rest of the [[2019–20 Swansea City A.F.C. season|2019–20 season]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Swansea City seal Conor Gallagher loan signing |url=https://www.swanseacity.com/news/swansea-city-seal-conor-gallagher-loan-signing |publisher=Swansea City A.F.C. |date=15 January 2020 |access-date=15 January 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/51106139 |title=Conor Gallagher: Swansea City sign Chelsea midfielder on loan |access-date=15 January 2020 |publisher=Swansea City A.F.C |date=15 January 2020}}</ref> He later said that his time at Swansea allowed him to be more creative as a player.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.walesonline.co.uk/sport/football/transfer-news/chelsea-conor-gallagher-swansea-charlton-18541300 |title=Chelsea's Conor Gallagher transfer decision explained |first=Ian |last=Mitchelmore |website=WalesOnline |publisher=Media Wales |date=4 July 2020}}</ref>

===2020–21 season: Loan to West Bromwich Albion===
On 17 September 2020, Gallagher signed a new five-year contract with Chelsea, and joined fellow Premier League club [[West Bromwich Albion F.C.|West Bromwich Albion]] on loan for the [[2020–21 West Bromwich Albion F.C. season|2020–21 season]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Gallagher signs new deal and completes Premier League loan |url=https://www.chelseafc.com/en/news/2020/09/17/gallagher-signs-new-deal-and-completes-premier-league-loan?cardIndex=0-0&fbclid=IwAR0K8-7p34YnIK28AI9r8YRmq715vBMGQtvbYZq5ko036fL_I_RJP-CHjfw |access-date=17 September 2020 |publisher=Chelsea F.C. |date=17 September 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54201979 |title=West Brom sign Chelsea's Gallagher on loan |website=BBC Sport |date=18 September 2020}}</ref> On 28 November, Gallagher scored his first Premier League goal and his first goal for West Brom in a 1–0 home league win over [[Sheffield United F.C.|Sheffield United]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/55025786 |title=West Bromwich Albion 1–0 Sheffield United: Baggies move out of relegation zone with win |website=BBC Sport |date=28 November 2020 |access-date=28 November 2020}}</ref> His only other goal for the club came in a 5–1 home defeat to future club [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]] on 6 December.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/55118791|title=West Bromwich Albion 1–5 Crystal Palace|date=6 December 2020|access-date=11 April 2023|website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

===2021–22 season: Loan to Crystal Palace===
In July 2021, Gallagher joined [[Premier League]] club [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]] on loan for the [[2021–22 Crystal Palace F.C. season|2021–22 season]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Conor Gallagher joins Palace on loan |url=https://www.cpfc.co.uk/news/announcement/conor-gallagher-joins-palace-on-loan/2021-07-30/|access-date=30 July 2021 |publisher=Crystal Palace F.C. |date=30 July 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58013275 |title=Chelsea's Gallagher joins Palace on loan |website=BBC Sport |date=30 July 2021}}</ref> He scored two goals in a 2–2 draw with [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]] on 28 August 2021, his first goals for Palace.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Gallagher rescues point for Palace at West Ham |website=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58289647|access-date=29 August 2021}}</ref> In November, Gallagher was subjected to [[Homosexuality in association football|homophobic chants]] from a group of [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]] fans; the club issued a statement which condemned this.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/59487764 |title=Leeds says homophobic chant 'unacceptable' |website=BBC Sport |date=1 December 2021}}</ref> By the start of December, he had six goals and three assists for Crystal Palace, the most of any Premier League player aged 21 or under.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/59631670 |title=What does future hold for Gallagher? |website=BBC Sport |date=12 December 2021}}</ref> On 15 April 2022, he was prevented by parent club Chelsea from playing against them in the FA Cup semi-final; Chelsea manager [[Thomas Tuchel]] apologised for doing so. Chelsea went on to defeat Palace 2–0.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/61118626 |title=Tuchel apologises to Gallagher for FA Cup block |website=BBC Sport|date=15 April 2022|access-date=19 March 2023}}</ref>

During his season with Palace, Gallagher was described by ''[[The Daily Telegraph]]'' as "one of the Premier League's most vibrant talents" and "a key player for Patrick Vieira", who was "thriving in his box-to-box role".<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2021/11/05/fourth-loan-lucky-conor-gallagher-has-become-one-premier-leagues/ |title=Fourth loan lucky: Conor Gallagher has become one of the Premier League's most vibrant talents |first=Sam |last=Dean |newspaper=The Daily Telegraph |location=London |date=5 November 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://theathletic.com/3463300/2022/08/01/crystal-palace-conor-gallagher-transfer/|title=Crystal Palace’s pre-season has been strong, but the loss of Conor Gallagher still hurts|date=1 August 2022|first=Matt|last=Woosnam|language=en|website=The Athletic}}</ref> For his performances with the club, he was later named Crystal Palace's [[Crystal Palace F.C. Player of the Year|Player of the Season]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cpfc.co.uk/news/announcement/conor-gallagher-wins-crystal-palace-player-of-the-season-2021-22/|title=Conor Gallagher wins Crystal Palace Player of the Season 2021/22|website=Crystal Palace F.C.|date=10 May 2022|access-date=19 March 2023|language=en}}</ref>

===2022–23 season: Return to Chelsea===
In July 2022, ahead of the [[2022–23 Chelsea F.C. season|2022–23 season]], Gallagher said he was determined to make a first-team breakthrough at Chelsea.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/62158217 |title=Gallagher determined to make Chelsea breakthrough |website=BBC Sport}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/articles/cd1z19ler4ro |title=Chelsea: Conor Gallagher ready to 'prove' worth to Tuchel |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> On 6 August 2022, he made his Chelsea debut, as a substitute, in a 1–0 away win against [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] in the Premier League.<ref>{{cite web |title=Chelsea opened the Premier League season with victory over Everton as Jorginho's penalty decided a scrappy contest at Goodison Park |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/62358042 |website=BBC Sport |access-date=6 August 2022 |date=6 August 2022}}</ref> On 1 October, he scored his first goal for Chelsea, a 90th-minute winner in a 2–1 away victory over his former club, Crystal Palace.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/oct/01/crystal-palace-chelsea-premier-league-match-report |title=Conor Gallagher's last-minute strike gives Chelsea win at Crystal Palace |newspaper=The Guardian |location=London |date=1 October 2022}}</ref> On 28 May 2023, Gallagher won Chelsea's Goal of the Season for the strike.<ref name = "goal">{{cite news |url=https://www.chelseafc.com/en/news/article/gallagher-wins-chelsea-goal-of-the-season |publisher=Chelsea F.C. |title=Gallagher wins Chelsea Goal of the Season |access-date=7 June 2023 |date=28 May 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
Gallagher's family heritage meant he was eligible to play for [[England national football team|England]], [[Scotland national football team|Scotland]] or the [[Republic of Ireland national football team|Republic of Ireland]] until he made a competitive appearance for any at senior level.<ref name=u17/><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.wba.co.uk/news/seven-facts-about-albions-seventh-signing-conor-gallagher |title=Seven facts about Albion's seventh signing Conor Gallagher |publisher=West Bromwich Albion F.C. |date=18 September 2020}}</ref> He represented England at under-17, under-18, under-19 and under-20 youth levels, winning the [[2017 FIFA U-17 World Cup]] in India.<ref name="auto1"/> 

On 8 October 2019, Gallagher received his first call up to the [[England national under-21 football team|England U21]] squad<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thefa.com/news/2019/oct/07/england-u21s-squad-update-sam-surridge-ben-wilmot-071019 |title=Sam Surridge, Ben Wilmot, and Conor Gallagher called into England U21s squad |publisher=The Football Association |date=8 October 2019 |access-date=8 October 2019}}</ref> and made his debut on 11 October as a substitute during a 2–2 draw against [[Slovenia national under-21 football team|Slovenia]] in Maribor.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thefa.com/news/2019/oct/11/england-mu21s-slovenia-report-111019 |title=Goals from Eddie Nketiah and Sam Surridge cancelled out as Young Lions tie in Maribor |publisher=The Football Association |date=11 October 2019 |access-date=12 October 2019}}</ref>

On 14 November 2021, he received his first call-up to the England senior squad.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/59278354 |title=Gallagher called up as five miss out |website=BBC Sport |date=14 November 2021}}</ref> The following day, he earned his first cap, coming on as a half-time substitute in England's 10–0 win over [[San Marino national football team|San Marino]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.cpfc.co.uk/news/first-team/crystal-palace-midfielder-conor-gallagher-makes-england-debut-v-san-marino/2021-11-15/ |title=Conor Gallagher makes senior England debut in San Marino romp |publisher=Crystal Palace F.C. |date=15 November 2021}}</ref>

He was named in England's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63585869 |title=Maddison, Rashford and Wilson make England squad |website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Gallagher has said his "best position is as a box-to-box midfielder [...] I can play deeper and I can play attacking as well",<ref name="auto2"/> and was described by [[Lee Bowyer]], his manager at Charlton at the time, as "an all-round midfielder. His work-rate is unreal, he puts his foot in for tackles and he can also see a pass".<ref name="auto3"/> [[Patrick Vieira]] likened Gallagher's style of play to that of former players [[Ray Parlour]] and [[Frank Lampard]],<ref>{{cite news |title=Conor Gallagher 'like Parlour and Lampard' says Patrick Vieira as Chelsea loanee shines at Crystal Palace |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/conor-gallagher-crystal-palace-chelsea-fc-patrick-vieira-ray-parlour-frank-lampard-b972049.html |access-date=15 December 2021 |newspaper=London Evening Standard |date=15 December 2021}}</ref> who was Gallagher's idol while he was growing up.<ref name="auto3"/>
