'''Bruno Miguel Borges Fernandes''' ({{IPA-pt|ˈbɾunu fɨɾˈnɐ̃dɨʃ|eu}};<ref>{{Cite web |last=Burns |first=Euan |date=2021-09-30 |title='We ought to try more': how to pronounce footballers' names |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/the-set-pieces-blog/2021/sep/30/how-pronounce-footballers-names-bruno-fernandes-gianluigi-buffon-wojciech-szczesny |access-date=2023-05-11 |website=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref> born 8 September 1994) is a Portuguese professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as an [[attacking midfielder]] for {{English football updater|ManchesU}} club [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] and the [[Portugal national football team|Portugal national team]]. Considered one of the best midfielders in the world, he is known for his passing, [[Playmaker|playmaking]], [[Penalty kick (association football)|penalty]] taking and work rate.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-04-19 |title=Top 16 Best attacking midfielders in the world 2023 - Interesting Football |url=https://interestingfootball.com/best-attacking-midfielders-in-the-world/ |access-date=2023-06-17 |language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-11-13 |title=The 25 best attacking midfielders in world football - ranked |url=https://www.90min.com/posts/the-25-best-attacking-midfielders-in-world-football-ranked |access-date=2023-06-17 |website=90min.com |language=en-GB}}</ref><ref name=PlayStyle>{{Cite web|url=https://sportitnow.com/bruno-fernandes-career-highlights-style-man-utd/|title=Bruno Fernandes – A look at the career and style of play of Manchester United's Portuguese Magnifico|first=Pranav|last=Unnikrishnan|date=7 September 2021|website=Sport It Now|accessdate=11 November 2021}}</ref> 

Born in [[Maia, Portugal|Maia]], [[Metropolitan Area of Porto|Porto]], Fernandes started his career at Italian [[Serie B]] side [[Novara Calcio|Novara]] but soon made a move to [[Serie A]] side [[Udinese Calcio|Udinese]] in 2013, followed by [[U.C. Sampdoria|Sampdoria]] three years later. After five years in Italy, he signed with [[Sporting CP]] in 2017, where he was named club captain. He won back-to-back [[Taça da Liga|Taças da Liga]] in [[2017–18 Taça da Liga|2018]] and [[2018–19 Taça da Liga|2019]], as well as the [[Taça de Portugal]], leading to him being named in the Primeira Liga Team of the Year and [[LPFP Primeira Liga Player of the Year|Primeira Liga Player of the Year]] in both seasons. In [[2018–19 Sporting CP season|2018–19]], he scored a record of 33 goals in all competitions, making him the highest-scoring Portuguese midfielder and the highest-scoring midfielder in Europe in a single season. In January 2020, Fernandes signed with Manchester United for an initial €55&nbsp;million (£47&nbsp;million), becoming at the time, the second-highest fee for a Portuguese player leaving the domestic league.

Fernandes is a former [[Portugal national youth football team|Portugal youth international]], representing his country at [[Portugal national under-19 football team|under-19]], [[Portugal national under-20 football team|under-20]], [[Portugal national under-21 football team|under-21]] and [[Portugal national under-23 football team|under-23]] levels. He represented Portugal at the [[Football at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|2016 Summer Olympics]]. He made his senior international debut in 2017, being chosen in Portugal's squads for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]], [[2019 UEFA Nations League Finals]], [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], winning the 2019 competition on home soil, while being named in the [[2019 UEFA Nations League Finals#Awards|Team of the Tournament]].

==Early life==
Born in [[Maia, Portugal|Maia]], [[Metropolitan Area of Porto]], Fernandes supported [[Boavista F.C.|Boavista]], and [[Manchester United]]. During his childhood, Fernandes used to play football on the streets with his older brother Ricardo, who was also a professional football player.<ref name=Bruno8>{{cite video|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8rgk-YGraUc&t=16s&ab_channel=SoccerStories-OhMyGoal|title=8 things you didn't know about Bruno Fernandes|author=Oh My Goal|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=12 December 2020}}</ref> His father, who had emigrated to Switzerland, wanted Fernandes to move with him, but he refused, due to the standard of [[football in Switzerland]].<ref name=BrunoEarly>{{cite video|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WJ8N6xAUgJY&t=117s&ab_channel=Goal90|title=10 Things you didn't know about Bruno Fernandes|author=Goal 90|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=24 August 2021}}</ref><ref name=Bruno99>{{cite video|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmMDiJOXQsM&t=88s&ab_channel=SoccerStories-OhMyGoal|title=99 facts that make Bruno Fernandes so special|author=Oh My Goal|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=1 June 2021}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Early career===
Fernandes began his youth career at local club [[F.C. Infesta|Infesta]]. Growing up, Fernandes was offered a youth contract at [[FC Porto|Porto]]'s academy, but he went to local rivals [[Boavista F.C.|Boavista]], who offered him transportation to training, with his parents being unable to take him to [[Porto]] on daily basis.<ref name=BrunoEarly/> Fernandes played most of his youth football with Boavista, starting his career as a [[centre-back]], before being converted to an [[attacking midfielder]] at the age of 15, following a loan to Pasteleira.<ref name=BrunoEarly/><ref name=Bruno99/>

Overlooked for selection by [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]] during his spell in Italy, Fernandes was first chosen on 28 August 2017, replacing the injured [[Pizzi (Portuguese footballer)|Pizzi]] for [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)|World Cup qualifiers]] against the [[Faroe Islands national football team|Faroe Islands]] and [[Hungary national football team|Hungary]] to be played the next month, although he did not play in either match.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://rr.sapo.pt/noticia/92067/bruno_fernandes_chamado_para_o_lugar_de_pizzi |title=Bruno Fernandes chamado para o lugar de Pizzi|trans-title=Bruno Fernandes called in place of Pizzi |publisher=Rádio Renascença |language=pt |date=28 August 2017 |access-date=12 September 2017}}</ref> Fernandes won his first full [[Cap (sport)|cap]] on 10 November 2017, replacing [[Manuel Fernandes (footballer, born 1986)|Manuel Fernandes]] for the last 34 minutes of the 3–0 [[Friendly (association football)|friendly]] win over [[Saudi Arabia national football team|Saudi Arabia]] in [[Viseu]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://desporto.sapo.pt/futebol/amigaveis-selecoes/artigos/portugal-vence-arabia-saudita-com-golos-de-manuel-fernandes-goncalo-guedes-e-joao-mario |title=Portugal vence Arábia Saudita com golos de Manuel Fernandes, Gonçalo Guedes e João Mário |trans-title=Portugal beat Saudi Arabia with goals from Manuel Fernandes, Gonçalo Guedes and João Mário |website=SAPO Desporto |language=pt |date=10 November 2017 |access-date=11 November 2017}}</ref> 

He was then selected by [[Fernando Santos (Portuguese footballer)|Fernando Santos]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.sbs.com.au/news/portugal-world-cup-squad |title=Nearly half Portugal's Euro squad to miss World Cup |publisher=Special Broadcasting Service |date=17 May 2018 |access-date=18 May 2018}}</ref> He scored his first goal on 7 June with a header in the last warm-up match before the tournament, a 3–0 defeat of [[Algeria national football team|Algeria]] at the [[Estádio da Luz]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44373997 |title=Portugal 3–0 Algeria |website=BBC Sport |date=7 June 2018 |access-date=8 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web profissional"|trans-title=Bruno Fernandes: «It was my first goal with the head as a professional» |website=Maisfutebol |publisher=IOL |language=pt |date=7 June 2018 |access-date=8 June 2018}}</ref> Fernandes' first World Cup game took place on 15 June 2018, when he played 66 minutes in a [[2018 FIFA World Cup Group B#Portugal vs Spain|3–3 group stage]] draw against [[Spain national football team|Spain]], being [[Penalty card#Yellow card|booked]] in the process.<ref>{{cite news === 2019–2021: Nations League title and subsequent struggles ===
Fernandes was selected for three matches in the league phase of the [[2018–19 UEFA Nations League]] group stage,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefanationsleague/season=2019/teams/player=250059115/statistics/ |title=Bernardo Silva |work=UEFA |access-date=26 February 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefanationsleague/season=2019/matches/round=2000959/match=2024021/index.html?iv=true&iv=true |title=Poland vs Portugal |work=UEFA |access-date=26 February 2019}}</ref> helping the hosts Portugal qualify to [[2019 UEFA Nations League Finals|the inaugural Nations League Finals]] in June 2019. In the [[2019 UEFA Nations League Finals|UEFA Nations League Finals]], Fernandes played both matches as Portugal defeated the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]] 1–0 in [[Porto]] to win the trophy.<ref>{{cite news |first=Daniel |last=Taylor |author-link=Daniel Taylor (journalist) |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2019/jun/09/portugal-netherlands-nations-league-final-match-report |title=Portugal win Nations League as Gonçalo Guedes does for the Netherlands |website=[[The Guardian]] |location=Estádio do Dragão |date=9 June 2019 |access-date=2 February 2020}}</ref> For his performances throughout the competition, he was named in the "Team of the Tournament".<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefanationsleague/news/newsid=2607717.html |title=UEFA Nations League Finals: Team of the Tournament |publisher=UEFA |date=10 June 2019 |access-date=10 June 2019}}</ref>

Fernandes was named in Portugal's final squad for the delayed [[UEFA Euro 2020]] tournament, appearing in all games of the eventual round of 16-exit.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pt.uefa.com/uefaeuro-2020/news/0269-1252942bc164-e2eac2467985-1000--convocados-de-portugal-para-o-euro-2020/|title=Convocados de Portugal para o EURO 2020|date=20 May 2021|website=UEFA.com|accessdate=11 November 2021}}</ref> Fernandes went on to be criticized for his performances during the tournament, as he struggled to replicate his club form for his country, being heavily fatigued and overshadowed by other teammates, he went to be a first-team player to a substitute, by the end of Portugal's campaign in tournament.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/football/news/bruno-fernandes-man-utd-portugal-24410128|title=Roy Keane gives verdict on Fernandes as Man Utd star dropped by Portugal again|first=Gareth|last=Bicknell|date=28 June 2021|newspaper=[[Daily Mirror]]|accessdate=11 November 2021}}</ref>

==Player profile==
===Style of play===
An [[attacking midfielder]], Fernandes is a direct, energetic [[box-to-box]] [[playmaker]] who is capable of both taking on defenders and setting the rhythm of play.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Bruno Fernandes age, position, Man Utd shirt number, stats, style|url=https://www.manutd.com/en/news/detail/facts-on-man-utd-new-signing-in-transfer-window-bruno-fernandes|access-date=10 July 2020|website=ManUtd.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|first=Michael |last=Cox |author-link=Michael Cox (journalist) |title=Bruno Fernandes: the man who is at his most dangerous when he stands still |url=https://theathletic.com/1669101/2020/03/14/bruno-fernandes-manchester-united-man-utd-tactics-goal-cox/ |url-access=subscription |date=13 March 2020 |work=The Athletic |access-date=29 July 2020}}</ref> Directness also means that Fernandes tends to take a lot of risks with the type and level of passes he attempts to complete. However, despite being prone to turnovers, he typically presses the opposition right after losing possession.<ref>{{cite web|first1=Laurie |last1=Whitwell |first2=Tom |last2=Worville |title=Bruno's passes are risky, but the rewards are well worth it |url=https://theathletic.com/1956276/2020/07/29/bruno-fernandes-manchester-united-passing-risk-reward/ |url-access=subscription |date=29 July 2020 |work=The Athletic |access-date=29 July 2020}}</ref> Fernandes boasts a prolific goalscoring record from the midfield position. He frequently shoots from outside the penalty area and is an accurate [[Free kick (association football)|free-kick]]<ref name=":0" /> and [[Penalty kick (association football)|penalty]] taker.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/bruno-fernandes-penalty-secret-technique-man-utd-stars-spot-kick-/x98oqu53kt4j1r6lwbnr59get |title=Does Bruno Fernandes have a penalty secret? Technique behind Man Utd star's spot-kick success |website=goal.com |last1=Kelly |first1=Ryan |date=22 November 2020 |access-date=1 June 2021 }}</ref> His movement off the ball is also key, with him often roaming around the attacking areas of the pitch, or dropping deep to pick up possession.<ref name=":0" /> As well as operating in the attacking midfielder role, he can also operate in a [[Holding midfielder|deeper]] [[central midfield]] role, or as a [[second striker]].<ref name=":0" />

Regarding his goalscoring ability, former Manchester United defender [[Rio Ferdinand]] commented that Fernandes "is player who knows how to create chances [...], who can for bit of imagination, creativity, the patience around the box, the ability to hit a ball from the edge of the box an put in the back of net, [...] manipulate and take ball".<ref>{{cite video|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pfsNilUXE_Q&ab_channel=RioFerdinandPresentsFIVE|title=Bruno Fernandes Finally Joins Manchester United|author=Rio Ferdinand present FIVE|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=30 January 2020}}</ref> Ferdinand also praised his leadership abilities, stating "he was one of the players in the game that lead's by example".<ref>{{cite video|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uJrzK4bQFos&ab_channel=ManUtdForever|title=Rio Ferdinand praises Bruno Fernandes |author=Man Utd Forever|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=11 September 2020}}</ref> Former Manchester United coach [[Ole Gunnar Solskjær]] stated that Fernandes was a "bit of a mix between [[Paul Scholes]] and [[Juan Sebastián Verón]]".<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11667/11941700/bruno-fernandes-a-mix-of-paul-scholes-and-juan-sebastian-veron-says-ole-gunnar-solskjaer|title=Bruno Fernandes a mix of Paul Scholes and Juan Sebastián Verón, says Ole Gunnar Solskjaer|date=24 February 2020 |access-date=24 February 2020|publisher=Sky Sports}}</ref> Fernandes goalscoring abilities have also drew comparisons with former Chelsea midfielder [[Frank Lampard]], after breaking his record of being the highest-scoring midfielder in Europe in a single season.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.planetfootball.com/quick-reads/comparing-bruno-fernandes-2020-21-with-frank-lampard-and-steven-gerrards-best-seasons/|title=Comparing Bruno Fernandes' 2020-21 with Lampard & Gerrard's best seasons|date=22 May 2021 |access-date=22 May 2021|publisher=Planet Football}}</ref>

===Goal celebration===
Fernandes has adopted a particular [[Goal celebration|goalscoring celebrations]] throughout his career: after scoring a goal, he usually celebrates by covering his ears with both of his hands, as a tribute to his daughter Matilde, who covered her ears pretending not to hear him.<ref name=BrunoEarly/><ref name=Bruno99/>

==Sponsorship==
Fernandes is sponsored by sportswear company [[Nike, Inc.|Nike]]. He wears [[Nike Mercurial Vapor]] [[Football boots|boots]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Bruno Fernandes 2021 – Net Worth, Salary and Endorsements |url=https://weallfollowunited.com/2021/08/26/bruno-fernandes-net-worth/|website=We All Follow United |date=26 August 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
When Fernandes moved to Italy at age 18 to join Novara, he was joined there by childhood sweetheart Ana Pinho; they married in 2015.<ref name=BrunoEarly/> The couple have a daughter named Matilde (born 30 January 2017) and a son named Gonçalo (born 6 September 2020).<ref name=BrunoEarly/> He has tattoos on both of his arms with the number 8, in which he tributes to his birth date and to his father, who was also a footballer and wore the number 8 shirt, during his playing career, the letter F symbolising his surname and the number 23 as a tribute to his debut with the [[Portugal national football team]], when he was aged 23.<ref name=BrunoEarly/><ref name=Bruno99/>

Fernandes is multilingual, apart from his native Portuguese, he is fluent in Spanish, English, and Italian. He also speaks some French.<ref name=Bruno99/>
