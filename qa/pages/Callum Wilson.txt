'''Callum Eddie Graham Wilson''' (born 27 February 1992) is an English professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Striker (association football)|striker]] for [[Premier League]] club [[Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United]] and the [[England national football team|England national team]].

==Early life==
Wilson was born in [[Coventry]], West Midlands.<ref>{{Hugman|25769|access-date=15 November 2018}}</ref> He is of Irish<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.balls.ie/football/13-players-eligible-for-ireland-461530|title=13 Big Name Players That Were Eligible To Play For Ireland|first=Gary|last=Connaughton|website=Balls.ie}}</ref> and Jamaican descent.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/international-football/2019/03/25/5c98afe3ca4741c80c8b463d.html |title=The origins of England: 14 of current squad are children of migrants |first=Jon |last=Prada |newspaper=Marca |location=Madrid |date=25 March 2019 |access-date=13 September 2020}}</ref> He attended [[President Kennedy School]] in Coventry.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.pks.coventry.sch.uk/news/?pid=36&nid=5&storyid=111 |title=A Visit From Ex-Pupil Callum Wilson and The FA Cup |publisher=President Kennedy School |access-date=13 September 2020}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Coventry City===
Wilson started his career with [[Coventry City F.C.|Coventry City]]'s academy.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.thefa.com/england/mens-seniors/stories/growing-up-callum-wilson |title=Growing Up: Callum Wilson |publisher=The Football Association |access-date=13 September 2020}}</ref> He made his first-team debut on 12 August 2009 as a [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] in a 1–0 [[EFL Cup|League Cup]] defeat to [[Hartlepool United F.C.|Hartlepool United]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/league_cup/8190167.stm |title=Coventry 0–1 Hartlepool |website=BBC Sport |date=12 August 2009 |access-date=13 August 2009}}</ref> He signed a professional deal, which saw him stay at the club for a further season, on 16 March 2010. Wilson became the first Coventry City youth teamer to win the national award for apprentice of the month in March 2010.{{citation needed|date=June 2011}}

Wilson made his league debut when he came on as a substitute against [[Queens Park Rangers F.C.|Queens Park Rangers]] in December 2010.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://nonleague.pitchero.com/uncategorized/lambs-borrow-young-coventry-striker-9463/ |title=Lambs borrow young Coventry striker |website=Pitchero Non-League |date=29 December 2011 |access-date=15 November 2018}}</ref> He made his first appearance of the 2012–13 season in a 1–0 loss against [[Carlisle United F.C.|Carlisle United]] coming on as a substitute for [[Carl Baker]].{{citation needed|date=November 2018}}

The start of the 2013–14 season saw Wilson establish himself in the starting line-up. Forming strike partnership [[Leon Clarke]], after just 11 league games Wilson had scored 10 goals, topping the League One scoring charts. He finished the season as League One's third-top scorer with 22 goals and also earned himself a place in the League One [[PFA Team of the Year]], despite having spent two months out with a dislocated shoulder in early 2014. Wilson won League One Player of the month award for March, and also won his club's Top Goalscorer award, Player's Player award voted for by his teammates and the Player of the season award voted for by Coventry City fans.{{citation needed|date=November 2018}}

====Loans====
Wilson joined [[Conference Premier]] club [[Kettering Town F.C.|Kettering Town]] on an initial one-month loan at the turn of the year in 2011.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thesouthend.co.uk/blog/a-web-stalkers-guide-to-callum-wilson/ |title=A web stalkers guide to Callum Wilson |website=Tales From The South End |date=5 July 2014 |access-date=15 November 2018}}</ref> His loan was extended to another three months upon completion of his first month. Wilson played 17 games, scoring once.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.coventrytelegraph.net/sport/football/football-news/gossip-callum-wilson-sad-leave-3045243 |title=Gossip: Callum Wilson sad to leave Kettering Town |website=Coventry Live |publisher=Reach |date=6 April 2011 |access-date=15 November 2018}}</ref>

On 29 December 2011, he was loaned to Conference Premier club [[Tamworth F.C.|Tamworth]] on a one-month loan.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/16355868 |title=Tamworth sign Callum Wilson on loan from Coventry City |website=BBC Sport |date=29 December 2011 |access-date=15 November 2018}}</ref> He made his debut for the club in a 2–2 draw with [[Alfreton Town F.C.|Alfreton Town]], where he played the full game.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_conf/16328502.stm |title=Tamworth 2–2 Alfreton Town |website=BBC Sport |date=1 January 2012 |access-date=15 November 2018}}</ref> He scored in just his second game for the club with a curling shot in a 2–1 defeat against [[Wrexham A.F.C.|Wrexham]] on 14 January. Wilson suffered a fractured foot in his third appearance for the Lambs which saw his short loan spell end.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_conf/16453252.stm |title=Tamworth 1–2 Wrexham |website=BBC Sport |date=14 January 2012 |access-date=14 January 2012}}</ref>

===AFC Bournemouth===
On 4 July 2014, Wilson signed for [[AFC Bournemouth]] for an undisclosed fee, believed to be in the region of £3&nbsp;million, after spending five years at Coventry.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.coventryobserver.co.uk/2014/08/06/sport-Callum-Wilson-thanks-Sky-Blues-after-signing-for-Bournemouth-110878.html |title=Callum Wilson thanks Sky Blues after signing for Bournemouth |first=Steve |last=Carpenter |newspaper=Coventry Observer |date=7 July 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141018145654/http://www.coventryobserver.co.uk/2014/08/06/sport-Callum-Wilson-thanks-Sky-Blues-after-signing-for-Bournemouth-110878.html |archive-date=18 October 2014}}</ref> He scored twice on his debut for the club, in a 4–0 win against [[Huddersfield Town A.F.C.|Huddersfield Town]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/28646430 |title=Huddersfield Town 0–4 AFC Bournemouth |first=Michael |last=Emons |website=BBC Sport |date=9 August 2014 |access-date=15 November 2018}}</ref>

In his first season at the club, Wilson helped them achieve promotion to the [[Premier League]] for the first time in the club's history, becoming the team's top scorer for that season in the process, scoring 20 league goals.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Callum Wilson {{!}} Football Stats {{!}} Newcastle United {{!}} Season 2014/2015 {{!}} Soccer Base|url=https://www.soccerbase.com/players/player.sd?player_id=51997&season_id=144|access-date=10 February 2021|website=www.soccerbase.com}}</ref> He was Bournemouth's only player to score in all three domestic competitions,{{citation needed|date=November 2018}} scoring the winning goal in a 2–1 victory against [[West Bromwich Albion F.C.|West Bromwich Albion]] in the fourth round of the League Cup.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/29699778 |title=AFC Bournemouth 2–1 West Bromwich Albion |website=BBC Sport |date=28 October 2014 |access-date=15 November 2018}}</ref>

On 22 August 2015, Wilson scored his first Premier League goals with a [[Hat-trick#Association football|hat-trick]] against [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/33944756 |title=West Ham United 3–4 AFC Bournemouth |first=Emlyn |last=Begley |website=BBC Sport |date=22 August 2015 |access-date=15 November 2018}}</ref> On 26 September 2015, he [[Anterior cruciate ligament injury|ruptured the anterior cruciate ligament]] (ACL) in his right knee in the match against [[Stoke City F.C.|Stoke City]] and was expected to be out for about six months.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/34382108 |title=Callum Wilson: Bournemouth striker out for six months |website=BBC Sport |date=29 September 2015 |access-date=15 November 2018}}</ref> Wilson made his return on 9 April 2016 as an injury-time substitute in Bournemouth's 2–1 win away to [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/35949318 |title=Aston Villa 1–2 AFC Bournemouth |first=Neil |last=Johnston |website=BBC Sport |date=9 April 2016 |access-date=15 November 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/36014372 |title=Callum Wilson: Bournemouth striker thrilled to return from long-term injury |website=BBC Sport |date=11 April 2016 |access-date=15 November 2018}}</ref> He ruptured the anterior cruciate ligament in his left knee on 1 February 2017 and was expected to be out for about six months.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/38843805 |title=Callum Wilson: Bournemouth striker ruled out for season with knee injury |website=BBC Sport |date=2 February 2017 |access-date=15 November 2018}}</ref>

===Newcastle United===
[[File:Callum Wilson 2021-08-07 1.jpg|thumb|upright|Wilson playing for [[Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United]] in 2021]]

Wilson signed for Premier League club [[Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United]] on 7 September 2020 on a four-year contract for an undisclosed fee,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nufc.co.uk/news/latest-news/newcastle-united-sign-callum-wilson/ |title=Newcastle United sign Callum Wilson |publisher=Newcastle United F.C. |date=7 September 2020 |access-date=13 September 2020}}</ref> reported by [[BBC Sport]] to be around £20&nbsp;million,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54055640 |title=Newcastle sign former Bournemouth pair Callum Wilson and Ryan Fraser |website=BBC Sport |date=7 September 2020 |access-date=13 September 2020}}</ref> making him the third-highest fee paid by Newcastle for a player.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Callum Wilson: Newcastle sign Bournemouth striker for £20m|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11678/12065301/callum-wilson-newcastle-sign-bournemouth-striker-for-20m|access-date=30 January 2021|website=Sky Sports}}</ref> He made his debut on 12 September, scoring the first goal in a 2–0 away win against [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54038554 |title=West Ham United 0–2 Newcastle United |first=Steve |last=Sutcliffe |website=BBC Sport |date=13 September 2012 |access-date=13 September 2020}}</ref> On 30 January 2021, Wilson scored his 50th Premier League goal in a 2–0 victory over [[Everton F.C.|Everton]].<ref>{{Cite news|title='Braver' Newcastle see off Everton|website=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/55769149|access-date=30 January 2021}}</ref> Wilson finished the 2022–23 season as Newcastle's top scorer with 18 goals.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Newcastle United Top Scorers |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/teams/newcastle-united/top-scorers |access-date=2023-06-15 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

==International career==
Wilson was called up to the [[England national under-21 football team|England national under-21 team]] for the first time in November 2014 for [[Exhibition game#Association football|friendly]] matches against [[Portugal national under-21 football team|Portugal]] and [[France national under-21 football team|France]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thefa.com/news/2014/nov/06/squad-named-for-portugal-france-061114 |title=England Under-21s squad named for double header |first=Nicholas |last=Veevers |publisher=The Football Association |date=6 November 2014 |access-date=15 November 2018}}</ref> He made his only appearance on 17 November in the match against France,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.englandfootballonline.com/MatchRsl/MatchRslTmU21pg4.html |title=England's matches: The under 21's: 2010–20 |website=England Football Online |date=11 October 2019 |access-date=14 October 2019}}</ref> as a 65th-minute substitute in a 3–2 away defeat.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/nov/17/france-under-21-england-under-21-friendly-match-report |title=France's Yaya Sanogo sinks England Under-21s after Harry Kane double |newspaper=The Guardian |location=London |agency=Press Association |date=17 November 2014 |access-date=15 November 2018}}</ref>

Wilson was called up to the [[England national football team|England senior team]] for the first time in November 2018 for a friendly match against the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]] and a [[UEFA Nations League]] match against [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/46142179 |title=Callum Wilson & Wayne Rooney called up by England for USA game |website=BBC Sport |date=8 November 2018 |access-date=15 November 2018}}</ref> He made his debut on 15 November when starting against the United States at [[Wembley Stadium]], and scored in the 77th minute of a 3–0 win with a near-post finish.<ref name=USA>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45174412 |title=England 3–0 USA |first=Phil |last=McNulty |website=BBC Sport |date=15 November 2018 |access-date=15 November 2018}}</ref> In doing so, he became the first Bournemouth player to score for England.<ref name=USA/>

After a three-year absence, he was named in England's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63585869|title=Maddison, Rashford and Wilson make England squad|work=BBC Sport }}</ref> Wilson came on as a 76th minute substitute in England's opening match against [[Iran national football team|Iran]], assisting [[Jack Grealish]] for England's sixth goal in a 6–2 victory.

After ending the 2022/23 season with 18 Premier League goals for Newcastle, Wilson was again recalled to the England squad for Euro 2024 qualifiers against Malta and North Macedonia. Wilson added his second international goal against Malta, scoring from the penalty spot in a 4-0 victory. <ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63603382|title=England 6-2 Iran: Three Lions Win World Cup Opener|date=10 November 2022|work=BBC Sport}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Wilson has a wife, Stacey,<ref>{{cite news |last1=Steinberg |first1=Jacob |title=Bournemouth's Callum Wilson speeds up scoring after slowing down eating |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2015/sep/25/bournemouth-callum-wilson-premier-league-joint-top-scorer |access-date=15 January 2021 |newspaper=The Guardian |location=London |date=25 September 2015}}</ref> and two children.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bournemouthecho.co.uk/sport/15670905.comeback-king-callum-wilson-revealed-his-son-thought-he-didnt-play-football-anymore/ |title=Comeback king Callum Wilson revealed his son thought he 'didn't play football anymore' |last=Rose |first=Dan |date=19 November 2017 |work=Bournemouth Echo |access-date=4 October 2020}}</ref>
