'''Anthony Taylor''' (born 20 October 1978) is an English professional [[Association football|football]] [[Referee (association football)|referee]] from [[Wythenshawe]], [[Manchester]]. In 2010, he was promoted to the list of [[Select Group Referees]] who officiate primarily in the [[Premier League]], and in 2013 became a listed referee for [[FIFA]] allowing him to referee European and international matches. In 2015, he officiated the [[2015 Football League Cup Final|Football League Cup final]] at [[Wembley Stadium]] when [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] defeated [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] 2–0. Taylor returned to Wembley later that year to officiate the [[2015 FA Community Shield|Community Shield]] as [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] beat Chelsea 1–0. He refereed the [[2017 FA Cup Final|2017]] and [[2020 FA Cup Final|2020]] FA Cup finals, both between Chelsea and Arsenal; Arsenal won on both occasions 2–1. Upon the selection, he became the first man to referee a second FA Cup final since [[Arthur Kingscott]] in [[1901 FA Cup Final|1901]].<ref>{{cite web |title=FA Cup Final: Anthony Taylor to referee second final |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/53504001 |work=BBC Sport |access-date=2 August 2020 |date=22 July 2020}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Taylor was a student at [[Altrincham Grammar School for Boys]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=Annual Dinner 2017 |pages=6 |work=OLD ALTRINCHAMIANS’ Newsletter 2016 - 2017 |url=https://oldalts.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Old-Alts-Newsletter-2017-web.pdf |access-date=1 June 2023}}</ref>

==Career==

===Early career===
Taylor started refereeing in the [[Northern Premier League]] in 2002,<ref>[http://www.soccerfactsuk.co.uk/s2001/reffixtures.php?refid=260 Northern Premier League], 2002: ''SoccerFactsUK'' website.</ref> progressing to officiate in the [[Conference North]] in 2004.<ref>[http://www.soccerfactsuk.co.uk/s2004/reffixtures.php?refid=260 Conference North] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120318173035/http://www.soccerfactsuk.co.uk/s2004/reffixtures.php?refid=260 |date=18 March 2012 }}, 2004: ''SoccerFactsUK'' website.</ref> He was appointed to [[the Football League]] referees' list at the start of the [[2006-07 in English football|2006–07 season]] and his first appointment was a 0–0 draw between [[Wrexham A.F.C.|Wrexham]] and [[Peterborough United F.C.|Peterborough United]] in a [[Football League Two|League Two]] match in August 2006.<ref>[http://www.soccerbase.com/results3.sd?gameid=492507 Wrexham v. Peterborough], first League match: [[soccerbase]].com website.</ref>

In November 2006 he refereed an [[England national under-19 football team|England under-19s]] international [[Exhibition game|friendly match]] against Switzerland under-19s at [[Alexandra Stadium|Gresty Road]], the home of [[Crewe Alexandra F.C.|Crewe Alexandra]]; England won 3–2.<ref>[https://archive.today/20071029052657/http://www.thefa.com/England/U19s/NewsAndFeatures/Postings/2006/11/EnglandSwiss.htm England v. Switzerland], Under-19 friendly: [[The Football Association|TheFA.com]] official website.</ref>

===Professional career===
Taylor's first [[Premier League]] appointment was a February 2010 encounter between [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]] and [[Portsmouth F.C.|Portsmouth]], which Fulham won 1–0. He refereed one more game in the top-flight during that season before being promoted to the League's list of [[Select Group Referees|Select Group of Referees]] for 2010–11.

In September 2010 he took charge of his fourth Premier League match, contested by [[Blackburn Rovers F.C.|Blackburn Rovers]] and Fulham. The match ended 1–1.<ref>[http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_prem/9012587.stm BBC Sport - Football], 2010: ''Bosses Allardyce and Hughes round on ref Anthony Taylor'' report.</ref>

Taylor sent off three players in his first game of the [[2011-12 in English football|2011–12 season]]. [[Middlesbrough F.C.|Middlesbrough]] won 1–0 at [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]] in a fixture which saw [[Jonny Howson]] and [[Max Gradel]] of Leeds and Boro's [[Tony McMahon]] dismissed, all for second bookable offences.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Hart |first1=Simon |title=Dark clouds gathering as nine-man Leeds fall to Emnes winner |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/football-league/dark-clouds-gathering-as-nine-man-leeds-fall-to-emnes-winner-2337375.html |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/archive/20220507/https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/football-league/dark-clouds-gathering-as-nine-man-leeds-fall-to-emnes-winner-2337375.html |archive-date=7 May 2022 |url-access=subscription |url-status=live |access-date=1 September 2020 |work=The Independent |date=14 August 2011}}{{cbignore}}</ref> Taylor refereed a total of 34 matches that season and dismissed eight players in total, including the three at Leeds.

Taylor became a [[FIFA International Referees List|FIFA listed]] referee on 1 January 2013, making him eligible to officiate [[UEFA]] European and FIFA international competitive games. In May 2013 he was fourth official to [[Andre Marriner]] for the [[2013 FA Cup Final|FA Cup Final]].

On the opening day of the [[2013–14 Premier League|2013–14 season]] Taylor refereed [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]]'s 3–1 victory away at Arsenal. He awarded Villa two penalties and dismissed Arsenal's [[Laurent Koscielny]] for two bookable offences. Arsène Wenger described Taylor's officiating of the match as "stubborn"<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://talksport.com/football/%E2%80%98stubborn%E2%80%99-referee-infuriates-arsenal-boss-wenger-13081756045 |title=‘Stubborn’ referee infuriates Arsenal boss Wenger {{!}} talkSPORT<!-- Bot generated title --> |access-date=17 August 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130820060530/http://talksport.com/football/%E2%80%98stubborn%E2%80%99-referee-infuriates-arsenal-boss-wenger-13081756045 |archive-date=20 August 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> but Villa manager [[Paul Lambert]] said that he thought the official "had a good game".<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/23644714 BBC Sport - Arsenal 1-3 Aston Villa<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

On 1 March 2015, Taylor was the referee for the [[2015 Football League Cup Final|Football League Cup final]] between Chelsea and [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.premierleague.com/referees/16961/Anthony-Taylor/overview|title = Anthony Taylor Referee Profile &#124; Premier League}}</ref>

In 2015, Taylor refereed both the [[2015 FA Community Shield]] and the [[2015 Football League Cup Final|2015 Football League Cup final]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thefa.com/news/competitions/fa-community-shield/2015/jul/anthony-taylor-community-shield-referee-2015 |title=Anthony Taylor appointed FA Community Shield referee |website=thefa.com |date=7 July 2015 |access-date=7 July 2015 |first=Jamie |last=Bradbury}}</ref><ref name="referee">{{cite news|title=Anthony Taylor to referee Capital One Cup Final |url=http://www.capitalonecup.co.uk/news/article/anthony-taylor-to-referee-capital-one-cup-final-2266463.aspx |website=capitalonecup.co.uk |publisher=The Football League |date=12 February 2015 |access-date=7 July 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150221012201/http://www.capitalonecup.co.uk/news/article/anthony-taylor-to-referee-capital-one-cup-final-2266463.aspx |archive-date=21 February 2015}}</ref>

On 26 April 2017, Taylor was chosen to be the main referee for the [[2017 FA Cup Final|2017 FA Cup final]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://metro.co.uk/2017/04/26/arsenal-fans-congratulate-chelsea-on-fa-cup-victory-after-anthony-taylor-is-appointed-referee-for-the-final-6599032/|title=Arsenal fans hand Chelsea the FA Cup after referee for the final is named|date=26 April 2017|website=Metro|access-date=27 May 2017}}</ref>

On 26 May 2018 he was chosen to officiate the [[2018 EFL Championship play-off Final]].

On 16 September 2020, Taylor was chosen to be the main referee for the [[2020 UEFA Super Cup]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=Hear from referee Anthony Taylor, following his appointment for UEFA Super Cup game|url=http://www.thefa.com/news/2020/sep/16/anthony-taylor-interview-on-uefa-super-cup-appointment-160920|access-date=24 September 2020|website=www.thefa.com|language=en}}</ref>

On 12 June 2021, Taylor officiated a [[UEFA Euro 2020]] group stage match between Finland and Denmark. In the 43rd minute, Danish midfielder [[Christian Eriksen]] collapsed on the pitch and required emergency treatment on the pitch before being transferred to a local hospital and stabilised. Taylor was praised for his calm but quick reaction to the situation, signalling for medical attention within seconds.<ref>{{Cite news|title='We managed to get Christian back' – the heroes of Copenhagen|language=en-GB|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57457388|access-date=13 June 2021}}</ref> The match was resumed later in the day once it became clear Eriksen's condition had improved. Taylor's earlier training in the prison service at [[HM Prison Manchester|HMP Manchester]] was cited as helpful for the situation by mentor [[Chris Foy (referee)|Chris Foy]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Scheerhout |first1=John |title=The ref who helped save Christian Eriksen used his experience as a prison guard |url=https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/news/greater-manchester-news/praise-one-heroes-copenhagen-ref-20822208 |website=[[Manchester Evening News]] |language=en |date=15 June 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|title=Finland win first game at major finals|language=en-GB|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/51197533|access-date=13 June 2021}}</ref>

In October 2021, Taylor was chosen to referee the [[2021 UEFA Nations League Final]] between [[Spain national football team|Spain]] and [[France national football team|France]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thefa.com/news/2021/oct/08/anthony-taylor-to-referee-uefa-nations-league-final-20210810|title=English Ref Anthony Taylor and His Team to Take Charge of Spain V France in Milan}}</ref>

In a May 2022 [[FIFA]] [[wiktionary:pronouncement|pronouncement]], Taylor was listed as one of six English officials to oversee matches at that November and December's [[2022 FIFA World Cup|World Cup]].  The list also included referee [[Michael Oliver (referee)|Michael Oliver]] and four compatriot assistant referees—Simon Bennett, Gary Beswick, Stuart Burt, and Adam Nunn.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/1da4b811328add8f/original/MEDIA-Alphabetical-order-List-of-Match-Officials-FWC-2022-Qatar.pdf|title=FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022}}</ref>

In May 2023, [[UEFA]] [[wiktionary:announced|announced]]  Taylor as the referee for the [[2023 UEFA Europa League final]], with [[Gary Beswick]] and [[Adam Nunn]] as assistants, and [[Michael Oliver (referee)|Michael Oliver]] as fourth official. <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/insideuefa/news/0281-1812baf4488c-70b9f3974d92-1000--referee-teams-appointed-for-2023-uefa-club-competition-f|title=Referee teams appointed for 2023 UEFA club competition finals}}</ref>
