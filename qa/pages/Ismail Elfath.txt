'''Ismail Elfath''' ({{lang-ar|اسماعيل الفتح}}; born March 3, 1982) is a [[Morocco|Moroccan]]-born [[United States|American]] [[Association football|soccer]] [[Referee (association football)|referee]] for the [[Professional Referee Organization]].  He has been a referee in [[Major League Soccer]] since 2012 and a [[FIFA]] listed referee since 2016.

== Early life ==
Elfath immigrated to the United States in 2001 at the age of 18 after winning a diversity visa lottery.<ref name="mala2016">{{Cite web |url=https://www.malanational.org/ismail-elfath-casablanca-fifa-referee-dreams-become-realities/ |title=Ismail Elfath: From Casablanca to FIFA Referee- Dreams Become Realities |date=July 18, 2016 |work=Muslim American Leadership Alliance |language=en-us |access-date=June 24, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221120073213/https://malanational.org/ismail-elfath-casablanca-fifa-referee-dreams-become-realities/ |archive-date=November 20, 2022 |url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="statesman2022">{{Cite web |url=https://www.statesman.com/story/sports/columns/2022/11/20/austins-ismail-elfath-to-be-only-u-s-referee-at-2022-world-cup/69661248007/ |title=Bohls: Whether it's World Cup or world peace, Austin's Ismail Elfath in the middle of it |date=November 20, 2022 |work=[[Austin American-Statesman]] |language=en-us |access-date=November 23, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221120153441/https://eu.statesman.com/story/sports/columns/2022/11/20/austins-ismail-elfath-to-be-only-u-s-referee-at-2022-world-cup/69661248007/ |archive-date=November 20, 2022 |url-status=live}}</ref> Elfath graduated from the [[University of Texas at Austin]] with a degree in mechanical engineering in 2006. As of 2022, Elfath resides in [[Austin, Texas]].<ref name="statesman2022" />

== Career ==
Elfath was first a Fourth official in MLS in 2011 and made his MLS refereeing debut in 2012.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://proreferees.com/roster/referees/ismail-elfath/ |title=Ismail Elfath – Professional Referee Organization |language=en-US |access-date=June 24, 2019}}</ref> Elfath became a FIFA listed official in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.fifa.com/development/education-and-technical/referees/association=usa/index.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150511192448/http://www.fifa.com/development/education-and-technical/referees/association=usa/index.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=May 11, 2015 |title=Education & Technical - Referees by Association |last=FIFA.com |website=FIFA.com |access-date=June 24, 2019}}</ref>

Elfath was the referee during an August 2016 match between [[New York Red Bulls II]] and [[Orlando City B]] where the worldwide first on-field [[Video assistant referee|Video Assistant Referee]] review took place as the system was being tested in [[United Soccer League|USL]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.uslchampionship.com/news_article/show/680495-pro-s-elfath-part-of-ever-evolving-history |title=PRO's Elfath Part of 'Ever-Evolving' History |last=nicholas.murray@uslsoccer.com |first=NICHOLAS MURRAY- |date=August 18, 2016 |website=USL Championship |access-date=June 24, 2019}}</ref>

On March 26, 2019, Elfath was selected to referee the [[2019 FIFA U-20 World Cup]] in Poland.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2019/03/three-us-soccer-referees-selected-to-officiate-at-2019-u20-world-cup |title=Three U.S. Soccer Referees Selected to Officiate at 2019 U-20 World Cup |website=www.ussoccer.com |access-date=June 24, 2019}}</ref> Elfath, along with American assistant referees Corey Parker and Kyle Atkins, was selected by FIFA to officiate the final of the tournament, which [[Ukraine national under-20 football team|Ukraine]] won 3-1 over [[South Korea national under-20 football team|South Korea]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.concacaf.com/en/article/concacaf-referees-earn-another-distinction-with-fifa-under-20-world-cup-final-assignment |title=Concacaf referees earn another distinction with FIFA Under-20 World Cup Final assignment |website=www.concacaf.com |access-date=June 24, 2019}}</ref>

On May 17, 2019, Elfath was appointed to officiate in the [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup]] in the United States following his participation in the 2019 FIFA U-20 World Cup.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.goldcup.org/en/article/match-officials-appointed-for-2019-concacaf-gold-cup |title=Match Officials Appointed for 2019 Concacaf Gold Cup |website=www.goldcup.org |access-date=June 26, 2019 |archive-date=May 17, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190517153005/https://www.goldcup.org/en/article/match-officials-appointed-for-2019-concacaf-gold-cup |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Later that year Elfath took charge of the semi-final match at the FIFA Qatar 2019 Club World Cup.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Ismail Elfath – Professional Referee Organization |url=http://proreferees.com/assignments/2019-fifa-club-world-cup-assignments/}}</ref>

Elfath was named the [[MLS Referee of the Year Award|MLS Referee of the Year]] for the first time in his career on November 18, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2020/11/19/ismail-elfath-named-mls-referee-year |title=Ismail Elfath named MLS Referee of the Year |website=www.mlssoccer.com |date=November 18, 2020 |access-date=November 19, 2020}}</ref> He received the honor for a second time two years later on October 14, 2022.<ref>[https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/ismail-elfath-named-mls-referee-of-the-year-corey-rockwell-named-mls-assistant-r "Ismail Elfath named MLS Referee of the Year, Corey Rockwell named MLS Assistant Referee of the Year," Major League Soccer (MLS), Friday, October 14, 2022.] Retrieved October 14, 2022.</ref>

In July 2021, Elfath took charge of three matches at the Tokyo [[2020 Olympics]], including a quarter -final match between host nation Japan and New Zealand.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Ismail Elfath – Professional Referee Organization |url=http://proreferees.com/assignments/2020-olympic-games/}}</ref>

Elfath was chosen as one of the two referees from [[CONCACAF]] for the [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations]] held in [[Cameroon]] from January 9 to February 6, 2022.<ref name="beninwebtv.com">{{cite web|url=https://beninwebtv.com/sport/2021/12/can-2022-la-liste-des-arbitres-retenus-par-la-caf-le-benin-absent-la-france-presente#|title=La liste des arbitres retenurs par la caf le Benin absent|date=December 14, 2021|publisher=beninwebtv.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Bohls |first1=Kirk |title=Bohls: Whether it's World Cup or world peace, Austin's Ismail Elfath in the middle of it |url=https://www.statesman.com/story/sports/columns/2022/11/20/austins-ismail-elfath-to-be-only-u-s-referee-at-2022-world-cup/69661248007/ |website=Austin American-Statesman |access-date=2 December 2022}}</ref>

In the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]], Elfath refereed two group stage games, the [[2022 FIFA World Cup#Round of 16|Round of 16]] match between [[Japan National Football Team|Japan]] and [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]], and was the fourth official for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup Final| Final]] between Argentina and France.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Gates |first1=Billy |title=Austin’s Ismail Elfath to be fourth official in 2022 FIFA World Cup final Sunday |url=https://www.kxan.com/sports-general/austin-fc/austins-ismail-elfath-to-be-fourth-official-in-2022-fifa-world-cup-final-sunday/ |access-date=22 December 2022 |agency=[[KXAN-TV]] |date=2022-12-17}}</ref>
