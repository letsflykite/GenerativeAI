'''Stéphanie Frappart''' (born 14 December 1983) is a French [[Association football|football]] [[Referee (association football)|referee]]. She has been on the [[FIFA International Referees List]] since 2009,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.fifa.com/development/education-and-technical/referees/index.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150512123412/http://www.fifa.com/development/education-and-technical/referees/index.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=12 May 2015|title=Football Development - Refereeing - Mission and Goals|last=FIFA.com|website=FIFA.com|language=en|access-date=4 July 2019}}</ref> and has refereed several high-profile matches. She became the first woman to referee a major men's European match and a French [[Ligue 1]] match, both in 2019, and the first woman to officiate a [[UEFA Champions League]] match in 2020.<ref>{{cite web |title=Frappart prva sutkinja u povijesti Lige prvaka |url=https://sport.hrt.hr/683929/frappart-prva-sutkinja-u-povijesti-lige-prvaka |website=sport.hrt.hr |access-date=25 December 2020}}</ref> In 2021, Frappart became the first woman to take charge of a men's World Cup qualifying match.<ref>{{cite web |title=Frappart becomes first woman EVER to referee World Cup qualifier|website=[[MSN]] |url=https://www.msn.com/en-gb/sport/football/frappart-becomes-first-woman-ever-to-referee-world-cup-qualifier/ar-BB1f34rr}}</ref> In 2022, she was one of the three women referees selected to officiate at the men's World Cup,<ref>{{Cite web |date=19 May 2022 |title=Female referees to officiate at men's World Cup for the first time |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/may/19/female-referees-to-officiate-at-mens-world-cup-for-the-first-time |access-date=19 May 2022 |website=The Guardian|first=Paul|last=MacInnes |language=en}}</ref> before she became the first woman to referee a men's World Cup match in an all-female referee team.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Referee Frappart makes history as first woman to referee men's World Cup game |url=https://www.fifa.com/fifaplus/en/articles/world-cup-qatar-2022-greater-than-stephanie-frappart-in-history-as-first-female-referee-in-costa-rica-v-germany |date=1 December 2022 |website=www.fifa.com }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |author=Issy Ronald |title=Stéphanie Frappart to make history as the first woman to referee a men’s World Cup match |url=https://edition.cnn.com/2022/11/30/football/stephanie-frappart-female-referees-world-cup-2022-spt-intl/index.html |date=1 December 2022 |website=CNN }}</ref>

== Career ==
Frappart grew up in [[Herblay-sur-Seine]] and began refereeing youth games at the age of 13.<ref name="Athletic-profile">{{cite web |last1=Whitehead |first1=Jacob |title=Stephanie Frappart on refereeing at the World Cup: ‘You’re there because you deserve it’ |url=https://theathletic.com/3736775/2022/11/15/stephanie-frappart-referee-world-cup/ |website=The Athletic|date=15 November 2022 |access-date=15 November 2022 |language=en}}</ref> By the time she was 18, she was already refereeing national u19 games.<ref name="Athletic-profile" /> In 2011, Frappart began to referee matches in the [[Championnat National]], the third division of men's [[football in France]].<ref name=":0" /> In 2014, Frappart became the first woman to referee in [[Ligue 2]], the second tier of [[Professional sports|professional]] men's football in France.<ref name=":0">{{cite news|title=Stéphanie Frappart, première femme arbitre centrale de Ligue 2|url=http://www.lexpress.fr/actualite/sport/football/stephanie-frappart-premiere-femme-arbitre-centrale-de-ligue-2_1545556.html|access-date=5 June 2015|publisher=[[L'Express (France)]]|date=22 May 2014|language=fr}}</ref>  She served as a referee at the [[2015 FIFA Women's World Cup]] in [[Canada]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/womensworldcup/news/y=2015/m=3/news=22-referees-7-support-referees-and-44-assistant-referees-appointed-for-2580523.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150331171445/http://www.fifa.com/womensworldcup/news/y=2015/m=3/news=22-referees-7-support-referees-and-44-assistant-referees-appointed-for-2580523.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=31 March 2015|title=22 referees, 7 support referees and 44 assistant referees appointed for FIFA Women's World Cup 2015™|publisher=FIFA.com|date=30 March 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://resources.fifa.com/mm/document/footballdevelopment/refereeing/02/58/05/09/matchofficialsfwwccanada2015_260315_neutral.pdf|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402102321/http://resources.fifa.com/mm/document/footballdevelopment/refereeing/02/58/05/09/matchofficialsfwwccanada2015_260315_neutral.pdf|url-status=dead|archive-date=2 April 2015|title=Referees and Assistant Referees for the FIFA Women's World Cup Canada 2015™|publisher=FIFA.com}}</ref>

On 3 December 2018, Frappart was appointed to officiate in the [[2019 FIFA Women's World Cup]] in France.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/womensworldcup/news/match-officials-appointed-for-fifa-women-s-world-cup-france-2019tm|title=FIFA Women's World Cup France 2019™ - News - Match officials appointed for FIFA Women's World Cup France 2019™ - FIFA.com|last=FIFA.com|website=www.fifa.com|language=en-GB|access-date=18 June 2019}}</ref> After the conclusion of the round of 16, it was announced that Frappart had been selected as one of 11 officials that would be retained for the remainder of the tournament.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/2828b48446f280f9/original/b7h9pras9shxrwulsfux-pdf.pdf|title=Refereeing - Media briefing|website=FIFA.com|access-date=26 June 2019}}</ref> Frappart would go on to be appointed to referee the [[2019 FIFA Women's World Cup Final|final]] of the tournament which was contested on 7 July 2019 between the [[United States women's national soccer team|United States]] and the [[Netherlands women's national football team|Netherlands]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/womensworldcup/news/frappart-final-role-a-huge-sense-of-pride|title=FIFA Women's World Cup 2019™ - News - Frappart: Final role a huge source of pride - FIFA.com|last=FIFA.com|website=www.fifa.com|language=en-GB|access-date=8 July 2019}}</ref>

In April 2019, it was announced that she would become the first female referee in [[Ligue 1]],<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/48035509|title=Stephanie Frappart first woman to referee Ligue 1 match|work=BBC Sport |date=24 April 2019}}</ref> with her first game coming on 28 April 2019, when she officiated a game between [[Amiens SC|SC Amiens]] and [[RC Strasbourg Alsace|RC Strasbourg]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/french-ligue-1/story/3837476/stephanie-frappart-becomes-first-woman-to-referee-ligue-1-match|title=Ligue of her own: Female ref makes historic debut|date=28 April 2019|website=ESPN.com|language=en|access-date=19 June 2019}}</ref> On 2 August 2019, Frappart was appointed to referee the [[2019 UEFA Super Cup]] between [[Liverpool F.C|Liverpool]] and [[Chelsea F.C|Chelsea]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2019/aug/02/stephanie-frappart-to-break-new-ground-by-refereeing-liverpool-v-chelsea-in-super-cup|title=Stéphanie Frappart to break new ground by refereeing Liverpool v Chelsea in Super Cup |date=2 August 2019 |access-date=2 August 2019 |work=The Guardian |issn=0261-3077}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49206450|title=Stephanie Frappart: Female referee to take charge of Uefa Super Cup |date=2 August 2019 |access-date=2 August 2019 |work=BBC Sport}}</ref> In doing so she became the first woman to officiate in a major men's European match.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49335062|title=Stephanie Frappart 'not afraid' of Super Cup between Chelsea and Liverpool |date=14 August 2019 |access-date=14 August 2019 |work=BBC Sport}}</ref> On 11 November 2019, Frappart officiated the second leg of the inaugural [[Champions Cup (All-Ireland)|Champions Cup]] competition, between the league winners of the [[League of Ireland Premier Division]] and the [[NIFL Premiership]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=O'Hehir |first=Paul |url=https://www.irishmirror.ie/sport/soccer/soccer-news/womens-world-cup-final-referee-20643451 |title=Women's World Cup final referee Stephanie Frappart set to officiate Dundalk v Linfield decider |date=17 October 2019 |access-date=17 October 2019 |work=Irish Mirror}}</ref> The match saw the [[Republic of Ireland]]'s champions [[Dundalk F.C.|Dundalk]] beat [[Northern Ireland]]'s champions [[Linfield F.C.|Linfield]] 6–0, with Frappart showing two yellow cards.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ie.soccerway.com/matches/2019/11/11/europe/unite-the-union-champions-cup/dundalk-football-club/linfield-fc/3182763/?ICID=PL_MS_02|title=Dundalk vs. Linfield - 11 November 2019 - Soccerway|date=11 November 2019|via=ie.soccerway.com}}</ref>

On 2 December 2020, she became the first woman to referee a [[UEFA Champions League]] match, between [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]] and [[FC Dynamo Kyiv|Dynamo Kyiv]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Sriram |first=Arvind |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/european/stephanie-frappart-referee-juventus-champions-league-b1764367.html |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/archive/20220525/https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/european/stephanie-frappart-referee-juventus-champions-league-b1764367.html |archive-date=25 May 2022 |url-access=subscription |url-status=live |title=Stephanie Frappart to become first woman to referee a men's Champions League game |date=1 December 2020 |access-date=2 December 2020 |work=The Independent}}</ref> In March 2021, Frappart officiated the second leg of the [[UEFA Women's Champions League]] match between [[Atlético Madrid Femenino|Atlético Madrid]] and [[Chelsea F.C. Women|Chelsea]].<ref>{{Cite web|url= https://www.uefa.com/womenschampionsleague/match/2030743--atletico-madrid-vs-chelsea/?iv=true|title=Matches - 04 March 2021 - UEFA|access-date=5 March 2021}}</ref> Later that month, she became the first woman to officiate a [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)|FIFA World Cup qualifier]], in a match between the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]] and [[Latvia national football team|Latvia]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/56550520 |title=Netherlands 2–0 Latvia |website=BBC Sport |date=27 March 2021 }}</ref>

On 7 May 2022, she was the referee for the [[2022 Coupe de France Final]] between [[OGC Nice|Nice]] and [[FC Nantes|Nantes]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fff.fr/competition/match/24469113-o-g-c-nice-cote-d-azur-f-c-nantes.html |title=OGC Nice 0–1 FC Nantes |website=Fédération Française de Football |language=fr |date=7 May 2022 }}</ref>

On 19 May 2022, she was selected for the officiating pool at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.aol.com/sports/fifa-makes-history-picks-first-153531155.html | title=FIFA makes history, picks first women referees for men's World Cup }}</ref> On 1 December 2022, she became the first woman to referee a men's [[FIFA World Cup]] match when she officiated the match between [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica]] and [[Germany national football team|Germany]].<ref>{{cite web |first=Tom|last=Nouvian |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2022/12/01/sports/soccer/world-cup-women-referee-crew.html |title=Referee Stéphanie Frappart Leads First All-Woman Team at World Cup |website=The New York Times |date=1 December 2022 }}</ref>
