'''Jesús Daniel Gallardo Vasconcelos''' (born 15 August 1994) is a Mexican professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|left-back]] for [[Liga MX]] club [[C.F. Monterrey|Monterrey]] and the [[Mexico national football team|Mexico national team]].

==Club career==
===Youth===
Gallardo began his career at 15 years old with [[Once Hermanos]] from 2009,<ref>{{cite web|title=Jesús Gallardo: 5 años de Primera|url=https://www.tabascohoy.com/nota/494980/cumple-jesus-gallardo-5-anos-de-primera-division|work=Tabasco Hoy|first=Mauricio|last=Valencia|date=26 November 2019|language=es}}</ref> until making the switch to [[Jaguares de la 48]] in 2011,<ref name="Descubriendo">{{cite web|title=Descubriendo a Gallardo|first=Rubén|last=Guerrero Atilano|url=https://www.milenio.com/deportes/descubriendo-a-gallardo|work=Milenio|date=4 October 2016|language=es}}</ref> and ultimately getting the attention of first division team [[Club Universidad Nacional]]. From there, in 2013,<ref name="Descubriendo"/> he joined [[Club Universidad Nacional|Pumas UNAM]] youth academy, successfully going through the U-20 team and [[Pumas Morelos]].

===UNAM===
In 2014, Gallardo was eventually promoted to the first team by coach [[Guillermo Vázquez]].<ref name="Descubriendo"/> Gallardo scored his first goal with UNAM in a [[Copa MX]] group stage match against [[Deportivo Toluca F.C.|Toluca]] on 16 September 2014 that marked his debut with the jersey that ended 2–2 but didn't make his competitive league debut in [[Liga MX]] until 23 November 2014 which ended in a 4–2 win against [[C.F. Monterrey|Monterrey]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.tabascohoy.com/nota/337841/jesus-gallardo-un-tabasqueno-con-piel-felina |title=Jesús Gallardo, un tabasceño con piel felina |publisher= Luis Chavez Jr. |website= Tabascohoy.com|date=13 July 2016 |access-date= 14 October 2017}}</ref>

On 21 November 2016, he would score a brace against W Connection F.C. during the [[2016–17 CONCACAF Champions League group stage]] that ended 8–1.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pumas UNAM 8–1 W Connection FC|url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/match?gameId=457202|work=ESPN|access-date=18 September 2018}}</ref>

On 9 August 2017, during an [[Apertura 2017 Copa MX]] group stage match against Monterrey, he would score the tying goal during stoppage time, leaving the match 1–1.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pumas le roba un punto al Monterrey en la Copa MX|url=https://mexico.as.com/mexico/2017/08/10/futbol/1502336721_662179.html|website=Mexico.as.com|publisher=AS|language=es|date=9 August 2017|access-date=22 October 2018}}</ref>

===Monterrey===
On 29 May 2018, Gallardo joined Monterrey.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mexico international winger Jesus Gallardo swaps Pumas for Monterrey|url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/soccer-transfers/story/3513082/mexico-international-winger-jesus-gallardo-swaps-pumas-for-monterrey|work=ESPN|first=Tom|last=Marshall|date=29 May 2018|access-date=18 September 2018}}</ref> He would make his debut with the team on 21 July 2018 as a starter against [[C.F. Pachuca|Pachuca]], winning 1–0.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pachuca 0–1 Monterrey|url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/match?gameId=517051|work=ESPN|access-date=20 September 2018}}</ref> On 29 September, he would score his first goal with Monterrey, scoring on the 74th minute in a 3–0 win over [[Club Tijuana|Tijuana]].<ref>{{cite web|title=A Rayados le bastó menos de 90' y una tormenta para golear a Xolos|url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/2018/09/29/a-rayados-le-basto-menos-de-90-y-una-tormenta-para-golear-a-xolos|first=Sahid|last=Hernández Rosete|date=29 September 2018|access-date=1 October 2018|language=es}}</ref> On 20 October, in a league match against [[Deportivo Toluca F.C.|Toluca]], he scored the tying goal at the 73rd minute in which Monterrey would later go on to win 2–1.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rayados remonta en casa para vencer al Toluca|url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/liga-mx/2018/10/21/5bcbfab122601d003d8b45c3.html|work=Marca|date=20 October 2018|access-date=22 October 2018|language=es}}</ref>

On 23 October he scored the decisive penalty in the shootout of the [[Apertura 2018 Copa MX]] semi-finals against Pachuca, sending his team to the [[Apertura 2018 Copa MX Final|final]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/2018/10/23/rayados-avanza-a-final-de-copa-mx-tras-derrotar-a-tuzos-en-penales|title=Rayados avanza a Final de Copa MX tras derrotar a Tuzos en penales|work=MedioTiempo|access-date=24 October 2018|language=es-MX}}</ref> On 3 November he provided an assist and scored a goal in Monterrey's 2–0 victory over [[C.D. Veracruz|Veracruz]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Los tres grandes refuerzos dieron los tres puntos|url=https://www.multimedios.com/deportes/liga-mx/los-tres-grandes-refuerzos-dieron-los-tres-puntos|website=Multimedios.com|publisher=Multimedios Televisión|first=Eduardo|last=Torres|date=5 November 2018|access-date=6 November 2018|language=es}}</ref>

As Monterrey won the [[2019 CONCACAF Champions League Finals]] against [[Clásico Regiomontano|crosstown rivals]] [[Tigres UANL]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Rayados vanquish frustration of previous Final Regia loss with Concacaf Champions League victory|url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/rayados-vanquish-frustration-of-previous-final-regia-loss/g5fh6dfy2t9z1u9kvqq7l9dz3|work=Goal.com|first=Jon|last=Arnold|date=2 May 2019}}</ref> he was included in the Team of the Tournament.<ref name="TotT">{{cite tweet |author=Scotiabank Concacaf Champions League|user=TheChampions |number=1124021294750732288 |title=Presenting the Scotiabank Concacaf Champions League 2019 Team of the Tournament!|date=2 May 2019 |access-date=2 May 2019}}</ref>

At the end of the December, he would win the [[Apertura 2019 Liga MX championship stage|Apertura championship]] finals against [[Club América|América]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Monterrey defeat Club America on penalties to win Liga MX 2019 Apertura title|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/report?gameId=560779|work=ESPN|first=Tom|last=Marshall|date=30 December 2019}}</ref>

With Monterrey's victory of the [[2019–20 Copa MX]], they had obtained the [[Treble (association football)|continental treble]].<ref>{{cite web|title=¡TRI-PLE-TE! {{!}} Rayados consiguió triplete y ahora va por el campeonato del Guard1anes 2020|url=https://www.90min.com/es/posts/tri-ple-te-rayados-consiguio-triplete-y-ahora-va-por-el-campeonato-del-guard1anes-2020|work=90 Min|language=es|date=5 November 2020}}</ref>

==International career==
Gallardo received his first call up to the [[Mexico national football team|senior national team]] to replace the injured [[Ángel Sepúlveda]] for matches against [[New Zealand national football team|New Zealand]] and [[Panama national football team|Panama]] in October 2016,<ref>{{cite news|title=De Pumas al Tri; Jesús Gallardo, convocado por Osorio |url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/2016/10/02/de-pumas-al-tri-jesus-gallardo-convocado-por-osorio |access-date=5 October 2016|publisher=mediotiempo.com|date=2 October 2016}}</ref> making his international debut on the 8th of the month as a starter against New Zealand in a 2–1 victory, contributing a crucial pass for the winning goal.<ref>{{cite web|title=Discreto debut de Jesús Gallardo con la Selección|url=https://www.vavel.com/mx/futbol-mexicano/2016/10/09/pumas/705388-discreto-debut-de-jesus-gallardo-con-la-seleccion.html|work=Vavel|first=Andrés|last=Ocampo|date=8 October 2016|access-date=10 October 2018}}</ref>

On 28 June 2017, Gallardo was called up to participate in the [[2017 CONCACAF Gold Cup]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Lista la convocatoria de México para la Copa Oro|url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/copa-oro/2017/06/29/59542cb0ca4741ab508b4609.html|work=Marca|date=28 June 2017|access-date=10 October 2018}}</ref> managing to appear in all games until their 1–0 semi-final loss against [[Jamaica national football team|Jamaica]].

[[File:Ger-Mex (14).jpg|thumb|upright|Gallardo celebrating following Mexico's 1–0 win over [[Germany national football team|Germany]] during the 2018 FIFA World Cup]]
In May 2018, Gallardo was named in Mexico's preliminary 28-man [[Mexico national football team|squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|World Cup]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/amp/news/revealed-every-world-cup-2018-squad-23-man-preliminary-lists/oa0atsduflsv1nsf6oqk576rb|title=Revealed: Every World Cup 2018 squad - Final 23-man lists - Goal.com|access-date=5 June 2018}}</ref> and in June, was ultimately included in the final 23-man roster.<ref>{{cite web|title=Convocatoria de la Selección Nacional de México |url=https://miseleccion.mx/convocatoria-la-seleccion-nacional-mexico-13/ |website=MiSeleccion.mx |language=es |access-date=4 June 2018}}</ref> During Mexico's final group stage match against Sweden, he would receive a [[penalty card|yellow card]] at the 13th second of the match for his elbow contact made with [[Ola Toivonen|Ola Toivonen's]] face, marking it as the fastest yellow card given in [[FIFA World Cup]] history.<ref>{{cite web|title=Jesus Gallardo's yellow card for Mexico fastest in World Cup history|url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/mexico/story/3547297/jesus-gallardos-yellow-card-for-mexico-fastest-in-world-cup-history|work=ESPN|date=27 June 2018 |access-date=27 June 2018}}</ref> He would go on to play as a starter for all of the group stage matches, including the [[2018 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Brazil vs Mexico|round-of-16 loss]] against [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]].<ref name="fifa">{{cite web|title=Jesus GALLARDO|url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/players/player/402772/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180623113448/https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/players/player/402772/|url-status=dead|archive-date=23 June 2018|work=FIFA|access-date=22 October 2018}}</ref>

He was included in [[Gerardo Martino]]'s preliminary roster for the [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Marco Fabián vuelve y no está Tecatito; anuncian lista previa de México para Copa Oro|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/seleccion-mayor/seleccion-mexicana-prelista-copa-oro-2019-tata-martino|publisher=Mediotiempo.com|first=Enrique|last=Martínez Villar|date=14 May 2019}}</ref> and was eventually in the final list.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://miseleccion.mx/noticias/2560/Convocatoria-Final-para-Copa-Oro-2019|title=Convocatoria Final para Copa Oro 2019|website=MiSeleccion.mx|publisher=Mexican Football Federation|date=5 June 2019|language=es}}</ref> He appeared in all matches of the tournament, as Mexico defeated the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]] in the [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup Final|final]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Mexico Turns Tide, Wins Gold Cup Title Again vs. Wasteful USMNT|url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2019/07/07/mexico-usa-gold-cup-title-dos-santos-pizarro-pulisic-berhalter|publisher=[[Sports Illustrated]]|first=Brian|last=Straus|date=7 July 2019}}</ref> He was listed in the tournament's Best XI.<ref name="2019 CGC XI">{{cite web|url=https://www.goldcup.org/en/awards/2019-concacaf-gold-cup-best-xi|title=2019 Concacaf Gold Cup - Best XI|website=goldcup.org|date=7 July 2019|access-date=8 July 2019|archive-date=30 July 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190730113130/https://www.goldcup.org/en/awards/2019-concacaf-gold-cup-best-xi|url-status=dead}}</ref> Following the victory, Martino declared Gallardo to be the best player of the match.<ref>{{cite web|title=Para Martino, Jesús Gallardo fue el mejor jugador del partido|url=https://www.goal.com/es/noticias/para-martino-jesus-gallardo-fue-el-mejor-jugador-del-partido/1e1gj9z9a4i1b16lzmve8fgang|website=Goal.com|language=es|date=8 July 2019}}</ref>

In October 2022, Gallardo was named in Mexico's preliminary 31-man [[Mexico national football team|squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|World Cup]], and in November, was ultimately included in the final 26-man roster.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lista de convocados de la Selección Mexicana al Mundial de Qatar 2022|url=https://www.tvazteca.com/aztecadeportes/lista-oficial-convocados-seleccion-mexicana-mundial-qatar-2022|work=Tvazteca.com|first=Omar|last=Moro|date=14 November 2022|language=Spanish}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Gallardo is known for intensity, pace, and physical strength, allowing him to be both dangerous and intense during a whole match.<ref>{{cite web|title=Talent Radar: CONCACAF Gold Cup 2017 U-23 Team of the Tournament |url=http://outsideoftheboot.com/2017/07/30/talent-radar-concacaf-gold-cup-2017-u-23-team-of-the-tournament/|work=Outsideoftheboot.com|first=Jose Miguel |last=Saraiva |access-date=20 September 2018}}</ref><ref name="ESPN"/> [[FIFA]] described him as "Gallardo's many assets include his speed, ability to take people on and his selfless distribution. A regular source of assists and the archetypal team player, he is adept at pressing high up the pitch and catching opponents out of position."<ref name="fifa"/>

Starting off as a [[Midfielder#Winger|left-winger]] on the club level, former Mexico national team coach [[Juan Carlos Osorio]] used him on a more defensive position as [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|left-back]].<ref name="Goal.com">{{cite web|title=Pumas winger Jesus Gallardo continues making Mexico case|url=http://www.goal.com/en-qa/news/pumas-winger-jesus-gallardo-continues-making-mexico-national/1bwo35onr3x961vwqvjzxx42hb|work=Goal.com|first=Jon|last=Arnold|date=21 January 2018|access-date=20 September 2018}}</ref><ref name="ESPN">{{cite web|title=Jesus Gallardo's versatility could earn him a place in Mexico's World Cup squad|url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/mexican-liga-mx/22/blog/post/3351545/jesus-gallardos-versatility-could-earn-him-a-place-in-mexicos-world-cup-squad|work=ESPN|first=Nayib|last=Moran|date=17 January 2018|access-date=20 September 2018}}</ref> With the arrival of new national team coach Gerardo Martino, he chose to keep him as a left-back.<ref>{{cite web|title=Martino finds ideal fullbacks for his system in Gallardo, Rodriguez|url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/martino-finds-ideal-fullbacks-for-his-system-in-gallardo/94ddw73eeub71xxddotp0kzkt|work=Goal.com|first=Jon|last=Arnold|date=28 June 2019}}</ref>
