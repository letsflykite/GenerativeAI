'''Luka Jović''' ({{lang-sr-Cyrl|Лука Јовић}}; born 23 December 1997) is a Serbian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Striker (association football)|striker]] for {{Italian football updater|Fiorentina}} club [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]] and the [[Serbia national football team|Serbia national team]].

==Early life==
Jović was born in [[Loznica]], FR Yugoslavia.<ref name="Loznica ref">{{cite web |url=https://sport.blic.rs/fudbal/evropski-hit-luka-jovic-za-blicsport-roditelji-su-dali-i-poslednji-dinar-da-bih-uspeo/l983nsx |author=Blic |author-link=Blic |title=EVROPSKI HIT Luka Jović za Blicsport: Roditelji su dali i poslednji dinar da bih uspeo |accessdate=29 March 2021 |date=10 March 2019 |language=Serbian}}</ref> He was raised in the small village of Batar near [[Bijeljina]], to [[Serbs of Bosnia and Herzegovina|Bosnian Serb]] parents Milan and Svetlana Jović.<ref name="Bleacher ref">{{cite web |url=https://bleacherreport.com/articles/2825629-luka-jovic-from-doughnut-guzzling-teen-to-one-of-europes-most-wanted-strikers |author=Bleacher Report |author-link=Bleacher Report |title=Luka Jovic: From Doughnut-Guzzling Teen to One of Europe's Most Wanted Strikers |website=[[Bleacher Report]] |accessdate=29 March 2021 |date=14 March 2019|language=English}}</ref> At the age of five, he started playing football in Loznica, where he was spotted in 2004 and offered to play in Mini Maxi, a development league for kids aged between 4 and 12 in [[Belgrade]]. After just one game where Jović scored three goals his father was offered €50 for each game he played and 2,000 [[Bosnia and Herzegovina dinar|dinars]] for travelling costs from Batar to Belgrade. At one of those games he was scouted by Toma Milićević, [[Red Star Belgrade|Red Star]]'s scout who invited him to trial with Red Star Belgrade.<ref name="Cudo">{{cite web|url=http://www.mozzartsport.com/vesti/cudesan-slucaj-luke-jovica-ili-kako-je-klinac-koji-je-spavao-u-gepeku-stigao-do-velike-pozornice/67871|title=Mozzart Sport: ''Čudesan slučaj Luke Jovića, ili – Kako je klinac koji je spavao u gepeku stigao do velike pozornice''|language=sr|date=27 December 2013}}</ref> His playing style attracted comparisons to [[Darko Pančev]] and [[Radamel Falcao]], who is also his idol.<ref name="TerzaJovic">{{cite web|url=http://www.sportske.net/vest/domaci-fudbal/terzic-luka-jovic-novi-falkao-190342.html|author=OBRENOVIĆ, Aleksandar|title=Terzić: Luka Jović je novi Falkao|date=11 August 2014|access-date=12 May 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190605065259/https://www.sportske.net/vest/domaci-fudbal/terzic-luka-jovic-novi-falkao-190342.html|archive-date=5 June 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="Novosti">{{cite web|url=http://www.novosti.rs/vesti/sport.294.html:459005-Zvezdin-biser-Jovic-Hocu-kao-Pancev|author=B. Cvetojevic|title=Zvezdin biser Jović: Hoću kao Pančev|date=16 October 2013|language=sr}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Red Star Belgrade===
====Youth====
Jović was offered the chance to play for [[Red Star Belgrade]] in 2005. Before he committed, his father took him to train with [[FK Partizan|Partizan]], who had been trying to sign Jović for some time. They offered his father €200 per month for Jović to play with them, but he insisted on staying with Red Star.<ref name="Cudo" />

====2013–14 season====
On 28 May 2014, at the age of 16 years, five months and five days, Jović made his professional debut against [[FK Vojvodina]] in [[Novi Sad]], where he broke [[Dejan Stanković]]'s record as the youngest goalscorer in a competitive match in the club's history.<ref name="Blic">{{cite web|url=http://sport.blic.rs/Fudbal/Domaci-fudbal/253382/Zvezda-je-rodjena-Luka-Jovic-srusio-rekord-Dekija-Stankovica-VIDEO|author=Todorović, N.|title=Zvezda je rođena: Luka Jović srušio rekord Dekija Stankovića|language=sr|date=28 May 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140722085218/http://sport.blic.rs/Fudbal/Domaci-fudbal/253382/Zvezda-je-rodjena-Luka-Jovic-srusio-rekord-Dekija-Stankovica-VIDEO|archive-date=22 July 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://mondo.rs/a696618/Sport/Fudbal/Luka-Jovic-nece-otici-dok-ne-napuni-18.html|title=Luka Jović neće otići dok ne napuni 18|work=Mondo Portal|access-date=20 March 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190810115536/http://mondo.rs/a696618/Sport/Fudbal/Luka-Jovic-nece-otici-dok-ne-napuni-18.html|archive-date=10 August 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref> He scored the goal only two minutes<ref name="Blic" /> after he was brought on as substitute, and the final score of 3–3 was enough to give Red Star the 2013–14 [[Serbian SuperLiga]] title.

====2014–15 season====
On 18 October 2014, Jović became the youngest player ever to play in [[Eternal derby (Serbia)|Eternal derby]] at the age of 16 years, 9 months and 25 days, breaking the previous record of [[Dejan Milovanović]] who was 17 years and 6 months old at the time.<ref>[http://www.mozzartsport.com/vesti/luka-jovic-najmladji-akter-u-istoriji-vecitih-derbija/87162] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303224245/http://www.mozzartsport.com/vesti/luka-jovic-najmladji-akter-u-istoriji-vecitih-derbija/87162|date=3 March 2016}} Mozzart Sport: Luka Jović – najmlađi akter u istoriji večitih derbija! (Serbian) 18 October 2014</ref> Jović missed some crucial chances, but in spring half of the season scored six goals. His first goal of the season came against Vojvodina, in a match where Red Star celebrated its 70th birthday. He then scored against [[FK Spartak Zlatibor Voda|Spartak]], a double against [[FK Borac Čačak|Borac]] and a decisive goal against [[FK Voždovac|Voždovac]], ahead of the Eternal derby clash.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mojacrvenazvezda.net/107215/2015/04/18/evrogol-i-nastavak-serije-luke-jovica-za-minimalac-zvezde-na-vracaru-pred-derbi/|title=Еврогол и наставак серије Луке Јовића за минималац Звезде на Врачару пред дерби!|author=Александар|date=18 April 2015|work=Moja Crvena Zvezda|access-date=20 March 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190604172217/http://www.mojacrvenazvezda.net/107215/2015/04/18/evrogol-i-nastavak-serije-luke-jovica-za-minimalac-zvezde-na-vracaru-pred-derbi/|archive-date=4 June 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref> He missed the end of the season and the [[2015 FIFA U-20 World Cup]] in [[New Zealand]] due to injury. On 17 May 2015, he signed a three-year contract with Red Star to last until 2018.

====2015–16 season====
From the beginning of the 2015–16 season, Jović started as a first choice in Red Star attack along with [[Hugo Filipe Oliveira|Hugo Vieira]] and scored 3 goals in first 5 games. He scored again against Voždovac a winning goal and brought another win to Red Star at [[Obilić Stadium|Stadion na Vračaru]]. He scored another game winner in Subotica, against Spartak. In November 2015, a [[racketeer]] from Loznica threatened him and his family that if they did not pay money to him, that he would "break Jović's legs".<ref name="Blicrek">{{cite web|url=http://www.blic.rs/vesti/hronika/reketas-pretio-da-ce-luki-jovicu-da-polomi-noge/d9cq2rz|author=Miljan Milošević|title=Reketaš pretio da će Luki Joviću da polomi noge|language=sr|date=16 November 2015|access-date=15 December 2015}}</ref> A suspect was apprehended immediately afterwards.<ref name="Blicrek" />

===Benfica===
In February 2016, Jović signed with Portuguese champions [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]] until 2021.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.slbenfica.pt/noticias/detalhedenoticia/tabid/2788/ArticleId/44570/language/pt-PT/.aspx |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160223213443/http://www.slbenfica.pt/noticias/detalhedenoticia/tabid/2788/ArticleId/44570/language/pt-PT/.aspx |url-status=dead |archive-date=23 February 2016 |title=Jović: "Aprender e melhorar todos os dias" |language=pt |trans-title=Jović: "To learn and improve everyday" |date=2 February 2016 |publisher=S.L. Benfica |access-date=2 February 2016}}</ref> On 9 March, he debuted for the club's [[S.L. Benfica|reserve team]] in a 2–2 home draw against [[S.C. Covilhã|Sporting da Covilhã]] in the [[Segunda Liga]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ligaportugal.pt/oou/jogo/20152016/ledmanligapro/34/9|title=SL Benfica B – SC Covilhã (Jornada 34 Ledman LigaPRO 2015–2016) – Liga Portugal|work=ligaportugal.pt|access-date=20 March 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160409215219/http://www.ligaportugal.pt/oou/jogo/20152016/ledmanligapro/34/9|archive-date=9 April 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> He debuted for the first-team on 20 March, as a substitute, in a 1–0 away victory against [[Boavista F.C.|Boavista]] in the [[2015–16 Primeira Liga|Primeira Liga]].

On 30 January 2017, Jović made his only appearance for the first team when he came on as a substitute in the 81st minute against [[Vitória F.C.|Vitória de Setúbal]].<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.whoscored.com/Matches/1104173/Live/Portugal-Liga-NOS-2016-2017-Vitoria-de-Setubal-Benfica | title = Match Report | website = whoscored.com | access-date = 16 April 2019}}</ref>

====Loan to Eintracht Frankfurt====
====2017–18 season====
In June 2017, Jović joined [[Eintracht Frankfurt]] on a two-season loan while his new club secured an option to sign him on a permanent basis.<ref>{{cite news|title=Fix: Eintracht leiht Jovic von Benfica aus|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/startseite/700477/artikel_fix_eintracht-leiht-jovic-von-benfica-aus.html|access-date=27 June 2017|work=kicker Online|date=27 June 2017|language=de}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|date=27 June 2017|title=Luka Jovic no Eintracht Frankfurt|trans-title=Luka Jovic at Eintracht Frankfurt|url=http://www.slbenfica.pt/30/news/info/Nan6iq1DhUCZJ-LoPcyJbg|language=pt|publisher=S.L. Benfica|access-date=4 July 2017}}</ref> He made five appearances as they won the [[2017–18 DFB-Pokal]], and scored the only goal of the win at [[FC Schalke 04|Schalke 04]] in the semi-finals on 18 April 2018,<ref>{{cite news |last1=Da Silva |first1=Michael |last2=Harding |first2=Jonathan |title=German Cup: Niko Kovac guides Frankfurt to Berlin for Bayern Munich showdown |url=https://www.dw.com/en/german-cup-niko-kovac-guides-frankfurt-to-berlin-for-bayern-munich-showdown/a-43445046 |access-date=21 October 2018 |publisher=Deutsche Welle |date=18 April 2018}}</ref> but was an unused substitute in the [[2018 DFB-Pokal Final|3–1 final win]] over [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]].

====2018–19 season====
20-year-old Jović became the youngest player to score five goals in a Bundesliga game when he did so in a 7–1 home win over [[Fortuna Düsseldorf]] on 19 October 2018.<ref>{{cite news |title=Eintracht Frankfurt 7–1 Fortuna Dusseldorf |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45922808 |access-date=21 October 2018 |work=BBC Sport |date=19 October 2018}}</ref> On 14 March 2019, he scored the only goal, his seventh for the campaign, in a 1–0 win over [[Inter Milan]] to secure the club's progression to the quarter finals of the [[2018–19 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]]. In doing so, he helped Eintracht become the first German club ever to record eight wins in a single season in the competition.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/inter-milan-eintracht-frankfurt-live-europa-league-line-ups-stats-icardi-jovic-2969|title=Eintracht Frankfurt through to UEFA Europa League quarter-finals after Luka Jovic chip downs Inter Milan|publisher=Bundesliga|date=14 March 2019|access-date=15 March 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190605232225/https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/inter-milan-eintracht-frankfurt-live-europa-league-line-ups-stats-icardi-jovic-2969|archive-date=5 June 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On 17 April 2019, Eintracht Frankfurt exercised a clause in Jović's contract to make his loan move permanent until 30 June 2023.<ref>{{cite news|date=17 April 2019|title=Eintracht Frankfurt exercise option to purchase Luka Jović|url=https://www.eintracht.de/en/news/article/eintracht-frankfurt-exercise-option-to-purchase-luka-jovic-72250/|publisher=Eintracht Frankfurt|access-date=18 April 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190418222346/https://www.eintracht.de/en/news/article/eintracht-frankfurt-exercise-option-to-purchase-luka-jovic-72250/|archive-date=18 April 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref> In May 2019, he scored in both semi-final legs against [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]], yet Eintracht Frankfurt lost 4–3 on penalties after drawing 1–1 in both matches.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.goal.com/en-sg/news/jovic-other-leagues-in-europe-are-a-much-better-fit-for-me/1qda2gels9heg1d23129x8kenp |title=Jovic: Serie A and the Premier League are the best fits for me |website=Goal.com |date=26 May 2019 }}</ref> However, Jović managed to score ten goals in total in the Europa League season.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/why-is-luka-jovic-so-good-eintracht-frankfurt-striker-scoring-goals-left-right-and-centre-3082 |title=Why is Luka Jovic so good? Eintracht Frankfurt striker catches Real Madrid's eye |publisher=Bundesliga |date=2019 }}</ref>

===Real Madrid===
====2019–20 season====
[[File:FC RB Salzburg versus Real Madrid (Testspiel, 7. August 2019) 17.jpg|thumb|upright|Jović playing for [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]] in 2019]]

On 4 June 2019, Jović signed for [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]] for a reported €60&nbsp;million, on a six-year contract.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2019/06/official-announcement-jovi%C4%87|title=Official Announcement: Jović|date=4 June 2019|publisher=Real Madrid CF|access-date=4 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/realmadrid/luka-jovic-real-madrid-transfer-confirmed-eintracht-frankfurt-striker-a4145291.html|title=Luka Jovic to Real Madrid transfer confirmed for Eintracht Frankfurt striker|last=Ouzia|first=Malik|date=4 June 2019|work=London Evening Standard|access-date=4 June 2019}}</ref> He made his debut on 17 August 2019, coming in as a substitute in a 3–1 win over [[RC Celta de Vigo|Celta Vigo]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/real-madrid/cronica/2019/08/17/5d5833a022601df81e8b4584.html|title=Real Madrid of old resurrected in Balaidos|date=17 August 2019|work=Marca|location=Spain|access-date=17 August 2019}}</ref> On 30 October 2019, he scored his first goal for the club in a 5–0 victory over [[CD Leganés]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Real Madrid vs. Leganes – Football Match Summary – October 30, 2019 – ESPN |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/match?gameId=550508 |publisher=ESPN |access-date=30 October 2019}}</ref>

Jović started both semi-final and [[2020 Supercopa de España Final|final]] match of [[2019–20 Supercopa de España]] against [[Valencia CF|Valencia]] and [[Atlético Madrid]], respectively. Real won the final 4–1 on [[Penalty shootout|penalties]] on 12 January 2020, earning Jović his first trophy as a Real Madrid player.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/51085601|title =Spanish Super Cup: Real Madrid beat Atletico Madrid on penalties|date=12 January 2020|work=BBC Sport|access-date=14 January 2020}}</ref> During an injury riddled season, he made 17 appearances during the league season, while scoring two goals, as Real Madrid won the [[2019–20 La Liga]] title.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/real-madrid/2020/07/17/5f10db2146163f3c2c8b45d7.html|title=Real Madrid win the longest LaLiga Santander season|date=17 July 2020|access-date=17 July 2020|website=Marca|location=Spain}}</ref>

====Loan to Eintracht Frankfurt ====
On 14 January 2021, Jović was loaned back to his former club [[Eintracht Frankfurt]] until the end of the 2020–21 season.<ref>{{cite news|title=Leihe bis Saisonende|url=https://profis.eintracht.de/news/leihe-bis-saisonende-130254|access-date=14 January 2021|publisher=profis.eintracht.de|language=de}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Official Announcement: Jović|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2021/01/14/official-announcement-jovi%C4%87|access-date=14 January 2021|publisher=Real Madric CF}}</ref> On 17 January, in his first match back with his former club, Jović came off the bench and scored two goals within half an hour as Frankfurt defeated [[FC Schalke 04|Schalke]] 3–1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/international-football/eintrach-vs-schalke-04/cronica/2021/01/17/60048f9522601db9258b460e.html|title=Luka Jovic scores two goals in 28 minutes on Frankfurt return!|website=Marca|date=17 January 2021|access-date=19 January 2021}}</ref>

===Fiorentina===
On 8 July 2022, [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]] announced the signing of Jović.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Jovic signs for Fiorentina |url=http://www.acffiorentina.com/en/news/all/news-men-first-team/2022-07-08/jovic-signs-for-fiorentina |access-date=8 July 2022 |website=ACF Fiorentina}}</ref>

==International career==
On 11 December 2013, Jović scored a [[hat-trick]] for [[Serbia national under-17 football team|Serbia U-17]] in a 4–1 win against [[Croatia national under-17 football team|Croatia U-17]].<ref name="Mozzart1">{{cite web|url=http://www.mozzartsport.com/vesti/luka-jovic-terorisao-hrvate-u-pazovi/66885|title=Luka Jović terorisao Hrvate u Pazovi!|language=sr|date=11 December 2013}}</ref> In July 2014, he was called up by youth coach [[Veljko Paunović]] to [[Serbia national under-19 football team|Serbia's U-19]] [[2014 UEFA European Under-19 Championship squads#Serbia|squad]] for the [[2014 UEFA European Under-19 Championship]].<ref>[http://novosti.rs/vesti/sport.294.html:499882-Na-Paunovicevom-spisku-po-cetiri-igraca-Zvezde-Vojvodine-i-OFK-Beograda-tri-Partizana] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190809132743/http://www.novosti.rs/vesti/sport.294.html:499882-Na-Paunovicevom-spisku-po-cetiri-igraca-Zvezde-Vojvodine-i-OFK-Beograda-tri-Partizana|date=9 August 2019}} Novosti: Na Paunovićevom spisku po četiri igrača Zvezde, Vojvodine i OFK Beograda, tri Partizana... (Serbian) 8 July 2014</ref>

In May 2018, Jović was named in [[Serbia national football team|Serbia]]'s preliminary squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.fss.rs/index.php?id=2021&aid=13898 |title=Један тим, срцем свим – Младен Крстајић одабрао |trans-title=One team, with all their hearts – Mladen Krstajić selects |website=FSS.rs |publisher=[[Football Association of Serbia]] |date=24 May 2018 |access-date=24 May 2018 |language=sr}}</ref> On 4 June, he made his international debut in a friendly match against [[Chile national football team|Chile]], coming on as a substitute for [[Aleksandar Mitrović]] in the 84th minute.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2018/06/04/world/friendlies/serbia/chile/2786472/|title=Serbia vs. Chile - 4 June 2018 - Soccerway|website=int.soccerway.com}}</ref> Later in June, Jović was included in the [[2018 FIFA World Cup squads#Serbia|final 23-man squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]],<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-soccer-worldcup-srb-squad/soccer-krstajic-names-serbias-final-23-man-world-cup-squad-idUSKCN1IX5O2 | title=Soccer: Krstajic names Serbia's final 23-man World Cup squad | work=Reuters | date=1 June 2018 | access-date=6 June 2018}}</ref> where he appeared as a late substitute against [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/06/27/sports/world-cup/brazil-vs-serbia.html|title=World Cup: Brazil cruises into next round with easy victory|newspaper=The New York Times|author=Andrew Das|date=27 June 2018|access-date=27 June 2018}}</ref>

On 20 March 2019, Jović scored his debut goal for the national team in a friendly against [[Germany national football team|Germany]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/47644286 |title =Germany 1 Serbia 1|date=20 March 2019|work=BBC Sport|access-date=28 March 2019}}</ref>

In November, 2022 he was selected in Serbia's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fss.rs/en/head-coach-dragan-stojkovic-announces-the-list-of-players-for-the-fifa-world-cup-2022-in-qatar/ |title=Head-coach Dragan Stojković announces the list of players for the FIFA World Cup 2022 in Qatar |publisher=[[Football Association of Serbia]] |date=11 November 2022 |access-date=11 November 2022}}</ref> He played in group stage matches against [[Cameroon national football team|Cameroon]]<ref>{{Cite news |last=Sky Sports |date=2022-11-28 |title=World Cup 2022 - Cameroon 3-3 Serbia: Vincent Aboubakar and Eric Maxim Choupo-Moting goals complete stunning comeback |language=en |work=Sky Sports |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/cameroon-vs-serbia/report/462988#:~:text=World%20Cup%202022%20%2D%20Cameroon%203,Moting%20goals%20complete%20stunning%20comeback&text=Cameroon%20kept%20alive%20their%20faint,with%20Serbia%20in%20Al%2DWakrah. |access-date=2022-11-28}}</ref> and [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]].<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/dec/02/serbia-switzerland-world-cup-group-g-match-report |title=Switzerland advance and Serbia go out as Freuler finish settles stormy contest |first=Nick |last=Ames |newspaper=The Guardian|location=London |date=2 December 2022 |access-date=7 December 2022}}</ref> Serbia finished fourth in the group.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://soccer.nbcsports.com/2022/12/02/world-cup-2022-group-g-brazil-serbia-cameroon-switzerland-rankings-table-standings/ |title=World Cup 2022 Group G: Brazil, Serbia, Cameroon, Switzerland schedule, fixtures, rankings |first=Nicholas |last=Mendola |newspaper=NBC Sports|location=Lusail |date=2 December 2022 |access-date=7 December 2022}}</ref>
