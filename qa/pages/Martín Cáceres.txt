'''José Martín Cáceres Silva''' ({{IPA-es|maɾˈtiŋ ˈkaseɾes}}; born 7 April 1987) is a Uruguayan professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays for [[Major League Soccer]] club [[LA Galaxy]] and the [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay national team]]. Mainly a [[centre-back]], he can also play on either flank, mostly as a [[right-back]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://gianlucadimarzio.com/it/martin-caceres-storia-di-un-guerriero-sfortunato-che-puo-rialzarsi-ancora-con-il-milan|title=Martin Caceres, storia di un guerriero sfortunato. Che può rialzarsi (ancora) con il Milan|trans-title=Martin Caceres, story of a hapless warrior. Who can bounce back (again) with Milan|publisher=Gianluca di Marzio|last=Bonomo|first=Marco|language=it|date=3 February 2017|access-date=17 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.90min.com/posts/6280204-juventus-reportedly-seal-loan-deal-to-bring-lazio-defender-martin-caceres-back-to-the-club|title=Juventus reportedly seal loan deal to bring Lazio defender Martin Caceres back to the club|publisher=90 Min|last=Laurence|first=Wilfred|date=25 January 2019|access-date=17 June 2019}}</ref>

After starting out at [[Defensor Sporting]], he was bought by Spanish club [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]] in 2007. In the following years he competed in Spain and Italy, notably winning six Serie A titles with [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]] and the [[2009 UEFA Champions League Final|2009 Champions League]] with [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]]. He later had a brief spell in England with [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] before returning to Italy in 2017; despite his ability, his career has been limited by several injuries.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://en.as.com/en/2016/09/05/football/1473087236_189611.html|title=Martín Cáceres on Antonio Conte's Chelsea radar|newspaper=[[Diario AS]]|date=5 September 2016|access-date=1 February 2017}}</ref>

An [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay international]] since the age of 20, Cáceres represented the country in four [[FIFA World Cup|World Cups]], three editions of the [[Copa América]] and the [[2013 FIFA Confederations Cup|2013 Confederations Cup]], winning the [[2011 Copa América]].

==Club career==
===Early years===
Born in [[Montevideo]], Cáceres started his career with his local team [[Defensor Sporting|Defensor]], becoming a regular at a young age and competing in one full [[Uruguayan Primera División|Primera División]] season with them. In early February 2007, he signed for Spanish club [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]], with the deal being made effective in July.<ref name=MERCA>{{cite web|url=http://www.mercafutbol.com/noticias/el-villarreal-ficha-martin-caceres-25032|title=El Villarreal ficha a Martín Cáceres|trans-title=Villarreal sign Martín Cáceres|publisher=Merca Fútbol|language=es|date=2 February 2007|access-date=11 February 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.as.com/futbol/articulo/juventud-calidad-futuro-nuevo-submarino/dasftb/20070604dasdaiftb_3/Tes|title=Juventud, calidad y futuro para el nuevo Submarino|trans-title=Youth, talent and future for new Submarine|newspaper=Diario AS|last=Granel|first=Vicent|language=es|date=4 June 2007|access-date=10 March 2014}}</ref>

Upon arriving at Villarreal, Cáceres was immediately sent on loan to fellow [[La Liga]] side [[Recreativo de Huelva|Recreativo]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cadenaser.com/deportes/articulo/recreativo-presenta-lateral-pampa-calvo/csrcsrpor/20070727csrcsrdep_8/Te|title=El Recreativo presenta al lateral Pampa Calvo y al central Cáceres|trans-title=Recreativo present full-back Pampa Calvo and centre-back Cáceres|publisher=[[Cadena SER]]|language=es|date=27 July 2007|access-date=11 February 2013}}</ref> being one of the most used players during [[2007–08 La Liga|the season]] and often partnering [[Beto (footballer, born May 1976)|Beto]] in central defence as the [[Andalusia]]ns managed to retain their division status, finishing in 16th position; on 9 January 2008, he scored against the club that owned his rights in the [[Copa del Rey]] ([[2007–08 Copa del Rey|1–0 home win, but 2–1 loss on aggregate]]),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.submarinoamarillo.net/news.php?extend.185|title=Otro chasco |trans-title=Another letdown|publisher=Submarino Amarillo|language=es|date=9 January 2008|access-date=11 February 2013}}</ref> and never appeared officially for them.

===Barcelona===
Cáceres joined [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] on 4 June 2008, with a reported transfer fee of [[Euro|€]]16.5 million being paid to Villarreal. A buyout clause of €50 million was included in the deal, which ran until June 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fcbarcelona.com/web/english/noticies/futbol/temporada07-08/06/n080604104496.html|title=Agreement for Cáceres with Villarreal|publisher=FC Barcelona|date=4 June 2008|access-date=11 February 2013}}</ref> During [[2008–09 La Liga|his only season]] at the [[Camp Nou]] he featured sparsely, due to both injury and technical decisions.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.diariosdefutbol.com/2009/05/06/martin-caceres-o-sylvinho/|title=¿Martín Cáceres o Sylvinho?|trans-title=Martín Cáceres or Sylvinho?|publisher=Diarios de Fútbol|last=Barba|first=Borja|language=es|date=6 May 2009|access-date=7 February 2017}}</ref>

Even after his recovery, Cáceres only managed to be fourth-choice stopper behind [[Rafael Márquez]], [[Gerard Piqué]] and [[Carles Puyol]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://futbol.as.com/futbol/2009/01/31/mas_futbol/1233386847_850215.html|title=Puyol y Márquez dejan la defensa en emergencia|trans-title=Puyol and Márquez leave the defence on red alert|newspaper=Diario AS|last=Ortiz|first=Fabián|language=es|date=31 January 2009|access-date=1 February 2017}}</ref> He appeared in three games in [[2008–09 UEFA Champions League|the campaign]]'s [[UEFA Champions League]] – two starts, 217 minutes – being an unused [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] in [[2009 UEFA Champions League Final|the final]] against [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] which completed [[Treble (association football)|the treble]] for the [[Catalonia|Catalans]].

===Loan to Juventus===
On 6 August 2009, Barcelona loaned Cáceres to [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]] in [[Serie A]], with the [[Turin]] team having an option to make his stay permanent at the end of [[2009–10 Serie A|the season]] for €11 million, plus €1 million in variables.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fcbarcelona.com/web/english/noticies/futbol/temporada09-10/08/n090806106094.html|title=Cáceres goes to Juventus on loan|publisher=FC Barcelona|date=6 August 2009|access-date=6 August 2009|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130102131030/http://www.fcbarcelona.com/web/english/noticies/futbol/temporada09-10/08/n090806106094.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=2 January 2013}}</ref> He made his debut in a pre-season [[Exhibition game|friendly]] against former team Villarreal the following day, replacing [[Jonathan Zebina]] at right-back at half-time.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://football-italia.net/aug8a.html|title=Friendly: Submarine sink Juve|publisher=Football Italia|date=7 August 2009|access-date=7 August 2009|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090813080217/http://www.football-italia.net/aug8a.html|archive-date=13 August 2009}}</ref>

The league season began well for Cáceres as he made a goal-scoring debut, netting the opener in a 2–0 away win against [[S.S. Lazio|Lazio]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/report?id=278461&cc=5739|title=Lazio 0–2 Juventus|publisher=[[ESPN FC|ESPN Soccernet]]|date=12 September 2009|access-date=11 February 2013}}</ref> He began as a regular under [[Ciro Ferrara]], but his campaign would be ultimately disrupted by a number of injuries, including one in January 2010 that put him out of action for several months.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://bleacherreport.com/articles/374195-arsenal-wengers-newbie-martin-caceres-to-replace-gallas|title=Martin Caceres to replace William Gallas as Arsenal's newbie?|publisher=[[Bleacher Report]]|last=Bhattacharjee|first=Saura|date=6 April 2010|access-date=11 February 2013}}</ref>

===Sevilla===
On 30 August 2010, Cáceres returned to Spain and joined [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]] on a [[2010–11 La Liga|season]]-long loan, with an option to purchase.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fcbarcelona.cat/web/english/noticies/futbol/temporada10-11/08/30/n100830112642.html|title=Cáceres loaned to Sevilla|publisher=FC Barcelona|date=30 August 2010|access-date=2 June 2011|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120730154311/http://www.fcbarcelona.cat/web/english/noticies/futbol/temporada10-11/08/30/n100830112642.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=30 July 2012}}</ref> He featured regularly for the [[Gregorio Manzano]]-led team, especially at right back following the return of [[Abdoulay Konko]] to [[Genoa C.F.C.|Genoa]] in January 2011.

On 1 May 2011, Cáceres was severely injured by [[Michael Jakobsen]] in a match against [[UD Almería|Almería]], suffering a lacerated [[kidney]] following a dangerous challenge. The [[Denmark|Dane]] was given a straight [[Ejection (sports)|red card]], and the [[South America]]n was initially ruled out for the rest of the campaign,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/12/spain/2011/05/04/2471024/sevillas-martin-caceres-ruled-out-for-rest-of-season|title=Sevilla's Martin Caceres ruled out for rest of season|publisher=[[Goal (website)|Goal]]|last=McKnight|first=Gareth|date=4 May 2011|access-date=30 July 2011}}</ref> but unexpectedly recovered for the final two games, with his side finishing fifth and [[2011–12 UEFA Europa League|qualifying]] to the [[UEFA Europa League]].

On 31 May 2011, Sevilla agreed a €3 million deal plus €1.5 million in variables with Barcelona for the permanent move of Cáceres to the [[Ramón Sánchez Pizjuán Stadium]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fcbarcelona.cat/web/english/noticies/futbol/temporada10-11/05/31/n110531117809.html|title=Sevilla exercise option to purchase Cáceres|publisher=FC Barcelona|date=31 May 2011|access-date=2 June 2011|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120730095000/http://www.fcbarcelona.cat/web/english/noticies/futbol/temporada10-11/05/31/n110531117809.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=30 July 2012}}</ref> In late January 2012, however, he returned to Juventus on loan.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.marca.com/2012/01/24/futbol/equipos/sevilla/1327444348.html|title=El Sevilla cede a Cáceres a la Juve|trans-title=Sevilla loan Cáceres to Juve|newspaper=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|language=es|date=24 January 2012|access-date=25 January 2012}}</ref>

===Juventus return===

Milan]] in [[2011–12 Coppa Italia|the semi-finals]] of the [[Coppa Italia]] and scoring both of his team's goals.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en/match/75460/ac-milan-vs-juventus/report|title=AC Milan 1–2 Juventus: Returning hero Caceres strikes double to put Conte's men in sight of Coppa Italia final|publisher=Goal|last=Voakes|first=Kris|date=8 February 2012|access-date=9 February 2012}}</ref> On 25 March he netted his first league goal, heading home an [[Andrea Pirlo]] [[corner kick]] in the 57th minute of a 2–0 home success over [[Inter Milan]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/report/_/id/326919?cc=5739|title=Juve win Derby D'Italia|publisher=ESPN Soccernet|date=25 March 2012|access-date=26 March 2012}}</ref>

Roma|Roma]] in October 2014 which sidelined him for three months. He returned to the starting line-up on 11 January 2015, scoring in a 3–1 defeat of [[S.S.C. Napoli|Napoli]] at the [[Stadio San Paolo]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.juventus.com/juve/it/news/11_gennaio_2015_napoli_juventus_caceres|title=Caceres: "Contento di essere tornato"|trans-title=Caceres: "Happy to be back"|publisher=Juventus F.C|language=it|date=11 January 2015|access-date=12 May 2015}}</ref> On 8 March, however, he fractured his ankle during a training session, requiring surgery; as a result, he was ruled out indefinitely.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://football-italia.net/63594/caceres-fractures-ankle|title=Caceres fractures ankle|publisher=Football Italia|date=8 March 2015|access-date=12 May 2015}}</ref>

Cáceres returned to the starting line-up on 8 August 2015, in Juventus's [[2015 Supercoppa Italiana|2–0 victory]] over Lazio in the [[Supercoppa Italiana]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tuttojuve.com/primo-piano/caceres-vogliamo-la-supercoppa-punto-a-non-farmi-male-i-nuovi-si-stanno-inserendo-bene-247396|title=CACERES: "Vogliamo la Supercoppa. He was alleged to have been over the legal alcohol limit for driving.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.com/juventus/story/2636884/juventus-defender-martin-caceres-in-car-crash-reports|title=Juventus defender Martin Caceres involved in car crash – reports|publisher=ESPN FC|last=Gladwell|first=Ben|date=30 September 2015|access-date=2 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/football/news/juventus-suspend-martin-caceres-after-6544879|title=Juventus suspend Martin Caceres after smashing Ferrari in alleged drink-driving crash|newspaper=[[Daily Mirror]]|last=Corless|first=Liam|date=30 September 2015|access-date=2 October 2015}}</ref>

Cáceres returned to action on 6 January 2016, starting and playing 84 minutes in a 3–0 home win against [[Hellas Verona F.C.|Hellas Verona]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.com/club/juventus/111/blog/post/2781412/paulo-dybala-stars-in-juventus-win-vs-hellas-verona|title=Paulo Dybala the star in Juventus' victory vs. Hellas Verona|publisher=ESPN FC|last=Rzouki|first=Mina|date=6 January 2016|access-date=6 January 2016}}</ref> On 4 February, it was confirmed that he would be sidelined for the rest of [[2015–16 Serie A|the season]] due to the rupture of his right [[Achilles tendon]] during the match the day before against Genoa.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.football-italia.net/79420/season-over-caceres|title=Season over for Caceres|publisher=Football Italia|date=4 February 2016|access-date=4 February 2016}}</ref> In May, Juventus' director of football [[Giuseppe Marotta]] announced that the player's contract would not be renewed, and that he would be leaving the club on 30 June.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.football-italia.net/84970/%E2%80%98caceres-will-leave-juventus%E2%80%99|title=Caceres will leave Juventus|publisher=Football Italia|date=26 May 2016|access-date=3 July 2016}}</ref>

===Southampton===
On 16 February 2017, Cáceres joined [[Premier League]] club [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] as a [[free agent]], signing an initial contract lasting until the end of [[2016–17 Premier League|the season]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/38997912|title=Martin Caceres: Southampton sign ex-Juventus and Barcelona defender|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|date=16 February 2017|access-date=17 February 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://southamptonfc.com/news/2017-02-16/announcement-southampton-sign-martin-caceres|title=Saints sign Cáceres|publisher=Southampton F.C|date=16 February 2017|access-date=17 February 2017}}</ref> Ten days later he was included in a matchday squad for the first time, remaining an unused substitute in [[2017 EFL Cup Final|the final]] of the [[EFL Cup]], a 3–2 loss to [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]].<ref name=EFL>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/39018813|title=Manchester United 3–2 Southampton|publisher=BBC Sport|last=McNulty|first=Phil|date=26 February 2017|access-date=27 February 2017}}</ref>

After three months at [[St Mary's Stadium]], Cáceres appeared in his first and only game for Southampton, starting in a 2–1 away win over [[Middlesbrough F.C.|Middlesbrough]] on 13 May.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/39827552|title=Middlesbrough 1–2 Southampton|publisher=BBC Sport|last=Skelton|first=Jack|date=13 May 2017|access-date=14 May 2017}}</ref> He was released on 25 May.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://southamptonfc.com/news/2017-05-25/southampton-2017-retained-list-players-released|title=Saints release four players|publisher=Southampton F.C|date=25 May 2017|access-date=26 May 2017}}</ref>

===Hellas Verona===
Cáceres returned to Italy in early August 2017, signing for Hellas Verona on a deal running until 30 June 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hellasverona.it/2017/08/04/__1_2_3_4_5_6_7_8|title=Benvenuto Martin Caceres|trans-title=Welcome Martin Caceres|publisher=Hellas Verona F.C|language=it|date=4 August 2017|access-date=5 August 2017}}</ref> However, it was reported that Lazio had already expressed an interest in acquiring his services, but were ultimately unable to as their quota of two non-EU signings from abroad had already been filled. He joined Verona instead as a result, although rumours circulated in the media linking him with a possible move to Lazio in January as there were no restrictions impeding the transfer of non-EU players between Italian clubs.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.corrieredellosport.it/news/calcio/calcio-mercato/2017/07/27-28456244/lazio-caceres_fatta_ma_da_gennaio/|title=Lazio-Caceres: è fatta. Ma da gennaio|trans-title=Lazio-Caceres: deal done. ===Lazio and Juventus===
On 8 January 2018, Cáceres joined [[S.S. Lazio|Lazio]] on a one-year contract with the option for a renewal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/115296/official-lazio-sign-caceres|title=Official: Lazio sign Caceres|publisher=Football Italia|date=8 January 2018|access-date=8 January 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite press release|url=http://www.sslazio.it/it/news/press-release-2/44036-comunicato-08-01-2018|title=Comunicato 08.01.2018|trans-title=Announcement 8 January 2018|publisher=S.S. Lazio|location=Rome|language=it|date=8 January 2018|access-date=8 January 2018}}</ref> He scored his only goal of [[2017–18 Serie A|the campaign]] on 18 April in a 4–3 away defeat of [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sport.sky.it/calcio/serie-a/2018/04/18/fiorentina-lazio-risultato-gol.html|title=Fiorentina-Lazio 3–4: espulsioni, sette reti, spettacolo e polemiche. On 29 January 2019, Juventus announced the signing of Cáceres on a five-month loan, marking this as his third spell at the club.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.juventus.com/en/news/news/2019/welcome-back-martin-caceres.php|title=Welcome back, Martin Caceres!|publisher=Juventus F.C|date=29 January 2019|access-date=29 January 2019}}</ref> He made his first appearance since 2016 on 2 February, in a 3–3 home draw against [[Parma Calcio 1913|Parma]] in the domestic league.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.calciomercato.com/en/news/juve-parma-3-3-ratings-infallible-cr7-caceres-terrible-debut-ger-99305|title=Juve-Parma 3–3, ratings: infallible CR7, Caceres' terrible debut, Gervinho MVP|publisher=Calcio Mercato|last=Bettoni|first=Lorenzo|date=2 February 2019|access-date=4 February 2019}}</ref> 

Cáceres left Juventus at the end of [[2018–19 Serie A|the season]], when his contract with Lazio also expired.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/139522/caceres-confirms-juve-exit|title=Caceres confirms Juve exit|publisher=Football Italia|date=17 June 2019|access-date=2 July 2019}}</ref>

===Fiorentina===
On 30 August 2019, Cáceres joined Fiorentina on a one-year deal with the option for a further season.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/143574/caceres-arrives-fiorentina|title=Caceres arrives for Fiorentina|publisher=Football Italia|date=30 August 2019|access-date=30 August 2019}}</ref> The following 18 May, he declared that he had tested positive for [[Coronavirus disease 2019|COVID-19]] for 60 days.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.gazzetta.it/Calcio/Serie-A/Fiorentina/18-05-2020/caceres-miei-60-giorni-il-virus-ora-mi-sento-uomo-nuovo-3701080742464_amp.shtml|title=Caceres: "I miei 60 giorni con il virus, ora mi sento un uomo nuovo"|trans-title=Caceres: "My 60 days with the virus, now I feel like a new man"|newspaper=[[La Gazzetta dello Sport]]|last=Sardelli|first=Giovanni|language=it|date=18 May 2020|access-date=26 May 2020}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:Uruguay - Costa Rica FIFA World Cup 2014 – Martín Cáceres.jpg|thumb|Cáceres in action at the [[2014 FIFA World Cup|2014 World Cup]]]]
Cáceres played for [[Uruguay national under-20 football team|Uruguay]] at the [[2007 FIFA U-20 World Cup]] held in Canada, appearing in all the matches in an eventual [[Single-elimination tournament|round-of-16]] exit.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.teradeportes.com/node/33277|title=Uruguay sufre una derrota inmerecida en el mundial sub 20|trans-title=Uruguay with loss they did not deserve at under 20 World Cup|publisher=Tera Deportes|language=es|date=11 July 2007|access-date=1 September 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.elobservador.com.uy/nota/la-generacion-dorada-de-la-seleccion-uruguaya-20201112112426|title=La generación dorada de la selección uruguaya|trans-title=The Uruguay national team's golden generation|newspaper=El Observador|language=es|date=12 November 2020|access-date=1 September 2022}}</ref> Previously, he was named the best defender at the [[2007 South American U-20 Championship]], helping his team to third place.<ref name=MERCA/>

Cáceres made his debut for [[Uruguay national football team|the full side]] on 12 September 2007, in [[Johannesburg]] against [[South Africa national football team|South Africa]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.eluniverso.com/2007/09/12/0001/15/7D2BB7BAE2C24A8391DA7F7C6B668223.html/|title=Uruguay empata 0–0 con Sudáfrica en amistoso|trans-title=Uruguay draw 0–0 with South Africa in friendly|newspaper=[[El Universo]]|language=es|date=12 September 2007|access-date=1 September 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.auf.org.uy/debut-de-mart-iacute-n-c-aacute-ceres/|title=Debut de Martín Cáceres|trans-title=Martín Cáceres' debut|publisher=[[Uruguayan Football Association]]|language=es|date=12 September 2017|access-date=1 September 2022}}</ref> In 2010, he was selected by manager [[Óscar Tabárez]] for his list of 23 players for [[2010 FIFA World Cup|that year]]'s [[FIFA World Cup]],<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.clarin.com/deportes/futbol/Tabarez-confirmo-Uruguay_0_Sk1sbCDXl.html|title=Tabárez confirmó a los 23 de Uruguay|trans-title=Tabárez confirmed Uruguay's 23|newspaper=[[Clarín (Argentine newspaper)|Clarín]]|language=es|date=29 May 2010|access-date=1 September 2022}}</ref> as back-up to [[FC Porto|Porto]]'s [[Jorge Fucile]] at right-back<ref>{{cite news|url=https://elpais.com/deportes/2010/07/05/actualidad/1278314515_850215.html|title=Juegan todos menos Cáceres|trans-title=Everybody plays but Cáceres|newspaper=[[El País]]|language=es|date=5 July 2010|access-date=1 September 2022}}</ref> and taking part in the semi-finals against the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]] and the third-place match against [[Germany national football team|Germany]] (both 3–2 losses).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/world_cup_2010/matches/match_61/default.stm|title=Uruguay 2–3 Netherlands|publisher=BBC Sport|last=Bevan|first=Chris|date=6 July 2010|access-date=1 September 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/world_cup_2010/matches/match_63/default.stm|title=Uruguay 2–3 Germany|publisher=BBC Sport|last=Ornstein|first=David|date=10 July 2010|access-date=1 September 2022}}</ref>

Cáceres scored his first international goal on 23 June 2011, in a 3–0 friendly home win over [[Estonia national football team|Estonia]].<ref name=First>{{cite web|url=http://espndeportes.espn.go.com/futbol/cronica?id=317586|title=Uruguay goleó a Estonia, a poco del comienzo de la Copa América|trans-title=Uruguay routed Estonia, shortly after start of Copa América|publisher=[[ESPN Deportes]]|language=es|date=23 June 2011|access-date=22 February 2014}}</ref> With Fucile not being selected to the [[2011 Copa América]] in Argentina, he played five out of six games during the tournament – as right and left back – with the ''Charrúas'' winning their 15th continental tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://espndeportes.espn.go.com/news/story?id=1337302|title=Uruguay: dos cambios ante Argentina|trans-title=Uruguay: two changes against Argentina|publisher=ESPN Deportes|language=es|date=15 July 2011|access-date=22 February 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.orgullodenervion.com/noticias/sevilla-fc/sevilla-temporada-20112012-caceres-campeon-copa-america-uruguay.html|title=Cáceres, campeón de la Copa América|trans-title=Cáceres, Copa América champion|publisher=Orgullo de Nervión|language=es|date=24 July 2011|access-date=22 February 2014}}</ref><ref name=CA>{{cite web|url=http://en.mercopress.com/2011/07/25/uruguay-celebrates-well-into-early-monday-its-record-winning-15th-copa-america|title=Uruguay celebrates well into early Monday its record winning 15th Copa America|publisher=Merco Press|date=25 July 2011|access-date=22 February 2014}}</ref> He notably scored the decisive penalty in a [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shootout]] victory over [[Argentina national football team|the hosts]] in the quarter-finals.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2011/jul/16/argentina-uruguay-live-copa-america|title=Argentina v Uruguay – as it happened|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|date=16 July 2011|access-date=12 May 2015}}</ref>

Cáceres made four appearances for Uruguay in the [[2013 FIFA Confederations Cup]]. In the third-place match against [[Italy national football team|Italy]], his penalty was saved by Juventus teammate [[Gianluigi Buffon]] as the opposition won the shootout by 3–2 after a 2–2 deadlock following extra time.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/confederationscup/matches/round=255209/match=300222463/summary.html|title=Buffon stars as Italy win shootout & bronze|publisher=FIFA|date=30 June 2013|access-date=30 June 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140709035458/http://www.fifa.com/confederationscup/matches/round%3D255209/match%3D300222463/summary.html|archive-date=9 July 2014}}</ref> He was also a member of the squad that took part at the [[2014 FIFA World Cup|2014 World Cup]],<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/world-cup/uruguay-world-cup-squad-profile-2018-fixtures-group-team-news-schedule-dates-times-odds-tv-channel-a8381781.html|title=Uruguay World Cup squad guide: Full fixtures, group, ones to watch, odds and more|newspaper=[[The Independent]]|last=Watson|first=Jack|date=4 June 2018|access-date=17 June 2019}}</ref> featuring four times in the tournament to help the nation reach the last-16 phase, where they suffered a 2–0 defeat to [[Colombia national football team|Colombia]] at the [[Maracanã Stadium]] on 28 June.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://uk.eurosport.yahoo.com/news/world-cup-colombia-uruguay-matchpack-will-suarez-prove-072519200--sow.html|title=World Cup&nbsp;– Genius Rodriguez fires Colombia past toothless Uruguay|publisher=Eurosport|last=Masefield|first=Fraser|date=28 June 2014|access-date=29 June 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140714183312/https://uk.eurosport.yahoo.com/news/world-cup-colombia-uruguay-matchpack-will-suarez-prove-072519200--sow.html|archive-date=14 July 2014}}</ref>

Due to an ankle injury suffered in March 2015, Cáceres was excluded from the [[2015 Copa América]] squad.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eurosport.com/football/copa-america/2015/injury-keeps-martin-caceres-out-of-uruguay-s-copa-america-squad_sto4724113/story.shtml|title=Injury keeps Martin Caceres out of Uruguay's Copa America squad|publisher=Eurosport|date=11 May 2015|access-date=12 May 2015}}</ref> A serious Achilles tendon ailment in February 2016 also prevented his participation in the [[Copa América Centenario]] the following summer.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://futbol.as.com/futbol/2016/02/05/internacional/1454687914_614873.html|title=Martín Cáceres dice adiós a la temporada: 5 meses de baja|trans-title=Martín Cáceres says goodbye to the season: out for 5 months|newspaper=Diario AS|language=es|date=5 February 2016|access-date=6 June 2016}}</ref>

Cáceres was included in Uruguay's final 23-man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018 World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.worldsoccer.com/world-cup/uruguay-world-cup-fixtures-399702|title=Uruguay World Cup fixtures, squad, group, guide|publisher=[[World Soccer (magazine)|World Soccer]]|last=Tremlett|first=Sam|date=15 June 2018|access-date=15 June 2018}}</ref> He played all the matches and minutes in a quarter-final exit.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.leggo.it/sport/calcio/lazio_caceres_inzaghi_uruguay-3852379.html|title=Super Caceres, Inzaghi lo promuove|trans-title=Super Caceres, Inzaghi promotes him|newspaper=[[Leggo]]|last=Sarzanini|first=Enrico|language=it|date=2018|access-date=22 February 2019}}</ref>

In March 2019, Tabárez named Cáceres in the squad for the [[2019 Copa América]] in Brazil.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://copaamerica2019.live/uruguay-team-squads/|title=Uruguay team squads Copa America 2019|publisher=Copa América 2019 Live|date=7 March 2019|access-date=17 June 2019}}</ref> On 16 June, he provided the [[Assist (association football)|assist]] to [[Luis Suárez]]'s goal in a 4–0 win over [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador]] in the team's opening group match of the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/48655964|title=Uruguay 4–0 Ecuador|publisher=BBC Sport|date=17 June 2019|access-date=17 June 2019}}</ref> He appeared four times in total throughout the tournament, including the quarter-final tie against [[Peru national football team|Peru]] in which his team were eliminated from the competition after losing 5–4 on penalties, following a 0–0 draw after regulation time.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/48777158|title=Uruguay 0–0 Peru|publisher=BBC Sport|date=29 June 2019|access-date=30 June 2019}}</ref> 

Cáceres earned his 100th [[Cap (sport)|cap]] on 13 October 2020, in a 4–2 loss to Ecuador in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONMEBOL)|2022 World Cup qualifiers]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.elobservador.com.uy/nota/tabarez-confirmo-el-equipo-con-tres-cambios-araujo-nandez-y-gomez-20201013171648|title=Ecuador revolcó a Uruguay en la altura: 4–2|trans-title=Ecuador steamrolled Uruguay in heights: 4–2|newspaper=El Observador|language=es|date=13 October 2020|access-date=20 October 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.elobservador.com.uy/nota/caceres-llego-a-100-partidos-la-mitad-del-plantel-tiene-menos-de-tres-anos-en-la-seleccion-2020101319298|title=Cáceres llegó a 100 partidos; la mitad del plantel tiene menos de tres años en la selección|trans-title=Cáceres reached 100 matches; half of the squad has less than three years in national team|newspaper=El Observador|language=es|date=13 October 2020|access-date=20 October 2020}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Cáceres's primary position is that of a [[Defender (association football)#Centre-back|central defender]], but he can also operate as a [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|left or right-back]], being noted for his pace, strength, tackling and aerial ability.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.gazzetta.it/Calcio/Calciomercato/22-07-2009/fonseca-caceres-juve-50832581612.shtml|title=Fonseca: "Caceres alla Juve" – Ecco chi è l'uruguaiano del Barça|trans-title=Fonseca: "Caceres to Juve" – Here is Barça's Uruguayan|newspaper=La Gazzetta dello Sport|language=it|date=22 July 2009|access-date=13 September 2014}}</ref> He has also been deployed as a [[Defender (association football)#Wing-back|wing-back]] or [[Midfielder#Winger|winger]], on either side of the pitch.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.gazzetta.it/Calcio/Squadre/Juventus/09-02-2012/juve-segreto-difesa-rinforzo-caceres-fa-pure-gol-81288295770.shtml|title=Juve, il segreto è la difesa – E il rinforzo Caceres fa pure gol|trans-title=Juve, the secret is in the defence – And the reinforcement Caceres even scores|newspaper=La Gazzetta dello Sport|last=Pratesi|first=Riccardo|language=it|date=9 February 2012|access-date=2 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/127362/lyon-caceres-talks|title=Lyon in Caceres talks?|publisher=Football Italia|date=31 August 2018|access-date=2 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/50932/caceres-i-know-italy-secrets|title=Caceres: 'I know Italy secrets'|publisher=Football Italia|date=22 June 2014|access-date=2 October 2018}}</ref>

During his time with Juventus, Cáceres' tenacious approach drew comparisons to compatriot Montero, who also played there.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.tuttosport.com/calcio/calciomercato/2009/07/16-33876/Barcellona-Juventus%2C+verso+lo+scambio+Poulsen-Caceres|title=Barcellona-Juventus, verso lo scambio Poulsen-Caceres|trans-title=Barcelona-Juventus, toward Poulsen-Caceres exchange|newspaper=Tuttosport|language=it|date=16 July 2009|access-date=13 September 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006070600/http://www.tuttosport.com/calcio/calciomercato/2009/07/16-33876/Barcellona-Juventus%2C+verso+lo+scambio+Poulsen-Caceres|archive-date=6 October 2014}}</ref>
