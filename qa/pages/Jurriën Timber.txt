'''Jurriën David Norman Timber''' (born 17 June 2001) is a Dutch professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]] for [[Eredivisie]] club [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]] and the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands national team]]. Mainly a [[centre-back]], he can also play as a [[right-back]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.vi.nl/nieuws/ten-hag-stelt-klaiber-teleur-en-verhuist-timber-naar-rechts |title=Ten Hag stelt Klaiber teleur en verhuist Timber naar rechts |trans-title=Ten Hag disappoints Klaiber and moves Timber to the right |website=vi.nl |language=nl |date=3 March 2021 |access-date=24 May 2022}}</ref>

Timber joined Ajax's youth academy in 2014, from [[Feyenoord]]. He made his first-team debut in March 2020 and has won two Eredivisie titles and one [[KNVB Cup]]. He won the [[List of AFC Ajax records and statistics#AFC Ajax prize winners|Marco van Basten Award]] in 2022.

Timber made 29 appearances for the Netherlands at youth international level. He was a member of the [[Netherlands national under-17 football team|under-17]] team that won the [[2018 UEFA European Under-17 Championship]]. He made his senior international debut in June 2021.

==Club career==
===Youth career===
Timber started playing for [[Utrecht]]-based club DVSU at the age of four, before joining [[Feyenoord]]'s youth system when he was six.<ref name="earlycareer">{{cite news |url=https://nos.nl/artikel/2294521-jurrien-en-quinten-timber-de-nieuwe-frank-en-ronald-bij-ajax |title=Jurriën en Quinten Timber, de nieuwe Frank en Ronald bij Ajax |trans-title=Jurriën and Quinten Timber, the new Frank and Ronald at Ajax |website=nos.nl |language=nl |date=22 July 2019 |access-date=20 November 2022}}</ref> In 2014, he joined [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]], where he signed his first professional contract in 2018.<ref name="earlycareer"/>

===Ajax===
Timber made his senior debut for Ajax in a 3–1 win over [[SC Heerenveen]] on 7 March 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tellerreport.com/sports/2020-03-07---ajax-starts-with-traor%C3%A9-and-debutant-timber-against-heerenveen-.H1ml9y_WHL.html|title=Ajax starts with Traoré and debutant Timber against Heerenveen|website=Teller Report|date=7 March 2020|accessdate=3 May 2021}}</ref>

He scored his first career goal for Ajax against [[FC Emmen|Emmen]] on 2 May 2021.<ref name=ERE /> It was the opening goal in an eventual 4–0 win. That result confirmed Ajax as [[2020–21 Eredivisie|Eredivisie]] champions for a record 35th time.<ref name=ERE />

==International career==
Timber played youth international football for the Netherlands at under-15,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.onsoranje.nl/teams/185192/uitslagen |title=Jeugd mannen onder 15 uitslagen |trans-title=Youth men under 15 results |website=onsoranje.nl |language=nl |access-date=20 November 2022}}</ref> [[Netherlands national under-16 football team|under-16]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.onsoranje.nl/teams/185191/speler/18786 |title=Jurrien Maduro Jeugd mannen onder 16 |trans-title=Jurrien Maduro Youth men under 16 |website=onsoranje.nl |language=nl |access-date=20 November 2022}}</ref> [[Netherlands national under-17 football team|under-17]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.onsoranje.nl/teams/184965/speler/18786 |title=Jurrien Timber Jeugd mannen onder 17 |trans-title=Jurrien Timber Youth men under 17 |website=onsoranje.nl |language=nl |access-date=20 November 2022}}</ref> [[Netherlands national under-19 football team|under-19]]<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.onsoranje.nl/teams/185189/speler/18786 |title=Jurrien Timber Jeugd mannen onder 19 |trans-title=Jurrien Timber Youth men under 19 |website=onsoranje.nl |language=nl |access-date=20 November 2022}}</ref> and [[Netherlands national under-21 football team|under-21]] levels.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.onsoranje.nl/teams/185185/speler/18786 |title=Jurrien Timber Jong Oranje |trans-title=Jurrien Timber Young Orange |website=onsoranje.nl |language=nl |access-date=20 November 2022}}</ref> He was selected for the senior [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]] squad for [[UEFA Euro 2020]]. He made his debut for the team on 2 June 2021 in a friendly against [[Scotland national football team|Scotland]], as a starter.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[ESPN]]|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/lineups?gameId=600284|title=Netherlands v Scotland game report|date=2 June 2021}}</ref>

Timber was included in the final selection of the Netherlands team for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] by manager [[Louis van Gaal]] in November 2022.<ref>{{cite tweet|number=1583413124987658240|user=OnsOranje|title=🦁 𝐏𝐫𝐨𝐯𝐢𝐬𝐢𝐨𝐧𝐚𝐥 𝐒𝐪𝐮𝐚𝐝: This is the longlist of 39 players where our final World Cup 2022 squad will be selected from! 📋 |date=21 October 2022}}</ref> After missing the opening [[2022 FIFA World Cup Group A|group stage]] win against [[Senegal national football team|Senegal]],<ref>{{Cite news |title=Late goals give Dutch victory over Senegal |website=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63619381 |access-date=29 November 2022}}</ref> he played 90 minutes in the 1–1 draw against [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador]]<ref>{{Cite news |title=Valencia scores as Ecuador draw with Netherlands |website=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63672202 |access-date=29 November 2022}}</ref> and the 2–0 win against [[Qatar national football team|Qatar]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=Gakpo nets again as Dutch beat Qatar to top group |website=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63711993 |access-date=29 November 2022}}</ref> He again played 90 minutes in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Round of 16|round of 16]] win against the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]]<ref>{{Cite news |title=Dutch overcome USA to book quarter-final spot |website=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63765128 |access-date=3 December 2022}}</ref> as well as the entirety of the [[2022 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Quarter-finals|quarter-final]] match against [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]], which the Netherlands eventually lost on [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalties]] and so were subsequently eliminated from the tournament.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Argentina beat the Netherlands in penalty shootout to reach semi-finals |website=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63830319 |access-date=9 December 2022}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Born in the Netherlands, Timber and his twin brother [[Quinten Timber]], who is also a footballer, are of Aruban and Curaçaoan descent. Their mother Marilyn is from Aruba and their father is from Curaçao, both part of the [[ABC islands (Leeward Antilles)|ABC Islands]] in the [[Dutch Caribbean]]. Due to past situations, the family took on their maternal name Timber instead of taking the last name of their father Maduro.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ajaxshowtime.com/article/hoofdnieuws/120799/het-verhaal-achter-de-gebroeders-timber|title=Het Verhaal Achter: De gebroeders Timber|website=Ajax Showtime|date=8 March 2020 }}</ref> The twins also have three older brothers Shamier, Chris, and [[Dylan Timber|Dylan]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ajax.nl/streams/jeugd/we-zijn-gewoon-de-nieuwe-timbers.htm|title='We zijn gewoon de nieuwe Timbers'|website=Ajax}}</ref>
