'''Thomas Alun Lockyer''' (born 3 December 1994) is a Welsh professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] for {{English football updater|LutonTow}} club [[Luton Town F.C.|Luton Town]] and the [[Wales national football team|Wales national team]].

==Club career==
===Bristol Rovers===
[[File:Tom Lockyer 30-04-2016 1.jpg|thumb|upright|Lockyer playing for [[Bristol Rovers F.C.|Bristol Rovers]] in 2016]]

Lockyer was born and raised in [[Cardiff]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bristolrovers.co.uk/news/2016/january/tom-lockyer-named-football-league-young-player-of-the-month/ |title=Tom Lockyer named Football League Young Player of the Month |publisher=Bristol Rovers F.C. |date=17 January 2016 |access-date=3 September 2020}}</ref> Lockyer started his career at Radyr Rangers.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://cardiffjournalism.co.uk/thecardiffian/2023/01/27/children-football-training-radyr-pitch-repairs/ |title=The Cardiffian |publisher=Bristol Rovers F.C. |date=27 January 2023 |access-date=27 May 2023}}</ref> He joined [[Cardiff City F.C.|Cardiff City]] as a youth aged 11, but was released aged 16, as Cardiff deemed him to be too small to play his preferred position of [[centre-back]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/35327424 |title=Tom Lockyer: Bristol Rovers defender on relegation & promotion |first=Nabil |last=Hassan |website=BBC Sport |date=17 January 2016 |access-date=5 January 2018}}</ref> Lockyer then signed a scholarship for [[Bristol Rovers F.C.|Bristol Rovers]] in 2011<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bristolrovers.co.uk/team/player-profile/tom-lockyer/4 |title=Bristol Rovers FC Player Profiles: Tom Lockyer |publisher=Bristol Rovers F.C. |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170503010649/http://www.bristolrovers.co.uk/team/player-profile/tom-lockyer/4 |archive-date=3 May 2017}}</ref> and went on to make his debut on 12 January 2013, replacing [[Ellis Harrison]], 85 minutes into a 3–0 win over [[Fleetwood Town F.C.|Fleetwood Town]] at [[Highbury Stadium (Fleetwood)|Highbury Stadium]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/20919199 |title=Fleetwood 0–3 Bristol Rovers |website=BBC Sport |date=12 January 2013 |access-date=5 January 2018}}</ref> He made his home debut two months later on 12 March 2013, replacing [[Oliver Norburn]] in the 74th minute in a 2–0 win over [[Port Vale F.C.|Port Vale]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/21665738 |title=Bristol Rovers 2–0 Port Vale |website=BBC Sport |date=12 March 2013 |access-date=5 January 2018}}</ref> He signed his first professional contract in May 2013, after making his previous appearances whilst still a youth team player.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bristolrovers.co.uk/news/2013/may/tom-lockyer-signs/ |title=Tom Lockyer signs |publisher=Bristol Rovers F.C. |date=13 May 2013 |access-date=5 January 2018}}</ref>

Lockyer scored his first league goal in a 1–0 win for Bristol Rovers over [[Northampton Town F.C.|Northampton Town]] on 31 August 2013.<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/23822812|title=Bristol Rovers 1–0 Northampton Town|publisher=BBC Sport|date=31 August 2013|accessdate=21 February 2022}}</ref>

On 17 March 2014, Lockyer signed a new two-year contract extension with Rovers.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bristolrovers.co.uk/news/2014/march/tom-lockyer-signs-contract-extension/ |title=Tom Lockyer signs contract extension |publisher=Bristol Rovers F.C. |date=17 March 2014 |access-date=5 January 2018}}</ref> After Rovers were [[Promotion and relegation|relegated]] out of [[EFL League Two|League Two]] in the [[2013–14 Bristol Rovers F.C. season|2013–14 season]], Lockyer became a very important part of a very successful season after seeing the club get promoted first time of asking. In that season he managed to score one goal vs [[Grimsby Town F.C.|Grimsby Town]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/31423683 |title=Grimsby Town 0–1 Bristol Rovers |website=BBC Sport |date=14 February 2015 |access-date=5 January 2018}}</ref> As Rovers returned to League Two, he was a first-team regular and was rewarded by being named the [[Football League Young Player of the Month]] for December 2015 after consistent good performances.<ref name=YPOTM>{{cite web |url=https://www.efl.com/news/2016/january/tom-lockyer-named-football-league-young-player-of-the-month/ |title=Tom Lockyer named Football League Young Player of the Month |publisher=The Football League |date=17 January 2016 |access-date=5 January 2018}}</ref>

He made his 200th appearance for Rovers on 19 August 2017, in a 3–2 victory over [[Bury F.C.|Bury]], in which he scored the opener.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bristolrovers.co.uk/news/2017/august/lockyerpostbury/ |title=Reaction: Tom Lockyer post Bury |publisher=Bristol Rovers F.C. |date=20 August 2017 |access-date=26 August 2017}}</ref>

He departed Bristol Rovers following the expiration of his contract at the end of the [[2018–19 Bristol Rovers F.C. season|2018–19 season]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/48200414 |title=Bristol Rovers: Chris Lines heads list of nine players to leave League One club |website=BBC Sport |date=8 May 2019 |access-date=14 September 2020}}</ref>

===Charlton Athletic===
Lockyer joined [[Charlton Athletic F.C.|Charlton Athletic]], who were newly promoted to the [[EFL Championship|Championship]], on 28 June 2019 on a two-year contract.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/48803798 |title=Charlton Athletic: Chuks Aneke and Tom Lockyer join on two-year contracts |website=BBC Sport |date=28 June 2019 |access-date=14 September 2020}}</ref> His first and only goal for the club was the equaliser in a 2–2 home draw with [[West Bromwich Albion F.C.|West Bromwich Albion]] on 11 January 2020.<ref name=sb1920/> After playing every minute of 43 league appearances in the [[2019–20 Charlton Athletic F.C. season|2019–20 season]] and missing only three matches due to [[Suspension (punishment)#Sport|suspension]], Lockyer triggered a relegation release clause in his contract in August 2020, allowing him to join another club for free.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://londonnewsonline.co.uk/exclusive-tom-lockyer-activates-release-clause-at-charlton-athletic/ |title=Exclusive: Tom Lockyer activates release clause at Charlton Athletic |first=Richard |last=Cawley |newspaper=South London Press |date=10 August 2020 |access-date=14 September 2020}}</ref>

===Luton Town===
Lockyer signed for another Championship club, [[Luton Town F.C.|Luton Town]], on 1 September 2020 on a [[Free transfer (association football)|free transfer]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.lutontoday.co.uk/sport/football/luton-town/town-chief-thrilled-sign-ambitious-lockyer-addicks-2958373 |title=Town chief thrilled to sign 'ambitious' Lockyer from Addicks |first=Mike |last=Simmonds |website=Luton Today |publisher=JPIMedia Publishing |date=1 September 2020 |access-date=14 September 2020}}</ref> His debut came two weeks later in a 1–0 [[EFL Cup]] second round victory over [[Reading F.C.|Reading]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.lutontoday.co.uk/sport/football/luton-town/hatters-rated-reading-0-luton-town-1-2972871 |title=Hatters rated: Reading 0 Luton Town 1 |first=Mike |last=Simmonds |website=Luton Today |publisher=JPIMedia Publishing |date=16 September 2020 |access-date=31 March 2021}}</ref> In January 2022, Lockyer scored his first goal for the club with his side's first in a 2–1 win over [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]],<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/59715162|title=Luton Town 2–1 Bristol City: Luton keep up good form with City win|publisher=BBC Sport|date=25 January 2022|accessdate=21 February 2022}}</ref> saying after the match that he had dreamed of scoring against his old rivals.<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.bristolpost.co.uk/sport/football/former-bristol-rovers-defender-dreamed-6551264|title=Former Bristol Rovers defender dreamed of scoring against Bristol City as he makes cheeky claim|publisher=Bristol Post|last1=Piercy|first1=James|date=26 January 2022|accessdate=21 February 2022}}</ref>

During the [[2022–23 Luton Town F.C. season|2022–23 season]], Lockyer continued to grow as a player, earning the captaincy following an injury to [[Sonny Bradley]]. As the season developed, he earned comparisons from his management team to legendary defenders [[Franco Baresi]]<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.lutontoday.co.uk/sport/football/luton-town/town-defender-likened-to-italy-and-ac-milan-legend-franco-baresi-by-hatters-assistant-4064690|title=Town defender likened to Italy and AC Milan legend Franco Baresi by Hatters assistant|publisher=Luton Today|last1=Simmonds|first1=Mike|date=15 March 2023|accessdate=5 April 2023}}</ref> and [[Franz Beckenbauer]].<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.lutontoday.co.uk/sport/football/luton-town/first-baresi-now-hatters-defender-lockyer-is-compared-to-bayern-munich-and-germany-legend-beckenbauer-4091215|title=First Baresi - now Hatters defender Lockyer is compared to Bayern Munich and Germany legend Beckenbauer|publisher=Luton Today|last1=Simmonds|first1=Mike|date=4 April 2023|accessdate=5 April 2023}}</ref> On 23 April 2023 Lockyer was named in the EFL Championship Team of the Season.<ref>[https://www.efl.com/news/2023/april/efl-team-of-the-season-line-ups-revealed/ EFL Team of the Season]</ref> At Luton's end of season awards, he took home five awards, most notably the [[Luton Town F.C. Player of the Season|Player of the Season award]].<ref name="22/23 POTS"> {{cite web|url=https://www.lutontown.co.uk/news/2023/may/ltst-awards/|title=LTST Presentation Evening 2023 round-up|website=www.lutontown.co.uk|date=4 May 2023|accessdate=4 May 2023}}</ref> On 16 May 2023, he scored Luton's all important second goal to defeat [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]] and send the club to [[Wembley Stadium]] for the [[2023 EFL Championship play-off final|Championship play-off final]].<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/65529573|title=Luton Town 2–0 Sunderland (Agg 3–2): Hatters power through to play-off final|publisher=BBC Sport|date=16 May 2023|accessdate=17 May 2023}}</ref> 

Lockyer was in the starting line-up for the [[2023 EFL Championship play-off final|2023 Championship play-off final]] against [[Coventry City F.C.|Coventry City]], but was stretchered off the pitch after collapsing in the 11th minute of the first half and taken to hospital. During Luton's victory celebrations following the penalty shoot-out, the Luton players paraded a Lockyer named playing shirt as they received the trophy and the club posted a photo of Lockyer celebrating in his hospital bed. Luton’s manager [[Rob Edwards (footballer, born 1982)|Rob Edwards]] admitted after the game that it felt wrong to celebrate and his thoughts were primarily with Lockyer.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11688/12890801/luton-promoted-to-premier-league-tom-lockyer-celebrates-from-hospital-after-collapsing-at-wembley |title=Luton promoted to Premier League: Tom Lockyer celebrates from hospital after collapsing at Wembley |publisher=Sky Sports |date=27 May 2023 }}</ref> On 31 May, it was confirmed by the club that he would be leaving hospital the following day,<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.lutontown.co.uk/news/2023/may/tom-lockyer/|title=Tom Lockyer to return home tomorrow!|website=www.lutontown.co.uk|date=31 May 2023|accessdate=1 June 2023}}</ref> with a follow-up report a week later stating that Lockyer had suffered [[atrial fibrillation]] of the heart, but given the all clear to resume his playing career following surgery. On 6 July, it was confirmed that Lockyer had renewed his contract with Luton Town, one week after his previous contract ran out.<ref>[https://www.lutontown.co.uk/news/2023/july/lockyer-2023-deal Lockyer 2023 Deal, LutonTown.co.uk]</ref>

==International career==
In October 2015, Lockyer made his [[Wales national under-21 football team|Wales under-21]] debut in a 0–0 draw against [[Denmark national under-21 football team|Denmark]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/34482055 |title=Denmark U21 0–0 Wales U21 |website=BBC Sport |date=9 October 2015 |access-date=5 January 2018}}</ref>

Lockyer was called up to the [[Wales national football team|Welsh senior team]] in June 2017, remaining an unused [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] during a 1–1 draw with [[Serbia national football team|Serbia]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40147354 |title=Serbia 1–1 Wales |first=Dafydd |last=Pritchard |website=BBC Sport |date=11 June 2017 |access-date=5 January 2018}}</ref> He received his second call-up for the senior squad on 25 August 2017, for the upcoming qualifiers against [[Austria national football team|Austria]] and [[Moldova national football team|Moldova]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bristolrovers.co.uk/news/2017/august/18.08.17---locks-wales/ |title=Tom Lockyer called up to Wales senior squad |publisher=Bristol Rovers F.C. |date=25 August 2017 |access-date=26 August 2017}}</ref> He made his debut for the senior team on 14 November 2017 as a half-time substitute during a 1–1 draw with [[Panama national football team|Panama]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41884895 |title=Wales 1–1 Panama |first=Dafydd |last=Pritchard |website=BBC Sport |date=14 November 2017 |access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref> In May 2021 he was selected for the Wales squad for the delayed [[UEFA Euro 2020]] tournament.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57301006|title=Uncapped Colwill in Wales Euro 2020 squad|work=BBC Sport }}</ref>

On 9 November 2022, more than a year since he last played for his country, Lockyer was called up to the Wales squad for the upcoming [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63545446|title=World Cup 2022: Wales name injury doubt Joe Allen in 26-man squad|publisher=BBC Sport|date=9 November 2022|accessdate=9 November 2022}}</ref>
