'''Andrew James Redmayne''' (born 13 January 1989) is an Australian professional [[soccer player]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Sydney FC]] in the [[A-League]] and the [[Australia men's national soccer team|Australia men's national team]].

Redmayne was born in [[Gosford]] and played youth football with the [[Australian Institute of Sport Football Program]] before starting his professional career with [[Central Coast Mariners FC|Central Coast Mariners]]. After moving to [[Brisbane Roar FC|Brisbane Roar]] in 2010, Redmayne moved to [[Melbourne City FC|Melbourne Heart]] (now City) in 2012. In 2015, he transferred to [[Western Sydney Wanderers FC|Western Sydney Wanderers]].

Redmayne has appeared for Australia at the [[Australia national under-20 football team|U-20]], [[Australia national under-23 football team|U-23]], and senior levels.

==Early life==
Redmayne was born in [[Gosford]], on the [[Central Coast (New South Wales)|Central Coast]] of [[New South Wales]].

==Club career==
===NSWIS and AIS===
Redmayne played youth football with both the New South Wales Institute of Sport and the [[Australian Institute of Sport Football Program|Australian Institute of Sport]]. As a result of a NSWIS tour of England, Redmayne had a short trial at [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal FC]]. Despite an initial offer of a move to England, Arsenal withdrew their offer after signing 15-year-old [[Wojciech Szczęsny]] instead. His time at the AIS included two seasons in the [[Victorian Premier League]].<ref name=ais/><ref name=aisfp/><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.smh.com.au/sport/soccer/western-sydney-wanderers/the-email-that-shattered-andrew-redmaynes-hopes-of-playing-for-arsenal-20160715-gq6tne.html|title=The email that shattered andrew redmaynes hopes of playing for Arsenal|newspaper=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|date=15 July 2015|accessdate=11 September 2016}}</ref>

===Central Coast Mariners===
[[File:Andrew Redmayne.jpg|left|thumb|Redmayne playing for the Central Coast Mariners youth team]]
Redmayne signed with hometown club [[Central Coast Mariners FC|Central Coast Mariners]] for the [[2007–08 A-League]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.foxsports.com.au/football/a-league/mariners-sign-teenage-defender/story-e6frf4gl-1111114085064|publisher=[[Fox Sports (Australia)]]|title=Mariners sign teenage defender|date=1 August 2007|accessdate=28 October 2014}}</ref> Redmayne made his competitive debut for the club in September 2008, coming on as an early substitute for the injured [[Mark Bosnich]] in a win over [[Perth Glory FC|Perth Glory]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://tvnz.co.nz/content/2109935/3453174.html|title=Mariners win tainted by Bosnich injury|publisher=[[One News (New Zealand)]]|date=27 September 2008|accessdate=28 October 2014}}</ref> Redmayne made his starting debut for the club one week later in a 3–3 draw with [[Adelaide United FC|Adelaide United]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.footballnews.com.au/reports.php?id=111|title=A-League 2008/2009 – Round 7 between Adelaide United and Central Coast Mariners at Hindmarsh Stadium|publisher=FootballNews.com.au|date=3 October 2008|accessdate=28 October 2014}}</ref>

Redmayne made what was to be his final competitive appearance for the Mariners in January 2010, a 2–0 loss to [[Wellington Phoenix FC|Wellington Phoenix]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.smh.com.au/sport/a-league/ifill-bags-a-double-as-phoenix-beat-mariners-20091231-lkwf.html|title=Ifill bags a double as Phoenix beat Mariners|newspaper=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|date=1 January 2010|accessdate=28 October 2014}}</ref>

===Brisbane Roar===
On 18 January 2010 it was announced he would be joining [[Brisbane Roar FC|Brisbane Roar]] after failing to find regular game time at the Mariners.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://au.fourfourtwo.com/news/120699,redmayne-on-his-way-to-roar.aspx |title=Redmayne on His Way To Roar |date=18 January 2012 |magazine=[[FourFourTwo (Australia)]] |accessdate=23 January 2012 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120921093129/http://au.fourfourtwo.com/news/120699%2Credmayne-on-his-way-to-roar.aspx |archivedate=21 September 2012}}</ref> Redmayne made his debut for the club as an 80th-minute substitute for regular goalkeeper [[Michael Theoklitos]] in a 4–0 win at home to [[Gold Coast United]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/news/ruthless-roar-celebrate-a-league-premiers-plate-with-4-0-win-over-gold-coast-united-on-return-to-suncorp-stadium/story-e6frf7jo-1226005093814|title=Ruthless Roar celebrate A-League Premier's Plate with 4–0 win over Gold Coast United on return to Suncorp Stadium|newspaper=[[Herald Sun]]|author=Monteverde, Marco|date=13 February 2011|accessdate=28 October 2014}}</ref> He made a second appearance for the Roar in the side's final match of the [[2011–12 A-League]] regular season – again in a win over [[Gold Coast United FC|Gold Coast United]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.foxsports.com.au/football/a-league/ss/story-e6frf4gl-1226309679007|author=Pace, Daniel|title=Stunning late strike from George Lambadaridis helps Brisbane Roar to victory over Gold Coast United|publisher=[[Fox Sports (Australia)]]|date=25 March 2012|accessdate=28 October 2014}}</ref>

===Melbourne City===
On 21 January 2012, [[Melbourne City FC|Melbourne Heart]] announced the Redmayne as their first signing for the 2012–13 A-League season.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://au.fourfourtwo.com/news/229493,heart-transplant-for-roars-redmayne.aspx|title=Heart Transplant For Roar's Redmayne|date=21 January 2012|magazine=[[FourFourTwo (Australia)]]|accessdate=23 January 2012|archive-date=29 October 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131029191913/http://au.fourfourtwo.com/news/229493,heart-transplant-for-roars-redmayne.aspx|url-status=dead}}</ref> In January 2013, he made his Heart debut in a victory over [[Newcastle Jets FC|Newcastle Jets]] and was subsequently promoted to first-choice 'keeper at the club.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/sport/football/former-second-choice-roar-keeper-andrew-redmayne-faces-old-teammates-on-sunday/story-e6frfg8x-1226547275931|title=Former second choice Roar keeper Andrew Redmayne faces old teammates on sunday|newspaper=[[Herald Sun]]|author=Monteverde, Marco|date=4 January 2013|accessdate=28 October 2014}}</ref>

===Western Sydney Wanderers===
Redmayne returned to New South Wales to play for [[Western Sydney Wanderers FC|Western Sydney Wanderers]] in 2015.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://theworldgame.sbs.com.au/article/2015/06/18/popovic-brings-redmayne-wanderers|title=Popovic brings Redmayne to Wanderers|date=18 June 2015|accessdate=27 August 2015|publisher=[[The World Game]]}}</ref>

===Sydney FC===
In January 2017, Redmayne moved to [[Sydney FC]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://theworldgame.sbs.com.au/article/2017/01/02/redmayne-departs-western-sydney-wanderers-sydney-fc|title=Redmayne departs Western Sydney Wanderers for Sydney FC|work=[[The World Game]]|publisher=[[Special Broadcasting Service|SBS]]|date=2 January 2017}}</ref>

With first choice keeper [[Danny Vukovic]] out on international duty, Redmayne made his first appearance for the Sky Blues against [[Perth Glory]] keeping a clean sheet in a 3–0 win.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.a-league.com.au/matchcentre/Perth-Glory-v-Sydney-FC/854444?matchreport#widget-match-day-tabs|title = Perth Glory vs Sydney FC, A-League Men, Round 24, 26th Mar 2017|date = 31 July 2017}}</ref> With the departure of Vukovic, Redmayne began pre-season as first-choice and starting every match. He started the first game of the season against rivals [[Melbourne Victory]], helping the team to a 1–0 win. He subsequently won the A-League Championship with Sydney in 2019 and again in 2020.

==International==
[[File:Andrew Redmayne-Olyroos.jpg|thumb|Redmayne playing for the [[Australia national under-23 association football team|Olyroos]] in 2011]]
===Senior===
Redmayne was first called up to the [[Australia national soccer team|Australian national squad]] in June 2019 for a friendly against [[South Korea national football team|South Korea]], after his performance in the [[2019 A-League Grand Final]].<ref>{{cite news|website=[[Sydney Morning Herald]]|last=Bossi|first=Dominic|url=https://www.smh.com.au/sport/soccer/redmayne-gets-a-glove-on-socceroos-debut-20190606-p51v6j.html|title=Redmayne gets a glove on Socceroos debut|access-date=5 July 2022}}</ref> He made his international debut in the friendly, playing a full game as Australia lost 1–0.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.socceroos.com.au/news/redmayne-its-a-moment-ill-remember-forever|title=Redmayne: 'It's a moment I'll remember forever'|website=[[Socceroos]]|first=Jacob|last=Wilson}}</ref>

On 13 June 2022, Redmayne came off the bench in the 120th minute of the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (AFC–CONMEBOL play-off)|FIFA World Cup play-off]] match against [[Peru national football team|Peru]]. In the penalty shoot-out, he successfully put off the Peru players and managed to save the vital penalty to help secure Australia's passage to the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Australia vs. Peru - Football Match Summary - June 13, 2022 - ESPN |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/match/_/gameId/633780 |access-date=13 June 2022 |website=ESPN.com |language=en}}</ref> In an interview after the game, Redmayne revealed that the tactic had been planned for months.<ref>{{Cite web |title=How the Socceroos 'planned' Redmayne's heroics months in advance |url=https://www.socceroos.com.au/news/how-socceroos-planned-redmaynes-heroics-months-advance |access-date=14 June 2022 |website=socceroos.com.au |date=14 June 2022 |language=en}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Outside of his football career, Redmayne has harboured ambitions to become a [[Education in Australia#Primary Schools|primary school teacher]]. During the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]], Redmayne finished his studies and received his teaching degree during the tournament. <ref>{{cite web |last1=D'Urbano |first1=Nick |title=Redmayne’s ‘amazing’ World Cup revelation: He’s graduated! |url=https://keepup.com.au/news/how-a-socceroos-hero-became-a-teacher-at-the-world-cup/ |website=Keep Up |publisher=Keep Up |access-date=4 April 2023}}</ref>
