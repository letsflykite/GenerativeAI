'''Conor David Coady''' (born 25 February 1993) is an English professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] for [[EFL Championship]] club [[Leicester City F.C|Leicester City]] and the [[England national football team|England national team]].

Coady came through the academy system at [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] and made two appearances for the first team before spending a season on loan at [[Sheffield United F.C.|Sheffield United]] and then moving to [[Huddersfield Town A.F.C.|Huddersfield Town]] on a permanent transfer in 2014. A year later he signed for Wolverhampton Wanderers for £2&nbsp;million and has played over 300 games for the club, winning the [[EFL Championship|Championship]] in the [[2017–18 EFL Championship|2017–18 season]].

Coady represented England at youth level, being named in the team of the tournament as England won the [[2010 UEFA European Under-17 Championship]] and captaining the [[England national under-20 football team|England under-20]] team at the [[2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup]]. He made his debut for the senior team in 2020 and was part of the squad that came runners-up at [[UEFA Euro 2020]].

==Early life==
Coady was born in [[St Helens, Merseyside]], and grew up in nearby [[Haydock]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.sthelensstar.co.uk/news/18707290.conor-coady-immensley-proud-england-debut/ |title=Conor Coady 'immensley' &#91;sic&#93; proud after England debut |first=Andrew |last=Kilmurray |newspaper=St Helens Star |date=9 September 2020 |access-date=1 October 2020}}</ref> He is of Irish descent through a grandparent.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.irishpost.com/news/how-many-of-the-england-team-could-play-for-ireland-215510|title=How many of the England team could play for Ireland?|first=Jack|last=Beresford|website=The Irish Post}}</ref> He attended Bleak Hill Primary School, [[Windle, St Helens|Windle]] and [[Rainford High Technology College]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.sthelensstar.co.uk/news/18685771.former-bleak-hill-pupil-conor-coady-earns-first-senior-england-call-up/ |title=Former Bleak Hill pupil Conor Coady earns first senior England call-up |first=Mike |last=Critchley |newspaper=St Helens Star |date=29 August 2020 |access-date=1 October 2020}}</ref> He grew up supporting [[Liverpool F.C.]]<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thestar.co.uk/sport/sheff-utd/sheffield-united-conor-would-be-keen-on-lane-option-1-6591466 |title=Conor would be keen on Lane option |newspaper=The Star |location=Sheffield |date=2 May 2014 |access-date=2 May 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402211249/http://www.thestar.co.uk/sport/sheff-utd/sheffield-united-conor-would-be-keen-on-lane-option-1-6591466 |archive-date=2 April 2015}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Liverpool===
Coady is a product of the [[Liverpool F.C. Reserves and Academy|Liverpool Youth Academy]] after joining the club in 2005. During the [[2010–11 Liverpool F.C. season|2010–11 season]], Coady was on the fringes of the first team, making the substitutes' bench twice but failed to make a first-team appearance.<ref>{{cite web |last=Whyatt |first=Chris |title=Sparta Prague 0–0 Liverpool |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/europe/9399199.stm |website=BBC Sport |access-date=22 March 2013 |date=17 February 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Dawkes |first=Phil |title=Liverpool 9–1 Birmingham |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/eng_prem/9463392.stm |website=BBC Sport |access-date=22 March 2013 |date=23 April 2011}}</ref> Coady played every [[Barclays Premier Reserve League|Reserve League]] and [[NextGen Series]] match during the [[2011–12 Liverpool F.C. season|2011–12 season]], scoring five goals. Despite being named in the senior squad list and being called up to the senior squad occasionally from 2009, he did not make his senior debut until 8 November 2012 in a [[UEFA Europa League]] group stage match against [[Anzhi Makhachkala]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/20164149 |title=Anzhi Makhachkala 1–0 Liverpool |date=8 November 2012 |website=BBC Sport |access-date=19 March 2013}}</ref> After [[Andre Wisdom]]'s promotion as a full-time senior squad member, Conor Coady was installed as full-time captain of the Under-21 squad and on 12 May 2013 he made his Premier League debut in a 3–1 win at [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Seven starlets who stepped up in 2012–13 |url=http://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/latest-news/seven-starlets-who-stepped-up-in-2012-13 |publisher=Liverpool F.C. |access-date=23 March 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402152829/http://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/latest-news/seven-starlets-who-stepped-up-in-2012-13 |archive-date=2 April 2015}}</ref>

Coady agreed a six-month loan with [[EFL League One|League One]] club [[Sheffield United F.C.|Sheffield United]] on 22 July 2013,<ref name="blades loan">{{cite web |url=http://www.sufc.co.uk/news/article/20130722-coady-in-on-loan-936404.aspx |title=Coady in on loan |publisher=Sheffield United F.C. |date=22 July 2013 |access-date=22 July 2013}}</ref> later stating that he had turned down the chance to go on Liverpool's pre-season tour of Australia and the Far East in order to join up with his new club.<ref name="Rodgers influence">{{cite news |title=Coady reveals Rodgers influence |url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/11683/8836859/coady-reveals-rodgers-infleunce |access-date=23 July 2013 |website=Sky Sports |date=23 July 2013}}</ref> Coady made his debut for the Blades in the opening fixture of the following season, coming on as a second-half substitute in a 2–1 home victory over [[Notts County F.C.|Notts County]],<ref name="blades debut">{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2013/aug/02/sheffield-united-notts-county-report |title=Sheffield United's David Weir off to winning start against Notts County |newspaper=The Guardian |date=2 August 2013 |access-date=3 August 2013}}</ref> and made his first start for the club in the following game, a [[EFL Cup|League Cup]] first round defeat to [[EFL League Two|League Two]] club [[Burton Albion F.C.|Burton Albion]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/23499353 |title=Sheffield United 1–2 Burton |website=BBC Sport |date=6 August 2013 |access-date=17 October 2013}}</ref>

Coady scored his first senior goal in a 1–1 draw at [[Leyton Orient F.C.|Leyton Orient]] on 30 November 2013.<ref name="blades goal">{{cite web |url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/live/match/288435/report |title=Cox snatches point for Orient |website=Sky Sports |date=30 November 2013 |access-date=2 December 2013}}</ref> Having been in and out of the team during the first half of the season, Coady began to cement a regular first-team place over the Christmas period, prompting United to extend his loan spell during the January transfer window,<ref name="blades loan extension">{{cite news |title=Liverpool youngster Conor Coady extends loan spell with Sheffield United |url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/football/transfer-news/liverpool-youngster-conor-coady-extends-3001910 |access-date=10 January 2014 |newspaper=Daily Mirror |date=8 January 2014}}</ref> and once more in February to extend his stay until the end of the season.<ref name="coady end season">{{cite news |title=Liverpool midfielder extends Sheffield United loan deal |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/26338499 |access-date=25 February 2014 |website=BBC Sport |date=25 February 2014}}</ref> Coady played regularly for the Blades for the remainder of the season including playing on the losing side in the 5-3 loss to Hull City in the FA Cup semi-final <ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.thefa.com/news/2014/apr/13/semi-final-hull-city-sheffield-united-match-report | title=Hull reach Final after second-half salvo slays Blades }}</ref> and returned to [[Anfield]] having played 50 games and scored 6 goals.<ref name=sb1314/>

===Huddersfield Town===
On 6 August 2014, Coady signed for [[EFL Championship|Championship]] club [[Huddersfield Town A.F.C.|Huddersfield Town]] on a three-year contract for a fee believed to be around £500,000.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Conor Coady Joins Huddersfield Town |url=http://www.htafc.com/news/article/first-team-news-conor-coady-1809298.aspx |publisher=Huddersfield Town A.F.C. |date=6 August 2014 |access-date=6 August 2014}}</ref> He made his debut as a substitute in the 4–0 defeat by [[AFC Bournemouth]] on 9 August.<ref name=sb1415/> On 1 October, he scored his first goal for the club against [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]] where Town won 3–1 at [[Molineux Stadium]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Huddersfield Town earned a surprise win away at Wolves to stop the hosts going top of the Championship. |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/29338891 |access-date=23 March 2015}}</ref> He again found the net, this time in a 2–2 draw against [[Rotherham United F.C.|Rotherham United]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Rotherham United 2 Huddersfield Town 2 |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/30543342 |access-date=23 March 2015}}</ref>

===Wolverhampton Wanderers===
[[File:Coady Ralls.jpg|thumb|Coady (left) playing for [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]] in 2015]]

On 3 July 2015, Coady signed for Championship club Wolverhampton Wanderers on a three-year contract for an undisclosed fee, believed to be around £2&nbsp;million.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Conor Coady: Wolves sign Huddersfield Town midfielder |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/33380799 |website=BBC Sport |date=3 July 2015}}</ref> He scored his first goal for Wolves in a 2–1 [[EFL Cup]] first round win against [[Crawley Town F.C.|Crawley Town]] on 9 August 2016.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/36949401 |title=Wolves 2–1 Crawley |website=BBC Sport |date=9 August 2016 |access-date=9 August 2016}}</ref>

Under new head coach [[Nuno Espírito Santo]], Coady moved to the centre of a three-man defence for Wolves from the 2017–18 season onward, where he spent much of the season as club captain, and has been full-time captain since the club's return to the [[Premier League]] from the 2018–19 season.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.wolves.co.uk/teams/first-team/conor-coady/ |title=Conor Coady: Biography |publisher=Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C. |access-date=6 September 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.expressandstar.com/sport/football/wolverhampton-wanderers-fc/2017/09/22/delighted-conor-coady-signs-four-year-wolves-contract/ |title=Delighted Conor Coady signs four-year Wolves contract |newspaper=Express & Star |date=22 September 2017 |access-date=28 August 2018}}</ref> In September 2017 he signed a new four-year contract.<ref>{{cite news |title=Four more years! |url=https://www.wolves.co.uk/news/first-team/20170922-four-more-years/ |publisher=Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C. |date=22 September 2017}}</ref> On 21 April 2018, during his 120th league appearance for Wolves, he scored a 66th-minute penalty in a 4–0 win against [[Bolton Wanderers F.C.|Bolton Wanderers]] to record his first league goal for the club, as Wolves sealed the Championship title.<ref>{{cite web |title=Bolton Wanderers 0–4 Wolves |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/43762334 |website=BBC Sport |date=21 April 2018}}</ref>

On 15 February 2019, Coady signed a new contract lasting to June 2023.<ref>{{cite web |title=Wolves: Conor Coady and Matt Doherty sign new deals until 2023 |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/47260570 |website=BBC Sport |date=15 February 2019 |access-date=16 February 2019}}</ref> He was an ever-present player for Wolves in both the 2018–19 and 2019–20 Premier League seasons, in which they finished 7th in both cases.<ref>{{cite web |title=Marathon man Conor Coady is Wolves' totem in an endless season |url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/marathon-man-conor-coady-is-wolves-totem-in-an-endless-season-zhcg3bdc6 |newspaper=The Times |date=2 August 2020}}</ref> He also played every minute of [[2019–20 UEFA Europa League|Wolves' Europa League]] campaign in 2019–20 as they reached the quarter-finals. At the end of the competition, he was named in UEFA's Squad of the Season.<ref>{{cite web |title=UEFA Europa League Squad of the Season |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/news/0260-10391fbb6c62-2c8b46747bf5-1000--official-squad-of-the-season/ |publisher=UEFA |date=26 August 2020}}</ref>

On 30 September 2020, three weeks after Coady won his first full England cap, he signed a new five-year deal with the club, keeping him at Wolves until 2025.<ref>{{cite news |url = https://www.wolves.co.uk/news/first-team/20200930-captain-coady-commits-future-to-wolves/# |title = Captain Coady commits future to Wolves |publisher = Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C. |date = 30 September 2020 |access-date = 30 September 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54362428|title=Conor Coady: Wolves captain signs new five-year deal |website=BBC Sport |date=30 September 2020|access-date=30 September 2020}}</ref> In late November, he missed the game against Southampton as he had been in contact with a person diagnosed with [[COVID-19]]; this broke an 84-game streak of playing every minute (7,560 in total), third-best for outfield players in the history of the Premier League.<ref>{{cite news |title=Conor Coady's long run of unbroken action comes to an end |url=https://www.expressandstar.com/sport/uk-sports/2020/11/23/conor-coadys-long-run-of-unbroken-action-comes-to-an-end/ |access-date=26 November 2020 |newspaper=Express & Star |date=23 November 2020}}</ref>

====Loan to Everton====
On 8 August 2022, Coady signed for Premier League club [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] on a season-long loan with an option to buy.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.evertonfc.com/news/2734912|title=Coady Signs for Everton|publisher=Everton F.C. |date=8 August 2022}}</ref> He made his debut five days later in a 2–1 loss at [[Aston Villa]], leaving the game with a late injury.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Gibbons |first1=Mike |title=Aston Villa 2-1 Everton - Danny Ings and Emi Buendia on target as Villa edge match after late drama |url=https://www.eurosport.com/football/premier-league/2022-2023/aston-villa-v-everton-premier-league-live_sto9082908/story.shtml |access-date=4 September 2022 |publisher=Eurosport |date=13 August 2022}}</ref> On 3 September, he put the ball in the net in the [[Merseyside derby]] against his childhood team Liverpool, but it was ruled [[Offside (association football)|offside]] in a goalless draw.<ref>{{cite news |title=Conor Coady denied fairytale goal against boyhood club Liverpool as Jordan Pickford rescues spirited Everton in frantic Merseyside derby stalemate |url=https://talksport.com/football/1185635/conor-coady-goal-var-liverpool-everton-jordan-pickford-merseyside-derby/ |access-date=4 September 2022 |publisher=Talksport |date=3 September 2022}}</ref> He scored his first goal for Everton in a 2–1 away win against [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] on 1 October.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63017882|title=Southampton 1-2 Everton|work=BBC Sport|date=1 October 2022}}</ref>

After [[Frank Lampard]] was replaced as manager by [[Sean Dyche]] in January 2023, Coady started the first four games of the new tenure. Following a 2–0 home loss to [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]] on 25 February, he was dropped as [[Michael Keane (footballer, born 1993)|Michael Keane]] and [[James Tarkowski]] – both of whom played for [[Burnley F.C.|Burnley]] under Dyche – were preferred.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Dicken |first1=Alex |title=Wolves braced for Conor Coady decision as Everton future takes another twist |url=https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/sport/football/transfer-news/wolves-conor-coady-everton-future-26263251 |access-date=24 April 2023 |work=Birmingham Mail |date=8 March 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Beesley |first1=Chris |title='Not always easy' - Sean Dyche explains Everton centre-back dilemma after Michael Keane and Conor Coady decision |url=https://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/not-always-easy-sean-dyche-26453177 |access-date=24 April 2023 |work=Liverpool Echo |date=12 March 2023}}</ref>

On 1 June 2023, Everton announced that Coady was returning to his home club.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Coady And Vinagre Leave Everton |url=https://www.evertonfc.com/news/3515907/coady-and-vinagre-leave-everton |access-date=2023-06-01 |website=www.evertonfc.com |language=en}}</ref> British media said Everton had decided not to take up an option to sign him permanently.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Everton reject offer to sign Wolves loanee Coady |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/65775147 |access-date=2023-06-01}}</ref>

[[England national under-20 football team|England under-20 team]] by manager [[Peter Taylor (footballer, born 1953)|Peter Taylor]] for the [[2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thefa.com/england/mens-u20s/News/2013/conor-coady-to-captain-young-lions-fifa-world-cup |title=Captain Coady |publisher=The Football Association |date=11 June 2013 |access-date=17 June 2013}}</ref> He made his debut for the team on 16 June, in a 3–0 win in a warm-up game against [[Uruguay national under-20 football team|Uruguay]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thefa.com/england/mens-u20s/News/2013/uruguay-report-160613 |archive-url=https://archive.today/2013.06.24-222816/http://www.thefa.com/england/mens-u20s/News/2013/uruguay-report-160613 |archive-date=24 June 2013 |title=England U20s continue World Cup preparations with a 3–0 win over Uruguay |publisher=The Football Association |date=16 June 2013 |access-date=17 June 2013}}</ref> On 23 June, he scored in the opening group-stage game against [[Iraq national under-20 football team|Iraq]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thefa.com/england/mens-u20s/News/2013/iraq-updates-230613 |title=England v Iraq |publisher=The Football Association |date=23 June 2013 |access-date=24 June 2013}}</ref>

In August 2020 Coady was called up to the [[England national football team|England senior squad]] by [[Gareth Southgate]] for the [[UEFA Nations League|Nations League]] matches against [[Iceland national football team|Iceland]] and [[Denmark national football team|Denmark]], starting in central defence against the latter on 8 September in a 0–0 draw, and in doing so becoming the first Wolves player to start for England since [[Steve Bull]] in 1990.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Coady & Maitland-Niles called up by England |website=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/53962211 |access-date=29 August 2020}}</ref> Coady was awarded man of the match by [[Sky Sports]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=Denmark 0–0 England: Gareth Southgate's men struggle to Nations League stalemate |website=Sky Sports |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/denmark-vs-england/423932 |access-date=9 September 2020}}</ref> On 8 October 2020, Coady scored his first goal for England in his second appearance, a 3–0 friendly win against [[Wales national football team|Wales]] at Wembley.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2020/oct/08/england-wales-international-friendly-match-report|title=Calvert-Lewin scores on fine England debut to set up victory over Wales|newspaper=The Guardian |first=David |last=Hytner |date=8 October 2020}}</ref>

Coady was in the England squad that came runners-up at [[UEFA Euro 2020]], held in 2021. Though he did not play any games, he was dubbed England's "player of the tournament" by assistant manager [[Steve Holland (footballer)|Steve Holland]], who believed that his presence in the team's camp was similar to that of [[John Terry]] when he played less often in his final years at Chelsea.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Edwards |first1=Joe |title=Wolves skipper Conor Coady hailed England's 'player of the tournament' |url=https://www.expressandstar.com/sport/football/wolverhampton-wanderers-fc/2021/07/07/wolves-skipper-conor-coady-hailed-englands-player-of-the-tournament/ |access-date=12 July 2021 |newspaper=Express & Star |date=7 July 2021}}</ref> He was later included in the squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar, though again he did not make any appearances in the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/12743496/england-world-cup-squad-james-maddison-and-callum-wilson-included-as-gareth-southgate-names-his-26-man-team-for-qatar-2022|website=Sky Sports|title=England World Cup squad: James Maddison and Callum Wilson included as Gareth Southgate names his 26-man team for Qatar 2022|date=11 November 2022}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In November 2021, Coady was named Football Ally of the Year at the [[British LGBT Awards]] for his outspoken support for gay footballers.<ref name="Gayally">{{cite news |last1=Smith |first1=Emma |title='Being gay will be part of everyday life in football' - England star Coady on his firm stance as an outspoken LGBT ally |url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/gay-everyday-life-football-england-coady-lgbt-ally/g2tlqbf5t7ah141dp35ow78s9 |access-date=28 January 2022 |website=Goal |date=9 November 2021}}</ref> Coady said that "Equality is a massive word, and when it comes to LGBTQ stuff, I'm big on making people feel involved."<ref name="Gayally"/> Coady concluded that in his team "[criticising a player based on their sexuality] would never be the case within [our] dressing room."<ref name="Gayally"/>
