'''Carlos Sebastián Sosa Silva''' (born 19 August 1986) is a Uruguayan professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Liga MX]] club [[Club Universidad Nacional|UNAM]] and the [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay national team]].

==Club career==
Sosa made his debut for [[Club Atlético Peñarol|Peñarol]] in 2007. In 2008, he was loaned to [[Central Español]], where he played 28 games in two years, before returning to Peñarol.

Upon his return, Sosa was the second choice goalkeeper behind former Argentine international [[Pablo Cavallero]], until coach [[Julio Ribas]] chose him for the starting eleven of Peñarol for the [[2009–10 Uruguayan Primera División season]]. During that season, Sosa helped Peñarol to win the league title. Afterwards, Sosa was a regular in the Peñarol team that also reached the final of the [[2011 Copa Libertadores]] (where his team was defeated by [[Santos Futebol Clube|Santos]].

[[File:Sosa atajando tiro de Ronaldo.jpg|thumb|left|Sosa saves a shot from [[Cristiano Ronaldo]] in a friendly between Peñarol and [[Real Madrid]]]]
In July 2011, the Uruguayan goalkeeper signed for Argentine giants [[Boca Juniors]]. During his one-year stay at Boca, Sosa was mostly a substitute for [[Agustín Orión]], but won with the team the [[2011–12 Argentine Primera División season#Torneo Apertura|2011 Apertura]] and the [[2011–12 Copa Argentina]].

Subsequently, Sosa joined [[Club Atlético Vélez Sársfield|Vélez Sársfield]] also of Argentina. There he again the second choice goalkeeper (behind [[Germán Montoya]]) at first, but then became a regular starter and played 9 games in his team's [[2012–13 Argentine Primera División season#Torneo Inicial|2012 Inicial]] winning campaign.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.argentinesoccer.com/player_apps.cfm?id=sssa&Championship=in12&Team=vel |title=Initial '12 statistics |publisher=Argentine Soccer |accessdate=10 February 2013 }}</ref> Sosa was also a starter in Vélez victories in the [[2012–13 Argentine Primera División season#Superfinal|2012–13 Superfinal]] and the [[2013 Supercopa Argentina|2013]].

In June 2015, after his contract with Vélez ended, he joined [[C.F. Pachuca|Pachuca]].

In January 2017, Sosa joined [[Monarcas Morelia]] on loan from Pachuca. He was the second choice goalkeeper behind [[Carlos Felipe Rodriguez]], for the majority of the season as Morelia battled relegation. After making a great impression that culminated with a [[Copa MX]] runner up finish, Sosa was promoted to first choice goalkeeper for the last 2 games of the season as Morelia inched away from relegation.

==International career==
Sosa has played for Uruguay's [[Uruguay national under-17 football team|under-17]] and [[Uruguay national under-20 football team|under-20]] teams at youth level. At the age of 35, he made his [[Uruguay national football team|senior team]] debut on 11 June 2022 in a 5–0 [[Exhibition game|friendly]] win against [[Panama national football team|Panama]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Sebastián Sosa, segundo jugador mayor de 30 años que debuta en la selección de Alonso|url=https://www.elobservador.com.uy/nota/sebastian-sosa-segundo-jugador-30-que-debuto-en-la-seleccion-de-alonso-202261119411|date=11 June 2022|access-date=11 June 2022}}</ref>
