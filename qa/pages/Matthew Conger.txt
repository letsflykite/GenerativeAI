'''Matthew Conger''' (born 11 October 1978) is a New Zealand [[Association football|football]] [[Referee (association football)|referee]] from [[Palmerston North]]. Born in [[Texas]], [[United States]], Conger operates in the Australian [[A-League]] and the [[New Zealand Football Championship]]. He is also a [[school teacher]] in Palmerston North.<ref name="stuff">{{cite news | url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/manawatu-standard/sport/8111312/Conger-climbs-referees-ladder | title=Conger climbs referees' ladder | work=[[Manawatu Standard]] | date=22 December 2012 | access-date=22 February 2016 | author=Heagney, George}}</ref>

==Refereeing career==
Conger began refereeing in the United States as a teenager, continuing after moving to New Zealand in his early 20s.<ref name="stuff" />

Conger has refereed at the [[2015 FIFA U-20 World Cup|2015 Under-20 World Cup]] in New Zealand, the [[Football at the 2016 Summer Olympics|2016 Olympic Games]] in Rio de Janeiro, the [[2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup|2017 Under-20 World Cup]] in South Korea, the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia, and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref name="2018 World Cup"/> He has also officiated in numerous A-League and New Zealand Football Championship games. He has also been the man in the middle for [[OFC Champions League]] games.<ref name="Worldfootball.net"/>

In 2016, Conger left full-time teaching in order to concentrate more on his refereeing, but in 2017 was engaged part-time at Palmerston North's [[Carncot School]].<ref name="2018 World Cup">{{cite web | url=https://www.stuff.co.nz/sport/football/99150012/kiwi-ref-matt-conger-off-to-world-cup-in-russia | title=Kiwi football referee Matt Conger off to 2018 Fifa World Cup finals in Russia | publisher=[[Stuff.co.nz]] | date=29 November 2017 | access-date=1 June 2018 | author=Heagney, George}}</ref>

In 2017, Conger was named the New Zealand referee of the year.<ref name="2018 World Cup"/>
