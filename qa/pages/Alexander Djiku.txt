'''Alexander Kwabena Baidoo Djiku''' (born 9 August 1994) is a professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[defender (association football)|defender]].<ref>{{WorldFootball.net|alexander-djiku}}</ref> Born in [[Montpellier]], France, he represents the [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national team]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=20 March 2022 |title=Alexander Djiku becomes Ghanaian with joint-most Ligue 1 appearances - MyJoyOnline.com |url=https://www.myjoyonline.com/alexander-djiku-becomes-ghanaian-with-joint-most-ligue-1-appearances/ |access-date=3 July 2022 |website=www.myjoyonline.com |language=en-US}}</ref>

==Club career==
Djiku spent his youth career at [[SC Bastia|Bastia]]. He played for [[SC Bastia Reserves and Academy|the reserve team]] until 2014, when he was promoted to the senior team.{{Citation needed|date=February 2023}} Djiku made his debut in a [[Coupe de la Ligue]] match against [[Évian Thonon Gaillard F.C.|Évian]] in December 2013.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.ligue1.com/joueur/djiku-alexander | title = Alexander Djiku | publisher = Ligue1.com | accessdate = 11 July 2014 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20180811064356/https://www.ligue1.com/joueur/djiku-alexander | archive-date = 11 August 2018 | url-status = dead }}</ref><ref>{{in lang|fr}} [http://www.lfp.fr/coupeLigue/feuille_match/78599 LFP]</ref>

On 11 July 2017, Djiku joined [[Ligue 1]] side [[Stade Malherbe Caen|Caen]] on a four-year contract.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/SMCaen/status/884792026558025728|title=Coucou @alex_djiku! Le défenseur en provenance du @SCBastia s'engage pour 4 ans avec le @SMCaen #SMCaen #TeamSMC #Ligue1pic.twitter.com/avxVN8UAL3|last=Caen|first=Stade Malherbe|date=11 July 2017|website=@SMCaen|access-date=11 July 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Mercato. SM Caen : le défenseur Alexander Djiku signe quatre ans|url=http://www.ouest-france.fr/sport/football/transferts/mercato-sm-caen-le-defenseur-alexander-djiku-signe-quatre-ans-5127911|accessdate=11 July 2017|work=Ouest-France|date=11 July 2017|language=French}}</ref> He was acquired by fellow Ligue 1 club [[RC Strasbourg Alsace|Strasbourg]] in July 2019, signing a four-year contract.<ref name="rcstrasbourgalsace">{{cite web|title=ALEXANDER DJIKU REJOINT LE RACING|url=http://www.rcstrasbourgalsace.fr/actualites/alexander-djiku-rejoint-le-racing|accessdate=23 July 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190723175730/http://www.rcstrasbourgalsace.fr/actualites/alexander-djiku-rejoint-le-racing|archive-date=23 July 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==International career==
Djiku is of Ghanaian [[Ewe people|Ewe]] and French descent.<ref>http://www.africa-football.com/2017/07/11/mercato-sm-caen-alexander-djiku-en-approche-senegal/{{Dead link|date=September 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.playmakerstats.com/player.php?id=297799|title=Alexander Djiku :: Alexander Djiku :: Strasbourg|website=www.playmakerstats.com|access-date=8 August 2019}}</ref> He debuted with the senior [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national team]] in a 3–0 friendly loss to [[Mali national football team|Mali]] on 9 October 2020. He played his second match for [[Ghana]] against [[Qatar national football team|Qatar]] on 12 October 2020 in which he impressed.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2020/10/09/world/friendlies/mali/ghana/3396568/|title=Mali vs. Ghana|website=Soccerway}}</ref> Djiku's third match came when he was given the opportunity by the Black Stars new head coach to start against [[Zimbabwe national football team|Zimbabwe]] in a [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (CAF)|World Cup qualifier]] where Ghana won 3–1.<ref>{{cite web|title=2022 World Cup qualifiers: Ghana player ratings against Zimbabwe|url=https://www.goal.com/en-gh/lists/2022-world-cup-qualifiers-ghana-player-ratings-against/btwafzcsuybu1uqynsddlig6s|access-date=12 October 2021|website=Goal.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|date=9 October 2021|title=Ghana 3–1 Zimbabwe: Player ratings from 2022 FIFA World Cup qualifier in Cape Coast|url=https://ghanasportsonline.com/2021/10/ghana-3-1-zimbabwe-player-ratings-from-2022-fifa-world-cup-qualifier-in-cape-coast/|website=Ghana Sports Online|access-date=12 October 2021}}</ref> He was part of Ghana's team at the [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations|2021 African Cup of Nations]] in Cameroon.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ayamga|first=Emmanuel|date=18 January 2022|title=Fatawu Issahaku starts as Milo names Ghana's starting line-up against Comoros|url=https://www.pulse.com.gh/sports/football/afcon-2021-heres-ghanas-starting-line-up-to-face-comoros/gcq3tt4|access-date=6 February 2022|website=Pulse Ghana|language=en}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Djiku is married to Heley Djiku. The couple have two children, Wesley and Elyana.<ref>{{Cite web |date=4 April 2022 |title=Meet the wife and kids of Black Stars defender Alexander Djiku |url=https://www.ghanaweb.com/GhanaHomePage/SportsArchive/Photos-Meet-the-wife-and-kids-of-Black-Stars-defender-Alexander-Djiku-1506899 |access-date=3 July 2022 |website=GhanaWeb |language=en}}</ref>
