'''Axel Laurent Angel Lambert Witsel''' (born 12 January 1989) is a Belgian professional [[Football player|footballer]] who plays as a [[defensive midfielder]] or [[centre-back]]<ref>{{cite news |last1=Barlow |first1=Ruairidh |title=Axel Witsel makes thoughts clear to Diego Simeone over position change |url=https://www.football-espana.net/2022/09/22/axel-witsel-makes-thoughts-clear-to-diego-simeone-over-position-change |access-date=1 May 2023 |publisher=Football Espana |date=22 September 2022}}</ref> for [[La Liga]] club [[Atlético Madrid]]. Witsel broke into [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]]'s first team as a [[Midfielder#Winger|right-winger]], and can also play [[Midfielder#Attacking midfielder|attacking midfielder]], though his natural position is as a [[Midfielder#Central midfielder|central midfielder]].

He began his career at hometown club [[Standard Liège]], making 183 appearances and scoring 42 goals while winning five domestic trophies. He was awarded the [[Belgian Golden Shoe]] in 2008. After a season in Portugal with [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]], he signed for [[FC Zenit Saint Petersburg|Zenit Saint Petersburg]] for a €40 million fee in 2012. Witsel won four honours during his time in Russia, before leaving for [[Tianjin Quanjian F.C.|Tianjin Quanjian]] in January 2017. He returned to Europe with [[Borussia Dortmund]] in August 2018. With the [[Bundesliga]] side, Witsel won the [[2019 DFL-Supercup]] and the [[2020–21 DFB-Pokal]].

Witsel made his senior international debut in 2008 and earned his 100th [[cap (sport)|cap]] for Belgium on 15 November 2018. He represented the country at the [[FIFA World Cup]] in [[2014 FIFA World Cup|2014]], [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022]], and the [[UEFA European Championship]] in [[UEFA Euro 2016|2016]] and [[UEFA Euro 2020|2020]], helping them to third place in the 2018 tournament.

==Club career==
===Standard Liège===
On 17 September 2006, a 17-year-old Witsel made his debut with [[Standard Liège]] during a competitive game against [[FC Brussels]] when he replaced [[Steven Defour]] in the 89th minute. Eleven days later, he made his European debut as a [[substitute (football)|substitute]].{{citation needed|date=January 2017}}

During the [[2007–08 Belgian First Division|2007–08]] season, at 18 years of age, he was a key member in the squad of Standard Liège, winners of the Belgian League title that year. During this year, he formed a very young midfield together with other Belgian talents Defour and [[Marouane Fellaini]]. In the [[2008–09 Belgian First Division|following season]], he scored the winning goal [[Penalty kick (association football)|from the penalty spot]] in a [[two-legged match|two-legged playoff]] to decide the championship.{{citation needed|date=January 2017}}

On 30 August 2009, Witsel sparked controversy when he broke [[Marcin Wasilewski]]'s leg by stamping on his ankle during the match between [[R.S.C. Anderlecht|Anderlecht]] and Standard.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=Sporza |title=Witsel breekt been van Wasilewski in tackle |url=http://www.sporza.be/cm/sporza/voetbal/Jupiler_Pro_League/090830_Wasilewski_tackle_Witsel |access-date=31 August 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090901061702/http://www.sporza.be/cm/sporza/voetbal/Jupiler_Pro_League/090830_Wasilewski_tackle_Witsel |archive-date=1 September 2009 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|publisher=YouTube|title=Anderlecht-Standard faute d'Axel Witsel sur Wasilewski |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_OapMvlhZOQ|access-date=31 August 2009}}{{cbignore}}{{Dead YouTube link|date=February 2022}}</ref> Following the tackle, Witsel protested the red card, claiming that it was an accident without intent, before being sent off the pitch. He soon apologised, but was the subject of criticism from several media outlets and numerous [[death threat]]s from angry Anderlecht and [[Poland national football team|Poland]] fans.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://goal.com/en/news/755/europe/2009/09/01/1474358/standard-liege-stars-defour-witsel-receive-death-threats|title=Standard Liege Stars Defour & Witsel Receive Death Threats - Report - Goal.com|date=1 September 2009|access-date=26 June 2016|archive-date=16 January 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140116093358/http://www.goal.com/en/news/755/europe/2009/09/01/1474358/standard-liege-stars-defour-witsel-receive-death-threats|url-status=dead}}</ref> The initial ban until 23 November by the [[Royal Belgian Football Association|Belgian Football Association]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/football/news/standard-liege-midfielder-axel-witsel-3372233|title=Standard Liege midfielder Axel Witsel banned for horror tackle|work=MirrorFootball|date=1 September 2009|access-date=26 June 2016}}</ref> was later reduced on appeal to eight matches.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://uk.eurosport.yahoo.com/05092009/58/champions-league-witsel-horror-tackle-ban-reduced.html|title=Yahoo UK & Ireland – Sports News – Live Scores – Results|access-date=26 June 2016}}</ref>

===Zenit Saint Petersburg===
On 3 September 2012, Witsel signed for [[FC Zenit Saint Petersburg|Zenit Saint Petersburg]] for €40 million, after signing a five-year contract.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fc-zenit.ru/main/news/60736.html|title=Официальный сайт ФК "Зенит"|access-date=26 June 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://web3.cmvm.pt/sdi2004/emitentes/docs/FR41312.pdf |title=Comunicado |language=pt |trans-title=Announcement |date=3 September 2012 |publisher=[[Portuguese Securities Market Commission|CMVM]] |access-date=31 December 2014}}</ref> Witsel made his debut in the [[2012–13 Russian Premier League|Russian Premier League]] on 14 September, replacing [[Konstantin Zyryanov]] in the 70th minute as Zenit fell 2–0 to [[FC Terek Grozny|Terek Grozny]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/match?id=342396&cc=5901 |title=Zenit 0 – 2 Terek Grozny |publisher=ESPNFC |date=14 September 2012}}</ref> He scored his first two goals for the club on 30 November when Zenit brushed aside [[FC Spartak Moscow|Spartak Moscow]] 4–2 at the [[Luzhniki Stadium]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://espnfc.com/us/en/gamecast/statistics/id/357869/statistics.html?soccernet=true&cc=5901 |title=Spartak Moscow 2 – 4 Zenit St Petersburg |publisher=ESPNFC |date=30 November 2012}}</ref> Witsel scored Zenit's winner against Terek Grozny on 30 March 2013, an eighth-minute strike, as his side cruised to a 3–0 league victory.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://espnfc.com/us/en/gamecast/statistics/id/358127/statistics.html?soccernet=true&cc=5901 |title=Terek Grozny 0–3 Zenit St. Petersburg |publisher=ESPNFC |date=30 March 2013 }}</ref>

In Zenit's first match of the [[2014–15 UEFA Champions League group stage#Group C|2014–15 UEFA Champions League group stage]] on 16 September 2014, Witsel scored Zenit's second goal in a 2–0 away victory over former club Benfica.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/news/newsid=2140402.html |title=Familiar Zenit faces come back to haunt Benfica |work=UEFA |date=16 September 2014 |access-date=17 July 2015 }}</ref> After finishing third in their Champions League group, Zenit were parachuted into the [[2014-15 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]] knockout phase; in the first leg of their Round of 16 clash against [[Torino F.C.|Torino]] on 12 March 2015, Witsel scored on a rebound as Zenit defeated the Italian club 2–0.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2015/matches/round=2000589/match=2014609/postmatch/report/index.html#zenit+beat+torino |title=Zenit ease to victory over ten-man Torino |work=UEFA |date=12 March 2015 |access-date=17 July 2015 }}</ref> On 26 April, Witsel scored the game's only goal in the seventeenth minute as Zenit defeated [[FC Arsenal Tula|Arsenal Tula]] in a league match at the [[Petrovsky Stadium]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://uk.eurosport.yahoo.com/football/russian-premier-league/zenit-st-petersburg-fc-arsenal-tula-18571/ |title=Zenit St Petersburg 1–0 FC Arsenal Tula |work=Yahoo Sports! |date=26 April 2015 |access-date=17 July 2015 }}</ref> On Sunday 17 May, Witsel played the full 90 minutes as Zenit drew 1–1 with [[FC Ufa]], earning the point they needed to secure the [[2014–15 Russian Premier League|Premier League]] crown, the club's fourth overall and Witsel's first with Zenit.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Grez |first1=Matias |title=Zenit St. Petersburg celebrate their fourth league title |url=https://edition.cnn.com/2015/06/01/football/gallery/zenit-title-celebrations/index.html |access-date=6 September 2020 |work=CNN |date=2 June 2015}}</ref>

==International career==
Prior to making his international debut for the senior side, Witsel played for the [[Belgium national under-21 football team|under-21 side]] in 2007, earning nine caps as well as helping his side reach the semi-finals of the [[2007 UEFA European Under-21 Championship]].

On 26 March 2008, Witsel made his senior international debut for [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]] during a friendly game against [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]]. The match resulted in a 1–4 loss, but Witsel scored his first goal.<ref name = "First int'l goal">{{cite news |title=Axel Witsel marque, Stijn Stijnen se troue deux fois |url=http://archives.lesoir.be/-p-axel-witsel-marque-stijn-stijnen-se-troue-deux-fois-_t-20080327-00FFGG.html |work=Le Soir |date=27 March 2008 |access-date=4 December 2014 |language=fr}}</ref>

[[File:Axel Witsel Belgium.jpg|thumb|left|Witsel lining up for [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]] before playing [[Austria national football team|Austria]] in the qualification for [[UEFA Euro 2012]]]]
Witsel's first goal in a competitive international match came a day short of three years after the Morocco goal, in the sixth minute against [[Austria national football team|Austria]] in [[UEFA Euro 2012 qualifying Group A|Group A]] of the [[UEFA Euro 2012 qualifying]] process, and his second would come in the second half.<ref name="Goals vs Austria" /> However, Belgian hopes of qualifying for [[UEFA Euro 2012]] were dashed on 11 October 2011, by defeat to [[Germany national football team|Germany]] coupled with [[Turkey national football team|Turkey]]'s victory over [[Azerbaijan national football team|Azerbaijan]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Germany stamp out Belgium's fire|date=11 October 2011|first=Steffen|last=Potter|work=uefa.com|publisher=Union of European Football Associations|access-date=24 January 2018|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro/season=2012/matches/round=15171/match=2002112/postmatch/report/index.html#germany+oust+belgium}}</ref>

On 13 May 2014, Witsel was named in [[2014 FIFA World Cup squads#Belgium|Belgium's squad]] for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Adnan Januzaj included in Belgium's World Cup squad|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/27395150|access-date=15 May 2014|newspaper=[[BBC Sport]]|date=13 May 2014}}</ref> He started in midfield in the team's first game of the tournament, a 2–1 win against [[Algeria national football team|Algeria]] in [[Belo Horizonte]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Chowdhury|first1=Saj|title=Belgium 2–1 Algeria|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/25285127|access-date=17 June 2014|work=[[BBC Sport]]|date=17 June 2014}}</ref>

Witsel also featured in the [[UEFA Euro 2016 squads#Belgium|Belgian squad]] at [[UEFA Euro 2016]], announced on 31 May 2016.<ref>{{Cite tweet |author=BelgianRedDevils |user=@BelRedDevils |number=737693491627720705 |date=31 May 2016 |title=.OFFICIAL ! Our final #Euro2016 squad list & numbers ! #TousenFrance |access-date=24 January 2018}}</ref> He scored his first tournament goal during the Championships, in a [[UEFA Euro 2016 Group E|Group E]] match against the [[Republic of Ireland national football team|Republic of Ireland]].<ref name = "Euro 2016 goal">{{cite news |first=Sid |last=Lowe |title=Belgium get going as Romelu Lukaku punishes Republic of Ireland |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/jun/18/belgium-republic-of-ireland-euro-2016-match-report |website=The Guardian |date=18 June 2016 |access-date=14 October 2016}}</ref>

[[File:Axel Witsel 2018.jpg|thumb|right|Axel Witsel playing for [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]] during the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]]]]
He scored twice in [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)|2018 FIFA World Cup qualification]], both in wide victories over [[Gibraltar national football team|Gibraltar]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Dorman |first1=Matt |title=Belgium 9 Gibraltar 0: Trebles for Meunier and Lukaku as Hazard makes scoring return |url=http://www.goal.com/en-us/news/55/main/2017/08/31/38240092/belgium-9-gibraltar-0-trebles-for-meunier-and-lukaku-as |access-date=4 September 2018 |publisher=Goal.com |date=31 August 2017}}</ref> Manager [[Roberto Martínez]] named him in the squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|final tournament]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite news |title=World Cup 2018: Belgium include Vincent Kompany but Christian Benteke misses out |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44353673 |access-date=4 September 2018 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=4 June 2018}}</ref> Witsel was part of the Belgian squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]] in [[Qatar]], where the team performed badly, failing to make it out of the group stage. On 12 May 2023, Witsel announced his retirement from the national team on [[Instagram]], having made 130 appearances for Belgium.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/sports/soccer/belgium-midfielder-witsel-retires-international-football-2023-05-12/|title=Belgium midfielder Witsel retires from international football|publisher=Reuters|date=12 May 2023|access-date=12 May 2023}}</ref>

==Personal life==
