'''Félix Sánchez Bas''' (born 13 December 1975) is a Spanish [[Head coach|football coach]], who is the current coach of the [[Ecuador national football team]].

He has spent most of his career in Qatar, first with the national youth teams until being appointed to the [[Qatar national football team|senior team]] in 2017. His side won the [[2019 AFC Asian Cup]] and were semi-finalists at the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup]]. He left after their group-stage elimination in their hosting of the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], and became manager of Ecuador.

==Career==
===Qatar===
An [[FC Barcelona]] youth coach, Sánchez moved to Qatar in 2006 and joined [[Aspire Academy]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sport.es/es/noticias/barca/felix-sanchez-cantera-cule-catari-2875949|title=Fèlix Sánchez, de la cantera culé a la catarí|trans-title=Fèlix Sánchez, from the ''culé'' to the Qatari youth setup|work=[[Sport (Spanish newspaper)|Sport]]|language=es|date=27 November 2013|access-date=17 May 2018}}</ref> In 2013, he was appointed coach of the Qatar under-19 side, winning [[2014 AFC U-19 Championship|the following year]]'s [[AFC U-19 Championship]].

On 3 July 2017, after a spell with the [[Qatar national under-20 football team|under-20]]s and [[Qatar national under-23 football team|under-23]]s, Sánchez replaced [[Jorge Fossati]] at the helm of [[Qatar national football team|the senior side]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/felix-sanchez-replaces-jorge-fossati-as-qatar-coach/1x8d2myrvr5i31ucl0xqhdywl1|title=Felix Sanchez replaces Jorge Fossati as Qatar coach|publisher=[[Goal (website)|Goal]]|date=3 July 2017|access-date=17 May 2018}}</ref> On his debut on 16 August, he won 1–0 against [[Andorra national football team|Andorra]] in a [[Exhibition game|friendly]] at [[St George's Park National Football Centre|St George's Park]] in England.<ref>{{cite news |title=Qatar defeat Andorra 1-0 in friendly |url=https://www.albawaba.com/sport/qatar-defeat-andorra-1-0-friendly-1010758 |access-date=8 December 2021 |publisher=[[Al Bawaba]] |date=17 August 2017}}</ref> The side finished the year without [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification|qualifying]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]],<ref>{{cite news |title=China out of 2018 World Cup despite winning in Qatar |url=https://www.scmp.com/sport/soccer/article/2109917/china-out-2018-world-cup-despite-winning-qatar |access-date=8 December 2021 |work=[[South China Morning Post]] |date=6 September 2017}}</ref> and were eliminated from the group stage of the [[23rd Arabian Gulf Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |title=BRAVO BAHRAIN! Qatar knocked out of Gulf Cup after 1-1 draw |url=https://www.gdnonline.com/Details/300731 |access-date=8 December 2021 |publisher=GDN Online |date=30 December 2017}}</ref>

Sánchez led Qatar to the [[AFC Asian Cup]] title for the first time in [[2019 AFC Asian Cup|2019]] tournament, having won all three group matches and knockout stage matches including a 3–1 win over [[Japan national football team|Japan]] in the [[2019 AFC Asian Cup Final|final]], scoring 19 times and conceding only once.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2019/feb/01/qatar-stun-japan-with-3-1-win-to-be-crowned-asian-cup-champions |title=Qatar stun Japan with 3-1 win to be crowned Asian Cup champions|date=1 February 2019|work=[[The Guardian]]|access-date=5 February 2019}}</ref> In May that year, he signed a new contract until the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], to be hosted by Qatar.<ref>{{cite news |title=Félix Sánchez renueva como seleccionador de Qatar hasta el Mundial |trans-title=Félix Sánchez renews as Qatar national team manager until the World Cup|url=https://www.mundodeportivo.com/futbol/20190506/462069887342/felix-sanchez-prolonga-su-contrato-como-tecnico-de-catar-hasta-2022.html |access-date=8 December 2021 |work=[[Mundo Deportivo]] |date=6 May 2019 |language=Spanish}}</ref> Weeks later, the team were invitees to the [[2019 Copa América]] in Brazil, being knocked out in the group.<ref>{{cite news |title=Sergio Agüero guides Argentina past Qatar to reach Copa América last eight |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2019/jun/23/qatar-argentina--copa-america-match-report |access-date=8 December 2021 |work=The Guardian |date=23 June 2019}}</ref> In December, at the [[24th Arabian Gulf Cup]] on home soil, the side reached the semi-finals.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Aziz |first1=Saba |title=Arabian Gulf Cup: Football, blockade and an 'historic' win |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/features/2019/12/9/arabian-gulf-cup-football-blockade-and-an-historic-win |access-date=8 December 2021 |publisher=[[Al Jazeera]] |date=9 December 2019}}</ref>

Qatar were also invited to the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup]] in the United States, where [[United States men's national soccer team|the hosts]] eliminated them 1–0 in the semi-finals.<ref>{{cite news |title=Gyasi Zardes' late strike lifts US over Qatar into record 12th Gold Cup final |url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2021/jul/29/usmnt-qatar-gold-cup-scores |access-date=8 December 2021 |work=The Guardian |date=30 July 2021}}</ref> At the end of the year, his side made it to the semi-finals of the [[2021 FIFA Arab Cup|inaugural]] [[FIFA Arab Cup]] on home turf,<ref>{{cite news |title=Qatar 1 Algeria 2 - Highlights |url=https://www.beinsports.com/en/football/news/qatar-1-algeria-2-highlights/1800704 |access-date=15 December 2021 |publisher=beIN Sports |date=15 December 2021}}</ref> eventually finishing third.<ref>{{cite news |last1=McAuley |first1=John |title=Qatar seal third place at Fifa Arab Cup with shootout joy against Egypt in Doha |url=https://www.thenationalnews.com/sport/football/2021/12/18/qatar-seal-third-place-at-fifa-arab-cup-with-shootout-joy-against-egypt-in-doha/ |access-date=18 December 2021 |work=[[The National (Abu Dhabi)|The National]] |date=18 December 2021}}</ref> In the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] hosted by Qatar, the national team lost all their matches in Group A, to become the worst performing host nation in the history of the competition. His contract with Qatar expired on 31 December of the same year and was not renewed.<ref>{{cite web |author=Jacob Whitehead |url=https://theathletic.com/4046326/2022/12/30/qatar-sanchez-world-cup/ |title=Qatar part company with coach Felix Sanchez after World Cup failure |website=The Athletic |date=30 December 2022 }}</ref>

===Ecuador===
On 11 March 2023, Sánchez signed a four-year deal to be manager of the [[Ecuador national football team]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Ecuador appoint former Qatar coach Sanchez as manager |url=https://www.reuters.com/lifestyle/sports/ecuador-appoint-former-qatar-coach-sanchez-manager-2023-03-12/ |access-date=3 May 2023 |publisher=Reuters |date=12 March 2023}}</ref> On his debut thirteen days later, the team lost 3–1 on his debut, a friendly with [[Australia men's national soccer team|Australia]] in [[Sydney]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Man City’s Robertson becomes third generation of family to play for Australia |url=https://sportstar.thehindu.com/football/international-football/alex-robertson-man-city-australia-vs-ecuador-third-generation-player-graham-arnold-felix-sanchez-guardiola-football/article66657262.ece |access-date=3 May 2023 |work=The HIndu |date=24 March 2023}}</ref>
