'''Bailey Colin Wright''' (born 28 July 1992) is an Australian professional [[association footballer|footballer]] who plays as a centre-back for Singapore premier club [[Lion City Sailors FC|Lion City Sailors]], and the [[Australia men's national soccer team|Australia national team]].

Wright has played his entire senior career in England, with [[Preston North End F.C.|Preston North End]], [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]] and Sunderland.

He was selected for Australia's 23-man squad for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Brazil]] and 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].

==Club career==
Wright spent his formative years at [[Langwarrin SC|Langwarrin]], before playing a season each with Mornington and [[Dandenong Thunder]]. He was identified as a promising talent, eventually earning selection for the Victorian state team in 2007.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.pnefc.net/page/NewsDetail/0,,10362~2026074,00.html |title=The Wright Stuff |date=16 April 2010 |publisher=PNEFC |access-date=11 August 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100418045803/http://www.pnefc.net/page/NewsDetail/0%2C%2C10362~2026074%2C00.html |archive-date=18 April 2010}}</ref> He honed his skills further with the [[Victorian Institute of Sport Football (Soccer) Program|Victorian Institute of Sport]] in 2008, returning to Langwarrin in 2009 where he made several senior league appearances, before moving overseas in July 2009, signing a two-year scholarship with [[Preston North End F.C.|Preston North End]] after a protracted clearance saga.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.goalweekly.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=387:ffa-ends-wright-parish-standoff&catid=93:local-news&Itemid=114 |title=FFA ends Wright, Parish standoff |date=10 November 2009 |publisher=Goal!Weekly |access-date=11 August 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110711103127/http://www.goalweekly.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=387%3Affa-ends-wright-parish-standoff&catid=93%3Alocal-news&Itemid=114 |archive-date=11 July 2011}}</ref>

===Preston North End===
In his first season with Preston North End, Wright played predominantly as a centre-half for the reserves and under-18 teams, but was eventually called up to the senior squad in April, sitting on the bench for the club's fixture away to [[Coventry City]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.pnefc.net/page/NewsDetail/0,,10362~2028252,00.html |title=Wright: An Amazing First Year |date=21 April 2010 |publisher=PNEFC |access-date=11 August 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100422005436/http://www.pnefc.net/page/NewsDetail/0%2C%2C10362~2028252%2C00.html |archive-date=22 April 2010}}</ref> He commenced the 2010–11 season with his debut senior appearance in the first round of the [[League Cup]], playing the full 90 minutes in the Lilywhites' 5–0 win against [[Stockport County]]. On 13 December 2010, Wright was handed a two-and-a-half-year professional contract.

Wright made his league debut for Preston on 5 March 2011 against [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://au.fourfourtwo.com/news/198177,aussies-abroad-weekend-wrap.aspx |title=Aussies Abroad Weekend Wrap – Australia News – Australian FourFourTwo – the Ultimate Football Website |access-date=7 March 2011 |archive-date=4 October 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121004032525/http://au.fourfourtwo.com/news/198177,aussies-abroad-weekend-wrap.aspx |url-status=dead}}</ref> He scored his first professional goal for Preston on 7 April 2012 against [[MK Dons]].

Wright was voted Young Player of the Year of Preston North End for the 2012–13 season.<ref>{{cite web |title=John Welsh Is 2013 Player Of The Year |url=http://www.pnefc.net/news/article/john-welsh-is-2013-player-of-the-year-280413-796916.aspx |work=Preston North End FC |date=28 April 2013 |access-date=29 April 2013}}</ref> On 1 May 2013, he was handed a new two-year contract, with the option of staying at Preston North End for another year.<ref>{{cite web |title=Preston North End: Bailey Wright signs new contract |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/22372168 |work=Preston North End FC |date=1 May 2013 |access-date=4 May 2013}}</ref>

Wright won promotion via the play-Offs with Preston in 2015, defeating Swindon Town 4–0 at Wembley on 24 May 2015.

===Bristol City===
[[File:Baily Wright BCFC.jpg|thumb|left|Wright with Bristol City in 2019]]
On 6 January 2017, it was announced that Wright joined [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]] for an undisclosed fee, signing a {{frac|2|1|2}}-year contract.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.bristolpost.co.uk/defender-bailey-wright-signs-for-bristol-city-from-preston-north-end/story-30035647-detail/story.html |title=Defender Bailey Wright signs for Bristol City from Preston North End |newspaper=[[Bristol Post]] |date=6 January 2017}}</ref> On 7 January 2017, he made his debut in the FA Cup third round tie against Fleetwood Town. He scored his first goal for Bristol City in a 1–1 draw with [[Norwich City]] on 7 March 2017.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/39111358 |title=Bristol City 1–1 Norwich |publisher=[[BBC Sport]] |date=7 March 2017|access-date=31 March 2017}}</ref> At the start of the 2017–18 season, Wright was named Bristol City captain.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com.au/news/socceroos-defender-named-bristol-city-skipper-470141 |title=SOCCEROOS DEFENDER NAMED BRISTOL CITY SKIPPER |publisher=FourFourTwo |date=4 August 2017|access-date=4 August 2017}}</ref>

Wright featured as Bristol City reached the semi-finals of the [[2017–18 EFL Cup]] with wins over [[Premier League]] opponents [[Watford F.C.|Watford]],<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40932205 |title=Watford 2–3 Bristol City |date=22 August 2017 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> [[Stoke City]],<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41235275 |title=Bristol City 2–0 Stoke City |date=19 September 2017 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]]<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41647095 |title=Bristol City 4–1 Crystal Palace |date=24 October 2017 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> and [[Manchester United]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/42334221 |title=Bristol City 2–1 Manchester United |date=20 December 2017 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> Wright played as City lost in the semi-final tie against Premier League leaders [[Manchester City]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/42537815 |title=Manchester City 2–1 Bristol City |date=9 January 2018 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> Wright was released by Bristol City at the end of his contract in July 2020.<ref>[https://www.bcfc.co.uk/news/best-wishes-to-bailey-wright/ BEST WISHES TO BAILEY WRIGHT], 2 July 2020, Bristol City official website</ref>

===Sunderland===
Wright signed a six-month loan deal with [[EFL League One|League One]] club [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]] on 21 January 2020.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/51190559 |title=Bailey Wright: Sunderland bring in Bristol City defender on loan |date=21 January 2020 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> On 2 August 2020, Wright signed for Sunderland on a permanent basis, on a two-year deal.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2 August 2020 |title=Wright returns to Sunderland |url=https://www.safc.com:443/news/team-news/2020/july/bailey-wright-returns-to-sunderland|access-date=5 August 2020 |publisher=Sunderland AFC}}</ref> He scored his first goal for Sunderland in a 2–2 draw with [[Rochdale A.F.C.|Rochdale]] on 27 October 2020.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54610182 |title=Rochdale 2-2 Sunderland |date=27 October 2020 |publisher=BBC|access-date=27 October 2020}}</ref>

Wright joined fellow Championship club [[Rotherham United F.C.|Rotherham United]] in a deadline day loan move on 31 January 2023.<ref>{{cite news |title=Signing {{!}} Millers get loan deal Wright on Deadline Day |url=https://www.themillers.co.uk/news/2023/january/signing--millers-get-it-wright-on-deadline-day/ |access-date=31 January 2023 |publisher=Rotherham United FC |date=31 January 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
Wright was a member of the [[Australia national under-17 soccer team|Australia under-17 squad]] that reached the quarter-finals of the [[AFC U-16 Championship]], narrowly missing a place at the [[2009 FIFA U-17 World Cup]] when they lost 2–3 against [[United Arab Emirates national under-17 football team|United Arab Emirates]].

He was also selected as part of [[Ange Postecoglou]] 23-man squad for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]] in Brazil, one of two new inclusions.

Wright made his national debut starting in a friendly match against [[Saudi Arabia national football team|Saudi Arabia]] at [[Craven Cottage]] in [[London]]. He scored a header in this debut giving Australia a two-goal lead.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.fourfourtwo.com/au/news/wright-target-dream-debut-socceroos |title=Wright on target in dream debut for Socceroos |publisher=[[FourFourTwo]] |first=John |last=Davidson |date=9 September 2014|access-date=9 September 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140909200558/http://www.fourfourtwo.com/au/news/wright-target-dream-debut-socceroos|archive-date=9 September 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

He was in the [[Australia national football team]] preliminary squad for the [[2018 World Cup]] in Russia but did not make [[Bert van Marwijk]]'s final 23.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.foxsports.com.au/football/world-cup/jamie-maclaren-mitch-langerak-among-big-names-cut-as-socceroos-world-cup-squad-trimmed-to-26/news-story/d079951795febf2d43b4e13092713f50 |title=Jamie Maclaren, Mitch Langerak among big names cut as Socceroos World Cup squad trimmed to 26 |date=14 May 2018}}</ref>

He was selected as part of [[Graham Arnold]] 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]] in which he came on as a substitution in the last group stage fixtures against [[Denmark national football team|Denmark]].
