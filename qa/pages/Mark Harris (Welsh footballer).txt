'''Thomas Mark Harris''' (born 29 December 1998) is a Welsh professional [[association football|footballer]] who last played for {{English football updater|CardiffC}} club [[Cardiff City F.C.|Cardiff City]] and the [[Wales national football team|Wales national team]]. A versatile [[Forward (association football)|forward]], he is able to play as a [[Midfielder#Winger|winger]] or as a striker.

He has represented Wales at [[Wales national under-17 football team|under-17]], [[Wales national under-19 football team|under-19]], [[Wales national under-20 football team|under-20]] and [[Wales national under-21 football team|under-21]] level. He turned professional at [[Cardiff City F.C.|Cardiff City]] in December 2016, and spent the 2018–19 season on loan at [[Newport County A.F.C.|Newport County]] and [[Port Vale F.C.|Port Vale]]. He spent the first half of the 2019–20 season on loan at [[Wrexham A.F.C.|Wrexham]], before breaking into the Cardiff first-team during the 2020–21 campaign, though was released in June 2023.

==Club career==

2–1 defeat to [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]] in the [[FA Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/38481666#tab-0 |title=Cardiff City 1–2 Fulham |publisher=BBC Sport |date=8 January 2017 |accessdate=8 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cardiffcityfc.co.uk/news/article/2016-17/mark-harris-signs-professional-deal-3517465.aspx |title=Mark Harris signs professional deal |url-status=dead }}</ref> A series of illnesses and injuries throughout the Cardiff squad gave Harris the chance to make his [[EFL Championship|Championship]] debut on 22 April, playing in a five-man midfield in a 0–0 draw at [[Wigan Athletic F.C.|Wigan Athletic]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Wigan Athletic 0–0 Cardiff City |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/39604693 |accessdate=23 January 2019 appearance in the [[2016–17 Cardiff City F.C. season|2016–17]] season, though did not feature in the "Bluebirds" [[2017–18 Cardiff City F.C. season|2017–18]] promotion campaign.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Mitchelmore |first1=Ian |title=Ball boy to first team starter – Mark Harris' incredible season On 2 August 2018, Harris joined [[EFL League Two|League Two]] side [[Newport County A.F.C.|Newport County]] on an initial six-month loan deal.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.newport-county.co.uk/news/2018/august/welcome-mark-harris/ |title=Newport County AFC sign Mark Harris on loan |publisher=Newport County A.F.C. |date=2 August 2018 |accessdate=2 August 2018}}</ref> He made his debut for the "Exiles" on 11 August, in a 1–0 victory over [[Crewe Alexandra F.C.|Crewe Alexandra]] at [[Rodney Parade]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45067083 |title=Newport County 1–0 Crewe Alexandra |publisher=BBC Sport |date=11 August 2018 |accessdate=11 August 2018}}</ref> He scored his first goal for the club seven days later, in a 1–1 draw at [[Exeter City F.C.|Exeter City]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45152980 |title=Exeter City 1–1 Newport County |publisher=BBC Sport |date=18 August 2018 |accessdate=18 August 2018}}</ref> After initially establishing himself in the first-team, Harris suffered a concussion while on international duty and was unable to regain his place on his return due to the form of fellow forwards [[Jamille Matt]] and [[Pádraig Amond]].<ref name="totf"/> He made a total of 20 appearances in all competitions during his loan spell, scoring three times, before returning to the [[Cardiff City Stadium]] in January 2019 despite Newport manager [[Michael Flynn (footballer)|Mike Flynn]]'s statement that he was confident of extending the loan deal.<ref>{{cite news |title=Mike Flynn: Newport boss 'confident' on Harris, Pring & Bakinson loan extensions |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/46619792 |accessdate=23 January 2019 |work=BBC Sport |date=19 December 2018}}</ref><ref name="totf">{{cite news |url=https://www.southwalesargus.co.uk/sport/17345549.newport-county-boss-michael-flynn-on-transfer-targets-and-mark-harris-return-to-cardiff-city/ |title=Newport County boss Michael Flynn on transfer targets and Mark Harris' return to Cardiff City |publisher=South Wales Argus |last=Penman |first=Andrew |date=9 January 2019 |accessdate=9 January 2019}}</ref>

On 23 January 2019, he signed for struggling League Two side [[Port Vale F.C.|Port Vale]] on loan for the remainder of the [[2018–19 Port Vale F.C. season|2018–19]] season.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/46976933 |title=Mark Harris: Port Vale sign striker on loan from Cardiff City |publisher=BBC Sport |date=23 January 2019 |accessdate=23 January 2019}}</ref> He made three starts and three substitute appearances for the "Valiants" before being allowed to return to Cardiff early by manager [[John Askey]] after going five weeks without a first-team game.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Baggaley |first1=Michael |title=Cardiff City striker leaves Port Vale |url=https://www.stokesentinel.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/cardiff-city-port-vale-harris-2816716 |accessdate=30 April 2019 |work=Stoke Sentinel |date=30 April 2019}}</ref>

On 24 June 2019, Harris signed for [[National League (division)|National League]] club [[Wrexham A.F.C.|Wrexham]] on a season-long loan.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/48752955|title=Mark Harris: Wrexham sign Cardiff City forward on loan|date=24 June 2019|accessdate=24 June 2019|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> "Red Dragons" manager [[Bryan Hughes]] commented that "he'll fit in well to our system. He can play across front three and he is an exciting talent".<ref>{{cite news |last1=Baggaley |first1=Michael |title=Former Port Vale striker joins fellow ex Valiants at National League hopefuls |url=https://www.stokesentinel.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/mark-harris-cardiff-wrexham-vale-3018226 |accessdate=25 June 2019 |work=Stoke Sentinel |date=25 June 2019}}</ref> Harris scored his first goal in his second game for Wrexham, in their 2–2 draw at [[Boreham Wood F.C.|Boreham Wood]] on 6 August.<ref>{{cite news |title=Boreham Wood 2–2 Wrexham |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49160105 |accessdate=15 August 2019 |work=BBC Sport |date=6 August 2019}}</ref> He then followed that up with goals in the next two games, in a 2–1 loss at [[Dover Athletic F.C.|Dover Athletic]] and then getting the only goal of the game against [[F.C. Halifax Town]] at the [[Racecourse Ground]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Dover Athletic 2–1 Wrexham |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49216037 |accessdate=15 August 2019 |work=BBC Sport |date=10 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Wrexham AFC 1 FC Halifax Town 0 – Harris stunner wins it for 10-man Dragons|url=https://www.dailypost.co.uk/sport/football/match-reports/wrexham-afc-1-fc-halifax-16750411 |accessdate=13 August 2019 |publisher=Daily Post |date=13 August 2019}}</ref> However he lost his first-team place under new manager [[Dean Keates]] and his loan was terminated early on 7 January.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Griffiths |first1=Rob |title=On loan winger signs off from Wrexham AFC stint with classy message |url=https://www.dailypost.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/loan-winger-signs-wrexham-afc-17523798 |accessdate=3 April 2020 |work=northwales |date=7 January 2020}}</ref>

Harris came off the bench to make his first appearance of the [[2020–21 Cardiff City F.C. season|2020–21]] season in a 3–2 defeat away at [[Queens Park Rangers F.C.|Queens Park Rangers]] on 31 October.<ref>{{cite news |title=Queens Park Rangers 3–2 Cardiff City |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54671011 |accessdate=1 November 2020 |work=BBC Sport |date=31 October 2020}}</ref> On 28 November, Harris started his first game of the season and scored his side's second goal in a 4–0 victory over [[Luton Town F.C.|Luton Town]], his first senior goal for the club.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/55025697 |title=Cardiff 4–0 Luton |publisher=BBC Sport |last=Pearlman |first=Michael |date=28 November 2020 |accessdate=29 November 2020}}</ref> With [[Neil Harris (footballer, born 1977)|Neil Harris]] favouring his namesake over £5.5 million signing [[Robert Glatzel]], his inclusion in the starting eleven was seen as a gamble by Harris.<ref name="hmc">{{cite news |last1=Williams |first1=Glen |title=What next for Mark Harris, the Cardiff City kid who now has to scrap for minutes |url=https://www.walesonline.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/what-next-mark-harris-cardiff-20543892 |access-date=12 May 2021 |work=WalesOnline |date=7 May 2021 |language=en}}</ref> He partnered well with [[Kieffer Moore]] however, until Moore was sidelined with injury after six games.<ref name="hmc"/> Harris then faced stiff competition in the second half of the campaign after new manager [[Mick McCarthy]] signed [[Max Watters]] and utilised wingers [[Josh Murphy]] and [[Sheyi Ojo]] as strikers.<ref name="hmc"/> He went on to score impressive goals in comfortable wins over [[Preston North End F.C.|Preston North End]] and [[Birmingham City F.C.|Birmingham City]] to end the season with three goals in 17 appearances.<ref name="20/21 appearances"/>

He made 17 starts and 17 substitute appearances in the [[2021–22 Cardiff City F.C. season|2021–22]] Championship campaign as Cardiff looked to intergrade more Welsh players into the first-team.<ref name="21/22 appearances"/><ref>{{cite news |title=Why more Welsh players have appeared for Cardiff in the last two seasons than the previous nine combined |url=https://nation.cymru/sport/why-more-welsh-players-have-appeared-for-cardiff-in-the-last-two-seasons-than-the-previous-nine-combined/ |access-date=22 May 2022 |work=Nation.Cymru |date=12 May 2022}}</ref> Speaking in February, manager [[Steve Morison]] said that he was giving Harris a run in the starting eleven due to his impressive work in training and was rewarded as he scored his fourth goal of the campaign.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Coleman |first1=Tom |title=Cardiff headlines as Harris a 'man possessed' and Tomlin's future turns again |url=https://www.walesonline.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/cardiff-city-headlines-mark-harris-23114408 |access-date=22 May 2022 |work=WalesOnline |date=16 February 2022 |language=en}}</ref> He was linked with a move to [[Millwall F.C.|Millwall]] in August 2023, though Morison denied having received any offers for Harris.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Coleman |first1=Tom |title=Cardiff news as Steve Morison lifts lid on Mark Harris transfer speculation |url=https://www.walesonline.co.uk/sport/football/transfer-news/cardiff-city-transfer-news-morison-24916326 |access-date=11 May 2023 |work=WalesOnline |date=2 September 2022 |language=en}}</ref> He featured 38 times in the [[2022–23 Cardiff City F.C. season|2022–23]] season, scoring three goals. Harris was offered a new contract by new manager [[Sabri Lamouchi]] in May 2023 but contract talks stalled and he left the club after the expiration of his contract.<ref name="22/23 appearances"/><ref>{{cite news |last1=Williams |first1=Glen |title=Cardiff City offer striker new deal as Lamouchi confirms others have to leave |url=https://www.walesonline.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/cardiff-city-offer-striker-new-26849245 |access-date=11 May 2023 |work=WalesOnline |date=5 May 2023 |language=en}}</ref>

==International career==
Harris represented Wales at [[Wales national under-17 football team|under-17]] level, scoring the winning goal against [[Montenegro national under-17 football team|Montenegro]] in [[Minsk]] to secure Wales a place in the Elite Round of [[2015 UEFA European Under-17 Championship qualification|qualification]] for the [[2015 UEFA European Under-17 Championship]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Under-17 – Montenegro-Wales |url=https://www.uefa.com/under17/season=2015/matches/round=2000513/match=2013429/index.html |website=UEFA.com |accessdate=23 January 2019 |language=en}}</ref> He went on to be called up to the [[Wales national under-19 football team|under-19]] team and on 12 November 2016 he scored Wales' second goal of a 3–2 victory over [[England national under-19 football team|England]], only to score an own goal two minutes later; despite the victory Wales failed to [[2017 UEFA European Under-19 Championship qualification|qualify]] for the [[2017 UEFA European Under-19 Championship]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Under-19 – England-Wales |url=https://www.uefa.com/under19/season=2017/matches/round=2000775/match=2019133/index.html |website=UEFA.com |accessdate=23 January 2019 |language=en}}</ref> In May 2017, [[Rob Page]] named Harris in the [[Wales national under-20 football team|Wales under-20]] squad for the [[2017 Toulon Tournament]].<ref>{{cite news |title=FAW / Wales squad announced for Toulon Tournament |url=http://www.faw.cymru/en/news/wales-squad-announced-toulon-tournament/ |accessdate=23 January 2019 |work=www.faw.cymru |date=15 May 2017 |language=en}}</ref> After making his debut for the side in the tournament opener against [[Ivory Coast national under-20 football team|Ivory Coast]] as a substitute in place of [[Tyler Roberts]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.festival-foot-espoirs.com/direct_festival_foot_uk.php?tournoi_id=15 |title=Wales U20 2–2 Cote D'Ivoire |publisher=Foot Espoirs |accessdate=16 June 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170601021623/http://www.festival-foot-espoirs.com/direct_festival_foot_uk.php?tournoi_id=15 |archive-date=1 June 2017 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Harris was named in the starting line-up in Wales' remaining two group matches against [[France national under-20 football team|France]] and [[Bahrain national under-20 football team|Bahrain]] as Wales were eliminated in the group stage.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40100903 |title=Toulon Tournament: France U20 0–0 Wales U20 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=30 May 2017 |accessdate=16 June 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40139643 |title=Toulon Tournament: Wales U20 1–0 Bahrain U20 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=2 June 2017}}</ref> He made a total of two appearances for the under-17s, five appearances for the under-19s and three appearances for the under-20s.<ref>{{cite web |title=Mark Harris – Forward – First Team – Newport County |url=https://www.newport-county.co.uk/teams/first-team/forward/mark-harris/ |website=www.newport-county.co.uk |accessdate=23 January 2019 debut for the [[Wales national under-21 football team|under-21]] side on 1 September 2017, coming on as a substitute in place of Tyler Roberts in a 3–0 victory over [[Switzerland national under-21 football team|Switzerland]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2017/09/01/europe/uefa-u21-championship/switzerland-under-21/wales-under-21/2429856/ |title=Switzerland U21 0–3 Wales U21 |publisher=Soccerway |accessdate=1 September 2017}}</ref>

On 30 August 2021, Harris received his first call up to the [[Wales national football team|senior squad]] as manager [[Rob Page]] sought replacements for the injured [[Aaron Ramsey]], [[Joe Rodon]], [[George Thomas (footballer, born 1997)|George Thomas]] and [[Neco Williams]].<ref>{{cite news |title=FAW / Cymru Squad Update |url=https://www.faw.cymru/en/news/cymru-squad-update/ |access-date=31 August 2021 |work=www.faw.cymru |date=30 August 2021 |language=en}}</ref> He made his debut on 5 September, replacing clubmate [[Rubin Colwill]] in the 57th-minute of a 3–2 victory against [[Belarus national football team|Belarus]] in a [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group E|2022 FIFA World Cup qualification]] game.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Pritchard |first1=Dafydd |title=Bale hat-trick gives Wales win over Belarus |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58372987 |access-date=6 September 2021 |work=BBC Sport |date=5 September 2021}}</ref> In November 2022, he was named in the Wales squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].<ref>{{cite news |title=The players carrying Welsh hopes at the World Cup |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63545011 |access-date=9 November 2022 |work=BBC Sport |date=9 November 2022}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Harris is a versatile [[Forward (association football)|forward]], able to play as a striker, [[Midfielder#Winger|winger]], or behind the striker. Harris has said that "I don't mind playing up front in a two, my main strength I would say is running in behind and getting at defenders".<ref name="sop">{{cite news |last1=Baggaley |first1=Michael |title=Who is Mark Harris: All about Port Vale's new striker on loan from Cardiff City |url=https://www.stokesentinel.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/mark-harris-port-vale-cardiff-2463140 |accessdate=23 January 2019 |work=Stoke Sentinel |date=23 January 2019}}</ref> Newport County manager Mike Flynn stated that "he's a good finisher, makes intelligent runs, and is very quick".<ref name="sop"/>
