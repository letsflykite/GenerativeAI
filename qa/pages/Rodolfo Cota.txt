'''Rodolfo Cota Robles''' (born 3 July 1987) is a Mexican professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Liga MX]] club [[Club León|León]] and the [[Mexico national football team|Mexico national team]].

==Club career==
===Pachuca===
Cota made his professional debut for [[C.F. Pachuca|Pachuca]] on 21 September 2007 against [[C.D. Veracruz|Veracruz]].<ref name = "SW">{{Soccerway|rodolfo-cota/20663|accessdate=15 March 2015}}</ref>

===Puebla===
In 2014 it was announced Cota was sent out on loan to [[Club Puebla|Puebla]].<ref>{{MedioTiempo|rodolfo-cota}}</ref> He made his debut against [[Club Tijuana|Tijuana]] in a 1–0 victory.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.espn.cl/futbol/reporte?juegoId=397690|title=Tijuana vs. Puebla - Reporte del Partido - 18 julio, 2014 - ESPN|website=ESPN.cl}}</ref> In April 2015, he won the [[Clausura 2015 Copa MX|Copa MX]] after defeating [[C.D. Guadalajara|Guadalajara]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Puebla 4-2 Chivas: Cuauhtémoc se retira como campeón de Copa MX|url=https://www.tudn.com/liga-mx/puebla-4-2-chivas-cuauhtemoc-se-retira-como-campeon-de-copa-mx|work=TUDN}}</ref>

===Guadalajara===
On 11 June 2015, [[C.D. Guadalajara|Guadalajara]] announced they had signed Cota on loan in order to create competition with [[José Antonio Rodríguez (Mexican footballer)|José Antonio Rodríguez]], after club veteran [[Luis Ernesto Michel]] was sold to [[Dorados de Sinaloa]]. Cota then quickly rose to be  the first-choice goalkeeper after his performances in the [[Clausura 2017 Copa MX|Copa MX]], becoming champion after defeating [[Monarcas Morelia]] the [[Clausura 2017 Copa MX Final|Copa MX final]], and became a key figure in the club's twelfth [[Liga MX]] title win against [[Tigres UANL]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.chivasdecorazon.com.mx/noticias/25428/comunicado-de-prensa-rodolfo-cota|title=Comunicado de Prensa (Rodolfo Cota)|website=www.chivasdecorazon.com.mx}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Chivas Win 12th Liga MX Championship After Beating Tigres 4-3 on Aggregate|url=https://bleacherreport.com/articles/2712373-chivas-win-12th-liga-mx-championship-after-beating-tigres-4-3-on-aggregate|work=Bleacher Report|date=29 May 2017|first=Tom|last=Sunderland}}</ref> He was named in the Best XI of the tournament and won the Golden Glove award for the season.<ref name = "bestXI">{{Cite web|url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/liga-mx/2017/05/31/592dfc86ca4741c1058b45bf.html|title=Chivas y Tigres dominan el 11 ideal del Clausura 2017|date=31 May 2017|website=MARCA Claro México}}</ref><ref name = "GG">{{cite web|title=Chivas y Almeyda acapararon el Balón de Oro|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/liga-mx/chivas-y-almeyda-acapararon-el-balon-de-oro|work=MedioTiempo.com|date=16 July 2017|language=es}}</ref>

The following season, he proved to be vital again as Guadalajara won the [[2018 CONCACAF Champions League]] against [[Major League Soccer]] club [[Toronto FC]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Chivas Win CONCACAF Champions League|url=https://www.beinsports.com/us/liga-mx/news/chivas-win-concacaf-champions-league/855746|work=BeinSport.com|date=25 April 2018}}</ref>  as he was rewarded the Golden Glove of the tournament and named in the Best XI.<ref name = "GG2">{{cite web|url=https://www.concacafchampionsleague.com/en/golden-glove|title=Golden Glove|publisher=CONCACAF.com|date=25 April 2018}}</ref><ref name = "best2">{{cite web|title=2018 SCCL - Best XI|url=https://www.concacafchampionsleague.com/en/article/2018-sccl-best-xi|website=CONCACAF|access-date=26 April 2018}}</ref>

===León===
On 16 May 2018, Cota was loaned out to Pachuca's sibling club [[Club León|León]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/former-chivas-goalkeeper-cota-moves-to-club-leon/5kzyvqhh63d11gclbiffd459i|title=Chivas transfer news: Goalkeeper Rodolfo Cota signs with Club Leon - Goal.com|website=www.goal.com}}</ref>

Cota controversially wore a jersey to protest against [[Violence against women in Mexico|femicide in Mexico]] during a match against [[Club Necaxa]] on 22 February 2020. The shirt had the figure of a woman lying in a pool of blood in the shape of a map of Mexico. Cota may be suspended for three matches and fined MXN $300,000 (US $15,000).<ref>[https://www.lajornadamaya.mx/2020-02-24/Portero-de-Leon-se-manifiesta-contra-feminicidios--podria-ser-sancionado Goalkeeper of León manifests against femicides; could be sanctioned] {{in lang|es}} La Jornada Maya, 24 February 2020</ref>

==International career==
===Youth===
Cota represented the [[Mexico national under-20 football team|under-20]] squad at the [[2007 FIFA U-20 World Cup]].<ref>{{FIFA player|268714}}</ref>

===Senior===
In September 2016, Cota received his first [[Mexico national football team|senior national team]] call-up under coach [[Juan Carlos Osorio]] for October [[Exhibition game|friendlies]] against [[New Zealand national football team|New Zealand]] and [[Panama national football team|Panama]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Gio y Jona dos Santos, en la convocatoria del Tri para Nueva Zelanda y Panamá|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/liga-mx/gio-jona-santos-convocatoria-tri-zelanda-panama|work=Medio Tiempo|date=29 September 2016|language=es}}</ref>

Cota was included in the preliminary roster for the [[2017 FIFA Confederations Cup]] in Russia as a replacement for [[José de Jesús Corona]] since he picked up an injury.<ref>{{cite web|title= Rodolfo Cota reemplazará a Jesús Corona en Selección Mexicana para Copa Confederaciones|url=https://www.telemundodeportes.com/seleccion-mexico/rodolfo-cota-reemplazara-jesus-corona-en-seleccion-mexicana-para-copa-confederaciones|work=Telemundo Deportes|date=25 May 2017|language=es}}</ref> Cota made his debut on 1 June 2017 in a friendly match against [[Republic of Ireland national football team|Ireland]] at [[MetLife Stadium]] in New Jersey, concluding in a 3–1 victory.<ref>{{cite web|title=Cota, Hernandez thrilled with Mexico debuts|url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/cota-hernandez-thrilled-with-mexico-debuts/197exxbiqgq1e1tew9hxhd9ni3|work=Goal.com|date=1 June 2017}}</ref> Cota was subsequently included in Mexico's final roster for the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|title=Los 23 de Osorio para la Confederaciones |url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/seleccion-mx/2017/06/08/593936b2468aeb80498b46a4.html |work=Marca |language=es |date=8 June 2017 |access-date=10 October 2018}}</ref>

In October 2022, Cota was named in Mexico's preliminary 31-man [[Mexico national football team|squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], and in November, he was ultimately included in the final 26-man roster, but did not receive any minutes on the field during the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lista de convocados de la Selección Mexicana al Mundial de Qatar 2022|url=https://www.tvazteca.com/aztecadeportes/lista-oficial-convocados-seleccion-mexicana-mundial-qatar-2022|work=Tvazteca.com|first=Omar|last=Moro|date=14 November 2022|language=Spanish}}</ref>
