==Playing career==

During his time as a player, Daliƒá played for a number of clubs. 

He started off his youth career at [[NK Troglav 1918 Livno|Troglav 1918 Livno]], before joining [[HNK Hajduk Split|Hajduk Split]] in 1983 and thus starting his senior career. He stayed at Hajduk Split until 1986, after joining [[HNK Cibalia|Cibalia]], which was at that time known as Dinamo Vinkovci. Apart from Hajduk Split and Cibalia, Daliƒá also played for [[FK Buduƒánost Podgorica|Buduƒánost Titograd]], [[FK Vele≈æ Mostar|Vele≈æ Mostar]] and [[NK Vara≈ædin (1931‚Äì2015)|Varteks]]. He finished his career in 2000 at Varteks.

==Coaching career==

===Varteks===
After ending his playing career in 2000, he became assistant coach at [[NK Vara≈ædin (1931‚Äì2015)|Varteks]]. From May 2002 to May 2005, Daliƒá worked as the club's sports director, and during the 2003‚Äì04 and 2004‚Äì05 seasons he simultaneously acted as assistant coach for [[Miroslav Bla≈æeviƒá]].

In May 2005, he was appointed manager of Varteks and in his first season at the helm he won third place in the [[Croatian First Football League|Croatian First League]] and reached the final of the [[2004‚Äì05 Croatian Football Cup|Croatian Cup]]. In the first leg of the final, which was played in [[Rijeka]], Varteks lost 0‚Äì4 to [[HNK Rijeka]]. In the second leg in [[Vara≈ædin]], Daliƒá nearly created a sensation leading the team to a 5‚Äì1 win, but they were one goal short from lifting the silverware.<ref name="varri" />

===Rijeka===
In the summer of 2007, after his contract with Varteks ended, he became manager of Rijeka, and finished fourth at the end of the [[2007‚Äì08 Croatian First Football League|2007‚Äì08 season]]. After being knocked out of the [[2008 UEFA Intertoto Cup]] by [[FK Renova]], Daliƒá was sacked on 1 July 2008 just before the start of the [[2008‚Äì09 Croatian First Football League|season]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sportnet.rtl.hr/vijesti/361939/nogomet-1-hnl/zlatko-dalic-smijenjen/|title=Zlatko Daliƒá smijenjen!|trans-title=Zlatko Daliƒá sacked!|publisher=Sportnet.rtl.hr/|language=hr|date=1 July 2008|access-date=23 December 2018|archive-date=24 December 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181224023723/https://sportnet.rtl.hr/vijesti/361939/nogomet-1-hnl/zlatko-dalic-smijenjen/|url-status=live}}</ref>

===Dinamo Tirana===
In the 2008‚Äì09 season, Daliƒá managed Albanian champions [[Dinamo Tirana]], with whom he won the [[Albanian Supercup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.arkivalajmeve.com/Dinamo-fiton-Superkupen-e-Shqiperise.70236/|title=Dinamo fiton Superkupen e Shqiperise|trans-title=Dinamo wins Albanian Supercup|publisher=Arkiva Shqiptare e Lajmeve|language=sq|date=18 August 2008|access-date=7 July 2018|archive-date=22 February 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210222023056/http://www.arkivalajmeve.com/Dinamo-fiton-Superkupen-e-Shqiperise.70236/|url-status=live}}</ref> He resigned in February 2009 after losing two derbies in a row versus fellow capital teams [[KF Tirana|Tirana]] and [[FK Partizani Tirana|Partizani]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://top-channel.tv/2008/02/05/dinamo-dalic-falenderon-lojtaret/|title=Dinamo, Dalic falenderon lojtaret|trans-title=Dinamo, Daliƒá thank the players|publisher=Top-Channel.tv|language=sq|date=5 February 2009|access-date=7 July 2018|archive-date=8 July 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180708074327/http://top-channel.tv/2008/02/05/dinamo-dalic-falenderon-lojtaret/|url-status=live}}</ref>

===Slaven Belupo===
After a short stint in Albania, he returned to Croatia in 2009 and took over his third Croatian football club, [[NK Slaven Belupo|Slaven Belupo]].<ref name="tportal" />

===Al-Faisaly===
In the 2010‚Äì11 season, he became head coach of [[Al-Faisaly FC|Al-Faisaly]]. At the end of the 2010‚Äì11 season under Daliƒá the club enjoyed the greatest success in its history, qualifying for the King's Cup in the [[Saudi Professional League]].<ref name="dalje" /> Daliƒá was named Coach of the Year in the [[Saudi Professional League]] for the 2010‚Äì11 season by Al Riyadh newspapers.<ref name="alriyadhnewspapers" /> In the selection, he beat other more famous coaches working in Saudi Arabia at the time, such as [[Gabriel Calder√≥n]], [[Walter Zenga]] and [[Eric Gerets]].
