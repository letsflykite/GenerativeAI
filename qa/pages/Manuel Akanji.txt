'''Manuel Obafemi Akanji''' (born 19 July 1995) is a Swiss professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] or [[left-back]] for {{English football updater|ManchesC}} club [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] and the [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland national team]].

==Club career==
===Youth football and early career===
Akanji began his youth career with the local club in [[Wiesendangen]]. In May 2007, Akanji changed clubs and was a youth-team player for [[FC Winterthur]], featuring for their Under-18 team and later with their second team. In the [[2014–15 Swiss Challenge League|2014–15 Challenge League]], he became a regular starter for Winterthur having played two games for them during the second half of the 2013–14 season.{{citation needed|date=August 2022}}

===FC Basel===
On 15 April 2015, it was announced that Akanji would transfer to [[FC Basel]] for the [[2015–16 Swiss Super League|2015–16 Swiss Super League season]].<ref name="TW">[http://www.tageswoche.ch/de/2015_16/sport/685528/ ''Ein neuer Verteidiger für den FCB: Manuel Akanji kommt vom FC Winterthur''] [[TagesWoche]] dated 15. April 2015</ref> He made his [[Swiss Super League]] debut on 26 September 2015 being substituted in against [[FC Lugano]]. Under trainer [[Urs Fischer (footballer)|Urs Fischer]], Akanji won the [[Swiss Super League]] championship at the end of the [[2015–16 Swiss Super League|2015–16 Super League season]]<ref name="fcb 2016-04-30">{{cite web | first = Casper | last = Marti | author-link = | year = 2016 | url = http://www.fcb.ch/de-CH/News/2016/04/30/FCB-Sion | title = Es ist vollbracht ! Der FCB ist zum 19. Mal Meister | publisher = FC Basel | accessdate = 30 April 2016}}</ref> and at the end of the [[2016–17 Swiss Super League|2016–17 Super League season]] for the second time. For the club this was the eighth title in a row and their 20th championship title in total. They also won the [[Swiss Cup]] for the twelfth time, which meant they had won the double for the sixth time in the club's history.<ref name="fcb 2017-06-02">{{cite web | first = Casper  | last = Marti | author-link = | year = 2017 | url = https://www.fcb.ch/de-CH/News/2017/06/02/Der-Saisonabschluss-im-Zeitraffer | title = Der Saisonabschluss im Zeitraffer | publisher = FC Basel | accessdate = 2 June 2017}}</ref>

As Swiss Champions, Basel qualified for the [[2017–18 UEFA Champions League]] and started in the [[2017–18 UEFA Champions League group stage|Group stage]]. Akanji played in all six games over the full 90 minutes and helped the team finish in second group position, thus qualifying for the next round. On 15 January 2018, Basel announced that Akanji had transferred to [[Borussia Dortmund]].<ref name="fcb 2018-01-15">{{cite web | last = FC Basel | author-link = | year = 2018 | url = https://www.fcb.ch/de-CH/News/2018/01/15/Manuel-Akanji-wechselt-zu-Borussia-Dortmund | title = Manuel Akanji wechselt zu Borussia Dortmund | publisher = FC Basel | accessdate = 15 January 2018}}</ref>

===Borussia Dortmund===
Akanji transferred to Borussia Dortmund on 15 January 2018 during the winter transfer window for a reported fee of 18 million euros.<ref>[http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/startseite/714978/artikel_wechsel-perfekt-bvb-bekommt-akanji-sofort.html "Wechsel perfekt! BVB bekommt Akanji sofort"] ''[[Kicker (sports magazine)|kicker]]'', 15 January 2018. Retrieved on 15 January 2018.</ref> He signed a four and a half year contract dated until June 2022.<ref name="bvb 2018-01-15">{{cite web | last = Borussia Dortmund | author-link = | year = 2018 | url = http://www.bvb.de/News/Uebersicht/BVB-verpflichtet-Schweizer-Nationalspieler-Manuel-Akanji | title = BVB verpflichtet Schweizer Nationalspieler Manuel Akanji| publisher = Borussia Dortmund | accessdate = 15 January 2018}}</ref> On 27 September, Akanji scored his first goal for the club and his first Bundesliga goal in a 7–0 victory over [[1. FC Nürnberg]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/borussia-dortmund-nuremberg-live-line-ups-stats-pulisic-reus-paco-alcacer-fantasy-512935.jsp|title=Marco Reus stars as breathtaking Borussia Dortmund put seven past Nuremberg|publisher=Bundesliga|accessdate=29 September 2018}}</ref> Akanji was widely criticised for his costly errors during Dortmund's failed challenge to Bayern during the 2019–20 season. Akanji was one of the main players singled out as a weak link in the team.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://bvbbuzz.com/2019/11/11/three-borussia-dortmund-players-need-improve/2/|title=Three Borussia Dortmund players who need to improve|date=11 November 2019}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:Manuel Akanji.jpg|thumb|upright|Akanji with [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]] at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]]]]

Akanji made two appearances for the [[Switzerland national under-20 football team|Switzerland U-20 national team]]. His debut was on 7 September 2014 in the 0–0 draw against the [[Germany national under-20 football team|German U-20 national team]]. From 2014, he was a member of the [[Switzerland national under-21 football team|Switzerland U-21 team]] and made his debut for them on 26 March in the 0–3 defeat by the [[Italy national under-21 football team|Italian U-21 team]].{{citation needed|date=August 2022}} Akanji made his debut in the [[Switzerland national football team|senior team]] in the 2–0 win against [[Faroe Islands national football team|Faroe Islands]] in the [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group B|2018 World Cup qualification]] match on 9 June 2017. He played the entire 90 minutes.<ref name="sfv 2017-06-09">{{cite web | first = | last = | author-link = SFV | date = 9 June 2017 | url = https://www.football.ch/SFV/Nationalteams/A-Team/Statistik-und-Resultate.aspx/tg-2628390/ | title = Färöer Inseln 0:2 Schweiz | website =football.ch | publisher = Schweizerischer Fussballverband | accessdate = 9 June 2017}}</ref> He was included in the Swiss team's 23 man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018 World Cup]] in Russia.{{citation needed|date=August 2022}}

In May 2019, Akanji played in [[2019 UEFA Nations League Finals]], where his team finished fourth.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/48191915|title=Pickford the hero in England shootout win|website=BBC Sport}}</ref> In 2021, he was called up to the national team for [[UEFA Euro 2020]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.football.ch/sfv/nationalteams/a-team/news/das-kader-des-schweizer-nationalteams-fuer-die-uefa-euro-ist-bekannt.aspx |title=Das Kader des Schweizer Nationalteams für die UEFA EURO ist bekannt |trans-title=The squad of the Swiss national team for the UEFA Euro is known |work=[[Swiss Football Association]] |date=31 May 2021 |access-date=31 May 2021 |language=de}}</ref>

Akanji scored his first senior international goal against [[Spain national football team|Spain]] in a 2–1 [[2022–23 UEFA Nations League A|UEFA Nations League]] victory on 24 September 2022.<ref>{{cite web|title=Zwei Eckbälle für ein Halleluja: Nati überrascht in Spanien|url=https://www.srf.ch/sport/fussball/nationalmannschaft/nations-league-in-saragossa-zwei-eckbaelle-fuer-ein-halleluja-nati-ueberrascht-in-spanien|date=24 September 2022|access-date=24 September 2022|publisher=[[Schweizer Radio und Fernsehen|SRF]]|language=de-ch}}</ref> On 7 December 2022, he scored the only goal for Switzerland in a 6–1 loss to [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]] in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]] round of 16.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Portugal vs. Switzerland - Football Match Summary - December 7, 2022 - ESPN |url=https://www.espn.com.sg/soccer/match/_/gameId/633842 |access-date=2022-12-07 |website=ESPN.com |language=en}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Akanji was born in [[Neftenbach]], Switzerland,<ref name="born_in_Neftenbach">[https://www.munzinger.de/search/go/document.jsp?id=01000009798 Manuel Akanji.] In: ''[[Munzinger-Archiv]]'', retrieved 15. September 2022.</ref> to a Swiss mother and a Nigerian father.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Manuel Akanji: 10 things on Borussia Dortmund's new Swiss international defender|url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/manuel-akanji-borussia-dortmund-defender-features-in-our-ten-things-profile-468262.jsp|access-date=30 October 2020|publisher=Bundesliga}}</ref>

In 2017, already a professional player, he completed his [[Vocational education#Switzerland|vocational training]] as a [[tradesman]] (″Kaufmann″). In Swiss TV in 2018 he showed excellent skills at [[mental calculation]].<ref>[https://sport.sky.de/fussball/artikel/mathe-genie-akanji-netz-steht-nach-tv-auftritt-kopf/11553056/34372 ''BVB-Profi schneller als der Taschenrechner''], sport.sky.de, retrieved 29 June 2021</ref><ref>[https://www.srf.ch/play/tv/-/video/-?urn=urn:srf:video:f8cfc7ba-7ab4-481e-b34a-b7e16761cbd3 ''Fussball: Manuel Akanji als Studiogast''], www.srf.ch, retrieved 29 June 2021</ref>
