'''Marko Grujić''' ({{lang-sr-Cyrl|Марко Грујић}}, {{IPA-sh|mâːrko ɡrûːjitɕ|pron}}; born 13 April 1996) is a Serbian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[central midfielder]] for Portuguese [[Primeira Liga]] club [[F.C. Porto|Porto]] and the [[Serbia national football team|Serbia national team]].

A native of [[Belgrade]], Grujić started his career with his hometown club [[Red Star Belgrade]], progressing through their [[youth system]] to the [[first team (association football)|first team]] squad. He made his professional debut in 2013, and won the [[Serbian SuperLiga|SuperLiga]] title in his final season with Red Star before completing a £5.1 million move to Liverpool. After five years with the Premier League club, he moved to Portuguese club Porto for just over £10 million in 2021. 

Grujić is a Serbian international, and represented Serbia at every youth level from under-16 upwards before making his full international debut in May 2016. He was part of the [[Serbia national under-20 football team|Serbian side]] which won the [[2015 FIFA U-20 World Cup|2015 U-20 World Cup]].

==Club career==
===Red Star Belgrade===
Grujić was born in [[Belgrade]], [[FR Yugoslavia]] (now [[Serbia]]) and is a product of [[Red Star Belgrade]], where he went through the youth program, passed all categories and was the captain of his generation. He made his professional debut for Red Star on 26 May 2013, in a [[Serbian SuperLiga]] match versus [[FK Vojvodina|Vojvodina]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.crvenazvezdafk.com/vesti/izvestaji/7369/Vojvodina+-+Crvena+zvezda+3%3A0+(1%3A0).html|script-title=sr:Војводина – Црвена звезда 3:0 (1:0)|website=Red Star Belgrade official website|language=sr|date=26 May 2013|access-date=3 October 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151223235817/http://www.crvenazvezdafk.com/vesti/izvestaji/7369/Vojvodina+-+Crvena+zvezda+3%3A0+(1%3A0).html|archive-date=23 December 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On 17 May 2015, Grujić signed a three-year contract with Red Star to last until 2018.<ref>[http://www.rtv.rs/sr_lat/sport/fudbal/grujic-i-jovic-do-2018.-u-zvezdi_600503.html Grujić and Jovic by 2018 in Zvezda] Radio Televizija Vojvodine: ''Grujić i Jović do 2018. u Zvezdi'' {{in lang|sr}} 17 May 2015</ref> Grujić scored his first senior goal on 26 September 2015 against [[FK Novi Pazar|Novi Pazar]] in a 2–0 victory; he also assisted [[Aleksandar Katai]] for the second goal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.onefootball.com/en/match/fk-red-star-belgrade-vs-fk-novi-pazar-overview-123-444846?period=FullTime&variable=g2|title=Red Star vs Novi Pazar report|publisher=onefootball.com}}</ref> Grujić was a part of an unprecedented run of Red Star victories, as the club set a new record for the number of consecutive victories (19), ending the first half of the season without defeat.<ref name="Count">{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.us/blog/espn-fc-united-blog/68/post/2758612/miodrag-bozovic-is-breaking-records-at-red-star-belgrade|author=Michael Yokhin|title=ESPN: ''Miodrag 'The Count' Bozovic breaking records at Red Star Belgrade''|date=15 December 2015|access-date=24 February 2016}}</ref>

===Liverpool===
Throughout autumn, Grujić was scouted by a number of high-profile European teams, including [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]], [[Internazionale]], [[Juventus]], [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]], and [[A.C. Milan|Milan]]. [[List of Liverpool F.C. managers|Liverpool manager]] [[Jürgen Klopp]] called Grujić personally to convince him to switch to [[Anfield]], but initially Grujić's father was against his son moving to Liverpool.<ref name="Novosti">{{cite web|url=http://www.kurir.rs/sport/fudbal/terzicu-ne-diraj-mi-sina-clanak-2069371|title=OTAC MARKA GRUJIĆA EKSKLUZIVNO ZA KURIR: Terziću, ne diraj mi sina|author=Aleksandar Radonić|language=sr|date=23 December 2015|access-date=24 February 2016}}</ref> Klopp's assistant, [[Željko Buvač]], flew to Belgrade to speak with Grujić and on 6 January 2016, it was confirmed that Grujić completed his move to [[Premier League]] side [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]], signing a five-year deal for a reported fee of £5.1 million.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Bell|first1=Tom|title=Marko Grujic confirmed as Liverpool manager Jurgen Klopp's first signing|url=http://www.espnfc.com/story/2780433/marko-grujic-confirmed-as-liverpool-player|publisher=[[ESPN FC]]|access-date=12 January 2016|date=6 January 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Marko Grujic: Liverpool sign Red Star Belgrade midfielder|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/35244115|access-date=6 January 2016|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> Upon joining, Grujić hailed Klopp for convincing him to join and told the press, "I would choose Liverpool over [[Real Madrid C.F.|Real Madrid]] and [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]]."<ref>{{Cite web|title = Marko Grujic reveals his Liverpool role, hints at Lazar Markovic's future – This Is Anfield|url = http://www.thisisanfield.com/2016/02/marko-grujic-reveals-his-liverpool-role-hints-at-markovic-future/|date = 24 February 2016|website = This Is Anfield|access-date = 24 February 2016|language = en-GB|first = Jack|last = Lusby}}</ref>

He was immediately loaned back to Red Star until the end of the season for a fee of £740,000, and won the [[Serbian SuperLiga|SuperLiga]] with Red Star while on loan. Grujić finished the season with 29 appearances, 6 goals, 7 assists and was voted into the SuperLiga Team of the Year for his contribution to Red Star's unbeaten season.

On 20 August 2016, Grujić made his [[Premier League]] debut for [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] in a 2–0 loss against [[Burnley F.C.|Burnley]], coming on in the place of [[Adam Lallana]] in the 78th minute.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/37067882|title=Burnley 2–0 Liverpool|last=Bevan|first=Chris|date=20 August 2016|website=BBC.com|publisher=[[BBC]]|access-date=21 August 2016}}</ref>

====Loan to Cardiff City====
On 17 January 2018, Grujić joined [[EFL Championship|Championship]] side [[Cardiff City F.C.|Cardiff City]] on loan for the remainder of the 2017–18 season.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.walesonline.co.uk/sport/football/transfer-news/liverpool-fc-star-marko-grujic-14167521 |title=Liverpool FC star Marko Grujic signs for Cardiff City and describes it as 'breakthrough moment' of his career |publisher=WalesOnline |author=Tom Coleman |date=17 January 2018 |access-date=17 January 2018}}</ref> He made his debut for the club three days later, playing in a 0–0 draw with [[Sheffield Wednesday F.C.|Sheffield Wednesday]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/42671933 |title=Sheffield Wednesday 0–0 Cardiff City |publisher=BBC Sport |date=20 January 2018 |access-date=20 January 2018}}</ref> Grujić's first goal for Cardiff came in a 2–1 home victory over [[Barnsley F.C.|Barnsley]] on 6 March 2018, scoring from range just after the start of the second half.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/43206633 |title=Cardiff City 2–1 Barnsley |publisher=BBC Sport |author=Dafydd Pritchard |date=6 March 2018 |access-date=15 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Marko Grujic|url=https://www.premierleague.com/players/13985/Marko-Grujic/overview|publisher=Premier League|access-date=31 January 2017}}</ref>

====Loan to Hertha BSC====
On 19 August 2018, Grujić joined [[Bundesliga]] club [[Hertha BSC]] on a one-year loan.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Marko Grujic kommt aus Liverpool|trans-title=Marko Grujić joins from Liverpool|date=19 August 2018|access-date=19 August 2018|publisher=[[Hertha BSC]]|url=https://www.herthabsc.de/de/intern/verpflichtung-marko-grujic/page/14925--17-17-.html}}</ref> He scored his first goal for the club on 8 December, netting the only goal in a 1–0 win over [[Eintracht Frankfurt]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/hertha-berlin-eintracht-frankfurt-live-line-ups-stats-luka-jovic-rebic-ibisevic-fantasy-518406.jsp|title=Marko Grujic wins Hertha Berlin all three points against Eintracht Frankfurt|publisher=Bundesliga|date=8 December 2018|access-date=9 December 2018}}</ref> Hertha coach [[Pal Dardai]] hailed Grujić as "by far the best midfielder at Hertha Berlin in 20 years".<ref>{{cite news|title=Marko Grujic "by far the best midfielder at Hertha Berlin in 20 years" – Pal Dardai
|date=10 December 2018|access-date=10 December 2018|publisher=Bundesliga|url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/liverpool-s-marko-grujic-by-far-the-best-midfielder-hertha-berlin-dardai-518902.jsp}}</ref>

On 1 July 2019, his loan deal was extended for a second season, with a reported loan fee of £2 million.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Liverpool secure £2 million fee to loan Marko Grujic to Hertha Berlin|date=1 July 2019|access-date=5 August 2019|publisher=[[The Daily Telegraph|Telegraph]]|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2019/07/01/liverpool-secure-2-million-fee-loan-marko-grujic-hertha-berlin/}}</ref>

====Return to Liverpool====
On 25 September 2020, Grujić started in the EFL Cup tie against Lincoln winning 7–2 and scored a goal, his first for the club.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Rabinowitz|first=Joel|date=25 September 2020|title=Marko Grujic's impressive Liverpool comeback serves timely reminder|url=https://www.liverpool.com/liverpool-fc-news/features/liverpool-transfers-marko-grujic-klopp-18995679|access-date=28 September 2020|website=Liverpool.com}}</ref>

===Porto===
On 6 October 2020, Grujić joined Portuguese club [[FC Porto|Porto]] on a [[2020–21 FC Porto season|season]]-long loan.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fcporto.pt/pt/noticias/20201006-pt-marko-grujic-reforca-o-fc-porto|title=Marko Grujic reforça o FC Porto|trans-title=Marko Grujic strengthens FC Porto|website=fcporto.pt|date=6 October 2020|access-date=6 October 2020|language=pt}}</ref>

On 20 July 2021, Porto made his deal permanent for a transfer worth £10,5 million and a 10% sell-on clause.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/first-team/438390-marko-grujic-seals-permanent-transfer-to-fc-porto |title=Marko Grujic seals permanent transfer to FC Porto |first=Chris |last=Shaw |publisher=Liverpool FC |date=20 July 2021 |accessdate=20 July 2021}}</ref>

==International career==
Grujić represented Serbia at all youth levels from under-16 to under-21, and was a member of the Serbia squad that won the [[2015 FIFA U-20 World Cup]] in [[New Zealand]].<ref name="sky1">{{cite news|url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/11669/10107776/who-is-marko-grujic-we-profile-the-red-star-belgrade-youngster|title=Who is Marko Grujic? We profile Liverpool signing|newspaper=Sky Sports |publisher=skysports.com}}</ref>

In May 2016, Grujić made his full international debut for the [[Serbia national football team|Serbia national team]] after coming on in place of [[Nemanja Matić]] in a 2–1 win against [[Cyprus national football team|Cyprus]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fss.rs/index.php?id=2021&aid=10348|title=Фудбалски савез Србије – званична веб презентација|website=www.fss.rs}}</ref>

In May 2018, he was named in Serbia's preliminary squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.fss.rs/index.php?id=2021&aid=13898 |script-title=sr:Један тим, срцем свим – Младен Крстајић одабрао |trans-title=One team, with all their hearts – Mladen Krstajić selects |website=FSS.rs |publisher=[[Football Association of Serbia]] |date=24 May 2018 |access-date=24 May 2018 |language=sr}}</ref>

In June 2018, he was selected in Serbia's squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018 World Cup]],<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44083365 | title = World Cup 2018: All the confirmed squads for this summer's finals in Russia | publisher = BBC Sport | date = 4 June 2018 | access-date = 25 June 2018}}</ref> but he failed to make any appearances in the competition.

In November 2022, he was selected in Serbia's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fss.rs/en/head-coach-dragan-stojkovic-announces-the-list-of-players-for-the-fifa-world-cup-2022-in-qatar/ |title=Head-coach Dragan Stojković announces the list of players for the FIFA World Cup 2022 in Qatar |publisher=[[Football Association of Serbia]] |date=11 November 2022 |access-date=11 November 2022}}</ref> He played in a group stage match against [[Cameroon national football team|Cameroon]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=Sky Sports |date=2022-11-28 |title=World Cup 2022 - Cameroon 3-3 Serbia: Vincent Aboubakar and Eric Maxim Choupo-Moting goals complete stunning comeback |language=en |work=Sky Sports |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/cameroon-vs-serbia/report/462988#:~:text=World%20Cup%202022%20%2D%20Cameroon%203,Moting%20goals%20complete%20stunning%20comeback&text=Cameroon%20kept%20alive%20their%20faint,with%20Serbia%20in%20Al%2DWakrah. |access-date=2022-11-28}}</ref> Serbia finished fourth in the group.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://soccer.nbcsports.com/2022/12/02/world-cup-2022-group-g-brazil-serbia-cameroon-switzerland-rankings-table-standings/ |title=World Cup 2022 Group G: Brazil, Serbia, Cameroon, Switzerland schedule, fixtures, rankings |first=Nicholas |last=Mendola |newspaper=NBC Sports|location=Lusail |date=2 December 2022 |access-date=7 December 2022}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Standing at 6-foot-3-inches (1.91 m), Grujić has been described as "tall, rangy, comfortable on the ball and with a good range of passing" and dubbed the "new Matić" because of his likeness in both appearance and position to fellow Serbian defensive midfielder [[Nemanja Matić]].<ref name="sky1"/>
