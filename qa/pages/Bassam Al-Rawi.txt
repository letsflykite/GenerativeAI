'''Bassam Hisham Ali Al-Rawi''' ({{lang-ar|بسام هشام الراوي}}; born 16 December 1997) is a Qatari professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]] for [[Al-Duhail SC|Al-Duhail]] and the [[Qatar national football team]]. He was born in [[Iraq]] and naturalized to represent Qatar in the international level.

==Personal life==
Born in [[Iraq]], Bassam was naturalized to represent the [[Qatar national football team|Qatar national team]].<ref>{{Soccerway|bassam-hisham-al-rawi/405568}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/soccer-asiancup-qat-bassam/soccer-qatars-bassam-certain-he-will-get-a-final-chance-to-shine-idINKCN1PK0AR|title=Soccer: Qatar's Bassam certain he will get a final chance to shine|publisher=Reuters|date=26 January 2019|accessdate=26 January 2019}}</ref> Bassam's father, Hisham Ali Al-Rawi, played for the [[Iraq national football team]] in the 1990s.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/soccer-asiancup-qat-irq-idUKL8N1ZM6BY|title=UPDATE 1-Soccer-Stunning free kick sends Qatar into Asian Cup quarter-finals|publisher=Reuters|date=22 January 2019|accessdate=22 January 2019}}</ref> Bassam attended school in Qatar before being admitted to the [[Aspire Academy]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.qatarday.com/news/local/iraq-born-qatar-footballer-bassam-al-rawi-shuts-down-trolls-with-epic-reply/66167|title=Iraq-born Qatar footballer Bassam Al-Rawi shuts down trolls with epic reply|publisher=Qatar Day|date=23 January 2019|accessdate=23 January 2019}}</ref>

==International career==

===2019 AFC Asian Cup===
Bassam was included in [[Félix Sánchez Bas]]' Qatari squad for the [[2019 AFC Asian Cup]] in the United Arab Emirates.<ref name="AFC">{{cite web |url=http://www.the-afc.com/afc/documents/PdfFiles/afc-asian-cup-uae-2019-complete-squad-lists |title=AFC Asian Cup UAE 2019 Complete Squad Lists |website=The-AFC.com |publisher=[[Asian Football Confederation]] |format=PDF |date=27 December 2018 |access-date=30 December 2018 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181228174458/http://www.the-afc.com/afc/documents/PdfFiles/afc-asian-cup-uae-2019-complete-squad-lists |archive-date=28 December 2018}}</ref> He made his first international goal on 9 January 2019 against Lebanon. On 22 January, he scored his second international and the winning goal for Qatar against his country of origin, [[Iraq national football team|Iraq]], which helped his team reach the quarter-finals of the competition.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thenational.ae/world/mena/iraqi-football-fans-angered-by-own-goal-against-qatar-in-the-asian-cup-1.817099|title=Iraqi football fans angered by 'own goal' against Qatar in the Asian Cup|work=The National|date=23 January 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.foxsportsasia.com/football/asian-football/afc-asian-cup/1025764/iraq-born-bassam-al-rawi-scores-the-goal-that-put-qatar-through-in-the-afc-asian-cup-2019/|title=Iraq-born Bassam Al-Rawi scores the goal that put Qatar through in the AFC Asian Cup 2019|work=Fox Sport Asia|date=22 January 2019}}</ref> His team would eventually conquer the Asian title for the first time.

===Eligibility dispute===
On 30 January 2019, soon after the 2019 Asian Cup semi-final tie between the UAE and Qatar, which Qatar won 4–0, the UAE Football Association lodged a formal appeal to the on the fact that Bassam did not qualify to play for Qatar on residency grounds of the Article 7 of the FIFA statutes, which states a player's eligibility to play for a representative team if he has "lived continuously for at least five years after reaching the age of 18 on the territory of the relevant association".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/7997491946d85267/original/qcpshqziazmnmikh6tk5-pdf.pdf|title=FIFA Statutes|website=|date=2015}}</ref> It was claimed that Bassam had not lived continuously in Qatar for at least five years over the age of 18.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thenational.ae/sport/football/uae-lodge-formal-protest-with-afc-over-eligibility-of-two-qatar-players-at-asian-cup-1.820102|title=UAE lodge formal protest with AFC over eligibility of two Qatar players at Asian Cup|website=The National|date=31 January 2019 }}</ref> On 1 February 2019, the AFC Disciplinary and Ethics Committee dismissed the protest lodged by the United Arab Emirates Football Association.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.the-afc.com/news/afcsection/uae-fa-protest-dismissed |title=UAE FA protest dismissed |website=The-AFC.com |publisher=[[Asian Football Confederation]] |date=1 February 2019 |access-date=1 February 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-soccer-asiancup-final/uae-protest-at-eligibility-of-qataris-dismissed-on-day-of-final-idUKKCN1PQ4JC|title=UAE protest at eligibility of Qataris dismissed on day of final|publisher=Reuters|date=1 February 2019|accessdate=1 February 2019}}</ref>
