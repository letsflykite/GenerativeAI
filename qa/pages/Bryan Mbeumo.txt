'''Bryan Tetsadong Marceau Mbeumo''' (born 7 August 1999) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Winger (association football)|winger]] or [[Forward (association football)|forward]] for {{English football updater|Brentfor}} club [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]] and the [[Cameroon national football team|Cameroon national team]].

Mbeumo is a product of the [[Troyes AC]] academy and began his senior career with the club. He joined Brentford in 2019 and was a part of the squad which was promoted to the Premier League in 2021.

Mbeumo was capped by [[France national football team|France]] at youth international level and made his full international debut for Cameroon in 2022. He was a member of Cameroon's [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]] squad.

==Club career==
===Troyes AC===
A [[Winger (association football)|winger]], Mbeumo joined [[Troyes AC]] at the age of 14 and graduated to the club's [[reserve team]] in the [[2016–17 Championnat de France Amateur 2|2016–17]] season.<ref name="Soccerway" /><ref name="JoinsBrentford">{{Cite web |title=Brentford sign Bryan Mbeumo from Troyes |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2019/august/brentford-sign-bryan-mbeumo-from-troyes/ |access-date=5 August 2019 |publisher=Brentford F.C.}}</ref> He was a part of the Troyes U19 team which won the 2018 [[Coupe Gambardella]] and scored twice in the final at the [[Stade de France]].<ref name="GetToKnow">{{Cite web |title=Get to Know: Bryan Mbeumo |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2019/august/bryan-muembo-10-things/ |access-date=7 August 2019 |publisher=Brentford F.C.}}</ref> Mbeumo made his senior debut for the club in a 1–0 [[Ligue 1]] win over [[FC Metz]] on 17 February 2018 and made three further appearances during the [[2017–18 Ligue 1|2017–18]] season,<ref name="Soccerway" /> which culminated in relegation to [[Ligue 2]].<ref name="GetToKnow" /> The relegation allowed Mbeumo to break into the first team squad and he made 40 appearances and scored 11 goals during the [[2018–19 Ligue 2|2018–19]] season,<ref name="Soccerway" /> which ended with defeat in the Ligue 2 promotion play-off semi-finals.<ref name="GetToKnow" /> He departed the [[Stade de l'Aube]] in August 2019, after making 46 appearances and scoring 12 goals for the club.<ref name="Soccerway" /><ref name="JoinsBrentford" />

===Brentford===
On 5 August 2019, Mbeumo moved to England to join [[EFL Championship|Championship]] club [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]] on a five-year contract for a club record £5.8 million fee.<ref name="JoinsBrentford" /><ref>{{Cite web |date=24 July 2020 |title=Brentford close on Premier League dream – just don't call it 'Moneyball' |url=https://www.espn.co.uk/football/english-league-championship/story/4145973/brentford-close-in-on-premier-league-dream-just-dont-thank-moneyball-for-their-success |access-date=20 August 2020 |publisher=ESPN}}</ref> He made 47 appearances and scored 16 goals during the [[2019–20 Brentford F.C. season|2019–20]] season,<ref name="Soccerbase1920" /> which ended with defeat in the [[2020 EFL Championship play-off Final|2020 Championship play-off Final]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Brentford 1 Fulham 2 |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2020/august/match-report-for-brentford-vs-fulham-on-04-aug-20/ |access-date=4 August 2020 |publisher=Brentford F.C.}}</ref> Mbeumo's performances were recognised with a nomination for the [[English Football League|EFL]] Player of the Year and Young Player of the Year awards at the 2020 London Football Awards.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Bees up for London Football Awards prizes |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2020/february/london-football-award-nominations/ |access-date=9 February 2020 |publisher=Brentford F.C.}}</ref> Mbeumo's goal tally dropped off during a "difficult" [[Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on association football#Europe|COVID-19]]-affected [[2020–21 Brentford F.C. season|2020–21]] season,<ref>{{Cite web |date=7 March 2021 |title=Mbeumo: This season has been so tough |url=https://www.theleaguepaper.com/latest-news/football-league-championship/372250/brentford-bryan-mbeumo-its-been-so-tough/ |url-access=subscription |access-date=26 April 2023 |website=The League Paper |language=en-US}}</ref> but by March 2021, his 10 [[Assist (association football)|assists]] and four goals saw him again nominated for the EFL Player of the Year award at the 2021 London Football Awards.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Thomas, Ivan, and Bryan shortlisted for London Football Awards |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2021/march/three-bees-nominated-at-london-football-awards/ |access-date=22 March 2021 |publisher=Brentford F.C.}}</ref> Mbeumo finished Brentford's [[2021 EFL Championship play-off Final|2021 Championship play-off Final]]-winning 2020–21 season with 49 appearances and eight goals.<ref name="Soccerbase2021" /><ref name="Vincent" />

Mbeumo was deployed as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]] alongside [[Ivan Toney]] early in the [[2021–22 Brentford F.C. season|2021–22]] season and by his 11th [[Premier League]] appearance,<ref>{{Cite web |last=Majid |first=Uzzi |date=11 September 2021 |title=Every word Frank said on Brentford's Brighton defeat and Shandon Baptiste |url=https://www.football.london/brentford-fc/frank-toney-baptiste-mbeumo-brighton-21546269 |access-date=25 November 2021 |website=Football.London |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Bryan Mbeumo named as someone to make a difference in FPL |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2021/october/bryan-mbeumo-named-as-someone-to-make-a-difference-in-fpl/ |access-date=25 November 2021 |website=www.brentfordfc.com b}}</ref> he had scored two goals and hit the [[Football pitch#Goals|woodwork]] seven times.<ref name="Soccerbase2122" /><ref>{{Cite web |title=Is Mbeumo the unluckiest Premier League player? |url=http://www.premierleague.com/news/2363744 |access-date=25 November 2021 |website=www.premierleague.com |language=en}}</ref> Mbeumo's first [[Hat-trick (association football)|hat-trick]] of his career (scored in a 4–1 [[FA Cup]] third round win over [[Port Vale F.C.|Port Vale]] on 8 January 2022) was also the first scored by a substitute in Brentford history and earned him a place in the Team of the Round and a nomination for Player of the Round.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Bryan misses our on Player of the Round prize |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2022/january/bryan-misses-our-on-player-of-the-round-prize/ |access-date=30 January 2022 |website=www.brentfordfc.com}}</ref> Three weeks later, he signed a new four-year contract, with a one-year option.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Bryan Mbeumo signs new contract |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2022/january/bryan-mbeumo-signs-new-contract/ |access-date=26 January 2022 |website=www.brentfordfc.com}}</ref> Mbeumo ended the 2021–22 season with 38 appearances, eight goals and he tied with [[Raphinha]] for the record of most [[Scoring in association football#Rules|woodwork]] hits during the Premier League season,<ref name="Soccerbase2122" /> with seven.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Premier League Player Stats – Hit woodwork |url=https://www.premierleague.com/stats/top/players/hit_woodwork |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220423015030/https://www.premierleague.com/stats/top/players/hit_woodwork |archive-date=23 April 2022 |access-date=26 May 2022 |website=www.premierleague.com |language=en}}</ref>

During the [[2022–23 Brentford F.C. season|2022–23]] [[pre-season friendlies]], Mbeumo proved his versatility by at times being deployed as a [[Wing-back (association football)|wing back]] in a [[Formation (association football)#3–4–3|3-4-3]] formation.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Harris |first=Jay |title=Replacing Eriksen, tactical flexibility and new faces – lessons from pre-season |url=https://theathletic.com/3466549/2022/08/02/what-have-we-learnt-from-brentfords-pre-season/ |url-access=subscription |access-date=8 August 2022 |website=The Athletic}}</ref> He was an ever-present in league matches during the season and made 39 appearances in all competitions, scoring 9 goals.<ref name="Soccerbase2223" /> Mbeumo's 9 league goals more than doubled that of the previous season, with head coach [[Thomas Frank (football manager)|Thomas Frank]] remarking that he "has grown more and more to be a key player for us. He works very hard on his finishing".<ref>{{Cite web |last=Harris |first=Jay |title=Bryan Mbeumo can be a 'key player' for Brentford in Ivan Toney's absence |url=https://theathletic.com/4538272/2023/05/21/bryan-mbeumo-brentford-leader-toney/ |url-access=subscription |access-date=11 June 2023 |website=The Athletic |language=en}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:Bryan Mbeumo.jpg|thumb|Mbeumo lining up for [[France national under-20 football team|France U20]] in 2019.|250x250px]]
Mbeumo won 10 caps and scored one goal for France at [[France national under-17 football team|U17]], [[France national under-20 football team|U20]] and [[France national under-21 football team|U21]] level.<ref>{{FFF male player|2543851605<!-- -bryan-mbeumo -->}}</ref> In August 2022, following a meeting in [[London]] with [[Samuel Eto'o]], Mbeumo declared his senior international allegiance to the [[Cameroon national football team|Cameroon national team]].<ref name="2022WCSquad">{{Cite web |title=Mbeumo heading to Qatar |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/en/news/article/bryan-mbeumo-in-cameroon-squad-for-2022-world-cup |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=www.brentfordfc.com |language=en-gb}}</ref> He made 9 appearances and scored one goal during the 2022–23 season, with three appearances coming during Cameroon's [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]] campaign,<ref name="NFT">{{NFT player|88687|access-date=27 September 2022}}</ref> prior to the team's group stage exit.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Cameroon beat Brazil but knocked out of World Cup |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63752145 |access-date=11 June 2023}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Mbeumo is of Cameroonian descent through his father, who is from [[Douala]] and his mother is French.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Cameroon's Mbeumo 'crazy' about World Cup chance |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/africa/63519145 |access-date=15 November 2022}}</ref> In June 2020, he tested positive for [[COVID-19 pandemic|COVID-19]], but showed no symptoms and missed one match.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Bryan Mbeumo: Brentford forward tests positive for coronavirus |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11748/12010966/bryan-mbeumo-brentford-forward-tests-positive-for-coronavirus |access-date=20 June 2020 |website=Sky Sports}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last=Fisher |first=Ben |date=26 June 2020 |title=Brentford sink West Brom to turn up heat in Championship promotion race |work=The Guardian |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2020/jun/26/brentford-sink-west-brom-to-turn-up-heat-in-championship-promotion-race |access-date=27 June 2020}}</ref>
