'''Denis Frimpong Odoi''' (born 27 May 1988) is a professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays for [[Belgian First Division A]] side [[Club Brugge KV|Club Brugge]]. A right-footed [[Full-back (association football)|full-back]] who can play on both sides, he is nicknamed the ''Professor'' because of his maturity and discipline on the field of play.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.sporza.be/permalink/1.1013325 |title=Anderlecht snatches Odoi away from Sint-Truiden |language=nl |date=29 April 2011 |publisher=sporza.be}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.sporza.be/permalink/1.1046497 |title=Odoi: "Standard did not show any respect" |language=nl |date=17 June 2011 |publisher=sporza.be}}</ref> Born in Belgium, and a one-time representative of the [[Belgium national football team|Belgium national team]], he represents the [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national team]].

==Club career==

===Oud-Heverlee Leuven===
Born in [[Leuven]], Belgium, Odoi began playing football when he was five years old, leading to join  [[K. Anderlecht|Anderlecht]].<ref name="Odoi Het Nieuwsblad 1"/><ref name="Odoi Anderlecht1"/> However, Odoi said: "When I was almost eleven, Anderlecht came to get me. That was great, but the transfers from Kessel-Lo, where I live, to Anderlecht and back were not without obstacles, I remember. Traveling went a lot smoother when [[K.R.C. Genk|Genk]] incorporated me a few years later."<ref name="Odoi Het Nieuwsblad 1"/><ref name="Odoi 2009"/> During his time at Genk, he met [[Steven Defour]] and became good friends with him.<ref name="Odoi 2009"/><ref>{{cite news|title=Denis Odoi wil met OH Leuven nederlaag in Eupen vanavond al rechtzetten|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/4a1q4nq0|publisher=Het Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=2 April 2008|access-date=8 September 2020}}<br>{{cite news|title=Gezworen vrienden tegenover elkaar|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/7s2c5tjr|publisher=Het Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=1 July 2009|access-date=8 September 2020}}</ref> After a year spent at Genk, Odoi joined [[Oud-Heverlee Leuven]] when he was eighteen years old and joined the club, citing "a lot more chance to play there".<ref name="Odoi Het Nieuwsblad 1"/><ref name="Odoi 2009"/><ref name="Odoi Anderlecht1"/>

Tubize|Tubize]] on 30 August 2007and didn't play for two months.<ref>{{cite news|title=RC Waasland 0 OH Leuven 3|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/hl1g2bra|publisher=Het Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=16 August 2007|access-date=8 September 2020}}<br>{{cite news|title=OH Leuven 1 Tubeke 2|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/2h1gj6hv|publisher=Het Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=30 August 2007|access-date=8 September 2020}}</ref> It wasn't until on 14 October 2007 when he returned to the starting line–up, in a 4–0 win against [[K.V. Kortrijk]].<ref>{{cite news|title=OHL als een titelkandidaat|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/9d1imhr5|publisher=Het Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=15 October 2007|access-date=8 September 2020}}</ref> Odoi's return was short–lived when he played 71 minutes before being substituted, in a 4–0 loss against [[Lommel S.K.|Overpelt-Lommel]] and didn't play for the next four matches.<ref>{{cite news|title=United rekent af met OHL|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/dmf20102007_062|publisher=Het Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=20 October 2007|access-date=8 September 2020}}</ref> He then returned to the starting line–up against [[K.A.S. ==Personal life==
Outside of football, Odoi studied to become a physical education teacher and was studying that in college while working his way of becoming a professional footballer.<ref>{{cite news|title=Odoi Denis|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/eb3bl8f2|publisher=Het Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=21 June 2011|access-date=8 September 2020}}</ref><ref name="Odoi My London News">{{cite news|title=From PE teacher to Wembley: How Denis Odoi was told he'd never be a professional footballer|url=https://www.football.london/fulham-fc/fulham-play-offs-denis-odoi-14705209|publisher=My London News|date=25 May 2018|access-date=8 September 2020}}</ref> In doing so, Odoi said in an interview with [[The Guardian]] that he received good grades at school.<ref name="Odoi The Guardian">{{cite news|title=Fulham's Denis Odoi: 'At 16 they told me I wouldn't become a footballer'|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2018/sep/28/fulham-denis-odoi-interview-dries-mertens-coffee|work=The Guardian|date=28 September 2018|access-date=8 September 2020}}</ref>

Odoi is close friends with [[S.S.C. Napoli|Napoli]] and [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]] striker [[Dries Mertens]], having known each other since they were both five.<ref name="Odoi My London News"/><ref>{{cite news|title=Dries Mertens over zijn Leven als voetballer|url=http://www.mijnleuven.be/artikel/dries-mertens-over-zijn-leven-als-voetballer|access-date=8 September 2020|publisher=Mijn Leuven|date=3 June 2015|language=nl}}</ref> However, at one point, Odoi revealed in the interview with The Guardian that he once tackled Mertens so seriously that he got "suspended from training for the rest of the week" and did not tell his parents, though it gave him "more time to do my homework."<ref name="Odoi The Guardian"/> Growing up, Odoi watched [[Premier League]] and [[Serie A]] when he was a youngster and served to be an inspiration for him to play football.<ref name="Odoi My London News"/>

In June 2017, Odoi married Katleen Thijs.<ref>{{cite news|title=Leuvenaar Denis Odoi: 'Ik heb geen medelijden met OHL'|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/db3d8ed6|publisher=Het Nieuwsblad|language=nl|date=27 July 2011|access-date=8 September 2020}}<br>{{cite news|title=Huwelijk voor Odoi, nog geen transfer|url=https://www.hln.be/de-krant/huwelijk-voor-odoi-nog-geen-transfer~a0b8faa6/|publisher=HLN.be|language=nl|date=6 June 2017|access-date=8 September 2020}}</ref> Two years later, he became a father.<ref name="Odoi Father"/>
