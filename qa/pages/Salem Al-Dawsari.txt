'''Salem Mohammed Shafi Al-Dawsari''' ({{lang-ar|سالم محمد شافي الدوسري}}; born 19 August 1991) is a Saudi Arabian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Midfielder#Winger|winger or second-striker]] for [[Saudi Professional League]] club [[Al Hilal SFC|Al Hilal]] and the [[Saudi Arabia national football team|Saudi Arabia national team]]. Al-Dawsari is widely considered to be one of the greatest Asian footballers of his generation.<ref>{{Cite news |url= https://www.theguardian.com/football/ng-interactive/2022/nov/16/world-cup-2022-player-profiles-qatar-match-ratings |title= World Cup 2022: complete guide to all 830 players |newspaper= [[The Guardian]] |access-date= 30 April 2023 |publisher= [[Guardian News and Media Limited]] |archive-date= 22 March 2023 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20230322124327/https://www.theguardian.com/football/ng-interactive/2022/nov/16/world-cup-2022-player-profiles-qatar-match-ratings |url-status= live }}</ref>

==Club career==
Al-Dawsari joined [[Al Hilal SFC|Al Hilal]] as a youth player. In 2018, as part of a deal between the [[Saudi Arabian Football Federation]] and [[La Liga]], he joined Spanish club [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]] on loan.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2018/may/30/salem-al-dawsari-saudi-arabia-winger-world-cup-villarreal |title=Salem al-Dawsari: the winger who represents a new breed of Saudi player |last=Wilcox |first=Gregory |date=30 May 2018 |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |access-date=31 May 2018 |publisher=[[Guardian News and Media Limited]] |archive-date=18 June 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180618051806/https://www.theguardian.com/football/2018/may/30/salem-al-dawsari-saudi-arabia-winger-world-cup-villarreal |url-status=live }}</ref> He made one appearance in Spain, coming on as a substitute against [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]] as Villareal came from behind to draw 2–2.<ref name=":0" />

In the second leg of the [[2019 AFC Champions League final]] on 24 November, Al-Dawsari scored the opening goal in an eventual 2–0 away win over [[Urawa Red Diamonds]], which saw Al-Hilal win the title following a 3–0 aggregate victory; the title allowed them to qualify for the [[2019 FIFA Club World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/clubworldcup/news/afc-champions-league-final-report |title=Al Hilal fire themselves to the Club World Cup |publisher=FIFA.com |date=24 November 2019 |access-date=24 November 2019 |archive-date=25 November 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191125005522/https://www.fifa.com/clubworldcup/news/afc-champions-league-final-report |url-status=live }}</ref>

On 7 February 2023, Al-Dawsari scored two penalties in a 3–2 win over [[CR Flamengo|Flamengo]] in the [[2022 FIFA Club World Cup]] semi-final, in which his club reached the final for the first time in their history.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.beinsports.com/au/football/news/al-dawsari-fires-al-hilal-into-club-world-cup/2035690 |title=Al Dawsari fires Al Hilal into Club World Cup final |work=beIN SPORTS |date=7 February 2023 |access-date=8 February 2023 |archive-date=8 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230208104804/https://www.beinsports.com/au/football/news/al-dawsari-fires-al-hilal-into-club-world-cup/2035690 |url-status=live }}</ref>

==International career==
Al-Dawsari was called up to the [[Saudi Arabia national football team|Saudi Arabia national team]] for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup qualification – AFC third round|2014 FIFA World Cup qualifiers]], and scored his first international goal in an away match against [[Australia men's national soccer team|Australia]] in 2012.<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2012-02-29/socceroos-bury-saudis-after-half-time/3861118 |title=Socceroos bury Saudis after half-time |date=29 February 2012 |work=[[ABC News (Australia)|ABC News]] |access-date=31 May 2018 |publisher=[[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] |archive-date=29 November 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201129104014/https://www.abc.net.au/news/2012-02-29/socceroos-bury-saudis-after-half-time/3861118 |url-status=live }}</ref> In May 2018, he was named in Saudi Arabia's preliminary [[Saudi Arabia national football team|squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44083365 |title=World Cup 2018: All the confirmed squads for this summer's finals in Russia |date=18 May 2018 |work=[[BBC Sport]] |access-date=31 May 2018 |publisher=[[BBC]] |archive-date=18 April 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210418134149/https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44083365 |url-status=live }}</ref> On 25 June, Al-Dawsari scored a late winning goal as his side won 2–1 over [[Egypt national football team|Egypt]] in their last group stage match of the World Cup.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2018/jun/25/saudi-arabia-egypt-world-cup-2018-group-a-match-report|title=Salem al-Dawsari snatches win for Saudi Arabia against Egypt|work=The Guardian|date=25 June 2018|access-date=25 June 2018|archive-date=30 June 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190630051622/https://www.theguardian.com/football/2018/jun/25/saudi-arabia-egypt-world-cup-2018-group-a-match-report|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 22 November 2022, Al-Dawsari scored his second goal at a [[FIFA World Cup]], his first at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 edition]] in Qatar, in a match against [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]]. He made a run around the box before finishing past [[Emiliano Martínez]] to put Saudi Arabia ahead by a score of 2–1, which ended in a historic shock victory for the [[Saudi Arabia national football team|Saudi Arabia national team]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-11-22 |title=Argentina 1-2 Saudi Arabia: Salem Al-Dawsari scores stunner as pre-tournament favourites are shocked |url=https://www.eurosport.com/football/world-cup/2022/fifa-world-cup-2022-in-qatar-live-argentina-v-saudi-arabia-updates_sto9237905/story.shtml |access-date=2022-11-22 |website=Eurosport |language=en |archive-date=22 November 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221122154214/https://www.eurosport.com/football/world-cup/2022/fifa-world-cup-2022-in-qatar-live-argentina-v-saudi-arabia-updates_sto9237905/story.shtml |url-status=live }}</ref> On 30 November, he scored a goal against [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]], in which he equaled the record of most goals scored by a Saudi player in World Cups, three goals by [[Sami Al-Jaber]], as Saudi Arabia exited the World Cup with a 2–1 loss to Mexico.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.thenationalnews.com/fifa-world-cup-2022/2022/11/30/mexico-end-saudi-arabias-world-cup-dream-but-still-exit-tournament/ |title=Mexico end Saudi Arabia's World Cup dream but still exit tournament |website=The National News |date=30 November 2022 |access-date=30 November 2022 |archive-date=30 November 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221130230812/https://www.thenationalnews.com/fifa-world-cup-2022/2022/11/30/mexico-end-saudi-arabias-world-cup-dream-but-still-exit-tournament/ |url-status=live }}</ref>
