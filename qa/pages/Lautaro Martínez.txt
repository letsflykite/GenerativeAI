'''Lautaro Javier Martínez''' (born 22 August 1997) is an Argentine professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)#Striker|striker or second-striker]] for {{Italian football updater|Internazionale}} club [[Inter Milan]] and the [[Argentina national football team|Argentina national team]].

Martínez began his football career in his native Argentina, where he made his senior debut in 2015 with [[Racing Club de Avellaneda|Racing Club]]. There he spent four seasons and represented the club in the league and [[Copa Libertadores]], scoring 28 goals in 60 appearances before joining Inter in 2018, where he won the [[2020–21 Serie A|Serie A title]] in his third season with the club.

Martínez also previously represented Argentina at various youth levels and competed at the [[2017 South American U-20 Championship]] and [[2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup]]. He made his senior international debut in 2018, and represented the senior squad at the [[Copa América]] in [[2019 Copa América|2019]], helping his team to a third place finish, and [[2021 Copa América|2021]], winning the latter edition of the tournament. He was also a part of the Argentina side that won the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Club career==
===Early career===
Martínez was born in [[Bahía Blanca]]. He followed in his father's footsteps to become a professional footballer, joining local side [[Liniers de Bahía Blanca|Liniers]], with whom he excelled at U–17 level.<ref name=EG>{{cite web|url=http://www.elgrafico.com.ar/2017/05/10/C-22832-lautaro-martinez-tradicion-familiar.php|title=Lautaro Martínez, tradición familiar|website=El Grafico|last=Estevez|first=Martin|date=10 May 2017|access-date=12 July 2017}}{{in lang|es}}</ref> In 2013, he scored 13 goals in the U–17 league and netted in the final of the National Cup, though Liniers ultimately lost on [[penalty kick (association football)|penalties]] to Rosario.<ref name=LR>{{cite web|url=http://www.lanueva.com/Deportes-/742224/el-juvenil-bahiense-lautaro-martinez-se-va-a-racing.html|title=El juvenil bahiense Lautaro Martínez se va a Racing|website=Lanueva|date=9 January 2014|access-date=13 July 2017}}{{in lang|es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lanueva.com/Deportes-/250307/el-sub-17-de-la-liga-del-sur-perdio-la-final-con-rosario.html|title=El Sub 17 de la Liga del Sur perdió la final con Rosario|website=Lanueva|date=14 December 2013|access-date=13 July 2017}}{{in lang|es}}</ref>

===Racing Club===
Martínez's form at youth level caught the attention of [[Racing Club de Avellaneda|Racing Club]] interim coach Fabio Radaelli who subsequently signed him in January 2014.<ref name=LR/> Soon after joining the club, Martínez began suffering from homesickness and wanted to return to his hometown. He was ultimately convinced by teammate [[Braian Mansilla]] to stay and went on to score 53 goals in 64 appearances for the club's reserve side.<ref name=EG/> In 2015, a deal was concluded between Racing Club and Spanish side [[Real Madrid C.F.|Real Madrid]] for Martínez's signature but he chose to remain in Argentina. Martínez's father, Mario later revealed that he chose to stay with Racing at the time as he was not ready to leave the club.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marca.com/en/football/real-madrid/2017/11/21/5a13f845268e3e8f058b464b.html|title=Lautaro Martinez's father reveals rejection of Real Madrid in 2015|website=Marca|date=21 November 2017|access-date=8 December 2017|last=Siffredi|first=Charly}}</ref> His [[Argentine Primera División|league debut]] followed on 1 November 2015 when he came on as a second-half [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] for [[Diego Milito]] in a 3–0 win over [[Crucero del Norte]].<ref name="Goal">{{cite web |last=Edwards |first=Danie |date=17 February 2017 |title=Coveted by Arsenal & Real Madrid: Meet Racing Club's whizzkid Lautaro Martinez |url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/coveted-by-arsenal-and-real-madrid-meet-racing-clubs-whizzkid-lautaro-martinez/q6nlwqx14pev1i12q4zlo5n2v |access-date=26 May 2017 |website=Goal}}</ref> On 17 April 2016, he was [[penalty card|sent off]] for the first time in his career after picking up two bookings in the space of five minutes in a 2–2 draw with [[Argentinos Juniors|Argentinos]].<ref name=EG/> He scored his first goal later that year, netting the opener in a 1–1 draw with [[Club Atlético Huracán|Huracán]] in November.<ref name=EG/>

Following an injury to regular starting striker [[Lisandro López (footballer, born 1983)|Lisandro López]], Martínez assumed a more senior role for the [[2016–17 Argentine Primera División|2016–17 season]], scoring 9 goals in 23 league appearances for the campaign.<ref name=EG/><ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2017/12/06/atletico-madrid-verge-beating-host-european-clubs-signing-young-argentine-star|title=Report: Atletico Madrid Inks Rising Argentine Star Lautaro Martinez to Long-Term Deal|magazine=Sports Illustrated|date=6 December 2017|access-date=8 December 2017}}</ref> He continued to lead the line for Racing during the first half of the following season and in December 2017 underwent a medical with [[La Liga]] side [[Atlético Madrid]].<ref name=fft>{{cite web|url= https://www.fourfourtwo.com/news/racing-club-raise-lautaro-martinez-release-clause|title=Racing Club raise Lautaro Martinez release clause|website=Four Four Two|date=11 January 2018|access-date=5 February 2018}}</ref> Reports followed that Martínez had signed for Atlético Madrid side but Racing later announced that he had signed a renewed contract which included an increased release clause. They also criticised their Spanish contemporaries for submitting him to a medical without their permission.<ref name=fft/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marca.com/en/football/spanish-football/2017/12/06/5a284b4a468aeb854a8b4595.html|title=Lautaro Martinez signs for Atletico Madrid|website=Marca|date=6 December 2017|access-date=8 December 2017|last=Siffredi|first=Charly}}</ref> He ended the year with a [[man of the match]] performance, scoring once and [[Assist (football)|assisting]] another in a 3–1 win over [[Club de Gimnasia y Esgrima La Plata|Gimnasia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en-us/news/could-lautaro-martinez-be-atleticos-answer-to-cristiano/1sw5i4wgq5r781xqhewp9248xu|title=Could Lautaro Martinez be Atletico's answer to Cristiano Ronaldo?|website=Goal|last=Edwards|first=Daniel|date=15 December 2017|access-date=19 December 2017}}</ref> On 27 February, he scored his second [[hat-trick]] of the season when he scored three times on his [[Copa Libertadores]] debut against Brazilian side [[Cruzeiro Esporte Clube|Cruzeiro]]. Racing won the match 4–2.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.serpentsofmadonnina.com/2018/2/28/17062234/inter-milan-target-lautaro-martinez-goes-triple-for-second-time-internazionale-milano-racing-club|title=Inter target Lautaro Martinez goes triple for second time|website=Serpents of Maradonna|last=Clark|first=Matthew|date=28 February 2018|access-date=28 February 2018}}</ref>

That same month, Martínez was linked with a transfer to Italian side [[Inter Milan]] and on 5 May Racing Club President Victor Blanco confirmed that the sale had gone through. Blanco indicated that Martínez was set to join Inter ahead of the [[2018–19 Serie A]] campaign but that he would attempt to keep him on [[Loan (association football)|loan]] until December 2018 in order for the striker to continue to participate in the Argentine side's [[Copa Libertadores 2018|Copa Libertadores]] campaign.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.90min.com/posts/5971375-inter-tie-up-deal-to-sign-argentine-wonderkid-lautaro-martinez-for-20m-fee|title=Inter Tie Up Deal to Sign Argentine Wonderkid Lautaro Martinez for €20m Fee|publisher=90min|last=Cudworth|first=Toby|date=7 February 2018|access-date=7 May 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2018/05/05/racing-club-president-lautaro-martinez-sale-inter-paperwork-signed|title=Racing Club President Confirms Lautaro Martinez Has Been Sold to Inter|publisher=Sports Illustrated|date=7 May 2018|access-date=7 May 2018}}</ref>

===Inter Milan===
Martínez officially joined Inter on 4 July 2018 for a reported fee of €22.7 million and he signed a five-year contract with the club.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en-sa/news/transfer-news-lautaro-martinez-joins-inter-from-racing-club/1ohdw7c730k5wz32in3gme9fv|title=Lautaro Martinez completes Inter move|publisher=Goal|last=Strachan|first=Iain|date=4 July 2018|access-date=4 July 2018}}</ref> He made his unofficial debut ten days later and scored in a 3–0 friendly win over Swiss club, [[FC Lugano|Lugano]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/lautaro-martinez-on-target-in-impressive-inter-debut/1du7bg24d15vy1od8om0y0lvvq|title=Lautaro Martinez on target in impressive Inter debut|publisher=Goal|last=Dorman|first=Matt|date=14 July 2018|access-date=15 July 2018}}</ref> His full debut followed on 19 August when he started in a 1–0 [[Serie A]] loss to [[U.S. Sassuolo Calcio|Sassuolo]] in the opening week.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.90min.com/posts/6148467-sassuolo-1-0-inter-report-ratings-reaction-as-the-nerazzurri-slip-to-defeat-in-serie-a-opener|title=Sassuolo 1-0 Inter: Report, Ratings & Reaction as the Nerazzurri Slip to Defeat in Serie A Opener|publisher=90min|last=Marcovitch|first=Joe|date=19 August 2018|access-date=20 August 2018}}</ref> He scored his first league goal against [[Cagliari Calcio|Cagliari]], the opener of a 2–0 win at [[San Siro]] on 29 September.<ref>{{cite web |title=Results: Inter 2–0 Cagliari |url=http://www.legaseriea.it/en/serie-a/match-report/2018-19/UNICO/UNI/7/INTCAG |website=legaseriea.it |access-date=30 September 2018}}</ref>

==International career==
===Youth team===
[[File:Lautaro Martínez (cropped).jpg|thumb|Martínez playing for [[Argentina national under-20 football team|Argentina U20]] at the [[2017 South American U-20 Championship]]]]
In 2017, Martínez represented [[Argentina national under-20 football team|Argentina]] at the [[2017 South American U-20 Championship]] where he ended as the tournament's joint-top scorer with five goals, in the process helping Argentina qualify for the [[2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup]].<ref name=Goal/>

He was subsequently named in the squad for the World Cup which kicked off in May that year. In the build-up to the tournament, he suffered damage to his nasal cartilage after being struck by a knee in a warm-up match against Vietnam.<ref name=ARG>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en-za/news/4682/transfer-zone/2017/05/30/35970632/arsenal-target-martinez-survives-u-20-disaster-to-prove-he|title=Arsenal target Martinez survives U-20 disaster to prove he can be an elite striker|website=Goal|last=Edwards|first=Daniel|date=30 May 2017|access-date=2 June 2017}}</ref> As a result of the injury, he started Argentina's opening match at the tournament against [[England national under-20 football team|England]] from the bench and was later [[penalty card|sent off]] after video technology indicated that he had lashed out at [[Fikayo Tomori]]. Upon doing so, he became the first ever player to be dismissed after a video referral, and Argentina ultimately lost the match 3–0.<ref name=ARG/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.afa.org.ar/7133/continua-la-preparacion-en-ezeiza|title=Los mundialistas llegaron a Vietnam|website=Argentina Football Association|access-date=23 May 2017}}{{in lang|es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.goal.com/en-za/news/4563/main/2017/05/20/35673122/video-referee-uses-video-technology-to-send-off-argentinas|title=VIDEO: Referee uses video technology to send off Argentina's Martinez at FIFA U20 World Cup|website=Goal|date=20 May 2017|access-date=24 May 2017}}</ref> On his return, he scored a brace in a 5–0 win over [[Guinea national under-20 football team|Guinea]], though his goals were not enough to prevent Argentina from being eliminated from the [[2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup#Group stage|group-stages]].<ref name=ARG/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/40057623|title=Guinea out of Under-20 World Cup|website=BBC|date=26 May 2017|access-date=26 May 2017}}</ref>

===Senior team===
On 12 March 2018, Martínez received his first call-up for the [[Argentina national football team|senior team]] for two [[Friendly (association football)|friendly]] matches against [[Italy national football team|Italy]] and [[Spain national football team|Spain]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://afa.org.ar/9134/lista-de-convocados-|title=Lista de convocados|website=AFA|date=12 March 2018|access-date=12 March 2018}}</ref> He made his debut against the latter on 27 March, coming on as a substitute for [[Gonzalo Higuaín]] in a 6–1 defeat.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2018/03/27/spain-argentina-friendly-goals-video-diego-costa-asensio-higuain-di-maria|title=WATCH: Isco Hat Trick Leads Spain's 6-1 Rout Over Messi-Less Argentina|publisher=Sports Illustrated|date=27 March 2018|access-date=28 March 2017}}</ref> In May 2018 he was named in Argentina’s preliminary squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Russia]] but was omitted from the final selection.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/amp/news/revealed-every-world-cup-2018-squad-23-man-preliminary-lists/oa0atsduflsv1nsf6oqk576rb|title=Revealed: Every World Cup 2018 squad|publisher=Goal|access-date=25 May 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/icardi-cut-from-argentinas-23-man-world-cup-squad/lo0f6fig55ih1jh7w9v4enau6 |title=Icardi cut from Argentina's 23-man World Cup squad |website=[[Goal (website)|Goal]] |publisher=[[Perform Group]] |date=21 May 2018 |access-date=21 May 2018}}</ref> Later that year, he made his full debut in a 4–0 friendly win over [[Iraq national football team|Iraq]] during which he also scored his first senior international goal.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/inters-lautaro-martinez-thrilled-with-debut-argentina-goal/10f5lsppt42r91wu6g9x6l0t48|title=Inter's Lautaro Martinez thrilled with debut Argentina goal|publisher=Goal|last=Creek|first=Stephen|date=12 October 2018|access-date=12 October 2018}}</ref>

In May 2019, Martínez was included in [[Lionel Scaloni]]'s final 23-man Argentina squad for the [[2019 Copa América]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2019/05/21/argentina-copa-america-squad-roster-messi-aguero-dybala-scaloni |title=Messi Leads Argentina's Copa America Squad; Icardi Omitted |publisher=Sports Illustrated |author1=Avi Creditor |date=21 May 2019 |access-date=21 May 2019 }}</ref> In Argentina's final group match against [[Qatar national football team|Qatar]] on 23 June, he scored the opening goal in a 2–0 win, which enabled them to advance to the knock-out stages of the competition.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.beinsports.com/en/copa-america-2019/video/copa-america-2019-qatar-0-2-argentina-match-r/1224974 |title=Copa America 2019 - Qatar 0-2 Argentina - Match Report |publisher=www.beinsports.com |date=23 June 2019 |access-date=24 June 2019 }}</ref> On 28 June, in the quarter-finals of the tournament, Martínez scored the opening goal in an eventual 2–0 win over [[Venezuela national football team|Venezuela]] with a back-heel in the opening ten minutes of the match; he was later named ''Man of the match'', and the victory enabled Argentina to advance to the semi-finals of the competition.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.tsn.ca/argentina-beats-venezuela-faces-brazil-in-copa-america-semi-1.1330093 |title=Argentina beats Venezuela, faces Brazil in Copa América semi |work=TSN |date=28 June 2019 |access-date=29 June 2019 }}</ref>

On 10 September 2019, Martínez scored his first international [[Hat-trick#Association football|hat-trick]] in a [[Friendly (association football)|friendly]] against [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]], which Argentina won, 4–0.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.eurosport.com/football/international-friendlies/2019/live-argentina-mexico_mtc1150655/live.shtml |title=Argentina 4-0 Mexico |work=[[Eurosport]] |date=10 September 2019|access-date=11 September 2019 }}</ref>

On 28 June 2021, he scored Argentina's final goal in a 4–1 win over [[Bolivia national football team|Bolivia]] in his nation's final group match of the [[2021 Copa América]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/report?gameId=560500 |title=Bolivia vs. Argentina - Football Match Report - June 28, 2021 - ESPN |work=ESPN.com |date=28 June 2021 |access-date=29 June 2021 }}</ref> On 3 July, he scored the second goal in a 3–0 win over [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador]] in the quarter-finals of the competition.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.cbssports.com/soccer/news/argentina-vs-ecuador-score-sensational-messi-leads-albiceleste-into-copa-america-semifinals/live/ |title=Argentina vs. Ecuador score: Sensational Messi leads Albiceleste into Copa America semifinals |work=CBS Sports |date=3 July 2021 |access-date=4 July 2021 }}</ref> On 6 July, Martínez scored the opening goal in a 1–1 draw in the semi-finals against [[Colombia national football team|Colombia]]; he later netted his spot kick in Argentina's eventual 3–2 penalty shoot-out victory to progress to the final.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/report?gameId=603184 |title=Argentina vs. Colombia - Football Match Report - July 6, 2021 - ESPN |work=ESPN.com |date=6 July 2021 |access-date=7 July 2021 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.thescore.com/esp_fed/news/2186119 |title=Messi driving Argentina at Copa America |website=theScore.com |date=6 July 2021 |access-date=7 July 2021 }}</ref>

On 1 June 2022, Martínez scored the opening goal and provided an assist for [[Ángel Di María]] in a 3–0 win over reigning [[UEFA Euro 2020|European Champions]] Italy at [[Wembley Stadium]] in the [[2022 Finalissima]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.uefa.com/finalissima/news/0276-1549514538ab-f782a73cf733-1000--argentina-outclass-italy-in-finalissima/ |title=Italy 0-3 Argentina: South American champions cruise to Finalissima glory |publisher=UEFA.com |date=1 June 2022 |access-date=2 June 2022 }}</ref>

He was included in the final 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] by Lionel Scaloni. On 9 December 2022, Martínez scored the winning penalty in Argentina's 4–3 shoot-out victory against the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]] to send his side into the semi-finals.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63830319|title=World Cup 2022: Argentina beat the Netherlands in penalty shootout to reach semi-finals|work=BBC Sport|date=9 December 2022|access-date=9 December 2022}}</ref> On 18 December, he replaced [[Julián Álvarez (footballer)|Julián Álvarez]] in extra-time as Argentina defeated [[France national football team|France]], 4–2, in the penalty shoot-out after the match ended 3–3 in extra-time to win the World Cup.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/fifaplus/en/articles/world-cup-qatar-2022-tournament-review-argentina-messi-mbappe-morocco-croatia-japan|title=A ground-breaking World Cup with a fairy tale ending|publisher=[[FIFA]]|accessdate=18 December 2022}}</ref>

==Style of play==
After initially playing as a [[Defender (association football)#Centre-back|central defender]] in his youth, Martínez later established himself as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]], citing [[Radamel Falcao]] as one of his major influences.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fcinter1908.it/primo-piano/lautaro-si-racconta-ero-difensore-falcao-modello-milito-quando-si-ritiro-mi-disse/ |title=Lautaro si racconta: "Ero difensore, Falcao modello. Milito? Quando si ritirò mi disse…" |publisher=FC Inter 1908 |language=Italian |last1=Macca |first1=Marco |date=9 June 2019 |access-date=21 February 2021 }}</ref> A dynamic, quick, agile, and technically gifted player, who is a prolific goalscorer, he is known for his elegant playing style, good [[Dribbling#Association football|dribbling]] skills, and clinical finishing ability, as well as surprising physical strength and aerial prowess despite his diminutive stature;<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.ultimouomo.com/innamorati-di-lautaro-martinez/ |title=Innamorati di Lautaro Martínez |publisher=L'Ultimo Uomo |language=Italian |last1=Gabrielli |first1=Fabrizio |date=10 April 2018 |access-date=21 February 2021 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.gazzetta.it/Calcio/Serie-A/Inter/14-06-2020/inter-lautaro-non-segna-piu-tocca-sanchez-3701825544706.shtml |title=Inter, Lautaro non segna più: tocca a Sanchez? |work=La Gazzetta dello Sport |language=Italian |last1=Angioni |first1=Carlo |date=5 June 2020 |access-date=21 February 2021 }}</ref><ref name="successor">{{cite web |last1=Yu |first1=Cronan |url=https://www.football-espana.net/2020/06/26/why-inter-striker-lautaro-martinez-is-the-perfect-luis-suarez-successor-at-barcelona |title=Why Inter striker Lautaro Martinez is the perfect Luis Suarez successor at Barcelona |publisher=Football España |date=26 June 2020 |access-date=21 February 2021 }}</ref> however, he has also drawn criticism from pundits for his lack of accuracy from the [[Penalty kick (association football)|penalty spot]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.gazzetta.it/Calcio/Serie-A/Inter/06-07-2020/i-rigori-lautaro-rapporto-complicato-quella-lite-paredes-argentina-380162461857.shtml |title=I rigori e Lautaro: il rapporto è complicato |work=La Gazzetta dello Sport |language=Italian |last1=Stoppini |first1=Davide |date=6 July 2020 |access-date=21 February 2021 }}</ref> Possessing good link-up play, defensive contribution, and an ability to drop deep off the ball to either press opponents or drag them out of position, he is also a highly creative and hard-working player, who is capable of [[Assist (football)|assisting]] goals as well as scoring them himself;<ref name="successor"/><ref>{{cite web |url=https://sport.sky.it/calcio/serie-a/2021/02/21/milan-inter-risultato-gol |title=Milan-Inter 0-3: doppietta di Lautaro e gol di Lukaku. Derby ai nerazzurri, ora a +4 |publisher=sport.sky.it |language=Italian |date=21 February 2021 |access-date=21 February 2021 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news February 2021 }}</ref> as such, he is also able to play as a [[Forward (association football)#Second striker|second striker]], in addition to his usual role as either a [[Forward (association football)#Centre-forward|centre-forward]] or out–and–out striker.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.ultimouomo.com/lautaro-martinez-inter/ |title=Come usare Lautaro Martinez? |publisher=L'Ultimo Uomo |language=Italian |last1=Pisani |first1=Angelo Andrea |date=8 November 2018 |access-date=21 February 2021 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news
