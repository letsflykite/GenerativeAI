'''Jeremy Leonel Sarmiento Morante''' (born 16 June 2002) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Winger (association football)|winger]] for [[Premier League]] club [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]] and the [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador national team]]. Born in Spain to Ecuadorian parents, he represented England at youth international level before making his senior Ecuador debut in October 2021.

==Club career==
===Brighton & Hove Albion===

====2021–22: Breakthrough into senior team====
A youth product of [[Charlton Athletic F.C.|Charlton Athletic]] and [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]], Sarmiento signed for the academy of Premier League side [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]] on 2 July 2021.<ref>{{cite news|title=Brighton sign Jeremy Sarmiento from Benfica|url=https://www.theargus.co.uk/sport/19416786.brighton-sign-jeremy-sarmiento-benfica/|first=Brian|last=Owen|publisher=The Argus|date=2 July 2021|access-date=23 September 2021}}</ref> He made his professional career debut on 22 September, coming on as a 69th-minute substitute for injured [[Alexis Mac Allister]] in the 2–0 home victory over [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]] in the third round of the [[2021-22 EFL Cup#third round|EFL Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Brighton & Hove Albion vs. Swansea City - 22 September 2021|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2021/09/22/england/league-cup/brighton--hove-albion-fc/swansea-city-afc/3630437/|publisher=Soccerway|access-date=23 September 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Brighton fans want first-team call for former Manchester City target who rejected Barcelona|url=https://www.sussexlive.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/brighton-fans-manchester-city-barcelona-5956757|first=Richard|last=Mills|publisher=SussexLive|date=23 September 2021|access-date=25 September 2021}}</ref> Sarmiento made a [[Premier League]] matchday squad for the first time on 2 October, remaining as an unused substitute in the 0–0 home draw against [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]].<ref>{{cite web| first = Matthew |last=Howarth | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58667889| title=Brighton & Hove Albion 0–0 Arsenal: Wasteful Seagulls held by Gunners|website=BBC Sport|date=2 October 2021|access-date=3 October 2021}}</ref> Sarmiento made his first start for ''The Seagulls'' on 27 October, playing 69 minutes of the eventual penalty shootout loss away at [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] in the EFL Cup.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58976543|title=Leicester City 2-2 Brighton & Hove Albion: Foxes win 4-2 on penalties - BBC Sport|publisher=BBC Sport |date=27 October 2021 |access-date=27 October 2021}}</ref> He made his Premier League debut on 27 November, coming on as an 82nd-minute substitute replacing [[Jakub Moder]] in the goalless draw at home against [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/59356978|title=Brighton & Hove Albion 0-0 Leeds United: Seagulls waste chances as winless run continues - BBC Sport|publisher=BBC Sport |date=27 November 2021 |access-date=27 November 2021}}</ref> Sarmiento made his first Premier League start four days later, where he ultimately played just 13 minutes due to an injury in which he was replaced, in the eventual 1–1 away draw at [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/59397419|title=West Ham 1-1 Brighton: Neal Maupay scores late equaliser after early Tomas Soucek goal for the Hammers - BBC Sport|publisher=BBC Sport |date=1 December 2021 |access-date=1 December 2021}}</ref> It was revealed his injury, which was to the hamstring, would require an operation with Brighton boss [[Graham Potter]] hinting he would be back towards the end of February 2022.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.sussexlive.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/jeremy-sarmiento-brighton-injury-news-6490115|title=Graham Potter offers positive Jeremy Sarmiento injury news and hints at Brighton return date - SussexLive|publisher=SussexLive|date=14 January 2022 |access-date=31 January 2022}}</ref> On 31 January 2022, he signed a new contract, committing a long-term future with Brighton signing on until June 2026.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/2468074/jeremy-sarmiento-signs-new-contract|title=Jeremy Sarmiento signs new contract
|publisher=Brighton & Hove Albion |date=31 January 2022 |access-date=31 January 2022}}</ref> Sarmiento made his return to the matchday squad remaining as an unused substitute in the 2–0 home loss against [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] on 16 March.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/59536759|title=Brighton & Hove Albion 0-2 Tottenham Hotspur: Harry Kane scores as visitors reignite top-four hopes
|publisher=BBC Sport |date=16 March 2022 |access-date=25 April 2022}}</ref> He made his first appearance since his injury on 2 April, coming on as an 88th-minute substitute in the 0–0 home draw against bottom side [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]]. Sarmiento replaced Moder who was subbed on five minutes previously but picked up an injury himself.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/60883948|title= Brighton 0-0 Norwich: Neal Maupay penalty miss costs hosts|publisher=BBC Sport|date=2 April 2022|access-date=25 April 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sussexlive.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/jakub-moder-injury-brighton-comeback-6905911|title=Jakub Moder's first words after suffering 'horrible' knee injury as he plans Brighton comeback|website=SussexLive|date=4 April 2022|access-date=25 April 2022}}</ref> At ''Albions''' end of season awards in May, Sarmiento won the Young Player of the Season thanking everyone for his support adding, "My first season and making my Premier League debut were really special and I am honoured to win this award."<ref name="Awards">{{cite news|url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/2612006/cucurella-at-the-double-at-players-awards|title=Cucurella at the double at Players' Awards|publisher= Brighton & Hove Albion|date=9 May 2022|access-date=10 May 2022}}</ref>

====2022–23====

Sarmiento made his first Premier League start of [[2022–23 Brighton & Hove Albion F.C. season|the season]] on 3 January 2023, where he helped set up [[Evan Ferguson|Evan Ferguson's]] goal in the 4–1 away win over [[Everton F.C.|Everton]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Awesome Albion hit four to start the year in style
|url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/3005862/awesome-albion-hit-four-to-start-the-year-in-style|date=3 January 2023|access-date=3 January 2023|website=Brighton & Hove Albion |language=en-GB}}</ref> On 4 February, Sarmiento came on from the bench and later assisted [[Kaoru Mitoma|Kaoru Mitoma's]] 87th minute winner, the only goal of the game in the victory over [[A.F.C. Bournemouth|Bournemouth]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=4 February 2023 |title=More Mitoma magic as Albion leave it late|url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/3048710/more-mitoma-magic-as-albion-leave-it-late|access-date=4 February 2023 |website=Brighton & Hove Albion |language=en-GB}}</ref>

====2023–24====

On the 21 June 2023 Sarmiento agreed a new contract until June 2027, with the option of an extra year for the club.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Jeremy Sarmiento signs new contract
|url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/3547483/jeremy-sarmiento-signs-new-contract|date=21 June 2023|access-date=21 June 2023|website=Brighton & Hove Albion |language=en-GB}}</ref>

==International career==

===Youth career===

====With England ====
Sarmiento was born in Spain to Ecuadorian parents, and moved to England at the age of 7.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-bwin/benfica/detalhe/pai-de-sarmiento-garante-que-ha-um-clube-de-topo-mundial-que-quer-o-filho|title=Pai de Sarmiento garante que "há um clube de topo mundial" que quer o extremo do Benfica|date=11 May 2020|publisher=[[Record (newspaper)|Record]]|accessdate=23 September 2021|language=Portuguese}}</ref> He is a youth international for England<ref>{{cite news|title=Brighton's Jeremy Sarmiento sets sights on Ecuador|url=https://www.theargus.co.uk/sport/19417602.brightons-jeremy-sarmiento-sets-sights-ecuador/|first=Brian|last=Owen|publisher=The Argus|date=3 July 2021|access-date=23 September 2021}}</ref> and represented them at the [[2019 UEFA European Under-17 Championship]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/48128613|publisher=BBC Sport|title=Under-17 Euros: Six England players to look out for at men's and women's tournaments|date=2 May 2019|accessdate=25 September 2021}}</ref> In October 2019 Sarmiento scored the winning goal for [[England national under-18 football team|England under-18]] in a 3–2 victory away to Austria.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thefa.com/news/2019/oct/16/england-u18s-austria-report-161019|publisher=The Football Association|title=Young Lions end Euro trip with three wins from three after victory in Austria|date=16 October 2019|accessdate=25 September 2021}}</ref>

===Senior career===

====Pledge with Ecuador and 2022 FIFA World Cup====

Sarmiento was called up for the [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador national team]] for the first time in October 2021 for [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification|2022 World Cup qualifiers]] against [[Bolivia national football team|Bolivia]], [[Venezuela national football team|Venezuela]] and [[Colombia national football team|Colombia]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theargus.co.uk/sport/19632091.jeremy-sarmiento-set-make-world-cup-debut-ecuador/|publisher=The Argus|title=Jeremy Sarmiento set to make World Cup debut for Ecuador - The Argus|date=8 October 2021|accessdate=8 October 2021}}</ref> He debuted in a 3–0 win over [[Bolivia national football team|Bolivia]] on 7 October 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/tournaments/mens/worldcup/qatar2022/qualifiers/conmebol/match-center/400104609|title=FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022™}}</ref>

On 14 November, Sarmiento was named in Ecuador's 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] alongside Brighton teammates [[Pervis Estupiñán]] and [[Moisés Caicedo]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/mundial/nota/_/id/11228629/ecuador-lista-convocados-qatar-2022-mundial-copa-mundo |title=Ecuador, el último en entregar la lista de los 26 jugadores convocados para la Copa del Mundo de Qatar 2022 |trans-title=Ecuador, the last to deliver the list of the 26 players summoned for the Qatar World Cup 2022 |work=[[ESPN Deportes]] |date=14 November 2022 |access-date=15 November 2022 |language=es}}</ref>

Sarmiento was born in Madrid, Spain, to Ecuadorian parents.<ref name=":0" /> He lived in Madrid until he was 7 years old when he moved to [[London]], England with his familly.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |last=Olé |first=Diario Deportivo |date=2023-03-22 |title=Jeremy Sarmiento sobre su niñez: "Los niños no me querían hacer jugar con ellos" |url=https://www.ole.com.ar/ecuador/la-tri/jeremy-sarmiento-ninez-londres-racismo_0_9XL35IiEaZ.html |access-date=2023-06-22 |website=Olé |language=es}}</ref> As a young boy, Sarmiento was a fan of the [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador national team]] and had always wished to play for them.<ref name=":0" />
