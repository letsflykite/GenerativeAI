'''Nicholas''' "'''Nico'''" '''Williams Arthuer''' (born 12 July 2002) is a Spanish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Winger (association football)|winger]] for [[La Liga]] club [[Athletic Bilbao]] and the [[Spain national football team|Spain national team]].

==Club career==
Born in [[Pamplona]], [[Navarre]], Williams joined the [[youth academy]] of [[Athletic Bilbao]] in 2013 from hometown side [[CA Osasuna]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://juvenildivisiondehonor.com/nico-williams-la-nueva-perla-del-athletic/|title=Nico Williams, la nueva gran promesa del Athletic|trans-title=Nico Williams, the new great promise of Athletic|first=Iago Castro|last=Rabuñal|date=8 May 2020|language=es}}</ref> He began his senior career with the club's [[farm team]], [[CD Basconia|Basconia]], during the [[2019–20 Tercera División|2019–20 season]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://as.com/futbol/2020/05/11/primera/1589194744_092976.html|title=Nico Williams sube al filial e Iñaki le espera pronto a su lado|date=11 May 2020|website=AS.com|trans-title=Nico Williams goes up to the subsidiary and Iñaki will be waiting for him soon by his side|language=es}}</ref>

On 11 May 2020, Williams was promoted to [[Athletic Bilbao B|the reserve team]] in the [[Segunda División B]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.athletic-club.eus/noticias/2020/05/11/nico-williams-y-nico-serrano-al-bilbao-athletic|title=Nico Williams y Nico Serrano, al Bilbao Athletic|trans-title=Nico Williams and Nico Serrano, to Bilbao Athletic|website=Athletic Club website |date=11 May 2020|language=es}}</ref> He made his first team – and [[La Liga]] – debut on 28 April of the following year, coming on as a second-half [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] for [[Jon Morcillo]] in a 2–2 home draw against [[Real Valladolid]];<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://uk.soccerway.com/matches/2021/04/28/spain/primera-division/athletic-club/real-valladolid-club-de-futbol/3359067/|title=Athletic Club vs. Real Valladolid|date=28 April 2021|website=Soccerway }}</ref> his brother Iñaki also came off the bench ten minutes later. It was the first time two siblings had been on the pitch at the same time for the club since [[Julio Salinas|Julio]] and [[Patxi Salinas]] in 1986.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.athletic-club.eus/en/teams/athletic-club/2020-21/matches/1_6056/chronicle|title=Draw against Real Valladolid at San Mamés|website=Athletic Club website |date=28 April 2021}}</ref>

Williams scored his first two goals for the ''Lions'' on 6 January 2022, in a 2–0 win over [[Atlético Mancha Real]] in [[2021–22 Copa del Rey|the campaign]]'s [[Copa del Rey]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://spainsnews.com/nico-williams-double-ends-the-illusion-of-atletico-mancha-real/|title=Nico Williams' double ends the illusion of Atlético Mancha Real|website=Spain's News|date=6 January 2022|accessdate=10 January 2022}}</ref> Seven days later, he scored the winning goal in a 2–1 victory over [[Atlético Madrid]] in the semi-final of the [[2021–22 Supercopa de España]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Nico Williams scores dramatic late winner as Athletic Club stun Atletico Madrid with comeback |url=https://www.eurosport.co.uk/football/spanish-supercopa/2021-2022/atletico-madrid-v-athletic-club-follow-spanish-super-cup-live_sto8701595/story.shtml |website=Eurosport |access-date=14 January 2022 |date=13 January 2022}}</ref> On 20 January 2022, Williams was given a first team contract after meeting a number of clauses.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.athletic-club.eus/en/news/2022/01/21/nico-williams-wins-first-team-contract|title=Nico Williams earns first-team contract|publisher=Athletic Bilbao|date=20 January 2022|accessdate=21 January 2022}}</ref>

Williams was a starter in four of the first five matches of the [[2022–23 La Liga]] campaign, and was named player of the match in a 4–1 away win against [[Elche CF]] on 11 September 2022. In the first half-hour of the match he pressurised an opponent into conceding an own goal, won a penalty for a trip (converted by [[Oihan Sancet]]) then scored himself via a dribble from the right wing and powerful shot for his first goal in the competition.<ref>[https://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/nico-williams-opens-la-liga-account-as-athletic-bilbao-thrash-elche/blt165417a40407e6d4 Nico Williams opens La Liga account as Athletic Bilbao thrash Elche], Shina Oludare, ''Goal.com'', 11 September 2022</ref> Coincidentally this was the same venue and opponent where his brother first found the net in the league seven years earlier.<ref>[https://ghanasoccernet.com/nico-williams-follows-in-brother-inaki-footsteps-after-scoring-for-athletic-bilbao-at-elche Nico Williams follows in brother Inaki footsteps after scoring for Athletic Bilbao at Elche], ''GhanaSoccernet'', 14 September 2022</ref> He scored again the following week, the decisive goal of a 3–2 victory over [[Rayo Vallecano]] and his first at [[San Mamés Stadium (2013)|San Mamés]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=19 September 2022 |title=Williams brothers enjoy stirring send-off at Athletic after international calls {{!}} Sid Lowe |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/sep/19/spain-la-liga-inaki-nico-williams-athletic-bilbao-rayo-vallecano-ghana-spain |access-date=11 November 2022 |website=the Guardian |language=en}}</ref>

==International career==
Williams first represented Spain with the [[Spain national under-18 football team|under-18 squad]] in 2020, scoring two goals in four games.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.besoccer.com/new/la-seleccion-sub-18-llama-a-nico-williams-hermano-de-inaki-779761|title=La Selección Sub 18 llama a Nico Williams, hermano de Iñaki|trans-title=The Under 18 National Team calls Nico Williams, Iñaki's brother|website=www.besoccer.com|date=19 January 2020|language=es}}</ref> He was called up to the [[Spain national under-19 football team|Spain under-19s]] in February 2021,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.athletic-club.eus/noticias/2021/02/16/nico-serrano-y-nico-williams-convocados-por-la-seleccion-espanola-sub-19|title=Nico Serrano y Nico Williams, convocados por la selección española sub-19|trans-title=Nico Serrano and Nico Williams, called up by the Spanish U19 team|website=Athletic Club website|date=16 February 2021|language=es}}</ref> and made his debut for the [[Spain national under-21 football team|under-21s]] in September of the same year.<ref>[https://www.athletic-club.eus/en/news/2021/09/03/agirrezabala-vencedor-and-nico-williams-make-their-spain-under-21-debuts Vencedor and Nico Williams make their Spain Under-21 debuts] Athletic Club official website, 3 September 2021</ref> He received his first call-up to the [[Spain national football team|senior squad]], coached by [[Luis Enrique]], for [[2022–23 UEFA Nations League]] fixtures in September 2022.<ref>[https://www.ghanaweb.com/GhanaHomePage/SportsArchive/Big-blow-for-Ghana-as-Spain-names-Nico-Williams-in-squad-for-September-friendly-matches-1624517 Big blow for Ghana as Spain names Nico Williams in squad for September friendly matches], GhanaWeb, 16 September 2022</ref>
On November 17 he scored his first goal for the national team in a friendly against [[Jordan national football team|Jordan]] and subsequently was called up for [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Personal life==
Williams was born in [[Pamplona]] to Ghanaian parents, who travelled across the [[Sahara]] Desert to reach [[Melilla]], an autonomous Spanish city located in North Africa.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://en.as.com/en/2020/03/04/videos/1583335992_941432.html?autoplay=1?omnil=resrelart|title=Iñaki Williams explains how he got his name and why Bilbao means so much to him|newspaper=Diario AS|date=4 March 2020|access-date=21 January 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://footy-ghana.com/2020/06/spain-u17-coach-david-gordo-tips-bilbao-youngster-nico-williams-for-greatness/|title=Nico Williams: Spain U-17 coach tips Bilbao youngster for greatness|website=Footy Ghana|date=1 June 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2021/oct/06/inaki-williams-my-parents-crossed-the-desert-barefoot-to-get-to-spain|title=Iñaki Williams: 'My parents crossed the desert barefoot to get to Spain'|newspaper=Guardian|date=6 October 2021|access-date=27 November 2022}}</ref> His elder brother [[Iñaki Williams]] is also a footballer and a forward; he too was brought up at Athletic Bilbao having been born in Spain a short time after his parents arrived there.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.mundodeportivo.com/futbol/athletic-bilbao/20190210/46341074569/nico-williams-inaki-athletic-juveniles.html|title=El hermano de Iñaki Williams debuta con gol en el juvenil del Athletic|trans-title=Iñaki Williams's brother debuts with goal for Athletic youths|newspaper=Mundo Deportivo|first=Endika|last=Río|language=es|date=10 February 2019|access-date=14 February 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.abc.es/deportes/futbol/abci-imparable-ascenso-nico-williams-athletic-202101310132_noticia.html|title=El imparable ascenso de Nico Williams en el Athletic|trans-title=The unstoppable rise of Nico Williams at Athletic|newspaper=[[ABC (newspaper)|ABC]]|first=Jorge|last=Abizanda|language=es|date=31 January 2021|access-date=25 February 2021}}</ref> Nico typically wears ''Williams Jr'' on the back of his shirt, including in international matches which do not involve Iñaki. Growing up, he was a fan of [[Asamoah Gyan]].<ref>{{Citation |title=Exclusive Interview With Nico Williams |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sVP1kuQuwtY&t=537s |language=en |access-date=2023-01-24}}</ref>
