'''Jesús David Ferreira Castro''' (born December 24, 2000) is a professional [[Association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]] for [[Major League Soccer]] club [[FC Dallas]]. Born in [[Colombia]], he represents the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States national team]].

Ferreira moved to [[Dallas]], Texas from Colombia, at the age of 10 after his father, [[David Ferreira]], signed with [[FC Dallas]]. He joined the club's youth side and progressed through the ranks before signing a [[Homegrown Player Rule (Major League Soccer)|homegrown]] contract in 2016. He scored his first professional goal on his debut in June 2017 against [[Real Salt Lake]]. In 2018, Ferreira was loaned to [[USL Championship|United Soccer League]] side [[FC Tulsa|Tulsa Roughnecks]] where he scored six goals in 14 matches, including his first career hat-trick. The next season, Ferreira returned to FC Dallas and broke through as a starter under coach [[Luchi Gonzalez]]. In January 2022, Ferreira signed a [[Designated Player Rule|designated player]] contract with Dallas.

Ferreira made his senior debut for the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]] in February 2020 against [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica]]. He was then part of the [[United States men's national under-23 soccer team|under-23]] side that failed to qualify for the [[Football at the 2020 Summer Olympics|Summer Olympics]] in March 2020. Ferreira scored his first two goals for the United States in January 2021 against [[Trinidad and Tobago national football team|Trinidad and Tobago]].

==Club career==
Ferreira moved from Colombia to [[Dallas]] when he was 10 years old.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://fansided.com/2021/04/15/jesus-ferreira-mls-fathers-footsteps/|title='Like a lion, he is hungry': MLS star Jesus Ferreira following in dad's footsteps|date=April 15, 2021|accessdate=February 1, 2022}}</ref> He signed a [[Homegrown Player Rule (Major League Soccer)|homegrown]] player contract with [[FC Dallas]] on November 17, 2016.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2016/11/17/fc-dallas-sign-jesus-ferreira-david-ferreiras-son-homegrown-contract | title=FC Dallas sign Jesus Ferreira, David Ferreira's son, to Homegrown contract| date=November 17, 2016}}</ref> He made his professional debut on June 3, 2017, as a 71st minute substitute during a 6–2 win over [[Real Salt Lake]], scoring his first goal in the 89th minute of the game.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://matchcenter.mlssoccer.com/matchcenter/2017-06-03-fc-dallas-vs-real-salt-lake/feed | title=FC Dallas vs. Real Salt Lake &#124; MatchCenter Timeline}}</ref>

In May 2018, Ferreira was loaned to [[FC Tulsa|Tulsa Roughnecks FC]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fctulsa.com/news_article/show/916083-jesus-ferreira-joins-roughnecks-on-loan-from-fc-dallas|title=Jesus Ferreira Joins Roughnecks on Loan from FC Dallas|website=Tulsa Roughnecks FC|date=May 8, 2018}}</ref> and scored his first goal for Tulsa on August 18, 2018, against [[Real Monarchs]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fcdallas.com/post/2018/08/20/loan-jesus-ferreira-scores-chris-richards-logs-minutes-bayern-and-adonijah-reid|title=ON LOAN: Jesus Ferreira Scores, Chris Richards Logs Minutes with Bayern and Adonijah Reid Plays in Win &#124; FC Dallas}}</ref> and his first career [[hat-trick]] on September 5, 2018, against [[Tacoma Defiance|Seattle Sounders FC 2]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dallasnews.com/sports/fc-dallas/2018/09/06/jesus-ferreira-bags-first-career-hat-trick-on-loan-to-tulsa-roughnecks/|title=Jesus Ferreira bags first career hat-trick on loan to Tulsa Roughnecks|date=September 6, 2018|website=Dallas News}}</ref>

On December 10, 2019, Ferreira signed a four-year contract with FC Dallas.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fcdallas.com/news/jes-s-ferreira-signs-new-contract-fc-dallas|title=Jesús Ferreira Signs New Contract with FC Dallas &#124; FC Dallas|website=fcdallas}}</ref>

Ferreira recorded his first career MLS brace on August 29, 2021, scoring twice in a 5-3 victory over [[Austin FC]]. <ref>{{Cite web|url= https://www.espn.com/soccer/player/matches/_/id/251571/team/185/type/usa.1/year/2021|title=Jesús Ferreira Game By Game Stats and Performance |website=ESPN}}</ref>

On January 18, 2022, Ferreira signed a [[Designated Player Rule|designated player]] contract with FC Dallas, keeping him at the club until 2025.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/fc-dallas-re-sign-jesus-ferreira-as-young-designated-player|title=FC Dallas re-sign Jesús Ferreira as Young Designated Player &#124; MLSSoccer.com|website=mlssoccer|accessdate=February 1, 2022}}</ref> Ferreira was selected as MLS player of the week in week 4 of the 2022 season after recording a hat-trick and an assist for FC Dallas in their 4–1 victory over the [[Portland Timbers]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/fc-dallas-forward-jesus-ferreira-named-mls-week-4-player-of-the-week|title=FC Dallas forward Jesús Ferreira named MLS Week 4 Player of the Week|website=mlssoccer.com|date=March 21, 2022|access-date=March 24, 2022}}</ref> In week two of the [[2023 Major League Soccer season|2023 season]], Ferreira was named to the league's Team of the Matchday after registering a brace in a 3–1 win over [[LA Galaxy]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/team-of-the-matchday-giorgio-chiellini-jesus-ferreira-more-step-up|title=Team of the Matchday: Giorgio Chiellini, Jesús Ferreira & more step up|date=March 6, 2023|last=Sigal|first=Jonathan|work=[[Major League Soccer]]|access-date=March 6, 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
Prior to obtaining United States citizenship, Ferreira attended [[United States Soccer Federation]] youth camps.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fcdallas.com/post/2019/12/16/jesus-ferreira-earns-us-citizenship-and-us-mens-national-team-eligibility|title=Jesus Ferreira Earns U.S. Citizenship and US Men's National Team Eligibility &#124; FC Dallas|accessdate=February 4, 2021}}</ref> In 2016, Ferreira was called up to the [[United States men's national under-17 soccer team|United States under-17]] squad.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://sportsday.dallasnews.com/soccer/soccer/2016/08/12/three-fc-dallas-academy-players-called-latest-us-youth-teams | title=Three FC Dallas Academy players called into latest US Youth Teams| date=August 12, 2016}}</ref> In 2019, Ferreira received a call-up to the [[United States men's national under-23 soccer team|United States under-23]] team ahead of qualifying games for the [[2020 Summer Olympics]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fcdallas.com/post/2019/08/30/jesus-ferreira-earns-call-us-u-23-national-team-following-breakout-2019-season|title=Jesus Ferreira Earns Call-Up to U.S. U-23 National Team Following Breakout 2019 Season &#124; FC Dallas|accessdate=February 4, 2021}}</ref> After that had been postponed due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], he was called up again to the [[2020 CONCACAF Men's Olympic Qualifying Championship]] team.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2021/03/us-u23-mnt-head-coach-jason-kreis-names-roster-for--concacaf-mens-olympic-qualifying-championship|title=U.S. Under-23 Men's National Team Head Coach Jason Kreis Names Roster for Concacaf Men's Olympic Qualifying Championship|website=www.ussoccer.com}}</ref> The team would fail to qualify for the [[Football at the Summer Olympics|Olympics]].

Ferreira made his first senior U.S. camp in January 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2019/12/berhalter-calls-25-players-for-annual-january-camp-at-start-of-important-year-for-usmnt|title=Berhalter Calls 25 Players for Annual January Camp at Start of Important Year for USMNT|website=www.ussoccer.com}}</ref> On February 1, 2020, he made his senior international debut for the United States in its friendly against [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2020/02/international-friendly-usmnt-1-costa-rica-0-match-report-and-stats|title=International Friendly: USA 1 – Costa Rica 0 &#124; Match Report & Stats|website=www.ussoccer.com}}</ref> On January 31, 2021, Ferreira scored his first and second goals at that level and was named Man of the Match in a game following his second January camp with the senior team.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2021/02/man-of-the-match-jesus-ferreira-shined-for-usmnt-vs-trinidad-and-tobago|title=Man of the Match: Jesús Ferreira Shined for USMNT vs. Trinidad & Tobago|website=www.ussoccer.com|accessdate=February 4, 2021}}</ref> 

On June 10, 2022, Ferreira scored his first international hat-trick by scoring four goals against [[Grenada national football team|Grenada]] in a 5–0 victory during his [[2022–23 CONCACAF Nations League A]] campaign.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Straus |first=Brian |date=June 11, 2022 |title=Jesus Ferreira Puts Forth His Case in USMNT’s World Cup Forward Search |work=[[Sports Illustrated]] |url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2022/06/11/jesus-ferreira-usmnt-grenada-concacaf-nations-league-world-cup |access-date=June 29, 2023}}</ref>

On June 28, 2023, Ferreira scored his second international hat-trick with three goals in a 6–0 victory over [[Saint Kitts and Nevis national football team|St. Kitts and Nevis]] during the [[2023 CONCACAF Gold Cup]] in the second round of the group stage, eliminating the Sugar Boys from the tournament.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Floyd |first=Thomas |date=June 29, 2023 |title=USMNT smashes St. Kitts and Nevis to get Gold Cup campaign back on track |work=[[The Washington Post]] |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/sports/2023/06/28/usmnt-stkitts-nevis-gold-cup/ |access-date=June 29, 2023}}</ref> He completed his third international hat-trick in the following match, a 6–0 victory against [[Trinidad and Tobago national football team|Trinidad and Tobago]], making him the first United States men's player to score hat-tricks in consecutive matches and securing his side .<ref>{{Cite news |date=July 2, 2023 |title=USA vs Trinidad and Tobago score, result, highlights as USMNT win Gold Cup group after another Ferreira hat trick |work=[[The Sporting News]] |url=https://www.sportingnews.com/us/soccer/news/usa-vs-trinidad-tobago-live-score-result-gold-cup-highlights-usmnt/qgor3witgjudkzbw7mglybp9 |access-date=July 2, 2023}}</ref>

==Personal life==
He is the son of [[David Ferreira]], a professional player who played for clubs including [[América de Cali]], [[Club Athletico Paranaense|Atlético Paranaense]], [[FC Dallas]] and the [[Colombia national football team|Colombia national team]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2015/09/24/24-under-24-former-mls-mvp-david-ferreiras-son-jesus-earning-high-praise-fc-dallas | title=24 Under 24: Former MLS MVP David Ferreira's son Jesus earning high praise at FC Dallas Academy| date=September 24, 2015}}</ref>

In December 2019, Ferreira received U.S. citizenship.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2019/12/16/fc-dallas-attacker-jesus-ferreira-obtains-us-citizenship|title=FC Dallas attacker Jesus Ferreira obtains US citizenship &#124; MLSsoccer.com|accessdate=February 4, 2021}}</ref>
