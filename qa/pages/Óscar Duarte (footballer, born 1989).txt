'''Óscar Esaú Duarte Gaitán''' (born 3 June 1989) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] for [[Saudi Professional League]] club [[Al Wehda FC|Al-Wehda]]. Born in Nicaragua, he represents the [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica national team]].

==Club career==
Duarte was born in [[Catarina, Masaya]], [[Nicaragua]].<ref name="Nica">[http://www.elnuevodiario.com.ni/mundial-futbol-2014/322401-nica-oscar-duarte-debuta-mundial-seleccion-de-costa-rica Nica Oscar Duarte debuta en el Mundial con la Selección de Costa Rica] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140618025857/http://www.elnuevodiario.com.ni/mundial-futbol-2014/322401-nica-oscar-duarte-debuta-mundial-seleccion-de-costa-rica |date=18 June 2014 }} – El Nuevo Diario {{in lang|es}}</ref> After making 52 appearances in five years at Costa Rican club [[Deportivo Saprissa]], Duarte joined [[Club Brugge KV|Club Brugge]] in the [[Belgian Pro League]] in 2013.<ref name=fifa>{{cite news|url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/players/player=312987/profile-detail.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140618082420/http://www.fifa.com/worldcup/players/player=312987/profile-detail.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=18 June 2014|title=Oscar DUARTE|accessdate=22 June 2014|publisher=FIFA}}</ref>

On 24 June 2022, Duarte joined Saudi Arabian club [[Al Wehda FC|Al-Wehda]].<ref>{{cite web |title=الوحدة يوقع مع المدافع الكوستاريكي دوارتي |url=https://www.alriyadh.com/1958292}}</ref>

==International career==
Duarte made his debut for the [[Costa Rica national football team]] against [[Jamaica national football team|Jamaica]] on 17 November 2010 and played for the team at the [[2011 Copa Centroamericana]], where they lost to [[Honduras national football team|Honduras]] in the final.<ref name=fifa/>

In June 2014, Duarte was named in Costa Rica's squad for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]],<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/football/teams/costa-rica/10810092/Costa-Rica-World-Cup-2014-squad.html|title=Costa Rica World Cup 2014 squad|date=2 June 2014|accessdate=20 June 2014|work=The Telegraph}}</ref> becoming the first Nicaragua-born player at a [[FIFA World Cup|World Cup finals]].<ref name="Nica" />

In the team's opening match, he scored his first goal for ''Los Ticos'' in a 3–1 defeat of [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/25285064|title=Uruguay 1–3 Costa Rica|date=14 June 2014|accessdate=20 June 2014|work=BBC}}</ref> In their second match, the team beat [[Italy national football team|Italy]], a 1–0 win that qualified Costa Rica for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup knockout stage|knockout stage]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/25285190|title=Italy 0–1 Costa Rica|date=20 June 2014|accessdate=22 June 2014|work=BBC}}</ref> Costa Rica completed the group stage unbeaten, recording a second consecutive clean sheet in a 0–0 draw with [[England national football team|England]] in [[Belo Horizonte]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Taylor|first1=Daniel|title=England end disappointing World Cup with barren draw against Costa Rica|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/jun/24/costa-rica-england-world-cup-group-d-match-report|accessdate=25 June 2014|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=24 June 2014}}</ref> On 29 June, Duarte was [[Ejection (sports)#Association football|sent off]] for receiving two [[Penalty card#Yellow card|yellow card]]s in Costa Rica's [[2014 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Round of 16|round of 16]] match against [[Greece national football team|Greece]]. The team advanced via a [[penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shootout]] to the quarter-finals for the first time in their history, where they lost on penalties to the Netherlands.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/27961189|title=Costa Rica 1–1 Greece|accessdate=30 June 2014|date=29 June 2014|work=BBC}}</ref>

In May 2018 he was named in Costa Rica’s [[Costa Rica national football team|23-man squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Arnold |first1=Jon |title=Keylor Navas & six MLS players named in Costa Rica's 23-man World Cup squad |url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/costa-rica-names-23-man-world-cup-squad-featuring-keylor/1bw2iz8pkma5614bzushqyyo7g |website=Goal |access-date=25 November 2020 |date=14 May 2018}}</ref>
