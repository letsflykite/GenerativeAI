'''Danilo Luiz da Silva''' (born 15 July 1991), known as '''Danilo''', is a Brazilian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays primarily as a [[Defender (association football)|right-back]] for {{Italian football updater|Juventus}} club [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]] and the [[Brazil national football team|Brazil national team]].

Danilo began his career with [[América Futebol Clube (MG)|América Mineiro]] before moving to [[Santos FC|Santos]], where he scored the goal that won the [[2011 Copa Libertadores]]. In January 2012, he moved to Portuguese club [[FC Porto|Porto]], where he won consecutive [[Primeira Liga]] titles. In 2015, he joined [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]] following a €31.5 million transfer fee. In 2017, he joined [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]]. In 2019, having won two [[Premier League]] titles, an [[FA Cup]] and two EFL [[Cup]]s with City, Danilo joined Juventus, winning the [[Serie A]] title during his first season with the club.

Danilo was first capped by the senior Brazil team in 2011, also winning the [[2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup|2011 U-20 World Cup]] and a silver medal at the [[Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|2012 Summer Olympics]]. He represented the nation at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]], the [[2021 Copa América]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]].

==Club career==
===Early years & Santos===
Danilo was born in [[Bicas]], [[Minas Gerais]]. He played youth football with [[Tupynambás Futebol Clube|Tupynambás]] and [[América Futebol Clube (MG)|América Mineiro]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://globoesporte.globo.com/mg/zona-da-mata-centro-oeste/futebol/noticia/apos-faturar-com-venda-para-real-time-de-mg-pode-lucrar-com-ida-de-danilo-para-o-city.ghtml|title=Após faturar com venda para Real, time de MG pode lucrar com ida de Danilo para o City|trans-title=After cashing in with sale to Real, MG team can make money with Danilo's departure to City|website=Globo Esporte|publisher=[[Grupo Globo]]|first=Bruno|last=Ribeiro|language=pt|date=25 July 2017|access-date=25 July 2017}}</ref> and competed at senior level with the latter club in the [[Campeonato Mineiro]] and the [[Campeonato Brasileiro Série C|Série C]], [[2009 Campeonato Brasileiro Série C|winning the latter in 2009 and gaining promotion]].<ref name=Multichampion>{{cite news|url=http://g1.globo.com/jornal-nacional/noticia/2018/06/danilo-sonhava-em-ser-goleiro-mas-virou-lateral-multicampeao.html|title=Danilo sonhava em ser goleiro, mas virou lateral multicampeão|trans-title=Danilo dreamt of being a goalkeeper, but turned multichampion full-back|newspaper=[[O Globo]]|language=pt|date=9 June 2018|access-date=22 January 2019}}</ref>

In May 2010, Danilo was transferred to [[Santos FC|Santos]]. Investor [[DIS Esporte]] acquired 37.5% of his economic rights, Santos another 37.5% for free and the remaining 25% were retained by América.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.santosfc.com.br/noticias/conteudo.asp?id=22422|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120920000401/http://www.santosfc.com.br/noticias/conteudo.asp?id=22422|url-status=dead|archive-date=20 September 2012|title=Santos FC apresenta lateral direito Danilo, maior revelação do futebol mineiro em 2010|trans-title=Santos FC present right-back Danilo, biggest revelation in ''mineiro'' football in 2010|publisher=Santos FC|language=pt|date=30 June 2010|access-date=27 August 2011}}</ref> He won the [[Campeonato Paulista]] in [[2011 Campeonato Paulista|2011]] and competed in two [[Campeonato Brasileiro Série A|Série A]] seasons with the team.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://globoesporte.globo.com/sp/santos-e-regiao/futebol/times/santos/noticia/2011/09/danilo-lamenta-fase-de-time-que-o-revelou-mas-foca-na-ascensao.html|title=Danilo lamenta fase de time que o revelou, mas foca na ascensão|trans-title=Danilo despondent over moment of team that unveiled him, but focused on rise|website=Globo Esporte|publisher=Grupo Globo|first=Adilson|last=Barros|language=pt|date=20 September 2011|access-date=22 January 2019}}</ref>

Danilo played the full 90 minutes of both legs of the [[2011 Copa Libertadores Finals]] against [[Peñarol]], the first in [[Midfielder#Central midfielder|central midfield]] and the second at right-back, and scored the decisive goal in a 2–1 victory following a goalless first match.<ref name="Neymar brilha">{{cite web|url=http://globoesporte.globo.com/jogo/libertadores-2011/22-06-2011/santos-penarol.html|title=Neymar brilha, meninos da vila fazem história e Peixe leva tri da Libertadores|trans-title=Neymar shines, local lads make history and Fish lifts third Libertadores|website=Globo Esporte|publisher=Grupo Globo|first1=Adilson|last1=Barros|first2=Julyana|last2=Travaglia|language=pt|date=22 June 2011|access-date=2 April 2015}}</ref>

===Juventus===
On 7 August 2019, Danilo signed for [[Serie A]] champions [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]] on a five-year contract following a €37&nbsp;million transfer fee paid by Juventus. The transaction involved a part-exchange that saw [[João Cancelo]] move to Manchester City.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.juventus.com/en/news/news/2019/official-danilo-is-bianconero.php |title=Official: Danilo is Bianconero! |publisher=Juventus F.C. |date=7 August 2019 |access-date=8 August 2019}}</ref> Danilo made his Juventus debut on 31 August in a match against [[S.S.C. Napoli|Napoli]], in which he scored the opening goal of an eventual 4–3 home victory, which was also the fastest goal scored by a foreign player in Serie A.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/SerieA/match/142381|title=Juventus 4 - 3 Napoli|publisher=Football Italia|date=31 August 2019}}</ref>

In the [[2020 Coppa Italia Final]] against Napoli on 17 June, following a 0–0 draw after regulation time, Danilo missed Juventus' second spot kick in the resulting shoot-out, hitting the ball over the crossbar. Napoli ultimately won the match 4–2 on penalties.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/154624/coppa-napoli-beat-juventus-penalties|title=Coppa: Napoli beat Juventus on penalties|publisher=Football Italia|date=17 June 2020}}</ref>

On 10 January 2021, in a Serie A game against [[U.S. Sassuolo Calcio|Sassuolo]], Danilo opened the scoring with a 25-meter goal; Juventus won 3–1.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Juventus-Sassuolo 3-1: gol di Danilo, Defrel, Ramsey e Ronaldo|url=https://www.gazzetta.it/Calcio/Serie-A/Juventus/10-01-2021/juventus-sassuolo-2-1-gol-danilo-defrel-ramsey-3902388955176.shtml|access-date=1 February 2021|date=10 January 2021|website=La Gazzetta dello Sport|language=it-IT}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:20141118 AUTBRA 5094.jpg|thumb|upright|Danilo lining up for [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]] in 2014]]

Courtesy of his Santos performances, Danilo made his debut for [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]] on 14 September 2011 at the age of just 20, featuring in the first leg of [[2011 Superclásico de las Américas|that year]]'s [[Superclásico de las Américas]], a 0–0 away draw against [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]] (2–0 aggregate win).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.afa.org.ar/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=15078:juegan-argentina-y-brasil-en-cordoba&catid=164:seleccion-mayor&Itemid=66|title=Argentina y Brasil igualaron 0–0 en Córdoba|trans-title=Argentina and Brasil went for 0–0 in Córdoba|publisher=[[Argentine Football Association]]|language=es|date=14 September 2011|access-date=14 November 2013}}</ref> In [[2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup|the same year]], he started for the [[Brazil national under-20 football team|under-20s]] at the [[FIFA U-20 World Cup]], with the tournament in Colombia ending in victory.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://globoesporte.globo.com/futebol/mundial-sub-20/noticia/2011/08/oscar-decide-brasil-danca-o-vira-contra-portugal-e-leva-penta-do-sub-20.html|title=Oscar decide, Brasil dança o vira contra Portugal e leva penta do sub-20|trans-title=Oscar the decider, Brazil dance the vira against Portugal and get under-20 ''penta''|website=Globo Esporte|publisher=Grupo Globo|first=Victor|last=Canedo|language=pt|date=21 August 2011|access-date=22 January 2019}}</ref>

Danilo was also named in [[Tite (football manager)|Tite]]'s [[2018 FIFA World Cup squads#Brazil|squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/amp/football/44116590|title=World Cup: Neymar named in Brazil's 23-man squad|website=BBC Sport|date=14 May 2018|access-date=15 May 2018}}</ref> making his debut in [[FIFA World Cup|the competition]] on 17 June by playing the entire 1–1 draw to [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44427968|title=Brazil 1–1 Switzerland|website=BBC Sport|first=Luke|last=Reddy|date=17 June 2018|access-date=17 June 2018}}</ref> He incurred an injury on 6 July the day before the quarter-final clash with [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]], being sidelined for the remainder of the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://soccer.nbcsports.com/2018/07/06/brazil-says-danilo-out-of-world-cup-with-ankle-injury/|title=Brazil says Danilo out of World Cup with ankle injury|website=[[NBC Sports]]|date=6 July 2018|access-date=6 July 2018}}</ref>

Danilo scored his first senior international goal on 19 November 2019 in a 3–0 [[Exhibition game#Association football|friendly]] win against [[South Korea national football team|South Korea]] played in [[Abu Dhabi]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/international/brazil-vs-south-korea-result-philippe-coutinho-paqueta-danilo-a9209351.html  |title=Brazil vs South Korea result: Philippe Coutinho dazzles to help snap five-game winless streak |work=The Independent |last1=Rathborn |first1=Jack |date=20 November 2019 |access-date=21 November 2019 }}</ref>

In June 2021, he was included in Brazil's squad for the [[2021 Copa América]] on home soil.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://en.as.com/en/2021/07/10/football/1625909312_472586.html |title=Brazil national team squad Copa America 2021: selected players, absences... |website=AS.com |language=en |date=10 July 2021 |access-date=11 July 2021 }}</ref> He started in his nation's 1–0 defeat to rivals Argentina in the [[2021 Copa América Final|final]] on 10 July.<ref name="2021CAFinalGuardian">{{cite news |last=Graham |first=Bryan Armen |date=10 July 2021 |title=Argentina 1-0 Brazil: Copa América final – as it happened |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2021/jul/10/argentina-v-brazil-copa-america-final-live |work=[[The Guardian]] |accessdate=13 July 2021 }}</ref>

On 7 November 2022, Danilo was named in the squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Seleção Brasileira está convocada para a Copa do Mundo FIFA Qatar 2022|url=https://www.cbf.com.br/selecao-brasileira/noticias/selecao-masculina/selecao-brasileira-esta-convocada-para-a-copa-do-mundo-fifa-qatar-2022|publisher=[[Brazilian Football Confederation|CBF]]|access-date=7 November 2022|date=7 November 2022|language=pt}}</ref> During the opening game against [[Serbia national football team|Serbia]], Danilo injured his ankle, forcing him to skip the rest of the group stage.<ref>{{cite press release|url=https://www.reuters.com/lifestyle/sports/neymar-danilo-miss-rest-group-stage-with-ankle-injuries-2022-11-25/|title=Neymar and Danilo to miss rest of group stage with ankle injuries|date=25 November 2022|author=Kallas, Fernando|publisher=Reuters|accessdate=25 November 2022}}</ref>

==Style of play==
In 2012, ''[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]'' compared Danilo to compatriot and fellow right-back [[Dani Alves]], describing him as "a tireless right back with an attacking streak and a polished technique. His continual runs up and down the flank also serve to make him a tough defender, who plays well as a [[Defender (association football)#Sweeper (libero)|sweeper]] and anticipates the play. He measures 6 feet tall and, in spite of his slender build, does well up top," also dubbing him as being "one of the best right backs of the future," due to his high quality performances at the time.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.marca.com/2012/06/02/en/football/real_madrid/1338651695.html |title=Ivanovic looking unlikely, Mou turns to Danilo |work=Marca |last1=Ulises |first1=Sánchez-Flor |date=6 February 2012 |access-date=27 April 2020 }}</ref> Although primarily a right-back, he has also been deployed as a [[Midfielder#Winger|right-winger]], or even as a central or [[Midfielder#Defensive midfielder|defensive midfielder]] in front of the back-line on occasion.<ref name="Neymar brilha"/><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.gazzetta.it/Calcio/Calciomercato/Estero/25-06-2011/big-italiane-sud-america-801750294316.shtml |title=Big italiane in Sud America, Per Danilo, Martinuccio, Dedè... |work=La Gazzetta dello Sport  |language=it |last1=Calamai |first1=Luca |date=25 June 2011 |access-date=8 July 2020 }}</ref> In 2015, [[Tim Vickery]], an expert on South American football, said the following of Danilo: "From [[Carlos Alberto Torres|Carlos Alberto]] to [[Cafu]], Brazil have had more spectacular players at right-back than most - but Porto man Danilo is proving a splendid all-rounder. He can join the attack as an element of surprise, but his defensive skills are also sound."<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/32141669 |title=Danilo: Real Madrid to sign £23m defender from Porto |work=BBC Sport |date=31 March 2015 |access-date=2 August 2020 }}</ref> Under his Juventus manager [[Maurizio Sarri]], Danilo has also been used as a makeshift left-back.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.football-italia.net/155521/probable-line-ups-milan-juventus |title=Probable line-ups: Milan-Juventus |publisher=Football Italia |date=7 July 2020 |access-date=8 July 2020 }}</ref> A 2019 profile in ''[[Tuttosport]]'' by Giovanni Aramini also praised him for his tactical sense, positioning, and ball-winning capabilities, describing him as a "complete" defender.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.tuttosport.com/news/calcio/serie-a/juventus/2019/08/02-59578630/juve_ecco_chi_danilo_il_tuttofare_della_fascia/ |title=Juve, ecco chi è Danilo: il tuttofare della fascia |work=www.tuttosport.com |language=it |last1=Armanini |first1=Giovanni |date=2 August 2019 |access-date=2 August 2020 }}</ref> Under [[Andrea Pirlo]], he has also been used as a right-sided [[Defender (association football)#Centre-back|centre-back]] in a three-man back-line.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.football-italia.net/157183/pirlos-juventus-revert-three |title=Pirlo's Juventus revert to three? |publisher=www.football-italia.net |date=14 August 2020 |access-date=20 September 2020 }}</ref>
