'''Joakim Mæhle Pedersen''' (born 20 May 1997) is a Danish professional [[footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|full-back]] for {{Italian football updater|Atalanta}} club [[Atalanta B.C.|Atalanta]] and the [[Denmark national football team|Denmark national team]].

==Club career==

===AaB===
Born in [[Østervrå]], [[Vendsyssel]], Mæhle started playing football with local club Østervrå IF and moved to the [[AaB Fodbold|AaB]] youth academy as a 12-year-old.<ref name="salg-genk" /> At the age of 19, Mæhle was promoted into the first team squad on 10 June 2016 and also signed a full-time senior contract with AaB.<ref>{{ cite web | title = AaB rykker seks talenter op på førsteholdet | url = http://www.bold.dk/fodbold/nyheder/aab-rykker-seks-talenter-op-paa-foersteholdet/ | publisher = bold.dk | date = 10 June 2016}}</ref> He made his debut for AaB on 7 August 2016.<ref>{{ cite web | title = NORDSJÆLLAND VS. AAB 1 - 2 | url = https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/08/07/denmark/superliga/fc-nordsjalland/aalborg-bk/2238296/ | publisher = soccerway.com | date = 7 August 2016}}</ref> He started on the bench, but replaced [[Thomas Enevoldsen]] in the 89th minute in a 2–1 victory against [[FC Nordsjælland]] in the [[Superligaen|Danish Superliga]].

On 11 November 2016, Mæhle earned a new contract until 2020.<ref>{{ cite web | title = AaB giver Mæhle til 2020 | url = http://www.bold.dk/fodbold/nyheder/aab-giver-maehle-til-2020/ | publisher = bold.dk | date = 11 November 2016 }}</ref> In April 2017, AaB confirmed that they were in negotiations with a foreign club about Mæhle.

===Genk===
On 9 May 2017, AaB confirmed that they had sold Mæhle to Belgian club [[K.R.C. Genk|Racing Genk]] for an undisclosed fee, starting from 1 July 2017.<ref name="salg-genk">[https://aabsport.dk/nyheder/2017/maj/aab-med-forventet-stort-overskud-i-foerste-kvartal/ AaB sælger Mæhle til KRC Genk]‚ aabsport.dk, 9 May 2017</ref> He was signed to succeed right-back [[Timothy Castagne]], who had moved to [[Atalanta B.C.|Atalanta]]. Mæhle made his debut for Genk on 29 July 2017 in the first league match of the season against [[Waasland-Beveren]] as a substitute for [[Amine Khammas]]. In his first season with the club, he was not a fixed starter, competing with [[Clinton Mata]] for his position. In his second season, after Mata's departure to [[Club Brugge]], he won a permanent starting position. That season, Mæhle grew into a key player in the Genk starting eleven. At the end of the season, Genk won the [[2018–19 Belgian First Division A|Belgian championship]].

On 11 September 2019, Mæhle extended his contract until June 2023.<ref>[https://www.bold.dk/fodbold/nyheder/maehle-forlaenger-aftalen-med-belgiske-mestre/ Mæhle forlænger aftalen med belgiske mestre], bold.dk, 11 September 2019</ref>

===Atalanta===
Genk announced on 22 December 2020 that Mæhle had signed for [[Atalanta B.C.|Atalanta]] on a five-year contract for a fee of €10 million. His transfer was made official on 4 January 2021.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.football-italia.net/163901/official-atalanta-deal-maehle |title=Official: Atalanta deal for Maelhe |publisher=Football Italia |date=22 December 2020 |accessdate=23 December 2020}}</ref> He made his [[Serie A]] debut for Atalanta on 6 January in the club's 3–0 defeat of [[Parma Calcio 1913|Parma]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.legaseriea.it/en/players/joakim-maehle/MHLJKM|title=Player - Joakim Maehle|website=Lega Serie A|accessdate=25 March 2021|archive-date=24 June 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210624202613/https://www.legaseriea.it/en/players/joakim-maehle/MHLJKM|url-status=dead}}</ref> On 28 February, Mæhle provided an assist in Atalanta's 2–0 win over [[U.C. Sampdoria|Sampdoria]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.legaseriea.it/en/serie-a/match-report/2020-21/UNICO/UNI/24/SAMATA|title=Sampodria 0 – 2 Atalanta|website=Lega Serie A|date=28 February 2021|accessdate=25 March 2021|archive-date=24 June 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210624214905/https://www.legaseriea.it/en/serie-a/match-report/2020-21/UNICO/UNI/24/SAMATA|url-status=dead}}</ref> Mæhle scored his first goal for Atalanta in a 6–2 Serie A victory over [[Udinese Calcio|Udinese]] on 9 January 2022.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.atalanta.it/en/news/udinese-2-6-atalanta/ |title=Udinese 2–6 Atalanta |website=atalanta.it |date=9 January 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
Mæhle made his [[Denmark national football team|Denmark national team]] debut on 5 September 2020 in a [[2020–21 UEFA Nations League A|Nations League]] game against [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]]; he substituted [[Martin Braithwaite]] in the 72nd minute of a 2–0 home loss.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[UEFA]]|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefanationsleague/match/2029993/|title=Denmark v Belgium game report|date=5 September 2020}}</ref> He scored his first international goal in a [[Exhibition game|friendly]] against the [[Faroe Islands national football team|Faroe Islands]] on 7 October 2020.

In June 2021, he was included in the national team's bid for [[2020 UEFA Euro]], where the team reached the semi-finals.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://dbu.dk/nyheder/2021/maj/26-spillere-klar-til-em-for-danmark/ |title=26 spillere klar til EM for Danmark |trans-title=26 players ready for the European Championship for Denmark |work=[[Danish Football Association]] |date=25 May 2021 |access-date=25 May 2021 |language=da}}</ref>

On 12 October 2021, Maehle scored the only goal as Denmark defeated [[Austria national football team|Austria]] to secure a place at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/58891941|title=Denmark captain Simon Kjaer says they "can go a long way" after qualifying for the 2022 World Cup in Qatar with a win over Austria in Copenhagen|website=BBC|date=12 October 2021|accessdate=12 October 2021}}</ref>
