'''Gerardo Daniel Arteaga Zamora''' (born 7 September 1998) is a Mexican professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[left-back]] for [[Belgian Pro League]] club [[K.R.C. Genk|Genk]] and the [[Mexico national football team|Mexico national team]].

==Club career==
===Santos Laguna===
Arteaga joined [[Santos Laguna]]'s youth academy in 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.clubsantos.mx/noticias/detalle/1480 |title=EL CANTERANO GERARDO ARTEAGA DEBUTÓ EN COPA MX |website= ClubSantos.mx |date=14 September 2016 |accessdate=4 January 2016}}</ref> On 1 October 2016, under manager [[José Manuel de la Torre]], he made his professional debut in [[Liga MX]] against [[Querétaro F.C.|Querétaro]] ending in a 1–1 draw.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://ligamx.net/cancha/jugador/99013/gerardo-daniel-arteaga-zamora |title=Gerardo Daniel Arteaga Zamora |publisher=LIGA BBVA Bancomer MX |website=LigaMX.net |date=24 April 1994 |accessdate=4 January 2016}}</ref>

===Genk===
On 26 July 2020, Santos Laguna announced the departure of Arteaga to Belgian club [[K.R.C. Genk|Genk]] for a reported fee of €3.5 million on a 5-year contract.<ref>{{cite web|title=¡Mexicano a Europa! Gerardo Arteaga es nuevo jugador del Genk de Bélgica|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/gerardo-arteaga-ficha-genk-belgica-nuevo-mexicano-europa|work=Medio Tiempo|date=26 July 2020}}</ref> Arteaga made his debut with the club on 24 August against [[K.V.C. Westerlo|Westerlo]] in a 2–2 draw.<ref>{{cite web|title=Gerardo Arteaga colaboró en su debut con el Genk|url=https://www.foxsports.com.mx/mx/article/gerardo-arteaga-colaboro-en-su-debut-con-el-genk_bwpzw6}}</ref>

On 25 April 2021, he played the entirety of the [[2021 Belgian Cup Final|Belgian Cup final]] in a 2–1 victory over [[Standard Liège]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/onuachu-with-an-assist-as-genk-clinch-belgian-cup-title/1jp81i8ofssju1k0yrdb9b6nu8|title=Onuachu with an assist as Genk clinch Belgian Cup title after Standard Liege win|website=Goal|date=26 April 2021|accessdate=29 April 2021}}</ref>

==International career==
===Youth===
Arteaga was included in the final roster that participated at the [[2018 Toulon Tournament]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Revelan convocatoria del Tri Sub-21 para el torneo Esperanzas de Toulon|url=http://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/mundial/nota/_/id/4333245/revelan-convocatoria-del-tri-sub-21-para-el-torneo-esperanzas-de-toulon|work=ESPN|language=Spanish|date=22 May 2018|accessdate=26 October 2018}}</ref> He would go on to appear in all five matches, as Mexico would go to the final against [[England national under-21 football team|England]], where Mexico lost 2–1.<ref>{{cite web|title=England Beat Mexico to Win 2018 Toulon Tournament Final, 3rd Straight Title|url=https://bleacherreport.com/articles/2780312-england-beat-mexico-to-win-2018-toulon-tournament-final-3rd-straight-title|work=Bleacher Report|first=Christopher|last=Simpson|accessdate=21 August 2018}}</ref>

Arteaga was called up by [[Jaime Lozano]] to participate with the [[Mexico national under-23 football team|under-22]] team at the [[2019 Toulon Tournament]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Convocatoria Final de la SNM Sub 22 Para el Torneo Maurice Revello |url=http://fmf.mx/#/Noticia/21261 |publisher=Federación Mexicana de Fútbol Asociación, A.C. |date=23 May 2019 |language=Spanish}}</ref> where Mexico won third place at the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mexico clinch third place in 2019 Toulon Tournament after win on penalty kicks|url=https://www.fmfstateofmind.com/2019/6/15/18680342/mexico-clinch-third-place-in-2019-toulon-tournament-after-win-on-penalty-kicks-match-highlights|date=15 June 2019|publisher=Fmfstateofmind.com}}</ref>

===Senior===
In September 2018, he was called up by interim manager [[Ricardo Ferretti]] for September friendlies against [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay]] and the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Revelan lista del Tricolor con 7 debutantes; Alanís es la otra novedad|url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/2018/08/29/lista-la-convocatoria-de-mexico-y-el-tuca-ferretti-alanis-es-la-otra-novedad|work=MedioTiempo|date=29 August 2018|accessdate=12 September 2018}}</ref> Arteaga made his [[Mexico national football team|senior national team]] debut against the United States on 11 September 2018, where Mexico lost 0–1.<ref>{{cite web|title=Con Lainez y Alvarado de titulares y 4 debuts más, así va el Tri vs. EE.UU.|url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/2018/09/11/con-lainez-y-alvarado-de-titulares-y-cuatro-debuts-mas-asi-va-el-tri-vs-eeuu|work=MedioTiempo|first=Enrique|last= Martínez|date=11 September 2018|accessdate=12 September 2018}}</ref> On 27 September 2022, he scored his first international goal during a 2–3 loss against [[Colombia national football team|Colombia]].

In October 2022, Arteaga was named in Mexico's preliminary 31-man [[Mexico national football team|squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], and in November, he was ultimately included in the final 26-man roster, but did not receive any minutes on the field during the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lista de convocados de la Selección Mexicana al Mundial de Qatar 2022|url=https://www.tvazteca.com/aztecadeportes/lista-oficial-convocados-seleccion-mexicana-mundial-qatar-2022|work=Tvazteca.com|first=Omar|last=Moro|date=14 November 2022|language=Spanish}}</ref>
