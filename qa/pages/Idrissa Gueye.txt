'''Idrissa Gana Gueye''' (born 26 September 1989) is a Senegalese professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[defensive midfielder]] for {{English football updater|Everton}} club [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] and the [[Senegal national football team|Senegal national team]].

Starting his career at [[Diambars FC|Diambars]] in his native Senegal, Gueye joined French club [[Lille OSC|Lille]]'s reserve side in 2008. He would go on to play for the side's first team from 2010 to 2015, winning a [[Ligue 1]] and [[Coupe de France]] [[Double (association football)|double]] in 2011, before moving to England with [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]]. A year later, he signed for fellow [[Premier League]] club [[Everton F.C.|Everton]]. In 2019, Gueye returned to France by signing for the reigning [[Ligue 1]] champions, [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] (PSG). He reached the [[2020 UEFA Champions League Final]] with the club, but did not play in the match in which PSG lost.

Gueye made his debut for Senegal in 2011. He was part of [[Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's team squads#Senegal|the Senegalese squad]] at the [[Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics|2012 Summer Olympics]], where he played one match. He  played for Senegal at two [[FIFA World Cup]] and four [[Africa Cup of Nations]] tournaments. After a defeat in the [[2019 Africa Cup of Nations Final|2019 final]], he helped his country win [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations Final|the following competition's final in 2022]]. He is Senegal's record cap holder and the first Senegalese player to reach over 100 caps.

==International career==
Gueye made his international debut for [[Senegal national football team|Senegal]] on 11 November 2011 as a half-time substitute for [[Deme N'Diaye]] in a 4–1 friendly win away to neighbours [[Guinea national football team|Guinea]].<ref name=NFT/> He was part of the team at the [[Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics&nbsp;– Men's tournament|2012 Olympics]], but was injured just before the interval in the opening game against hosts [[Great Britain men's Olympic football team|Great Britain]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Smythe |first1=Rob |title=London 2012 Olympics: men's football, GB 1-1 Senegal – as it happened |url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2012/jul/26/london-2012-britain-senegal-live-mbm |access-date=20 May 2022 |work=[[The Guardian]] |date=26 July 2012}}</ref> He was selected for the [[Africa Cup of Nations]] in [[2015 Africa Cup of Nations|2015]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/30743603|title=2015 Africa Cup of Nations: Sadio Mane included in Senegal squad|date=9 January 2015|access-date=2 July 2015|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> and [[2017 Africa Cup of Nations|2017]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Senegal name final 23 man squad for AFCON 2017 |url=https://www.soccer24.co.zw/2016/12/30/16080/ |access-date=20 May 2022 |publisher=Soccer 24 |date=30 December 2016}}</ref>

== Controversy ==
{{see also|Homosexuality in association football}}
In the 37th matchday of the [[2021–22 Ligue 1]] season, Gueye travelled with the PSG squad for the away match against [[Montpellier HSC|Montpellier]]. However, he was not included in the team sheet for what manager [[Mauricio Pochettino]] revealed to be "personal reasons", and not an injury.<ref>{{Cite web |last=O'Callaghan |first=Rory |date=16 May 2022 |title=Idrissa Gana Gueye 'Missed PSG's Win At Montpellier After Refusing To Wear Rainbow Flag On Kit' |url=https://www.sportbible.com/football/idrissa-gana-gueye-missed-psg-win-montpellier-rainbow-flag-lgbtq-20220516 |access-date=16 May 2022 |website=[[SPORTbible]]}}</ref> This brought the media spotlight on Gueye; [[RMC (France)|RMC Sport]] reported that he had refused to play in the match due to PSG's shirts featuring the [[Rainbow flag (LGBT)|rainbow flag]] in support of the [[LGBT movements|LGBT movement]], an initiative taken by Ligue 1 for the occasion of the [[International Day Against Homophobia, Transphobia and Biphobia]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=15 May 2022 |title=PSG: Gueye a bien boycotté le match à Montpellier en raison du maillot contre l'homophobie |trans-title=PSG: Gueye indeed boycotted the match against Montpellier due to the shirt against homophobia |url=https://rmcsport.bfmtv.com/football/ligue-1/psg-gueye-a-bien-boycotte-le-match-a-montpellier-en-raison-du-maillot-contre-l-homophobie_AV-202205150268.html |access-date=16 May 2022 |website=[[RMC (France)|RMC Sport]] |language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/61492696|title=Gueye refused to wear PSG shirt with rainbow symbol|first=John|last=Bennett|date=18 May 2022|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> He had also notably missed the same fixture in the previous season, with the reasoning behind his non-participation then being that he was suffering from [[gastroenteritis]].<ref>{{cite news |date=14 May 2022 |title=L'absence d'Idrissa Gueye contre Montpellier fait parler |trans-title=The absence of Idrissa Gueye against Montpellier is making people talk |url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/L-absence-d-idrissa-gueye-contre-montpellier-fait-parler/1333138 |access-date=16 May 2022 |website=[[L'Équipe]] |language=fr}}</ref>

For his controversial absence against Montpellier, Gueye received backlash and calls for sanctions from Rouge Direct, an organization against [[homophobia]],<ref>{{Cite web |date=16 May 2022 |title=Rouge Direct, association contre l'homophobie, interpelle Idrissa Gueye (PSG) |trans-title=Rouge Direct, association against homophobia, challenges Idrissa Gueye (PSG) |url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Rouge-direct-association-contre-l-homophobie-interpelle-idrissa-gueye-psg/1333338 |access-date=16 May 2022 |website=[[L'Équipe]] |language=fr}}</ref> and [[Île-de-France]] regional president [[Valérie Pécresse]], among others.<ref>{{Cite web |date=16 May 2022 |title=French politician calls on Idrissa Gueye to be sanctioned for refusal to wear rainbow shirt |url=https://www.getfootballnewsfrance.com/2022/french-politician-calls-on-idrissa-gueye-to-be-sanctioned-for-refusal-to-wear-rainbow-shirt/ |access-date=16 May 2022 |website=Get French Football News}}</ref> The [[French Football Federation]] (FFF) asked him to confirm the reason for his absence, in order to dispel rumours.<ref>{{cite news |title=PSG's Idrissa Gueye asked to explain absence after homophobia accusations |url=https://www.france24.com/en/sport/20220518-psg-s-idrissa-gueye-asked-to-explain-absence-after-homophobia-accusations |access-date=20 May 2022 |publisher=France 24 |date=18 May 2022}}</ref>

Senegalese president [[Macky Sall]] expressed his support for Gueye, justifying the position by stating that Gueye's "religious beliefs must be respected".<ref>{{Cite web |date=17 May 2022 |title=Senegal's president supports Gueye after homophobia accusations |url=https://sports.yahoo.com/senegals-president-supports-gueye-homophobia-173205499.html |access-date=17 May 2022 |website=[[Yahoo! Sports]] |archive-date=17 May 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220517184952/https://sports.yahoo.com/senegals-president-supports-gueye-homophobia-173205499.html |url-status=dead }}</ref> Gueye's Senegal teammates [[Cheikhou Kouyaté]], [[Ismaïla Sarr]], and [[Nampalys Mendy]], who all play in England, also praised him for his actions.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Woosnam |first=Matt |date=18 May 2022 |title=Patrick Vieira to speak to Cheikhou Kouyate over post in support of Idrissa Gueye |url=https://theathletic.com/news/idrissa-gueye-kouyate-vieira/H7LeoTCvCnTY/ |access-date=19 May 2022 |website=The Athletic}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Cole |first1=Jackson |title=Leicester midfielder Nampalys Mendy 'didn't intend to cause offence' with social media post appearing to show support for Paris Saint-Germain man Idrissa Gueye|url=https://talksport.com/football/1112627/leicester-nampalys-mendy-statement-social-media-post-idrissa-gueye/ |access-date=20 May 2022 |publisher=Talksport |date=18 May 2022}}</ref> The hashtag #WeAreAllIdrissa, in support of Gueye, started trending over social media.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Frostick |first=Nancy |date=19 May 2022 |title=Idrissa Gueye and the LGBT problem football needs to solve |url=https://theathletic.com/3321894/2022/05/20/idrissa-gueye-and-the-lgbt-problem-football-needs-to-solve/ |access-date=20 May 2022 |website=[[The Athletic]]}}</ref>
