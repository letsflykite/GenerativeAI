'''Jhegson Sebastián Méndez Carabalí''' (born 26 April 1997) is an Ecuadorian professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a midfielder for [[Campeonato Brasileiro Série A]] club [[São Paulo FC|São Paulo]] and the [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador national team]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://uk.soccerway.com/players/jhegson-sebastian-mendez-carabali/290648/|title=Ecuador – J. Méndez – Profile with news, career statistics and history – Soccerway|website=uk.soccerway.com}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Independiente DV===
Méndez joined the [[C.S.D. Independiente del Valle|Independiente del Valle]] academy in 2011. By 2014 he had been promoted to the club's B team and made his first-team debut on 7 March 2015 in a victory over [[L.D.U. Loja]] in the [[2015 Campeonato Ecuatoriano de Fútbol Serie A|Serie A Primera Etapa]]. After two substitute appearances, Méndez was loaned to [[Cultural y Deportiva Leonesa|Cultural Leonesa]] in Spain, making one substitute appearance for the club on 29 November 2015. Upon his return to the club, Méndez had to wait until gameweek 15 of the [[2016 Campeonato Ecuatoriano de Fútbol Serie A|2016 season]] to play his next game, appearing as a substitute in a defeat to [[Fuerza Amarilla S.C.|Fuerza Amarilla]] on 13 May 2016. He followed that up by playing the full 90 minutes in six of the final ten games of the Serie A Primera Etapa and a further 12 appearances in the Segunda Etapa as he cemented himself as a first-team regular.

===Orlando City===
On 28 December 2018, Méndez signed to [[Major League Soccer]] team [[Orlando City SC|Orlando City]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.orlandocitysc.com/post/2018/12/28/orlando-city-sc-signs-ecuadorian-midfielder-jhegson-sebasti-n-m-ndez|title=Orlando City SC Signs Ecuadorian Midfielder Jhegson Sebastián Méndez &#124; Orlando City Soccer Club}}</ref> He made his debut in the season opener at home to [[New York City FC]] on 2 March 2019. In July 2019, Méndez was part of the Orlando City team alongside [[Nani (footballer)|Nani]] and [[Chris Mueller (soccer)|Chris Mueller]] that won the [[2019 MLS All-Star Game#Pre-match|2019 MLS All-Star Skills Challenge]], beating teams from the [[2019 MLS All-Star Game|MLS All-Stars]] and [[Atlético Madrid]] to win the $25,000 prize for charity.<ref>{{cite web |title=Nani gives Orlando City the win at the MLS Skills Challenge |url=https://en.as.com/en/2019/07/31/football/1564598732_614915.html |website=AS.com |language=en |date=31 July 2019}}</ref>

On 7 November 2021, Méndez scored his first goal for the club on his 65th appearance. The goal, a spectacular knuckling 20-yard strike, was the opener in an eventual 2–0 win away at [[CF Montréal]]. It was the final match of the regular season in which Orlando entered knowing a draw would guarantee qualification to the [[2021 MLS Cup Playoffs]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Man of the Match Spotlight: Sebas Mendez |url=https://www.orlandocitysc.com/news/the-heineken-man-of-the-match-spotlight-sebas-mendez |website=www.orlandocitysc.com}}</ref>

===Los Angeles FC===
On 19 July 2022, Méndez was acquired by [[Los Angeles FC]] via trade in exchange for $300,000 of General Allocation Money (GAM). Orlando could receive an additional $450,000 in GAM if certain contract conditions are met and also retained an undisclosed percentage of any future transfer fee.<ref>{{cite web |title=LAFC Acquires Midfielder Sebastian Méndez |url=https://www.lafc.com/news/lafc-acquires-midfielder-sebastian-mendez |website=LAFC}}</ref>

With LAFC, he won both the [[Supporters' Shield]], after a 3-0 win over [[Austin FC]],<ref>{{cite news |date=October 30, 2022 |title=LAFC advances to MLS Cup final with 3-0 win over Austin FC |newspaper=[[The Washington Post]] |agency=Associated Press |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/sports/soccer/lafc-advances-to-mls-cup-final-with-3-0-win-over-austin-fc/2022/10/30/f70a4a1c-589b-11ed-bc40-b5a130f95ee7_story.html |accessdate=November 5, 2022}}</ref> and the [[MLS Cup 2022|MLS Cup Final]], following a penalty shoot-out win over [[Philadelphia Union]].<ref name="Athletic-Recap">{{cite news |last=Stejskal |first=Sam |date=November 5, 2022 |title=LAFC finds Hollywood ending, beats Philadelphia on penalty kicks for first MLS Cup title |work=The Athletic |url=https://theathletic.com/3766090/2022/11/05/lafc-philadelphia-mls-cup-final/ |url-access=subscription |accessdate=November 5, 2022}}</ref> At the end of the 2022 season, he was released by the club.<ref>{{cite web |date=November 15, 2022 |title=LAFC Announces Roster Moves For 2023 Season |url=https://www.lafc.com/news/lafc-announces-roster-moves-for-2023-season |access-date=November 22, 2022 |publisher=Los Angeles FC}}</ref>

===São Paulo===
On 9 January 2023, Méndez joined Brazilian club [[São Paulo FC|São Paulo]] on a [[Free transfer (association football)|free transfer]], signing a three-year contract.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-01-09 |title=São Paulo contrata equatoriano Méndez |url=http://www.saopaulofc.net/noticias/noticias/futebol/2023/1/9/sao-paulo-contrata-equatoriano-mendez |access-date=2023-01-09 |website=São Paulo FC |language=pt-BR}}</ref>
==International career==
Méndez was a member of Ecuador’s [[2017 South American U-20 Championship]] squad which reached the final of the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.eluniverso.com/deportes/2017/01/04/nota/5978965/mini-tri-definida-sudamericano?device=mobile|title=Selección Sub-20 de Ecuador definida para Sudamericano|date=4 January 2017|website=eluniverso.com}}</ref>

In October 2018, Méndez was called up to the senior [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador national team]] by manager [[Hernán Darío Gómez]] for the first time.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.eltiempo.com.ec/noticias/deportes/3/carlos-cuero-lesion-muscular|title=Carlos Cuero sufre lesión muscular|first=EL|last=TIEMPO|date=6 October 2018}}</ref> He made his debut on 11 September 2018, playing 73 minutes in a 2–0 [[Exhibition game#Association football|friendly]] victory over [[Guatemala national football team|Guatemala]] at [[SeatGeek Stadium|Toyota Park]], [[Bridgeview, Illinois]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Ecuador vs. Guatemala – Football Match Summary – September 11, 2018 – ESPN |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/match?gameId=526025 |website=ESPN.com |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=PERU21 |first1=NOTICIAS |title=Deportes: Ecuador ganó 2–0 a Guatemala por amistoso internacional en Chicago {{!}} NOTICIAS PERU21 PERÚ |url=https://peru21.pe/deportes/ecuador-vs-guatemala-vivo-directo-amistoso-fifa-via-directv-sports-nndc-427178-noticia/ |website=Peru21 |language=es |date=12 September 2018}}</ref> In May 2019, he was called into the squad for the [[2019 Copa América]], his first senior international tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ecuafutbol.org/web/noticia.php?idn=53303&idc=11#.XOPJQtThArh|title=LOS 23 CONVOCADOS DE ECUADOR PARA COPA AMÉRICA DE BRASIL 2019|publisher=FEF|date=20 May 2019|language=es}}{{Dead link|date=June 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> After sitting out the first group game, Méndez started against [[Chile national football team|Chile]] and drew a foul from goalkeeper [[Gabriel Arias (footballer)|Gabriel Arias]] to win a penalty, Ecuador's only goal of a 2–1 defeat.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ecuador vs. Chile – Football Match Report – June 21, 2019 – ESPN |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/report?gameId=535889 |website=ESPN.com |language=en}}</ref> He started the following game, a 1–1 draw with [[Japan national football team|Japan]]. Ecuador were eliminated after finishing bottom of [[2019 Copa América Group C|Group C]] with one point.

In June 2021, Mendez was named to the final roster for the [[2021 Copa América]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Copa America 2021: Ecuador's squad for the competition |url=https://bolavip.com/en/soccer/copa-america-2021-ecuador-s-squad-for-the-competition-20210610-0017.html |website=Bolavip US}}</ref>
