'''Ángel Martín Correa Martínez''' ({{IPA-es|ˈaŋxel koˈrea}}; born 9 March 1995) is an Argentine professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]] and [[Midfielder#Winger|winger]] for [[La Liga]] club [[Atlético Madrid]] and the [[Argentina national football team|Argentina national team]].

Correa began his professional career at the age of 18 with Argentine club [[San Lorenzo de Almagro|San Lorenzo]], winning the [[2013–14 Argentine Primera División season#Torneo Inicial|Torneo Inicial]] in 2013 and the [[Copa Libertadores]] in [[2014 Copa Libertadores|2014]]. In 2015, he signed with Atlético Madrid of [[La Liga]].

Correa [[Captain (association football)|captained]] the [[Argentina national under-20 football team|Argentina under-20]] team to victory at the [[2015 South American U-20 Championship]], where he was named the player of the tournament. Since 2015, he has been a regular selection for the Argentina senior squad, and represented the side at the [[2021 Copa América]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], with whom he won both tournaments.

==Club career==
===San Lorenzo===
Born in [[Rosario, Santa Fe|Rosario]], [[Santa Fe Province]], Correa joined [[San Lorenzo de Almagro|San Lorenzo]]'s youth setup in 2007, aged 12, after a trial period.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.colocolo.cl/araya-y-correa-dos-joyas-del-esperanza-alba/|title=Araya y Correa, dos joyas del Esperanza Alba|trans-title=Araya and Correa, two pearls of ''Esperanza Alba''|publisher=Colo-Colo's official profile|language=es|date=31 January 2013|accessdate=28 May 2014}}</ref> In the summer of 2012, he was due to join Portuguese club [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]] on a free transfer, but the deal later collapsed.

Correa signed a four-year professional deal with the ''Ciclón'' on 23 September 2012,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mundoazulgrana.com.ar/san-lorenzo/noticias/%C3%81ngel_correa_firmo_un_contrato_por_cuatro_temporadas-11498.html|title=Ángel Correa firmó un contrato por cuatro temporadas|trans-title=Ángel Correa signed a four-year contract|publisher=Mundo Azulgrana|language=es|date=23 September 2012|accessdate=28 May 2014}}</ref> and was promoted to the first-team in January 2013. On 31 March he played his first match as a professional, coming on as a second-half [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] in a 0–1 loss at [[Newell's Old Boys]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tycsports.com/notas/78146-newells-llego-la-cima-el-gasometro|title=Newell's llegó a la cima en el Gasómetro|trans-title=Newells' reaches the top at ''Gasómetro''|publisher=TyC Sports|language=es|date=31 March 2013|accessdate=28 May 2014|archive-date=29 May 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140529052109/http://www.tycsports.com/notas/78146-newells-llego-la-cima-el-gasometro|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On 11 May, Correa scored his first professional goal, netting the last of a 3–0 home success against [[Boca Juniors]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mundod.lavoz.com.ar/futbol/torneo-final-2013-san-lorenzo-goleo-boca-con-una-gran-actuacion-de-buffarini|title=Torneo Final 2013: San Lorenzo goleó a Boca con una gran actuación de Buffarini|trans-title=''Torneo Final'' 2013: San Lorenzo thrashed Boca with a great performance from Buffarini|publisher=Mundo D|language=es|date=11 May 2013|accessdate=28 May 2014}}</ref> He finished [[2012–13 Argentine Primera División season|the campaign]] with 13 appearances (eight starts, 747 minutes of action), scoring four goals.

===Atlético Madrid===
[[File:Loko-AM2018 tren (7).jpg|thumb|180px|Correa with [[Atlético Madrid]] in 2018]]
On 27 May 2014, after scoring six goals in [[2013–14 Argentine Primera División season|2013–14]], Correa had agreed a deal with [[La Liga]] holders [[Atlético Madrid]] for a reported fee of €7.5 million.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marca.com/2014/05/27/futbol/equipos/atletico/1401226021.html|title=Ángel Correa ya es del Atleti|trans-title=Ángel Correa is already an ''Atleti'' player|publisher=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|language=es|date=27 May 2014|access-date=28 May 2014}}</ref> He signed a five-year contract with the [[Madrid]] side, joining the club after the [[2014 Copa Libertadores]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.clubatleticodemadrid.com/noticias/atleti-y-san-lorenzo-alcanzan-un-acuerdo-por-angel-correa|title=Atleti y San Lorenzo alcanzan un acuerdo por Ángel Correa|trans-title=''Atleti'' and San Lorenzo reach agreement for Ángel Correa|publisher=Atlético Madrid's official website|language=es|date=27 May 2014|access-date=28 May 2014}}</ref>

In June 2014, however, Correa was sidelined for six months due to a heart tumour. After undergoing surgery on 18 June in New York, he started a light training in August,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espndeportes.com/news/story/_/id/2166175/angel-correa-trabajo-por-primera-vez-luego-de-su-operacion-del-corazon|title=Ángel Correa trabajó por primera vez luego de su operación del corazón|trans-title=Ángel Correa trained for the first time shortly after his heart surgery|publisher=[[ESPN Deportes]]|language=es|date=28 August 2014|access-date=13 December 2014}}</ref> and officially joined the club on 13 December.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/30464385|title=Atletico Madrid sign San Lorenzo's Angel Correa after heart surgery|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|date=13 December 2014|access-date=13 December 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.clubatleticodemadrid.com/noticias/angel-correa-ya-es-rojiblanco|title=Ángel Correa ya es rojiblanco|trans-title=Ángel Correa already is a ''rojiblanco''|publisher=Atlético Madrid's official website|language=es|date=13 December 2014|access-date=13 December 2014}}</ref>

On 22 August 2015, Correa finally made his ''Atleti'' debut, replacing [[Óliver Torres]] in a 1–0 home league match win against [[UD Las Palmas|Las Palmas]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marca.com/eventos/marcador/futbol/2015_16/la-liga/jornada_1/atm_pal/|title=Al Atlético le falta|trans-title=Atlético lacks something|publisher=Marca|language=es|date=22 August 2015|access-date=22 August 2015}}</ref> He scored his first goal for the club on 19 September, netting the first in a 2–0 away win against [[SD Eibar|Eibar]]; he also [[Assist (football)|assisted]] [[Fernando Torres]] in the second goal.

On 6 November 2019, Correa marked his 200th appearance for Atlético, starting in a 1–2 away loss against [[Bayer 04 Leverkusen|Bayer Leverkusen]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://en.atleticodemadrid.com/noticias/correa-reaches-200-games-for-atleti|title=Correa reaches 200 games for Atleti|date=6 November 2019|website=Atlético de Madrid|access-date=9 November 2019}}</ref> On 10 August, Atlético announced that Correa and teammate [[Šime Vrsaljko]] were both positive for [[Coronavirus disease 2019|COVID-19]], ruling them out of the team's quarter-final fixture against [[RB Leipzig]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://en.atleticodemadrid.com/noticias/pcr-tests-for-remaining-first-team-players-coaches-come-back-negative|title=PCR tests for remaining first team players, coaches come back negative|date=10 August 2020|website=Atlético de Madrid|access-date=13 August 2020}}</ref>

On 22 May 2021, Correa scored in Atlético's 2–1 win over [[Real Valladolid]] on the final day of the [[2020–21 La Liga]] season to win the league title for the first time since 2014.<ref name=LaLigaW />

Correa started his [[2021–22 La Liga|2021–22 La Liga season]] on the right foot, as he scored in consecutive matches against [[RC Celta de Vigo|Celta Vigo]] and [[Elche CF|Elche]] to hand Atlético back-to-back wins.<ref>{{cite web|title=Angel Correa double earns defending Liga champions Atletico Madrid narrow win at Celta Vigo|url=https://www.eurosport.com/geoblocking.shtml|url-status=live|date=15 August 2021|access-date=30 August 2021|website=Eurosport}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Atletico Madrid striker Angel Correa hits winner against Elche - Tribal Football|url=http://www.tribalfootball.com/articles/atletico-madrid-striker-angel-correa-hits-winner-against-elche-4383034|date=23 August 2021|access-date=30 August 2021|website=Tribal Football}}</ref>

==International career==
===South American U-20 Championship selection===
On 6 January 2015, Humberto Grondona, Under-20 Argentina Selection Technical Director, submitted a list of 32 Argentine players including Correa, invited to train to make the final roster for the South American Under-20s Championship.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.tycsports.com/noticias/La-Sub-20-tiene-su-preseleccion-para-el-Sudamericano-de-Uruguay-20141211-0026.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141213012306/http://www.tycsports.com/noticias/La-Sub-20-tiene-su-preseleccion-para-el-Sudamericano-de-Uruguay-20141211-0026.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=13 December 2014|title=La Sub 20 tiene su preselección para el Sudamericano de Uruguay {{!}} Noticias {{!}} TyCSports.com|date=13 December 2014|access-date=16 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mundodeportivo.com/20141210/atletico-madrid/atletico-ok-de-argentina-sub-20-a-angel-correa_54421266946.html|title=Atlético: Ok de Argentina Sub-20 a Ángel Correa|date=10 December 2014|website=Mundo Deportivo|language=es|access-date=16 April 2020}}</ref> After four days of training Grondona announced the final 23-man roster that featured Correa as the captain of the Under-20 Argentine side.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mdzol.com/nota/581103-angel-correa-sera-el-capitan-del-sub-20/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150112211500/http://www.mdzol.com/nota/581103-angel-correa-sera-el-capitan-del-sub-20/|url-status=dead|archive-date=12 January 2015|title=Ángel Correa será el capitán del Sub 20 - MDZ Online|date=12 January 2015|access-date=16 April 2020}}</ref>

On 14 January, Correa made his debut for Argentina U20 national football team against Ecuador in the first game of the group stage. He scored a goal and twice assisted to Giovanni Simeone for a 5–2 win.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Correa, el pibe que superó un momento delicado y volvió a lo grande|url=https://www.clarin.com/deportes/futbol-internacional/angel-correa_0_SkbTH85D7e.html|last=Clarín.com|website=www.clarin.com|date=15 January 2015 |language=es|access-date=30 April 2020}}</ref>

On 18 January, Correa scored a goal during a 6–2 win against Peru, helping Argentina advance out of the group stage.<ref>{{Cite web|title=6-2. Simeone y Correa siguen imponiendo su ley y Perú ayudó a su mala suerte – Fútbol – Noticias, última hora, vídeos y fotos de Fútbol en lainformacion.com|url=http://noticias.lainformacion.com/deporte/futbol/6-2-simeone-y-correa-siguen-imponiendo-su-ley-y-peru-ayudo-a-su-mala-suerte_hrJb6nqxvt1lNBdwi9yBD7/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150120164758/http://noticias.lainformacion.com/deporte/futbol/6-2-simeone-y-correa-siguen-imponiendo-su-ley-y-peru-ayudo-a-su-mala-suerte_hrJb6nqxvt1lNBdwi9yBD7/|url-status=dead|archive-date=20 January 2015|date=20 January 2015|access-date=30 April 2020}}</ref>

On 26 January, Argentina once again facing Peru in the playoff elimination stage of the tournament, Correa scored again during this matchup, helping Argentina advance to the final with a 2–0 win.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Argentina festejó por Simeone y la magia de Correa|url=https://www.clarin.com/deportes/futbol-internacional/peru-arranque-hexagonal-final_0_S1Lesr9Pmx.html|last=Clarín.com|website=www.clarin.com|date=26 January 2015 |language=es|access-date=30 April 2020}}</ref>

On 7 February, Argentina played against tournament host Uruguay in the U-20 South America Cup final. Throughout the game was a stalemate at 1–1, until Correa scored at the 81st minute to put Argentina up for 2–1 win for the U-20 South America Championship,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Argentina, un campeón con ángel y corazón|url=https://www.clarin.com/a_correr/Argentina-campeon-angel-corazon_0_SySlAEqv7x.html|last=Clarín.com|website=www.clarin.com|date=8 February 2015 |language=es|access-date=30 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Selección Sub 20: ¡Argentina campeón!|url=http://www.diaadia.com.ar/deportes/seleccion-sub-20-argentina-campeon|last=Día|first=DaD-Día a|website=Día a Día|language=es|access-date=30 April 2020}}</ref> and Correa earning honors for the Best Player of the Tournament.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Los premios del Sudamericano|url=https://www.ovaciondigital.com.uy/futbol/premios-sudamericano.html|last=ElPais|website=Diario EL PAIS Uruguay|date=8 February 2015 |language=spanish|access-date=30 April 2020}}</ref>

===U-20 World Cup selection===
On 13 May 2015, Humberto Grondona confirmed the list of 21 football players including Correa to represent Argentina in the U-20 World Cup hosted by New Zealand. On 25 May, in the team's second last friendly before the U-20 World Cup competition, Correa scored a header from a corner set for a 3–1 loss against Tahiti.

On 30 May, during Argentina's opening match for the U-20 World Cup group stage against Panama, Correa scored both goals for Argentina for a 2–2 draw.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Con Ángel Correa inspirado, Argentina empató ante Panamá|url=https://ar.blastingnews.com/deportes/2015/05/con-angel-correa-inspirado-argentina-empato-ante-panama-00418149.html|date=30 May 2015|website=Blasting News|language=es|access-date=1 May 2020}}</ref> Argentina only earned two points in the group stage meaning they failed to advance into the knockout round.

===Senior team===
[[File:Argentina x Honduras - Futebol masculino - Olimpíadas Rio 2016 (28611940950).jpg|thumb|Correa playing for Argentina in 2016]]
On 4 September 2015, Correa made his [[Argentina national football team|full squad]] debut, coming on as a late [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] for [[Ezequiel Lavezzi]] in a [[Exhibition match|friendly]] match against [[Bolivia national football team|Bolivia]], which ended in 7-0 victory. He scored the last goal of the match.

Correa was a member of the [[2021 Copa América]] winning side, appearing twice in group stage matches against Bolivia and Paraguay.

On 17 November 2022, he received a late call up to the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] squad, as an emergency replacement for the injured [[Nicolás González (footballer, born 1998)|Nicolás González]].<ref>{{Cite tweet|user=Argentina|number=1593315253227487233|title=#SelecciónMayor Tras el entrenamiento de hoy, el futbolista Nicolás González sufrió una lesión muscular y quedará desafectado de la nómina mundialista. En su reemplazo, el CT de @Argentina, convoca a Ángel Correa.|date=17 November 2022}}</ref> 

==Style of play==
Correa has been compared to compatriot [[Sergio Agüero]], due to his similar height. However, his playing style is more similar to [[Carlos Tevez]], in a more attacking positional role.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/11096/9235570/sky-sports-scout-in-association-with-wyscout-reports-on-argentine-starlet-angel-correa|title=Sky Sports Scout, in association with Wyscout, reports on Argentine starlet Angel Correa|publisher=[[Sky Sports]]|date=21 May 2014|accessdate=28 May 2014}}</ref> He holds good skill, pace and low centre of gravity, aside from a good technique and first touch. <ref>{{cite web|url=http://outsideoftheboot.com/2014/01/26/angel-correa-scout-report/|title=Ángel Correa: Scout Report {{!}} Argentina's emerging Young Talent|publisher=Outside the Boot|date=26 January 2014|accessdate=28 May 2014|archive-date=11 November 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201111203408/http://outsideoftheboot.com/2014/01/26/angel-correa-scout-report/|url-status=dead}}</ref>
