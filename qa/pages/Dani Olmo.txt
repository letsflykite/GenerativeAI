'''Daniel Olmo Carvajal''' (born 7 May 1998) is a Spanish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays for [[Bundesliga]] club [[RB Leipzig]] and the [[Spain national football team|Spain national team]]. He can play as either an [[attacking midfielder]] or a [[Winger (association football)|winger]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Onyekwe|first=Samuel|title=Five Spain stars to keep an eye on as European Under-21 Championship continues|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/football/news/five-spain-stars-euro-under-16611149|access-date=2 July 2019|work=Daily Mirror|date=22 June 2019}}</ref>

After a period at [[FC Barcelona]], he made his professional debut for [[GNK Dinamo Zagreb|Dinamo Zagreb]] in Croatia in 2015. He made 124 total appearances for the club, scoring 34 goals and winning the [[Croatian Football League|league]] five times and the [[Croatian Football Cup|cup]] three times. In 2020, he joined [[RB Leipzig]], where he won the [[DFB-Pokal]] in [[2022 DFB-Pokal Final|2022]].

Olmo won the [[2019 UEFA European Under-21 Championship|2019 UEFA European Championship]] with the [[Spain national under-21 football team|Spain under-21 team]], and a silver medal at the [[Football at the 2020 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|2020 Olympic tournament]]. He made his senior international debut in 2019, was part of the team that reached the semi-finals of [[UEFA Euro 2020]], and won the [[2022–23 UEFA Nations League|UEFA Nations League 2023]].

==Club career==
===Early career===
Born in [[Terrassa]], [[Province of Barcelona|Barcelona]], [[Catalonia]], Olmo arrived in [[FC Barcelona]]'s youth academy aged nine, from neighbours [[RCD Espanyol]].<ref name="Profile"/><ref>{{cite web |last1=Pandey |first1=Kaustubh |title=Lowdown on Manchester United target Dani Olmo: Scout Report |url=https://www.calciomercato.com/en/news/lowdown-on-manchester-united-target-dani-olmo-scout-report-54073 |publisher=Calcio Mercato |access-date=18 August 2019 |date=3 July 2019}}</ref>

In a surprise move, Olmo joined [[GNK Dinamo Zagreb|Dinamo Zagreb]] on 31 July 2014, aged 16.<ref>{{cite news |title=Dinamo Zagreb Sign Talented Spain & Barcelona Youth Captain |url=https://www.croatiaweek.com/dinamo-zagreb-sign-talented-spain-barcelona-youth-captain/ |access-date=18 November 2019 |publisher=Croatia Week |date=31 July 2014}}</ref> He made his first team debut against [[NK Lokomotiva Zagreb|Lokomotiva Zagreb]] on 7 February 2015, as a 76th-minute substitute for [[Paulo Machado]] in a 2–1 home win.<ref>[http://gnkdinamo.hr/hr/novosti/vijesti/modra-buducnost-sjajni-mladici-koji-nezaustavljivo-dolaze.html MODRA BUDUĆNOST: SJAJNI MLADIĆI KOJI NEZAUSTAVLJIVO DOLAZE] at gnkdinamo.hr (in Croatian), 12 February 2015</ref> On 22 September, he scored his first goal in a 7–1 win at Oštrc Zlatar in the [[2015–16 Croatian Football Cup|first round]] of the [[Croatian Football Cup|cup]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Ramírez |first1=Álvaro |title=Darder se estrena en Francia y Soriano ya manda en Austria |url=https://www.eldesmarque.com/futbol/84188-darder-se-estrena-en-francia-y-soriano-ya-manda-en-austria?amp=1 |access-date=7 October 2022 |publisher=El Desmarque |date=25 October 2015 |language=Spanish}}</ref>

== International career ==
=== Youth teams ===
Olmo was part of the [[2015 UEFA European Under-17 Championship squads#Spain|Spanish squad]] at the [[2015 UEFA European Under-17 Championship|2015 European Under-17 Championship]] in [[Bulgaria]]; he netted in the [[penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shoot-out]] as they were eliminated by [[Germany national under-17 football team|Germany]] in the [[2015 UEFA European Under-17 Championship#Quarter-finals|quarter-finals]], but then had his attempt saved by [[Will Huffer]] as [[Spain national under-17 football team|Spain]] lost to [[England national under-17 football team|England]] by the same means in a [[2015 UEFA European Under-17 Championship#FIFA U-17 World Cup play-offs|play-off]] for [[2015 FIFA U-17 World Cup|that year's World Cup for the category]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.thefa.com/news/2015/may/19/england-v-spain-u17-world-cup-play-off-match-19052015 | title=England see off Spain from the spot to seal World Cup berth }}</ref> Towards the end of 2017, [[GNK Dinamo Zagreb|Dinamo Zagreb]] director Tomislav Svetina said that the club was doing all it could to get the teenager [[Croatian nationality law|Croatian citizenship]]. ==Personal life==
Olmo's father, [[Miquel Olmo|Miquel]], is a retired footballer. As a [[Forward (association football)|forward]], he played professionally for lower-league teams.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.vozpopuli.com/deportes/Miquel_Olmo-Dani_Olmo-Futbol_Club_Barcelona-La_Masia_0_720827969.html|title=Al Barça se le fugan los canteranos: Dani Olmo rompe su contrato y se marcha al Dinamo de Zagreb|publisher=Voz Populy|language=es|date=1 August 2014|access-date=24 February 2017}}</ref> Dani's older brother Carlos is also a footballer and plays as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]]; he spent several years in Croatia, for Dinamo's reserves and also for [[NK Lokomotiva Zagreb|Lokomotiva Zagreb]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Manchón |first1=Martín |title=Carlos, el Olmo que sigue en Zagreb |url=https://as.com/futbol/2020/03/25/migrantes_del_balon/1585167878_636575.html |access-date=7 October 2022 |work=Diario AS |date=25 March 2020 |language=Spanish}}</ref> Olmo speaks [[Croatian language|Croatian]] fluently.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://direktno.hr/sport/bravo-ljudino-dani-olmo-sjetio-se-vukovara-nije-cudo-da-je-miljenik-navijaca-139571|title=BRAVO, LJUDINO!
