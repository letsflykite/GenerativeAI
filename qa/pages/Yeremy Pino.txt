'''Yeremy Jesús Pino Santos''' (born 20 October 2002) is a Spanish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays mainly as a [[Midfielder#Winger|right winger]] or [[right midfielder]] for [[La Liga]] club [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]] and the [[Spain national football team|Spain national team]].

==Club career==
Yeremy was born in [[Las Palmas]], [[Canary Islands]], and joined [[UD Las Palmas]]' youth setup in 2014, after representing [[AD Huracán]] and Barrio Atlántico CF.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rodagonsport.com/index.php/noticias/311-yeremi-la-copa-rodagon-es-un-torneo-muy-especial-para-mi|title=Yéremi: "La Copa Rodagon es un torneo muy especial para mí"|trans-title=Yéremi: "The Rodagon Cup is a very special tournament for me"|publisher=Rodagon Sport|language=es|date=20 February 2019|access-date=22 November 2020}}</ref> On 22 June 2017, after refusing an offer from [[FC Barcelona]], he signed for [[Villarreal CF]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.laprovincia.es/ud-las-palmas/2017/06/22/yeremi-rechaza-oferta-barca-ficha-9683606.html|title=Yéremi rechaza la oferta del Barça y ficha en el Villarreal|trans-title=Yéremi refuses the offer of ''Barça'' and signs for Villarreal|first=Mario|last=García de laSanta|publisher=La Provincia|language=es|date=22 June 2017}}</ref>

Yeremy made his senior debut with [[Villarreal CF C|the C-team]] on 24 August 2019, coming on as a second-half [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] for [[Fernando Niño (footballer, born 2000)|Fer Niño]] and scoring the third of a 3–0 [[Tercera División]] away win against [[CF Recambios Colón]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://golsmedia.com/comunidad-valenciana/futbol/recambios-colon-cd/2019/08/24/villarreal-c-demostro-sus-hechuras-perdiguer/|title=El Villarreal CF C demostró sus hechuras y ganó bien en El Perdiguer|trans-title=Villarreal C show their size and win comfortably at the El Perdiguer|publisher=Golsmedia|language=es|date=24 August 2019|access-date=22 November 2020}}</ref> After finishing [[2019–20 Tercera División|his first senior season]] with three goals in 20 appearances, he was promoted to [[Villarreal CF B|the reserves]] in [[Segunda División B]].

After spending the pre-season with the first team under [[Unai Emery]], Pino made his professional debut on 22 October 2020, replacing [[Francis Coquelin]] in a 5–3 [[2020–21 UEFA Europa League|UEFA Europa League]] win over [[Sivasspor]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/match/2029721/|title=Villarreal 5–3 Sivasspor|publisher=UEFA.com|date=22 October 2020|access-date=22 November 2020}}</ref> He made his [[La Liga]] debut three days later, again from the bench in a 0–0 draw at [[Cádiz CF]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.marca.com/futbol/primera-division/cadiz-vs-villarreal/cronica/2020/10/25/5f957ecae2704e56748b457f.html|title=La única que gana es la lógica|trans-title=The only one who wins is logic|publisher=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|language=es|date=25 October 2020|access-date=22 November 2020}}</ref>

Yeremy scored his first professional goal on 29 October 2020, netting the equalizer in a 3–1 away success over [[Qarabağ FK]] also in the European competition.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/match/2029733--qarabag-vs-villarreal/|title=Qarabağ 1–3 Villarreal|publisher=UEFA.com|date=29 October 2020|access-date=22 November 2020}}</ref> On 12 November, he renewed his contract with the club until 2024.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sport.es/es/noticias/villarreal/villarreal-renueva-blinda-baena-yeremi-pino-jorgensen-fer-nino-8201227|title=El Villarreal renueva y blinda a Baena, Yeremi Pino, Jorgensen y Fer Niño|trans-title=Villarreal renew and shield Baena, Yeremi Pino, Jorgensen and Fer Niño|publisher=[[Sport (Spanish newspaper)|Sport]]|language=es|date=12 November 2020|access-date=22 November 2020}}</ref>

On 26 May 2021, he played in the [[2021 UEFA Europa League Final]], to become the youngest Spanish player to start a major European final, aged 18 years and 218 days, breaking the previous record of [[Iker Casillas]] in the [[2000 UEFA Champions League Final]], aged 19 years and 4 days.<ref name="auto1">{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/match/2029909--villarreal-vs-man-united/postmatch/report/ |title=Villarreal 1-1 Manchester United (aet, 11-10 pens): Spanish side win Europa League in marathon shoot-out |website=UEFA |date=26 May 2021 }}</ref> He also became the youngest player to win the competition, eclipsing the record set by [[Robin van Persie]] in the [[2002 UEFA Cup Final]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.goal.com/en-my/news/yeremy-pino-villarreal-wonderkid-rejected-barcelona/d5ry69ihu4rb155hxj2p0ne1c |title=Yeremy Pino: Villarreal wonderkid who rejected Barcelona |website=Goal.com |date=26 May 2021 }}</ref>

Yeremy scored four times on 27 February 2022 as Villarreal won 5–1 at home to [[RCD Espanyol]], having only two goals for the season to his name before the game. He also became the youngest player to score a hat-trick in the first half of a La Liga match.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Martin |first1=Richard |title=Ronaldo, Messi, Suarez, Griezmann and... Yeremy Pino |url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/us/news/ronaldo-messi-suarez-griezmann-and-yeremy-pino |access-date=27 February 2022 |work=[[FourFourTwo]] |date=27 February 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
Yeremy represented Spain at [[Spain national under-17 football team|under-17]] and [[Spain national under-18 football team|under-18]] levels, also acting as [[Captain (association football)|team captain]] for the latter side.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://villarrealcf.es/en/news/item/26424-yeremi-pino-shines-with-spain-u18s|title=Yeremi Pino shines with Spain U18s|first=Fran|last=Segovia|publisher=Villarreal CF|date=12 December 2019}}</ref> Due to the isolation of some national team players following the positive [[COVID-19]] test of [[Sergio Busquets]], Spain's under-21 squad were called up for the international friendly against [[Lithuania national football team|Lithuania]] on 8 June 2021.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://sefutbol.com/oficial-lista-convocados-encuentro-lituania |title=Oficial {{!}} Lista de convocados para el encuentro ante Lituania |trans-title=Official {{!}} Squad list for the match against Lithuania |work=[[Royal Spanish Football Federation]] |date=6 June 2021 |access-date=7 June 2021 |language=es}}</ref> 

Yeremy made his debut for the [[Spain national football team|senior squad]] on 6 October 2021 in the [[2021 UEFA Nations League Finals|Nations League semi-final]] against [[Italy national football team|Italy]], replacing the injured [[Ferran Torres]] after 49 minutes in a 2–1 victory;<ref>{{cite news |title=Debuta el grancanario Yéremi [''sic''] Pino con la selección frente a Italia|trans-title=Gran Canaria native Yeremi Pino deuts with the national team against Italy |url=https://www.canarias7.es/deportes/futbol/debuta-grancanario-yeremi-20211006222021-nt.html |access-date=5 April 2022 |publisher=Canarias7 |date=6 October 2021 |language=Spanish}}</ref> four days later he came off the bench in a defeat by the same score in the final against [[France national football team|France]].<ref>{{cite news |title=España juega pero Francia impone sus golpes y se lleva la Liga de Naciones|trans-title=Spain play but France dish out their blows and lift the Nations League |url=https://www.france24.com/es/minuto-a-minuto/20211010-espa%C3%B1a-juega-pero-francia-impone-sus-golpes-y-se-lleva-la-liga-de-naciones |access-date=5 April 2022 |publisher=[[France 24]] |date=10 October 2021 |language=Spanish}}</ref> The following 29 March, he made his first start in a [[Exhibition game|friendly]] against [[Iceland national football team|Iceland]] in [[A Coruña]], heading his first goal in a 5–0 win.<ref>{{cite news |title=Spain 5&ndash;0 Iceland |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/60921671 |access-date=5 April 2022 |publisher=[[BBC Sport]] |date=29 March 2022}}</ref>
