'''Dayne Tristan St. Clair''' (born May 9, 1997) is a Canadian professional [[association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Major League Soccer]] club [[Minnesota United FC|Minnesota United]] and the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada national team]].

Prior to playing for Minnesota United, St. Clair was a two-year starter for the [[Maryland Terrapins men's soccer]] program. With Maryland, he was the starting goalkeeper for their [[2018 Maryland Terrapins men's soccer team|national championship]] run, where he earned the [[NCAA Division I Men's Soccer Tournament Most Outstanding Player]] for the best defensive soccer player in the national tournament.

Prior to playing [[college soccer]] for the University of Maryland, St. Clair played youth soccer for [[Vaughan SC]] and helped the Azzuri win a [[League1 Ontario]] title. St. Clair also played high school soccer for [[Notre Dame Catholic Secondary School (Ajax)|Notre Dame Catholic Secondary School]]. St. Clair also appeared for the [[Canada men's national under-18 soccer team]].

==Early life==
Born to a [[Trinidadian]] father and [[Scottish Canadian]] mother,<ref>{{cite web | url=https://newsday.co.tt/2022/11/18/goalie-with-trinidad-and-tobago-roots-in-canada-world-cup-squad/#:~:text=GOALKEEPER%20Dayne%20St%20Clair%2C%2025,grew%20up%20in%20Sangre%20Grande | title=Goalie with Trinidad and Tobago roots in Canada World Cup squad - Trinidad and Tobago Newsday | date=November 18, 2022 }}</ref> he began playing soccer at age three with the Epiphany Church Soccer League, before joining North Scarborough SC at age 4.<ref>{{Canada Soccer player|id=3905}}</ref> He also played with [[Pickering FC|Pickering SC]], Ajax SC, and [[Vaughan Azzurri]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://canadasoccer.com/news/canada-soccer-announce-squad-for-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022/|title=Canada Soccer announces squad for FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022|date=November 12, 2022|work=[[Canadian Soccer Association]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com/sports/soccer/2020/09/29/canadian-keeper-dayne-st-clair-makes-most-of-mls-opportunity-with-minnesota.html|title=Canadian 'keeper Dayne St. Clair makes most of MLS opportunity with Minnesota|date=September 29, 2020|work=[[Toronto Star]]|first=Neil|last=Davidson}}</ref>

== College career ==
Entering the [[2015 NCAA Division I men's soccer season]], St. Clair was redshirted. During his redshirt freshman year in 2016, St. Clair served as a backup to Cody Niedermeier, making two college appearances for the program. Entering the [[2017 NCAA Division I men's soccer season]], St. Clair became the regular starter for Maryland, making his first college soccer start for Maryland on August 25, 2017, in a 4&ndash;2 win against [[Santa Clara Broncos men's soccer|Santa Clara]].<ref>{{cite web |title=2017 Men's Soccer Cumulative Statistics: Game-by-Game |url=https://umterps.com/cumestats.aspx?path=msoc&year=2017 |website=umterps.com |publisher=University of Maryland Athletics |access-date=January 12, 2019}}</ref> St. Clair finished his Maryland career making a total of 43 appearances for the program.

== Club career ==

=== Early career ===
St. Clair played for [[League1 Ontario]] club [[Vaughan Azzurri]] in 2014 and 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ontariosoccer.net/news_article/show/744891-success-stories-keep-coming-for-league1-ontario|title=Success Stories Keep Coming for League1 Ontario|date=February 11, 2015|work=[[Ontario Soccer Association]]}}</ref> During the [[2016 PDL season|2016]] and [[2017 PDL season]]s, St. Clair made fourteen appearances with [[K-W United FC]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Dayne St Clair - Game Log - Goalkeeper |url=https://www.kwunitedfc.com/roster_players/18286489?mirror_id=3232352&subseason=418168 |website=[[K-W United FC]] |access-date=January 12, 2019}}</ref> During the [[2018 PDL season]], St. Clair played with the [[New York Red Bulls]] [[New York Red Bulls U-23|under 23]] team.

=== Minnesota United ===
On January 4, 2019, ahead of the [[2019 MLS SuperDraft]], St. Clair signed a [[Generation Adidas]] contract with [[Major League Soccer]], forgoing his final season of eligibility.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Rodriguez |first1=Alicia |title=MLS announces 2019 Generation adidas class ahead of Combine |url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2019/01/04/mls-announces-2019-generation-adidas-class-ahead-combine |website=Major League Soccer |access-date=January 12, 2019 |date=January 4, 2019}}</ref> On January 11, 2019, St. Clair was drafted seventh overall in the 2019 MLS SuperDraft by [[Minnesota United FC]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Zgoda |first1=Jerry |title=Minnesota United takes Maryland teammates Dayne St. Clair, Chase Gasper with SuperDraft picks |url=http://m.startribune.com//minnesota-united-takes-maryland-goalkeeper-dayne-st-clair-with-its-first-superdraft-pick/504223962/ |website=[[Star Tribune]] |publisher=Star Tribune Media Company LLC |access-date=January 12, 2019 |date=January 12, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Greder |first1=Andy |title=Minnesota United picks goalkeeper Dayne St. Clair in MLS SuperDraft |url=https://www.twincities.com/2019/01/11/minnesota-united-picked-goalkeeper-dayne-st-clair/ |website=[[Twin Cities Pioneer Press]] |publisher=[[Digital First Media]] |access-date=January 12, 2019 |date=January 11, 2019}}</ref> St. Clair said of getting drafted, "I wanted to make a statement to the person I am, the player I am. This is my statement."<ref>{{cite web |title=Minnesota United takes Maryland teammates Dayne St. Clair, Chase Gasper with SuperDraft picks |url=http://www.startribune.com/minnesota-united-takes-maryland-goalkeeper-dayne-st-clair-with-its-first-superdraft-pick/504223962/ |website=Star Tribune |access-date=January 12, 2019 |date=January 11, 2019 |quote=I wanted to make a statement to the person I am, the player I am,” the 21-year-old St. Clair said. “This is my statement.”}}</ref> In April 2019, St. Clair was loaned out to Minnesota's USL affiliate club, [[Forward Madison FC]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.twincities.com/2019/04/04/four-minnesota-united-players-loaned-to-madison-affiliate/ |title=Four Minnesota United players loaned to Madison affiliate |website=twincities.com |date=April 4, 2019 |access-date=April 19, 2019}}</ref> He made his league debut for the club on April 6, 2019, playing all ninety minutes in a 1–0 defeat to [[Chattanooga Red Wolves SC|Chattanooga Red Wolves]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2019/04/07/united-states/usl-league-one/chattanooga-red-wolves/forward-madison/2945941/ |title=Chattanooga Red Wolves vs. Forward Madison – 6 April 2019 – Soccerway |website=int.soccerway.com |access-date=April 19, 2019}}</ref>

In February 2020, it was announced that Minnesota had loaned St. Clair out once again, this time to [[USL Championship]] side [[San Antonio FC]].<ref>{{cite web |title=SAN ANTONIO FC ADDS GOALKEEPER DAYNE ST. CLAIR ON LOAN FROM MINNESOTA UNITED |url=https://www.sanantoniofc.com/news_article/show/1086647 |website=SanAntonioFC.com |date=February 12, 2020 |access-date=February 12, 2020 |archive-date=January 16, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210116170539/https://www.sanantoniofc.com/news_article/show/1086647 |url-status=dead }}</ref> On August 15, 2020, San Antonio announced that St. Clair had been recalled by Minnesota,<ref>{{cite tweet|number=1294765270565879811|user=SanAntonioFC|title=Roster Update: SAFC today announced...|date=August 15, 2020}}</ref> where he played the last 13 games of the regular season and three [[2020 MLS Cup Playoffs|2020 MLS Cup Playoff]] games, conceding just 15 goals and with eight clean sheets.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Attfield |first1=Paul |title=Dayne St. Clair standing tall despite Minnesota's early season struggles |url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/sports/article-dayne-st-clair-standing-tall-despite-minnesotas-early-season-struggles/ |access-date=May 3, 2021 |work=[[The Globe and Mail]] |date=May 3, 2021 |location=[[Toronto]] |page=B10}}</ref> Upon conclusion of the 2021 season, St Clair would sign a 3 year extension with Minnesota United through the end of the 2024 season, with a club option for 2025.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mnufc.com/news/dayne-st-clair-signs-new-contract|title=Dayne St. Clair Signs New Contract|website=Minnesota United FC|date=November 30, 2021}}</ref>

==International career==
St. Clair was named to the [[Canada men's national under-23 soccer team|Canada U-23]] provisional roster for the [[2020 CONCACAF Men's Olympic Qualifying Championship]] on February 26, 2020.<ref>{{cite web |title=Canada Soccer announces Provisional List for Concacaf Men's Olympic Qualifying |url=https://www.canadasoccer.com/canada-soccer-announces-provisional-list-for-concacaf-men-s-olympic-qualifying-p162594-preview-1 |publisher=Canada Soccer |access-date=February 26, 2020 |date=February 26, 2020 |archive-date=March 9, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200309191149/https://www.canadasoccer.com/canada-soccer-announces-provisional-list-for-concacaf-men-s-olympic-qualifying-p162594-preview-1 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He accepted an invite for the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada senior national team]] camp for January 2021.<ref name=can2021>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/canada-soccer-ayo-akinola-national-team-camp-1.5853734|title=TFC's Ayo Akinola accepts invite to Canadian national team camp|date=December 23, 2020|first=Neil|last=Davidson|work=[[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation|CBC]]}}</ref> He made his debut for the senior team on June 5, 2021, playing the whole game in a 7-0 [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification|FIFA World Cup qualification]] victory over [[Aruba national football team|Aruba]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Canada beats Aruba, sets up crucial World Cup qualifier against Suriname - Sportsnet.ca|url=https://www.sportsnet.ca/soccer/article/canada-beats-aruba-sets-crucial-world-cup-qualifier-suriname/|access-date=June 6, 2021|website=www.sportsnet.ca}}</ref> On July 1 St. Clair was named to the Canada squad for the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.canadasoccer.com/news/canada-soccer-announces-2021-concacaf-gold-cup-roster/|title=CANADA SOCCER ANNOUNCES 2021 CONCACAF GOLD CUP ROSTER|date=July 1, 2021}}</ref>

In November 2022 St. Clair was named to Canada's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canada-announces-26-man-squad-for-2022-fifa-world-cup-in-qatar|date=November 13, 2022|title=Canada announces 26-man squad for 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]|first=Charlie|last=O'Connor-Clarke}}</ref> In June 2023, St. Clair was called-up to the 23-man squad contesting the [[2023 CONCACAF Nations League Finals]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Former CPLers Loturi, Zator called up by Canada for 2023 Concacaf Nations League Finals|date=June 7, 2023|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canada-canmnt-2023-concacaf-nations-league-final-roster|first=Mitchell|last=Tierney|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]}}</ref> On June 19 he was named to the 23-man squad for the [[2023 CONCACAF Gold Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canmnt-announces-final-23-man-squad-for-concacaf-gold-cup|date=June 19, 2023|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]|title=CanMNT announces final 23-man squad for Concacaf Gold Cup|first=Charlie|last=O'Connor-Clarke}}</ref>
