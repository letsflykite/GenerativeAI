'''Esteban Alvarado Brown''' (born 28 April 1989) is a Costa Rican professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Liga FPD]] club [[Deportivo Saprissa|Saprissa]] and the [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica national team]].

==Club career==
===Saprissa===
Born in [[Siquirres]], Costa Rica, Alvarado started his academy career at [[Santos de Guápiles]] before moving to [[C.D. Saprissa|Saprissa]]. Alvarado made his Saprissa debut, in a 3–1 win over [[Municipal Liberia]] on 29 November 2009.{{Citation needed|date=August 2015}} With his move to [[AZ Alkmaar]] finalised and expected to move in the summer, Alvarado made four appearances for the club and helped the club win the league.

==International career==
At the [[2009 FIFA U-20 World Cup]],<ref>{{FIFA player|268832}}</ref> he was named the best goalkeeper of the tournament. He made his national team début in an August 2010 [[friendly match]] against Paraguay.<ref name="Alvarado Best Player"/>

On 12 March 2012, it was announced that Alvarado was barred from the national team for five months after leaving the team before a friendly match against [[Spain national football team|Spain]] without communicating the staff.<ref>{{cite news|title=Esteban Alvarado inhabilitado 5 meses de la selección|url=http://www.monumental.co.cr/deportes/esteban-alvarado-inhabilitado-5-meses-de-la-seleccion|publisher=Momumemental|language=es|date=22 March 2012|access-date=24 August 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150902234711/http://www.monumental.co.cr/deportes/esteban-alvarado-inhabilitado-5-meses-de-la-seleccion|archive-date=2 September 2015}}</ref> In March 2014, he ruled out a selection for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]] due to an apparent strife with national team manager [[Jorge Luis Pinto]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Esteban sluit deelname WK uit]|url=http://www.ad.nl/ad/nl/1442/AZ/article/detail/3623682/2014/03/27/Esteban-sluit-deelname-WK-uit.dhtml|publisher=Algemeen Daglad|language=nl|date=27 March 2014|access-date=24 August 2015}}</ref> But in May 2014, Alvarado was named in a provisional 26-man squad in the FIFA World Cup<ref>{{cite news|title=Esteban ondanks conflict in voorselectie Costa Rica|url=http://www.vi.nl/nieuws/esteban-ondanks-conflict-in-voorselectie-costa-rica.htm|publisher=Voetbal International|language=nl|date=12 May 2014|access-date=24 August 2015}}</ref> By the end of May, Alvarado was not named in the 23 men squad, presumed to be a continuous strife with national team manager Pinto.<ref>{{cite news|title=Ruiz opgenomen in definitieve WK-selectie Costa Rica|url=http://www.vi.nl/nieuws/ruiz-opgenomen-in-definitieve-wkselectie-costa-rica.htm|publisher=Voetbal International|language=nl|date=31 May 2014|access-date=24 August 2015}}</ref>

Following Pinto's departure as manager of Costa, Alvarado returned to the national football team, under new management of [[Paulo Wanchope]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Esteban Alvarado regresa a la formación titular de la Selección|url=http://www.monumental.co.cr/deportes/esteban-alvarado-regresa-la-formacion-titular-de-la-seleccion|publisher=Monumental|language=es|date=30 March 2015|access-date=24 August 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150401133010/http://www.monumental.co.cr/deportes/esteban-alvarado-regresa-la-formacion-titular-de-la-seleccion|archive-date=1 April 2015}}</ref> In the [[2015 CONCACAF Gold Cup|CONCACAF Gold Cup]], Alvarado was included in the squad and replaced [[Keylor Navas]] as a first choice goalkeeper following his injury.<ref>{{cite news|title=Blessure Navas maakt weg vrij voor Esteban op Gold Cup|url=http://www.vi.nl/nieuws/blessure-navas-maakt-weg-vrij-voor-esteban-op-gold-cup.htm|publisher=Voetbal International|language=nl|date=16 June 2015|access-date=24 August 2015}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Born in Costa Rica, Alvarado is of Jamaican descent.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rsqNSuRu2rQ|title=How many players of Jamaican Heritage will be at the World Cup?|via=www.youtube.com}}</ref> In June 2011, Alvarado was accused by his girlfriend, Marcela Juárez Castillo, of domestic violence.<ref>{{cite news|title=Doelman AZ verdacht van poging tot moord|url=http://www.ad.nl/ad/nl/1001/Sport/article/detail/2457909/2011/06/27/Doelman-AZ-verdacht-van-poging-tot-moord.dhtml|publisher=Algemeen Daglad|language=nl|date=27 June 2011|access-date=24 August 2015}}</ref> However, two months later, charges against Alvarado were later dropped.<ref>{{cite news|title=Aanklacht Esteban loopt met sisser af|url=http://www.rtvnh.nl/sport/61583/Aanklacht+Esteban+loop+met+sisser+af|publisher=RTVNH|language=nl|date=10 August 2011|access-date=24 August 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924143344/http://www.rtvnh.nl/sport/61583/Aanklacht+Esteban+loop+met+sisser+af|archive-date=24 September 2015}}</ref>

While growing up in Costa Rica, Alvarado made good friends with [[Joel Campbell]], having played together in childhood. Alvarado also revealed that he played as striker before switching position to become goalkeeper at fourteen.<ref>{{cite news|title=Esteban: 'Ik was een betere spits dan Campbell'|url=http://www.vi.nl/nieuws/esteban-ik-was-een-betere-spits-dan-campbell.htm|publisher=Voetbal International|language=nl|date=1 July 2014|access-date=24 August 2015}}</ref>
