'''Davy Klaassen''' (born 21 February 1993) is a Dutch professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[Eredivisie]] club [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]] and the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands national team]].<ref name="sw">{{Cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/players/davy-klaassen/158581/ |title=D. Klaassen |website=Soccerway |access-date=4 November 2013}}</ref>

==Club career==

===Early career===
Klaassen began his football career in the youth ranks of local amateur side HVV de Zebra's, where he played until 2003, when he transferred to HSV Wasmeer. A year later he was recruited into the famed [[AFC Ajax Youth Academy|Ajax Youth Academy]]. While playing for the Ajax A1 youth squad in 2011–12, Klaassen helped his side to win the Nike Eredivisie league title,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://bin617-02.website-voetbal.nl/sites/voetbal.nl/files/04%20A%20-%20Beste%20spelers%20A-jun%20eredivisie.pdf|title=Beste Spelers Nike A-junioren Eredivisie seizoen 2011/'12 |date=26 January 2012|publisher=Nikefootball.com|access-date=11 August 2013}}</ref> as well as finishing as runners-up to [[Inter Milan]] in the [[NextGen Series]] (the [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] equivalent for under-19 teams) after losing on penalties (5–3) following a 1–1 deadlock after extra time.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.voetbalcentraal.nl/nieuws/75887/ajax-a1-verliest-finale-van-nextgen-series-van-inter-milan|title=Ajax A1 verliest finale van NextGen Series van Inter Milan|date=25 March 2012|publisher=Voetbal Centraal|access-date=11 August 2013}}</ref>

===Ajax===
Klaassen made his debut for the first team in the [[UEFA Champions League]] group stage away draw (0–0) against [[Olympique Lyonnais]] on 22 November 2011 replacing [[Lorenzo Ebecilio]] in the 85th minute.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ajax.nl/Ajax-Nieuws/Ajax-nieuwsarchief/Ajax-nieuwsartikel/183009/Klaassen-belt-Ajaxnieuws-aan-opa-door.htm |title=Klaassen belt Ajax-nieuws aan opa door |publisher=Ajax.nl |access-date=4 November 2013}}</ref> While still competing in the A-Juniors Nike Eredivisie league competing for the Ajax A1 under-19 squad, he made his Eredivisie debut on 27 November as a substitute against [[N.E.C. (football club)|NEC]] and scored only 42 seconds after having been subbed on.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ajax.nl/Ajax-Nieuws/Ajax-nieuwsarchief/Ajax-nieuwsartikel/182927/Aissati-en-Klaassen-mee-naar-NEC.htm |title=Aissati en Klaassen mee naar NEC |publisher=Ajax.nl |access-date=4 November 2013}}</ref> He made his debut in the starting XI of the Ajax first team on 11 December 2011, due to starting center midfielder [[Theo Janssen]] catching the flu. Klaassen played the entire match which ended in a 0–1 away win for Ajax against [[RKC Waalwijk]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.nu.nl/sport/2690791/ajax-met-klaassen-rkc-waalwijk.html |title=Ajax met Klaassen tegen RKC Waalwijk |date=11 December 2011 |publisher=NU.nl |access-date=4 November 2013}}</ref>

On 5 August 2013, Klaassen made his debut in the Dutch [[Eerste Divisie]] in the 2–0 win against [[Telstar]] while playing for the newly promoted reserves team [[Jong Ajax]], in what would be the first match in the 2nd tier of professional football ever for the reserves.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2013/08/05/netherlands/eerste-divisie/jong-ajax/stormvogels-telstar-combinatie/1478649/ |title=Jong Ajax 2–0 Telstar |publisher=Soccerway |access-date=4 November 2013}}</ref> He scored his first goal for Jong Ajax on 20 September 2013 in the 1–2 away victory against [[FC Eindhoven]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2013/09/20/netherlands/eerste-divisie/fc-eindhoven/jong-ajax/1478729/ |title=FC Eindhoven 1–2 Jong Ajax |publisher=Soccerway |access-date=4 November 2013}}</ref> On 6 October 2013, while playing for the Ajax first team, he scored his team's second goal in the 59th minute in the 3–0 home win against [[FC Utrecht]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2013/10/06/netherlands/eredivisie/afc-ajax/stichting-fc-utrecht/1478408/ |title=Ajax 3–0 FC Utrecht |publisher=Soccerway |access-date=4 November 2013}}</ref> On 7 December 2013, Klaassen scored his first [[hat-trick]] while playing for the first team, in a 4–0 victory at home over [[NAC Breda]], taking home the match ball and earning himself the [[Man of the Match]] award.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ajaxshowtime.com/hoofdnieuws/75500/live-18-45u-ajax-nac-breda-0-0-.html |title=Ajax met fantastische Klaassen langs NAC |publisher=Ajax Showtime|access-date=7 December 2013}}</ref> Following the departure of Ajax captain [[Siem de Jong]], Klaassen switched to the vacant number 10 shirt for the upcoming season.

For the [[2015–16 AFC Ajax season|2015–16 season]], Klaassen was named Ajax' captain, replacing the departed [[Niklas Moisander]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.vi.nl/nieuws/klaassen-volgt-moisander-op-als-aanvoerder-ajax.htm |title=Klaassen volgt Moisander op als aanvoerder Ajax |publisher=Voetbal International |access-date=6 July 2014}}</ref>

===Everton===
On 15 June 2017, Klaassen signed for [[Premier League]] side [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] on a five-year deal worth £23.6&nbsp;million.<ref>{{cite news|last1=McNulty|first1=Phil|title=Davy Klaassen: Everton complete £23.6m signing of Ajax midfielder and captain|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/40297081|access-date=21 June 2017|publisher=BBC Sport|date=15 June 2017}}</ref> He was handed the number 20 jersey at his new club for the 2017–18 season.<ref>{{cite news |title=Everton Squad Numbers Announced For 2017/18 |url=http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2017/07/11/everton-squad-numbers-announced-for-201718 |publisher=Everton F.C. |date=11 July 2017 |access-date=12 July 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170713065355/http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2017/07/11/everton-squad-numbers-announced-for-201718 |archive-date=13 July 2017 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He made his first official appearance for Everton in a 1–0 win over [[MFK Ružomberok]] in the [[2017–18 UEFA Europa League|UEFA Europa League]] third qualifying round first leg.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40724022 |title=Baines gives Everton Europa lead on Rooney return |date=27 July 2017 |publisher=BBC Sport |first=Tom |last=Rostance |access-date=27 July 2017 }}</ref> Klaassen made his league debut for Everton on 12 August 2017, when his club beat [[Stoke City F.C.|Stoke City]] 1–0 at Goodison Park.<ref>{{cite news |title=Everton 1–0 Stoke City|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/40835425 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=12 August 2017 |access-date=12 August 2017}}</ref> 

Klaassen endured a difficult season with the club, falling out of favor with both [[Sam Allardyce]] and [[Marco Silva]] after [[Ronald Koeman]] was fired in October 2017.<ref>{{cite news|title=Davy Klaassen delivers parting message to Everton after completing transfer to Werder Bremen|url=https://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/davy-klaassen-delivers-parting-message-14964038|publisher=Liverpool Echo|last1=Gorst|first1=Paul|date=28 July 2018|access-date=22 November 2022}}</ref> Between late September 2017 and early March 2018, he failed to make a Premier League appearance. At the end of the January transfer window, Klaassen neared a loan move to [[S.S.C. Napoli|Napoli]], although the move would never materialize. Allardyce claimed the deal collapsed as a result of an image rights dispute between Klaassen and the Italian club,<ref>{{cite news|title=Davy Klaassen should have made football not money his priority, says Allardyce|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2018/feb/02/davy-klaassen-everton-football-money-allardyce|work=The Guardian|last1=Hunter|first1=Andy|date=2 February 2018|access-date=22 November 2022}}</ref> although Klaassen would later reject this statement.<ref name="Everton">{{cite news|title=Davy Klaassen opens up on "difficult" life at Everton - and big reason why Blues failed last season|url=https://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/davy-klaassen-opens-up-difficult-15301010|publisher=Liverpool Echo|last1=Jones|first1=Adam|date=19 October 2018|access-date=22 November 2022}}</ref> Ahead of his summer move to Werder Bremen, Klaassen expressed his desire to prove his worth for the following season.<ref>{{cite news|title=Davy Klaassen Wants Everton Stay as Dutchman Is Eager to Prove His Worth in the Premier League|url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2018/05/29/davy-klaassen-wants-everton-stay-dutchman-eager-prove-his-worth-premier-league|publisher=Sports Illustrated|date=29 May 2018|access-date=22 November 2022}}</ref> Klaassen stated that he struggled to adapt to the different style of play in the Premier League, hence his struggles with the club.<ref name="Everton"/>

===Werder Bremen===
On 27 July 2018, Klaassen joined [[SV Werder Bremen|Werder Bremen]] on a four-year deal while the transfer fee paid to Everton was reported as €13.5&nbsp;million (£12 million).<ref>{{cite news |title=Davy Klaassen: Everton midfielder joins Werder Bremen |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44977948 |access-date=29 July 2018 |work=BBC Sport |date=27 July 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title="Absoluter Wunschspieler": Werder verpflichtet Klaassen|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/startseite/728359/artikel_absoluter-wunschspieler_werder-verpflichtet-klaassen.html|access-date=27 July 2018|publisher=[[Kicker (sports magazine)|Kicker]]|date=27 July 2018}}</ref>

===Return to Ajax===
On 5 October 2020, Klaassen rejoined Ajax on a contract until 30 June 2024, with a transfer fee which could rise to €14 million.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.ajax.nl/artikelen/davy-klaassen-keert-terug-bij-ajax/ |title=Davy Klaassen keert terug bij Ajax |website=Ajax |language=nl |date=5 October 2020 }}</ref>

On 5 April 2023, during a [[KNVB Cup|Dutch cup]] semifinal away against [[Feyenoord]], he suffered a head injury when he was hit by an object thrown from the stands. The incident caused a 30-minute delay and he was substituted shortly after the resumption, gesturing that he was suffering from dizziness.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Ajax's Klaassen hurt by object thrown from stands |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/65197563 |access-date=2023-04-06}}</ref>

==International career==
===Youth===
Klaassen made his debut for the Dutch national team at youth levels, debuting for the Netherlands U-16 team on 28 October 2008 in the 10th edition of the [[Val-de-Marne|Tournoi Val de Marne '08]] in the 3–0 victory over Italy U-16, while scoring the second goal for the Netherlands in his first international encounter.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.azjeugd.nl/2008/10/ |title=Oranje onder 16 start sterk op Val de Marne Tournoi |publisher=AZJeugd.nl |access-date=4 November 2013}}</ref> Klaassen scored in his second appearance for the Netherlands U-16 in the same tournament as well in 0–1 win over France U-16.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.azjeugd.nl/2008/10/ |title=Oranje onder 16, verslaat ook Frankrijk (1–0). |publisher=AZJeugd.nl |access-date=4 November 2013}}</ref> In total Klaassen made 5 appearances for the Netherlands U-16 while scoring 4 goals.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.wasmeer.nl/Nieuws/ShowItem.asp?id=2451 |title=Davy Klaassen in Oranje |publisher=Wasmeer.nl |access-date=4 November 2013}}</ref> Scoring two more goals in friendly encounters against Ukraine U-16 and Ireland U-16 as well.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.onsoranje.nl/team-statistieken/teams/speler/23/50a3c210-e12dd411-867998 |title=Davy Klaassen Onder-16 Statistieken |publisher=OnsOranje.nl |access-date=4 November 2013}}</ref> On 22 September 2009, Klaassen made his first appearance for the [[Netherlands national under-17 football team|Netherlands U17]] team in a friendly encounter against [[France national under-17 football team|France U17]] which ended in a 1–0 loss for the Dutch. On 3 March 2010, he scored his first goal for the under-17 side in his 8th appearance, a friendly match against [[Greece national under-17 football team|Greece U17]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.onsoranje.nl/nieuws/artikel/1788/onder-17-simpel-langs-grieken |title=Onder 17 simpel langs Grieken |publisher=OnsOranje.nl |access-date=4 November 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131105033754/http://www.onsoranje.nl/nieuws/artikel/1788/onder-17-simpel-langs-grieken |archive-date=5 November 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He also played a major role in the Netherlands qualifying campaign ahead of the [[2010 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship]] as well as playing in the 2009 edition of [[La Manga Cup]]. On 2 September 2010, Klaassen made his debut for the [[Netherlands national under-19 football team|Netherlands U19]] team in a friendly match against [[Germany national under-19 football team|Germany U19]] which ended in a 2–2 draw. In his 7th appearance for the under-19 side he scored both his first and second goal on 10 November 2011 in a qualification match ahead of the [[2012 UEFA European Under-19 Championship]] against [[Moldova national under-19 football team|Moldova U19]] with the Netherlands failing to qualify for the final tournament in [[Estonia]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.onsoranje.nl/nieuws/artikel/10405/klaassen-karwei-afmaken |title=Klaassen: 'Karwei afmaken' |publisher=OnsOranje.nl |access-date=4 November 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131105033742/http://www.onsoranje.nl/nieuws/artikel/10405/klaassen-karwei-afmaken |archive-date=5 November 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Due to injuries sustained by [[Ruben Ligeon]], [[Yassin Ayoub]] and [[Jürgen Locadia]] for the [[Netherlands national under-21 football team|Netherlands U21]] selection, Klaassen received his first call-up for Jong Oranje the under-21 selection of the Netherlands as a replacement along with [[Thomas Bruns]] and [[Timo Letschert]] for the [[2015 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship]] qualification match against [[Georgia national under-21 football team|Georgia U21]], as well as the friendly match against [[Austria national under-21 football team|Austria U21]] four days later.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ajax.nl/Ajax-Nieuws/Ajax-Nieuws/Ajax-nieuws-artikel/193292/Zege-Jong-Oranje-debuut-Klaassen.htm |title=Zege Jong Oranje, debuut Klaassen |publisher=Ajax.nl |access-date=4 November 2013}}</ref> He made his debut for the under-21 side on 10 October 2013 in that qualification match helping the Dutch to defeat Georgia 6–0 in which he replaced [[Luc Castaignos]] in the 81st minute of the match.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.onsoranje.nl/nieuws/artikel/17099/bruns-klaassen-en-letschert-bij-jo |title=Bruns, Klaassen en Letschert bij JO |publisher=OnsOranje.nl |access-date=4 November 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131105032133/http://www.onsoranje.nl/nieuws/artikel/17099/bruns-klaassen-en-letschert-bij-jo |archive-date=5 November 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

===Senior===
On 5 March 2014, Klaassen made his debut for the Netherlands first team under head coach [[Louis van Gaal]] at the [[Stade de France]] in [[Paris]], in a friendly match against [[France national football team|France]] ahead of the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]]. He was substituted on in the 72nd minute for [[Wesley Sneijder]], as the Netherlands lost the match 2–0 to the French.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2014/03/05/world/friendlies/france/netherlands/1597796/ |title=France vs Netherlands 2–0 |publisher=Soccerway |access-date=5 April 2014}}</ref> While making his debut for the Netherlands, Klaassen became the 100th Ajax player in the history of the club to make his debut for the Dutch national team while playing his club football at Ajax.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ajaxshowtime.com/bijzaken-en-geruchten/77508/honderste-ajax-debutant-met-applaus-ontvangen.html |title=Honderdste Ajax-debutant ontvangt applaus |publisher=Ajax Showtime|access-date=6 March 2014}}</ref>

On 31 March 2015, Klaassen scored his first international goal and the team's second goal in a 2–0 win over [[Spain national football team|Spain]] in a friendly match.<ref>{{cite news|title=Netherlands 2–0 Spain: Rejuvenated Dutch ease pressure on Hiddink|url=http://www.goal.com/en/match/netherlands-vs-spain/1973973/report?ICID=MP_MS_5|access-date=31 March 2015|publisher=Goal.com|date=31 March 2015}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Klaassen's nickname is "cheese straw" (Dutch: kaasstengel) due to his physical appearance.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Olives-Berthet|first1=Cyril|title=Ligue Europa : L'Ajax Amsterdam et ses quatre ténors|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Ligue-europa-l-ajax-amsterdam-et-ses-quatre-tenors/795090|access-date=21 June 2017|work=L'Équipe|date=24 May 2017}}</ref> Another nickname is "Mister 1-0" due to him often scoring the first goal of a match.<ref>{{cite web |title='Mister 1-0': De succesvolle terugkeer van Davy Klaassen |url=https://www.ajax.nl/artikelen/mister-1-0-de-succesvolle-terugkeer-van-davy-klaassen/ |website=www.ajax.nl |publisher=AFC Ajax |access-date=12 May 2022}}</ref> On the signing of the former Ajax captain, then Everton manager [[Ronald Koeman]] was quoted saying Klaassen was a leader on the pitch. Koeman further described Klaassen's playing style as hardworking, liked to press and would provide creativity and goals.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Kirkbride-|first1=Phil|title=Everton boss Ronald Koeman explains Davy Klaassen signing after midfielder joins Blues|url=http://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/transfer-news/everton-boss-ronald-koeman-explains-13193651|access-date=1 July 2017|work=Liverpool Echo|date=16 June 2017}}</ref>
