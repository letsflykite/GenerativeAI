'''Edson Omar Álvarez Velázquez''' ({{IPA-es|ˈetson ˈalβaɾes}}; born 24 October 1997) is a Mexican professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Midfielder#Defensive-midfielder|defensive midfielder]] or [[Defender (association football)#Centre-back|centre-back]] for [[Eredivisie]] club [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]] and the [[Mexico national football team|Mexico national team]].

==Early life==
Álvarez was born in [[Tlalnepantla de Baz]], a municipality just north of Mexico City, to Adriana Velázquez and Evaristo Álvarez.<ref name="ESPN bio">{{cite web|title=Edson Álvarez, un águila de calle |date=4 November 2016 |url=http://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/mexico/nota/_/id/2839745/edson-alvarez-un-aguila-de-calle |trans-title=Edson Álvarez, an eagle from the streets |publisher=ESPN Deportes |language=es |access-date=4 November 2016}}</ref> As a child he would work for his family's business of manufacturing football jerseys for local teams, which he cites as his first foray into the sport.<ref name="FT bio">{{cite web|title=Causa y Efecto de… Edson Álvarez |url=https://www.futboltotal.com.mx/2017/07/causa-efecto-edson-alvarez/ |trans-title=Cause and Effect of… Edson Álvarez |publisher=Futbol Total |language=es |access-date=2 July 2017}}</ref> At age 14, Álvarez tried out for the youth team of [[C.F. Pachuca|Pachuca]], though Álvarez did not make the team due to his short stature.<ref name=" FT bio" /> Contemplating quitting football, to the resistance of his parents who believed he had the talent to pursue his dream of playing professionally, Álvarez decided to try out for [[Club América]]'s youth team, eventually making the team after a three-month tryout. He would make it to the daily team practices by way of a three-to-four-hour round trip commute from his home in Tlalnepantla and the club's [[Instalaciones Club América en Coapa|training ground]] in [[Coapa]]. According to Álvarez, he would spend almost 70 percent of his monthly salary toward transportation.<ref name="FT bio" />

==Club career==
===América===
In 2014, at age 16, Álvarez joined [[Club América]]'s youth system, playing for the under-17 team.<ref name="ESPN bio" /> The following year, while still playing for the under-17 squad, Álvarez was promoted to América's [[Segunda División de México|second division]] team.<ref>{{cite web|title=Edson Omar Álvarez Velazquez |url=http://ligamx.net/cancha/jugador/102742/edson-omar-alvarez-velazquez |publisher=Liga MX |language=es |access-date=24 November 2016}}</ref>

In August 2016, first team coach [[Ignacio Ambríz]] gave Álvarez his first call-up, sitting on the bench in América's week 5 [[Liga MX|league]] match against [[Monarcas Morelia]], using the jersey number 282.<ref name="FT bio" /> On 29 October, newly appointed coach [[Ricardo La Volpe]] gave 19 year-old Álvarez his Liga MX debut in the team's win over [[Santos Laguna]]; he played all 90 minutes and was voted third in a ''Man of the Match'' online poll by club fans.<ref>{{cite web|title=Edson Álvarez debuta en la Liga MX |url=http://www.clubamerica.com.mx/comunicados/edson-alvarez-debuta-la-liga-mx/ |website=ClubAmérica.com.mx |publisher=Club América |language=es |access-date=29 October 2016}}</ref> On 25 December, he scored his first career goal in the [[2016–17 Liga MX season#Torneo Apertura|Apertura]] final against [[Tigres UANL]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Video: Gol de Edson Álvarez contra Tigres |url=http://www.goal.com/es-mx/news/4774/liga-bancomer-mx/2016/12/25/30851422/video-el-gol-de-edson-%C3%A1lvarez-contra-tigres |work=Goal.com |language=es |access-date=25 December 2016}}</ref>

Prior to the start of the [[2017–18 Liga MX season|2017–18 season]], Álvarez was handed the number 4 shirt, which was vacated following the departure of [[Erik Pimentel]].<ref>{{cite web|title=América registra 11 jugadores de 21 años o menos |trans-title=America registers 11 players aged 21 and under |url=https://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/mexico/nota/_/id/3386279/america-registra-11-jugadores-de-21-anos-o-menos |website=espn.com |publisher=ESPN Deportes |language=es |date=12 July 2017 |access-date=12 July 2017}}</ref>

On 16 December 2018, Álvarez was given the start for the return leg of the [[2018–19 Liga MX season#Torneo Apertura|Apertura final]] against [[Cruz Azul]], playing in midfield in place of the injured [[Mateus Uribe]], and scored twice as América won its 13th league title following a 2–0 aggregate score.<ref>{{cite web|title=Edson Alvarez delivers dream finish to Club America after World Cup fiasco|url=https://theathletic.com/721973/2018/12/18/edson-alvarez-club-america-liga-mx-mexico-el-tri/|website=The Athletic|access-date=18 December 2018}}</ref> On 23 February 2019, Álvarez made his 100th competitive appearance for América in the team's 3–0 victory over [[Lobos BUAP]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Edson Álvarez llegó a 100 partidos oficiales con América |trans-title=Edson Álvarez reached 100 official matches with America |language=es |url=https://www.telemundodeportes.com/liga-mx/edson-alvarez-llego-100-partidos-oficiales-con-america |website=telemundodeportes.com |publisher=Telemundo |date=23 February 2019 |access-date=23 February 2019}}</ref>

===Ajax===
On 19 July 2019, Dutch club [[AFC Ajax]] announced an agreement with Club América for the signing of Álvarez on a five-year contract, subject to a medical, for a reported fee of US$17 million.<ref>{{cite web|last=Valentine |first=Ben |title=Ajax complete $17m signing of versatile Mexico star Alvarez |url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/ajax-complete-17m-signing-of-versatile-mexico-star-alvarez/hdlym7xyzhv414vc74sjhj9pm |website=Goal.com |access-date=19 July 2019}}</ref> He passed his medical and was officially presented on 22 July, being handed the number 4 shirt which had been vacated following the departure of [[Matthijs de Ligt]]. The club announced an official transfer sum of €15 million.<ref>{{cite web|title=Transfer completed: Edson Álvarez is now Ajacied! |url=https://english.ajax.nl/streams/ajax-now/transfer-completed-edson-alvarez-is-now-ajacied-.htm |publisher=[[AFC Ajax]] |access-date=22 July 2019}}</ref> On 17 August, Álvarez made his competitive debut for Ajax as a 74th minute substitute in the team's 4–1 [[Eredivisie|league]] win over [[VVV-Venlo]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Ajax brings 3 points back to Amsterdam |url=https://english.ajax.nl/streams/ajax-now/ajax-brings-3-points-back-to-amsterdam-.htm |publisher=AFC Ajax |access-date=18 August 2019}}</ref> On 29 August, Álvarez scored his first goal in his first start against [[APOEL F.C.|APOEL]] in the second-leg of the [[2019–20 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round#Play-off round|UEFA Champions League play-off round]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Edson Alvarez scores first Ajax goal in Champions League playoff |url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/edson-alvarez-scores-first-ajax-goal-in-champions-league/47dkdz5c4ygz1tz8q22ykkrhe |website=Goal.com |access-date=29 August 2019}}</ref> On 17 September, Álvarez scored the second goal in the 3–0 Champions League group stage victory over [[Lille OSC|Lille]], becoming the first Mexican player to score on his Champions League debut.<ref>{{cite web|title=Report: Ajax 3-0 Lille |url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/match/ajax-v-lille/report/6gwdth6s62a4zgei83d3uh5yi |website=Goal.com |access-date=17 September 2019}}</ref>

On 21 March 2021, Álvarez scored his first [[Eredivisie]] goal for [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]] in a 5–0 victory over [[ADO Den Haag]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Report: Ajax ease to win over ADO Den Haag |url=http://www.football-oranje.com/ajax-ease-to-win-over-ado-den-haag/ |website= football-oranje.com |date=21 March 2021 |access-date=21 March 2021}}</ref> On 18 April, in the [[2021 KNVB Cup Final|Dutch Cup final]] against [[SBV Vitesse|Vitesse]], he played the entirety of the match in a 2–1 victory.<ref>{{cite web|title=Neres' stoppage-time goal seals Dutch Cup win|url=https://english.ajax.nl/articles/neres-stoppage-time-goal-seals-dutch-cup-win/}}</ref> At the end of his second season, he was nominated for the league Player of the Year Award.<ref>{{cite web|title=Edson Álvarez, nominado a jugador del año en Holanda|date=6 May 2021 |url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/mexicanos-mundo/2021/05/06/609430c5268e3e22478b45a6.html}}</ref>

On 27 October 2021, it was announced Álvarez had signed a contract extension with Ajax, keeping him with the club until 2025.<ref>{{cite web|title=Edson Álvarez verlengt contract bij Ajax|url=https://www.ajax.nl/artikelen/edson-alvarez-verlengt-contract-bij-ajax/}}</ref> On 30 April 2022 he was inducted into the club's ''[[Club van 100 (AFC Ajax)|Club van 100]]'', making his 100th appearance in a 3–0 league victory over [[PEC Zwolle]], becoming the 174th player in the history of the club to join the ranks.<ref>{{cite web|title=How Edson Álvarez came to make his one hundredth appearance for the Ajax first team |url=https://english.ajax.nl/articles/how-edson-alvarez-came-to-make-his-one-hundredth-appearance-for-the-ajax-first-team/ |website= Ajax.nl |access-date=1 May 2021}}</ref>

==International career==
===Youth===
Álvarez was called up to the [[Mexico national under-20 football team|under-20]] team camp<ref>{{cite web|title=Convocatoria de la Selección Nacional de México Sub 20 |url=http://miseleccion.mx/convocatoria-de-la-seleccion-nacional-de-mexico-sub-20/ |website=MiSeleccion.com.mx |language=es}}</ref> preparing for the [[2017 CONCACAF U-20 Championship]] en route to the [[2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup]]. He was included in the tournament's best XI.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.concacaf.com/article/tsg-announces-cu20-best-xi-awards-2|title=TSG announces CU20 Best XI, Awards|publisher=CONCACAF.com|date=8 March 2017|access-date=10 March 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170312051624/http://www.concacaf.com/article/tsg-announces-cu20-best-xi-awards-2|archive-date=12 March 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> Álvarez was included in the World Cup under-20 squad, and scored the winning goal in Mexico's 3–2 win over Group B minnows [[Vanuatu national under-20 football team|Vanuatu]].

===Senior===
[[File:Mex-Kor (3).jpg|thumb|351x351px|Álvarez in a [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] match against [[South Korea national football team|South Korea]]]]
On 30 January 2017, Álvarez received his first call up to the [[Mexico national football team|senior national team]] for a friendly against [[Iceland national football team|Iceland]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Zacarias |first=Mariana |title=Edson Álvarez se dice 'en shock' por la noticia del Tri |url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/2017/01/30/edson-alvarez-se-dice-en-shock-por-la-noticia-del-tri |website=Medio Tiempo |language=es |access-date=30 January 2017}}</ref> He made his senior debut on 8 February against Iceland, replacing [[Jesús Molina]] in the 60th minute.<ref>{{cite web|title=Juan Carlos Osorio debutó a Edson Álvarez y Luis Reyes |url=http://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/mexico/nota/_/id/2970196/juan-carlos-osorio-debuto-a-edson-alvarez-y-luis-reyes |website=ESPN Deportes |date=9 February 2017 |language=es |access-date=9 February 2017}}</ref> Along with [[Alejandro Mayorga]], Álvarez was cited to be a supporting practice squad player with the [[2017 FIFA Confederations Cup|Confederations Cup]] team.<ref>{{cite web|title=Edson Álvarez y Alejandro Mayorga, 'invitados' de Osorio a Confederaciones|url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/2017/06/09/edson-alvarez-y-alejandro-mayorga-invitados-de-osorio-a-confederaciones |website= Medio Tiempo |language=es |access-date=19 June 2017}}</ref> He was called up to the [[2017 CONCACAF Gold Cup|Gold Cup]], making him the youngest player on the squad. The roster was largely composed of alternate players, as the primary squad was in Russia competing in the Confederations Cup.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.am.com.mx/2017/06/07/leon/deportes/elias-en-la-lista-preliminar-de-copa-oro-360107|title=Elías en la lista preliminar de Copa Oro|trans-title=Elías in the preliminary list of the Gold Cup|language=es|website=am.com|publisher=Periódico am|date=7 June 2017|access-date=6 April 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170607174658/https://www.am.com.mx/2017/06/07/leon/deportes/elias-en-la-lista-preliminar-de-copa-oro-360107|archive-date=7 June 2017}}</ref> On 17 July, in the last game of the group stage against [[Curaçao national football team|Curaçao]], Álvarez scored his first goal for the national team in their 2–0 win, becoming the youngest Mexican player to score a goal in a Gold Cup tournament at 19 years old.<ref>{{cite web|title=Édson Álvarez, el mexicano más joven en convertir un gol en la Copa Oro |url=http://www.univision.com/deportes/futbol/copa-oro/edson-alvarez-el-mexicano-mas-joven-en-convertir-un-gol-en-la-copa-oro-video|website=Univision Deportes |language=es |access-date=18 July 2017}}</ref>

In May 2018, Álvarez was named to Mexico's preliminary 28-man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Revealed: Every World Cup 2018 Squad - 23-Man Preliminary Lists & When Will They Be Announced? |url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/amp/news/revealed-every-world-cup-2018-squad-23-man-preliminary-lists/oa0atsduflsv1nsf6oqk576rb |website=Goal.com |access-date=23 May 2018}}</ref> He was the youngest player on the list.<ref>{{cite web |last=Arnold |first=Jon |title=Get to know Hernandez, Lozano & Mexico's World Cup roster |url=http://www.goal.com/en-us/lists/get-to-know-hernandez-lozano-mexicos-world-cup-roster/ap4jr4lgpkj21f6893xm4wx58 |access-date=23 May 2018 |website=Goal.com}}</ref> He was ultimately included in the final 23-man roster revealed on 4 June.<ref>{{cite web|title=Convocatoria de la Selección Nacional de México |url=https://miseleccion.mx/convocatoria-la-seleccion-nacional-mexico-13/ |website=MiSeleccion.mx |language=es |access-date=4 June 2018}}</ref> He appeared in all of Mexico's group stage matches; in Mexico's final group match against [[Sweden national football team|Sweden]], Álvarez started at right-back and scored an own goal at the 74th minute in Mexico's 3–0 loss.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sweden v. Mexico |url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/matches/match/300331548/#match-liveblog|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180612214518/https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/matches/match/300331548/#match-liveblog|url-status=dead|archive-date=12 June 2018|publisher=FIFA.com|date=27 June 2018|access-date=18 September 2018}}</ref> He also started in the round-of-16 defeat to [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]].

In May 2019, Álvarez was included in [[Gerardo Martino]]'s provisional [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup|Gold Cup]] roster.<ref>{{cite web|title=Tata Martino Announces Mexico Preliminary Gold Cup Squad; Vela, Chicharito, Herrera, Corona left out|url=https://www.fmfstateofmind.com/2019/5/14/18534851/mexico-el-tri-concacaf-gold-cup-copa-oro-squad-final-list-preliminary-corona-vela-chicharito-herrera|work=Fmfstateofmind.com|first=Antonio|last=Tinajero|date=14 May 2019}}</ref> In a friendly match against [[Venezuela national football team|Venezuela]], he suffered an apparent knee injury<ref>{{cite web|title=Alvarez injury in Venezuela friendly puts focus on Martino's provisional roster error|url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/alvarez-injury-in-venezuela-friendly-puts-focus-on-martinos/k2xp4ra2lmfh1sl6r58keglir|date=5 June 2019|publisher=Goal.com|first=Jon|last=Arnold}}</ref> but was revealed to only be a scare.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mexico midfielder Alvarez says injury 'only a scare'|url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/mexico-midfielder-alvarez-says-injury-only-a-scare/1iotvtiqkj68710g1bybod3izc|publisher=Goal.com|first=Jon|last=Arnold}}</ref> He was included in the final list for the competition.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mexico unveil final Gold Cup roster; Edson Alvarez doubtful|url=https://www.fmfstateofmind.com/2019/6/5/18654884/mexico-unveil-final-gold-cup-roster-edson-alvarez-doubtful|publisher=Fmfstateofmind.com|date=5 June 2019|first=Rafael|last=Diaz}}</ref> Álvarez appeared as a starter in five matches, including the [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup Final|final]], as Mexico would go on to win the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mexico Turns Tide, Wins Gold Cup Title Again vs. Wasteful USMNT|url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2019/07/07/mexico-usa-gold-cup-title-dos-santos-pizarro-pulisic-berhalter|publisher=[[Sports Illustrated]]|first=Brian|last=Straus|date=7 July 2019}}</ref>

In October 2022, Álvarez was named in Mexico's preliminary 31-man [[Mexico national football team|squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|World Cup]], and in November, was ultimately included in the final 26-man roster.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lista de convocados de la Selección Mexicana al Mundial de Qatar 2022|url=https://www.tvazteca.com/aztecadeportes/lista-oficial-convocados-seleccion-mexicana-mundial-qatar-2022|work=Tvazteca.com|first=Omar|last=Moro|date=14 November 2022|language=Spanish}}</ref>
