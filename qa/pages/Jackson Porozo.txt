'''Jackson Gabriel Porozo Vernaza''' (born 4 August 2000) is an Ecuadorian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] for {{French football updater|Troyes}} club [[ES Troyes AC|Troyes]].

==Club career==
===Early career===
Born in [[San Lorenzo, Ecuador|San Lorenzo]], [[Esmeraldas Province|Esmeraldas]], Porozo started his career with [[L.D.U. Quito|LDU Quito]], joining their youth setup in 2012. He subsequently represented Sandino FC, [[Manta F.C.|Manta]] and [[C.S.D. Independiente del Valle|Independiente del Valle]] before returning to Manta in 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.benditofutbol.com/la-tri/jackson-porozo-juvenil-triunfar-santos.html|title=Jackson Porozo, el juvenil que busca triunfar en el fútbol de Brasil|trans-title=Jackson Porozo, the youngster who seeks to triumph in Brazil|publisher=Bendito Fútbol|language=es|date=13 January 2019|accessdate=7 March 2019}}</ref> Porozo made his senior debut during the 2017 campaign, in the [[Ecuadorian Serie B]]. He scored his first senior goal on 22 April of that year, netting his team's only in a 3–1 away loss against Colón FC.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eldiario.ec/lamarea/noticias-manta-ecuador/430576-colon-fc-vencio-3-1-al-manta-fc-en-santa-ana/|title=Colón FC venció 3–1 al Manta FC en Santa Ana|trans-title=Colón FC defeated Manta FC by 3–1 in Santa Ana|publisher=El Diario|language=es|date=22 April 2017|accessdate=7 March 2019}}</ref> Porozo finished his first season with one goal in 16 appearances, and also had a trial at [[Sociedade Esportiva Palmeiras|Palmeiras]] in December 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.futbolecuador.com/site/noticia/jovenes-del-manta-fc-partieron-a-brasil-y-mexico/86735|title=Jóvenes del Manta FC partieron a Brasil y México|trans-title=Manta FC youngsters moved to Brazil and Mexico|publisher=Fútbol Ecuador|language=es|date=19 December 2017|accessdate=7 March 2019}}</ref>

===Santos===
On 23 March 2018, Porozo agreed to a contract with [[Santos FC|Santos]], being initially assigned to the under-20s.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.terra.com.br/esportes/palmeiras/santos-supera-palmeiras-e-contrata-jovem-equatoriano-conheca,09b4dcfa60b75dd1d76b18b7cf508c01dswhi00z.html|title=Santos supera Palmeiras e contrata jovem equatoriano; conheça|trans-title=Santos overcome Palmeiras and sign young Ecuadorian; know|publisher=Terra|language=pt-BR|date=23 March 2018|accessdate=7 March 2019}}</ref> After having problems in his contract,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://globoesporte.globo.com/sp/santos-e-regiao/futebol/times/santos/noticia/presidente-do-santos-diz-que-renegociara-contrato-de-zagueiro-equatoriano.ghtml|title=Presidente do Santos diz que renegociará contrato de zagueiro equatoriano|trans-title=Santos president says that he will renegotiate contract of Ecuadorian stopper|publisher=[[Globo Esporte]]|language=pt-BR|date=8 June 2018|accessdate=7 March 2019}}</ref> he joined the club in August, after his 18th birthday.

===Boavista===
On 1 October 2020, Porozo was announced at [[Primeira Liga]] side [[Boavista F.C.|Boavista]], agreeing to a five-year contract,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.esporteinterativo.com.br/futebolbrasileiro/Boavista-de-Portugal-anuncia-a-contratao-de-Jackson-Porozo-ex-Santos-20201001-0020.html|title=Boavista, de Portugal, anuncia a contratação de Jackson Porozo, ex-Santos|trans-title=Boavista, from Portugal, announce the signing of Jackson Porozo, formerly of Santos|publisher=[[Esporte Interativo]]|language=pt-BR|date=1 October 2020|accessdate=2 October 2020}}</ref> for a total fee of [[Euro|€]]500,000; Santos received 80% of the value, and also retained a 16% over a future sale.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com.br/futebol/artigo/_/id/7308642/veja-os-detalhes-da-saida-de-porozo-do-santos-para-o-boavista|title=Veja os detalhes da saída de Porozo do Santos para o Boavista|trans-title=See the details of the departure of Porozo from Santos to Boavista|publisher=[[ESPN Brasil]]|language=pt-BR|date=18 August 2020|accessdate=2 October 2020}}</ref>

===Troyes===
On 24 June 2022, Porozo signed for [[Ligue 1]] side [[ES Troyes AC|Troyes]] on a five-year contract.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Bakhkhar |first=Anas |date=24 June 2022 |title=L'ESTAC signe Jackson Porozo |url=https://www.footmercato.net/a416319332738589134-lestac-signe-jackson-porozo |access-date=26 June 2022 |website={{Interlanguage link|Foot Mercato|lt=Foot Mercato|fr|Foot Mercato}} |language=fr}}</ref>

==International career==
On 14 February 2017, Porozo was called up to [[Ecuador national under-17 football team|Ecuador under-17s]] for [[2017 South American U-17 Championship|the year]]'s [[South American U-17 Championship]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.conmebol.com/pt-br/equador-confirma-lista-de-convocados-para-o-sul-americano-sub-17|title=Equador confirma lista de convocados para o Sul-Americano Sub 17|trans-title=Ecuador confirm list for the South American under-17|publisher=[[CONMEBOL]]|language=pt-BR|date=14 February 2017|accessdate=7 March 2019}}</ref> being a starter during the tournament and scoring a goal against [[Uruguay national under-17 football team|Uruguay]] on 26 February. On 2 January 2019, he was included in [[Jorge Célico]]'s 23-man list of [[Ecuador national under-20 football team|the under-20s]] for the [[2019 South American U-20 Championship]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ecuafutbol.org/web/noticia.php?idn=52974&idc=|title=Prof. Jorge Célico da nómina de la Tri para el Sudamericano Sub-20|trans-title=Manager Jorge Célico gives the list of ''Tri'' for the South American U-20|publisher=[[Ecuadorian Football Federation]]|language=es|date=2 January 2019|accessdate=7 March 2019|archive-date=20 January 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190120093644/http://www.ecuafutbol.org/web/noticia.php?idn=52974&idc=|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Porozo was an undisputed starter during the competition, being included in the tournament's best eleven as his side was crowned champions.<ref name="CONMEBOL2">{{cite web|url=http://www.conmebol.com/es/el-11-ideal-del-sudamericano-sub-20-chile-2019|title=El 11 ideal del Sudamericano Sub 20 – Chile 2019|trans-title=The best eleven of Sudamericano Under-20 – Chile 2019|publisher=CONMEBOL|language=es|date=12 February 2019|accessdate=7 March 2019}}</ref> On 12 March 2019, he was called up to the [[Ecuador national football team|full side]] for friendlies against [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]] and [[Honduras national football team|Honduras]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/futbol-ecuador-amistosos-idLTAKBN1QT2T3|title=Ecuador convoca a figuras consagradas y a dos campeones sub-20 para los amistosos ante EEUU y Honduras|trans-title=Ecuador call up known figures and two under-20 champions for the friendlies against USA and Honduras|publisher=[[Reuters]]|language=es|date=12 March 2019|accessdate=13 March 2019}}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref>

Porozo made his full international debut on 10 September 2019, coming on as a late [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] for [[Félix Torres (Ecuadorian footballer)|Félix Torres]] in a 3–0 win against [[Bolivia national football team|Bolivia]] in [[Cuenca, Ecuador|Cuenca]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.atribuna.com.br/esportes/santosfc/jackson-porozo-vibra-com-estreia-pela-sele%C3%A7%C3%A3o-equatoriana-lembrarei-por-toda-minha-vida-1.66969|title=Jackson Porozo vibra com estreia pela seleção equatoriana: 'Lembrarei por toda minha vida'|trans-title=Jackson Porozo celebrates his debut for the Ecuador national team: 'I will remember it for my whole life'|publisher=A Tribuna|language=pt-BR|date=11 September 2019|accessdate=10 October 2019}}</ref>
