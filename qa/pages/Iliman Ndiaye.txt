'''Iliman Cheikh Baroy Ndiaye''' (born 6 March 2000) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as an [[Midfielder#Attacking midfielder|attacking midfielder]] for [[Premier League|premiership]] club [[Sheffield United F.C.|Sheffield United]]. Born in France, he plays for the [[Senegal national football team|Senegal national team]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://archive.ph/wip/KSdhT|title=Iliman Ndiaye's extraordinary rise from Sunday League football to Qatar World Cup}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/dec/03/iliman-ndiayes-rise-from-sunday-league-to-senegal-world-cup-ace|title=Iliman Ndiaye’s rise from Sunday league to Senegal World Cup ace|publisher=theguardian.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/senegal/story/4826215/senegals-iliman-ndiaye-is-a-great-world-cup-story|title=Iliman Ndiaye's remarkable World Cup journey with Senegal is just the beginning|publisher=espn.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://archive.ph/nnST7|title=How Senegal’s Iliman Ndiaye went from unused Sheffield United substitute to the World Cup in 14 months|publisher=theathletic.com (Archived)}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11096/12760028/iliman-ndiaye-sheffield-united-and-senegal-forward-is-a-special-talent-who-went-from-non-league-to-world-cup|title=Iliman Ndiaye: Sheffield United and Senegal forward is a special talent who went from non-League to World Cup|publisher=skysports.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thestar.co.uk/sport/football/sheffield-united/sheffield-united-he-had-three-bloody-lungs-how-senegals-iliman-ndiaye-reached-the-world-cup-3925034|title='He had three bloody lungs' - ==Club career==
Ndiaye began playing football with the French clubs Rouen Sapins, [[FC Rouen|Rouen]], and [[Olympique de Marseille|Marseille]] before moving to Senegal with [[AS Dakar Sacré-Cœur|Dakar Sacré-Cœur]]. His father moved to England and Ndiaye joined him where he joined the youth side of [[Boreham Wood F.C.|Boreham Wood]], also playing Sunday League matches with [[Rising Ballers Kensington F.C.|Rising Ballers]] on the side.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/dec/03/iliman-ndiayes-rise-from-sunday-league-to-senegal-world-cup-ace|title=Iliman Ndiaye’s rise from Sunday league to Senegal World Cup ace|first=Will|last=Unwin|date=3 December 2022|via=The Guardian}}</ref> On 17 November 2017, Ndiaye signed his first contract with [[Boreham Wood F.C.|Boreham Wood]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.borehamwoodfootballclub.co.uk/uncategorized/iliman-ndiaye-signs-pro-deal/ |title=ILIMAN NDIAYE SIGNS PRO DEAL|date=21 November 2017}}</ref> On 31 August 2019, he signed with [[Sheffield United F.C.|Sheffield United]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://wiwsport.com/2019/08/31/mercato-iliman-ndiaye-signe-a-sheffield-united-pour-trois-ans/|title=Mercato: Iliman Ndiaye signe à Sheffield United pour trois ans - Mercato|date=31 August 2019}}</ref> He joined [[Hyde United F.C.|Hyde United]] on loan for the latter half of the 2019–20 season.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thestar.co.uk/sport/football/sheffield-united/free-scoring-midfielder-sheffield-united-squad-first-time-who-iliman-ndiaye-3110603|title=A free-scoring midfielder in the Sheffield United squad for the first time - who is Iliman Ndiaye?|website=www.thestar.co.uk}}</ref> He made his professional debut with Sheffield United as a late substitute in a 5–0 [[Premier League]] loss to [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] on 14 March 2021.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://uk.soccerway.com/matches/2021/03/14/england/premier-league/leicester-city-fc/sheffield-united-fc/3344407/|title=Leicester City vs. Sheffield United - 14 March 2021 - Soccerway|website=uk.soccerway.com}}</ref> On his full Sheffield United debut, he scored twice against [[Peterborough United F.C.|Peterborough United]] on 11 September in a 6–2 win.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58444600 |title=Sheff Utd 6-2 Peterborough |publisher=[[BBC]] |date=11 September 2021 |access-date=13 September 2021}}</ref> On 3 March 2023 in the FA Cup he scored in the 79th minute against [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] to secure victory and ensure Sheffield United's place in the quarter-finals.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.theguardian.com/football/2023/mar/01/sheffield-united-tottenham-fa-cup-fifth-round-match-report |title= Sheffield United stun Tottenham in FA Cup thanks to Iliman Ndiaye’s solo goal |publisher=[[The Guardian]] | date= 1 March 2023 |access-date=2 March 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
Ndiaye was born in France to a Senegalese father and French mother.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.seneweb.com/blogs/dakartimes/le-futur-lionel-messi-est-t-il-senegalais_b_4.html|title=Le futur Lionel Messi est-t-il Sénégalais ? &#124; Seneweb Blogs|date=14 March 2021|website=dakartimes}}</ref> He debuted with the [[Senegal national football team|Senegal national team]] in a 3–1 [[2023 Africa Cup of Nations qualification]] win over [[Benin national football team|Benin]] in the 67th minute when he was substituted on for [[Boulaye Dia]] on 4 June 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dakaractu.com/Iliman-Ndiaye-apres-Senegal-Benin-3-1-J-ai-eu-des-frissons-quand-les-supporters-ont-crie-mon-nom_a219800.html|title=Iliman Ndiaye après Sénégal-Bénin (3-1) : " J'ai eu des frissons quand les supporters ont crié mon nom! "|website=DAKARACTU.COM}}</ref> Ndiaye scored his first international goal against Mozambique in the AFCON Qualifiers on the 24th March 2023. Ndiaye scored the third goal in a 5-1 victory.
