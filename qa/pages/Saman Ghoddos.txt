'''Saman Ghoddos'''{{NoteTag|His full name is '''Seyed Saman Ghoddoos''' in Iran<ref name=fifalist/>}} ({{lang-fa|سامان قدّوس}}; born 6 September 1993) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as an [[attacking midfielder]], [[Striker (association football)|striker]] or [[Winger (association football)|winger]] for {{English football updater|Brentfor}} club [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]] and the [[Iran national football team|Iran national team]].

Born in Sweden to [[Swedish Iranians|Iranian parents]], he initially played for the [[Sweden national football team|Sweden national team]], before switching allegiance to Iran in 2017.

==Club career==
===Östersund===
====2016 season====
Saman Ghoddos scored 10 league goals in his first season in the [[Allsvenskan]] for [[Östersunds FK]], including their first top-flight goal in his Allsvenskan debut on 4 April 2016 against [[Hammarby Fotboll|Hammarby IF]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://svenskfotboll.se/allsvenskan/statistikligor/?scr=sl|title=Allsvensk statistik — svenskfotboll.se|website=svenskfotboll.se}}</ref> He scored a brace at his hometown club [[Malmö FF]] on 22 October in [[Swedbank Stadion]]. After his good performances in the [[2016 Allsvenskan|2016 season]], Ghoddos drew interest from German club [[Hertha Berlin]] and Dutch club [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bz-berlin.de/berlin-sport/hertha-bsc/holt-hertha-jetzt-schwedens-offensivstar-ghoddos|title=Holt Hertha jetzt Schwedens Offensivstar Ghoddos?|date=28 December 2016 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogg.fotbollskanalen.se/sillybloggen/2016/12/28/ajax-ryktas-visa-intresse-for-ghoddos-inte-kommit-nagot-bud/|title=Ajax ryktas visa intresse för Ghoddos: "Inte kommit något bud" - Sillybloggen|website=blogg.fotbollskanalen.se}}</ref>

====2017 season====
Ghoddos started the [[2017 Allsvenskan|2017 season]] in fine form, leading Östersund to the [[Svenska Cupen|Swedish Cup]] final with six goals in six games, including an acrobatic goal in the quarter-final against [[Trelleborgs FF]] and a brace in the semi-final against [[BK Häcken|Häcken]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fotbollskanalen.se/svenska-cupen-1/tv-ostersund-till-cupfinal---ghoddos-sankte-hacken-stolt-over-killarna/|title=TV: Östersund till cupfinal - Ghoddos sänkte Häcken: "Stolt över killarna"|website=fotbollskanalen|accessdate=21 November 2022}}</ref> He scored his seventh domestic cup goal of the season in the [[2017 Svenska Cupen Final|final]], leading Östersund to the second qualifying round of the [[2017–18 UEFA Europa League]] with manager [[Graham Potter]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://svenskfotboll.se/cuper-och-serier/arkiv/cuper-serier/2017/04/cupfinal2017/|title=Svenska Cupen, Slutspel Matchinformation: Östersund mot IFK Norrköping FK 2017-04-13 (4-1) — svenskfotboll.se|website=svenskfotboll.se}}</ref>

In his [[UEFA]] debut, Ghoddos scored a goal and provided an assist for a 2–0 win against [[Galatasaray S.K. (football)|Galatasaray]] on 13 July 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2018/matches/round=2000887/match=2021836/index.html|title=UEFA Europa League - Östersund-Galatasaray|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In the [[2017–18 UEFA Europa League qualifying phase and play-off round|return leg]], Ghoddos won a penalty with a dribble past [[Fernando Muslera]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2018/matches/round=2000887/match=2021876/index.html|title=UEFA Europa League - Galatasaray-Östersund|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In a league match on 13 August, he scored a long-range goal from 40 meters out against Hammarby IF in [[Stockholm]]'s [[Tele2 Arena]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.aftonbladet.se/a/k57qX|title=Graham Potter: "Visar bara hur dum jag är"|website=www.aftonbladet.se|date=13 August 2017 |accessdate=21 November 2022}}</ref> On 24 August, Ghoddos scored two goals in the [[UEFA Europa League|Europa League]] play-offs against [[PAOK FC|PAOK]] to qualify his team to the group stages of the competition. He scored the club's first tournament proper goal in their [[2017–18 UEFA Europa League group stage|group stage]] debut on 14 September.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.expressen.se/sport/fotboll/har-chockar-ghoddos-zorya-malskytt-igen/|title=Östersund tar ledningen mot Zorya Luhansk|website=www.expressen.se|accessdate=21 November 2022}}</ref> He won the attacker of the year award at the end of the [[2017 Allsvenskan]] season.<ref name="2017allsvenskan">{{Cite web|url=https://www.fotbollskanalen.se/allsvenskan/tv-ghoddos-arets-anfallare-i-allsvenskan-gatt-helt-okej-i-ar/|title=TV: Ghoddos årets anfallare i allsvenskan: "Gått helt okej i år"|website=fotbollskanalen|accessdate=21 November 2022}}</ref> On 23 November, Ghoddos scored the winning goal to send his club to the [[2017–18 UEFA Europa League knockout phase|knockout phase]] of the Europa League.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2017/11/23/europa-league-underdogs-ostersund-knock-out-phase-english-coach/|title=Europa League underdogs Ostersund through to knock-out phase under English coach Graham Potter|date=23 November 2017|newspaper=The Daily Telegraph|access-date=28 August 2019}}</ref>

===Amiens===
On 23 August 2018, Ghoddos transferred to French [[Ligue 1]] side [[Amiens SC]] signing a five-year contract. The transfer fee paid to Östersund was reported as 40 million [[Swedish krona|SEK]] (about €3.8 million) plus bonuses which would make the transfer the third-most expensive in Allsvenskan history after [[Alexander Isak]] and [[Zlatan Ibrahimović]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Thedéen |first1=Jesper |title=Klart: Ghoddos till Amiens - skriver femårskontrakt |url=https://www.fotbollskanalen.se/franska-ligan/klart-ghoddos-till-amiens---skriver-femarskontrakt/ |website=fotbollskanalen |access-date=24 August 2018 |language=sv |date=23 August 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Här skriver Ghoddos på för den franska klubben |url=https://www.expressen.se/sport/fotboll/allsvenskan/har-skriver-ghoddos-pa-for-den-franska-klubben/ |access-date=24 August 2018 |work=Expressen |date=23 August 2018 |language=sv}}</ref> However, the transfer was put in doubt by [[La Liga]] newcomers [[SD Huesca]] contacting [[FIFA]] to enquire about the validity of their own agreement to sign Ghoddos. Ghoddos had signed a contract with the Spanish club which also had a written agreement with Östersund. Previously, [[Stade Rennais F.C.|Stade Rennais]] had decided against signing Ghoddos.<ref>{{cite news |title=Transferts : Saman Ghoddos à Amiens, oui mais... |url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Transferts-saman-ghoddos-a-amiens-oui-mais/933168 |access-date=24 August 2018 |newspaper=L'Équipe |date=24 August 2018 |language=fr}}</ref>

Ghoddos made his competitive debut for Amiens on 25 August 2018 in the Ligue 1 4–1 home win over [[Stade de Reims|Reims]], scoring a goal for Amiens in the 58th minute. He thus became the first Iranian player to play in [[Ligue 1]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.ligue1.com/ligue1/feuille_match/84630 |title=Ligue1.com - French Football League - Ligue 1 Conforama - Season 2018/2019 - Week 3 - Amiens SC / Stade de Reims |website=www.ligue1.com |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180826043831/https://www.ligue1.com/ligue1/feuille_match/84630 |archive-date=26 August 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.persianfootball.com/news/2018/08/24/saman-ghoddos-joins-ligue-1-club-amiens/|title=Saman Ghoddos joins Ligue 1 club Amiens – PersianFootball.com|accessdate=21 November 2022}}</ref>

On 29 August 2019, FIFA suspended Ghoddos from all games for four months plus a €4 million fine for his failed transfer to Huesca.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fotbollskanalen.se/spanien/beskedet-ghoddos-tvingas-bota-over-40-miljoner-kronor/|title=Beskedet: Ghoddos tvingas böta över 40 miljoner kronor|website=fotbollskanalen|accessdate=21 November 2022}}</ref> On 10 November 2020, the [[Court of Arbitration for Sport]] partially upheld the ruling with the fine being removed but Östersund being banned from signing new players for the next two transfer windows.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-soccer-cas-ghoddos-idUKKBN27Q2WR|title=Iran's Ghoddos and Ostersunds avoid fine in transfer dispute - CAS|newspaper=Reuters|date=10 November 2020}}</ref>

====Loan to Brentford====
On 21 September 2020, Ghoddos joined English [[EFL Championship|Championship]] side [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]] on a one-year loan deal with a subsequent option for a two-year permanent deal.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2020/september/saman-ghoddos-signs-for-brentford/|title = Saman Ghoddos signs for Brentford}}</ref> He made his club debut on 1 October 2020 with an assist to [[Marcus Forss]] in an [[2020–21 EFL Cup|EFL Cup]] match against [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/54298220|title = Benrahma scores twice to knock out Fulham|work = BBC Sport}}</ref> He scored his first goal for the club and provided an assist on 9 January 2021 in an [[2020–21 FA Cup|FA Cup]] match against [[Middlesbrough F.C.|Middlesbrough]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/Brentford-vs-Mboro/stats/440130|title=The FA Cup (Sky Sports)|website=Sky Sports|accessdate=21 November 2022}}</ref>

===Brentford===
On 14 January 2021, Ghoddos completed a permanent move to Brentford, with a contract that runs until the summer of 2023 with the option for an extra year.<ref>{{cite web |title=Saman Ghoddos completes permanent Brentford move |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2021/january/saman-ghoddos-makes-permanent-brentford-switch/ |publisher=Brentford F.C. |date=14 January 2021}}</ref> He scored his first league goal for the club on 20 January 2021 against [[Luton Town F.C.|Luton Town]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/55642312|title = Bees defeat Luton and move up to third|work = BBC Sport}}</ref> In May 2021, Ghoddos and Brentford clinched promotion to the [[2021–22 Premier League|Premier League]], the first top-flight appearance for the club in 74 years.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.persianfootball.com/news/2021/05/29/brentford-promoted-to-premier-league-after-74-years/|title=Brentford promoted to Premier League after 74 years – PersianFootball.com|accessdate=21 November 2022}}</ref> He made his Premier League debut on 21 August 2021 against [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]] at [[Selhurst Park]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/58193415|title = Brentford hold Palace to goalless draw|work = BBC Sport}}</ref> Ghoddos scored his first goal in the Premier League on 30 October 2021 against [[Burnley F.C.|Burnley]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.burnleyexpress.net/sport/football/burnley-fc/burnley-3-brentford-1-burnley-seal-first-premier-league-win-of-the-season-3438729|title=Here's how Burnley won their first Premier League game of the season|date=30 October 2021|website=www.burnleyexpress.net|accessdate=21 November 2022}}</ref> On 14 May 2023, Brentford announced that Ghoddos would be departing the club at the end of the [[2022–23 Brentford F.C. season|2022–23 season]].<ref>https://www.brentfordfc.com/en/news/article/first-team-saman-ghoddos-to-leave-brentford</ref>

==International career==
===Sweden===
Due to his [[Swedish Iranians|Iranian heritage]], Ghoddos was eligible to be called up by both [[Iran national football team|Iran]] and [[Sweden national football team|Sweden]]. In December 2016, Ghoddos was called up by Sweden manager [[Janne Andersson]] for friendlies against [[Ivory Coast national football team|Ivory Coast]] and [[Slovakia national football team|Slovakia]]. He made his debut against the Ivory Coast on 8 January 2017 and scored his first international goal for Sweden in a 6–0 win over Slovakia on 12 January 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/lineups/_/gameId/469147|title=Sweden vs. Ivory Coast - Football Match Line-Ups - January 8, 2017 - ESPN|website=ESPN.com|accessdate=21 November 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eurosport.com/football/international-friendlies/2017/live-sweden-slovakia_mtc917116/live.shtml|title=LIVE Sweden - Slovakia - International friendlies - 12 January 2017|date=12 January 2017}}</ref>

Blågult|website=www.aftonbladet.se|date=27 September 2017 |accessdate=21 November 2022}}</ref> However, on 29 September, the [[Swedish Football Association]] released a statement on their website stating Ghoddos had declined the offer, choosing to play for Iran instead.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://svenskfotboll.se/landslag/herrar/arkiv/landslag/2017/09/ghoddos-tackar-nej-till-svenska-landslaget/|title=Ghoddos tackar nej till svenska landslaget|language=sv|publisher=Swedish Football Association}}</ref> He made his debut for Iran in a 2–0 friendly victory against [[Togo national football team|Togo]] on 5 October 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tarafdari.com/خبر/814953/ترکیب-تیم-ملی-ایران-برای-دیدار-با-توگو-؛-حضور-کاوه-رضایی-و-نیمکت-نشینی-بیرانوند|title=ترکیب تیم ملی ایران برای دیدار با توگو ؛ حضور کاوه رضایی و نیمکت team|Panama]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tarafdari.com/خبر/841417/ایران-2-1-پاناما؛-ضعف-هایی-که-باید-ترمیم-شوند|title=ایران 2-1 پاناما؛ ضعف هایی که باید ترمیم شوند|publisher=Tarafdari|language=fa|date=9 November 2017|access-date=9 November 2017}}</ref>

On 13 May 2018, Ghoddos was named in Queiroz's preliminary squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.persianfootball.com/news/2018/05/13/35-named-in-irans-preliminary-world-cup-squad/|title=35 named in Iran's preliminary World Cup squad|website=www.persianfootball.com}}</ref> In an interview with [[Olof Lundh]] in May 2018, Ghoddos stated that if the Swedish federation had contacted him earlier, it's very likely for him to have chosen to represent Sweden, though he does not regret his decision to play for Iran.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fotbollskanalen.se/lundhs-podcast/ghoddos-hade-forbundet-ringt-tidigare-ar-det-valdigt-stor-chans-att-jag-hade-v/|title=Ghoddos: "Hade förbundet ringt tidigare är det väldigt stor chans att jag hade valt Sverige"|website=fotbollskanalen|accessdate=21 November 2022}}</ref> Club teammate [[Emil Krafth]] later added that Ghoddos told him he wanted to play for Sweden.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.aftonbladet.se/sportbladet/fotboll/a/0Ed4A6/sjalvklart-hade-ghoddos-velat-spela-for-sverige|title = "Självklart hade Ghoddos velat spela för Sverige"| date=14 October 2018 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fotbollskanalen.se/sverige/landslagsstjarnan-om-att-sverige-missade-ghoddos-han-ville-spela-i-sverige/|title=Landslagsstjärnan om att Sverige missade Ghoddos: "Han ville spela i Sverige"|website=fotbollskanalen|accessdate=21 November 2022}}</ref> He was named in Iran's final 23-man squad for the 2018 World Cup on 4 June 2018.<ref name="fifalist"/> Ghoddos made his World Cup debut against [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]] on 15 June 2018, winning a free kick that led to a goal for Iran’s first World Cup win in twenty years.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44408322|title=World Cup 2018: Iran beat Morocco to win first World Cup finals tie in 20 years|date=15 June 2018|website=BBC Sport|access-date=28 August 2019|first=Michael|last=Emons}}</ref> He was used as a substitute in all three World Cup matches against Morocco, [[Spain national football team|Spain]] and [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/players/player/411644/|title=2018 FIFA World Cup Russia™ - Players - Saman GHODDOS - Saman Ghoddoos - FIFA.com|date=22 June 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180622164739/https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/players/player/411644/ |accessdate=21 November 2022|archive-date=22 June 2018 }}</ref> After the World Cup in November 2018, Ghoddos faced [[conscription]] issues in Iran.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://le11amienois.fr/amiens-sc-saman-ghoddos-prive-de-competition/|title=Amiens SC : Saman Ghoddos privé de compétition|date=16 November 2018}}</ref>

He was named in Queiroz’s final squad for the [[2019 AFC Asian Cup]] on 26 December 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.persianfootball.com/news/2018/12/26/queiroz-names-final-23-for-asian-cup/|title=Queiroz names final 23 for Asian Cup – PersianFootball.com|accessdate=21 November 2022}}</ref> In the team's opening match of the tournament, Ghoddos came on as a second-half substitute and scored the final goal in a 5–0 victory over [[Yemen national football team|Yemen]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.the-afc.com/asiancup/news/group-d-ir-iran-5-0-yemen|title=Group D: IR Iran 5-0 Yemen|publisher=AFC|date=7 January 2019|access-date=8 January 2019}}</ref>

In November 2022, Ghoddos was named in the final squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/iran-world-cup-2022-squad-dragan-skocic-names-his-latest-squad|title=Iran World Cup 2022 squad: Carlos Queiroz announces final 25-man team|author1=Greg LeaContributions from Michael Yokhin|date=21 November 2022|website=fourfourtwo.com|accessdate=21 November 2022}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Ghoddos plays as an [[Midfielder#Attacking midfielder|attacking midfielder]], [[Forward (association football)#Striker|striker]] or [[Midfielder#Winger|winger]], and has been praised for his technical ability and clinical finishing.<ref name="espn"/>

==Outside football==
===Personal life===
Ghoddos was born and raised in [[Malmö]] and speaks [[English language|English]], [[Swedish language|Swedish]], and [[Persian language|Persian]]. His parents were born in Iran and hail from the city of [[Ahvaz]].<ref name="varzesh"/>

===Sponsorship===
He has a contract with American sportswear supplier [[Nike, Inc.|Nike]] and wears the [[Nike Mercurial Vapor|Mercurial Vapor]] line.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fotbollskanalen.se/vm-2018/ghoddos-far-inte-spela-i-nike-skor-inget-politiskt/|title=Ghoddos får inte spela i Nike-skor: "Inget politiskt"|website=fotbollskanalen|accessdate=21 November 2022}}</ref>

===Name===
Ghoddos was born in [[Malmö]] as ''Saman Ghoddos''.<ref name=name1/><ref name=name2/><ref name=name3/><ref name=name4/><ref name=born>{{Cite web|url=https://www.teammelli.com/saman-ghoddos-on-iran-the-world-cup-and-playing-for-the-people/|title=Saman Ghoddos on Iran, the World Cup and 'playing for the people'|date=16 November 2022|accessdate=21 November 2022}}</ref> Upon switching allegiance to [[Iran national football team|Iran]] in 2017, he was given the name ''[[Sayyid|Seyed]] Saman Ghoddoos'' in Iran.<ref name=fifalist/>
