'''Miloš Degenek''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|m|ɪ|l|ɒ|ʃ|_|ˈ|d|ɛ|ɡ|ə|n|ɛ|k}} {{respell|MIL|osh|_|DEG|ə|nek}};<ref>{{Citation|title=Miloš Degenek player profile – Red Star Belgrade web site|date=5 July 2018|url=http://www.crvenazvezdafk.com/en/igraci/prvi-tim/389/milos-degenek|access-date=16 December 2018}}<br /></ref> {{lang-sr-Cyrl|Милош Дегенек}}, {{IPA-sh|mǐloʃ děɡenek|pron}};<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hjp.znanje.hr/index.php?show=search_by_id&id=e1lmXRI%3D|title=mȉo|quote=Mìloš|website=Hrvatski jezični portal|access-date=18 March 2018|language=sh}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://hjp.znanje.hr/index.php?show=search_by_id&id=f1lgUBc%3D&keyword=degenek|title=degènek|quote=Degènek|website=Hrvatski jezični portal|access-date=18 March 2018|language=sh}}</ref> born 28 April 1994) is a professional [[Association football|soccer player]] who plays as a [[defender (association football)|defender]] for [[Major League Soccer]] club [[Columbus Crew]]. Born in Croatia, he represents the [[Australia men's national soccer team|Australia national team]]. He is eligible to play for Australia as his family emigrated to Australia in 2000.

Degenek represented both Serbia and Australia at youth levels before making his senior international debut for [[Australia men's national soccer team|Australia]] against [[England national football team|England]] in 2016.

==Early life==
Degenek was born in 1994 in [[Knin]], Croatia, which was then the capital of the self-proclaimed [[Serbs of Croatia|Serb]] [[proto-state]] [[Republic of Serbian Krajina]]. He lived in Orlić, a village near [[Biskupija]], Croatia, where his father's family is from.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web |title=EKSKLUZIVNO - MILOŠ DEGENEK: Neki ljudi su me oterali iz Srbije! |url=https://www.kurir.rs/sport/fudbal/3047893/ekskluzivno-milos-degenek-neki-ljudi-su-me-oterali-iz-srbije |access-date=2023-02-28 |website=kurir.rs |language=sr}}</ref> His family was part of the [[Serbs of Croatia|Serb population of Croatia]] that fled the [[Croatian War of Independence]] to [[Serbia and Montenegro|Yugoslav]] and [[Republic of Serbia (1992–2006)|Serbian]] capital [[Belgrade]] in 1995 during [[Operation Storm]] where they lived as refugees.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.merkur.de/sport/tsv-1860/tsv-1860-milos-degenek-harte-pruefungen-schule-lebens-5367697.html|title=Flucht, Armut, Bomben: Milos Degenek ist ein Kämpfer |trans-title=Flight, poverty, bombs: Milos Degenek is a fighter|author=Armin Gibis|publisher=[[Münchner Merkur]]|language=de|access-date=6 June 2016|date=21 August 2015}}</ref> In 2000, at six years of age, Degenek and his family emigrated to [[Sydney]], Australia, settling in the suburb of [[Campsie, New South Wales|Campsie]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Degenek's tumultuous journey to the top|url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/y=2017/m=8/news=degenek-s-tumultuous-journey-to-the-top-2905645.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170829113058/http://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/y=2017/m=8/news=degenek-s-tumultuous-journey-to-the-top-2905645.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=29 August 2017|publisher=FIFA Official Website|date=29 August 2017|access-date=23 February 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2018-06-10 |title='The siren, then the bomb shelter' by Milos Degenek - PlayersVoice |url=https://www.athletesvoice.com.au/milos-degenek-siren-then-bomb-shelter/ |access-date=2022-12-01 |website=AthletesVoice |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2016-05-24 |title=Why new Caltex Socceroo Milos Degenek has no fear |url=https://www.myfootball.com.au/news/why-new-caltex-socceroo-milos-degenek-has-no-fear |access-date=2022-12-01 |website=MyFootball |language=en}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Early career===

In November 2012, Degenek was called up to the first team for the first time by manager [[Bruno Labbadia]].<ref>{{cite news|title=VfB-Verteidiger Niedermeier fällt in Mönchengladbach aus|url=https://www.stuttgarter-nachrichten.de/inhalt.vfb-stuttgart-vfb-verteidiger-niedermeier-faellt-in-moenchengladbach-aus.71bf94b3-c56c-44ec-b752-20c056c3cbb5.html|publisher=Stuttgarter Nachrichten|language=de|date=16 November 2012|access-date=23 February 2018}}</ref> Although he was never called up again to the senior team, Degenek spent the rest of the 2012–13 season, playing for the U19 side.<ref>{{cite news|title=Tasci beschwört Treue zum VfB|url=https://www.stuttgarter-nachrichten.de/inhalt.vfb-stuttgart-tasci-beschwoert-treue-zum-vfb.298c6df9-dd8d-4d41-a7be-dfe6c4cfe76d.html|publisher=Stuttgart Nachrichten|language=de|date=18 January 2013|access-date=23 February 2018}}<br>{{cite news|title=Dritte Liga: VfB II: Kramny Belohnt Team Mit Drei Freien Tagen|url=https://www.stuttgarter-nachrichten.de/inhalt.dritte-liga-vfb-ii-kramny-belohnt-team-mit-drei-freien-tagen.d03af3b6-4184-4f11-b6f7-2f2e9eeacff4.html|publisher=Stuttgart Nachrichten|language=de|date=22 April 2013|access-date=3 February 2018}}</ref>

Miloš Degenek made his first appearance for [[VfB Stuttgart II]] on 26 July 2013 in the [[3. Liga]] against [[SV Darmstadt 98]].<ref>{{cite magazine|title= Stroh-Engel rettet zumindest einen Punkt|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/3liga/spieltag/3-liga/2013-14/2/1897101/spielanalyse_vfb-stuttgart-ii-1293_sv-darmstadt-98-98.html|magazine=kicker.de|language=de|access-date=26 July 2013|date=26 July 2013}}</ref> However, Degenek made only 9 appearances in the 2013–14 season, due to being sidelined with injuries.<ref>{{cite news|title=Kramny stellt auf zwei Spitzen um|url=https://www.stuttgarter-nachrichten.de/inhalt.stuttgarter-kickers-kramny-stellt-auf-zwei-spitzen-um.4fe292aa-34c7-4e9f-8f2c-91069d1e9add.html|publisher=Stuttgart Nachrichten|language=de|date=30 November 2013|access-date=23 February 2018}}<br>{{cite news|title=Maletic bleibt wohl in den Niederlanden|url=https://www.stuttgarter-nachrichten.de/inhalt.vfb-stuttgart-ii-maletic-bleibt-wohl-in-den-niederlanden.7ba1ad09-cecd-4709-b09f-5719aef5e921.html|publisher=Stuttgart Nachrichten|language=de|date=20 June 2014|access-date=23 February 2018}}</ref> He continued to be sidelined for most of the 2014–15 season, and as a result would not make a single appearance that season.<ref>{{cite news|title=Die Spannung hochhalten|url=https://www.stuttgarter-zeitung.de/inhalt.stuttgarter-kickers-die-spannung-hochhalten.2086c3a4-18b9-4420-954a-627a1a16f346.html|publisher=Stuttgarter Zeitung|language=de|date=4 October 2014|access-date=23 February 2018}}</ref> At the end of the 2014–15 season, Degenek was among those expected to leave the side.<ref>{{cite news|title=Großer Umbruch beim VfB II|url=https://www.stuttgarter-nachrichten.de/inhalt.zweite-stuttgarter-mannschaft-grosser-umbruch-beim-vfb-ii.05c0eb33-5677-421a-ad69-a970ef34e1a2.html|publisher=Stuttgart Nachrichten|language=de|date=27 May 2015|access-date=23 February 2018}}</ref>

Degenek made his [[Australia men's national soccer team|Australia national team]] debut against [[England national football team|England]] on 27 May 2016, coming on as a second-half substitute. He set up Australia's only goal in a 2–1 loss, his cross from the right forcing English defender [[Eric Dier]] to head into his [[own goal]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2016-05-28/socceroos-lose-to-england-in-friendly/7455640?section=sport|title=England beat Socceroos 2–1 thanks to Marcus Rashford and Wayne Rooney goals|publisher=[[ABC News (Australia)|ABC News]]|date=28 May 2016|access-date=28 May 2016}}</ref> He made his first start a week later, playing a full match in a win over [[Greece national football team|Greece]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://theworldgame.sbs.com.au/article/2016/06/05/composed-degenek-leaves-socceroos-spoilt-choice|title=Composed Degenek leaves Socceroos spoilt for choice|date=5 June 2016|access-date=5 June 2016|publisher=[[The World Game]]}}</ref>

Degenek then received a lot of playing time under the management of Ange Postecoglou.<ref>{{cite news|title=Degenek's Roos Right-Back Chance|url=http://www.fourfourtwo.com.au/news/degeneks-roos-right-back-chance-435047|publisher=Four Four Two Official Website|date=26 August 2016|access-date=23 February 2018}}</ref> On 31 May 2017, it was announced that Degenek made it to the final cut for the [[2017 FIFA Confederations Cup|FIFA Confederations Cup]] squad.<ref>{{cite news|title=Postecoglou names 23-man Australia squad for Confederations Cup|url=http://www.goal.com/en-au/news/postecoglou-names-23-man-australia-squad-for-confederations/1w79w16jwr66n19s0bm1q7crbq|publisher=Goal.com|date=31 May 2017|access-date=23 February 2018}}</ref> Degenek played two times in the tournament, which were against [[Germany national football team|Germany]] and [[Cameroon national football team|Cameroon]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Germany vs. Australia: Final score 3–2 in excellent match at Confederations Cup|url=https://www.sbnation.com/soccer/2017/6/19/15831208/confederations-cup-2017-results-germany-vs-australia-final-score|publisher=SB Nation|date=19 June 2017|access-date=23 February 2018}}<br>{{cite news|title=Confederations Cup: Australia, Cameroon draw 1–1|url=https://www.theaustralian.com.au/sport/football/confederations-cup-australia-cameroon-draw-11/news-story/5a043f521d0688ac0d5130f90eb17c7a|newspaper=The Australian|date=22 June 2017|access-date=23 February 2018}}</ref> However, he was dropped to the substitute bench on the last game of the group stage, as they drew 1–1 against [[Chile national football team|Chile]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Socceroos eye Russia return after Confederations Cup exit|url=https://theworldgame.sbs.com.au/article/2017/06/27/socceroos-eye-russia-return-after-confederations-cup-exit|publisher=The World Game|date=27 June 2017|access-date=23 February 2018}}</ref>

Degenek continued to feature in the [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification – AFC fourth round|AFC fourth round]] against [[Syria national football team|Syria]], where he played both legs, in a 3–2 win on aggregate to qualify for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONCACAF–AFC play-off)|CONCACAF–AFC play-off]] against [[Honduras national football team|Honduras]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Syria 1 – 1 Australia|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41516822|work=BBC Sport|date=5 October 2017|access-date=23 February 2018}}<br>{{cite news|title=Australia 2 – 1 Syria|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41562035|work=BBC Sport|date=10 October 2017|access-date=23 February 2018}}</ref> In the CONCACAF–AFC play-off, Degenek played once in the first leg, as Australia won 3–1 to qualify for the World Cup.<ref>{{cite news|title=Honduras v Australia: Socceroos finally click in best away performance in 0–0 World Cup qualifying draw|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2017-11-11/socceroos-best-away-performance-against-honduras/9141088|publisher=ABC (Australian Broadcasting Corporation)|date=11 November 2017|access-date=23 February 2018}}<br>{{cite news|title=Socceroos beat Honduras 3–1 to qualify for FIFA World Cup in Russia thanks to Mile Jedinak|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2017-11-15/socceroos-v-honduras/9154548|publisher=ABC (Australian Broadcasting Corporation)|date=15 November 2017|access-date=23 February 2018}}</ref>

In May 2018 he was named in Australia's 23-man [[Australia national soccer team|squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite news|title=Revealed: Every World Cup 2018 Squad – Final 23-Man Lists|url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/amp/news/revealed-every-world-cup-2018-squad-23-man-preliminary-lists/oa0atsduflsv1nsf6oqk576rb|publisher=Goal.com|date=2 June 2018}}</ref> However, Degenek did not play in any of the three group stage matches, as Australia were eventually eliminated from the tournament.<ref>{{cite news|title=Bert has bolted – so who were the winners and losers?|url=https://www.smh.com.au/sport/soccer/fifa-world-cup-2018/bert-has-bolted-so-who-were-the-winners-and-losers-20180630-p4zoom.html|newspaper=Sydney Morning Herald|date=30 June 2018|access-date=26 July 2018}}</ref>

On 30 December 2018, Degenek scored his first international goal for the Australian national team during an international friendly against [[Oman national football team|Oman]] in the [[United Arab Emirates|UAE]]. The goal came from a near post flick, as Degenek redirected a low [[corner kick]] from [[Chris Ikonomidis]]. The ball deflected over the defenders and found its way into the net on the far post side of the goal. In a post-match interview, Degenek acknowledged that the goal involved a "fair bit of luck," but also expressed that it held special significance for him.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.abc.net.au/news/2018-12-31/socceroos-beat-oman-5-0-in-asian-cup-warm-up/10674684 | title = Socceroos thrash Oman 5–0 in Asian Cup warm-up in Dubai | publisher= [[ABC News (Australia)|ABC News]] | date=31 December 2018 | access-date = 31 December 2018 }}</ref>

During the quarterfinals of the [[2019 AFC Asian Cup]], Degenek made an errant back-pass to goalkeeper [[Mathew Ryan]] that proved costly. [[United Arab Emirates national football team|United Arab Emirates]] striker [[Ali Mabkhout]] was able to intercept the pass and score the only goal in a 1–0 defeat, knocking the defending champion [[Australia men's national soccer team|Socceroos]] out of the tournament.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2019-01-25 |title=The Socceroos were beaten 1-0 by the UAE in the Asian Cup quarter-finals |url=https://www.foxsports.com.au/football/asian-cup/asian-cup-2019-socceroos-vs-uae-quarterfinal-live-coverage/news-story/97e07dfff81c8591e830f034efa7b189 |access-date=2022-12-01 |website=Fox Sports |language=en}}<br>{{Cite web |date=2019-01-25 |title=Australia out of Asian Cup as UAE pounce on Milos Degenek error |url=http://www.theguardian.com/football/2019/jan/25/asian-cup-report-australia-uae-south-korea-qatar-son-heung-min-spurs |access-date=2022-12-01 |website=the Guardian |language=en}}</ref>

On 8 November 2022, Degenek was selected to his second World Cup tournament, being named in Australia's 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-11-08 |title=SOCCEROOS SQUAD ANNOUNCED: FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022™ {{!}} Socceroos |url=https://www.socceroos.com.au/news/socceroos-squad-announced-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022tm |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230626035806/https://www.socceroos.com.au/news/socceroos-squad-announced-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022tm |archive-date=2023-06-26 |access-date=2023-06-26 |website=www.socceroos.com.au |language=en}}</ref> He made his World Cup debut against [[France national football team|France]], coming on as a second-half substitute in a 4–1 defeat. Degenek would go on to play in all four of Australia's World Cup matches, losing in the round of 16 to eventual champions [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-12-03 |title=Argentina vs. Australia Qatar 2022 |url=https://www.fifa.com/fifaplus/en/match-centre/match/17/255711/285073/400128131 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230626041119/https://www.fifa.com/fifaplus/en/match-centre/match/17/255711/285073/400128131 |archive-date=2023-06-26 |access-date=2023-06-26 |website=FIFA.com}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Degenek has an older brother, Đorđe Degenek.<ref name="Degenek Djordje">{{cite news|url=https://sport.blic.rs/fudbal/evropski-fudbal/potresna-zivotna-prica-junaka-zvezde-degenek-od-izbeglice-proteranog-u-oluji-do/74g556f|title=Potresna životna priča junaka Zvezde Degenek, od izbeglice proteranog u "Oluji" do heroja borbe za Ligu šampiona (in Serbian)|date=30 August 2018|access-date=16 December 2018}}</ref> In addition to speaking Serbian, Degenek speaks English and German; In 2018 after joining Yokohama F. Marinos, he was learning Japanese.<ref>{{cite news|title=Caltex Socceroo's harsh lesson in J-League|url=https://www.socceroos.com.au/news/caltex-socceroos-harsh-lesson-j-league|publisher=Socceroos Website|date=18 February 2017|access-date=23 February 2018}}</ref>

Degenek used his salary to help his parents and brother, quoting: "My family supported me when I was little. Now I support them."<ref>{{cite news|title=Löwe Degenek: Vom Amateur zum Nationalspieler|url=http://www.bild.de/sport/fussball/2-bundesliga/loewe-degenek-arbeitet-an-seinen-naechsten-traeumen-46644398.bild.html|publisher=Bild.de|language=de|date=5 July 2016|access-date=23 February 2018}}</ref> 

Degenek supports [[Red Star Belgrade]],<ref>{{cite news|title=Из Книна На Маракану, Дегенек: Навијам за Звезду, али...|url=http://www.zurnal.rs/fudbal/zvezda/28922/iz-knina-na-marakanu-degenek-navijam-za-zvezdu-ali|publisher=Sportski žurnal|language=sr|date=30 December 2015|access-date=23 February 2018}}</ref> and stated that his dream centre-back partner would be Sergio Ramos.<ref>{{Cite web |last=James |first=Euan |date=2021-09-21 |title=‘No feeling like it in club football’: Degenek living out Red Star dreams {{!}} Socceroos |url=https://www.socceroos.com.au/news/no-feeling-it-club-football-degenek-provides-red-star-belgrade-insight |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230709065803/https://www.socceroos.com.au/news/no-feeling-it-club-football-degenek-provides-red-star-belgrade-insight?_ga=2.208668178.1446521802.1688878548-5677592.1688878545 |archive-date=2023-07-09 |access-date=2023-07-09 |website=www.socceroos.com.au |language=en}}</ref>
