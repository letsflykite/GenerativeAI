'''Carlos Henrique Casimiro''' (born 23 February 1992), known as '''Casemiro''',<ref>{{cite news |url=https://brasil.elpais.com/brasil/2017/06/04/deportes/1496603217_401643.html |title="Aonde você vai, Casemiro?" A história do segredo mais bem guardado do Real Madrid|trans-title="Where are you going, Casemiro?" The story of Real Madrid's best kept secret |newspaper=El País |language=pt |date=4 June 2017|access-date=17 November 2017}}</ref> is a Brazilian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[defensive midfielder]] for {{English football updater|ManchesU}} club [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] and [[Captain (association football)|captains]] the [[Brazil national football team|Brazil national team]]. Known for his strength, work rate, and aggressive play, Casemiro is widely regarded as one of the best defensive-midfielders in the world<ref>{{cite news|url= https://www.unitedinfocus.com/news/best-defensive-midfielder-in-the-world-casemiro-named-in-fifpro-world-xi/|title=Best defensive midfielder in the world: Casemiro named in FIFPro World XI}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://sportsbrief.com/football/12269-who-defensive-midfielders-world/|title=Top 20 best defensive midfielders in the world today: who are they?}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://boardroom.tv/casemiro-transfer-man-united/|title=Casemiro to Manchester United: Everything You Need to Know}}</ref>, and of all time.

Casemiro started his career with [[São Paulo FC|São Paulo]], where he scored 11 goals in 111 games. He moved to [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]] in 2013, and also spent the 2014–15 season on [[Loan (association football)|loan]] at [[FC Porto|Porto]]. He won eighteen trophies at Real Madrid, including five [[UEFA Champions League]]s, three [[La Liga]] titles, one [[Copa del Rey]] and three [[FIFA Club World Cup]]s. In 2022, he left Madrid to join Manchester United, winning the [[EFL Cup]] in his first season.

A full international since 2011, Casemiro was in Brazil's squad at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022]] [[FIFA World Cup]]s, as well as four [[Copa América]] tournaments, winning the [[2019 Copa América|2019 edition]].

==Club career==
===São Paulo===
====2003–2010: Youth career====
Born in [[São José dos Campos]], [[São Paulo]], Casemiro was a product of [[São Paulo FC|São Paulo]]'s youth system. From the age of 11 upwards, he acted as [[Captain (association football)|captain]] to its sides;<ref>{{cite web |url=http://pitacodogringo.wordpress.com/2010/09/20/brazil-the-talent-factory-2010/ |title=Brazil: the talent factory 2010 featuring Eron, Casemiro, Lucas Moura, Alan Patrick, Bernardo, Elkeson and Neto Berola |publisher=Pitaco do Gringo |date=20 September 2010|access-date=19 February 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/world-match-centre/news/newsid/217/267/4/index.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131219064111/http://www.fifa.com/world-match-centre/news/newsid/217/267/4/index.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=19 December 2013 |title=Casemiro & Neymar, forever rivals |publisher=FIFA |date=12 September 2013|access-date=19 December 2013}}</ref> he was known as "Carlão" – an augmentative form of his first name in Portuguese – early on, and would be called up for the [[2009 FIFA U-17 World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://uolesporte.blogosfera.uol.com.br/2016/10/15/11-jogadores-que-mudaram-de-nome-durante-a-carreira/?cpmid=fbesp-geral |title=11 jogadores que 'mudaram de nome' durante a carreira|trans-title=11 players who "changed names" during their careers |publisher=Universo Online |language=pt |date=15 October 2016|access-date=23 October 2016}}</ref>

====2010–2013: Consistent success====
Casemiro made his [[Campeonato Brasileiro Série A|Série A]] debut on 25 July 2010, in an away loss against [[Santos FC|Santos]]. He scored his first goal as a professional on 15 August, helping to a 2–2 draw with [[Cruzeiro Esporte Clube|Cruzeiro]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://globoesporte.globo.com/jogo/brasileirao2010/2010-08-15/sao-paulo-cruzeiro.html |title=No finzinho, São Paulo empata com o Cruzeiro em ótimo jogo no Morumbi|trans-title=In the very end, São Paulo draw with Cruzeiro in great game at the Morumbi |publisher=Globo Esporte |language=pt |date=15 August 2010|access-date=19 December 2013}}</ref>

On 7 April 2012, Casemiro scored the first goal of a 2–0 win over [[Mogi Mirim Esporte Clube|Mogi Mirim]] at the [[Arena Barueri]] in [[2012 Campeonato Paulista|that year]]'s [[Campeonato Paulista]] after [[Substitute (association football)|replacing]] the injured [[Fabrício de Souza|Fabrício]] early on, but was later [[Sent off (association football)|sent off]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://veja.abril.com.br/esporte/lider-sao-paulo-freia-mogi-mirim-e-completa-dez-vitorias-seguidas/ |title=Líder, São Paulo freia Mogi Mirim e completa dez vitórias seguidas|trans-title=Leaders, São Paulo halt Mogi Mirim and complete ten consecutive victories |website=Veja |publisher=Abril Comunicações |language=pt |date=7 April 2012|access-date=9 April 2018}}</ref> São Paulo also [[2012 Copa Sudamericana|won]] the [[Copa Sudamericana]], with the player making one substitute appearance in a 5–0 home success against [[Club Universidad de Chile|Universidad de Chile]] in the quarter-final second leg on 7 November.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cooperativa.cl/noticias/deportes/futbol/universidad-de-chile/sao-paulo-termino-con-el-sueno-de-u-de-chile-en-la-copa-sudamericana/2012-11-07/224910.html |title=Sao Paulo terminó con el sueño de U. de Chile en la Copa Sudamericana|trans-title=São Paulo ended the dream of U. de Chile in the South American Cup |publisher=Radio Cooperativa |language=es |date=7 July 2012|access-date=16 March 2016}}</ref>

====2015–2016: Becoming first-choice====
[[File:Shahter-Reak M 2015 (16).jpg|thumb|upright|Casemiro playing for [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]] in 2015]]

On 5 June 2015, Casemiro returned to Real Madrid who activated his buyback clause,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/06/official-announcement-casemiro |title=Official announcement: Casemiro |publisher=Real Madrid CF |date=5 June 2015|access-date=5 June 2015}}</ref> and two months later his contract was extended until 2021.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/08/official-announcement-casemiro |title=Official announcement: Casemiro |publisher=Real Madrid CF |date=31 August 2015|access-date=31 August 2015}}</ref> On 13 March 2016, he scored his first competitive goal for the ''Merengues'', heading home an 89th-minute [[corner kick]] by [[Jesé]] in a 2–1 victory at [[UD Las Palmas|Las Palmas]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/35749297 |title=Las Palmas 1–2 Real Madrid |website=BBC Sport |date=13 March 2016 |access-date=14 March 2016}}</ref> After being mostly a reserve player under [[Rafael Benítez]], Casemiro became first-choice under his successor [[Zinedine Zidane]],<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.marca.com/futbol/real-madrid/2016/04/03/57012708268e3e567b8b464d.html |title=La diferencia es Casemiro|trans-title=Casemiro is the difference |newspaper=Marca |location=Spain |language=es |date=3 April 2016|access-date=30 May 2016}}</ref> and contributed with 11 appearances in [[2015–16 UEFA Champions League|that season's Champions League]]. In the [[2016 UEFA Champions League Final|final]] against [[Atlético Madrid]], he featured the full 120 minutes, as Real Madrid won their 11th title in a [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shoot-out]] after a 1–1 draw.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2016/matches/round=2000638/match=2015789/postmatch/report/index.html |title=Spot-on Real Madrid defeat Atlético in final again |publisher=UEFA |date=28 May 2016|access-date=29 May 2016}}</ref>

====2016–2022: Prolific success====
Casemiro scored four goals in 25 matches in the [[2016–17 La Liga]], helping his team win the league title for the first time in five years.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.laliga.es/noticias/real-madrid-campeon-de-laliga-santander-2016-17 |title=El Real Madrid, campeón de LaLiga Santander 2016/17|trans-title=Real Madrid, LaLiga Santander champions 2016/17 |publisher=La Liga |language=es |date=21 May 2017|access-date=22 May 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/39926301 |title=Malaga 0–2 Real Madrid |website=BBC Sport |date=21 May 2017|access-date=22 May 2017}}</ref> He then scored a long-range strike in the [[2017 UEFA Champions League Final|Champions League final]] against [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]], helping his team to a 4–1 victory.<ref name=UEFA>{{cite web |url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2017/matches/round=2000787/match=2019641/postmatch/report/index.html |title=Majestic Real Madrid win Champions League in Cardiff |publisher=UEFA |date=3 June 2017|access-date=4 June 2017}}</ref> He again found the net on 8 August, putting his team ahead in a 2–1 victory over [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] in the [[2017 UEFA Super Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2017/aug/08/real-madrid-manchester-united-super-cup-match-report |title=Isco goal gives Real Madrid victory over Manchester United in Super Cup |newspaper=The Guardian |location=London |date=8 August 2017|access-date=10 August 2017}}</ref>

During the [[2017–18 UEFA Champions League|2017–18 Champions League]], Casemiro made 12 appearances while scoring one goal,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rcnradio.com/deportes/asi-llegan-las-plantillas-de-real-madrid-y-liverpool-la-final-de-la-champions |title=Así llegan Real Madrid y Liverpool a la final de la Champions|trans-title=That is how Real Madrid and Liverpool arrive to the Champions final |publisher=RCN Radio |language=es |date=25 May 2018|access-date=29 May 2018}}</ref> as Madrid won their third consecutive and 13th overall title in the competition.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2018/matches/round=2000885/match=2021711/postmatch/report/index.html |title=Madrid beat Liverpool to complete hat-trick |publisher=UEFA |date=26 May 2018|access-date=28 May 2018}}</ref> He was a regular starter during the season, as Real Madrid won the [[2019–20 La Liga]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/real-madrid/2020/07/17/5f10db2146163f3c2c8b45d7.html |title=Real Madrid win the longest LaLiga Santander season |date=17 July 2020|access-date=17 July 2020 |newspaper=Marca |location=Spain}}</ref> In August 2021, he extended his contract until June 2025.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Official Announcement: Casemiro |url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2021/08/27/official-announcement-casemiro |publisher=Real Madrid CF |access-date=27 August 2021}}</ref> Casemiro was a starter for Real Madrid when they won their [[2022 UEFA Champions League final|14th Champions League title.]] Casemiro was named Man of the Match, as Real Madrid defeated [[Eintracht Frankfurt]] 2–0 for the [[2022 UEFA Super Cup|2022 Super Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefasupercup/news/0278-15d6268709ab-38bef8ec34df-1000/ |title=Real Madrid 2–0 Frankfurt: Five-star Madrid triumph in Helsinki |publisher=UEFA |date=10 August 2022 |access-date=10 August 2022}}</ref>

Casemiro was highly praised by his manager [[Erik ten Hag]] in January, who commented: "Casemiro is magnificent. In front of the back line, always taking good positions, in and out of position. And he can deal with the ball, he can give the right pass. So, he can accelerate the game and if necessary, he can score."<ref>{{cite news|url= https://www.manutd.com/en/news/detail/erik-ten-hag-reacts-to-man-utd-vs-reading-28-january-2023 |title= Erik ten Hag reacts to Manchester United versus Reading|work=90min|date=28 January 2023 |access-date=29 January 2023}}</ref> On 4 February, Casemiro was given a [[Fouls and misconduct (association football)|red card]] for violent conduct during a match against [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]] for putting his hands around the neck of [[Will Hughes]] during a confrontation which also saw [[Antony (footballer, born 2000)|Antony]] and [[Jeffrey Schlupp]] receive yellow cards. This resulted in a three-match domestic ban; United would go on to win the match 2–1.<ref>{{cite news |last=Stone |first=Simon |title=Manchester United 2-1 Crystal Palace: Marcus Rashford helps hosts to 13th straight home win |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/64436135 |access-date=4 February 2023 |website=BBC Sport |date=4 February 2023}}</ref> On 26 February, Man Utd won the [[EFL Cup]], Casemiro's first trophy at the club and United's first trophy since [[2016–17 UEFA Europa League|2017]], with Casemiro scoring the opening goal in the 33rd minute of the [[2023 EFL Cup final|2-0 victory]] against [[Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United]]. His performance also earned him the [[Alan Hardaker Trophy]]. On 12 March, Casemiro was given a red card for a tackle on [[Carlos Alcaraz (footballer)|Carlos Alcaraz]] in a match against Southampton where referee [[Anthony Taylor (referee)|Anthony Taylor]] overturned his initial yellow card decision for a straight red card after a VAR review, and Casemiro received a four match suspension; Man Utd would go on to draw the match 0-0.<ref>{{cite news |first=Phil |last=McNulty |title=Man Utd 0-0 Southampton: Saints earn draw against 10-man Man Utd |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/64852076 |date=12 March 2023 |access-date=30 May 2023 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> Casemiro would be the only player of the [[2022-23 Premier League]] to receive 2 red cards, and the only Man Utd player to receive a red card for that season.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.premierleague.com/stats/top/players/red_card |title=Premier League Player Stats |access-date=30 May 2023}}</ref>

===Youth teams===
Casemiro scored one goal in seven appearances for the [[Brazil national under-17 football team|Brazil under-17]] team.<ref name="brazilstatsyouth">{{cite web |title=Casemiro |url=https://www.besoccer.com/player/casemiro-92909 |website=besoccer.com |access-date=16 August 2022}}</ref> He played for the [[Brazil national under-20 football team|Brazil under-20]] team at the [[2011 South American U-20 Championship]] and the [[2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/news/y=2011/m=4/news=casemiro-gunning-for-the-title-1422986.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150611210319/http://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/news/y=2011/m=4/news=casemiro-gunning-for-the-title-1422986.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=11 June 2015 |title=Casemiro: We're gunning for the title |publisher=FIFA |date=22 April 2011|access-date=18 July 2011}}</ref> scoring three goals in 15 appearances in total at this level.<ref name="brazilstatsyouth"/>

===Senior team===
[[File:Casemiro.jpg|thumb|upright|Casemiro playing for [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]] in 2018]]

Casemiro made his debut for the [[Brazil national football team|Brazil senior team]] on 14 September 2011, in a 0–0 draw against [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]], aged 19.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://globoesporte.globo.com/futebol/selecao-brasileira/noticia/2011/09/show-de-ronaldinho-no-aquecimento-agita-insosso-0-0-de-brasil-e-argentina.html |title=Bolas na trave não resolvem: Brasil e Argentina ficam no zero em Córdoba|trans-title=Balls in the bar do not solve it: Brazil and Argentina stay blank in Córdoba |publisher=Globo Esporte |language=pt |date=14 September 2011|access-date=19 December 2013}}</ref> He was named by manager [[Dunga]] in the Brazilian squad for the [[2015 Copa América]], but did not play any matches in the quarter-final exit in Chile.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/2557/news/2015/05/05/11441562/oscar-left-out-of-brazils-copa-america-squad |title=Oscar left out of Brazil's Copa America squad |website=Goal |date=5 May 2015|access-date=16 March 2016}}</ref>

On 5 May 2016, he was named among the 23-man list for the [[Copa América Centenario]] to be held in the United States.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://globoesporte.globo.com/futebol/selecao-brasileira/noticia/2016/05/dunga-convoca-selecao-brasileira-para-disputa-da-copa-america.html |title=Dunga convoca Seleção para a Copa América com 7 jogadores olímpicos|trans-title=Dunga calls up ''Seleção'' to the Copa América with seven Olympic players |publisher=Globo Esporte |language=pt |date=5 May 2016|access-date=10 May 2016}}</ref>

In May 2018, Casemiro was selected by manager [[Tite (football manager)|Tite]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/amp/football/44116590 |title=World Cup: Neymar named in Brazil's 23-man squad |website=BBC Sport |date=14 May 2018|access-date=15 May 2018}}</ref> He made his debut in the competition on 17 June, playing 60 minutes in a 1–1 group stage draw to [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44427968 |title=Brazil 1–1 Switzerland |website=BBC Sport |first=Luke |last=Reddy |date=17 June 2018|access-date=17 June 2018}}</ref>

In May 2019, he was included in Brazil's 23-man squad for the [[2019 Copa América]] on home soil.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.football-italia.net/138152/brazil-name-copa-america-squad |title=Brazil name Copa America squad |website=Football Italia |publisher=Tiro Media |date=17 May 2019 |access-date=21 May 2019}}</ref> In the final group game at the [[Arena Corinthians]] against [[Peru national football team|Peru]], he scored his first international goal to open a 5–0 victory, but was also sent off for two yellow cards and suspended from the next match.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Clancy |first1=Conor |title=Casemiro suspended for Brazil's Copa America quarter-final |url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/international-football/2019/06/22/5d0e8e89ca4741e2748b456a.html |access-date=1 July 2019 |newspaper=Marca |location=Madrid |date=22 June 2019}}</ref> He started in Brazil's 3–1 victory over Peru in the [[2019 Copa América Final]] on 7 July, at the [[Maracanã Stadium]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2019/jul/06/copa-america-final-brazil-peru-live |title=Brazil 3–1 Peru |newspaper=The Guardian |location=London |last1=Smyth |first1=Rob |date=7 July 2019 |access-date=8 July 2019}}</ref>

He was named to the [[2021 Copa América]] squad on 9 June 2021.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cbf.com.br/selecao-brasileira/noticias/selecao-masculina/selecao-brasileira-e-convocada-para-a-copa-america-2021 |title=Tite define convocados da Seleção Brasileira para a Copa América 2021 |publisher=Brazilian Football Confederation |date=9 June 2021|access-date=9 June 2021 |language=pt}}</ref> In Brazil's third group match on 23 June, he scored the match–winning goal following a [[Neymar]] corner in injury time, to help his team defeat [[Colombia national football team|Colombia]] 2–1.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/57592206 |title=Casemiro gives Brazil controversial win |website=BBC Sport |date=24 June 2021 |access-date=24 June 2021}}</ref> On 10 July, he started in his nation's 1–0 defeat to rivals Argentina in the [[2021 Copa América Final|final]].<ref name="2021CAFinalGuardian">{{cite news |last=Graham |first=Bryan Armen |date=10 July 2021 |title=Argentina 1–0 Brazil: Copa América final – as it happened |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2021/jul/10/argentina-v-brazil-copa-america-final-live |newspaper=The Guardian |location=London |accessdate=13 July 2021}}</ref>

On 7 November 2022, Casemiro was named in the squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web |title=Seleção Brasileira está convocada para a Copa do Mundo FIFA Qatar 2022 |url=https://www.cbf.com.br/selecao-brasileira/noticias/selecao-masculina/selecao-brasileira-esta-convocada-para-a-copa-do-mundo-fifa-qatar-2022 |publisher=CBF|access-date=7 November 2022 |date=7 November 2022 |language=pt}}</ref> On 28 November, he scored the winning goal in the second group match against Switzerland.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/nov/28/brazil-switzerland-world-cup-group-g-match-report |title=Casemiro stunner ends Switzerland resistance to fire Brazil into last 16 |newspaper=The Guardian |location=London |date=28 November 2022}}</ref> Brazil were eliminated by [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]] in the quarter-finals on 9 December, following a 4–2 penalty shoot-out loss after a 1–1 draw following extra-time, although Casemiro was able to net his spot kick.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2022/dec/09/croatia-v-brazil-world-cup-2022-quarter-final-live-score-update |title=World Cup quarter-final: Croatia 1–1 Brazil (4–2 pens) – as it happened |newspaper=The Guardian |location=London |date=9 December 2022 |access-date=17 December 2022}}</ref>

==Style of play==
