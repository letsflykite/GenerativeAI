'''Paik Seung-ho''' ({{Korean|hangul=백승호|rr=Baek Seung-ho}}; born 17 March 1997) is a South Korean professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[Jeonbuk Hyundai Motors FC|Jeonbuk Hyundai Motors]] and the [[South Korea national football team|South Korea national team]].<ref>{{soccerway|seung-ho-baek/382803}}</ref> Starting playing football since May in 2005 in a football academy, Paik was awarded MVP award in juvenile team and MVP award from [https://www.kfa.or.kr/ KFA(Korea Football Association)] in youth team for the first time at the age of 11. While he played in [[FC Barcelona (youth)|FC Barcelona youth team]] at the age of 13, he was expected to join [[FC Barcelona|FC Barcelona A]] for the first time as Korean player.<ref>{{Cite web|date=24 May 2017|title=[U-20]父가 들려준 '축구천재'의 부활 스토리|url=https://sports.chosun.com/news/ntype.htm?id=201705250100213130015359&servicedate=20170524|access-date=13 April 2021|website=Sports Chosun|language=ko}}</ref>

==Early life==
[[File:2012 2013 - Seungho-Paik - Flickr - Castroquini-FCB.jpg|thumb|upright|left|Paik in 2012]]
In 2009, as a member of [[Seoul]] Daedong Elementary School, he played 18 games in the Elementary Weekend League and scored 30 goals. Since then, he has received attention as an outstanding prospect, winning the 22nd [[Cha Bum-kun|Cha Bum-keun]] Football Grand Prize. In December, he participated in the 1st [[Copa Catalunya|Korea-Catalonia Youth Cup]] in Barcelona, Spain as a member of the South Korean U-14 national football team,<ref>{{Cite web|title=유소년축구 대표, 스페인 카탈루냐컵 출전|url=https://news.naver.com/main/read.nhn?mode=LSD&mid=sec&oid=001&aid=0003003107&sid1=001|access-date=13 April 2021|website=news.naver.com|language=ko}}</ref> and was offered a contract as a result of his impressive performance.<ref>{{Cite web|date=28 January 2019|title=6년 만에 흩어진 바르사 3총사…"정상에서 만나자"|url=https://www.yna.co.kr/view/AKR20170830037700007?input=1195m|access-date=13 April 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190128135809/https://www.yna.co.kr/view/AKR20170830037700007?input=1195m|archive-date=28 January 2019}}</ref> However, he was already scouted by Maetan Middle School, [[Suwon Samsung Bluewings Academy|a youth team of Samsung Bluewings]] in [[Suwon]], and was scheduled to enter, but Samsung Bluewings decided to send Paik Seung-ho to Spain with a long term vision and also to support him.<ref>{{Cite web|last=OSEN|date=27 June 2012|title="수원 이겨라" 바르샤 유스 백승호, 빅버드 찾아 '응원'|url=http://mosen.mt.co.kr/article/G1109424047|access-date=13 April 2021|website=mosen.mt.co.kr|language=ko}}</ref>

Paik Seung-ho played for Maetan Middle School, U-15 youth team of the Suwon Samsung Bluewings until March 2010, before moving to FC Barcelona's Under 13 youth team, Infantil A, in April 2010. In July 2011, Paik signed a five-year contract with FC Barcelona's youth team, leaving him in Barcelona's uniform until the age of 19.<ref>{{Cite web|date=6 July 2011|title='축구신동' 백승호, 바르셀로나 유소년팀과 5년 계약|url=https://www.hankyung.com/sports/article/2011070628617|access-date=13 April 2021|website=hankyung.com|language=ko}}</ref> However, in February 2013, [[FIFA]] suspended six players, including Paik, from FC Barcelona's youth team, for violating Article 19 of the [[FIFA rules|FIFA Rules]]<ref>{{Cite web|last=Kim|first=Yeong-rok|date=25 February 2013|title='바르샤 코리안' 적신호…이승우 이어 백승호-장결희도 출전금지|url=https://sports.donga.com/3/all/20130225/53281791/2|url-status=live|website=Sports Donga}}</ref> that made Paik not participate in FC Barcelona's official matches until January 2016. After the disciplinary action was released in January 2016, he played actively for FC Barcelona Juvenile A, and made his professional debut on 21 February, as a substitute in [[Segunda División B|Segunda División B match]] with [[Levante UD]] in the 45th minute of the second half.<ref>{{Cite web|last=OSEN|date=21 February 2016|title=백승호, 바르사 B 공식 데뷔|url=http://mosen.mt.co.kr/article/G1110357430|access-date=13 April 2021|website=mosen.mt.co.kr|language=ko}}</ref>

==Club career==

===Girona===
He played for Barcelona as a youth player for seven years, but he had difficulty competing in the senior team. On 21 July 2017, he joined [[Girona FC|Girona]] and played for [[CF Peralada|Peralada]], the reserve team of Girona at that time, in the Segunda División B. He made his first team debut on 10 January 2019 in the [[Copa del Rey]].<ref>[http://english.chosun.com/site/data/html_dir/2019/01/11/2019011101258.html Paik Seung-ho Debuts in Spain's Top-Tier League]</ref>

===Darmstadt 98===
On 31 August 2019, Paik signed for [[2. Bundesliga]] side [[SV Darmstadt 98|Darmstadt 98]] on a three-year deal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sv98.de/home/lilien/aktuelles/details/news/sv-98-verpflichtet-seung-ho-paik/?tx_news_pi1%5Bcontroller%5D=News&tx_news_pi1%5Baction%5D=detail&cHash=24d4b9d40dea99e294991854c35bcd3e|title=SV 98 verpflichtet Seung-Ho Paik|language=German|publisher=SV Darmstadt 98|date=31 August 2019|access-date=31 August 2019}}</ref> He played for Darmstadt for one and a half years.

===Jeonbuk Hyundai Motors===
Paik returned to his native South Korea in March 2021, joining [[Jeonbuk Hyundai Motors FC|Jeonbuk Hyundai Motors]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Seung-Ho Paik verlässt den SV Darmstadt 98 Richtung Südkorea |url=https://www.kicker.de/paik-verlaesst-den-sv-darmstadt-richtung-suedkorea-801039/artikel |access-date=30 March 2021 |work=kicker |date=30 March 2021 |language=de}}</ref>

==International career==
He made his [[South Korea national football team|South Korea national team]] debut on 11 June 2019 in a friendly against Iran, as a starter.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[ESPN]]|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/match?gameId=539548|title=South Korea v Iran game report|date=11 June 2019}}</ref>
