'''Rui Pedro dos Santos Patrício''' {{post-nominals|post-noms=[[Order of Merit (Portugal)|ComM]]}} ({{IPA-pt|ˈʁuj pɐˈtɾisju}}; born 15 February 1988) is a Portuguese professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Serie A]] club [[A.S. Roma|Roma]] and the [[Portugal national football team|Portugal national team]]. He is often nicknamed '''"São Patrício"''' (''Saint Patrício'') for his saves for club and country.<ref>{{cite news |title=O Mula de Marrazes que se tornou São Patrício|trans-title=The Mule from Marrazes who became Saint Patrício|url=https://observador.pt/programas/codigo-postal/o-mula-de-marrazes-que-se-tornou-sao-patricio/ |access-date=17 June 2022 |work=[[Observador]] |date=14 June 2021 |language=Portuguese}}</ref>

A product of [[Sporting CP]]'s youth academy, Patricio made his first team debut at the age of 18, and went on to appear in 467 official games. He won five trophies during his 12 years with the club, including two [[Taça de Portugal|Portuguese Cups]], before moving to [[Premier League|England]] to join [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]] in 2018. In 2021, he signed for [[A.S. Roma|Roma]], where he won the [[UEFA Europa Conference League|Europa Conference League]] in his first season.

Patrício earned his first [[Cap (sport)|cap]] for Portugal in 2010, after [[Paulo Bento]]'s appointment as head coach, and his [[List of men's footballers with 100 or more international caps|100th]] in 2021. He represented the nation in three [[FIFA World Cup|World Cups]] and four [[UEFA European Championship|European Championships]], winning the [[UEFA Euro 2016|2016 edition]] of the latter tournament as well as the [[2019 UEFA Nations League Finals|2019 Nations League]].

==Club career==
===Sporting===
Born in the village of Marrazes in [[Leiria]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.maisfutebol.iol.pt/liga/sporting/patricio-inaugura-estatua-em-leiria-que-seja-exemplo-para-mais-novos|title=Patrício inaugura estátua em Leiria: "Que seja exemplo para mais novos"|trans-title=Patrício inaugurates statue in Leiria: "Let it be an example for the young ones"|publisher=Mais Futebol|language=pt|date=22 May 2017|access-date=30 August 2018}}</ref> Patrício played as a [[Forward (association football)#Striker|striker]] at a young age. Reportedly, a [[Sporting CP]] scout was in the area and saw him play [[Goalkeeper (association football)|in goal]], being impressed enough to sign the 12-year-old to the club's youth academy.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.cmjornal.pt/desporto/detalhe/heroi-ja-foi-avancado|title=Herói já foi avançado|trans-title=Hero used to be a forward|newspaper=[[Correio da Manhã (Portugal)|Correio da Manhã]]|language=pt|date=6 August 2009|access-date=7 April 2018}}</ref> He made his [[Primeira Liga]] debut on 19 November 2006, in a 1–0 away win against [[C.S. Marítimo]] in the tenth round: standing in for habitual club and [[Portugal national football team|country]] first-choice [[Ricardo (footballer, born 1976)|Ricardo]], he saved a [[Penalty kick (association football)|penalty kick]] 15 minutes before the end of the game.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.record.xl.pt/arquivo/interior.aspx?content_ild=289099|title=Rui Patrício entra na história|trans-title=Rui Patrício makes history|newspaper=[[Record (newspaper)|Record]]|last=Freitas|first=José Carlos|language=pt|date=20 November 2006|access-date=15 February 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130416114235/http://www.record.xl.pt/arquivo/interior.aspx?content_id=289099|archive-date=16 April 2013}}</ref>
[[File:Rui Patrício.jpg|left|thumb|271x271px|Patrício with Sporting in 2012]]
In the [[2007–08 Primeira Liga|2007–08 season]], after Ricardo's departure to [[Real Betis]], Patrício beat competition from Sporting veteran [[Tiago Ferreira (footballer, born 1975)|Tiago]] and new signing [[Vladimir Stojković]] to become the undisputed starter. He made his [[UEFA Champions League]] debut on 27 November 2007, in a [[2007–08 UEFA Champions League|1–2 group stage loss]] at [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2008/matches/round=15105/match=301234/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Ronaldo underlines United authority|publisher=UEFA|date=27 November 2007|access-date=15 March 2016}}</ref>

During the 2008 off-season, Patrício was the subject of a rumoured transfer to Italian giants [[Inter Milan]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.record.pt/noticia.asp?id=791670&idCanal=24|title=Rui Patrício por Toldo|trans-title=Rui Patrício for Toldo|newspaper=Record|last=Roseiro|first=Bruno|language=pt|date=17 June 2008|access-date=21 June 2008|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080809164349/http://www.record.pt/noticia.asp?id=791670&idCanal=24|archive-date=9 August 2008}}</ref> Nothing came of it, however, and in [[2008 Supertaça Cândido de Oliveira|that year]]'s [[Supertaça Cândido de Oliveira]], against [[FC Porto]], he stopped a [[Lucho González]] penalty in a 2–0 final success,<ref name=SUPERCUP>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/news/newsid=739742.html|title=Djaló double steers Sporting success|publisher=UEFA|date=16 August 2008|access-date=22 October 2013}}</ref> also being an everpresent fixture in [[2008–09 Primeira Liga|the league]].

In the qualifying rounds of the [[2009–10 UEFA Champions League|2009–10 Champions League]], at [[FC Twente]], Sporting were trailing 0–1 in the 94th minute, after a 0–0 tie in the first leg: Patrício rushed to the opposing area for a [[corner kick]], where he went up for a header with [[Nikita Rukavytsya]]. Both players seemed to make contact with the ball, and it was helped into the net for an [[own goal]] via the boot of [[Peter Wisgerhof]] as the latter side qualified for the last games prior to the group stage.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/matches/season=2010/round=2000026/match=2000341/index.html|title=Sporting late show shatters Twente|publisher=UEFA|date=4 August 2009|access-date=5 August 2009}}</ref>

On 20 December 2012, Patrício was awarded Sporting's Footballer of the Year award for a second consecutive year.<ref name="POY12">{{cite news|url=http://www.record.xl.pt/Futebol/Nacional/1a_liga/Sporting//interior.aspx?content_id=795037|title=Vencedores dos prémios Stromp|trans-title=Stromp awards winners|newspaper=Record|language=pt|date=20 December 2012|access-date=23 December 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121226102314/http://www.record.xl.pt/Futebol/Nacional/1a_liga/Sporting/interior.aspx?content_id=795037|archive-date=26 December 2012}}</ref> On 18 October 2014, with the score at 2–1 for his team, he saved a penalty by [[Jackson Martínez]] to help [[2014–15 Taça de Portugal|oust Porto]] from the [[Taça de Portugal]] with a 3–1 win at the ''[[Estádio do Dragão]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.portugoal.net/club-news/77-sporting-dump-porto-out-of-the-portuguese-cup|title=Sporting dump Porto out of the Portuguese Cup|publisher=PortuGOAL|date=18 October 2014|access-date=21 October 2014}}</ref>

Patrício was one of 30 players nominated for the 2016 [[Ballon d'Or]] award alongside [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]] teammates [[Pepe (footballer, born 1983)|Pepe]] and [[Cristiano Ronaldo]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.com/uefa-champions-league/story/2980138/ballon-dor-nominees-cristiano-ronaldo-and-lionel-messi-lead-list-of-30|title=Ballon d'Or: Cristiano Ronaldo and Lionel Messi lead list of 30 nominees|publisher=[[ESPN FC]]|last=Holyman|first=Ian|date=24 October 2016|access-date=3 November 2016}}</ref> On 18 February the following year he appeared in his 400th competitive game with Sporting, putting on a [[Player of the match]] performance in a 1–0 home win against [[Rio Ave F.C.|Rio Ave FC]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sporting.pt/en/news/football/main-team/2017-02-19/sporting-cp-1-rio-ave-0|title=Patrício secures 400th {{sic|nolink=y|appe|rance}} with fantastic performance|publisher=Sporting CP|date=18 February 2017|access-date=19 February 2017}}</ref>

On 15 May 2018, Patrício and several of his teammates, including coaches, were injured following an attack by around 50 supporters of Sporting at the club's training ground after the team [[2017–18 Primeira Liga|finished third in the league]] and missed out on [[2018–19 UEFA Champions League|Champions League qualification]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.abola.pt/Nnh/Noticias/Ver/730529|title=Jesus e jogadores agredidos na Academia|trans-title=Jesus and players assaulted at the Academy|newspaper=[[A Bola]]|language=pt|date=15 May 2018|access-date=16 May 2018|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180517024802/https://www.abola.pt/Nnh/Noticias/Ver/730529|archive-date=17 May 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sportsjoe.ie/football/sporting-lisbon-attack-160186|title=Sporting Lisbon players attacked at training ground after failing to secure Champions League spot|publisher=Sports Joe|last=Kiley|first=Ben|date=15 May 2018|access-date=16 May 2018}}</ref> Despite the events, he and the rest of the team agreed to play in the [[2018 Taça de Portugal Final|Portuguese Cup final]] scheduled for the following weekend,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/amp/football/44142885|title=Sporting Lisbon: Players agree to play Portuguese Cup final after attack|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|date=16 May 2018|access-date=18 May 2018}}</ref> eventually losing 2–1 to [[C.D. Aves]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.ojogo.pt/futebol/taca-portugal/noticias/interior/aves-ganha-a-taca-de-portugal-gracas-a-bis-de-guedes-9360212.html|title=Aves ganha a Taça de Portugal graças a bis de Guedes|trans-title=Aves win Portuguese Cup thanks to Guedes brace|newspaper=[[O Jogo]]|language=pt|date=20 May 2018|access-date=26 July 2018}}</ref>

Patrício's record of 467 official games was the second-highest in Sporting's history, behind only [[Hilário da Conceição]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Gouveia |first1=Ricardo |title=Rui Patrício: sai o jogador, fica uma lenda|trans-title=Rui Patrício: the player leaves, the legend remains|url=https://maisfutebol.iol.pt/sporting/01-06-2018/rui-patricio-sai-o-jogador-fica-uma-lenda-1999-2018 |access-date=19 March 2021 |publisher=Mais Futebol |date=1 June 2018 |language=Portuguese}}</ref>

===Wolverhampton Wanderers===
On 1 June 2018, Patrício submitted a request to terminate his Sporting contract with grounds for just cause, due to the dressing room violence from supporters as well as seeing an €18&nbsp;million move to newly promoted [[Premier League]] team [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]] fall through, after Sporting president [[Bruno de Carvalho]] pulled out of negotiations at the last minute.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.abola.pt/img/carta-rui-patricio-A.pdf|title=Rescisão com justa causa do contrato de trabalho desportivo|trans-title=Just cause termination of sporting work contract|newspaper=A Bola|language=pt|date=31 May 2018|access-date=3 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://kwese.espn.com/football/soccer-transfers/story/3515658/rui-patricio-wants-sporting-deal-terminated-as-jorge-mendes-accused-of-blackmail|title=Rui Patricio wants Sporting deal terminated as Jorge Mendes accused of 'blackmail'|publisher=[[ESPN]]|date=2 June 2018|access-date=3 June 2018}}</ref> He completed the move on 18 June, on a four-year deal,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wolves.co.uk/news/first-team/20180618-wolves-complete-patricio-signing/|title=Wolves complete Patricio signing|publisher=Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C|date=18 June 2018|access-date=18 June 2018}}</ref> and it was announced on 31 October that both clubs had agreed a €18&nbsp;million deal as settlement of his departure to England.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/46052450|title=Rui Patricio: Wolves agree £16m fee with Sporting Lisbon for goalkeeper|publisher=BBC Sport|date=31 October 2018|access-date=4 November 2018}}</ref>

Patrício opted to wear the No.11 shirt for Wolves as opposed to the habitual No.1, in honour of [[Carl Ikeme]], who had just retired following treatment for [[Acute leukemia|acute leukaemia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.expressandstar.com/sport/football/wolverhampton-wanderers-fc/2018/08/10/rui-patricio-honours-carl-ikeme-as-wolves-reveal-new-kit-numbers/|title=Rui Patricio honours Carl Ikeme as Wolves reveal new kit numbers|newspaper=[[Express & Star]]|last=Hatfield|first=Luke|date=10 August 2018|access-date=14 August 2018}}</ref> He made his league debut on 11 August 2018, in a 2–2 home draw against [[Everton F.C.|Everton]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/45067374|title=Wolverhampton Wanderers 2–2 Everton|publisher=BBC Sport|last=Hafez|first=Shamoon|date=11 August 2018|access-date=14 August 2018}}</ref> He kept nine clean sheets – a club record – as they finished [[2018–19 Premier League|his first season]] in seventh place and qualified for the Europa League.<ref name=wolves>{{cite news |title=Rui Patricio's 100 Premier League games |url=https://www.wolves.co.uk/news/features/20210225-long-read-rui-patricios-100-premier-league-games/# |access-date=19 March 2021 |publisher=Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C. |date=25 February 2021}}</ref>

In [[2019–20 Premier League|2019–20]], Patrício increased his number of clean sheets to 13, and conceded 40 goals compared to 46 the season before. [[2019–20 UEFA Europa League knockout phase#Quarter-finals|On the European front]], he helped the team to a first continental quarter-final since 1972.<ref name=wolves/> On 19 February 2021, he played his 100th Premier League match in a 1–0 win over [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]].<ref name=wolves/>

==International career==
[[File:Rui Patrício 2017.jpg|thumb|upright|Patrício with Portugal at the [[2017 FIFA Confederations Cup|2017 Confederations Cup]]]]
From 2007, Patrício started appearing for the [[Portugal national under-21 football team|Portuguese under-21 side]]. On 29 January of the following year, [[Portugal national football team|senior team]] coach [[Luiz Felipe Scolari]] called him up for a 1–3 [[Exhibition game|friendly]] defeat against [[Italy national football team|Italy]] in [[Zürich]],<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.record.xl.pt/arquivo/interior.aspx?content_id=331094|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130416121220/http://www.record.xl.pt/arquivo/interior.aspx?content_id=331094|url-status=dead|archive-date=16 April 2013|title=Rui Patrício chamado por Scolari|trans-title=Rui Patrício called by Scolari|newspaper=Record|language=pt|date=29 January 2008|access-date=15 February 2013}}</ref> although he did not leave the bench; on 12 May he was picked to the squad for [[UEFA Euro 2008]],<ref>{{cite news |last1=Pontes |first1=Carlos |title=UPDATE 1-Soccer-Maniche left out of Portugal's Euro 2008 squad |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/soccer-euro-portugal-squad-idUKL129697520080512 |access-date=12 October 2021 |work=[[Reuters]] |date=12 May 2008}}</ref> but did not play in the tournament.

Although not part of the provisional 24-player list for the [[2010 FIFA World Cup]] in South Africa,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fpf.pt/portal/page/portal/PORTAL_FUTEBOL/SELECCOES/NOTICIA?notid=9126513|title=Convocados revelados|trans-title=Squad revealed|publisher=[[Portuguese Football Federation]]|language=pt|date=10 May 2010|access-date=11 May 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110615170243/http://www.fpf.pt/portal/page/portal/PORTAL_FUTEBOL/SELECCOES/NOTICIA?notid=9126513|archive-date=15 June 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/newsid=1208424/index.html|title=Pepe in Portugal squad|publisher=FIFA|date=10 May 2010|access-date=11 May 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100512155936/https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/newsid=1208424/index.html|archive-date=12 May 2010}}</ref> Patrício was named in a backup list of six players.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/mm/document/tournament/competition/01/20/86/87/provisional1305.pdf|title=Release list of up to 30 players|publisher=FIFA|access-date=14 May 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100601094155/https://www.fifa.com/mm/document/tournament/competition/01/20/86/87/provisional1305.pdf|archive-date=1 June 2010}}</ref> He made his debut on 17 November 2010, playing the second half of a 4–0 friendly win against [[Spain national football team|Spain]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnstar.com/football/news/detail/item533498/Portugal-4-0-Spain/|title=Portugal 4–0 Spain|publisher=[[ESPN Star Sports]]|date=18 November 2010|access-date=11 February 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120731010828/http://www.espnstar.com/football/news/detail/item533498/Portugal-4-0-Spain/|archive-date=31 July 2012}}</ref>

After [[Eduardo Carvalho|Eduardo]] was relegated to the bench at his new club, [[S.L. Benfica]], Patrício became the starter under national team boss [[Paulo Bento]], and both players finished the [[UEFA Euro 2012 qualifying|Euro 2012 qualifying campaign]] with five games (450 minutes) as Portugal qualified for the final stages. He was the starter in the finals in [[Poland]] and [[Ukraine]], conceding four goals in five matches in an eventual semi-final exit.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro/season=2012/matches/round=15174/match=2003378/postmatch/report/index.html#spain+survive+test+nerve+reach+final|title=Spain survive test of nerve to reach final|publisher=UEFA|last=Burke|first=Chris|date=27 June 2012|access-date=26 June 2014}}</ref>

Patrício was included in Bento's 23-man squad for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]] as first-choice,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/football/teams/portugal/10810443/Portugal-World-Cup-2014-squad.html|title=Portugal World Cup 2014 squad|newspaper=[[The Daily Telegraph]]|date=2 June 2014|access-date=26 June 2014}}</ref> and made his debut in [[FIFA World Cup|the competition]] in the first encounter against [[Germany national football team|Germany]], which ended with a 0–4 loss.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/y=2014/m=6/news=german-lessons-for-cr7-s-portugal-2373410.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140617210838/http://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/y=2014/m=6/news=german-lessons-for-cr7-s-portugal-2373410.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=17 June 2014|title=Muller-inspired Germany thrash ten-man Portugal|publisher=FIFA|date=16 June 2014|access-date=26 June 2014}}</ref> He missed the second game against the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]], due to injury.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.sbnation.com/soccer/2014/6/16/5815362/rui-patricio-injury-portugal-2014-world-cup |title = Rui Patricio injured, could miss match against USA - SBNation.com| date=16 June 2014 }}</ref>

Patrício appeared in his 50th international on 30 June 2016, during [[UEFA Euro 2016|Euro 2016]]: after the 1–1 draw to [[Poland national football team|Poland]] at the [[Stade Vélodrome]] during the first 120 minutes, he saved [[Jakub Błaszczykowski]]'s attempt in a 5–3 [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shootout]] win that qualified to the semi-finals.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro/season=2016/matches/round=2000449/match=2017901/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Draw specialists Portugal beat Poland on penalties|publisher=UEFA|last=James|first=Andy|date=30 June 2016|access-date=30 June 2016}}</ref> Having won the tournament with a solid display in [[UEFA Euro 2016 Final|the final]],<ref name=BBC>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/36696772|title=Portugal 1–0 France|publisher=BBC Sport|last=McNulty|first=Phil|date=10 July 2016|access-date=18 May 2018}}</ref> he also made the squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sbs.com.au/news/portugal-world-cup-squad|title=Nearly half Portugal's Euro squad to miss World Cup|publisher=[[Special Broadcasting Service]]|date=17 May 2018|access-date=17 May 2018}}</ref> On 17 November that year, he [[List of Portugal national football team captains|captained the side]] for the first time in a goalless draw with [[Italy national football team|Italy]] in the [[UEFA Nations League]].<ref>{{cite news |title=FPF divulgou "foto exclusiva" de Rui Patrício na primeira parte |trans-title=FPF released "exclusive photo" of Rui Patrício in the first half|url=https://www.record.pt/internacional/competicoes-de-selecoes/liga-das-nacoes/detalhe/fpf-divulgou-foto-exclusiva-de-rui-patricio-na-primeira-parte |access-date=1 March 2020 |work=Record |date=17 November 2018 |language=pt}}</ref>

As Portugal defeated the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]] in the [[2019 UEFA Nations League Final]] on home soil at the ''[[Estádio do Dragão]]'', Patrício earned his 81st cap, thereby surpassing [[Vítor Baía]] as the nation's most capped goalkeeper.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.expressandstar.com/sport/football/wolverhampton-wanderers-fc/2019/06/10/wolves-keeper-rui-patricios-pride-at-breaking-record/|title=Wolves keeper Rui Patricio's pride at breaking record|newspaper=Express & Star|last=Spiers|first=Tim|date=11 June 2019|access-date=2 July 2019}}</ref> After keeping his place at the delayed [[UEFA Euro 2020]] in 2021,<ref>{{cite news |title=Star-Laden Cast Joins Cristiano Ronaldo on Portugal's Euro Roster |url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2021/05/20/portugal-euro-roster-cristiano-ronaldo-full-squad |access-date=12 October 2021 |work=[[Sports Illustrated]] |date=20 May 2021}}</ref> he reached [[List of men's footballers with 100 or more international caps|100 caps]] on 12 October that year in a 5–0 [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)|World Cup qualifying]] win over [[Luxembourg national football team|Luxembourg]], becoming the seventh Portuguese and first goalkeeper to the landmark.<ref>{{cite news |title=Luxemburgo desceu ao inferno português à custa de 'dois diabos e um leão'|trans-title=Luxembourg descended into the Portuguese hell at a cost of 'two devils and a lion'|url=https://www.noticiasaominuto.com/desporto/1850054/luxemburgo-desceu-ao-inferno-portugues-a-custa-de-dois-diabos-e-um-leao |access-date=12 October 2021 |publisher=Notícias ao Minuto |date=12 October 2021 |language=Portuguese}}</ref> 

In March 2022, Patrício did not play in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)#Path C|World Cup playoffs]], being replaced by Porto's [[Diogo Costa]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Ferreira |first1=Sébastien |title=Barrages Mondial 2022 : avec Diogo Costa, le Portugal a baptisé son nouvel ange-gardien |trans-title=2022 World Cup playoffs: in Diogo Costa, Portugal have baptised their new guardian angel|url=https://www.lefigaro.fr/sports/football/coupe-du-monde/barrages-mondial-2022-avec-diogo-costa-le-portugal-a-baptise-son-nouvel-ange-gardien-20220328 |access-date=29 March 2022 |work=[[Le Figaro]] |date=28 March 2022 |language=French}}</ref> At the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|finals in Qatar]], the latter played all five matches in a run to the quarter-finals.<ref>{{cite news |title=Diogo Costa nomeado para Melhor Guarda-Redes do Mundo de 2022|trans-title=Diogo Costa nominated for 2022 World's Best Goalkeeper|url=https://portocanal.sapo.pt/noticia/317535 |access-date=17 January 2023 |publisher=[[Porto Canal]] |date=23 December 2022 |language=Portuguese}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Patrício married Joana Pereira in June 2011, after a seven-year relationship. He filed for divorce on 24 December of the same year, aged 23.<ref>{{cite news |title=Goleiro que salvou Portugal pediu divórcio da ex-mulher na véspera de Natal|trans-title=Goalkeeper who saved Portugal filed for divorce from ex-wife on Christmas Eve|url=https://www.uol.com.br/esporte/futebol/copa-do-mundo/2018/noticias/2018/06/20/goleiro-que-salvou-portugal-pediu-divorcio-da-ex-mulher-na-vespera-de-natal.htm |access-date=17 June 2022 |publisher=[[Universo Online]] |date=20 June 2018 |language=Portuguese}}</ref> As of 2019, he is married to television [[Sexology|sexologist]] Vera Ribeiro, with whom he has a son and a daughter.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Oliveira |first1=Sara |title=Mulher de Rui Patrício quebra tabus na TV|trans-title=Rui Patrício's wife breaks taboos on TV|url=https://www.jn.pt/pessoas/mulher-de-rui-patricio-quebra-tabus-na-tv-11500721.html |access-date=17 June 2022 |work=Jornal de Notícias |date=11 November 2019 |language=Portuguese}}</ref>
