'''Raphael Dias Belloli''' (born 14 December 1996), known as '''Raphinha''', is a Brazilian professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Winger (association football)|winger]] for [[La Liga]] club [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] and the [[Brazil national football team|Brazil national team]].

He began his career in with [[Avaí FC|Avaí]], but left for Portuguese side [[Vitória S.C.|Vitória Guimarães]] in 2016, where he made his professional début. After impressive performances, he signed for [[Sporting CP]], where he spent a year before signing for French club [[Stade Rennais F.C.|Rennes]]. After a year he was on the move again, signing for [[Leeds United]] before moving to Spanish club [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] in a deal reportedly worth £50 million.

Having made his debut in October 2021, Raphinha is a Brazil international. His first two goals for the national team came in a win over [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay]] later that month. He was selected for Brazil's squad at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.

==Early life==
Raphinha was born in [[Porto Alegre]], Brazil where he grew up in [[Restinga, Porto Alegre|Restinga]], a favela far from the city centre.<ref name="12Things">{{cite web |title=12 Things You Don't Know About Me By Raphinha |url=https://www.theplayerstribune.com/posts/raphinha-leeds-united-premier-league-soccer |website=The Players' Tribune |date=26 August 2021 |publisher=Minute Media |access-date=2 September 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=12 Histórias que Você Não Sabe Sobre Mim por Raphinha |url=https://www.theplayerstribune.com/br/posts/carta-raphinha-leeds-12-historias-sobre-mim-que-voce-nao-sabe |website=The Players' Tribune |date=26 August 2021 |publisher=Minute Media |access-date=2 September 2021}}</ref><ref name="yep" /> His father was a jobbing musician.<ref name="athletic">{{Cite web |last1=Hay |first1=Phil |last2=Lang |first2=Jack |date=26 November 2022 |title=Raphinha, the fiery Brazilian who has swapped the Champions League for Leeds |url=https://theathletic.com/2121303/2020/10/13/raphinha-leeds-united-transfer/ |url-access=subscription |access-date=3 October 2021 |website=The Athletic }}</ref> He had a difficult upbringing where he describes sharing a bedroom with his parents, younger brother and pets; struggling to pay travel costs and at points having to beg for food.<ref name="12Things" /> He is of Italian descent.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.uol.com.br/esporte/futebol/colunas/rafael-reis/2021/03/18/italia-deve-convocar-toloi-para-eliminatorias-e-ja-mira-outros-brasileiros.htm|title=Reportagem: Rafael Reis - Itália deve convocar Tolói para eliminatórias e já mira outros brasileiros|website=www.uol.com.br}}</ref>

At the age of seven, he attended [[Ronaldinho]]'s birthday party due to his father and uncle's relationship with the player.<ref name="12Things" /> They have met numerous times since and have developed a lasting friendship.<ref name="12Things" />

Before starting his professional football career, Raphinha participated in the ''várzea'' tournaments until the age of 18,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://gauchazh.clicrbs.com.br/esportes/copa-do-mundo/noticia/2021/10/de-cria-da-restinga-e-recusado-na-dupla-gre-nal-a-parceiro-de-neymar-na-selecao-a-historia-de-raphinha-ckvcgkuzf003l017f9h4k434r.html|title=De cria da Restinga e recusado na dupla Gre-Nal a parceiro de Neymar na Seleção: a história de Raphinha|trans-title=From Restinga's offspring and refused in the duo Gre-Nal to Neymar's partner in the national team: the story of Raphinha|website=GZH.com|date=11 January 2021|access-date=11 May 2022|language=portuguese}}</ref> which he describes as "a network of independent matches and tournaments organised by the local community" below academy level in which any prospective player is allowed to participate.<ref name="12Things"/> The matches in these tournaments are played under harsh conditions, including home fans harassing opponent team players near changing rooms before matches, gunshots, clay pitches with dust and sand, intense heat, posts instead of nets and shirtless teams due to the lack of bibs.<ref name="12Things"/>

He has a long standing friendship with [[Bruno Fernandes]], starting before they became teammates at [[Sporting CP]].<ref name="12Things"/> According to Raphinha, Fernandes has been a huge help to him and his footballing career.<ref name="12Things"/> Before joining [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]], Fernandes told Raphinha that his style would "fit the league" (in reference to the [[Premier League]]).<ref name="12Things"/>

==Club career==
===Avaí===
After unsuccessful trials with [[Sport Club Internacional|International]] and [[Grêmio Foot-Ball Porto Alegrense|Grêmio]], Raphinha started his career playing youth football with [[Imbituba Futebol Clube|Imbituba]], where he was later scouted by [[Avaí FC|Avaí]].<ref name="athletic" /> At 18 years old, Raphinha started his career with the Avaí's under-20 team in the [[Campeonato Brasileiro Série A]] in 2014.<ref name="yep" /><ref name="12Things"/> After being injured, he was unable to make the squad but continued to train on his own.<ref name="12Things"/> Despite growing interest in Raphinha from many of Brazil's top clubs, Avaí kept hold of him until 2016, where he impressed at the [[Copa São Paulo de Futebol Júnior]].<ref name="athletic" />

===Vitória Guimarães===
[[File:FC Salzburg versus Vitória Guimarães (UEFA Euroleague 23. November 2017) 12.jpg|thumb|left|Raphinha (behind) playing for Vitória Guimarães  in the UEFA Europa League]]
On 2 February 2016, Raphinha signed for Portuguese side [[Vitória S.C.|Vitória Guimarães]], having been scouted at the Copa São Paulo de Futebol Júnior by [[Deco]], who signed him to his agency, D20 Sports, and arranged his transfer to Vitória.<ref name="athletic" /><ref>{{cite web |url=http://globoesporte.globo.com/sc/futebol/noticia/2016/02/avai-vende-jovem-rapinha-para-o-vitoria-de-guimaraes-de-portugal.html|title=Avaí vende jovem Raphinha para o Vitória de Guimarães, de Portugal|trans-title=Avaí sells youngster Raphinha to Vitória de Guimarães, of Portugal|language=pt|work=Globo Esporte |date=2 February 2016|access-date=3 January 2018}}</ref> He made his debut for the ''Vimaranenses'' on 13 March 2016 against [[F.C. Paços de Ferreira|Paços de Ferreira]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Vitória Guimarães 0–1 Paços de Ferreira|url=https://www.foradejogo.net/match.php?match=201615400012605 |website=ForaDeJogo|access-date=4 January 2018}}</ref> He scored his first goal for the club against [[C.S. Marítimo]] on 20 August 2016.<ref name="auto">{{cite web |url=https://www.leedsunited.com/news/team-news/27150/10-facts-about-raphinha|title=10 FACTS ABOUT RAPHINHA|publisher=Leeds United|date=5 October 2020 |access-date=7 October 2020}}</ref> He made his first appearance in the [[2017–18 UEFA Europa League]] on 14 September 2017 against [[FC Red Bull Salzburg|Red Bull Salzburg]].<ref name="auto"/> He won the Vitória Guimarães Breakthrough Player of the Year in 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.terra.com.br/esportes/lance/atacante-brasileiro-conquista-premio-inedito-de-revelacao-em-portugal,eb161fbb2f7c6e97437f423e40463970l89r3wfl.html |title=Brasileiro conquista prêmio inédito de revelação em Portugal|trans-title=Brazilian wins unprecedented award for revelation in Portugal |publisher=Terra |language=pt|date=7 December 2017|access-date=7 October 2020}}</ref> He scored 18 goals during 43 games (in all competitions) during the [[2017–18 Primeira Liga]] season for Vitória Guimarães.<ref name="yep">{{cite web|url=https://www.yorkshireeveningpost.co.uk/sport/football/leeds-united/who-raphinha-rennes-winger-profile-leeds-united-target-deadline-day-transfer-2992920|title=Who is Raphinha – Rennes winger in profile as Leeds United target deadline day transfer|work=Yorkshire Evening Post|date=5 October 2020|access-date=7 October 2020}}</ref>

===Sporting CP===
In May 2018, he transferred to Portuguese club [[Sporting CP]] until 2019.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.publico.pt/2018/05/23/desporto/noticia/v-guimaraes-confirma-saida-de-raphinha-para-o-sporting-1831393 |title=Raphinha assina por cinco épocas pelo Sporting |website=Público |language=pt |date=23 May 2018 }}</ref> He made his debut on 12 August against [[Moreirense F.C.|Moreirense]]. He scored his first goal for the club on 20 September 2018 against [[FK Qarabag]] in the 2–0 victory in the [[2018–19 UEFA Europa League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/match/2025157--sporting-cp-vs-qarabag/|title=Sporting CP 2 FK Qarabag 0 |publisher=UEFA.com|date=20 September 2018|access-date=7 October 2020}}</ref> Raphinha was a part of the side that won the [[2018–19 Taça de Portugal|2018–19]] [[Taça de Portugal]] scoring a penalty in the penalty shootout victory against [[FC Porto|Porto]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Penalties smile on Sporting again as Lions retain Taça da Liga crown|url=https://portugoal.net/club-news/1045-penalties-give-sporting-the-taca-da-liga|access-date=7 October 2020|publisher=PortuGOAL.net|date=26 January 2019}}</ref><ref name="auto"/>

===Rennes===
He signed for French club [[Stade Rennais F.C.|Rennes]] in 2019, with a transfer fee around €21 million,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://desporto.sapo.pt/futebol/primeira-liga/artigos/raphinha-no-rennes-e-a-sexta-maior-transferencia-de-sempre-do-sporting |title=Raphinha no Rennes é a sexta maior transferência de sempre do Sporting |website=Desporto |language=pt |date=25 November 2022 }}</ref> the club's record signing.<ref name="auto"/> He scored and gained an assist in his final game for the club during the [[2020–21 Ligue 1]] fixture against [[Stade de Reims|Reims]] in a 2–2 draw on 4 October 2020.<ref>{{Cite news |date=5 October 2020 |title=Raphinha's Rennes swansong gave Leeds United an enticing showcase of his undoubted potential |work=Leeds Live |url=https://www.leeds-live.co.uk/sport/leeds-united/leeds-united-raphinha-scout-report-19050107|access-date=7 October 2020}}</ref> He scored eight goals and gained seven assists during his time at the club, where he helped Rennes to a third place finish and qualify for the [[2020–21 UEFA Champions League]] during the [[2019–20 Ligue 1]] season.<ref>{{Cite news |date=5 October 2020 |title=Leeds Utd transfer news: Raphinha signs from Rennes for £17m  |publisher=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54415316|access-date=7 October 2020}}</ref>

====2021–22 season====
Raphinha scored his first goal of the season on 21 August 2021, in Leeds' first home game, a low-struck 72nd-minute equaliser from just inside the penalty area, in a 2–2 result against [[Everton F.C.|Everton]],<ref>{{Cite web |last=Dawkes|first=Phil|date=21 August 2021 |title=Leeds United 2-2 Everton|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58193422|access-date=3 September 2021 |website=BBC Sport }}</ref> which was nominated as one of the Premier League's Goals of the Month for August.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.leedsunited.com/news/team-news/28665/luke-and-raphinha-nominated-for-premier-league-goal-of-the-month|date=2 September 2021 |title=Luke and Raphinha Nominated for Premier League Goal of the Month!|access-date=3 September 2021 |work=Leeds Ynited F.C.}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.premierleague.com/match/66356|date=2 September 2021 |title=Pick August's Budweiser Goal of the Month!|access-date=3 September 2021 |work=Premier League}}</ref>

Raphinha ended the season as Leeds' top goalscorer with 11 goals, including the first goal of the game in the 2–1 away win against [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]] on the last day of the season, from the penalty spot after being fouled by the Brentford goalkeeper [[David Raya]], confirming Leeds' Premier League status.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/61453546 |title=Brentford 1–2 Leeds |first=Simon |last=Stone |website=BBC Sport |date=22 May 2022 |access-date=22 May 2022}}</ref>

===Barcelona===
On 15 July 2022, Raphinha signed for La Liga club [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] on a five-year contract for a reported initial fee of £50m, potentially rising to £55&nbsp;million in add-ons.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Agreement in principle to sign Raphinha |url=https://www.fcbarcelona.com/en/football/first-team/news/2676187/agreement-in-principle-to-sign-raphinha |access-date=24 July 2022 |website=www.fcbarcelona.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/62168557 |title=Raphinha: Barcelona sign attacker from Leeds United in deal worth up to £55m |first=Adam |last=Pope |website=BBC Sport |date=15 July 2022 |access-date=15 July 2022}}</ref> On 13 August, he made his debut for the club in 0–0 draw against [[Rayo Vallecano]] in the league.<ref>{{Cite web |date=13 August 2022 |title=Barcelona frustrated by Rayo Vallecano as Lewandowski & Raphinha's La Liga debuts spoiled |url=https://www.goal.com/en-in/news/barcelona-frustrated-rayo-vallecano-opening-night/bltcd5e7b01eea1ab09 |access-date=16 August 2022}}</ref> On 3 September, he scored his first Barcelona goal in a 3–0 victory over Sevilla at the Ramón Sánchez Pizjuán.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/62783164 |title=Barca maintain unbeaten start with win at Sevilla |author=Shamoon Hafez |publisher=BBC Sport |date=3 September 2022 |access-date=26 January 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
In August 2021, Raphinha was called up to represent [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]] for the team's [[FIFA World Cup|World Cup]] qualifiers against [[Chile national football team|Chile]], [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]] and [[Peru national football team|Peru]].<ref name="Raphinha Called Up to Brazil's National Squad">{{cite web|date=13 August 2021|title=Raphinha Called Up to Brazil's National Squad|url=https://www.leedsunited.com/news/team-news/28509/raphinha-called-up-to-brazil-s-national-squad|access-date=2 September 2021|website=Leeds United|publisher=Leeds United}}</ref>

On 7 October 2021, Raphinha made his full debut for the national side, coming on as a substitute at half time in a 3&ndash;1 comeback win against Venezuela.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Reuters|date=8 October 2021|title=Brazil rally to beat Venezuela in qualifier as Raphinha enjoys dream debut|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2021/oct/08/brazil-venezuela-world-cup-qualifier-raphinha|access-date=15 October 2021|website=The Guardian}}</ref> He assisted two goals and won a penalty in 45 minutes on the pitch, receiving praise from sports pundits and fans.<ref>{{Cite web|date=14 October 2021|title=Raphinha, la nueva joya de Brasil, fue dirigida por Deco y encantó al Loco Bielsa en la Premier League|url=https://www.espn.com.ec/futbol/mundial/nota/_/id/9355119/brasil-raphinha-nueva-joya-dirigida-deco-encanto-loco-bielsa-premier-league|access-date=15 October 2021|website=ESPN.com.co|language=es}}</ref>

In his third appearance and first start, he scored his first two international goals in a qualifying match against [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=15 October 2021|title=Brazil 4-1 Uruguay: Raphinha scores twice as home side cruise to World Cup qualifying victory|url=https://www.eurosport.com/football/world-cup-qualification-conmebol/2022/brazil-4-1-uruguay-raphinha-scores-twice-as-home-side-cruise-to-world-cup-qualifying-victory_sto8585883/story.shtml|access-date=15 October 2021|website=Eurosport}}</ref>

On 7 November 2022, Raphinha was named in the squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Seleção Brasileira está convocada para a Copa do Mundo FIFA Qatar 2022|url=https://www.cbf.com.br/selecao-brasileira/noticias/selecao-masculina/selecao-brasileira-esta-convocada-para-a-copa-do-mundo-fifa-qatar-2022|publisher=[[Brazilian Football Confederation|CBF]]|access-date=7 November 2022|date=7 November 2022|language=pt}}</ref>
