'''Fernando Manuel Fernandes da Costa Santos''' {{post-nominals|post-noms=[[Order of Merit (Portugal)|GOM]]}} (born 10 October 1954) is a Portuguese professional [[Association football|football]] [[Manager (association football)|manager]] and former player who played as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]]. He is the manager of the [[Poland national football team|Poland national team]].

He amassed [[Primeira Liga]] totals of 161 games and two goals over eight seasons, almost always with [[G.D. Estoril Praia|Estoril]]. After retiring, he worked as a coach for several decades, starting out at his main club in 1988. 

Santos managed Portugal's [[Big Three (Portugal)|Big Three]], winning five major titles with [[FC Porto|Porto]]. For the better part of the 2000s he worked in Greece, mainly with [[AEK Athens F.C.|AEK Athens]] and [[PAOK FC|PAOK]]. In 2010, he was appointed at the helm of the [[Greece national football team|Greece national team]], coaching them in a [[FIFA World Cup|World Cup]] and one [[UEFA European Championship|European Championship]]. Subsequently, he led [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]] to victory in the [[UEFA Euro 2016|Euro 2016]] and the [[2019 UEFA Nations League Finals|2019 Nations League]], which were the first two major titles in the nation's history, before leaving in 2022. The following year, he took over as manager of Poland.

==Playing career==
Born in [[Lisbon]], Santos finished his development with [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]], having joined its youth system at the age of 16. His senior debut was made with [[G.D. Estoril Praia|Estoril]], which he went on to represent in all three major [[Portuguese football league system|levels of Portuguese football]].<ref name=UEFA>{{UEFA coach|45805}}</ref>

Santos made his [[Primeira Liga]] debut on 7 September 1975, playing the full 90 minutes in a 2–0 home win against [[S.C. Farense|Farense]]. He finished his [[1975–76 Primeira Divisão|first season]] with a further 12 appearances, helping his team to the eighth position.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://arquivista.wordpress.com/2007/04/05/19/|title=Época 1975/76: Primeira Divisão|trans-title=1975/76 season: First Division|publisher=Arquivos da Bola|language=pt|date=5 April 2007|access-date=19 February 2016}}</ref>

Santos scored his first goals in the top division in the [[1978–79 Primeira Divisão|1978–79 campaign]], only missing one league game in an eventual 11th-place finish.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://arquivista.wordpress.com/2007/03/15/epoca-197879-primeira-divisao/|title=Época 1978/79: Primeira Divisão|trans-title=1978/79 season: First Division|publisher=Arquivos da Bola|language=pt|date=15 March 2007|access-date=19 February 2016}}</ref> For [[1979–80 Primeira Divisão|1979–80]], he moved to [[C.S. Marítimo|Marítimo]], where he was also first choice.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://arquivista.wordpress.com/2007/03/08/epoca-197980/|title=Época 1979/80: Primeira Divisão|trans-title=1979/80 season: First Division|publisher=Arquivos da Bola|language=pt|date=8 March 2007|access-date=19 February 2016}}</ref> Having returned to his previous club, he played with them a further eight years (five being spent in the [[Segunda Liga]]) before retiring at the age of 33.

==Coaching career==
===Estoril and Porto===
Santos started working as a manager immediately after retiring. He helped Estoril return to the top tier in [[1990–91 Segunda Divisão de Honra|1991]] and, in the following ten years, only worked in that competition, being in charge of [[C.F. Estrela da Amadora|Estrela da Amadora]] and [[FC Porto|Porto]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.publico.pt/2017/01/09/desporto/noticia/fernando-santos-o-treinador-que-deixou-de-ser-engenheiro-1757551|title=Fernando Santos, o treinador que deixou de ser engenheiro|trans-title=Fernando Santos, the manager who is no longer an engineer|newspaper=[[Público (Portugal)|Público]]|last=Vaza|first=Marco|language=pt|date=9 January 2017|access-date=14 July 2021}}</ref>

Having signed for the latter side in summer 1998, Santos won the national championship and the [[Supertaça Cândido de Oliveira|Portuguese Supercup]] in his [[1998–99 Primeira Liga|first season]]. He finished second in [[1999–2000 Primeira Liga|the following]] to [[Sporting CP]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://maisfutebol.iol.pt/dossier/dossier/dez-herois-que-decidiram-o-fc-porto-sporting/6037e5650cf255f226fdcae3|title=Dez heróis que decidiram o FC Porto-Sporting|trans-title=Ten heroes that decided FC Porto-Sporting|publisher=Mais Futebol|language=pt|access-date=27 January 2023}}</ref> and led the team to [[1999–2000 UEFA Champions League|the quarter-finals]] of the [[UEFA Champions League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://maisfutebol.iol.pt/fc-porto-bayern/fc-porto/da-gloria-ao-roubo-ate-fernando-santos-perder-a-cabeca|title=Da glória ao roubo, até Fernando Santos perder a cabeça|trans-title=From the glory to the theft, until Fernando Santos lost his mind|publisher=Mais Futebol|last=Da Cunha|first=Pedro Jorge|language=pt|date=15 April 2015|access-date=27 January 2023}}</ref>

===Greece and Sporting CP===
In 2001, Santos was appointed at [[AEK Athens F.C.|AEK Athens]] in the [[Super League Greece]], [[2001–02 Greek Football Cup|winning]] the [[Greek Football Cup|domestic cup]] and [[2001–02 Alpha Ethniki|losing the league]] to [[Olympiacos F.C.|Olympiacos]] on [[goal difference]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/insideuefa/national-associations/news/025e-0f906e54f384-44d7c5236ec7-1000--santos-quits-aek/|title=Santos quits AEK|publisher=UEFA|date=9 May 2002|access-date=27 January 2023}}</ref> Staying in the country, he then joined [[Panathinaikos F.C.|Panathinaikos]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/news/0185-0e6a417ad770-29f5a368c7b3-1000--santos-moves-to-panathinaikos/|title=Santos moves to Panathinaikos|publisher=UEFA|date=19 May 2002|access-date=27 January 2023}}</ref> leaving by mutual consent after only four months.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/news/newsid=38736.html|title=Santos out at Panathinaikos|publisher=UEFA|date=16 October 2002|access-date=19 February 2016}}</ref>

Santos returned to his country for the [[2003–04 Primeira Liga|2003–04 campaign]], replacing [[László Bölöni]] at the helm of Sporting.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/news/newsid=72880.html|title=Sporting put faith in Santos|publisher=UEFA|date=3 June 2003|access-date=19 February 2016}}</ref> He was relieved of his duties on 2 June 2004, after his team was only able to rank third.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/news/newsid=185531.html|title=Santos ousted at Sporting|publisher=UEFA|date=1 June 2004|access-date=19 February 2016}}</ref>

From [[2004–05 Alpha Ethniki|2004]] to [[2005–06 Alpha Ethniki|2006]], Santos again managed AEK.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/news/newsid=208245.html|title=Santos returns to AEK|publisher=UEFA|date=16 July 2004|access-date=19 February 2016}}</ref> He led them to consecutive top-three finishes during his tenure, being voted [[Greek football PSAP awards|Manager of the Year]] in 2005.

===Benfica===
{{Wikinews|Benfica sacks head coach after first match of the season}}
On 20 May 2006, Santos joined former youth club Benfica.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/news/01b5-0e6d862919e1-a5943e3dec4f-1000--santos-fills-coaching-void-at-benfica/|title=Santos fills coaching void at Benfica|publisher=UEFA|date=21 May 2006|access-date=27 January 2023}}</ref> He was responsible for the signing of former AEK player [[Kostas Katsouranis]] the following month.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/insideuefa/national-associations/news/01b6-0f848b3bd534-6068c3bdcca4-1000--katsouranis-joins-santos-at-benfica/|title=Katsouranis joins Santos at Benfica|publisher=UEFA|date=22 June 2006|access-date=27 January 2023}}</ref>

After a third place in his [[2006–07 Primeira Liga|debut season]], only two points behind champions Porto, Santos witnessed the departure of [[Captain (association football)|captain]] and top scorer [[Simão Sabrosa]] to [[Atlético Madrid]] during pre-season. On 20 August 2007, after a 1–1 away draw against [[Leixões S.C.|Leixões]], he was sacked and replaced with [[José Antonio Camacho]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/newsid=573152.html|title=Santos shown door at Benfica|publisher=UEFA|date=20 August 2007|access-date=19 February 2016}}</ref>

===PAOK===
Santos returned to Greece and its top flight in early September 2007, signing a three-year contract with [[PAOK FC|PAOK]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/insideuefa/national-associations/news/0254-0d7baba50f28-811f05fad701-1000--paok-plump-for-santos-experience/|title=PAOK plump for Santos experience|publisher=UEFA|date=5 September 2007|access-date=27 January 2023}}</ref> There, he joined forces with [[director of football]] – and former international – [[Theodoros Zagorakis]], leading the team to the second position in [[2009–10 Super League Greece|2009–10]].

On 19 May 2010, despite a chance of [[2010–11 UEFA Champions League|competing again in the Champions League]], Santos announced his decision of leaving the [[Thessaloniki]]-based side in a press conference.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.paokfc.gr/swift.jsp?CMRCode=152LHASQH&extLang=|title=Η Συνέντευξη Τύπου του Fernando Santos|trans-title=Fernando Santos' press conference|publisher=PAOK FC|language=el|date=19 May 2010|access-date=20 May 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100521053849/http://paokfc.gr/swift.jsp?CMRCode=152LHASQH&extLang=|archive-date=21 May 2010}}</ref>

===Greece national team===
On 1 July 2010, Santos was named the new coach of the [[Greece national football team|Greece national team]], succeeding longtime incumbent [[Otto Rehhagel]] on a two-year deal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/news/newsid=1502493.html|title=Santos replaces Rehhagel for Greece|publisher=UEFA|date=1 July 2010|access-date=19 February 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/world_cup_2010/8780517.stm|title=World Cup 2012: Fernando Santos named new Greece coach|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|date=1 July 2010|access-date=1 July 2010}}</ref> He [[UEFA Euro 2012 qualifying|qualified]] the country to the [[UEFA Euro 2012]] tournament, subsequently reaching the last-eight stage.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro/season=2012/matches/round=15173/match=2003344/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Germany overpower Greece in UEFA EURO 2012 quarter-finals|publisher=UEFA|last=Hart|first=Simon|date=22 June 2012|access-date=19 February 2016}}</ref>

Santos was also in charge during the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]] in Brazil, as Greece reached the knockout stage for the first time ever. Shortly before the start of the [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shootout]] against [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica]], eventually lost 5–3, he was [[Ejection (sports)|sent off]] by referee [[Ben Williams (referee)|Ben Williams]] for apparent dissent. He watched the decision unfold on a television from inside the stadium, and the defeat marked the end of his tenure as his contract expired the very next day;<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/soccer-world-gre-santos/santos-comments-attract-criticism-back-in-greece-idINKBN0F641P20140701|title=Santos comments attract criticism back in Greece|publisher=[[Reuters]]|last=Wood|first=Graham|date=1 July 2014|access-date=25 August 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/27961189|title=Costa Rica 1–1 Greece (Costa Rica win 5–3 on penalties)|publisher=BBC Sport|last=Hassan|first=Nabil|date=30 June 2014|access-date=30 June 2014}}</ref> he was initially banned for eight matches for the incident,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/sep/24/fernando-santos-loses-appeal-ban-fifa-portugal|title=Portugal coach Fernando Santos loses appeal against eight-match suspension|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|date=24 September 2014|access-date=19 February 2016}}</ref> reduced to six upon appeal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.com/fifa-world-cup/story/2363145/portugal-coach-santos-wins-cut-in-world-cup-misconduct-ban|title=Portugal coach Santos wins cut in World Cup misconduct ban|publisher=[[ESPN FC]]|date=23 March 2015|access-date=19 February 2016}}</ref>

===Portugal national team===
[[File:New Zealand-Portugal (32).jpg|thumb|Santos with Portugal at the [[2017 FIFA Confederations Cup]]]]
On 23 September 2014, Santos was chosen as the new manager of [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]], after [[Paulo Bento]] was fired due to poor results.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/news/newsid=2152649.html|title=Ex-Greece boss Santos appointed Portugal coach|publisher=UEFA|last=Marques|first=Pedro|date=24 September 2014|access-date=19 February 2016}}</ref> His first game in charge took place on 14 October in a 1–0 win in [[Denmark national football team|Denmark]] for the [[UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying|Euro 2016 qualifiers]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro/season=2016/matches/round=2000446/match=2013889/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Ronaldo wins it for Portugal|publisher=UEFA|date=14 October 2014|access-date=19 February 2016}}</ref> and the side went on to reach the finals in France;<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/news/newsid=2290395.html|title=Portugal set for home-from-home EURO|publisher=UEFA|date=8 October 2015|access-date=19 February 2016}}</ref> during his suspension, it was [[Ilídio Vale]] that sat on the bench.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.publico.pt/desporto/noticia/pepe-integrado-no-treino-da-selecao-portuguesa-1690525|title=Pepe integrado no treino da selecção portuguesa|trans-title=Pepe added to Portugal national team training|newspaper=Público|language=pt|date=27 March 2015|access-date=19 July 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://desporto.sapo.pt/futebol/seleccao/portugal/clube-portugal/artigo/2015/06/13/flash-ilidio-vale|title=Ilídio Vale: "Vencemos graças ao espírito coletivo"|trans-title=Ilídio Vale: "We won thanks to team spirit"|publisher=SAPO|language=pt|date=13 June 2015|access-date=18 July 2016}}</ref>

On 10 July 2016, after three group stage draws that enabled group stage qualification as third,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/36570911|title=Euro 2016: England to face Iceland in last 16|publisher=BBC Sport|date=22 June 2016|access-date=10 July 2016}}</ref> Santos coached Portugal to its first-ever major international conquest, after a 1–0 [[Overtime (sports)|extra time]] defeat of [[France national football team|the hosts]].<ref name=BBC>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/36696772|title=Portugal 1–0 France|publisher=BBC Sport|last=McNulty|first=Phil|date=10 July 2016|access-date=10 July 2016}}</ref> The only win in 90 minutes occurred in the semi-finals, against [[Wales national football team|Wales]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro/season=2016/matches/round=2000450/match=2017905/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Portugal reach EURO final as Wales fairy tale ends|publisher=UEFA|last=Atkin|first=John|date=6 July 2016|access-date=18 July 2016}}</ref> but his team was also the only undefeated in the tournament.

===Poland national team===
On 24 January 2023, Santos was unveiled as the new manager of [[Poland national football team|Poland]], succeeding [[Czesław Michniewicz]] who left the team shortly after the 2022 World Cup.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.euro-football.ru/article/1003876948/1004994449_santush_naznachen_glavnyim_trenerom_sbornoy_polshi|title=Сантуш назначен главным тренером сборной Польши|trans-title=Santos named Poland manager|publisher=Euro Football|last=Belyaev|first=Egor|language=ru|date=23 January 2023|access-date=24 January 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rfi.fr/en/sports/20230124-former-portugal-boss-santos-named-as-poland-coach|title=Former Portugal boss Santos named as Poland coach|publisher=[[Radio France Internationale]]|date=24 January 2023|access-date=24 January 2023}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Santos earned a degree in electrical and telecommunications engineering, awarded in 1977 by the [[Instituto Superior de Engenharia de Lisboa]].<ref name=UEFA/><ref name=SOL>{{cite news|url=http://www.sol.pt/noticia/115524|title=Fernando Santos: 'Como treinador não tenho coração'|trans-title=Fernando Santos: 'As a manager I have no heart'|newspaper=[[Sol (newspaper)|Sol]]|language=pt|date=23 September 2014|access-date=19 February 2016}}</ref> As he was in charge when Porto won its fifth consecutive championship in [[1998–99 Primeira Liga|1999]], he was nicknamed ''Engenheiro do [[Numeral prefix|Penta]]'' (Penta's engineer).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.maisfutebol.iol.pt/fernando-santos/apuramento/o-engenheiro-das-qualificacoes-que-so-perdeu-uma-vez|title=O engenheiro das qualificações que só perdeu uma vez|trans-title=The qualifying engineer that has only lost once|publisher=Mais Futebol|last=Calhau|first=Pedro|language=pt|date=8 October 2015|access-date=19 February 2016}}</ref>

In addition to his native Portuguese, Santos also speaks English<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mpgVV7hbACk|title=Fernando Santos – Best Coach of the Year 2016|publisher=[[YouTube]]|date=4 January 2017|access-date=6 May 2020|url-status=live|archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211222/mpgVV7hbACk|archive-date=22 December 2021}}{{cbignore}}</ref> and Greek.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mWvV3scKUwY|title=Ο Fernando Santos Μιλάει Ελληνικά και συγκινήθηκε.|trans-title=Fernando Santos spoke Greek and was moved.|publisher=YouTube|language=el|date=19 November 2013|access-date=6 June 2021|url-status=live|archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211222/mWvV3scKUwY|archive-date=22 December 2021}}{{cbignore}}</ref>
