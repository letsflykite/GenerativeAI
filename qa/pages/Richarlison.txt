'''Richarlison de Andrade''' (born 10 May 1997), known [[mononym]]ously as '''Richarlison''' ({{IPA-pt|ʁiˈʃaʁl(i)sõ|br}}),<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://forvo.com/word/richarlison/ |title=Richarlison pronunciation: How to pronounce Richarlison in Portuguese |first=Forvo |last=Team |website=Forvo.com}}</ref> is a Brazilian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]] for {{English football updater|Everton}} club [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] and the [[Brazil national football team|Brazil national team]].

He began his professional career with [[América Futebol Clube (MG)|América Mineiro]] in 2015, winning promotion from the [[Campeonato Brasileiro Série B]] in his only season before transferring to [[Fluminense FC|Fluminense]]. He totalled 67 matches and 19 goals in his two years there and was named in the Team of the Season when the club finished as runners-up in the [[2017 Campeonato Carioca]]. After this spell he then signed for [[Watford F.C.|Watford]], and a year later [[Everton F.C.|Everton]]. He later signed for Tottenham Hotspur in 2022.

At international level, Richarlison made his senior debut for Brazil in 2018. He was a member of the team that won the [[2019 Copa América]], came runners-up at the [[2021 Copa América]] and won a gold medal at the [[Football at the 2020 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|2020 Olympic tournament]], also playing at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

===Fluminense===
On 29 December 2015, Richarlison signed a five-year contract with Série A club [[Fluminense FC|Fluminense]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://globoesporte.globo.com/futebol/times/fluminense/noticia/2015/12/terceiro-reforco-fluminense-confirma-contratacao-do-atacante-richarlison.html |title=Terceiro reforço: Fluminense confirma a contratação do atacante Richarlison|trans-title=Third addition: Fluminense confirm the signing of forward Richarlison |website=Globo Esporte |language=pt |date=29 December 2015 |access-date=22 October 2016}}</ref> He made his debut on 13 May 2016 in the second leg of the second round of the [[Copa do Brasil]], contributing to all of his team's goals in a 3–3 home draw (6–3 aggregate) against [[Associação Ferroviária de Esportes|Ferroviaria]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Scarpa brilha e Richarlison faz estreia de gala|trans-title=Scarpa shines and Richarlison puts on a show on debut |url=https://www.lance.com.br/fluminense/atuacoes-scarpa-brilha-richarlison-faz-estreia-gala.html |access-date=22 July 2018 |newspaper=Lance! |date=13 May 2016 |language=pt}}</ref>

He made his league debut two days later, starting in a 1–0 away win against former club América. His first goal in the division came on 26 June, the winner in a 2–1 success against [[Clube de Regatas do Flamengo|rivals Flamengo]], a game in which he came on as a late substitute but was himself taken off injured.<ref>{{cite news |title=Vaz erra, Richarlison desencanta, e Flu bate o Fla por 2 a 1 em Natal |trans-title=Vaz makes a mistake, Richarlison disenchants and ''Flu'' beat ''Fla'' 2–1 in Natal |url=http://globoesporte.globo.com/rj/futebol/brasileirao-serie-a/jogo/26-06-2016/flamengo-fluminense/ |access-date=22 July 2018 |website=Globo Esporte |date=26 June 2016 |language=pt}}</ref>

In the [[2017 Campeonato Carioca]], Richarlison scored 8 goals in 12 matches and was named in the team of the season as his club finished as runner-up to Flamengo.<ref name=ferj/> This included one on 22 April in a 3–0 semi-final win over [[CR Vasco da Gama|Vasco da Gama]] at the [[Maracanã Stadium]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Fluminense 'esquece' vantagem, atropela Vasco no Maracanã e está na final do Carioca |trans-title=Fluminense 'forget' lead, trample Vasco in the Maracanã and are in the final of the Carioca |url=http://www.espn.com.br/noticia/688859_fluminense-esquece-vantagem-atropela-vasco-no-maracana-e-esta-na-final-do-carioca |access-date=22 July 2018 |publisher=ESPN |date=22 April 2017 |language=pt}}</ref>

In the same year, Richarlison also took part in his first continental competition, the [[2017 Copa Sudamericana]]. He played four games in the [[Rio de Janeiro]]-based club's run to the quarter-finals, and scored in wins over [[Liverpool F.C. (Montevideo)|Liverpool (Uruguay)]] and [[C.D. Universidad Católica del Ecuador|Universidad Católica (Ecuador)]] in the first two phases.<ref>{{cite news |title=Em dia de Maracanã cheio, Fluminense bate o Liverpool pela Sul-Americana|trans-title=On day of full Maracanã, Fluminense beat Liverpool in the Sudamericana |url=https://odia.ig.com.br/_conteudo/esporte/fluminense/2017-04-06/em-dia-de-maracana-cheio-fluminense-bate-o-liverpool-pela-sul-americana.html |access-date=22 July 2018 |newspaper=O Dia |date=6 April 2017 |language=pt}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Fluminense 4&nbsp;×&nbsp;0 Universidad Católica-EQU |url=http://esportes.estadao.com.br/ao-vivo/futebol/66f60f2acd |access-date=22 July 2018 |publisher=Estadão |date=29 June 2017 |language=pt}}</ref>

===Watford===
[[File:Chelsea 4 Watford 2 (Richarlison).jpg|thumb|upright|Richarlison playing for [[Watford F.C.|Watford]] in 2017]]

Richarlison completed a move to English [[Premier League]] club [[Watford F.C.|Watford]] on 8 August 2017, signing a five-year contract for a £11.2&nbsp;million transfer fee.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://globoesporte.globo.com/futebol/times/fluminense/noticia/richarlison-viaja-para-londres-e-abad-confirma-venda-ao-watford-por-125-milhoes-de-euros.ghtml |title=Abad confirma venda de Richarlison ao Watford por 12,5 milhões de euros|trans-title=Abad confirms the selling of Richarlison to Watford for 12,5&nbsp;million euros |website=Globo Esporte |language=pt |date=31 July 2017 |access-date=31 July 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.watfordfc.com/teams/first-team/official-richarlison-signs |title=Official: Richarlison signs |publisher=Watford F.C. |date=8 August 2017 |access-date=26 December 2018}}</ref> He made his debut for the club as a substitute in a 3–3 draw against [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] on the opening day of the [[2017–18 Premier League]] season. In his next match, on 19 August against [[AFC Bournemouth]], he scored his first goal for the club in a 2–0 win.<ref>{{cite news |last1=MacInnes |first1=Paul |title=Richarlison and Capoue strikes reward battling Watford at AFC Bournemouth |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2017/aug/19/bournemouth-watford-premier-league-match-report |newspaper=The Guardian |date=19 August 2017 |access-date=20 August 2017}}</ref> He was the only player in the Watford squad to play every match of the Premier League season and scored five goals over its course.<ref>{{cite web |title=Season in stats: 2017/18 |url=https://www.watfordfc.com/teams/first-team/season-stats-201718 |access-date=24 July 2018 |publisher=Watford F.C.}}</ref>

===Everton===
Richarlison transferred to fellow Premier League club [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] on 24 July 2018 for a transfer fee starting at £35&nbsp;million and potentially rising to £50&nbsp;million,<ref>{{cite news |title=Richarlison: Everton sign Brazilian from Watford in £50m deal |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44946473 |website=BBC Sport |date=24 July 2018 |access-date=11 June 2022}}</ref> reuniting him with former Watford manager [[Marco Silva]]. In his competitive debut on 11 August, he scored twice in a 2–2 draw at [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Tyers |first1=Alan |title=Richarlison the star as he scores twice on debut for ten-man Everton but game Wolves dig in for a point |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2018/08/11/wolves-vs-everton-premier-league-2018-19-live-score-latest-updates/ |access-date=11 August 2018 |newspaper=The Daily Telegraph |date=11 August 2018}}</ref> Two weeks later, he was sent off in the first half of a match of the same score away to Bournemouth for clashing heads with [[Adam Smith (footballer, born 1991)|Adam Smith]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Morgan |first1=Richard |title=Bournemouth 2–2 Everton: Nathan Ake scores late equaliser as two see red at the Vitality |url=http://www.skysports.com/football/bmouth-vs-everton/390778 |access-date=26 August 2018 |publisher=Sky Sports |date=26 August 2018}}</ref>

[[File:Everton 2 Chelsea 0 (47471909111).jpg|thumb|left|upright|Richarlison (right) playing for Everton in 2019]]

Due to the poor form of fellow Everton strikers, Richarlison was deployed in the centre-forward role for Everton's game against [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] on 6 October, scoring in the 7th minute in a 2–1 away win.<ref>{{cite news |title=Leicester City 1–2 Everton |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45689302 |access-date=6 October 2018 |website=BBC Sport |date=6 October 2018}}</ref> Four weeks later, he scored twice in a 3–1 victory over [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Everton boss Marco Silva said Richarlison is a 'fantastic football player' after the Brazilian put in a match-winning display against Brighton. |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/46000539 |access-date=3 November 2018 |website=BBC Sport |date=3 November 2018}}</ref> Richarlison finished the season as Everton's joint-highest goalscorer along with [[Gylfi Sigurðsson]] on 13 Premier League goals, while both had 14 goals in all competitions.<ref>{{cite news |date=27 May 2019 |title=Everton Top Scorers |website=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/teams/everton/top-scorers|access-date=27 May 2019}}</ref> Ahead of the 2019–20 Richarlison was handed the number 7 shirt. He finished that season as the club's joint-highest goalscorer, this time sharing the honour with [[Dominic Calvert-Lewin]] on 13 Premier League goals, while both had 15 goals in all competitions.

On 3 December 2019, Richarlison signed a new five-year contract with Everton.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/50650094 |title=Richarlison signs new Everton deal until summer 2024 |date=3 December 2019 |website=BBC Sport|access-date=31 March 2021}}</ref> On 20 February 2021, he scored the first goal in a 2–0 win over Liverpool at [[Anfield]], Everton's first away win over their [[Merseyside derby|rivals]] since September 1999.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/56033685 |title=Liverpool 0–2 Everton |website=BBC Sport |date=20 February 2021}}</ref>

On 27 August 2018, he received his first call-up to the [[Brazil national football team|senior team]] by coach [[Tite (football manager)|Tite]], for friendlies against the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]] and [[El Salvador national football team|El Salvador]], after [[Pedro (footballer, born 1997)|Pedro]] withdrew injured.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45324608 |title=Richarlison called up by Brazil for the first time for friendly matches |website=BBC Sport |date=27 August 2018 |access-date=12 September 2018}}</ref> He made his debut against the Americans on 7 September at the [[MetLife Stadium]] in [[New Jersey]], as a 75th-minute substitute for [[Roberto Firmino]] in a 2–0 win,<ref>{{cite news |title=USA 0–2 Brazil |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45454542 |access-date=13 September 2018 |website=BBC Sport |date=8 September 2018}}</ref> and scored his first goals in the latter match four days later, netting twice in a 5–0 win.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/my/news/brazil-5-el-salvador-0-two-goal-richarlison-leads-rout-neto-ends-eight-year-wait |title=Brazil 5 El Salvador 0: Two-goal Richarlison leads rout as Neto ends eight-year wait |website=FourFourTwo |date=12 September 2018 |access-date=12 September 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180912130921/https://www.fourfourtwo.com/my/news/brazil-5-el-salvador-0-two-goal-richarlison-leads-rout-neto-ends-eight-year-wait |archive-date=12 September 2018 |url-status=dead}}</ref>
