'''Joshua Thomas Sargent''' (born February 20, 2000) is an American professional [[Association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]] or [[winger (association football)|winger]] for [[EFL Championship]] club [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]] and the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States national team]]. In May 2017, at the age of 17, Sargent became the youngest U.S. player to score at the [[FIFA U-20 World Cup]].<ref name=ussf_052217>{{cite web|title=SEVENTEEN YEAR OLD JOSH SARGENT BECOMES YOUNGEST U.S. PLAYER TO SCORE AT FIFA U-20 WORLD CUP|url=http://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2017/05/22/17/06/20170522-feat-u20mnt-josh-sargent-youngest-us-player-to-score-at-fifa-u20-world-cup|publisher=U.S. Soccer Federation|access-date=May 24, 2017|date=May 22, 2017}}</ref>

==Early life==
Sargent was born in [[O'Fallon, Missouri]], to Jeff and Liane Sargent, both of whom played college-level soccer. Sargent joined the Scott Gallagher Soccer Club at eight years old.<ref>[https://www.socceramerica.com/article/73544/josh-sargent-the-pride-of-soccer-rich-st-louis.html "Josh Sargent: The pride of soccer-rich St. Louis"], Soccer America, Mike Wotalla, May 26, 2017.</ref> He attended [[St. Dominic High School (O'Fallon, Missouri)|St. Dominic]] for part of his high school years before moving to Florida to join the U.S. residency program.<ref name="stltoday.com">[http://www.stltoday.com/sports/high-school/boys-soccer/former-st-dominic-student-sargent-realizing-dream-with-u-/article_76a5f758-8da0-11e6-a31e-57083590b305.html "Former St. Dominic student Sargent realizing dream with U-17 national team"], ''St. Louis Post-Dispatch'', Steve Overbey, October 8, 2016.</ref> He was ranked as the No. 2 high school soccer player in the country.<ref name="stltoday.com"/>

==Club career==
===Early career===
Sargent played for Scott Gallagher Missouri, a [[U.S. Soccer Development Academy]] club from [[St. Louis]], [[Missouri]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Is Josh Sargent USA Soccer's Next Star? Amazing Goal Vs. Mexico Hints 'Yes'|date=April 27, 2017|url=http://nesn.com/2017/04/josh-sargents-amazing-goal-fuels-usa-u-17-soccers-win-over-mexico/|publisher=NESN|access-date=May 24, 2017}}</ref> After his impressive performance at the 2016 Nike International Friendlies, he spent two weeks training with [[Sporting Kansas City]] in the summer of 2016 and the [[Major League Soccer|MLS]] club acquired the player's acquisition rights,<ref>{{cite web|last1=Galarcep|first1=Ives|title=The Chinese league is continuing to spend big money, and it's time for MLS to start taking it seriously as a threat to the American league's transfer plans.|url=http://www.goal.com/en-us/news/1110/major-league-soccer/2016/12/21/30719412/on-american-soccer-chinese-super-league-hijacks-atlanta?ICID=HP_HN_HP_RI_1_1|website=Goal|access-date=May 24, 2017}}</ref> as St. Louis is in Sporting Kansas City's homegrown player territory.<ref>[http://soccerstl.net/2016/08/24/josh-sargent-u17-national-team-update/ "Josh Sargent U17 National Team Update"], Soccer STL, August 24, 2016.</ref> In October of the same year, Sargent spent a week-long training spell with Dutch club [[PSV Eindhoven]]. In January 2017, after being called for the [[United States men's national under-20 soccer team|United States U20 team]], he trained with German club [[FC Schalke 04]].<ref name="ussoccer.com">{{cite web|title=Five Things to Know About Forward Josh Sargent|url=http://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2017/04/20/20/16/20170420-feat-u17-mnt-five-things-u17mnt-josh-sargent|website=USSoccer|access-date=May 24, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Overbey|first1=Steve|title=Former St. Dominic student Sargent realizing dream with U-17 national team|url=http://www.stltoday.com/sports/high-school/boys-soccer/former-st-dominic-student-sargent-realizing-dream-with-u-/article_76a5f758-8da0-11e6-a31e-57083590b305.html|website=St. Louis Post-Dispatch|access-date=May 24, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Josh Sargent Training at Schalke|url=http://soccerstl.net/2017/01/29/josh-sargent-training-schalke/|website=Soccer STL|date=January 29, 2017|access-date=May 24, 2017}}</ref>

===Werder Bremen===
On September 20, 2017, [[SV Werder Bremen|Werder Bremen]] announced that Sargent would join the club on January 1, 2018, and then sign a professional contract on his 18th birthday, per FIFA rules.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Goff|first1=Steven|title=U.S. teenager Josh Sargent to sign with Werder Bremen|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/soccer-insider/wp/2017/09/20/u-s-teenager-josh-sargent-to-sign-with-werder-bremen/|newspaper=Washington Post|access-date=September 21, 2017}}</ref> He played with the club's under-23 squad in a friendly match and signed his contract on February 20, 2018, making him eligible to join the first team for the 2018–19 season.<ref>{{cite news |last=Creditor |first=Avi |date=February 20, 2018 |title=USA's Josh Sargent Turns 18, Signs His Contract With Werder Bremen |url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2018/02/20/josh-sargent-werder-bremen-signs-contract-birthday |work=[[Sports Illustrated]] |access-date=February 20, 2018}}</ref>

On December 7, 2018, Sargent made his debut for the [[SV Werder Bremen|Werder Bremen]] first team, coming on as a substitute in the 76th minute in a match against [[Fortuna Düsseldorf]]. He scored with his first touch of the match in the 78th minute, the fastest goal by a debutant in club history.<ref>{{cite news |date=December 7, 2018 |title='Beginner's luck' – Sargent scores with first touch on Bundesliga debut |url=https://www.goal.com/en-ae/news/beginners-luck-sargent-scores-with-first-touch-on-bundesliga/13g4uqzed2m5e1psxgorcppyz3 |work=[[Goal (website)|Goal]] |access-date=December 7, 2018}}</ref>

In February 2019, Sargent agreed to a "long-term" contract extension with the club.<ref>{{cite web |title=Josh Sargent signs long-term extension with Werder Bremen |url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/werder-bremen/story/3784556/josh-sargent-signs-long-term-extension-with-werder-bremen |website=ESPN |access-date=February 26, 2019 |date=February 26, 2019}}</ref>

===Norwich City===

====2021–22 season====
On August 9, 2021, Sargent left Werder Bremen to join [[Premier League]] club [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]] on a four-year deal for an undisclosed fee,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.canaries.co.uk/News/2021/august/city-land-usa-international-sargent/|title=City land USA international Sargent|publisher=Norwich City F.C.|date=August 9, 2021}}</ref> reported as in the region of €9.5 million.<ref>{{cite news |title=Josh Sargent transfer: Norwich City signs USMNT forward |url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2021/08/09/josh-sargent-transfer-norwich-city-werder-bremen-usmnt |access-date=August 10, 2021 |work=Sports Illustrated |date=August 9, 2021}}</ref> Five days later, he made his debut in a 3–0 home defeat against [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] after coming on as a substitute at 77th minute in place of his former Werder Bremen teammate [[Milot Rashica]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Glendenning |first1=Barry |title=Norwich City 0-3 Liverpool: Premier League – as it happened! |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2021/aug/14/norwich-city-v-liverpool-premier-league-live |access-date=January 21, 2022 |work=The Guardian |date=August 14, 2022}}</ref> He scored his first two goals for the club on August 24, in an emphatic 6–0 victory over [[AFC Bournemouth|Bournemouth]] in the second round of the [[2021–22 EFL Cup|EFL Cup]].<ref>{{Cite news|title=Norwich hit Bournemouth for six in cup|language=en-GB|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58239592|access-date=August 24, 2021}}</ref>
On January 21, 2022, Sargent scored his first league goals of the season, one of them a [[Scorpion kick|scorpion-kick]], in a 3–0 win over fellow [[Premier League]] relegation battlers [[Watford F.C.|Watford]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Norwich out of bottom three as Sargent double puts Watford in trouble|language=en-GB|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/59990315|access-date=January 21, 2022}}</ref>

====2022–23 season====
Sargent remained at Norwich following their relegation. Sargent scored his first goal of the season in Norwich's first league win of the season against [[Huddersfield Town A.F.C.|Huddersfield Town]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Match centre - Norwich City vs Huddersfield Town |url=https://www.canaries.co.uk/match/2299760/lineups |access-date=October 7, 2022 |website=Norwich City Football Club |language=en}}</ref> Three days later, he notched a brace in a 2–0 win against [[Millwall F.C.|Millwall]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Match centre - Norwich City vs Millwall |url=https://www.canaries.co.uk/match/2299772/lineups |access-date=October 7, 2022 |website=Norwich City Football Club |language=en}}</ref> Sargent continued his goal-scoring form, netting the winner for Norwich away to [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]],<ref>{{Cite web |title=Norwich City vs Sunderland live score, H2H results, standings and prediction |url=sofascore.com/sunderland-norwich-city/Qnf |url-status=live}}</ref> before netting in back-to-back games for the second time of the season in victories against [[Coventry City F.C.|Coventry City]]<ref>{{Cite web |title=Coventry City vs Norwich City live score, H2H and lineups |url=https://www.sofascore.com/norwich-city-coventry-city/lsnf |access-date=2023-03-06 |website=www.sofascore.com |language=en}}</ref> and [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]]<ref>{{Cite web |title=Bristol City vs Norwich City live score, H2H and lineups |url=https://www.sofascore.com/bristol-city-norwich-city/ibsnf |access-date=2023-03-06 |website=www.sofascore.com |language=en}}</ref> respectively. He scored in back-to-back games again, this time in defeats at home against [[Preston North End F.C.|Preston North End]],<ref>{{Cite web |title=Preston North End vs Norwich City live score, H2H and lineups |url=https://www.sofascore.com/norwich-city-preston-north-end/wnf |access-date=2023-03-06 |website=www.sofascore.com |language=en}}</ref> and away to [[Watford F.C.|Watford]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Norwich City vs Watford live score, H2H and lineups |url=https://www.sofascore.com/norwich-city-watford/znf |access-date=2023-03-06 |website=www.sofascore.com |language=en}}</ref> He scored again in a 2–1 home defeat against Middlesbrough, in Norwich's final match before the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Middlesbrough vs Norwich City live score, H2H and lineups |url=https://www.sofascore.com/middlesbrough-norwich-city/Lsnf |access-date=2023-03-06 |website=www.sofascore.com |language=en}}</ref>

After returning from representing the United States at the World Cup, Sargent's goal scoring form slowed, however he still netted in Norwich's 4–2 away win against Coventry,<ref>{{Cite web |title=Coventry City vs Norwich City live score, H2H and lineups |url=https://www.sofascore.com/norwich-city-coventry-city/lsnf |access-date=2023-03-06 |website=www.sofascore.com |language=en}}</ref> and again two weeks later in a 3–1 home win over [[Hull City A.F.C.|Hull City]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Norwich City vs Hull City live score, H2H and lineups |url=https://www.sofascore.com/hull-city-norwich-city/Wbsnf |access-date=2023-03-06 |website=www.sofascore.com |language=en}}</ref> However Sargent suffered an ankle injury in a goal-less draw away to [[Wigan Athletic F.C.|Wigan Athletic]], and would miss the next three games.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-02-20 |title=Josh Sargent ruled out for next two Norwich City matches - SBI Soccer |url=https://sbisoccer.com/2023/02/josh-sargent-ruled-out-for-next-two-norwich-city-matches |access-date=2023-03-06 |website=sbisoccer.com |language=en-US}}</ref>

==International career==
===Youth===
Sargent first appeared in a United States under-14 camp in 2013. In the same year, he was called for the United States under-15.

In 2015, he joined the [[IMG Academy Bradenton|Under-17 MNT Residency Program]] in [[Bradenton, Florida]]. He was a key part of the team that won the 2016 Nike International Friendlies, scoring four goals and notching two assists in three matches.<ref name="ussoccer.com"/> On April 17, 2017, Sargent was included by coach [[John Hackworth]] in the 20-man squad chosen to represent United States at the [[2017 CONCACAF U-17 Championship]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Hackworth Names 20-player Roster for 2017 CONCACAF U-17 Championship|url=http://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2017/04/17/14/41/20170417-u17mnt-hackworth-names-20-player-roster-for-2017-concacaf-u17-championship|website=USSoccer|access-date=May 24, 2017}}</ref> He scored five goals in the tournament, including two goals in a victory over Mexico.<ref>[https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/soccer-insider/wp/2017/04/26/want-to-see-a-great-goal-watch-josh-sargent-in-u-s-u-17s-4-3-win-over-mexico/ "Want to see a great goal? Watch Josh Sargent in U.S. U-17’s 4–3 win over Mexico"], ''Washington Post'', Steven Goff, April 26, 2017.</ref> He led the United States as they reached the final and qualified for the [[2017 FIFA U-17 World Cup]].

Only two days later, Sargent was surprisingly included by coach [[Tab Ramos]] in the 21-man squad called to represent United States at the [[2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|title=RAMOS NAMES USA ROSTER FOR 2017 FIFA U-20 WORLD CUP IN KOREA REPUBLIC|url=http://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2017/05/09/16/25/20170509-news-u20mnt-tab-ramos-names-usa-roster-2017-fifa-u20-world-cup-korea-republic|website=USSoccer|access-date=May 24, 2017}}</ref> On May 22, the Americans debuted in the tournament against [[Ecuador national under-20 football team|Ecuador]]. Sargent started the match and scored two goals, helping his team to tie the match and becoming the youngest American player to ever score in an under-20 World Cup.<ref name=ussf_052217/><ref>{{cite web|last1=Simpson|first1=Christopher|title=Josh Sargent Brace Inspires USA to 3–3 Ecuador Comeback at 2017 U-20 World Cup|url=http://bleacherreport.com/articles/2711035-josh-sargent-brace-inspires-usa-to-3-3-ecuador-comeback-at-2017-u-20-world-cup|website=Bleacher Report|access-date=May 24, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Young Guns Sargent, De La Torre Fire USA to 3–3 Comeback Draw in World Cup Opener vs. Ecuador|url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2017/05/22/08/20/20170522-recap-u20mnt-ecuador-game-story-2017-fifa-u20-world-cup|website=USSoccer|access-date=May 24, 2017}}</ref> Sargent is the second American player, after [[Freddy Adu]], to play in both the under-17 and under-20 World Cups in the same year.<ref>[https://www.si.com/planet-futbol/2017/05/25/josh-sargent-usa-soccer-forward-under-20 "Josh Sargent: Who is USA's 17-year-old rising soccer star?"], ''Sports Illustrated'', May 25, 2017.</ref>
On June 1, 2017, after scoring against [[New Zealand national under-20 football team|New Zealand]], Sargent joined [[Jozy Altidore]], [[Eddie Johnson (American soccer)|Eddie Johnson]] and [[Taylor Twellman]], becoming one of the United States's all-time leading goalscorers at the under-20 World Cup.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sargent comes of age in Korea Republic|url=https://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/news/y=2017/m=6/news=sargent-comes-of-age-in-korea-republic-2892096.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170601200200/http://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/news/y=2017/m=6/news=sargent-comes-of-age-in-korea-republic-2892096.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=June 1, 2017|website=FIFA.com|access-date=June 1, 2017}}</ref>

===Senior===
On November 7, 2017, Sargent received his first senior call up for a friendly against [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]]. He thus became the only American player ever to appear in an under-17, under-20, and senior camp in the same calendar year.<ref>{{cite web|title=SARACHAN CALLS 21 PLAYERS FOR FRIENDLY VS. EUROPEAN CHAMPIONS PORTUGAL|url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2017/11/07/14/39/20171107-news-mnt-sarachan-calls-21-players-for-friendly-against-third-ranked-portugal|website=USSOCCER|access-date=November 8, 2017}}</ref> On May 28, 2018, Sargent earned his first cap for the senior team in a friendly against [[Bolivia national football team|Bolivia]] and scored his first international goal. He became the fourth-youngest player to score for the United States, until [[Timothy Weah]], two days younger than Sargent, scored his first goal minutes later.<ref>{{cite news |last=Blum |first=Ronald |date=May 28, 2018 |title=Sargent & Weah, both 18, score as US beats Bolivia 3–0 |url=https://www.sfchronicle.com/sports/article/Sargent-Weah-both-18-score-as-US-beats-12949557.php |work=[[San Francisco Chronicle]] |agency=Associated Press |access-date=May 28, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180529013056/https://www.sfchronicle.com/sports/article/Sargent-Weah-both-18-score-as-US-beats-12949557.php |archive-date=May 29, 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=USA 3–0 Bolivia |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44287954 |access-date=August 5, 2018 |work=BBC Sport |date=May 29, 2018}}</ref>

On 9 November 2022, Sargent was named in the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]] 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]]. Sargent would play in all 3 of the United States' group matches, starting the games against [[Wales national football team|Wales]] and [[Iran national football team|Iran]]. Sargent hit the post with a header early in the match against Wales.<ref>{{Cite web |title=USA vs Wales live score, H2H and lineups |url=https://www.sofascore.com/usa-wales/cUbszUb |access-date=2023-03-06 |website=www.sofascore.com |language=en}}</ref> He was substituted after picking up an ankle injury against Iran. This ruled Sargent out of the United States' [[2022 FIFA World Cup knockout stage|round of 16 clash]] against the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-11-30 |title='I can't even look' - Norwich star Sargent suffers freak injury during USA win |url=https://talksport.com/football/1261804/josh-sargent-freak-injury-usa-iran-world-cup/ |access-date=2023-03-06 |website=talkSPORT |language=en-US}}</ref> 

== Personal life ==
He is married to Kirsten Lepping. They were engaged in August 2020. Sargent has one daughter who was born on January 13, 2022.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Mutch |first1=Michael |title=Every word Norwich boss Dean Smith said on Todd Cantwell, Josh Sargent and the West Ham defeat |url=https://www.norfolklive.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/norwich-dean-smith-west-ham-6485677 |website=Norfolk Live |access-date=January 13, 2022 |date=January 13, 2022}}</ref>
