'''Pierre-Emile Kordt Højbjerg''' (born 5 August 1995) is a Danish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[central midfielder]] for [[Premier League]] club [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] and the [[Denmark national football team|Denmark national team]].

Højbjerg started his career as a youth for Danish clubs [[BK Skjold]], [[Kjøbenhavns Boldklub]] and [[Brøndby IF]] before joining German club [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] in 2012. He made his senior debut for [[FC Bayern Munich II|the reserves]] that year and his professional debut for the first team in April 2013; at 17 he was the club's youngest player in the [[Bundesliga]]. He made 25 total appearances for Bayern and won a variety of domestic and international tournaments, also having loans at fellow league teams [[FC Augsburg]] and [[FC Schalke 04|Schalke 04]]. In 2016, he signed a five-year contract for [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] for an estimated £12.8 million fee. After making 134 appearances for the Saints, he joined Tottenham Hotspur in 2020 for a reported initial fee of £15 million.

Højbjerg was a youth international at various levels from [[Denmark national under-16 football team|under-16]] to [[Denmark national under-21 football team|under-21]]. He made his debut for the [[Denmark national football team|Denmark national team]] in 2014, and represented the side at [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Club career==
===Early career===
While initially playing for [[BK Skjold]] and [[Kjøbenhavns Boldklub]] he moved to [[Brøndby IF]] as a junior player at age 14.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://worldfootball.net/spieler_profil/pierre-hoejbjerg/ | title = Pierre Højbjerg | publisher = worldfootball.net | access-date = 14 May 2013 | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20130518062827/http://www.worldfootball.net/spieler_profil/pierre-hoejbjerg/ | archive-date = 18 May 2013 | df = dmy-all }}</ref> He looks upon [[Zinedine Zidane]] as his role model.<ref>{{cite web|title= Bayerns Supertalent: Zidane ist sein Vorbild |trans-title=Bayern's super talent: Zidane is his role model |url=http://www.fussball-vorort.de/vereine/m/fc-bayern-muenchen/fc-bayern-muenchen-2/bayern-muenchen-pierre-emile-hojbjerg-stammspieler-mehmet-scholl-2550882.html|publisher=fussball-vorort.de|access-date=26 April 2013 | date = 17 October 2012 | language = de}}</ref> Aged just 5, Højbjerg began attending training sessions with his six-year-old brother at local club Skjold. He was deployed as a striker by Copenhagen. Starting from his debut, Højbjerg plays as a central midfielder and sometimes right wide midfielder. He has since switched to a defensive midfield role.

In 2011, Højbjerg was honoured with the title "Danish under-17 player of the year".<ref name="DBU's Talentpris" /> In 2013, he was honoured with the title "Danish talent of the year" by [[Spillerforeningen]].<ref name="2013 Danish talent of the year"/>

===Bayern Munich===
[[File:2014-05-18-Pierre Emile Højbjerg.jpg|thumb|upright|Højbjerg after winning the [[2014 DFB-Pokal Final]] with [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]]]]
Højbjerg joined Bayern in July 2012<ref name="Augsburg leiht Højbjerg aus">{{cite news|title=Augsburg leiht Højbjerg aus|url=http://www.sueddeutsche.de/sport/wechsel-von-fc-bayern-profi-augsburg-leiht-hjbjerg-aus-1.2294812|access-date=9 January 2015|publisher=Süddeutsche Zeitung|date=7 January 2015|language=de}}</ref> and, at the age of 17 years and 251 days,<ref name="Pierre-Emile Hojbjerg: Das ist Augsburgs Neuzugang">{{cite news|title=Pierre-Emile Hojbjerg: Das ist Augsburgs 2015}}</ref> made his professional debut on 13 April 2013<ref name="Pierre-Emile Hojbjerg: Das ist Augsburgs Neuzugang"/> in the [[Bundesliga]] against [[1. FC Nürnberg]],<ref name="FC Bayern demontiert schwachen Club">{{cite news|title=FC Bayern demontiert schwachen Club|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/spieltag/1-bundesliga/2012-13/29/1441895/spielanalyse_bayern-muenchen-14_1-fc-nuernberg-81.html|access-date=9 January 2015|publisher=kicker|date=13 April 2013|language=de}}</ref> as a substitute for [[Xherdan Shaqiri]]<ref name="FC Bayern demontiert schwachen Club"/> and thus became the youngest player ever to play for the Bayern Munich first team in the Bundesliga.<ref name="Pierre-Emile Hojbjerg: Das ist Augsburgs Neuzugang"/> He finished the 2012–13 season with no goals in two matches played for the first team and eight goals in 30 matches played for the [[FC Bayern Munich II|reserve team]].<ref name="Spieler-Statistik 2012/13">{{cite web | url = http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/vereine/2012-13/74860/vereinsspieler_hoejbjerg-pierre-emile.html | title = Pierre-Emile Höjbjerg | language = de | publisher = kicker.de | access-date = 21 March 2015}}</ref>

In December of that year, he was in the Bayern squad which won the [[2013 FIFA Club World Cup]] in Morocco, although he did not enter the field of play during the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|title=Guangzhou Evergrande FC – FC Bayern München|url=https://www.fifa.com/clubworldcup/matches/round=259715/match=300260484/report.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131216190942/http://www.fifa.com/clubworldcup/matches/round=259715/match=300260484/report.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=16 December 2013|publisher=[[FIFA]]|access-date=12 September 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Bayern Munich 2–0 Raja Casablanca|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/25406403|access-date=12 September 2014|work=[[BBC Sport]]|date=21 December 2014}}</ref> On 17 May 2014, Højbjerg started the [[2014 DFB-Pokal Final|DFB-Pokal final]] for Bayern Munich, a 2–0 extra-time win over [[Borussia Dortmund]]. He played for a total of 102 minutes before being substituted in the first half of extra time for [[Daniel Van Buyten]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Robben bestraft BVB-Fehler in der Verlängerung [Robben punishes BVB's mistake in extra time]|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/dfbpokal/spielrunde/dfb-pokal/2013-14/6/2393337/spielbericht_borussia-dortmund-17_bayern-muenchen-14.html|access-date=20 May 2014|newspaper=[[Kicker (sports magazine)|kicker]]|date=17 May 2014}}</ref> He finished the 2013–14 season with seven Bundesliga appearances and two German Cup appearances for the first team and four goals from 14 appearances for the reserve team.<ref name="Spieler-Statistik 2013/14">{{cite web | url = http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/vereine/2013-14/74860/vereinsspieler_hoejbjerg-pierre-emile.html | title = Pierre-Emile Höjbjerg | language = de | publisher = kicker.de | access-date = 21 March 2015}}</ref>

His first match of the 2014–15 season was the [[2014 DFL-Supercup|DFL-Supercup]] on 13 August, playing the first hour and then being substituted for [[Mario Götze]], as Bayern lost 2–0 at Borussia Dortmund.<ref name="2014dflsupercup">{{cite web | title = Aubameyang köpft BVB zum Supercup-Sieg | url = http://www.kicker.de/d-supercup-fb-1/2014/1/2396815/spielbericht_bayern-muenchen-14_borussia-dortmund-17.html | date = 13 August 2014 | language = de | access-date = 17 June 2020 | website = kicker.de}}</ref> On 8 January 2015, Bayern extended Højbjerg's contract to 2018.<ref>{{cite news|title=Hojbjerg-Leihe bestätigt - On 9 May, he assisted [[Raúl Bobadilla]] for the only goal as Augsburg won away at Bayern, who had already won the league by that point.<ref>{{cite news|title=Bayern Mun 0-1 FC Augsburg|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/32589589|access-date=9 May 2015|work=BBC Sport|date=9 May 2015}}</ref> He finished his loan spell with two goals from 16 Bundesliga appearances.<ref name="Club matches" />

On 28 August 2015, Højbjerg joined fellow Bundesliga side [[Schalke 04]] on a season-long loan.<ref>{{cite news|title=Hojbjerg joins Schalke on loan from Bayern|url=http://www.goal.com/en-za/news/4682/transfer-zone/2015/08/28/14839302/hojbjerg-joins-schalke-on-loan-from-bayern|access-date=28 August 2015|work=Goal.com|date=28 August 2015}}</ref> He played 30 games for the [[Gelsenkirchen]]-based club, including six in the Europa League, and at the end of the season he told Danish newspaper [[B.T. (tabloid)|''B.T.'']] that the time had arrived to leave Bayern Munich and become a first-team regular elsewhere.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Bienkowski|first1=Stefan|title=Bayern Munich midfielder Pierre-Emile Hojbjerg confirms club exit|url=http://www.dw.com/en/bayern-munich-midfielder-pierre-emile-hojbjerg-confirms-club-exit/a-19279446|access-date=18 March 2018|publisher=Deutsche Welle|date=24 May 2016}}</ref>

===Southampton===
[[File:Southampton FC versus FC Augsburg (36228025891) (cropped).jpg|thumb|upright|Højbjerg playing for [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] in 2017]]
On 11 July 2016, Højbjerg joined English club [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] on a five-year deal for an estimated fee of £12.8 million.<ref name="BBC36764528">{{cite web|title=Pierre-Emile Hojbjerg: Southampton sign Bayern Munich midfielder|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/36764528|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=11 July 2016|date=11 July 2016}}</ref> He made his debut in the [[Premier League]] on 13 August as the season began with a 1–1 home draw with [[Watford F.C.|Watford]], coming on in the 55th minute in place of [[James Ward-Prowse]]. [[BBC Sport]]'s Neil Johnston wrote that he "injected pace and urgency" into the Saints, "was not afraid to shoot and was hard-working".<ref>{{cite news| last1=Johnston|first1=Neil|title=Southampton 1–1 Watford| url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/36994444|access-date=29 December 2017|publisher=BBC Sport|date=13 August 2017}}</ref> Although he played all five matches on the way to the [[2017 EFL Cup Final]], Højbjerg was an unused substitute in the match at [[Wembley Stadium]], which Southampton lost 3–2 to [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Burt|first1=Jason|title=Manchester United 3 Southampton 2, EFL Cup final: Zlatan Ibrahimovic inspires Jose Mourinho's side to cup glory|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2017/02/26/manchester-united-vs-southampton-efl-cup-final-live-score-updates/|access-date=29 December 2017|work=The Daily Telegraph|date=27 February 2017}}</ref>

Højbjerg scored his first goal for the Saints in his 54th match on 18 March 2018, opening a 2–0 win at [[EFL League One|League One]] club [[Wigan Athletic F.C.|Wigan Athletic]] in the sixth round of the [[FA Cup]], the first match under new manager [[Mark Hughes]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Sanders|first1=Emma|title=Wigan Athletic 0&ndash;2 Southampton|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/43361316|access-date=18 March 2018|publisher=BBC Sport|date=18 March 2018}}</ref> In December that year, Hughes' successor [[Ralph Hasenhüttl]] made him the Saints' captain.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Rudd |first1=Alyson |title=Pierre-Emile Hojbjerg interview: 'Pep Guardiola is the most demanding person I've met' |url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/pierre-emile-hojbjerg-interview-pep-guardiola-is-the-most-demanding-person-ive-met-qq237mzg8 |access-date=29 September 2019 |work=The Times |date=29 December 2018}}</ref> However in June 2020, Hojbjerg was stripped of the captaincy after publicly speaking about his desire to leave the club, with Ward-Prowse replacing him as captain.<ref>{{cite news | url = https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2020/06/17/premier-league-returns-lockdowngrounds-optimism-guinea-pig-stadium/ | title = Pierre-Emile Hojbjerg stripped of Southampton captaincy after stating desire to leave club  | work = The Telegraph | date = 17 June 2020 | access-date = 17 June 2020 | first = Sam | last = Dean}}</ref>

==International career==
Højbjerg was born in Denmark to a Danish father and French mother.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.dr.dk/sporten/fodbold/landsholdet/hoejbjerg-lidt-mere-amour-i-frankrig|title=Højbjerg: Lidt mere 'amour' i Frankrig | language = da | website = dr.dk | date = 11 June 2015 | access-date = 17 July 2017 | first = Aleksandar | last = Josevski}}</ref> He was called up to the [[Denmark national football team|Denmark senior side]] for the first time in May 2014 for friendlies against [[Hungary national football team|Hungary]] and [[Sweden national football team|Sweden]] and made his debut against Sweden on 28 May, playing the full 90 minutes.<ref>{{cite news|title=Morten O. storroser Højbjerg efter debut [Morten O. commend Højbjerg after his debut]|url=http://www.bold.dk/nyt/Morten-O-storroser-Hoejbjerg-efter-debut|access-date=28 May 2014|newspaper=Bold.dk|date=28 May 2014}}</ref> On his third cap on 7 September 2014 he scored his first international goal, equalising as Denmark began their [[UEFA Euro 2016 qualification]] campaign with a 2–1 win over [[Armenia national football team|Armenia]] in Copenhagen.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Vinde|first1=Nicklas|title=Denmark dig deep to down Armenia|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro/qualifiers/season=2016/matches/round=2000446/match=2013820/postmatch/report/|access-date=12 September 2014|publisher=[[UEFA]]|date=7 September 2014}}</ref>

In May 2018, he was named in Denmark's preliminary 35-man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia,<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.goal.com/en-gb/amp/news/revealed-every-world-cup-2018-squad-23-man-preliminary-lists/oa0atsduflsv1nsf6oqk576rb | title = Revealed: Every World Cup 2018 squad - 23-man & preliminary lists & when will they be announced? | website =  goal.com | access-date = 17 May 2018}}</ref> although he did not make the final 23.<ref>{{cite web |title=Confirmed: Denmark cut ex-Arsenal star from FIFA World Cup squad |url=https://www.socceroos.com.au/news/confirmed-denmark-cut-ex-arsenal-star-fifa-world-cup-squad |website=socceroos.com |access-date=16 June 2018 |date=3 June 2018}}</ref>

Højbjerg was named in the Danish squad for the delayed [[UEFA Euro 2020]] in June 2021.<ref>{{cite news |title=Denmark name Schmeichel and Hojbjerg in Euro 2020 squad |url=https://theathletic.com/news/denmark-euro-2020-squad-schmeichel-hojbjerg/s2Qd0osUjLpy |access-date=13 June 2021 |work=The Athletic |date=26 May 2021}}</ref> In their opening match, a 1–0 loss to [[Finland national football team|Finland]], he missed a penalty; the game had been stopped and resumed due to [[Christian Eriksen]]'s medical emergency.<ref>{{cite news |last1=O'Brien |first1=Sean |title=Denmark lose to Finland after Christian Eriksen collapse as Euro 2020 match resumes with Kasper Schmeichel mistake and Pierre-Emile Hojbjerg penalty miss after midfielder was confirmed to be OK in hospital |url=https://talksport.com/football/894271/denmark-finland-eriksen-collapse-euro-2020-schmeichel-hojbjerg/ |access-date=13 June 2021 |publisher=Talksport |date=12 June 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Højbjerg was born in [[Copenhagen]] and grew up in the [[Østerbro]] district of the city. He is the second of three children. Højbjerg was introduced to football when he was five years old.<ref>{{cite web|title=Hojbjerg: I won't let Pep down|url=https://www.fcbayernmunich.com/en/news/news/2014/birthday-interview-with-pierre-emile-hojbjerg-050814.php/|publisher=FC Bayern Munich|access-date=5 August 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151222115844/https://www.fcbayernmunich.com/en/news/news/2014/birthday-interview-with-pierre-emile-hojbjerg-050814.php/|archive-date=22 December 2015}}</ref> Højbjerg's father Christian died of stomach cancer in April 2014, five days after his first start for Bayern.<ref>{{cite web |last=Cunningham |first=Sam |url=https://inews.co.uk/sport/football/pierre-emile-hojbjerg-guardiola-bayern-munich-146386 |title=Pierre-Emile Højbjerg: 'Grief is a difficult thing – I learned the hard way' |work=[[i (newspaper)|i]] |date=20 April 2018 |access-date=28 October 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63418546 |title=Pierre-Emile Hojbjerg: Tottenham midfielder on caring for his dying father |work=[[BBC Sport]] |date=28 October 2022 |access-date=28 October 2022}}</ref>
