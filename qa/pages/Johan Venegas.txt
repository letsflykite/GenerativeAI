'''Johan Alberto Venegas Ulloa''' (born 27 November 1988) is a Costa Rican professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays for [[Liga FPD]] club [[Liga Deportiva Alajuelense|Alajuelense]] and the [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica national team]].

==Club career==
Venegas started his career at [[Santos de Guápiles]] and had a short loan spell at [[Club Deportivo Barrio México|Barrio México]] in 2011. He joined [[Puntarenas F.C.|Puntarenas]] in 2012,<ref>[http://161.58.182.33/2012-07-25/Deportes/Un-nuevo-proceso-que-se-inicia-con-ilusion.aspx Un nuevo proceso que se inicia con ilusión] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141229132854/http://161.58.182.33/2012-07-25/Deportes/Un-nuevo-proceso-que-se-inicia-con-ilusion.aspx |date=29 December 2014 }} - Nación {{in lang|es}}</ref>  and had a big season with the club scoring 12 goals in 37 appearances. This led to a move to top club [[L.D. Alajuelense|Alajuelense]] in 2013.<ref>[http://www.crhoy.com/ariel-rodriguez-y-johan-venegas-se-unen-a-los-refuerzos-de-alajuelense/ Ariel Rodríguez y Johan Venegas se unen a los refuerzos de Alajuelense] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303234225/http://www.crhoy.com/ariel-rodriguez-y-johan-venegas-se-unen-a-los-refuerzos-de-alajuelense/ |date=3 March 2016 }} - CRHoy {{in lang|es}}</ref> In his first year for Alajuelense Vanegas quickly established himself as a starter on the club making 34 appearances and scoring 4 goals. The following season, he continued his fine form and ended the season with a career high 13 goals in 38 matches.

In July 2015, Venegas joined [[Major League Soccer]] team [[CF Montréal|Montreal Impact]], after impressing the Canadian club during the [[2014–15 CONCACAF Champions League]].<ref>[http://www.nacion.com/deportes/futbol-costa-rica/Johan_Venegas-Alajuelense-Impact_de_Montreal_0_1501649951.html Venegas se une al Montreal Impact] - La Nacion {{in lang|es}}</ref> During his time with Montreal, Venegas appeared in 32 league matches and scored two goals. He also made seven appearances during the Playoffs, scoring one goal.

On 13 December 2016, Venegas was traded to [[Minnesota United FC]] in exchange for [[Chris Duvall]].<ref>[http://www.mnufc.com/post/2016/12/13/loons-select-johan-venegas-trade Loons Select Johan Venegas In Trade] - mnufc.com {{in lang|en}}</ref>

After spending the 2017 season with Minnesota, the club announced that Venegas would be loaned to Costa Rican side [[Deportivo Saprissa]] for 2018.<ref>[https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2017/12/22/minnesota-united-loan-johan-venegas-costa-rican-club-saprissa Minnesota United loan Johan Venegas to Costa Rican club Saprissa] - MLSSoccer.com {{in lang|en}}</ref> His contract with Minnesota ended following the 2018 season.

On 26 December 2020, Venegas was announced as Alajuelense's new signing.

==International career==
Venegas made his debut for the [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica national team]] in the September [[2014 Copa Centroamericana]] opening match against [[Nicaragua national football team|Nicaragua]], in which he scored the third goal.<ref>[http://www.aldia.cr/futbol-costa-rica/seleccion/Johan-Venegas-debuto-gol-Tricolor_0_421157899.html Johan Venegas debutó con gol en la Selección de Costa Rica] - Al Día {{in lang|es}}</ref> He was again on target in his second game against [[Panama national football team|Panama]], netting Costa Rica's 100th goal in all Copa Centroamerica tournaments<ref>[http://www.aldia.cr/futbol-costa-rica/seleccion/Johan_Venegas-Seleccion_Nacional-Costa_Rica-Panama-Copa_Uncaf_0_423557663.html Johan Venegas anotó el gol 100 de Costa Rica en Copas Centroamericanas] -</ref> in the process.

In May 2018 he was named in Costa Rica's 23-man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.goal.com/en/amp/news/costa-rica-names-23-man-world-cup-squad-featuring-keylor/1bw2iz8pkma5614bzushqyyo7g |title=Costa Rica national team World Cup squad: Oscar Ramirez names 23-man roster featuring Keylor Navas, six MLS players {{!}} Goal.com}}</ref>
