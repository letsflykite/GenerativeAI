'''Benjamin Thomas Davies''' (born 24 April 1993) is a Welsh professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[left-back]] and [[central defender]] for {{English football updater|TottenhH}} club [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] and the [[Wales national football team|Wales national team]].

Davies is an academy graduate of [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]], a Welsh club based in [[Swansea]] who play in the [[English football league system]], also joining Danish club [[Viborg FF]]'s academy while his family lived in Denmark. He made his professional debut for Swansea City in the second [[Premier League]] game of the [[2012–13 Swansea City A.F.C. season|2012–13]] season against [[West Ham United]], scoring his first professional goal in a 3–1 Premier League win against [[Stoke City FC|Stoke City]] in January 2013. In the [[2013–14 Swansea City A.F.C. season|2013–14]] season, Davies was once again a regular for Swansea City.

In July 2014, Davies was signed by Tottenham Hotspur for an undisclosed fee, making his debut for Tottenham in a [[2014–15 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]] game against Cypriot side [[AEL Limassol]]. During [[Mauricio Pochettino]]'s tenure, Davies battled [[Danny Rose (footballer, born 1990)|Danny Rose]] for the starting left-back spot, but Davies was redeployed as a left-sided centre-back in a defensive back three by [[Antonio Conte]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |title=A degree, COVID and life in the back three - a special interview with Ben Davies |url=https://www.tottenhamhotspur.com/news/2021/december/a-degree-covid-and-life-in-the-back-three-a-special-interview-with-ben-davies/ |access-date=11 April 2022 |website=Tottenham Hotspur |language=en}}</ref>

Davies made three appearances for the [[Wales national under-19 football team|Welsh national U-19 football team]] in his youth. His appearances all came as part of [[2012 UEFA European Under-19 Championship qualification|qualification for the 2012 UEFA European Under-19 Championship]]. Davies made his debut senior [[Wales national football team|Welsh national team]] against Scotland at the [[2014 FIFA World Cup qualification]] in 2012. He has since made over 70 senior appearances for Wales, and represented the side at [[UEFA Euro 2016]], reaching the semi-finals, and [[UEFA Euro 2020]]. Davies also helped Wales qualify for the [[FIFA World Cup]] in 2022, the last time was in 1958.

==Early life==
Davies was born in [[Neath]], West Glamorgan.<ref name=Hugman/> After spending time in the youth academy at [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]], he and his family moved to [[Viborg, Denmark|Viborg]], Denmark, after his father accepted a job offer. They spent three years living in Denmark, where he played for [[Viborg FF]]'s youth team before returning to Swansea.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ben Davies Interview: Made in Wales, Nurtured by Denmark|url=https://lostboyos.wordpress.com/2013/07/31/ben-davies-interview-made-in-wales-nurtured-by-denmark/|website=Lost Boyos|access-date=17 February 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191114211817/https://lostboyos.wordpress.com/2013/07/31/ben-davies-interview-made-in-wales-nurtured-by-denmark/|archive-date=14 November 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref> Davies attended [[Ysgol Gyfun Ystalyfera]] and he is a fluent [[Welsh language]] speaker.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/wales/20644833|title=Swansea City's Ben Davies thrilled with his rise into the first team|date=7 December 2012|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=2 October 2017}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Swansea City===
After spending time with the youth ranks, Davies signed a two-year contract with [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]], making his first-team debut against [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]] for the [[2012–13 Premier League|Premier League]] on 25 August 2012, coming on as an 84th minute [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] for [[Neil Taylor (footballer)|Neil Taylor]] in a 3–0 home win.<ref name=2YearContract>{{cite news|title=Defender Ben Davies signs new Swansea City contract|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/18862403|publisher=BBC Sport|date=16 July 2012|access-date=25 August 2012}}</ref><ref name=PremierLeagueDebut>{{cite news|last=Williams|first=Aled|title=Swansea 3–0 West Ham|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/19304610|publisher=BBC Sport|date=25 August 2012|access-date=25 August 2012}}</ref>

Davies became a regular starter for Swansea in the 2012–13 season, following a long-term injury to Taylor.<ref>{{cite news|title=Swansea City youngster Ben Davies happy to be thrown in deep end|url=http://www.walesonline.co.uk/footballnation/football-news/2012/10/09/swansea-city-youngster-ben-davies-hapy-to-be-thrown-in-deep-end-91466-31992834/|publisher=Wales Online|date=9 October 2012|access-date=21 November 2012}}</ref>

On 23 November 2012, Davies signed a new three-and-a-half-year contract with Swansea.<ref>{{cite news|title=Ben Davies signs new Swansea City contract|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/20467822|publisher=BBC Sport|date=23 November 2012}}</ref>

On 19 January 2013, Davies scored his first goal for Swansea against [[Stoke City F.C.|Stoke City]] in a 3–1 victory.<ref name=age>{{cite news|title=Swansea 3–1 Stoke|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/21098582|publisher=BBC Sport|date=19 January 2013|access-date=19 January 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130119170734/http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/21098582|archive-date=19 January 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> In doing so, at 19 years and 270 days, he became Swansea's youngest scorer in the Premier League.<ref name=age/> In September 2013, he scored his second and third Premier League goals for Swansea, against [[West Bromwich Albion|West Brom]] and [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] respectively.<ref>{{cite news|title=West Brom 0–2 Swansea|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/23830994|publisher=BBC Sport|date=1 September 2013|access-date=1 September 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Swansea 1–2 Arsenal|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/24185190|publisher=BBC Sport|date=28 September 2013|access-date=28 September 2013}}</ref>

On 24 December 2013, Davies signed a one-year contract extension, keeping him at Swansea until June 2017.<ref>{{cite news|title=Swansea defender Ben Davies signs a contract extension until 2017|url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/11731/9087040/premier-league-swansea-defender-ben-davies-signs-a-contract-extension-until-2017|publisher=Sky Sports|date=24 December 2013}}</ref>

===Tottenham Hotspur===
[[File:Ben Davies 2016.jpg|thumb|upright|Davies playing for [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] in 2016]]

On 23 July 2014, Davies joined [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] on a five-year contract for an undisclosed fee. He joined on the same day as his Swansea teammate [[Michel Vorm]], with [[Gylfi Sigurðsson]] being exchanged as part of the deal.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/28413195|title=Ben Davies & Michel Vorm join Spurs, Swansea sign Sigurdsson|date=23 July 2014|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Deal for Swansea duo agreed|url=http://www.tottenhamhotspur.com/news/deal-for-swansea-duo-agreed-230714/|publisher=Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|date=23 July 2014}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |title=Sigurdsson seals Swans return |url=http://www.swanseacity.com/news/article/gylfi-joins-1777692.aspx |publisher=Swansea City A.F.C |date=23 July 2014 |access-date=23 July 2014 }}{{Dead link|date=October 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Davies made his Premier League debut for Tottenham Hotspur as a late substitute in a 0–3 defeat to [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/28917060 |title=Tottenham Hotspur 0–3 Liverpool |publisher=BBC Sport|date=31 August 2014 |access-date=31 August 2014}}</ref> He made his first start for the club in the league in a 2–1 away win at [[Hull City A.F.C.|Hull City]] on 23 November 2014.{{citation needed|date=December 2017}} He was an unused substitute for the [[2015 Football League Cup Final|2015 League Cup Final]] at [[Wembley Stadium]] on 1 March 2015, in which Tottenham were beaten 2–0 by [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]].<ref name=2015LC>{{cite news |first=Phil |last=McNulty |title=Chelsea 2–0 Tottenham Hotspur |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/31572594 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=1 March 2015 |access-date=9 December 2017}}</ref>

Davies scored his first goal for the club on 8 January 2017, a header in an FA Cup tie against [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/ben-davies-tottenham-proved-they-possess-winning-mentality-with-fa-cup-victory-over-aston-villa-a3435526.html|title=Spurs proved they have 'winning mentality' with Villa victory&nbsp;– Davies|date=8 January 2017|newspaper=Evening Standard|access-date=13 March 2017}}</ref> He signed a new four-year contract at Tottenham on 9 March 2017, extending his deal to 2021.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/11675/10795806/tottenham-left-back-ben-davies-signs-new-four-year-contract|title=Tottenham left-back Ben Davies signs new four-year contract|publisher=Sky Sports|access-date=13 March 2017}}</ref>

Davies scored his second Tottenham goal – and his first for them in the Premier League – against Hull City on the final day of the 2016–17 season; a first-time strike from outside the box into the top left corner added a sixth goal in an eventual 7–1 win for Tottenham.<ref>{{cite news |title=Hull City 1–7 Tottenham Hotspur|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/39912022 |publisher=BBC Sport |first=Alex |last=Bysouth |access-date=21 May 2017|date=21 May 2017}}</ref>

Davies started for Tottenham in the opening match of the [[2017–18 Tottenham Hotspur F.C. season|2017–18 season]], and scored a goal in a 2–0 win away to [[Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2017/08/13/newcastle-0-tottenham-2-jonjo-shelvey-sees-red-stamp-dele-alli/ |title=Newcastle 0 Tottenham 2: Jonjo Shelvey sees red for stamp on Dele Alli as Spurs cruise to opening win |first=Jason|last= Mellor|date=13 August 2017 |newspaper=The Daily Telegraph |location=London |url-access=subscription}}</ref>

Davies signed a new 5-year contract with Tottenham in July 2019.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/tottenham/harry-winks-and-ben-davies-sign-new-fiveyear-contracts-with-tottenham-a4185171.html |title=Harry Winks and Ben Davies sign new five-year contracts with Tottenham |first=Alex|last= Young |date=8 July 2019 |work=Evening Standard }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/48915066|title=Tottenham: Ben Davies and Harry Winks sign new five-year contracts with Spurs|date=8 July 2019|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref>

Davies scored his first goal of the [[2020–21 Tottenham Hotspur F.C. season|2020–21 season]], his first in over three years, in the [[2020–21 EFL Cup|Carabao Cup]] tie against [[Stoke City F.C.|Stoke City]], helping the team win 3–1 to reach the semi-finals.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/fa-league-cups/stoke-vs-tottenham-result-score-carabao-b1778389.html |title=Ben Davies and Harry Kane seal Tottenham win over Stoke to reach Carabao Cup semi-finals |work=The Independent |date=22 December 2020 }}</ref>

At the start of the [[2021–22 Tottenham Hotspur F.C. season|2021–22 season]], Davies started only one Premier League game during [[Nuno Espírito Santo]]'s brief tenure as head coach. He has been a regular starter since [[Antonio Conte]] was hired as head coach in November 2021, where he has been redeployed as a left-sided centre-back as part of a defensive back three, the same role he tends to play for the Wales national team.<ref name=":0" />

Davies scored his first goal of the [[2022–23 Tottenham Hotspur F.C. season|2022–23 season]] in a league match against [[AFC Bournemouth|Bournemouth]] on 29 October, an equaliser in an eventual 3–2 comeback victory.<ref>{{cite web |title=Bournemouth 2–3 Tottenham Hotspur: Bentancur scores late winner as Spurs fight back |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63354914 |date=29 October 2022 |access-date=29 October 2022}}</ref> For Tottenham's 2-0 victory against West Ham on 19 February 2023, Davies was deployed in his old position of left wing-back; he assisted [[Emerson Royal]] for the opening goal, and described the attacking role as "fun".<ref>{{Cite web |title=It was fun – Ben Davies happy to continue in wing-back role for Tottenham |url=https://uk.sports.yahoo.com/news/fun-ben-davies-happy-continue-120047254.html |access-date=2023-02-20 |website=uk.sports.yahoo.com |language=en-GB}}</ref>

==International career==
In September 2012, Davies was selected for the [[Wales national football team|Wales]] squad for [[2014 FIFA World Cup qualification]] matches in September 2012. He replaced club teammate Neil Taylor, who had suffered a broken ankle in a Premier League match against [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Swansea's Ben Davies has been added to Wales' squad to face Belgium and Serbia|url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/12018/8048235/swanseas-ben-davies-has-been-added-to-wales-squad-to-face-belgium-and-serbia|newspaper=Sky Sports|date=3 September 2012|access-date=30 June 2018}}</ref> Davies made his full international debut in the 2–1 win against [[Scotland national football team|Scotland]] on 12 October 2012.<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.faw.org.uk/MatchReport.ink?MatchID=58372&type=m|title = Match Report: Wales v Scotland 12 October 2012|date = 12 October 2012|access-date = 27 December 2014|publisher = Football Association of Wales|url-status = dead|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20141006130707/http://www.faw.org.uk/MatchReport.ink?MatchID=58372&type=m|archive-date = 6 October 2014|df = dmy-all}}</ref> In May 2021 he was selected for the Wales squad for the delayed [[UEFA Euro 2020]] tournament.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57301006|title=Uncapped Colwill in Wales Euro 2020 squad|work=BBC Sport }}</ref> In November 2022 he was named in the Wales squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63545011 2022 Wales World Cup squad]</ref>

== Outside football ==
In the summer of 2021, Davies was awarded a 2:1 degree in Economics and Business from the [[Open University]], after balancing his studies with his job as a professional footballer over a period of five years.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Gold |first=Alasdair |date=29 December 2021 |title=Ben Davies on his new role under Antonio Conte and his recent Covid experience |url=https://www.football.london/tottenham-hotspur-fc/players/ben-davies-covid-antonio-conte-22599139 |access-date=11 April 2022 |website=Football.London |language=en}}</ref>
