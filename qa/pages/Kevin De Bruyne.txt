'''Kevin De Bruyne''' (born 28 June 1991) is a Belgian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] and captains for {{English football updater|ManchesC}} club [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] and [[Captain (association football)|captains]] the [[Belgium national football team|Belgium national team]]. He is widely regarded as one of the best players of his generation as well as one of the best midfielders in the world. Pundits often describe him as a complete footballer.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.si.com/soccer/manchestercity/features-opinions/pep-guardiola-believes-kevin-de-bruyne-is-the-worlds-best-midfielder|title=Pep Guardiola Believes Kevin De Bruyne Is The World's Best Midfielder|publisher=Sports Illustrated|date=28 July 2022|access-date=16 September 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.si.com/soccer/manchestercity/news/kevin-de-bruyne-voted-second-in-uefa-mens-player-of-the-year-award|title=Kevin De Bruyne Voted Second In UEFA Men's Player Of The Year Award|publisher=Sports Illustrated|date=25 August 2022|access-date=16 September 2022}}</ref> 

De Bruyne began his career at [[K.R.C. Genk|Genk]], where he was a regular player when they won the [[2010–11 Belgian Pro League]]. In 2012 he joined English club [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]], where he was used sparingly and then loaned to [[SV Werder Bremen|Werder Bremen]]. He signed with [[VfL Wolfsburg|Wolfsburg]] for £18&nbsp;million in 2014, where he established himself as one of the best players in the [[Bundesliga]] and was integral in the club's [[2014–15 DFB-Pokal]] win. In the summer of 2015 De Bruyne joined Manchester City for a club record £54&nbsp;million. He has since  won the [[UEFA Champions League]], five Premier League titles, five [[EFL Cup|League Cups]] and two [[FA Cup]]s with the club. In [[2017–18 Premier League|2017–18]] he had a significant role in Manchester City's record in becoming the only Premier League team to [[Premier League records and statistics#Points|attain 100 points]] in a single season. In [[2019–20 Premier League|2019–20]], De Bruyne equalled the record for most assists in a Premier League season and was awarded [[Premier League Player of the Season|Player of the Season]] (which he won again in [[2021–22 Premier League|2021–22]]). He was again heavily involved in [[2022–23 Manchester City F.C. season|2022–23]] as City sealed their first [[Treble (association football)|continental treble]].

De Bruyne made his full international debut in 2010, and he has since earned over 90 [[Caps (football)|caps]] and scored 26 goals for Belgium. He was a member of the Belgian squads that reached the quarter-finals both at the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]] and at [[UEFA Euro 2016]]. He was named in the [[FIFA World Cup All-Star Team|FIFA World Cup Dream Team]] of the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] as Belgium finished in [[2018 FIFA World Cup#Third place play-off|third place]], as well as appearing in [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

De Bruyne has been named in the [[UEFA Champions League]] Squad of the Season and [[International Federation of Football History & Statistics#IFFHS Men's World Team|IFFHS Men's World Team]] five times each, the [[European Sports Magazines#ESM Team of the Year|ESM Team of the Year]] four times, the [[FIFPRO#FIFA FIFPRO Men's World 11|FIFA FIFPRO World 11]]  and [[UEFA Team of the Year]] three times each, the ''[[France Football]]'' World XI, and the Bundesliga Team of the Year. He has also won the [[Premier League Playmaker of the Season]] three times, the [[PFA Players' Player of the Year]] twice, [[Manchester City F.C.#Player of the Year|Manchester City's Player of the Year]] four times, the [[UEFA Champions League Best Midfielder|UEFA Champions League Midfielder of the Season]], the Bundesliga Player of the Year, the [[Footballer of the Year (Germany)]], the [[Belgian Sportsman of the year|Belgian Sportsman of the Year]] and the [[International Federation of Football History & Statistics#The World's Best Playmaker|IFFHS World's Best Playmaker]] twice.

===Chelsea===
[[File:Kevin De Bruyne Chelsea vs AS-Roma 10AUG2013.jpg|thumb|upright|De Bruyne warming up with Chelsea in 2013]]

On 31 January 2012, on the winter transfer deadline day, [[Premier League]] club Chelsea and Genk announced the permanent signing of De Bruyne, with the fee rumoured to be in the region of £7&nbsp;million.<ref>{{cite news| title = Genk confirm De Bruyne sale| url =http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/11095/7467271/Genk-confirm-De-Bruyne-sale | publisher = Sky Sport| date = 31 January 2012| access-date = 31 January 2012 | first = Graeme | last = Bailey}}</ref> He signed a five-and-a-half-year contract at [[Stamford Bridge (stadium)|Stamford Bridge]], but would stay at Genk for the remainder of the 2011–12 season. De Bruyne told the club website, "To come to a team like Chelsea is a dream but now I have to work hard to achieve the level that's necessary."<ref>{{cite news|title=Kevin becomes a blue |url=http://www.chelseafc.com/news-article/article/2594667 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130425091338/http://www.chelseafc.com/news-article/article/2594667 |url-status=dead |archive-date=25 April 2013 |publisher=Chelsea F.C. |date=31 January 2012 |access-date=7 July 2014 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news| title = Chelsea sign Kevin De Bruyne from Genk| url = http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/16721393.stm | work = BBC Sport| date = 31 January 2012| access-date = 31 January 2012}}</ref> On 18 July 2012, De Bruyne made his debut for Chelsea in a friendly match against [[Major League Soccer]] (MLS) side [[Seattle Sounders FC]] in a 4–2 win. De Bruyne also played the first half against [[Ligue 1]] giants [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] at [[Yankee Stadium]], New York City.

====Werder Bremen (loan)====
[[File:Kevin de bruyne werder.JPG|thumb|left|upright|De Bruyne during his spell with Werder Bremen in 2012]]

On 31 July 2012, Chelsea announced that De Bruyne was to join [[SV Werder Bremen|Werder Bremen]] in the [[Bundesliga]] on a season-long loan deal.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.theguardian.com/football/2012/jul/31/chelsea-kevin-de-bruyne-werder-bremen|title=Chelsea's Kevin De Bruyne joins Werder Bremen on season-long loan|date=31 July 2012|work=The Guardian}}</ref> He scored his first goal for Bremen in a 3–2 defeat to [[Hannover 96]] on 15 September, netting from 11 yards out after being played in by [[Eljero Elia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.com/gamecast/346384/gamecast.html |title=Hannover 96 3–2 Werder Bremen |publisher=ESPN FC |date=15 September 2012| access-date = 7 July 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/live/match/264956/report|title=Huszti is hero and villain|publisher=Sky Sports|date=15 September 2012|access-date=7 July 2014}}</ref> He continued his good form, scoring in Bremen's next game, a 2–2 draw with [[VfB Stuttgart]], on 23 September.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.com/gamecast/346376/gamecast.html |title=Werder Bremen 2–2 VfB Stuttgart |publisher=ESPN FC |date=23 September 2012 | access-date = 7 July 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/live/match/267321/report|title=Honours even at Weser Stadion|publisher=Sky Sports|date=23 September 2012|access-date=7 July 2014}}</ref> De Bruyne got back on the score sheet on 18 November, scoring the winning goal — despite his team being down to 10 men — as Bremen came from a goal down to defeat [[Fortuna Düsseldorf]] 2–1.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.com/gamecast/346299/gamecast.html |title= Werder Bremen 2–1 Fortuna Düsseldorf |publisher=ESPN FC |date=18 November 2012 | access-date = 7 July 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/live/match/279413/report|title=Winning start for Schaaf|publisher=Sky Sports|date=19 November 2012|access-date=7 July 2014}}</ref>

De Bruyne scored his first goal in over two months on 4 May 2013, since netting a consolation goal in [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]]'s 6–1 hammering of Bremen, putting his side up 2–0 at home to [[TSG 1899 Hoffenheim]] before a late brace from [[Sven Schipplock]] meant that the game finished 2–2.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.com/gamecast/346123/gamecast.html |title=Werder Bremen 2–2 TSG Hoffenheim |publisher=ESPNFC |date=4 May 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/live/match/265457/report|title=Hoffenheim fight back to draw|publisher=Sky Sports|date=4 May 2013|access-date=7 July 2014}}</ref> He followed this up with a goal in Bremen's next match, securing a place in the Bundesliga for the next season with a 1–1 draw against [[Eintracht Frankfurt]] on 11 May.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.com/gamecast/346110/gamecast.htm |title=Werder Bremen 1–1 Eintracht Frankfurt |publisher=ESPNFC |date=11 May 2013 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/live/match/265466/report|title=Eagles secure a point|publisher=Sky Sports|date=11 May 2013|access-date=7 July 2014}}</ref>

====Return to Chelsea====
After a successful loan spell in the Bundesliga with Werder Bremen, De Bruyne was linked with a move to stay in Germany with either [[Borussia Dortmund]] or [[Bayer 04 Leverkusen|Bayer Leverkusen]]. Incoming manager [[José Mourinho]], however, assured De Bruyne he was a part of Chelsea's plan for the future, and the player officially returned to Chelsea on 1 July 2013.<ref>{{cite web|title=De Bruyne to fight for Chelsea place| url=http://espnfc.com/news/story/_/id/1481782/de-bruyne-fight-chelsea-place?cc=5901|publisher=ESPNFC|date=21 June 2013}}</ref>

De Bruyne injured a knee while scoring his first goal for Chelsea, in a pre-season friendly game against a Malaysia XI,<ref>{{cite news |title=Chelsea: Kevin De Bruyne set for scan on knee injury |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/23400115 |first=Richard |last=Conway |website=BBC Sport |date=22 July 2013 |access-date=3 September 2014}}</ref> but was fit to make his competitive debut on the opening day of the [[2013–14 Premier League]] season against [[Hull City A.F.C.|Hull City]], and made an assist for the first goal in a 2–0 win.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2013/aug/18/chelsea-hull-city-premier-league |title=José Mourinho enjoys stylish return as Chelsea cruise against Hull |first=Daniel |last=Taylor |newspaper=The Guardian |date=18 August 2013 |access-date=3 September 2014}}</ref>

====2014–15 season====
[[File:Emirates Cup - Arsenal v Wolfsburg (20011199918).jpg|thumbnail|De Bruyne playing for Wolfsburg against [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] in July 2015]]

De Bruyne scored his first goal of the [[2014–15 VfL Wolfsburg season|2014–15 season]] on 2 October 2014, volleying in a clearance from outside the box to salvage a 1–1 draw against [[LOSC Lille Métropole|Lille]] in the [[2014-15 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2015/matches/round=2000587/match=2014469/postmatch/report/index.html |title=Wolfsburg rally to peg back LOSC |publisher=UEFA |date=2 October 2014 |access-date=3 October 2014 }}</ref> In the third group match away to [[FC Krasnodar|Krasnodar]] on 23 October, De Bruyne scored twice as Wolfsburg secured their first win in the competition with a 4–2 victory.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bundesliga.com/en/el/news/2014/0000305024.php |title=De Bruyne Double Fires Wolfsburg to First European Win |work=Bundesliga |date=23 October 2014 |access-date=23 October 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141101165628/http://www.bundesliga.com/en/el/news/2014/0000305024.php |archive-date=1 November 2014}}</ref> On 30 January 2015, he scored another brace in a 4–1 home win against Bayern Munich, their first Bundesliga defeat since April 2014.<ref name="VfL Wolfsburg 4 1 Bayern Mun">{{cite news|last1=Begley|first1=Emlyn|title= VfL Wolfsburg 4–1 Bayern Munich|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/30991047|access-date=17 March 2015|work=BBC Sport|date=30 January 2015}}</ref> On 1 March 2015, De Bruyne assisted three goals in a 5–3 win over his former club Werder Bremen.<ref>{{cite web |title=Werder Bremen 3–5 VfL Wolfsburg |url=http://www.espn.co.uk/football/sport/match/540771.html |publisher=ESPN (UK) |date=1 March 2015 |access-date=17 March 2015}}</ref>

On 12 March 2015, De Bruyne scored two goals in a 3–1 first leg Europa League round of 16 victory over [[Inter Milan|Internazionale]].<ref>{{cite web|url = http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2015/matches/round=2000589/match=2014610/postmatch/report/index.html#de+bruyne+unpicks+inter|title = De Bruyne-powered Wolfsburg snap back at Inter |publisher=UEFA |date = 12 March 2015|access-date = 15 March 2015}}</ref> On 15 March 2015, he scored one goal and assisted another two in 3–0 victory over [[SC Freiburg]].<ref>{{cite news|url = https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/31875612|title = VfL Wolfsburg 3–0 Sport-Club Freiburg|date = 15 March 2015|access-date = 17 March 2015|work = BBC Sport}}</ref>

De Bruyne ended the league season with 10 goals and 21 assists, the latter a new [[Bundesliga records#Assists|Bundesliga record]], as Wolfsburg finished second in the [[2014–15 Bundesliga|Bundesliga]] and qualified for the [[2015–16 UEFA Champions League]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.rtbf.be/sport/football/etranger/allemagne/detail_de-bruyne-entre-dans-l-histoire-de-la-bundesliga?id=8982166|title=De Bruyne entre dans l'histoire de la Bundesliga|language=fr|date=16 May 2015|work=RTBF Sport}}</ref>{{Cref2|N1}} On 30 May 2015, he started and scored in the [[2015 DFB-Pokal Final]] as ''Die Wölfe'' defeated [[Borussia Dortmund]] 3–1 at the [[Olympiastadion (Berlin)|Olympiastadion]] in Berlin.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/32938029|title=Borussia Dortmd 1–3 VfL Wolfsburg|date=30 May 2015|work=BBC Sport}}</ref>

De Bruyne ended his breakout season with 16 goals and 27 assists in all competitions,<ref>{{cite web|title='Kevin De Bruyne is off to Man City' says Wolfsburg director Klaus Allofs|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/story/2585622/kevin-de-bruyne-is-off-to-manchester-city-says-wolfsburg|publisher=[[ESPN]]|access-date=28 August 2015|date=28 August 2015}}</ref> and was named the 2015 [[Footballer of the Year (Germany)|Germany Footballer of the Year]].<ref name="Sporza">{{cite web|title=De Bruyne is Speler van het Jaar volgens Duitse Journalisten|url=http://sporza.be/cm/sporza/voetbal/buitenland/Duitsland/1.2400701|date=26 July 2015|work=[[Sporza]]|language=nl|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160716195857/http://sporza.be/cm/sporza/voetbal/buitenland/Duitsland/1.2400701|archive-date=16 July 2016|access-date=26 July 2015}}</ref>

====2015–16 season====
De Bruyne began the season by winning the [[2015 DFL-Supercup]] against [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]], providing the cross for [[Nicklas Bendtner]]'s 89th-minute equaliser for a 1–1 draw and then scoring in the subsequent [[penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shootout]].<ref name=supercup>{{cite news|last1=Dunbar|first1=Ross|title=Bendtner leads Wolfsburg over Bayern Munich on penalties in German Super Cup clash|url=http://www.foxsports.com/soccer/story/bendtner-leads-wolfsburg-over-bayern-munich-on-penalties-in-german-super-cup-clash-080115?vid=null|access-date=3 August 2015|work=Fox Sports|date=1 August 2015}}</ref> On 8 August 2015, he continued his good form by scoring his first goal of the season, and providing two assists in a 4–1 win at [[Stuttgarter Kickers]] in the first round of the [[2015–16 DFB-Pokal|DFB-Pokal]].<ref>{{cite web|title=De Bruynes artwork rewarded wide awake Wolves|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/dfbpokal/spielrunde/dfb-pokal/2015-16/1/2841065/spielbericht_stuttgarter-kickers-26_vfl-wolfsburg-24.html|publisher=Kicker|access-date=10 August 2015|date=8 August 2015}}</ref>

In August, in the midst of transfer speculation, De Bruyne said that he would not force Wolfsburg to sell him, but also said that he could not ignore interest from [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]], saying: "If an offer does come, I will hear about it and how much it is, but I have not yet heard anything... I would not go to England just to prove that I can play there. I do not have to go to England... If I go there it's because for me and for my family it is a good choice. That's the key for me."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/2892/transfer-zone/2015/08/03/14113752/de-bruyne-i-will-not-force-wolfsburg-to-sell-me |title=Manchester City transfer news: Kevin De Bruyne: I will not force Wolfsburg to sell me |publisher=Goal.com |date=3 August 2015 |access-date=10 August 2015}}</ref>

On 10 August, it was reported that Manchester City had made a second bid for De Bruyne worth £47&nbsp;million. Wolfsburg sporting director, [[Klaus Allofs]], said the club would fight to keep him, saying "I think some other clubs have definitely turned Kevin's head... Some huge figures are doing the rounds and I can understand why Kevin is leaving everything open."<ref>{{cite news|last=Jackson |first=Jamie |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2015/aug/10/kevin-de-bruyne-manchester-city-wolfsburg-47m |title=Manchester City make second bid of £47m for Wolfsburg's Kevin De Bruyne |work=The Guardian |date=10 August 2015 |access-date=10 August 2015}}</ref> On 27 August, it was reported that City had made a further bid for De Bruyne worth £58&nbsp;million. Klaus Allofs said that City had made an "astonishing" wage offer to De Bruyne.<ref>{{cite news|title = Kevin de Bruyne: Man City offer 'astonishing' wages |url = https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/34081920 |website=BBC Sport |date=27 August 2015 |access-date=27 August 2015}}</ref>

The injury was expected to keep him out for 5–6 weeks<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mancity.com/news/first-team/first-team-news/2018/november/kevin-de-bruyne-injury-news-man-city-latest-update|title=Kevin De Bruyne suffers ligament damage|website=mancity.com|access-date=20 November 2018}}</ref> but he resumed full training much earlier than expected after only 3 weeks out.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2018/nov/12/kevin-de-bruyne-manchester-city-back-early-injury|title=Kevin De Bruyne set to be back for Manchester City earlier than expected|last=Jackson|first=Jamie|date=12 November 2018|work=The Guardian|access-date=20 November 2018}}</ref> He came on as substitute in the [[2019 FA Cup Final|FA Cup final]] against [[Watford F.C.|Watford]], scoring the third goal and assisting two more, as City won the first-ever domestic treble in English men's football.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2019/may/18/manchester-city-win-treble-watford-fa-cup-final-match-report|title=Manchester City win FA Cup to seal treble with 6–0 demolition of Watford|last=Wembley|first=Daniel Taylor at|date=18 May 2019|work=The Observer|access-date=18 May 2019|issn=0029-7712}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/48236232|title=Man City 6–0 Watford: Raheem Sterling scores FA Cup final hat-trick|date=18 May 2019|access-date=18 May 2019}}</ref> His performances meant he was named ''Man of the Match''.<ref name=":0" />

====2019–20 season====
On 30 November 2019, De Bruyne scored a half-volley in a 2–2 draw against Newcastle in the league, that strike was later voted as [[Premier League Goal of the Month]] for November.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Shelvey stunner holds Man City|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/50526605|access-date=5 March 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Edwards|first=John|title=De Bruyne strike scoops Goal of the Month|url=https://www.mancity.com/news/first-team/first-team-news/2019/december/kevin-de-bruyne-wins-premier-league-goal-of-the-month|access-date=5 March 2021|website=mancity.com}}</ref> On 15 December 2019, he scored a brace in a 3–0 [[2019–20 Premier League|Premier League]] win over Arsenal at the Emirates.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/50702875|title=Manchester City pushed Arsenal deeper into crisis as they won with embarrassing ease at Emirates Stadium.|website=BBC|date=15 December 2019|access-date=26 February 2020}}</ref>

De Bruyne scored his 50th City goal in all competitions on 26 February 2020 in their 2–1 victory, coming from behind, over [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]] in their Champions League [[2019–20 UEFA Champions League#Round of 16|Round of 16]] clash.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sport.bt.com/news/manchester-city-fight-back-to-beat-real-madrid-at-bernabeu-S11364433721545|title=Manchester City fight back to beat Real Madrid at Bernabeu|website=BT Sport|date=26 February 2020|access-date=26 February 2020}}</ref>

==Player profile==
===Style of play===
A versatile and highly talented player in the media, he plays mainly as a [[Central midfielder|central]] or an [[attacking midfielder]] but can also operate in several other positions and has been used as a [[Left winger|winger]] or [[second striker]]. He has even been deployed in a [[Deep-lying playmaker|deeper creative midfield position]] and on occasion, a [[Box-to-box|box-to-box role]] or as a [[false 9]]. He is often described by pundits as one of the best and most complete modern day [[Playmaker|advanced playmakers]] due to his technique, skill, athleticism, work-rate off the ball, vision, [[Cross (football)|crossing]] accuracy, wide range of passing, and powerful long-range shooting ability with either foot.<ref>{{cite
