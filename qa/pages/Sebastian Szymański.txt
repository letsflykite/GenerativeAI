'''Sebastian Szymański''' (born 10 May 1999) is a Polish professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as an [[attacking midfielder]] for [[FC Dynamo Moscow|Dynamo Moscow]], and the [[Poland national football team|Poland national team]].

==Club career==
[[File:Sebastian Szymański 2021.jpg|upright=0.7|left|thumb|277x277px|Szymański playing for [[FC Dynamo Moscow|Dynamo Moscow]] in 2021]]
On 31 May 2019, Szymański signed a five-year contract with the [[Russian Premier League]] club [[FC Dynamo Moscow|Dynamo Moscow]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[FC Dynamo Moscow]]|url=http://fcdynamo.ru/news/events/?id_4=14271|title=Себастьян Шимански стал игроком "Динамо"|trans-title=Sebastian Szymański became a Dynamo player|date=31 May 2019|language=Russian}}</ref> He was voted by Dynamo fans as "player of the month" for October 2019.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[FC Dynamo Moscow]]|url=http://fcdynamo.ru/news/events/?id_4=14805|title=Себастиан Шиманьски - лучший игрок октября|trans-title=Sebastian Szymański is the best player of October|date=31 October 2019|language=Russian}}</ref> On 9 November 2019, he scored his first goal for Dynamo, the only goal of the game in a 1–0 victory over [[FC Rubin Kazan]]. It was the first Dynamo away victory against Rubin in 13 years.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[Russian Premier League]]|url=https://eng.premierliga.ru/match/match_13242.html|title=Rubin v Dynamo game report|date=9 November 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|publisher=Sports.ru|url=https://www.sports.ru/football/1080009965.html|title="Рубин" опустился в зону стыковых матчей, проиграв "Динамо" дома впервые за 13 лет|trans-title=Rubin drops into the relegation playoffs spot after losing to Dynamo at home for the first time in 13 years|date=9 November 2019|language=Russian}}</ref>

On 2 June 2021, he signed a new contract with Dynamo for a five-year term.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[FC Dynamo Moscow]]|url=https://fcdynamo.ru/news/dinamo-prodlilo-kontrakt-s-sebastianom-simanski|title="Динамо" продлило контракт с Себастианом Шиманьски|date=2 June 2021|language=ru}}</ref> He was voted player of the month by Dynamo fans for July 2021,<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[FC Dynamo Moscow]]|url=https://fcdynamo.ru/news/sebastian-simanski-vtb-igrok-mesyaca-v-iyule|title=Себастиан Шиманьски — ВТБ Игрок месяца в июле|date=8 August 2021|language=ru}}</ref> August 2021<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[FC Dynamo Moscow]]|url=https://fcdynamo.ru/news/sebastian-simanski-vtb-igrok-mesyaca-v-avguste|title=Себастиан Шиманьски — ВТБ Игрок месяца в августе|date=3 September 2021|language=ru}}</ref> and November 2021.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[FC Dynamo Moscow]]|url=https://fcdm.ru/news/sebastian-simanski-vtb-igrok-mesyaca-v-noyabre|title=Себастиан Шиманьски — ВТБ Игрок месяца в ноябре|date=3 December 2021|language=ru}}</ref>

On 22 July 2022, Szymański joined Dutch club [[Feyenoord]] on a one-year loan with an option to buy for the club.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Feyenoord heeft beet: Szymanski tekent in De Kuip |url=https://www.vi.nl/nieuws/feyenoord-heeft-beet-szymanski-tekent-in-de-kuip |access-date=22 July 2022 |website=www.vi.nl}}</ref> He scored his first goal for the club on 27 August 2022, scoring the fourth goal in the club's 4–0 win over [[FC Emmen]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.vi.nl/nieuws/feyenoord-in-debuutgoalgala-langs-fc-emmen |title=Feyenoord in debuutgoalgala langs FC Emmen |language=nl |date=27 August 2022 |access-date=27 August 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
In May 2018 he was named in Poland's preliminary 35-man [[Poland national football team|squad]] for the [[2018 World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/revealed-every-world-cup-2018-squad-23-man-preliminary-lists/oa0atsduflsv1nsf6oqk576rb|title=Revealed: Every World Cup 2018 squad - Final 23-man lists|website=Goal.com|access-date=14 December 2021}}</ref> However, he did not make the final 23.<ref>{{Cite web|publisher=[[BBC News]]|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44356860|title=World Cup 2018: Kamil Glik picked in Poland squad, then gets injured|date=4 June 2021|access-date=15 December 2021}}</ref>

He made his [[Poland national football team|Poland national team]] debut on 9 September 2019 in a [[UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying Group G|Euro 2020 qualifier]] against Austria. He replaced [[Kamil Grosicki]] in the 70th minute.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[UEFA]]|url=https://www.uefa.com/european-qualifiers/season=2020/matches/round=2001086/match=2026204/index.html|title=Poland v Austria game report|date=9 September 2019}}</ref> He made his first start in the next qualifier on 10 October 2019 against Latvia and assisted on one of the goals as Poland won 3–0 and secured their spot at the final tournament. He scored his first goal for his country on 19 November 2019 in the last qualifying group game against Slovenia.{{citation needed|date=March 2022}}
