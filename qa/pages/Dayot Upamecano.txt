'''Dayotchanculle Oswald Upamecano''' (born 27 October 1998) is a French professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] for [[Bundesliga]] club [[Bayern Munich]] and the [[France national football team|France national team]].

==Early life and career==
Dayotchanculle Oswald Upamecano<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fdp.fifa.org/assetspublic/ce44/pdf/SquadLists-English.pdf |title=Squad List: FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022: France (FRA) |publisher=FIFA |page=12 |date=15 November 2022 |access-date=15 November 2022}}</ref> was born on 27 October 1998<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.espn.co.uk/football/player/_/id/222793/dayot-upamecano |title=Dayot Upamecano: Overview |publisher=ESPN |access-date=6 September 2021}}</ref> in [[Évreux]], Normandy<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/FootballFicheJoueur56597.html |title=Dayot Upamecano |newspaper=L'Équipe |location=Paris |access-date=15 November 2022 |language=fr}}</ref> and is of [[Bissau-Guinean]] descent.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.espn.co.uk/football/africa-cup-of-nations/story/4176956/africas-if-only-kids-starlets-who-could-have-played-at-afcon-instead-of-the-nations-league |title=Africa's If Only kids Starlets who could have played at Afcon instead of the Nations League |first=Colin |last=Udoh |publisher=ESPN |date=9 September 2020 |access-date=15 November 2022}}</ref> He was raised in [[La Madeleine-de-Nonancourt|La Madeleine]], a neighbourhood with many African immigrants.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.leprogres.fr/sports/2017/11/21/dayot-upamecano-l-autre-francais-de-leipzig|title=Dayot Upamecano, l'autre Français de Leipzig|website=leprogres.fr|access-date=6 May 2018}}</ref><ref name="sofoot1" /> He was named after his great-grandfather, who was king of the village on the island of [[Jeta (Guinea-Bissau)|Jeta]] in Guinea-Bissau, which his parents are from.<ref name="ff1" /> He is the first son after four daughters, and has one younger brother.<ref name="sofoot1">{{cite news |last1=Jucha |first1=Nicolas |title=Dayot Upamecano: J'ai toujours eu un corps d'adulte |url=https://www.sofoot.com/dayot-upamecano-j-ai-toujours-eu-un-corps-d-adulte-465944.html |work=SOFOOT.com |date=2 March 2019 |language=fr}}</ref> As a 15-year-old, he followed [[Speech–language pathology|speech therapy sessions]] to overcome [[dyslexia]] and [[stuttering]] disorders, which caused shyness and certain difficulties in expressing himself.<ref name="ff1">{{cite news |last1=Crépin |first1=Timothé |title=Dayot Upamecano (RB Leipzig), la douce puissance |url=https://www.francefootball.fr/news/Dayot-upamecano-rb-leipzig-la-douce-puissance/1134813 |work=France Football |date=15 May 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220422130457/https://www.francefootball.fr/news/Dayot-upamecano-rb-leipzig-la-douce-puissance/1134813#federation=archive.wikiwix.com |archive-date=22 April 2022 |language=fr |access-date=2 April 2022 |url-status= live }}</ref>

Upamecano began playing football with local club [[Évreux FC 27|Évreux]], alongside the likes of [[Ousmane Dembélé]], [[Rafik Guitane]] and [[Samuel Grandsir]].<ref name="sofoot1" /> He covered a variety of positions in his youth, before being fixed on the [[centre-back]] position.<ref name="sofoot1" /> He joined the youth academy of [[Valenciennes FC|Valenciennes]] in 2013.<ref name="auto"/> Described during the time as a youth in an adult body, Upamecano shaped his play after [[Sergio Ramos]], and developed into a physically imposing defender.<ref name="sofoot1" />

==Club career==
===Red Bull Salzburg===
[[File:ÖFB-Cup Halbfinale FC RB Salzburg gegen FK Austria Wien 04.JPG|thumb|upright|Upamecano with [[FC Red Bull Salzburg|Red Bull Salzburg]] in 2016]]

After attracting the attention of a number of major European clubs, including [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]], Upamecano joined [[FC Red Bull Salzburg|Red Bull Salzburg]] in July 2015 for a reported fee of €2.2 million.<ref>{{cite news |title=Manchester United target Dayot Upamecano signs for FC Red Bull Salzburg|url= http://www.ibtimes.co.uk/manchester-united-target-dayot-upamecano-signs-fc-red-bull-salzburg-1510417 |work=International Business Times |date=11 July 2015|access-date=23 September 2015}}</ref> He was an unused substitute in Red Bull's [[UEFA Champions League]] game against [[Malmö FF]] on 29 July 2015.<ref>{{cite news |title=Red Bull Salzburg 2–0 Malmö FF |url=http://www.eurosport.co.uk/football/champions-league/2015-2016/red-bull-salzburg-malmo-ff_mtc784130/live.shtml |publisher=Eurosport |access-date=23 September 2015}}</ref> He made his professional debut for Red Bull's farm team, [[FC Liefering]], two days later, in a 2–1 league win against [[SKN St. Pölten|St. Pölten]].<ref>{{cite news |title=FC Liefering gewinnt zu neunt gegen St. Pölten|url=http://www.redbullsalzburg.at/de/fc-liefering/news/saison_2015_16/fc-liefering-gewinnt-zu-neunt-gegen-st.poelten.html |language=de |publisher=FC Red Bull Salzburg |date=31 July 2015|access-date=23 September 2015}}</ref> On 19 March 2016, he made his league debut with Red Bull Salzburg in a 2–1 win over [[SV Mattersburg]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/03/19/austria/bundesliga/fc-red-bull-salzburg/sportverein-mattersburg/2047016/ |title=Salzburg 2–1 Mattersburg |website=Soccerway |date=19 March 2016 }}</ref>

In the first half of the 2016–17 season, Upamecano earned a regular place with the first team in both league and [[2016–17 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]] matches.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://de.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/news/0252-0cda47be1036-5984148c4806-1000--europas-wunderkinder-dayot-upamecano/ |title=Europas Wunderkinder: Dayot Upamecano |publisher=UEFA |language=German |date=13 January 2017 }}</ref>

===RB Leipzig===
On 13 January 2017, Upamecano joined [[RB Leipzig]] on a {{frac|4|1|2}}-year deal, for a reported €10 million fee.<ref>{{cite web|title=RB Leipzig sign Dayot Upamecano from Red Bull Salzburg|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/11/transfer-zone/2017/01/13/31498772/rb-leipzig-win-race-for-barcelona-target-upamecano|website=Goal.com |date=13 January 2017}}</ref> On 4 February 2017, he made his [[Bundesliga]] debut under coach [[Ralph Hasenhüttl]] in a 1–0 defeat against [[Borussia Dortmund]].<ref name="auto">{{cite web |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/dayot-upamecano-rb-leipzig-france-wonderkid-centre-back-510015.jsp |title=Dayot Upamecano: The RB Leipzig defender with the world at his assured feet |publisher=Bundesliga |date=2018 }}</ref> On 13 September 2017, he made his [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] debut in a 1–1 draw against [[AS Monaco FC|Monaco]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/match/2021599--leipzig-vs-monaco/?awc=15387_1613275483_e924269dd6f8053cbe06599dc2ee3374 |title=Leipzig 1–1 Monaco |publisher=UEFA |date=2018 }}</ref> On 9 February 2018, he scored his first goal in a 2–0 win over [[FC Augsburg|Augsburg]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://sport.sky.de/fussball/rb-leipzig-vs-augsburg/spielbericht/377707 |title=Verdienter Erfolg von RB gegen Augsburg |website=Sky Sport |language=German |date=10 February 2018 }}</ref> On 2 July 2018, he was shortlisted for the 2018 [[Golden Boy (award)|Golden Boy]] award.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/golden-boy-2018-nominees-pulisic-mckennie-havertz-lovren-isak-509451.jsp |title=Pulisic, Upamecano and more: Meet the Bundesliga's 5 Golden Boy nominations for 2018 |publisher=Bundesliga |date=2018 }}</ref>

On 31 July 2020, Upamecano signed a new deal until 2023.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://talksport.com/football/740111/arsenal-dealt-major-transfer-upamecano-new-rb-leipzig-contract/|title=Arsenal dealt major blow as transfer target signs new RB Leipzig contract|website=Talksport |date=31 July 2020}}</ref> In the 2019–20 season, he reached with RB Leipzig the [[2019–20 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] semi-finals under coach [[Julian Nagelsmann]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/rb-leipzig-psg-champions-league-live-line-ups-stats-neymar-mbappe-upamecano-12433 |title=RB Leipzig 0–3 Paris Saint-Germain UEFA Champions League semi-final: as it happened! |publisher=Bundesliga |date=2020 }}</ref>

===Bayern Munich===
On 14 February 2021, [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] announced an agreement for the signing of Upamecano on a five-year deal, to be effective as of 1 July 2021.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2021/02/fc-bayern-sign-dayot-upamecano |title=FC Bayern sign Dayot Upamecano |publisher=FC Bayern Munich |date=14 February 2021 | access-date=14 February 2021}}</ref> The club reportedly met his release clause from Leipzig, set at €42.5 million.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/upamecanos-425m-release-clause-confirmed-with-bayern-target/l4pgfp25byj11p0kcrk9plqip |title=Upamecano's €42.5m release clause confirmed with Bayern target to choose between Bundesliga and Premier League |website=Goal.com |date=31 January 2021 | access-date=14 February 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/soccer-transfers/story/4314935/bayern-munich-confirm-dayot-upamecano-signing |title= Bayern Munich confirm Dayot Upamecano signing |publisher=ESPN |date=14 February 2021 | access-date=14 February 2021}}</ref> Upamecano's first game as a Bayern player came in a [[pre-season friendly]] on 16 July 2021 against fellow Bundesliga side [[1. FC Köln]] that saw Bayern lose 3–2 after fielding a side largely consisting of reserve and youth players.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Bayern boss Nagelsmann won't panic over transfer activity |url=https://www.goal.com/en-sg/news/bayern-boss-nagelsmann-wont-panic-over-transfer-activity/1sbqitt98p46x1gvnca522l53k|access-date=23 July 2021|website=Goal.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=18 July 2021|title=Dayot Upamecano had "good feelings" during his Bayern Munich debut in 3–2 friendly loss vs. FC Koln|url=https://www.bavarianfootballworks.com/2021/7/18/22581798/bayern-munich-fc-koln-friendly-dayot-upamecano-performance-pleased-julian-nagelsmann-omar-richards|access-date=23 July 2021|website=Bavarian Football Works}}</ref>

He made his competitive debut on the opening day of the [[2021–22 Bundesliga|2021–22 Bundesliga season]], starting at centre-back alongside [[Niklas Süle]] in a 1–1 away draw against [[Borussia Mönchengladbach]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Lewandowski sets opening-day record |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/58206961 |work=BBC Sport |date=13 August 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210813205117/https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/58206961 |archive-date=13 August 2021}}</ref> He scored his first goal for the club in a 4–0 win over [[VfL Wolfsburg]] on 17 December, heading in a cross from [[Thomas Müller]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Lewandowski breaks Muller goals record |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/59702618 |work=BBC Sport |date=17 December 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211219205240/https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/59702618 |archive-date=19 December 2021}}</ref>

==International career==
Upamecano played for the [[France national under-16 football team|France under-16]] team that finished third in the 2014 Aegean Cup.<ref name=Sky>{{cite news |title=Dayot Upamecano: Who is the Man Utd, Man City and Arsenal target?|url= http://www.skysports.com/football/news/11096/9873037/dayot-upamecano-who-is-the-man-utd-man-city-and-arsenal-target |publisher=Sky Sports|date=2 June 2015|access-date=23 September 2015}}</ref> He played in three of his country's four games and was named the competition's best defender.<ref name=FFF>{{cite news|title=Dayot Upamecano|url=https://www.fff.fr/equipes-de-france/tous-les-joueurs/fiche-joueur/2543561904-dayot-upamecano|language=fr|publisher=French Football Federation|access-date=23 September 2015|archive-date=12 June 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180612140152/https://www.fff.fr/equipes-de-france/tous-les-joueurs/fiche-joueur/2543561904-dayot-upamecano|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=USA win 2014 Mercedes-Benz Aegean Cup|url= http://www.tff.org/default.aspx?pageID=471&ftxtID=19837 |publisher=Turkish Football Federation |date=26 January 2014|access-date=23 September 2015}}</ref> He subsequently moved up to the [[France national under-17 football team|France under-17]] squad, making his debut in a [[2015 UEFA European Under-17 Championship qualification|European Championship qualifier]] against Cyprus on 27 October 2014.<ref name=FFF/> France won the [[2015 UEFA European Under-17 Championship]], with Upamecano playing in all six of his country's matches and being named in the team of the tournament.<ref>{{cite news |title=Dayot Upamecano|url= http://www.uefa.com/under17/season=2015/teams/player=250079545/index.html |publisher=UEFA |access-date=23 September 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Team of the Tournament|url= http://www.uefa.com/under17/season=2015/technical-report/uefa-technical-team/index.html |publisher=UEFA|access-date=23 September 2015}}</ref> He made his debut for [[France national under-18 football team|France under-18]] in a 0–0 [[Exhibition game|friendly]] draw with [[United States men's national under-18 soccer team|United States under-18]] on 4 September 2015.<ref>{{cite news|title=France – Etats-Unis|url=https://www.fff.fr/equipes-de-france/8/u18/matchs/2173/2015-09-04/france-etats-unis|language=fr|publisher=French Football Federation|access-date=23 September 2015|archive-date=23 September 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150923180509/https://www.fff.fr/equipes-de-france/8/u18/matchs/2173/2015-09-04/france-etats-unis|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Upamecano made his debut for the [[France national football team]], on 5 September 2020, starting in a [[UEFA Nations League]] group-stage match against [[Sweden national football team|Sweden]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sweden-France Line-ups |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefanationsleague/match/2029992--sweden-vs-france/lineups/?iv=true|access-date=5 September 2020|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> Three days later on 8 September, he scored his first international goal in a 4–2 win against [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://uk.sports.yahoo.com/news/france-4-2-croatia-les-203955764.html |title=France 4–2 Croatia: Les Bleus win replay of 2018 World Cup final |website=Yahoo! Sports |date=8 September 2020 }}</ref>

Upamecano played a pivotal role in France's [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], starting five of France's seven matches.{{citation needed|date=January 2023}} This includes playing the entire 120 minutes in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup Final|final]] in an eventual 3–3 loss in penalties to [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Argentina-France Line-ups |url=https://www.fifa.com/fifaplus/en/match-centre/match/17/255711/285077/400128145?country=US&wtw-filter=ALL|publisher=FIFA}}</ref>
