'''Zakaria Aboukhlal''' ({{lang-ar|زكريا أبوخلال}}; born 18 February 2000) is a professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as [[forward (association football)|forward]] for [[Ligue 1]] club [[Toulouse FC|Toulouse]]. Born in the Netherlands, he plays for the [[Morocco national football team|Morocco national team]], having previously played for the Netherlands at various youth levels.

==Club career==
Aboukhlal made his professional debut for [[Jong PSV]] in a 2–1 [[Eerste Divisie]] win over [[FC Eindhoven]] on 17 August 2018.<ref>{{Cite web |title=PSV II vs. Eindhoven - 17 August 2018 |url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2018/08/17/netherlands/eerste-divisie/jong-psv/fc-eindhoven/2794379/ |website=Soccerway}}</ref>

In August 2019, after making only two appearances for PSV's senior side, Aboukhlal transferred to [[Eredivisie]] rivals [[AZ Alkmaar]] in a deal worth €2 million.<ref>{{Cite web |date=26 August 2019 |title=Aboukhlal leaves PSV for AZ Alkmaar |url=http://www.football-oranje.com/aboukhlal-leaves-psv-for-az-alkmaar/ |access-date=26 January 2020 |website=Football Oranje}}</ref> In a match for [[Jong AZ]] on 9 December 2019, he scored all four of his team's goals in a win against [[Helmond Sport]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=9 December 2019 |title=AZ II VS. HELMOND SPORT 4 - 1 |url=https://www.soccerway.com/matches/2019/12/09/netherlands/eerste-divisie/jong-az/stichting-helmond-sport/3031852/ |access-date=26 January 2020 |website=Soccerway}}</ref>

On 24 June 2022, Aboukhlal signed with French club [[Toulouse FC|Toulouse]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=24 June 2022 |title=ZAKARIA ABOUKHLAL PREMIÈRE RECRUE DU MERCATO ESTIVAL |url=https://www.toulousefc.com/fr/zakaria-aboukhlal-premiere-recrue-du-mercato-estival |access-date=27 June 2022 |publisher=Toulouse FC |language=fr}}</ref> In his first season at the club, he won the [[Coupe de France]]. He scored a 79th-minute goal in the [[2023 Coupe de France final]] to help his club beat defending champions [[FC Nantes|Nantes]] 5–1.<ref>{{Cite web |date=29 April 2023 |title=Nantes 1-5 Toulouse: Toulouse win first major trophy in French Cup final |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/65439267 |access-date=29 April 2023 |publisher=BBC |language=en}}</ref>

==International career==
Born in the Netherlands, Aboukhlal was born to a Moroccan mother and Libyan father.<ref>{{Cite web |title=PSV Eindhoven youngster Zakaria Aboukhlal wanted by Libya |url=https://africanfootball.com/news/758653/PSV-Eindhoven-youngster-Zakaria-Aboukhlal-wanted-by-Libya |website=African Football}}</ref> He is a former youth international for the Netherlands. He decided not to represent the Netherlands but the [[Morocco national football team|Morocco national team]] because of his growing up among the Moroccan community residing in the Netherlands, he was called up to represent in November 2020.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Portrait. Qui est Zakaria Aboukhlal, le nouveau Lion de l'Atlas? |url=https://sport.le360.ma//football/portrait-qui-est-zakaria-aboukhlal-le-nouveau-lion-de-latlas-80988 |website=Le360 Sport}}</ref> He was called up for the Libyan National Team but refused. Aboukhlal debuted for Morocco in a 4–1 [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations qualification]] win over [[Central African Republic national football team|Central African Republic]] on 13 November 2020, and scored his side's fourth goal in his debut.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Tomegah |first=Rodolph |title=Elim.CAN 2021 : Hakim Ziyech porte le Maroc contre la Centrafrique |date=13 November 2020 |url=https://www.africatopsports.com/2020/11/13/elim-can-2021-hakim-ziyech-porte-le-maroc-contre-la-centrafrique/}}</ref>

On 10 November 2022, he was named in Morocco's 23-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Morocco World Cup 2022 squad: Who's in and who's out? {{!}} Goal.com |url=https://www.goal.com/en-bh/lists/morocco-2022-world-cup-squad-hakimi-ziyech-mazraoui-qatar/blta36257fc4feeb66a |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=www.goal.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=10 November 2022 |title=Moroccan coach unveils list of 26 Atlas Lions in 2022 World Cup |url=https://en.hespress.com/52697-moroccan-coach-unveils-list-of-26-atlas-lions-in-2022-world-cup.html |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=HESPRESS English - Morocco News |language=en-US}}</ref> He scored his first goal in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|World Cup]] against Belgium on 27 November 2022.

== Controversy ==
On 14 May 2023, Aboukhlal refused to represent Toulouse in a Ligue 1 match against Nantes due to the game being a part of a league-wide campaign against [[homophobia]], meaning all teams' kits for the week featured rainbow-themed decorations.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-05-14 |title=Two Ligue 1 players refuse to play in protest at anti-homophobia campaign |url=https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20230514-two-ligue-1-players-refuse-to-play-in-protest-at-anti-homophobia-campaign |access-date=2023-05-15 |website=France 24 |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last= |date=2023-05-14 |title=Toulouse players left out of squad after refusal to offer LGBT support |language=en-GB |work=The Guardian |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2023/may/14/toulouse-players-left-out-of-squad-after-refusal-to-offer-lgbt-support |access-date=2023-05-15 |issn=0261-3077}}</ref>  He was in the same week accused by several news outlets, of having been  been involved in an alleged [[sexism|sexist]] altercation with [[Toulouse]]'s female deputy major [[Laurence Arribagé]], during Toulouse's Coupe de France title festivities at the city hall in April. Aboukhlal, who was alleged to have told Arribagé that women should behave in a subservient manner to men after Arribagé had asked some of the players to tone down their loud behaviour, was temporarily suspended from all first team activities.<ref>{{Cite news |last= |date=2023-05-16 |title=Scandal at Toulouse: In my house, women don't talk to men like that |language=en-GB |work=Marca |url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/ligue-1/2023/05/16/646354d1e2704ed5528b458b.html |access-date=2023-05-16}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Toulouse: Aboukhlal mis à l'écart suite à son altercation avec une élue lors des festivités de la Coupe de France |url=https://rmcsport.bfmtv.com/football/ligue-1/toulouse-aboukhlal-mis-a-l-ecart-suite-a-son-altercation-avec-une-elue-lors-des-festivites-de-la-coupe-de-france_AV-202305150887.html |access-date=2023-05-18 |website=RMC SPORT |language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |title=Aboukhlal training alone after altercation claims |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/65610571 |access-date=2023-05-18}}</ref> Following this accusation, Aboukhlal tweeted: "Following the article published by [[RMC (France)|RMC]] on Monday, May 15th, I had the opportunity to meet and discuss with [[Laurence Arribagé]]. During this meeting, we both confirmed the RMC sport report does not match what has really happened on 30th of April at the Capitole. We would like to put an end to this controversy which has strongly affected both of us as well as our families. Thank you for respecting our decision."<ref>{{Cite web |last=Kasraoui |first=Safaa |title=Zakaria Aboukhlal’s Clarification Exposes RMC Claims |url=https://www.moroccoworldnews.com/2023/05/355507/zakaria-aboukhlals-clarification-exposes-rmc-claims |access-date=2023-05-18 |website=moroccoworldnews |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last= |date=2023-05-18 |title=Morocco's Aboukhlal draws line under dispute with Toulouse city official |language=en |work=Reuters |url=https://www.reuters.com/sports/soccer/moroccos-aboukhlal-draws-line-under-dispute-with-toulouse-city-official-2023-05-18/ |access-date=2023-05-18}}</ref> Aboukhlal was reinstated to the team.
