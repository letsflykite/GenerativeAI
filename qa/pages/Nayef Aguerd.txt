'''Nayef Aguerd''' ({{lang-ar|نايف أكرد}}; {{lang-ber|ⵏⴰⵢⴼ ⴰⴳⵕⴹ}}; born 30 March 1996) is a Moroccan professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Centre-back|centre-back]] for [[Premier League]] club [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]] and the [[Morocco national football team|Morocco national team]]. 

He began his professional career playing for [[Botola]] side [[Fath Union Sport|FUS Rabat]], before leaving to [[France]] and playing for [[Dijon FCO|Dijon]] and [[Stade Rennais F.C.|Rennes]]. He was chosen in Morocco's squads for the [[African Nations Championship]] in [[2018 African Nations Championship|2018]], the  [[Africa Cup of Nations]] in [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations|2021]] and the [[FIFA World Cup]] in [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022]].

== Early life ==
Nayef Aguerd was born and raised in the port city of [[Kenitra]] in north western [[Morocco]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Eight things you need to know about Nayef Aguerd {{!}} West Ham United F.C. |url=https://www.whufc.com/news/eight-things-you-need-know-about-nayef-aguerd |access-date=2023-04-02 |website=www.whufc.com |language=en}}</ref>

Aguerd began his footballing career with the [[Mohammed VI Football Academy]], before moving to [[Fath Union Sport|FUS Rabat]] in 2014.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Made in Morocco, Flourishing in France; Is London Calling For Nayef Aguerd? |url=https://breakingthelines.com/player-analysis/made-in-morocco-flourishing-in-france-is-london-calling-for-nayef-aguerd/ |access-date=18 June 2022}}</ref> The player then played a season with the club amateurs before signing his first professional contract with FUS Rabat in [[Botola]] in 2014.

==Club career==

=== Early career ===
During his first season with FUS Rabat, he was the runner-up of the 2015 Throne cup after losing the final against [[Olympique Club de Khouribga]]. On 15 February 2015, he scored his first goal for the team in a 3–1 victory against [[Wydad AC]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Goalzz.com: live sports scores and news |url=https://www.goalzz.com/?player=111541 |access-date=6 March 2022 |website=www.goalzz.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Nayef Aguerd: Who is the Morocco international signed by West Ham United? {{!}} Goal.com English Bahrain |url=https://www.goal.com/en-bh/news/nayef-aguerd-who-is-the-morocco-international-signed-by-west-ham-united/bltaca3bd40a7cbde7c |access-date=2023-04-02 |website=www.goal.com |language=en-BH}}</ref> On 3 March, he scored his first continental goal in a 1–1 draw against [[UMS de Loum]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=UMS de Loum 1-1 FUS Rabat :: Taça da Confederação CAF 2016 :: Détails du jeu :: leballonrond.fr |url=https://www.leballonrond.fr/jogo.php?id=4913670 |access-date=6 March 2022 |website=www.leballonrond.fr |language=fr}}</ref> 

=== Dijon ===
After four seasons, he joined [[Dijon FCO|Dijon]] in [[Ligue 1]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dfco.fr/articles/82FE01A8-8394-4903-B88B-2530B3F0D51F|title=Nayef Aguerd première recrue du DFCO !|website=www.dfco.fr}}</ref> He made his professional debut with Dijon in a 4–0 win over [[OGC Nice|Nice]] on 25 August 2018, scoring the first goal for his side on his debut.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lfp.fr/ligue1/feuille_match/84637|title=LFP.fr – Ligue de Football Professionnel – Ligue 1 Conforama – Saison 2018/2019 – 3ème journée – OGC Nice / Dijon FCO|website=www.lfp.fr}}</ref>

=== Rennes ===
On 14 August 2020, Aguerd signed a contract with Ligue 1 team Rennes from Dijon for an undisclosed fee believed to be between €4m and €5m.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Official {{!}} Rennes sign central defender Nayef Aguerd {{!}} Get French Football News|url=https://www.getfootballnewsfrance.com/2020/official-rennes-sign-central-defender-nayef-aguerd/|access-date=1 January 2022|website=www.getfootballnewsfrance.com|date=14 August 2020 }}</ref> He scored his first goal for the club in a [[Friendlies|friendly]] against Nice; the game ended in a 3–2 loss.<ref>{{Cite web |title=A win to end preseason |url=https://www.ogcnice.com/fr/article/37014/a-win-to-end-preseason.html |access-date=6 March 2022 |website=OGC Nice |language=fr}}</ref> On 13 September 2020, Aguerd scored his first league goal in a 4–2 victory against [[Nîmes Olympique|Nîmes]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=14 September 2020 |title=Ligue 1: Guirassy Scores Twice as Rennes Beats Nimes 4-2; Marseille Down PSG 1-0 |url=https://www.news18.com/news/sports/guirassy-scores-twice-as-rennes-beats-nimes-4-2-monaco-wins-2874221.html |access-date=6 March 2022 |website=News18 |language=en}}</ref>

On 17 May 2021, He was placed 11th place in the [[Prix Marc-Vivien Foé]] as the best player who represents an African national football team in [[Ligue 1]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=17 May 2021 |title=Prix Marc-Vivien Foé: Gaël Kakuta, premier Lensois et Congolais sacré |url=https://www.rfi.fr/fr/afrique-foot/20210517-prix-marc-vivien-fo%C3%A9-ga%C3%ABl-kakuta-premier-lensois-et-congolais-sacr%C3%A9 |access-date=17 October 2022 |website=RFI |language=fr}}</ref> On 19 August 2021, Aguerd scored a header, which marked his first European goal, in a 2–0 victory against [[Rosenborg BK|Rosenborg]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Rennes v Rosenborg Live Commentary & Result, 19/08/21, UEFA Europa Conference League {{!}} Goal.com |url=https://www.goal.com/en-in/match/rennes-v-rosenborg/7r4glautwcqw9k7rvuaydj8d0 |access-date=6 March 2022 |website=www.goal.com}}</ref> A week later, Aguerd scored again in the second leg against Rosenborg in the [[2021–22 UEFA Europa Conference League qualifying phase and play-off round|2021–22 UEFA Europa Conference League play-off round]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Rosenborg vs. Stade Rennais - Football Match Summary - August 26, 2021 - ESPN |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/match/_/gameId/618183 |access-date=6 March 2022 |website=ESPN.com |language=en}}</ref> On 16 May 2022, Aguerd was placed 3rd as the best African player in [[Ligue 1]] for the [[Prix Marc-Vivien Foé]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=16 May 2022 |title=Prix Marc-Vivien Foé: l'Ivoirien Seko Fofana succède à Gaël Kakuta |url=https://www.rfi.fr/fr/afrique-foot/20220516-prix-marc-vivien-fo%C3%A9-l-ivoirien-seko-fofana-succ%C3%A8de-%C3%A0-ga%C3%ABl-kakuta |access-date=17 October 2022 |website=RFI |language=fr}}</ref>

==International career==
Aguerd made his debut for the [[Morocco national football team|Morocco national team]] in a friendly 0–0 tie with [[Albania national football team|Albania]] on 31 August 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mountakhab.net/www/modules/fiches_joueurs/feuille_match.php?id=1233492|title=Rapport de match: Albanie 0–0 Maroc|first=Achraf|last=Bakkali|website=Mountakhab.net}}</ref> Aguerd represented Morocco in the [[2018 African Nations Championship]], helping his country to achieve their first CHAN title.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/42939957|title=Hosts Morocco crowned CHAN champions|work=BBC Sport }}</ref> On 6 September 2021, Aguerd scored his first goal against [[Sudan national football team|Sudan]] in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – CAF second round|2022 FIFA World Cup qualification]], he sent a sharp shot in the net from a cross.<ref>{{Cite web |title=الشوط الأول من مباراة المنتخب ضد السودان.. استحواذ كلي للأسود ونايف أكرد يسجل هدف التقدم للفريق الوطني |url=https://www.elbotola.com/article/2021-09-02-19-55-361.html |access-date=6 March 2022 |website=www.elbotola.com |language=Ar}}</ref>

He was later invited by [[Vahid Halilhodžić]] to represent Morocco in the [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=23 December 2021|title=Vahid Halilhodzic reveals final list of players for CAN 2022|url=https://en.hespress.com/32767-vahid-halilhodzic-reveals-final-list-of-players-for-can-2022.html|access-date=11 January 2022|website=HESPRESS English - Morocco’s leading digital media|language=en-US}}</ref> Nayef started all of his matches in the group stages.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Africa Cup of Nations (Sky Sports)|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/morocco-vs-ghana/teams/457198|access-date=28 January 2022|website=Sky Sports|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Africa Cup of Nations (Sky Sports)|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/morocco-vs-comoros/teams/457210|access-date=28 January 2022|website=Sky Sports|language=en}}</ref> He scored an own-goal in a 2–2 draw against [[Gabon national football team|Gabon]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Gabon v Morocco Match Report, 18/01/2022, Africa Cup of Nations {{!}} Goal.com|url=https://www.goal.com/en/match/gabon-v-morocco/7o6548ig1nn0zm2jg1tsh7504|access-date=28 January 2022|website=www.goal.com}}</ref>

On 10 November 2022, he was named in Morocco's 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Morocco World Cup 2022 squad: Who's in and who's out? {{!}} Goal.com |url=https://www.goal.com/en-bh/lists/morocco-2022-world-cup-squad-hakimi-ziyech-mazraoui-qatar/blta36257fc4feeb66a |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=www.goal.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=10 November 2022 |title=Moroccan coach unveils list of 26 Atlas Lions in 2022 World Cup |url=https://en.hespress.com/52697-moroccan-coach-unveils-list-of-26-atlas-lions-in-2022-world-cup.html |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=HESPRESS English - Morocco News |language=en-US}}</ref> Aguerd played all three group games and the round of 16 match against Spain. However, he missed the quarter-final vs Portugal but still played an important role in defence along with [[Romain Saïss]] and was instrumental in Morocco reaching the Semi-finals of the world Cup. However, he missed the decisive game against France due to injury and was withdrawn minutes before the game, despite having been mentioned in the official starting line-ups prior to the match.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Simmonds |first=Kadeem |date=2022-12-15 |title=Walid Regragui says Nayef Aguerd missed Morocco's semi-final loss due to the flu |url=https://www.football.london/west-ham-united-fc/news/nayef-aguerd-france-semi-final-25757484 |access-date=2022-12-24 |website=Football.London |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Winehouse |first=Amitai |title=Morocco's Aguerd pulls out of semi-final before kick-off |url=https://theathletic.com/4002465/2022/12/14/aguerd-morocco-world-cup-injury/ |access-date=2022-12-24 |website=The Athletic |language=en}}</ref>
