'''Danny Desmond Makkelie''' (born 28 January 1983) is a Dutch professional [[Referee (association football)|football referee]]. Besides refereeing he works as a police inspector in [[Rotterdam]] and as a referee coach for the [[Royal Dutch Football Association]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=McGrath|first=Mike|date=7 July 2021|title=Meet Danny Makkelie, the part-time police inspector and referee for England vs Denmark|language=en-GB|work=The Telegraph|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/euro-2021/2021/07/07/danny-makkelie-who-referee-england-vs-denmark-euro-semi-final/|access-date=25 July 2021|issn=0307-1235}}</ref> He has been a [[FIFA International Referees List|FIFA listed]] referee since 2011. 

== Career ==
He took charge of the final of the [[2012 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship]] as a [[UEFA]] Elite referee.<ref>WorldReferees. [http://worldreferee.com/site/copy.php?linkID=7390&contextType=bio Biography of Danny Makkelie].</ref>  On 18 August 2020, UEFA named 37-years old Makkelie as the referee for the [[2020 UEFA Europa League Final]] between [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]] and [[Inter Milan]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Referee team appointed for UEFA Europa League final|url=https://www.uefa.com/insideuefa/mediaservices/mediareleases/news/0260-102923d796cc-d6eade3bdb66-1000--referee-team-appointed-for-europa-league-final/|access-date=18 August 2020|publisher=UEFA Official Site|date=18 August 2020}}</ref> Previously, he was an additional assistant referee in the [[2018 UEFA Europa League Final]]. He also refereed the semifinal between Brazil's [[Sociedade Esportiva Palmeiras|Palmeiras]] and Mexico's [[Tigres UANL|UANL]] in the [[2020 FIFA Club World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite news|title=FIFA Club World Cup Qatar 2020|url= https://www.fifa.com/clubworldcup/matches/match/400154011/#match-info|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20210207172025/https://www.fifa.com/clubworldcup/matches/match/400154011/#match-info|url-status= dead|archive-date= 7 February 2021|access-date=7 February 2021|publisher=FIFA Official Site|date=7 February 2021}}</ref> Makkelie was selected as a referee for the [[UEFA Euro 2020]], where he was in charge of two group stage matches, the R16 clash between [[England national football team|England]] and [[Germany national football team|Germany]] and the [[Wembley Stadium|Wembley]] semi-final between [[England national football team|England]] and [[Denmark national football team|Denmark]]. Danny Makkelie was also present as a referee during the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in which he was the referee for two group stage matches.

== Controversies ==

Makkelie was criticised for a decision in the [[Euro 2020]] semi-final between [[England national football team|England]] and [[Denmark national football team|Denmark]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Danny Makkelie: The referee who made the biggest error of the season|url= https://www.marca.com/en/football/uefa-euro/2021/07/08/60e6caf746163fe95a8b462a.html|access-date=10 July 2021|publisher=Marca Official Site|date=10 July 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Danny Makkelie makes first contentious refereeing call of Euro 2020 in England's favour|url=https://www.newindianexpress.com/sport/football/2021/jul/08/danny-makkelie-makes-first-contentious-refereeing-call-of-euro-2020-in-englands-favour-2327231.html|access-date=25 July 2021|website=The New Indian Express}}</ref> He awarded England a contentious penalty in extra time for a foul on [[Raheem Sterling]]. Makkelie's decision was backed by [[Roberto Rosetti]], the chairman of the [[UEFA]] referees’ committee, who pointed out that the match VAR officials told Makkelie the penalty was "correct".<ref>{{cite news |last1=Jackson |first1=Jamie |title=Raheem Sterling and Jorginho decisions were right, insists Uefa’s referee chief |url=https://www.irishtimes.com/sport/soccer/international/raheem-sterling-and-jorginho-decisions-were-right-insists-uefa-s-referee-chief-1.4620426 |access-date=31 July 2021 |work=Irish Times |date=14 July 2021}}</ref>

== Video assistant refereeing ==

Makkelie was the [[video assistant referee]] in the [[2016 FIFA Club World Cup Final]], video assistant referee in the [[2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup Final]], video assistant referee in the [[2018 FIFA World Cup Final]], video assistant referee in the [[2018 FIFA Club World Cup Final]] and video assistant referee in the [[2019 UEFA Champions League Final]]. He was also video assistant referee during the [[2019 FIFA Women's World Cup]] in which he applied the new law changes in order to let penalties retaken in the [[France women's national football team|France]]–[[Nigeria women's national football team|Nigeria]] and [[Jamaica women's national football team|Jamaica]]–[[Italy women's national football team|Italy]] matches.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/womensworldcup/matches/match/300438230/#match-info|title=FIFA Women's World Cup France 2019 - Matches - Jamaica vs Italy|publisher=FIFA Official Site|date=14 June 2019|accessdate=8 July 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190709212646/https://www.fifa.com/womensworldcup/matches/match/300438230/#match-liveblog|archive-date=9 July 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/womensworldcup/matches/match/300438255/#match-info|title=FIFA Women's World Cup France 2019 - Matches - Nigeria vs France|publisher=FIFA Official Site|date=17 June 2019|accessdate=8 July 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190709165815/https://www.fifa.com/womensworldcup/matches/match/300438255/#match-liveblog|archive-date=9 July 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref>
