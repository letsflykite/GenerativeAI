'''Kamil Mieczysław Grabara''' (born 8 January 1999) is a Polish professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Danish Superliga]] club [[F.C. Copenhagen|Copenhagen]] and the [[Poland national football team|Poland national team]].

==Club career==
Grabara joined Liverpool ahead of the [[2016–17 Liverpool F.C. season|2016–17 season]] from [[Ruch Chorzów]] for £250,000,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/jan/06/kamil-grabara-liverpool-sign-teenage-goalkeeper-ruch-warsaw|title=Liverpool sign teenage Polish keeper Kamil Grabara from Ruch Chorzow|first=Andy|last=Hunter|date=6 January 2016|website=The Guardian|access-date=29 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.liverpoolfc.com/team/first-team/player/kamil-grabara|title=Profile|access-date=23 August 2018|publisher=Liverpool F.C.}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/football/news/who-kamil-grabara-liverpool-goalkeeper-12996084|title=Who is Kamil Grabara? Liverpool goalkeeper starts pre-season clash against Manchester United|last=Smith|first=Alex|date=28 July 2018|work=Mirror|access-date=30 October 2018}}</ref> and was promoted to the first-team squad for the [[2018–19 Liverpool F.C. season|2018–19 season]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/liverpool-turn-teenage-goalkeepers-kamil-14962857|title=Liverpool turn to teenage goalkeepers against Man Utd|last=Walsh|first=Kristian|date=27 July 2018|work=Liverpool Echo|access-date=30 October 2018}}</ref> He trained regularly with the first-team squad at [[Melwood]] and made his maiden senior outing in a pre-season friendly against [[Tranmere Rovers F.C.|Tranmere Rovers]] in July 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/meet-kamil-grabara-liverpools-fearless-14964745|title=Meet Kamil Grabara - Liverpool's 'fearless' teenage goalkeeper liked by Steven Gerrard and Manchester United|first=James|last=Pearce|date=28 July 2018|website=Liverpool Echo|access-date=29 August 2019}}</ref>
On 26 September 2018, Grabara was named on the substitutes' bench for the first time in Liverpool's 2–1 [[EFL Cup]] defeat to [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] at Anfield.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45569330|title=Liverpool 1–2 Chelsea|publisher=BBC Sport|first=Shamoon|last=Hafez|date=27 September 2018|access-date=29 August 2019}}</ref> On 6 January 2019, Grabara joined [[Aarhus Gymnastikforening|AGF]] on loan until the end of the season.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/academy/333040-kamil-grabara-aarhus-liverpool-loan|title=Kamil Grabara joins AGF Aarhus on loan|publisher=Liverpool F.C.|date=6 January 2019|access-date=6 January 2019}}</ref> On 15 July 2019, Grabara joined [[EFL Championship|Championship]] side [[Huddersfield Town A.F.C.|Huddersfield Town]] on a season-long loan deal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/48997808|title=Liverpool: Kamil Grabara signs new deal then joins Huddersfield on loan|publisher=BBC Sport|date=15 July 2019|access-date=16 July 2019}}</ref>

At the end of September 2020, Grabara returned to Danish club [[Aarhus Gymnastikforening|AGF]] on loan for the 2020&ndash;21 season.<ref>[https://www.agf.dk/klubben/nyhedsarkiv/2020/september/kamil-grabara-vender-tilbage-til-agf/?page=0 KAMIL GRABARA VENDER TILBAGE TIL AGF], agf.dk, 28 September 2020</ref>
[[File:Kamil Grabara FC København (cropped).jpg|thumb|202x202px|Grabara with [[F.C. Copenhagen|Copenhagen]] during training in 2022.]]
On 3 July 2021, Grabara joined [[F.C. Copenhagen|Copenhagen]] on a permanent basis, signing a five-year deal with the Danish side.<ref>{{cite news |date=3 July 2021 |title=Kamil Grabara completes permanent move to FC Copenhagen |work=Liverpool FC |url=https://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/first-team/437227-kamil-grabara-completes-permanent-move-to-fc-copenhagen}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Kamil Grabara ny FCK-keeper |url=https://www.fck.dk/nyhed/kamil-grabara-ny-fck-keeper |access-date=3 July 2021 |work=F.C. København |date=3 July 2021 |language=da}}</ref> Following his performances for the club both in the [[Danish Superliga]] and the [[UEFA Champions League]], in December 2022 Grabara received the "Goalkeeper of the Year" award by local sports magazine [[Tipsbladet]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-12-16 |title=Kamil Grabara vinder Det Gyldne Bur |url=https://www.tipsbladet.dk/nyhed/superliga/kamil-grabara-vinder-det-gyldne-bur |access-date=2023-01-28 |website=Tipsbladet.dk}}</ref>

==International career==
Grabara was a first-choice goalkeeper for [[Poland under-21 national football team|Poland U21]] throughout most of his tenure with the squad, having made his debut against Denmark on 14 November 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/under21/season=2019/teams/team=200109/index.html|title=Poland - U21 EURO|last=UEFA.com|website=UEFA.com}}</ref>

He was called-up in the senior [[Poland national football team|Poland]] for a [[friendly match]] with [[Scotland national football team|Scotland]] on 24 March 2022 and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA second round|2022 FIFA World Cup qualification]] playoff against [[Sweden national football team|Sweden]] on 29 March 2022.<ref>{{cite news 
|url=https://eurosport.tvn24.pl/pilka-nozna,105/polska-szwecja-kiedy-mecz-i-o-ktorej-godzinie-baraze-o-ms-2022-pilka-nozna,1100466.html
|title=Poland - Sweden - what time does the match start? 2022 World Cup Play-Off Final in Qatar (https://tvn24.pl)
|publisher=eurosport.tvn24.pl
|access-date=31 March 2022}}</ref>

On 1 June 2022, Grabara made his debut for the [[Poland national football team|senior team]] as a starter in a 2–1 home [[2022–23 UEFA Nations League A#Group 4|2022–23 UEFA Nations League]] win against [[Wales national football team|Wales]].

On 20 October 2022, Grabara was selected by manager [[Czesław Michniewicz]] as part of the final squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], who replaced [[Bartłomiej Drągowski]] who got injured just before the tournament. Grabara did not make a single appearance during the tournament as Poland were eliminated in the Round of 16 by [[France national football team|France]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |title=Znamy szeroką kadrę reprezentacji Polski na mundial w Katarze! - Reprezentacja A |url=https://pzpn.pl/reprezentacje/reprezentacja-a/aktualnosci/2022-10-20/znamy-szeroka-kadre-reprezentacji-polski-na-mundial-w-katarze |access-date=20 October 2022 |website=PZPN - Łączy nas piłka |language=pl}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Grabara is described as an allround goalkeeper, able to dominate the penalty area, pull off reflex saves, play out from the back and, as he develops, operate as a sweeper.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thisisanfield.com/2018/07/kamil-grabara-liverpool-youngster-with-the-confidence-to-become-future-no-1/|title=Who is young Liverpool goalkeeper Kamil Grabara? |publisher=This is Anfield |first=Jack |last=Lusby |date=15 July 2018 |access-date=22 March 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Grabara is from [[Ruda Śląska]], in Southern Poland. He was the second Polish goalkeeper for Liverpool, after the [[2005 UEFA Champions League Final]] hero [[Jerzy Dudek]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/football/news/who-kamil-grabara-liverpool-goalkeeper-12996084|title=Everything you need to know about Liverpool youngster Kamil Grabara|first=Alex|last=Smith|date=28 July 2018|website=mirror}}</ref>
