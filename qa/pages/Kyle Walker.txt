'''Kyle Andrew Walker''' (born 28 May 1990) is an English professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[right-back]] for {{English football updater|ManchesC}} club [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] and the [[England national football team|England national team]]. Considered one of the best full-backs in the world, Walker is known for his pace, strength and vision.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/ranked-10-best-right-backs-world-trent-alexander-arnold-liverpool-man-city-cancelo |title= Ranked! The 10 best right-backs in the world |newspaper=FourFourTwo |date=28 October 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.mancity.com/features/kyle-walker/ |title= Kyle Walker: The modern right-back City rely on |publisher=Manchester City F.C. |access-date=22 February 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.sportsmole.co.uk/football/spurs/news/trippier-walker-is-best-right-back-in-world_334285.html |title= Spurs defender Kieran Trippier: 'Kyle Walker is best right-back in the world' |work=Sports Mole |access-date=22 February 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.sportsmole.co.uk/football/man-city/news/stones-walker-best-full-back-in-world_309702.html |title= John Stones: 'Kyle Walker is best full-back in the world' |work=Sports Mole |access-date=22 February 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title= The 100 best male footballers in the world 2022 |url= https://www.theguardian.com/football/ng-interactive/2023/jan/24/the-100-best-male-footballers-in-the-world-2022 |newspaper=The Guardian |date=27 January 2023 |access-date=26 March 2023}}</ref>

Walker started his career at his boyhood club [[Sheffield United F.C.|Sheffield United]] at the age of seven. He made his [[First team (association football)|first-team]] debut aged 18 after a [[Loan (association football)|loan]] spell at [[Northampton Town F.C.|Northampton Town]]. He impressed whilst playing for United and played in the [[2009 Football League Championship play-off final|2009 Championship play-off final]]. His performances earned him a move to Premier League club [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]], before being immediately loaned back to Sheffield United. After further loan spells at [[Queens Park Rangers F.C.|Queens Park Rangers]] and [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]], Walker cemented his place in Tottenham's first team. Walker joined Manchester City in 2017 for a fee of £45 million. Whilst at City, he has since won five Premier League titles, four [[EFL Cup]]s, two [[FA Cup]]s, and a [[UEFA Champions League]] in [[2023 UEFA Champions League final|2023]] as part of a [[Treble (association football)|continental treble]]. He has been named in the [[PFA Team of the Year]] three times, in the [[2011–12 Premier League|2011–12]], [[2016–17 Premier League|2016–17]] and [[2017–18 Premier League|2017–18 seasons]].

Walker played for England at [[England national under-19 football team|under-19]] and [[England national under-21 football team|under-21]] levels before making his debut for the senior national team in September 2011. He has represented England at two [[UEFA European Championship]]s, in [[UEFA Euro 2016|2016]] and [[UEFA Euro 2020|2020]], and at two [[FIFA World Cup]]s, in [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022]].

===Tottenham Hotspur===
====2009–2011====
[[File:Kyle Walker Spurs USA tour 2010.jpg|thumb|left|upright|Walker with [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] in 2010]]

On 22 July 2009, Walker signed for [[Premier League]] club [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]], along with fellow Sheffield United player [[Kyle Naughton]], for a combined fee of £9&nbsp;million. Walker was loaned back to United for the duration of the 2009–10 season as part of the deal.<ref name="spurs transfer">{{cite news |title=Spurs capture Naughton and Walker |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/s/sheff_utd/8144711.stm |website=BBC Sport |date=22 July 2009 |access-date=22 July 2009}}</ref> While at [[Bramall Lane]] he was virtually ever-present at right-back for the first half of the season but was unexpectedly recalled by Tottenham on 1 February 2010, just before the close of the January 2010 [[transfer window]], after [[Alan Hutton]] was loaned out to [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Tottenham Hotspur recall Sheffield United's Walker |url=http://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/sport/Tottenham-Hotspur-recall-Sheffield-United39s.6034450.jp |newspaper=The Yorkshire Post |location=Leeds |date=2 January 2010 |access-date=2 February 2010}}</ref> Walker made his debut for Tottenham on 27 March, in a 2–0 victory over [[Portsmouth F.C.|Portsmouth]] and went on to make one more league appearance that season.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_prem/8587876.stm|website=BBC Sport |title=Tottenham 2–0 Southampton |date=27 March 2010 |access-date=12 May 2020}}</ref>

At the start of the following season, Walker went out on loan once more signing an initial six-month deal with [[Queens Park Rangers F.C.|Queens Park Rangers]] (QPR) due to injuries at the club in the right-back position.<ref name="qpr loan">{{cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/q/qpr/8995384.stm |website=BBC Sport |title=Spurs defender Walker joins QPR |date=13 September 2010 |access-date=13 September 2010}}</ref> In mid-October, his loan was extended until 3 January 2011.<ref name="extended loan">{{cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/q/qpr/9093535.stm |website=BBC Sport |title=QPR extend loan of Spurs' Walker |date=14 October 2010 |access-date=14 October 2010}}</ref>

After leaving QPR, Walker joined Tottenham's Premier League rivals [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]] on loan until the end of the [[2010–11 Aston Villa F.C. season|2010–11 season]].<ref name="villa loan">{{cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/a/aston_villa/9339872.stm |website=BBC Sport |title=Villa sign Spurs defender on loan |date=6 January 2011 |access-date=6 January 2011}}</ref> He scored on his debut only nine minutes into the match against former club Sheffield United in the third round of the FA Cup in January 2011.<ref name="fa cup goal">{{cite news |title=A Houl lot of relief at Bramall Lane |url=http://www.people.co.uk/sport/football/match-reports/fa-cup-match-reports/2011/01/09/sheffield-united-1-3-aston-villa-a-houl-lot-of-relief-at-bramall-lane-102039-22835698/ |newspaper=The Sunday People |location=London |date=9 January 2011 |access-date=6 February 2011}}</ref> At the start of February, Walker scored his first Premier League goal and first senior league goal, a 30-yard shot into the bottom left corner of the goal against [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]].<ref name="prem goal">{{cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/eng_prem/9385528.stm |website=BBC Sport |title=Aston Villa 2–2 Fulham |date=5 February 2011 |access-date=6 February 2011}}</ref> He returned to Tottenham at the end of the 2010–11 season after scoring two goals in 18 appearances for Villa.<ref name=sb1011/>

==International career==
===Youth career===
Walker made his debut for the [[England national under-19 football team|England national under-19 team]] on 10 February 2009, coming on as a 78th-minute substitute in a 3–0 home loss to [[Spain national under-19 football team|Spain]] in a [[Friendly (association football)|friendly]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sufc.co.uk/page/NewsDetail/0%2C%2C10418~1553137%2C00.html |title=The Kyles get international cameos |publisher=Sheffield United F.C. |date=11 February 2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120426182823/http://www.sufc.co.uk/page/NewsDetail/0,,10418~1553137,00.html |archive-date=26 April 2012}}</ref><ref name="EnglandU19">{{cite web |url=http://www.englandfootballonline.com/MatchRsl/MatchRslTmU19pg1.html |title=Match Results Under 19: 1985–2010 |website=England Football Online |date=4 June 2021 |access-date=28 May 2023}}</ref> His first start came on 25 March in another friendly, in which England drew 0–0 at home to the [[Czech Republic national under-19 football team|Czech Republic]].<ref name="EnglandU19"/> Walker was part of the England squad at the [[2009 UEFA European Under-19 Championship]], and played in every match as England went on to lose 2–0 to [[Ukraine national under-19 football team|Ukraine]] in the final.<ref name="EnglandU19"/> This was the last of his seven appearances for the under-19s.<ref name="EnglandU19"/>

Walker's debut for the [[England national under-21 football team|England national under-21 team]] came on 3 March 2010 when starting a 2–1 home defeat to [[Greece national under-21 football team|Greece]] in a [[2011 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification Group 9|2011 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification group]] match.<ref name="EnglandU21">{{cite web |url=http://www.englandfootballonline.com/MatchRsl/MatchRslTmU21pg3.html |title=Match Results Under 21: 2000–2010 |website=England Football Online |date=29 September 2017 |access-date=28 May 2023}}<br />{{cite web |url=http://www.englandfootballonline.com/MatchRsl/MatchRslTmU21pg4.html |title=Match Results Under 21: 2010–2020 |website=England Football Online |date=23 March 2023 |access-date=28 May 2023}}</ref> At the end of the 2010–11 season, he was named in the England squad for the [[2011 UEFA European Under-21 Championship]] in Denmark.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thefa.com/England/mens-under-21s/News/2011/englnd-u21s-squad-numbers |title=Squad numbers confirmed |publisher=The Football Association |date=7 June 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121017122848/http://www.thefa.com/England/mens-under-21s/News/2011/englnd-u21s-squad-numbers |archive-date=17 October 2012}}</ref> Walker played in England's three matches at the tournament, and despite them being eliminated in the group stage he was named in the Team of the Tournament.<ref name="EnglandU21"/><ref name="TOTT">{{cite news |url=https://www.uefa.com/under21/news/0254-0d7caecaa0cb-d2351f519df8-1000--u21-all-star-squad-named-by-uefa-technical-team/ |title=U21 all-star squad named by UEFA technical team |publisher=UEFA |date=29 June 2011 |access-date=28 May 2023}}</ref> He finished his under-21 career with seven appearances.<ref name="EnglandU21"/>

==Personal life==
Walker married Annie Kilner, with whom he has three children, in 2022, after a 12-year relationship.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.thestar.co.uk/sport/football/sheffield-united/kyle-walker-england-where-did-ex-sheffield-united-star-grow-up-is-man-city-man-married-when-can-we-watch-him-at-world-cup-3924484 |title=Kyle Walker England: Where did ex-Sheffield United star grow up? On 5 April 2020, Manchester City began a disciplinary procedure against Walker after it was reported that he had broken national lockdown rules and invited two sex workers to his home amid the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2020/apr/05/city-to-investigate-kyle-walker-over-sex-party-that-broke-quarantine-rules|title=Manchester City to investigate Kyle Walker over sex party during lockdown|date=5 April 2020|newspaper=The Guardian}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/kyle-walker-man-city-disciplinary-18045379|title=Man City full statement on Kyle Walker disciplinary action|date=5 April 2020|newspaper=Manchester Evening News}}</ref> On 7 May, it was again reported that he had broken lockdown rules by travelling to Sheffield to give a present to his sister and hugging her. He later admitted that he also visited his parents to pick up food. The club said they would not discipline him for this, citing the trips being for personal reasons. He later tweeted that he felt he and his family were being harassed and that the reports were affecting the mental health of his whole family.<ref>{{cite news |title=Kyle Walker: Man City defender says he is 'harassed' after admitting breaking lockdown rules |website=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/52587293 |access-date=9 May 2020}}</ref>

In March 2023, it was reported that Walker exposed himself to a woman in a Manchester bar.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://talksport.com/football/1355129/kyle-walker-video-man-city-premier-league/ |title=Kyle Walker allegedly caught flashing and kissing woman in bar the day after Man City win |publisher=talkSPORT |date=8 March 2023 }}</ref> It was later reported that Walker would not face criminal charges for his behaviour.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.theguardian.com/football/2023/mar/17/kyle-walker-will-not-face-criminal-charges-manchester-bar-manchester-city-england |title= Kyle Walker will not face criminal charges for behaviour in Manchester bar |publisher=The Guardian|access-date=21 June 2023 }}</ref>
