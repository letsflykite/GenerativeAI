'''Ricardo Ivan Rodriguez Araya''' (born 25 August 1992) is a Swiss professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[left-back]] for [[Serie A]] club [[Torino F.C.|Torino]] and the [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland national team]].

Rodriguez began his professional career at local club [[FC Zürich]] in 2010, having spent eight years in the youth setup. After breaking through into the first team at Zürich, Rodriguez was sold to [[VfL Wolfsburg]] in January 2012 for £7.5 million.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.fcz.ch/de/profis/news_archiv.htm?id=1514&page=98&ipp=10&archiv=1 |title=Ricardo Rodriguez wechselt definitiv zum VfL Wolfsburg |work=FC Zürich |date=13 January 2012 |access-date=26 September 2014 |language=de |archive-date=6 March 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160306123357/http://www.fcz.ch/de/profis/news_archiv.htm?id=1514&page=98&ipp=10&archiv=1 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He played 184 games across all competitions for Wolfsburg, scoring 22 goals and winning the [[DFB-Pokal]] and [[DFL-Supercup]] in 2015. In 2017, he signed for [[A.C. Milan|AC Milan]].

Rodriguez has earned caps at every level of the Swiss national team setup, winning the [[FIFA U-17 World Cup|FIFA U17 World Cup]] in [[2009 FIFA U-17 World Cup|2009]]. A full international for [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]] since 2011, Rodriguez has earned over 100 caps for the country. He was part of the Swiss team at the [[Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|2012 Olympics]], and also represented the senior side at the [[FIFA World Cup]] in [[2014 FIFA World Cup|2014]], [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022]], and the [[UEFA European Championship]] in [[UEFA Euro 2016|2016]] and [[UEFA Euro 2020|2020]]. In 2014, he was voted [[Swiss Footballer of the Year]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.bundesliga.com/en/liga/news/2014/0000300753.php |title=Rodriguez wins Player of the Year Award |work=Bundesliga |date=2 September 2014 |access-date=22 September 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141203103138/http://www.bundesliga.com/en/liga/news/2014/0000300753.php |archive-date=3 December 2014 }}</ref>

==Club career==
===Zürich===
Born to a Spanish father and a Chilean mother of Spanish (Basque) descent, Rodriguez began playing football at the highly regarded youth club FC Schwamendingen in 2001 before joining the youth setup of [[FC Zürich]] as an 11-year-old in 2002.<ref name="Sky Sports">{{cite web|url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/15198/8615777/sky-sports-scout-ricardo-rodriguez|title=Sky Sports Scout – Ricardo Rodriguez|date=21 May 2014|work=Sky Sports|access-date=22 September 2014}}</ref> He was promoted to the senior squad in 2009 at age 16, making the bench for Zürich's 2–3 defeat to [[NK Maribor]] in the first leg of their [[2009–10 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round#First leg 3|third qualifying round]] Champions League tie on 29 July 2009.<ref name="Sky Sports" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2010/matches/round=2000026/match=2000316/postmatch/report/index.html |title=Pavlovič puts Maribor in command |work=UEFA |date=29 July 2009 |access-date=22 September 2014 }}</ref>

He made his Zürich debut as a 17-year-old in the 25th round of the [[2009–10 Swiss Super League|Swiss Super League]] on 21 March 2010, replacing the injured [[Hannu Tihinen]] in the first half of the 2–0 win over [[AC Bellinzona|Bellinzona]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2010/03/21/switzerland/super-league/fc-zurich/ac-bellinzona/893179/ |title=Zürich vs. Bellinzona 2–0 |work=Soccerway |date=21 March 2010 |access-date=22 September 2014 }}</ref> His full debut came in the [[Zürich Derby]] against [[Grasshopper Club Zürich|Grasshoppers]] on 5 April, playing the entire 3–2 win for the hosts.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2010/04/05/switzerland/super-league/fc-zurich/grasshopper-club-zurich/893156/ |title=Zürich vs. Grasshopper 3–2 |work=Soccerway |date=5 April 2010 |access-date=22 September 2014 }}</ref>

He made only his second start in the first match of the [[2010–11 Swiss Super League|Swiss Super League]] season on 20 July 2010, playing the full 90 minutes in a 2–3 defeat to [[FC Basel#Supporters and rivalries|rivals]] [[FC Basel]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2010/07/20/switzerland/super-league/fc-basel/fc-zurich/947257/ |title=Basel vs. Zürich 3–2 |work=Soccerway |date=20 July 2010 |access-date=22 September 2014 }}</ref> On 28 April 2011, he scored his first professional goal for the club, opening the scoring for the hosts as they ran out 3–0 winners over [[Neuchâtel Xamax]] at the [[Letzigrund]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2011/04/28/switzerland/super-league/fc-zurich/neuchatel-xamax-fc/1057072/ |title=Zürich vs. Neuchâtel Xamax 3–0 |work=Soccerway |date=28 April 2011 |access-date=22 September 2014 }}</ref>

===VfL Wolfsburg===
====Early career====
[[VfL Wolfsburg]] purchased Rodriguez as a 19-year-old on 13 January 2012 for £7.5 million with the player signing a four-and-a-half-year deal.<ref name="Four Four Two" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/11/transfer-zone/2012/01/13/2845205/official-wolfsburg-sign-ricardo-rodriguez-from-zurich |title=Official: Wolfsburg sign Ricardo Rodriguez from Zurich |work=Goal.com |date=13 January 2012 |access-date=22 September 2014 }}</ref> He quickly asserted himself into the first-team lineup, making his Bundesliga debut the next day against [[1. FC Köln]], a 1–0 victory for the Wolves.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/spieltag/1-bundesliga/2011-12/18/1143447/spielanalyse_vfl-wolfsburg-24_1-fc-koeln-16.html |title=Polter versüßt Magaths Debütantenball |work=Kicker |date=21 January 2012 |access-date=22 September 2014 |language=de }}</ref> Rodriguez went on to play every game of the season from that point on without being substituted, becoming a favorite on the left of the defense for manager [[Felix Magath]].<ref name="Sky Sports" />

Through the first ten league games of the following campaign, Rodriguez was once again an ever-present in the squad, featuring in all the outings, starting eight.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/player/_/id/136865/ricardo-rodriguez-?cc=5901 |title=Ricardo Rodriguez Bio |publisher=ESPNFC |access-date=7 May 2013 }}{{Dead link|date=November 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> But Magath was sacked after a poor run of form in the league and when [[Lorenz-Günther Köstner]] was installed as interim manager, the more experienced [[Marcel Schäfer]] was preferred at left back.<ref name="Sky Sports" /> Once [[Dieter Hecking]] was appointed manager though, Rodriguez won his place back in the first team.<ref name="Sky Sports" />

====2013–14 season====
On 9 November 2013, he scored his first goal for Wolfsburg, from a direct [[Direct free kick|free kick]] in a home Bundesliga match against [[Borussia Dortmund]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/live/match/291041/report |title=Wolfsburg trip up Dortmund |work=Sky Sports |date=9 November 2013 |access-date=18 September 2014 }}</ref> The match ended in a 2–1 win for Wolfsburg, although they were losing at half-time.<ref>{{cite news|title=Wolfsburg vs. Borussia Dortmund|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2013/11/09/germany/bundesliga/vfl-wolfsburg/bv-borussia-09-dortmund/1487128/|newspaper=Soccerway|date=9 November 2013}}</ref> His second goal for Wolfsburg came from the [[Penalty kick (association football)|penalty spot]] against [[Hamburger SV|Hamburg]] on 29 November, earning his side a 1–1 draw.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/live/match/304843/report |title=Wolfsburg hang on for point |work=Sky Sports |date=29 November 2013 |access-date=18 September 2014 }}</ref> He opened the scoring in their Bundesliga match on 14 December, curling the ball past keeper [[Sven Ulreich]], as Wolfsburg won 3–1.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/live/match/291103/report |title=Home comforts for Wolfsburg |work=Sky Sports |date=14 December 2013 |access-date=18 September 2014 }}</ref>

He finished the 2013–14 season with five league goals, in addition to nine assists.<ref name="Four Four Two" /><ref>{{cite web|title=Scorer|url=http://www.bundesliga.de/de/liga/scorer/|publisher=[[Bundesliga]]|access-date=14 May 2014}}</ref> He failed to make it into the [[2013–14 Bundesliga|Bundesliga]] Team of the Season, but was included in a list of the Best Defenders for the 2013–14 campaign.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga-tv/inc/video_flv/interview/0000290220.inc.php |title=Team of the Season: Defenders |work=Bundesliga |access-date=22 September 2014 }}</ref> Rodriguez's nine assists also meant that he created more goals than any other full-back in Europe.<ref name="FFT">{{cite web |url=http://www.fourfourtwo.com/my/features/how-full-backs-are-making-world-cup-brilliant-and-why-england-need-more-theirs |title=How full-backs are making this World Cup brilliant |work=Four Four Two |date=19 June 2014 |access-date=22 September 2014 |archive-date=4 November 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141104221630/http://www.fourfourtwo.com/my/features/how-full-backs-are-making-world-cup-brilliant-and-why-england-need-more-theirs |url-status=dead }}</ref> His impressive displays during the season included 2.3 key passes per game, the same number as [[Andrea Pirlo]], the two-time reigning [[Serie A Footballer of the Year]], and 2.6 successful dribbles per game, more than [[Cristiano Ronaldo]], the reigning [[2013 FIFA Ballon d'Or|Ballon d'Or]] winner.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://grantland.com/the-triangle/soccer-players-you-need-to-know-before-the-world-cup-ricardo-rodriguez/ |title=World Cup Players to Know: Switzerland's Ricardo Rodriguez |work=Grantland |date=29 May 2014 |access-date=22 September 2014 }}</ref>

Crotone|Crotone]] on 21 August,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Atkinson |first1=Guy |title=AC Milan 2 SPAL 0: Penalty double seals comfortable win for Montella's side |url=https://www.goal.com/en-ph/amp/news/ac-milan-2-spal-0-penalty-double-seals-comfortable-win-for/14vf5jey3btp51ged830is8sm0 |website=goal.com |access-date=15 September 2020 |date=21 September 2017}}</ref> and his first league goal came from a penalty kick in a 2–0 win against [[S.P.A.L. 2013|SPAL]] at the [[San Siro]] on 20 September.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.espn.co.uk/football/club/ac-milan/103/blog/post/3209495/kessie-rodriguez-penalties-see-ac-milan-breeze-past-spal|title=Kessie, Rodriguez penalties see AC Milan breeze past Spal|publisher=ESPN|date=21 September 2017|access-date=20 September 2017}}</ref> On 15 October in the ''[[Derby della Madonnina]]'', he gave away a late penalty from which [[Mauro Icardi]] completed his hat-trick to win the game 3–2 for Inter. Since then, whilst being a regular for Milan, he has not reached the heights that were expected of him, with Milan failing repeatedly to reach the Champions League. Most recently they failed in 2019 when Atalanta qualified for the first time at their expense.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Paul|first1=Sumeet|title=Leonardo Bonucci's troubles continue as Milan lose derby at the death|url=http://www.espn.co.uk/football/club/ac-milan/103/blog/post/3230546/leonardo-bonuccis-troubles-continue-as-milan-falter-at-the-death-in-the-derby|access-date=2 December 2017|publisher=ESPN|date=15 October 2017}}</ref>

==International career==

|access-date=22 September 2014 }}</ref> In the [[2009 FIFA U-17 World Cup#Semi-finals|semi-finals]] against [[Colombia national under-17 football team|Colombia]], he scored Switzerland's final goal of ==Style of play==
[[File:Suisse - France 2016.jpg|thumb|Rodriguez (red, number 13) defending against [[France national football team|France]] at [[UEFA Euro 2016|Euro 2016]]]]

Due to his wide range of skills, Rodriguez is known for being equally adept at defending as he is going forward.<ref name="Wolfsburg's Rodriguez won't come cheap"/> Although usually a left-back, his size and physique have also seen him be deployed as a [[centre-back]] on occasion.<ref name="Wolfsburg's Rodriguez won't come cheap">{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.us/blog/soccer-usa/3/post/3046029/wolfsburg-ricardo-rodriguez-wont-come-cheap-but-hes-ready-for-the-big-times|title=Wolfsburg's Rodriguez won't come cheap, but he's ready for the big times|publisher=ESPN FC|author1=Michael Yokhin|date=25 January 2017|access-date=17 July 2017}}</ref> Experts consider him as an accomplished [[Defender (association football)|defender]] and note his strong heading ability, as well his personality.<ref name="Sky Sports" /> He is also considered a continual attacking threat, and has been noted by analysts for his exceptional stamina and pace, which allow for his constant, buccaneering runs up the wing;<ref name="Sky Sports" /><ref name="FFT" /><ref name="Four Four Two" /> he has also drawn praise from pundits for rarely conceding careless fouls.<ref name="Wolfsburg's Rodriguez won't come cheap"/> Conversely, he has been criticised for giving too much room to opposition attackers, taking up poor positioning and lacking in defensive discipline,<ref name="Four Four Two" /> while other analysts have criticised his concentration.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/player/97381/ricardo-rodriguez |title=Ricardo Rodriguez |work=Sky Sports |access-date=22 September 2014 }}</ref>

Possessing good technique and distribution, he has become known for his pinpoint crossing and accurate left foot, which have earned him comparisons with [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] and [[England national football team|England]] left back [[Leighton Baines]].<ref name="FIFA Profile" /><ref name="FFT" /><ref name="Bleacher Report Scouting">{{cite web|url=http://bleacherreport.com/articles/1856748-scouting-reported-chelsea-transfer-target-ricardo-rodriguez |title=Scouting Report for Chelsea Transfer-Target Ricardo Rodriguez |work=Bleacher Report |date=19 November 2013 |access-date=22 September 2014 }}</ref><ref name="Wolfsburg's Rodriguez won't come cheap"/> He is also noted for his speciality in dead ball situations, such as corner kicks, direct free kicks, and indirect free kicks, as well as for his excellent penalty taking abilities, emerging as Wolfsburg's first-choice penalty taker during the [[2013–14 VfL Wolfsburg season|2013–14 season]].<ref name="Bundesliga" /><ref name="Four Four Two" /><ref name="Wolfsburg's Rodriguez won't come cheap"/> In 2014, his manager [[Dieter Hecking]] praised Rodriguez as "the best left-back in the Bundesliga".<ref name="Wolfsburg ESPN Rodriguez" />

== Personal life ==
Rodriguez was born in [[Zürich]] to a [[Galician people|Galician]] father and a Chilean mother.<ref name="Bundesliga">{{cite web |url=http://www.bundesliga.com/en/liga/news/2012/0000217379.php |title=Ricardo Rodriguez |work=Bundesliga |access-date=22 September 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141203103141/http://www.bundesliga.com/en/liga/news/2012/0000217379.php |archive-date=3 December 2014 }}</ref> His mother, Marcela Araya, died of cancer in 2015. Rodriguez had the number 68, the year of her birth, tattooed on his back.<ref name="theguardian.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2018/jun/07/ricardo-rodriguez-switzerland-defender-milan-emergency-surgery|title=Ricardo Rodriguez: Swiss defender who was given a 50% chance of living|last=Kern|first=Max|website=theguardian.com|publisher=Guardian News & Media Limited|date=7 June 2018|access-date=17 March 2020}}</ref> Rodriguez was born with a [[diaphragmatic hernia]], meaning that his stomach, spleen, liver and intestine had migrated into the chest. Rodriguez was given a 50% chance of survival and was monitored every six months for the first three years of his life.<ref name="theguardian.com"/> He is the younger brother of [[midfielder]] [[Roberto Rodríguez (footballer)|Roberto Rodríguez]] and the older brother of midfielder [[Francisco Rodríguez (Swiss footballer)|Francisco Rodríguez]].<ref name="Four Four Two">{{cite web|url=http://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/everything-you-need-know-about-ricardo-rodriguez |title=Everything you need to know about... Ricardo Rodriguez |work=Four Four Two |date=24 July 2014 |access-date=22 September 2014 }}</ref>
