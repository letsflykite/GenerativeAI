'''Srđan Babić''' ({{lang-sr-cyr|Срђан Бабић}}; born 22 April 1996) is a Serbian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]] for Spanish club [[UD Almería|Almería]].

==Club career==
===Vojvodina===
Babić started out at his hometown club [[FK Borac Banja Luka|Borac Banja Luka]], before joining the youth system of [[FK Vojvodina|Vojvodina]] in August 2011. He made his first team debut under manager [[Branko Babić]] on 1 March 2014, playing the full 90 minutes in a 2–0 home win over [[FK Voždovac|Voždovac]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.fkvojvodina.rs/2014/03/vojvodina-vozdovac-2-0/ | title=VOJVODINA-VOŽDOVAC 2 : 0 | language=Serbian | publisher=fkvojvodina.rs | date=1 March 2014 | accessdate=2 March 2014}}</ref> Despite making only eight league appearances in the [[2013–14 Serbian SuperLiga|2013–14 season]], Babić was named in the competition's best eleven. He also helped his team win the [[Serbian Cup]], scoring a goal in a 2–0 victory over [[FK Jagodina|Jagodina]] in the final.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.novosti.rs/vesti/sport.294.html:545939-Babic-za-Sport-Banjalucki-i-na-rastanku-od-Novog-Sada | title=Babić za "Sport": Banjalučki i na rastanku od Novog Sada | language=Serbian | publisher=novosti.rs | date=1 May 2015 | accessdate=7 July 2015}}</ref> In the 2014–15 season, Babić was a regular choice at centre-back, making 27 appearances in all competitions.

===Real Sociedad===
On 8 July 2015, Babić signed a four-year contract with Spanish side [[Real Sociedad]], being assigned to [[Real Sociedad B|the reserves]] in [[Segunda División B]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.realsociedad.com//document/view/spa//188432/srdjan-babic--a-new-reinforcement-for-sanse | title=Srdjan Babic, a new reinforcement for Sanse | publisher=realsociedad.com | date=8 July 2015 | accessdate=31 August 2016}}</ref> He was an undisputed starter during the [[2015–16 Segunda División B|2015–16 campaign]], making 31 appearances and scoring three goals.

====Reus (loan)====
On 31 August 2016, Babić was loaned to [[Segunda División]] club [[CF Reus Deportiu|Reus Deportiu]], on a [[2016–17 Segunda División|season]]-long loan.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.realsociedad.com//document/view/spa//204955/babic-loaned-to-cf-reus-deportiu- | title=Babic loaned to CF Reus Deportiu | publisher=realsociedad.com | date=31 August 2016 | accessdate=31 August 2016}}</ref> A backup option to [[Pichu Atienza]] and [[Jesús Olmo]], he featured in eleven matches, and also scored his first professional goal while at the club, netting his team's only in a 1–2 home loss against [[Córdoba CF|Córdoba]] on 4 December.

===Red Star Belgrade===
On 7 July 2017, Babić made a one-year loan deal with [[Serbian SuperLiga]] club [[Red Star Belgrade]].<ref name="2017loan">{{cite web |url=http://crvenazvezdafk.com/scc/vest/6040/sran-babic-zvezdin|title=Срђан Бабић Звездин!|website=Red Star Belgrade official website|language=Serbian|date=7 July 2017|accessdate=8 July 2017}}</ref> In the mid-season of the 2017–18 campaign, Red Star purchased his contract and Babić signed with the club permanently for a fee of €800,000.<ref name="permanent">{{cite web |url=http://www.crvenazvezdafk.com/scc/vest/6759/Zvezda-otkupila-Babicev-ugovor|title=Звезда откупила Бабићев уговор|website=Red Star Belgrade official website|language=Serbian|date=5 February 2018|accessdate=5 February 2018}}</ref> On 9 February 2018, Babić signed a new four-year contract with Red Star Belgrade.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.crvenazvezdafk.com/scc/vest/6770/Babic-produzio-ugovor|title=Бабић продужио уговор|website=Red Star Belgrade official website|language=Serbian|date=9 February 2018|accessdate=9 February 2018}}</ref> Babić scored his first goal for Red Star in 5–2 away victory over [[FK Mladost Lučani|Mladost Lučani]] on 1 April 2018, on [[Slavoljub Srnić]]'s assist from [[corner kick]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.crvenazvezdafk.com/scc/vest/6930/Babic-Prvenac-za-nase-navijace|title=Бабић – Првенац за наше навијаче|website=Red Star Belgrade official website|language=Serbian|date=2 April 2018|accessdate=2 April 2018}}</ref> On 12 August 2018, Babić scored in 3–0 home win against [[FK Spartak Subotica|Spartak Subotica]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.mozzartsport.com/fudbal/vesti/pavkov-prosao-audiciju-za-trnavu-zvezda-je-i-dalje-na-sto-posto-video/303289|title=Pavkov prošao audiciju za Trnavu: Zvezda je i dalje na sto posto (VIDEO)|website=mozzartsport.com|language=Serbian|date=12 August 2018|accessdate=14 August 2018}}</ref>

===Almería===
On 30 August 2021, Babić returned to Spain after agreeing to a one-year loan deal with [[UD Almería]] in the second division.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.udalmeriasad.com/noticia/srdjan-babic-sexto-fichaje-para-reforzar-la-defensa|title=Srdjan Babic, sexto fichaje para reforzar la defensa|trans-title=Srdjan Babic, sixth signing to bolster the defense|publisher=UD Almería|language=es|date=30 August 2021|accessdate=30 August 2021}}</ref> The following 5 June, after contributing with the club's [[2021–22 Segunda División|promotion]] to [[La Liga]] as champions, he signed a permanent contract until 2026.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.udalmeriasad.com/en/notice/babic-extends-stay-at-ud-almeria-until-2026|title=Babić extends stay at UD Almeria until 2026|publisher=UD Almería|date=5 June 2022|accessdate=5 July 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
Babić represented [[Serbia national under-19 football team|Serbia]] at the [[2014 UEFA European Under-19 Championship|2014 UEFA Under-19 Championship]], as the team was eliminated in the semi-final by [[Portugal national under-19 football team|Portugal]]. He was also a regular member of the team that won the [[2015 FIFA U-20 World Cup]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/news/y=2015/m=6/news=ecstasy-in-auckland-as-serbia-make-history-2651306.html | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150620212444/http://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/news/y=2015/m=6/news=ecstasy-in-auckland-as-serbia-make-history-2651306.html | url-status=dead | archive-date=20 June 2015 | title=Ecstasy in Auckland as Serbia make history | publisher=fifa.com | date=20 June 2015 | accessdate=7 July 2015}}</ref>

In November 2022, he was selected in Serbia's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fss.rs/en/head-coach-dragan-stojkovic-announces-the-list-of-players-for-the-fifa-world-cup-2022-in-qatar/ |title=Head-coach Dragan Stojković announces the list of players for the FIFA World Cup 2022 in Qatar |publisher=[[Football Association of Serbia]] |date=11 November 2022 |access-date=11 November 2022}}</ref> He played in a group stage match against [[Cameroon national football team|Cameroon]], coming on as a sub in 78th minute, replacing [[Miloš Veljković]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=Sky Sports |date=2022-11-28 |title=World Cup 2022 - Cameroon 3-3 Serbia: Vincent Aboubakar and Eric Maxim Choupo-Moting goals complete stunning comeback |language=en |work=Sky Sports |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/cameroon-vs-serbia/report/462988#:~:text=World%20Cup%202022%20%2D%20Cameroon%203,Moting%20goals%20complete%20stunning%20comeback&text=Cameroon%20kept%20alive%20their%20faint,with%20Serbia%20in%20Al%2DWakrah. |access-date=2022-11-28}}</ref>
