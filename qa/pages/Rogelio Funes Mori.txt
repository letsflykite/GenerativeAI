'''Rogelio Gabriel Funes Mori''' (born 5 March 1991) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]] for [[Liga MX]] club [[C.F. Monterrey|Monterrey]]. Born in Argentina, he represents the [[Mexico national football team|Mexico national team]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=1 July 2021|title=México va a la Copa Oro sin Chicharito Hernández y con Funes Mori|url=https://www.espn.com.mx/futbol/selecciones/nota/_/id/8853729/mexico-convocatoria-copa-oro-funes-mori-incluido-chicharito-ausente|access-date=3 July 2021|website=ESPN.com.mx|language=es}}</ref>

He began his career at [[Club Atlético River Plate|River Plate]] before transferring to [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]], after a brief stint in Turkey he signed with Liga MX club Monterrey in the summer of 2015 and quickly established himself as one of the league's best strikers, winning the league, two domestic [[Copa MX|cups]] and two [[CONCACAF Champions League]] titles and surpassing [[Humberto Suazo]] to become the club's all-time top scorer in all competitions.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Fernando Rodriguez|date=28 December 2019|title=Rogelio Funes Mori, el mejor delantero del futbol mexicano en 2019|url=https://naciondeportes.com/funes-mori-el-delantero-con-mas-goles-este-ano/|access-date=3 July 2021|website=Nación Deportes|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=17 April 2020|title=Ángel Zaldívar: "Funes Mori es el mejor delantero de la Liga MX"|url=https://mexico.as.com/mexico/2020/04/17/futbol/1587152004_401126.html|access-date=3 July 2021|website=AS México|language=es-mx}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Rogelio Funes Mori empata a Humberto Suazo como goleador de Monterrey|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/liga-mx/rogelio-funes-mori-empata-humberto-suazo-goleador-historico-rayados|access-date=3 July 2021|website=www.mediotiempo.com|language=es-MX}}</ref>

His identical twin brother [[Ramiro Funes Mori|José Ramiro]] is also a footballer who played as a defender for [[Premier League]] club [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] and [[La Liga]] outfit [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]] and for the [[Argentina national football team|Argentina national team]].

==Early life==
Funes Mori was born in [[Mendoza, Argentina]]. In 2001, he moved to [[Arlington, Texas]] and attended Workman Junior High and [[Arlington High School (Texas)|Arlington High School]] where he was coached by Jeffrey Waldrop playing football with his twin brother, [[Ramiro Funes Mori|Ramiro]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/newsid=1983349.html |title=Funes Mori joins Benfica's Argentinian contingent |date= 20 August 2013 |publisher=[[UEFA]] |access-date=15 May 2015 |quote=whose twin brother Ramiro still plays for River Plate}}</ref> The twins' father, Miguel, played professional football in Argentina with clubs such as [[Independiente Rivadavia]] and [[Argentino de Mendoza|Club Atlético Argentino]] in the 1980s.<ref>{{cite web|title=La historia de Gabriel Funes Mori y su papá|url=http://www.diariouno.com.ar/deportes/La-historia-de-Gabriel-Funes-Mori-y-su-papa-20100824-113294.html|website=Diario Uno|access-date=24 August 2015|language=es|date=24 August 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150923215149/http://www.diariouno.com.ar/deportes/La-historia-de-Gabriel-Funes-Mori-y-su-papa-20100824-113294.html|archive-date=23 September 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> Funes Mori scored 40 goals in the 2008 season leading his team to District Champions and Area Finalist with a record of 24–2–1. Rogelio and Ramiro, who are identical twins, also played several seasons for the Associated Soccer Group (ASG) 91' team. His coach with ASG was [[Uche Okafor]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Former ASG Star Nets 11 Million Dollar Transfer|url=http://www.asgfc.com/Funes_11million.htm|work=asgfc.com|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131231082858/http://www.asgfc.com/Funes_11million.htm|archive-date=31 December 2013}}</ref>

After that, [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] scout Jorge Alvial scouted Rogelio and Ramiro on the first day of the reality show ''[[Sueño MLS]]'', and spoke to his parents about a future tryout at Chelsea.<ref>{{cite web |title=Cómo los gemelos Funes Mori fueron descubiertos afuera de un estadio en EEUU y por qué no lograron quedarse en Chelsea |trans-title=How the Funes Mori twins were discovered outside a stadium in the US and why they couldn't stay at Chelsea |url=https://www.espn.com.mx/futbol/nota/_/id/6945639/gemelos-funes-mori-descubiertos-afuera-estadio-no-quedaron-chelsea |website=ESPN |access-date=23 January 2021 |language=Spanish |date=15 May 2020}}</ref> He participated in ''Sueño MLS'' and won the contest, therefore becoming part of [[FC Dallas]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Rogelio Funes Mori podría jugar en la selección de los Estados Unidos|trans-title=Funes Mori could play for US Soccer national team|url=http://www.infobae.com/2013/02/19/697220-rogelio-funes-mori-podria-jugar-la-seleccion-los-estados-unidos|work=infobae.com|date=19 February 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Rogelio Funes Mori to Manchester United?|url=http://hastaelgolsiempre.com/2010/05/18/rogelio-funes-mori-to-manchester-united/|work=hastaelgolsiempre.com|date=18 August 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Sueño MLS Past Winners|url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/sueno/winners|work=mlssoccer.com}}</ref>

==Club career==
===River Plate===
Funes Mori began his professional career with [[Club Atlético River Plate|River Plate]] in the [[Argentine Primera División]] on 6 December 2009, on a 1–3 defeat against [[Club Atlético Vélez Sársfield|Vélez Sársfield]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Torneo Apertura 2009: round 17 scores and scorers|url=http://hastaelgolsiempre.com/2009/12/07/torneo-apertura-2009-round-17-scores-and-scorers/|work=hastaelgolsiempre.com|date=7 December 2009}}</ref> He played the last two games of that year at home against [[Racing Club de Avellaneda|Racing Club]] and away to [[Club Atlético Tigre|Tigre]], scoring his first goal with the club in their visit to the latter.<ref>{{cite web|title=Torneo Apertura 2009: round 19 scores, scorers and Monday goal|url=http://hastaelgolsiempre.com/2009/12/15/torneo-apertura-2009-round-19-scores-scorers-and-monday-goal/|work=hastaelgolsiempre.com|date=15 December 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=FormerASG Player, Gabriel Funes, scores for River Plate|url=http://www.asgfc.com/Gabriel%20Funes%20scores%20River%20Plate.htm|work=asgfc.com|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150923175201/http://www.asgfc.com/Gabriel%20Funes%20scores%20River%20Plate.htm|archive-date=23 September 2015}}</ref>

In the traditional pre-season encounters for the big clubs in Argentina, the [[Torneos de Verano (Argentina)|Torneos de Verano]], he scored for River in a 3–1 victory over their rivals [[Boca Juniors]] in [[Mar del Plata]]'s ''Copa Desafío''.<ref>{{cite web|title=River Plate derrota o Boca Juniors e conquista Torneio de Mar del Plata|trans-title=River Plate beats Boca Juniors and wins Tournament Mal Del Plata|url=http://globoesporte.globo.com/Esportes/Noticias/Futebol/0,,MUL1456846-9842,00.html|work=globoesporte.globo.com|date=21 January 2010}}</ref>

On 9 May, during the [[2009–10 Argentine Primera División season#Torneo Clausura|2010 Clausura tournament]], he scored his first [[hat-trick]] for River Plate in a game against Racing Club. He achieved the three goals in only 24 minutes, breaking a personal 11-match streak without scoring a goal.<ref>{{cite web|title=Funes Mori desencanta e marca três no clássico contra o Racing|trans-title=Funes Mori scores three in the classic against Racing|url=http://www.futebolportenho.com.br/2010/05/09/funes-mori-desencanta-e-marca-tres-no-classico-contra-o-racing/|work=futebolportenho.com.br|date=9 May 2010}}</ref> In the last game of the tournament, the forward scored River's only goal in a 1–5 loss to Tigre at home.<ref>{{cite web|title=Clausura 2010: Round 19 Saturday goals|url=http://hastaelgolsiempre.com/2010/05/16/clausura-2010-round-19-saturday-goals/|work=hastaelgolsiempre.com|date=16 May 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Highlights: Clausura 2011 Argentina – Fecha 1|url=http://southamerican-futbol.blogspot.pt/2011/02/highlights-clausura-2011-argentina.html|work=southamerican-futbol.blogspot.pt|date=15 February 2011}}</ref>

In the first game of the [[2010–11 Argentine Primera División season#Torneo Apertura|2010 Apertura]], he scored a 90th-minute goal following an assist from [[Ariel Ortega]] to secure a 1–0 victory over Tigre.<ref>{{cite web|title=Funes Mori faz gol nos acréscimos e River vence o Tigre por 1 a 0|trans-title=Funes Mori scores in overtime and River beat Tigre for 1–0|url=http://www.futnet.com.br/futebolinternacional/futebolargentino/noticias/?108207|work=futnet.com.br|date=8 August 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131231120616/http://www.futnet.com.br/futebolinternacional/futebolargentino/noticias/?108207|archive-date=31 December 2013}}</ref> This was his third goal in three games against that team. In the third fixture, against [[Club Atletico Independiente|Independiente]], he scored two goals in the first half as River went on to win 3–2.<ref>{{cite web|title=River beat Independiente 3–2; Boca lose 2–0 against All Boys|url=http://www.buenosairesherald.com/BreakingNews/View/43037|work=www.buenosairesherald.com|date=23 August 2010}}</ref> The forward scored his fourth goal of the season in the 1–0 victory over [[Arsenal de Sarandí]].<ref>{{cite web|title=River Plate Coach Angel Cappa Laughs Off Criticism After Slim Victory Over Arsenal De Sarandi|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/585/argentina/2010/09/13/2117055/river-plate-coach-angel-cappa-laughs-off-criticism-after|work=goal.com|date=13 September 2010}}</ref>

===Benfica===
On 10 August 2013, Funes Mori joined [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]] on a five-year contract. [[Club Atlético River Plate|River Plate]] received €2&nbsp;million from an investment group who kept a share of its economic right's, with Benfica receiving half, plus the sports rights.<ref>{{cite web|title=Former Sueño MLS winner Rogelio Funes Mori continues his adventures through world soccer|url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/2013/08/10/former-sueno-mls-winner-rogelio-funes-mori-continues-his-adventures-through-|work=mlssoccer.com|date=10 August 2013}}</ref>

On 15 September, he made his debut for [[S.L. Benfica B|Benfica B]], scoring his first goal in a Benfica shirt. On 15 March, he scored four goals for the B side against [[Sporting Covilhã]] in a 4–0 win. On 9 July 2014, he was loaned to [[Eskişehirspor]] in [[Süper Lig|Turkey]] for one year, with the option to make the move permanent for a €3.5&nbsp;million fee.<ref>{{cite news|title=Funes Mori Eskişehirspor'da|url=http://www.eskisehirspor.org/haberler.asp?id=2066|work=eskisehirspor.org|date=9 July 2014|language=tr}}</ref>

===Monterrey===
On 12 June 2015, Funes Mori moved to [[Liga MX]] club [[C.F. Monterrey|Monterrey]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.rayados.com/noticias/8271/conoce_a_rogelio_funes_mori |title=Conoce a Rogelio Funes Mori |language=es |trans-title=Meet Rogelio Funes Mori |date=12 June 2015 |publisher=[[C.F. Monterrey]] |access-date=12 June 2015}}</ref> He made his debut in a 3–2 friendly win over Morelia, scoring in the 91st minute.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.record.xl.pt/multimedia/videos/interior.aspx?content_id=959691 |title=.: Um golaço de Funes Mori na estreia pelo Monterrey - Vídeos - Jornal Record :. |access-date=12 July 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150707100740/http://www.record.xl.pt/multimedia/videos/interior.aspx?content_id=959691 |archive-date=7 July 2015}}</ref> In his [[2015–16 Liga MX season#Torneo Apertura|debut tournament]] he finished fourth-joint top scorer of the league with 11 goals.

On 6 August 2016, he scored his first [[hat-trick]] against [[Club León|León]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Rogelio Funes Mori firmó un 'hat-trick' y Monterrey venció a un León sin garra|url=https://www.tudn.com/futbol/competencias-de-futbol/liga-mx/rogelio-funes-mori-firmo-un-hat-trick-y-monterrey-vencio-a-un-leon-sin-garra}}</ref>

In December 2017, he won the [[Apertura 2017 Copa MX|Copa MX]] after [[Apertura 2017 Copa MX Final|defeating Pachuca]] 1–0 in a home win.<ref>{{cite web|title=Monterrey wins Copa MX final thanks to Hurtado goal|url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/monterrey-wins-copa-mx-final-thanks-to-hurtado-goal/19dm4kqzyegiu1uk4yycxoujlw|work=Goal.com|first=Jon|last=Arnold|date=22 December 2017}}</ref>

He scored his 100th goal for the club on 18 December 2019 in the [[2019 FIFA Club World Cup|Club World Cup]] semi-final against [[Premiere League]] club [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]], making it 1–1 partial at the 14th minute but culminating in a 2–1 loss.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mundial de Clubes: Rogelio Funes Mori mete su gol 100 con Rayados ante Liverpool|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/mundial-de-clubes/rogelio-funes-mori-mete-gol-100-rayados-liverpool-video|work=Medio Tiempo|date=18 December 2019}}</ref> After their participation at the Club World Cup, Monterrey disputed the [[Apertura 2019 Liga MX championship stage|Apertura championship finals]] against [[Club América|América]]. In the second leg, he scored the tying aggregate goal in order to take the match into overtime; where the winner was determined via a [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shootout]] and Monterrey ultimately won 4–2.<ref>{{cite web|title=Monterrey defeat Club America on penalties to win Liga MX 2019 Apertura title|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/report?gameId=560779|work=ESPN|first=Tom|last=Marshall|date=30 December 2019}}</ref> He was included in the tournament's Best XI.<ref name="A2019">{{cite web|title=La Liga MX da a conocer el once ideal del Apertura 2019|url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/liga-mx/2020/01/04/5e0fcab746163fa2138b4589.html|work=Marca Claro México|date=3 January 2020|language=es}}</ref>

With Monterrey's victory of the [[2019–20 Copa MX]], they had obtained the [[Treble (association football)|continental treble]].<ref>{{cite web|title=¡TRI-PLE-TE! {{!}} Rayados consiguió triplete y ahora va por el campeonato del Guard1anes 2020|url=https://www.90min.com/es/posts/tri-ple-te-rayados-consiguio-triplete-y-ahora-va-por-el-campeonato-del-guard1anes-2020|work=90 Min|language=es|date=5 November 2020}}</ref>

==International career==
===Argentina===
Funes Mori was called up by [[Walter Perazzo]] to be part of the [[Argentina national under-20 football team|Argentina under-20]] squad that participated in the [[2011 South American Youth Championship]], scoring twice.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mundoalbiceleste.com/Article.aspx?id=2219|title=Squad for South American Youth Championship announced|date=4 January 2011|publisher=Mundo Albiceleste|access-date=13 November 2011|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120403091847/http://www.mundoalbiceleste.com/Article.aspx?id=2219|archive-date=3 April 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Gabriel Funes Mori – Leading Argentina's Under 20s From the Front|url=http://www.argentinafootballworld.com/features/63-funes-mori-sub-20.html|work=argentinafootballworld.com|date=30 January 2011|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110707161136/http://www.argentinafootballworld.com/features/63-funes-mori-sub-20.html|archive-date=7 July 2011}}</ref>

In 2012, Funes Mori made his senior debut for [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]] during a [[Exhibition game|friendly]] match in the [[Superclásico de las Américas]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Superclasico de las Americas 2012|url=http://www.football-lineups.com/match/142075/|work=football-lineups.com}}</ref>

===Mexico===
Early in 2019, Funes Mori inquired [[FIFA]] about the possibility to play for [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Rogelio Funes Mori acudiría a la FIFA para jugar con México|date=19 February 2019 |url=https://mexico.as.com/mexico/2019/02/19/futbol/1550613863_068413.html}}</ref> but was deemed ineligible.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rogelio Funes Mori no puede jugar con Selección Mexicana|date=22 January 2019 |url=https://mexico.as.com/mexico/2019/01/23/futbol/1548207465_198506.html}}</ref> In 2020, following a [[List of association footballers who have been capped for two senior national teams|change of rules]] regarding naturalized players by FIFA, he was deemed eligible to play for Mexico and restated his interest.<ref>{{cite web|title=Funes Mori ya podrá jugar para México por cambio en Reglas|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/seleccion-mayor/funes-mori-podra-jugar-seleccion-mexicana-cambio-fifa}}</ref> On 14 June 2021, Funes Mori was granted his [[Mexican nationality law|Mexican citizenship]] making him eligible to play for Mexico.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fmfstateofmind.com/2021/6/18/22539447/rogelio-funes-mori-becomes-eligible-for-the-mexico-national-team |title=Rogelio Funes Mori becomes eligible for the Mexican National Team |date=18 June 2021 |publisher=FMF State of Mind |access-date=18 June 2021}}</ref>

On 3 July 2021, he earned his first cap with the [[Mexico national football team|senior national team]] under manager [[Gerardo Martino]] in a friendly match against [[Nigeria national football team|Nigeria]], scoring on his debut in the team's 4–0 victory.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rogelio Funes Mori scores on Mexico debut|date=4 July 2021 |url=https://en.as.com/en/2021/07/04/soccer/1625369991_693382.html}}</ref>

Funes Mori participated at the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2021 Gold Cup]],<ref>{{cite web|title=México va a la Copa Oro sin Chicharito Hernández y con Funes Mori|date=July 2021 |url=https://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/selecciones/nota/_/id/8853729/mexico-convocatoria-copa-oro-funes-mori-incluido-chicharito-ausente}}</ref> scoring a total of 3 times.<ref>{{cite web|title=CONCACAF Gold Cup Scoring Stats - 2021-22|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/stats/_/league/concacaf.gold}}</ref> Mexico finished runner-up after losing the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup Final|final]] to the United States 0–1.<ref>{{cite web|title=¡Dolor de cabeza del Tata! México pierde Final de Copa Oro ante equipo B de EE.UU.|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/copa-oro/unidos-vs-mexico-1-0-ee-uu-campeon-copa-oro}}</ref>

In October 2022, Funes Mori was named in Mexico's preliminary 31-man [[Mexico national football team|squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], and in November, he was ultimately included in the final 26-man roster.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lista de convocados de la Selección Mexicana al Mundial de Qatar 2022|url=https://www.tvazteca.com/aztecadeportes/lista-oficial-convocados-seleccion-mexicana-mundial-qatar-2022|work=Tvazteca.com|first=Omar|last=Moro|date=14 November 2022|language=Spanish}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Funes Mori is married to Jorgelina who is the sister of former FC Dallas midfielder [[Mauro Díaz]].<ref>{{cite web|title=¿Quién es Jorgelina Díaz, la esposa de Rogelio Funes Mori|url=https://www.elfutbolero.com.mx/ligamx/Rogelio-Funes-Mori-Quien-es-Jorgelina-Diaz-la-esposa-del-goleador-de-Monterrey-20210909-0031.html}}</ref> His [[Twin|twin brother]], [[Ramiro Funes Mori|Ramiro]], is also a footballer and plays for [[Liga MX]] club [[Cruz Azul]] as a defender and played for the [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]] national team at two [[Copa América]] tournaments.
