'''Joseff John Morrell''' (born 3 January 1997) is a professional footballer who plays as a [[Midfielder#Central midfielder|central-midfielder]] for {{English football updater|Portsmou}} club [[Portsmouth F.C.|Portsmouth]] and the [[Wales national football team|Wales national team]].

==Club career==
Morrell began his career with [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]] whilst attending The Castle School in Thornbury, and signed a professional contract with the club in December 2012 after turning down an offer from [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]].<ref>{{cite web|title=City see off the pull of Liverpool as Joe Morrell stays in Bristol|url=http://www.thisisbristol.co.uk/City-pull-Liverpool-Joe-Morrell-stays-Bristol/story-17493554-detail/story.html#axzz2hRMgj7XB|work=This is Bristol|access-date=11 October 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130528021832/http://www.thisisbristol.co.uk/City-pull-Liverpool-Joe-Morrell-stays-Bristol/story-17493554-detail/story.html#axzz2hRMgj7XB|archive-date=28 May 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Former Liverpool target and Wales under-17 captain Joe Morrell looks forward to starting professional career at Bristol City after leaving The Castle School|url=http://www.gazetteseries.co.uk/sport/10436479.Football__Talented_Morrell_looking_to_progress_at_Bristol_City/|work=Gazette Series|access-date=11 October 2013}}</ref> He made his debut on 8 October 2013 in a 2‚Äì1 [[Football League Trophy]] defeat against [[Wycombe Wanderers F.C.|Wycombe Wanderers]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Full-time: Wycombe Wanderers 2 Bristol City 1|url=http://www.thisisbristol.co.uk/time-Wycombe-Wanderers-2-Bristol-City-1/story-19903535-detail/story.html#axzz2hRMgj7XB|work=This is Bristol|access-date=11 October 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131014200133/http://www.thisisbristol.co.uk/time-Wycombe-Wanderers-2-Bristol-City-1/story-19903535-detail/story.html#axzz2hRMgj7XB|archive-date=14 October 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In August 2016 it was announced that Morrell would join [[National League (division)|National League]] club [[Sutton United F.C.|Sutton United]], initially on a one-month loan.<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/36987097 | title=Joe Morrell: Sutton United sign Bristol City midfielder on loan|publisher=BBC Sport| date=5 August 2016}}</ref> He made his first appearance for the U's as a substitute coming on at 61' in a 3‚Äì1 victory over [[Lincoln City F.C.|Lincoln City]] at [[Sincil Bank]] on 13 August 2016, Sutton's first win in English football's fifth tier in 16 years.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/36994658|title=Lincoln City 1‚Äì3 Sutton United|work=BBC Sport}}</ref> One reporter described Morrell as having "had a great competitive debut overall".<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.suttonguardian.co.uk/sport/14683073.A_Sutton_United_front__Great_away_day_at_Lincoln_City__and_I_am_sure_there_many_more_to_come/|title=A Sutton United front: Great away day at Lincoln City|date=15 August 2016|access-date=2 September 2016|publisher=[[Sutton Guardian]]}}</ref> He made two further substitute appearances in home victories against [[Torquay United F.C.|Torquay United]] and [[Macclesfield Town F.C.|Macclesfield Town]] before returning to his parent club in early September 2016.

On 24 February 2017, Morrell joined [[Margate F.C.|Margate]] on loan for a month. He made his debut as a 58th minute substitute during a 2‚Äì0 home loss to [[Whitehawk F.C.|Whitehawk]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thenationalleague.org.uk/matchinfo.php?fixture_id=28280&hometeam_id=1702&awayteam=1669|title=The National League Official Website ‚Äì Match Info|website=www.thenationalleague.org.uk|access-date=26 February 2017|archive-date=27 February 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170227160021/http://www.thenationalleague.org.uk/matchinfo.php?fixture_id=28280&hometeam_id=1702&awayteam=1669|url-status=dead}}</ref>

=== Cheltenham Town (loan) ===

On 30 August 2017, Morrell signed for [[Cheltenham Town F.C.|Cheltenham Town]] on loan until January 2018.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41095749 |title=Bristol City's Joe Morrell, Taylor Moore and Freddie Hinds join Cheltenham on loan |publisher=BBC Sport |date=30 August 2017 |access-date=30 August 2017 }}</ref> His first goal for the club came in a 3‚Äì0 win against [[Mansfield Town F.C.|Mansfield Town]] and scored three times in total for the club.<ref>[https://www.soccerbase.com/players/player.sd?player_id=73077&season_id=150 Morrell goals 2017/18]</ref>

=== Lincoln City (loan) ===
On 27 June 2019, Morrell signed for [[Lincoln City F.C.|Lincoln City]] on a season-long loan until May 2020.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.redimps.co.uk/news/2019/june/190627-morrell2/ |publisher=Lincoln City Football Club |date=27 June 2019 |access-date=27 June 2019 |title=Morrell becomes City's first summer signing }}</ref> He scored his first goal in a dominant 5‚Äì1 win against [[Bolton Wanderers]] on 14 January 2020, however it was later given as an own-goal for [[Josh Emmanuel]].<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/51007744 Morrell first Lincoln goal]</ref>

On 7 March 2020, Morrell played his final game for the Imps against [[Burton Albion F.C.|Burton Albion]], due to the season being cancelled early because of [[COVID-19]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.bbc.com/sport/amp/football/51685212|title=Lincoln City 3‚Äì2 Burton Albion|work=BBC Sport|date=7 March 2020}}</ref>

In June 2020, Morrell was named Lincoln City's Player of the Season.<ref>{{cite tweet|number=1276885585970507778|user=LincolnCity_FC|title=Our final award of the day is the Lincoln and District Player of the Year üëè Brought to you by SRP Hire Solutions,‚Ä¶<!-- full text of tweet that Twitter returned to the bot (excluding links) added by TweetCiteBot. This may be better truncated or may need expanding (TW limits responses to 140 characters) or case changes. --> |date=27 June 2020}}</ref>

=== Return to Bristol City ===
In November 2019 he signed a new contract with Bristol City.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/50497009|title=Joe Morrell: Wales midfielder extends Bristol City contract while still out on loan|date=20 November 2019|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=6 December 2019}}</ref>

=== Luton Town ===
On 15 October 2020 Morrell joined Luton Town for an undisclosed fee.<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54560399 Morrell joins Luton]</ref>

=== Portsmouth ===

On 9 August 2021, Morrell joined [[EFL League One]] Portsmouth for an undisclosed fee on a 3-year contract with a 12-month extension clause.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58156841|title=Joe Morrell: Portsmouth sign Wales midfielder from Luton Town|date=10 August 2021|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=10 August 2021}}</ref> On the 21st January 2023, Morell scored his first goal for Portsmouth against Exeter City after 49 games and 13 league appearances in the 2022/23 season <ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.portsmouthfc.co.uk/news/2023/january/morrell-scabby-goal-but-we-earned-it/ | title=Morrell: Scabby Goal, but We Earned It }}</ref>

==International career==
Morrell qualifies to play for [[Wales national football team|Wales]] through his Welsh mother Sian.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lincolnshirelive.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/this-just-first-step-joe-3252374|title=Morrell determined to make his mark after being called up by Wales|first=Mark|last=Whiley|date=26 August 2019|website=lincolnshirelive}}</ref>

In October 2017, Morrell made his [[Wales national under-21 football team|Wales U21]] debut in a 3‚Äì1 victory against [[Liechtenstein national under-21 football team|Liechtenstein]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.uefa.com/under21/season=2019/matches/round=2000803/match=2022479/index.html |title=Liectenstein U21 3‚Äì2 Wales U21 |publisher=UEFA |access-date=5 October 2017}}</ref>

In August 2019, Morrell was named in the [[Wales national football team|Wales]] squad ahead of their Euro 2020 qualifier against Azerbaijan.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49404349 |title=Giggs names Wales squad for September internationals |work=BBC Sport |access-date=20 August 2019}}</ref> He made his senior international debut for [[Wales national football team|Wales]] against Belarus on 9 September 2019.

In May 2021 he was selected for the Wales squad for the delayed [[UEFA Euro 2020]] tournament.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57301006|title=Uncapped Colwill in Wales Euro 2020 squad|work=BBC Sport }}</ref> In November 2022 he was named in the Wales squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63545011 2022 Wales World Cup squad]</ref> Morrell made his [[FIFA World Cup|World cup]] debut coming on as a late substitute against the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]] in Wales' opening [[2022 FIFA World Cup Group B]] game, Morrell also came on as a substitute in the group stage game against England.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Morrell Features For Wales In World Cup Opener |url=https://www.portsmouthfc.co.uk/news/2022/november/morrell-features-for-wales-in-world-cup-opener/ |access-date=2022-11-26 |website=www.portsmouthfc.co.uk |language=en-gb}}</ref>
