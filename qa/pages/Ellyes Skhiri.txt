'''Ellyes Joris Skhiri''' ({{lang-ar|إلياس صخيري}}; born 10 May 1995) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who last played as a [[defensive midfielder]] for [[Bundesliga]] club [[Eintracht Frankfurt]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.kicker.de/nach-vier-jahren-skhiri-verlaesst-koeln-954219/artikel |title=Nach vier Jahren: Skhiri verlässt Köln |access-date=2023-07-01 |website=kicker |language=de}}</ref> A dual French-Tunisian national, he plays for the [[Tunisia national football team|Tunisia national team]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://int.soccerway.com/players/ellyes-skhiri/280199/ |title=France – E. Skhiri – Soccerway – Results, fixtures, tables and statistics |publisher=Soccerway |access-date=10 August 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://fc.de/fc-info/news/detailseite/details/fc-verpflichtet-ellyes-skhiri/ |title=FC VERPFLICHTET ELLYES SKHIRI |publisher=1. FC Köln |date=29 July 2019|access-date=29 July 2019}}</ref>

==Club career==

===Montpellier===
Skhiri is a youth product of [[Montpellier HSC]]. He made his [[Ligue 1]] debut on 21 March 2015 against [[Evian Thonon Gaillard F.C.|Evian Thonon Gaillard]] replacing [[Paul Lasne]] in extra time in a 1–0 away defeat.<ref name="soccerway2">{{cite web |url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2015/03/21/france/ligue-1/football-croix-de-savoie-74/montpellier-herault-sport-club/1687283/ |title=Evian TG vs. Montpellier – 21 March 2015 – Soccerway |publisher=soccerway.com|access-date=23 May 2015}}</ref>

===1. FC Köln===
On 29 July 2019, Skhiri joined [[1. FC Köln]] on a four-year deal.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fc.de/fc-info/news/detailseite/details/fc-verpflichtet-ellyes-skhiri/ |title=FC verpflichtet Ellyes Skhiri |publisher=1. FC Köln |language=de |date=29 July 2019|access-date=29 July 2019}}</ref> On 28 November 2020, he scored a brace in a 2–1 away win over [[Borussia Dortmund]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/ellyes-skhiri-gives-cologne-shot-in-arm-with-borussia-dortmund-win-first-in-18-13713 |title=Ellyes Skhiri gives Cologne a much-needed shot in the arm with first win since March |website=bundesliga.com |date=28 November 2020}}</ref>

==International career==
Skhiri was born and raised in France to Tunisian father and French mother. He was called up to the [[Tunisia national under-23 football team|Tunisia Olympic team]] several times.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ftf.org.tn/fr/selection-nationale-olympique-maher-kanzari-convoque-29-joueurs/ |title=Sélection Nationale Olympique : Maher Kanzari convoque 29 Joueurs – Fédération Tunisienne de Football |website=www.ftf.org.tn}}</ref> Skhiri made his debut for the Tunisia Olympic team in a friendly against the [[Morocco national under-23 football team|Morocco U23 squad]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.vavel.com/fr/football/montpellier/402822-la-semaine-des-internationaux-pailladins.html |title=La semaine des internationaux Pailladins |last=Vavel |date=13 October 2014}}</ref>

Skhiri made his senior debut for the [[Tunisia national football team]] in a friendly 1–0 win over [[Iran national football team|Iran]] on 23 March 2018.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.skysports.com/football/tunisia-vs-iran/388207 |title=Internationals (Sky Sports) |website=SkySports}}</ref>

In June 2018, he was named in Tunisia's 23-man squad for the [[2018 World Cup]] in [[Russia]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Okeleji |first1=Oluwashina |title=Tunisia World Cup squad: Leicester City's Benalouane in 23-man squad |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44342860 |access-date=10 June 2018 |work=BBC Sport |date=2 June 2018}}</ref> He was part of the national team which reached the semi-finals of the [[2019 Africa Cup of Nations]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/ellyes-skhiri-who-is-cologne-s-tunisian-running-man-midfielder-18416 |title=Ellyes Skhiri: Who is Cologne's Tunisian running man? |work=Bundesliga |date=29 November 2022 }}</ref> In November 2022, he was called up for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/fifaplus/en/articles/world-cup-qatar-2022-tunisia-squad-announcement |title=Khazri and Hannibal headline Tunisia squad announcement |publisher=FIFA |date=14 November 2022 }}</ref>
