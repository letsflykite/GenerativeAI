'''Edimilson Fernandes Ribeiro''' (born 15 April 1996) is a Swiss professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as an [[attacking midfielder]] for German club [[1. FSV Mainz 05|Mainz 05]] and the [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland national team]].<ref name="soccerway">{{Soccerway|edimilson-fernandes/298173}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Sion===
Fernandes is a youth exponent from [[FC Sion]]. He made his [[Swiss Super League]] debut on 1 June 2013 against [[FC Zürich]] in 4–2 home win. He started in the first eleven and was substituted after 53 minutes.<ref name="soccerway2">{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2013/06/01/switzerland/super-league/fc-sion/fc-zurich/1293283/|title=FC Sion vs. FC Zürich - 1 June 2013 - Soccerway|website=soccerway.com|publisher=Soccerway|access-date=29 August 2015}}</ref> He scored his first senior goal on 1 March 2015 in a 2–2 draw at home to [[FC Luzern]].<ref name=10fast/> Fernandes played every minute of Sion's [[2015–16 UEFA Europa League|2015–16 Europa League]] campaign before the side was knocked out by [[S.C. Braga|Braga]] in the [[2015–16 UEFA Europa League knockout phase#Braga v Sion|last 32]].<ref name=10fast/> In all games, he played 65 times for Sion's first team and 41 games for the club's under-21 side.<ref name=10fast/>

===West Ham United===
On 25 August 2016, Fernandes signed a four-year deal with English club [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]] for a fee of £5 million.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.whufc.com/News/Articles/2016/August/August-25/Hammers-secure-Swiss-star-Fernandes|title=Hammers secure Swiss star Fernandes|date=25 August 2016|website=whufc.com|publisher=West Ham United F.C.|access-date=25 August 2016|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160826094825/http://www.whufc.com/News/Articles/2016/August/August-25/Hammers-secure-Swiss-star-Fernandes|archive-date=26 August 2016}}</ref> He made his West Ham debut on 25 September 2016 in a 3–0 home defeat by [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]], coming on as an 82nd-minute substitute for [[Mark Noble]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/09/25/england/premier-league/west-ham-united-fc/southampton-fc/2241818/ | title=West Ham 0-3 Southampton | website=soccerway.com | publisher=Soccerway | access-date=28 October 2016}}</ref> On 26 October 2016, Fernandes scored his first goal for West Ham in a 2–1 win against [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] in the [[EFL Cup]], winning praise from manager [[Slaven Bilić]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://bbc.co.uk/sport/football/37700008|title=West Ham United 2-1 Chelsea|website=BBC Sport|publisher=British Broadcasting Corporation|date=26 October 2016|access-date=27 October 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.hitc.com/en-gb/2016/10/27/slaven-bilic-admits-west-hams-edimilson-fernandes-has-surprised/ | title=Slaven Bilic admits West Ham's Edimilson Fernandes has surprised him this season | website=hitc.com | date=27 October 2016 | access-date=28 October 2016}}</ref>

====Loan to Fiorentina====
On 13 August 2018, Fernandes joined Italian side [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]] on a one-year loan deal with an option to buy.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/west-ham-youngster-edimilson-fernandes-joins-fiorentina-on-loan-with-option-to-buy-a3910121.html | title = West Ham's Fernandes joins Fiorentina on loan with purchase option | first = James | last = Benge | work = London Evening Standard | date = 13 August 2018 | access-date = 13 August 2018}}</ref>

===Mainz 05===
On 3 June 2019, Fernandes joined [[Bundesliga]] side [[1. FSV Mainz 05|Mainz 05]] on a four-year deal for an undisclosed fee.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.whufc.com/news/articles/2019/june/03-june/edimilson-fernandes-joins-german-club-mainz|title=Edimilson Fernandes joins German club Mainz|publisher=[[West Ham United F.C.]]|date=3 June 2019|access-date=16 May 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mainz05.de/en/news/edimilson-fernandes-joins-mainz-05/|title=Edimilson Fernandes joins Mainz 05|publisher=[[1. FSV Mainz 05]]|date=3 June 2019|access-date=16 May 2021}}</ref> On 17 August 2019, he made his full debut for the club in the league season opener against [[SC Freiburg]], starting and playing 84 minutes of a 3–0 loss while picking up a first-half yellow card.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dfb.de/bundesliga/spieltagtabelle/?spieledb_path=/competitions/12/seasons/current/matchday/1&spieledb_path=%2Fmatches%2F2279224|title=SC Freiburg v. 1. FSV Mainz 05|publisher=[[German Football Association|DFB]]|date=17 August 2019|access-date=16 May 2021}}</ref>

Fernandes scored his first goal for Mainz in the penultimate [[2019–20 Bundesliga|Bundesliga]] game of the [[2019–20 1. FSV Mainz 05 season|2019–2020 season]]. After being substituted on in the second half, he scored the third goal in Mainz's 3–1 win over [[SV Werder Bremen|Werder Bremen]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/mainz-werder-bremen-live-line-ups-stats-rashica-sargent-sahin-quaison-awoniyi-11646|title=Werder Bremen move closer to relegation as Mainz secure survival|publisher=[[Bundesliga]]|date=20 June 2020|access-date=16 May 2021}}</ref>

====Loan to Young Boys====
On 15 February 2022, Fernandes moved on loan to [[BSC Young Boys|Young Boys]] until the end of the season.<ref>{{cite press release|publisher=[[BSC Young Boys|Young Boys]]|url=https://www.bscyb.ch/news?nID=12915|title=EDIMILSON FERNANDES BIS SAISONENDE ZU YB|date=15 February 2022|access-date=15 February 2022|language=de}}</ref>

==International career==
Fernandes made his debut for the [[Switzerland national under-21 football team|Switzerland under-21 team]] in March 2016 in a 1–1 draw with [[England national under-21 football team|England]]. In his second game, on 2 September 2016, he scored his first international goal in a 3–0 win against [[Kazakhstan national under-21 football team|Kazakhstan]] in [[Biel/Bienne]].<ref name="Sway">{{cite web | url=https://int.soccerway.com/players/edimilson-fernandes/298173/ | title=E Fernandes | publisher=Soccerway | access-date=3 September 2016}}</ref><ref name="u21goal">{{cite web | url=http://www.whufc.com/News/Articles/2016/September/2-September/U21-pair-taste-victory | title=U21 pair taste victory | publisher=www.whufc.com | access-date=3 September 2016 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160907202921/http://www.whufc.com/News/Articles/2016/September/2-September/U21-pair-taste-victory | archive-date=7 September 2016 | url-status=dead }}</ref>

In November 2016, Fernandes received his first call-up to the senior [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]] squad for a match against the [[Faroe Islands national football team|Faroe Islands]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.lematin.ch/sports/football/lacroix-edimilson-fernandes/story/29089329 |title=Football Avec Lacroix et Edimilson Fernandes |publisher=lematin.ch |date=4 November 2016 |access-date=7 November 2016}}</ref>

He made his senior debut against them, coming off the bench in the 69th minute.

In May 2019, he played in [[2019 UEFA Nations League Finals]], where his team finished fourth.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/48191915|title=Pickford the hero in England shootout win|work=BBC Sport}}</ref>

In 2021, he was called up to the national team for the [[UEFA European Championship|2020 UEFA European Championship]], where the team created one of the main sensations of the tournament reaching the quarter-finals.<ref>https://www.uefa.com / uefaeuro-2020 / match / 2024485 - switzerland-vs-spain / lineups /? iv = true</ref>

==Personal life==
Fernandes was born in [[Sion, Switzerland|Sion]], Switzerland, to a family of Portuguese and Cape Verdean descent.<ref name="10fast">{{cite web | url=http://www.whufc.com/News/Articles/2016/August/26-August/Ten-Fast-Facts-Edimilson-Fernandes | title=Ten Fast Facts&nbsp;— Edimilson Fernandes | publisher=www.whufc.com | access-date=27 August 2016}}</ref> He is the cousin of Swiss international footballer [[Gelson Fernandes]], Portuguese international footballer [[Manuel Fernandes (footballer, born 1986)|Manuel Fernandes]] and ex-[[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]] midfielder [[Cabral (footballer)|Cabral]].<ref name=10fast/>
