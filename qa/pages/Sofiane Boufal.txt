'''Sofiane Boufal''' ({{lang-ar|سفيان بوفال}}; born 17 September 1993) is a professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[winger (association football)|winger]] or [[attacking midfielder]] for [[Qatar Stars League]] club [[Al-Rayyan SC|Al-Rayyan]]. Born in France, he plays for the [[Morocco national football team|Morocco national team]].

==Club career==
===Angers===
Boufal came through the [[Angers SCO|Angers]] youth system, making his debut for the club at the age of 18, replacing [[Rayan Frikeche]] in the closing stages of a 1–0 home loss to [[FC Istres|Istres]] in August 2012. He made his first ever professional start the next [[Ligue 2]] season, in a 4–2 win away at Istres, becoming a regular that season, making 31 appearances in all competitions. In the 2014–15 season, he helped Angers win promotion to [[Ligue 1]]. He racked up a total of 4 goals in 16 appearances in the first half of the season, before attracting interest from Ligue 1 clubs.{{citation needed|date=August 2019}}

===Lille===
On 9 January 2015, Boufal transferred to [[Lille OSC|Lille]] in Ligue 1, instantly making an impression, scoring 3 goals in 14 league games.<ref>{{Soccerbase season|80669|2014|access-date=7 July 2016}}</ref> The next season, he continued to impress, scoring 12 goals in 35 appearances in all competitions and attracted interest from some of the biggest clubs around the world.<ref>{{Soccerbase season|80669|2015|access-date=7 July 2016}}</ref>

===Southampton===
On 29 August 2016, [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] announced they had signed Boufal on a five-year contract for a club-record undisclosed fee.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.saintsfc.co.uk/news/article/2016-17/20160829-sofiane-boufal-announcement-southampton-lille-3278679.aspx|title= Sofiane Boufal joins Southampton from Lille on five-year contract|date=29 August 2016| publisher=Southampton F.C.|access-date=29 August 2016}}</ref> The fee was reported as [[Pound sterling|£]]16 million, slightly more than the £15 million paid to [[A.S. Roma|Roma]] for [[Dani Osvaldo]] in October 2013.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Thomas|first1=Lall|title=Southampton confirm signing of Sofiane Boufal - Sky sources believe it to be for club record £16m| url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/11700/10556970/southampton-are-closing-in-on-a-deal-for-sofiane-boufalS|access-date=29 August 2016|publisher=Sky Sports|date=29 August 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Sofiane Boufal: Southampton break transfer record to sign midfielder from Lille|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/37184192|access-date=29 August 2016|publisher=BBC Sport|date=29 August 2016}}</ref>

In the 2016–17 season, Boufal scored only one [[EFL Cup|League Cup]] goal and one [[Premier League]] goal; his strike in Saints' 26 October 2016 1–0 win over [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]] in the fourth round of the League Cup won Southampton's Goal of the Season award.<ref>{{cite web| title=Southampton 1–0 Sunderland  |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/37700028|access-date=21 October 2017|publisher=BBC Sport|date=26 October 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Boufal wins Goal of the Season|url=https://southamptonfc.com/news/2017-05-06/video-sofiane-boufal-collects-goal-of-the-season-award|access-date=21 October 2017 |publisher=Southampton F.C.|date=6 May 2017}}</ref>

On 21 October 2017, Boufal scored a virtuoso 85th-minute goal<ref>{{cite news|first=Ben | last= Fisher| title=Sofiane Boufal wonder goal gives Southampton win against West Brom| url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2017/oct/21/southampton-west-bromwich-albion-premier-league-match-report |access-date=21 October 2017|work=The Guardian |date=21 October 2017}}</ref> in a home tie against [[West Bromwich Albion F.C.|West Bromwich Albion]] to lead his team to a 1–0 victory and send them into the top half of the Premier League. Having come on as a substitute in the 80th minute, he gained possession deep within his own half and took the ball past six opposing players before sending it past keeper [[Ben Foster (footballer)|Ben Foster]] from just inside the box.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Reddy| first1=Luke| title= Southampton 1–0 West Bromwich Albion |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41618804|publisher=BBC Sport |access-date=22 October 2017|date=21 October 2017}}</ref> This goal was voted the 2017–18 Premier League Goal of the Season.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.premierleague.com/news/691852 |title=Boufal wins Carling Goal of the Season |publisher=Premier League |date=19 May 2018 |access-date=19 May 2018}}</ref>

In July 2018, Boufal joined [[La Liga]] side Celta Vigo on loan for the 2018–19 campaign.<ref>{{cite web|title=Boufal set to join Celta Vigo on loan|url=https://southamptonfc.com/news/2018-07-20/sofiane-boufal-southampton-celta-vigo-loan-announcement|access-date=20 July 2018|publisher=BBC Sport|date=20 July 2018}}</ref>

After returning to Southampton for the 2019–20 season, he played in 10 of his team's 13 league games before injuring his toe at his home, when he ran into a kitchen table.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/50592211|title=Sofiane Boufal: Southampton midfielder injures toe running into kitchen table|date=28 November 2019| access-date=30 November 2019 |publisher= BBC Sport}}</ref>

===Return to Angers===
On 5 October 2020, Boufal returned to his first club [[Angers SCO|Angers]] on a free transfer.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.southamptonfc.com/news/2020-10-05/announcement-sofiane-boufal-transfer-southampton-football-club-angers|title=Boufal completes Angers move|website=www.southamptonfc.com|date=5 October 2020}}</ref>

=== Al-Rayyan ===
On 31 January 2023, Boufal joined Qatari club [[Al-Rayyan SC]] on a three-and-a-half-year contract.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Morocco star Boufal joins Al-Rayyan |url=https://www.besoccer.com/new/morocco-star-boufal-joins-al-rayyan-1224995 |access-date=2023-01-31 |website=www.besoccer.com |language=en}}</ref> Boufal made his debut against [[Al-Gharafa SC]] and scored his first goal for the club against [[Al-Duhail SC]].{{citation needed|date=May 2023}}
==International career==
Boufal made his debut for the [[Morocco national football team|Morocco national team]] on 26 March 2016 starting in a 1–0 [[2017 Africa Cup of Nations qualification|African Cup of Nations qualification]] victory against [[Cape Verde national football team|Cape Verde]].<ref name="soccerway2">{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/03/26/africa/africa-cup-of-nations-qualification/cape-verde-islands/morocco/2038224/|title=Cape Verde Islands vs. Morocco - 25 March 2016 |publisher=Soccerway|access-date=29 March 2016}}</ref>

Boufal withdrew from Morocco's squad for the [[2017 Africa Cup of Nations]] due to injury.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/38552806|title=Afcon 2017: Sofiane Boufal of Southampton misses out for Morocco |work=BBC Sport |access-date=19 April 2020}}</ref> He was also unexpectedly omitted from the final squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] by coach [[Hervé Renard]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/morocco/story/3501911/sofiane-boufal-overlooked-for-morocco-world-cup-squad|title=Sofiane Boufal overlooked for Morocco World Cup squad|date=17 May 2018|publisher=ESPN|access-date=19 April 2020}}</ref>

Boufal represented Morocco at the [[2019 Africa Cup of Nations]]. He was one of two Moroccan players to fail to score in their 4–1 penalty shootout defeat to [[Benin national football team|Benin]] in the Round of 16.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/48795602|title=Africa Cup of Nations: Morocco 1-1 Benin (Benin win 4-1 on pens)|work=BBC Sport|access-date=19 April 2020}}</ref>

On 10 November 2022, he was named in Morocco's 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Morocco World Cup 2022 squad: Who's in and who's out? {{!}} Goal.com |url=https://www.goal.com/en-bh/lists/morocco-2022-world-cup-squad-hakimi-ziyech-mazraoui-qatar/blta36257fc4feeb66a |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=www.goal.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=10 November 2022 |title=Moroccan coach unveils list of 26 Atlas Lions in 2022 World Cup |url=https://en.hespress.com/52697-moroccan-coach-unveils-list-of-26-atlas-lions-in-2022-world-cup.html |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=HESPRESS English - Morocco News |language=en-US}}</ref>
