'''Jamal Musiala''' (born 26 February 2003) is a German professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as an [[Midfielder#Attacking midfielder|attacking midfielder]] or [[Winger (association football)|winger]] for {{German football updater|BaMunich}} club [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] and the [[Germany national football team|Germany national team]]. Nicknamed "[[Bambi (character)|Bambi]]", Musiala is known for his world-class dribbling ability and is often considered one of the best young attackers in the world.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Veth |first=Manuel |title=Jamal Musiala: Is The Bayern Munich Star The Best In The Bundesliga? |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/manuelveth/2022/10/22/jamal-musiala-is-the-bayern-munich-star-the-best-in-the-bundesliga/ |access-date=2023-02-19 |website=Forbes |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Jamal Musiala: Who is the Bayern Munich and Germany teen sensation? |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/who-is-jamal-musiala-bayern-munich-s-half-england-germany-star-of-the-future-11525 |access-date=2023-02-19 |website=bundesliga.com - the official Bundesliga website |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title="Jude Bellingham and Jamal Musiala the standout players in 2022" - Philipp Lahm |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/jude-bellingham-jamal-musiala-best-players-in-2022-philipp-lahm-interview-22102 |access-date=2023-02-19 |website=bundesliga.com - the official Bundesliga website |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Telford |first=Stuart |date=2022-12-01 |title=Jamal Musiala, the shy 'Bambi' who became the world's best teenager |url=https://www.footballtransfers.com/us/transfer-news/de-bundesliga/2022/12/jamal-musiala-shy-bambi-world-s-best-teenager-us |access-date=2023-02-19 |website=www.footballtransfers.com |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Germany's own Messi? Born in Germany to a Nigerian father and German mother, he was raised from the age of seven in England. Musiala represented both Germany and England at youth level, and eventually pledged his allegiance to the Germany national team for future games in February 2021, representing the side at [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref name="right21" /><ref name="feels21" />

==Club career==
===2019–20: Move to Bayern Munich and debut===
In July 2019, aged 16, Musiala left [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] to join [[Bundesliga]] club [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]].<ref name="bundesliga">{{cite web | url = https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/bayern-munich-sign-chelsea-teenagers-jamal-musiala-bright-arrey-mbi-england-germany-5437 | title = Bayern Munich sign 16-year-old Chelsea talents Jamal Musiala and Bright Arrey-Mbi |publisher=Bundesliga | date = 2 July 2019 | access-date = 5 June 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Kollmann|first=Steffen|date=29 February 2020|title=Der Ex-Lehnerzer Jamal Musiala startet beim FC Bayern München durch|url=https://www.fuldaerzeitung.de/fulda/ex-lehnerzer-jamal-musiala-startet-beim-bayern-muenchen-durch-13656624.html|access-date=13 December 2020|language=de|newspaper=Fuldaer Zeitung}}</ref> On 3 June 2020, Musiala made his professional debut, coming on as a substitute for [[FC Bayern Munich II|Bayern Munich II]] in their 3–2 win over [[SC Preußen Münster|Preußen Münster]] in the [[3. Liga]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://uk.soccerway.com/matches/2020/06/03/germany/3-liga/fc-bayern-munchen-ii/sc-preussen-06-ev-munster/3053420/ |title=Bayern München II vs. Preußen Münster 3–2: Summary |website=Soccerway |publisher=Perform Group |access-date=25 February 2021}}</ref> On 20 June 2020, he made his Bundesliga debut against [[SC Freiburg]], and he became the youngest player to play a match for Bayern in the Bundesliga, aged 17 years and 115 days.<ref>{{Cite web|date=20 June 2020|title=Jamal Musiala: Jüngster Bundesliga-Debütant des FC Bayern|url=https://fcbayern.com/de/news/2020/06/jamal-musiala-juengster-bundesliga-debuetant-des-fc-bayern|access-date=8 August 2020|publisher=FC Bayern Munich|language=de}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=21 June 2020|title=Fünf Bayern-Talente feierten diese Saison ihr Bundesliga-Debüt|url=https://fcbayern.com/de/news/2020/06/fuenf-bayern-talente-feierten-diese-saison-ihr-bundesliga-debuet|access-date=8 August 2020|publisher=FC Bayern Munich|language=de}}</ref>
Musiala was a part of the squad that won the [[2019–20 UEFA Champions League]], although he did not make any appearances in the tournament.<ref>{{cite news | url = https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/53867676 | title = Paris St-Germain 0-1 Bayern Munich: German Side win Champions League | work = BBC Sport | access-date = 13 September 2022}}</ref>

===2020–21: First-team breakthrough===
On 18 September 2020, Musiala scored his first Bundesliga goal in an 8–0 win over [[FC Schalke 04|Schalke]], to become Bayern's youngest goalscorer, aged 17 years 205 days, breaking the previous record of [[Roque Santa Cruz]], aged 18 years 12 days.<ref name="whois" /><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/54213163 |title=Bayern Munich 8–0 FC Schalke 04 |website=BBC Sport |date=18 September 2020 | access-date = 18 September 2020}}</ref> On 3 November, Musiala made his [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] debut as a substitute to [[Thomas Müller]] in a 6–2 away win over [[FC Red Bull Salzburg|Red Bull Salzburg]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fcbayern.com/en/news/matchreports/2020/11/match-report-champions-league-red-bull-salzburg-v-fc-bayern-031120 |title=Resolute Reds down battling Salzburg |publisher=FC Bayern Munich |date=3 November 2020 }}</ref> On 1 December, he started his first Champions League match in a 1–1 away draw against [[Atlético Madrid]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2020/dec/02/jamal-musiala-shines-for-bayern-munich-champions-league-atletico-madrid |title=Jamal Musiala shines for Bayern with flashing feet, touch and tackles |newspaper=The Guardian |date=2 December 2020 }}</ref> On 23 February 2021, Musiala scored his first Champions League goal in a 4–1 away win over [[S.S. Lazio|Lazio]] in the first leg of the [[2020–21 UEFA Champions League knockout phase#Round of 16|round of 16 tie]], becoming the competition's youngest goalscorer of both English and German nationalities.<ref>{{cite news |title=Lazio 1-4 Bayern Munich: Jamal Musiala becomes youngest English scorer|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/56158031 |access-date=23 February 2021 |website=BBC Sport |date=23 February 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Musiala toppt alle: Die jüngsten deutschen CL-Torschützen|url=https://www.kicker.de/musiala-toppt-alle-die-juengsten-deutschen-cl-torschuetzen-791168/slideshow|access-date=24 February 2021 |website=kicker |publisher=Olympia-Verlag |date=24 February 2021}}</ref> On 5 March, he signed his first professional contract at Bayern Munich until 2026.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2021/03/pm-musiala |title=Musiala signs until 2026 |website= Bayern Munich|date=5 March 2021 }}</ref>

===2022–23===
On 27 May 2023, Musiala scored the winning goal in the 89th minute in a 2–1 away victory over [[1. FC Köln|FC Köln]], to clinch the 11th consecutive league title for his club ahead of rivals Borussia Dortmund on goal difference.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.dw.com/en/bayern-munich-champions-again-after-dortmund-meltdown/a-65753627 |title=Bayern Munich champions again after Dortmund meltdown |publisher=DW |date=27 May 2023 }}</ref> Moreover, he finished the season as Bayern's second top scorer in the league with 12 goals, behind [[Serge Gnabry]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://onefootball.com/en/news/jamal-musiala-i-didnt-think-about-it-i-just-celebrated-37567210 |title=Jamal Musiala: “I didn't think about it, I just celebrated.” |publisher=OneFootball |date=27 May 2023 }}</ref>

==International career==
Musiala, who was also eligible to play for [[Nigeria national football team|Nigeria]] via his father, represented both [[England national football team|England]] and [[Germany national football team|Germany]] at youth international level.<ref name="bundesliga"/><ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.dfb.de/datencenter/personen/jamal-musiala/spieler | title = Jamal Musiala - Spielerprofil |publisher=German Football Association | language = de | access-date = 13 December 2020}}</ref>

===Youth===
[[File:Jamal Musiala October 2019.jpg|thumb|191x191px|Musiala with England in 2019]]
In November 2020, Musiala was called up to the [[England national under-21 football team|England under-21]] squad for the first time for their [[2021 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification]] matches.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thefa.com/news/2020/nov/06/england-mu21s-squad-named-for-november-games-with-albania-and-andorra-061120|title= Two new faces included in the England MU21s squad for games with Andorra and Albania |publisher=The Football Association |date=6 November 2020|access-date=6 November 2020}}</ref> He made his U21 debut as a substitute  during a 3–1 victory over [[Andorra national under-21 football team|Andorra under-21s]] at [[Molineux Stadium]] on 13 November 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54934110|title= England U21 3-1 Andorra U21 |website=BBC Sport |date=13 November 2020|access-date=13 November 2020}}</ref> He scored his first under-21 goal during a 5–0 win over [[Albania national under-21 football team|Albania]] at Molineux Stadium on 17 November 2020.<ref>{{Cite news |date=17 November 2020 |title=England U21 5-0 Albania U21 |website=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54979326 |access-date=17 November 2020}}</ref> After choosing to represent England's under-21s, the German football association indicated that they had stopped pursuing Musiala, with youth team coach Meikel Schonweitz stating: "He has clearly signalled to us that he currently sees his future with the English national teams. We accept his decision and wish him all the best for his sporting career."<ref name= "Goal">{{cite news |title=Bayern wonderkid Musiala 'sees his future' with England amid international tussle with Germany |url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/bayern-wonderkid-musiala-sees-his-future-with-england-amid/1n0bofp7ggojr16c8jupqeejg9 |access-date=2 February 2021 |website=Goal.com |publisher=Perform Group |date=12 November 2020}}</ref> Musiala had previously indicated that he felt more comfortable playing for England at youth level than for Germany, as he grew up as a footballer at Chelsea with other English players, whereas for Germany he did not know any of the other players.<ref name = "DW">{{cite news |title=Bayern Munich's Jamal Musiala in Germany-England tug-of-war |url=https://www.dw.com/en/bayern-munichs-jamal-musiala-in-germany-england-tug-of-war/a-56406533 |access-date=2 February 2021 |publisher=Deutsche Welle |date=1 February 2021}}</ref>

===Senior===
On 24 February 2021, Musiala announced he had decided to represent his birth nation, Germany, for full international matches.<ref name="right21">{{Cite web|title=Jamal Musiala exclusive: Why it's the right decision to play for Germany |url=https://theathletic.com/2408268/2021/02/24/jamal-musiala-bayern-munich-exclusive-why-right-decision-to-play-for-germany-england/|date=24 February 2021|access-date=24 February 2021|website=The Athletic}}</ref><ref name="feels21">{{Cite web|title=Musiala: "Entscheidung fühlt sich 100 Prozent richtig an"|trans-title=Musiala: "Decision feels 100 percent right"|url=https://www.sportschau.de/fussball/bundesliga/jamal-musiala-nationalmannschaft-interview-100.html|author=Philipp Nagel |date=24 February 2021|access-date=24 February 2021|website=Sportschau |language=de}}</ref> He subsequently received his first call up to the senior team for [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] qualifiers in March 2021,<ref>{{cite web | title = Start ins EM-Jahr: Musiala und Wirtz erstmals im DFB-Team | url = https://www.dfb.de/news/detail/start-ins-em-jahr-musiala-und-wirtz-erstmals-im-dfb-team-225461/ | website = dfb.de | language = de | date = 19 March 2021 | access-date = 25 March 2021}}</ref> making his debut on 25 March 2021 as a 79th minute substitute in a 3–0 win against [[Iceland national football team|Iceland]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Germany vs. Iceland 3–0 |url=https://uk.soccerway.com/matches/2021/03/25/europe/wc-qualifying-europe/germany/iceland/3430239/ |website=Soccerway |publisher=Perform Group |access-date=28 March 2021}}</ref>

====Euro 2020====
On 19 May 2021, Musiala was selected to the squad for the [[UEFA Euro 2020]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.kicker.de/em-kader-offiziell-loew-beruft-mueller-hummels-und-volland-804642/artikel|title=EM-Kader offiziell: Löw beruft Müller, Hummels und Volland|date=19 May 2021|website=kicker |publisher=Olympia-Verlag |language=de|access-date=19 May 2021}}</ref> On 23 June 2021, he became the youngest German player to feature in a major tournament in a 2–2 draw with [[Hungary national football team|Hungary]] in Euro 2020, aged 18 years and 117 days.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/19692/12340079/germany-2-2-hungary-leon-goretzkas-late-equaliser-sees-germany-survive-euro-2020-scare |title=Germany 2-2 Hungary: Leon Goretzka's late equaliser sees Germany survive Euro 2020 scare |website=Sky Sports |date=23 June 2021 }}</ref> During the match, Musiala set up [[Leon Goretzka]]'s late equalizer which sent Germany through to the [[UEFA Euro 2020 knockout phase#Round of 16|round of 16]]. In doing so, he received praise from pundits and fans worldwide.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://apnews.com/article/euro-2020-europe-germany-hungary-soccer-662ecf63e622ad072c7288bd94e50ca5 |title=Goretzka keeps Germany in Euro 2020 after draw with Hungary|website=AP News | date = 24 June 2021 | access-date = 14 September 2022 | first = Ciarán | last = Fahey}}</ref>

====2022 World Cup qualification====
On 11 October 2021, Musiala scored his first goal in a 4–0 win over [[North Macedonia national football team|North Macedonia]] during the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)|2022 World Cup qualification]], becoming the second-youngest player to score for Germany, aged 18 years and 227 days, only behind [[Marius Hiller]], aged 17 years and 241 days, in 1910.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/north-macedonia-germany-live-world-cup-qualifying-line-ups-stats-musiala-sane-17552 |title=Jamal Musiala among the goals in North Macedonia as Germany qualify for Qatar 2022 |website=Bundesliga |date=11 October 2021 }}</ref>

====2022 World Cup====
On 10 November 2022, Musiala received a call-up for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.dfb.de/news/detail/flick-beruft-moukoko-fuellkrug-und-goetze-in-wm-kader-245974/ |title=Flick beruft Moukoko, Füllkrug und Götze in WM-Kader |trans-title=Flick names Moukoko, Füllkrug and Götze to World Cup squad |publisher=[[German Football Association]] |date=10 November 2022 |access-date=10 November 2022 |language=de}}</ref> On 23 November, he made his World Cup debut by starting against [[Japan national football team|Japan]], to become the first German teenager to feature in the competition since 1958, and the fourth youngest German player, aged 19 years and 270 days, in all World Cup participations, only behind: [[Karl-Heinz Schnellinger]], [[Leopold Neumer]] and [[Edmund Conen]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.sport1.de/news/fussball/wm/2022/11/wm-2022-deutschland-japan-historischer-einsatz-von-jamal-musiala |title=Historischer Einsatz von Musiala |website=sport1.de |language=de |date=23 November 2022 }}</ref> In the same match, [[Youssoufa Moukoko]] was subbed on in the 90th minute, to be the youngest ever, and Musiala became the fifth.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.kicker.de/moukoko-knackt-64-jahre-alten-rekord-musiala-fuenfter-926905/artikel |title=Moukoko knackt 64 Jahre alten Rekord - Musiala Fünfter |website=kicker.de |language=de |date=23 November 2022 }}</ref> On 1 December, he completed 13 dribbles, two shy of [[Jay-Jay Okocha]]'s record of 15 in [[1994 FIFA World Cup|1994]], in a 4–2 win over [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica]], yet Germany was eliminated from the group stage as they finished third in their group.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.sportskeeda.com/football/news-gives-us-lionel-messi-vibes-fans-believe-germany-star-doesn-t-deserve-go-fifa-world-cup-performance-costa-rica |title="Gives us Lionel Messi vibes" – Fans believe Germany star doesn't deserve to go out of the FIFA World Cup after performance against Costa Rica |website=Sportskeeda |date=1 December 2022 }}</ref>

==Style of play==
As a versatile player, Musiala plays both on the wings and at all positions of the midfield.<ref name="Hodson">{{Cite web |last=Hodson |first=Tony |title=Jamal Musiala: Bundesliga Player Watch |url=https://www.coachesvoice.com/cv/jamal-musiala-bayern-munich-chelsea-germany/ |access-date=11 July 2022 |website=The Coaches' Voice}}</ref> According to [[Hansi Flick]], who gave Musiala his debut at FC Bayern Munich, "He has a great eye and feeling for choosing the right spaces. He is very confident on the ball and can play well between the lines."<ref name="Hodson"/>
