'''Christopher James Mepham''' (born 5 November 1997) is a professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre back]] for {{English football updater|AFCBourn}} club [[AFC Bournemouth]] and the [[Wales national football team|Wales national team]]. He is a product of the [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]] and [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] academies.

==Club career==
===Early years===
A [[centre back]] and occasional [[right back]],<ref name="contracts">{{Cite news |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2016/february/youth-team-duo-sign-first-professional-contracts-/ |title=Brentford FC Youth Team duo sign first professional contracts |publisher=Brentford F.C. |access-date=25 August 2017}}</ref> Mepham began his career in the [[Chelsea F.C. Academy|academy]] at [[Premier League]] club [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] at the age of 10.<ref name="MND">{{Cite news |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2014/june/youth-team-player-backs-football-v-mnd/ |title=Brentford FC |publisher=Brentford F.C. |access-date=25 August 2017}}</ref> He was released at the age of 14 and was then turned down by [[Watford F.C.|Watford]] and [[Queens Park Rangers F.C.|Queens Park Rangers]].<ref name="James">{{Cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2018/mar/21/chris-mepham-ryan-giggs-brentford-premier-league-wales |title=Chris Mepham: 'It's surreal that Ryan Giggs wants to have a chat with you' |last=James |first=Stuart |date=21 March 2018 |newspaper=The Guardian |access-date=22 March 2018}}</ref>

===Brentford===
====Youth years (2012–2016)====
Mepham joined the [[Brentford F.C. Reserves and Academy|academy]] at [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]] in 2012,<ref name="MND" /> after being spotted by the club's Head of Academy Recruitment while making a one-off appearance for [[North Greenford United F.C.|North Greenford United]]'s reserve team.<ref name="James" /> After making three appearances for the [[Brentford F.C. Reserves and Academy|youth team]] as an U16,<ref name="YT1314">{{Cite news |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2014/april/201314-youth-team-statistics/ |title=Brentford FC |publisher=Brentford F.C. |access-date=25 August 2017}}</ref> Mepham signed a scholarship deal in June 2014.<ref name="MND" /> On 2 February 2016 it was announced that Mepham had signed his first professional contract to be a member of the club's [[Brentford F.C. Reserves and Academy|development squad]] and he finished his scholarship having made 43 appearances and scored two goals for the youth team.<ref name="contracts" /><ref name="YT1314" /><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2015/may/201415-youth-team-player-statistics/ |title=Brentford FC |publisher=Brentford F.C. |access-date=25 August 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.brentfordfc.co.uk/stats/youth-team-player-stats.aspx |title=Youth Team Player Stats |publisher=Brentford F.C. |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160907050836/http://www.brentfordfc.co.uk/stats/youth-team-player-stats.aspx |archive-date=7 September 2016 |access-date=6 January 2017}}</ref> After consistently good performances for the reformed [[Brentford F.C. Reserves and Academy#Relaunch (2016–present)|B team]] during the first half of the [[2016–17 Brentford F.C. season|2016–17]] season, Mepham signed a two-year contract extension on 6 January 2017 and the following day,<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2017/january/chris-mepham-signs-contract-extension/ |title=Brentford FC Chris Mepham signs contract extension |publisher=Brentford F.C. |access-date=25 August 2017}}</ref> he made his senior debut as a late substitute for [[Harlee Dean]] during a 5–1 [[FA Cup]] third round victory over [[Eastleigh F.C.|Eastleigh]].<ref name="Soccerbase1617" /> A successful 2016–17 season saw Mepham named as the club's B Team Player of the Year.<ref name="POTY">{{Cite news |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2017/may2/201617-awards-winners/ |title=Brentford FC 2016/17 Awards Winners |publisher=Brentford F.C. |access-date=26 May 2017}}</ref>

====Breakthrough (2016–2019)====
After the departure of centre-back Harlee Dean on 30 August 2017,<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.getwestlondon.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/brentford-hand-first-team-promotion-13587908 |title=Brentford hand first team promotion to young defender |last=Moore |first=Tom |date=7 September 2017 |website=Get West London |access-date=7 September 2017}}</ref> Mepham was promoted into the first team squad and signed a new five-year contract.<ref name="brentfordfc">{{Cite news |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2017/september/chris-mepham/ |title=Brentford FC Chris Mepham signs new long-term contract |publisher=Brentford F.C. |access-date=18 September 2017}}</ref> He made his first start for the club in a 3–1 [[EFL Cup]] third round defeat to [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]] on 19 September 2017 and gave away the [[Penalty kick (association football)|penalty]] which led to the Canaries' opening goal.<ref name="Soccerbase1718" /><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/fixtures/first-team/201718/september/brentford-vs-norwich-city-on-19-sep-17/#report |title=Brentford vs Norwich City on 19 Sep 17 – Match Centre – Brentford FC |publisher=Brentford F.C. |access-date=19 September 2017}}</ref> After appearing sporadically throughout the autumn,<ref name="Soccerbase1718" /> a head injury suffered by [[John Egan (footballer, born 1992)|John Egan]] in December allowed Mepham a run in the team from the Christmas period until early February 2018.<ref name="Soccerbase1718" /><ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.getwestlondon.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/dean-smith-outlines-hopes-plans-14098357 |title=Dean Smith outlines hopes and plans for Brentford's youngsters |last=Moore |first=Tom |date=2 January 2018 |website=Get West London |access-date=2 January 2018}}</ref> An achilles injury suffered by [[Andreas Bjelland]] in mid-March allowed Mepham back into the team and he scored the first senior goal of his career with the equaliser in a 1–1 draw with [[Sheffield United F.C.|Sheffield United]] on [[Good Friday]].<ref name="Soccerbase1718" /><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.getwestlondon.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/brentford-boss-explains-andreas-bjelland-14394783 |title=Brentford boss on Bjelland injury and Millwall loss |last=Moore |first=Tom |date=10 March 2018 |website=Get West London |access-date=1 April 2018}}</ref> He finished the [[2017–18 Brentford F.C. season|2017–18]] season with 23 appearances and one goal.<ref name="Soccerbase1718" />

Mepham began the [[2018–19 Brentford F.C. season|2018–19]] season as the Bees' undisputed first-choice centre back in league matches, but was [[Red card (association football)|sent off]] for the first time in his career for two [[Yellow card (association football)|bookable offences]] committed during a 1–0 defeat to [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]] on 20 October 2018.<ref name="Soccerbase1819" /> He made 27 appearances before departing the club for a club-record transfer fee in January 2019.<ref name="Soccerbase1819" /><ref name="LeavesBrentford">{{Cite web |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2019/january/chris-mepham-departs-22.01.19/ |title=Chris Mepham leaves Brentford to join AFC Bournemouth |publisher=Brentford F.C. |access-date=31 January 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite AV media |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fmHPAO0lnKM |title=Phil Giles' January Round Up |date=31 January 2019 |publisher=Brentford F.C. |time=0:59 |accessdate=31 January 2019 |medium=Interview |via=YouTube}}</ref> During his two-and-a-half seasons as a professional at [[Griffin Park]], Mepham made 48 appearances and scored one goal.<ref>{{Soccerbase|id=100492}}</ref>

===AFC Bournemouth===
On 22 January 2019, Mepham signed a long-term contract with [[Premier League]] club [[AFC Bournemouth]] for an undisclosed fee,<ref name="LeavesBrentford" /> reported to be £12&nbsp;million.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11743/11614317/chris-mepham-joins-bournemouth-from-brentford-for-12m |title=Chris Mepham joins Bournemouth from Brentford for £12m |website=Sky Sports |access-date=31 January 2019}}</ref> Eight days later, he made his debut for the club as a substitute for [[Junior Stanislas]] late in a 4–0 win over Chelsea.<ref name="Soccerbase1819" /> He continued to be regularly selected within the back line at Bournemouth due to the absence of [[Steve Cook]] through injury. Mepham finished the 2018–19 season with Bournemouth having made 13 Premier League appearances in total.

On 10 August 2019, Mepham scored his first Premier League goal, and his first ever goal for the Cherries, in a 1–1 home draw against newly promoted Sheffield United.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49215986 |title=AFC Bournemouth 1–1 Sheffield United |work=BBC Sport |access-date=6 March 2020}}</ref> Mepham made a further ten appearances in the Premier League for the Cherries throughout the first half of the 2019–20 season, including a starring role in a 1–0 away win and clean sheet at Chelsea on 14 December.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/50696487 |title=Chelsea 0–1 AFC Bournemouth |work=BBC Sport |access-date=6 March 2020}}</ref> However, Mepham would suffer a "significant knee injury" in the FA Cup game against [[Luton Town F.C.|Luton Town]] on 4 January, which would potentially rule him out for up to three months.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/51069890 |title=Chris Mepham: Bournemouth defender is ruled out for up to three months with injury |work=BBC Sport |access-date=6 March 2020}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:Wales-Switzerland match at Euro 2020 in Baku 5 (cropped 2).jpg|thumb|Mepham (N°22, center right) with [[Wales national football team|Wales]] at [[UEFA Euro 2020]]]]
Mepham was called into the [[Wales national under-20 football team|Wales U20]] squad for the [[2017 Toulon Tournament]] and made one appearance,<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2017/may2/chris-mepham-wales/ |title=Brentford FC Chris Mepham heading to Toulon Tournament |publisher=Brentford F.C. |access-date=27 May 2017}}</ref> in a 2–2 group stage draw with [[Ivory Coast national under-20 football team|Ivory Coast]] on 5 June 2017.<ref>{{Soccerway|id=471436|accessdate=5 June 2017}}</ref> He made his [[Wales national under-21 football team|U21]] debut with a start in a 3–0 [[2019 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification Group 8|2019 UEFA U21 Championship qualifying]] victory over [[Switzerland national under-21 football team|Switzerland]] on 2 September 2017.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2017/september/international-round-up-b-team/ |title=Brentford FC Mepham and Balcombe help home nations to wins |publisher=Brentford F.C. |access-date=3 September 2017}}</ref> Mepham [[Captain (association football)|captained]] the U21 team for the first time in a 0–0 qualifying draw with [[Romania national under-21 football team|Romania]] six weeks later.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2017/november/youth-international-round-up-20171115/ |title=Brentford FC Mepham and Mads captain nations in Euro Qualifiers |publisher=Brentford F.C. |access-date=15 November 2017}}</ref>

In March 2018, Mepham won his maiden call-up to the [[Wales national football team|senior team]] for the [[2018 China Cup]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2018/march/chris-mepham-wales/ |title=Brentford FC Chris Mepham gets maiden full Wales Call-up |publisher=Brentford F.C. |access-date=15 March 2018}}</ref> He made his debut as a substitute for [[Ben Davies (footballer, born 1993)|Ben Davies]] after 70 minutes of a 6–0 victory over [[China national football team|China]] on 22 March 2018.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2018/march/international-round-up-23-march/ |title=Brentford FC Wins for Mepham and Dalsgaard on international stage |publisher=Brentford F.C. |access-date=23 March 2018}}</ref> Two months later, he made his first international start, in a 0–0 [[Friendly match|friendly]] draw with [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]] on 28 May.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2018/may/international-review-20180529/ |title=Chris Mepham impresses on full debut for Wales |publisher=Brentford F.C. |access-date=29 May 2018}}</ref>

In May 2021, he was selected for the Wales squad for the delayed [[UEFA Euro 2020]] tournament.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57301006|title=Uncapped Colwill in Wales Euro 2020 squad|work=BBC Sport }}</ref> In November 2022, he was named in the Wales squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63545011 2022 Wales World Cup squad]</ref>

==Personal life==
Mepham was born in [[Northwick Park Hospital|Northwick Park]], the London Borough of Harrow.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Chris Mepham |url=https://www.afcb.co.uk/teams/first-team/chris-mepham/ |access-date=2022-11-16 |website=AFCB}}</ref> He attended [[Queensmead School]] in [[South Ruislip]] and grew up as a [[Queens Park Rangers F.C.|Queens Park Rangers]] supporter.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://doczz.net/doc/4077071/newsletter---queensmead-school |title=newsletter – Queensmead School – football |access-date=25 August 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2014/september/brentford-gave-everything-in-west-london-derby/ |title=Brentford FC BRENTFORD 'GAVE EVERYTHING' IN WEST LONDON DERBY |publisher=Brentford F.C. |access-date=8 September 2017}}</ref> His uncle Roy was a member of the Brentford youth team in the 1960s.<ref name="MND" />
