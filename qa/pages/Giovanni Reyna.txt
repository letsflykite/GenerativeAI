'''Giovanni Alejandro Reyna''' (born November 13, 2002) is an American professional [[Association Football|soccer]] player<!--Do not change to "football". Wikipedia articles are written in the style of English most associated with the subject, and as Reyna is American, please use American English --> who plays as an [[attacking midfielder]] or [[Winger (association football)|winger]] for {{German football updater|BoDortmu}} club [[Borussia Dortmund]] and the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States national team]]. He is known for his playmaking, positioning, and [[dribbling]] abilities. 

Reyna began his youth career with his hometown club, [[New York City FC]], and had his breakthrough with Bundesliga club [[Borussia Dortmund]]. During the [[2019–20 DFB-Pokal|2019-20 DFB-Pokal]] he became the youngest scorer in the German Cup's history at the age of 17.<ref name="CBS" /> In 2020 he became the youngest Bundesliga player to create a hat trick of assists.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Gio Reyna makes history with assist hat-trick for Borussia Dortmund |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/gio-reyna-makes-history-assist-hat-trick-for-borussia-dortmund-usa-usmnt-13112 |access-date=2023-06-06 |website=bundesliga.com - the official Bundesliga website |language=en}}</ref> He was a final candidate for the 2020, 2021, and 2022 [[Golden Boy (award)|Golden Boy]] award.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Golden Boy 2022: Nominees, how to vote, previous winners & everything you need to know {{!}} Goal.com |url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/golden-boy-2022-nominees-vote-previous-winners/blt877110aa6c874cfd |access-date=September 10, 2022 |website=www.goal.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Reyna Nominated For 2021 Golden Boy Award, Marking Second-Straight Season With USMNT Nominee |url=https://the18.com/soccer-news/2021-golden-boy-finalists-list-gio-reyna |access-date=2023-06-06 |website=The18}}</ref>

Born to U.S. national team players [[Claudio Reyna]] and [[Danielle Reyna]], he has represented the United States at several youth levels, scoring 16 goals in 31 youth caps. Reyna made his debut for the senior team in November 2020 and won the [[CONCACAF Nations League]] in [[2021 CONCACAF Nations League Finals|2021]] and [[2023 CONCACAF Nations League Finals|2023]].<ref name=":1" /> Individually he won a [[U.S. Soccer Player of the Year|U.S. Soccer Young Male Player of the Year]] award in 2020.

==Early life==
Reyna was born in [[Sunderland|Sunderland, England]] to American parents, [[Claudio Reyna]] and [[Danielle Reyna]], when his father was playing for [[Sunderland A.F.C.]] Both of his parents are former soccer players who played for the United States [[United States men's national soccer team|men's]] and [[United States women's national soccer team|women's]] national teams, respectively. His family moved back to the United States, settling in [[Bedford (town), New York|Bedford, New York]], in 2007 when Giovanni was five.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://jr10corp.com/pages/about-jr10-corp |title=ABOUT JR10 CORP - Jr10clothing |access-date=August 17, 2021 |archive-date=September 18, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210918131841/https://jr10corp.com/pages/about-jr10-corp |url-status=dead }}</ref> Giovanni joined the academy team of his hometown club, [[New York City FC]], in 2015 and kept playing with the City FC Academy teams until 2019 when he moved to Germany to join [[Borussia Dortmund]]'s academy.<ref>{{cite web |title=Dortmund sign U.S. youth Gio Reyna from NYCFC |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/soccer-transfers/story/3890386/dortmund-sign-us-youth-gio-reyna-from-nycfc |website=ESPN FC |access-date=May 25, 2020 |date=July 1, 2019}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Borussia Dortmund===
====2019–20 season: Breakthrough====
On January 18, 2020, Reyna made his [[Bundesliga]] debut for [[Borussia Dortmund]], coming on as a substitute in the 72nd minute, in a 5–3 win against [[FC Augsburg]].<ref name="mlssoccer">{{cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2020/01/18/new-york-city-fc-academy-alum-giovanni-reyna-makes-bundesliga-debut-borussia|title=New York City FC academy alum Giovanni Reyna makes Bundesliga debut in Borussia Dortmund win|website=mlssoccer.com|date=January 18, 2020}}</ref> Hence, he became the youngest American, aged 17 years and 66 days, ever to appear in the Bundesliga, breaking a record previously set by [[Christian Pulisic]].<ref name="mlssoccer" />

On February 4, Reyna scored his first professional goal in a 3–2 defeat to [[SV Werder Bremen|Werder Bremen]] in the [[2019–20 DFB-Pokal|DFB-Pokal]] Round of 16.<ref name="CBS" /> In doing so, he became the youngest goalscorer in [[DFB-Pokal|German Cup]] history.<ref name="CBS">{{cite web|url=https://www.cbssports.com/soccer/news/gio-reyna-rising-u-s-soccer-star-scores-gorgeous-strike-for-first-professional-goal-with-dortmund/|title=Gio Reyna, rising U.S. soccer star, scores gorgeous strike for first professional goal with Dortmund|website=CBS Sports|date=February 4, 2020|access-date=February 5, 2020}}</ref> On February 18, 2020, Reyna became the third-youngest player ever to appear in a knockout game of the [[UEFA Champions League]] when he came on as a substitute in the 67th minute against [[Paris Saint-Germain]]. Nine minutes later, Reyna set up [[Erling Haaland]]'s game-winning goal for Borussia Dortmund in the first leg of the Round of 16, becoming the youngest American to play and record an assist in a Champions League fixture.<ref>{{Cite web|title=USA teenager Gio Reyna overtakes Christian Pulisic in Borussia Dortmund and UEFA Champions League history books|url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/gio-reyna-makes-champions-league-history-with-debut-assist-overtakes-pulisic-dortmund-psg-10029|access-date=May 15, 2021|website=bundesliga.com - the official Bundesliga website|language=en}}</ref>

On May 16, Reyna was set to start his first Bundesliga match for Borussia Dortmund in their derby against [[FC Schalke 04|Schalke]], but suffered a calf injury during warm-ups.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Hamilton |first1=Tom |title=Dortmund, USMNT prospect Giovanni Reyna injured when set for first start |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/borussia-dortmund/story/4095121/dortmundusmnt-prospect-giovanni-reyna-injured-when-set-for-first-start |website=ESPN |access-date=May 16, 2020 |date=May 16, 2020}}</ref> Reyna returned on May 23 and played in the final 11 minutes of a 2–0 victory at [[VfL Wolfsburg]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Uersfeld |first1=Stephan |date=May 22, 2020 |title=USMNT hopeful Gio Reyna back for Dortmund, Favre admits Bundesliga return a 'danger' |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/borussia-dortmund/story/4098146/usmnt-hopeful-gio-reyna-back-for-dortmundfavre-admits-return-a-danger |access-date=May 24, 2020 |website=ESPN FC}}</ref>

On December 5, Reyna scored his second [[Bundesliga]] goal against [[Eintracht Frankfurt]], with a near post finish just inside the 18-yard box. Reyna was a second-half substitute for Dortmund in their 4–1 victory over [[RB Leipzig]] in the [[2021 DFB-Pokal Final]] on May 13, 2021. His involvement in the match marks him as the youngest American to appear in a domestic cup final in Europe, a record previously held by his international teammate Christian Pulisic.<ref name=DFBW>{{cite web|url=https://apnews.com/article/bundesliga-borussia-dortmund-dortmund-europe-leipzig-57d576ad46190c226251b8f92669dbf8|title=Borussia Dortmund beats Leipzig 4-1 in German Cup final|website=AP|date=May 13, 2021}}</ref>

==International career==
After representing the United States at several youth levels, Reyna received his first call up to the senior [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]] squad for matches against [[Wales national football team|Wales]] and [[Panama national football team|Panama]] in November 2020.<ref name=":1">{{cite web|url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2020/11/usmnt-roster-announcement-berhalter-24-players-november-camp-friendlies-wales-panama |title=USMNT ROSTER ANNOUNCEMENT: BERHALTER CALLS 24 PLAYERS FOR NOVEMBER CAMP MATCHES VS WALES AND PANAMA |date=November 3, 2020 |publisher=ussoccer.com |access-date=November 3, 2020}}</ref> On November 12, 2020, a day before his eighteenth birthday, Reyna made his senior national team debut against Wales.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2020/11/usmnt-0-wales-0-match-report-stats|title=USA 0 - Wales 0: Match Report & Stats}}</ref> In the following game, a 6–2 friendly victory over Panama, Reyna started and scored his first senior goal directly from a free kick.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2020/11/usmnt-6-panama-2-match-report-stats|title = USA 6 - Panama 2: Match Report & Stats}}</ref>

On March 24, 2022, during a third-round [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – CONCACAF third round#Matchday 12|2022 World Cup qualification match]] against [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]] at [[Estadio Azteca]], Reyna dribbled past six Mexican defenders in a single run before being dispossessed; after the match it was compared to [[Diego Maradona|Diego Maradona's]] "''Goal of the Century''" run during the quarter-finals of the [[Argentina v England (1986 FIFA World Cup)|1986 World Cup]], which was also at the Azteca.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Creditor |first=Avi |title=USMNT's Reyna Dazzles With Slaloming Dribble vs. Mexico |url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2022/03/25/gio-reyna-dribble-usmnt-mexico-video-world-cup-qualifying |access-date=September 10, 2022 |website=Sports Illustrated |language=en-us}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=mlssoccer |title=USMNT's Gio Reyna draws Maradona comparison after solo run vs. Mexico {{!}} MLSSoccer.com |url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/usmnt-s-gio-reyna-draws-maradona-comparison-after-solo-run-vs-mexico |access-date=September 10, 2022 |website=mlssoccer |language=en}}</ref>

In the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], Reyna played only 52 minutes in four games. His sidelining throughout the group stage of the tournament led to speculation from analysts of possible issues within the team.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Stejskal |first=Paul Tenorio and Sam |title=Gio Reyna minutes limited |url=https://theathletic.com/3991695/2022/12/11/gio-reyna-usmnt-gregg-berhalter/ |access-date=2022-12-12 |website=The Athletic |language=en}}</ref> USMNT Head Coach [[Gregg Berhalter]] publicly addressed issues regarding an anonymous player for a lack of commitment and poor attitude, and confirmed that the team had held a meeting to determine if that player were to remain with the team in Qatar for the remainder of the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://worldsoccertalk.com/news/gregg-berhalter-airs-out-dirty-laundry-on-gio-reyna-20221212-WST-412086.html|title=Gregg Berhalter airs dirty laundry on Gio Reyna|author=Kyle Fansler|website=worldsoccertalk.com|date=December 12, 2022|accessdate=January 4, 2023}}</ref> On December 12, 2022, Reyna confirmed that he was the aforementioned player about whom Berhalter was speaking, and apologized for his behavior while criticizing the decision to publicize the information.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Gio Reyna criticizes Gregg Berhalter in Instagram post |url=https://worldsoccertalk.com/news/gio-reyna-criticizes-gregg-berhalter-in-instagram-post-20221212-WST-412150.html |access-date=2023-01-04 |website=World Soccer Talk |language=en}}</ref> In January 2023, it was revealed that Berhalter had been confronted during the tournament about damaging personal information that was floated to superiors. [[Danielle Reyna]], Reyna's mother, confirmed that she was the one who informed the U.S. Soccer Federation about the episode of violent behavior between Berhalter and his wife from 1991.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-01-04 |title=Gio Reyna's mother gave USSF Berhalter info |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/united-states-usa/story/4844539/reynas-told-us-soccer-about-past-gregg-berhalter-domestic-violence-incident-sources |access-date=2023-01-04 |website=ESPN.com |language=en}}</ref>

During the [[2023 CONCACAF Nations League Finals|2023 CONCACAF Nations League]], Reyna created 2 assists in the United States' 2-0 victory over [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada]] in the [[2023 CONCACAF Nations League final|final]], where he was named Man of the Match.<ref>{{Cite web |last=mlssoccer |title=USMNT Player Ratings: Gio Reyna dismantles Canada in Nations League final {{!}} MLSSoccer.com |url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/usmnt-player-ratings-gio-reyna-dismantles-canada-in-nations-league-final |access-date=2023-06-19 |website=mlssoccer |language=en}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Giovanni is the son of former [[Rangers F.C.|Rangers]], [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]], [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]], [[New York Red Bulls]], and [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]] player [[Claudio Reyna]],<ref name="US Soccer">{{Cite web|url=https://www.ussoccer.com/players/r/giovanni-reyna|title=Giovanni Reyna {{!}} U.S. Soccer Official Website|website=ussoccer.com|language=en|access-date=October 27, 2019}}</ref> and [[Danielle Reyna]], a former member of the [[United States women's national soccer team]].<ref>{{Cite web| url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/gio-reyna-who-is-borussia-dortmund-s-latest-american-sensation-new-york-city-fc-3914 |title= Gio Reyna: Who is Borussia Dortmund's latest American sensation? |access-date=January 18, 2020 |website=bundesliga.com}}</ref> Reyna is of Argentine-Portuguese descent through father’s parents and of [[Irish Americans|Irish-American]] descent through his mother.<ref name="Wahl 2018">{{cite web | last=Wahl | first=Grant | title=The Reyna Family's Story of Loss and Legacy | website=Sports Illustrated | date=December 12, 2018 | url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2018/12/12/claudio-reyna-family-jack-death-giovanni-dortmund | access-date=April 7, 2021}}</ref> His older brother, Jack, died of brain cancer in 2012, at the age of 13.<ref>{{cite news |title=The Reyna Family's Story of Loss and Legacy|url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2018/12/12/claudio-reyna-family-jack-death-giovanni-dortmund |access-date=November 29, 2022 |work=Sports Illustrated |date=December 12, 2018|first=Grant|last=Wahl}}</ref>

Reyna was born in [[Sunderland]], England, while his father was playing for Sunderland. Reyna started his international career by playing with the United States' youth teams, but after impressing with Borussia Dortmund, he attracted interest from the [[Portugal national football team|Portuguese]], [[England national football team|English]], and [[Argentina national football team|Argentine]] national teams.<ref>{{Cite web|title='The U.S. is my home' - Reyna shuts down reports of England call-up {{!}} Goal.com|url=https://www.goal.com/en-ph/news/the-us-is-my-home-reyna-shuts-down-reports-of-england-call/nmxe1kebyysf1169c3gxzuoug|access-date=March 26, 2021|website=www.goal.com}}</ref> Ultimately, in a March 2020 interview with ''[[Sports Illustrated]]'', Reyna declared his intent to play for the United States: "I'm aware of the rumors, but it's quite clear for me. I only want to play for the United States. That's my home country."<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2020/03/05/gio-reyna-usa-international-choice-borussia-dortmund |title=Gio Reyna Ends Faux Suspense Over His International 'Choice', Will Play for USA |website=si.com |date=March 5, 2020 }}</ref>

He was named after his father's former Rangers teammate [[Giovanni van Bronckhorst]].<ref name="US Soccer"/>

Reyna's high level of play has earned him the nickname "The American Dream" by former teammate [[Erling Haaland]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=June 21, 2020 |title=Erling Haaland Reveals Nickname for Wonderkid Teammate Gio Reyna |url=https://www.90min.com/posts/erling-haaland-nickname-wonderkid-gio-reyna |access-date=September 10, 2022 |website=90min.com |language=en-GB}}</ref>
