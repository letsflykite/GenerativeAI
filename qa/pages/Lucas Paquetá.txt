'''Lucas Tolentino Coelho de Lima''' (born 27 August 1997), better known as '''Lucas Paquetá''' ({{IPA-pt|ˈlukas pakɛˈta|br}}), is a Brazilian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as an [[attacking midfielder]] for [[Premier League]] club [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]] and the [[Brazil national football team|Brazil national team]].

==Club career==
===Early career===
Lucas Paquetá arrived at Flamengo in 2007 at the age of 10. By 15, he was very technically skilled but did not have the physicality of other players his age (he was only 5&nbsp;ft tall). By 18, however, he had grown 27&nbsp;cm (10.6 inches). With the Flamengo youth team he won the 2016 Copa São Paulo de Júniores title, the most important youth tournament in [[Football in Brazil|Brazil]]. Alongside other standout players [[Léo Duarte]], [[Felipe Vizeu]], and [[Ronaldo da Silva Souza|Ronaldo]], Lucas was promoted to the club's professional team right after the competition.<ref>{{cite web |title= Flamengo é tricampeão da Copa São Paulo de Futebol Jr |url=http://www.flamengo.com.br/site/noticia/detalhe/22870/flamengo-e-tricampeao-da-copa-sao-paulo-de-futebol-jr |publisher=flamengo.com.br |date=25 January 2016 |access-date=5 January 2017}}</ref>

===Flamengo===
[[file:LucasPaqueta2018.jpg|200px|thumb|left|Lucas playing for [[Clube de Regatas do Flamengo|Flamengo]] in 2018.]]
In March 2016 Paquetá got promoted to Flamengo's professional team and signed a contract extension until 2020.<ref>{{cite web |title= Destaque no título da Copinha, Lucas Paquetá renova com o Flamengo |url=http://www.gazetaesportiva.com/flamengo/destaque-no-titulo-da-copinha-lucas-paqueta-renova-com-o-flamengo/ |publisher=Gazeta Esportiva |date=4 March 2016 |access-date=5 January 2017}}</ref> On 5 March 2016, Lucas debuted as a starter in a 3–1 win over Bangu in a [[2016 Campeonato Carioca|2016 Rio de Janeiro State League]] match.<ref>{{cite web |title= Base decide, Muralha pega pênalti, e Fla vence Bangu de virada por 3 a 1 |url=http://globoesporte.globo.com/rj/futebol/campeonato-carioca/jogo/05-03-2016/flamengo-bangu/ |publisher=globoesporte.com |date=5 March 2016 |access-date=5 January 2017}}</ref> Paquetá scored his first professional goal on 19 February 2017 in a 4–0 victory against [[Madureira Esporte Clube|Madureira]] at the [[Estádio Raulino de Oliveira]] in [[Volta Redonda]].

Paquetá scored in both the [[2017 Copa do Brasil]] final in a 1–1 draw against [[Cruzeiro Esporte Clube|Cruzeiro]] at the [[Maracanã Stadium]] and in the [[2017 Copa Sudamericana]] final against [[Club Atlético Independiente|Independiente]], also a 1–1 draw in the Maracanã. In both matches, Flamengo were defeated and were runners-up to their opponents in the competitions. In the Copa Sudamericana final, he was named the best Flamengo player in the match. He finished 2017 considered by fans to be, alongside [[Juan (footballer, born 1979)|Juan]], the highlight of Flamengo.

In the [[2018 Campeonato Carioca]], Lucas Paquetá was chosen as the best left-winger of the tournament and was the only Flamengo player selected for the team of the tournament.

===AC Milan===
On 10 October 2018, news agencies across Brazil and Italy reported that a €35M deal had been made between [[A.C. Milan|AC Milan]] and [[Clube de Regatas do Flamengo|Flamengo]] for the transfer of player, beating stiff competition from French club [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/sport/football/2018/10/18/flamengos-lucas-paqueta-to-join-ac-milan/|title=Football: Flamengo's Lucas Paqueta to join AC Milan {{!}} The Star Online|website=www.thestar.com.my|access-date=4 February 2019}}</ref>

On 4 January 2019, the transfer was made official, with Milan announcing the player had signed a five-year contract.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.acmilan.com/en/news/media/2019-01-04/official-statement-lucas-paqueta|title=OFFICIAL STATEMENT: LUCAS PAQUETÁ|last=Milan|first=A. C.|website=AC Milan|access-date=4 February 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.espn.com.au/football/soccer-transfers/story/3742998/ac-milan-sign-lucas-paqueta-from-flamengo-for-35m|title=AC Milan sign Paqueta for €35m in January|date=4 January 2019|website=ESPN.com|language=en|access-date=4 February 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://globoesporte.globo.com/futebol/times/flamengo/noticia/lucas-paqueta-realiza-exames-medicos-para-assinar-com-o-milan.ghtml|title=Lucas Paquetá realiza exames médicos para assinar com o Milan|work=Globoesporte|access-date=10 October 2018|language=pt-BR}}</ref> He chose to wear the shirt number 39. Because of his mother's Portuguese descent, he was eligible for registration as a European player.<ref>[https://www.calciomercato.com/en/news/revealed-why-paqueta-s-arrival-at-ac-milan-is-even-more-worthwhi-75175 Revealed: Why Paqueta's arrival at AC Milan is even more worthwhile]</ref>

Paquetá made his club debut on 12 January 2019, playing 85 minutes before being replaced by [[Fabio Borini]] as Milan beat [[U.C. Sampdoria|Sampdoria]] 2–0 in extra time in the [[2018–19 Coppa Italia|Coppa Italia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.legaseriea.it/en/coppa-italia/match-report/2018-19/OTTAV/UNI/1/SAMMIL|title=Coppa Italia|date=12 January 2019|access-date=12 January 2019|publisher=Legaseriea}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/en-au/match/sampdoria-v-milan/report/7e78mywuph86wex5f480uv6ui|title=Sampdoria 0 AC Milan 2 (after extra time): Super-sub Cutrone spares Rossoneri|date=12 January 2019|access-date=12 January 2019|publisher=Goal}}</ref> Four days later, Paquetá played in the [[2018 Supercoppa Italiana]], held at the [[King Abdullah Sports City]] in [[Jeddah]], [[Saudi Arabia]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.juventus.com/en/news/news/2018/italian-supercup-to-be-played-in-saudi-arabia.php|title=Italian Supercup to be played in Saudi Arabia|website=Juventus|access-date=18 July 2018}}</ref> where [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]] beat Milan 1–0. On 21 January 2019, Paquetá made his [[2018–19 Serie A|Serie A]] debut in a 2–0 win over [[Genoa C.F.C.|Genoa]] at the [[Stadio Luigi Ferraris]]. During the match, Paquetá performed a [[rainbow kick|rainbow flick]] over opponent [[Daniel Bessa]], flicking the rolling ball up and propelling it over the head of his opponent.<ref>{{cite news |title=El alumno supera al maestro: impresionante 'lambretta' a lo Neymar de Paquetá |url=https://www.marca.com/futbol/liga-italiana/2019/01/21/5c45ec6be5fdea077b8b45fd.html |access-date=5 December 2022 |work=Marca |date=21 January 2019 |language=es}}</ref> Paquetá scored his first goal for Milan in 3–0 win against [[Cagliari Calcio|Cagliari]], scoring the second goal. He dedicated his goal for the victims of a fire at his old club Flamengo, all members of the under-15 team.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.sportbible.com/football/news-paqueta-scores-his-first-goal-for-milan-dedicates-moment-to-flamengo-20190211|title=Lucas Paquetá Scores His First Goal For AC Milan And Dedicates The Moment To Flamengo|date=11 February 2019|website=www.sportbible.com|language=en|access-date=18 February 2019}}</ref>

===Lyon===
On 30 September 2020, French club [[Olympique Lyonnais|Lyon]] announced the signing of Paquetá, who signed for €20 million on a five-year contract.<ref>{{cite press release|url=https://www.ol.fr/en/contents/articles/2020/09/30/lucas-paqueta-joins-ol-for-5-seasons|title=Lucas Paquetá joins OL on a five year contract |website=OL.fr/en|publisher= Olympique Lyonnais|access-date=30 September 2020}}</ref>

In the 21/22 season, the midfielder was elected the best foreign player of [[Ligue 1]]. The election was carried out by popular vote and was supported by more than 20,000 people. Paquetá, 25 years old, competed with the defender of [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|PSG]] [[Marquinhos]], the marfileño midfielder of Lens Fofana and the Canadian leader of Lille [[Jonathan David]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://ge.globo.com/futebol/futebol-internacional/futebol-frances/noticia/2022/06/13/paqueta-do-lyon-e-eleito-melhor-estrangeiro-do-campeonato-frances.ghtml | title=Paquetá, do Lyon, é eleito melhor estrangeiro do Campeonato Francês }}</ref>

===West Ham United===
On 29 August 2022, West Ham United announced the signing of Paquetá for a club record fee undisclosed by the club.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Paqueta signs for West Ham United |url=https://www.whufc.com/news/west-ham-united-sign-brazil-star-lucas-paqueta-record-fee}}</ref> He signed a five-year contract for a fee estimated by the [[BBC]] to be more than £50m with additional add-ons, beating the fee paid for [[Sebastien Haller]] in 2019.<ref name="BBC Sport2022">{{cite web | title=West Ham sign Lyon's Paqueta for club record fee | website=BBC Sport | date=29 August 2022 | url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/62695883 | access-date=30 August 2022}}</ref> He made his West Ham and [[Premier League]] debut on 31 August in a 1–1 home draw against [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]].<ref name="Football365">{{cite web | title=West Ham 1-1 Tottenham: Paqueta makes Hammers debut in London derby draw | website=Football365 | date=31 August 2022 | url=https://www.football365.com/news/west-ham-1-1-tottenham-paqueta-hammers-debut-london-derby-draw | access-date=1 September 2022}}</ref>
On 4 January 2023, Paquetá scored his first Premier League goal and West Ham goal against [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]] at [[Elland Road]] in a 2–2 draw.<ref name="BBC Sport LeedsA">{{cite web | title=Rodrigo denies West Ham with Leeds leveller | website=BBC Sport | date=3 January 2023 | url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/64106277 | access-date=18 April 2023}}</ref> On 7 June 2023, Paquetá assisted [[Jarrod Bowen]]'s 90th minute winner in the [[2023 UEFA Europa Conference League final]] in a 2–1 win against [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]] in Prague.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2023/jun/07/fiorentina-west-ham-europa-conference-league-final-match-report|title=Jarrod Bowen strikes at the last to earn Conference League glory for West Ham|website=The Guardian|accessdate=7 June 2023}}</ref> A day later, Paquetá was named in [[UEFA]]'s team of the season for the tournament.<ref name="ConfTOTS">{{cite news |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaconferenceleague/news/0282-1834ffd20659-410cffa51d2a-1000--team-of-the-season/ |title=2022/23 Europa Conference League Team of the Season |publisher=UEFA |date=8 June 2022 |access-date=8 June 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
===Under-20===
[[file:URUGUAY vs BRASIL SUDAMERICANO SUB 20 (Lucas Paquetá).jpg|150px|thumb|Lucas playing for [[Brazil national under-20 football team|Brazil U20]] in 2017]]

On 1 April 2016, Paquetá was called up for the first time to represent [[Brazil national under-20 football team|Brazil U20]] in two friendly matches in Europe.<ref>{{cite web |title= Felipe Vizeu e Lucas Paquetá são convocados para Seleção sub-20 |url=http://www.flamengo.com.br/site/noticia/detalhe/23145/felipe-vizeu-e-lucas-paqueta-sao-convocados-para-selecao-sub-20 |publisher=flamengo.com.br |date=1 April 2016 |access-date=5 January 2017}}</ref> He scored his first goal in his debut in a 2–1 win against [[England national under-20 football team|England U20]] on 4 September.

In October 2016, he was called up for the ''Quadrangular de Seleções'' tournament, a friendly round-robin against the U20 squads of [[Ecuador national under-20 football team|Ecuador]], [[Uruguay national under-20 football team|Uruguay]], and [[Chile national under-20 football team|Chile]].

===Under-23===
When he was 18, Paquetá was called to practice with the [[Brazil national under-23 football team|Brazil U23 team]] in preparation for the [[2016 Summer Olympics]]. Coach [[Rogério Micale]] called some younger players born in 1997 and 1998, including Lucas, to participate in group training in hopes of them eventually competing in the [[2020 Summer Olympics]] in Tokyo.<ref>{{cite web |title= Lucas Paquetá é um dos sparrings da Seleção Olímpica |url=http://www.flamengo.com.br/site/noticia/detalhe/23768/lucas-paqueta-e-um-dos-sparrings-da-selecao-olimpica |publisher=flamengo.com.br |date=21 July 2016 |access-date=5 January 2017}}</ref>

He received his first official call-up to the under-23 team in March 2020.

===Senior team===
In 2018, he was selected to be one of the twelve alternatives for the [[Brazil national football team|Brazil national team]] by coach [[Tite (football manager)|Tite]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia. Of the 35 players on the 23-man roster and provisional roster, Lucas was the youngest.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://globoesporte.globo.com/futebol/selecao-brasileira/noticia/lista-de-35-de-tite-para-a-copa-tem-tres-novatos-dede-maicon-e-paqueta.ghtml|title=Lista de 35 de Tite para a Copa tem três novatos: Dedé, Maicon e Paquetá|publisher=globoesporte.com|date=25 May 2018|access-date=8 June 2018}}</ref>

On 17 August 2018, Paquetá was called for the first time for the [[Brazil national football team|Brazil national team]] by manager [[Tite (football manager)|Tite]] to play in friendly matches against [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]] and [[El Salvador national football team|El Salvador]] in September.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://globoesporte.globo.com/futebol/selecao-brasileira/noticia/tite-convoca-selecao-com-paqueta-pedro-e-andreas-pereira-para-amistosos.ghtml|title=Tite convoca Seleção com Paquetá, Pedro, Dedé e Everton para amistosos|publisher=globoesporte.com|date=17 August 2018|access-date=17 August 2018}}</ref> On 7 September, he made his debut with the national team, coming off the bench to replace [[Philippe Coutinho]] in the 71st minute during a friendly match against the United States.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2018/sep/07/usa-v-brazil-international-friendly-live|title=Usa v Brazil – match report|date=8 September 2019|website=theguardian.com|language=en|access-date=15 April 2019}}</ref> On 23 March 2019, he scored his first goal in a 1–1 friendly draw against [[Panama national football team|Panama]], wearing Brazil's iconic No. 10 shirt.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.espn.co.uk/football/report?gameId=537099|title=Lucas Paqueta scores first Brazil goal in friendly draw with Panama|date=23 March 2019|website=ESPN.com|language=en|access-date=15 April 2019}}</ref> In May 2019, he was included in Brazil's 23-man squad for the [[2019 Copa América]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.football-italia.net/138152/brazil-name-copa-america-squad |title=Brazil name Copa America squad |publisher=Football Italia |date=17 May 2019 |access-date=21 May 2019 }}</ref>

On 2 July 2021, in the [[2021 Copa América]] quarter-final clash against [[Chile national football team|Chile]], he came on as a substitute at half-time and scored in the 47th minute to give Brazil a 1–0 win.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/12347711/copa-america-quarter-finals-brazil-edge-chile-peru-see-off-paraguay-on-penalties-in-thriller |title=Copa America quarter-finals: Brazil edge Chile, Peru see off Paraguay on penalties in thriller |publisher=Sky Sports |date=3 July 2021 |access-date=4 July 2021 }}</ref> 
On 6 July 2021, he once again scored the only goal after an assist from Neymar Junior in the semi-final against [[Peru national football team|Peru]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://footballexpress.co.uk/copa-america-2021-brazil-1-0-peru-post-match-analysis/ |title=Copa America 2021: Brazil 1-0 Peru Post-Match Analysis|website=Football Express.co.uk|date=5 July 2021 |access-date=6 July 2021 }}</ref>

On 7 November 2022, Paquetá was named in the squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Seleção Brasileira está convocada para a Copa do Mundo FIFA Qatar 2022|url=https://www.cbf.com.br/selecao-brasileira/noticias/selecao-masculina/selecao-brasileira-esta-convocada-para-a-copa-do-mundo-fifa-qatar-2022|publisher=[[Brazilian Football Confederation|CBF]]|access-date=7 November 2022|date=7 November 2022|language=pt}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Lucas has an older brother, [[Matheus Paquetá|Matheus]], who also plays football.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.milanlive.it/2019/01/23/monza-acquistato-matheus-paqueta-prestito-lucas/|title=Monza: arriva in prestito Matheus Paquetà, fratello di Lucas|last=Giuffrida|first=Giacomo|date=23 January 2019|website=MilanLive.it tutte le News del Milan prima degli altri|language=it-IT|access-date=31 January 2019}}</ref> His nickname, Paquetá, refers to [[Paquetá Island]] and the eponymous [[Paquetá, Rio de Janeiro|neighborhood]] he grew up in.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.90min.com/posts/6194225-6-things-fans-need-to-know-about-expected-new-ac-milan-signing-lucas-paqueta|title = 6 Things Fans Need to Know About Expected New AC Milan Signing Lucas Paqueta|date = 11 October 2018}}</ref> He is a big fan of [[funk carioca]] and sometimes imitates funk dance steps during his goal celebrations.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Paquetá homenageia 'Os Havaianos' com dancinha ao marcar pela Seleção: 'Parte da minha infância'|url=http://www.lance.com.br/fora-de-campo/paqueta-homenageia-havaianos-com-dancinha-marcar-pela-selecao-parte-minha-infancia.html|access-date=15 November 2021|website=Lance!|language=pt-BR}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=12 November 2021|title=Galvão Bueno comenta dancinha de Paquetá em gol do Brasil e web não perdoa|url=https://www.torcedores.com/noticias/2021/11/galvao-bueno-comenta-coreografia-paqueta-gol-brasil|access-date=15 November 2021|website=Torcedores {{!}} Notícias sobre Futebol, Games e outros esportes|language=pt-BR}}</ref>
