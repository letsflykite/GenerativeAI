Henderson joined the [[Sunderland A.F.C. Reserves and Academy|Sunderland Academy]] at the age of eight, making his first-team debut a decade later in November 2008. He spent six months on [[Loan (association football)|loan]] at [[Coventry City F.C.|Coventry City]] in 2009, before returning to Sunderland. In 2011, Henderson signed for Liverpool, winning his first trophy with the club, the [[2012 Football League Cup Final|League Cup]], one year later. Appointed Liverpool [[Captain (association football)|captain]] in 2015, Henderson won the [[2019 UEFA Champions League Final|UEFA Champions League]], the [[2019 UEFA Super Cup|UEFA Super Cup]] and the [[2019 FIFA Club World Cup|FIFA Club World Cup]] in 2019, and in 2020 led his team to the [[2019–20 Premier League|Premier League title]], the club's first league title in 30 years. For his performances in the title-winning season, Henderson was named [[FWA Footballer of the Year]]. In 2022, Henderson lifted the [[FA Cup]] and the [[EFL Cup]], where Liverpool defeated [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] on penalties.

An England international, Henderson has over 70 appearances for his country since his debut in 2010. He has appeared at six international tournaments with England, the [[UEFA Euro 2012|2012]], [[UEFA Euro 2016|2016]] and [[UEFA Euro 2020|2020]] [[UEFA European Championship]]s and the [[2014 FIFA World Cup|2014]], [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022]] [[FIFA World Cup]]s. He was the first player to win the [[The FA England Awards#Senior Men's Player of the Year|England Player of the Year]] award at both the U-21 and senior level.

====Return to Sunderland====
[[File:Jordan Henderson.jpg|thumb|left|upright|Henderson playing for [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]] in 2011]]

In the [[2009–10 Sunderland A.F.C. season|2009–10 season]], Henderson broke into the Sunderland first team and made 38 appearances, scoring 2 goals.<ref name="sb0910"/> He scored his first senior goal for the club against [[Birmingham City F.C.|Birmingham City]] in the League Cup third round.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/league_cup/8256923.stm |title=Sunderland 2–0 Birmingham |website=BBC Sport |date=22 September 2009 |access-date=26 September 2009 |archive-date=22 August 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190822032338/http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/league_cup/8256923.stm |url-status=live }}</ref> He then went on to score his first Premier League goal against [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] on 19 December 2009.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_prem/8414079.stm |title=Man City 4–3 Sunderland |website=BBC Sport |date=19 December 2009 |access-date=20 December 2009 |archive-date=12 January 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160112204941/http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_prem/8414079.stm |url-status=live }}</ref>

Henderson spent the majority of the season on the right side of midfield but played centrally in the absence of [[Lee Cattermole]]; his versatility and consistency earned him a new five-year contract on 23 April 2010, keeping him with the club until 2015.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.safc.com/news/20100424/young-pair-sign-new-deals_2256213_2033419 |title=Young pair sign new deals |publisher=Sunderland A.F.C. |access-date=23 April 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100831145835/http://www.safc.com/news/20100424/young-pair-sign-new-deals_2256213_2033419 |archive-date=31 August 2010}}</ref> He also went on to win the Sunderland Young Player of the Year award for the 2009–10 season.<ref name="poy10">{{cite web |url=https://www.lfchistory.net/Players/Player/Profile/1233 |title=Players – Jordan Henderson |website=lfchistory.net |access-date=17 April 2020 |archive-date=1 November 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211101155153/https://www.lfchistory.net/Players/Player/Profile/1233 |url-status=live }}</ref>

Henderson was a major part of the team during the [[2010–11 Sunderland A.F.C. season|2010–11 season]] making 39 appearances and scoring 3 goals,<ref name="sb1011"/> including his first league double, on 23 April at home to [[Wigan Athletic F.C.|Wigan Athletic]].<ref>{{cite web |date=23 April 2011 |url=https://www.premierleague.com/match/7419 |publisher=Premier League |title=Sunderlnd v Wigan |access-date=16 April 2020 |archive-date=4 November 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211104215138/https://www.premierleague.com/match/7419 |url-status=live }}</ref> On 13 January 2011, Henderson was listed on the official FIFA website as one of 13 young players to watch in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|date=13 January 2011|title=Players to watch in 2011|url=https://www.fifa.com/news/players-watch-2011-1363573|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200726042753/https://www.fifa.com/news/players-watch-2011-1363573|url-status=dead|archive-date=26 July 2020|access-date=13 January 2011|publisher=FIFA}}</ref> He was named Sunderland's Young Player of the Year for the second season running.<ref name=poy11>{{cite web |url=http://www.safc.com/news/20110518/bardsley-secures-awards-double_2256213_2362723 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110811200341/http://www.safc.com/news/20110518/bardsley-secures-awards-double_2256213_2362723 |archive-date=11 August 2011 |date=18 May 2011 |publisher=Sunderland A.F.C. |title=Bardsley secures awards double}}</ref>

On 14 May 2022, Henderson became the first Liverpool captain to win 6 different trophies after winning the [[2021–22 FA Cup]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.thisisanfield.com/2022/05/jordan-hendersons-chance-to-make-history-with-6th-different-trophy-shuffle/ | title=Henderson's chance to make history with SIXTH different trophy shuffle | date=14 May 2022 | access-date=14 May 2022 | archive-date=17 May 2022 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220517125237/https://www.thisisanfield.com/2022/05/jordan-hendersons-chance-to-make-history-with-6th-different-trophy-shuffle/ | url-status=live }}</ref> Liverpool just missed out on the chance to achieve a historic quadruple, coming second in the [[2021–22 Premier League|Premier League]] and the 2021–22 UEFA Champions League but winning both the [[2021–22 EFL Cup|EFL Cup]] and the [[2021–22 FA Cup|FA Cup]].<ref>{{Cite news |title= Liverpool's quadruple, treble chase is over: Reds settle for double in 2021-22 |website=Sporting News |url= https://www.sportingnews.com/uk/football/news/liverpool-quadruple-chase-2021-22-reds-man-united-treble/pmwqorelovih6pge6xnpv3av#:~:text=The%20Reds%20were%20battling%20on,making%20history%20with%20a%20quadruple. |access-date=2023-06-02}}</ref> At the end of the [[2022–23 Liverpool F.C. season|2022–23 season]], Liverpool narrowly missed out on UEFA Champions League qualification.<ref>{{Cite news |title= Mohamed Salah ‘totally devastated’ Liverpool miss out on Champions League football: ‘absolutely no excuse’ |publisher= EuroSport |url= https://www.eurosport.co.uk/football/premier-league/2022-2023/mohamed-salah-totally-devastated-liverpool-miss-out-on-champions-league-football-absolutely-no-excus_sto9623012/story.shtml |access-date= 29 May 2023 |url-status= live }}</ref>

===Reception===
Although he received media criticism early in his career for being dispensable, he later drew praise from pundits for his ability to develop his game and establish himself as an essential player, with Flamengo manager [[Jorge Jesus]] describing him as the "best midfielder in the world in his position" following their encounter in the 2019 FIFA Club World Cup Final. Liverpool coach Klopp described him as "exceptional" and "outstanding,"<ref name="reinvention"/><ref name="evolved"/><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/jordan-henderson-liverpool-indispensable-midfielder-captain-anfield-jurgen-klopp |title=Once, he struggled for respect at Anfield – now Jordan Henderson is indispensable to Liverpool |publisher=FourFourTwo |date=10 January 2020 |access-date=25 June 2020 |archive-date=27 June 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200627155740/https://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/jordan-henderson-liverpool-indispensable-midfielder-captain-anfield-jurgen-klopp |url-status=live }}</ref> while Steven Gerrard said about Henderson, "Teams can't function at Liverpool's level without a cog like Jordan Henderson."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/steven-gerrard-jordan-henderson-liverpool-17799344|title=Gerrard hails Henderson and details pivotal chat in Liverpool career|first=Paul|last=Clarke|date=23 February 2020|website=liverpoolecho|access-date=23 February 2020|archive-date=30 May 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200530160354/https://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/steven-gerrard-jordan-henderson-liverpool-17799344|url-status=live}}</ref>
