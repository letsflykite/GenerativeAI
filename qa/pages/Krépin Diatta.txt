'''Krépin Diatta''' (born 25 February 1999) is a Senegalese professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[winger (association football)|winger]] for [[Ligue 1]] club [[AS Monaco FC|Monaco]] and the [[Senegal national football team|Senegal national team]].

==Club career==
=== Sarpsborg 08 ===
On 26 February 2017, Diatta signed a four-year contract with [[Sarpsborg 08 FF|Sarpsborg 08]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Krépin Diatta klar for Sarpsborg 08 |url=http://www.sarpsborg08.no/nyheter/krepin-diatta-klar-for-sarpsborg-08 |publisher=[[Sarpsborg 08 FF]] |access-date=28 February 2017 |language=no |date=26 February 2017}}</ref> On 4 April 2017, he made his Sarpsborg debut in a 3–1 home win against [[Sogndal Fotball|Sogndal]], subbing-in, in the 83rd minute for [[Ole Jørgen Halvorsen]]. On 26 April 2017, he scored his first goal for the club in a 10–1 win against [[3. divisjon|Division 3]] side [[Drøbak-Frogn IL|Drøbak-Frogn]], scoring a hat-trick along with teammates [[Erton Fejzullahu]] and [[Jørgen Strand Larsen|Jørgen Larsen]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Sarpsborg med målshow i Drøbak |url=https://www.smp.no/ntb/sport/2017/04/26/Sarpsborg-med-m%C3%A5lshow-i-Dr%C3%B8bak-14646191.ece |access-date=29 June 2019 |agency=[[Sunnmørsposten]] |date=26 April 2017 |language=no}}</ref> On 13 August 2017, he scored his first league goal for Sarpsborg in a 2–2 away draw against [[Kristiansund BK|Kristiansund]], heading in, in the 80th minute from a cross from Halvorsen.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Herrebrøden |first1=Øyvind |title=Bakke raste mot dommeren da Kristiansund utlignet på overtid |url=https://www.vg.no/sport/fotball/i/xqa4X/bakke-raste-mot-dommeren-da-kristiansund-utlignet-paa-overtid |access-date=29 June 2019 |agency=[[Verdens Gang]] |date=14 August 2017 |language=no}}</ref> He helped Sarpsborg reach the [[2017 Norwegian Football Cup Final]], scoring 5 goals in the process. Sarpsborg eventually lost 3–2 against [[Lillestrøm SK|Lillestrøm]] and finished as  runners-up.<ref>{{cite news|title=Lillestrøm tok sitt sjette cupgull |url=https://www.dagbladet.no/sport/lillestrom-tok-sitt-sjette-cupgull---dette-er-rorende/68943941|work=[[Dagbladet]]|date=3 December 2017|access-date=29 June 2019}}</ref>

=== Club Brugge ===
On 3 January 2018, Diatta signed a four-and-a-half-year contract with [[Club Brugge KV]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Krépin Diatta naar Club Brugge|url=https://www.clubbrugge.be/nl/nieuws/kr%C3%A9pin-diatta%C2%A0naar-club-brugge|website=clubbrugge.be|publisher=Club Brugge KV|access-date=3 January 2018|language=nl|date=3 January 2018}}</ref> On 8 April 2018, he made his Club Brugge debut in a 1–0 away loss against [[K.A.A. Gent|Gent]], subbing-in, in the 46th minute for [[Ahmed Touba]]. On 22 July 2018, Diatta played in the [[2018 Belgian Super Cup]], playing 80 minutes until he was subbed-out for [[Dion Cools]]. The match eventually ended 2–1 in favour of Club Brugge. On 24 October 2018, Diatta made his [[UEFA Champions League]] debut in a 1–1 home draw against [[AS Monaco FC|AS Monaco]], coming on as an injury time substitute for [[Emmanuel Bonaventure Dennis|Emmanuel Dennis]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Club Brugge holds Monaco to 1-1 draw in Champions League |url=https://www.foxsports.com/soccer/story/club-brugge-holds-monaco-to-1-1-draw-in-champions-league-102418 |access-date=29 June 2019 |agency=[[Fox Sports Networks|Fox Sports]] |date=24 October 2018}}</ref> On 14 February 2019, he made his [[UEFA Europa League]] debut in a 2–1 home win against [[FC Red Bull Salzburg|FC Salzburg]] in the [[2018–19 UEFA Europa League knockout phase#R32 2.1|2018–19 UEFA Europa League round of 32]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Lagae |first1=Bart |title=Denswil en Wesley koppen Club Brugge in sterke tweede helft voorbij Salzburg |url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/sportwereld/live/voetbal/279922 |access-date=29 June 2019 |agency=[[Het Nieuwsblad]] |date=14 February 2019 |language=nl}}</ref> On 10 March 2019, he scored his first goal for the club in a 4–0 away win against [[K.A.S. Eupen|Eupen]], shooting from the left side of the box and into the bottom right corner of the net in the 23rd minute.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Taecke |first1=Tomas |title=Vanaken wijst Club de weg in Eupen |url=https://www.hln.be/sport/voetbal/belgisch-voetbal/jupiler-pro-league/video-vanaken-wijst-club-de-weg-in-eupen-gouden-schoen-goed-voor-twee-goals~a8cae2d9/?referer=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.google.com%2F |access-date=30 June 2019 |agency=[[Het Laatste Nieuws]] |date=10 March 2013 |language=nl}}</ref>

=== Monaco ===
On 21 January 2021, Diatta joined [[Ligue 1]] side [[AS Monaco FC|Monaco]] in a deal running until 2025.<ref>{{cite news |title=Krépin Diatta joins AS Monaco |url=https://www.asmonaco.com/en/krepin-diatta-joins-as-monaco/ |access-date=21 January 2021 | publisher=[[AS Monaco FC]] |date=21 January 2021}}</ref> He scored his first European goal for Monaco against [[SK Sturm Graz|Sturm Graz]] with a header in a win in the group stage of the [[UEFA Europa League|Europa League]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=UEFA.com|title=Monaco-Sturm {{!}} UEFA Europa League|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/match/2033173--monaco-vs-sturm/|access-date=24 November 2021|website=UEFA.com|language=en}}</ref> On 19 November 2021, he scored his first league goal in Ligue 1 of the season against [[OSC Lille|Lille]]. During the match, he suffered an injury of the [[anterior cruciate ligament]] of the left knee.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Monaco : mauvaise nouvelle confirmée pour Diatta|url=https://news.maxifoot.fr/monaco/mauvaise-nouvelle-confirmee-pour-diatta-foot-358479.htm|access-date=24 November 2021|website=news.maxifoot.fr|language=fr}}</ref>

==International career==
===Under-20===
Diatta was part of the [[Senegal national under-20 football team|Senegal U20s]] who participated in the [[2017 Africa U-20 Cup of Nations]]. He scored two goals in the tournament, both of which came in the group stage his first a 70th minute header against [[South Africa national under-20 football team|South Africa]] in a 7 goal thriller, and his second, a goal in the added time of the first half against [[Cameroon national under-20 football team|Cameroon]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Senegal edge South Africa in seven-goal thriller |url=http://www.cafonline.com/en-US/NewsCenter/News/NewsDetails?id=Jf3wznZpfnZAHn8YWrfeUg%3d%3d |access-date=30 June 2019 |publisher=[[Confederation of African Football|CAF]] |date=2 March 2017}}</ref> Senegal eventually lost 2–0 against [[Zambia national under-20 football team|Zambia]] in the final and finished runners-up. They thus qualified for the [[2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Host Zambia beat Senegal to claim title |url=http://www.cafonline.com/en-US/NewsCenter/News/NewsDetails?id=rq%2f%2bZdE8HHttlDQJS9gW1w%3d%3d |access-date=30 June 2019 |publisher=CAF |date=12 March 2017}}</ref> Diatta was part of the Senegal U20s who participated in the 2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup in South Korea. On 22 May 2017, he played in Senegal's 2–0 opening match win against [[Saudi Arabia national under-20 football team|Saudi Arabia]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Senegal enjoy comfortable win over Saudi Arabia |url=https://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/matches/round=275960/match=300367078/match-report.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170525095913/http://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/matches/round=275960/match=300367078/match-report.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=25 May 2017 |access-date=30 June 2019 |agency=FIFA |date=22 May 2017}}</ref> Senegal were knocked out of the tournament in the round of 16 after they lost 1–0 against [[Mexico national under-20 football team|Mexico]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Cisneros the hero as Mexico advance to quarters |url=https://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/matches/round=275958/match=300367057/match-report.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170601103555/http://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/matches/round=275958/match=300367057/match-report.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=1 June 2017 |access-date=30 June 2019 |agency=FIFA |date=1 June 2017}}</ref>

===Senior===
In March 2019 Diatta was one of four young Senegalese players to receive a debut call-up to the national team.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bah |first1=Momodou |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/47401874|title=Senegal coach Aliou Cisse opts for youth in latest squad |date=28 February 2019 |publisher=[[BBC Sport]]}}</ref> On 23 March 2019, he made his national team debut in a 2–0 win against [[Madagascar national football team|Madagascar]] in the [[2019 Africa Cup of Nations qualification Group A|2019 Africa Cup of Nations qualifiers]].<ref>{{cite web |publisher=African Football |url=https://africanfootball.com/match-report/44822/Senegal-vs-Madagascar |title=Senegal v Madagascar |date=23 March 2019 |access-date=30 June 2016 |archive-date=3 June 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210603063753/https://africanfootball.com/match-report/44822/Senegal-vs-Madagascar |url-status=dead }}</ref> On 13 June 2019, he was named in Senegal's 23-man squad for the [[2019 Africa Cup of Nations]] in Egypt.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.kingfut.com/2019/06/13/senegal-announce-strong-afcon-squad/ |title=Senegal announce strong AFCON squad |work=[[KingFut]] |date=13 June 2019 |access-date=30 June 2019}}</ref> On 23 June 2019, he scored his first-ever senior international goal in Senegal's 2–0 opening match win against [[Tanzania national football team|Tanzania]], scoring in the 64th minute from a 20-yard first time finish, after a corner had not been cleared properly seconds earlier.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Willoughby |first1=James |title=Balde, Diatta on target in routine AFCON win |url=https://sportstar.thehindu.com/football/senegal-tanzania-afcon-match-report-balde-diatta-sadio-mane-liverpool/article28119604.ece |access-date=30 June 2019 |agency=[[Sportstar]] |date=24 June 2019}}</ref> For his performance, Diatta was named the Best Young Player of the tournament.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://english.ahram.org.eg/NewsAFCON/2019/338316.aspx|title=Africa Cup of Nations 2019 awards|website=Ahram Online|access-date=21 August 2019}}</ref>
