'''Hervé Jean-Marie Roger Renard''' (born 30 September 1968) is a French professional [[Association football|football]] coach and former player who is the manager of the [[France women's national football team|France women's national team]].

Renard has previously been the manager of [[Zambia national football team|Zambia national team]], with whom he won the [[2012 Africa Cup of Nations]]; he also won the competition in [[2015 Africa Cup of Nations|2015]] with the [[Ivory Coast national football team|Ivory Coast]], becoming the first coach to win two [[Africa Cup of Nations]] with different teams. In addition, he coached [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]] at the [[2018 World Cup]]. In 2019, he was appointed as manager of [[Saudi Arabia national football team|Saudi Arabia]].

==Early life==
Hervé Jean-Marie Roger Renard<ref name = "name">{{cite web |url=https://www.rsssf.org/players/3098f-coaches.html |title=World Cup 1930–2022 – Info on Coaches |first=Jaroslaw |last1=Owsianski |first2=Roberto |last2=Di Maggio|publisher=RSSSF |date=29 December 2022 |access-date=8 June 2023}}</ref> was born on 30 September 1968 in [[Aix-les-Bains]], Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/FootballFicheEntraineur1039.html |title=Hervé Renard |newspaper=L'Équipe |location=Paris |access-date=3 July 2022 |language=fr}}</ref>

==Playing career==
Born in [[Aix-les-Bains]], Renard played as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]] for French sides [[AS Cannes]], [[Stade de Vallauris]] and [[SC Draguignan]] in a playing career which lasted from 1983 to 1998.{{citation needed|date=February 2015}} After retiring as a professional player he worked as a cleaner, working there in the morning and training with Draguignan in the evening, eventually starting his own cleaning company.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/av/world-africa-48806025/afcon-2019-morocco-coach-herve-renard-s-journey-from-binman-to-bench|title=My journey from binman to cup-winning coach|website=BBC News}}</ref>

==Coaching career==
===Early years===
Renard began his coaching career with [[SC Draguignan]] in 1999, leaving in 2001.{{citation needed|date=February 2015}} He was Assistant at Chinese side [[Shanghai Cosco]] with head coach [[Claude Le Roy]] from 2002 to 2003,<ref name="auto"/> and managed English side [[Cambridge United F.C.|Cambridge United]] in 2004,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/c/cambridge_utd/4089843.stm|title=Cambridge dismiss manager Renard|access-date=7 May 2008|date=12 December 2004|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2018/jun/14/morocco-herve-renard-cambridge-world-cup|title=Morocco’s Hervé Renard: a maestro in Africa who cut his teeth at Cambridge - Michael Butler|first=Michael|last=Butler|date=14 June 2018|website=www.theguardian.com}}</ref> having first joined the club with Le Roy to serve as a coach.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2004/mar/23/newsstory.sport6|title=Cambridge opt for Claude|first=Press|last=Association|date=23 March 2004|website=www.theguardian.com}}</ref>

He became manager of Vietnamese club [[Nam Dinh F.C.|Song Da Nam Dinh]] in 2004, leaving them after several months.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://baobariavungtau.com.vn/the-thao/200610/huan-luyen-vien-nguyen-ngoc-hao-ve-mai-nha-xua-189842/|title=Huấn luyện viên Nguyễn Ngọc Hảo về mái nhà xưa…|access-date=17 February 2015|date=23 October 2006|publisher=Báo Bà Rịa Vũng Tàu|language=Vietnamese}}</ref> He became manager of [[AS Cherbourg Football|AS Cherbourg]] in 2005, leaving them in 2007.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rsssf.org/players/trainers-fran-clubs.html |title=France - Trainers of First and Second Division Clubs |access-date=7 May 2008 |url-status=live |website=[[RSSSF]] |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090618093752/http://www.rsssf.com/players/trainers-fran-clubs.html |archive-date=18 June 2009}}</ref> He next became Assistant to [[Claude Le Roy]] for the [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national side]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.ghanafa.org/renard-gets-ghana-appointment|title=Renard Gets Ghana Appointment|access-date=23 March 2023|work=Ghana Football Association}}</ref>

In May 2008, he was appointed manager of the [[Zambia national football team|Zambia national team]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/africa/7387884.stm|title=Renard named as new Zambia coach|access-date=7 May 2008|date=7 May 2008|author=Kennedy Gondwe|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> At the 2010 Africa Cup of Nations, he led Zambia to the quarter final stage of the tournament for the first time in 14 years.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.eurosport.com/football/african-cup-of-nations/2010/zambia-reach-last-eight_sto2192553/story.shtml|title=ZAMBIA REACH LAST EIGHT|work=Eurosport|date=22 January 2010|access-date=23 March 2023}}</ref> Renard left his duties as Zambia manager in April 2010 with only two months remaining on his contract.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/africa/8605965.stm|title=Renard quits as Zambia coach|access-date=6 October 2010|date=6 April 2010|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> Two days later he agreed to become manager of [[Angola national football team|Angola]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/africa/8609753.stm|title=Angola appoint Frenchman Herve Renard as coach|access-date=6 October 2010|date=8 April 2010|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> He resigned from his position as Angola manager in October 2010, and was replaced by [[Zeca Amaral]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/africa/9068083.stm|title=Akwa backs Angola coach Amaral|access-date=6 October 2010|date=6 October 2010|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref>

===USM Alger===
On 21 January 2011, Renard reached an agreement with Algerian club [[USM Alger]] to become the head coach of the club.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dzfoot.com/news-8222/l1-herve-renard-nouvel-entraineur-de-l-usm-alger/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131024035230/http://www.dzfoot.com/news-8222/l1-herve-renard-nouvel-entraineur-de-l-usm-alger/|url-status=dead|title=L1 : Hervé Renard nouvel entraîneur de l'USM Alger|archive-date=24 October 2013}}</ref>

===Second spell with Zambia===
On 22 October 2011, it was announced that Renard had returned for a second stint as coach of Zambia on a one-year contract.<ref>{{Cite news|date=21 October 2011|title=Herve Renard signs a contract to coach Zambia again|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/15406419|access-date=23 March 2023}}</ref> He led the team to their first victory in the African Cup Of Nations in [[2012 Africa Cup of Nations|2012]]. The win was dedicated to [[1993 Zambia national football team air disaster|the 18 players who perished in April 1993]], after a plane carrying the squad crashed just miles from the site of the 2012 final in Gabon.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/17008081|title=Zambia coach dedicates Africa Cup of Nations win to crash dead|publisher=BBC Sport|date=13 February 2012|access-date=13 February 2012}}</ref>

In May 2012, [[Chishimba Kambwili]], the Zambian sports minister, announced he expected Renard to sign a new contract by the end of the month.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/18118108|title=Zambia's Sports Minister confident about Renard future|date=18 May 2012|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref>

After Zambia were eliminated from the group stages of the 2013 African Cup of Nations, Renard said that it was his fault.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/21254402|title=Africa Cup of Nations: Zambia exit my fault, says Herve Renard|date=29 January 2013|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> He later criticised CAF for not allowing Zambia, as the 2012 winners of the AFCON, the chance to compete at the [[2013 Confederations Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/21257573|title=Zambia coach Renard hits out at Caf|date=30 January 2013|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref>

Renard was released from his contract by the Football Association of Zambia in October 2013, in preparation for a role with French club [[FC Sochaux-Montbéliard|FC Sochaux]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/24430450|title=Zambia and coach Herve Renard part company|date=7 October 2013|access-date=7 October 2013|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref>

===Sochaux===
On 7 October 2013 it was announced that Renard was appointed as the new manager of French [[Ligue 1]] side [[FC Sochaux-Montbéliard|Sochaux]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.fcsochaux.fr/fr/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=5670:herve-renard-nouvel-entraineur-du-fcsm/|title=Football Club Sochaux-Montbéliard le site officiel www.fcsochaux.fr|website=--|language=fr-fr|access-date=22 March 2018}}</ref> In April 2014 he was linked with the [[Morocco national football team|Morocco national team]] job.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/26852044|title=Oussama Assaidi wants Morocco to appoint foreign coach|date=2 April 2014|access-date=3 April 2014|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/27142928|title=Herve Renard plays down links to Morocco coach position|date=24 April 2014|access-date=25 April 2014|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref>

The club was involved in a relegation fight in May 2014,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=fra/news/newsid=2104699.html|title=Sochaux can fox the drop under Renard|date=12 May 2014|access-date=6 June 2014|author=Christian Châtelet|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> and after being relegated, he left the club later that month.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rtl.fr/actualites/sport/football/article/sochaux-l-entraineur-herve-renard-confirme-son-depart-7772078852|title=Sochaux : l'entraîneur Hervé Renard confirme son départ|date=18 May 2014|access-date=6 June 2014|language=fr|publisher=RTL}}</ref> In July 2014 he was announced to be on the shortlist for the Ivory Coast job.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/28420130|title=Renard among names on Ivory Coast coach shortlist|date=22 July 2014|access-date=22 July 2014|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref>

===Ivory Coast===
[[File:ACON 2015 CIV ALG (16487072416).jpg|thumb|Renard as [[Ivory Coast national football team|Ivory Coast]] at the [[2015 Africa Cup of Nations]].]]
Renard was appointed as manager of the [[Ivory Coast national football team|Ivory Coast national team]] in July 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/28583566|title=Herve Renard appointed Ivory Coast boss|date=31 July 2014|access-date=1 August 2014|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> He was manager at the [[2015 Africa Cup of Nations]], and praised the organization of the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/30951944|title=Afcon 2015: Tournament is a great success - Renard|date=23 January 2015|access-date=24 January 2015|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> He won the competition, becoming the first coach to win two Africa Cup of Nations with different countries.<ref name="AFCON">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/31271441|title=Afcon 2015: Yaya Toure hails 'unbelievable' Ivory Coast win|date=9 February 2015|access-date=9 February 2015|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref>

===Lille===
Renard became manager of French club [[Lille OSC|Lille]] in May 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/32855286|title=Former Ivory Coast boss Herve Renard joins Lille as coach|date=25 May 2015|access-date=26 May 2015|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> On 11 November 2015, he was sacked after getting only 13 points in 13 league games.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lille part ways with coach Herve Renard after only six months|url=http://www.foxsports.com/soccer/story/lille-part-ways-with-coach-herve-renard-after-only-six-months-111115|publisher=FOX Sports|access-date=11 November 2015}}</ref>

===Morocco===
[[File:Por-Mar (11).jpg|thumb|Renard as [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]] manager at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]].]]

In February 2016, Renard was linked with the vacant [[Morocco national football team|Morocco national team]] job.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/35542209|title=Badou Zaki leaves post as Morocco coach by mutual consent|date=10 February 2016|access-date=11 February 2016|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> Later that month he was appointed as the new Morocco manager.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/35586217|title=Morocco unveil Frenchman Herve Renard as coach|date=16 February 2016|access-date=17 February 2016|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> In October 2016 he was linked with the vacant [[Algeria national football team|Algeria national team]] job.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/37667895|title=Algeria deny reports of approach to Morocco coach Herve Renard|date=15 October 2016|access-date=16 October 2016|author=Oluwashina Okeleji|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref>

In November 2017, he qualified Morocco to the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia, their first since 1998.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41955698|title=Ivory Coast 0-2 Morocco|date=11 November 2017|work=BBC Sport|access-date=22 March 2018}}</ref> Later that month, he signed a new contract, until 2022.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/42064091|title=Morocco extend Herve Renard's contract until 2022|date=21 November 2017|work=BBC Sport|access-date=22 March 2018}}</ref> In July 2019 the Moroccan team was eliminated from the [[2019 Africa Cup of Nations]], with Renard taking responsibility for the "shock exit".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.timeslive.co.za/sport/soccer/2019-07-06-morocco-coach-herve-renard-accepts-responsibility-for-shock-defeat/|title=Morocco coach Herve Renard accepts responsibility for shock defeat|website=TimesLIVE}}</ref> He resigned a few days later, on 15 July 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://en.yabiladi.com/articles/details/81049/atlas-lions--coach-herve-renard.html|title=Atlas Lions' coach Hervé Renard resigns|website=en.yabiladi.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49045999|title=Herve Renard: Morocco coach steps down after Africa Cup of Nations|date=21 July 2019|website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

===Saudi Arabia===
Later in July 2019 he became manager of [[Saudi Arabia national football team|Saudi Arabia]], the first Frenchman to do so.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://en.yabiladi.com/articles/details/81627/herve-renard-becomes-saudi-arabia-s.html|title=Hervé Renard becomes Saudi Arabia’s new manager|website=en.yabiladi.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49159032|title=Herve Renard: Double Nations Cup winner is named as Saudi Arabia's new coach|date=29 July 2019|website=BBC Sport}}</ref> On 10 September 2019, he managed his first official match against [[Yemen national football team|Yemen]] in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.footmercato.net/flash-mercato/herve-renard-explique-le-choix-arabie-saoudite_263105|title=Hervé Renard explique le choix Arabie Saoudite|website=Foot Mercato|language=fr|date=10 September 2019}}</ref> In March 2022, Renard led Saudi Arabia to qualification for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], and in the process became the foreign-born manager with the most wins (18) in the nation's history.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://makkahnewspaper.com/article/1560784/الملعب/رينارد-أكثر-الأجانب-فوزا-مع-الأخضر|title=رينارد أكثر الأجانب فوزا مع الأخضر|website=makkahnewspaper.com|language=ar|date=30 March 2022}}</ref> In their opening game of the World Cup, he led Saudi Arabia to a 2–1 win over [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]], one of the pre-tournament favourites, in what was considered one of the biggest shock results in World Cup history.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63718202|title= World Cup 2022: Saudi Arabia deliver 'seismic' shock, but don't count Argentina out|last=Cryer|first=Andy|date=22 November 2022|website=BBC Sport|language=en}}</ref> However, Saudi Arabia lost the other two matches against [[Poland national football team|Poland]] and [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]], to be eliminated from the group stage.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.arabnews.com/node/2209291/sport|title= 5 things we learned from Saudi Arabia’s loss to Mexico and Qatar 2022 exit|last=Duerden|first=John|date=1 December 2022|website=Arab News|language=en}}</ref>

===France women===

In March 2023, Renard was linked with the job as coach of [[France women's national football team]] following the dismissal of [[Corinne Diacre]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Article/Herve-renard-interesse-par-les-bleues-mais-toujours-sous-contrat-avec-l-arabie-saoudite/1385118|title=Hervé Renard intéressé par les Bleues, mais toujours sous contrat avec l'Arabie saoudite|website=L'Équipe}}</ref> On 29 March, the [[Saudi Arabian Football Federation]] accepted Renard's resignation, in order to begin his tenure as coach of the France women's team.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2023/mar/29/herve-renard-quits-saudi-arabia-to-coach-france-womens-world-cup|title=Hervé Renard quits Saudi Arabia to lead France at Women’s World Cup|publisher=The Guardian|date=29 March 2023}}</ref> On 30 March, Renard officially became the manager of France's women's national team, succeeding Diacre with a contract lasted until August 2024.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Herve-renard-officiellement-nomme-selectionneur-des-bleues/1388772|title=Hervé Renard officiellement nommé sélectionneur des Bleues|trans-title=Hervé Renard officially named Les Bleues coach|language=fr|publisher=L'Équipe|date=30 March 2023|access-date=30 March 2023}}</ref>
