'''Moustapha Name''' (born 5 May 1995) is a Senegalese professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[Cypriot First Division]] club [[Pafos FC|Pafos]] and the [[Senegal national football team|Senegal national team]].

==Club career==

=== AS Douanes ===
Moustapha Name started his career at [[AS Douanes (Senegal)|AS Douanes]] in his native Senegal in 2016. During the 2017–18 season, he scored ten goals in the [[Senegal Premier League]].{{Citation needed|date=April 2022}}

=== Pau ===
In 2018, Name joined [[Pau Football Club|Pau]] and adapted quickly to French football in the [[Béarn]]ese capital. In his second season, he occasionally captained the team.{{Citation needed|date=April 2022}}

In the [[2019–20 Coupe de France]], Pau reached the [[2019–20 Coupe de France|round of 16]] and lost to a [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] (PSG) side that went on to win [[Coupe de France|the entire competition]].{{Citation needed|date=April 2022}} The 2019–20 season was very successful for both Name and Pau; Pau achieved promoted to [[Ligue 2]], and Name's displays against [[Ligue 1]] heavyweights [[FC Girondins de Bordeaux|Bordeaux]] and PSG in the Coupe de France helped him make a name for himself.{{Citation needed|date=April 2022}}

=== Paris FC ===
On 27 June 2020, Name signed with [[Paris FC]] after successful seasons with Pau in the [[Championnat National]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://parisfc.fr/equipe-pro/moustapha-name-rejoint-le-paris-fc/|title=Moustapha Name rejoint le Paris FC|first=Kévin|last=Bri|date=27 June 2020}}</ref> He made his professional debut with [[Paris FC]] in a 3–0 Ligue 2 win over [[FC Chambly Oise|Chambly]] on 22 August 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://uk.soccerway.com/matches/2020/08/22/france/ligue-2/fc-chambly-thelle/paris-football-club-98/3287817/|title=Chambly vs. Paris FC - 22 August 2020 - Soccerway|website=Soccerway}}</ref>

==International career==
Name debuted with the [[Senegal national football team|Senegal national team]] in a 2–0 [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations qualification]] win over [[Guinea Bissau national football team|Guinea Bissau]] on 11 November 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://wiwsport.com/2020/11/11/senegal-guinee-bissau-2-0-moustapha-name-une-excellente-premiere-pour-moi/|title=Sénégal - Guinée-Bissau (2-0) / Moustapha Name : " Une excellente première pour moi ... " - Equipe nationale Football, Football|date=11 November 2020}}</ref>

He was part of [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations squads#Senegal|Senegal's squad]] for the [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations]]; the Lions of Teranga went on to win the tournament for the first time in their history.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cafonline.com/total-africa-cup-of-nations/news/cisse-unveils-senegal-squad-hopes-for-elusive-totalenergies-afcon-title|title=Cisse unveils Senegal squad, hopes for elusive TotalEnergies AFCON title|publisher=[[Confederation of African Football]]|date=25 December 2021|access-date=28 March 2023|archive-date=9 January 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220109145849/https://www.cafonline.com/total-africa-cup-of-nations/news/cisse-unveils-senegal-squad-hopes-for-elusive-totalenergies-afcon-title|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Senegal 0-0 Egypt |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/60186276 |website=[[BBC Sport]] |access-date=28 March 2023 |date=7 February 2022}}</ref>

Name was appointed a [[National Order of the Lion|Grand Officer of the National Order of the Lion]] by [[President of Senegal]] [[Macky Sall]] following the nation's victory at the tournament.<ref name= "reuters">{{cite web |title=Cash prizes, real estate, and highest honours as Senegal celebrates Nations Cup heroes |url=https://www.reuters.com/lifestyle/sports/cash-prizes-real-estate-highest-honours-senegal-celebrates-nations-cup-heroes-2022-02-08/ |website=[[Reuters]] |access-date=28 March 2023 |date=8 February 2022}}</ref>
