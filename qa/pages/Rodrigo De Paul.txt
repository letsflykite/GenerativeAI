'''Rodrigo Javier De Paul''' ({{IPA-es|roˈðɾiɣo ðe pol}}; born 24 May 1994) is an Argentine professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[La Liga]] club [[Atlético Madrid]] and the [[Argentina national football team|Argentina national team]]. He was a member of the Argentina team that won the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Club career==
===Racing Club===
Born in [[Sarandí, Buenos Aires|Sarandí]], [[Greater Buenos Aires]], De Paul joined [[Racing Club de Avellaneda|Racing Club]]'s youth setup in 2002, aged eight.<ref>[http://www.deportevalenciano.com/2014/05/02/as%C3%AD-es-rodrigo-de-paul-nuevo-jugador-del-valencia-cf Así es Rodrigo de Paul, nuevo jugador del Valencia CF (This is Rodrigo de Paul, new player of Valencia CF)]; Deporte Valenciano, 2 May 2014 {{in lang|es}}</ref> He was called up to the main squad on 24 June 2012, while still a junior, for a match against [[Club Atlético Vélez Sarsfield|Vélez Sarsfield]], but remained unused in the 1–2 home loss.

On 10 February 2013, De Paul played his first match as a professional, [[Substitute (association football)|replacing]] [[Mauro Camoranesi]] in the 86th minute of a 0–3 loss at [[Atlético de Rafaela]].<ref>[http://mundod.lavoz.com.ar/futbol/en-su-casa-atletico-de-rafaela-goleo-racing En su casa, Atlético de Rafaela goleó a Racing (At home, Atlético de Rafaela thrashed Racing)]; Mundo D, 10 February 2013 {{in lang|es}}</ref> He scored his first goal a month later, netting the last through a long-range shot in a 3–0 win at [[San Martín de San Juan]].<ref>[http://canchallena.lanacion.com.ar/1561653-san-martin-racing-un-encuentro-rodeado-de-pensamientos-maliciosos En otro partido con incidentes, Racing goleó y ayudó a Independiente (In another match with incidents, Racing thrashed and helped Independiente)]; Cancha Llena, 10 March 2013 {{in lang|es}}</ref>

De Paul appeared in 19 matches during the [[2012–13 Argentine Primera División season|2012–13 campaign]]. He then played a key part in [[2013–14 Argentine Primera División season|2013–14]], featuring in 35 matches and scoring four times.

===Valencia===
On 9 May 2014, it was announced that [[Valencia CF]] agreed to a US$6.5 million deal for De Paul with Racing.<ref>[http://www.valenciacf.com/ver/35926/comunicado-oficial.html Comunicado Oficial (Official announcement)]; Valencia's official website, 9 May 2014 {{in lang|es}}</ref> He signed a five-year deal with ''Los Che'' on 6 June,<ref>[http://www.valenciacf.com/ver/36138/benvingutdepaul---rodrigo-de-paul--nuevo-jugador-del-valencia-cf.html Rodrigo de Paul, nuevo jugador del Valencia CF (Rodrigo de Paul, new player of Valencia CF)]; Valencia's official website, 6 June 2014 {{in lang|es}}</ref> and made his [[La Liga]] debut on 23 August under head coach [[Nuno Espírito Santo]], replacing [[Paco Alcácer]] in the 65th minute in a 1–1 away draw against [[Sevilla FC]], but being [[Ejection (sports)|sent off]] just one minute later due to fouling [[Aleix Vidal]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marca.com/eventos/marcador/futbol/2014_15/primera/jornada_1/sev_val/|title=Punto de fe del Valencia|trans-title=Valencia point of faith|publisher=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|language=es|date=23 August 2014|access-date=3 September 2014}}</ref>

He scored his first goal for the club on 4 December 2014 in the 2–1 win against [[Rayo Vallecano]] in the [[Copa del Rey]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/ath-bilbao-vs-valencia/322437|title=Copa del Rey: Rodrigo de Paul gives Valencia 2-1 advantage over Rayo|publisher=ESPN|language=en|date=4 December 2014|access-date=30 August 2020}}</ref> He followed this up with his first La Liga goal on 9 April 2015 against [[Athletic Bilbao]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/ath-bilbao-vs-valencia/322437|title=Athletic Bilbao 1 Valencia 1|publisher=Sports Mole|language=en|date=9 April 2015|access-date=30 August 2020}}</ref> making 29 appearances in all competitions during his first season at the club. 

After making 14 appearances in all competitions during the first half of the 2015-16 season, including two in the [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]]. On 4 February 2016, he was loaned out by then Valencia manager [[Gary Neville]] to his former side [[Racing Club de Avellaneda|Racing Club]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sportsmole.co.uk/football/valencia/transfer-talk/news/valencia-youngster-joins-racing-on-loan_265563.html|title=Valencia youngster Rodrigo de Paul joins Racing Club on loan|publisher=Sports Mole|language=en|date=4 February 2016|access-date=30 August 2020}}</ref> He scored his first and only goal in a victory against Bolívar on 24 February in the [[Copa Libertadores]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.foxsports.com/stories/soccer/racing-open-group-stage-campaign-with-comfy-bolivar-win|title=RACING OPEN GROUP STAGE CAMPAIGN WITH COMFY BOLIVAR WIN|work=Fox Sports|language=en|date=25 February 2016|access-date=30 August 2020}}</ref>

===Udinese===
On 20 July 2016, De Paul was transferred to Italian [[Serie A]] club [[Udinese Calcio|Udinese]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.valenciacf.com/ver/59684/comunicado-oficial--traspaso-de-rodrigo-de-paul.html|title=VCF Official Statement {{!}} Rodrigo de Paul transferred to Udinese|publisher=Valencia CF|date=20 July 2016|access-date=21 July 2016}}</ref> He made his debut on 20 August 2016 against [[AS Roma]] in a 4–0 defeat. He scored his first goal for the club on 29 January 2017 against [[AC Milan]] in a 2–1 victory.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/en-gb/match/udinese-v-milan/report/1gd1syr5vv2dqh5xvgbamnjl5|title=Udinese 2 AC Milan 1|publisher=Goal.com|language=en|date=29 January 2017|access-date=30 August 2020}}</ref> 

He started the [[2018–19 Udinese Calcio season|2018–19]] season with four goals in the first six matches of the Serie A season.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.efe.com/efe/english/sports/udinese-claims-2-0-win-over-last-placed-chievo-in-serie-a/50000266-3758643|title=Udinese claims 2–0 win over last-placed Chievo in Serie A|newspaper=www.efe.com|publisher=Efe|language=en|date=23 September 2018|access-date=30 August 2020}}</ref> He would finish the season as Udinese top goal scorer with nine goals during the [[2018–19 Udinese Calcio season]] and also nine assists.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/145277/official-de-paul-extends-udinese|title=Official: De Paul extends at Udinese|publisher=Football Italia|language=en|date=29 January 2017|access-date=1 September 2020}}</ref> 

On 15 October 2019, De Paul signed a new five-year contract at Udinese.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/145277/official-de-paul-extends-udinese|title=Official: De Paul extends at Udinese|publisher=Football Italia|language=en|date=29 January 2017|access-date=1 September 2020}}</ref> He would score seven goals and gain six assists for Udinese in Serie A during the 2019–20 season.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/156821/report-udinese-want-%E2%82%AC40m-de-paul|title=Report: Udinese want €40m for De Paul|publisher=Football Italia|language=en|date=5 August 2020|access-date=1 September 2020}}</ref>

De Paul became Udinese's [[Captain (association football)|club captain]] in December 2020, replacing [[Kevin Lasagna]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.tuttoudinese.it/notizie/lasagna-declassato-nonostante-la-sua-titolarita-ieri-il-capitano-era-de-paul-79609 | title=Lasagna "declassato"? Nonostante la sua titolarità ieri il capitano era De Paul |website=tuttoudinese.it |publisher=Tutto Udinese |date=24 December 2020 |access-date=6 February 2021 |language=it}}</ref>

===Atlético Madrid===

On 12 July 2021, De Paul signed a five-year contract with [[Atlético Madrid]]. He joined his new club just days after winning the [[2021 Copa América|Copa América]] with Argentina.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Club Atlético de Madrid - Welcome, Rodrigo de Paul!|url=https://en.atleticodemadrid.com/noticias/welcome-rodrigo-de-paul|access-date=12 July 2021|website=Club Atlético de Madrid|language=en}}</ref> On 7 December 2021, he scored his first goal for the club in a 3–1 away win against [[FC Porto]] in the final group game of the [[2021–22 UEFA Champions League]].

==International career==
De Paul made his senior international debut for [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]] in a 4–0 victory against [[Iraq national football team|Iraq]] on 11 October 2018,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/iraq-vs-argentina/402273|title=Iraq 0 Argentina 4|publisher=Sky Sports|language=en|date=11 October 2018|access-date=30 August 2020}}</ref> and later became a regular under manager [[Lionel Scaloni]]; he was part of the Argentina squad that finished third in the [[Copa América 2019]] after beating [[Chile national football team|Chile]] 2–1 in the third-place match.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://copaamerica.com/es/noticias/Argentina-vencio-2-1-a-Chile-y-se-quedo-con-el-tercer-puesto/|title=Argentina venció 2-1 a Chile y se quedó con el tercer puesto|publisher=CONMEBOL|language=es|date=6 July 2019|access-date=20 August 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190708015400/https://copaamerica.com/es/noticias/argentina-vencio-2-1-a-chile-y-se-quedo-con-el-tercer-puesto/|archive-date=8 July 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref> 

On 3 July 2021, De Paul scored the opening goal in a 3–0 win over [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador]] in the quarter-finals of the [[2021 Copa América]] in Brazil.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.reuters.com/lifestyle/sports/argentina-beat-ecuador-3-0-move-into-copa-america-semi-2021-07-04/ |title=Argentina beat Ecuador 3-0 to move into Copa America semi |work=Reuters |date=4 July 2021 |access-date=11 July 2021 }}</ref> In the [[2021 Copa América Final|final]] of the tournament against the hosts [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]], De Paul's long pass set up [[Ángel Di María]] to score the only goal of the match, allowing Argentina to capture their joint record 15th Copa América title and their first major international title since [[Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|2008]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sportingnews.com/us/soccer/news/brazil-argentina-live-score-updates-highlights-copa-america-final/1tfk0vd23j2gj1a70vsyw2emub|title=Highlights of Copa America final 2021}}</ref><ref name="Copa América final">{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2021/jul/10/argentina-v-brazil-copa-america-final-live |title=Argentina 1-0 Brazil: Copa América final – live! |work=The Guardian |last1=Graham |first1=Bryan Armen |date=11 July 2021 |access-date=11 July 2021 }}</ref> He was also part of the squad that won the [[2022 FIFA World Cup |2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Pope |first=Greg LeaContributions from Conor |last2=Hancock |first2=Tom |last3=Mazur |first3=Martin |last4=published |first4=Alasdair Mackenzie |date=2022-12-18 |title=Argentina World Cup 2022 squad: Lionel Scaloni's full team |url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/argentina-world-cup-2022-squad-players-line-up-qatar |access-date=2023-03-03 |website=fourfourtwo.com |language=en}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Rodrigo's mother is of [[Italians|Italian]] descent, which earned him [[Italian citizenship]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Mosca |first=Lorenzo |date=2021-07-12 |title=De Paul, addio Udinese. All'Atletico Madrid dell'argentino Simeone per 35 milioni |url=https://www.gauchonews.it/calcio-argentino-sport/rodrigo-de-paul-udinese-venduto-atletico-madrid/ |access-date=2022-12-19 |website=Gaucho News |language=it-IT}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Redazione |date=2017-02-18 |title=Italia, oriundi-mania: ecco la top 11 dei "convocabili" |url=http://radiogoal24.it/2017/02/18/italia-oriundi-mania-ecco-la-top-11-dei-convocabili/ |access-date=2022-12-19 |website=RadioGoal24 |language=it-IT}}</ref> 

De Paul was in a relationship with Argentine model Camila Homs for twelve years. They have two children together. In January 2022, the couple confirmed that they were separating.<ref>{{Cite web |last= |date=2022-02-18|title=Rodrigo De Paul y Camila Homs: Cómo la historia de un amor terminó en un escándalo internacional|url=https://www.prensalibre.com/vida/escenario/rodrigo-de-paul-y-camila-homs-la-historia-de-un-amor-que-termino-en-un-escandalo-internacional/|access-date=2022-12-19 |website=www.prensalibre.com |language=es-SP}}</ref> De Paul has been in a relationship with Argentine singer [[Tini (singer)|Tini Stoessel]] since August 2022.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-12-18|title=Who is Rodrigo de Paul's girlfriend Tini Stoessel? All you need to know about the famous couple|url=https://firstsportz.com/football-news-who-is-rodrigo-de-pauls-girlfriend/|website=firstsportz.com |language=}}</ref>
