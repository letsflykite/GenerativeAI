'''Strahinja Pavlović''' ({{Lang-sr-Cyrl|Страхиња Павловић}}; born 24 May 2001) is a Serbian professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] for [[Austrian Football Bundesliga|Austrian Bundesliga]] club [[FC Red Bull Salzburg|Red Bull Salzburg]] and the [[Serbia national football team|Serbia national team]].

==Club career==

=== Partizan ===

==== 2015–2018: Youth years ====
Born in [[Šabac]], Pavlović started out at local club Savacium. He officially joined the youth system of [[FK Partizan|Partizan]] in the summer of 2015. In September 2018, Pavlović signed his first professional contract with the club, agreeing to a three-year deal.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://partizan.rs/197136-crno-beli-potpisali-ugovore-svojim-klincima/?pismo=lat | title=Crno-beli potpisali 9 klinaca | language=Serbian | publisher=partizan.rs | date=13 September 2018 | access-date=10 October 2019}}</ref> He was promoted to the first team by manager [[Zoran Mirković]] ahead of winter preparations for the second half of [[2018–19 FK Partizan season|the season]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.telegraf.rs/sport/fudbal/3023045-ovo-su-tri-nova-bisera-partizana-da-li-crno-beli-na-njima-zele-da-grade-buducnost | title=Ovo su tri nova bisera Partizana: Da li crno-beli na njima žele da grade budućnost? | language=Serbian | publisher=telegraf.rs | date=11 January 2019 | access-date=10 October 2019}}</ref>

==== 2018–19: First-team breakthrough ====
On 23 February 2019, Pavlović made his competitive senior debut for the club in a 3–0 home league win over [[FK Proleter Novi Sad|Proleter Novi Sad]], playing the full 90 minutes and receiving a yellow card in the process.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://partizan.rs/205814-sa-kicom-i-strahinjom-pred-derbi/?pismo=lat | title=Sa Kićom i Strahinjom pred derbi! | language=Serbian | publisher=partizan.rs | date=25 February 2019 | access-date=3 March 2019}}</ref> A week later, on 2 March of the same year, Pavlović played his first [[Eternal derby (Serbia)|Eternal derby]], and at the time of the match his age was 17 years and 282 days. As in the match of the previous round, Pavlović received a yellow card in that meeting as well. He quickly established himself in the team's starting lineup, collecting 15 official appearances and helping Partizan win the [[2018–19 Serbian Cup]].<ref name="SW" />

==== 2019–20: New contract and first goal ====
At the beginning of the [[2019–20 FK Partizan season|2019–20 season]], Pavlović confirmed himself as a first-team regular, forming a central defensive partnership with the experienced [[Bojan Ostojić]]. He was reportedly close to signing with Italian club [[S.S. Lazio|Lazio]] near the end of the summer transfer window. However, the negotiations failed<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.soccernews.com/strahinja-pavlovics-move-to-lazio-falls-through/293057/ | title=Strahinja Pavlovic's move to Lazio falls through | publisher=soccernews.com | date=2 September 2019 | access-date=10 October 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2 September 2019|title=Nota del Direttore Sportivo|url=http://www.sslazio.it/it/news/press-release-2/55113-nota-del-direttore-sportivo|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=21 July 2020|website=sslazio.it|language=it}}</ref> and Pavlović signed a new five-year contract with Partizan.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://partizan.rs/221568-pavlovicu-ugovor-na-pet-godina/?pismo=lat | title=Pavloviću ugovor na pet godina! | language=Serbian | publisher=partizan.rs | date=2 September 2019 | access-date=8 September 2019}}</ref> He scored his first career goal in a controversial 2–1 home league loss to [[FK Voždovac|Voždovac]] on 6 October.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://mozzartsport.com/fudbal/vesti/sokovi-u-humskoj-nepostojeci-penal-izjednacenje-u-91-hladan-tus-devica-u-95-minutu-video/339221 | title=Šokovi u Humskoj! Nepostojeći penal, izjednačenje u 91, hladan tuš Devića u 95. minutu (VIDEO) | language=Serbian | publisher=mozzartsport.com | date=6 October 2019 | access-date=10 October 2019}}</ref>

=== Monaco ===

==== 2019–20: Transfer and Partizan loan ====
On 18 December 2019, it was officially announced that Partizan and [[AS Monaco FC|Monaco]] had agreed terms for the transfer of Pavlović over the forthcoming winter transfer window.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://partizan.rs/231679-pavlovic-zvanicno-u-monaku/?pismo=lat | title=Pavlović zvanično u Monaku! | language=Serbian | publisher=partizan.rs | date=18 December 2019 | access-date=18 December 2019}}</ref> Pavlović would remain on loan with Partizan until June 2020.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.asmonaco.com/accord-avec-le-partizan-belgrade-pour-pavlovic/ | title=Accord avec le Partizan Belgrade pour Pavlovic | language=French | publisher=asmonaco.com | date=18 December 2019 | access-date=18 December 2019}}</ref>

==== 2020–21: Loan to Cercle Brugge ====
Pavlović's debut for Monaco came on 20 December 2020, as he came on as a late-match substitute in a 1–0 away victory over [[Dijon FCO|Dijon]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Dijon vs. Monaco - 20 December 2020 - Soccerway|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2020/12/20/france/ligue-1/dijon-football-cote-dor/association-sportive-monaco-fc/3287546/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=21 January 2021|website=Soccerway}}</ref> On 21 January 2021, he joined [[Cercle Brugge K.S.V.|Cercle Brugge]] on loan until the end of the season, having seen limited game time at Monaco due to being the fifth-choice centre-back in [[Niko Kovač]]'s team.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=21 January 2021|title=Strahinja Pavlović joining Cercle Brugge on loan|url=https://www.getfootballnewsfrance.com/2021/strahinja-pavlovic-joining-cercle-brugge-on-loan/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=21 January 2021|website=Get French Football News}}</ref>

==== 2021–22: Loan to Basel ====
After making 11 appearances for Monaco in all competitions in the first half of the 2021–22 season, on 16 February 2022 Pavlović was loaned to [[FC Basel|Basel]] in Switzerland until the end of the season.<ref name="fcb-aktuell-2021/22-Strahinja Pavlović">{{cite web | last = FC Basel 1893 | author-link = | date = 16 February 2022 | url = https://www.fcb.ch/aktuell/news/1-mannschaft/strahinja-pavlovic-leihweise-zum-fcb | title = Strahinja Pavlović wechselt bis Sommer 2022 to RotBlau | format = | work = Strahinja Pavlović transfers to RotBlau until the summer of 2022 | publisher = FC Basel 1893 AG | access-date = 16 February 2022}}</ref> Pavlović joined Basel's first team for their [[2021–22 FC Basel season|2021–22 season]] under head coach [[Patrick Rahmen]]. He played his domestic league debut for the club in the home game in the [[St. Jakob-Park]] on 19 February 2022 as Basel won 3–0 against [[FC Lausanne-Sport|Lausanne-Sport]].<ref name="fcb-matchcenter-2021/22-FCB-LS">{{cite web | last = FC Basel 1893 | author-link = | date = 19 February 2022 | url = https://www.fcb.ch/matchcenter/startcenter/61ee4e85d951ad103122ebf4/2021-FC_Basel_1893_-_FC_Lausanne-Sport | title = Hochverdienter 3:0-Erfolg gegen Lausanne-Sport | format = | work = Well-deserved 3-0 win against Lausanne-Sport | publisher = FC Basel 1893 AG | access-date = 19 February 2022}}</ref> During his short loan period with the club, Pavlović played a total of 11 games for Basel without scoring a goal. 10 of these games were in the [[Swiss Super League|Nationalliga A]] and the other was a friendly game.<ref name="fcb-achiv-Strahinja Pavlović">{{cite web | last = Verein "Basler Fussballarchiv” | authorlink = | year = 2002 | url = https://www.fcb-archiv.ch/spieler?command=detail&id=10248 | title = Strahinja Pavlović - FCB statistic | format = | publisher = Verein "Basler Fussballarchiv” | accessdate = 1 July 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
Pavlović was capped for Serbia at [[Serbia national under-17 football team|under-17]] and [[Serbia national under-19 football team|under-19]] levels. He made his [[Serbia national under-21 football team|national under-21 team]] debut on 6 September 2019, playing the full 90 minutes in a 1–0 [[Mordovia Arena|away]] loss against [[Russia national under-21 football team|Russia]] in their [[2021 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification Group 5|2021 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualifier]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.b92.net/sport/fudbal/vesti.php?yyyy=2019&mm=09&dd=06&nav_id=1587621 | title=Poraz Milovanovića na debiju – Rusija nadigrala Srbiju | language=Serbian | publisher=b92.net | date=6 September 2019 | access-date=8 September 2019}}</ref>

He made his senior team debut on 3 September 2020, when he started in an away [[2020–21 UEFA Nations League]] game against [[Russia national football team|Russia]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[UEFA]]|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefanationsleague/match/2029971--russia-vs-serbia/|title=Russia v Serbia game report|date=3 September 2020}}</ref>

In November 2022, he was selected in Serbia's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fss.rs/en/head-coach-dragan-stojkovic-announces-the-list-of-players-for-the-fifa-world-cup-2022-in-qatar/ |title=Head-coach Dragan Stojković announces the list of players for the FIFA World Cup 2022 in Qatar |publisher=[[Football Association of Serbia]] |date=11 November 2022 |access-date=11 November 2022}}</ref> He played in all three group stage matches, against [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]],<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/sports/world-cup-2022/richarlison-brings-brazil-to-life-in-the-second-half-defeats-serbia-2-0/3971175/ |title=Richarlison Brings Brazil to Life in the Second Half, Defeats Serbia 2-0 |first=Kristen |last=Conti |newspaper=NBCNew York |location=New York |date=24 November 2022 |access-date=7 December 2022}}</ref> [[Cameroon national football team|Cameroon]],<ref>{{Cite news |last=Sky Sports |date=2022-11-28 |title=World Cup 2022 - Cameroon 3-3 Serbia: Vincent Aboubakar and Eric Maxim Choupo-Moting goals complete stunning comeback |language=en |work=Sky Sports |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/cameroon-vs-serbia/report/462988#:~:text=World%20Cup%202022%20%2D%20Cameroon%203,Moting%20goals%20complete%20stunning%20comeback&text=Cameroon%20kept%20alive%20their%20faint,with%20Serbia%20in%20Al%2DWakrah. |access-date=2022-11-28}}</ref> and [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]].<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/dec/02/serbia-switzerland-world-cup-group-g-match-report |title=Switzerland advance and Serbia go out as Freuler finish settles stormy contest |first=Nick |last=Ames |newspaper=The Guardian|location=London |date=2 December 2022 |access-date=7 December 2022}}</ref> He scored a header goal in a group stage match match against Cameroon, on assist from [[Dušan Tadić]].<ref name="SRB-CAM">{{Cite news |last=Sky Sports |date=2022-11-28 |title=World Cup 2022 - Cameroon 3-3 Serbia: Vincent Aboubakar and Eric Maxim Choupo-Moting goals complete stunning comeback |language=en |work=Sky Sports |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/cameroon-vs-serbia/report/462988#:~:text=World%20Cup%202022%20%2D%20Cameroon%203,Moting%20goals%20complete%20stunning%20comeback&text=Cameroon%20kept%20alive%20their%20faint,with%20Serbia%20in%20Al%2DWakrah. |access-date=2022-11-28}}</ref> Serbia finished fourth in the group.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://soccer.nbcsports.com/2022/12/02/world-cup-2022-group-g-brazil-serbia-cameroon-switzerland-rankings-table-standings/ |title=World Cup 2022 Group G: Brazil, Serbia, Cameroon, Switzerland schedule, fixtures, rankings |first=Nicholas |last=Mendola |newspaper=NBC Sports|location=Lusail |date=2 December 2022 |access-date=7 December 2022}}</ref>
