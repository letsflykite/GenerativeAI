'''Martin Hongla Yma II''' (born 16 March 1998) is a Cameroonian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[central defender]] or a [[defensive midfielder]] for Italian club [[Hellas Verona F.C.|Hellas Verona]] and the [[Cameroon national football team|Cameroon national team]].

==Club career==
Born in [[Yaoundé]], Hongla joined [[Granada CF]] in 2016, from [[Blaise Nkufo]]'s Nkufo Sports Academy.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.granadadigital.es/martin-hongla-conviccion-y-atrevimiento/|title=Martin Hongla, convicción y atrevimiento|trans-title=Martin Hongla, confidence and audacity|publisher=Granada Digital|language=es|date=30 September 2016|accessdate=28 January 2017}}</ref> He was initially assigned to [[Granada CF B|the reserves]] in [[Segunda División B]].

Hongla made his senior debut on 21 August 2016, starting in a 1–1 home draw against [[Atlético Mancha Real]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eldeportedejaen.com/2016/08/el-atletico-mancha-real-debuta-con-un-empate-en-granada/|title=El Atlético Mancha Real debuta con un empate en Granada|trans-title=Atlético Mancha Real debut with a draw in Granada|publisher=El Deporte de Jaén|language=es|date=21 August 2016|accessdate=28 January 2017}}</ref> He made his first team – and [[La Liga]] – debut on 28 January 2017, playing the full 90 minutes in a 2–0 defeat at [[Villarreal CF]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marca.com/eventos/marcador/futbol/2016_17/la-liga/jornada_20/vil_gda/|title=El Villarreal vuela sin Pato|trans-title=Villarreal fly without Pato|publisher=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|language=es|date=28 January 2017|accessdate=28 January 2017}}</ref>

Ahead of the [[2017–18 Segunda División|2017–18 season]], Hongla was definitely promoted to the main squad, now in [[Segunda División]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://granadacf.ideal.es/martin-hongla-ficha-20170714030116-ntvo.html|title=Martin Hongla tendrá ficha con el primer equipo|trans-title=Martin Hongla will have a first team place|publisher=Ideal|language=es|date=14 July 2017|accessdate=18 July 2017}}</ref> On 16 January 2018, he was loaned to fellow league team [[FC Barcelona B]], for six months.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fcbarcelona.com/football/barca-b/news/2017-2018/granada-cf-agree-to-loan-of-martin-hongla|title=Granada CF agree to loan of Martin Hongla|publisher=FC Barcelona|date=16 January 2018|access-date=18 January 2018}}</ref>

On 3 July 2019, after a six-month loan to [[FC Karpaty Lviv]], Hongla joined [[Royal Antwerp F.C.|Royal Antwerp FC]] in the [[Belgian First Division A]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.granadacf.es/noticia/comunicado-el-granada-cf-acuerda-el-traspaso-de-hongla-al-royal-antwerp-fc|title=Comunicado {{!}} El Granada CF acuerda el traspaso de Hongla al Royal Antwerp FC|trans-title=Announcement {{!}} Granada CF agree the transfer of Hongla to Royal Antwerp FC|publisher=Granada CF|language=es|date=3 July 2019|accessdate=5 July 2019}}</ref> On 9 July 2021, he joined Italian [[Serie A]] club [[Hellas Verona F.C.|Hellas Verona]] on loan with a conditional purchase obligation.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[Hellas Verona F.C.|Hellas Verona]]|url=https://www.hellasverona.it/it/news/un-nuovo-volto-gialloblu-dal-royal-antwerp-arriva-il-centrocampista-martin-hongla|title=Benvenuto Martin! Dal Royal Antwerp arriva il centrocampista Hongla|date=9 July 2021|language=it}}</ref>

On 31 January 2023, Hongla returned to Spain and its top tier, after agreeing to a six-month loan deal with [[Real Valladolid]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realvalladolid.es/noticia/hongla-fortalece-la-plantilla|title=Hongla fortalece la plantilla|trans-title=Hongla bolsters the squad|publisher=Real Valladolid|language=es|date=31 January 2023|accessdate=31 January 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
Hongla played at the [[2015 African U-17 Championship]], [[2017 Africa U-20 Cup of Nations qualification]] and the [[2019 Africa U-23 Cup of Nations]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://globalsportsarchive.com/people/soccer/martin-hongla/96132/|title=Martin Hongla profile|publisher=Global Sports Archive|accessdate=2 November 2020}}</ref>

He made his debut for [[Cameroon national football team|Cameroon national team]] on 16 November 2020 in a [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations qualification Group F|Cup of Nations qualifier]] game against [[Mozambique national football team|Mozambique]], as a starter.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[ESPN]]|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/match?gameId=588026|title=Mozambique v Cameroon game report|date=16 November 2020}}</ref>
