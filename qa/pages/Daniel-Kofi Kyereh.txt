'''Daniel-Kofi Kyereh''' (born 8 March 1996) is a Ghanaian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]]<ref>{{Cite news|date=23 January 2022|title=Black Stars player admits poor performance after early AFCON exit|language=en-US|work=Adomonline.com|url=https://www.adomonline.com/black-stars-player-admits-poor-performance-after-early-afcon-exit/|access-date=6 February 2022}}</ref> for [[SC Freiburg]] and the [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national team]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.worldfootball.net/player_summary/daniel-kyereh/ |title=Daniel-Kofi Kyereh |website=worldfootball.net |publisher=HEIM:SPIEL |access-date=28 July 2018}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Wehen Wiesbaden===
On 6 June 2018, Kyereh signed with [[3. Liga]] club, [[SV Wehen Wiesbaden|Wehen Wiesbaden]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.svwehen-wiesbaden.de/aktuell/news/news/frischer-wind-aus-dem-norden!.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180905215047/https://www.svwehen-wiesbaden.de/aktuell/news/news/frischer-wind-aus-dem-norden!.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=5 September 2018|title=HERZLICH WILLKOMMEN, DANIEL-KOFI KYEREH!n|work=svwehen-wiesbaden.de}}</ref> He helped Wehen secure promotion to the [[2. Bundesliga]] with a goal in a 3–1 victory over [[FC Ingolstadt 04|Ingolstadt]] in a relegation match in May 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tellerreport.com/news/2019-05-28---second-division-promotion--wehen-wiesbaden-shocks-ingolstadt-in-the-relegation-mystery-.rJlGi8bi6N.html|title=Second division promotion: Wehen Wiesbaden shocks Ingolstadt in the relegation mystery|work=tellerreport.com}}</ref> Following the club's relegation in the [[2019–20 2. Bundesliga|2019–20 season]], Kyereh was released on 30 June 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://footballghana.com/daniel-kofi-kyereh-released-by-sv-wehen-wiesbaden|title=Daniel Kofi Kyereh released by SV Wehen Wiesbaden|work=footballghana.com}}</ref>

===FC St. Pauli===
In July 2020, Kyereh signed a three-year contract with [[FC St. Pauli]]. In his first game in 2020 he scored two late goals to secure a point.<ref>{{cite web |title="Dynamik und Tempo": St. Pauli verpflichtet Kyereh |url=https://www.kicker.de/780659/artikel/_dynamik_und_tempo_st_pauli_verpflichtet_kyereh |website=kicker |accessdate=29 July 2020 |language=German |date=29 July 2020}}</ref>

===SC Freiburg===
On 27 June 2022, Kyereh signed with [[Bundesliga]] side [[SC Freiburg]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Herzlich Willkommen, Kofi!|url=https://www.scfreiburg.com/aktuell/nachrichten/profis/news/20212022/herzlich-willkommen-kofi/|website=www.scfreiburg.com|accessdate=27 June 2022|language=German|date=27 June 2022}}</ref> He scored his first competitive goal for Freiburg in a league match against [[1. FSV Mainz 05|Mainz]] on 1 October 2022.<ref name="Nantes">{{cite web|url=https://footy-ghana.com/2022/10/kyereh-scores-for-the-second-straight-game-in-freiburg-2-0-nantes/|title= Kyereh scores for the second straight game in Freiburg 2-0 Nantes|website=Footy Ghana|date=5 October 2022|accessdate=10 January 2023}}</ref> Five days later, he scored his first goal in [[UEFA competitions|European football]] in a 2–0 win over [[FC Nantes]] in the group stage of the [[2022–23 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]].<ref name="Nantes" />

==International career==
Kyereh was born in Ghana to a German mother and Ghanaian father, and moved to Germany at the age of 1.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wiesbadener-kurier.de/sport/fussball/sv-wehen-wiesbaden/sv-wehen-wiesbaden-youngster-kyereh-hat-sich-vor-spiel-in-rostock-an-startelf-herangetastet_18981592|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200729095344/https://www.wiesbadener-kurier.de/sport/fussball/sv-wehen-wiesbaden/sv-wehen-wiesbaden-youngster-kyereh-hat-sich-vor-spiel-in-rostock-an-startelf-herangetastet_18981592|url-status=dead|archive-date=29 July 2020|title=SV Wehen Wiesbaden: Youngster Kyereh hat sich vor Spiel in Rostock an Startelf herangetastet|date=29 July 2020}}</ref> He debuted for the [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national team]] in a 1–0 [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification]] win over [[Ethiopia national football team|Ethiopia]] on 3 September 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://fifa.com/|title=FIFA|website=fifa.com}}</ref>

On 14 November 2022, Ghana manager [[Otto Addo]] selected Kyereh as part of the final squad to represent the nation at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.ghanafa.org/otto-addo-announces-squad-for-world-cup-finals |title=Otto Addo announces squad for World Cup finals |publisher=[[Ghana Football Association]] |date=14 November 2022 |access-date=14 November 2022}}</ref> He featured as a second-half substitute in all three matches as the Black Stars were eliminated in the group stage.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ghanasoccernet.com/ghana-midfielder-daniel-kofi-kyereh-presented-with-hearts-of-oak-jersey|title=Ghana midfielder Daniel-Kofi Kyereh presented with Hearts of Oak jersey|website=Ghana Soccer Net|date=12 December 2022|access-date=10 January 2023}}</ref>
