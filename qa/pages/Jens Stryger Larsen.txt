'''Jens Stryger Larsen''' ({{IPA-da|ˈjens stʁyɐ̯ ˈlɑːsn̩}}; born 21 February 1991) is a Danish professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[defender (association football)|defender]] for [[Super Lig]] club [[Trabzonspor]] and the [[Denmark national football team|Denmark national team]].

==Club career==
===Brøndby===
Stryger Larsen made his debut for [[Brøndby IF]] starting against [[FC Nordsjælland]] in November 2009 before being replaced by [[Jan Kristiansen]] after an hour. During his time at Brøndby, he quickly became very popular among Brøndby supporters because of his energy, and in the end of the 2009–2010 season and at the beginning of the 2010–2011 season he featured regularly in Brøndby's team, keeping players such as Sweden international [[Alexander Farnerud]] on the bench. In August 2010 he was named as one of the greatest talents in Denmark by the Danish media, and at 23 September, Danish football magazine [[Tipsbladet]] reported interest in him from [[Serie A]] club [[Genoa C.F.C.|Genoa]].<ref>[http://tipsbladet.dk/nyhed/superliga/broendby-stjerneskud-loefter-oejenbryn-i-udlandet Brøndby stjerneskud løfter øjenbryn i udlandet]</ref><ref>{{cite news | url = http://onside.dk/nyheder/jens-stryger-larsen-hot-i-genoa | title = Jens Stryger Larsen hot i Genoa | first = Morten | last = Espersen | work = Onside.dk | publisher = Onside | date = 21 September 2010 | access-date = 21 October 2010 | location=Copenhagen}}</ref>

===FC Nordsjælland===
On 9 July 2013, Stryger Larsen joined division rivals FC Nordsjælland on a free transfer, signing a three-year deal.<ref>{{cite news|title=FC Nordsjælland henter Jens Stryger Larsen|url=https://sn.dk/Sport/FC-Nordsjaelland-henter-Jens-Stryger-Larsen/artikel/340273|access-date=19 September 2019|publisher=sn.dk|date=9 July 2013}}</ref> He was a regular during his time in Nordsjælland, playing 30 matches in the Superliga during the [[2013–14 Danish Superliga|2013–14 season]].

===Austria Wien===
On 18 June 2014, Stryger Larsen was signed by [[Austrian Football Bundesliga]] side [[FK Austria Wien|Austria Wien]] on a four-year deal.<ref>{{cite news|title=Stryger stryger til Austria Wien|url=https://www.dr.dk/sporten/fodbold/stryger-stryger-til-austria-wien|access-date=19 September 2019|publisher=dr.dk|date=18 June 2014}}</ref> He made his debut for the club on the first day of the [[2014–15 FK Austria Wien season|2014–15 season]] in a match against [[SV Grödig]].

===Udinese===
On 24 August 2017, Stryger Larsen joined Italian [[Serie A]] club [[Udinese Calcio|Udinese]] on a four-year contract.<ref>{{cite news|title=Quattro anni di contratto per Jens Stryger Larsen|url=http://www.udinese.it/news/squadra/quattro-anni-di-contratto-per-jens-stryger-larsen|access-date=19 September 2019|publisher=udinese.it|date=24 August 2017}}</ref> He scored his first goal for ''I Bianconeri'' on 27 September against [[A.S. Roma|Roma]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Roma-Udinese 3-1: Dzeko ed El Shaarawy in gol Larsen|url=http://www.gazzetta.it/Calcio/Serie-A/Udinese/23-09-2017/serie-a-roma-udinese-3-1-el-sha-dzeko-non-c-storia-larsen-220794561716.shtml|access-date=19 September 2019|publisher=Gazzetta.it|date=23 September 2017}}</ref> He soon grew out to become a first team regular at Udinese.

On 19 January 2020, Stryger Larsen scored and assisted in a 3&ndash;2 loss against [[A.C. Milan|Milan]], his goal being scored from a tight angle after a poor clearance by Milan-goalkeeper [[Gianluigi Donnarumma]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Stryger strålede forgæves mod Kjær og co.|url=https://www.bold.dk/fodbold/nyheder/stryger-straalede-forgaeves-mod-kjaer-og-co/|access-date=19 January 2020|publisher=bold.dk|date=19 January 2020}}</ref>

===Trabzonspor===
In June 2022, Stryger Larsen joined [[Trabzonspor]] in Turkey.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=Trabzonspor|url=https://www.trabzonspor.org.tr/tr/dogucan-haspolat-eren-elmali-ve-jens-stryger-larsen-saglik-kontrolunden-gecti-16-6-2022|title=Doğucan Haspolat, Eren Elmalı ve Jens Stryger Larsen sağlık kontrolünden geçti|date=16 June 2022|access-date=2 July 2022|language=tr}}</ref>

==International career==
Stryger Larsen was called up to the senior [[Denmark national football team|Denmark national team]] to face [[Liechtenstein national football team|Liechtenstein]] and [[Armenia national football team|Armenia]] in August 2016.<ref>{{cite news|title=Hareide udtager Cornelius og Stryger Larsen|url=http://sport.tv2.dk/fodbold/2016-08-22-hareide-udtager-cornelius-og-stryger-larsen|access-date=29 August 2016|publisher=sport.tv2.dk|date=22 August 2016}}</ref> He made his debut in the friendly match against [[Liechtenstein national football team|Liechtenstein]] on 31 August 2016, and he scored the final goal in the Danish 5–0 victory.<ref>{{cite news|title=Danmark – Liechtenstein 5 – 0|url=http://www.dbu.dk/landshold/Landsholdsdatabasen/LBaseMatchInfo.aspx?matchid=8707|access-date=1 September 2016|publisher=Danish FA|date=31 August 2016}}</ref>

In June 2018 he was named in Denmark's squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Crawford |first1=Stephen |title=Revealed: Every World Cup 2018 squad - Final 23-man lists |url=https://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/revealed-every-world-cup-2018-squad-23-man-preliminary-lists/oa0atsduflsv1nsf6oqk576rb |website=Goal |accessdate=16 July 2019 |date=4 June 2018}}</ref>

In June 2021, he was included in the national team's bid for [[2020 UEFA Euro]], where the team reached the semi-finals.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://dbu.dk/nyheder/2021/maj/26-spillere-klar-til-em-for-danmark/ |title=26 spillere klar til EM for Danmark |trans-title=26 players ready for the European Championship for Denmark |work=[[Danish Football Association]] |date=25 May 2021 |access-date=25 May 2021 |language=da}}</ref>
