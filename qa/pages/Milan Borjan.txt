'''Milan Borjan''' ({{lang-sr-cyr|Милан Борјан}}; born 23 October 1987) is a [[Canada|Canadian]] professional [[Association football|soccer]] player who plays as a  [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for Slovak club [[ŠK Slovan Bratislava|Slovan Bratislava]], on loan from [[Red Star Belgrade]], and captains the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada national team]].

==Early life==
Borjan was born on 23 October 1987 in [[Knin]], former [[Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia|SFR Yugoslavia]] today Croatia, to [[Serbs of Croatia|ethnic Serb]] parents Boško and Mirjana.<ref name="B92 2022">{{cite web |url=https://www.b92.net/zivot/vesti.php?yyyy=2022&mm=12&dd=01&nav_id=2251841 |website=[[B92]] |title=Borjanova baba nije ni čula uvrede na račun golmana: "Zato sam rekla j**ala ga Krajina" |accessdate=2022-12-01 |date=2022-12-01 |language=Serbian}}</ref> Borjan got his first taste of organized soccer in the town, playing with the [[HNK Dinara|Dinara Knin]] youth side.<ref name="Borjan Dinara" /> Borjan's family lived in Knin until [[Operation Storm]] in 1995,<ref name="Milan Borjan happy to pay his dues">{{cite news |url=http://m.theglobeandmail.com/sports/soccer/milan-borjan-happy-to-pay-his-dues/article582345/?service=mobile |author=[[The Globe and Mail]] |title=Milan Borjan happy to pay his dues |newspaper=The Globe and Mail |access-date=27 May 2013 |date=24 August 2012 }}</ref> at which point his family fled to [[Belgrade]], and Borjan began playing soccer with [[FK Radnički Beograd]]. In 2000, the family emigrated to Canada, first living in [[Winnipeg, Manitoba]] for a few months,<ref name=paying>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thespec.com/sports/hamilton-region/2016/06/13/national-team-keeper-milan-borjan-is-paying-back-canada.html|title=National team keeper Milan Borjan is paying back Canada|first=Steve|last=Milton|work=[[The Hamilton Spectator]]|date=13 June 2016}}</ref> before settling in [[Hamilton, Ontario]] where his parents still reside.<ref name="Milan Borjan happy to pay his dues"/> He attended [[Glendale Secondary School]]. He played youth soccer in Canada with East Hamilton SC for six months, before joining Mount Hamilton SC.<ref name=paying/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://canpl.ca/video/milan-borjan-cpl-was-a-long-time-coming-for-hamilton-canada|first=Armen|last=Bedakian|title=Milan Borjan: CPL was a long-time coming for Hamilton, Canada|date=12 September 2018|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Early career===
Borjan went on trial with [[Boca Juniors]] in 2005, but failed to sign a contract with the club.<ref name="r1">{{cite news|last=Davidson|first=Neil|authorlink=|url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/sports/soccer/article-canadian-keeper-milan-borjan-savours-life-in-belgrade-looks-to-lift/ |title=Canadian goalkeeper Borjan savours life in Belgrade, looks to lift up Canada |agency=[[The Globe and Mail]] |date=3 September 2019 |access-date=2 March 2022 |language=en}}</ref> In 2006, he joined [[Club Nacional de Football]] in [[Montevideo]], Uruguay, playing for their youth team,<ref name="r1" /> and in July 2007, he went on trial with the Argentine club [[Club Atlético River Plate]].<ref name="ole ref">{{cite web |url=http://edant.ole.com.ar/notas/2007/07/28/01466554.html |author=Ole.com.ar |title=Ataja en todos los idiomas |access-date=2 March 2022 |date=28 July 2007 |language=es |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181020140851/http://edant.ole.com.ar/notas/2007/07/28/01466554.html |archive-date=20 October 2018 |url-status=dead}}</ref> His final South American stint was with [[Quilmes Atlético Club|Quilmes]], with whom he played from January 2008<ref>{{cite web|title=Con calor y con Batalla|url=http://www.elsolnoticias.com.ar/archivo/notas/1498-con-calor-y-con-batalla|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170907075405/http://www.elsolnoticias.com.ar/archivo/notas/1498-con-calor-y-con-batalla|archive-date=7 September 2017|access-date=30 June 2017|publisher=elsolnoticias.com}}</ref> through the end of the [[2007–08 Primera B Nacional]] season.<ref>{{cite web|title=Quilmes vuelve al trabajo en una semana donde habrá novedades|url=http://www.perspectivasur.com/herramientas/imprimir_noticia.php?id=9186|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170907034220/http://www.perspectivasur.com/herramientas/imprimir_noticia.php?id=9186|archive-date=7 September 2017|access-date=30 June 2017|publisher=perspectivasur.com}}</ref>

===Rad===
Borjan returned to Serbia in January 2009, signing with the Belgrade [[Serbian SuperLiga]] club [[FK Rad]].<ref name="B92 2012 ref">{{cite news|last=Vujčić|first=Đurađ|authorlink=|url=http://www.b92.net/sport/intervjui/intervjui.php?yyyy=2012&mm=09&nav_category=91&nav_id=644134 |title=Borjan za B92: Kanada može na SP |agency=[[B92]] |date=18 September 2012 |access-date=17 July 2021 |language=sr}}</ref> He initially had difficulty breaking into the team, signed as the third-string goalkeeper.{{Citation needed|date=February 2021}} However, due to trouble with the starting keeper's work permits,{{Citation needed|date=February 2021}} and an injury from the second string,{{Citation needed|date=February 2021}} Borjan soon became the only available goalkeeper on the club roster. He made his debut on 16 August 2009 in a 3–2 victory over [[FK Smederevo]]. By fall 2010, he was the starting keeper, with a nine-game winning streak.<ref>[http://www.rednationonline.ca/Borjan_grateful_for_opportunity_Canada_presents_january_24_11_article.shtml Borjan grateful for opportunity Canada presents] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110225174848/http://www.rednationonline.ca/Borjan_grateful_for_opportunity_Canada_presents_january_24_11_article.shtml |date=25 February 2011 }}; [[Red Nation Online]], 24 January 2011</ref> Under his tenure, FK Rad became fourth in the league, gaining them a berth in the [[2011–12 UEFA Europa League]] qualifying rounds.<ref>[http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/2011/05/24/canadian-exports-hoilett-helps-keep-blackburn-drop Canadian Exports: Hoilett helps keep Blackburn from drop] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141007132453/http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/2011/05/24/canadian-exports-hoilett-helps-keep-blackburn-drop |date=7 October 2014 }}; Major League Soccer, 23 May 2011</ref>

===Sivasspor===
Following an appearance in the [[2011 CONCACAF Gold Cup]], Borjan joined the Turkish club [[Sivasspor]],<ref>[http://www.fcrad.co.rs/av/2011/milan-borjan-u-sivasporu.htm Milan Borjan u Sivasporu] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110729025021/http://www.fcrad.co.rs/av/2011/milan-borjan-u-sivasporu.htm |date=29 July 2011 }}; FK Rad, 23 June 2011</ref> and made his debut on 11 September 2011 in a 2–1 loss against [[Karabükspor]].<ref>[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/sports/Shelton-scores-on-debut-in-Turkey-s-top-division_9687965  	Shelton scores on debut in Turkey] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120819022835/http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/sports/Shelton-scores-on-debut-in-Turkey-s-top-division_9687965 |date=19 August 2012 }}; Jamaica Observer, 12 September 2011</ref>

====Loan to Vaslui====
Due to a lack of playing time in Turkey, Borjan was loaned to Romanian club [[SC Vaslui]] in a deal that would keep him in Romania through the end of the season, with an option to purchase the player for an undisclosed transfer fee.<ref>{{cite news |title=Borjan positive on loan to FC Vaslui |url=http://www.rednationonline.ca/BorjanPositiveOnLoanToFCVaslui.aspx |website=Rednationonline.ca|date=23 February 2012 |access-date=25 February 2012 }}</ref> Borjan joined the club as the starting goalkeeper and played seven [[Shutout|clean sheet]] games in 16 starts, securing Vaslui a spot in the [[UEFA Champions League]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Across the Pong: May 21, 2012 |url=http://www.rednationonline.ca/AcrossthePondMay212012.aspx|website=Rednationonline.ca |date=23 May 2012 |access-date=24 May 2012 }}</ref>

====Return to Sivasspor====
[[File:Milan Borjan '13.JPG|thumb|190px|right|Borjan with [[Sivasspor]] in 2013]]

After returning from a successful stint in Romania, Borjan returned to the starting lineup in the third match of the 2012–13 season in a 0–0 draw against Fenerbahçe on 2 September 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://live.sporx.com/futbol/superlig/sivasspor-fenerbahce-2-dSXHBQ293847SXQ|title=Sivas'ta kör dövüşü!|work=sporx.com|date=2 September 2012}}</ref> In February 2014, following a decrease in playing time under new manager [[Roberto Carlos]], Borjan's contract with Sivasspor was terminated.<ref name="rednationonline.ca">{{cite web|url=http://www.rednationonline.ca/articles2014/milanborjanlinkedwithnapoliofseriea.aspx|title=Milan Borjan linked with Napoli of Serie A|website=Rednationonline.ca}}</ref> Speculation arose, after the termination, that a deal had been put in place for Borjan to join [[S.S.C. Napoli|Napoli]] in June, as the [[Turkish Football Association]] blocked a January move.<ref name="rednationonline.ca"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.b92.net/sport/fudbal/vesti.php?yyyy=2014&mm=02&dd=28&nav_id=817736|title=Milan Borjan ipak ne ide u Napoli|work=B92.net}}</ref>

===Ludogorets Razgrad===
Following reports that he had signed for [[Premier League of Bosnia and Herzegovina|Bosnian Premier League]] club [[FK Sarajevo|Sarajevo]] as a free agent, Borjan accepted an offer from Bulgarian club [[PFC Ludogorets Razgrad]] on 12 September 2014.<ref>{{cite news |title=Ništa od dolaska na Koševo: Borjan ide u Ludogorets: September 12, 2014 |url=http://scsport.ba/vijesti/nista-od-dolaska-na-kosevo-borjan-ide-u-ludogorets/79767 |date=12 September 2014 |access-date=12 September 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140913013818/http://scsport.ba/vijesti/nista-od-dolaska-na-kosevo-borjan-ide-u-ludogorets/79767 |archive-date=13 September 2014  }}</ref> The club had already qualified for the [[2014–15 UEFA Champions League group stage]], and four days after signing, Borjan made his club debut in a 2–1 Champions League away defeat to [[Liverpool F.C.]]<ref>{{cite news|title=Liverpool 2-1 Ludo Razgd|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/29209558|access-date=16 September 2014|work=[[BBC Sport]]|date=16 September 2014}}</ref> That game marked the first time that a Canadian international played in the group stage since [[Lars Hirschfeld]] played with [[Rosenborg BK]] in 2007.<ref>{{cite news |title=Milan Borjan set to face Liverpool in Champions League |url=http://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/milan-borjan-set-to-face-liverpool-in-champions-league-1.2767622 |date=16 September 2014 |access-date=16 September 2014 }}</ref> Razgrad released Borjan shortly thereafter, having secured first-choice goalkeeper [[Vladislav Stoyanov]] through the summer of 2015.<ref>{{cite web|title=Милан Борян си тръгна от Лудогорец|url=http://www.sportal.bg/news.php?news=528625|website=sportal.bg|publisher=Sportal|access-date=27 January 2015|language=bg|date=21 January 2015}}</ref>

===Radnički Niš===
Spurning interest from Sarajevo,<ref>{{cite news|publisher=sportsport.ba|url=http://sportsport.ba/ino_fudbal/nista-od-sarajeva-borjan-potpisao-za-radnicki-iz-nisa/153789|date=15 February 2015|access-date=16 February 2015|title=Ništa od Sarajeva: Borjan potpisao za Radnički iz Niša|newspaper=Sportsport }}</ref> Borjan signed a one-year contract with SuperLiga club [[FK Radnički Niš]] on 15 February 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mondo.rs/a770477/Sport/Fudbal/Milan-Borjan-u-Radnickom-iz-Nisa.html|date=15 February 2015|title=Nišlije se baš pojačale|website=Mondo.rs}}</ref> Due to his Serbian passport, Borjan did not count as a foreigner while playing at Radnički.<ref>[http://fkradnickinis.rs/radnicki-dobio-veliko-pojacanje-na-golu-borjan-godinu-dana-u-nisu/?lang=lat Radnički dobio veliko pojačanje na golu, Borjan godinu dana u Nišu] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150216055136/http://fkradnickinis.rs/radnicki-dobio-veliko-pojacanje-na-golu-borjan-godinu-dana-u-nisu/?lang=lat |date=16 February 2015 }} at FK Radnički Niš official website, 15 February 2015 {{in lang|sr}}</ref> He debuted in a 0–0 draw against [[Red Star Belgrade]] on 23 February,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://fkradnickinis.rs/zvezda-odnela-bod-sa-caira/|date=23 February 2015|title=Звезда однела бод са "Чаира"|access-date=23 February 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150223231040/http://fkradnickinis.rs/zvezda-odnela-bod-sa-caira/|archive-date=23 February 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> the first of three clean sheets in a row.

===Return to Ludogorets Razgrad===
Borjan turned down a contract to return to Radnički, instead returning to the [[Bulgarian A Football Group]] of Ludogorets, where he signed a three-year deal on 2 June 2015.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://mondo.rs/a800366/Sport/Fudbal/Milan-Borjan-napustio-Radnicki-iz-Nisa.html|date=2 June 2015|title=Borjan u starom jatu|newspaper=Mondo Portal}}</ref> Although he was signed as the second-string goalkeeper, Borjan began to start after Stoyanov sustained a major injury.{{Citation needed|date=February 2021}}

====Loan to Corona Kielce====
On 15 February 2017, Borjan was loaned to the [[Ekstraklasa]] club [[Korona Kielce]] through the end of the season,<ref>{{cite web|title=Milan Borjan piłkarzem Korony!|url=http://korona-kielce.pl/news,9610-milan-borjan-pilkarzem-korony.html|publisher=Korona Kielce|access-date=15 February 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170216050345/http://korona-kielce.pl/news,9610-milan-borjan-pilkarzem-korony.html|archive-date=16 February 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> where he made 14 league appearances and five clean sheets.<ref name="soccerway" />

===Red Star Belgrade===
[[File:Milan Borjan o5a2453.jpg|thumb|220px|right|Borjan playing for [[Red Star Belgrade]] in 2017]]

Three years after rumors of a transfer to Red Star Belgrade,<ref name="Borjan Wigan">{{cite news|last=Vujcic|first=Djuradj|authorlink=|url=http://rednationonline.ca/Articles2014/MilanBorjantojoinWiganAthletic.aspx |title=Milan Borjan to join Wigan Athletic? |agency=[[RedNation Online]] |date=25 July 2014 |access-date=2 March 2022 |language=en}}</ref> Borjan made the transfer official, replacing [[Filip Manojlović]] on 24 July 2017. Borjan signed a three-year contract with the club and chose the jersey number 82.<ref>{{cite web|title=Predstavljen Milan Borjan|url=http://www.crvenazvezdafk.com/sr/vest/6098/Predstavljen-Milan-Borjan|website=Red Star Belgrade official website|publisher=Red Star Belgrade|access-date=24 July 2017|language=sr|date=24 July 2017}}</ref> He made his debut in the first leg of the third qualifying round for the [[2017–18 UEFA Europa League]], keeping a clean sheet in a home victory against [[Sparta Prague]] on 27 July 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.crvenazvezdafk.com/scc/vest/6107/crvena-zvezda-sparta-prag-2-0-1-0|script-title=sr:Црвена звезда - Спарта Праг 2:0 (1:0)|website=Red Star Belgrade official website|language=sr|date=27 July 2017|access-date=27 July 2017}}</ref> He played his first [[Eternal Derby (Serbia)|eternal derby]] with the club a month later.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mozzartsport.com/vesti/sastavi-crvena-zvezda-partizan-2/176605|title=KRAJ: Crvena zvezda – Partizan 0:0 (VIDEO)|website=mozzartsport.com|language=sr|date=27 August 2017|access-date=27 August 2017}}</ref>

In his second season with the club, Borjan would help Red Star qualify for the [[2018–19 UEFA Champions League]], and would earn two clean sheets: once in the group stage opener against Napoli,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tnp.straitstimes.com/sports/football/red-star-hold-napoli-champions-league-debut|title=Red Star hold Napoli on Champions League debut|website=The New Star|date=20 September 2018}}</ref> and again in a 2–0 home win against Liverpool.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/c-zvezda-vs-liverpool/401874|title=C Zvezda 2-0 Liverpool - Match Report & Highlights|website=skysports.com|publisher=Sky UK|date=6 November 2018|access-date=28 March 2020}}</ref> In February 2019, [[Zvezdan Terzić]] announced that Borjan would sign a contract extension until 2023, and would finish his career after that. He was honoured by Red Star shortly after as the club athlete of the year.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://nationalpost.com/pmn/sports-pmn/canadian-international-goalkeeper-milan-borjan-honoured-by-red-star-belgrade|title=Canadian international goalkeeper Milan Borjan honoured by Red Star Belgrade|website=National Post|author=Canadian Press|date=5 March 2019}}</ref>

On 23 May 2022 Borjan notably scored a penalty for Red Star in their final game of the [[2021–22 Serbian SuperLiga]] against [[FK Voždovac|Voždovac]], as the club clinched their fifth-straight title.<ref>{{cite tweet|author=[[CBC Sports]]|user=cbcsports|number=1528456746070679553|title=#CanMNT goalkeeper Milan Borjan helps his club Red Star Belgrade win their 5th consecutive Serbian League title with a penalty shot goal|date=22 May 2022|access-date=2 August 2022}}</ref> In July 2022, despite earlier talk of retirement after his current deal ended, Borjan signed a contract extension until 2026.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://crvenazvezdafk.com/scc/vest/13539/Borjan-u-Zvezdi-do-kraja-karijere|title=Борјан у Звезди до краја каријере!|date=27 July 2022}}</ref>

In June 2023, Borjan revealed in an interview with [[Sportski žurnal]] that after meeting with new Red Star coach [[Barak Bakhar]], he was told by his coaching staff that he would no longer the first-choice option at the club going forward.<ref>{{cite web|title=Борјан: Нисам заслужио да ме тренер који је два дана у клубу позове и саопшти такву информацију|date=June 1, 2023|url=https://www.zurnal.rs/scc/clanak/155410/%D0%91%D0%BE%D1%80%D1%98%D0%B0%D0%BD-%D0%9D%D0%B8%D1%81%D0%B0%D0%BC-%D0%B7%D0%B0%D1%81%D0%BB%D1%83%D0%B6%D0%B8%D0%BE-%D0%B4%D0%B0-%D0%BC%D0%B5-%D1%82%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BD%D0%B5%D1%80-%D0%BA%D0%BE%D1%98%D0%B8-%D1%98%D0%B5-%D0%B4%D0%B2%D0%B0-%D0%B4%D0%B0%D0%BD%D0%B0-%D1%83-%D0%BA%D0%BB%D1%83%D0%B1%D1%83-%D0%BF%D0%BE%D0%B7%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%B5-%D0%B8-%D1%81%D0%B0%D0%BE%D0%BF%D1%88%D1%82%D0%B8-%D1%82%D0%B0%D0%BA%D0%B2%D1%83-%D0%B8%D0%BD%D1%84%D0%BE%D1%80%D0%BC%D0%B0%D1%86%D0%B8%D1%98%D1%83|first=Nikola|last=Stamenić|work=[[Sportski žurnal]]}}</ref>

====Loan to Slovan Bratislava====
In June 2023, Borjan joined [[ŠK Slovan Bratislava|Slovan Bratislava]] of the [[Niké Liga]] on loan.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skslovan.com/clanok10581-Brankarskou_posilou_ucastnik_poslednych_majstrovstiev_sveta.htm|date=July 4, 2023|title=Brankárskou posilou účastník posledných majstrovstiev sveta}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:Milan Borjan.jpg|thumb|190px|right|Borjan with the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada national team]] in 2011]]

Borjan was called to join the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada national team]] in 2010, following strong performances with [[FK Rad]].<ref>[http://www.mondo.rs/s189889/Srbin_iz_Superlige_na_golu_Kanade.html Srbin iz Superlige na golu Kanade!]; mondo.rs, 23 November 2010 {{in lang|sr}}</ref> He was called up to the Canadian senior team on 3 February 2011, and made his team debut in an [[exhibition game]] against Greece a week later.<ref>[http://canadasoccer.com/news/viewArtical.asp?Press_ID=4650 Canada announces roster for Greece friendly] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110309034932/http://www.canadasoccer.com/news/viewArtical.asp?Press_ID=4650 |date=9 March 2011 }}; Canadasoccer.com, 3 February 2011</ref> That summer, Borjan was called up to the 2011 CONCACAF Gold Cup roster, making his Gold Cup debut on 11 June 2011 in a 1–0 victory over [[Guadeloupe national football team|Guadeloupe]] at [[Raymond James Stadium]].<ref>[https://calgaryherald.com/Canada+edges+Guadeloupe/4933435/story.html Canada edges Guadeloupe] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110809101724/http://www.calgaryherald.com/Canada+edges+Guadeloupe/4933435/story.html |date=9 August 2011 }}; Calgary Herald, 12 June 2011</ref> He also recorded his first national team game on home soil at [[BMO Field]] on 1 June 2011 in an exhibition against [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador]] that ended in a 2–2 draw.

Due to the suspension of first choice goalkeeper [[Lars Hirschfeld]], Borjan started in the 0:0 home draw against [[Honduras national football team|Honduras]] in a [[2014 FIFA World Cup]] qualifier on 12 June 2012. Since the end of 2012, Borjan has been Canada's starting goalkeeper, named to the final squad in the [[2013 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2013]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/2014/09/09/canada-3-jamaica-1-international-friendly-match-recap|title=Canada 3, Jamaica 1 - International Friendly Match Recap|first=Armen|last=Bedakian|website=Mlssoccer.com|date=9 September 2014|access-date=16 February 2015|archive-date=8 September 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150908043633/http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/2014/09/09/canada-3-jamaica-1-international-friendly-match-recap|url-status=dead}}</ref> [[2017 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2017]],<ref>{{cite web|first=Dave|last=Zeitlin|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2017/06/27/canada-names-nine-mls-players-23-man-concacaf-gold-cup-roster|title=Canada names nine MLS players to 23-man CONCACAF Gold Cup roster|website=Mlssoccer.com|date=27 June 2017}}</ref> and [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.canadasoccer.com/together-we-rise-canada-soccer-announces-squad-for-the-2019-concacaf-gold-cup-p162198|date=30 May 2019|title=Together We Rise: Canada Soccer announces squad for the 2019 Concacaf Gold Cup|website=Canadasoccer.com|access-date=17 June 2019|archive-date=3 June 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190603025211/https://www.canadasoccer.com/together-we-rise-canada-soccer-announces-squad-for-the-2019-concacaf-gold-cup-p162198|url-status=dead}}</ref> He earned his first red card for Canada on 11 June 2015 in a [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification|2018 World Cup qualifying match]], handling the ball outside the box after a mistake from [[Julian de Guzman]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://matchcenter.mlssoccer.com/matchcenter/2015-06-11-dominica-vs-canada-mens-national-team/recap|title=Dominica 0, Canada 2 - World Cup Qualifying Match Recap|website=Mlssoccer.com|date=11 June 2015|access-date=12 June 2015|archive-date=3 March 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303221958/http://matchcenter.mlssoccer.com/matchcenter/2015-06-11-dominica-vs-canada-mens-national-team/recap|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Borjan remained Canada's starting goalkeeper for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – CONCACAF third round|2022 FIFA World Cup qualifiers]]. During this campaign, he played a crucial role in one of Canada's best ever results, making a goal-line save to keep Canada in the lead during stoppage time in a 2-1 victory over Mexico in Edmonton, Canada's first win against Mexico in 21 years.<ref>{{Cite web|date=17 November 2021|title=Match Analysis: CanMNT 2-1 Mexico -- World Cup Qualifying|url=https://canpl.ca/article/match-analysis-canmnt-2-1-mexico-world-cup-qualifying|access-date=17 November 2021|website=Canadian Premier League|language=en-CA}}</ref> He also kept a clean sheet against the United States in a 2–0 victory in [[Hamilton, Ontario]], after which he was named by [[Canadian Soccer Association|Canada Soccer]] as Canada's player of the month for January.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/milan-borjan-deanne-rose-canada-soccer-1.6334438 |title=Milan Borjan, Deanne Rose named Canada Soccer players of the month|website=Cbc.ca|access-date=9 February 2022}}</ref> On November 13, 2022, Borjan was named to Canada's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canada-announces-26-man-squad-for-2022-fifa-world-cup-in-qatar|date=November 13, 2022|title=Canada announces 26-man squad for 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]|first=Charlie|last=O'Connor-Clarke}}</ref> In Canada's second match against [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]], Borjan was the target of verbal taunts and abuse from Croatian supporters, with [[Canada Soccer]] filing a formal complaint with [[FIFA]] post-game.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/worldcup/fifa-croatia-disciplinary-case-1.6667609|date=November 29, 2022|title=FIFA opens disciplinary case against Croatia for fan taunts of Canadian goalkeeper Milan Borjan|work=[[CBC Sports]]}}</ref>

In June 2023, Borjan was named to Canada's final squad for the [[2023 CONCACAF Nations League Finals]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Former CPLers Loturi, Zator called up by Canada for 2023 Concacaf Nations League Finals|date=June 7, 2023|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canada-canmnt-2023-concacaf-nations-league-final-roster|first=Mitchell|last=Tierney|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]}}</ref> He played in both the Semi-final and Final as Canada finished runners-up to the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://onesoccer.ca/a/analysis-highlights-usmnt-claim-kings-of-concacaf-crown-as-canmnt-fall-flat|first=Alexandre|last=Gangué-Ruzic|work=[[OneSoccer]]|title=ANALYSIS + HIGHLIGHTS: USMNT claim Kings of Concacaf crown as CanMNT fall flat}}</ref> On June 19 Borjan was named to the final squad for the [[2023 CONCACAF Gold Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canmnt-announces-final-23-man-squad-for-concacaf-gold-cup|date=June 19, 2023|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]|title=CanMNT announces final 23-man squad for Concacaf Gold Cup|first=Charlie|last=O'Connor-Clarke}}</ref> He departed the team prior to their third match of the tournament against [[Cuba national football team|Cuba]] due to injury.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canadasoccer.com/news/milan-borjan-to-miss-remainder-of-2023-concacaf-gold-cup/|date=July 3, 2023|title=BORJAN TO MISS REMAINDER OF 2023 CONCACAF GOLD CUP}}</ref>
