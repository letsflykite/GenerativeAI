'''Leroy Aziz Sané''' ({{IPA-de|ˈliːʁɔʏ zaˈneː}};<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-06-10 |title=Copy the Penalty Challenge - FC Bayern Summer Games 2022 - Episode 1 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oGfF3EN0g74&t=16s |access-date=2023-05-09 |website=Youtube |language=German}}</ref> born 11 January 1996) is a German professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Winger (association football)|winger]] for {{German football updater|BaMunich}} club [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] and the [[Germany national football team|German national team]]. Sané is known for his exceptional pace and dribbling ability.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Is the 2021/22 Leroy Sane the best ever? |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/is-the-2021-22-leroy-sane-the-best-sane-ever-bayern-guardiola-18342 |access-date=2023-02-20 |website=bundesliga.com - the official Bundesliga website |language=en |archive-date=20 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230220004658/https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/is-the-2021-22-leroy-sane-the-best-sane-ever-bayern-guardiola-18342 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=TomAdams71 |date=2021-12-22 |title=Lothar Matthäus feels Leroy Sane has been Bayern Munich’s best player of the Hinrunde |url=https://www.bavarianfootballworks.com/2021/12/22/22849037/bayern-munich-lothar-matthaus-leroy-sane-best-player-hinrunde-julian-nagelsmann-hansi-flick-germany |access-date=2023-02-20 |website=Bavarian Football Works |language=en |archive-date=20 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230220004658/https://www.bavarianfootballworks.com/2021/12/22/22849037/bayern-munich-lothar-matthaus-leroy-sane-best-player-hinrunde-julian-nagelsmann-hansi-flick-germany |url-status=live }}</ref>

Sané made his professional debut for [[FC Schalke 04|Schalke 04]] in 2014 and transferred to Manchester City in 2016 for an initial £37 million fee. He was voted [[PFA Young Player of the Year]] in 2017–18, becoming the first and so far only German player to win the award, after helping City win the [[Premier League]] and [[EFL Cup]].

Sané made his senior international debut for Germany in November 2015 and was part of their squad that reached the semi-finals of [[UEFA Euro 2016]].

==Club career==
===Early career===
Sané began playing football in 2001 for the youth team of SG Wattenscheid 09. In 2005, he joined [[FC Schalke 04|Schalke 04]]. Three years later, he joined [[Bayer 04 Leverkusen|Bayer Leverkusen]], before returning to the Schalke youth academy (''Nachwuchsleistungszentrum'') in 2011.{{citation needed|date=September 2021}}

Sané was omitted from Germany's final 23-man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in favour of another young player, Julian Brandt, on 4 June 2018.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dw.com/en/germany-announce-final-world-cup-squad-with-manuel-neuer-without-leroy-san%C3%A9/a-44064011 |title=Germany announce final World Cup squad, with Manuel Neuer, without Leroy Sané |publisher=Deutsche Welle |access-date=4 June 2018 |archive-date=9 August 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200809080555/https://www.dw.com/en/germany-announce-final-world-cup-squad-with-manuel-neuer-without-leroy-san%C3%A9/a-44064011 |url-status=live }}</ref> In September 2018, after he was selected for the squad to face games against France and Peru, he left the team hotel after a discussion with Joachim Löw, citing "personal reasons" as his cause for leaving.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/45452552 |title=Leroy Sane: Man City winger leaves Germany team hotel after talks with Joachim Low |date=7 September 2018 |work=BBC Sport |access-date=7 September 2018 |archive-date=9 September 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180909121103/https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/45452552 |url-status=live }}</ref> It was later revealed that the reason for leaving was due to the birth of his daughter.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/45452552 |title=Leroy Sane: Man City winger announces birth of daughter after leaving Germany team hotel |date=8 September 2018 |work=BBC Sport |access-date=8 September 2018 |archive-date=9 September 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180909121103/https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/45452552 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite tweet|user=LeroySane19|last=Sané|first=Leroy|number=1038393386821210112|title=Incredibly happy to let you know about the birth of my daughter last night ❤🙏🏾 Mother and child are doing fine. 😊👍🏾|access-date=8 September 2018}}</ref>{{Primary source inline|date=June 2020}}

On 16 November 2018, he scored his first goal for Germany against [[Russia national football team|Russia]] following an assist from [[Serge Gnabry]]. The goal came in the eighth minute of the game as Germany won 3–0.<ref>{{Cite web | url = https://www.goal.com/en-ar/news/18722/main/2018/11/16/49389132/germany-3-russia-0-sane-on-target-as-lows-men-stroll | title = Germany 3 Russia 0: Sane on target as Low's men stroll | website = goal.com | date = 16 November 2018 | access-date = 3 January 2021 | first = Stephen | last = Creek | archive-date = 18 November 2018 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20181118164728/https://www.goal.com/en-ar/news/18722/main/2018/11/16/49389132/germany-3-russia-0-sane-on-target-as-lows-men-stroll | url-status = live }}</ref> On 19 May 2021, he was selected to the squad for the [[UEFA Euro 2020]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.kicker.de/em-kader-offiziell-loew-beruft-mueller-hummels-und-volland-804642/artikel|title=EM-Kader offiziell: Löw beruft Müller, Hummels und Volland|date=19 May 2021|website=kicker.de|language=de|access-date=19 May 2021|archive-date=7 June 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210607123628/https://www.kicker.de/em-kader-offiziell-loew-beruft-mueller-hummels-und-volland-804642/artikel|url-status=live}}</ref>
