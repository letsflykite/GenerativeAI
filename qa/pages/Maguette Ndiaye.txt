'''Maguette Ndiaye''' (born 1 September 1986), is a Senegalese [[Association football|football]] referee who is a listed international referee for [[FIFA]] since 2011.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2018-06-27 |title=Education & Technical - Referees by Association - FIFA.com |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180627173355/https://www.fifa.com/development/education-and-technical/referees/association=sen/index.html |access-date=2022-12-21 |website=web.archive.org}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |year=2021 |title=FIFA 2021 Refereeing International Lists |url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/73018d1c158c1fd5/original/2021-FIFA-Refereeing-International-Lists.pdf |access-date=30 January 2021 |publisher=FIFA}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://wiwsport.com/2022/02/14/portrait-maguette-ndiaye-arbitre-international-la-promesse-dune-passion-precoce/|title=Maguette Ndiaye, Arbitre International : La promesse d’une passion précoce|publisher=wiwsport.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ghanaweb.com/GhanaHomePage/SportsArchive/All-you-need-to-know-about-Maguette-Ndiaye-The-referee-at-the-centre-of-Ghana-vs-South-Africa-controversy-1414957|title=All you need to know about Maguette Ndiaye: The referee at the centre of Ghana vs South Africa controversy|publisher=ghanaweb.com}}</ref>

==Career==

Ndiaye was selected to be a referee at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]]. He previously officiated at the [[Club World Cup]], [[CAF Super Cup]], [[Africa Cup of Nations|AFCON finals]], [[FIFA Under-20 World Cup]], [[African Nations Championship]], [[CAF Champions League]] and [[CAF Confederations Cup]] matches.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://archive.ph/wip/3g9Br|title=Who is Maguette Ndiaye? Senegalese referee at the World Cup 2022}}</ref>
