'''Pedro Miguel Carvalho Deus Correia''' ({{lang-ar|بيدرو ميغيل كارفاليو ديوس كورييا}}; born 6 August 1990), known as '''Ró-Ró''' ({{lang-ar|رُو رُو}}), is a professional [[footballer]] who plays for [[Al Sadd SC]] and the [[Qatar national football team|Qatar national team]] as a [[right-back]] or a [[central defender]].

Having played lower-league football in Portugal, he appeared for [[Al Ahli SC (Doha)|Al Ahli]] and [[Al Sadd SC|Al Sadd]] in the [[Qatar Stars League]] after arriving in the country in January 2011. He won several honours with the latter, including three league titles.

Born in Portugal, Ró-Ró represented Cape Verde and Qatar at under-21 and [[Qatar national football team|senior]] levels, respectively. He was part of the squads at the [[2019 AFC Asian Cup|2019 Asian Cup]], [[2019 Copa América]], [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2021 Gold Cup]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]], winning the first of those tournaments.

==Club career==
Born in [[Algueirão–Mem Martins]], [[Sintra]], Portugal of [[Cape Verde]]an descent, Ró-Ró received his nickname in tribute to his idols, the [[Brazil national football team|Brazilian internationals]] [[Ronaldo (Brazilian footballer)|Ronaldo]] and [[Romário]].<ref name=Chosen>{{cite news|url=https://www.dn.pt/desporto/as-escolhas-do-anfitriao-15343144.html|title=Pedro Correia. Há um português entre os 26 eleitos do Qatar|trans-title=Pedro Correia. There's a Portuguese among Qatar's 26 chosen players|newspaper=[[Diário de Notícias]]|first=Isaura|last=Almeida|language=pt|date=11 November 2022|access-date=17 November 2022}}</ref> He played for several clubs as a youth, including [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]] where he spent nearly six seasons, and made his senior debut with [[S.C. Farense|Farense]] in the [[Terceira Divisão|fourth division]] in 2009; he switched the following season to another team in that tier, [[S.C. Mineiro Aljustrelense|Aljustrelense]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.dn.pt/desportos/interior/pedro-correia-representa-selecao-do-qatar-com-mundial2022-como-objetivo-10284299.html|title=Há um português a representar a seleção do Qatar com o Mundial2022 em mente|trans-title=There's a Portuguese representing the Qatar national team with the 2022World Cup in mind|newspaper=Diário de Notícias|first=Isaura|last=Almeida|language=pt|date=6 December 2018|access-date=10 January 2019}}</ref>

In January 2011, Ró-Ró left his country of birth and signed for [[Al Ahli SC (Doha)|Al Ahli]] in [[Qatar]]. He scored his first goal against [[Al Kharaitiyat SC|Al Kharaitiyat]] on 7 January 2012, but in a 2–4 loss.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dohastadiumplusqatar.com/kharaitiyat-snap-winless-run/|title=Kharaitiyat snap winless run|publisher=Doha Stadium Plus|date=7 January 2012|access-date=25 June 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120110081050/http://www.dohastadiumplusqatar.com/kharaitiyat-snap-winless-run/|archive-date=10 January 2012}}</ref>

Ró-Ró appeared in only six matches in his [[2011–12 Qatar Stars League|first full season]], and the [[Doha]]-based side was also relegated from the [[Qatar Stars League|Stars League]]. He joined [[Al Sadd SC|Al Sadd]] of the same league in 2016 and, upon his arrival, revealed his interest in playing for the [[Qatar national football team|Qatar national team]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.al-saddclub.com/English/News/Pages/Display.aspx?NewsID=1091&SportsID=1|title=Pedro: Happy to play for Al-Sadd, need to develop to win titles|publisher=Al Sadd SC|date=1 February 2016|access-date=2 February 2016}}</ref> He netted for the first time in the league for the [[Jesualdo Ferreira]]-led squad on 12 December of that year, contributing to an 8–0 home demolition of [[Umm Salal SC|Umm Salal]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.gulf-times.com/story/524491/Bounedjah-scores-brace-as-Sadd-blast-eight-past-Um|title=Bounedjah scores brace as Sadd blast eight past Umm Salal|newspaper=[[Gulf Times]]|date=12 December 2016|access-date=13 December 2016}}</ref>

At the [[2019 FIFA Club World Cup]] on home soil, Ró-Ró scored in extra time as Al Sadd won 3–1 against [[New Caledonia]]'s [[Hienghène Sport]] in the first round.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/50750229|title=Al-Sadd 3–1 Hienghène Sport|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|date=11 December 2019|access-date=17 November 2021}}</ref> Playing in an attacking [[Formation (association football)|formation]] under former teammate [[Xavi]], he was a league winner again in [[2020–21 Qatar Stars League|2020–21]], in addition to winning two [[Qatar Cup|national cups]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.co.uk/football/barcelona-espbarcelona/story/4508089/is-xavi-ready-for-barcelona-job-after-conquering-qatar-with-al-sadd|title=Is Xavi ready for Barcelona job after conquering Qatar with Al Sadd?|publisher=[[ESPN]]|first=Gabriel|last=Tan|date=28 October 2021|access-date=17 November 2021}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:Qatar - Japan, AFC Asian Cup 2019 03.jpg|thumb|Ró-Ró (left) in action for Qatar against [[Japan national football team|Japan]] in the [[2019 AFC Asian Cup Final|2019 Asian Cup Final]]]]
Ró-Ró appeared for the Cape Verde national under-21 team on 24 March 2009, playing entirely in a 2–0 [[Exhibition game#Association football|friendly]] away loss to [[Portugal national under-21 football team|Portugal]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://maisfutebol.iol.pt/torneio-da-madeira-portugal-vence-cabo-verde-entre-brindes|title=Torneio da Madeira: Portugal vence Cabo Verde entre brindes|trans-title=Madeira Tournament: Portugal defeat Cape Verde among offers|publisher=Mais Futebol|language=pt|date=24 March 2009|access-date=23 November 2022}}</ref> He also represented the nation at the [[2009 Lusofonia Games]] [[Football at the 2009 Lusofonia Games|tournament]], where his team won the [[gold medal]].<ref name=Gold>{{cite web|url=https://www.criolosports.com/index.php/en/futebol/copa-do-mundo-fifa-ex-internacional-cabo-verdiano-pedro-miguel-correia-ro-ro-vai-representar-qatar-na-copa-do-mundo|title=Copa do Mundo FIFA: Ex-Internacional cabo-verdiano Pedro Miguel Correia (Ró-Ró) vai representar Qatar na Copa do Mundo|trans-title=FIFA World Cup: Former Cape Verdean international Pedro Miguel Correira (Ró-Ró) will represent Qatar at the World Cup|publisher=Criolo Sports|language=pt|date=14 November 2022|access-date=17 November 2022}}</ref>

On 29 March 2016, Ró-Ró made his debut for Qatar, starting in a 2–0 away loss to [[China national football team|China]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification (AFC)|qualifiers]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.com/blog/football-asia/153/post/2839288/chinas-win-over-qatar-promises-to-end-years-of-heartbreak|title=China's victory over Qatar promises to end years of World Cup heartbreak|publisher=[[ESPN FC]]|date=29 March 2016|access-date=12 December 2016}}</ref> On 23 December 2017, he scored his first goal to conclude a 4–0 victory over [[Yemen national football team|Yemen]] at the [[23rd Arabian Gulf Cup]]; the side exited in the group stage in [[Kuwait]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.omanobserver.om/article/65992/Sports/qatar-thrash-yemen-bahrain-hold-iraq|title=Qatar thrash Yemen, Bahrain hold Iraq|newspaper=[[Oman Daily Observer]]|date=24 December 2017|access-date=17 November 2021}}</ref> He played all the matches at the [[2019 AFC Asian Cup]], as the tournament ended in triumph in the [[United Arab Emirates]].<ref name=JAZEERA>{{cite web|url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2019/02/joy-qatar-football-history-asian-cup-win-190202062711839.html|title=Qatar prepares for Maroons' homecoming after Asian Cup win|publisher=[[Al Jazeera]]|date=2 February 2019|access-date=4 February 2019}}</ref>

Ró-Ró was also called up for the country's guest appearances at the [[2019 Copa América]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.qfa.qa/coach-sanchez-announces-squad-for-copa-america-2019/|title=Coach Sanchez announces squad for Copa America 2019|publisher=[[Qatar Football Association]]|date=30 May 2019|access-date=9 October 2021}}</ref> and the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup]], reaching the semi-finals of the latter.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.insideworldfootball.com/2021/07/24/qatar-cling-semi-final-spot-face-gutsy-el-salvador-comeback/|title=Qatar cling on for semi-final spot in face of gutsy El Salvador comeback|publisher=Inside World Football|date=24 July 2021|access-date=9 October 2021}}</ref> At the [[2021 FIFA Arab Cup|inaugural]] [[FIFA Arab Cup]], in which Qatar came third on home turf that December, his campaign ended with a first-half injury against [[Oman national football team|Oman]] in the second group game.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://thepeninsulaqatar.com/article/03/12/2021/dramatic-winner-sends-qatar-into-quarter-finals|title=Qatar reach quarter-finals after dramatic victory over Oman|newspaper=[[The Peninsula (newspaper)|The Peninsula]]|date=3 December 2021|access-date=18 December 2021}}</ref>

Coach [[Félix Sánchez (football manager)|Félix Sánchez]] called up Ró-Ró for Qatar's hosting of the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref name=Chosen/> He made three starts for the hosts, who finished bottom of their group.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63603375|title=Qatar 0–2 Ecuador|publisher=BBC Sport|first=Shamoon|last=Hafez|date=20 November 2022|access-date=31 December 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63672200|title=Qatar 1–3 Senegal|publisher=BBC Sport|first=Shamoon|last=Hafez|date=25 November 2022|access-date=31 December 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63711993|title=Netherlands 2–0 Qatar|publisher=BBC Sport|first=Shamoon|last=Hafez|date=29 November 2022|access-date=31 December 2022}}</ref>
