'''Youri Marion A. Tielemans''' (born 7 May 1997) is a Belgian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for {{English football updater|AstonVil}} club [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]] and the [[Belgium national football team|Belgium national team]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |last= |first= |date=2023-06-10 |title=Aston Villa announce Youri Tielemans agreement |url=https://www.avfc.co.uk/news/2023/june/10/villa-announce-tielemans-agreement/ |access-date=2023-06-10 |website=Aston Villa Football Club}}</ref>

Tielemans began his career at [[R.S.C. Anderlecht|Anderlecht]], where he made 185 official appearances and scored 35 goals across four seasons. He won the [[Belgian First Division A|league title]] twice and in 2017 was chosen as the [[Belgian professional football awards|Belgian Professional Footballer of the Year]]. He is the youngest Belgian to play in the [[UEFA Champions League]], making his debut in the competition at the age of 16 years and 148 days. After two years at [[AS Monaco|Monaco]] in [[Ligue 1]], he joined Leicester in 2019, initially on loan and then for an estimated fee of £32 million. He scored the only goal of the [[2021 FA Cup final]].

Tielemans represented Belgium at several youth levels and before gaining his first [[Cap (sport)|cap]] for [[Belgium national football team|the national team]] in November 2016, aged 19. He was part of the squad that came third at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]], also featuring at [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]].

==Club career==
===Anderlecht===
[[File:Youri Tielemans 2016.jpg|thumb|upright|Tielemans playing for [[R.S.C. Anderlecht|Anderlecht]] in 2016]]

Tielemans is a youth product of [[R.S.C. Anderlecht|Anderlecht]] and signed his first professional contract with the club at the age of 16.<ref>{{cite web|last1=De Cock|first1=Bjorn|title=In the footsteps of Vincent Kompany: Youri Tielemans|url=http://www.benefoot.net/in-the-footsteps-of-vincent-kompany-youri-tielemans/|website=Benefoot|access-date=13 April 2016|date=28 October 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150913041953/http://www.benefoot.net/in-the-footsteps-of-vincent-kompany-youri-tielemans/|archive-date=13 September 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On 21 July 2013, he was included in the senior squad for the first time, remaining an unused substitute in the 1–0 win over [[KRC Genk|Genk]] in the [[2013 Belgian Super Cup]]. On 28 July 2013, he made his first-team debut in the opening round of the [[Belgian First Division A|Belgian Pro League]] season against [[K.S.C. Lokeren Oost-Vlaanderen|Lokeren]], replacing injured [[Sacha Kljestan]] after 25 minutes of a 2–3 home loss.<ref>{{cite news|title=Lokeren smeert jong Anderlecht al nederlaag aan|url=http://sporza.be/cm/sporza/matchcenter/mc_voetbal/2.28512/speeldag1/1.1685335|access-date=24 December 2016|publisher=Sporza|date=28 July 2013|language=nl|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161225080736/http://sporza.be/cm/sporza/matchcenter/mc_voetbal/2.28512/speeldag1/1.1685335|archive-date=25 December 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=28 July 2013 |title=AND 2–3 LOK {{!}} Anderlecht – Lokeren {{!}} Summary |url=https://www.flashscore.com/match/CIzXEDXn/#/match-summary/match-summary |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221125105306/https://www.flashscore.com/match/CIzXEDXn/#/match-summary/match-summary |archive-date=25 November 2022 |access-date=25 November 2022 |website=Flashscore}}</ref> This made him the fourth-youngest player in the league's history.<ref name=sky/>

On 2 October 2013, he became the youngest Belgian player to play in the [[UEFA Champions League]], starting in a match against [[Olympiacos F.C.|Olympiacos]] at the age of 16 years and 148 days.<ref>{{cite news |first=Berend |last=Scholten |title=UEFA.com's weekly wonderkid: Youri Tielemans |url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/news/newsid=2209100.html |website=UEFA.com |publisher=Union of European Football Associations |date=14 February 2015 |access-date=6 May 2015 }}</ref> He played 29 games – of which 21 starts – as Anderlecht won the [[2013–14 Belgian Pro League]], scoring to open a 3–0 win over [[Club Brugge KV|Club Brugge]] at the [[Constant Vanden Stock Stadium]] on 6 April 2014, a game in which he also assisted [[Cyriac]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Club go down 3–0 at Anderlecht|url=http://clubbrugge.be/en/news/game-reports/17958/club-gaat-met-3-0-onderuit-op-anderlecht?page=15|access-date=24 December 2016|publisher=Club Brugge|date=7 April 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161225000932/http://clubbrugge.be/en/news/game-reports/17958/club-gaat-met-3-0-onderuit-op-anderlecht?page=15#|archive-date=25 December 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> Tielemans won the [[Belgian professional football awards#Young Professional Footballer of the Year|Belgian Young Player of the Year]] award in each of his first two seasons.<ref name=sky>{{cite news|title=Who is Youri Tielemans? We profile exciting Anderlecht midfielder|url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/10052669/anderlecht-midfielder-youri-tielemans-profiled|access-date=24 December 2016|publisher=Sky Sports|date=2 November 2015}}</ref>

Tielemans scored 13 times in 37 Belgian First Division A matches as Anderlecht won the [[2016–17 Belgian First Division A]]. He won the 2017 [[Ebony Shoe Award]] for best player of African origin,<ref name="ebony">{{cite web |date=8 May 2017 |title=Youri Tielemans verzilvert topstatus Ebbenhouten Schoen |url=http://www.hln.be/hln/nl/1284/Anderlecht/article/detail/3152483/2017/05/08/Youri-Tielemans-verzilvert-topstatus-Ebbenhouten-Schoen.dhtml |website=HLN}}</ref> and was named [[Belgian professional football awards|2016–17 Belgian Professional Footballer of the Year]].<ref name=gala>{{cite news|title=Gala du Footballeur Pro: Youri Tielemans élu joueur de l'année, René Weiler sacré entraîneur!|trans-title=Professional Footballer Awards: Youri Tielemans elected player of the year, René Weiler best manager!|url=http://www.dhnet.be/sports/football/division-1a/gala-du-footballeur-pro-youri-tielemans-elu-joueur-de-l-annee-rene-weiler-sacre-entraineur-photos-videos-59233294cd70022542fa3c48|access-date=5 September 2017|publisher=DH|date=22 May 2017|language=fr}}</ref> He scored 5 goals in 15 [[2016–17 UEFA Europa League]] matches, with his club reaching the quarter-finals, and was selected in its Squad of the Season.<ref name=squad/>

===Monaco===
On 24 May 2017, Tielemans joined [[2016–17 Ligue 1]] champions [[AS Monaco FC|Monaco]] on a five-year deal for a fee of around €25 million.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Officieel: Youri Tielemans tekent contract tot 2022 bij Franse kampioen Monaco |trans-title=Official: Youri Tielemans signs contract until 2022 with French champion Monaco |url=http://www.hln.be/hln/nl/1284/Voetbal/article/detail/3166600/2017/05/24/Officieel-Youri-Tielemans-tekent-contract-tot-2022-bij-Franse-kampioen-Monaco.dhtml |publisher=HLN |date=24 May 2017 |access-date=24 May 2017|language=nl}}</ref> He made his competitive debut on 29 July in the [[2017 Trophée des Champions|Trophée des Champions]] at the [[Grand Stade de Tanger]], playing the full 90 minutes in the 2–1 loss to [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] and assisting [[Djibril Sidibé (footballer, born 1992)|Djibril Sidibé's]] opening goal.<ref>{{cite news|last1=De Beck|first1=Mike|title=Tielemans meteen goed voor splijtende assist in eerste officiële wedstrijd|trans-title=Tielemans shows his worth with a clever assist in first official appearance|url=http://www.hln.be/hln/nl/1299/Ligue-1/article/detail/3220138/2017/07/29/Tielemans-meteen-goed-voor-splijtende-assist-in-eerste-officiele-wedstrijd.dhtml|access-date=5 September 2017|publisher=HLN|date=30 July 2017|language=nl}}</ref> On 4 August, Tielemans made his [[Ligue 1]] debut in the 3–2 home win over [[Toulouse FC|Toulouse]], coming on as a substitute for [[Radamel Falcao]] in the 87th minute. He made his [[UEFA competitions|UEFA club competition]] debut for Monaco on 13 September in the [[2017–18 UEFA Champions League group stage|UEFA Champions League group]] match away to [[RB Leipzig]], playing the full 90 minutes and registering his first competitive goal for Monaco by equalizing in a 1–1 draw. On 16 September, Tielemans made his first Ligue 1 start and played the entire match in the 3–0 home win over [[RC Strasbourg Alsace|Strasbourg]], after having played a total of 56 minutes as a substitute in his first four Ligue 1 matches.<ref>{{cite news|title=Monaco and Leipzig share Champions League spoils|url=http://www.ligue1.com/autresCompetitions/article/monaco-and-leipzig-share-champions-league-spoils.htm |publisher=Ligue 1|date=13 September 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=career data of Youri Tielemans|url=https://int.soccerway.com/players/yoeri-tielemans/297294/|publisher=Soccerway|date=16 September 2017}}</ref>

''[[France Football]]'' named Tielemans in their lists of the biggest flops of the first half of the season and the season overall.<ref>{{cite news |title=Youri Tielemans dans les flops de la première partie de saison en Ligue 1 |trans-title=Youri Tielemans among the flops of the first half of the Ligue 1 season|url=https://www.7sur7.be/7s7/fr/1760/Ligue-1/article/detail/3331832/2017/12/22/Youri-Tielemans-dans-les-flops-de-la-premiere-partie-de-saison-en-Ligue-1.dhtml |access-date=7 July 2018 |publisher=7sur7 |date=22 December 2017 |language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Vens |first1=Clint |title=Recordtransfer Anderlecht opgenomen in lijst grootste flops van de Ligue 1|trans-title=Anderlecht's record transfer included in list of Ligue 1's biggest flops |url=https://www.voetbalkrant.com/nl/nieuws/lees/2018-05-20/youri-tielemans-is-opgenomen-in-een-lijstje-met-grootste-flops-van-de-ligue-1 |access-date=7 July 2018 |publisher=Voetbalkrant |date=20 May 2018 |language=nl}}</ref> He scored his first Ligue 1 goal in his 31st game on 2 September 2018, in a 3–2 home loss to [[Olympique de Marseille|Marseille]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Buron |first1=Sébastien |title=Ligue 1 : Marseille arrache la victoire à Monaco|trans-title=Ligue 1: Marseille snatch victory from Monaco |url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Ligue-1-marseille-arrache-la-victoire-a-monaco/936628 |access-date=19 May 2021 |newspaper=L'Équipe |date=2 September 2018 |language=fr}}</ref>

===Leicester City===
[[File:Youri Tielemans 2021.jpg|thumb|upright|Tielemans playing for [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] in 2021]]

On 31 January 2019, Tielemans joined [[Premier League]] club [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] on [[Loan (sports)|loan]] until the end of the season, with [[Adrien Silva]] going the other way in a swap deal.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.90min.com/posts/6285456-leicester-complete-loan-signing-of-youri-tielemans-from-monaco-as-adrien-silva-departs|title=Leicester Complete Loan Signing of Youri Tielemans From Monaco as Adrien Silva Departs|date=31 January 2019|website=90min.com|language=en|access-date=31 January 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Youri Tielemans Joins On Loan As Adrien Silva Heads To Monaco |url=https://www.lcfc.com/news/999996/youri-tielemans-joins-on-loan-as-adrien-silva-heads-to-monaco/press-release |publisher=Leicester City F.C. |date=31 January 2019 |access-date=31 January 2019}}</ref> On 9 March, Tielemans scored his first goal for the club in a 3–1 win over [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Leicester 3–1 Fulham: Jamie Vardy double earns Brendan Rodgers first win |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/47424846 |access-date=9 March 2019 |website=BBC Sport |date=9 March 2019}}</ref>

On 8 July 2019, Tielemans completed a permanent move to Leicester City, on a four-year deal for an estimated fee of £32 million.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Cooper |first1=Barry |last2=Davies |first2=Matt |last3=Kemble |first3=Jamie |title=City transfer news live – Youri Tielemans officially signs |url=https://www.leicestermercury.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/leicester-city-transfer-news-live-3065069 |newspaper=Leicester Mercury |access-date=8 July 2019 |date=8 July 2019}}</ref> He scored in a 3–1 home win over [[A.F.C. Bournemouth|Bournemouth]] on 31 August, but courted controversy with a high challenge on [[Callum Wilson (footballer, born 1992)|Callum Wilson]] that escaped punishment even after consultation with the [[video assistant referee]];<ref>{{cite news |title=Soccer-Bournemouth fume after Tielemans' rash tackle goes unpunished by VAR |url=https://uk.reuters.com/article/soccer-england-lei-bou/soccer-bournemouth-fume-after-tielemans-rash-tackle-goes-unpunished-by-var-idUKL3N25R07H |access-date=20 September 2019 |publisher=Reuters |date=31 August 2019}}</ref> referees' chief [[Mike Riley (referee)|Mike Riley]] ruled that this was an incorrect decision and Tielemans should have been sent off.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Stone |first1=Simon |title=VAR: Four incorrect decisions fail to be overturned in Premier League |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/49683036 |access-date=20 September 2019 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=12 September 2019}}</ref>

On 15 May 2021, Tielemans scored the only goal of the [[2021 FA Cup Final]] with a long-range strike against [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] in the 63rd minute, sealing Leicester's first ever [[FA Cup]] title.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57055571|title=Chelsea 0 v Leicester City 1|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|date=15 May 2021|accessdate=15 May 2021}}</ref> He was named the [[man of the match]].<ref>{{cite news|first=Phil|last=McNulty|title= Youri Tielemans produced one of the great FA Cup final goals to give Leicester City the trophy for the first time in their history with victory over Chelsea at Wembley|date=15 May 2021|website=BBC.com|url= https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/57055571|access-date=17 May 2021}}</ref>

=== Aston Villa ===
On 10 June 2023, Premier League club [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]] announced that they had reached an agreement to sign Tielemans on a free transfer, upon the expiry of his Leicester City contract on 1 July.<ref name=":0" />

==International career==
In June 2015, Tielemans was called up to the senior [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]] squad for a [[UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying|UEFA Euro 2016 qualifier]] against [[Wales national football team|Wales]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Belgium's Marouane Fellaini out of Wales tie|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/32849871|access-date=13 November 2015|publisher=BBC|date=10 June 2015}}</ref> He finally made his senior international debut on 9 November 2016, in a 1–1 [[Exhibition game#Association football|friendly]] away draw to [[Low Countries derby|rival]] [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]], replacing [[Steven Defour]] in the 82nd minute.<ref>{{cite news|title=Netherlands 1–1 Belgium|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/37896400|access-date=23 December 2016|publisher=BBC Sport|date=10 November 2016}}</ref>

Tielemans was included in manager [[Roberto Martínez]]'s 23-man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite news |title=World Cup 2018: Belgium include Vincent Kompany but Christian Benteke misses out |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44353673 |access-date=7 July 2018 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=4 June 2018}}</ref> He played four matches in the tournament, including the 2–0 win over [[England national football team|England]] in the third-place match.<ref>{{cite news |last1=McNulty |first1=Phil |title=Belgium 2–0 England |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44748604 |access-date=9 March 2019 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=14 July 2018}}</ref>

Tielemans scored his first senior goal for Belgium on 21 March 2019, in a [[UEFA Euro 2020]] [[UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying|qualifying]] match against [[Russia national football team|Russia]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/47661854 |title=Belgium 3–1 Russia: Eden Hazard scores twice in Euro 2020 qualifier |date=21 March 2019 |website=[[BBC Sport]] |access-date=21 March 2019}}</ref> He was called up for the delayed final tournament in May 2021.<ref>{{cite news |title=Belgium Euro 2020 squad: The 26 players they'll take – plus star player, manager and Euros record |url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/belgium-euro-2020-squad-the-complete-line-up-for-marchs-internationals |access-date=19 May 2021 |website=FourFourTwo |date=18 May 2021}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Tielemans began as a [[holding midfielder]], but was moved by Anderlecht manager [[Besnik Hasi]] to a more attacking role, where he scored long-range goals and was compared to [[Frank Lampard]] and [[Axel Witsel]].<ref name=sky/> A 2015 profile by [[Sky Sports]] noted his versatility as a [[Defensive midfielder|defensive]] or [[attacking midfielder]], concluding that his pace, passing and shot power suited the latter role better.<ref name=sky/> [[Paul Van Himst]], a former Anderlecht and Belgium player, noted at the same time that Tielemans had good skills with [[Long ball|long passes]].<ref name=sky/>

==Personal life==
Tielemans was born in [[Sint-Pieters-Leeuw]], Flemish Brabant.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.worldfootball.net/player_summary/youri-tielemans/ |title=Youri Tielemans: Profile |website=worldfootball.net |publisher=HEIM:SPIEL |access-date=10 July 2020}}</ref> His father is of [[Flemish people|Flemish]] descent and his mother is of [[Democratic Republic of the Congo|Congolese]] descent.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Bate|first1=Adam|last2=Terreur|first2=Kristof|title=Targets: Belgium|url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/11096/9094094/targets-belgium|access-date=30 January 2016|website=Sky Sports|date=6 January 2014}}</ref> He continued education until the age of 18, combining studies with his professional career.<ref name=sky/> He lives in the [[Leicestershire]] village of [[Quorn, Leicestershire|Quorn]] with his wife, Mendy, and their two daughters, Melina and Leana.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Davies |first1=Matt |title=Leicester City star announces arrival of new baby day after scoring for his country |url=https://www.leicestermercury.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/youri-tielemans-leicester-city-baby-3419633 |access-date=20 August 2020 |website=Leicestershire Live |date=14 October 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Percy |first1=John |title=Youri Tielemans exclusive interview: 'Lucky Leicester?
