{{Nihongo|'''Hidemasa Morita'''|守田 英正|Morita Hidemasa|born 10 May 1995}} is a Japanese professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Midfielder (association football)#Defensive midfielder|defensive midfielder]] for [[Primeira Liga]] club [[Sporting CP]] and the [[Japan national football team|Japan national team]].

== Club career ==
=== Kawasaki Frontale ===
In 2018, Morita joined [[Kawasaki Frontale]]. On February 10, he made his professional debut as a substitute in the [[Japanese Super Cup]] against [[Cerezo Osaka]]. On April 14, he made his first start in the league match against [[Vegalta Sendai]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=川崎F、家長ポスト2本など決め切れずスコアレス…仙台はJデビューの青森山田出身DF常田が完封貢献 |url=https://web.gekisaka.jp/news/jleague/detail/?242254-242254-fl |access-date=2022-12-01 |website=ゲキサカ |language=ja}}</ref> After that, he managed to secure an active role in the team, and they would go on to win the [[J1 League]] title in 2018. In 2020, he was selected in the [[J.League Best XI|J-League Best Eleven]],<ref>{{Cite web |title=[J.League Awards] Michael Olunga (Kashiwa Reysol) crowns the MVP to be the eighth top scoring player to double. Kawasaki Frontale produce nine members in the Best Eleven Players {{!}} News |url=https://www.jleague.co/news/jleague-awards-michael-olunga-kashiwa-reysol-crowns-the-mvp-to-be-the-eighth-top-scoring-player-to-double-kawasaki-frontale-produce-nine-members-in-the-best-eleven-players/ |access-date=2022-12-01 |website=J.LEAGUE |language=en}}</ref> along with 8 other Kawasaki players as they would win the J1 League title again.

=== Santa Clara ===
On 8 January 2021, it was announced that Morita would sign for Portuguese club [[C.D. Santa Clara|CD Santa Clara]], in the [[Primeira Liga]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=AcoresPRO.com |first=André Amaral- |title=Clube Desportivo Santa Clara » Oficial- Hidemasa Morita |url=https://cdsantaclara.com/oficial-hidemasa-morita/ |access-date=2022-12-01 |language=pt-pt}}</ref> When he made his debut on 25 January, he netted his first goal for the club, scoring the winner in the 89th minute of a 2–1 victory over [[Rio Ave F.C.|Rio Ave]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-01-26 |title=Hidemasa Morita nets winner in debut for Santa Clara |url=https://www.japantimes.co.jp/sports/2021/01/26/soccer/hidemasa-morita-santa-clara/ |access-date=2022-12-01 |website=The Japan Times |language=en-US}}</ref>

=== Sporting CP ===
On 1 July 2022, it was announced that he would join [[Sporting CP]] on a permanent transfer.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-07-03 |title=Morita joins Sporting CP |url=https://www.sporting.pt/en/news/football/main-team/2022-07-03/morita-joins-sporting-cp |access-date=2022-12-01 |website=www.sporting.pt |language=en}}</ref> He would go on to make his debut for the club against SC Braga on 7 August.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-08-08 |title=Draw against SC Braga opens 2022/23 season |url=https://www.sporting.pt/en/news/football/main-team/2022-08-08/draw-against-sc-braga-opens-2022/23-season |access-date=2022-12-01 |website=www.sporting.pt |language=en}}</ref> On 7 September, he made his [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] debut against [[Eintracht Frankfurt]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-09-08 |title=Soccer: Morita assists opener as Sporting stun Kamada's Frankfurt |url=https://www.nippon.com/en/news/kd940432573750034432/ |access-date=2022-12-01 |website=nippon.com |language=en}}</ref> On 30 September, he scored his first goal for the club in a win against [[Gil Vicente F.C.|Gil Vicente]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-10-01 |title=Soccer: Morita nets 1st goal for Sporting in win over Gil Vicente |url=https://www.nippon.com/en/news/kd948774498750627840/ |access-date=2022-12-01 |website=nippon.com |language=en}}</ref>

== International career ==
On 2 September 2018, [[Ryota Oshima]] and [[Hotaru Yamaguchi]] withdrew from the national team due to injury, so Morita was called up to the [[Japan national football team|Japanese national team]] in their place for the first time in his first year as a professional.<ref>{{Cite web |title=森保J初陣、山口蛍と大島僚太が不参加…代わって2選手を初招集へ |url=https://web.gekisaka.jp/news/japan/detail/?253551-253551-fl |access-date=2022-12-01 |website=ゲキサカ |language=ja}}</ref> He made his national team debut on 11 September, coming on against [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica]], the first game under new coach [[Hajime Moriyasu]].

In January 2019, he was called up to the Japanese national team for the [[2019 AFC Asian Cup|AFC Asian Cup 2019]] but withdrew due to injury.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2019-01-05 |title=Takashi Inui, Tsukasa Shiotani named as injury replacements for Japan's Asian Cup squad |url=https://www.japantimes.co.jp/sports/2019/01/05/soccer/takashi-inui-tsukasa-shiotani-named-injury-replacements-japans-asian-cup-squad/ |access-date=2022-12-01 |website=The Japan Times |language=en-US}}</ref>

In March 2021, he was called up to the national team for the first time in just under two years, and on 30 March, he scored his first goal for the national team in a 14–0 win against [[Mongolia national football team|Mongolia]] in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (AFC)|2022 FIFA World Cup Asian Qualifiers]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=AP |date=2021-03-30 |title=Japan routs Mongolia 14-0 in World Cup qualifying |url=https://www.business-standard.com/article/sports/japan-routs-mongolia-14-0-in-world-cup-qualifying-121033001054_1.html |access-date=2022-12-01 |website=www.business-standard.com |language=en}}</ref>

On 1 November 2022, Morita was named in the Japan squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].<ref>{{Cite web |last1=Duerden |first1=Greg LeaContributions from John |last2=published |first2=Ryan Dabbs |date=2022-11-23 |title=Japan World Cup 2022 squad: Team announced for tournament in Qatar |url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/japan-world-cup-2022-squad-hajime-moriyasu-names-his-latest-squad |access-date=2022-12-01 |website=fourfourtwo.com |language=en}}</ref>
