'''Ivo Grbić''' ({{IPA-sh|ǐːʋo ɡř̩bitɕ|hr}};<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hjp.znanje.hr/index.php?show=search_by_id&id=fVhlWRI%3D|title=Ìvan|quote=Ívo|website=Hrvatski jezični portal|access-date=19 March 2018|language=hr}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://hjp.znanje.hr/index.php?show=search_by_id&id=fV9kXxI%3D|title=gȑba|quote=Gr̀bić|website=Hrvatski jezični portal|access-date=19 March 2018|language=hr}}</ref> born 18 January 1996) is a Croatian professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[La Liga]] club [[Atlético Madrid]] and the [[Croatia national football team|Croatia national team]].

==Club career==
===Hajduk Split===
Born in [[Split, Croatia|Split]], Croatia, Grbić moved to the [[HNK Hajduk Split Reserves and Academy|Hajduk Split academy]] aged 9,<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Jovanović|first=Željko|date=12 March 2015|title=Ivo Grbić: San mi je jednog dana zaigrati pred punim Poljudom|url=https://www.vecernji.hr/sport/ivo-grbic-san-mi-je-jednog-dana-zaigrati-pred-punim-poljudom-994559|access-date=14 August 2020|website=Večernji list|language=hr}}</ref> where he remained for the rest of his youth career. A youth international since the U14 level, he has drawn high praise from experts such as [[Vladimir Beara]].<ref name=":1" /> At the beginning of the [[2014–15 HNK Hajduk Split season|2014–15 season]] he became the first goalkeeper of [[HNK Hajduk Split Reserves and Academy#Reserves (Hajduk II)|Hajduk Split II]] in the [[Croatian Third Football League|Treća HNL Jug]]. His first glimpse of first-team football was in October of the same year, when he acted as the reserve goalkeeper, but he only made his debut on 18 April 2015 in the home defeat to [[HNK Rijeka|Rijeka]], conceding two goals in the last 10 minutes of the game.<ref>{{Cite web|date=18 April 2015|title=Poljud: Hajduk- Rijeka 1:2|url=https://hajduk.hr/vijest/poljud-hajduk--rijeka-1-2/5656|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170917024249/http://hajduk.hr/vijest/poljud-hajduk--rijeka-1-2/5656|archive-date=17 September 2017|access-date=14 August 2020|website=HNK Hajduk Split|language=hr}}</ref>

===Lokomotiva===
After lack of opportunities, Grbić became frustrated about his club status, thus eventually rejecting the contract extension and joining [[NK Lokomotiva|Lokomotiva]] in summer 2018.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Jurišić|first=Predrag|date=14 August 2020|title=Lokomotivin as oglasio se uoči transfera života i seobe u Madrid! Na Kajzerici osigurali egzistenciju|url=https://sportske.jutarnji.hr/sn/nogomet/lokomotivin-as-oglasio-se-uoci-transfera-zivota-i-seobe-u-madrid-na-kajzerici-osigurali-egzistenciju-15013505|website=Sportske novosti|language=hr-hr}}</ref> He made his debut for Lokomotiva on 28 July 2018 in a 4–0 victory over [[NK Inter Zaprešić|Inter Zaprešić]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=28 July 2018|title=Lokomotiva - Inter 4:0 - NK Lokomotiva|url=https://nklokomotiva.hr/vijest/lokomotiva-inter-4-0-769|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190103193043/http://www.nklokomotiva.hr/vijest/lokomotiva-inter-4-0-769|archive-date=3 January 2019|access-date=14 August 2020|website=Nogometni klub Lokomotiva|language=hr-HR}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Milić|first=Jakov David|date=28 July 2018|title=Lokomotiva pobijedila Inter 4:0, Maksimir rekorder, Krstanovićev jubilej|url=https://www.goal.com/hr-ba/utakmica/lokomotiva-v-inter-zapre%C5%A1i%C4%87/izvje%C5%A1taj/3q2bxgtc3l32carv2517wc5ey|access-date=14 August 2020|website=Goal.com|language=hr}}</ref> During 2019–20 season, Grbić played a key role in Lokomotiva's run as they finished second in the [[2019–20 Croatian First Football League|Prva HNL]] and the [[2019–20 Croatian Football Cup|Cup]], qualifying for the [[2020–21 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] [[2020–21 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round#Second qualifying round|second qualifying round]].<ref name=":0" />

===Atlético Madrid===
On 20 August 2020, Grbić signed a four-year contract with [[Atlético Madrid]] in a transfer worth [[Euro|€]]3.5 million, including additional bonuses and a percentage of the next transfer.<ref>{{Cite web|date=20 August 2020|title=Agreement with NK Lokomotiva Zagreb over the transfer of Ivo Grbic.|url=https://en.atleticodemadrid.com/noticias/agreement-with-nk-lokomotiva-zagreb-over-the-transfer-of-ivo-grbi|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200820165522/https://en.atleticodemadrid.com/noticias/agreement-with-nk-lokomotiva-zagreb-over-the-transfer-of-ivo-grbi|archive-date=20 August 2020|website=Club Atlético de Madrid}}</ref> His first experience away from his homeland.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Guarda-redes Ivo Grbic é reforço do Atlético Madrid|url=https://www.record.pt/internacional/paises/espanha/atletico-madrid/detalhe/guarda-rede-ivo-grbic-e-reforco-do-atletico-madrid|access-date=27 April 2021|website=www.record.pt|language=pt-PT}}</ref> He debuted on 16 December, in a [[2020–21 Copa del Rey|Copa del Rey]] 3–0 victory over [[CD Cardassar|Cardassar]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hina|date=16 December 2020|title=Kakva večer za Hrvate u Atleticu! Vrsaljko zabio gol u povratku nakon 9 mjeseci, a na vratima Splićanin!|url=https://sportske.jutarnji.hr/sn/nogomet/primera/kakva-vecer-za-hrvate-u-atleticu-vrsaljko-zabio-gol-u-povratku-nakon-9-mjeseci-a-na-vratima-splicanin-15037398|access-date=16 December 2020|website=Sportske novosti|language=hr-hr}}</ref> At his debut for the club, he maintained a clean sheet.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Atletico Madrid's Grbic tests positive for COVID-19|url=https://www.besoccer.com/new/atletico-madrid-s-grbic-tests-positive-for-covid-19-930813|access-date=27 April 2021|website=www.besoccer.com|language=en}}</ref>

==== Lille (loan) ====
After winning the [[2020–21 La Liga]] with Atlético, Grbić was loaned out to [[2020–21 Ligue 1|French champions]] [[Lille OSC|Lille]] for a [[2021–22 Lille OSC season|season]] in desire for playtime.<ref>{{Cite web|date=19 August 2021|title=Le Croate Ivo Grbić, nouveau gardien du LOSC|url=https://www.losc.fr/actualites-foot-lille/le-croate-ivo-grbi%C4%87-nouveau-gardien-du-losc|url-status=live|access-date=19 August 2021|website=Lille OSC|language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Tesko|first=Thea|date=19 August 2021|title=Ivo Grbić ima novi klub: Stigao je u redove francuskog prvaka!|url=https://www.24sata.hr/sport/ivo-grbic-ima-novi-klub-stigao-je-u-redove-francuskog-prvaka-779636|url-status=live|access-date=19 August 2021|website=24sata|language=hr}}</ref> He made his [[2021–22 Ligue 1|Ligue 1]] debut two days later, on 21 August, in a 1–1 draw with [[AS Saint-Étienne|Saint-Étienne]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hina|date=21 August 2021|title=Grbić debitirao za Lille|url=https://www.glasistre.hr/sport/grbic-debitirao-za-lille-740994|url-status=live|access-date=21 August 2021|website=Glas Istre|language=hr}}</ref> He made his [[2021–22 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] debut on 14 September, in a goalless draw with [[VfL Wolfsburg]].

==International career==
Grbić was capped 23 times for Croatia at youth levels.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ivo Grbić - Croatian Football Federation|url=https://hns-cff.hr/en/players/98139/ivo-grbic/|access-date=14 August 2020|website=Hrvatski nogometni savez}}</ref> He was part of [[Croatia national under-21 football team|Croatia]]'s [[2019 UEFA European Under-21 Championship|UEFA Under-21 Euro 2019]] [[2019 UEFA European Under-21 Championship squads#Croatia|squad]]. He made only one appearance during the tournament, on 24 June in a 3–3 draw with [[England national under-21 football team|England]], when Croatia was already eliminated in the [[2019 UEFA European Under-21 Championship#Group C|group stage]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=S. M.|date=24 June 2019|title=U-21 HRVATSKA - ENGLESKA 3:3 Hrvatska izbjegla poraz, završila zadnja u grupi|url=https://www.index.hr/clanak.aspx?id=2095613|access-date=14 August 2020|website=Index.hr|language=hr}}</ref>

Grbić received his first call-up for the [[Croatia national football team|senior team]] on 17 August 2020 for September [[2020–21 UEFA Nations League A#Group 3|Nations League]] fixtures against [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]] and [[France national football team|France]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Kovačević|first=Davor|date=17 August 2020|title=Dalić pozvao dva debitanta za Ligu nacija: Idemo na Portugal i Francuze s najjačim sastavom|url=https://www.24sata.hr/sport/dalicev-pozvao-dva-debitanta-za-ligu-nacija-idemo-na-portugal-i-francuze-s-najjacim-sastavom-711417|access-date=19 August 2020|website=24sata|language=hr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=20 August 2020|title=Official: Ivo Grbic signs for Atletico Madrid|url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/spanish-football/2020/08/20/5f3eba8b46163ff76d8b461f.html|access-date=27 April 2021|website=MARCA in English|language=en}}</ref> However, he did not make his debut until 11 November 2021, when he started in a 7–1 victory over [[Malta national football team|Malta]] in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group H|2022 World Cup qualifying]]. On 9 November 2022, Grbić was named in [[Zlatko Dalić]]'s [[2022 FIFA World Cup squads#Croatia|26-man squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.index.hr/sport/clanak/dalic-objavio-popis-za-svjetsko-prvenstvo-u-kataru/2410337.aspx |title=Dalić objavio popis za Svjetsko prvenstvo u Kataru |website=Index.hr |access-date=2022-12-18 |date=2022-11-09 |lang=hr}}</ref> where he remained an unused substitute as Croatia finished third.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.index.hr/mobile/sport/clanak/pet-igraca-hrvatske-nije-dobilo-ni-minutu-na-svjetskom-prvenstvu/2421887.aspx |title=Pet igrača Hrvatske nije dobilo ni minutu na Svjetskom prvenstvu |website=Index.hr |date=2022-12-17 |access-date=2022-12-18 |lang=hr}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Grbić has named [[Oliver Kahn]] as his idol. His father, Josip, was the acting president of [[HNK Hajduk Split|Hajduk Split]] during the [[2010–11 HNK Hajduk Split season|2010–11 season]]. He died in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Gizdić|first=Jurica|date=10 June 2016|title=Preminuo Josip Grbić|url=https://hajduk.hr/vijest/preminuo-josip-grbic/7333|access-date=21 August 2020|website=Hajduk Split|language=hr}}</ref>

After his debut with [[Atlético Madrid]], Grbić tested positive for [[COVID-19]] but was considered  asymptomatic.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Atletico Madrid's Grbic tests positive for COVID-19|url=https://www.besoccer.com/new/atletico-madrid-s-grbic-tests-positive-for-covid-19-930813|access-date=27 April 2021|website=www.besoccer.com|language=en}}</ref>
