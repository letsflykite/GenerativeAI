'''Daniel Owen James''' (born 10 November 1997) is a professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Winger (association football)|winger]] for {{English football updater|LeedsUni}} club [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]]. Born in England, he represents the [[Wales national football team|Wales national team]].

James made his professional debut for [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]] in February 2018, and signed for [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] in June 2019. He made 74 appearances for the club, before joining Leeds United in August 2021. He was loaned to [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]] in the 2022–23 season.

He made his senior Wales debut in November 2018, having previously represented the nation at various youth levels, and was part of their squad at [[UEFA Euro 2020]]. James also helped Wales qualify for the [[FIFA World Cup]] in 2022 for the first time since 1958.

==Early life==
James was born in [[Kingston upon Hull]], East Riding of Yorkshire,<ref name="HullLive">{{cite news |url=https://www.hulldailymail.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/former-hull-city-rookie-daniel-2110936 |title=Former Hull City rookie Daniel James in line for full international honours with Wales |first=Philip |last=Buckingham |website=Hull Live |publisher=Local World |date=15 October 2018 |access-date=12 June 2019}}</ref> to Elaine and Kevan James.<ref name="Athletic">{{cite news |url=https://theathletic.co.uk/1357297/2019/11/11/the-making-of-dan-james-uniteds-flying-machine/ |title=From a 'funny little fat kid' to United's flying machine – the making of Daniel James |first=Laurie  |last=Whitwell |website=The Athletic |date=11 November 2019 |access-date=12 June 2021}}</ref> He was raised in the nearby village of [[South Cave]]<ref name="Athletic"/> and attended [[South Hunsley School]] in [[Melton, East Riding of Yorkshire|Melton]].<ref name="HullLive"/>

==Club career==
===Swansea City===
====Youth and beginnings====
A highly regarded prospect of the [[Hull City A.F.C.|Hull City]] academy side, James joined [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]] in 2014 for an initial fee of £72,000,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hulldailymail.co.uk/highly-rated-hull-city-youngster-dan-james-8216/story-21286534-detail/story.html |title=Highly-rated Hull City youngster Dan James 'lost' to Swansea City for £72,000 |newspaper=Hull Daily Mail |date=25 June 2014 |access-date=9 June 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150416004129/http://www.hulldailymail.co.uk/Highly-rated-Hull-City-youngster-Dan-James-8216/story-21286534-detail/story.html# |archive-date=16 April 2015}}</ref> immediately becoming part of their U18 academy side. By the 2016–17 season, James had become an integral part of the Swansea U23 side who gained promotion to the PDL Division 1, winning the league by an 11-point margin. Additionally, James contributed to the side winning the Premier League Cup, and reaching the semi-finals of the Premier League International Cup and the quarter-finals of the [[EFL Trophy]].

Following his impressive displays in the development squad,<ref name="auto2">{{cite web |url=https://www.swanseacity.com/teams/swansea-city/daniel-james |title=Daniel James |publisher=Swansea City A.F.C. |access-date=13 January 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190510165348/https://www.swanseacity.com/teams/swansea-city/daniel-james |archive-date=10 May 2019}}</ref> James was included in the first-team squad for the first time in January 2016 for the FA Cup defeat to [[Oxford United F.C.|Oxford United]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Higginson |first1=Marc |title=Oxford United 3–2 Swansea City |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/35216450 |access-date=7 February 2018 |website=BBC Sport |date=10 January 2016}}</ref> Although James did not make an appearance off the bench, his progress was deemed sufficient for him to be offered a new three-year contract the following week.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.swanseacity.net/news/article/swansea-city-under-21s-keston-davies-daniel-james-new-contracts-2958209.aspx |title=Swans U21s' Keston Davies and Daniel James sign new contracts |publisher=Swansea City A.F.C. |access-date=9 June 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170528115017/http://www.swanseacity.net/news/article/swansea-city-under-21s-keston-davies-daniel-james-new-contracts-2958209.aspx# |archive-date=28 May 2017}}</ref> He was again named on the bench for a [[Premier League]] fixture against [[Stoke City F.C.|Stoke City]] in October 2016.<ref name="auto">{{cite web |url=https://www.walesonline.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/who-daniel-james-what-you-12108205 |title=Who is Daniel James? What you need to know about the exciting Swansea City teenager who made the bench at Stoke City |first=Dominic |last=Booth |date=1 November 2016 |website=WalesOnline |publisher=Media Wales}}</ref>

On 30 June 2017, James signed for [[EFL League One|League One]] club [[Shrewsbury Town F.C.|Shrewsbury Town]] on [[Loan (association football)|loan]] until the end of the season.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40457596 |title=Daniel James: Swansea City winger joins Shrewsbury Town on season-long loan |website=BBC Sport |date=30 June 2017 |access-date=1 July 2017}}</ref> His loan deal was terminated by mutual consent on 31 August 2017, after he failed to force his way into the starting 11.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.shrewsburytown.com/news/2017/august/dan-james-returns-to-swansea/ |title=Dan James returns to Swansea |publisher=Shrewsbury Town F.C.}}</ref> James' only inclusion in a matchday squad was as an unused substitute in an [[EFL Cup]] first round tie against [[Nottingham Forest F.C.|Nottingham Forest]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40785845 |title=Nottingham Forest 2-1 Shrewsbury Town |date=8 August 2017 |access-date=2 September 2019 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

===Leeds United===
James signed for Premier League rivals Leeds United on 31 August 2021 for an undisclosed fee on a five-year contract, having nearly joined the club two-and-a-half years prior to the transfer.<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.leedsunited.com/news/team-news/28640/annuncement|title=Daniel James signs for Leeds United|publisher=Leeds United F.C.|date=31 August 2021}}</ref> Many news outlets were reporting the fee to be £25&nbsp;million.<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/12395604/daniel-james-leeds-agree-25m-fee-with-man-utd-for-wales-winger|title=Daniel James: Leeds agree £25m fee with Man Utd for Wales winger|website=Sky Sports|date=31 August 2021}}</ref> He scored his first Premier League goal for Leeds on 21 November 2021, in the team's 2–1 loss at the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2021/nov/21/tottenham-v-leeds-united-premier-league-live-score-updates |title=Tottenham Hotspur 2-1 Leeds United|website=Guardian.co.uk |last=Smyth|first=Rob|date=21 November 2021|access-date=21 November 2021}}</ref>
