'''José Andrés Guardado Hernández''' ({{IPA-es|anˈdɾes ɣwaɾˈðaðo}}; born 28 September 1986) is a Mexican professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[La Liga]] club [[Real Betis]].

Popularly known as ''Principito'' (Spanish for ''[[The Little Prince|Little Prince]]''),<ref name=TEN>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/es/news/4194/editoriales/2015/08/20/14607112/andr%C3%A9s-guardado-el-principito-cumple-10-a%C3%B1os|title=Andrés Guardado, el Principito cumple 10 años|trans-title=Andrés Guardado, the Little Prince celebrates 10 years|publisher=[[Goal (website)|Goal]]|first=Roberto|last=Navarro|language=es|date=20 August 2015|access-date=1 May 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.elmundo.es/deportes/2016/03/15/56e72076e2704ed7568b45c1.html|title=Guardado, 'el Principito' de Eindhoven|trans-title=Guardado, Eindhoven's 'Little Prince'|newspaper=[[El Mundo (Spain)|El Mundo]]|first=Miguel|last=Riaño|language=es|date=15 March 2016|access-date=1 May 2016}}</ref> Guardado came through the youth ranks at [[Atlas F.C.|Atlas]], making his professional debut in 2005 before signing with Spain's [[Deportivo de La Coruña|Deportivo]] two years later, where he spent five seasons. In 2012 he joined [[Valencia CF|Valencia]], with brief loan stints at [[Bayer 04 Leverkusen|Bayer Leverkusen]] and [[PSV Eindhoven|PSV]], before permanently joining the latter club in 2015 and winning two [[Eredivisie]] titles. He returned to Spain two years later with Real Betis, where he has since made over 195 appearances.

A [[Mexico national football team|Mexican international]] since 2005, Guardado represented the country in five [[FIFA World Cup|World Cups]], four [[CONCACAF Gold Cup|Gold Cups]] – winning the [[2011 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2011]], [[2015 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2015]] and [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2019]] tournaments – two [[Copa América]]s and two [[FIFA Confederations Cup|Confederations Cups]].<ref>{{cite news | url = https://elpais.com/deportes/2018/01/15/tiempo_extra/1516038138_681908.html | title = Andrés Guardado, el capitán sin brazalete |trans-title=Andrés Guardado, the captain without an armband | newspaper = [[El País]] | first = Diego | last = Mancera | language = es | date = 15 January 2018 | access-date = 4 July 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.90min.com/es/posts/6327261-los-jugadores-del-tri-con-mas-de-100-partidos-disputados|title=Los jugadores del Tri con más de 100 partidos disputados|trans-title=The ''Tri'' players with more than 100 matches played|publisher=90 Min|first=Elias|last=Manzur Carrera|language=es|date=23 March 2019|access-date=16 June 2019}}</ref> He is the [[Mexico national football team#Player records|most-capped player]] in the history of the national team with 179 appearances, and [[Mexico national football team#Player records|joint tenth]] on the all-time scorer list with 28 goals.

==Club career==
===Atlas===
Born in [[Guadalajara]], [[Jalisco]], to Andrés Manuel and Teresa (''née'' Hernández),<ref name=AndresG>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=oLaZDAAAQBAJ|title=Andrés Guardado|first=Jorge Arturo|last=Miranda Bravo|publisher=Mason Crest|date=2013|isbn=9781422292075|access-date=14 April 2020}}</ref> Guardado began playing professionally for local side [[Atlas F.C.|Atlas]], joining the team's youth system at the age of seven and going on to appear in two full [[Liga MX|Primera División]] seasons. He made his league debut on 20 August 2005, in a 3–2 home win against [[C.F. Pachuca]],<ref name=TEN/> and on 15 October, scored his first goal against [[Tigres UANL]] in a 2–1 away victory.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/reporte?leagueCup=MEX.1&id=187346|title=Otra sorpresa|trans-title=Another upset|publisher=[[ESPN Deportes]]|language=es|date=15 October 2005|access-date=2 November 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/la-liga/andres-guardado-debuto-15-anos-empezar-real-madrid|title=¡Está de fiesta! Guardado debutó hace 15 años; pudo empezar en el Real Madrid|trans-title=Celebrations! Guardado made debut 15 years ago; he could have started at Real Madrid|publisher=Medio Tiempo|first=Juan Manuel|last=Figueroa|language=es|date=20 August 2020|access-date=2 November 2020}}</ref> At the end of [[2005 Primera División de México Apertura|the tournament]], he was voted best rookie.<ref name=Rookie>{{cite web|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/liga-mx/entrega-balon-oro-apertura-2005-clausura-2006-expo-futbol|title=Se entrega El Balón de Oro del Apertura 2005 y Clausura 2006 en la Expo Futbol|trans-title=Golden Ball award for Apertura 2005 and Clausura 2006 at Expo Futbol|publisher=Medio Tiempo|language=es|date=25 July 2006|access-date=14 December 2019}}</ref>

On 19 August 2006, Guardado scored his first professional brace against [[Club América]] in a 2–0 home victory, both of them being long-range shots from outside the box.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/reporte?juegoId=198756|title=Sólo dos tiros les dio|trans-title=Two shots were enough|publisher=ESPN Deportes|language=es|date=19 August 2006|access-date=17 November 2020}}</ref> His performances in his second season led him to win back-to-back ([[Primera División de México Apertura 2006|Apertura]] and [[Primera División de México Clausura 2007|Clausura]]) awards for best full-back.<ref name=Oro>{{cite web|url=https://espndeportes.espn.com/noticias/nota?s=mex&id=585023&type=story|title=Estrellas de oro|trans-title=Golden stars|publisher=ESPN Deportes|language=es|date=7 August 2007|access-date=14 December 2019}}</ref>

In the summer of 2006, press speculation linked Guardado to several [[Serie A]] teams<ref>{{cite news | url = http://www.eluniversal.com.mx/notas/358440.html | title = Italia, tras Guardado |trans-title=Italy, following Guardado | newspaper = [[El Universal (Mexico City)|El Universal]] | first = Ángel | last = Parra | language = es | date = 29 June 2006 | access-date = 16 February 2014}}</ref> as well as [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]],<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.esmas.com/deportes/futbol/557591.html | title = Interesa Andrés Guardado al Real Madrid | trans-title = Andrés Guardado interests Real Madrid | publisher = Es Más | language = es | date = 10 August 2006 | access-date = 16 February 2014 | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20100104150657/http://www.esmas.com/deportes/futbol/557591.html | archive-date = 4 January 2010 | df = dmy-all}}</ref> but [[Deportivo de La Coruña]] moved quickly and made an offer of [[Euro|€]]7 million for 75% of his rights. An offer was accepted and the transfer was finalised on 7 July 2007, making him the most expensive ever Mexican player at the time; Atlas retained a 25% sell-on-fee as long as it held the remaining 25% of his rights.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.jornada.unam.mx/2007/07/11/index.php?section=deportes&article=a17n1dep | title = Andres Guardado se va al Deportivo La Coruña de España |trans-title = Andres Guardado goes to Spain's Deportivo de La Coruña | publisher = La Jornada | language = es | date = 10 July 2007 | access-date = 9 June 2014}}</ref>

Guardado played his final match at the [[Estadio Jalisco]] in a [[Exhibition match|friendly]] against the [[2006–07 Argentine Primera División#Torneo Clausura|Argentine]] champions [[San Lorenzo de Almagro]], featuring 30 minutes. At half-time, he was awarded the club's highest honor, the Athletic Excellence Award.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://espndeportes.espn.com/noticias/nota?s=mex&id=579589&type=story|title=Se despide de su hogar|trans-title=He bids home farewell|publisher=[[ESPN Deportes]]|language=es|date=18 July 2007|access-date=16 November 2020}}</ref>

FC Kaiserslautern]] for the [[DFB-Pokal]], playing as a left [[wing-back (association football)|wing-back]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.starmedia.com/deportes/futbol/liga-alemana/derrota-bayer-leverkusen-en-debut-mexicano-andres-guardado.html|title=Derrota del Bayer Leverkusen en debut del mexicano Andrés Guardado|trans-title=Defeat for Bayer Leverkusen in Mexican Andrés Guardado's debut|publisher=[[Starmedia]]|first=Hansel|last=Tobar|language=es|date=2 February 2014|access-date=9 June 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.today/20140609071457/http://www.starmedia.com/deportes/futbol/liga-alemana/derrota-bayer-leverkusen-en-debut-mexicano-andres-guardado.html|archive-date=9 June 2014}}</ref>

Despite rumors linking him to a possible move to [[Atlanta United]] and [[Los Angeles FC]] of the [[Major League Soccer]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.si.com/planet-futbol/2017/07/07/andres-guardado-real-betis-psv-transfer-mls-mexico|title=Despite MLS rumors and links, Mexico's Andres Guardado leaves PSV for Real Betis|publisher=[[Sports Illustrated]]|date=7 July 2017|access-date=8 July 2017}}</ref> it was announced on 27 July 2017 that Spanish club [[Real Betis]] had signed Guardado for a reported €2.3 million on a three-year deal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.football-oranje.com/guardado-leaves-psv-real-betis/|title=Guardado leaves PSV for Real Betis|publisher=Football Oranje|date=7 July 2017|access-date=7 July 2017}}</ref> The following month he was named their co-captain,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/la-liga/andres-guardado-elegido-como-capitan-del-betis|title=Andrés Guardado, elegido como capitán del Betis|trans-title=Andrés Guardado, chosen as Betis captain|publisher=Medio Tiempo|language=es|date=19 August 2017|access-date=27 November 2019}}</ref> and on 20 August he made his competitive debut, playing all 90 minutes in a 2–0 loss to Barcelona.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://siete24.mx/deportes/duro-debut-de-guardado-ante-barcelona|title=Duro debut de Guardado ante Barcelona|trans-title=Hard debut for Guardado against Barcelona|publisher=Siete 24|language=es|date=20 August 2017|access-date=5 December 2019}}</ref> He scored his first goal on 25 November, in a 2–2 home draw against [[Girona FC]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/mexicanos-mundo/2017/11/25/5a19c634ca4741613d8b4649.html|title=Guardado marca su primer gol con el Betis|trans-title=Guardado scores his first goal with Betis|newspaper=Marca|language=es|date=25 November 2017|access-date=18 January 2020}}</ref>

In December 2019, the 33-year-old Guardado renewed his contract until 2022.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://playingfor90.com/2019/12/04/andres-guardado-renews-real-betis/|title=Andres Guardado renews with Real Betis|publisher=Playing for 90|first=Ernesto|last=Hernandez|date=4 December 2019|access-date=5 December 2019}}</ref> On 4 July 2020 he made his 100th official appearance for the [[Andalusians]], captaining the side in their 1–1 league draw to [[RC Celta de Vigo]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.esto.com.mx/491567-laliga-betis-andres-guardado-cien-partidos/|title=Andrés Guardado hace historia con el Betis|trans-title=Andrés Guardado makes history with Betis|publisher=Esto|first=Yael|last=Rueda|language=es|date=6 July 2020|access-date=8 July 2020}}</ref> and becoming the first Mexican to reach that mark for three European clubs in the process.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://heraldodemexico.com.mx/deportes/2020/7/4/andres-guardado-el-primer-mexicano-con-100-partidos-en-equipos-de-europa-190007.html|title=Andrés Guardado, el primer mexicano con 100 partidos en 3 equipos de Europa|trans-title=Andrés Guardado, first Mexican with 100 matches in 3 European teams|newspaper=[[El Heraldo de México]]|first=Irvin|last=Rios|language=es|date=4 July 2020|access-date=18 October 2020}}</ref> On 11 July, he picked up a hamstring injury in a 1–0 league loss to [[Atlético Madrid]], causing him to miss the rest of [[2019–20 Real Betis season|the season]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Andrés Guardado sale lesionado en el duelo ante el Atlético y se pierde el resto de la temporada|url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/mexicanos-mundo/2020/07/12/5f0a49fd268e3e3a0c8b45ae.html|trans-title=Andrés Guardado gets injured in the match against Atlético and will miss out the rest of the season|work=Marca Claro|language=Spanish|date=11 July 2020|access-date=28 January 2021}}</ref>

Guardado started his career as a [[Midfielder#Winger|winger]] at Atlas, occasionally playing as a [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|left-back]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/la-liga/andres-guardado-satisfecho-con-su-labor-como-lateral|title=Andrés Guardado, satisfecho con su labor como lateral|trans-title=Andrés Guardado, happy with full-back role|publisher=Medio Tiempo|language=es|date=13 January 2013|access-date=14 December 2019}}</ref> He was mainly deployed in the former position at Deportivo by [[Miguel Ángel Lotina]],<ref name=Goal>{{cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/es-mx/noticias/la-evolucion-de-guardado-de-carrilero-inagotable-a-cerebro-en-el-/vhq868xp5fip10n9snoa2xfe3|title=La evolución de Guardado: de carrilero inagotable a cerebro en el mediocampo|trans-title=Guardado's evolution: from tireless wingback to midfield brain|publisher=Goal|first=Jhon|last=Guiñan|language=es|date=4 October 2018|access-date=14 December 2019}}</ref> and changed to the latter after moving to Valencia.<ref name=Lecture/> When played as a winger, he was an incisive and quick footballer, known to play deep into the field, take on defenders one-on-one and provide accurate crosses, managing to score for both club and country.<ref name=Goal/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://bleacherreport.com/articles/1943270-scouting-report-bayer-leverkusen-signing-andres-guardado-is-a-transfer-steal|title=Scouting report: Bayer Leverkusen signing Andres Guardado is a transfer steal|publisher=Bleacher Report|first=Allan|last=Jiang|date=31 January 2014|access-date=27 February 2020}}</ref>

A creative player with excellent vision,<ref name=Importance/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hitc.com/en-gb/2017/12/04/andrs-guardado-can-the-little-prince-lift-leeds-up/|title=Andrés Guardado: can the little prince lift Leeds up?|publisher=Here Is The City|first=Muhammad|last=Butt|date=4 December 2017|access-date=16 March 2020}}</ref> Guardado is also a technically proficient player known for establishing the tempo of his team.<ref name="Best 10"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2018/08/21/mexicos-andres-guardado-mulls-mls-move-notes-good-contract-offer|title=Mexico's Andres Guardado mulls MLS move, notes "good contract" offer|publisher=Major League Soccer|first=Charles|last=Boehm|date=21 August 2018|access-date=25 February 2020}}</ref> He also has good passing and [[Dribbling#Association football|dribbling]] skills, also being proficient at [[Free kick (association football)|free kicks]], penalties and holding up the ball. He also possesses a powerful and accurate shot from distance, and is also capable of aiding his team defensively due to his [[work rate]] and stamina;<ref name=Taught/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/es-mx/news/4947/f%C3%BAtbol-internacional/2015/05/10/11607882/andr%C3%A9s-guardado-de-principito-a-rey|title=Andrés Guardado, ¿de Principito a Rey?|trans-title=Andrés Guardado, from Little Prince to King?|publisher=Goal|language=es|date=10 May 2015|access-date=1 May 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://futbol.as.com/futbol/2016/03/08/portada/1457430163_974749.html|title=Los 11 'jugadores reconvertidos' más importantes de la Liga|trans-title=La Liga's 11 most important 'reconverted players'|newspaper=[[Diario AS]]|first=Edu|last=Herrero|language=es|date=8 March 2016|access-date=1 May 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.whoscored.com/Players/21019|title=Andrés Guardado|publisher=Who Scored|date=29 March 2016}}</ref> additionally, he was described as playing closer to the "Iberian midfielder archetype," focused on possession in the center of the field, winning the ball back with smart positioning instead of tackles and then pushing it forward.<ref name=Engine/>

Guardado has cited his compatriot and national teammate [[Rafael Márquez]] as an influence in his playing style and leadership.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://somosinvictos.com/2016/03/27/admiracion-total-herrera-torrado-vela-y-guardado-explican-lo-que-representa-jugar-con-rafa-marquez|title=Admiración total: Herrera, Torrado, Vela y Guardado explican lo que representa jugar con Rafa Márquez|trans-title=Total admiration: Herrera, Torrado, Vela and Guardado explain what it means to play with Rafa Márquez|publisher=Somos Invictos|language=es|date=27 March 2016|access-date=29 March 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/seleccion-mx/entrevistas/2018/05/10/5af33d09e2704e9b158b4688.html|title=Guardado: "Rafa Márquez es un espejo en el cual siempre me he tratado de reflejar"|trans-title=Guardado: "Rafa Márquez is a mirror in which I always try to reflect myself off of"|newspaper=Marca|language=es|date=10 May 2018|access-date=13 March 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Andrés Guardado se rinde en elogios para Rafa Márquez|url=https://www.esto.com.mx/272469-andres-guardado-se-rinde-en-elogios-para-rafa-marquez/|trans-title=Andrés Guardado praises Rafa Márquez|work=Esto|language=Spanish|date=30 May 2017|access-date=8 February 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Guardado married fellow Guadalajara native Briana Morales in December 2006. Five years later, they divorced.<ref name=AndresG/>

Guardado is an "honorary associate" of the ''Asociación Mexicana de Futbolistas'' (Mexican Footballers Association), created in October 2017. An association that its main goal is, according to him, to "create a dialogue with league, federation and club authorities" with the domestic footballers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.amfpro.mx/comunicados-amfpro/nace-asociacion/|title=Nace la Asociación Mexicana de Futbolistas|trans-title=Mexican Footballers Association is born|publisher=Mexican Footballers Association|language=es|date=7 October 2017|access-date=12 February 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/mexico/story/3223712/chicharito-backs-new-mexican-footballers-association|title=Chicharito throws support behind new Mexican footballers' association|publisher=ESPN|first=Tom|last=Marshall|date=8 October 2017|access-date=12 February 2020}}</ref>

Much of Guardado's first year at Betis' [[Estadio Benito Villamarín]] was captured in the [[Amazon Prime Video|Amazon Prime]] television documentary series ''Six Dreams'', in which he was one of the stars.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://en.as.com/en/2018/03/08/football/1520517172_303503.amp.html|title=Six Dreams: Amazon launch series following LaLiga stars lives|newspaper=Diario AS|first=Rafael|last=García Domínguez|date=8 March 2018|access-date=27 December 2019}}</ref>

On 1 January 2021, it was reported that Guardado tested positive for [[COVID-19]].<ref name="tested">{{cite web|title=Andrés Guardado positivo a COVID-19 en Betis|url=https://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/espana/nota/_/id/7977154/betis-guardado-lainez-covid-19-coronavirus-posibles-casos|trans-title=Andrés Guardado positive for COVID-19 in Betis|date=1 January 2021|language=es|publisher=ESPN Deportes|access-date=4 January 2021}}</ref> By 13 January, he recovered.<ref name="recovered">{{cite web|title=Andrés Guardado volvió a entrenar tras superar la covid-19|url=https://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/espana/nota/_/id/8028245/andres-guardado-regreso-entrenar-superar-covid-19|trans-title=Andrés Guardado returned to training after overcoming covid-19|date=13 January 2021|language=Spanish|publisher=ESPN Deportes|access-date=19 January 2021}}</ref>
