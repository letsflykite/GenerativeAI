'''Néstor Alejandro Araujo Razo''' (born 29 August 1991) is a Mexican professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] for [[Liga MX]] club [[Club América|América]] and the [[Mexico national football team|Mexico national team]]. He is an [[Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|Olympic gold medalist]].<ref name="bbc.com">{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/olympics/18912627 |title=Olympics football: Mexico shock Brazil to win gold |publisher=BBC |date=11 August 2012 |access-date=7 September 2014}}</ref>

Araujo began his career with [[Cruz Azul]] in 2010, and played with the club for three years before joining [[Santos Laguna]], initially in a loan deal which the club made permanent the following year. At Santos, he played in over 100 matches, winning the [[Liga MX]] on two occasions.

He competed internationally since 2011: Araujo played at the [[2011 Copa America]], [[2012 Toulon Tournament]], [[Copa America Centenario]], and the [[2017 FIFA Confederations Cup]]. He was on the Mexico team at the [[Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|2012 Summer Olympics]] that won the gold medal.<ref name="bbc.com"/>

==Club career==

===Cruz Azul===
Araujo joined [[Cruz Azul]]'s [[Cruz Azul Reserves and Academy|youth academy]] in 2007, making his way through the [[Cruz Azul Premier]] team and the under-20s.

In 2010, [[Enrique Meza]] promoted Araujo to the first team.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://ligamx.net/cancha/jugador/63500/nestor-alejandro-araujo-razo |title=Néstor Alejandro Araujo Razo |publisher=LIGA BBVA Bancomer MX |website=LigaMX.net |access-date=30 October 2017}}</ref> On 19 September, Araujo made his [[Liga MX|Primera División]] debut for Cruz Azul,<ref>{{MedioTiempo|nestor-alejandro-araujo}}</ref> playing 90 minutes in the 3–0 home victory against [[Querétaro F.C.|Querétaro]].<ref>{{cite web |publisher=Medio Tiempo |title=Néstor Araujo, feliz por su debut en triunfo celeste |author=Ballinas, Diana |url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/mexico/noticias/2010/09/19/nestor-araujo-feliz-por-su-debut-en-triunfo-celeste |language=es |date=19 September 2010}}</ref>

===Santos Laguna===
[[File:Néstor Araujo2016.jpg|180px|thumb|left|Araujo lining up for Santos Laguna in 2016.]]
On 5 June 2013, Araujo joined [[Santos Laguna]] on a year-long loan deal with an option to buy.<ref>{{cite web |title=Oficial: Néstor Araujo es nuevo jugador de Santos Laguna |url=https://www.elsiglodetorreon.com.mx/noticia/878268.oficial-nestor-araujo-es-nuevo-jugador-de-santos-laguna.html |work=El Siglo de Torreón |date=5 June 2013 |access-date=18 October 2018}}</ref> He made his league debut on 26 July against his former club Cruz Azul, playing the entire match as Santos earned a 3–2 victory. On 19 August 2014, Araujo scored his first goal for Santos in a 3–0 win over [[Correcaminos UAT|UAT]] in the [[Apertura 2014 Copa MX|Copa MX]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://estoenlinea.oem.com.mx/14310-santos-laguna-goleo-3-0-a-correcaminos-uat-en-copa-mx/ |title=Santos Laguna goleó 3-0 a Correcaminos UAT en Copa MX |access-date=20 August 2014 |work=Fox Sports}}</ref> He won his first cup with Santos Laguna following their victory over [[Club Puebla|Puebla]] in the [[Apertura 2014 Copa MX Final|final]] of the Apertura Copa MX.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/mexico/cronicas/2014/11/04/la-copa-mx-se-quedo-en-el-cielo-santos-campeon_59573 |title=La Copa MX se quedó en el cielo... Santos, Campeón |last=Ruiz |first=Alberto |access-date=5 November 2014 |website=MedioTiempo |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141106010752/http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/mexico/cronicas/2014/11/04/la-copa-mx-se-quedo-en-el-cielo-santos-campeon_59573 |archive-date=6 November 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The following year, Araujo won his first league title as Santos defeated [[Querétaro F.C.|Querétaro]] in the finals of the [[2014–15 Liga MX season#Torneo Clausura|Clausura]] tournament.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/mexico/cronicas/2015/05/31/santos-se-proclamo-campeon-ante-un-historico-gallo_61982 |title=Santos se proclamó Campeón ante un histórico Gallo |access-date=31 May 2015 |website=MedioTiempo}}</ref> On 20 July, Santos defeated [[Club América]] to win the [[2015 Campeón de Campeones]] cup; Araujo started the match and played all 90 minutes.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.elsiglodetorreon.com.mx/noticia/1135207.santos-laguna-campeon-de-campeones.html |title=¡Santos Laguna, Campeón de Campeones! |date=20 July 2015 |access-date=20 July 2015 |publisher=El Siglo de Torreón}}</ref>

In May 2018, Santos Laguna defeated [[Deportivo Toluca F.C.|Toluca]] to win the [[2017–18 Liga MX season#Torneo Clausura|Clausura tournament]] following a 3–2 aggregate score in the finals. Araujo missed the finals due to an injury he suffered while on international duty with the Mexico national team.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/2018/05/20/la-tercera-ya-no-santos-es-campeon-en-el-que-habia-sido-su-infierno |title=¡La tercera, ya no! Santos es Campeón en el que había sido su Infierno |access-date=20 May 2018 |website=Medio Tiempo}}</ref>

===Celta de Vigo===
On 14 June 2018, Araujo joined Spanish club [[RC Celta de Vigo|Celta de Vigo]] on a five-year contract.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.espn.com.mx/futbol/mexico/nota/_/id/4408604/celta-de-vigo-anuncia-fichaje-de-nestor-araujo |title=Celta de Vigo anuncia fichaje de Nestor Araujo |work=espn.com.mx |date=14 June 2018 |access-date=14 June 2018}}</ref> On 21 July, he made his debut in a friendly match against [[RCD Espanyol|Espanyol]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Néstor Araujo debutó en partido amistoso con Celta de Vigo |url=http://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/espana/nota/_/id/4566225/nestor-araujo-debuto-en-partido-amistoso-con-celta-de-vigo |work=ESPN |date=21 July 2018 |access-date=17 October 2018}}</ref>

===América===
On 24 June 2022, Araujo returned to Mexico and joined [[Club América]] on permanent basis.<ref>{{Cite tweet |user=ClubAmerica |number=1540338125653803009|title=Bienvenido al Nido.}}</ref>

==International career==
Araujo received his first international call up in 2011 during the [[2011 Copa America]]. In his international debut, he scored in a 2–1 loss against [[Chile national football team|Chile]] during their first group stage match. He would go on to appear in the rest of the group stage matches and [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]] would end up last in the group.

Araujo would be an international regular under [[Juan Carlos Osorio]], participating in various friendlies, the [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONCACAF)|CONCACAF 2018 FIFA World Cup qualifiers]], the [[Copa America Centenario]], and the [[2017 FIFA Confederations Cup]].

On 27 March 2018, during a friendly match against [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]], Araujo was forced off the pitch after 15 minutes when suffering an knee injury.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/seleccion-mx/2018/03/28/5abafe9d268e3ec4548b4657.html |title=Néstor Araujo abandona el partido ante Croacia por una lesión en la rodilla |access-date=27 March 2018 |website=MARCA Claro}}</ref> He was sidelined since then, however in May he was named in Mexico's preliminary 28-man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|World Cup]] in Russia but had to withdraw due to experiencing [[tendonitis]] during his recovery.<ref>{{cite web |title=Rafa Marquez makes Mexico's preliminary World Cup squad |url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/mexico/story/3498297/rafa-marquez-makes-mexicos-preliminary-world-cup-squad |work=ESPN |first=Tom |last=Marshall |date=14 May 2018 |access-date=18 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Mexico's 2018 World Cup roster: Who joins Chicharito & Guardado in final 23-man squad? |url=http://www.sportingnews.com/soccer/news/mexicos-2018-world-cup-roster-who-chicharito-guardado-final-23-man-squad/tyzxo1cfbypf1o1tiieriet0k |work=Sporting News |first=Jon |last=Arnold |access-date=27 June 2018}}</ref>

Araujo was part of the squad that won the [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=23 July 2021|title=Nestor Araujo: "To win the Gold Cup is an amazing feeling"|url=https://www.concacaf.com/gold-cup/article/nestor-araujo-to-win-the-gold-cup-is-an-amazing-feeling/|access-date=25 December 2021|website=Gold Cup}}</ref>

In October 2022, Araujo was named in Mexico's preliminary 31-man [[Mexico national football team|squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], and in November, he was ultimately included in the final 26-man roster.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lista de convocados de la Selección Mexicana al Mundial de Qatar 2022|url=https://www.tvazteca.com/aztecadeportes/lista-oficial-convocados-seleccion-mexicana-mundial-qatar-2022|work=Tvazteca.com|first=Omar|last=Moro|date=14 November 2022|language=Spanish}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Araujo has an older brother, [[Félix Araujo|Félix]], who is also a professional footballer.<ref>{{cite web |title=Félix Araujo vs. Néstor Araujo, disputa familiar en la Selva |url=http://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/liga-mx/2017/04/30/590514f222601d8b758b4647.html |publisher=Marca Claro |date=29 April 2017 |access-date=28 February 2018 |language=es}}</ref>
