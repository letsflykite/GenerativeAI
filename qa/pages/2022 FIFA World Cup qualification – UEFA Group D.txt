The '''2022 FIFA World Cup qualification UEFA Group D''' was one of the ten [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)|UEFA groups]] in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification|World Cup qualification]] tournament to decide which teams would qualify for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] finals tournament in Qatar.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/58b8109657d40e3d/original/ijoruwm3ly5kgz9wbu6j-pdf.pdf |title=UEFA preliminary competition for the FIFA World Cup 2022: Draw procedures |work=[[FIFA]] |format=PDF |date=22 October 2020 |access-date=22 October 2020}}</ref> Group D consisted of five teams: [[Bosnia and Herzegovina national football team|Bosnia and Herzegovina]], [[Finland national football team|Finland]], [[France national football team|France]] (the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|title holders]]), [[Kazakhstan national football team|Kazakhstan]] and [[Ukraine national football team|Ukraine]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://editorial.uefa.com/resources/0264-1108c702e548-758110ebe57c-1000/draw_result_sheet.pdf |title=FIFA World Cup Preliminary Competition – Draw Results |work=UEFA |format=PDF |date=7 December 2020 |access-date=7 December 2020}}</ref> The teams played against each other home-and-away in a [[round-robin tournament|round-robin format]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/271202c2bf80db17/original/adjlwts3o6c57g0skqor-pdf.pdf |title=UEFA preliminary competition format for the FIFA World Cup 2022 |work=FIFA |format=PDF |date=4 December 2020 |access-date=7 December 2020}}</ref>

The group winners, France, qualified directly for the World Cup finals, while the runners-up, Ukraine, advanced to the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA second round|second round (play-offs)]].
