The third round of [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONCACAF)|CONCACAF matches]] for [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification]], nicknamed the Octagonal (for the eight teams participating),<ref>{{cite news |last=Creditor |first=Avi |date=1 September 2021 |title=The Favorites, Potential Pitfalls and Features of Concacaf's World Cup Qualifying Octagonal |url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2021/09/01/concacaf-world-cup-qualifying-2022-octagonal-favorites-format-preview |work=[[Sports Illustrated]] |accessdate=2 September 2021}}</ref> was played from September 2021 to March 2022.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.concacaf.com/en/world-cup-qualifying-men/article/draw-to-determine-schedule-for-final-round-of-concacaf-qualifiers-to-the-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022 |title=Draw to determine schedule for Final Round of CONCACAF Qualifiers to the FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022 |work=[[CONCACAF]] |date=12 August 2020 |access-date=19 August 2020}}</ref> [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada]] (qualifying for the first time since [[1986 FIFA World Cup|1986]]), [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]], and the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]] qualified for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], while [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica]] advanced to the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (inter-confederation play-offs)|inter-confederation play-offs]]. [[Panama national football team|Panama]], [[Jamaica national football team|Jamaica]], [[El Salvador national football team|El Salvador]], and [[Honduras national football team|Honduras]] were eliminated in this round.

The match on 16 November 2021 between Canada and Mexico, held in [[Edmonton]], marked the coldest match played in Mexican football history, kicking off at {{convert|−9|C}} with a heavy snowstorm the day before.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/sports/soccer/the-iceteca-canada-beats-mexico-2-1-in-edmonton-chill/2021/11/16/c82be3c6-475e-11ec-beca-3cc7103bd814_story.html|title=The Iceteca: Canada beats Mexico 2-1 in Edmonton chill|access-date=17 November 2021|archive-date=17 November 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211117144037/https://www.washingtonpost.com/sports/soccer/the-iceteca-canada-beats-mexico-2-1-in-edmonton-chill/2021/11/16/c82be3c6-475e-11ec-beca-3cc7103bd814_story.html|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/mexico-mex/story/4522577/mexicos-defeat-to-canada-el-tris-coldest-game-on-record-sources|title=Sources: Canada loss Mexico's coldest on record|date=17 November 2021|website=ESPN.com}}</ref> The match was seen by 2.1&nbsp;million viewers in the United States on the day of its original broadcast, becoming Telemundo's most-watched sporting event since the [[2019 Copa América Final]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2021/11/telemundo-deportes-canada-vs-mexico-world-cup-match-most-watched-sporting-event-in-two-years-1234876554/#comment-3305553|title=Telemundo Deportes' Canada Vs. Mexico World Cup Match Is Its Most-Watched Sporting Event in Two Years|date=17 November 2021|first=Bruce|last=Haring|website=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|access-date=18 November 2021}}</ref>

==Format==
Eight teams (CONCACAF teams ranked 1 to 5 based on the [[FIFA rankings]] of July 2020, and the three winners of the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – CONCACAF second round|second round]]) played against each other home-and-away in a [[round-robin tournament|round-robin]] format for a total of 14 matches per team. The top three teams qualified for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], while the fourth-placed team advanced to the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (inter-confederation play-offs)|inter-confederation play-offs]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.concacaf.com/en/article/new-concacaf-qualifiers-announced-for-regional-qualification-to-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022 |title=New CONCACAF Qualifiers announced for regional qualification to FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022 |work=CONCACAF |date=27 July 2020 |access-date=18 August 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.fifa.com/news/european-and-intercontinental-play-off-draw-results-and-reaction |title=European and intercontinental play-off draw results and reaction |work=FIFA |date=26 November 2021 |access-date=26 November 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://en.as.com/en/2021/11/26/soccer/1637955253_226019.html |title=2022 World Cup inter-confederation play-off draw: fixtures and format |last=Allen |first=William |work=as.com |date=26 November 2021 |access-date=30 November 2021}}</ref>

==Schedule==
The third round was originally scheduled to begin in June 2021, but was postponed by the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.concacaf.com/en/world-cup-qualifying-men/article/concacaf-confirms-schedule-for-concacaf-qualifiers-for-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022 |title=CONCACAF confirms schedule for Concacaf Qualifiers for FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022 |work=CONCACAF |date=4 December 2020 |access-date=4 December 2020 |archive-date=20 January 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210120132650/https://www.concacaf.com/en/world-cup-qualifying-men/article/concacaf-confirms-schedule-for-concacaf-qualifiers-for-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The full schedule for the third round was announced on 16 June 2021,<ref>{{Cite web|date=16 June 2021|title=Eight teams confirmed for Final Round of Concacaf WCQ|url=https://www.concacaf.com/world-cup-qualifying-men/article/eight-teams-confirmed-for-final-round-of-concacaf-wcq/|access-date=16 June 2021|website=CONCACAF}}</ref> and confirmed on 9 August 2021.<ref name="kickoff_times_1-6">{{cite web|url=https://www.concacaf.com/world-cup-qualifying-men/article/concacaf-and-fifa-confirm-schedule-for-region-s-final-round-of-world-cup-qualifying/|title=Concacaf and FIFA confirm schedule for region's Final Round of World Cup Qualifying|publisher=CONCACAF|date=9 August 2021}}</ref>

==Draw==
The draw for the third round was held, along with the draw for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – CONCACAF first round|first round]], on 19 August 2020, 19:00 [[Central European Summer Time|CEST]] ([[UTC+02:00|UTC+2]]), at the [[FIFA headquarters]] in [[Zürich]], Switzerland.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/concacaf-preliminary-draw-set-to-map-qualification-pathway-towards-fifa-world-cu |title=CONCACAF preliminary draw for FIFA World Cup 2022 to take place on 19 August |work=[[FIFA]] |date=12 August 2020 |access-date=18 August 2020}}</ref>

Teams were drawn from a single pot, and then were allocated a position (from 1 to 8) to determine the match schedule. The identity of the three [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – CONCACAF second round|second round]] winners was not known at the time of the draw.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/54834f7a91d5ce00/original/ygwzvy8jhzkxjqiubexv-pdf.pdf |title=FIFA World Cup 2022 Preliminary Draw (CONCACAF): Draw procedures |work=FIFA |date=12 August 2020 |access-date=18 August 2020}}</ref>

Note: '''Bolded''' teams qualified for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|World Cup]]. ''Costa Rica'' advanced to the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (inter-confederation play-offs)|inter-confederation play-offs]].
