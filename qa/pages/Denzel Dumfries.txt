'''Denzel Justus Morris Dumfries''' (born 18 April 1996) is a Dutch professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[right-back]] or a [[right wing-back]] for {{Italian football updater|Internazionale}} club [[Inter Milan]] and the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands national team]].

Dumfries began his senior career in 2014 at [[Sparta Rotterdam]], helping them gain promotion to the [[Eredivisie]] in 2016 as the [[Eerste Divisie]] Talent of the Year. In 2017, he joined [[SC Heerenveen|Heerenveen]], before moving to [[PSV Eindhoven|PSV]] the following year, eventually becoming the club [[Captain (association football)|captain]]. In 2021, Dumfries joined Serie A side Inter Milan.

Born in the Netherlands to an Aruban father, Dumfries initially represented [[Aruba national football team|Aruba]] internationally in 2014, before switching allegiance to the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]]. He made his senior debut in 2018, and represented the Netherlands at [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Early life==
Denzel Justus Morris Dumfries was born on 18 April 1996 in [[Rotterdam]], [[South Holland]], to a [[Suriname|Surinamese]] mother and Aruban father.<ref name="Dumfries The Guardian"/> His parents named him after American actor [[Denzel Washington]].<ref name="Dumfries Backstory BBC"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.inter.it/it/news/2021/08/25/10-domande-dumfries-instagram-inter.html|title=10 DOMANDE PER DENZEL DUMFRIES|website=Inter Milan|language=it|date=25 August 2021|access-date=11 September 2021|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20210910165727/https://www.inter.it/it/news/2021/08/25/10-domande-dumfries-instagram-inter.html|archive-date=10 September 2021|url-status=live}}</ref>

Growing up, Dumfries said he was determined to make it as a professional footballer, for which he was ridiculed and mocked by his peers, due to his lack of technical skills at the time.<ref name="Dumfries RTV Rijnmond 14 June 2021"/><ref name="Never Offside1"/> But Dumfries proved his doubters wrong, saying in an interview with ''[[Voetbal International]]'': "I knew I would become a professional football player and I said it to everyone: I'm sure I'll make the Dutch national team. I knew that they all thought that doesn't make sense. But I have always proclaimed it. The people who have not seen it in me have been limited in their vision. They only looked at me at the time, but they should have looked further. How can he develop? I think everyone should look at themselves that way, not limit yourself to someone small. You have to look up, not down. What can I achieve, what is my potential and how am I going to get there? I knew exactly what I had to do to take certain steps."<ref name="Dumfries Voetbal International January 2019"/>

==Style of play==
Former teammate Daniël Esajas said about Dumfries: "As humble, almost shy as Denzel could be off the field, he was so convinced of his footballing qualities. He never doubted that he would reach the absolute top. Denzel had one quality that many of his peers lacked: an unwavering commitment. But he sometimes took it too far. He wanted to give the pass and then head the ball in himself. I can still see Denzel racing across that field. Then he would have picked up the ball from his own keeper. And there he then went with those big steps, in one straight line to the other side, directly towards his goal. Denzel wasn't even super wide, rather tall. But he was just unbearable for opponents. He went right through them like an elephant. Boom Boom Boom!".<ref name="Dumfries BND Estem"/> Dumfries said about his position: "Still, I think eliminating the opponent is the most important task of a right back. You are, after all, on the defensive. I can make quite a few meters and I like to do that. It's part of my game. With personal trainer Hans Kroon we do a lot of strength training so that we become stronger every day. I may have to act smarter sometimes, but things like that come with time. Every game I get better and I gain experience. I can use that experience, for example, to ensure that I never get into positional problems again."<ref name="Never Offside1"/> He further stated: "They used to say I had [[ADHD]], but I like to give gas, I always want to do that. I was thinking about it and thought: these are nice games that I play. I played them last year and not the year before. Very nice. It is addictive to act at the highest level."<ref>{{cite news|title=Denzel Dumfries: 'Vroeger zeiden ze altijd dat ik ADHD had'|url=https://psv.supporters.nl/nieuws/artikel/45522/Denzel-Dumfries-'Vroeger-zeiden-ze-altijd-dat-ik-ADHD-had'|website=PSV Supporters News|language=nl|date=19 November 2018|access-date=11 September 2021}}</ref>

Then manager [[Alex Pastoor]] said about Dumfries, saying: "He has a lot of conditions to be able to reach the absolute top. Just look at his physique. If you can come up eighty times per half... Those are things that ultimately the biggest clubs in the world fall for."<ref name="Dumfries FC Update May 2016"/> His performance against Ajax on 23 October 2018 led former defender [[Ben Wijnstekers]] commenting: "I think he's great. Dumfries led the fight and that was typical of PSV. He was incredibly aggressive in the duels, bit into his opponent and kept coming up. He has an enormous condition and was very irritating for Ajax. Such a player is great to have on your team. He is sometimes too hot-tempered and because of his enthusiasm he occasionally makes unnecessary violations. He has to be a little smarter in that. And in terms of standing up, he might also be able to dose a little more."<ref>{{cite news|title="Zo'n speler is heerlijk om in je ploeg te hebben"|url=https://www.goal.com/nl/nieuws/zon-speler-is-heerlijk-om-in-je-ploeg-te-hebben/v1h10n36e94m1faeul5bc6sfa|website=Goal.com|language=nl|date=24 September 2018|access-date=11 September 2021}}</ref> Italian newspaper ''[[La Gazzetta dello Sport]]'' compared Dumfries to the cryptocurrency [[Bitcoin]], writing: "In the beginning nobody trusts it, but you had better invest in it", which he responded by laughing in the press conference.<ref name="Dumfries Backstory BBC"/> ''[[De Telegraaf]]'' reporter John de Wolf commented about Dumfries, saying: "Denzel Dumfries played less conspicuously this time, not as compelling as usual. That's possible once. I worked with Denzel at Jong Sparta and later experienced as an assistant how he came to the first of Sparta. That was four years ago and when you see what he has achieved in the meantime, it is very impressive. You could then did not predict that he would achieve this. But Denzel stood out with his head power, jumping power and speed. I got to know him as a very enthusiastic boy, who had the absolute drive and passion to improve himself. It is admirable how he has done that."<ref>{{cite news|title=John de Wolf: 'Ik vind De Jong het perfecte voorbeeld van het huidige PSV'|url=https://psv.supporters.nl/nieuws/artikel/45547/John-de-Wolf-%27Ik-vind-De-Jong-het-perfecte-voorbeeld-van-het-huidige-PSV%27|website=PSV Supporters News|language=nl|date=26 November 2018|access-date=11 September 2021}}<br>{{cite news|title=De Jong perfecte voorbeeld van huidig PSV |url=https://www.telegraaf.nl/sport/2841699/de-jong-perfecte-voorbeeld-van-huidig-psv|website=De Telegraaf|language=nl|date=26 November 2018|access-date=11 September 2021}}</ref>

Both Netherlands manager Koeman and then PSV Eindhoven manager [[Mark van Bommel]] praised Dumfries' development during the 2018–19 season.<ref>{{cite news|title=Ronald Koeman: 'Denzel Dumfries heeft zich ongelooflijk goed en snel ontwikkeld'|url=https://psv.supporters.nl/nieuws/artikel/45687/Ronald-Koeman-'Denzel-Dumfries-heeft-zich-ongelooflijk-goed-en-snel-ontwikkeld'|website=PSV Supporters News|language=nl|date=2 January 2019|access-date=11 September 2021}}<br>{{cite news|title=Deel II: Koeman, Van Bommel, Ten Hag en Gio kijken naar de toekomst|url=https://www.vi.nl/pro/overig/deel-ii-koeman-van-bommel-ten-hag-en-gio-kijken-naar-de-toekomst#lees-meer|website=Voetbal International (subscription required)|language=nl|date=2 January 2019|access-date=11 September 2021}}</ref> However, he faced criticism for playing the ball back instead of pressing more forward often.<ref>{{cite news|title=Pastoor springt ook in de bres voor uitgefloten Dumfries|url=https://www.fcupdate.nl/voetbalnieuws/347288/pastoor-springt-ook-in-de-bres-voor-uitgefloten-dumfries/|website=FC Update|language=nl|date=12 March 2019|access-date=11 September 2021}}</ref> Dumfries responded to criticism, saying: "Then it's up to me to remain undisturbed. ==Personal life==
Dumfries has two cousins, [[Jason Wilnis|Jason]] and [[Jahfarr Wilnis]], who are both kickboxers.<ref name="Dumfries Eindhoven Dagblad 2019">{{cite news|title=Dumfries: 'Ik weet waar ik vandaan kom en dat is misschien ook wel mijn kracht'|url=https://www.ed.nl/psv/dumfries-ik-weet-waar-ik-vandaan-kom-en-dat-is-misschien-ook-wel-mijn-kracht~a6037eff/|website=Eindhoven Dagblad|language=nl|date=14 January 2019|access-date=11 September 2021}}</ref> In September 2020, he became a father for the first time.<ref>{{cite news|title=Lodeweges verandert niets en kiest weer voor Hateboer |url=https://www.fcupdate.nl/voetbalnieuws/372097/lodeweges-verandert-niet-en-kiest-weer-voor-hateboer-en-ake/|website=FC Update|language=nl|date=7 September 2020|access-date=11 September 2021}}</ref>

In May 2020, Dumfries was among several players and staff members who agreed to give up a month's salary, due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in the Netherlands|COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.psv.nl/nieuwspagina/artikel/psv-en-spelers-akkoord-over-tijdelijke-salarisverlaging.htm|title=PSV en spelers akkoord over tijdelijke salarisverlaging|website=PSV Eindhoven|language=nl|date=20 May 2020|access-date=11 September 2021|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20200614142729/https://www.psv.nl/nieuwspagina/artikel/psv-en-spelers-akkoord-over-tijdelijke-salarisverlaging.htm|archive-date=14 June 2020|url-status=live}}</ref> On 25 October 2020, Dumfries tested positive for [[COVID-19]] amid the [[COVID-19 pandemic in the Netherlands|pandemic in the Netherlands]].<ref name="Dumfries Positive"/>

Dumfries has spoken out about [[Racism in association football|racism]], saying: "I don't like to play the victim role, but such incidents do come. I want to hear what drives someone, to think that this is funny. I am very open about that. I want to start such a conversation. That is the only way to learn to understand each other. In a football team, jokes are part and parcel, but it is always done within frameworks and with respect. It is binding, we also show interest in each other, regardless of origin or religion. That is what makes a team and that should be the case for the whole of society apply."<ref>{{cite news|title=Dumfries over racisme: "Ik wil zo'n gesprek dan aangaan"|url=https://www.fcupdate.nl/voetbalnieuws/379326/dumfries-over-racisme-ik-wil-zo-n-gesprek-dan-aangaan/|website=FC Update|language=nl|date=30 January 2021|access-date=11 September 2021}}</ref>
