'''Celso Borges Mora''' (born 27 May 1988), is a Costa Rican professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Midfielder#Central midfielder|central midfielder]] for [[Liga FPD]] club [[Liga Deportiva Alajuelense|Alajuelense]] and the [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica national team]]. With [[List of men's footballers with 100 or more international caps|163 international appearances]], he is the most capped player in Costa Rica's history.

==Early years==
His father is [[Alexandre Guimarães]],<ref>{{cite web|publisher=FIFA.com|title=Celso Borges, new Tico idol|url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/newsid=1022196.html|date=10 February 2009|access-date=1 July 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121104141718/http://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/newsid=1022196.html|archive-date=4 November 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> Brazilian-born, [[Costa Rica]]n-raised ex-[[association football|footballer]] and coach. He was born in Costa Rica's capital city San José and during his highschool years he attended Sistema Educativo Saint Clare Highschool, where he also took part in the school's team and was taught everything about soccer by Costa Rican football legend don Juan Varela. His studies were often interrupted by his football training and playing outside the country.

==Club career==
===Saprissa===
At the age of 18, Borges made his professional debut for Saprissa on 15 January 2006.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=Saprissa |title=Celso Borges |url=http://www.saprissa.co.cr/content/articles/87/1/Celso-Borges-Mora/Page1.html |access-date=1 July 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080822033423/http://www.saprissa.co.cr/content/articles/87/1/Celso-Borges-Mora/Page1.html |archive-date=22 August 2008 |url-status=dead}}</ref> He won five national championships with Saprissa.

===Fredrikstad FK===
On 18 January 2009, [[Fredrikstad FK]] sporting director Tor-Kristian Karlsen confirmed that Borges had signed for them, joining on 1 July when Borges's contract with Saprissa expired. [[Fredrikstad FK|Fredrikstad]] later agreed to pay 1 million [[Norwegian Kroner|kroner]] (€125,000 ) to [[Deportivo Saprissa]] so Borges could join the team immediately and play for [[Fredrikstad FK|Fredrikstad]], when the season started on 14 April, against the former league champion [[Stabæk]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=VG.no|title=Fredrikstad sign "young ballack"|url=http://www.vg.no/pub/vgart.hbs?artid=546334|date=18 January 2009|access-date=1 July 2011|language=no}}</ref> In March 2011, Borges stated he would leave Fredrikstad during or after the 2011 season.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=TV2.no|title=Borges to leave FFK|url=http://www.tv2.no/sport/fotball/celso-borges-forlater-fredrikstad-3441545.html|date=14 March 2011|access-date=1 July 2011|language=no}}</ref>

===AIK===
On 1 January 2012, it was confirmed that Borges, as a free agent, was to leave [[Fredrikstad FK]] signing a three-year contract with the Swedish football club [[AIK Fotboll|AIK Stockholm]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=aftonbladet.se|title=Bekräftat: AIK värvar landslagsstjärna|url=http://www.aftonbladet.se/sportbladet/fotboll/sverige/allsvenskan/aik/article14152594.ab|date=1 January 2012|access-date=1 January 2012|language=sv}}</ref>

He received the vacant number 10 jersey, last worn by [[Martin Kayongo-Mutumba]].

On 1 April 2012, Borges made his first match for [[AIK Fotboll|AIK]], in a match against [[Mjällby AIF]]. He got his first goal for the club in the next game, scoring the game-winning goal against [[Kalmar FF]]. During the first half of the season he was used as a striker by manager [[Andreas Alm]]. But after the summer vacation he dropped down and started playing as a central midfielder and subsequently was the team's first choice in central midfield. He finished the season ending up as the team's best goalscorer.

===Deportivo La Coruña===
On 14 January 2015, Borges joined [[Deportivo de La Coruña]], signing with them until the end of [[2014-15 La Liga|the season]], with an option of a two-year extension.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.canaldeportivo.com/servlet/es.iris.servlets.Noticias?accion=4&ver=1&nid=29497|title= Celso Borges nuevo jugador del Deportivo|trans-title=Celso Borges new player of Deportivo|publisher=Deportivo's official website|language=es|date=14 January 2015|access-date=31 January 2015}}</ref> He scored twice in his first official appearance for ''Dépor'', having started in a 1–2 away win against [[Rayo Vallecano]] on 30 January which also marked his [[La Liga]] debut.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.canaldeportivo.com/servlet/es.iris.servlets.Noticias?accion=4&ver=1&nid=29622&mid=12|title=Celso Borges da pura vida al Dépor en Vallecas (1-2)|trans-title=Celso Borges gives pure life to Depor in Vallecas (1-2)|publisher=Deportivo's official website|language=es|date=30 January 2015|access-date=31 January 2015}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:Bras-Cos (21).jpg|thumb|right|Borges disputing a ball against Brazil's [[Gabriel Jesus]]]]Borges currently holds the appearance record for the [[Costa Rica national football team]] with 163 [[Cap (association football)|caps]], scoring 27 goals.<ref name=wc>{{cite news|url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/players/player=236530/index.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140616194859/http://www.fifa.com/worldcup/players/player=236530/index.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=16 June 2014|title=Celso BORGES|access-date=30 June 2014|work=FIFA}}</ref> He has represented his country in over 50 [[FIFA World Cup qualification]] matches<ref name="FIFA" /> and at the [[2014 FIFA World Cup|2014]], [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] finals. He has also played at the [[2011 Copa Centroamericana|2011]]<ref>[https://www.rsssf.org/tablesg/gold-cam2011.html Copa Centroamericana 2011 (UNCAF Nations Cup)] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131105045831/http://www.rsssf.com/tablesg/gold-cam2011.html |date=5 November 2013 }} - RSSSF</ref> and [[2013 Copa Centroamericana]],<ref>[https://www.rsssf.org/tablesg/gold-cam2013.html Copa Centroamericana 2013 (UNCAF Nations Cup)] - RSSSF</ref> as well as at the [[2009 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2009]],<ref>[https://www.rsssf.org/tables/09gc-full.html CONCACAF Championship, Gold Cup 2009 - Full Details] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140726152032/http://www.rsssf.com/tables/09gc-full.html |date=26 July 2014 }} - RSSSF</ref> [[2011 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2011]],<ref>[http://www.rsssf.com/tables/11gc-full.html CONCACAF Championship, Gold Cup 2011 - Full Details]{{dead link|date=February 2023|bot=medic}}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}  - RSSSF</ref> [[2013 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2013]],<ref>[http://www.rsssf.com/tables/13gc-full.html CONCACAF Championship, Gold Cup 2013 - Full Details]{{dead link|date=February 2023|bot=medic}}{{cbignore|bot=medic}} - RSSSF</ref> [[2015 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2015]], [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2019]], [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2021]] and [[2023 CONCACAF Gold Cup]]s, and the [[Copa América Centenario]].

Borges played in the [[2005 FIFA U-17 World Championship]] held in [[Peru]], in which he scored a goal and was considered among the best players of the tournament. After the tournament he was picked as one of ten players to watch out for in the future.<ref name="FIFA">{{FIFA player|236530|Celso Borges}}</ref> Borges also participated in the [[2007 FIFA U-20 World Cup]] held in Canada.<ref name="FIFA" />

He made his debut for the Costa Rican senior team in a June 2008 [[FIFA World Cup qualification]] match against [[Grenada national football team|Grenada]]<ref name=wc/> and, in September of the same year, scored his first international goal against [[Suriname national football team|Suriname]].

After featuring prominently during the team's unsuccessful [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification - CONCACAF fourth round|2010 FIFA World Cup qualification campaign]], Borges helped the team to a second-place finish at the 2011 Copa Centroamericana, as well as the quarter-finals of the CONCACAF Gold Cup later that year.<ref name=wc/>

In June 2014, Borges was named in Costa Rica's squad for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/football/teams/costa-rica/10810092/Costa-Rica-World-Cup-2014-squad.html|title=Costa Rica World Cup 2014 squad|date=2 June 2014|access-date=30 June 2014|work=The Telegraph}}</ref> During the [[2014 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Round of 16|round of 16]], Borges was the first of five Costa Rican players to successfully convert his kick in a 5–3 [[penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shootout]] win over [[Greece national football team|Greece]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/27961189|title=Costa Rica 1-1 Greece|access-date=30 June 2014|date=29 June 2014|work=BBC}}</ref>

In May 2018 he was named in Costa Rica's [[Costa Rica national football team|23 man squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.goal.com/en/amp/news/costa-rica-names-23-man-world-cup-squad-featuring-keylor/1bw2iz8pkma5614bzushqyyo7g|title=Costa Rica national team World Cup squad: Oscar Ramirez names 23-man roster featuring Keylor Navas, six MLS players|last=Arnold|first=Jon|date=14 May 2018|work=Goal.com}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Through [[Alexandre Guimarães|his father]], Borges was also a Brazilian national until 2017, when [[Brazilian nationality law#Dual nationality and loss of Brazilian nationality|he relinquished his Brazilian nationality]] to [[Spanish nationality law#Dual citizenship|become a Spanish citizen]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Celso Borges renunciará a nacionalidad brasileña para obtener la española |url=https://www.elpais.cr/2017/02/08/celso-borges-renunciara-a-nacionalidad-brasilena-para-obtener-la-espanola/ |access-date=14 November 2021 |work=Diario Digital Nuestro País |date=8 February 2017 |language=es}}</ref>

Borges is usually considered one of the most educated players of the [[Costa Rica national football team]]. ''[[El País]]''<nowiki>'</nowiki> Diego Torres described him as "the brain of Costa Rica" and "a gleaned midfielder with elegant diction", while ''[[La Nación (San José)|La Nación]]''<nowiki>'</nowiki>s Antonio Alfaro called him "the exemplary Costa Rican player."<ref>{{cite news|last1=Torres|first1=Diego|title=La fuerza de la educación|url=https://elpais.com/deportes/2014/06/28/mundial_futbol/1403990912_397430.html|access-date=15 August 2017|work=[[El País]]|date=28 June 2014|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Alfaro|first1=Antonio|title=Celso Borges: 'Yo estaba decaído en espíritu y mis papás me hablaron'|url=http://www.nacion.com/dialogos/Celso-Borges-decaido-espiritu-papas-ayudaron_0_1570043096.html|access-date=15 August 2017|work=[[La Nación (San José)|La Nación]]|date=1 July 2016|language=es}}</ref> Aside from his native Spanish, Borges can also speak English, Portuguese and Swedish.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Fonseca|first1=Alejandro|title=Celso con acento español: 'No soy un juega de vivo'|url=http://www.diarioextra.com/Noticia/detalle/335197/celso-con-acento-espanol:--no-soy-un-juega-de-vivo|access-date=15 August 2017|work=[[Diario Extra (Costa Rica)|Diario Extra]]|date=15 June 2017|language=ES}}</ref>

A heavy metal fan, citing [[Slipknot (band)|Slipknot]], [[Metallica]], [[Dream Theater]] and [[Killswitch Engage]] as his favorite bands,<ref>{{cite news|last1=Solano|first1=Gabriela|title=Celso Borges: "Me atrae Scarlett Johansson"|url=http://www.aldia.cr/fuera-cancha/celsoborges-entrevista_0_70193016.html|access-date=16 August 2017|work=Al Día|date=27 January 2013|language=es|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170816062658/http://www.aldia.cr/fuera-cancha/celsoborges-entrevista_0_70193016.html|archive-date=16 August 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> Borges plays the drums, and has covered [[System of a Down]]'s "[[Toxicity (song)|Toxicity]]" alongside Peruvian musician Kurt Dyer.<ref>{{cite news|title=El talento oculto de Celso Borges|url=https://www.lavozdegalicia.es/noticia/torremarathon/2017/06/19/talento-oculto-celso-borges/00031497881460127588378.htm|access-date=15 August 2017|work=[[La Voz de Galicia]]|date=19 June 2017|language=es-ES}}</ref>
