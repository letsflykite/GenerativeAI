'''Marco Asensio Willemsen''' (born 21 January 1996) is a Spanish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Winger (association football)|winger]] and [[attacking midfielder]] for [[Ligue 1]] club [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] and the [[Spain national football team|Spain national team]]. 

After starting his career with [[RCD Mallorca|Mallorca]], Asensio signed for [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]] in November 2014, being consecutively loaned to his former club as well as [[RCD Espanyol|Espanyol]]. Upon his return, he has gone on to win several honours, including three [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] and three [[La Liga]] trophies.

Asensio made his senior debut for Spain in 2016, representing them at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Early life==
Born in [[Palma, Majorca]], [[Balearic Islands]] to a Dutch mother and a Spanish father,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.diariodemallorca.es/rcd-mallorca/2014/08/27/seguido-meses-elite-europea/957496.html#.WvF3Ai7wdPdgguL|title=Seguido desde hace meses por la élite europe|trans-title=Followed by the European elite for months|newspaper=Diario de Mallorca|language=es|date=27 August 2014|access-date=1 December 2014}}</ref><ref name=Dreamed>{{cite news|url=https://en.as.com/en/2016/10/22/football/1477131768_167624.html|title=Asensio's father dreamed his son would play for Athletic Club|newspaper=[[Diario AS]]|date=22 October 2016|access-date=15 October 2018}}</ref> Asensio joined [[RCD Mallorca]] in 2006 from [[CF Platges de Calvià B|CF Platges de Calvià]]. His mother, Maria Willemsen, died of cancer when he was 15.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://okdiario.com/real-madrid/futbol/primer-equipo/2016/08/16/asensio-no-pudo-contener-lagrimas-recordar-madre-37274|title=Asensio no pudo retener las lágrimas al recordar a su madre|trans-title=Asensio could not hold back the tears when remembering his mother|publisher=OK Diario|language=es|date=16 August 2016|access-date=16 August 2017}}</ref>

Asensio struggled with growth deficiencies in his knees during his youth, which only subsided when he was a teenager.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://donbalonrosa.defensacentral.com/el-grave-problema-que-sufrio-marco-asensio-en-su-infancia|title=El grave problema que sufrió Marco Asensio en su infancia|trans-title=The serious problem from which Marco Asensio suffered in his youth|publisher=Don Balón Rosa|language=es|date=10 May 2017|access-date=16 August 2017}}</ref>

After being scouted by [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]] and [[FC Barcelona]],<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.diariodemallorca.es/deportes/2013/02/25/madrid-barca-pugnan-asensio/828920.html|title=Madrid y Barça pugnan por Asensio|trans-title=Madrid and Barça fight for Asensio|newspaper=Diario de Mallorca|language=es|date=25 February 2013|access-date=18 November 2013}}</ref> Asensio made his senior debut with [[RCD Mallorca B|Mallorca's reserves]] in the [[2013–14 Tercera División|2013–14 season]] in the [[Tercera División]], despite still being a junior; he made his first competitive appearance for the first team on 27 October 2013, playing the last six minutes in a 1–3 away loss against [[Recreativo de Huelva]] in the [[Segunda División]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.marca.com/2013/10/27/futbol/2adivision/1382878841.html|title=Linares reafirma el liderato del Decano|trans-title=Linares confirms first place for the Dean|newspaper=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|language=es|date=27 October 2013|access-date=18 November 2013}}</ref>

Again [[Substitute (association football)|from the bench]], Asensio played the last six minutes in a 0–0 home draw against [[CD Lugo]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.marca.com/2013/11/02/futbol/2adivision/1383420718.html|title=Los lucenses tiran de oficio para resistir en el Iberostar|trans-title=''Lucences'' resort to know-how to resist at Iberostar|newspaper=Marca|location=Spain|language=es|date=2 November 2013|access-date=18 November 2013}}</ref> After impressing in his first games, he was promoted to the first team by manager [[José Luis Oltra]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://ultimahora.es/mallorca/noticia/deportes/real-mallorca/marco-asensio-llama-puerta.html|archive-url=https://archive.today/2013.11.18-184225/http://ultimahora.es/mallorca/noticia/deportes/real-mallorca/marco-asensio-llama-puerta.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=18 November 2013|title=Marco Asensio llama a la puerta|trans-title=Marco Asensio knocks on the door|newspaper=[[Última Hora (Spain)|Última Hora]]|language=es|date=4 November 2013|access-date=18 November 2013}}</ref>

====2015–16: Loan to Espanyol====
On 20 August 2015, after making the whole pre-season with Real Madrid, Asensio was loaned to [[RCD Espanyol]] in [[La Liga]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/08/official-announcement-marco-asensio|title=Official announcement: Marco Asensio|publisher=Real Madrid CF|date=20 August 2015|access-date=20 August 2015}}</ref> He played his first game in the competition on 19 September, featuring 86 minutes in a 3–2 success at [[Real Sociedad]],<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mundodeportivo.com/futbol/rcd-espanyol/20150919/201775146000/2-3-el-espanyol-se-rehabilita-en-anoeta.html|title=El Espanyol se rehabilita en Anoeta|trans-title=Espanyol rehabilitate in Anoeta|newspaper=Mundo Deportivo|language=es|date=19 September 2015|access-date=2 May 2016}}</ref> and he ended his spell at the [[RCDE Stadium|Estadi Cornellà-El Prat]] with 12 overall [[Assist (football)|assists]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://futbol.as.com/futbol/2016/03/17/primera/1458203456_171382.html|title=Asensio crece en el Espanyol: está en el Top-10 de asistencias|trans-title=Asensio grows in Espanyol: he makes Top-10 in assists|newspaper=Diario AS|language=es|date=17 March 2016|access-date=10 August 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://futbol.as.com/futbol/2016/05/31/primera/1464713838_662484.html|title=El Espanyol colgará el cuadro de su internacional 43: Asensio|trans-title=Espanyol will hang its 43rd international in the rafters: Asensio|newspaper=Diario AS|language=es|date=1 June 2016|access-date=10 August 2016}}</ref>

====2016–17 season: Return to Real Madrid====
Returning to the [[Santiago Bernabéu Stadium]] for [[2016–17 La Liga|2016–17]], Asensio's first competitive appearance took place on 9 August, as he played the full 120 minutes and scored a 25-meter effort in a [[2016 UEFA Super Cup|3–2 win]] against fellow Spaniards [[Sevilla FC]] in the [[UEFA Super Cup]].<ref name="ESPN">{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.co.uk/report?gameId=450351|title=Dani Carvajal's late goal in ET helps Real Madrid win UEFA Super Cup|publisher=[[ESPN FC]]|date=9 August 2016|access-date=9 August 2016}}</ref> He made his first league start 12 days later, netting the second goal in a 3–0 success at Real Sociedad.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://futbol.as.com/futbol/2016/08/21/primera/1471809433_290537.html|title=Morata y Marco Asensio modernizan al Real Madrid|trans-title=Morata and Marco Asensio modernise Real Madrid|newspaper=Diario AS|language=es|date=21 August 2016|access-date=21 August 2016}}</ref>

Asensio featured 23 times over his first season, scoring three times<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.marca.com/eventos/marcador/futbol/2016_17/la-liga/jornada_6/pal_rma/|title=Fiebre amarilla|trans-title=Yellow fever|newspaper=Marca|location=Spain|language=es|date=24 September 2016|access-date=22 May 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.lavanguardia.com/deportes/futbol/20170304/42539634788/eibar-real-madrid-resumen.html|title=El Madrid se pasea en Eibar|trans-title=Madrid go sightseeing in Eibar|newspaper=[[La Vanguardia]]|language=es|date=4 March 2017|access-date=22 May 2017}}</ref> as the club won the national championship for the first time since 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.laliga.es/noticias/real-madrid-campeon-de-laliga-santander-2016-17|title=El Real Madrid, campeón de LaLiga Santander 2016/17|trans-title=Real Madrid, LaLiga Santander champions 2016/17|publisher=[[La Liga]]|language=es|date=21 May 2017|access-date=22 May 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/39926301|title=Malaga 0–2 Real Madrid|work=[[BBC Sport]]|date=21 May 2017|access-date=22 May 2017}}</ref> He added as many goals in [[2016–17 UEFA Champions League|that year]]'s [[UEFA Champions League]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/37671862|title=Real Madrid 5–1 Legia Warsaw|work=BBC Sport|date=18 October 2016|access-date=6 June 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/39611654|title=Real Madrid 4–2 Bayern Munich|work=BBC Sport|date=18 April 2017|access-date=6 June 2017}}</ref> including one in [[2017 UEFA Champions League Final|the final]] against [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]] (4–1) to give the Spaniards their 12th title in the latter competition.<ref name="UEFA">{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2017/matches/round=2000787/match=2019641/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Majestic Real Madrid win Champions League in Cardiff|publisher=UEFA|date=3 June 2017|access-date=4 June 2017}}</ref>

====2017–18 season====
[[File:Asensio 2018 (cropped).jpg|left|thumb|180px|upright|Asensio playing for Real Madrid against [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]] in 2018]]
Asensio scored his first goal of the [[2017–18 Real Madrid CF season|2017–18 season]] with a 25-yard finish in a [[2017 Supercopa de España|3–1 win]] over Barcelona at the [[Camp Nou]], for the [[Supercopa de España]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.com/spanish-super-cup/431/blog/post/3179259/cristiano-ronaldo-sent-off-but-real-madrid-easily-beat-barcelona-in-spanish-super-cup|title=Cristiano Ronaldo sent off but Real Madrid easily beat Barcelona|publisher=ESPN FC|date=13 August 2017|access-date=14 August 2017}}</ref> He repeated the feat with a similarly spectacular effort in the second leg, in a 5–1 aggregate triumph.<ref name="TELEGRAPH">{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2017/08/16/real-madrid-vs-barcelona-spanish-super-cup-live-score-updates/|title=Real Madrid 2 Barcelona 0 (5–1 on aggregate): Woeful Barca dismissed as Zinedine Zidane's unstoppable side win Super Cup|newspaper=[[The Daily Telegraph]]|date=16 August 2017|access-date=17 August 2017}}</ref>

On 28 September 2017, Asensio extended his contract until 2023.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2017/09/ceremony-to-mark-asensios-contract-renewal|title=Ceremony to mark Asensio's contract renewal|publisher=Real Madrid CF|date=28 September 2017|access-date=28 September 2017}}</ref> On 18 February 2018, in a match where he contributed with a brace to a 5–3 away win over [[Real Betis]], he scored Real Madrid's 6,000th goal in the [[Spanish football league system|Spanish top division]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.sport.es/es/noticias/real-madrid/doblete-asensio-gol-6000-del-madrid-liga-6633442|title=Doblete de Asensio y gol 6000 del Madrid en la Liga|trans-title=Asensio brace and goal 6000 from Madrid in League|newspaper=[[Sport (Spanish newspaper)|Sport]]|language=es|date=18 February 2018|access-date=18 February 2018}}</ref>

Asensio made 11 appearances while scoring one goal during the [[2017–18 UEFA Champions League|2017–18 Champions League]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rcnradio.com/deportes/asi-llegan-las-plantillas-de-real-madrid-y-liverpool-la-final-de-la-champions|title=Así llegan Real Madrid y Liverpool a la final de la Champions|trans-title=That is how Real Madrid and Liverpool arrive to the Champions final|publisher=[[RCN Radio]]|language=es|date=25 May 2018|access-date=29 May 2018}}</ref> and the club won its third consecutive and 13th overall title in the competition.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2018/matches/round=2000885/match=2021711/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Madrid beat Liverpool to complete hat-trick|publisher=UEFA|date=26 May 2018|access-date=28 May 2018}}</ref>

====2019–2023: Final seasons ====
[[File:Ofrenda de la Liga y la Champions-22-L.Millán (52109789880).jpg|thumb|300x300px|upright|Asensio in trophy presentation ceremony at [[Virgin of Almudena]] in 2022]]
On 24 July 2019, during a preseason match against [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]], he tore his [[Anterior cruciate ligament injury|ACL]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/real-madrid/2019/07/24/5d3841ac268e3e490d8b45e8.html|title=Real Madrid confirm Asensio's ACL injury |work=Marca|location=Spain |date=24 July 2019|access-date=24 July 2019}}</ref> On 19 June 2020, in a 3–0 win over [[Valencia CF|Valencia]], he came on for [[Federico Valverde]] in 74th minute, scored the second goal with his first touch, and provided the assist to [[Karim Benzema]] for the third goal.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Kirkland |first=Alex|date=18 June 2020|title=Benzema's brace earns him 9/10 as Real Madrid stroll vs. Valencia|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/real-madrid/story/4115447/benzemas-brace-earns-him-9-10-as-real-madrid-stroll-while-asensios-comeback-cameo-steals-the-show|publisher=ESPN}}</ref> He returned for the final stretch of the season, scoring three goals in nine games, as Real Madrid won the [[2019–20 La Liga]].<ref name=Liga>{{cite web |url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/real-madrid/2020/07/17/5f10db2146163f3c2c8b45d7.html|title=Real Madrid win the longest LaLiga Santander season|date=17 July 2020|access-date=17 July 2020|website=Marca|location=Spain}}</ref>

On 22 September 2021, he scored his first senior hat-trick against his former club [[RCD Mallorca|Mallorca]], which ended in a 6–1 victory.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Football news – Marcos Asensio and Karim Benzema star as Real Madrid rout Mallorca to go top of La Liga|date=22 September 2021 |url=https://www.eurosport.com/football/liga/2021-2022/football-news-real-madrid-v-rcd-mallorca-follow-live_sto8550530/story.shtml|access-date=22 September 2021|publisher=eurosport.com}}</ref> 

On 28 May 2022, Asensio was on the bench against [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] in the UEFA Champions League Final as Real Madrid went on to win their 14th [[UEFA Champions League]] title.<ref name=CL21>{{cite news |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/0275-15415a62aaf0-36d3e17d7404-1000/ |title=Champions League final: Vinícius Júnior scores only goal as Real Madrid deny Liverpool again |publisher=UEFA |date=28 May 2022 |access-date=28 May 2022}}</ref> Asensio ended the 2021–22 season with 10 goals in 30 [[La Liga]] games. He also won the [[2021–22 La Liga]] with his club Real Madrid.<ref name=Liga21>{{cite web |last=Westwood |first=James |date=30 April 2022 |title=Real Madrid clinch 35th La Liga title with four games to spare after victory over Espanyol |url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/real-madrid-35th-la-liga-title/bltb8dfbd27f32f5feb |access-date=30 April 2022 |website=Goal (website)}}</ref>

On 3 June 2023, Real Madrid confirmed that Asensio would be departing upon the expiry of his contract, thus becoming a free agent.<ref>{{cite news|title=Official Announcement: Marco Asensio|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2023/06/03/official-announcement-marco-asensio|publisher=Real Madrid CF|date=3 June 2023|access-date=3 June 2023}}</ref>

===Paris Saint-Germain===
On 6 July 2023, [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] announced the signing of Asensio, with a contract running until June 2026.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-07-06 |title=Marco Asensio joins Paris Saint-Germain through to 2026 |url=https://en.psg.fr/teams/first-team/content/marco-asensio-joins-paris-saint-germain-through-to-2026 |access-date=2023-07-06 |website=Paris Saint-Germain F.C.}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:Marco Asensio, UEFA U21 2017 c.jpg|thumb|right|Asensio during a [[2017 UEFA European Under-21 Championship|2017 European Under-21 Championship]] match against [[Portugal national under-21 football team|Portugal]]]]
Being of both Dutch and Spanish descent, he chose to represent Spain although he received an invitation from the [[Royal Dutch Football Association]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.besoccer.com/new/marco-asensio-could-have-played-for-holland|title=Marco Asensio could have played for Holland}}</ref> After being a regular for [[Spain national youth football team|Spain at youth level]], Asensio made his debut for the [[Spain national under-21 football team|under-21 team]] on 26 March 2015, coming on as a late substitute for goalscorer [[Gerard Deulofeu]] in a 2–0 [[Exhibition game|friendly]] win over [[Norway national football team|Norway]] in [[Cartagena, Spain|Cartagena]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sefutbol.com/en/report-spain-beats-norway-and-strengthens-team-2-0|title=Spain beats Norway and strengthens the team (2–0)|publisher=[[Royal Spanish Football Federation]]|last=Muñoz|first=Antonio D.|date=26 March 2015|access-date=30 March 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150328194045/http://www.sefutbol.com/en/report-spain-beats-norway-and-strengthens-team-2-0|archive-date=28 March 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> In July, he was part of the victorious [[Spain national under-19 football team|under-19 side]] at the [[2015 UEFA European Under-19 Championship|UEFA European Championship]] in Greece, where he scored both goals in a semi-final victory over [[France national under-19 football team|France]] in [[Katerini]], in the 88th minute and in added time.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/under19/season=2015/matches/round=2000535/match=2017611/postmatch/report/index.html#spain+edge+france|title=Late Asensio strikes take Spain into final again|publisher=UEFA|last=Haslam|first=Andrew|date=16 July 2015|access-date=29 July 2015}}</ref>

On 17 May 2016, Asensio and Espanyol teammate [[Pau López]] were called up to [[Spain national football team|the full side]] for a [[Exhibition match|friendly]] against [[Bosnia and Herzegovina national football team|Bosnia and Herzegovina]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rcdespanyol.com/es/actualidad/pau-lopez-y-marco-asensio-convocados-por-del-bosque/_n:2915/|title=Pau López y Marco Asensio, convocados por Del Bosque|trans-title=Pau López and Marco Asensio, called up by Del Bosque|publisher=RCD Espanyol|language=es|date=17 May 2016|access-date=29 May 2016}}</ref> He made his debut on the 29th, starting in the 3–1 win in Switzerland.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.marca.com/eventos/marcador/futbol/2015_16/amistosos_seleccion/3/spa_bih/|title=Nolito endulza un duelo atípico|trans-title=Nolito sweetens an atypical duel|newspaper=Marca|location=Spain|language=es|date=29 May 2016|access-date=29 May 2016}}</ref>
[[File:ESP-RUS (14).jpg|left|thumb|upright|301x301px|Asensio playing for [[Spain national football team|Spain]] in [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] against [[Russia national football team|Russia]]]]
Selected in the [[2017 UEFA European Under-21 Championship squads#Spain|squad]] for the [[2017 UEFA European Under-21 Championship|2017 European Under-21 Championship]] by manager [[Albert Celades]], Asensio scored a [[hat-trick]] in his debut in the competition, helping to a 5–0 group stage routing of [[Macedonia national under-21 football team|Macedonia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/under21/news/newsid=2479414.html|title=Asensio scores fifth U21 EURO finals hat-trick|publisher=UEFA|date=17 June 2017|access-date=18 June 2017}}</ref> He reached with his teammates the [[2017 UEFA European Under-21 Championship Final|final]] of the tournament, losing 1–0 against [[Germany national under-21 football team|Germany]].

Back with the seniors, he was included in [[Julen Lopetegui]]'s [[2018 FIFA World Cup squads#Spain|squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/morata-misses-out-on-spain-world-cup-squad/1ub1qz7jod09i1lfvdcy84ea2l|title=Morata misses out on Spain's 23-man World Cup squad|work=[[Goal (website)]]|date=21 May 2018|access-date=21 May 2018}}</ref> making his debut in [[FIFA World Cup|the competition]] on 20 June by replacing Real Madrid teammate [[Lucas Vázquez]] for the final ten minutes of the 1–0 group stage win against [[Iran national football team|Iran]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/aponline/2018/06/20/sports/soccer/ap-soc-wcup-iran-spain.html|title=Diego Costa scores again, Spain beats Iran 1–0 at World Cup|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|date=20 June 2018|access-date=20 June 2018}}</ref>

Asensio scored his first goal for Spain on 11 September 2018, playing the entire 6–0 home rout of [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]] for the [[2018–19 UEFA Nations League|UEFA Nations League]] and also being directly involved in the play that led to [[Lovre Kalinić]]'s [[own goal]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://en.as.com/en/2018/09/11/football/1536687949_030758.html?omnaut=1|title=Spain – Croatia match report: UEFA Nations League|newspaper=Diario AS|last=Dunne|first=Robbie|date=11 September 2018|access-date=11 September 2018}}</ref>

In July 2021, he was included as overage player in the 22-player squad of the [[Spain national under-23 football team|under-23 team]] for the [[Football at the 2020 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|2020 Summer Olympics]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Estos son los internacionales que representarán a España en Tokio | date=29 June 2021 | url=https://www.sefutbol.com/oficial-estos-son-internacionales-que-representaran-espana-tokio |publisher=SEFutbol |access-date=29 June 2021 |language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|agency=Associated Press|title=Spain Names Six From Euro Squad to Olympic Roster|url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2021/06/29/spain-olympics-squad-euro-2020-pedri-dani-olmo|access-date=1 July 2021|website=Sports Illustrated|language=en-us}}</ref> 

In November 2022, Asensio was included in Spain's final 26-man squad for the 2022 World Cup. He started against Costa Rica in the opening fixture of Spain's World Cup campaign, and scored the second goal against Costa Rica which Spain won 7–0.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Cardenas |first=Felipe |title=Asensio lights up the most lopsided game in World Cup history |url=https://theathletic.com/3928860/2022/11/23/asensio-spain-costa-rica-world-cup/ |access-date=28 February 2023 |website=The Athletic |language=en}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Asensio's father, Gilberto (a [[Basques|Basque]] who spent his childhood in [[Essen]], Germany), was also a footballer.<ref name=Dreamed/> Also an attacking midfielder, he represented [[Barakaldo CF]] as a youth;<ref name=Dreamed/> Marco's elder brother Igor played for [[CF Platges de Calvià|Platges de Calvià]], as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.marca.com/2014/10/14/futbol/equipos/mallorca/1413275888.html|title=Marco Asensio, la joya mileurista|trans-title=Marco Asensio, the ''mileurista'' pearl|newspaper=Marca|location=Spain|language=es|date=14 October 2014|access-date=16 August 2017}}</ref>

Asensio's [[goal celebration]] in the 2017 Champions League final was dedicated to his family: "I told them that if I scored then I'd run to where they were. I knew where they were and that is what I did."<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/spanish-football/2018/10/11/5bbf29a0e2704e765e8b45e1.html|title=Asensio returns to Cardiff, a city of happy memories|newspaper=Marca|location=Spain|last1=Lara|first1=Miguel Ángel|last2=McTear|first2=Euan|date=11 October 2018|access-date=12 October 2018}}</ref>
