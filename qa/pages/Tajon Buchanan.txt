'''Tajon Trevor Buchanan''' (born February 8, 1999) is a Canadian professional [[association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[winger (association football)|winger]] for [[Belgian Pro League]] side [[Club Brugge KV|Club Brugge]] and the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada national team]].

==Club career==
===Early career===
Buchanan was born and raised in [[Brampton]], [[Ontario]] and is of Jamaican descent.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://breakingthelines.com/opinion/the-canadian-national-football-team-when-football-meets-multiculturalism/|title=The Canadian National Football Team: When Football Meets Multiculturalism}}</ref> His father died when Buchanan was seven years old.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.sportsnet.ca/soccer/tajon-buchanan-became-canadas-fastest-rising-star/|title=How Tajon Buchanan became Canada's fastest-rising star|website=Sportsnet.ca}}</ref> He began playing soccer with Brampton Youth SC, when he was eight.<ref>{{Canada Soccer player|id=13457}}</ref> Buchanan won the 2014 IMG Cup with the Mississauga Falcons.<ref>{{cite web |title=Tajon Buchanan - Men's Soccer |url=https://cuse.com/roster.aspx?rp_id=15560 |publisher=Syracuse University |accessdate=May 27, 2019}}</ref> At age 15, he moved with his best friend and his best friend's father (and his former coach), Chrys Chrysanthou, to Colorado where he joined the [[U.S. Soccer Development Academy|USSDA]] academy Real Colorado.<ref name="auto"/>

Buchanan spent two years playing [[college soccer]] at [[Syracuse University]] between 2017 and 2018, making 33 appearances, scoring 12 goals, and tallying six assists.<ref>{{cite web|title=Tajon Buchanan - Men's Soccer - Syracuse University Athletics|url=https://cuse.com/roster.aspx?rp_id=13865|url-status=live}}</ref> After two years with the Orange, Buchanan left early and signed a [[Generation Adidas]] contract with [[Major League Soccer]] ahead of the [[2019 MLS SuperDraft]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/amaya-buchanan-to-headline-mls-generation-adidas-class/23ke1chi5oh81q0pqi2y4gi52|first=Ives|last=Galarcep|date=January 1, 2019|title=MLS SuperDraft: Amaya, Buchanan to headline MLS Generation adidas class}}</ref>

In summer 2018, Buchanan played for [[League1 Ontario]] side [[Sigma FC]], making seven appearances and scoring two goals.<ref>{{cite web |title=Tajon Buchanan 2018 |url=https://www.league1ontario.com/roster_players/24798561 |publisher=League1 Ontario |accessdate=May 27, 2019}}</ref>

===New England Revolution===
On January 11, 2019, Buchanan was selected 9th overall in the 2019 MLS SuperDraft by [[New England Revolution]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.boston.com/sports/soccer/2019/01/11/revolution-take-forwards-tajon-buchanan-dejuan-jones-in-mls-draft|date=January 11, 2019|title=Revolution take forwards Tajon Buchanan, DeJuan Jones in MLS draft}}</ref> He made his professional debut on March 9, 2019, as an 81st-minute substitute during a 2–0 loss to [[Columbus Crew SC]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://matchcenter.mlssoccer.com/matchcenter/2019-03-09-new-england-revolution-vs-columbus-crew-sc/boxscore|date=March 9, 2019|title=Boxscore: New England Revolution vs. Columbus Crew SC 03/09/19 - Matchcenter}}</ref> On November 24, 2020, he scored his first [[MLS Cup playoffs]] goal, scoring the second and final goal in a 2–0 win over the [[Philadelphia Union]].

During the [[2021 MLS season|2021 season]] Buchanan's continued good form for the Revolution earned him a nomination to the MLS squad for the [[2021 MLS All-Star Game]] in August 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/2021-mls-all-star-team-roster|date=August 4, 2021|title=2021 MLS All-Star Team Roster}}</ref>

===Club Brugge===
In August 2021, Buchanan signed a 3 and a half year deal with [[Belgian First Division A]] champions [[Club Brugge]]. Buchanan was loaned back to New England Revolution for the remainder of the 2021 MLS season.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.clubbrugge.be/nl/nieuws/tajon-buchanan-naar-club-brugge|title=Tajon Buchanan to Club Brugge|website=Club Brugge|date=August 24, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.revolutionsoccer.net/news/new-england-revolution-transfer-tajon-buchanan-to-club-brugge|title=New England Revolution transfer Tajon Buchanan to Club Brugge|website=New England Revolution|date=August 24, 2021}}</ref> He made his debut for Brugge against [[Sint-Truidense V.V.|Sint-Truiden]] on January 15, 2022.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sportsnet.ca/soccer/article/canadians-abroad-roundup-buchanan-corbeanu-shining-new-clubs/|work=[[Sportsnet]]|first=Peter|last=Galindo|title=Canadians Abroad Roundup: Buchanan, Corbeanu shining with new clubs|date=January 16, 2022}}</ref> Buchanan scored his first goal for Brugge on April 24 against [[Royal Antwerp F.C.|Antwerp]].<ref>{{cite web|title=WATCH: Tajon Buchanan scores 1st goal for Club Brugge|date=April 25, 2022|url=https://onesoccer.ca/a/watch-tajon-buchanan-scores-1st-goal-for-club-brugge|first=Armen|last=Bedakian|work=[[OneSoccer]]}}</ref>

He is known in Belgium for his aggressive playing style.<ref>{{Cite web |title=FACTCHECK. Is Tajon Buchanan echt een recidivist? Onze Club Brugge-watcher bekeek alle 167 overtredingen van de Canadees |url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/dmf20230227_96976184 |access-date=2023-02-28 |website=www.nieuwsblad.be |language=nl-BE}}</ref> In 2023 there was an incident when he threw his elbow in the face of [[Gift Orban]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Gent deelt nieuwe beelden Buchanan-incident: "Pure schande" - VoetbalNieuws |url=https://www.voetbalnieuws.be/news/702806/gent-deelt-nieuwe-beelden-buchananincident-pure-schande |access-date=2023-02-27 |website=www.voetbalnieuws.be |language=nl}}</ref>

==International career==
===Youth===
Buchanan was named to the [[Canada men's national under-23 soccer team|Canadian U-23]] provisional roster for the [[2020 CONCACAF Men's Olympic Qualifying Championship]] on February 26, 2020.<ref>{{cite web |title=Canada Soccer announces Provisional List for Concacaf Men's Olympic Qualifying |url=https://www.canadasoccer.com/canada-soccer-announces-provisional-list-for-concacaf-men-s-olympic-qualifying-p162594-preview-1 |publisher=Canada Soccer |accessdate=February 26, 2020 |date=February 26, 2020 |archive-date=March 9, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200309191149/https://www.canadasoccer.com/canada-soccer-announces-provisional-list-for-concacaf-men-s-olympic-qualifying-p162594-preview-1 |url-status=dead }}</ref>  A year later, he was named to the final squad for the rescheduled tournament on March 10, 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canadasoccer.com/news/canada-soccer-announces-squad-for-concacaf-mens-olympic-qualifying/|title=CANADA SOCCER ANNOUNCES SQUAD FOR CONCACAF MEN'S OLYMPIC QUALIFYING|date=March 10, 2021}}</ref> In Canada's opening match, Buchanan scored a brace in a 2–0 victory over [[El Salvador national under-23 football team|El Salvador]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://northerntribune.ca/canada-olympic-qualifying-el-salvador-2021/|first=John|last=Jacques|title=Buchanan Brace Leads Canada To Olympic Qualifying Opener Win|date=March 19, 2021}}</ref> He was named the 2020 [[Canada Soccer Youth International Player of the Year]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tsn.ca/tajon-buchanan-jade-rose-named-canada-soccer-s-youth-players-of-the-year-1.1558688|title=Buchanan, Rose named Canada Soccer's youth players of the year|date=December 1, 2020|work=[[The Sports Network|TSN]]}}</ref>

===Senior===
His performances have earned him recognition, not only in Belgium but also on the international stage.<ref>{{cite web|date=12 April 2023|title =Canadian Inter Milan is in the running to sign €12 million|url=https://footballexpress.in/canadian-inter-milan-is-in-the-running-to-sign-e12-million/website= FootballExpress.in}}</ref>
He accepted an invite for the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canadian senior national team]] camp for January 2021.<ref name=can2021>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/canada-soccer-ayo-akinola-national-team-camp-1.5853734|title=TFC's Ayo Akinola accepts invite to Canadian national team camp|date=December 23, 2020|first=Neil|last=Davidson|publisher=[[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation]]}}</ref> Buchanan was called up in June 2021 for the coming [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONCACAF)|World Cup qualification]] matches against [[Aruba national football team|Aruba]] and [[Suriname national football team|Suriname]], and subsequently made his senior-team debut against Aruba, setting up two goals in a 7–0 victory.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Canada beats Aruba, sets up crucial World Cup qualifier against Suriname |url=https://www.sportsnet.ca/soccer/article/canada-beats-aruba-sets-crucial-world-cup-qualifier-suriname/ |publisher=Sportsnet |accessdate=June 5, 2021}}</ref> On June 18 Buchanan was named to [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada's]] 60-man preliminary squad for the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tsn.ca/canada-names-60-man-preliminary-roster-for-next-month-s-gold-cup-1.1656962|date=June 18, 2021|title=Davies, David highlight Canada's preliminary roster for Gold Cup}}</ref> On July 1 he was called up to the final 23-man squad.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.canadasoccer.com/news/canada-soccer-announces-2021-concacaf-gold-cup-roster/|title=CANADA SOCCER ANNOUNCES 2021 CONCACAF GOLD CUP ROSTER|date=July 1, 2021}}</ref> He scored his first goal for the senior team on July 29 against [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]] in the semi-final stage of the Gold Cup, netting the equalizer in an eventual 2–1 defeat.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canadasoccer.com/news/canada-impresses-in-12-loss-to-mexico-in-concacaf-gold-cup-semifinal/|date=July 30, 2021|title=CANADA IMPRESSES IN 1:2 LOSS TO MEXICO IN CONCACAF GOLD CUP SEMIFINAL}}</ref> Buchanan's play at the tournament was widely praised by the region's media and at the conclusion of the competition he was named as the recipient of the 2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup Best Young Player Award.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/revolution-stars-turner-buchanan-earn-major-2021-gold-cup-awards|date=August 2, 2021|title=Revolution stars Turner, Buchanan earn major 2021 Gold Cup awards}}</ref>

In November 2022, Buchanan was named to Canada's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canada-announces-26-man-squad-for-2022-fifa-world-cup-in-qatar|date=November 13, 2022|title=Canada announces 26-man squad for 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]|first=Charlie|last=O'Connor-Clarke}}</ref> In Canada's second match on November 27 against [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]], the team scored their first ever goal at a men's World Cup, with Buchanan sending in a cross that was met by the head of [[Alphonso Davies]] and sent into the back of the net.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thebentmusket.com/2022/11/28/23481616/fifa-world-cup-2022-group-f-recaps-tajon-buchanan-canada-croatia-hakim-ziyech-morocco-belgium|date=November 27, 2022|title=FIFA World Cup 2022 Group F Recaps: Buchanan’s historic assist not enough as Canada eliminated|work=The Bent Musket|first=Jake|last=Catanese}}</ref>

In June 2023, Buchanan was named to the 23-man squad for Canada ahead of the [[2023 CONCACAF Nations League Finals]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Former CPLers Loturi, Zator called up by Canada for 2023 Concacaf Nations League Finals|date=June 7, 2023|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canada-canmnt-2023-concacaf-nations-league-final-roster|first=Mitchell|last=Tierney|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]}}</ref>
