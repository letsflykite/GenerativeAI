'''Fran Karačić''' ({{IPA-sh|frâŋ kâratʃitɕ|hr}};{{Efn-ua|In isolation, ''Fran'' is pronounced {{IPA-sh|frân|}}.}} born 12 May 1996) is a professional [[association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[Defender (association football)|right back]] for [[Serie C]] club [[Brescia Calcio|Brescia]]. Born in Croatia, he represents the [[Australia national soccer team|Australia national team]].

==Club career==
On 14 January 2021, Karačić joined [[Serie B]] side [[Brescia Calcio|Brescia]] on a two-and-a-half year deal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bresciacalcio.it/news/ultime-news/13152/fran-karacic-e-un-nuovo-giocatore-del-brescia|title=Fran Karačić è un nuovo giocatore del Brescia|publisher=[[Brescia Calcio]]|date=14 January 2021|access-date=14 January 2021}}</ref>

==International career==
Karacic was eligible to represent [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]] or [[Australia men's national soccer team|Australia]] due to his father being born in [[Sydney]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.smh.com.au/sport/soccer/like-a-dream-the-socceroo-proud-to-represent-the-country-he-s-never-been-to-20211009-p58yll.html|title='Like a dream': The Socceroo proud to represent the country he's never visited|website=smh.com.au|date=9 October 2021 |access-date=10 November 2022}}</ref>

In May 2018, Karačić was named in [[Australia men's national soccer team|Australia]]'s preliminary 26-man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Russia]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/revealed-every-world-cup-2018-squad-23-man-preliminary-lists/oa0atsduflsv1nsf6oqk576rb|title=Revealed: Every World Cup 2018 squad - Final 23-man lists|website=Goal.com|access-date=10 December 2021}}</ref> On 25 May 2018, he was officially cleared by [[FIFA]] to represent Australia.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.smh.com.au/sport/soccer/fifa-gives-seal-of-approval-to-karacic-s-socceroos-switch-20180525-p4zhem.html|title=FIFA gives seal of approval to Karacic's Socceroos switch|date=24 May 2018|website=The Sydney Morning Herald|language=en|access-date=27 May 2018}}</ref> Karačić was later cut from the final squad.<ref>[https://theworldgame.sbs.com.au/almost-socceroo-karacic-no-regrets-over-chasing-world-cup-spot 'Almost Socceroo' Karacic: No regrets over chasing World Cup spot] theworldgame.sbs.com.au</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44345161|title=World Cup 2018: Tim Cahill selected in Australia's 23-man squad|date=22 June 2018|website=BBC Sport|access-date=6 June 2018}}</ref>

Karačić was called up to the Australian squad again for a series of [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (AFC)|2022 FIFA World Cup qualifying]] games in June 2021, where he made his international debut against [[Kuwait national football team|Kuwait]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://au.sports.yahoo.com/socceroos-quartet-back-wilderness-173134137.html|title=Socceroos quartet back from wilderness|date=26 May 2021|access-date=31 May 2021|website=Yahoo Sports}}</ref>

He was named in Australia's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in November 2022.<ref>{{Cite web |date=8 November 2022 |title=SOCCEROOS SQUAD ANNOUNCED: FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022™ |url=https://www.socceroos.com.au/news/socceroos-squad-announced-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022tm |access-date=9 November 2022 |website=Socceroos |language=en}}</ref>
