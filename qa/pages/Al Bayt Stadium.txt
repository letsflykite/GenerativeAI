== Plans ==
Al Bayt Stadium hosted the [[2022 FIFA World Cup Group A#Qatar vs Ecuador|opening match]] of the 2022 World Cup, and hosted a semi-final and a quarter-final match.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thejakartapost.com/news/2020/07/16/qatar-world-cup-to-start-at-al-bayt-stadium-as-schedule-announced.html |title=Qatar World Cup to start at Al Bayt Stadium as schedule announced |publisher=thejakartapost.com |date=16 July 2020 |accessdate=7 January 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/2022-fifa-world-cup-qatar-al-bayt-stadium-sky-box-5-star/p0lb869nh1501fut7hhhmz9zp |title= A 5-star view of the World Cup: Qatar's Al Bayt stadium set to unveil jaw-dropping Sky Boxes |publisher=goal.com |date=18 November 2019 |accessdate=2 August 2021}}</ref> The stadium hosted around 60,000 World Cup fans,<ref name="alj1">{{cite web|url=https://www.aljazeera.com/sports/2019/9/18/world-cup-2022-a-room-with-a-view-at-qatars-al-bayt-stadium |title=World Cup 2022: A room with a view at Qatar's Al Bayt Stadium |publisher=aljazeera.com |date=18 September 2019 |accessdate=7 March 2022}}</ref> including 1,000 seats for press. The architectural design takes its inspiration from the traditional tents of the [[nomad]]ic peoples of Qatar and the region.<ref name="thepeninsulaqatar">{{cite web|url=https://thepeninsulaqatar.com/article/27/07/2020/Al-Bayt-Stadium-achieves-outstanding-sustainability-rating |title=Al Bayt Stadium achieves outstanding sustainability rating |publisher=thepeninsulaqatar.com |date=27 July 2020 |accessdate=7 January 2021}}</ref> It features a retractable roof, providing covered seating for all spectators. It connects to transportation systems and have onsite parking for 6,000 cars, 350 buses and the coming and going of 150 public buses/shuttles, as well as 1,000 taxis and water taxis. The stadium is certified for its sustainability credentials under the [[Global Sustainability Assessment System]] (GSAS) for a number of certifications representing [[sustainable design]] & build, construction management practices and the efficiency of its energy centre. The stadium also received a five-star GSAS rating.<ref name="thepeninsulaqatar" />

The stadium also includes luxurious hotel suites and rooms with balcony views of the football field.<ref name="alj1"/>

To mark the [[National Sports Day#Qatar|National Sports Day]], stadium's adjacent park's official opening was announced to be held on Qatar's sports day itself, 11 February 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.iloveqatar.net/news/sports/al-bayt-stadium-park-to-open-for-the-public-on-11-february|title=Al Bayt Stadium Park to open to public on 11 February|accessdate=3 February 2020|publisher=ILoveQatar}}</ref>

== Construction ==
The Al Bayt Stadium in Qatar was one of eight stadiums used in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.arabianbusiness.com/qatar-unveils-al-bayt-stadium-design-554717.html |title=Qatar unveils Al Bayt Stadium design |publisher=arabianbusiness.com |date=22 June 2014 |accessdate=2 August 2021}}</ref> the second largest after [[Lusail Stadium]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.as.com/en/2020/10/19/football/1603133765_207682.html |title=New images of Al-Bayt World Cup stadium confirm completion |publisher=en.as.com |date=19 October 2020 |accessdate=2 August 2021}}</ref> The stadium was designed by [[Dar Al-Handasah]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thepeninsulaqatar.com/article/04/01/2021/Qatar-2022-stadiums-continue-to-take-shape-despite-pandemic |title=Qatar 2022 stadiums continue to take shape despite pandemic |publisher=thepeninsulaqatar.com |date=4 January 2021 |accessdate=4 August 2021}}</ref> Following the World Cup, it is expected to be reconfigured into a 32,000-seat stadium. Excess seats will be removed from the upper tier and donated to other countries or placed on the infrastructure planned for the [[2030 Asian Games]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.arabianbusiness.com/qatar-unveils-al-bayt-stadium-design-554717.html |title=Qatar unveils Al Bayt Stadium design |publisher=arabianbusiness.com |date=22 June 2014 |accessdate=4 August 2021}}</ref> The vacated space will then be converted into a five-star hotel, shopping mall and another sports facilities.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/al-bayt-stadium-achieves-outstanding-sustainability-rating |title=Al Bayt Stadium achieves outstanding sustainability rating |publisher=fifa.com |date= |accessdate=10 August 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://footballcoal.com/al-bayt-stadium-in-al-khor-qatar-for-fifa-world-cup-2022/ |title=Al-Bayt Stadium in Al Khor, Qatar for FIFA World Cup 2022 |publisher=footballcoal.com|date= |accessdate=1 April 2022}}</ref>

The tent-like structure has four stands, each of whose exterior walls and peaked roofs are covered in [[Polytetrafluoroethylene|polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE)]] woven fibreglass membrane. The exterior part of the PTFE membrane is coloured in traditional black, white and red colours to further reference Qatar's [[nomad]]ic people's tents. A retractable roof connects the four stands to enclose the stadium.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-12-06|title=Stadium in giant tent completes ahead of World Cup in Qatar|url=https://www.dezeen.com/2021/12/06/al-bayt-stadium-world-cup-qatar-giant-tent/|access-date=2021-12-08|website=Dezeen|language=en}}</ref>

== History ==
The inauguration of the stadium took place on 30 November 2021, on the occasion of opening ceremony for the [[2021 FIFA Arab Cup]], followed by a match between the host [[Qatar national football team|Qatar]] and [[Bahrain national football team|Bahrain]], in which the reigning Asian champion Qatar survived a last-minute scare to fend off the visitor 1–0, courtesy of a header from [[Abdulaziz Hatem]] in the 69th minute.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/tournaments/mens/arabcup/arabcup2021/news/follow-fifa-arab-cups-final-day |title=Algeria win the FIFA Arab Cup |publisher=fifa.com |date=18 December 2021 |accessdate=7 March 2022}}</ref>

This event was attended by the Emir (head of state) of Qatar, [[Tamim bin Hamad Al Thani|Sheikh Tamim bin Hamad Al Thani]]. [[FIFA]] President [[Gianni Infantino]],  several Heads of State and authorities and Presidents from member associations to enjoy the inauguration ceremony of Al Bayt Stadium and to mark the official opening of the 2021 FIFA Arab Cup.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/news/fifa-president-reflects-on-stunning-opening-day-at-fifa-arab-cup |title=FIFA President reflects on stunning opening day at FIFA Arab Cup™ |publisher=fifa.com |date=1 December 2021 |accessdate=28 February 2022}}</ref> The newly built stadium hosted five matches during FIFA Arab Cup 2021, including the final of the tournament on 18 December 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.gulf-times.com/story/706470/Amir-attends-FIFA-Arab-Cup-final-match-closing-cer |title=Amir attends FIFA Arab Cup final match,closing ceremony |publisher=gulf-times.com |date=18 December 2021 |accessdate=10 March 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/2021-fifa-arab-cup-qatar-all-you-need-to-know/blta3bed73f82f8fd58 |title=2021 FIFA Arab Cup: Participating teams, fixtures and all you need to know |publisher=goal.com|date=18 December 2021 |accessdate=10 March 2022}}</ref>

On 20 November 2022, the stadium hosted the opening game of the FIFA World Cup between Qatar and Ecuador; 67,372 were reported to be in attendance at kick-off, despite the capacity of the stadium being 60,000.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/world-cup/qatar-ecuador-fans-leave-half-time-b2229203.html |title=Thousands of Qatar fans appear to leave World Cup opener at half-time |last=Pattle |first=Alex |date=20 November 2022 |website=independent.co.uk |access-date=23 November 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.reuters.com/lifestyle/sports/qatar-v-ecuador-world-cup-2022-kick-off-time-venue-stats-2022-11-18/ |title=Factbox: Qatar v Ecuador World Cup 2022: kick-off time, venue and stats |date=18 November 2022 |website=reuters.com |access-date=23 November 2022}}</ref>
