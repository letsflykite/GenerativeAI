'''Jesper Grænge Lindstrøm''' ({{IPA-da|ˈle̝nˌstʁɶmˀ}}; born 29 February 2000), also known as '''Jobbe''',<ref name="min-vej" /><ref>{{cite news |last1=Hoffskov |first1=Ole |last2=Risager |first2=Victor |title=Jobbe: Det skrev jeg til Frederiksen efter fyring |url=https://www.tipsbladet.dk/nyhed/superliga/jobbe-det-skrev-jeg-til-frederiksen-efter-fyring |access-date=26 January 2023 |work=Tipsbladet |date=17 November 2022 |language=da}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Bagger |first1=Sebastian Taarsted |title=Tyske tunneller har givet 22-årige 'Jobbe' en plads på landsholdet: 'Kæmpestort at være med' |url=https://www.dr.dk/sporten/fodbold/landsholdet/hvorfor-kan-jobbe-lindstroem-pludselig-loebe-om-hjoerner-med-tysklands |access-date=26 January 2023 |work=DR |date=24 March 2022 |language=da-DK}}</ref> is a Danish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[Bundesliga]] club [[Eintracht Frankfurt]] and the [[Denmark national football team|Denmark national team]].

Lindstrøm made his full international debut for Denmark on 11 November 2020 against [[Sweden national football team|Sweden]]. He has also represented Denmark at [[Denmark national under-19 football team|under-19]] and [[Denmark national under-19 football team|under-21]] levels.

== Club career ==
=== Brøndby ===
====Early career====
Lindstrøm began his career at [[Taastrup FC|Tåstrup B.70]] at age three, close to where he lived with his family in [[Høje-Taastrup Municipality|Høje-Tåstrup]].<ref name="min-vej">{{cite web |title=Min vej til førsteholdet: Jesper 'Jobbe' Lindstrøm {{!}} 3point.dk |url=https://3point.dk/nyheder/min-vej-til-forsteholdet-jesper-jobbe-lindstrom |website=3point.dk |language=da |date=31 May 2020}}</ref> When his parents divorced, he moved to [[Brøndby]] with his mother as a six-year-old, and began playing for BSI within a stone's throw from his new home.<ref name="min-vej" /> He only played there for six months, as the school he attended closed, and he joined Vallensbæk IF closer to his new school on the recommendation of his stepfather's friend, coach Henrik From, the father of [[Rasmus From]], a goalkeeper who has played for the [[FC Nordsjælland|Nordsjælland]] academy as well as [[VSK Aarhus]].<ref name="min-vej" /> He then joined the [[Football academy|academy]] at Danish top side [[Brøndby IF]] at under-13 level, a club which his family supported.<ref name="brondby" />

In August 2018, he signed a three-year contract with the club. He made his first team debut, aged 18, as a substitute in a 4–1 win over [[Danish 2nd Division|Danish third tier]] side [[BK Marienlyst]] in the [[Danish Cup]] on 22 November 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.fyens.dk/fodbold/Marienlyst-kom-tilbage-fra-grum-start/artikel/3303116|title=Marienlyst kom tilbage fra grum start|last=Abildtrup|first=Niels|date=22 November 2018|work=Fyens Stiftstidense|access-date=29 July 2019|language=da}}</ref> Eventually, he was promoted to the first team in the summer of 2019.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://3point.dk/broendby-forlaenger-med-jesper-lindstroem-og-rykker-ham-i-superliga-truppen-til-sommer/|title=Brøndby forlænger med Jesper Lindstrøm og rykker ham i Superliga-truppen til sommer|newspaper=3point.dk|access-date=29 July 2019|language=da}}</ref>

====2019–20====
Lindstrøm made his breakthrough for the first team in the [[2019–20 Danish Superliga|2019–20 season]] under new coach [[Niels Frederiksen]]. On 11 July, Lindstrøm made his first European appearance for Brøndby, coming on as a substitute in the first leg of their [[UEFA Europa League]] qualification round against [[FC Inter Turku|Inter Turku]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bold.dk/fodbold/nyheder/jublende-jobbe-bif-debut-var-som-en-droem/ |title=Jublende 'Jobbe': BIF-debut var som en drøm |work=bold.dk |language=da |date=12 July 2019 |accessdate=29 July 2019}}</ref> On 28 July, he made his first start and scored the first senior goal of his career to contribute to a 3–2 victory in a league match against [[Odense Boldklub|OB]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://ekstrabladet.dk/sport/fodbold/dansk_fodbold/superligaen/broendby-i-dramatisk-comeback/7729405|title=Brøndby i dramatisk comeback|last=Kjeldtoft|first=Jan|date=28 July 2019|work=Ekstra Bladet|access-date=29 July 2019|language=da}}</ref> On 1 August, he came on as a substitute in the last minute of regulation and scored two goals in extra time as Brøndby defeated [[Lechia Gdańsk]] 4-1 (5-3 aggregate) in the second round of Europa League qualification.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bold.dk/fodbold/nyheder/stortalent-blev-broendby-helt-i-elektrisk-kulisse/|title=Stortalent blev Brøndby-helt i elektrisk kulisse|last=Tøfting|first=Nicolai|date=1 August 2019|work=bold.dk|access-date=1 August 2019|language=da}}</ref> Due to his performances in the first months of the [[2019–20 Danish Superliga|2019–20 season]], he received a lot of attention and was pointed out as the club's ''jóia da coroa'' ([[Crown jewels|crown jewel]]) in a scouting report by [[S.C. Braga|Braga]], Brøndby's opponent in Europa League qualifiers.<ref>{{cite web |title=Brøndby: Cinco jogadores a estar atento |url=https://scbraga.pt/6052-2/ |website=[[SC Braga]] |access-date=27 May 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191018114201/https://scbraga.pt/6052-2/ |archive-date=18 October 2019 |language=pt |date=2 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://3point.dk/braga-lindstroem-er-broendbys-kronjuvel/|title=Braga: Lindstrøm er Brøndbys kronjuvel|last=Møller Karlsen|first=Nanna|date=7 August 2019|work=3point.dk|access-date=18 October 2019|language=da}}</ref> After the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Denmark|COVID-19 pandemic]] had suspended the Superliga for some months, Lindstrøm scored the club's first goal in competition after the return, securing a 1&ndash;0 win over [[SønderjyskE Fodbold|SønderjyskE]] on 2 June 2020.<ref>{{cite web |author1=[[Ritzau]] |title=MINUT FOR MINUT Unge Lindstrøm blev matchvinder i Brøndby |url=https://www.dr.dk/sporten/fodbold/superliga/minut-minut-unge-lindstroem-blev-matchvinder-i-broendby |website=dr.dk |publisher=[[Danmarks Radio]] |accessdate=3 June 2020 |date=2 June 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Qvirin Petersen |first1=Jacob |title=Indskifter sikrer Brøndby sejren i hård genstart |url=https://sport.tv2.dk/fodbold/2020-06-02-indskifter-sikrer-broendby-sejren-i-haard-genstart |website=sport.tv2.dk |publisher=[[TV 2 (Denmark)|TV 2]] |accessdate=3 June 2020 |date=2 June 2020}}</ref>

On 3 July 2020, Lindstrøm signed a new contract with Brøndby, keeping him at the club until 2023.<ref>{{cite web |title=Brøndby IF forlænger med Jobbe og Anis |url=https://brondby.com/nyhed/2020/07/03/broendby-if-forlaenger-med-jobbe-og-anis |website=brondby.com |publisher=[[Brøndby IF]] |accessdate=3 July 2020 |date=3 July 2020}}</ref> He finished his first season at senior level with 33 total appearances in which he scored five goals.<ref name="SW"/>

== International career ==
===Youth career and early senior career===
Lindstrøm won three caps for Denmark at [[Denmark national under-19 football team|under-19]] level.<ref name="DBU">{{Cite web |url=https://www.dbu.dk/landshold/landsholdsdatabasen/playerInfo/8021 |title=Landsholdsdatabasen spiller information |website=www.dbu.dk |accessdate=29 July 2019}}</ref>

On 29 September 2020, Lindstrøm was named in manager Albert Capella's squad for the [[2021 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification|2021 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualifiers]] against [[Malta national under-21 football team|Malta]] and [[Finland national under-21 football team|Finland]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Møller Karlsen |first1=Nanna |title=Jesper Lindstrøm udtaget til U/21-landsholdet |url=https://3point.dk/nyheder/jesper-lindstrom-udtaget-til-u21-landsholdet |website=3point.dk |accessdate=12 October 2020 |language=Danish |date=29 September 2020}}</ref> He made his debut on 9 October, in a 3–1 win over Malta, scoring his first goal in the dying minutes of the game to secure the win.<ref>{{cite web |author1=[[Ritzau|/ritzau/]] |title=U21-landsholdet tæt på EM trods problemer mod miniput |url=https://sport.tv2.dk/fodbold/2020-10-09-u21-landsholdet-taet-paa-em-trods-problemer-mod-miniput |website=sport.tv2.dk |publisher=TV 2 |accessdate=12 October 2020 |language=Danish |date=9 October 2020}}</ref>
Lindstrøm was included in the 23-person squad for the [[2021 UEFA European Under-21 Championship|2021 European Under-21 Championship]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://dbu.dk/landshold/herrelandshold/u21-landsholdet/ |title=U21-Landsholdet - Trup |website=www.dbu.dk |accessdate=27 March 2021}}</ref>

On 9 November 2020, Lindstrøm was called up to [[Kasper Hjulmand]]'s senior squad for the [[Exhibition game|friendly]] against [[Sweden national football team|Sweden]] due to several cancellations from, among others, the Danish national team players playing in England, due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in England|COVID-19 restrictions]], as well as a case of [[Coronavirus disease 2019|COVID-19]] in the squad, which had put several national team players in quarantine. He came on as a substitute in the second-half during the match against Sweden at [[Brøndby Stadium]] in his debut match, on 11 November 2020.<ref>{{cite news |title=Wind og Bah sikrer dansk 2-0 sejr over Sverige |url=https://dbu.dk/nyheder/2020/november/wind-og-bah-sikrer-dansk-2-0-sejr-over-sverige/ |accessdate=12 November 2020 |work=[[Danish Football Association]] |date=11 November 2020 |language=Danish }}</ref>

===2022 FIFA World Cup===
On 7 November 2022, Lindstrøm was called up to the [[2022 FIFA World Cup squads#Denmark|Denmark squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web |title=De første spillere til VM-truppen er fundet |url=https://www.dbu.dk/nyheder/2022/november/de-foerste-spillere-til-vm-truppen-er-fundet/ |website=DBU |access-date=22 November 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221121234236/https://www.dbu.dk/nyheder/2022/november/de-foerste-spillere-til-vm-truppen-er-fundet/ |archive-date=21 November 2022 |language=da |date=7 November 2022 |url-status=live}}</ref> On 22 November 2022, he made his debut in a major tournament in Denmark's opening World Cup game against [[Tunisia national football team|Tunisia]], coming on as a replacement for [[Andreas Skov Olsen]] in the 65th minute of a 0–0 draw at [[Education City Stadium]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Denmark 0–0 Tunisia |url=https://www.fifa.com/fifaplus/en/match-centre/match/17/255711/285063/400235466 |website=FIFA |access-date=22 November 2022 |date=22 November 2022}}</ref>
