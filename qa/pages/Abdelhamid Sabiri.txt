'''Abdelhamid Sabiri''' ({{lang-ar|عبد الحميد صابيري}}; {{lang-ber|ⵄⴱⴷⴻⵍⵀⴰⵎⵉⴷ ⵙⴰⴱⵉⵔⵉ}}; born 28 November 1996) is a Moroccan professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Midfielder#Attacking midfielder|attacking midfielder]] for {{Italian football updater|Sampdoria}} club [[U.C. Sampdoria|Sampdoria]], on loan from [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]], and the [[Morocco national football team|Morocco national team]].

Sabiri has previously played for [[Sportfreunde Siegen]], [[1. FC Nürnberg]], [[Huddersfield Town A.F.C.|Huddersfield Town]] and [[SC Paderborn 07|SC Paderborn]]. Born in Morocco, he represented Germany at [[Germany national football team|under-21]] level, before switching allegiance to Morocco in 2022.

==Early life==
Sabiri was born in Morocco and moved to Germany at the age of three. There, he was raised in [[Frankfurt]] and holds dual citizenship of Germany and Morocco.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sabiri spielt für Mamas Miete|url=http://www.bild.de/sport/fussball/1-fc-nuernberg/sabiri-spielt-fuer-mamas-miete-52516750.bild.html|website=bild.de|language=German|date=11 July 2017|access-date=23 August 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title= Huddersfield seal 11th summer signing as No.10 Abdelhamid Sabiri joins from Nurnberg |url=http://www.squawka.com/news/huddersfield-seal-summer-signing-as-no-10-abdelhamid-sabiri-joins-from-nurnberg/983498|website=squawka.com|date=23 August 2017|access-date=23 August 2017}}</ref>
Sabiri comes from an Amazigh (Berber) family <ref>Interview with Assahifa channel {{YouTube|id=6g0spFSTF-8|time=7m25s}}</ref> originating from the city of [[Goulmima]], [[Errachidia Province]], [[Morocco]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-09-24 |title=عبد الحميد الصابيري يفخر بأصله كلميمة - المحرر |url=https://www.almoharir.com/218348.html |access-date=2022-12-21 |language=ar}}</ref>

==Club career==
After spending his youth career with [[TuS Koblenz]] and [[SV Darmstadt 98|Darmstadt 98]], he began his club career with [[Sportfreunde Siegen]] in the [[Oberliga (football)|German fifth tier]]. After achieving 20 goals and six assists in 33 appearances in all competitions, he moved to [[1. FC Nürnberg]] in 2016, initially playing for [[1. FC Nürnberg II|the club's second team]]. He was promoted to the first team in January 2017 and went on to score five times in nine [[2. Bundesliga]] appearances towards the end of the season.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Threlfall-Sykes|first=David|date=23 August 2017|title=TRANSFER: ABDELHAMID SABIRI ARRIVES AT TOWN|url=https://www.htafc.com/news/2017/august/transfer-abdelhamid-sabiri-arrives-at-town/|url-status=live|access-date=3 March 2021|website=[[Huddersfield Town]]|language=en-gb|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20170823161500/https://www.htafc.com/news/2017/august/transfer-abdelhamid-sabiri-arrives-at-town/  |archive-date = 23 August 2017 }}</ref>

===Huddersfield Town===
Sabiri joined English club [[Huddersfield Town A.F.C.|Huddersfield Town]] on 23 August 2017 on a three-year deal, for an undisclosed fee. The club had recently been promoted to the [[Premier League]], the highest tier of [[English football league system|English league football]].<ref name=":0" /> He made his Premier League debut in a 2–0 loss away to [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham]] on 11 September 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.footballfallout.com/premier-league-2017-2018/game/west-ham-united-vs-huddersfield-town-11-09-2017|title=West Ham United v Huddersfield Town|website=Football Fallout|language=en|access-date=12 September 2017}}</ref> He made sporadic appearances for the Terriers, after suffering injuries, before his contract was terminated by mutual agreement on 27 August 2019.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Limb|first=Callum|date=27 August 2019|title=SABIRI DEPARTS HUDDERSFIELD TOWN|url=https://www.htafc.com/news/2019/august/sabiri-departs-huddersfield-town/|url-status=live|access-date=3 March 2021|website=[[Huddersfield Town]]|language=en-gb|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20190827133650/https://www.htafc.com/news/2019/august/sabiri-departs-huddersfield-town/  |archive-date = 27 August 2019 }}</ref>

===SC Paderborn===
On 27 August 2019, Sabiri joined [[SC Paderborn 07|SC Paderborn]] on a two-year deal, with the option of a third year.<ref>{{cite web|date=27 August 2019|title=Abdelhamid Sabiri kommt von Huudersfield Town|url=https://www.scp07.de/Newsarchiv/Offensiv-mit-Potenzial.html|url-status=live|access-date=27 August 2019|website=[[SC Paderborn]]|publisher=|language=de|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20190827164731/https://www.scp07.de/Newsarchiv/Offensiv-mit-Potenzial.html  |archive-date = 27 August 2019 }}</ref>

===Ascoli===
On 28 September 2020, he joined Italian [[Serie B]] club [[Ascoli Calcio 1898 F.C.|Ascoli]] on a two-year contract.<ref>{{cite press release|publisher=[[Ascoli Calcio 1898 F.C.|Ascoli]]|url=https://www.ascolicalcio1898.it/index.php/news/dettaglio/calciomercato-biennale-con-opzione-per-sabiri|title=Calciomercato: biennale con opzione per Sabiri.|date=28 September 2020|language=Italian}}</ref>

===Sampdoria===
On 29 January 2022, he moved to [[Serie A]] club [[U.C. Sampdoria|Sampdoria]] on loan with an option to buy and a conditional obligation to buy.<ref>{{cite press release|publisher=[[U.C. Sampdoria|Sampdoria]]|url=https://www.sampdoria.it/news/club/2022/01/29/sabiri-e-blucerchiato-arriva-dallascoli-a-titolo-temporaneo/|title=Sabiri è blucerchiato: arriva dall'Ascoli a titolo temporaneo|date=29 January 2022|access-date=29 January 2022|language=it}}</ref> Sabiri made his debut in a 2–0 loss against [[Atalanta B.C.]]<ref>{{Cite web |date=13 August 2022 |title=Sampdoria 0-2 Atalanta |url=https://www.atalanta.it/en/news/sampdoria-0-2-atalanta-2022-2023/ |access-date=24 September 2022 |website=Atalanta |language=en-US}}</ref> He scored his first goal for the club against [[Spezia Calcio|Spezia]], in his seventh appearance for the team.<ref>{{Cite web |date=17 September 2022 |title=Serie A, Spezia-Sampdoria 2-1, pagelle: Nzola decisivo, Sabiri da cineteca |url=https://www.eurosport.it/calcio/serie-a/2022-2023/serie-a-spezia-sampdoria-2-1-pagelle-nzola-decisivo-sabiri-da-cineteca_sto9148028/story.shtml |access-date=24 September 2022 |website=Eurosport |language=it}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=VIDEO {{!}} Spezia-Sampdoria 2-1: derby da incubo, non basta la magia di Sabiri |url=https://www.genovatoday.it/sport/calcio/spezia-sampdoria-2-1-video-gol.html |access-date=24 September 2022 |website=GenovaToday |language=it}}</ref> On 30 January 2023, Italian sources confirmed talks between Sampadoria and [[Fiorentina]] to sign Sabiri for 4 million euros.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-01-30 |title=Fiorentina close in on Abdelhamid Sabiri |url=https://en.hespress.com/57774-fiorentina-close-in-on-abdelhamid-sabiri.html |access-date=2023-01-31 |website=HESPRESS English - Morocco News |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Kasraoui |first=Safaa |title=Fiorentina Eying Morocco's Midfielder Abdelhamid Sabiri |url=https://www.moroccoworldnews.com/2023/01/353769/fiorentina-eying-moroccos-midfielder-abdelhamid-sabiri |access-date=2023-01-31 |website=moroccoworldnews |language=en}}</ref>

===Fiorentina===
On 31 January 2023, Sabiri signed with [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]] and was loaned back to Sampdoria for the rest of the 2022–23 season.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=ACF Fiorentina|url=https://www.acffiorentina.com/it/news/tutte/prima-squadra-maschile/2023-01-31/la-fiorentina-acquista-sabiri-restera-in-prestito-alla-samp-fino-a-giugno|title=LA FIORENTINA ACQUISTA SABIRI, RESTERÀ IN PRESTITO ALLA SAMP FINO A GIUGNO|date=31 January 2023|access-date=31 January 2023|language=it}}</ref>

==International career==
Sabiri was called up to [[Germany national under-21 football team|Germany's under-21 side]] in October 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.examinerlive.co.uk/sport/football/news/you-can-see-seven-huddersfield-15283797|title=When you can see SEVEN Town internationals in action tomorrow|last=Benson|first=Rory|date=15 October 2018|work=Huddersfield Examiner|access-date=15 October 2018}}</ref> He scored once in a total of five appearances for the team, against the [[Netherlands national under-21 football team|Netherlands]].<ref name=":1" />

In April 2022, Sabiri said he wanted to play in the World Cup with the [[Morocco national football team|Moroccan national team]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://lionsdelatlas.ma/abdelhamid-sabiri-jaimerais-jouer-la-coupe-du-monde-avec-le-maroc/|title=Abdelhamid Sabiri : " J'aimerais jouer la Coupe du Monde avec le Maroc " |date=8 April 2022|access-date=30 April 2022}}</ref> In September 2022, Sabiri was called up to join the Moroccan national team.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.facebook.com/ENMAROCofficiel/photos/ms.c.eJw1x8kJACAQBMGMZNyx98g~;MUGwfkVia6srTkiLdyUede1~;QhSeC~_IbCXI~-.bps.a.123756449548237/565330108724200/|title=Équipe du Maroc|date=12 September 2022|access-date=12 September 2022}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=12 September 2022 |title=Hakim Ziyech makes return to World Cup team under Regragui's preselection |url=https://en.hespress.com/49144-hakim-ziyech-makes-return-to-word-cup-team-under-regraguis-selection.html |access-date=24 September 2022 |website=HESPRESS English - Morocco News |language=en-US}}</ref> He played his first match in a friendlies against [[Chile national football team|Chile]], in which he managed to score a goal in a 2–0 victory that took place in the [[RCDE Stadium]] in [[Barcelona]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=23 September 2022 |title=Morocco defeats Chili in friendly game 2-0 |url=https://en.hespress.com/49880-morocco-defeats-chili-in-friendly-game-2-0.html |access-date=24 September 2022 |website=HESPRESS English - Morocco News |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=24 September 2022 |title=England booed as World Cup prep hits horror eight-year low; Brazil make huge statement |url=https://www.foxsports.com.au/football/world-cup/nations-league-england-vs-italy-brazil-vs-ghana-news-scores-results-world-cup-2022/news-story/2de75e18d5da7378ad99eddd3e664dfa |access-date=24 September 2022 |website=Fox Sports |language=en}}</ref>

On 10 November 2022, Sabiri was named in Morocco's 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Morocco World Cup 2022 squad: Who's in and who's out? |url=https://www.goal.com/en-bh/lists/morocco-2022-world-cup-squad-hakimi-ziyech-mazraoui-qatar/blta36257fc4feeb66a |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=Goal.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=10 November 2022 |title=Moroccan coach unveils list of 26 Atlas Lions in 2022 World Cup |url=https://en.hespress.com/52697-moroccan-coach-unveils-list-of-26-atlas-lions-in-2022-world-cup.html |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=HESPRESS English - Morocco News |language=en-US}}</ref>
