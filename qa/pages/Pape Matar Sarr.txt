'''Pape Matar Sarr''' (born 14 September 2002) is a Senegalese professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[Premier League]] club [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] and the [[Senegal national football team|Senegal national team]].

== Club career ==
Pape Matar Sarr made his professional debut with [[Génération Foot]] in his native country of [[Senegal]], before signing a five-year contract with [[Ligue 1]] side [[FC Metz|Metz]] on 15 September 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|date=15 September 2020|title=Official {{!}} Metz sign Pape Matar Sarr from Génération Foot {{!}} Get French Football News|url=https://www.getfootballnewsfrance.com/2020/official-metz-signe-pape-matar-sarr-from-generation-foot/|url-status=live|access-date=8 June 2021|website=www.getfootballnewsfrance.com}}</ref>

Sarr was initially sent to play for Metz's second team in [[Championnat National 2]], but made only one appearance before he was recalled to the first team squad following the season being halted due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in France]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Bouchacourt|first=Jérome|date=23 April 2021|title=La FFF a décidé d'arrêter les championnats de National 2 et D2 Féminine|url=https://www.footamateur.fr/la-fff-a-decide-darreter-les-championnats-de-national-2-et-d2-feminine/|access-date=8 June 2021|website=Foot Amateur|language=fr-FR}}</ref> He made his debut for the Metz first team on 29 November 2020 in a Ligue 1 game against [[Stade Brestois 29|Brest]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Secret|first=Bryan|date=29 November 2020|title=Résultat et résumé Metz - Brest, Ligue 1 Uber Eats, 12e journée|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/match-direct/ligue-1/2020-2021/metz-brest-live/477270|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=17 January 2021|website=[[L'Équipe]]|language=fr}}</ref> On 31 January 2021, Sarr scored his first goal in Ligue 1 in the reverse fixture, a 4–2 away victory over Brest.<ref>{{Cite web|date=31 January 2021|title=BREST 2-4 METZ|url=https://www.ligue1.com/match|url-status=live|access-date=8 June 2021|website=Ligue1|language=en}}</ref>

On 27 August 2021, Sarr signed for [[Premier League]] club [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]]. He was loaned back to Metz until the end of the [[2021–22 FC Metz season|2021–22 season]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=27 August 2021|title=Pape Matar Sarr signs from Metz|url=https://www.tottenhamhotspur.com/news/2021/august/pape-matar-sarr-signs-from-metz/|url-status=live|access-date=27 August 2021|website=|publisher=[[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.]]}}</ref> On 1 January 2023, Sarr finally made his long awaited [[Premier League]] debut coming on as an 80th minute substitute for [[Yves Bissouma]] in a 2-0 home loss to [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Sang|first=Kiplagat|date=1 January 2023|title=Pape Matar Sarr: Senegal wonderkid makes Tottenham Hotspur debut after 18 months|url=https://www.goal.com/en-gb/amp/news/pape-matar-sarr-senegal-wonderkid-makes-tottenham-hotspur-debut-after-18-months/bltf7640624f893bda7|access-date=4 January 2023|website=goal.com}}</ref> Since his first appearance against [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]] Sarr has featured regularly for Spurs, either coming off the bench or starting as was the case in the North London derby loss to [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]]. He got his first goal contribution, an assist on 28 May 2023, in the last game of the season, a 4-1 away win at Leeds United.

== International career ==
Sarr made his debut for [[Senegal national football team|Senegal national team]] on 26 March 2021 in an [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations qualification Group I|AFCON 2021 qualifier]] against [[Congo national football team|Congo]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[ESPN]]|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/match?gameId=596273|title=Congo v Senegal game report|date=26 March 2021}}</ref> On 6 February 2022, he won the [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations]] with Senegal.<ref name=":0" />

He was appointed a [[National Order of the Lion|Grand Officer of the National Order of the Lion]] by [[President of Senegal]] [[Macky Sall]] following the nation's victory at the 2021 Africa Cup of Nations.<ref name= "reuters">{{cite web |title=Cash prizes, real estate, and highest honours as Senegal celebrates Nations Cup heroes |url=https://www.reuters.com/lifestyle/sports/cash-prizes-real-estate-highest-honours-senegal-celebrates-nations-cup-heroes-2022-02-08/ |website=[[Reuters]] |access-date=28 March 2023 |date=8 February 2022}}</ref>
