'''Antonee Robinson''' (born August 8, 1997), nicknamed '''Jedi''',<ref>{{cite web |date=January 28, 2022 |title=Antonee 'Jedi' Robinson gives Berhalter, USMNT the World Cup qualifying win they were looking for |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/united-states-usa/story/4580735/antonee-jedi-robinson-gives-berhalterusmnt-the-world-cup-qualifying-win-they-were-looking-for |access-date=May 4, 2022 |website=ESPN}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |date=August 12, 2018 |title=GETTING TO KNOW: ANTONEE ROBINSON |url=https://wiganathletic.com/news/2018/august/getting-to-know-antonee-robinson/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180812194559/https://www.wiganathletic.com/news/2018/august/getting-to-know-antonee-robinson/ |archive-date=August 12, 2018 |access-date=May 4, 2022 |publisher=Wigan Athletic F.C.}}</ref> is an American professional [[Association football|soccer]]<!--Robinson is an American, and the name of the sport in the United States is "soccer". MOS:TIES applies to the WP:LANGVAG used.--> player who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|left-back]] for [[Premier League]] club [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]] and the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States national team]].

==Youth soccer==
Born in [[Milton Keynes]], England, Robinson came through Everton's [[Everton F.C. Reserves and Academy|academy]], having been with the academy since the age of 11.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.evertonfc.com/players/a/ar/antonee-robinson|title=Antonee Robinson|publisher=Everton Official Site|access-date=August 10, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170810170424/http://www.evertonfc.com/players/a/ar/antonee-robinson|archive-date=August 10, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> In June 2013, he signed a scholarship with the club, becoming a full-time player.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2013/06/29/14-become-scholars|title=14 Become Scholars|publisher=Everton F.C.|date=June 29, 2013|access-date=February 14, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180112194918/http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2013/06/29/14-become-scholars|archive-date=January 12, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Everton===
While with Everton's U18 side, Robinson suffered injuries that saw him sidelined most of the time. He appeared for the U21 side toward the end of the 2014–15 season.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2015/05/26/thorniley-wins-top-prize-at-academy-awards|title=Thorniley Takes Top Academy Prize|publisher=Everton F.C.|date=May 26, 2015|access-date=February 14, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150920085615/http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2015/05/26/thorniley-wins-top-prize-at-academy-awards|archive-date=September 20, 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> As a result, he was awarded the club's Under-18 Players' Player of the Season.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2015/06/05/robinson-pleased-with-recognition|title=Robinson Pleased With Recognition|publisher=Everton F.C.|date=June 5, 2015|access-date=February 14, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180214203032/http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2015/06/05/robinson-pleased-with-recognition|archive-date=February 14, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> At the end of the 2014–15 season, he signed his first professional contract with the club after being offered a new contract.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2015/06/10/club-submits-retained-list|title=Club Submits Retained List|publisher=Everton F.C.|date=June 10, 2015|access-date=February 14, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170825210345/http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2015/06/10/club-submits-retained-list|archive-date=August 25, 2017|url-status=dead}}<br>{{cite web|url=http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2015/07/03/grant-pens-new-deal|title=Grant Pens New Deal|publisher=Everton F.C.|date=July 3, 2015|access-date=February 14, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924040604/http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2015/07/03/grant-pens-new-deal|archive-date=February 14, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

However, Robinson spent the most of the 2015–16 season sidelined by torn cartilage under his kneecap, resulting in knee surgery.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2016/05/29/next-generation-whos-next|title=Next Generation: Who's Next?|publisher=Everton F.C.|date=May 29, 2016|access-date=February 14, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170825212413/http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2016/05/29/next-generation-whos-next|archive-date=August 25, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> Despite this, he signed a one-year contract extension on July 15, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2016/07/15/new-deals-for-charsley-and-robinson|title=Charsley & Robinson Extend Deals|publisher=Everton F.C.|date=July 15, 2016|access-date=February 14, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170825212604/http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2016/07/15/new-deals-for-charsley-and-robinson|archive-date=August 25, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> Robinson then played all three matches in the [[EFL Trophy]], which saw [[Everton F.C. Reserves and Academy|Everton U23s]] eliminated in the Group stage. He also became a regular for the U23 side.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.evertonfc.com/fixtures/2010-to-2019/2016-2017/football-league-trophy/cheltenham-town|title=Robinson Targets Trophy Progress|publisher=Everton F.C.|date=October 4, 2016|access-date=February 14, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180214212355/http://www.evertonfc.com/fixtures/2010-to-2019/2016-2017/football-league-trophy/cheltenham-town|archive-date=February 14, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> At the end of the 2016–17 season, Robinson signed a two-year contract extension with the club.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2017/07/06/robinson-signs-contract-extension|title=New Deal For Young Blues Defender|publisher=Everton F.C.|date=July 6, 2017|access-date=February 14, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170709005741/http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2017/07/06/robinson-signs-contract-extension|archive-date=July 9, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>

====Loan to Bolton Wanderers====
On August 4, 2017, it was announced that Antonee Robinson had signed on loan with [[Bolton Wanderers F.C.|Bolton Wanderers]] until January 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bwfc.co.uk/news/2017/august/wanderers-sign-premier-league-starlet-on-loan/|title=Wanderers sign Antonee Robinson on loan|publisher=Bolton Wanderers F.C.|date=August 4, 2017|access-date=February 14, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170805060157/https://www.bwfc.co.uk/news/2017/august/wanderers-sign-premier-league-starlet-on-loan/|archive-date=August 5, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref>

Five days after joining the club, on August 9, 2017, he made his debut in a 2–1 [[EFL Cup|League Cup]] win at [[Crewe Alexandra F.C.|Crewe Alexandra]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Crewe Alexandra 1 – 2 Bolton Wanderers|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40798476|publisher=BBC Sport|date=August 9, 2017|access-date=February 14, 2018}}</ref> Two weeks later, on August 22, 2017, he set up a goal for [[Jem Karacan]] to score the club's third goal in a 3–2 win over [[Sheffield Wednesday F.C.|Sheffield Wednesday]] in the League Cup.<ref>{{cite news|title=Bolton Wanderers 3 – 2 Sheffield Wednesday|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40932011|publisher=BBC Sport|date=August 22, 2017|access-date=February 14, 2018}}</ref> He made his league debut for the side on September 9, 2017, in a 3–0 loss against [[Middlesbrough F.C.|Middlesbrough]], playing the full 90 minutes.<ref>{{cite news|title=Bolton Wandrers 0 – 3 Middlesbrough|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41132802|publisher=BBC Sport|date=September 9, 2017|access-date=February 14, 2018}}</ref> Robinson then established himself in the left-back position, beating out competition from [[Andrew Taylor (footballer, born 1986)|Andrew Taylor]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Dynamic Everton full-back Antonee Robinson can be out of this world for Bolton Wanderers, says Phil Parkinson|url=http://www.theboltonnews.co.uk/sport/15636276.Dynamic_Everton_loanee_Robinson_can_be_out_of_this_world/|publisher=The Bolton News|date=November 5, 2017|access-date=February 14, 2018}}</ref> It was announced on January 5, 2018, that Robinson had agreed to stay at Bolton for the remainder of the season.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2018/01/05/robinson-bolton-loan-extended|title=Robinson's Bolton Loan Extended|publisher=Everton F.C.|date=January 5, 2018|access-date=February 14, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180108174846/http://www.evertonfc.com/news/2018/01/05/robinson-bolton-loan-extended|archive-date=January 8, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===Wigan Athletic===
On July 15, 2019, Robinson joined [[Wigan Athletic]] on a permanent three-year contract.<ref>{{cite news|title=Return of the Jedi – Wigan Athletic Complete Signing of USMNT International Antonee Robinson from Everton|url=https://www.wiganathletic.com/news/2019/july/return-of-the-jedi--wigan-athletic-complete-signing-of-usmnt-international-antonee-robinson-from-everton/|publisher=Wigan Athletic|date=July 15, 2019|access-date=July 15, 2019}}</ref> He made his debut for Wigan against [[Rotherham United F.C.|Rotherham United]] and scored his first goal for the club in a 2–2 draw at [[Millwall F.C.|Millwall]] on November 26, 2019.<ref>{{cite news|title=Millwall 2 – 2 Wigan Athletic|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/50469233|work=BBC Sport|date=November 26, 2019|access-date=November 27, 2019}}</ref>

After impressing for Wigan in the [[EFL Championship]] as one of the league's top left backs, Robinson was set to sign with [[Serie A]] side [[A.C. Milan]] for $13 million before the transfer deadline of January 31, 2020.<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Report: AC Milan to Sign USMNT's Antonee Robinson From Wigan|url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2020/01/30/antonee-robinson-ac-milan-wigan-usmnt-transfer|magazine=Sports Illustrated|date=January 30, 2020|access-date=March 19, 2020}}</ref> However, the deal fell through when Robinson's medical examination at Milan revealed a heart rhythm irregularity for which further testing could not be completed before the deadline, and for which he would undergo an ablation procedure as treatment.<ref>{{cite news|title=STATEMENT ON BEHALF OF ANTONEE ROBINSON|url=https://wiganathletic.com/news/2020/march/Statement-On-Behalf-of-Antonee-Robinson/|work=Wigan Athletic|date=March 12, 2020|access-date=March 19, 2020}}</ref>

===Fulham===
Following Wigan's relegation from the Championship, Robinson moved to Premier League club Fulham on August 20, 2020, for £2 million.<ref>{{cite news|title=Robinson Signs|url=http://www.fulhamfc.com/news/2020/august/20/antonee-robinson-signs|work=Fulham FC|date=August 20, 2020|access-date=August 20, 2020}}</ref>
He made his debut for the club in a league cup game against Ipswich Town on September 16 in a Fulham win. He then played in the next round on September 23 against Sheffield Wednesday in 2–0 win. He played in the 3–0 loss to Brentford in the league cup on October 1. He made his Premier League debut on October 4 against [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton]]. He scored his first goal for the club in an [[EFL Cup]] tie against [[Birmingham City]] on August 24, 2021.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Birmingham 0-2 Fulham|language=en-GB|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58239544|access-date=August 25, 2021}}</ref>

In July 2023, he signed a new contract with Fulham until 2028.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/66139981|title=Fulham's Robinson signs new contract until 2028|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>

==International career==
Robinson was eligible for both [[England national football team|England]] and the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]]. He was born in [[Milton Keynes]] and raised in Liverpool, England. His father was born in England but raised in [[White Plains, New York]] and gained American citizenship.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://americansoccernow.com/articles/antonee-robinson-making-most-of-loan-to-bolton|title=Antonee Robinson Making Most of Loan to Bolton|publisher=American Soccer Now|access-date=November 26, 2017}}</ref> Robinson is also of Jamaican descent through his paternal grandmother.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2020/09/07/antonee-robinson-fulham-wigan-milan-heart-usmnt-berhalter|title=Amid a Tumultuous Year, it All Works Out for Antonee Robinson|first=Brian|last=Straus|magazine=Sports Illustrated|date=September 7, 2020 }}</ref>

Robinson was first [[Cap (sport)|capped]] at under-18 level with the United States.<ref>{{cite web|title=U.S. U-18 MNT FALLS TO GERMANY 3–2 IN MARBELLA, SPAIN|url=http://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2014/12/19/14/22/141219-u18mnt-falls-to-germany-in-spain|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141225202324/http://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2014/12/19/14/22/141219-u18mnt-falls-to-germany-in-spain|archive-date=December 25, 2014|access-date=August 10, 2017|publisher=U.S. Soccer}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://lionofviennasuite.sbnation.com/2017/8/4/16095540/done-deal-bolton-wanderers-complete-loan-signing-of-everton-defender-usa-efl-epl|title=Done Deal: Bolton Wanderers complete loan signing of Everton defender|date=August 4, 2017 |publisher=SB Nation|access-date=August 10, 2017}}</ref> He was also called into the United States under-20 side, although was not capped.<ref>{{cite attracts interest|date=March 29, 2017 |publisher=HITC|access-date=August 10, 2017}}</ref> In March 2018, Robinson was invited to both the United States senior and [[England national under-21 football team|England under-21]] camps, and accepted the United States senior call for a friendly against [[Paraguay national football team|Paraguay]] in which he was named to the bench.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/england-born-robinson-is-fully-committed-to-usmnt-and-looks-like-/dfinmw1ycr71mp5se7h95zkt|title=England-born Robinson is fully committed to USMNT, and looks like left back of the future|publisher=Goal.com|access-date=January 29, 2020}}</ref> He made his United States senior debut on May 28, 2018, playing the full 90 minutes in a 3–0 friendly win against Bolivia and recording an assist.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/club/united-states/660/blog/post/3511977/antonee-robinson-excels-in-us-debut-as-americans-breeze-past-bolivia|title=Antonee Robinson excels in U.S. debut as
