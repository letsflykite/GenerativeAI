'''Filip Kostić''' ({{Lang-sr-cyr|Филип Костић}}, {{IPA-sr|fǐlip kôstitɕ|pron}}; born 1 November 1992) is a Serbian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Winger (association football)|left midfielder]] or [[left wing-back]] for [[Serie A]] club [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]] and the [[Serbia national football team|Serbia national team]].

==Club career==

===Radnički Kragujevac===
Kostić made his senior debut with [[FK Radnički Kragujevac|Radnički Kragujevac]] in 2010 at the age of 17. This was after the club was promoted to the [[Serbian SuperLiga]] from the [[Serbian First League]].<ref name="SCW">[https://int.soccerway.com/players/filip-kosti/145254/ Filip Kostić] at Soccerway</ref> He began attracting the interest of several clubs including [[Red Star Belgrade]], [[Anderlecht]], [[Udinese]], and [[Tottenham Hotspur]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mirrorfootball.co.uk/transfer-news/Tottenham-have-joined-Red-Star-Belgrade-Udinese-and-Anderlecht-in-chase-for-Filip-Kostic-article846894.html|title=Tottenham enter chase for Serbian teenager|publisher=Daily Mirror|date=28 December 2011|access-date=3 May 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111229015351/http://www.mirrorfootball.co.uk/transfer-news/Tottenham-have-joined-Red-Star-Belgrade-Udinese-and-Anderlecht-in-chase-for-Filip-Kostic-article846894.html|archive-date=29 December 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> On 15 April 2012, he was named starting left winger in [[Sportal]]'s ideal team of [[2011-12 Serbian SuperLiga|round 24 in the SuperLiga]].<ref>[http://www.sportal.rs/news.php?news=72199] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120510203338/http://www.sportal.rs/news.php?news=72199|date=10 May 2012}} SPORTAL.RS tim 24. kola JSL – 15 April 2012</ref>

===Groningen===
[[File:Filip Kostic.jpg|thumb|170px|left|Kostić training with [[FC Groningen]] in 2013]]
On 4 April 2012, he signed a contract with [[Eredivisie]] side [[FC Groningen|Groningen]] and moved to the [[Netherlands]] in June 2012 after the end of the season.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.fkradnicki.com/2012/04/kostic-potpisao-za-groningen/ |title=FK Radnicki – April 4, 2012 – Kostic potpisao za Groningen |access-date=1 May 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924013624/http://www.fkradnicki.com/2012/04/kostic-potpisao-za-groningen/ |archive-date=24 September 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He made his debut for Groningen on 21 October 2012.<ref>[http://www.b92.net/sport/fudbal/vesti.php?yyyy=2012&mm=10&dd=21&nav_id=653735 B92 Sport (Serbian): Filip Kostić debitovao za Groningen] 21 October 2012</ref> After getting little playing time, Kostić finally began showing promising potential from the beginning of the [[2013–14 Eredivisie]]; he attracted media attention after playing a brilliant game in which he had one assist against [[N.E.C. (football club)|NEC]],<ref>[http://www.sportske.net/vest/medjunarodni-fudbal/zivkovic-strelac-kostic-asistent-139743.html] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141217192531/http://www.sportske.net/vest/medjunarodni-fudbal/zivkovic-strelac-kostic-asistent-139743.html|date=17 December 2014}} Sportske: Živković strelac, Kostić asistent – 4 August 2013 {{in lang|sr}}</ref> and then on 25 August he added to his reputation of play-making by assisting twice against [[Go Ahead Eagles]].<ref>[http://www.sportske.net/vest/medjunarodni-fudbal/kostic-asistirao-groningen-do-boda-142598.html] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141217193807/http://www.sportske.net/vest/medjunarodni-fudbal/kostic-asistirao-groningen-do-boda-142598.html|date=17 December 2014}} Sportske: Kostić asistirao, Groningen do boda – 25 August 2013 {{in lang|sr}}</ref> Finally, on 20 October 2013, Kostić scored his first ever goal for Groningen in a 1–0 win against [[PSV Eindhoven]].<ref>[http://www.sportske.net/vest/medjunarodni-fudbal/kostic-srusio-psv-150460.html] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141217193544/http://www.sportske.net/vest/medjunarodni-fudbal/kostic-srusio-psv-150460.html|date=17 December 2014}} Sportske: Kostić srušio PSV! {{in lang|sr}} 20 October 2013</ref> Kostić received criticism for a media interview after drawing 0–0 with [[Aberdeen F.C.]] in the second round of the Europa League qualifiers. He went on record after the match saying, “We go home happy, we have done 80% of what we need to go through”.<ref>{{cite web|title=Cocky winger Filip Kostic says FC Groningen are 80% through|url=http://www.eveningexpress.co.uk/sport/football/the-dons/cocky-winger-filip-kostic-says-fc-groningen-are-80-through-1.479358|date=18 July 2014|access-date=22 December 2014}}</ref> [[FC Groningen|Groningen]] went on to lose the tie with the Scottish club 1–2 at the [[Euroborg]].<ref>{{cite news|title=FC Groningen 1 – 2 Aberdeen|work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/28331359|date=24 July 2014|access-date=22 December 2014}}</ref> [[Aberdeen F.C.|Aberdeen]] winger Jonny Hayes was later quoted as saying "I was quite happy ramming their words down their throats".<ref>{{cite web|title=Dons winger Jonny Hayes: 'I was quite happy ramming their words down their throats'|url=http://www.eveningexpress.co.uk/sport/football/the-dons/dons-winger-jonny-hayes-i-was-quite-happy-ramming-their-words-down-their-throats-1.490822|date=25 July 2014|access-date=22 December 2014}}</ref>

===VfB Stuttgart===
On 9 August 2014 Filip Kostić moved to [[Germany|German]] side [[VfB Stuttgart]] for €6 million and a possible bonus of €1 million. Groningen also would get 15% of a future transfer fee received by Stuttgart.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.fcgroningen.nl/home/nieuws/nieuwsbericht/artikel/transfer-kostic-naar-vfb-stuttgart-afgerond// | title = Transfer Kostic naar VfB Stuttgart afgerond | date = 9 August 2014 | access-date = 9 August 2014 | work = fcgroningen.nl | publisher = FC Groningen | language = nl | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20140810025645/http://www.fcgroningen.nl/home/nieuws/nieuwsbericht/artikel/transfer-kostic-naar-vfb-stuttgart-afgerond// | archive-date = 10 August 2014 | url-status = dead }}</ref> Kostić signed a contract until June 2019 with VfB Stuttgart.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.vfb.de/en/aktuell/meldungen/news/2014/transfer-filip-kostic/page/3224-1-1-.html | title = Filip Kostic transfer completed | date = 9 August 2014 | access-date = 9 August 2014 | work = vfb.de | publisher = VfB Stuttgart | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20140812223730/http://www.vfb.de/en/aktuell/meldungen/news/2014/transfer-filip-kostic/page/3224-1-1-.html | archive-date = 12 August 2014 | url-status = dead }}</ref>

===Hamburger SV===
After the relegation of VfB Stuttgart at the end of the season 2015–16, Kostić was transferred to Hamburger SV for a fee of €14 million, making him the most expensive player in the history of the club.<ref>[http://www.dw.com/en/busy-summer-fuels-expectations-for-hamburg/a-19485254 ''Busy summer fuels expectations for Hamburg'']. Deutsche Welle, 18 August 2016</ref>

[[File:FilipKostic.jpg|thumb|170px|Kostić with [[Eintracht Frankfurt]] in 2019]]

===Eintracht Frankfurt===
On 20 August 2018, after the relegation of Hamburg at the end of the season 2017–18, Kostić joined [[Eintracht Frankfurt]] on loan until the end of 2018–19 season.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.eintracht.de/news/artikel/eintracht-frankfurt-verpflichtet-filip-kostic-66807/|title=Eintracht Frankfurt verpflichtet Filip Kostic|language=de|publisher=Eintracht Frankfurt|date=20 August 2018|access-date=20 August 2018|archive-date=3 April 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190403215814/https://www.eintracht.de/news/artikel/eintracht-frankfurt-verpflichtet-filip-kostic-66807/|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On 17 May 2019, Frankfurt announced the permanent signing of Kostić on a permanent deal until 2023.

On 14 April 2022, Kostić scored two goals as Frankfurt defeated [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] 3–2 at the [[Camp Nou]] and 4–3 on aggregate in the [[2021–22 UEFA Europa League|UEFA Europa League]] to qualify for the [[2021–22 UEFA Europa League knockout phase|semi-finals]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/apr/14/eintrachy-frankfurt-filip-kostic-doubles-up-to-dump-barcelona-out-of-europa-league|title=Frankfurt's Filip Kostic doubles up to dump Barcelona out of Europa League|date=14 April 2022|work=The Guardian|access-date=15 April 2022}}</ref> Eintracht later won the Europa League, and Kostic was pronounced [[2021–22 UEFA Europa League#Player of the Season|player of the season]]<ref name=POTS/> and was also [[2021–22 UEFA Europa League#Top assists|top assists provider]].<ref name=POTS/>

=== Juventus ===
On 12 August 2022, [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]] announced the signing of Kostić on a four-year deal.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Juventus.com |title=Official {{!}} Filip Kostić signs for Juventus! - Juventus |url=https://www.juventus.com/en/news/articles/official-filip-kostic-signs-for-juventus |access-date=12 August 2022 |website=Juventus.com |language=en}}</ref> In [[Derby d'Italia]] played on 19 March 2023, he scored the only goal in 1-0 victory against Inter. <ref>{{Cite web |last=gazzetta.it |title=Kostic il soldato di Max: la macchina degli assist è diventato un bomber|url=https://www.gazzetta.it/Calcio/Serie-A/Juventus/20-03-2023/juve-kostic-soldati-allegri-macchina-assist-ora-fa-gol-460753860861.shtml |access-date=20 March 2023 |website=gazzetta.it |date=20 March 2023 |language=it}}</ref>

==International career==
Kostić has played for [[Serbia]] at [[Serbia national under-19 football team|under-19 level]] and [[Serbia national under-21 football team|under-21 level]].<ref>[http://www.srbijafudbal.net/radnicki_kragujevac/kostic_f.htm Filip Kostić] at Srbijafudbal</ref> In the [[2015 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification play-offs]], he scored the winner against Spain.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.uefa.com/under21/season=2015/matches/round=2000413/match=2015242/postmatch/report/index.html | title = Superb Serbia end Spain reign | date = 14 October 2014 | access-date = 14 October 2014 | work = uefa.com | publisher = UEFA}}</ref>

On 7 June 2015, Kostić made his debut for the [[Serbia national football team|Serbia national senior team]] against [[Azerbaijan national football team|Azerbaijan]] in a 4–1 friendly win in [[Sankt Pölten]], Austria, making way for [[Lazar Marković]] after 56 minutes.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.fss.rs/index.php?id=21&aid=8086 | title = Ubedljiva pobeda: Srbija – Azerbejdžan 4:1 (1:1) | date = 7 June 2015 | access-date = 7 June 2015 | work = fss.rs | publisher = Football Association of Serbia | language= sr}}</ref> He played in his first competitive match for Serbia on 13 June 2015 in the [[UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying Group I]] against [[Denmark national football team|Denmark]].<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro/qualifiers/season=2016/matches/round=2000446/match=2013960/index.html | title = Poulsen and Poulsen score as Denmark see off Serbia | date = 13 June 2015 | access-date = 13 June 2015 | work = uefa.com | publisher = UEFA}}</ref> On 5 September 2016, he scored his 1st International goal against the Republic of Ireland in a 2–2 draw.

In June 2018, he was selected in Serbia's squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018 World Cup]],<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44083365 | title = World Cup 2018: All the confirmed squads for this summer's finals in Russia | publisher = BBC Sport | date = 4 June 2018 | access-date = 25 June 2018}}</ref> playing all three group stage matches.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44427970 | title = Costa Rica 0–1 Serbia | publisher = BBC Sport | author = Timothy Abraham | date = 17 June 2018 | access-date = 25 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44439145 | title = Serbia 1–2 Switzerland | publisher = BBC Sport | author = Matthew Henry | date = 23 June 2018 | access-date = 25 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/06/27/sports/world-cup/brazil-vs-serbia.html|title=World Cup: Brazil cruises into next round with easy victory|newspaper=The New York Times|author=Andrew Das|date=27 June 2018|access-date=27 June 2018}}</ref>

In November 2022, he was selected in Serbia's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fss.rs/en/head-coach-dragan-stojkovic-announces-the-list-of-players-for-the-fifa-world-cup-2022-in-qatar/ |title=Head-coach Dragan Stojković announces the list of players for the FIFA World Cup 2022 in Qatar |publisher=[[Football Association of Serbia]] |date=11 November 2022 |access-date=11 November 2022}}</ref> He played in group stage matches against [[Cameroon national football team|Cameroon]]<ref>{{Cite news |last=Sky Sports |date=2022-11-28 |title=World Cup 2022 - Cameroon 3-3 Serbia: Vincent Aboubakar and Eric Maxim Choupo-Moting goals complete stunning comeback |language=en |work=Sky Sports |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/cameroon-vs-serbia/report/462988#:~:text=World%20Cup%202022%20%2D%20Cameroon%203,Moting%20goals%20complete%20stunning%20comeback&text=Cameroon%20kept%20alive%20their%20faint,with%20Serbia%20in%20Al%2DWakrah. |access-date=2022-11-28}}</ref> and [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]].<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/dec/02/serbia-switzerland-world-cup-group-g-match-report |title=Switzerland advance and Serbia go out as Freuler finish settles stormy contest |first=Nick |last=Ames |newspaper=The Guardian|location=London |date=2 December 2022 |access-date=7 December 2022}}</ref> Serbia finished fourth in the group.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://soccer.nbcsports.com/2022/12/02/world-cup-2022-group-g-brazil-serbia-cameroon-switzerland-rankings-table-standings/ |title=World Cup 2022 Group G: Brazil, Serbia, Cameroon, Switzerland schedule, fixtures, rankings |first=Nicholas |last=Mendola |newspaper=NBC Sports|location=Lusail |date=2 December 2022 |access-date=7 December 2022}}</ref>
