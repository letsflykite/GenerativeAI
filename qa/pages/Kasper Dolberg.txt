'''Kasper Dolberg Rasmussen''' ({{IPA-da|ˈkʰæspɐ ˈtʌlˌpɛɐ̯ˀ}}; born 6 October 1997) is a Danish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]] for [[Belgian Pro League]] club [[R.S.C. Anderlecht|Anderlecht]] and the [[Denmark national football team|Denmark national team]].<ref name="RSCA">{{cite news|url=https://www.rsca.be/en/Dolberg|title=Kasper Dolberg signs for RSCA|publisher=R.S.C. Anderlecht|date=7 July 2023|access-date=7 July 2023}}</ref>

Dolberg made his senior debut at [[Silkeborg IF]] in May 2015. He joined Ajax in July 2015 and made his debut for the club in July 2016. He represented Denmark at [[Denmark national under-16 football team|under-16]], [[Denmark national under-17 football team|under-17]], [[Denmark national under-19 football team|under-19]] and [[Denmark national under-21 football team|under-21]] level before making his senior international debut in November 2016.

In May 2020, Dolberg was voted as player of the year by the fans of his club [[OGC Nice]]. While at Ajax, he was named [[Dutch Footballer of the Year|Dutch Football Talent of the year]] after his first season in the [[2016–17 Eredivisie|Eredivisie]] in 2017.

==Club career==
===Silkeborg IF===
On 17 May 2015, he made his senior debut for Silkeborg IF in a [[Danish Superliga]] game against [[Brøndby IF]], replacing [[Adeola Lanre Runsewe]] after 63 minutes in a 0-2 loss.<ref name="soccerway2">{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2015/05/17/denmark/superliga/silkeborg-if-fs/brondby-if/1690355/|title=Silkeborg vs. Brøndby IF - 17 May 2015 - Soccerway|publisher=soccerway.com|access-date=28 June 2015}}</ref>

===Ajax===
[[File:Ajax Open Training (28285488372).jpg|thumb|left|Dolberg with Ajax teammates (including [[Matthijs de Ligt]] and [[Donny van de Beek]]) in 2016.]]
On 5 January 2015, [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]] announced that 17-year-old Dolberg would join from Silkeborg IF in the summer, signing a three-year contract valid from summer 2015 until summer 2018.<ref>{{cite news|title=Ajax legt 'buitengewoon talent' Dolberg vast|trans-title=Ajax secures "exceptional talent" Dolberg|url=http://www.vi.nl/nieuws/ajax-legt-buitengewoon-talent-dolberg-vast.htm|access-date=5 October 2016|website=vi.nl|publisher=Voetbal International|date=5 January 2015|language=nl}}</ref> He had been spotted by Danish scout John Steen Olsen, the same scout who discovered players such as [[Zlatan Ibrahimović]], [[Viktor Fischer]], and [[Christian Eriksen]] for the Dutch capital club.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kijktip: Portret Deense meesterscout|url=http://www.ajax.nl/streams/ajax-actueel/kijktip-portret-deense-meesterscout.htm|website=Ajax|access-date=5 October 2016|language=nl|date=12 January 2016}}</ref> On 13 May 2016, Dolberg agreed to have his contract extended until 2021.<ref>{{cite news|title=Ajax breekt contract met talent Holberg open|url=http://www.vi.nl/nieuws/ajax-breekt-contract-met-talent-dolberg-open.htm|access-date=5 October 2016|website=vi.nl|publisher=Voetbal International|date=5 January 2015|language=nl}}</ref>

On 26 July 2016, Dolberg scored his first goal in European competition on his competitive debut for Ajax, in a 1–1 [[2016–17 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round#Third qualifying round|Champions League qualification]] match against [[PAOK FC|PAOK]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/07/26/europe/uefa-champions-league/afc-ajax/paok-fc/2282253/|title=Ajax vs. PAOK |website=soccerway.com|publisher=Soccerway|date=26 July 2016}}</ref> He made his [[Eredivisie]] debut for the club on 7 August 2016 in a match against [[Sparta Rotterdam]], coming on as a substitute for [[Mateo Cassierra]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/08/07/netherlands/eredivisie/sparta-rotterdam-bv/afc-ajax/2240300/|title=Sparta Rotterdam vs. Ajax|website=soccerway.com|publisher=Soccerway|date=7 August 2016}}</ref> The following week he was in the starting line-up for the league match against [[Roda JC]] and he scored both of Ajax's goals in the 2–2 draw, his first goals in the Eredivisie.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/08/13/netherlands/eredivisie/afc-ajax/roda-jc/2240304/|title=Ajax vs. Roda JC |website=soccerway.com|publisher=Soccerway|date=13 August 2016}}</ref> On 29 September 2016, Dolberg scored the only goal in a 1–0 victory over [[Standard Liège]] in a [[2016–17 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]] match.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/09/29/europe/uefa-cup/afc-ajax/standard-de-liege/2348968/|title=Ajax vs. Standard Liège |website=soccerway.com|publisher=Soccerway|date=29 September 2016}}</ref> He scored another goal in his first [[De Klassieker|''Klassieker'']] against Ajax's great rival [[Feyenoord]] on 23 October 2016, securing a 1–1 draw away in Rotterdam.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/10/23/netherlands/eredivisie/feyenoord-rotterdam-nv/afc-ajax/2240371/|title=Feyenoord vs. Ajax |website=soccerway.com|publisher=Soccerway|date=23 October 2016}}</ref> On 20 November 2016, he scored a first-half [[hat-trick]] in an [[Eredivisie]] match against [[N.E.C. (football club)|NEC]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Razendsnelle hattrick Dolberg|url=http://www.telegraaf.nl/telesport/27069608/__Razendsnelle_hattrick_Dolberg__.html|access-date=20 November 2016|work=[[De Telegraaf]]|date=20 November 2016|language=nl}}</ref> This made him the youngest non-Dutch player ever to score a hattrick for Ajax.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ajax.nl/streams/wedstrijden/dolberg-verwerft-zich-plek-in-de-recordboeken-.htm|title=Dolberg verwerft zich plek in de recordboeken|publisher=AFC Ajax|language=nl|date=20 November 2016}}</ref> On 9 March 2017, Dolberg scored the equalizer in what ended in a 2–1 loss to [[F.C. Copenhagen]] in the round of 16 of the [[2016–17 UEFA Europa League]] campaign, thus ending a club record eight-month period in which Copenhagen had not conceded any goals at their home ground at [[Parken Stadium]] since 20 August 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ajaxshowtime.com/article/hoofdnieuws/103028/dolberg-ik-weet-niet-wat-ik-verkeerd-deed|title=Dolberg: 'Ik weet niet wat ik verkeerd deed'|publisher=AjaxShowtime|language=nl|date=10 March 2017}}</ref>

===Nice===
Dolberg signed for [[OGC Nice|Nice]] in [[Ligue 1]] on 29 August 2019 for a fee of 20.5 million euros.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ajax.nl/archief-2019-2020/officieel-kasper-dolberg-naar-ogc-nice/|title=OFFICIEEL: Kasper Dolberg naar OGC Nice|publisher=AFC Ajax|language=nl|date=29 August 2019}}</ref> He finished his first [[2019–20 OGC Nice season|season]] in France as the club's top scorer with 11 goals and was named the club's player of the season, as Nice finished in fifth place, securing a spot in the group stage for next season's [[2020–21 UEFA Europa League|UEFA Europa League]].<ref name=POYOGCN />

Dolberg started the [[2020–21 Ligue 1|2020–21 season]] with a brace on 29 August 2020 against [[RC Strasbourg|Strasbourg]], securing his team a 2–0 away win.<ref>{{cite news |title=Dolberg delights as Nice beat Strasbourg, Rennes edge Montpellier |url=https://www.eurosport.com/football/dolberg-delights-as-nice-beat-strasbourg-rennes-edge-montpellier_sto7855764/story.shtml |work=Eurosport |date=29 August 2020}}</ref>

====Loan to Sevilla====
Dolberg joined [[La Liga]] club [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]] on loan on 1 September 2022, initially planned to last for a season.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://sevillafc.es/en/actual/news/agreement-kasper-dolberg-loan|title=Kasper Dolberg joins on loan|publisher=Sevilla FC|date=1 September 2022|access-date=1 September 2022}}</ref> However, the loan was terminated prematurely on 2 January 2023, with Dolberg only making eight appearances for the Spanish club.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://sevillafc.es/en/actual/news/dolberg-loan-deal-terminated|title=Agreement with OGC Nice to terminate Dolberg's loan deal|publisher=Sevilla FC|date=2 January 2023|access-date=2 January 2023}}</ref>

====Loan to Hoffenheim====
On 2 January 2023, Dolberg was loaned to [[Bundesliga]] side [[TSG 1899 Hoffenheim|1899 Hoffenheim]] for the remainder of the 2022–23 season.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.tsg-hoffenheim.de/en/news/overview/2023/01/tsg-sign-striker-kasper-dolberg-on-loan/|title=TSG sign striker Kasper Dolberg on loan|publisher=TSG 1899 Hoffenheim|date=2 January 2023|access-date=2 January 2023}}</ref>

===Anderlecht===
On 7 July 2023, [[Belgian Pro League]] club [[R.S.C. Anderlecht|Anderlecht]] announced the signing of Dolberg from Nice on a four-year contract.<ref name="RSCA">{{cite news|url=https://www.rsca.be/en/Dolberg|title=Kasper Dolberg signs for RSCA|publisher=R.S.C. Anderlecht|date=7 July 2023|access-date=7 July 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
In November 2016, Dolberg received his first call-up to the senior [[Denmark national football team|Denmark squad]] for matches against [[Kazakhstan national football team|Kazakhstan]] and [[Czech Republic national football team|Czech Republic]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.b.dk/sport/hareide-tager-teenagekomet-med-paa-landsholdet |title=Hareide tager teenagekomet med på landsholdet |publisher=b.dk |language=da |date=4 November 2016 |access-date=7 November 2016}}</ref> On 11 November 2016, he made his senior international debut, coming on as a second-half substitute in a 4–1 victory for Denmark over Kazakhstan.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.dbu.dk/nyheder/2016/november/dan_kas_4-1 |title=Eriksen sprudleri dansk 4–1 sejr |publisher=dbu.dk |language=da |date=11 November 2016 |access-date=21 November 2016}}</ref> On 10 June 2017, in a 1–3 away win against [[Kazakhstan national football team|Kazakhstan]] in the [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)|qualifiers for the 2018 World Cup]], he came on as a substitute for [[Yussuf Poulsen]] after 68 minutes and scored his first international goal in the 81st minute.<ref>{{cite news|last1=W|first1=Christian|title=Very nice! Danes get past Kazakhstan in World Cup qualifier|url=https://cphpost.dk/?p=86713|access-date=10 June 2017|work=[[The Copenhagen Post]]|date=10 June 2017}}</ref>

In June 2018 he was named in Denmark's [[Denmark national football team|squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Russia]].<ref>{{cite web| url = http://www.goal.com/en-gb/amp/news/revealed-every-world-cup-2018-squad-23-man-preliminary-lists/oa0atsduflsv1nsf6oqk576rb| title = Revealed: Every World Cup 2018 squad - Final 23-man lists {{!}} Goal.com}} </ref>

In May 2021, he was selected in the final 26-man squad for the postponed [[UEFA Euro 2020]] tournament.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://dbu.dk/nyheder/2021/maj/26-spillere-klar-til-em-for-danmark/ |title=26 spillere klar til EM for Danmark |trans-title=26 players ready for the European Championship for Denmark |work=[[Danish Football Association]] |date=25 May 2021 |access-date=25 May 2021 |language=da}}</ref> Dolberg made his first start in the tournament, replacing the injured Yussuf Poulsen, in Denmark's [[UEFA Euro 2020 knockout phase#Round of 16|round of 16]] tie against [[Wales national football team|Wales]] on 26 June and scored twice in a 4–0 win.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro-2020/match/2024478/|title=Wales 0-4 Denmark|website=UEFA|date=26 June 2021}}</ref> He scored his third goal of the tournament in the quarter-finals against [[Czech Republic national football team|Czech Republic]] after a spectacular cross from [[Joakim Mæhle]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Doyle |first1=Paul |title=Dolberg and Delaney send Denmark past Czechs and into semi-finals |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2021/jul/03/czech-republic-denmark-euro-2020-match-report |work=the Guardian |date=3 July 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Maehle cross, assist in Denmark - Czech Republic - Video |url=https://soccer.nbcsports.com/2021/07/03/maehle-cross-video-assist/ |work=ProSoccerTalk {{!}} NBC Sports |date=3 July 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
On 16 September 2019, Dolberg reported to the police that his luxury watch at an estimated value of €70,000 was stolen. Following a police investigation, in which the police recovered a surveillance video showing the culprit stealing the watch, his teammate [[Lamine Diaby-Fadiga]] confessed to the crime ten days later.<ref name="Eurosport">{{cite web|url=https://www.eurosport.fr/football/ligue-1/2018-2019/diaby-fadiga-reconnait-avoir-vole-la-montre-de-dolberg-dans-le-vestiaire_sto7475571/story-amp.shtml|title=Diaby Gadiga reconnait avoir volé-la montre de Dolberg|date=26 September 2019|publisher=Eurosport|access-date=27 September 2019}}</ref> Diaby was later sacked by the club.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49897317|title=Lamine Diaby-Fadiga sacked by Nice after stealing team-mate's watch|work=BBC Sport |date=1 October 2019}}</ref>

On 8 November 2021, Dolberg announced that he had been diagnosed with [[Type 1 diabetes]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.berlingske.dk/sport/kasper-dolberg-har-faaet-konstateret-sukkersyge|title=Kasper Dolberg har fået konstateret sukkersyge|publisher=b.dk |language=da |date=8 November 2021 |access-date=8 November 2021}}</ref>
