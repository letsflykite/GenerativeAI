'''Benjamin William White''' (born 8 October 1997) is an English professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]] for {{English football updater|Arsenal}} club [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] and the [[England national football team|England national team]]. Mainly a [[centre-back]], he can also play as a [[right-back]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/ben-white-graham-potter-gareth-southgate-brighton-premier-league-b1853501.html |title=Ben White: An in-demand defender who paddleboards – all you need to know about England call-up |first=Ed |last=Elliot |newspaper=The Independent |location=London |date=25 May 2021 |access-date=4 June 2021}}</ref>

White began his senior club career at age 18 in 2016 with [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]]. He spent time with [[Newport County A.F.C.|Newport County]], [[Peterborough United F.C.|Peterborough United]], and [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]] on successive [[Loan (association football)|loan]]s between 2016 and 2019. He subsequently played in the [[English football league system|four professional English leagues]], and won the [[2019–20 EFL Championship|Championship]] with Leeds. After a season with Brighton, White was the subject of a club [[List of most expensive association football transfers|record association football transfer]] when he signed for Arsenal in 2021 in a [[Transfer (association football)|transfer]] worth up to £50 million, becoming their most expensive defender.

White made his senior international debut for England in 2021 at the age of 23, and was selected as part of their squads for [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

====2017–18 season: Loan to Newport County====
On 1 August 2017, White signed for [[EFL League Two|League Two]] club [[Newport County A.F.C.|Newport County]] on [[Loan (association football)|loan]] until the end of the 2017–18 season in what was described by manager [[Michael Flynn (footballer)|Michael Flynn]] as a "big coup for the club."<ref>{{Cite news |last=David Williams |date=1 August 2017 |title=Ben White loan signing a 'big coup' for Newport County AFC – boss Michael Flynn |url=http://www.southwalesargus.co.uk/sport/football/newportcounty/15446301.White_loan_signing_a__big_coup__for_County___boss_Flynn/ |access-date=1 August 2017 |newspaper=South Wales Argus}}</ref> White made his debut for Newport on 8 August 2017 in a 2–0 victory over [[Southend United F.C.|Southend United]] in the first round of the EFL Cup.<ref>{{Cite web |date=8 August 2017 |title=Southend United 0–2 Newport County |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40785914 |access-date=8 August 2017 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> He made his debut on 12 August 2017 in a 1–1 draw versus [[Crewe Alexandra F.C.|Crewe Alexandra]]. White scored his first career goal on 21 November for Newport against Barnet in a 2–1 home defeat.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41978338 |date=21 November 2017 |title=Newport County 1–2 Barnet|access-date=10 December 2020 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

On 7 January 2018, White started for Newport in a 2–1 home win over [[EFL Championship|Championship]] club Leeds United in the [[FA Cup]] third round, which meant Newport progressed to the FA Cup fourth round for the first time since the [[1978–79 FA Cup]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=7 January 2018 |title=Newport County 2–1 Leeds United |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/42526173 |access-date=7 January 2018 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> In the following round, Newport were drawn at home to Premier League team [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]]. On 27 January 2018, White started the match as Newport drew 1–1 to force a replay at Tottenham's temporary home ground [[Wembley Stadium]], with White praised for his performance against [[Harry Kane]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=15 May 2018 |title=Newport County 1–1 Tottenham Hotspur |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/42756217 |access-date=15 May 2018 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref><ref name="auto4"/>

On 15 March 2018, White won the ''[[South Wales Argus]]'' Player of the Year Award for Newport for the 2017–18 season. At the awards ceremony, manager Michael Flynn described White as "the best loan signing the club has ever made."<ref name=":1">{{Cite web |date=15 March 2018 |title=Newport County boss heaps praise on Brighton & Hove Albion loan star Ben White |url=https://www.southwalesargus.co.uk/sport/16090619.newport-county-boss-heaps-praise-on-brighton-hove-albion-loan-star-ben-white/ |access-date=1 July 2019 |website=South Wales Argus}}</ref>

This was their first league victory against City since 1989.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57066733 |title=Brighton 3–2 Man City |website=BBC Sport |date=18 May 2021|access-date=18 May 2021}}</ref> After a strong season breaking into the first team White picked up the Player Of The Season award on 6 June, on the same day as his first England start.<ref>{{cite web|date=30 July 2021|title=Welcome, Ben! {{!}} White signs on long-term contract|url=https://www.arsenal.com/news/welcome-ben-white-signs-long-term-contract|url-status=live|access-date=|publisher=Arsenal Media}}</ref>

===Arsenal===
On 30 July 2021, White signed for Premier League club [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] for £50 million.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ben White: Arsenal sign England defender from Brighton in £50m deal |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57898497 |publisher=BBC Sport |access-date=30 July 2021 |date=30 July 2021}}</ref> He made his debut on 13 August, in the opening game of the [[2021–22 Premier League|2021–22 Premier League season]], starting and playing the whole of the 2–0 defeat at Premier League newcomers [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]].<ref>{{cite web|first= Phil |last=McNulty| title=Brentford 2–0 Arsenal: Bees earn fully deserved win over Gunners |url= https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58109868 |website=BBC Sport |access-date=13 August 2021 |date=13 August 2021}}</ref> White missed Arsenal's next match on 22 August, a home fixture against London rivals Chelsea, due to a positive [[COVID-19]] test.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://theathletic.com/news/ben-white-unwell-and-unavailable-for-arsenals-game-against-chelsea/0Ma9hyFTAsY8|title=Arsenal team news: White to miss Chelsea game due to positive coronavirus test|website=The Athletic|date=22 August 2021|access-date=23 August 2021}}</ref> He eventually made his home debut at the [[Emirates Stadium]] after returning on 11 September, helping keep a clean sheet in a 1–0 victory over [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]] for the club's first league win of [[2021–22 Arsenal F.C. season|the season]].<ref>{{cite web|first= Phil |last=McNulty| url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58436972| title=Arsenal 1–0 Norwich City: Aubameyang scores winner as Arsenal beat Norwich |website=BBC Sport|date=11 September 2021|access-date=11 September 2021}}</ref>

White played in his first [[North London derby]] against [[Tottenham Hotspurs F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] on 26 September, he played the whole match where Arsenal took all three points in the 3–1 home victory.<ref>{{cite web|first= Phil |last=McNulty| url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58613174| title=Arsenal 3–1 Tottenham: Gunners defeat north London rivals at Emirates Stadium |website=BBC Sport|date=26 September 2021|access-date=26 September 2021}}</ref> On 2 October, White made his first return to [[Falmer Stadium]] since leaving Brighton where he started and played the full match for Arsenal in the 0–0 draw with his former club.<ref>{{cite web| first = Matthew |last=Howarth | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58667889| title=Brighton & Hove Albion 0–0 Arsenal: Wasteful Seagulls held by Gunners|website=BBC Sport|date=2 October 2021|access-date=2 October 2021}}</ref> 

During the 2022–23 season, White saw his position being moved to right-back, changing from his usual position at centre-back from the previous season. On 4 March 2023, he scored his first goal for the club in a 3–2 home win over [[AFC Bournemouth|Bournemouth]], scoring a volley in the 70th minute.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Nelson's 97th-minute stunner gives Arsenal victory |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport| url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/64768041 |first=Emma |last=Smith |date=4 March 2023 |access-date=2023-03-04}}</ref>

==International career==
On 25 May 2021, White was named in England's [[UEFA Euro 2020 squads|provisional 33-man squad]] for [[UEFA Euro 2020]], with the tournament taking place in the summer of 2021 due to the previous year's postponement as a result of [[Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on sports|the COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.uefa.com/insideuefa/news/newsid=2641077.html |title=Resolution of the European football family on a coordinated response to the impact of the COVID-19 on competitions |publisher=UEFA |date=17 March 2020 |access-date=17 March 2020}}</ref> This was White's first ever call-up to the national team at any level.<ref>{{Cite news |title=England name provisional Euro 2020 squad: Ben White, Ben Godfrey, Aaron Ramsdale and Sam Johnstone in |website=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57239842|access-date=25 May 2021}}</ref> He was one of seven players cut from the squad on 1 June,<ref>{{Cite news |title=Euro 2020: Trent Alexander-Arnold named in England's 26-man squad |website=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57321188|access-date=1 June 2021}}</ref> although the players who were cut remained in the squad for the two warm up games against [[Austria national football team|Austria]] and [[Romania national football team|Romania]]. White made his England debut coming on as a substitute, replacing [[Jack Grealish]] in the 1–0 victory over Austria at the [[Riverside Stadium]] on 2 June. He cleared a shot from [[Alessandro Schöpf]] off the line, preventing a late equaliser.<ref>{{Cite news |title=England 1–0 Austria |website=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57250378|access-date=2 June 2021}}</ref> He became the fifth Brighton player to play for England, and the second in just under three years after Albion team mate and captain Lewis Dunk was capped in November 2018.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.brightonandhoveindependent.co.uk/sport/football/lewis-dunk-has-certainly-seized-moment-get-england-call-ian-hine-563893 |title=Lewis Dunk has certainly seized the moment to get England call-up – Ian Hine |website=Brighton & Hove Independent |date=12 October 2018 |access-date=8 June 2020}}</ref> White made his first start in the second and final warm up match on 6 June, playing the whole match in the 1–0 victory over Romania, again played at the Riverside Stadium.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57297066|title=England 1-0 Romania |website=BBC Sport |date=6 June 2021 |access-date=6 June 2021}}</ref> 

On 7 June 2021, White was named in the 26-man squad for Euro 2020 as a replacement for the injured [[Trent Alexander-Arnold]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.englandfootball.com/articles/2021/Jun/07/20210607-england-final-squad-member-confirmed|title=White completes EURO squad |website=EnglandFootball |date=7 June 2021 |access-date=7 June 2021}}</ref> White remained unused in the tournament where England finished as runners-up in the [[UEFA Euro 2020 Final|Championships]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/live/football/50941458|title=Italy v England Euro 2020 final: Watch live, score, updates & reaction - Live - BBC Sport |website=BBC Sport |date=11 July 2021 |access-date=11 July 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2021/jul/09/going-spare-the-five-players-giving-gareth-southgate-selection-headache|title=Going spare: the five England players yet to play but no less important|website=The Guardian |date=9 July 2021 |access-date=11 July 2021}}</ref>

On 10 November 2022, White was named in England's 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63585869|title=World Cup 2022: England squad features Maddison, Rashford and Wilson|website=The Guardian |date=10 November 2022 |access-date=12 November 2022}}</ref> He left the tournament for personal reasons on 30 November, and was not expected to return.<ref>{{Cite web |title=FA statement regarding Ben White |url=https://www.arsenal.com/news/fa-statement-regarding-ben-white |access-date=2022-11-30 |website=FA statement regarding Ben White |language=en}}</ref>

==Style of play==
White is known as a ball playing [[centre-back]] with good technical ability and was also praised by former manager Michael Flynn for his tough tackling style, White can also play as a [[defensive midfielder]] or as a [[right-back]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=Why Brighton and Hove Albion defender Ben White is earning rave reviews at Newport County |url=https://www.theargus.co.uk/sport/15563705.why-brighton-and-hove-albion-defender-ben-white-is-earning-rave-reviews-at-newport-county/ |access-date=30 June 2019 |newspaper=The Argus}}</ref> White describes his own style as a "cool" and "calm" player.<ref>{{Cite web |title="I'M ABSOLUTELY BUZZING TO BE HERE" BEN WHITE |url=https://www.leedsunited.com/video/112671/i-m-absolutely-buzzing-to-be-here-ben-white |access-date=1 July 2019 |publisher=Leeds United F.C.}}</ref> White is also known for his surging forward runs with the ball helping create quick counterattacks from deep.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Why Leeds United signed centre-back Ben White – and what to expect from him |url=https://www.leeds-live.co.uk/sport/leeds-united/leeds-united-ben-white-defender-16517162 |access-date=27 July 2019 |website=Leeds Live}}</ref> Arsenal manager [[Mikel Arteta]] described White as "brave", "really talented" and a "fighter", adding: "He trains like he's playing the Champions League final. That's why he's come a long way in such a short time in his career."<ref>{{Cite web |last=O'Brien |first=Josh |date=16 September 2022 |title=Ben White's training ground conduct analysed as Arsenal star dropped by England |url=https://www.irishmirror.ie/sport/soccer/white-arsenal-england-squad-dropped-28007768 |access-date=11 October 2022 |website=Irish Mirror |language=en}}</ref>
