'''Rafael Alexandre da Conceição Leão''' ({{IPA-pt|ʁɐfɐˈɛl ˈljɐ̃w}}; born 10 June 1999) is a Portuguese professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[forward (association football)|forward]] for [[Serie A]] club [[A.C. Milan|AC Milan]] and the [[Portugal national football team|Portugal national team]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Guest |first=Dylan |date=24 May 2022 |title=Rafael Leao Awarded Serie A Best Player |url=https://lastwordonsports.com/football/2022/05/24/rafael-leao-serie-a-best-player/ |access-date=19 April 2023 |website=Last Word on Football}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |date=27 November 2022 |title=Why every club wants to sign Portugal and Milan forward Rafael Leao |url=https://www.espn.co.uk/football/fifa-world-cup/story/4813627/why-every-top-club-wants-portugal-and-milans-rafael-leao |access-date=19 April 2023 |website=ESPN.}}</ref>

Graduating from [[Sporting CP]] youth system, Leão made his first-team debut in 2018, winning the [[2017–18 Taça da Liga]], before unilaterally terminating his contract, following an incident within the club. Shortly after, he joined for [[Ligue 1]] side [[Lille OSC|Lille]] on a free transfer. AC Milan signed him in 2019 for a reported fee of €35 million. In his third season at the club, he helped the team win the [[2021–22 Serie A]], which ended the club's 11-year league title drought. He was also voted [[Serie A Awards|Serie A Most Valuable Player]] and was listed among the 30-man shortlist for the [[Ballon d'Or]].

Leão is a former [[Portugal national youth football team|Portugal youth international]], representing his country at various youth levels, being part of [[Portugal national under-17 football team|the under-17 team]] that won the [[UEFA European Under-17 Championship|2016 European Championship]]. He made his senior international debut in 2021.

Leão made his competitive debut on 30 September 2018, replacing [[Loïc Rémy]] in the 78th minute, in a 3–0 home win over [[Olympique de Marseille|Marseille]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.eurosport.com/football/ligue-1/2018-2019/live-lille-osc-olympique-de-marseille_mtc1029531/live.shtml|title=Lille against Marseille|publisher=[[EuroSport]]|language=en|date=30 September 2018|access-date=30 September 2018}}</ref> He scored his first [[Ligue 1]] goal in only his second appearance, playing 67 minutes and helping the hosts defeat [[Stade Malherbe Caen|Caen]] in a 1–0 victory.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Article/Leao-rugit-lille-deroule/953802|title=Leao rugit, Lille déroule|trans-title=Leao roars (pun on player's name, "Lion" in Portuguese), Lille unwind|newspaper=[[L'Équipe]]|first=Romain|last=Lafont|language=fr|date=28 October 2018|access-date=28 October 2018}}</ref> On 1 February 2019, he scored his sixth goal of the season and made an assist to [[Nicolas Pépé]], his first for the club, in a 4–0 home win over [[OGC Nice|Nice]].<ref>{{Cite web |language=fr |url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/match-direct/ligue-1/2018-2019/lille-nice-live/428366 |title=Résultat et résumé Lille - Nice, Ligue 1, 23e journée |date=1 February 2019 |website=[[L'Équipe]] |access-date=29 September 2022}}</ref>

During his spell, Leão scored a total of eight goals in 26 competitive games, being mainly used as a striker by Lille coach [[Christophe Galtier]], and shared teams with compatriots [[José Fonte]], [[Rui Fonte]], [[Edgar Ié]] and [[Xeka]], as they finished as runners-up in a brilliant [[2018–19 Ligue 1]] season.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.losc.fr/actualites-foot-lille/un-renfort-offensif-nomm%C3%A9-rui-fonte|title=Un renfort offensif nommé Rui Fonte|trans-title=An attacking addition called Rui Fonte|publisher=Lille OSC|language=fr|date=1 September 2018|access-date=24 July 2020}}</ref>

===AC Milan===
====2019–21: Adaptation to Italy====

Milan|date=25 August 2019|access-date=24 February 2020}}</ref> Leão scored his first goal of the club, as a late consolation in a 1–3 home loss to [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]] on 29 September.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49873763|title=AC Milan 1–3 Fiorentina|publisher=BBC Sport|date=29 September 2019|access-date=24 February 2020}}</ref> On 19 March 2020, the [[Court of Arbitration for Sport]] (CAS) condemned Leão to pay €16.5 million to Sporting CP for his unilateral breach of contract.<ref>{{Cite news|date=19 March 2020|title=Rafael Leao (AC Milan) ordered by the CAS to pay 16.5 million euros to Sporting|language=fr|work=[[L'Équipe]]|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Rafael-leao-ac-milan-condamne-par-le-tas-a-verser-16-5-millions-d-euros-au-sporting/1120945|access-date=25 July 2020}}</ref> On 7 July 2020, Leão scored a goal against league-leaders [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]], to help Milan to a 4–2 win, after being down by two goals. At the end of the season, he had scored six goals in 31 league matches, helping Milan to a sixth-place finish and a 10-match unbeaten streak.<ref>{{Cite news |date=22 July 2020 |title=Stefano Pioli: AC Milan boss signs new two-year contract |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/53498250|access-date=25 July 2020}}</ref>

At the beginning of the [[2020–21 A.C. Milan season|following season]], on 4 October 2020, Leão scored a brace in a 3–0 home victory at [[San Siro]] against [[Spezia Calcio|Spezia]].<ref>{{Cite web|url =https://www.eurosport.it/calcio/serie-a/2020-2021/milan-spezia-3-0-hernandez-in-mezzo-alla-doppietta-di-leao_sto7924700/story.shtml|trans-title=Milan-Spezia 3-0: Hernandez in mezzo alla doppietta di Leão| title = Milan-Spezia 3-0: Hernandez between Leão's brace|publisher = [[Eurosport]]|date =4 October 2020|access-date = 5 October 2020|language=Italian}}</ref> On 29 October, he scored his first goal in European competitions, in a 3–0 home group stage victory in the [[UEFA Europa League]] against [[AC Sparta Prague|Sparta Prague]].<ref>{{Cite web|first=Marco|last=Fallisi|url = https://www.gazzetta.it/Calcio/Europa-League/29-10-2020/milan-sparta-praga-3-0-diaz-leao-dalot-gol-ibra-mista-penalty-390411569625.shtml|title=Questo Milan non sbaglia un colpo: tris allo Sparta Praga ed è già fuga|trans-title = This Milan doesn't miss a beat: three of a kind at Sparta Prague and it's already an escape|work=La Gazzetta dello Sport|date = 29 October 2020| access-date = 29 October 2020|language=Italian}}</ref> He scored the fastest goal in the history of Serie A and in [[Big Five (association football)|Europe's top-five leagues]], after six seconds on 20 December at [[U.S. Sassuolo Calcio|Sassuolo]]; the match ended 2–1 for his team.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/163779/leao-scores-fastest-goal-serie-history|title=Leao scores fastest goal in Serie A history|publisher=Football Italia|date=20 December 2020|access-date=20 December 2020}}</ref>

On 3 January 2021, he scored the second goal against Benevento in eventual 2–0 away win, becoming the second youngest foreign player (21 years and 207 days) to score more than ten goals for Milan in Serie A, after [[Alexandre Pato]] (19 years and 19 days).<ref>{{cite news |last1=Greaves |first1=Russell |title=Benevento 0-2 Milan: Pioli's men top again despite Tonali red |url=https://www.goal.com/en/match/benevento-v-milan/bp1vsji95jdeokz5h1uozeexg}}</ref>

==Player profile==
===Style of play===
Leão is regarded as a skillful and technical player, capable of playing on either flank, due to his versatility. Tall and lean, Leão has a burst and speed to match, making him a dynamic forward.<ref name="Styleofplay">{{cite web|title=Rafael Leão|url=https://www.scoutedftbl.com/best-young-football-players/portugal/|date=24 May 2022|access-date=24 May 2022}}</ref> Having developed as a [[Forward (association football)#Centre-forward|centre-forward]] at youth level, he offers a dynamic presence in the forward line. He stretches and disrupts defences with his movements, with and without the ball.<ref name="Styleofplay"/> Leão is a prolific and proficient dribbler; he drives from deep and then overtakes players in one-on-one situations around the box with maneuvers and changes to his speed.<ref name="Styleofplay"/> He is also capable of creating chances and providing assists for teammates from the left due to his vision.<ref name="Styleofplay"/> Inside the [[penalty area]], he uses dribbles and movement to get into good positions.<ref name="Styleofplay"/> Leão has also been played as a [[Forward (association football)#Striker|striker]] because of his composure and eye for goal.<ref name="Styleofplay"/> During his managerial spell at Milan, [[Stefano Pioli]] deployed Leão as a [[Forward (association football)#Second striker|supporting striker]] or a [[Forward (association football)#Winger|winger]] on either side of the pitch, rather than an out-and-out [[Forward (association football)#Striker|striker]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.milannews.it/news/pioli-su-leao-ha-un-potenziale-incredibile-oggi-e-entrato-bene-ma-puo-fare-di-piu-378807|title=Pioli su Leao: "Ha un potenziale incredibile. Oggi è entrato bene ma può fare di più"|trans-title=Pioli on Leao: "He has an incredible potential. He came on well today but he can do better"|publisher=Milan News|first=Manuel|last=Del Vecchio|language=it|date=25 July 2020|access-date=31 July 2020}}</ref>
