'''Ilias Emilian Chair''' ({{lang-ar|إلياس إميليان شاعر}}; born 30 October 1997) is a Moroccan professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as an [[attacking midfielder]] for {{English football updater|QueensPR}} club [[Queens Park Rangers F.C.|Queens Park Rangers]]. Born in Belgium, he plays for the [[Morocco national football team|Morocco national team]].

==Early life==
Ilias Chair was born in [[Antwerp]] in Belgium to a Moroccan father and Polish mother.<ref name="Ilias Chair set for Morocco Under-20 training camp">{{Cite web |url=https://www.qpr.co.uk/news/under-23s-news/chair-set-for-morocco-youth-camp/ |title=Ilias Chair set for Morocco Under-20 training camp |date=24 May 2017 |publisher=Queens Park Rangers F.C. |access-date=20 April 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.soccerbase.com/players/player.sd?player_id=106669 |title=Ilias Chair - Queen's Park Rangers - Soccer Base |website=www.soccerbase.com |access-date=27 March 2020}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Early career===
Chair began his career in the youth system at [[Lierse S.K.|Lierse]].<ref name="Under-23s add Belgian attacker to ranks">{{Cite web |url=https://www.qpr.co.uk/news/under-23s-news/under-23s-add-belgian-attacker-to-ranks/ |title=Under-23s add Belgian attacker to ranks |date=31 January 2017 |publisher=Queens Park Rangers F.C. |access-date=20 April 2019}}</ref> He also spent time at the academy of [[Club Brugge KV|Club Brugge]], as well as the [[JMG Academy|JMG Academy Belgium]].<ref name="JMG Football – Ilias Chair">{{Cite web |url=https://www.jmgfootball.com/en/joueurs/chair-ilias/ |title=JMG Football – Ilias Chair |publisher=JMG Academy |access-date=20 April 2019}}</ref> Chair made his professional debut for Lierse at the age of 17, playing in the [[Belgian Second Division]], when he came as a 76th-minute substitute in Lierse's 1–1 away draw at [[K.V.V. Coxyde|Coxyde]] on 9 August 2015.<ref name="Soccerway – Ilias Chair">{{soccerway|413607|accessdate = 20 April 2019}}</ref><ref name="Coxyde 1–1 Lierse">{{Cite web |url=https://uk.soccerway.com/matches/2015/08/09/belgium/second-division/kvv-coxyde/koninklijke-lierse-sportkring/2062073/ |title=Coxyde 1–1 Lierse |date=9 August 2015 |publisher=Soccerway |access-date=20 April 2019}}</ref> He subsequently started his first match a month later, on 9 September 2015, playing the full 90 minutes in a 3–2 home defeat to [[Cercle Brugge K.S.V.|Cercle Brugge]].<ref name="Soccerway – Ilias Chair" /><ref name="Lierse 2–3 Cercle Brugge">{{Cite web |url=https://uk.soccerway.com/matches/2015/09/09/belgium/second-division/koninklijke-lierse-sportkring/cercle-brugge-ksv/2062080/ |title=Lierse 2–3 Cercle Brugge |date=9 August 2015 |publisher=Soccerway |access-date=20 April 2019}}</ref>

===Queens Park Rangers===
Chair went on trial at [[EFL Championship|Championship]] club [[Queens Park Rangers F.C.|QPR]] in January 2017.<ref name="Under-23s add Belgian attacker to ranks" /> During the trial period, he scored in a 3–1 friendly win for the U23s against [[A.F.C. Bournemouth|Bournemouth]].<ref name="Under-23s add Belgian attacker to ranks" /> He subsequently signed for QPR on a permanent basis on 31 January 2017.<ref name="Under-23s add Belgian attacker to ranks" /> Chair was added to the club's Elite Development Squad and spent the remainder of the [[2016–17 in English football|2016–17 season]] playing for the club's U23 team.<ref name="Under-23s add Belgian attacker to ranks" /> 

Having impressed QPR manager [[Ian Holloway]] in training,<ref name="QPR Profile – Ilias Chair">{{Cite web |url=https://www.qpr.co.uk/squads/first-team/ilias-chair/ |title=QPR Profile – Ilias Chair |work=BBC Sport |access-date=20 April 2019}}</ref> He replaced [[Luke Freeman]] in the 63rd minute of the match to make his first-team debut.<ref name="Soccerway – Ilias Chair" /><ref name="QPR 1–0 Northampton Town" /> Chair made his first starting appearance for QPR in a 1–0 defeat against [[Preston North End F.C.|Preston North End]] at [[Deepdale]] on 2 December 2017.<ref name="Preston North End 1–0 QPR">{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/42119757 |title=Preston North End 1–0 QPR |date=2 December 2017 |work=BBC Sport |access-date=20 April 2019}}</ref> He signed a two-year contract extension with the club on 9 February 2018, keeping him at the club until the summer of 2020.<ref name="Ilias Chair: QPR midfielder signs new contract until 2020">{{Cite news Upon his return to QPR, he signed a new three-year contract with the club in September 2019.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49755243 |title=Ilias Chair: QPR midfielder signs new three-year contract |date=19 September 2019 |publisher=BBC Sport |access-date=6 December 2019}}</ref> Under new manager [[Mark Warburton]], Chair became a key player for QPR at the start of the 2019–20 season.

On 29 January 2021, Chair signed a new four-and-a-half year deal that would see him remain at the club until 2025, with the club having the option to extend this contract by another year.<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.qpr.co.uk/news/club-news/ilias-chair-extends-qpr-stay-until-2025-290121/|title=Ilias Chair extends QPR stay until 2025|website=www.qpr.co.uk|date=29 January 2021}}</ref>

Chair started the [[2021–22 Queens Park Rangers F.C. season|2021–22 season]] in good form and won the Championship Goal of the Month award for October 2021 after an impressive strike against [[Blackburn Rovers F.C.|Blackburn Rovers]].<ref name="Oct21 GOTM"> {{cite web|url=https://www.qpr.co.uk/news/club-news/chair-scoops-goal-of-the-month-accolade-261121/|title=Chair scoops Goal of the Month accolade|website=www.qpr.co.uk|date=26 November 2021|accessdate=26 November 2021}}</ref>

==International career==
Chair was born in Belgium and is of Moroccan descent.<ref name="Ilias Chair set for Morocco Under-20 training camp" /> He was called up to the [[Morocco national under-20 football team|Morocco U20]] squad for a week-long training camp in [[Rabat]] in June 2017.<ref name="Ilias Chair set for Morocco Under-20 training camp" /> Chair represented the [[Morocco national under-23 football team|Morocco U23]]s in a 1–0 friendly defeat to the [[Senegal national under-23 football team|Senegal U23]]s on 23 March 2018.<ref name="Chair features in Morocco U23 defeat">{{Cite web |url=https://www.qpr.co.uk/news/club-news/chair-features-in-morocco-u23-defeat/ |title=Chair features in Morocco U23 defeat |date=23 March 2018 |access-date=20 April 2019 |publisher=Queens Park Rangers F.C.}}</ref> 

He debuted with the senior [[Morocco national football team|Morocco national team]] in a friendly 1–0 win over [[Ghana national football team|Ghana]] on 9 June 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://globalsportsarchive.com/match/soccer/2021-06-08/morocco-vs-ghana/2375898/|title=Match Report of Morocco vs Ghana - 2021-06-08 - FIFA Friendlies - Global Sports Archive|website=globalsportsarchive.com}}</ref> On 6 October 2021, in his fourth appearance for his country, Chair scored his first Morocco goal with the third in a 5–0 win over [[Guinea-Bissau national football team|Guinea-Bissau]].<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/tournaments/mens/worldcup/qatar2022/qualifiers/caf/match-center/400204267|title=Morocco 5–0 Guinea-Bissau|website=www.fifa.com|date=6 October 2021|accessdate=26 November 2021}}</ref>

On 10 November 2022, he was named in Morocco's 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Morocco World Cup 2022 squad: Who's in and who's out? {{!}} Goal.com |url=https://www.goal.com/en-bh/lists/morocco-2022-world-cup-squad-hakimi-ziyech-mazraoui-qatar/blta36257fc4feeb66a |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=www.goal.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=10 November 2022 |title=Moroccan coach unveils list of 26 Atlas Lions in 2022 World Cup |url=https://en.hespress.com/52697-moroccan-coach-unveils-list-of-26-atlas-lions-in-2022-world-cup.html |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=HESPRESS English - Morocco News |language=en-US}}</ref>

On December 17th 2022, Chair made his [[FIFA World Cup]] debut in the 3rd place playoff against [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]] in an eventual 2–1 loss to Croatia.
