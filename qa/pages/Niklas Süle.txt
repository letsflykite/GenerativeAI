'''Niklas Süle''' (born 3 September 1995) is a German professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] for {{German football updater|BoDortmu}} club [[Borussia Dortmund]] and the [[Germany national football team|Germany national team]].

== Club career ==
=== Early career ===
Süle started his career with Rot-Weiß Walldorf. In July 2006, he signed for the youth team of [[Eintracht Frankfurt]], where he played until the end of the 2008–09 season. In July 2009, he signed for the youth team of [[SV Darmstadt 98]], and only half a year later he left for the youth team at [[TSG 1899 Hoffenheim|1899 Hoffenheim]].{{citation needed|date=June 2020}}

=== 1899 Hoffenheim ===
[[file:Niklas Süle.jpg|thumb|left|Süle with Hoffenheim in 2014]]
On 11 May 2013, Süle made his debut for Hoffenheim in a [[Bundesliga]] game against [[Hamburger SV]]. He started the match but was substituted for [[Andreas Ludwig]] in the 81st minute. Hoffenheim lost the game 4–1. During the 2012–13 season, Süle made two Bundesliga appearances and two [[Professional sports league organization#Structure of European leagues (Promotion and relegation system)|relegation playoff]] appearances as Hoffenheim defeated [[1. FC Kaiserslautern]] to stay in the Bundesliga.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/0000255732.jsp | title = Kaiserslautern Vs Hoffenheim LIVE! | publisher = bundesliga.com | access-date = 24 August 2018}}</ref>

During the 2013–14 season Süle established himself as an important first team player for Hoffenheim. Süle played in 25 Bundesliga matches and scored four goals as he helped Hoffenheim finish 9th in the league.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Detailed N. Süle player statistics|url=https://rowdie.co.uk/players/31775/niklas-sule|access-date=6 January 2021|website=rowdie.co.uk}}</ref>

Süle started the 2014–15 season strong and played every minute during the first 14 Bundesliga matches. On 12 December 2014, Süle suffered a torn [[anterior cruciate ligament]] during matchday 15 against Frankfurt. Süle was sidelined for the rest of the season due to the injury.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.achtzehn99.de/newsarchive/news-archive-2014/dezember-2014/niklas-suele-suffers-acl-tear-in-left-knee/?lang=new | title = NIKLAS SÜLE SUFFERS ACL TEAR IN LEFT KNEE | publisher = achtzehn99.de | access-date = 24 August 2018}}</ref> Hoffenheim finished 8th in the league.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Bolland|first=Bevan|title=Hoffenheim: The Fairytale Football Club?|url=https://bleacherreport.com/articles/49413-hoffenheim-the-fairytale-football-club|access-date=11 August 2021|website=Bleacher Report|language=en}}</ref>

Süle returned for the 2015–16 season after the ACL tear. He played every minute in 33 Bundesliga matches as Hoffenheim finished a disappointing 15th in the league.{{cn|date=June 2022}}

Süle had a very impressive 2016–17 season for Hoffenheim as he played in 33 Bundesliga matches. Hoffenheim finished in an impressive 4th in the Bundesliga. Süle's performance drew interest from clubs like Bayern Munich and [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]].<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/transfers/chelsea-transfer-news-bayern-munich-niklas-sule-not-stimulating-offer-a7777571.html | title = Bayern Munich new boy Niklas Sule says he rejected Chelsea as offer was not stimulating enough | date = 7 June 2017 | publisher = independent.co.uk | access-date = 24 August 2018}}</ref>

On 15 January 2017, Bayern Munich had announced that they had signed Süle along with [[Sebastian Rudy]] from Hoffenheim as a double swoop. Both Süle and Rudy joined Bayern Munich on 1 July 2017.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2017/01/press-release-fcb-sign-suele-and-rudy | title = FC Bayern sign Niklas Süle and Sebastian Rudy | website = FC Bayern Munich | date = 15 January 2017 | access-date = 14 August 2017}}</ref>

=== Bayern Munich ===
[[file:Niklas Suele Training 2018-05-08 FC Bayern Muenchen-1.jpg|thumb|upright|Süle training with Bayern in 2018]]
Süle's first appearance came as he was named a starter for Bayern Munich's season opener against [[Bayer 04 Leverkusen]] on 18 August 2017. He scored the first goal of the [[2017–18 Bundesliga]] season, heading in a free kick from fellow Bayern newcomer and Hoffenheim-product Sebastian Rudy.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/dream-debuts-for-bayern-munich-trio-sule-tolisso-rudy-457190.jsp|title=Dream debuts for Bayern Munich trio Süle, Rudy and Tolisso|publisher=Bundesliga| date = 20 August 2017 | access-date=19 May 2018}}</ref> On 12 September 2017, Süle made his [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] debut against [[R.S.C. Anderlecht|Anderlecht]]. Süle played in 27 Bundesliga matches and scored two goals. He made nine appearances in the Champions League during the season.<ref name=":0" />

Süle won his first Bundesliga title after Bayern Munich finished 21 points ahead of second-placed [[FC Schalke 04|Schalke 04]]. Süle also reached his first [[DFB-Pokal]] final as Bayern Munich were beaten 3–1 by Süle's former youth club, Eintracht Frankfurt.{{cn|date=June 2022}}

Süle started the [[2018–19 FC Bayern Munich season|2018–19 season]] by playing in the 2018 [[DFL-Supercup|German Super Cup]], which Bayern won 5–0.<ref name="FCB gewinnt Supercup - Lewandowski macht den Unterschied">{{cite web |title=FCB gewinnt Supercup – Lewandowski macht den Unterschied |url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/startseite/d-supercup-fb-1/2018/1/4213948/spielanalyse_eintracht-frankfurt-32_bayern-muenchen-14.html |website=kicker.de |publisher=kicker |access-date=14 August 2018 |language=de}}</ref> On 20 April 2019, Süle scored the winning goal in a 1–0 win over [[SV Werder Bremen|Werder Bremen]]. The win kept Bayern at the top of the Bundesliga table.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/bayern-munich-werder-bremen-live-line-ups-stats-lewandowski-james-rodriguez-3799 | title = Niklas Süle keeps Bayern Munich top with winning goal against Werder Bremen | publisher = bundesliga.com | access-date = 19 May 2019}}</ref>

On 18 May 2019, Süle won his second Bundesliga title as Bayern finished with 78 points, two points above Dortmund. A week later, Süle won his first DFB-Pokal as Bayern defeated [[RB Leipzig]] 3–0 in the [[2019 DFB-Pokal Final]].<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/rb-leipzig-bayern-munich-live-dfb-cup-final-james-rodriguez-lewandowski-neuer-4678 | title = Robert Lewandowski hits brace as Bayern Munich beat RB Leipzig in DFB Cup final to seal the double | publisher = bundesliga.com | access-date = 25 May 2019}}</ref> He played in 31 Bundesliga matches and scored two goals. During the Bundesliga season, Süle had a 95 percent pass completion while having the fourth most touches of any Bundesliga player.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/bayern-munich-s-title-makers-lewandowski-sule-gnabry-kimmich-martinez-4613 | title = Lewandowski, Süle, Gnabry, Kimmich and Martinez: Bayern Munich's title-makers | publisher = bundesliga.com | access-date = 19 May 2019}}</ref> Süle appeared in 42 matches in all competitions and scored two goals.<ref name=":0" />

Süle tore his [[anterior cruciate ligament injury]] in the 12th minute of the team's eighth league match of the 2019-20 season and was expected to be out of the line-up for eight to ten months, preventing him from participating with Germany for [[UEFA Euro 2020]], before the tournament was postponed.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bavarianfootballworks.com/2019/10/20/20923122/bayern-munich-niklas-sule-niko-kovac-augsburg-joachim-low-germany-euro-2020-champions-league|title=BREAKING: Bayern Munich confirm Niklas Süle has torn the anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) of his left knee|date=20 October 2019|website=Bavarian Football Works}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.kicker.de/760606/artikel|title=Kreuzbandriss bei Süle – "Herber Schlag" für FCB und Löw|trans-title=Cruciate Ligament Rupture for Süle – "Major Blow" for FCB and Löw|website=kicker}}</ref> On 8 August 2020, Süle played against Chelsea in the Champions League, marking his first match since October 2019.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://sportbild.bild.de/fussball/champions-league/champions-league/bayern-muenchen-niklas-suele-comeback-72294664.sport.html |title=Warum Süle sich sein Comeback anders vorgestellt hat |website=sportbild.bild.de |language=de |date=9 August 2020 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.tz.de/sport/fc-bayern/fc-bayern-niklas-suele-jerome-boateng-hansi-flick-fc-barcelona-champions-league-zr-90020089.html |title="Dann lasse ich mir es schmecken": Süles ungewöhnlich offenes Geständnis live am Mikro nach dem Chelsea-Spiel |website=tz.de |language=de |date=11 August 2020 }}</ref> He then started from the bench in the later games in the Champions League. On 23 August 2020, Süle came on from the bench in the [[2020 UEFA Champions League Final]] and replaced [[Jérôme Boateng]] after he suffered an early injury. He won the Champions League, completing the continental treble for that season.

On 9 December 2020, Süle scored his first Champions League goal in a 2–0 win over [[FC Lokomotiv Moscow|Lokomotiv Moscow]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/55221522 |title=Bayern Munich 2–0 Lokomotiv Moscow |website=BBC Sport |date=9 December 2020 }}</ref>

=== Borussia Dortmund ===
Süle agreed to join [[Borussia Dortmund]] starting from the 2022–23 season on a free transfer on a four-year contract.<ref>{{Cite web|title=BVB verpflichtet Niklas Süle zur Saison 2022/23|url=https://www.bvb.de/News/Uebersicht/BVB-verpflichtet-Niklas-Suele-zur-Saison-2022-23|access-date=7 February 2022|website=www.bvb.de|language=de}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/niklas-sule-to-join-borussia-dortmund-from-bayern-munich-18821 |title= Niklas Süle to join Borussia Dortmund from Bayern Munich|website=bundesliga.com|accessdate=20 May 2022}}</ref>

== International career ==
Süle participated in the [[2012 UEFA European Under-17 Championship]] with the [[Germany national under-17 football team|German U17]] team.<ref>{{Cite web|last=UEFA.com|title=Under-17|url=https://www.uefa.com/under17/|access-date=11 August 2021|website=UEFA.com|language=en}}</ref>

He was part of the squad for the [[Football at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|2016 Summer Olympics]], where Germany won the silver medal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/mensolympic/news/y=2016/m=8/news=neymar-s-golden-penalty-sees-brazil-to-victory-2823069.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160821102147/http://www.fifa.com/mensolympic/news/y=2016/m=8/news=neymar-s-golden-penalty-sees-brazil-to-victory-2823069.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=21 August 2016|title=Neymar's golden penalty sees Brazil to victory|date=20 August 2016|work= FIFA.com | access-date = 10 June 2017}}</ref>

Süle was called up in August 2016 when the [[Germany national football team]] played [[Finland national football team|Finland]] and [[Norway national football team|Norway]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Uncapped Niklas Sule in Germany squad with Bastian Schweinsteiger set to bow out |url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/12023/10552618/uncapped-niklas-sule-in-germany-squad-with-bastian-schweinsteiger-set-to-bow-out|access-date=29 August 2016|publisher=Sky Sports|date=26 August 2016}}</ref> He was brought off in the 59th minute against Finland.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dfb.de/en/national-teams/the-mannschaft/schedule/?spieledb_path=/en/matches/2232702|title=Schedule :: The "Mannschaft" :: National Teams :: DFB – Deutscher Fußball-Bund e.V.|access-date=31 August 2016}}</ref>

Süle was named in Germany's final 23-man squad by [[Joachim Löw]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dw.com/en/germany-announce-final-world-cup-squad-with-manuel-neuer-without-leroy-san%C3%A9/a-44064011|title=Germany announce final World Cup squad, with Manuel Neuer, without Leroy Sané|publisher=Deutsche Welle|access-date=4 June 2018}}</ref> On 27 June, Süle made his first [[FIFA World Cup|World Cup]] appearance in the last match of the group stage in a 2–0 defeat to [[South Korea national football team|South Korea]], as Germany got knocked out of the World Cup in the first round for the first time since [[1938 FIFA World Cup|1938]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dw.com/en/world-cup-2018-germany-exit-at-group-stage-after-shock-south-korea-loss/a-44418816|title=World Cup 2018: Germany exit at group stage after shock South Korea loss|publisher=Deutsche Welle|access-date=28 June 2018}}</ref> On 19 May 2021, he was selected for the UEFA Euro 2020 squad.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.kicker.de/em-kader-offiziell-loew-beruft-mueller-hummels-und-volland-804642/artikel|title=EM-Kader offiziell: Löw beruft Müller, Hummels und Volland|date=19 May 2021|website=kicker.de|language=de|access-date=19 May 2021}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Süle was born in [[Frankfurt]], [[Hesse]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.worldfootball.net/player_summary/niklas-suele/ |title=Niklas Süle: Profile |website=worldfootball.net |publisher=HEIM:SPIEL |access-date=28 June 2020}}</ref> His grandfather György emigrated with his wife from [[Budapest]] to Germany.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.tsg-hoffenheim.de/aktuelles/news/2015/06/julius-illes-tapemaster-j-im-ruhestand/ |title=Julius Illes: "Tapemaster J." im Ruhestand |website=TSG 1899 Hoffenheim |language=de |date=23 June 2015 }}</ref> His father Georg worked as a coach at Rot-Weiss Walldorf; meanwhile, his elder brother, Fabian, had a football scholarship at the [[St. Francis College]] in [[Brooklyn]], [[New York City|New York]], to study Business Management, Economics and Finance.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/niklas-sule-10-things-on-bayern-munich-s-towering-centre-back-boateng-5487 |title=Niklas Süle: 10 things on Bayern Munich's towering centre-back |website=Bundesliga |date=2019 }}</ref> His family name is of [[Hungarian language|Hungarian]] origin, but the manager of the [[Turkish Football Federation]] under-16 national team contacted Süle during his teenage years about the possibility of playing for them as Süle's surname seemed [[Turkish language|Turkish]] to him.<ref>{{cite web|title=Süles kuriose Anfrage aus der Türkei|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/startseite/674154/artikel_sueles-kuriose-anfrage-aus-der-tuerkei.html|publisher=kicker.de|access-date=13 June 2017|language=de-DE|date=25 March 2017}}</ref> He was also eligible for the [[Hungary national football team|Hungarian national team]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.origo.hu/sport/futball/20170325-niklas-sule-nemet-focivalogatott-kozephatved.html|title=Valójában magyar a Bayern töröknek nézett németje|last=Kft|first=New Wave Media Group|website=origo.hu |language=hu|access-date=25 May 2019}}</ref> since he holds [[Hungarian citizenship]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://bulinews.com/news/1315/how-turkey-tried-recruit-niklas-sule-national-team |title=How Turkey tried to recruit Niklas Süle for the national team |website=Bundesliga News |date=18 November 2018 |access-date=27 September 2022 |lang=en |last=Gjerulff |first=Rune}}</ref>

Süle had a son in late 2020 with his girlfriend Melissa Halte.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.sport1.de/fussball/bundesliga/2020/12/nationalspieler-suele-wird-vater |title=Bayern-Nationalspieler wird Vater |website=sport1.de |language=de |date=19 December 2020 }}</ref>
