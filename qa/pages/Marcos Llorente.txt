'''Marcos Llorente Moreno''' (born 30 January 1995) is a Spanish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] or a [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|right-back]] for [[La Liga]] club [[Atlético Madrid]] and the [[Spain national football team|Spain national team]]. 

Developed at [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]], he appeared in 39 competitive matches over three seasons, also serving a loan at [[Deportivo Alavés|Alavés]]. He signed with [[Atlético Madrid]] in June 2019, winning [[La Liga]] in his [[2020–21 La Liga|second season]] while being deployed in several positions.

Llorente made his full debut for [[Spain national football team|Spain]] in 2020. He was part of the squads at [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Club career==
===Real Madrid===
[[File:Marcos Llorente.JPG|thumb|upright|Llorente playing for [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]] in 2015]]
Born in [[Madrid]], Llorente joined [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]]'s youth setup in 2008, aged 13.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.defensacentral.com/la_fabrica/119889-marcos-llorente-empieza-llamar-puerta-reportaje/|title=Marcos Llorente empieza a llamar a la puerta|trans-title=Marcos Llorente starts calling the door|publisher=Defensa Central|last=Vargas|first=Diego|language=es|date=24 February 2015|access-date=17 October 2015}}</ref> In July 2014, after impressing with the ''Juvenil'' squad, he was promoted straight to [[Real Madrid Castilla|the reserves]] by manager [[Zinedine Zidane]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.marca.com/2014/07/25/futbol/equipos/real_madrid/1406303675.html|title=Flechazo con Marcos Llorente|trans-title=Love at first sight with Marcos Llorente|newspaper=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|last=Polo|first=Pablo|language=es|date=25 July 2014|access-date=17 October 2015}}</ref>

On 24 August 2014, Llorente made his senior debut, starting in a 2–1 away loss against [[Atlético Madrid B]] in the [[Segunda División B]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2014/08/2-1-castilla-let-the-three-points-slip-against-atletico-b|title=2–1: Castilla let the three points slip against Atlético B|publisher=Real Madrid CF|language=es|date=24 August 2014|access-date=17 October 2015}}</ref> He appeared in 25 matches during [[2014–15 Segunda División B|the campaign]], totalling 1,637 minutes of action.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.noticiasdealava.eus/alaves/2016/08/09/marcos-llorente-llegara-cedido/333870.html|title=Marcos Llorente llegará cedido|trans-title=Marcos Llorente will arrive on loan|newspaper=Noticias de Álava|last=Del Campo|first=José Luis|language=es|date=9 August 2016|access-date=23 June 2021}}</ref>

Llorente spent the 2015 pre-season with the first team, appearing in [[Friendly (association football)|friendlies]] against [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]], [[Inter Milan]] and [[Vålerenga Fotball]]. He made his professional – and [[La Liga]] – debut on 17 October of that year, coming on as a second-half [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] for [[Mateo Kovačić]] in the 3–0 home win over [[Levante UD]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.marca.com/eventos/marcador/futbol/2015_16/la-liga/jornada_8/rma_lev/|title=Antibalas Keylor y tres fogonazos|trans-title=Bulletproof Keylor and three flashes|newspaper=Marca|last=Jiménez|first=Rubén|language=es|date=17 October 2015|access-date=17 October 2015}}</ref>

On 10 August 2016, Llorente was [[Loan (association football)|loaned]] to [[Deportivo Alavés]] for [[2016–17 La Liga|the season]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.deportivoalaves.com/noticia/marcos-llorente-calidad-y-vision-de-juego-para-el-centro-del-campo|title=Marcos Llorente, calidad y visión de juego para el centro del campo|trans-title=Marcos Llorente, quality and vision for the midfield|publisher=Deportivo Alavés|language=es|date=10 August 2016|access-date=13 February 2021}}</ref> On 10 September, he featured the full 90 minutes in a 2–1 victory at [[FC Barcelona]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://realidadblanca.com/2016/09/11/marcos-llorente-se-luce-camp-nou/|title=Marcos Llorente se luce en el Camp Nou|trans-title=Marcos Llorente shows his stuff at the Camp Nou|publisher=Realidad Blanca|last=Parrón Estévez|first=Ignacio|language=es|date=11 September 2016|access-date=11 September 2016|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160913052734/http://realidadblanca.com/2016/09/11/marcos-llorente-se-luce-camp-nou/|archive-date=13 September 2016}}</ref>

On 23 September 2017, Llorente's contract was extended until 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2017/09/marcos-llorentes-contract-extension|title=Ceremony to mark Marcos Llorente's contract renewal|publisher=Real Madrid CF|date=23 September 2017|access-date=24 September 2017}}</ref> He made one appearance in [[2017–18 UEFA Champions League|that campaign]]'s [[UEFA Champions League]],<ref name=RCN>{{cite web|url=https://www.rcnradio.com/deportes/asi-llegan-las-plantillas-de-real-madrid-y-liverpool-la-final-de-la-champions|title=Así llegan Real Madrid y Liverpool a la final de la Champions|trans-title=That is how Real Madrid and Liverpool arrive to the Champions final|publisher=[[RCN Radio]]|language=es|date=25 May 2018|access-date=29 May 2018}}</ref> when Madrid won their third consecutive and 13th overall title in the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2018/matches/round=2000885/match=2021711/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Madrid beat Liverpool to complete hat-trick|publisher=UEFA|date=26 May 2018|access-date=28 May 2018}}</ref>

Llorente scored his first competitive goal for the team on 22 December 2018, netting the second in the 4–1 defeat of [[Al Ain FC]] in [[2018 FIFA Club World Cup|the final]] of the [[FIFA Club World Cup]] and being named [[player of the match]] in the process.<ref name=FCWC>{{cite news|url=https://www.marca.com/futbol/mundial-de-clubes/cronica/2018/12/22/5c1e81a2e5fdea02688b4580.html|title=El Madrid agranda su leyenda|trans-title=Madrid largen their legend|newspaper=Marca|last=Rodríguez|first=José María|language=es|date=22 December 2018|access-date=22 December 2018|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191008171528/https://www.marca.com/futbol/mundial-de-clubes/cronica/2018/12/22/5c1e81a2e5fdea02688b4580.html|archive-date=8 October 2019}}</ref>

===Atlético Madrid===
On 20 June 2019, Llorente was signed by local rivals [[Atlético Madrid]] on a five-year contract for a reported fee of £35&nbsp;million.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.atleticodemadrid.com/noticias/agreement-over-the-transfer-of-marcos-llorente|title=Agreement over the transfer of Marcos Llorente|publisher=Atlético Madrid|date=20 June 2019|access-date=20 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2019/06/official-announcement-marcos-llorente|title=Official announcement: Marcos Llorente|publisher=Real Madrid CF|date=20 June 2019|access-date=20 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11838/11745511/marcos-llorente-agrees-to-join-atletico-madrid-from-real-madrid|title=Marcos Llorente agrees to join Atletico Madrid from Real Madrid|publisher=[[Sky Sports]]|date=21 June 2019|access-date=19 July 2019}}</ref> He made his official debut on 18 August, playing 25 minutes in the 1–0 home win against [[Getafe CF]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.co.uk/football/report?gameId=550606|title=Atleti beat Getafe as both teams finish with 10 men|publisher=[[ESPN]]|date=18 August 2019|access-date=21 August 2019}}</ref> His first goal came the following 14 February, when he put his team ahead in a 2–2 draw at [[Valencia CF]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.in/football/report?gameId=550375|title=Valencia come from behind to draw with Atletico Madrid in La Liga thriller|publisher=ESPN|date=14 February 2020|access-date=18 July 2020}}</ref>

On 11 March 2020, during [[Extra time (association football)|extra time]] of the [[2019–20 UEFA Champions League|Champions League round-of-16 tie]] away to defending champions [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]], Llorente scored twice to tie the match 2–2 after replacing [[Diego Costa]] early into the second half, in an eventual 3–2 victory and qualification.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/liverpool-vs-atletico-madrid-live-score-champions-league-result-a4385036.html?amp|title=Liverpool crash out of Champions League to Atletico Madrid – LIVE! Latest news and result reaction|newspaper=[[Evening Standard]]|last=Doyle|first=Tom|date=11 March 2020|access-date=11 March 2020}}</ref> On 17 June, he was named man of the match in the league game at [[CA Osasuna]] after coming from the bench in the 63rd minute, scoring a solo goal and later [[Assist (association football)|assisting]] twice in the 5–0 rout.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://as.com/futbol/2020/06/17/primera/1592416179_639585.html|title=Goleada de Champions|trans-title=Champions rout|newspaper=[[Diario AS]]|last=Cazón|first=Patricia|language=es|date=17 June 2020|access-date=23 June 2020}}</ref>

Llorente scored a career-best 12 times in [[2020–21 La Liga|2020–21]] (second-best in the squad behind [[Luis Suárez]]'s 21), adding 11 assists as the ''Colchoneros'' were crowned champions after a seven-year wait; in the process, he became the club's first player to register double digits in both categories since [[Diego Forlán]] in [[2008–09 La Liga|2008–09]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.elespanol.com/deportes/futbol/20210522/suarez-marcos-llorente-barca-madrid-liga-atletico/577193242_0.html|title=Suárez y Marcos Llorente, el 'regalo' del Barça y el 'descarte' del Madrid que dieron La Liga al Atlético|trans-title=Suárez and Marcos Llorente, the Barça 'gift' and the Madrid 'surplus' that gave La Liga to Atlético|newspaper=[[El Español]]|last=Pacheco|first=Jorge|language=es|date=22 May 2021|access-date=21 April 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.sport.es/es/noticias/apuestas-deportivas/marcos-llorente-lider-simeone-12004970|title=Marcos Llorente, un líder para Simeone|trans-title=Marcos Llorente, a leader to Simeone|newspaper=[[Sport (Spanish newspaper)|Sport]]|language=es|date=24 August 2021|access-date=21 April 2022}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Originally a [[defensive midfielder]] or a [[playmaker]] in front of a back four, Llorente is also capable of playing in more advanced positions such as a [[Central midfielder|central]] or [[box-to-box midfielder]]. At Atlético Madrid, under coach [[Diego Simeone]], he was also fielded out of his regular position, first on the right side of the midfield in a [[Formation (association football)#4–4–2|4–4–2 formation]] and later as a supporting [[Forward (association football)|forward]] in the same system.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://en.as.com/en/2020/02/15/football/1581764913_178012.html|title=Atlético's Marcos Llorente enjoying new role and relishing Liverpool clash|newspaper=Diario AS|last=Hall|first=Andy|date=15 February 2020|access-date=18 June 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://ghanasoccernet.com/heres-how-simeone-has-moulded-the-new-marcos-llorente|title=Here's how Simeone has moulded the new Marcos Llorente|publisher=Ghana Soccernet|date=10 June 2020|access-date=18 June 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.mundodeportivo.com/futbol/atletico-madrid/20200614/481772667930/marcos-llorente-saco-nota-en-su-examen-como-punta.html|title=Marcos Llorente sacó nota en su examen como punta|trans-title=Marcos Llorente passed in his forward test|newspaper=[[Mundo Deportivo]]|last=Zimmermann|first=Walter|language=es|date=14 June 2020|access-date=18 June 2020}}</ref>

At the conclusion of [[2019–20 La Liga|2019–20]], Llorente was the sixth fastest player in the competition, with his top running speed clocked at 35.09 km/h.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://junipersports.com/marcos-llorente-in-the-top-10-league-sprinters|title=Marcos Llorente in the Top 10 League sprinters|publisher=Juniper Sports|date=22 July 2020|access-date=24 July 2020}}</ref> Apart from his physical attributes, such as pace, height, body fitness and stamina, his strengths also included excellent ball winning skills, visionary passing over both short and long distances, dribbling, first touch and shooting from just outside the penalty area, which led him to score several goals.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.marca.com/futbol/laboratorio-datos/2020/07/21/5f15cc32ca4741ba3b8b4593.html|title=Marcos Llorente, la metamorfosis de un mediocentro reconvertido a delantero|trans-title=Marcos Llorente, the metamorphosis of a central midfielder turned forward|newspaper=Marca|language=es|date=21 July 2020|access-date=24 July 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.squawka.com/en/marcos-llorente-atletico-position-change-liverpool-anfield|title=Stunning Liverpool may have changed the course of Marcos Llorente's career|publisher=Squawka|date=23 July 2020|access-date=24 July 2020}}</ref> The following season, as Atlético went through a tactical switch, he was usually deployed as either a box-to-box or an [[attacking midfielder]] in a [[Formation (association football)#5–3–2|5–3–2]] or 3–4–2–1 formations. On occasion, he also filled in as a right [[Defender (association football)#Wing-back|wingback]] for his club,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.mundodeportivo.com/futbol/atletico-madrid/20210213/492017189499/llorente-ya-es-el-todocampista-mas-decisivo-de-laliga.html|title=Llorente ya es el 'todocampista' más decisivo de LaLiga|trans-title=Llorente is already LaLiga's most decisive "allfielder"|newspaper=Mundo Deportivo|last=G. Fuente|first=Chema|language=es|date=13 February 2021|access-date=17 February 2021}}</ref> or a right back in a four-man defence for the national team.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-espana.net/2021/03/29/atletico-madrid-star-marcos-llorente-set-for-defensive-role-on-spain-duty|title=Atletico Madrid star Marcos Llorente set for defensive role on Spain duty|publisher=Football España|last=Brennan|first=Feargal|date=29 March 2021|access-date=14 April 2021}}</ref>

For his training attitude and patience, Llorente was praised by Simeone and [[Jorge Valdano]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://elpais.com/deportes/2018/12/28/actualidad/1545993223_461399.html|title=Marcos Llorente, marine y monje|trans-title=Marcos Llorente, marine and monk|newspaper=[[El País]]|last=Valdano|first=Jorge|language=es|date=28 December 2018|access-date=24 July 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.intothecalderon.com/2020/6/19/21292615/marcos-llorente-virtue-patience-atletico-real-madrid-diego-simeone-laliga|title=Marcos Llorente and the virtue of patience|publisher=[[SB Nation]]|last=Dunne|first=Robbie|date=19 June 2020|access-date=24 July 2020}}</ref>

==Personal life==
A boyhood supporter of Real Madrid,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/real-madrid/2018/12/19/5c1ab165e5fdea70168b4571.html|title=The great success of Santiago Solari is called Marcos Llorente|newspaper=Marca|last1=Díaz|first1=José Félix|last2=Whelan|first2=Padraig|date=19 December 2018|access-date=22 December 2018}}</ref> Llorente has ties in his family to both football and the club. His father [[Paco Llorente|Francisco]] and great uncle [[Francisco Gento]] were [[Winger (association football)|wingers]], while his maternal grandfather [[Ramón Grosso]] was a forward.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://futbol.as.com/futbol/2015/10/17/primera/1445096623_689050.html|title=Debutó con el Madrid Marcos Llorente, sobrino-nieto de Gento|trans-title=Madrid debut for Marcos Llorente, grand nephew of Gento|newspaper=Diario AS|language=es|date=17 October 2015|access-date=17 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/noticias/2014/04/marcos-llorente-seguir-los-pasos-de-mi-familia-es-un-orgullo-y-una-motivacion-extra|title=Marcos Llorente: "Seguir los pasos de mi familia es un orgullo y una motivación extra"|trans-title=Marcos Llorente: "To follow in the footsteps of my family is an honour and an extra motivation"|publisher=Real Madrid CF|language=es|date=9 April 2014|access-date=17 October 2015}}</ref>
