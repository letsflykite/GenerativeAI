{{Nihongo|'''Shuto Machino'''|町野 修斗|Machino Shūto|born 30 September 1999}} is a Japanese professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[striker (association football)|striker]] for [[2. Bundesliga]] club, [[Holstein Kiel]] and the [[Japan national football team|Japan national team]]. He was called-up for the national team to represent Japan at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], as a replacement for the injured [[Yuta Nakayama]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://jfa.jp/news/00030908/ |title=SAMURAI BLUE 選手変更のお知らせ FIFAワールドカップカタール2022|access-date=8 November 2022 |date=8 November 2022 |language=ja |website=jfa.jp |publisher=[[Japan Football Association]]}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Before turning professional===
Influenced by his older brother, he started playing soccer at the age of 3. During Junior High School, he played at FC Avenida Sol. Machino entered Riseisha High School in [[Osaka]] and became a regular player in there since the first grade.<ref>[http://footies.jp/pickup/6605 町野修斗(履正社高校2年／FW)イブラ系の要注目ストライカー自身の活躍でチームを選手権に導けるか] footies!（2017年2月13日）2018年1月17日閲覧。</ref> In his second year, he was selected for the high school selection, which caught the attention of [[Yokohama F. Marinos]], who invited the young player in his third year to participate in trainings with the club. After successfully concluding his trainings, he was evaluated, and officially joined Yokohama F. Marinos from the 2018 season, after his High School graduation.<ref>{{Cite news|title=サッカーJ1横浜入団 「感謝忘れず成長」 伊賀市出身・町野修斗君|newspaper=伊賀タウン情報ユー|date=13 November 2017|url=http://www.iga-younet.co.jp/news1/2017/11/j1.html|accessdate=16 January 2018}}</ref>

===After turning professional===
In 1 February 2019, Machino earned a loan transfer to Giravanz Kitakyushu from [[Yokohama F. Marinos]]. On Matchweek 11, he scored his first professional goal in his first time starting a match for the club in five games. On Matchweek 12, he scored another goal, scoring then, two goals in two consecutive games. Following decent performances for his club, he became a starting member after the 21st round, when the J3 League resumed, right after the first wave [[COVID-19]] global threat. He became the team's top scorer at the end of the season. On 6 January 2020, he permanently transferred to Kitakyushu.<ref>{{Cite press release|date=6 January 2020|url=https://www.giravanz.jp/news/p34335.html|title=町野 修斗 選手 横浜F・マリノスより完全移籍加入のお知らせ|publisher=ギラヴァンツ北九州|accessdate=6 January 2020|archive-date=10 November 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221110175421/https://www.giravanz.jp/news/p34335.html|url-status=dead}}</ref> After participating in the opening game of the same season, he started 5 consecutive games on the bench. After another league break due to another [[COVID-19 pandemic in Japan|COVID-19]] wave,from the 7th round, he participated in 29 consecutive games. Although he recorded 7 goals and 7 assists in the first half of the season, he was not able to replicate his performances for the remaining of it, having directly contributed to just 2 goals. On 26 December 2020, he was signed to [[Shonan Bellmare]] in a permanent transfer.<ref>{{Cite press release|date=26 December 2020|url=http://www.bellmare.co.jp/251645|title=ギラヴァンツ北九州 町野修斗選手 完全移籍加入のお知らせ|publisher=湘南ベルマーレ|accessdate=26 December 2020}}</ref> On 21 and 25 May 2022, he scored two braces in two matches, scoring it on Shonan's 2–1 win over [[Vissel Kobe]], and on a 4–0 win against [[Kawasaki Frontale]], becoming the second player in club history since [[Wagner Lopes]] in 1998 to score braces in consecutive matches.<ref>[https://www.nikkansports.com/soccer/news/202205250001228.html 【データ】湘南FW町野修斗が呂比須以来の２試合連続２得点　クラブ24年ぶり２人目] 日刊スポーツ（2022年5月25日）2022年6月25日閲覧。</ref><ref>[https://web.gekisaka.jp/news/jleague/detail/?370024-370024-fl 阿部浩之スーパーミドルに町野は初の2桁弾!!残留争いの中で湘南が5試合ぶりの貴重な白星] ゲキサカ（2022年10月8日）2022年10月9日閲覧。</ref> At the end of the 2022 season, he finished second in the J1 League goalscoring rankings, scoring 13 goals, just one goal behind Brazilian [[Thiago Santana]]. Machino ended the season as the Japanese player with most goals scored during the season. On 29 June  2023, Machino was announcement officially permanent transfer to 2. Bundesliga club, [[Holstein Kiel]] from 2023–24 season.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bellmare.co.jp/316420 |title=町野修斗選手 ホルシュタイン・キール（ドイツ2部）へ完全移籍のお知らせ |access-date=2023-06-29 |publisher=湘南ベルマーレ}}</ref>

==International career==
On 13 July 2022, Machino was selected for the first time as a member of the [[Japan national football team|Japan national team]], which is composed only of domestic groups participating in the [[2022 EAFF E-1 Football Championship]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.nikkansports.com/soccer/japan/news/202207130000563.html |title=【日本代表】初選出の湘南FW町野修斗「積み上げてきたものを、すべて発揮してこようと」 |publisher=日刊スポーツ |date=13 July 2022 |accessdate=14 July 2022}}</ref> On 19 July 2022, he scored two goals, including his first goal for the national team, against Hong Kong in the first match of the EAFF E-1 Football Championship 2022.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.nikkansports.com/soccer/japan/news/202207190001502.html|title=【日本代表】FW町野修斗と西村拓真が代表デビュー戦ともに２ゴール Ｅ－１香港戦で結果|publisher=日刊スポーツ|date=19 July 2022|accessdate=20 July 2022}}</ref> On 27 July of the same year, he scored the game-deciding third goal in a match against [[South Korea national football team|South Korea]]. He was selected to play in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] by [[Hajime Moriyasu]], the then Japan national team's manager, after an injury saw left-back [[Yuta Nakayama]] being ruled out of it. He did not make an appearance in the tournament.
