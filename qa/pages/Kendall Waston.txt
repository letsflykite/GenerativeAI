'''Kendall Jamaal Waston Manley''' (born 1 January 1988) is a Costa Rican professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Centre-back|centre-back]] for [[Liga FPD]] club [[Deportivo Saprissa|Saprissa]] and the [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica national team]].

==Club career==
===Saprissa and loans===
Waston made his professional debut for [[Deportivo Saprissa|Saprissa]] in 2006<ref>[http://www.aldia.cr/columnistas-danilo_jimenez/curioso-caso-Kendall-Waston_0_304169636.html El curioso caso de Kendall Waston] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160604165849/http://www.aldia.cr/columnistas-danilo_jimenez/curioso-caso-Kendall-Waston_0_304169636.html |date=4 June 2016 }} - Al Día {{in lang|es}}</ref> and played on loan for [[Asociación Deportiva Carmelita|Carmelita]] in the [[Primera División de Costa Rica]] before moving abroad for a six-month loan spell with [[Club Nacional de Football]] in the [[Primera División Uruguaya]] from February 2008.<ref>[http://wvw.nacion.com/ln_ee/2008/febrero/26/deportes1439371.html Kendall Waston se enrola en Nacional] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304053410/http://wvw.nacion.com/ln_ee/2008/febrero/26/deportes1439371.html |date=4 March 2016 }} - Nación {{in lang|es}}</ref> In March 2010, Waston signed a three-month loan deal with [[Bayamón FC|Bayamón]] of the [[Puerto Rico Soccer League]] to help the club qualify for the [[CONCACAF Champions League]]. He later had three more loan spells, one at [[Club de Futbol Universidad de Costa Rica|UCR]] and two at [[Municipal Pérez Zeledón|Pérez Zeledón]].<ref>[http://www.nacion.com/deportes/futbol-costa-rica/Solis-Loaiza-Waston-suenan-Perez_0_1195080592.html Solís sigue a la espera, Loaiza y Waston suenan en Pérez] - Nación {{in lang|es}}</ref> In May 2012 Waston returned to Deportivo Saprissa to be part of the first team.

===Vancouver Whitecaps FC===
On 8 August 2014, Vancouver Whitecaps FC announced a deal that they had acquired the defender from Saprissa. Waston joined the first team in the midst of their 2014 MLS season.<ref>{{cite web |author=Brandon Timko |url=http://www.whitecapsfc.com/news/2014/08/waston-defensive-and-offensive-help |title=Addition of centre back Kendall Waston targeted for defensive and offensive help &#124; Vancouver Whitecaps FC |publisher=Whitecapsfc.com |date=8 August 2014 |access-date=13 August 2014}}</ref>

On 25 October 2014, Waston scored the only goal in the Whitecaps' final game of the regular season, heading in a corner kick from [[Pedro Morales (footballer)|Pedro Morales]], sending the Whitecaps to the playoffs for the second time in their four years since joining MLS.<ref>{{cite web |author=Michael McColl |url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/2014/10/27/vancouver-whitecaps-kendall-waston-says-biggest-goal-lies-ahead |title=After sending Vancouver Whitecaps to playoffs, Kendall Waston says "biggest" goal lies ahead |publisher=MLSSoccer.com |date=27 October 2014 |access-date=28 October 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141029195917/http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/2014/10/27/vancouver-whitecaps-kendall-waston-says-biggest-goal-lies-ahead |archive-date=29 October 2014 |url-status=dead}}</ref> The impact that Waston had on the Whitecaps' season after joining the club earned him a nomination for MLS Player of the Month for October.<ref>{{cite web |author=MLSSoccer staff |url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/2014/10/27/vote-now-etihad-airways-mls-player-month-october |title=Vote now for Etihad Airways MLS Player of the Month for October |publisher=MLSSoccer.com |date=27 October 2014 |access-date=28 October 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141029173340/http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/2014/10/27/vote-now-etihad-airways-mls-player-month-october |archive-date=29 October 2014 |url-status=dead}}</ref> Waston played his 100th MLS game for the Whitecaps on 26 May 2018, against San Jose. He signed a new contract before the 2018 season, becoming a designated player under league rules. Waston was the captain of Vancouver Whitecaps, having been named so before the 2017 MLS season.

===FC Cincinnati===
On 11 December 2018, Waston was traded to [[FC Cincinnati]] ahead of their inaugural season in MLS. Vancouver received $450,000 of General Allocation Money, $300,000 of Targeted Allocation Money and an international roster spot.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fccincinnati.com/news/fc-cincinnati-acquires-defender-kendall-waston-vancouver-whitecaps-fc|title=Cincinnati Adds Two-Time MLS Best XI Selection, Kendall Waston &#124; FC Cincinnati|website=fccincinnati}}</ref> Waston captained the team for the majority of the 2019 MLS season.{{citation needed|date=November 2019}} He was released by Cincinnati at the end of their 2020 season.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fccincinnati.com/news/fc-cincinnati-announce-roster-decisions-2021-season|title=FC Cincinnati announce roster decisions for the 2021 season &#124; FC Cincinnati|website=fccincinnati}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:Kendall Waston 2018.jpg|thumb|upright=0.8|Waston with [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica]] at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]]]]
A big defender, Waston was part of Costa Rica's [[2007 FIFA U-20 World Cup squads|2007 FIFA U-20 World Cup squad]].<ref>{{FIFA player|236536}}</ref>

Born in Costa Rica, Waston is of Jamaican descent.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.whitecapsfc.com/news/heart-soul-passion-inside-kendall-wastons-journey-2018-fifa-world-cup|title='Heart, soul, passion:' Inside Kendall Waston's journey to the 2018 FIFA World Cup &#124; Vancouver Whitecaps|website=whitecapsfc}}</ref> He made his senior debut for [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica]] in a May 2013 match against [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada]] and earned, as of December 2014, one more cap in a 2014 [[FIFA World Cup qualification]] against [[Honduras national football team|Honduras]].<ref name=FIFA/> On 8 October, he scored a 95th-minute equaliser for Costa Rica against Honduras to send Costa Rica to the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]]. While placed on the bench for the first two games of the FIFA world cup Kendall had a chance to play in the third match and was able to score off a header and gain his first World Cup goal.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.concacaf.com/category/world-cup-qualifying-men/game-detail/225963 |title=World Cup Qualifying – Men |website=CONCACAF|access-date=8 October 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170902225910/http://www.concacaf.com/category/world-cup-qualifying-men/game-detail/225963|archive-date=2 September 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>
