'''Nikola Vlašić''' ({{IPA-sh|nǐkola ʋlâʃitɕ|hr}};<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hjp.znanje.hr/index.php?show=search_by_id&id=eF1gUBk%3D|title=Nìkola|quote=Nìkola|website=Hrvatski jezični portal|access-date=17 March 2018|language=sh}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://hjp.znanje.hr/index.php?show=search_by_id&id=f19vXBJ%2B|title=Vlȁškā|quote=Vlȁšić|website=Hrvatski jezični portal|access-date=17 March 2018|language=sh}}</ref> born 4 October 1997) is a Croatian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as an [[Midfielder (association football)#Attacking midfielder|attacking midfielder]] or [[Forward (association football)#Winger|winger]] for [[Serie A]] club [[Torino F.C.|Torino]], on loan from [[Premier League]] club [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]], and the [[Croatia national football team|Croatia national team]].

Born in the Vlašić family, a prominent Croatian sports family, Nikola is a youth product of [[HNK Hajduk Split|Hajduk Split]] [[HNK Hajduk Split Reserves and Academy|academy]]. He made his senior debut in summer 2014, making ''[[The Guardian]]''<nowiki/>'s ''Next Generation'' list later that year.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/blog/ng-interactive/2014/oct/14/next-generation-2014-40-of-the-best-young-talents-in-world-football|title=Next Generation 2014: 40 of the best young talents in world football|date=14 October 2014|work=The Guardian|access-date=14 October 2014}}</ref> In 2017, his performances earned him a move to [[Everton F.C.|Everton]]; however, after an unsuccessful [[2017–18 Everton F.C. season|season]], he was loaned out to [[PFC CSKA Moscow|CSKA Moscow]] who made the move permanent upon the end of the [[2018–19 PFC CSKA Moscow season|season]].

Vlašić made his international debut in 2017, before becoming a regular international<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rsssf.org/miscellaneous/kroa-recintlp.html|title=Appearances for Croatia National Team|accessdate=14 January 2023|website=RSSSF}}</ref> after [[Croatia at the 2018 FIFA World Cup|Croatia's 2018 FIFA World Cup campaign]]. He represented his country at the [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Club career==
===Hajduk Split===
Vlašić joined the [[NK Omladinac Vranjic|Omladinac Vranjic]] academy, before he was brought to [[HNK Hajduk Split|Hajduk Split]], aged 12. He excelled at youth club level, and in the [[2013–14 HNK Hajduk Split season|2013–14 season]] he formed a potent midfield partnership for the Hajduk U17 team with [[Andrija Balić]], which resulted in the team finishing the first half of the season in first place, without a single loss. The two then received and signed professional contracts,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.24sata.hr/nogomet/potpisali-vlasic-hairlahovic-i-balic-ocekujemo-puno-od-njih-349501|title=Potpisali Vlašić, Hairlahović i Balić: Očekujemo puno od njih|date=16 January 2014|publisher=24sata|language=hr|access-date=16 January 2014}}</ref> and were moved to the U19 team, where he played regularly.

He made his first team debut in the [[2014–15 UEFA Europa League|UEFA Europa League]] [[2014–15 UEFA Europa League qualifying phase and play-off round#Second qualifying round|qualifying]] match on 17 July 2014, playing in the away match against [[Dundalk F.C.|Dundalk]]. Scoring on his debut, he became Hajduk Split's youngest scorer in international competitions, aged 16 years and 9 months, 2 months less than the previous record holder [[Mario Tičinović]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://gol.dnevnik.hr/clanak/nogomet/rijeka-protiv-fradija-na-kantridi-hajduk-u-irskoj-split-na-parku-mladezi---344500.html|title=Hajduk slavio u Irskoj: Nikola Vlašić najmlađi strijelac 'bijelih' u povijesti!|date=17 July 2014|publisher=Gol.hr|language=hr|access-date=17 July 2014}}</ref> That season, Vlašić went on to make 37 appearances in all competitions, scoring four goals.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hajduk.hr/prva-momcad/nikola-vlasic/8|title=Prva momčad • HNK Hajduk Split|website=hajduk.hr}}</ref>

On 30 June 2016, Hajduk announced that Vlašić had been appointed vice-captain of the club, with [[Lovre Kalinić]] continuing to be the captain.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hajduk.hr/vijest/kalinic-je-kapetan--a-nikola-vlasic-njegov-nasljednik/7423|title=Kalinić je kapetan, a Nikola Vlašić njegov nasljednik!|website=hajduk.hr}}</ref> As Kalinić was on extended holidays as a result of being part of the [[UEFA Euro 2016 squads#Croatia|Croatian squad]] for the [[UEFA Euro 2016]], Vlašić captained Hajduk for the first time on 14 July, the [[2016–17 HNK Hajduk Split season|season]] opening fixture; a 2–2 away draw against [[FC Politehnica Iași (2010)|CSM Politehnica Iași]] in the [[2016–17 UEFA Europa League qualifying phase and play-off round#Second qualifying round|second round]] of the [[2016–17 UEFA Europa League qualifying phase and play-off round|2016–17 Europa League qualifying phase]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2017/matches/round=2000789/match=2020096/postmatch/index.html|title=UEFA Europa League 2016/17 - History - CSMS laşi-Hajduk Split – UEFA.com|last=uefa.com|website=Uefa.com}}</ref> Despite his tender age, Vlašić would captain the side four more times that season.

==International career==
[[File:20150328 1426 U19 AUTHRV 1317.jpg|thumb|upright|Vlašić with [[Croatia national under-19 football team|Croatia U19]] in 2015]]

Vlašić made his debut for [[Croatia national under-16 football team|Croatia U16]] at the age of 14,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hns-cff.hr/en/players/119418/nikola-vlasic/|title=Statistics - Caps: Nikola Vlašić|publisher=Croatian Football Federation|access-date=5 March 2015}}</ref> playing regularly with older teammates at [[Croatia national under-17 football team|U17]] and [[Croatia national under-18 football team|U18]] levels as well. On 28 May 2017, he made his debut for the [[Croatia national football team|senior team]] in a [[Friendly match#Association football|friendly match]] against [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]] after being named in the starting line-up.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://kwese.espn.com/football/report?gameId=475660|title=Croatia beat Mexico to spoil Hernandez's record-breaking night|website=ESPN|last=Marshall|first=Tom|date=28 May 2017|access-date=29 August 2017}}</ref> On 18 November 2018, he provided [[Andrej Kramarić]] with an assist in a [[2018–19 UEFA Nations League A#Group 4|UEFA Nations League]] match against [[England national football team|England]] for a 0–1 lead, which Croatia eventually lost 2–1.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Dawes|first=Olly|date=19 November 2018|title=Everton fans react to Nikola Vlasic's performance against England|url=https://www.hitc.com/en-gb/2018/11/19/everton-fans-react-to-nikola-vlasics-performance-against-england/|access-date=19 August 2020|website=HITC|language=en-GB}}</ref>

Vlašić was named in [[Croatia national under-21 football team|Croatia]]'s [[2019 UEFA European Under-21 Championship squads#Croatia|squad]] for [[2019 UEFA European Under-21 Championship|UEFA Under-21 Euro 2019]]. He scored in a 4–1 defeat to [[Romania national under-21 football team|Romania]] and a 3–3 draw with [[England national under-21 football team|England]], on 18 and 24 June respectively.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Škrlec|first=Silvijo|date=25 June 2019|title=Nikola Vlašić nije mogao zamisliti tužniji i nesretniji završetak Europskog prvenstva|url=https://www.tportal.hr/sport/clanak/nikola-vlasic-nije-mogao-zamisliti-tuzniji-i-nesretniji-zavrsetak-europskog-prvenstva-foto-20190625|access-date=19 August 2020|website=Tportal.hr|language=hr}}</ref>

On 6 September 2019, he scored his first goal for the senior squad in a [[UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying Group E|Euro 2020 qualifying]] match against [[Slovakia national football team|Slovakia]] for a 0–1 lead. Croatia won the game 0–4.<ref>{{Cite web|date=6 September 2019|title=EURO 2020: Impressive Croatia beat Slovakia 4-0 away|url=https://www.croatiaweek.com/euro-2020-impressive-croatia-beat-slovakia-4-0-away/|access-date=19 August 2020|website=Croatia Week|language=en}}</ref> On 13 October, Vlašić scored yet another opening goal in a 1–1 draw with [[Wales national football team|Wales]].<ref>{{Cite news|title=Bale earns Wales draw with Croatia|language=en-GB|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49949349|access-date=23 June 2021}}</ref> On 16 November, he scored the equalizer against Slovakia at home, leading to Croatia's 3–1 victory and qualification for the [[UEFA Euro 2020|tournament]].<ref>{{Cite news|date=16 November 2019|title=Croatia 3-1 Slovakia: World Cup finalists top group to qualify for Euro 2020|language=en-GB|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/50446884|access-date=19 August 2020}}</ref> However, in March 2020, the tournament was postponed for a year due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Europe|COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Euro 2020 postponed until 2021 |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/51909518 |website=BBC Sport |access-date=23 June 2021 |language=en-GB |date=18 March 2020}}</ref>

On 11 October 2020, Vlašić scored the opener in a 2–1 [[2020–21 UEFA Nations League A#Group 3|UEFA Nations League]] victory over [[Sweden national football team|Sweden]] at [[Stadion Maksimir]] and followed it up three days later with a goal in a 2–1 loss to reigning [[2018 FIFA World Cup Final|world champions]] [[France national football team|France]] at the same location.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mbappe late show sees France snatch Nations League win over Croatia|url=https://theworldgame.sbs.com.au/mbappe-late-show-sees-france-snatch-nations-league-win-over-croatia|access-date=15 October 2020|website=The World Game|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Croatia vs. Sweden - Football Match Report - October 11, 2020 - ESPN|url=https://www.espn.com.au/football/report?gameId=570731|access-date=15 October 2020|website=ESPN.com|language=en}}</ref>

On 17 May 2021, Vlašić was selected in [[Zlatko Dalić]]'s [[UEFA Euro 2020 squads#Croatia|26-man squad]] for the UEFA Euro 2020,<ref>{{Cite web|date=17 May 2021|title=Zlatko Dalić objavio popis igrača za Euro, ovo su imena 26 igrača koje će voditi|url=https://www.vecernji.hr/sport/uzivo-od-13-sati-zlatko-dalic-objavljuje-popis-igraca-za-euro-1493130|url-status=live|access-date=23 June 2021|website=Večernji list|language=hr}}</ref> marking Vlašić's first major tournament appearance.<ref>{{Cite web|last=[[Croatian News Agency|HINA]]|date=27 May 2021|title=Vlašić prokomentirao Dalićevu primjedbu da Vatrenima nedostaje zajedništva i kemije iz Rusije|url=https://gol.dnevnik.hr/clanak/hrvatska-reprezentacija/nikola-vlasic-svi-jedva-cekamo-utakmicu-s-englezima-na-wembleyju---653541.html|url-status=live|access-date=23 June 2021|website=Gol.hr|language=hr}}</ref> He started off the bench in Croatia's first two matches against England and the [[Czech Republic national football team|Czech Republic]],<ref>{{Cite web|last=Juranović|first=Tomislav|date=24 June 2021|title=On je Dalićev 'čovjek-spajalica'! Tri godine mijenjao je Rakitića, a sad je predvodnik nove generacije|url=https://sportske.jutarnji.hr/sn/nogomet/reprezentacija/on-je-dalicev-covjek-spajalica-tri-godine-mijenjao-je-rakitica-a-sad-je-predvodnik-nove-generacije-15083340|url-status=live|access-date=25 June 2021|website=Sportske novosti|language=hr-hr}}</ref> but on 22 June, he was named the [[UEFA Euro 2020 statistics#Man of the Match|Star of the Match]] in the 3–1 victory over [[Scotland national football team|Scotland]]―which qualified Croatia for the [[UEFA Euro 2020 knockout phase#Round of 16|Round of 16]]―after scoring the opening goal.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Killen|first=Stephen|date=22 June 2021|title=Everton fans can't help wonder same thing about Nikola Vlasic after Croatia win|url=https://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/everton-fans-cant-help-wonder-20879756|access-date=23 June 2021|website=Liverpool Echo|language=en}}</ref>

On 9 November 2022, Vlašić was named in Dalić's [[2022 FIFA World Cup squads#Croatia|26-man squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.index.hr/sport/clanak/dalic-objavio-popis-za-svjetsko-prvenstvo-u-kataru/2410337.aspx |title=Dalić objavio popis za Svjetsko prvenstvo u Kataru |date=9 November 2022 |access-date=13 December 2022 |website=[[Index.hr]] |lang=hr}}</ref> In the [[2022 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Japan vs Croatia|round of 16]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Croatia vs Brazil|quarter-final]] matches against [[Japan national football team|Japan]] and [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]] on 5 and 9 December, respectively—both of which led to a [[Penalty shootout (association football)|penalty shootout]]—Vlašić was the first to shoot for Croatia, scoring both times as Croatia progressed to the [[2022 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Argentina vs Croatia|semi-final]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/pidgin/articles/cn0ylp95zpeo |title=Croatia beat Japan on penalties to reach quarter-finals |lang=pcm |website=[[BBC News Pidgin]] |date=5 December 2022 |access-date=13 December 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Croatia vs Brazil summary: Croats win on penalties, score, goals, highlights {{!}} Qatar World Cup 2022 |website=[[Diario AS]] |url=https://en.as.com/soccer/croatia-vs-brazil-live-online-score-stats-and-updates-qatar-world-cup-2022-n/ |first1=Roddy |last1=Cons |first2=William |last2=Allen |date=10 December 2022 |access-date=13 December 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://telesport.telegram.hr/kolumne/juris/nepoderivi/ |title=Nepoderivi |date=10 December 2022 |access-date=13 December 2022 |first=Bernard |last=Jurišić |website=Telesport |lang=hr}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.index.hr/sport/clanak/nikola-vlasic-je-prisao-klupi-i-rekao-da-ce-pucati-prvi-penal-to-je-on/2420009.aspx |title="Nikola Vlašić je prišao klupi i rekao da će pucati prvi penal. ==Personal life==
Vlašić was born in [[Split, Croatia|Split]] to the family of former [[Cross-country skiing (sport)|cross-country skier]] Venera Milin and athletics coach [[Joško Vlašić]].  He is the younger brother of the world champion [[high jump]]er [[Blanka Vlašić]]. His father hails from Dubrava near [[Tisno]]. His mother was born and raised in [[Delnice]], but her roots are from [[Jezera, Tisno|Jezera]] on the [[List of islands of Croatia|island]] of [[Murter]]. Vlašić's future football career was viewed as his father's "project", who started training him personally ever since he was four years old.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vecernji.hr/ostali-sportovi/josko-vlasic-nije-nikola-projekt-za-zaradu-922719|title=Joško Vlašić: Nije Nikola projekt za zaradu|date=22 February 2014|publisher=Večernji list|language=hr|access-date=22 February 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Morić |first1=Danijela-Ana |title=Sestrin ponos: Svi govore o talentiranom i zgodnom bratu Blanke Vlašić |url=https://www.tportal.hr/showtime/clanak/sestrin-ponos-svi-govore-o-talentiranom-i-zgodnom-bratu-blanke-vlasic-foto-20190910 |access-date=9 July 2020 |work=tportal.hr |date=10 September 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=4 September 2007|title=Godine odricanja za jednu čarobnu noć u Osaki|url=https://www.jutarnji.hr/naslovnica/godine-odricanja-za-jednu-carobnu-noc-u-osaki-3957435|url-status=live|access-date=6 March 2021|website=Jutarnji list|language=hr-hr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Mikuličin|first=Ivo|date=11 November 2018|title=Blanka Vlašić: Znam svaku murtersku valu, i tamo je moj dom!|url=https://slobodnadalmacija.hr/sibenik/susur/lifestyle/blanka-vlasic-znam-svaku-murtersku-valu-i-tamo-je-moj-dom-574316|url-status=live|access-date=7 March 2021|website=Šibenski|language=hr-hr}}</ref>

On 22 May 2021, Vlašić married his long-time girlfriend Ana in St. Lawrence's Church in Split.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Gabelić|first=Tomislav|date=22 May 2021|title=Nikola Vlašić u Splitu je oženio djevojku Anu, a stiže i prinova|url=https://www.24sata.hr/sport/nikola-vlasic-u-splitu-je-ozenio-djevojku-anu-a-stize-i-prinova-763587|url-status=live|access-date=23 May 2021|website=24sata|language=hr}}</ref> In late June of the same year, they became parents of a baby boy whom they named Noa.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Gabelić |first1=Tomislav |title=Nikola Vlašić postao otac: Puno lijepih emocija bilo je ovih dana |url=https://www.24sata.hr/sport/nikola-vlasic-dobio-sina-puno-lijepih-emocija-bilo-je-ovih-dana-770602 |website=24sata |access-date=3 July 2021 |language=hr |date=28 June 2021}}</ref>
