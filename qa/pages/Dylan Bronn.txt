'''Dylan Daniel Mahmoud Bronn''' ({{lang-ar|دِيلاَن دَانِيَال مَحْمُود بْرون}}; born 19 June 1995) is a French-Tunisian professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]] for Italian {{Italian football updater|Salernitana}} club [[U.S. Salernitana 1919|Salernitana]] and for the [[Tunisia national football team|Tunisia national team]]. Before becoming professional, Bronn played in the French lower leagues with [[AS Cannes|Cannes]], his hometown club.

==Club career==
Bronn was signed by [[Chamois Niortais F.C.|Niort]] in the summer of 2016, initially as a reserve team player on an amateur contract. However, first team manager [[Denis Renaud]] gave Bronn the chance to play in three of the side's pre-season friendly matches. His performances in these games led Renaud to hand Bronn his professional debut on 29 July 2016 in the 0–0 draw with [[RC Lens]] on the first matchday of the year at the [[Stade René Gaillard]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lanouvellerepublique.fr/Deux-Sevres/Sport/Football/Ligue-2/n/Contenus/Articles/2016/08/01/La-bonne-surprise-Dylan-Bronn-2798491|title=La (bonne) surprise Dylan Bronn|publisher=lanouvellerepublique.fr|date=1 August 2016|access-date=31 March 2017}}</ref> Over the course of the 2016–17 season, he became a regular starter for the first team, forming a central defensive partnership with [[Jérémy Choplin]]. On 18 October 2016, Bronn was given a three-year professional contract by Niort.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chamoisniortais.fr/actualites/2016/10/18/dylan-bronn-passe-professionnel/|title=Dylan Bronn passe professionnel|publisher=chamoisniortais.fr|date=18 October 2016|access-date=31 March 2017}}</ref>

Bronn joined [[Belgian First Division A]] side [[K.A.A. Gent]] in 2017. He was elected best player of the season of KAA Gent for the 2018–19 season.{{citation needed|date=July 2020}}

He joined [[FC Metz]] in 2020.

On 12 August 2022, Bronn signed a three-year contract with [[U.S. Salernitana 1919|Salernitana]] in Italy.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=Salernitana|url=https://www.ussalernitana1919.it/dylan-bronn-e-un-nuovo-giocatore-della-salernitana/|title=DYLAN BRONN È UN NUOVO GIOCATORE DELLA SALERNITANA|date=12 August 2022|access-date=15 August 2022|language=it}}</ref>

==International career==
Bronn was born in France, and is Tunisian by descent through his mother, and German through his father. Bronn was called up to the [[Tunisia national football team|Tunisia national team]] in March 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chamoisniortais.fr/actualites/2017/03/14/dylan-bronn-appele-en-equipe-tunisie/|title=Dylan Bronn appelé en Tunisie|publisher=chamoisniortais.fr|date=14 March 2017|access-date=31 March 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ftf.org.tn/fr/liste-des-convoques-pour-tunisie-vs-cameroun-et-maroc-vs-tunisie/|title=Liste des convoqués pour Tunisie vs Cameroun et Maroc vs Tunisie|publisher=ftf.org.tn|date=16 March 2017|access-date=31 March 2017}}</ref> He won his first cap for Tunisia in a friendly match against [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]] on 28 March 2017, playing the whole match in a 1–0 defeat.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lanouvellerepublique.fr/Deux-Sevres/Communes/Niort/n/Contenus/Articles/2017/03/30/Et-d-une-pour-Dylan-Bronn-3050789|title=Et d'une pour Dylan Bronn|publisher=lanouvellerepublique.fr|date=30 March 2017|access-date=31 March 2017}}</ref>

In June 2018 he was named in Tunisia’s final 23-man squad for the [[2018 World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Crawford |first1=Stephen |title=Revealed: Every World Cup 2018 squad - Final 23-man lists |url=https://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/revealed-every-world-cup-2018-squad-23-man-preliminary-lists/oa0atsduflsv1nsf6oqk576rb |website=Goal |access-date=16 July 2019 |date=4 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44342860|title=Tunisia World Cup squad: Leicester City's Benalouane in 23-man squad|date=2 June 2018|website=BBC Sport}}</ref>
