'''Álisson Ramsés Becker''' (born 2 October 1992), known as '''Alisson Becker''' or simply '''Alisson''', is a Brazilian professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Premier League]] club [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] and the [[Brazil national football team|Brazil national team]].<ref name="Alisson LFC"/> He is regarded as one of the best goalkeepers in the world due to his immense shot stopping, distribution and ability in one-on-one situations.<ref>{{cite web |date=13 February 2022 |title=Top 10 Best Football Goalkeeper in the World 2022 |url=https://www.sportsunfold.com/top-10-best-football-goalkeeper-in-the-world-2022/ |access-date=29 May 2022 |website=SportsUnfold}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |date=13 May 2022 |title=Top 10 Best Goalkeepers in The World [2022 Ranking Report] |url=https://sportsvirsa.com/best-goalkeepers-in-the-world/ |access-date=29 May 2022 |website=Sports Virsa}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |date=6 March 2022 |title=Top 10 best goalkeepers in the world [2022 Ranking Report] |url=https://themoney.co/en/top-10-des-meilleurs-gardiens-de-but-au-monde-2022-ranking-report/ |access-date=29 May 2022 |publisher=The Money Co}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |date=29 April 2022 |title=Top 10 Best Goalkeepers in the World 2022 |url=https://nubiapage.com/top-10-best-goalkeepers-in-the-world-2022/ |access-date=29 May 2022 |website=NUBIA MAGAZINE!}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |date=19 February 2022 |title=Top 10 Goalkeepers in the World Right Now in 2022 |url=https://www.owogram.com/top-10-goalkeepers-world/ |access-date=29 May 2022 |website=Owogram}}</ref>

Alisson joined [[Sport Club Internacional|Internacional]]'s academy in 2002, progressing through the youth set up before making his senior debut in 2013. During his four years with Internacional's senior side, Alisson won the [[Campeonato Gaúcho]] title in each season.<ref name="Roma" /> He signed for Roma in July 2016 and was awarded [[Serie A Goalkeeper of the Year]] in [[2017–18 Serie A|2017–18]].<ref name=":0" /> In July 2018, Liverpool signed Alisson for a fee of £66.8&nbsp;million (€72.5&nbsp;million), making him the [[List of most expensive association football transfers|most expensive goalkeeper of all time]]. At Liverpool, Alisson has won the Premier League, [[FA Cup]], [[EFL Cup]], [[UEFA Champions League]] and [[FIFA Club World Cup]]. In 2019, he was named [[The Best FIFA Goalkeeper]] and was also the recipient of the inaugural [[Yashin Trophy]].<ref>{{cite web |last=McCambridge |first=Ed |date=10 April 2020 |title=Ranked! The 10 best goalkeepers in the world |url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/best-goalkeepers-world-top-ranked-jan-oblak-alisson-ter-stegen-courtois-de-gea-liverpool-real-madrid-man-utd |access-date=20 September 2020 |website=FourFourTwo |archive-date=21 April 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210421035750/https://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/best-goalkeepers-world-top-ranked-jan-oblak-alisson-ter-stegen-courtois-de-gea-liverpool-real-madrid-man-utd |url-status=dead}}</ref> Alisson has twice been selected in the [[FIFPRO#FIFA FIFPRO Men's World 11|FIFA FIFPRO Men's World 11]].

Alisson represented Brazil at various youth levels before making his senior international debut in 2015. He represented the nation at the [[FIFA World Cup]] in [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022]], and the [[Copa América]] in [[Copa América Centenario|2016]], [[2019 Copa América|2019]] and [[2021 Copa América|2021]], winning the 2019 tournament while also being named its best goalkeeper.<ref name="prêmios">{{cite web |url=https://globoesporte.globo.com/futebol/selecao-brasileira/noticia/copa-america-selecao-recebe-o-trofeu-fair-play.ghtml |title=Daniel Alves é eleito craque da Copa América; Alisson e Everton também ganham prêmios |trans-title=Daniel Alves is elected the best player of the Copa América; Alisson and Everton also win awards |publisher=Globo |language=pt |date=7 July 2019}}</ref>
