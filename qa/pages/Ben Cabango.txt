'''Benjamin George Cabango''' (born 30 May 2000) is a Welsh professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]] for {{English football updater|SwanseaC}} club [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]] and the  [[Wales national football team|Wales national team]].

==Early life==
Cabango was born in [[Cardiff]] to an Angolan father and a Welsh mother. He has a younger brother named [[Theo Cabango|Theo]] who plays rugby union for [[Cardiff Rugby|Cardiff]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.walesonline.co.uk/sport/rugby/rugby-news/introducing-cabango-brothers-super-talented-17245471 |title=Introducing the Cabango brothers, the super-talented siblings excelling in Welsh rugby and at Swansea City |work=WalesOnline |publisher=Media Wales |last=Thomas |first=Simon |date=15 November 2019 |access-date=6 January 2020}}</ref> He attended [[Ysgol Gyfun Gymraeg Plasmawr]], a Welsh language comprehensive school.<ref name="tns">{{cite news |url=https://www.tnsfc.co.uk/2018/06/12/saints-sign-swansea-starlet/ |title=Saints Sign Swansea Starlet |publisher=The New Saints |last=Bloor |first=Stewart |date=12 June 2018 |access-date=13 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ysgolplasmawr.cymru/images/docs/adroddiadblynyddol15-16s.pdf |title=Annual Report of the Governing Body 2015–16 |publisher=Ysgol Gyfun Gymraeg Plasmawr |access-date=13 August 2019}}</ref>

==Club career==
Cabango began his career as a youth player with local amateur side Maindy Corries, where his father Paolo was a coach, before joining the youth academy at [[Newport County A.F.C.|Newport County]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.maindy.co.uk/news/maindy-boy-selected-for-welsh-squad-1413540.html |title=Maindy Boy Selected for Welsh Squad |publisher=Maindy Corries F.C. |last=Masrani |first=Daniel |date=15 August 2015 |access-date=13 August 2019 |archive-date=13 August 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190813200446/http://www.maindy.co.uk/news/maindy-boy-selected-for-welsh-squad-1413540.html |url-status=dead }}</ref> where he played from under-13 to under-15 levels. He moved to the academy at [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]] where he captained the under-19 side to win the [[FAW Welsh Youth Cup]] in 2018 and helped the under-23 side reach the final of the [[Premier League Cup (football)|Premier League Cup]].<ref name="tns"/><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.shropshirestar.com/sport/football/the-new-saints/2018/06/12/the-new-saints-sign-swansea-youngster/ |title=The New Saints sign Swansea youngster |newspaper=Shropshire Star |last=Edwards |first=Joe |date=12 June 2018 |access-date=13 August 2019}}</ref>

In June 2018, Cabango signed for reigning [[Cymru Premier|Welsh Premier League]] champions [[The New Saints F.C.|The New Saints]] on a six-month loan deal.<ref name="tns"/> He made his senior debut for the side in a 5–0 defeat to Macedonian side [[KF Shkëndija]] in the first leg of the first qualifying round of the [[UEFA Champions League]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44733795 |title=KF Shkendija 5–0 The New Saints |publisher=BBC Sport |date=10 July 2018 |access-date=13 August 2019}}</ref> In the return leg, Cabango scored his first senior goal as The New Saints won 4–0 but were eliminated on aggregate.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44851728 |title=The New Saints 4–0 KF Shkendija |publisher=BBC Sport |date=17 July 2018 |access-date=13 August 2019}}</ref>

On 13 August 2019, he made his professional debut for Swansea in a 3–1 victory over [[Northampton Town F.C.|Northampton Town]] in the first round of the [[EFL Cup|League Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49245335 |title=Swansea City 3–1 Northampton Town |publisher=BBC Sport |date=13 August 2019 |access-date=13 August 2019}}</ref> He made his league debut as a substitute against [[Huddersfield Town A.F.C.|Huddersfield Town]] on 26 November 2019.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/50469231 |title=Huddersfield 1-1 Swansea |publisher=BBC |date=26 November 2019|access-date=9 December 2019}}</ref>

==International career==
Cabango made his senior Wales international debut as a second-half substitute during the team's [[2020–21 UEFA Nations League|UEFA Nations League]] [[2020–21 UEFA Nations League B|League B]] match against [[Finland national football team|Finland]] on 3 September 2020, which Wales won 1–0.<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/53927793 Cabango Wales debut]</ref> In May 2021 he was selected for the Wales squad for the delayed [[UEFA Euro 2020]] tournament.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57301006|title=Uncapped Colwill in Wales Euro 2020 squad|work=BBC Sport }}</ref> In November 2022 he was named in the Wales squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63545011 2022 Wales World Cup squad]</ref>

==Personal life==
Ben Cabango is the elder brother of professional [[rugby union]] player [[Theo Cabango]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Theo Cabango: Brother of Swansea City's Ben bids to seize Cardiff Rugby chance |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/rugby-union/59672275 |website=BBC Sport |date=15 December 2021 |accessdate=21 December 2021 }}</ref>
