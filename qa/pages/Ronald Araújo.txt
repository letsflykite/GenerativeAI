'''Ronald Federico Araújo da Silva''' (born 7 March 1999) is a Uruguayan professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] or [[right back]] for [[La Liga]] club [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] and the [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay national team]].

==Club career==
===Rentistas===
Born to a Uruguayan father and a Brazilian mother in [[Rivera]], a city near the Brazil border,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://elpais.com/deportes/2020-10-03/ronald-araujo-el-velocista-que-se-mira-en-puyol.html|title=Ronald Araujo, el velocista que se mira en Puyol|first=Jordi|last=Quixano|language=es|website=El País|date=3 October 2020|accessdate=3 February 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sport.es/es/noticias/barca/ronald-araujo-el-central-humilde-que-el-real-madrid-no-pudo-birlar-8151257|title=Ronald Araujo, el central humilde que el Real Madrid no pudo 'birlar'|first=German|last=Bona|language=es|website=Sport|date=11 October 2020|accessdate=3 February 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.marca.com/futbol/barcelona/2020/12/21/5fdfabe7ca4741273c8b4606.html|title=Ronald Araujo: un soldado de Dios que esprinta a 35km/h y está bendecido por los jefes|website=Marca|language=es|first=Hugo|last=Cerezo|date=21 December 2020|accessdate=3 February 2021}}</ref> Araújo joined [[C.A. Rentistas|Rentistas]] from hometown club Huracán de Rivera.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ovaciondigital.com.uy/futbol/ronald-araujo-boston-river-barcelona.html|title=Ronald Araújo: de Boston River a Barcelona|trans-title=Ronald Araújo: from Boston River to Barcelona|publisher=Ovación|language=es|date=28 August 2018|access-date=3 September 2018}}</ref> He made his senior debut with the former on 24 September 2016, coming on as a late [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] in a 1–0 [[Uruguayan Segunda División|Segunda División]] away win against [[Tacuarembó F.C.|Tacuarembó]].

Araújo scored his first senior goal on 9 December 2016, netting the equaliser in a 2–2 home draw against [[Central Español]]. He became a regular starter during the 2017 season, and scored a [[hat-trick]] in a 3–2 away win over [[Villa Española]] on 17 June of that year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://elascenso.com/destacados/villa-espanola-2-rentistas-3/|title=Villa Española 2 Rentistas 3|publisher=El Ascenso|language=es|date=17 June 2017|access-date=3 September 2018}}</ref>

It was at Rentistas that Araújo made the significant switch in position from forward to defender.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Lowe |first=Sid |last2=@sidlowe |date=2023-02-23 |title=Barcelona’s Ronald Araújo: ‘I came from a different football. But I told myself, I have to play here’ |language=en-GB |work=The Guardian |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2023/feb/23/barcelonas-ronald-araujo-i-came-from-a-different-football-but-i-told-myself-i-have-to-play-here |access-date=2023-02-23 |issn=0261-3077}}</ref>

===Boston River===
On 28 July 2017, Araújo joined [[Uruguayan Primera División|Primera División]] side [[Boston River]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tenfield.com.uy/ronald-araujo-ya-es-de-boston/|title=Boston River: llega Ronald Araújo|trans-title=Boston River: Ronald Araújo arrives|publisher=[[Tenfield]]|language=es|date=28 July 2017|access-date=3 September 2018}}</ref> He made his debut in the category on 18 September, replacing [[Maximiliano Sigales]] late into a 1–0 home success over [[El Tanque Sisley]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tenfield.com.uy/boston-river-00-el-tanque-sisley/|title=Boston River volvió a festejar, 1:0|trans-title=Boston River celebrate again, 1–0|publisher=Tenfield|language=es|date=18 September 2017|access-date=3 September 2018}}</ref>

===Barcelona===
On 29 August 2018, Araújo signed a five-year contract with [[FC Barcelona]] for a fee of [[Euro|€]]1.7 million, plus €3.5 million in variables; he was initially assigned to [[FC Barcelona B|the reserves]] in [[Segunda División B]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fcbarcelona.es/futbol/barca-b/noticias/2018-2019/acuerdo-con-el-boston-river-para-el-traspaso-de-ronald-araujo?_ga=2.224128731.1587900172.1535999485-1095797129.1535999485|title=Acuerdo con el Boston River para el traspaso de Ronald Araujo|trans-title=Agremeent with Boston River for the transfer of Ronald Araujo|publisher=FC Barcelona|language=es|date=29 August 2018|access-date=3 September 2018}}</ref> He made his first team – and [[La Liga]] – debut on 6 October of the following year, coming off the bench to replace [[Jean-Clair Todibo]] in the 73rd minute of a 4–0 home win against [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]]; however, he was sent off in the 86th minute for tackling [[Javier Hernández]].<ref>[https://en.as.com/en/2019/10/06/football/1570396360_867051.html Dembélé and Araújo sent off during Barcelona win over Sevilla]; ''[[Diario AS]]''. 6 October 2019, Retrieved 18 August 2020</ref>

For the [[2020–21 FC Barcelona season|2020–21 season]], Araújo was promoted to the main squad, being handed the number 4 jersey, which was previously worn by [[Ivan Rakitić]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://as.com/futbol/2020/10/05/primera/1601923973_013966.html|title=Riqui Puig y Araújo ya son jugadores del primer equipo|trans-title=Riqui Puig and Araújo are already first team players|publisher=Diario AS|language=es|date=5 October 2020|accessdate=18 October 2020}}</ref> On 19 December 2020, he scored his first [[La Liga]] goal for Barcelona in a 2–2 home draw against [[Valencia CF|Valencia]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/barcelona/2020/12/19/5fde40c946163f2e908b45e4.html |title=Araujo is a man of many talents |website=Marca |date=19 December 2020 }}</ref>

On 20 March 2022, Araújo scored his first goal in [[El Clásico]], heading in a corner from [[Ousmane Dembélé]] in a 4–0 away victory against the league leaders at [[Santiago Bernabéu Stadium|Santiago Bernabéu]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Barcelona thrash leaders Real Madrid |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/60816848 |access-date=20 March 2022 |work=BBC Sport |date=20 March 2022}}</ref>

For the first 21 league games of the [[2022–23 FC Barcelona season|2022–23 season]], Araújo formed a defensive partnership alongside [[Andreas Christensen]] and occasionally [[Jules Koundé]] at centre-back, helping Barcelona keep the most clean sheets (16) in all of [[Big Five (association football)|Europe's top 5 leagues]]. <ref>{{cite news |title=Ronald Araujo vital as Barcelona nab 16th clean sheet, push LaLiga title further from Real Madrid|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/barcelona-espbarcelona/story/4876613/barcelona-nab-18th-clean-sheet-grip-laliga-title-over-real-madrid |work=ESPN |date=13 February 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
After representing [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay]] at [[Uruguay national under-17 football team|under-18]] and [[Uruguay national under-20 football team|under-20]] levels, Araújo received his first call up for the senior side on 5 October 2020, for two [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONMEBOL)|2022 FIFA World Cup qualifiers]] against [[Chile national football team|Chile]] and [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mundodeportivo.com/futbol/fc-barcelona/20200305/473976093569/primera-convocatoria-de-ronald-araujo-con-la-absoluta-de-uruguay.html|title=Primera convocatoria de Ronald Araujo con la absoluta de Uruguay|trans-title=First call up of Ronald Araújo with the full side of Uruguay|publisher=[[Mundo Deportivo]]|language=es|date=5 March 2020|accessdate=18 October 2020}}</ref> He made his full international debut eight days later, starting in a 2–4 loss against the latter.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mundodeportivo.com/futbol/fc-barcelona/20201014/484053950703/-ecuador-barre-a-la-uruguay-de-suarez-valverde-y-araujo.html|title=Ecuador barre a la Uruguay de Suárez, Valverde y Araujo (4–2)|trans-title=Ecuador beat Suárez, Valverde and Araújo's Uruguay (4–2)|publisher=Mundo Deportivo|language=es|date=14 October 2020|accessdate=18 October 2020}}</ref>

In June 2021, Araújo was included in the final 26-man Uruguay squad for the [[2021 Copa América]] in Brazil, but he didn't feature in any of Uruguay's five games in the tournament while also suffering a muscle injury.<ref>{{cite web|title=Plantel de Uruguay para la Copa América|trans-title=Uruguay squad for the Copa América|publisher=auf.org|language=es|url=https://www.auf.org.uy/plantel-de-uruguay-para-la-copa-america/|date=10 June 2021|access-date=19 July 2021}}</ref>

Despite nursing an injury, Araújo was included in the Uruguay squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], having expected recovery during knockout phase.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fcbarcelona.com/en/news/2903008/araujo-included-in-uruguay-squad|title=Araujo included in Uruguay squad|publisher=FC Barcelona|date=10 November 2022|accessdate=10 December 2022}}</ref> He didn't make an appearance as Uruguay were eliminated in the group stage.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/dec/02/ghana-uruguay-world-cup-group-h-match-report|title=Uruguay beat Ghana but crash out of World Cup on goals scored in late twist|work=The Guardian|date=2 December 2022|accessdate=10 December 2022}}</ref>
