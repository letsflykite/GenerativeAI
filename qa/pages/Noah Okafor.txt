'''Noah Arinzechukwu Okafor''' (born 24 May 2000) is a Swiss professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as [[Striker (association football)|striker]] for [[Austrian Football Bundesliga|Austrian Bundesliga]] club [[FC Red Bull Salzburg|Red Bull Salzburg]] and the [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland national team]].

==Club career==
===FC Basel===
Okafor played his first youth football with local team FC Arisdorf. In 2009 he moved to the youth of [[FC Basel]] and continued through all the stages of their youth academy. He advanced to their first team during their [[2018–19 FC Basel season|2018–19 season]] and on 31 January 2018, Okafor signed his first professional contract with his club under head coach [[Raphaël Wicky]].<ref name="fcb-aktuell-2018/19-Okafor">{{cite web | last = FC Basel 1893 | author-link = | date = 31 January 2018 | url = https://www.fcb.ch/aktuell/news/1-mannschaft/2018/01/der-fcb-verlaengert-mit-noah-okafor-bis-2020 | title = Der FCB verlängert mit Noah Okafor bis 2020 | format = | work = FCB extends contract with Noah Okafor until 2020 | publisher = FC Basel 1893 AG | language = de | access-date = 2022-11-16}}</ref> He played his debut for their first team on 19 May 2018 in the home game against [[FC Luzern]]. The trainer substituted him in for the injured [[Mohamed Elyounoussi]] in the 34th minute and the game ended in a 2–2 draw.<ref name="fcb-achiv-2018/19-FCB-FCL">{{cite web | last = Verein "Basler Fussballarchiv” | author-link = | date = 19 May 2018 | url = https://www.fcb-archiv.ch/saison/spiele?command=detail&id=31250 | title = FC Basel - FC Luzern 2:2 (1:1) | format = | publisher = Verein "Basler Fussballarchiv” | access-date = 2022-11-16}}</ref> He scored his first goal for his club in the second round of the 2018–19 season on 28 July 2018 in the 1–1 away draw against [[Neuchâtel Xamax FCS|Xamax]].<ref name="fcb-aktuell-2018/19-Xamax-FCB">{{cite web | last = FC Basel 1893 | author-link = | date = 28 July 2018 | url = https://www.fcb.ch/aktuell/news/1-mannschaft/2018/07/kein-sieger-zwischen-xamax-und-basel | title = Kein Sieger zwischen Xamax und Basel | format = | work = No winner between Xamax and Basel | publisher = FC Basel 1893 AG | language = de | access-date = 2022-11-16}}</ref>

Under trainer [[Marcel Koller]] Basel won the [[2018–19 Swiss Cup|Swiss Cup in the 2018–19 season]]. In the first round Basel beat FC Montlingen 3–0, in the second round [[FC Echallens|Echallens Région]] 7–2 and in the round of 16 [[FC Winterthur|Winterthur]] 1–0. In the quarter-finals [[FC Sion|Sion]] were defeated 4–2 after extra time and in the semi-finals [[FC Zürich|Zürich]] were defeated 3–1. All these games were played away from home. The final was held on 19 May 2019 in the [[Stade de Suisse|Stade de Suisse Wankdorf Bern]] against [[FC Thun|Thun]]. Striker [[Albian Ajeti]] scored the first goal, [[Fabian Frei]] the second for Basel, then [[Dejan Sorgić]] netted a goal for Thun, but the end result was 2–1 for Basel.<ref name="fcb-achiv-fcb-thun-cupfinal-2018–19">{{cite web | last = Verein "Basler Fussballarchiv” | author-link = | year = | url = https://www.fcb-archiv.ch/saison/spiele?command=detail&id=31396 | title = FC Basel - FC Thun 2:1 (1:0) | format = | publisher = Verein "Basler Fussballarchiv” | accessdate = 2022-11-16}}</ref> Okafor played in four cup games and scored a goal in the semi-final against Zürich.

Between the years 2017 and 2020 Okafor played a total of 72 games for Basel scoring a total of nine goals. 39 of these games were in the [[Swiss Super League]], seven in the [[Swiss Cup]], eight in the UEFA competitions (Champios League and Europa League) and 18 were friendly games. He scored three goals in the domestic league, two in the cup, two in the European competitions and the other two were scored during the test games.<ref name="fcb-achiv-Noah Okafor">{{cite web | last = Verein "Basler Fussballarchiv” | authorlink = | year = 2020 | url = https://www.fcb-archiv.ch/spieler?command=detail&id=9788 | title = Noah Okafor - FCB statistic | format = | publisher = Verein "Basler Fussballarchiv” | accessdate = 2022-11-16}}</ref>

===Red Bull Salzburg===
On 31 January 2020, Okafor signed for [[FC Red Bull Salzburg|Red Bull Salzburg]].<ref>{{cite web |title=FC Red Bull Salzburg - Welcome to Salzburg Noah Okafor |url=https://www.redbullsalzburg.at/en/fc-red-bull-salzburg/news/saison_2019_20/welcome-to-salzburg-noah-okafor.html |website=redbulls |accessdate=26 February 2020 |language=de}}</ref>

On 8 December 2021, Okafor scored the only goal of the game as Salzburg defeated [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]] in the final group stage match of the [[2021–22 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]]. The win meant Salzburg secured progression to the Round of 16 and became the first-ever Austrian club to qualify for the knockout stages of the Champions League.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/match/2032708--salzburg-vs-sevilla/|title=Salzburg-Sevilla|website=UEFA|date=8 December 2021|accessdate=8 December 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/lifestyle/sports/okafor-strike-sees-salzburg-historic-win-over-sevilla-2021-12-08/|title=Okafor strike sees Salzburg to historic win over Sevilla|website=Reuters|date=8 December 2021|accessdate=8 December 2021}}</ref>

==International career==
Okafor played various international games for the [[Switzerland national under-15 football team|Swiss U-15]] and [[Switzerland national under-17 football team|U-17 teams]]. He played his first game for the [[Switzerland national under-18 football team|U-18 team]] on 9 May 2018 in the 1–1 draw against the [[Italy national under-18 football team|Italian U-18 team]].

He made his debut for the [[Switzerland national football team|senior national team]] debut on 9 June 2019 in the [[2019 UEFA Nations League Finals|2019 UEFA Nations League third place game]] against England, as an 113th-minute substitute for [[Haris Seferovic]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[UEFA]]|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefanationsleague/season=2019/matches/round=2000957/match=2024421/index.html|title=Switzerland v England game report|date=9 June 2019}}</ref>
Okafor scored his first international goal for Switzerland on 15 November 2021, in their World Cup qualifying game against [[Bulgaria national football team|Bulgaria]], a win which secured automatic qualification for Switzerland to the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://onefootball.com/en/news/noah-okafor-sends-switzerland-into-world-cup-heaven-34082736|title=Noah Okafor sends Switzerland into World Cup heaven|website=One Football|date=15 November 2021|accessdate=24 November 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.eurosport.com/football/world-cup-qualification-uefa/2022/switzerland-v-bulgaria-follow-live-world-cup-qualifying_sto8629288/story.shtml|title=Switzerland beat Bulgaria to snatch group c top spot from Italy to qualify automatically for World Cup|website=Eurosport|date=15 November 2021|accessdate=24 November 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Born in [[Binningen, Switzerland]], Okafor is of [[Igbo people|Igbo]] descent.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allnigeriasoccer.com/read_news.php?nid=25068|title=Basel's Nigerian Midfielder Noah Okafor Notches Two Assists vs CSKA :: All Nigeria Soccer - The Complete Nigerian Football Portal|website=www.allnigeriasoccer.com}}</ref> His father is from Nigeria and his mother is Swiss.
