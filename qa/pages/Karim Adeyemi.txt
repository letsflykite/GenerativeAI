'''Karim-David Adeyemi''' (born 18 January 2002) is a German professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]] for [[Bundesliga]] club [[Borussia Dortmund]] and the [[Germany national football team|Germany national team]].

== Club career ==
===Red Bull Salzburg===
Adeyemi played as a youth for TSV Forstenried, and at the age of eight he joined local [[Bundesliga]] side [[FC Bayern München]] in 2010. Due to an impasse in negotiating his continued stay at the club, there was a dispute and Adeyemi eventually had to leave the club, which meant that he joined [[SpVgg Unterhaching]] in 2012.<ref name="s-z">{{cite web |last1=Hummel |first1=Thomas |title=Wie ein Talent aus Unterhaching zum Millionengeschäft wurde |url=https://www.sueddeutsche.de/sport/adeyemi-fritz-walter-medaille-transfers-jugendfussball-1.4191947 |website=sueddeutsche.de |publisher=[[Süddeutsche Zeitung]] |access-date=5 July 2020 |date=12 August 2019}}</ref> After progressing through the youth departments, he made his debut in March 2018 for the U19 team in the ''[[Under 19 Bundesliga|A-Junioren-Bundesliga]]'' (Under 19 Bundesliga). He scored his first goal in this league in April 2018 in a 2&ndash;3 loss to [[Eintracht Frankfurt|Eintracht Frankfurt U19]]. With Unterhaching, he suffered relegation to the ''[[Under 19 Bayernliga|A-Jugend Bayernliga]]'' (Under 19 Bayernliga) at the end of the season.

Prior to the [[2018–19 Austrian Football Bundesliga|2018–19 season]], Adeyemi was signed by Austrian club [[FC Red Bull Salzburg]] where he signed a three-year contract.<ref>{{cite web |title=Servus in Salzburg, Karim Adeyemi! |url=https://www.redbullsalzburg.at/de/fc-red-bull-salzburg/news/saison_2017_18/servus-in-salzburg-karim-adeyemi.html |website=redbullsalzburg.at |publisher=[[FC Red Bull Salzburg]] |access-date=5 July 2020}}</ref> He was subsequently loaned out to their [[Farm team|feeder club]] [[FC Liefering]] for the season. Adeyemi made his [[Austrian Football Second League|2. Liga]] debut on 1 September 2018 against [[Austria Lustenau]], where he played the full game as Liefering lost 1&ndash;0.<ref name="soccerway2">{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2018/09/01/austria/1-liga/sc-austria-lustenau/usk-anif/2802706/|title=Austria Lustenau vs. Liefering - Liefering - 1 September 2018 - Soccerway|publisher=soccerway.com|access-date=2 September 2018}}</ref> On 1 December 2020, he scored his first [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] goal in a 3–1 away win over [[FC Lokomotiv Moscow|Lokomotiv Moscow]] during the [[2020–21 UEFA Champions League|2020–21]] season.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/match/2029446--lokomotiv-moskva-vs-salzburg/ |title=Lokomotiv Moskva 1–3 Salzburg |publisher=UEFA |date=1 December 2020 }}</ref>

===Borussia Dortmund===
On 10 May 2022, Adeyemi was announced to have joined [[Bundesliga]] club [[Borussia Dortmund]] on a deal until the summer of 2027.<ref>{{Cite news |date=23 April 2022 |title=Borussia Dortmund: Karim Adeyemi wechselt von RB Salzburg zum BVB |language=de |work=Der Spiegel |url=https://www.spiegel.de/sport/fussball/karim-adeyemi-wechselt-von-rb-salzburg-zu-borussia-dortmund-a-6b04527b-48b1-4a03-940d-e101f21c7237 |access-date=11 May 2022 |issn=2195-1349}}</ref> On 5 October, he scored his first Champions League goal with Borussia Dortmund in a 4–1 away win over [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.zdf.de/sport/uefa-champions-league/fc-sevilla-borussia-dortmund-highlights-zusammenfassung-100.html |title=Borussia Dortmund triumphiert in Sevilla |publisher=ZDF |language=de |date=5 October 2022 }}</ref> On 15 February 2023, he scored the only goal from a solo strike in a 1–0 victory over [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] in the Champions League round of 16 and revealed that [[Fufu]] - an African dish makes him fast.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2023/feb/15/borussia-dortmund-chelsea-champions-league-last-16-match-report |title=Karim Adeyemi's superb solo strike leaves Chelsea's hopes in balance |work=The Guardian |date=15 February 2023 }}</ref> <ref>{{Cite web |last=Mumuni |first=Lukman |date=2023-02-17 |title=Video of Adeyemi skillfully eating fufu goes viral after winner against Chelsea |url=https://sportsbrief.com/football/borussia-dortmund/34703-footage-fastest-player-bundesliga-karim-adeyemi-eating-favourite-food-fufu-emerges/ |access-date=2023-02-22 |website=SportsBrief - Sport news. |language=en}}</ref>

==International career==
Adeyemi was born in [[Munich]], Germany, to a Nigerian father and Romanian mother.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sueddeutsche.de/sport/adeyemi-jugendfussball-geschaeft-1.4191947|title=Wie ein Talent aus Unterhaching zum Millionengeschäft wurde|author=Thomas Hummel|work=[[Süddeutsche Zeitung]]|language=de|date=31 October 2018|access-date=10 November 2018}}</ref> He is a youth international for Germany, having represented the [[Germany national youth football team|Germany under-16s]], [[Germany national youth football team|under-17s]] and [[Germany national under-21 football team|under-21s]]. He made his debut for the [[Germany national football team|Germany senior team]] in a 6–0 [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification]] win over [[Armenia national football team|Armenia]] on 5 September 2021, coming on as a late substitute and scoring his side's sixth goal in the first minute of second-half injury time.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://fifa.com/|title=FIFA|website=fifa.com}}</ref> As Adeyemi was a Red Bull Salzburg player on his debut, this marked the first time in the post-war period that a player from an Austrian club played a match for the German senior national team.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wettfreunde.net/sportwetten-news/karim-adeyemi-privat-karriere-erfolge/|title=Herkunft, Bayern-Zeit & schneller als Bolt – Deutschlands neues Sturm-Juwel Karim Adeyemi|first=Thomas|last=Haider|publisher=|date=|accessdate=7 September 2021|language=de}}</ref>

In November 2022, he was named in the German squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dfb.de/news/detail/flick-beruft-moukoko-fuellkrug-und-goetze-in-wm-kader-245974/|title=Flick beruft Moukoko, Füllkrug und Götze in WM-Kader|publisher=[[German Football Association]]|date=10 November 2022|access-date=10 November 2022|language=de}}</ref> However, he did not feature in any match, as Germany were knocked out at the group stage.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://soccernet.ng/2022/12/no-show-adeyemi-nigerian-descent-makes-no-impact-as-germany-crash-out-of-fifa-world-cup.html|title=No show Adeyemi: Nigerian descent makes no impact as Germany crash out of FIFA World Cup|publisher=soccernet.ng|date=December 2022}}</ref>
