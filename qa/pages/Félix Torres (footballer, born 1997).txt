'''Felix Eduardo Torres Caicedo''' (born 11 January 1997) is an Ecuadorian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]] for [[Liga MX]] club [[Santos Laguna]] and the [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador national team]].<ref name="soccerway">{{Soccerway|felix-eduardo-torres-caicedo/445270}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.atribuna.com.br/esportes/santosfc/jackson-porozo-vibra-com-estreia-pela-sele%C3%A7%C3%A3o-equatoriana-lembrarei-por-toda-minha-vida-1.66969|title=Jackson Porozo vibra com estreia pela seleção equatoriana: 'Lembrarei por toda minha vida'|trans-title=Jackson Porozo celebrates his debut for the Ecuador national team: 'I will remember it for my whole life'|publisher=A Tribuna|language=pt-BR|date=11 September 2019|accessdate=10 October 2019}}</ref>

After beginning his career with [[LDU Portoviejo]], Torres signed for [[Barcelona S.C.|Barcelona]] in 2017 before joining Santos Laguna in 2019. He made his international debut in 2019 and represented Ecuador at both the [[2021 Copa America]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Club career==
Torres began his career with [[LDU Portoviejo]] before signing for [[Ecuadorian Serie A]] side [[Barcelona S.C.|Barcelona]] in December 2026, on an initial one-year loan deal.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.benditofutbol.com/barcelona/barcelona-presento-cuatro-refuerzos-2017.html |title=Barcelona presentó cuatro refuerzos para el 2017 |publisher=Bendito Futbol |last=Ladines |first=Ronald |date=14 December 2016 |accessdate=4 December 2022 |language=es}}</ref> He made his debut for the side in a 2–1 victory over [[C.S.D. Macará|Macará]] on 2 July and scored his first goal in a win over [[Guayaquil City F.C.]] on 5 November.<ref name="soccerway"/> Two weeks later, Torres joined the club permanently in November 2017 having made six appearances during his loan spell, signing a six-year contract.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.benditofutbol.com/barcelona/felix-torres-renovo-contrato-seis.html |title=Félix Torres renovó su contrato con Barcelona SC por seis temporadas |publisher=Bendito Futbol |last=Martinez |first=Rodrigo |date=23 November 2017 |accessdate=4 December 2022 |language=es}}</ref>

In January 2019 Torres transferred over to Mexican club [[Santos Laguna]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Santos Laguna: Félix Torres, último refuerzo, llegó a Torreón|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/liga-mx/santos-laguna-felix-torres-refuerzo-torreon|publisher=Medio Tiempo|date=4 July 2019|accessdate=16 November 2019|language=Spanish}}</ref> following his former Barcelona coach [[Guillermo Almada]] who had taken charge of the side.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.benditofutbol.com/legionarios/felixtorres-titular-santoslaguna-mexico-guillermoalmada.html |title=Félix Torres aseguró que luchará por ganarse la titularidad en el Santos  |publisher=Bendito Futbol |date=16 July 2019 |accessdate=4 December 2022 |language=es}}</ref> In October 2019 Torres was punished for indiscipline by the club and as of November 2019 has not played since.<ref>{{cite web|title=Torres castigado en Santos Laguna por indisciplina|url=https://www.ecuavisa.com/articulo/estadio/internacional/538536-felix-torres-castigado-mexico-indisciplina|language=Spanish|publisher=Ecuavisa|date=20 October 2019|accessdate=16 November 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Guillermo Almada castigó a Félix Torres en Santos Laguna|url=https://www.eluniverso.com/deportes/2019/10/20/nota/7567976/guillermo-almada-castigo-felix-torres-santos-laguna-indisciplina|language=Spanish|publisher=[[El Universo]]|date=20 October 2019|accessdate=16 November 2019}}</ref>

==International career==
After playing for Ecuador at the [[2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup]] Torres was called up for the national team in February 2017 but did not play.<ref>{{cite web|title=La selección ecuatoriana se refuerza con tres jugadores menores de 20 años|url=https://www.efe.com/efe/america/deportes/la-seleccion-ecuatoriana-se-refuerza-con-tres-jugadores-menores-de-20-anos/20000010-3179477#|publisher=[[Ecuadorian Football Federation|EFE]]|date=14 February 2017|accessdate=16 November 2019|language=Spanish}}</ref>

Torres was named in the Ecuadorian squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], playing in all three of his nation's matches as they were eliminated in the group stage.<ref name="soccerway"/>
