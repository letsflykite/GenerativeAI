'''Martin Christensen Braithwaite''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|b|r|æ|θ|w|eɪ|t}};<ref name=sport>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=__Jlu9fPqp4 |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211220/__Jlu9fPqp4 |archive-date=20 December 2021 |url-status=live|title=Martin Braithwaite explica cómo se pronuncia su nombre y ¡reacciona a nuestro vídeo!|work=[[Sport (Spanish newspaper)|Sport]]|date=7 March 2020|access-date=18 February 2021|language=Spanish}}{{cbignore}}</ref> born 5 June 1991) is a Danish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays for [[Segunda División]] club [[RCD Espanyol|Espanyol]] and the [[Denmark national football team|Denmark national team]]. He plays mainly as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]], but he can also play as a [[Winger (association football)|winger]].

Having begun his career at [[Esbjerg fB]], he went on to play for [[Toulouse FC|Toulouse]] and [[FC Girondins de Bordeaux|Bordeaux]] in [[Ligue 1]], and [[CD Leganés|Leganés]] and [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] of La Liga. He also spent two years in England with [[Middlesbrough F.C.|Middlesbrough]].

Braithwaite made his senior international debut for [[Denmark national football team|Denmark]] in 2013 and has earned over 60 [[Cap (sport)|caps]]. He was part of their squads at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]], [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], reaching the semi-finals of the 2020 tournament. he is also known as Bratoite or Bratorout

==Club career==
===Esbjerg fB===
Braithwaite started out in the academy at Sædding-Guldager Idrætsforening (SGI), after which he joined the biggest club in his city, [[Esbjerg fB]]. He then spent a short time at [[FC Midtjylland]]'s sports academy, before moving back to Esbjerg, signing a three-year youth contract in 2007.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bold.dk/nyt/Esbjerg-skriver-med-Braithwaite|title=Esbjerg skriver med Braithwaite|last=bold.dk|website=bold.dk|date=12 October 2007 }}</ref> During his second stint at the Esbjerg academy, Braithwaite went on trials with both [[Reggina Calcio|Reggina]] and [[Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bold.dk/nyt/Reggina-tester-Esbjerg-talenter|title=Reggina tester Esbjerg-talenter|last=bold.dk|website=bold.dk|date=16 November 2007 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bold.dk/nyt/Esbjerg-talent-glad-for-engelsk-proevetraening|title=Esbjerg-talent glad for engelsk prøvetræning|last=bold.dk|website=bold.dk|date=21 July 2008 }}</ref> He eventually made his Esbjerg debut in 2009, making 63 [[Danish Superliga|Superliga]] appearances for the club and scoring 12 goals, including nine in the 2012–13 season during which he appeared in every league game for the club.<ref name=dkstats>[http://danskfodbold.com/spiller.php?ligaid=2001&spillerid=11282 Martin Braithwaite], Danskfodbold.com. Retrieved 15 August 2013.</ref> He won the Danish Cup with the club in 2012–13, scoring two goals in the semi-final second leg win over [[Brøndby IF]].{{citation needed|date=November 2020}}

Following his impressive displays for Esbjerg in the 2013 Superliga spring season and the [[Danish Cup]] he was called up to the Danish national squad in the summer of 2013. At the same time he was being rumoured to be attracting the interest of a number of European clubs including [[AJ Auxerre|Auxerre]], [[Stade Rennais F.C.|Rennes]], [[Celtic F.C.|Celtic]] and [[Hull City A.F.C.|Hull City]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bold.dk/nyt/Medie-EfBer-varm-i-England-og-Skotland|title=Medie: EfB'er varm i England og Skotland|last=bold.dk|website=bold.dk|date=8 August 2013 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bold.dk/nyt/Braithwaite-kender-til-fransk-interesse|title=Braithwaite kender til fransk interesse|last=bold.dk|website=bold.dk|date=13 August 2013 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tribalfootball.com/articles/celtic-and-hull-city-keen-esbjerg-s-braithwaite-3973700|title=Celtic and Hull City keen on Esbjerg's Braithwaite|last=Slevison|first=Andrew|date=9 August 2013|publisher=Tribal Football}}</ref>

===Toulouse===
On 14 August 2013, while away on national duty for a friendly international against [[Poland national football team|Poland]], Danish [[Public broadcasting|public broadcaster]] ''[[DR (broadcaster)|DR]]'' reported that Braithwaite had been sold to French club [[Toulouse FC|Toulouse]] for an estimated [[Danish krone|DKK]] 15 million (roughly €2 million).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dr.dk/Sporten/Fodbold/Superliga/2013/08/14/141000.htm|title=Martin Braithwaite til Toulouse|date=14 August 2013 |publisher=DR}}</ref>

Braithwaite scored a career-best 11 league goals in both 2015–16 and 2016–17. His tally for the latter campaign included two goals in a 4–1 home win over rivals [[FC Girondins de Bordeaux|Bordeaux]] in the ''[[Derby de la Garonne]]'' on 20 August 2016, and another two on 14 October in a 3–1 win over eventual champions [[AS Monaco FC|Monaco]], also at the [[Stadium de Toulouse]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Ligue 1: Toulouse, puissance quatre contre Bordeaux|trans-title=Ligue 1: Toulouse, to the power of four against Bordeaux|url=https://www.lexpress.fr/actualites/1/sport/ligue-1-toulouse-puissance-quatre-contre-bordeaux_1823086.html |access-date=13 August 2021 |work=L'Express |date=21 August 2016 |language=French}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Stival |first1=Nicolas |title=TFC: Maladroit à Caen, Martin Braithwaite s'est vengé en coulant Monaco|trans-title=TFC: Clumsy against Caen, Martin Braithwaite made up for himself by sinking Monaco|url=https://www.20minutes.fr/toulouse/1942987-20161014-tfc-maladroit-caen-martin-braithwaite-venge-coulant-monaco |access-date=13 August 2021 |work=20 minutes |date=14 October 2016 |language=French}}</ref>

===Middlesbrough===
On 13 July 2017, Braithwaite signed a four-year contract with [[EFL Championship]] club [[Middlesbrough F.C.|Middlesbrough]] for an undisclosed fee, reported to be around £9 million.<ref>{{cite news|title=Martin Braithwaite: Middlesbrough sign Denmark forward from Toulouse|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/40595549|access-date=13 July 2017|work=BBC Sport|date=13 July 2017}}</ref> Under the initial management of [[Garry Monk]],<ref>{{cite news|url=http://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-soccer-england-mid-monk-idUKKBN1902BV|title=Middlesbrough appoint Monk as new manager|publisher=Reuters|date=9 June 2017|access-date=21 July 2017}}</ref> he became the club's third signing of the season, after [[Jonny Howson]] and [[Cyrus Christie]].<ref name="Cyrus Signs">{{cite news|title=Cyrus Christie Set For Boro Medical|url=http://www.mfc.co.uk/news/cyrus-christie-set-for-boro-medical|access-date=21 July 2017|publisher=Middlesbrough Official Site|date=7 July 2017}}</ref><ref name="Howson Signs">{{cite news|title=Boro Set To Swoop For Jonny Howson|url=http://www.mfc.co.uk/news/boro-set-to-swoop-for-jonny-howson|access-date=21 July 2017|publisher=Middlesbrough Official Site|date=7 July 2017}}</ref> Braithwaite made his league debut for the club on 5 August 2017 at the [[Molineux Stadium]], where Middlesbrough were defeated 1–0 by [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40760787|title=Wolverhampton Wanderers 1-0 Middlesbrough|date=5 August 2017|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> He scored his first goal for the club on 30 September 2017, in a 2–2 draw against [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]] at the [[Riverside Stadium]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41369647|title=Middlesbrough 2-2 Brentford|date=30 September 2017|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref>

Braithwaite was loaned out to his former club Toulouse's rivals Bordeaux on 31 January 2018 for the rest of the season.<ref>[https://www.bold.dk/fodbold/nyheder/officielt-braithwaite-til-bordeaux/ Officielt: Braithwaite til Bordeaux]‚ bold.dk, 31 January 2018</ref> He scored four goals during his time there, including two in a 4–2 home derby win over his previous employer on 12 May.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Coignac |first1=Thomas |title=Ligue 1 : Bordeaux punit Toulouse et s'offre une finale pour l'Europe (4-2)|trans-title=Ligue 1: Bordeaux punish Toulouse and offer themselves a final for Europe (4-2)|url=https://www.francebleu.fr/sports/football/ligue-1-bordeaux-toulouse-1526148506 |access-date=13 August 2021 |publisher=France Bleu |date=12 May 2018 |language=French}}</ref> He returned to Middlesbrough for the 2018–19 season, but quickly expressed his desire to leave the club and go to Spain to play instead. This was much to the dismay of ''Boro'' [[Manager (association football)|manager]], [[Tony Pulis]].<ref>[https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/45354282/ Martin Braithwaite: Middlesbrough striker wants to leave club for Spain], bbc.co.uk, 30 August 2019</ref> After his unsuccessful attempt to leave the club in the 2018 summer transfer window, Braithwaite played the first half of the season for Middlesbrough, scoring three goals in 18 appearances.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}}

===Leganés===
Braithwaite joined [[La Liga]] side [[CD Leganés]] at the beginning of the 2019 January transfer window on a loan deal until the end of season.<ref>[https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11680/11597666/middlesbroughs-martin-braithwaite-joins-leganes-on-loan/ Middlesbrough's Martin Braithwaite joins Leganes on loan] Sky Sports, 3 January 2019</ref> He made his league debut for the club on 12 January, in a 1–0 victory against [[SD Huesca]], coming on as a substitute for [[Guido Carrillo]] for the last 26 minutes, and scored his first goal four days later in a 1–0 [[2018–19 Copa del Rey|Copa del Rey]] home win against [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.eurosport.com/football/copa-del-rey/2018-2019/live-cd-leganes-real-madrid_mtc1060219/live.shtml |title=CD Leganés 1–0 Real Madrid |website=eurosport.com |date=16 January 2019 }}</ref>

Braithwaite scored his first league goal in a 1–3 loss at [[FC Barcelona]] on 20 January 2019, and finished [[2018–19 La Liga|the campaign]] with four goals in 19 league appearances. On 24 July, he agreed to a permanent four-year deal with the ''Pepineros'' for a rumoured fee of €5 million, hence becoming the second most expensive player in the club's history.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mfc.co.uk/news/braithwaite-joins-leganes|title=Braithwaite Joins Leganes|publisher=Middlesbrough FC|date=24 July 2019|access-date=25 July 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cdleganes.com/en/en/new/c-d-leganes-signs-the-goals-of-martin-braithwaite|title=C.D. Leganés signs the goals of Martin Braithwaite|publisher=CD Leganés|date=24 July 2019|access-date=25 July 2019}}{{Dead link|date=March 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

===Barcelona===
On 20 February 2020, Barcelona triggered Braithwaite's release clause of €18 million and signed him on a four-and-a-half-year contract. His release clause was set to €300 million.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fcbarcelona.com/en/football/first-team/news/1615162/fc-barcelona-sign-martin-braithwaite|title=FC Barcelona sign Martin Braithwaite|publisher=FC Barcelona|date=20 February 2020|access-date=20 February 2020}}</ref> Barcelona were granted an emergency exception to sign Braithwaite outside of a transfer window because of the long-term injury to [[Ousmane Dembélé]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Martin Braithwaite: Barcelona make emergency signing from Leganes|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/51570136|date=20 February 2020|access-date=20 February 2020|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]}}</ref> Two days after signing, Braithwaite made his debut in a home match against [[SD Eibar|Eibar]], coming on as a substitute in the 72nd minute. He assisted the fourth and fifth goals in the 5&ndash;0 win, both off deflections.<ref>{{cite news|title=Barcelona 5–0 Eibar: Lionel Messi scores four in thumping win|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/51598759|access-date=22 February 2020|website=BBC Sport|date=22 February 2020}}</ref> On 13 June 2020, Braithwaite scored his first goal for Barcelona following an assist by [[Lionel Messi]] in a 4–0 victory against [[RCD Mallorca]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/griezmann-should-be-worried-braithwaite-a-better-fit-for/19nky10q27sp8138vgmerhxwwm|title=Griezmann should be worried: Braithwaite a better fit for Barcelona attack right now|website=Goal|date=13 June 2020|access-date=14 June 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.squawka.com/en/mallorca-0-4-barcelona-five-things-learned-match-analysis-messi-braithwaite-griezmann-kubo/|title="He is the striker that Barcelona need" – Five things learned from the Blaugrana's 4–0 battering of Mallorca|website=Squawka|date=13 June 2020|access-date=14 June 2020}}</ref>

On 24 November 2020, Braithwaite scored his first two [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] goals in a 4–0 away win over [[FC Dynamo Kyiv|Dynamo Kyiv]], a match in which he also had an assist, in the [[2020–21 UEFA Champions League|2020–21 season]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/match/2029435--dynamo-kyiv-vs-barcelona/ |title=Dynamo Kyiv 0–4 Barcelona |website=UEFA |date=24 November 2020 }}</ref> On 2 December, he scored his third goal in the competition in the first half of the match against [[Ferencvárosi TC|Ferencváros]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Carmen Torres |first1=M. |title=A purple patch for Griezmann and Braithwaite |url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/barcelona/2020/12/03/5fc82dce268e3ea27a8b4657.html |access-date=19 February 2021 |work=MARCA in English |date=3 December 2020 |language=en}}</ref> On 3 March 2021, he scored the winning goal in a 3–0 win over [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]] after extra time to secure Barcelona's place in the [[2021 Copa del Rey Final]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.laliga.com/en-GB/match/temporada-2020-2021-copa-del-rey-fc-barcelona-sevilla-fc-7 |title=FC Barcelona 3–0 Sevilla FC |website=La Liga |date=3 March 2021 }}</ref>

On 15 August 2021, Braithwaite scored a brace and assisted teammate [[Sergi Roberto]]'s goal in Barcelona's opening-day match of the [[2021–22 La Liga|2021&ndash;22 season]]; a 4&ndash;2 home win against [[Real Sociedad]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Barcelona vs Real Sociedad live ticker in LaLiga|url=https://www.fotmob.com/livescores/3629093/liveticker/barcelona-vs-real-sociedad|access-date=17 August 2021|website=FotMob}}</ref> The following month, he was ruled out for three to four months with a knee cartilage injury against [[Getafe CF]]; fellow attackers Dembélé, [[Ansu Fati]] and [[Sergio Agüero]] were already injured, while Messi had left and [[Antoine Griezmann]] was out on loan.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Feldman |first1=Ben |title=Serious injury could rule Braithwaite out for four months |url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/barcelona/2021/09/12/613df03246163f3cb88b4592.html |access-date=2 November 2021 |work=Marca |date=12 September 2021}}</ref>

On 1 September 2022, Braithwaite terminated his contract with Barcelona effective immediately.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Agreement to terminate Martin Braithwaite's contract with FC Barcelona |url=https://www.fcbarcelona.com/en/football/first-team/news/2771838/agreement-to-terminate-martin-braithwaites-contract-with-fc-barcelona |access-date=1 September 2022 |website=www.fcbarcelona.com |language=en}}</ref>

=== Espanyol ===
On 1 September 2022, Braithwaite signed a three-year contract with [[RCD Espanyol|Espanyol]] as a free agent.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Braithwaite signs for Espanyol |url=https://www.rcdespanyol.com/en/new/martin-braithwaite-signs-for-rcd-espanyol/14507 |website=RCD Espanyol |date=1 September 2022 }}</ref> On 4 September, he scored a goal on his debut in a 1–0 away victory over [[Athletic Bilbao]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://apnews.com/article/sports-soccer-international-la-liga-spain-c48b68b587486b5b9372fd0f4bd16157 |title=Braithwaite comes through with winner in Espanyol debut |website=Associated Press |date=4 September 2022 }}</ref>

==International career==
Having already represented several Danish national youth teams, in the summer of 2012, Braithwaite was offered the chance to represent [[Guyana national football team|Guyana]], being eligible through his Guyanese father. He declined the offer<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bold.dk/nyt/Esbjerg-profil-takker-nej-til-landshold|title=Esbjerg-profil takker nej til landshold|last=bold.dk|website=bold.dk|date=18 July 2012 }}</ref> and went on to make his senior debut for the [[Denmark national football team|Danish national team]] in June 2013 in a friendly against [[Georgia national football team|Georgia]], starting the game and playing the first half. He remained in the squad for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup qualification|2014 World Cup qualification match]] against [[Armenia national football team|Armenia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bold.dk/nyt/EfB-duo-og-Stryger-i-Olsens-hat|title=EfB-duo og Stryger i Olsens hat|last=bold.dk|website=bold.dk|date=17 May 2013 }}</ref> Braithwaite scored his first international goal in his second match in a friendly in August 2013 against [[Poland national football team|Poland]].<ref>{{cite news |title=TV: Esbjerg-stjerne header Danmark foran|trans-title=TV: Esbjerg star heads Denmark ahead|url=https://sport.tv2.dk/fodbold/2013-08-14-tv-esbjerg-stjerne-header-danmark-foran |access-date=13 August 2021 |publisher=TV2 |date=14 August 2013 |language=Danish}}</ref>

In May 2018, he was named in Denmark’s final 23-man [[Denmark national football team|squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/amp/news/revealed-every-world-cup-2018-squad-23-man-preliminary-lists/oa0atsduflsv1nsf6oqk576rb|title=Revealed: Every World Cup 2018 squad - Final 23-man lists|last=Crawford|first=Stephen|date=4 June 2018|publisher=Goal.com}}</ref> On 16 October that year, he scored in a 2–0 friendly win over Austria, ending a five-year goal drought.<ref>{{cite news |title=Middlesbrough news: Young striker nets a hat-trick for England, Martin Braithwaite scores for Denmark plus Checkatrade Trophy exit |url=https://www.hartlepoolmail.co.uk/news/middlesbrough-news-young-striker-nets-hat-trick-england-martin-braithwaite-scores-denmark-plus-checkatrade-trophy-exit-388183 |access-date=13 August 2021 |work=Hartlepool Mail |date=17 October 2018}}</ref>

Braithwaite was part of the Danish squad that reached the semi-finals of [[UEFA Euro 2020]], held in 2021. He scored the last goal of their 4–0 last-16 win over [[Wales national football team|Wales]] in [[Amsterdam]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Denmark end Wales' Euro 2020 dreams as Dolberg double caps dominant win |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2021/jun/26/wales-denmark-euro-2020-match-report |access-date=13 August 2021 |work=The Observer |date=26 June 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Braithwaite has a [[Guyana|Guyanese]] father<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/40595549 |title=Martin Braithwaite: Middlesbrough sign Denmark forward from Toulouse|publisher=BBC|date=30 September 2017 |access-date=1 October 2017}}</ref> and his younger sister Mathilde (born 2002) plays football for [[KoldingQ]] and for Denmark's U-16 national women's football team.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/KoldingQ/posts/1811014428929708 |title=Debut til Mathilde Braithwaite på U16 Pigelandsholdet...|publisher=KoldingQ|date=30 September 2017 |access-date=1 October 2017}}</ref> His father Keith's birth surname was ''Brathwaite'', the more common spelling in the Caribbean, but it was changed by US authorities to ''Braithwaite'' when Keith arrived in the country.<ref name=sport/>

When Braithwaite was 5 years old he was diagnosed with [[Legg–Calvé–Perthes disease|Legg-Calve-Perthes disease]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Braithwaite: 'I broke my leg at 18. It was one of the best things that ever happened to me'|url=https://tribuna.com/en/news/fcbarcelona-2021-05-19-braithwaite-i-broke-my-leg-at-18-it-was-one-of-the-best-things-that-ever-happened-to-me/|access-date=1 July 2021|website=Tribuna.com}}</ref>

Braithwaite is the nephew and business partner of the Danish-born, American-based, real estate developer Gareth Roger James Smith.<ref name="FORBES">{{cite news | last = Pettigrove | first = Jason | title =New Barcelona Signing Martin Braithwaite And His Uncle Just Broke Ground On Smart Home Project In Philadelphia | newspaper = [[Forbes]] | date =20 February 2020 | url = https://www.forbes.com/sites/jasonpettigrove/2020/02/20/new-barcelona-signing-martin-braithwaite-breaks-ground-on-smart-home-project-in-philadelphia/#4a0bd447199d| access-date =12 March 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news | last = Mazariegos | first = Luis | title = Braithwaite could start El Clásico for Barcelona | publisher =barcablaugranes.com| date =1 March 2020 | url =https://www.barcablaugranes.com/2020/3/1/21157827/braithwaite-could-start-el-clasico-for-barcelona | access-date =12 March 2020}}</ref> They co-own real estate in the New York Market and are building a high-rise in [[Jersey City, New Jersey]].<ref name="Black Enterprise">{{cite news | title = World Cup Star Reveals His Favorite Investment Strategy | publisher = blackenterprise.com| date = 18 December 2018 | url =https://www.blackenterprise.com/qa-world-cup-star-reveals-his-favorite-investment-strategy/| access-date =24 March 2020}}</ref><ref name="WealthLab">{{cite news | title =EXCLUSIVE: World Cup Star Reveals His No. 1 Investment Strategy (And It's Brilliant) | publisher =wealthlab.co| date =20 November 2019 | url =https://wealthlab.co/wealthlab-exclusive-world-cup-star-reveals-his-no-1-investment-strategy-and-its-brilliant| access-date =24 March 2020}}</ref>

Braithwaite is married to French entrepreneur, journalist and television personality Anne-Laure Louis Braithwaite. They have two sons together. He is also the stepfather of his wife's two children from a previous relationship.<ref name="SE og Hør">{{cite news | last = Erik Lagerstorf | first = Nicklas | title = Martin Braithwaite has become a father again| newspaper = [[Se og Hør]] | publisher =seoghoer.dk| date =21 April 2020| url = https://www.seoghoer.dk/kendte/martin-braithwaite-er-blevet-far-igen | access-date =3 March 2021}}</ref>
