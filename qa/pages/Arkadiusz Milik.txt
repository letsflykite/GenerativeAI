'''Arkadiusz Krystian Milik''' ({{IPA-pl|arˈkadjuʂ ˈmilik|pron|Pl-Arkadiusz_Milik.ogg}}; born 28 February 1994) is a Polish professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[striker (association football)|striker]] for [[Serie A]] club [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]], and the [[Poland national football team|Poland national team]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[Serie A]]|url=http://www.legaseriea.it/en/serie-a/teams/napoli/team|title=Napoli Serie A squad|access-date=13 October 2020}}</ref>

Milik began his career as a footballer in the [[Rozwój Katowice]] youth squad. In 2011, he signed a one-year contract with [[Górnik Zabrze]], and in 2012 he transferred to [[Bayer 04 Leverkusen|Bayer Leverkusen]] and later played on loan for [[FC Augsburg]] and [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]] respectively. In [[2015–16 AFC Ajax season|2015–16]], he was purchased by Ajax on a four-year deal for a reported [[Euro|€]]2.8&nbsp;million fee. While playing for Ajax, Milik became one of the top goalscorers in [[Eredivisie]]. On 1 August 2016, he joined Italian club [[S.S.C. Napoli|Napoli]] for €35&nbsp;million, effectively replacing [[Gonzalo Higuaín]]. On 21 January 2021, he joined French club Marseille on loan from Napoli.

At international level, Milik participated at [[UEFA Euro 2016]] with Poland and reached the quarter-final stage of the tournament. He also took part at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]], where his team was eliminated in the group stage, and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]], where his team advanced to the round of 16.

==Early life==
Sławomir "Moki" Mogilan, a football coach, recognized Milik's footballing talent and helped develop his abilities, fostering a greater professionalism in his outlook. When he was 16 years old, reports of the talented youngster had reached England and he was invited to trial with [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] and [[Reading F.C.|Reading]], but ultimately decided to focus on his football in Poland.<ref>{{cite web|title=Piłkarze Rozwoju Katowice na testach w Tottenhamie|date=19 January 2011 |trans-title=Rozwój Katowice players on trial at Tottenham|url=http://warszawa.naszemiasto.pl/artykul/pilkarze-rozwoju-katowice-na-testach-w-tottenhamie,3221265,art,t,id,tm.html|publisher=warszawa.naszemiasto.pl|language=pl}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Rozwój Katowice===
Milik began his career as a youth player for [[Rozwój Katowice]] and played in the reserve team during the 2009–10 season.<ref name=90mkariera/> On 23 October 2010, the 16-year-old made his [[III liga (Poland)|III liga]] debut when he scored two goals in a 4–0 victory over KS Krasiejów.<ref name=90min697125/> In November, he went on trial at [[Górnik Zabrze]], where he scored one goal in two appearances for the club's [[Młoda Ekstraklasa]] team.<ref name=sez1011/> In winter, he and his teammate Wojciech Król were on trial with Reading, Tottenham Hotspur and [[Legia Warszawa]].<ref name=trials/><ref name=trials2/> Milik ended the season with ten appearances and four goals for Rozwój Katowice.<ref name=sez1011/>

===Górnik Zabrze===
Despite offers from other clubs, on 1 July 2011 Milik signed a one-year contract with Górnik Zabrze for a fee of 500,000 [[Polish zloty|zloty]]s.<ref name=gz110701/><ref name=ps1/> Milik made his [[Ekstraklasa]] debut on 31 July in a 1–1 draw with [[Śląsk Wrocław]];<ref name=sez1112/> in the 53rd minute, he was [[Substitute (association football)|substituted]] by [[Daniel Gołębiewski]].<ref name=90m783466/>

===Bayer Leverkusen===
On 17 December 2012, German [[Bundesliga]] side [[Bayer 04 Leverkusen|Bayer Leverkusen]] confirmed they had signed Milik on a contract until summer 2018,<ref name=uefa121217/> for a reported transfer fee of €2.6&nbsp;million.<ref>{{cite web|title=Milik geht zu Ajax Amsterdam|url=http://www.reviersport.de/273407---leverkusen-leiht-milik-ajax-amsterdam-aus.html|publisher=RevierSport|access-date=25 July 2014|language=de|date=16 May 2014}}</ref> Milik failed to impress at Leverkusen though, making only 6 appearances before being demoted to the Leverkusen reserve team.

====Loan to FC Augsburg====
On 30 August 2013, [[FC Augsburg]] signed Milik on a one-year loan deal.<ref>{{cite web|title=FCA leiht Arkadiusz Milik aus|url=http://www.fcaugsburg.de/cms/website.php?id=/index/news/allenews/data11739.htm|publisher=[[FC Augsburg]]|access-date=1 October 2013|language=de|date=30 August 2013}}</ref> The loan fee was reported to be €150,000, and composed of a base amount that decreased the more games Milik played for Augsburg and increased if Augsburg maintained its Bundesliga status.<ref>{{cite web|title=Milik: Erst wird er billiger - dann teurer?|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/startseite/592939/artikel_milik_erst-wird-er-billiger---dann-teurer.html|publisher=[[Kicker (sports magazine)|kicker]]|access-date=25 July 2014|language=de|date=1 October 2013}}</ref> During his spell in Augsburg, he was capped in 18 matches, but he only scored 2 goals. However, he scored an equalizer in the 88th minute in a 2–2 home draw against [[Borussia Mönchengladbach]], helping Augsburg earn a vital point.<ref>{{cite web|title=Was Kruse kann, kann Hahn auch|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/spieltag/1-bundesliga/2013-14/7/1894933/spielanalyse_fc-augsburg-91_borussia-mgladbach-15.html|publisher=[[Kicker (sports magazine)|kicker]]|access-date=25 July 2014|language=de|date=27 September 2013}}</ref>

===Ajax===
On 15 May 2014, it was announced that Milik would spend the [[2014–15 AFC Ajax season|2014–15 season]] on loan at reigning Dutch champions [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]], with a purchase option of €2.8&nbsp;million.<ref>{{cite web|title=Milik on loan at Ajax Amsterdam|url=http://www.bayer04.de/b04-eng/en/_site_index.aspx|publisher=[[Bayer 04 Leverkusen|Bayer Leverkusen]]|access-date=25 July 2014|date=15 May 2014}}</ref>

====2014–15 season (loan)====
On 24 September 2014, Milik played in the first official [[Amsterdam derby]] to be contested since 1983 when Ajax took on [[JOS Watergraafsmeer]] in the [[KNVB Cup]]. Milik earned the man of the match award after scoring six out of the club's nine goals and assisting a further two in the [[Olympic Stadium (Amsterdam)|Olympic Stadium]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Ajax door 6 treffers Milik fluitend langs JOS|url=http://www.gelderlander.nl/algemeen/sport/ajax-door-6-treffers-milik-fluitend-langs-jos-1.4548801|publisher=[[De Gelderlander]]|access-date=24 September 2014|language=nl|date=24 September 2014}}</ref> On 10 December 2014, he scored his first [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] goal in a 4–0 victory over [[APOEL FC|APOEL]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://eurosport.tvn24.pl/pilka-nozna,105/ajax-apoel-4-0-gol-arkadiusza-milika,497571.html |title=Milik bohaterem Ajaksu. Polak z historycznym trafieniem |website=tvn24.pl |language=pl |date=10 December 2014 }}</ref> On 1 April 2015, Ajax reached an agreement with Bayer Leverkusen to purchase Milik outright on a four-year deal for a reported €2.8&nbsp;million fee.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ajax contracteert Arek Milik|url=http://www.ajax.nl/Ajax-Nieuws/Ajax-Nieuws/Ajax-nieuws-artikel/201155/Ajax-contracteert-Arek-Milik.htm|publisher=AFC Ajax|access-date=1 April 2015|date=1 April 2015}}</ref>

====2015–16 season====
[[File:FC Salzburg gegen SSC Napoli (UEFA Euroleague Achtelfinalrückspiel 14. März 2019) 21.jpg|170px|thumb|Arkadiusz Milik, 2019]]
On 26 June 2015, Ajax announced Milik would replace departing striker [[Kolbeinn Sigþórsson]] as the club's number 9 for the [[2015–16 AFC Ajax season|2015–16 season]], switching from kit number 19. Milik scored his first goal that season in a [[2015–16 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round|UEFA Champions League qualifier]] against [[SK Rapid Wien|Rapid Wien]], making the scoreline 1–2. The game was eventually lost 2–3 and the two-legged tie 4–5 on aggregate to Rapid, relegating Ajax to the [[2015–16 UEFA Europa League qualifying phase and play-off round|UEFA Europa League play-off]] against Czech side [[FK Jablonec|Jablonec]].

Milik scored his first goal of the [[2015–16 Eredivisie]] season against [[Willem II (football club)|Willem II]] after newly appointed captain [[Davy Klaassen]] provided him with a cross, which Milik headed-in. Ajax went on to win the game 3–0 after a brace from [[Anwar El Ghazi]]. In the first leg of the Europa League play-off qualifier against Jablonec, Milik scored a penalty after Klaassen was fouled in the box; the goal was the winner in a 1–0 match. During the beginning of the season, Milik also scored goals against Willem II, [[N.E.C. (football club)|NEC]] and [[S.B.V. Excelsior|Excelsior]]. Nearly a month later, on 31 October, he began a scoring streak again, this time scoring two against [[Roda JC Kerkrade|Roda JC]] in a 6–0 rout. Milik also scored league goals against [[SC Cambuur|Cambuur]] on 11 November and [[SC Heerenveen|Heerenveen]] on 5 December, the latter in which he also missed a penalty. On 26 November, Milik scored the 1–1 equalizer against [[Celtic F.C.|Celtic]] in the [[2015–16 UEFA Europa League group stage|Europa League group stage]], where Ajax went on to win 2–1 away in [[Glasgow]]. On 20 December, Ajax's final match before the winter break, he also scored the opening goal against [[De Graafschap]] in an eventual 2–1 home victory at the [[Amsterdam Arena]].

After the winter break, Milik was out of form and got a lot of criticism, having gone scoreless in Ajax's first three Eredivisie matches. He broke his poor run of form by scoring against Roda JC on 31 January 2016 in a 2–2 away draw. On 21 February, against Excelsior, he then scored a brace, while in Ajax's next home game, on 28 February, he scored another two goals against [[AZ Alkmaar|AZ]]. One week later, on 6 March, Milik scored against Willem II in an eventual 0–4 away win in [[Tilburg]].

===Napoli===
[[File:FC Salzburg versus SSC Napoli (UEFA Euroleague Achtelfinalrückspiel 14. März 2019) 36.jpg|thumb|left|Milik (second from right) playing for Napoli against [[FC Salzburg]] in 2019]]
Milik signed with [[S.S.C. Napoli|Napoli]] on 1 August 2016;<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.com/story/2921554/napoli-confirm-arkadiusz-milik-signing-from-ajax-to-replace-gonzalo-higuain|title=Napoli confirm Arkadiusz Milik signing from Ajax to replace Gonzalo Higuain|publisher=ESPN FC|author1=Ben Gladwell|date=1 August 2016|access-date=2 August 2016}}</ref> his €35&nbsp;million transfer was officially confirmed by the club the following afternoon.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.football-italia.net/88680/official-napoli-announce-milik|title=Official: Napoli announce Milik|publisher=Football Italia|date=2 August 2016|access-date=2 August 2016}}</ref> He made his club debut on 27 August, scoring his first two goals for Napoli in a 4–2 home win over [[A.C. Milan|Milan]] in [[2016–17 Serie A|Serie A]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.football-italia.net/90505/milik-dream-napoli-debut|title=Milik: 'Dream Napoli debut'|publisher=Football Italia|date=27 August 2016|access-date=29 August 2016}}</ref> He scored braces again against [[Dynamo Kyiv]] and [[Bologna F.C. 1909|Bologna]] in September,<ref name="Arkadiusz Milik's Match History">{{cite web|url=https://www.whoscored.com/Players/104257/Fixtures/Arkadiusz-Milik|title=Arkadiusz Milik's Match History}}</ref> but on 8 October, Milik tore his anterior cruciate ligament in a Polish international fixture against Denmark and was expected to be out for the rest of the season.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.squawka.com/news/napoli-confirm-striker-arkadiusz-milik-has-ruptured-his-knee-ligaments/793946#4gKlrKxSxl1Ji31R.97|title=Napoli confirm striker Arkadiusz Milik has ruptured his knee ligaments}}</ref> However, as of 12 February 2017, he resumed playing and was fielded again on 15 February in the [[2016–17 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] match against [[Real Madrid C.F.|Real Madrid]].<ref name="Arkadiusz Milik's Match History"/>

In September, Milik suffered his second anterior cruciate ligament injury in under 12 months – in his other knee, on this occasion – and was expected to be ruled out for at least four months; in January 2018, he underwent medical tests and was cleared to resume training.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2018/01/13/napolis-arkadiusz-milik-and-faouzi-ghoulam-undergo-medical-tests-monday|title=Napoli's Arkadiusz Milik and Faouzi Ghoulam to Undergo Medical Tests on Monday|publisher=www.si.com|date=13 January 2018|access-date=16 January 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/115530/ghoulam-and-milik-prepare-comeback|title=Ghoulam and Milik prepare comeback|publisher=Football Italia|date=13 January 2018|access-date=16 January 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/115631/official-ghoulam-milik-back-napoli|title=Official: Ghoulam, Milik back for Napoli|publisher=Football Italia|date=15 January 2018|access-date=16 January 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/115628/napoli-recover-milik-ghoulam|title=Napoli recover Milik, Ghoulam|publisher=Football Italia|date=15 January 2018|access-date=16 January 2018}}</ref>

In the [[2020 Coppa Italia Final]] on 17 June, following a 0–0 draw after regulation time, Milik scored the winning spot kick in a 4–2 penalty shoot-out victory against [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]].<ref name="2020=CI">{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/154624/coppa-napoli-beat-juventus-penalties|title=Coppa: Napoli beat Juventus on penalties|publisher=Football Italia|date=17 June 2020}}</ref>

In the 2020–21 season, after having decided not to extend his contract and at the same time rejected a number of transfer options, Milik was excluded from the official Serie A and UEFA Europa League squads.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://footballinside.net/2020/10/08/napoli-decide-to-punish-milik-for-rejecting-deadline-day-transfers/|title=UPDATE: MILIK AXED FROM NAPOLI SERIE A AND EUROPA LEAGUE SQUADS|publisher=Football Inside|date=8 October 2020}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:Milik vs Ireland 2013.jpg|thumb|upright|Milik playing for Poland in 2013.]]
On 13 April 2011, Milik scored a goal for [[Poland national under-17 football team|Poland under-17 side]] in a 4–1 victory over [[English Schools' Football Association|England Schools U18]]. On 15 July 2011, he was called up to the [[Poland national under-19 football team|under-19 team]].<ref name=U19callup/>

On 12 October 2012, Milik made his debut for the [[Poland national football team|Polish senior team]] against [[South Africa national football team|South Africa]]. On 14 December, he scored his first goal for the national side in the 4–1 friendly victory over [[Macedonia national football team|Macedonia]].

Milik scored nine times for [[Poland national under-21 football team|Poland under-21s]] during [[2015 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification|qualification]] for the [[2015 UEFA European Under-21 Championship]], including back-to-back hat-tricks against [[Malta national under-21 football team|Malta]] and [[Greece national under-21 football team|Greece]] in November 2013.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.uefa.com/under21/season=2015/matches/round=2000412/match=2011523/postmatch/report/|title=Poland 3-1 Greece|access-date=14 June 2015|work=UEFA}}</ref>

===UEFA Euro 2016===
On 11 October 2014, Milik scored the opening goal in a 2–0 win over world champions [[Germany national football team|Germany]] in a [[UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying|2016 UEFA Euro qualifying]] match. This was Poland's first win over Germany, who had not lost in 19 competitive games and 33 previous qualifiers.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Lamont|first1=Alasdair|title=Poland 2–0 Germany|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/29583599|access-date=13 October 2014|publisher=BBC Sport|date=11 October 2014}}</ref> Milik formed a successful strike partnership with [[Robert Lewandowski]] during qualification for the European Championships, with additional goals coming in a 2–2 draw with [[Scotland national football team|Scotland]], a 4–0 win away to [[Georgia national football team|Georgia]] and another 4–0 defeat of the same opposition at the [[National Stadium, Warsaw|National Stadium]] in [[Warsaw]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nzz.ch/sport/drei-tore-von-lewandowski-bei-polens-40-1.18561613|title=Drei Tore von Lewandowski bei Polens 4:0|date=13 June 2015|work=Neue Zürcher Zeitung}}</ref>

In Euro 2016, Milik scored for Poland in their opening game, beating [[Northern Ireland national football team|Northern Ireland]] 1–0.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/euro2016/poland-1-northern-ireland-0-arkadiusz-milik-seals-euro-2016-group-c-opener-a3269711.html|title=Arkadiusz Milik earns Poland Euro 2016 win vs limited Northern Ireland|date=12 June 2016|website=standard.co.uk}}</ref> He also netted his attempt in his nation's penalty shootout exit to eventual champions [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]] in the quarter-finals.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/36603665|title=Poland 1–1 Portugal|publisher=BBC Sport|date=30 June 2016|access-date=30 June 2016}}</ref>

===2018 FIFA World Cup===
Milik appeared in the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/world-cup/poland-world-cup-squad-profile-russia-2018-fixtures-group-team-news-schedule-a8393021.html | title = Poland World Cup squad guide: Full fixtures, group, ones to watch, odds | publisher = independent.co.uk | access-date = 8 July 2018| date = 11 June 2018 }}</ref> His nation was defeated 2–1 in their opening match against [[Senegal national football team|Senegal]], and then lost 3–0 in their second group match against [[Colombia national football team|Colombia]]. Poland managed a 1–0 win over [[Japan national football team|Japan]] in their final group match, but were eliminated in the first round.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/teams/team=43962/index.html | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20071129220613/http://www.fifa.com/worldcup/teams/team=43962/index.html | url-status = dead | archive-date = 29 November 2007 | title = Poland | publisher = fifa.com | access-date = 8 July 2018}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Usually deployed as an out-and-out striker, Milik is a versatile and well-rounded left-footed [[Forward (association football)|forward]] who is also capable of playing off another forward as a [[Forward (association football)#Second striker|second striker]] or as a [[Forward (association football)#Winger|winger]], due to his ability to both score and create goals. He possesses pace and good movement off the ball, as well as a powerful and accurate shot, which make him an excellent finisher and goalscorer; he is also physically strong, agile, and a good header of the ball, which allows him to excel in the air.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.spazionapoli.it/2016/07/27/milik-napoli-higuain/|title=Milik per il dopo Higuain: pro e contro dell'attaccante polacco|publisher=www.spazionapoli.it|language=it|date=27 July 2016|access-date=1 September 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://it.eurosport.com/calcio/serie-a/2015-2016/napoli-il-dopo-higuain-e-arkadiusz-milik-ma-e-davvero-piu-forte-di-gabbiadini_sto5700592/story.shtml|title=Napoli, il dopo Higuain è Arkadiusz Milik: ma è davvero più forte di Gabbiadini?|publisher=Eurosport.com|language=it|date=28 July 2016|access-date=1 September 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/it/news/3785/generazione-di-fenomeni/2016/04/11/22114302/profilo-arkadiusz-milik-lerede-di-lewandowski-corteggiato-da|title=Profilo - Arkadiusz Milik, l'erede di Lewandowski corteggiato da Inter e Lazio|publisher=Goal.com|language=it|date=7 April 2016|access-date=1 September 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.tuttonapoli.net/calciomercato/rapido-e-tecnico-o-forte-fisicamente-alla-ricerca-del-partner-per-il-duttile-milik-ci-sono-gia-tre-ipotesi-354827 |title=Rapido e tecnico o forte fisicamente? ==Personal life==
In 2013, Milik began dating Polish model Jessica Ziółek. 

Milik features on the cover of the Polish version of ''[[FIFA 16]]'' alongside global cover star [[Lionel Messi]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Jessica Ziolek|url=http://fashionlovesbundesliga.blogspot.com/2013/11/jessica-ziolek.html|access-date=25 August 2015|date=13 November 2013}}</ref>

Milik is a devoted fan of [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]]. Milik says he "dreams of playing for Manchester United" but insists he's "far from it".<ref>{{cite web |title=Arkadiusz Milik: I dream of playing for Manchester United, but I'm far from it |date=29 December 2012 |access-date=31 July 2015 |language=en | url=http://eurosport.onet.pl/pilka-nozna/liga-mistrzow/arkadiusz-milik-marze-o-grze-w-manchesterze-united-ale-daleko-mi-do-tego/rrh3e}}</ref>

Milik's childhood football hero is [[Cristiano Ronaldo]]; in an interview during Euro 2016, he said he followed Ronaldo closely and had been inspired by the Portuguese striker.<ref name=uefa2016interview/>
