'''Diogo Meireles da Costa''' {{post-nominals|post-noms=[[Order of Merit (Portugal)|ComM]]}} ({{IPA-pt|diˈoɣuˈkɔʃtɐ}}; born 19 September 1999) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Primeira Liga]] club [[FC Porto|Porto]] and the [[Portugal national football team|Portugal national team]].

Coming through [[FC Porto|Porto]]'s youth system, Costa won the [[UEFA Youth League]] in [[2018–19 UEFA Youth League|2019]]. He was promoted from the [[FC Porto B|reserve side]] to the first-team in 2019, winning a [[Double (association football)|domestic double]] of the [[Primeira Liga]] and the [[Taça de Portugal]] in his [[2019–20 FC Porto season|first season]]. He broke into the starting line-up in 2021, aged 22, helping Porto to a second domestic double and being named in the Primeira Liga Team of the Year.

Born in Switzerland to parents from [[Portugal]], Costa represented Portugal at various [[Portugal national youth football team|youth levels]], being part of [[Portugal national under-17 football team|the under-17 team]] that won the [[UEFA European Under-17 Championship|2016 European Championship]], the [[Portugal national under-19 football team|under-19 team]] that won the [[2018 UEFA European Under-19 Championship|2018 European Championship]] and the [[Portugal national under-21 football team|under-21 team]] that finished as runners-up at the [[2021 UEFA European Under-21 Championship|2021 European Championship]]. He made his senior international debut in 2021, representing Portugal at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Club career==
===Early career===
Born in [[Rothrist]], [[Canton of Aargau]] to [[Portuguese people|Portuguese parents]], Costa relocated to [[Santo Tirso]] at the age of 7.<ref name=Newcomer>{{cite web|url=https://www.fcporto.pt/pt/noticias/20180929-pt-atleta-revelacao-do-ano-diogo-costa|title=Atleta revelação do ano: Diogo Costa|trans-title=Newcomer athlete of the year: Diogo Costa|publisher=FC Porto|language=Portuguese|date=29 September 2018|accessdate=31 December 2018}}</ref> In his childhood, he played football with his cousin Vitor, with their idol being [[FC Porto]] legend [[Vítor Baía]], whom they looked to emulate. He started playing football at a local academy AMCH Ringe, and later began going through some training sessions with [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]], being integrated into one of their feeder club's Póvoa de Lanhoso, where he stayed for two years, standing out alongside future teammate [[Vitinha (footballer, born February 2000)|Vitinha]], before joining FC Porto's academy in 2011, following the consent of his parents.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://maisfutebol.iol.pt/reportagem/liga/diogo-costa-o-bom-gigante-que-o-fc-porto-viu-numa-casa-do-benfica|title=Diogo Costa: o bom gigante que o FC Porto viu numa Casa do Benfica|trans-title=Diogo Costa: the good giant FC Porto saw in a Benfica House|publisher=Mais Futebol|first=Pedro Jorge|last=Da Cunha|language=Portuguese|date=15 November 2019|accessdate=26 July 2020}}</ref>

===Porto===
====2017–2021: Youth career and reserves====
Costa made his senior debut with [[FC Porto B|the reserve team]] on 6 August 2017, in a 1–2 home loss against [[Gil Vicente F.C.|Gil Vicente]] for the [[LigaPro]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.dn.pt/lusa/interior/comentario-gil-vicente-estreou-se-com-vitoria-em-casa-do-fc-porto-b-8688669.html|title=COMENTÁRIO: Gil Vicente estreou-se com vitória em casa do FC Porto B|trans-title=COMMENT: Gil Vicente had winning debut at FC Porto B|newspaper=[[Diário de Notícias]]|language=Portuguese|date=6 August 2017|accessdate=31 December 2018}}</ref> He finished [[2017–18 LigaPro|the season]] with a further 31 appearances, helping to a seventh-place finish, and on 15 May he renewed his contract until June 2022.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://desporto.sapo.pt/futebol/primeira-liga/artigos/diogo-costa-prolonga-contrato-com-o-fc-porto-ate-202122|title=Diogo Costa prolonga contrato com o FC Porto até 2021/22|trans-title=Diogo Costa extends contract with FC Porto until 2021/22|publisher=SAPO|language=Portuguese|date=15 May 2018|accessdate=31 December 2018}}</ref> In September, he was named the club's Newcomer of the Year;<ref name=Newcomer/> late in the same year, Spaniard [[Iker Casillas]] – who started for the first team – heaped praise upon him, regarding him as his "successor".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.ojogo.pt/futebol/1a-liga/porto/noticias/interior/casillas-elege-sucessor-vai-ser-um-craque-10316558.html|title=Casillas elege sucessor na baliza do FC Porto: "Vem aí um craque"|trans-title=Casillas chooses successor in goal of FC Porto: "A star is coming"|newspaper=[[O Jogo]]|first=Carlos|last=Gouveia|language=Portuguese|date=13 December 2018|accessdate=31 December 2018}}</ref>

Costa won the [[2018–19 UEFA Youth League]] with Porto, defeating [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] 3–1 in the final in [[Nyon]], Switzerland on 29 April.<ref name=Youth>{{cite news|url=https://www.dn.pt/desportos/interior/fc-porto-e-campeao-europeu-sub-19-10845534.html|title=FC Porto é campeão Europeu Sub-19|trans-title=FC Porto are Under-19 European champions|newspaper=Diário de Notícias|last=Almeida|first=Isaura|language=Portuguese|date=29 April 2019|accessdate=1 May 2019}}</ref> Days later, after Casillas suffered a heart attack, [[Vaná]] replaced him as starting goalkeeper and Costa was called up to the bench for the final three games of [[2018–19 Primeira Liga|the season]], starting with a 4–0 win at [[C.D. At the start of the 2020–21 season, Costa inherited Porto's 99 shirt, made famous by goalkeeper and club legend [[Vítor Baía]]. He remained Marchesín's backup, only appearing in one league game, and made his [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] debut on 9 December 2020; he kept a clean sheet in a 2–0 [[2020–21 UEFA Champions League group stage|group stage]] win at [[Olympiakos F.C.|Olympiakos]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-bwin/fc-porto/detalhe/diogo-costa-e-o-99-nas-costas-e-uma-grande-responsabilidade|title=Diogo Costa e o '99' nas costas: "É uma grande responsabilidade»|trans-title=Diogo Costa and the '99' on his back: «It's a big responsibility"|newspaper=[[Record (newspaper)|Record]]|language=Portuguese|date=9 December 2020|accessdate=10 November 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/match/2029459--olympiacos-vs-porto/|title=[Match report] Olympiakos vs Porto - UEFA Champions League - 2020–21|publisher=[[UEFA]]|date=9 December 2020|accessdate=10 November 2021}}</ref>

==International career==
=== 2014–2021: Youth career ===
Costa started all of [[Portugal national under-17 football team|Portugal]]'s matches in the [[2016 UEFA European Under-17 Championship]]. In the final against [[Spain national under-17 football team|Spain]], he saved [[Manu Morlanes]]' spot kick in a 5–4 penalty shoot-out victory after a 1–1 draw, helping his team win the tournament for the sixth time.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.uefa.com/under17/season=2016/matches/round=2000595/match=2019912/index.html |title=Portugal win second U17 EURO title on penalties |publisher=UEFA |date=21 May 2016 |access-date=7 June 2017}}</ref> With the [[Portugal national under-19 football team|under-19s]], he participated in the [[2017 UEFA European Under-19 Championship|2017 European Championship]], playing four out of five matches as they lost in the final to [[England national under-19 football team|England]].<ref name="U19 runner-up">{{cite web|title=European Under-19 Championship: England beat Portugal in final|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40619862|publisher=BBC Sport|date=15 July 2017|access-date=17 March 2019}}</ref> He represented [[Portugal national under-20 football team|the nation]] at the [[2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/news/y=2017/m=5/news=dalot-targeting-back-to-back-golden-summers-2886251.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170523084012/http://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/news/y=2017/m=5/news=dalot-targeting-back-to-back-golden-summers-2886251.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=23 May 2017 |title=Dalot targeting back-to-back golden summers |publisher=FIFA |date=23 May 2017 |access-date=7 June 2017}}</ref> starting in all the matches in a quarter-final exit.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/matches/round=275956/match=300367044/match-report.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170604142900/http://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/matches/round=275956/match=300367044/match-report.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=4 June 2017 |title=Uruguay progress, Portugal pay the penalty |publisher=FIFA |date=4 June 2017 |access-date=7 June 2017}}</ref>

In [[2018 UEFA European Under-19 Championship|July 2018]], Costa played four matches out of five at the [[UEFA European Under-19 Championship]] in Finland, helping [[Portugal national under-19 football team|Portugal]] win the tournament for the first time,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://maisfutebol.iol.pt/internacional/europeu-sub-19/conheca-os-20-mais-um-campeoes-europeus-de-sub-19|title=Conheça os 20 (mais um) campeões europeus de sub-19|trans-title=Meet the 21 (plus one) under-19 European champions|publisher=Mais Futebol|first=Adérito|last=Esteves|language=Portuguese|date=30 July 2018|accessdate=31 December 2018}}</ref> but missed the final due to a muscle injury.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.dn.pt/desportos/interior/europeu-sub-19-guarda-redes-diogo-costa-substituido-por-ricardo-benjamin-9648076.html|title=Guarda-redes de Portugal falha final do Europeu sub-19|trans-title=Portugal goalkeeper misses under-19 European Championship final|newspaper=Diário de Notícias|first=David|last=Pereira|language=Portuguese|date=27 July 2018|accessdate=31 December 2018}}</ref>

Previously, on 25 May, the 18-year-old won his first [[Cap (sport)|cap]] for the [[Portugal national under-21 football team|under-21 side]], coming on as a second-half [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] in a 3–2 [[Exhibition game|friendly]] win over [[Italy national under-21 football team|Italy]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://desporto.sapo.pt/futebol/selecao/artigos/selecao-sub-21-de-portugal-vence-italia-em-amigavel-disputado-no-estoril|title=Seleção sub-21 de Portugal vence Itália em amigável disputado no Estoril|trans-title=Under-21 Portuguese national team beat Italy in friendly held in Estoril|publisher=SAPO|language=Portuguese|date=25 May 2018|accessdate=31 December 2018}}</ref> In March 2021, Costa played all of Portugal's matches in the [[2021 UEFA European Under-21 Championship]], as Portugal finished the tournament as runners-up, losing the final 1–0 to [[Germany national under-21 football team|Germany]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Highlights, report: Germany beat Portugal for third U21 EURO title |url=https://www.uefa.com/under21/news/026a-127532d17acc-e079bf3c8a1c-1000--highlights-report-germany-beat-portugal-for-third-u21-euro-titl/ |access-date=29 March 2022 |publisher=UEFA |date=6 June 2021}}</ref>

=== 2021–Present: Senior career===
Costa was called up to [[Portugal national football team|the senior team]] for the first time on 26 August 2021, for [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)|qualifiers]] against the [[Republic of Ireland national football team|Republic of Ireland]] and [[Azerbaijan national football team|Azerbaijan]] and a friendly with [[Qatar national football team|Qatar]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://maisfutebol.iol.pt/portugal/selecao-nacional/selecao-os-convocados-de-fernando-santos|title=Seleção: os convocados de Fernando Santos|trans-title=National team: those called up by Fernando Santos|publisher=Mais Futebol|language=Portuguese|date=26 August 2021|accessdate=27 August 2021}}</ref> He made his debut on 9 October against the last of those opponents, in a 3–0 victory at [[Algarve Stadium]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://portugoal.net/selecao/2287-portugal-3-0-qatar-matheus-nunes-rafael-leao-make-their-selecao-debuts|title=Portugal 3–0 Qatar – Diogo Costa, Matheus Nunes & Rafael Leão make their Seleção debuts|publisher=PortuGOAL|date=9 October 2021|accessdate=10 October 2021}}</ref>

Costa was chosen as the first-choice goalkeeper by the head coach [[Fernando Santos (footballer, born 1954)|Fernando Santos]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA second round|2022 FIFA World Cup qualification play-offs]], relegating usual starter [[Rui Patrício]] to the bench.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Ferreira |first1=Sébastien |title=Barrages Mondial 2022 : avec Diogo Costa, le Portugal a baptisé son nouvel ange-gardien|trans-title=2022 World Cup playoffs: in Diogo Costa, Portugal have baptised their new guardian angel|url=https://www.lefigaro.fr/sports/football/coupe-du-monde/barrages-mondial-2022-avec-diogo-costa-le-portugal-a-baptise-son-nouvel-ange-gardien-20220328 |access-date=29 March 2022 |work=[[Le Figaro]] |date=28 March 2022 |language=French}}</ref> On 24 March, at his club ground, he played his first competitive game in a 3–1 win over [[Turkey national football team|Turkey]] in the play-off semi-finals;<ref>{{cite news |title=Diogo Costa e a titularidade frente à Turquia: "Foi muito especial para mim"|trans-title=Diogo Costa and the starting role against Turkey: "It was very special for me" |url=https://www.record.pt/internacional/competicoes-de-selecoes/mundial/mundial-2022/detalhe/diogo-costa-e-a-titularidade-frente-a-turquia-foi-muito-especial-para-mim |access-date=29 March 2022 |work=Record |date=24 March 2022 |language=Portuguese}}</ref> he featured again five days later in a 2–0 victory over [[North Macedonia national football team|North Macedonia]] that sealed a place for the tournament.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Fernandes |first1=Mariana |last2=Paredes |first2=Diogo |title=Portugal vence Macedónia do Norte com bis de Bruno Fernandes e está no Mundial do Qatar (2-0) - como aconteceu|trans-title=Portugal defeat North Macedonia with a brace from Bruno Fernandes and are in the World Cup in Qatar (2-0) - how it happened |url=https://observador.pt/liveblogs/portugal-macedonia-do-norte-danilo-mantem-se-a-central-ao-lado-de-pepe-diogo-costa-volta-a-ser-titular-na-baliza/ |access-date=29 March 2022 |work=Observador |date=29 March 2022 |language=Portuguese}}</ref>

Costa was called up for the final 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://desporto.sapo.pt/futebol/mundial/artigos/os-26-convocados-de-portugal-para-o-mundial2022|title=Os 26 convocados de Portugal: Gonçalo Ramos, António Silva, Otávio e Matheus Nunes chamados|trans-title=Portugal select 26: Gonçalo Ramos, António Silva, Otávio and Matheus Nunes called|publisher=SAPO|language=pt|date=10 November 2022|access-date=10 November 2022}}</ref> On 25 November, he played his first World Cup match, a 3–2 group stage win against [[Ghana national football team|Ghana]]. This made him the youngest Portuguese goalkeeper to play in a major international tournament, at age 23.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Garcia |first=Adriana |url=https://www.espn.co.uk/football/portugal-por/story/4805137/cristiano-ronaldo-becomes-first-man-to-score-in-5-world-cups |title=Portugal's Cristiano Ronaldo becomes first male player to score in 5 World Cups |date=24 November 2022 |access-date=24 November 2022 |work=ESPN}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Sousa |first=Vasco |url=https://www.zerozero.pt/news.php?id=424068 |title=Diogo Costa torna-se o guardião mais jovem de sempre pela seleção em fases finais |date=24 November 2022 |access-date=24 November 2022 |work=ZeroZero}}</ref> He played every minute of the campaign, as they were knocked out of the tournament after losing in the quarter-finals to [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]], where he misjudged a cross when coming for the ball, and [[Youssef En-Nesyri]] headed the only goal of the game.<ref>{{Cite web |date=10 December 2022 |title=Morocco 1-0 Portugal: World Cup 2022 quarter-final – as it happened |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2022/dec/10/morocco-portugal-world-cup-2022-quarter-final-live |access-date=11 December 2022 |website=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref>
