'''Munir Mohand Mohamedi El Kajoui''' ({{lang-ar|منير مهند محمدي الكجوي}}; born 10 May 1989), known simply as '''Munir''', is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Saudi Professional League]] club [[Al Wehda FC|Al Wehda]] and the [[Morocco national football team|Morocco national team]].

He played for [[AD Ceuta B|Ceuta B]], [[UD Almería B|Almería B]], [[UD Melilla|Melilla]], [[CD Numancia|Numancia]], [[Málaga CF|Málaga]], [[Hatayspor]] and [[Al Wehda FC|Al Wehda]].

Born in Spain, Munir represented [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]] at international level, making his debut in 2015 and being part of the squads at two [[FIFA World Cup|World Cups]] and three [[Africa Cup of Nations]] tournaments.

==Club career==
Born in [[Melilla]], Munir played youth football with CG Goyu Ryu and [[AD Ceuta]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ceutadeportiva.com/2022/12/el-ex-del-ceuta-munir-se-enfrenta-a-espana-con-marruecos-vamos-a-luchar-con-nuestra-armas/|title=El ex del Ceuta Munir se enfrenta a España con Marruecos: “Vamos a luchar con nuestra armas”|trans-title=Former Ceuta man Munir takes on Spain with Morocco: "We will fight with our weapons"|publisher=Ceuta Deportiva|language=es|date=5 December 2022|access-date=13 February 2023}}</ref> and made his debut as a senior with [[AD Ceuta B|the latter's reserves]] in the regional leagues.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.adceuta.org/munir-rescinde-su-contrato-con-el-ceuta/|title=Munir rescinde su contrato con el Ceuta|trans-title=Munir terminates his contract with Ceuta|publisher=AD Ceuta|language=es|date=28 January 2009|access-date=15 November 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110102213827/http://www.adceuta.org/munir-rescinde-su-contrato-con-el-ceuta/|archive-date=2 January 2011}}</ref> On 29 January 2009 he moved to another [[reserve team]], [[UD Almería B]] from [[Tercera División]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.esfutbol.net/espana-3division/almeriab-se-incorpora-munir-ceuta-39333|title=Almería "B": se incorpora Munir (Ceuta)|trans-title=Almería "B": Munir (Ceuta) added|publisher=esFutbol|language=es|date=29 January 2009|access-date=16 November 2015}}</ref> but appeared sparingly and was subsequently released.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.joinfutbol.com/es/el_almeria_b_con_galca_al_frente_comienza_la_pretemporada__20823.php|title=El Almería B, con Constantin Galca al frente, comienza la pretemporada|trans-title=Almería B, with Constantin Galca at the helm, start the pre-season|publisher=Join Futbol|language=es|date=16 July 2009|access-date=13 September 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140913213751/http://www.joinfutbol.com/es/el_almeria_b_con_galca_al_frente_comienza_la_pretemporada__20823.php|archive-date=13 September 2014}}</ref>

Munir joined [[UD Melilla]] in summer 2009, initially being assigned to [[UD Melilla B|the reserves]] and being definitely promoted to the main squad in the [[Segunda División B]] the following year. He acted mainly as a backup to [[Pedro Dorronsoro]] in his first two seasons at the club, becoming a starter afterwards.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.eldesmarque.com/malaga/malaga-cf/malaga-noticias/160635-munir-retoma-el-hilo-conector-entre-melilla-y-malaga|title=Munir retoma el hilo conector entre Melilla y Málaga|trans-title=Munir the new connecting thread between Melilla and Málaga|publisher=El Desmarque|first=Enrique|last=Salvatierra|language=es|date=19 July 2018|access-date=23 November 2022}}</ref>

On 17 June 2014, Munir signed a two-year contract with [[Segunda División]] side [[CD Numancia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cdnumancia.com/-/munir-portero-del-melilla-primer-fichaje-numantino-para-la-temporada-2014-15|title=Munir, portero del Melilla, primer fichaje numantino para la temporada 2014–15|trans-title=Munir, Melilla's goalkeeper, first ''numantino'' signing for the 2014–15 season|publisher=CD Numancia|language=es|date=17 June 2014|access-date=13 September 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140703033246/http://www.cdnumancia.com/-/munir-portero-del-melilla-primer-fichaje-numantino-para-la-temporada-2014-15|archive-date=3 July 2014}}</ref> On 10 September he made his professional debut, starting in a 1–1 away draw against [[CD Leganés]] in [[2014–15 Copa del Rey|the second round]] of the [[Copa del Rey]] and also saving two [[Penalty kick (association football)|penalties]] during [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|the shootout]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.marca.com/2014/09/11/futbol/copa_rey/1410387300.html|title=Munir frena el sueño copero del Leganés|trans-title=Munir halts Leganés' cup dream|newspaper=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|language=es|date=11 September 2014|access-date=13 September 2014}}</ref>

Munir made his debut in the second tier on 19 October 2014, playing the full 90 minutes in a 2–0 loss at [[UD Las Palmas]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.marca.com/2014/10/19/futbol/2adivision/1413748030.html|title=Las Palmas vuelve a ganar al Numancia jugando media hora con uno menos|trans-title=Las Palmas beat Numancia again playing half an hour with one less|newspaper=Marca|language=es|date=19 October 2014|access-date=3 February 2021}}</ref> He was first choice for the remainder of [[2014–15 Segunda División|the campaign]], overtaking [[Biel Ribas]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vavel.com/es/futbol/liga-adelante/numancia/428914-munir-le-quita-a-biel-ribas-la-titularidad-en-la-porteria-del-numancia.html|title=Munir le quita a Biel Ribas la titularidad en la portería del Numancia|trans-title=Munir takes starting spot in Numancia's goal from Biel Ribas|publisher=[[Vavel]]|language=es|date=23 December 2014|access-date=16 November 2015}}</ref>

Munir left the [[Soria]] team at the end of [[2017–18 Segunda División|2017–18]], after losing his starting spot to [[Aitor Fernández (footballer, born 1991)|Aitor Fernández]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.heraldodiariodesoria.es/noticias/deportes/aitor-portero-suma-puntos_111674.html|title=Aitor, un portero que suma puntos|trans-title=Aitor, a goalkeeper who keeps adding points|newspaper=Heraldo de Soria|first=Luis|last=Hernández Casado|language=es|date=12 June 2018|access-date=17 August 2018}}</ref> On 19 July 2018, he joined [[Málaga CF]] also in the second division.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.malagacf.com/noticia/una-porteria-mas-internacional-con-munir|title=Una portería más internacional con Munir|trans-title=A more international goal with Munir|publisher=Málaga CF|language=es|date=19 July 2018|access-date=19 July 2018}}</ref>

From [[2020–21 Süper Lig|2020]] to [[2021–22 Süper Lig|2022]], Munir competed in the Turkish [[Süper Lig]] with [[Hatayspor]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://es.besoccer.com/noticia/de-malaga-a-turquia-munir-ya-es-del-hatayspor-882729|title=De Málaga a Turquía: Munir ya es del Hatayspor|trans-title=From Málaga to Turkey: Munir already belongs to Hatayspor|publisher=Be Soccer|language=es|date=5 September 2020|access-date=14 July 2022}}</ref> On 10 June 2022, he agreed to a two-year deal at Saudi Arabian club [[Al Wehda FC]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sabq.org/sports/%D8%A7%D9%84%D9%88%D8%AD%D8%AF%D8%A9-%D9%8A%D8%AA%D8%B9%D8%A7%D9%82%D8%AF-%D9%85%D8%B9-%D8%AD%D8%A7%D8%B1%D8%B3-%D9%85%D9%86%D8%AA%D8%AE%D8%A8-%D8%A7%D9%84%D9%85%D8%BA%D8%B1%D8%A8-%D9%85%D9%86%D9%8A%D8%B1-%D8%A7%D9%84%D9%85%D8%AD%D9%85%D8%AF%D9%8A|title=الوحدة" يتعاقد مع حارس منتخب المغرب منير المحمدي|trans-title=Al-Wehda sign Moroccan goalkeeper Munir Mohamedi|publisher=Sabq|language=ar|date=10 June 2022|access-date=19 June 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
Having Moroccan heritage, Munir switched his allegiance to [[Morocco national football team|its national team]] in 2014. On 9 March 2015, he was called up by manager [[Ezzaki Badou]] for a [[Exhibition game|friendly]] against [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay]] to be held late in the month,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.diariodesoria.es/noticias/deportes/munir-convocado-marruecos_33271.html|title=Munir, convocado con Marruecos|trans-title=Munir, called up with Morocco|newspaper=Diario de Soria|language=es|date=9 March 2015|access-date=3 July 2015}}</ref> and made his debut by starting in the 0–1 loss.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.com/international-friendly/match/413742/morocco-uruguay/report|title=Edinson Cavani scores penalty to give Uruguay win in Morocco|publisher=[[ESPN FC]]|date=28 March 2015|access-date=3 July 2015}}</ref>

On 29 December 2016, Munir was included in [[Hervé Renard]]'s 23-man squad for the [[2017 Africa Cup of Nations]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://africanfootball.com/news/674483/Morocco-Name-Afcon-2017-Squad|title=Morocco name AFCON 2017 squad|publisher=African Football|date=29 December 2016|access-date=1 August 2017}}</ref> and started the whole tournament ahead of [[Yassine Bounou]]. He was also selected to the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Russia]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44160388|title=World Cup 2018: Sofiane Boufal left out of Morocco squad|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|date=17 May 2018|access-date=22 May 2018}}</ref> making his first appearance in the [[FIFA World Cup|World Cup]] on 15 June in the 1–0 group stage defeat to [[Iran national football team|Iran]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/06/15/sports/world-cup/morocco-iran-live-score.html|title=Morocco own goal gives Iran a World Cup victory|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|first=Victor|last=Mather|date=15 June 2018|access-date=15 June 2018}}</ref>

Munir was also selected for the [[2019 Africa Cup of Nations|2019]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/48366746|title=Africa Cup of Nations squads|publisher=BBC Sport|date=22 May 2019|access-date=23 November 2022}}</ref> and [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/africa/59808945|title=Afcon 2021: Morocco add three to squad for Cameroon|publisher=BBC Sport|date=28 December 2021|access-date=23 November 2022}}</ref> and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/en-bh/lists/morocco-2022-world-cup-squad-hakimi-ziyech-mazraoui-qatar/blta36257fc4feeb66a|title=Morocco World Cup 2022 squad: Who's in and who's out?|publisher=[[Goal (website)|Goal]]|first=Soham|last=Mukherjee|date=10 November 2022|access-date=10 November 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.hespress.com/52697-moroccan-coach-unveils-list-of-26-atlas-lions-in-2022-world-cup.html|title=Moroccan coach unveils list of 26 Atlas Lions in 2022 World Cup|publisher=[[Hespress]]|date=10 November 2022|access-date=10 November 2022}}</ref> After starter Bounou fell ill shortly before the start of the group-stage fixture against [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]] in the latter tournament, he replaced him and eventually contributed to a 2–0 win in [[Doha]];<ref>{{cite web|url=https://talksport.com/football/1258750/yassine-bounou-morocco-goalkeeper-swapped-belgium-world-cup/|title=LATE CHANGE {{!}} Where is Yassine Bounou? Morocco fans confused over late goalkeeper switch as Sevilla man on pitch for national anthem before Munir Mohamedi starts World Cup clash against Belgium|publisher=[[Talksport]]|first=Josh|last=Fordham|date=27 November 2022|access-date=27 November 2022}}</ref> he and his teammates were part of the first African nation ever to reach the semi-finals of the competition.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rtbf.be/article/walid-regragui-avant-daffronter-la-france-nous-avons-faim-nous-voulons-que-lafrique-soit-au-sommet-du-monde-11122333|title=Walid Regragui avant d'affronter la France: "Nous avons faim, nous voulons que l'Afrique soit au sommet du monde"|trans-title=Walid Regragui before facing France: "We are hungry, we want Africa to be on top of the world"|publisher=[[RTBF]]|first=Jâd|last=El Nakadi|language=fr|date=13 December 2022|access-date=13 December 2022}}</ref>
