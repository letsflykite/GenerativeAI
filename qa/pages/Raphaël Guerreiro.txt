'''Raphaël Adelino José Guerreiro''' {{post-nominals|post-noms=[[Order of Merit (Portugal)|ComM]]}} ({{IPA-pt|ʁɐfɐˈɛl ɡɨˈʁɐjɾu|eu}};<ref>{{Cite web |date=11 July 2020 |title=Kick it like Raphael Guerreiro - Name his trick |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Li1y-GErcg0&t=21s |access-date=23 June 2023 |website=YouTube}}</ref> born 22 December 1993) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Left-back|left-back]] or [[midfielder]] for [[Bundesliga]] club [[Bayern Munich]] and the [[Portugal national football team|Portugal national team]]. 

He began his career at [[Stade Malherbe Caen|Caen]], signing in 2013 with [[FC Lorient|Lorient]] where he made his [[Ligue 1]] debut. In June 2016, he joined [[Borussia Dortmund]].

Born in France, Guerreiro represented Portugal at [[Portugal national under-21 football team|under-21]] and [[Portugal national football team|senior level]], first appearing for the latter in 2014. He was part of their squad at two [[FIFA World Cup|World Cups]] and two [[UEFA European Championship|European Championships]], winning [[UEFA Euro 2016|Euro 2016]].

==Club career==
===Caen===
Born in [[Le Blanc-Mesnil]], [[Seine-Saint-Denis]] to a Portuguese father and a French mother, Guerreiro played youth football for three clubs, finishing his formation at [[Stade Malherbe Caen|Caen]] after signing in 2009 at the age of 15.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.caen.maville.com/sport/detail_-Pour-Raphael-Guerreiro-c-est-l-annee-d-apres_fil-2274375_actu.Htm|title=Pour Raphaël Guerreiro, c'est l'année d'après|trans-title=For Raphaël Guerreiro, it's the year after|newspaper=[[Ouest-France]]|first=Guillaume|last=Lainé|language=fr|date=11 January 2013|access-date=16 November 2014}}</ref> After starting as a senior with the [[reserve team]], he made his professional debut in the [[2012–13 Ligue 2|2012–13 season]], appearing in all the games and failing to start only once in an eventual fourth-place finish in [[Ligue 2]]; he was also elected to the [[Trophées UNFP du football#Ligue 2|Team of the Year]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.maxifoot.fr/unfp/l-equipe-type-de-l2-foot-178705.htm|title=UNFP: l'équipe type de L2|trans-title=NUPF: L2's All-Star Team|publisher=Maxi Foot|first=Romain|last=Rigaux|language=fr|date=19 May 2013|access-date=29 May 2023}}</ref>

===Lorient===
On 27 June 2013, Guerreiro moved to [[Ligue 1]] after signing a four-year contract with [[FC Lorient|Lorient]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fclweb.fr/fr/info/article/raphael-guerreiro-et-vincent-aboubakar-au-fc-lorient|title=Raphaël Guerreiro et Vincent Aboubakar au FC Lorient|trans-title=Raphaël Guerreiro and Vincent Aboubakar to FC Lorient|publisher=FC Lorient|language=fr|date=27 June 2013|access-date=12 August 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130702171455/http://www.fclweb.fr/fr/info/article/raphael-guerreiro-et-vincent-aboubakar-au-fc-lorient|archive-date=2 July 2013}}</ref> His maiden appearance in the competition took place on 10 August, as he featured the full 90 minutes in a 1–0 away loss against [[Lille OSC|Lille]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.losc.fr/actualites-foot-lille/losc-fcl-1-0-trois-bons-points-pour-commencer|title=LOSC-FCL (1–0): Trois bons points pour commencer!|trans-title=LOSC-FCL (1–0): Three good points for starters!|publisher=Lille OSC|language=fr|date=10 August 2013|access-date=19 June 2021}}</ref>

On 1 November 2014, Guerreiro scored his first goal for Lorient, opening the scoring at title-holders [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] in an eventual 2–1 defeat.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.ojogo.pt/internacional/portugueses/noticias/interior/raphael-guerreiro-marcou-mas-o-psg-ganhou-4214555.html|title=Raphael Guerreiro marcou, mas o PSG ganhou|trans-title=Raphael Guerreiro scored, but PSG won|newspaper=[[O Jogo]]|language=pt|date=1 November 2014|access-date=16 November 2014}}</ref> He finished [[2014–15 Ligue 1|the campaign]] with seven goals to help his team stave off relegation, including the equaliser as they came from behind to defeat his former employers 2–1 at the [[Stade du Moustoir]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/match/316453|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150315093242/http://www.lequipe.fr/Football/match/316453|url-status=dead|archive-date=15 March 2015|title=Ayew offre une victoire capitale à Lorient|trans-title=Ayew offers a key victory to Lorient|newspaper=[[L'Équipe]]|language=fr|date=14 March 2015|access-date=19 November 2015}}</ref> 

Guerreiro opened the scoring in a [[Derby Breton]] on 24 October 2015, [[Assist (association football)|assisted]] by [[Majeed Waris]] in a 1–1 home draw against [[Stade Rennais F.C.|Rennes]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://fr.sports.yahoo.com/news/ligue-1-rennes-tenu-%C3%A9chec-%C3%A0-lorient-navance-171827547--sow.html|title=Rennes, tenu en échec à Lorient, n'avance plus|trans-title=Rennes, held in check by Lorient, advance no further|publisher=[[Yahoo! Sports]]|language=fr|date=24 October 2015|access-date=19 November 2015}}</ref>

===Borussia Dortmund===
On 16 June 2016, [[Borussia Dortmund]] signed Guerreiro on a four-year contract<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bvb.de/eng/News/Overview/Borussia-Dortmund-sign-Raphael-Guerreiro|title=Borussia Dortmund sign Raphaël Guerreiro|publisher=Borussia Dortmund|date=16 June 2016|access-date=16 June 2016}}</ref> for a reported fee of €12 million (£9.5 million).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://bleacherreport.com/articles/2643367-raphael-guerreiro-to-borussia-dortmund-latest-transfer-details-reaction-more|title=Raphael Guerreiro to Borussia Dortmund: Latest transfer details, reaction, more|publisher=[[Bleacher Report]]|first=Matt|last=Jones|date=16 June 2016|access-date=16 June 2016}}</ref> Under coach [[Thomas Tuchel]], he was primarily deployed as a [[midfielder]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.portugoal.net/portuguese-abroad/538-raphael-guerreiro-makes-flying-start-to-life-at-borussia-dortmund|title=Raphael Guerreiro makes flying start to life at Borussia Dortmund|publisher=PortuGOAL|first=Tom|last=Kundert|date=21 September 2016|access-date=25 September 2016}}</ref>

Guerreiro scored his first goal in the [[UEFA Champions League]] on 14 September 2016, in a 6–0 away win against [[Legia Warsaw]] in the [[2016–17 UEFA Champions League|group phase]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/noblmd2-legia-warszawa-0-6-borussia-dortmund-champions-league-report.jsp|title=Rampant Dortmund hit the ground running in Warsaw|publisher=[[Bundesliga]]|date=14 September 2016|access-date=22 October 2020}}</ref> In the [[2018–19 UEFA Champions League|2018–19 edition of the competition]], and also at that stage, he added braces in victories over [[Atlético Madrid]] (4–0, home)<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/45907614|title=Borussia Dortmund 4–0 Atlético Madrid|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|date=24 October 2018|access-date=22 October 2020}}</ref> and [[AS Monaco FC|Monaco]] (2–0).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/monaco-borussia-dortmund-live-champions-league-line-ups-stats-sancho-reus-pulisic-518884.jsp|title=Raphael Guerreiro double downs Monaco as Borussia Dortmund top UEFA Champions League Group A|publisher=Bundesliga|date=11 December 2018|access-date=22 October 2020}}</ref>

On 27 May 2023, in the last matchday, Guerreiro scored his fourth goal of [[2022–23 Bundesliga|the season]] to help the hosts to come back from a 2–0 deficit against [[1. FSV Mainz 05|Mainz 05]] and draw 2–2, but [[Der Klassiker]] rivals [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] defeated [[1. FC Köln]] 2–1 away and were crowned [[Bundesliga]] champions instead.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.zdf.de/nachrichten/sport/deutscher-fussball-meister-bundesliga-bayern-muenchen-borussia-dortmund-100.html|title=Dortmund schenkt Bayern die Meisterschaft|trans-title=Dortmund offer championship to Bayern|publisher=[[ZDF]]|language=de|date=27 May 2023|access-date=29 May 2023}}</ref> The following day, Dortmund announced the departure of five players, including Guerreiro.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bvb.de/eng/News/Overview/BVB-bid-farewell-to-five-players|title=BVB bid farewell to five players|publisher=Borussia Dortmund|date=28 May 2023|access-date=29 May 2023}}</ref>

=== Bayern Munich ===
On June 23, 2023, Bayern Munich announced that they had secured Guerreiro as a free agent.<ref>{{Cite web |date=23 June 2023 |title=FC Bayern sign Raphaël Guerreiro |url=https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2023/06/abloesefreier-neuzugang-fc-bayern-nimmt-raphael-guerreiro-unter-vertrag |access-date=23 June 2023 |website=FC Bayern München}}</ref> His contract, set to last for the next three years, would keep him at the club until June 2026.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Raphael Guerreiro joins Bayern Munich on free transfer |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/raphael-guerreiro-joins-bayern-munich-borussia-dortmund-free-transfer-23768 |access-date=2023-06-23 |website=bundesliga.com - the official Bundesliga website |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |title=Bayern sign Guerreiro from Dortmund on a free |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/65999152 |access-date=2023-06-23}}</ref>

Guerreiro returned to the under-21s for the [[2015 UEFA European Under-21 Championship|2015 UEFA European Championship]] in the Czech Republic, helping them finish in second place.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/under21/season=2015/matches/round=2000411/match=2015383/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Spot-on Sweden beat Portugal to win U21 EURO|publisher=UEFA|first=Tom|last=Kell|date=30 June 2015|access-date=1 October 2018}}</ref> He was one of five Portuguese included in the Team of the Tournament.<ref name=U21>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/under21/news/newsid=2262826.html|title=The official Under-21 Team of the Tournament|publisher=UEFA|date=1 July 2015|access-date=23 March 2016}}</ref>

Guerreiro was selected by the main squad for their [[UEFA Euro 2016]] campaign,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.com/portugal/story/2874983/new-bayern-munich-signing-renato-sanches-picked-for-portugal-squad|title=Portugal name Bayern Munich signing Renato Sanches for Euros squad|publisher=[[ESPN FC]]|date=17 May 2016|access-date=14 June 2016}}</ref> playing the full 90 minutes in the first game, a 1–1 draw with [[Iceland national football team|Iceland]] in [[Saint-Étienne]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro-2020/match/2017959--portugal-vs-iceland/postmatch/report/|title=Ice-cool Iceland claim Portugal point in EURO 2016 Group F|publisher=UEFA|first=Chris|last=Burke|date=14 June 2016|access-date=26 September 2020}}</ref> Portugal went on to win the competition, defeating hosts [[France national football team|France]] 1–0 in the [[UEFA Euro 2016 Final|final]] in [[Extra time (association football)|extra time]];<ref name=EC>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/36696772|title=Portugal 1–0 France|publisher=BBC Sport|first=Phil|last=McNulty|date=10 July 2016|access-date=17 March 2020}}</ref> following his performances throughout the competition, he was nominated for the [[UEFA Euro 2016#Awards|Young Player of the Tournament]] Award, which ultimately went to his teammate [[Renato Sanches]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/news/newsid=2389937.html |title=Renato Sanches named Young Player of the Tournament|quote=New European champion Renato Sanches has been chosen above Kingsley Coman and Portugal team-mate Raphael Guerreiro for the SOCAR Young Player of the Tournament award.|publisher=UEFA|date=10 July 2016|access-date=11 July 2016}}</ref>

In spite of an injury-riddled [[2017–18 Bundesliga|season]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/borussia-dortmund/story/3408469/raphael-guerreiro-returns-to-dortmund-training-marco-reus-on-way-back|title=Raphael Guerreiro returns to Dortmund training, Marco Reus on way back|publisher=[[ESPN]]|first=Stephan|last=Uersfeld|date=6 March 2018|access-date=21 May 2018}}</ref> Guerreiro was picked for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sbs.com.au/news/portugal-world-cup-squad|title=Nearly half Portugal's Euro squad to miss World Cup|publisher=[[Special Broadcasting Service]]|date=17 May 2018|access-date=17 May 2018}}</ref> He started four matches in Russia, in a round-of-16 exit.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44439361|title=Uruguay 2–1 Portugal|publisher=BBC Sport|first=Gary|last=Rose|date=30 June 2018|access-date=19 June 2021}}</ref>

Guerreiro also made the squad for [[UEFA Euro 2020]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pt.uefa.com/uefaeuro-2020/news/0269-1252942bc164-e2eac2467985-1000--convocados-de-portugal-para-o-euro/|title=Convocados de Portugal para o EURO 2020|trans-title=Portugal squad for EURO 2020|publisher=UEFA|language=pt|date=20 May 2021|access-date=19 June 2021}}</ref> He opened the 3–0 victory over [[Hungary national football team|Hungary]] in the first group fixture,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/51197797|title=Hungary 0–3 Portugal|publisher=BBC Sport|first=Michael|last=Emons|date=15 June 2021|access-date=19 June 2021}}</ref> but scored an [[own goal]] the following one in a 4–2 loss to [[Germany national football team|Germany]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/51197617|title=Portugal 2–4 Germany|publisher=BBC Sport|first=Alex|last=Bysouth|date=19 June 2021|access-date=19 June 2021}}</ref>

In November 2022, Guerreiro was named in the final squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://desporto.sapo.pt/futebol/mundial/artigos/os-26-convocados-de-portugal-para-o-mundial2022|title=Os 26 convocados de Portugal: Gonçalo Ramos, António Silva, Otávio e Matheus Nunes chamados|trans-title=Portugal select 26: Gonçalo Ramos, António Silva, Otávio and Matheus Nunes called|publisher=SAPO|language=pt|date=10 November 2022|access-date=10 November 2022}}</ref> On 6 December, he scored his team's fourth in an eventual 6–1 rout of [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]] in [[Lusail]] in the round of 16.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63789753|title=Portugal 6–1 Switzerland|publisher=BBC Sport|first=Phil|last=McNulty|date=6 December 2022|access-date=6 December 2022}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In 2014, Guerreiro said that he supported [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]], dreamed of playing for [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]], and that his favourite player was [[Cristiano Ronaldo]]. His Portugal under-21 manager, Rui Jorge, remembered him as a very introverted character, in part due to his language difficulties.<ref name=Expresso/>

Guerreiro and his family used to watch matches of the Portugal national team, of which [[Pauleta]] was a footballer he admired.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://maisfutebol.iol.pt/raphael-guerreiro/sporting/o-portugues-por-amor-que-vai-estar-do-lado-de-la|title=O português por amor que vai estar do lado de lá|trans-title=The out-of-love Portuguese who will be on the other side|publisher=Mais Futebol|first=Sérgio|last=Pereira|language=pt|date=28 September 2021|access-date=28 September 2021}}</ref>
