'''Cristian "Cuti" Gabriel Romero''' (born 27 April 1998) is an Argentine professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] for {{English football updater|TottenhH}} club [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] and the [[Argentina national football team|Argentina national team]].

Coming through the youth system, Romero began his senior career in 2016 at [[Club Atlético Belgrano|Belgrano]]. He moved to Italy in 2018, playing for [[Genoa C.F.C.|Genoa]]. Romero was purchased by [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]] the following season; he was sent back on loan to Genoa, before being sent on loan to Atalanta in 2020. After being nominated [[Serie A Awards|Serie A Best Defender]] in [[2020–21 Serie A#Seasonal awards|2020–21]] with Atalanta, the club exercised the option of purchase before promptly loaning Romero to Tottenham with obligation to buy. 

Romero represented Argentina at [[Argentina national under-20 football team|youth level]] at the [[2017 South American U-20 Championship]]. He made his senior debut in 2021, and was part of the squad that won the [[2021 Copa América]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Club career==
===Belgrano===
Having joined the youth team in 2014,<ref>{{Cite web|date=6 May 2019|title=Cristian Romero: la scheda tecnica|url=https://jmania.it/cristian-romero-scheda-tecnica/|access-date=10 August 2021|website=Jmania.it|language=it-IT}}</ref> Romero was promoted to [[Argentine Primera División]] side [[Club Atlético Belgrano|Belgrano]]'s first team in 2016;<ref name="Belgrano pro deal">{{cite news|title=Cristian Romero firmó su primer contrato|url=http://www.belgranocordoba.com/noticia-belgrano/1684/|access-date=15 April 2017|work=Club Atlético Belgrano|date=27 May 2016}}</ref> his debut came on 28 August, in a league match with [[Club Atlético Independiente|Independiente]].<ref name="Soccerway profile"/> A month later, Romero played in both of Belgrano's [[2016 Copa Sudamericana]] round of sixteen ties against [[Coritiba Foot Ball Club|Coritiba]].<ref name="Soccerway profile"/> In two seasons with Belgrano's first-team, he made nineteen appearances in all competitions.<ref name="Soccerway profile"/>

===Genoa, Juventus and Atalanta===
In July 2018, Romero joined [[Genoa C.F.C.|Genoa]] of [[Serie A]].<ref name="Soccerway profile"/> Romero scored his first senior goal in his second Genoa match, netting in a 2–2 draw with [[Udinese Calcio|Udinese]] on 28 October, a match in which he was later sent off.<ref name="Soccerway profile"/> On 9 July 2019 [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]] announced Romero was having a medical ahead of a proposed transfer.<ref name="JUV1">{{cite news|title=Cristian Romero begins Juventus medical|url=https://www.juventus.com/en/news/news/2019/cristian-romero-begins-juventus-medical.php|access-date=9 July 2019|work=Juventus|date=9 July 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190709101414/https://www.juventus.com/en/news/news/2019/cristian-romero-begins-juventus-medical.php|archive-date=9 July 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref> 

On 12 July 2019, Juventus announced the permanent acquisition of Romero from Genoa for €26 million, with the player remaining at his former club on loan for the rest of the season.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.juventus.com/it/news/news/2019/romero--gli-accordi-con-il-genoa.php|title=Romero, gli accordi con il Genoa|website=Juventus.com|access-date=12 July 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190712183711/https://www.juventus.com/it/news/news/2019/romero--gli-accordi-con-il-genoa.php|archive-date=12 July 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On 5 September 2020, Romero joined [[Atalanta B.C.|Atalanta]] on loan until 30 June 2022 with an option to buy.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://m.tuttomercatoweb.com/serie-a/ufficiale-juve-ceduto-romero-in-prestito-biennale-con-diritto-di-riscatto-all-atalanta-1428834 |title=UFFICIALE: Juve, ceduto Romero in prestito biennale con diritto di riscatto all'Atalanta |date=5 September 2020 |website=tuttomercatoweb.com |language=it}}</ref> He was elected the best defender of the [[2020–21 Serie A]] season.<ref name="Tottenham"/>

===Tottenham Hotspur===
On 6 August 2021, Juventus announced that Atalanta had activated the option to sign Romero for €16 million.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Juventus.com|title=ESERCIZIO DEL DIRITTO DI OPZIONE PER IL CALCIATORE CRISTIAN ROMERO - Juventus|url=https://www.juventus.com/it/news/articoli/esercizio-del-diritto-di-opzione-per-il-calciatore-cristian-romero|access-date=6 August 2021|website=Juventus.com|language=it}}</ref> Later that day, [[Premier League]] club [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] announced his signing from Atalanta on an initial season-long loan with an option to make the deal permanent.<ref name="Tottenham">{{cite web |url=https://www.tottenhamhotspur.com/news/2021/august/romero-signs-from-atalanta/ |title=Romero signs from Atalanta |date=6 August 2021 |website=tottenhamhotspur.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.atalanta.it/en/news/cristian-romero-joins-tottenham-hotspur/ |title=Cristian Romero joins Tottenham Hotspur |date=6 August 2021 |website=atalanta.it}}</ref>

Romero made his debut for the club on 15 August, coming on as a late substitute for [[Pierre-Emile Højbjerg]] in a match against [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] that Tottenham won 1–0.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2021/aug/15/tottenham-v-manchester-city-premier-league-live |title=Tottenham 1-0 Manchester City: Premier League - as it happened|first=Simon |last=Burnton |date= 15 August 2021 |work=The Guardian }}</ref> On 19 August 2021, he made his first start in the [[UEFA Europa Conference League]] first leg tie against [[F.C. Paços de Ferreira|Paços de Ferreira]], which ended in a 1–0 defeat.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/pacos-ferreira-vs-tottenham-hotspur/457263 |title=Pacos de Ferreira 1-0 Tottenham: Much-changed Spurs lose Europa Conference League play-off first-leg tie |first=Richard |last=Morgan|work=Sky Sports |date=20 August 2021 }}</ref> Romero scored his first goal for Tottenham Hotspur in a 2–0 win over [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]].

On 30 August 2022, he joined Tottenham Hotspur on a permanent deal with a contract lasting until 2027.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.tottenhamhotspur.com/news/2022/august/romero-completes-permanent-move/ |title=Romero completes permanent move |date=30 August 2022 |website=tottenhamhotspur.com}}</ref>

==International career==

=== Youth ===
Romero has represented Argentina at [[Argentina national under-20 football team|U20]] level, winning seven caps at the [[2017 South American U-20 Championship]] in Ecuador.<ref name="Soccerway profile"/> In April 2017, he was called up for Argentina's training camp ahead of the [[2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup]] in South Korea but was not selected on the tournament squad list.<ref name="Arg U20s camp">{{cite news|title=Lista para la semana que viene|url=http://afa.com.ar/7040/lista-para-la-semana-que-viene|access-date=15 April 2017|work=Argentine Football Association|date=15 April 2017}}</ref> Romero was called up for the [[Argentina national under-23 football team|U23s]] in September 2019.<ref name="AFA1">{{cite news|title=La Selección Sub 23 jugará ante Bolivia desde las 19, en el estadio de Banfield|url=https://www.afa.com.ar/es/posts/la-seleccion-sub-23-enfrentara-a-bolivia-desde-las-19-en-el-estadio-de-banfield|access-date=4 September 2019|work=AFA|date=3 September 2019}}</ref>

=== Senior ===
Romero made his debut for the [[Argentina national football team|senior national team]] on 3 June 2021 in a [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONMEBOL)|World Cup qualifier]] against [[Chile national football team|Chile]], playing the full match.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[ESPN]]|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/lineups?gameId=561064|title=Argentina v Chile game report|date=3 June 2021}}</ref> In his following game for Argentina on 8 June, he scored his first international goal, coming from a thumping header against [[Colombia national football team|Colombia]] after only 130 seconds. This goal also broke the record for the fastest ever scored for Argentina in a professional match, surpassing [[Diego Maradona]]'s goal after 168 seconds against [[Venezuela national football team|Venezuela]] in 1985.<ref>{{cite web|website=www.sport-fm.gr|url=https://www.sport-fm.gr/article/podosfairo/prokrimatika%20mountial/romero-perase-ton-maradona-kai-egrapse-istoria/4183845|title=Πέρασε τον Μαραντόνα και… έγραψε ιστορία ο Ρομέρο!|trans-title=Romero surpassed Maradona... and made history!|date=9 June 2021|access-date=9 June 2021|language=el}}</ref>

In June 2021, he was included in [[Lionel Scaloni]]'s final Argentina 28-man squad for the [[2021 Copa América]].<ref>{{cite web |url = https://en.as.com/en/2021/06/14/football/1623671691_620875.html |title = Argentina National Team Squad 2021 |work = Diario AS |date = 15 June 2021 }}</ref> Romero was included in the Team of the Tournament at the conclusion, as Argentina won the competition.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://copaamerica.com/es/noticias/equipo-ideal-get-conmebol-copa-america-2021/ |website=Copa América |date=13 July 2021 |title=Uno por uno, el equipo ideal de la CONMEBOL Copa América 2021 elegido por el GET |access-date=27 March 2022 |language=es}}</ref>
