'''Vincent Petrus Anna Sebastiaan Janssen''' (born 15 June 1994) is a Dutch professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]] for [[Belgian Pro League]] club [[Royal Antwerp F.C.|Antwerp]].

He began his professional career in the [[Eerste Divisie]] with [[Almere City]] before playing in the [[Eredivisie]] with [[AZ Alkmaar]] in [[2015–16 Eredivisie|2015–16]], finishing as the league's top scorer. He then moved to [[Tottenham Hotspur]] of the [[Premier League]] for a fee of £17 million, spending his second season on loan at [[Fenerbahçe S.K. (football)|Fenerbahçe]] in the Turkish [[Süper Lig]]. He spent three years in [[Liga MX]] with [[C.F. Monterrey|Monterrey]], winning honours including  the [[CONCACAF Champions League]] in [[2021 CONCACAF Champions League|2021]]. He signed for [[Royal Antwerp F.C.|Royal Antwerp]] in 2022, and won the [[Belgian Cup]] in his first season while scoring in the [[2023 Belgian Cup final|final]].

Janssen earned 22 [[Cap (sport)|caps]] for the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]] between 2016 and 2022, scoring seven goals. He was part of their squad at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Club career==
===Early career===
Janssen started playing at the amateur branch of [[TOP Oss|TOP]], after which he became part of the youth academy of the professional branch of the club. Through a merger of the TOP and N.E.C. academies, he became part of the academy of [[N.E.C. (football club)|NEC]]. From NEC, he transferred to [[Feyenoord]] youth teams, winning the U19s national title with Feyenoord in 2013.<ref>{{cite news|last1=May|first1=Sam|title=The rise of Vincent Janssen: From Almere City to Oranje in 12 months|url=http://www.football-oranje.com/rise-vincent-janssen/|access-date=2 July 2016|work=Football Oranje|date=18 March 2016}}</ref>

After being released by Feyenoord, he joined [[Eerste Divisie]] team [[Almere City FC|Almere City]] in 2013.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Vos|first1=Tim|title=Van Almere naar Wembley: een kleine stap voor Janssen|url=http://www.vi.nl/nieuws/van-almere-naar-wembley-een-kleine-stap-voor-janssen.htm|access-date=2 July 2016|work=Voetbal International|date=30 March 2016|language=nl}}</ref><ref name="skysports">{{cite news|last1=Kabelu|first1=Beya|title=Who is Vincent Janssen? ===AZ===
After two seasons with Almere, Janssen moved to [[Eredivisie]] side [[AZ Alkmaar|AZ]] in 2015, signing a contract with the club until 2019.<ref>{{cite news|title=AZ legt Coutinho en Janssen vast|trans-title=AZ signs Coutinho and Janssen|url=http://www.ad.nl/eredivisie/az-legt-coutinho-en-janssen-vast~a515e62f/|access-date=8 June 2016|work=[[Algemeen Dagblad]]|date=27 June 2015|language=nl}}</ref> He was signed by director [[Robert Eenhoorn]], a Dutch former [[Major League Baseball]] player who signed based on data, following the example of his friend and advisor [[Billy Beane]]; due to financial constraints, Eenhorn signed Janssen and fellow second division player [[Alireza Jahanbakhsh]] for under £1 million and sold them for a combined £33 million.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Stone |first1=Simon |title=West Ham v AZ Alkmaar: The Dutch Moneyball team chasing Europa Conference victory |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/65549007 |access-date=19 May 2023 |work=BBC Sport |date=11 May 2023}}</ref>

===Monterrey===
On 23 July 2019, [[Liga MX]] club [[C.F. Monterrey|Monterrey]] signed Janssen on a permanent deal for a fee of an estimated £6 million.<ref>{{cite news |title=Tottenham flop Janssen joins Liga MX side Monterrey in £6m deal |url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/tottenham-flop-janssen-joins-liga-mx-side-monterrey-in-6m-deal/h8raxozpi0yszbru30z41186 |access-date=19 May 2023 |publisher=Goal |date=23 July 2019}}</ref> He made his official debut on 17 August as a substitute for [[Rogelio Funes Mori]] in a 2–0 win against [[Deportivo Toluca F.C.|Toluca]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/liga-mx/rayados-vincent-janssen-debuta-futbol-mexicano-liga-mx|title=Rayados: Vincent Janssen debuta en el futbol mexicano; Liga MX|website=www.mediotiempo.com|access-date=18 August 2019}}</ref> and a week later he scored his first goal in Mexican football against [[Santos Laguna]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.milenio.com/futbol/club-monterrey/vincent-janssen-debuta-con-gol-en-el-monterrey|title=Liga MX: Vincent Janssen marca su gol con Monterrey|website=www.milenio.com|access-date=27 August 2019}}</ref>

He scored his first hat-trick for the club on 5 November in a [[Copa MX]] group stage match against [[Cafetaleros de Chiapas|Chiapas]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/copa-mx/2019/11/06/5dc25146268e3e46558b4611.html|title='Hat-Trick' de Janssen ante Cafetaleros y Monterrey se clasifica sin problema|date=6 November 2019|website=MARCA Claro México|language=es-MX|access-date=6 November 2019}}</ref>

He proved to be a vital part of the team as he helped Monterrey win the [[2019–20 Liga MX season#Torneo Apertura|Apertura 2019]] finals against [[Club América|América]]. Coming on as substitute in the second leg, he managed to score in the eventual [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shoot-out]] as Monterrey won their first title since 2010, his first league title.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.football-oranje.com/janssen-wins-mexican-title-with-monterrey/|title=Janssen wins Mexican title with Monterrey|date=30 December 2019|website=Football Oranje}}</ref>

In the [[2021 CONCACAF Champions League]], Janssen scored in each leg of a 6–1 aggregate win over [[Atlético Pantoja]] of the Dominican Republic in April,<ref>{{cite news |last1=Guerrero |first1=Mario |title=Atlético Pantoja pierde contra Monterrey (0-3): Resumen y goles del partido|trans-title=Atlético Pantoja lose to Monterrey (0-3): Match report and goals |url=https://mexico.as.com/mexico/2021/04/09/futbol/1617930502_120694.html |access-date=19 May 2023 |work=Diario AS |date=8 April 2021 |language=Spanish}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Rayados elimina al Atlético Pantoja|trans-title=Rayados eliminate Atlético Pantoja |url=https://mexico.as.com/mexico/2021/04/16/futbol/1618535875_755304.html |access-date=19 May 2023 |date=15 April 2021 |language=Spanish}}</ref> as his team went on to win the [[2021 CONCACAF Champions League final|final]] against América.<ref name=fifa>{{cite news |title=UAE-bound Monterrey seal Concacaf crown |url=https://www.fifa.com/tournaments/mens/clubworldcup/uae2021/news/uae-bound-monterrey-seal-concacaf-crown |access-date=19 May 2023 |publisher=FIFA |date=28 October 2021}}</ref>

==International career==
Janssen received his first call up to the senior [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands team]] in March 2016 for friendlies against [[France national football team|France]] and [[England national football team|England]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.football-oranje.com/spotlight-vincent-janssen/|title=Spotlight on Vincent Janssen: Netherlands next goal machine|date=31 March 2016|website=Football Oranje}}</ref> In his first international start, a 2–1 win against England on 29 March, he scored his first senior international goal and provided his first assist on the other.<ref>{{cite news|title=England 1 Holland 2: Three Lions fall to just second defeat since World Cup|url=https://www.yorkshireeveningpost.co.uk/sport/football/england-1-holland-2-three-lions-fall-just-second-defeat-world-cup-623283|access-date=30 March 2016|publisher=Yorkshire Evening Post|date=29 March 2016}}</ref>

In May 2022, Janssen received a call up from coach [[Louis van Gaal]] for [[2022–23 UEFA Nations League A|UEFA Nations League]] fixtures, ending a nearly five-year hiatus from the national team.<ref>{{cite web|title=Schouten, Janssen and Martins Indi named in Netherlands squad|url=http://www.football-oranje.com/schouten-janssen-and-martins-indi-named-in-netherlands-squad/|access-date=22 November 2022|website=Football Oranje|date=27 May 2022}}</ref> In November, he was included in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] squad.<ref>{{cite news|title=Ex-Tottenham star Vincent Janssen selected for Netherlands World Cup squad, as Louis van Gaal leaves out Man United man Donny van de Beek and top rated talent Ryan Gravenberch|url=https://talksport.com/football/1243295/the-netherlands-world-cup-squad-botman-van-de-beek-gravenberch-janssen/|access-date=22 November 2022|publisher=talkSPORT|date=11 November 2022}}</ref> Janssen announced his international retirement via social media on 12 March 2023, aged 28, citing the schedule for club football. He was criticised for his decision by former internationals [[Rafael van der Vaart]] and [[Ibrahim Afellay]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Van der Vaart maakt zich kwaad over beslissing Janssen: 'Ik vind het bijna kwalijk'|trans-title=Van der Vaart is angry about Janssen's decision: 'I almost think it's a bad thing' |url=https://www.vi.nl/nieuws/van-der-vaart-maakt-zich-kwaad-over-beslissing-janssen-ik-vind-het-bijna-kwalijk |access-date=19 May 2023 |work=Voetbal International |date=12 March 2023 |language=Dutch}}</ref>

==Style of play==
[[File:Vincent_Janssen_Penalty_(25866552990).jpg|thumb|300px|Vincent Janssen lining up to take a penalty against England in March 2016.]]

A two-footed player,<ref name="goal"/><ref name="fourfourtwo">{{cite web|last1=Ramesh|first1=Priya|title=All about Vincent Janssen: The Eredivisie top scorer wooing Wenger and Arsenal|url=http://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/vincent-janssen-dutchman-wooing-arsenal-liverpool-and-manchester|website=FourFourTwo|access-date=2 July 2016|date=10 May 2016}}</ref> Janssen is credited with good finishing, movement and pace.<ref name="skysports"/><ref name="fourfourtwo"/><ref name="outside">{{cite web|last1=Lynch|first1=Alex|title=Scout Report: Vincent Janssen|url=http://outsideoftheboot.com/2016/04/19/36320/|website=Outside of the boot|access-date=2 July 2016|date=19 April 2016}}</ref> Due to his strength,<ref name="skysports"/><ref name="goal"/><ref name="fourfourtwo"/> he is an "exceptional" hold-up man<ref name="skysports"/> who can bring teammates into play,<ref name="goal"/> and as a good passer of the ball,<ref name="skysports"/><ref name="fourfourtwo"/><ref name="outside"/> he can set up goal-scoring opportunities for teammates.<ref name="bleacher">{{cite web|last1=McNicholas|first1=James|title=Who Is Vincent Janssen and What Would He Bring to Arsenal?|url=http://bleacherreport.com/articles/2638363-who-is-vincent-janssen-and-what-would-he-bring-to-arsenal|website=Bleacher Report|access-date=2 July 2016|date=6 May 2016}}</ref> He is also praised for his work ethic,<ref name="skysports"/><ref name="fourfourtwo"/> as well as his competitive mentality.<ref name="espnfc2"/> Janssen's style of play has invited comparisons with [[Ruud van Nistelrooy]].<ref name="skysports"/><ref name="espnfc"/>

His weaknesses are shots from outside the penalty box, the timing of his movement and ball control.<ref name="fourfourtwo"/>

==Personal life==
Janssen is the son of [[Annemarie Verstappen]], a former world champion swimmer and [[Dutch Sportswoman of the year]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Wilson|first1=Jeremy|title=Arsenal transfer news: Holland striker Vincent Janssen a summer target|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2016/05/05/arsenal-transfer-news-holland-striker-vincent-janssen-a-summer-t/|access-date=8 June 2016|work=The Telegraph|date=5 May 2016}}</ref> He was awarded the 2016 [[Dutch Footballer of the Year#Dutch Football Talent of the Year .28Young player.29|Johan Cruyff Trophy]], the Dutch "Talent of the Year" award.
