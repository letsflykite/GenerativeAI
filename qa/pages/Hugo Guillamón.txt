'''Hugo Guillamón Sanmartín''' (born 31 January 2000) is a Spanish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[central defender]] or [[midfielder]] for [[La Liga]] club [[Valencia CF|Valencia]] and the [[Spain national football team|Spain national team]].

He came through the youth ranks at [[Valencia CF|Valencia]], where he made his first-team debut in 2020. He made his senior international debut for [[Spain national football team|Spain]] in 2021 and was chosen for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Club career==
Born in [[San Sebastián]], [[Gipuzkoa]], [[Basque Country (autonomous community)|Basque Country]], Guillamón moved to [[L'Eliana]], [[Valencian Community]] at the age of two.<ref name="Nacio">{{cite web|url=https://www.superdeporte.es/valencia/2020/02/22/hugo-guillamon-debuta-san-sebastian/442973.html|title=Hugo Guillamón debuta en San Sebastián... ¡Donde nació!|trans-title=Hugo Guillamón makes his debut in San Sebastián... Where he was born!|publisher=[[Super Deporte]]|language=es|date=22 February 2020|accessdate=28 June 2020}}</ref> He made his senior debut with [[Valencia CF Mestalla|the reserves]] on 16 December 2017, coming on as a first-half [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] for [[Ivan Zotko]] in a 0–0 [[Segunda División B]] home draw against [[CF Badalona]].

On 23 December 2017, Guillamón renewed his contract with the ''Che'' until 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.levante-emv.com/valencia-cf/2017/12/23/valencia-renueva-hugo-guillamon/1658719.html|title=El Valencia renueva a Hugo Guillamón|trans-title=Valencia renew Hugo Guillamón|publisher=[[Levante-EMV]]|language=es|date=23 December 2017|accessdate=28 June 2020}}</ref> The following 27 October he scored his first senior goal, netting the opener in a 3–2 home loss against [[CD Alcoyano]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elespanol.com/deportes/futbol/segunda-division-b/20181028/alcoyano-vence-antonio-puchades/1001396900345_29.html|title=El Alcoyano vence en el Antonio Puchades (2–3)|trans-title=Alcoyano win at the Antonio Puchades (2–3)|publisher=El Español|language=es|date=28 October 2018|accessdate=28 June 2020}}</ref>

Guillamón made his first team – and [[La Liga]] – debut on 22 February 2020, replacing injured [[Eliaquim Mangala]] at half-time in a 3–0 defeat away to [[Real Sociedad]].<ref name="Nacio"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.marca.com/futbol/primera-division/cronica/2020/02/22/5e5162f4268e3e6e3a8b45ee.html|title=La Real se exhibe ante un Valencia hundido|trans-title=Real put on a show against a sunken Valencia|publisher=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|language=es|date=22 February 2020|accessdate=28 June 2020}}</ref> On 22 July, he renewed his contract until 2023, being definitely promoted to the main squad.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.valenciacf.com/en/article/en-official-statement-i-hugo-guillamon-2020-07-22|title=Official Statement {{!}} Hugo Guillamón|publisher=Valencia CF|date=22 July 2020|accessdate=8 August 2020}}</ref> He scored his first goal for the team on 22 November in a 2–2 draw at [[Deportivo Alavés]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Fuster |first1=Vicente |title=(2-2) El Valencia paga su mal inicio con un empate en Vitoria|trans-title=(2-2) Valencia pay for their bad start with a draw in Vitoria|url=https://plazadeportiva.valenciaplaza.com/cronica-alaves-valencia-cf1 |publisher=Plaza Deportiva|date=22 November 2020|access-date=6 September 2021 |language=Spanish}}</ref>

On 13 August 2021, in the first game of the season at home to [[Getafe CF]], Guillamón fouled [[Nemanja Maksimović]] after 39 seconds. After consulting the [[video assistant referee]], his third-minute [[Ejection (sports)|dismissal]] was the earliest on a La Liga opening weekend; his team nonetheless won 1–0.<ref>{{cite web |title=Football: Ten-man Valencia overcome third-minute red card to beat Getafe in season opener |url=https://www.straitstimes.com/sport/football/football-ten-man-valencia-overcome-third-minute-red-card-to-beat-getafe-in-season |publisher=[[The Straits Times]] |access-date=6 September 2021 |date=14 August 2021}}</ref>

Guillamón started in the [[2022 Copa del Rey Final]], being substituted in the 85th minute as Valencia lost on penalties to [[Real Betis]].<ref>{{cite news |title=El Betis levanta su tercera Copa del Rey tras ganar en los penaltis en una final histórica|trans-title=Betis lift their third Copa del Rey after winning on penalties in a historic final |url=https://www.cope.es/deportes/futbol/copa-del-rey/noticias/directo-final-copa-del-rey-betis-valencia-23-04-2022-20220423_2043293 |access-date=17 November 2022 |publisher=[[Cadena COPE]] |date=23 April 2022 |language=Spanish}}</ref> On 3 October that year, he extended his contract to last until June 2026.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Fuster |first1=Vicente |title=Oficial: Hugo Guillamón renueva con el Valencia hasta junio de 2026|trans-title=Official: Hugo Guillamón renews with Valencia until June 2026|url=https://plazadeportiva.valenciaplaza.com/oficial-hugo-guillamon-renueva-con-el-valencia-hasta-junio-de-2026 |access-date=17 November 2022 |publisher=Plaza Deportiva |date=3 October 2022 |language=Spanish}}</ref>

==International career==
Guillamón represented [[Spain national football team|Spain]] at [[Spain national under-17 football team|under-17]] and [[Spain national under-19 football team|under-19]] levels, having won the [[2017 UEFA European Under-17 Championship]] for the former<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/under17/history/season=2017/index.html|title=Spain secure record third U17 EURO title|publisher=UEFA.com|date=19 May 2017|accessdate=28 June 2020}}</ref> and [[2019 UEFA European Under-19 Championship]] for the latter.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://plazadeportiva.valenciaplaza.com/hugo-guillamon-un-campeon-de-europa-sub19-en-apuros|title=Hugo Guillamón, un campeón de Europa sub19 en apuros|trans-title=Hugo Guillamón, a champion of the European Under-19 Championship in troubles|publisher=Plaza Deportiva|language=es|date=18 October 2019|accessdate=28 June 2020}}</ref>

Due to the isolation of some national team players following the positive [[COVID-19]] test of [[Sergio Busquets]], Spain's under-21 squad were called up for the international friendly against [[Lithuania national football team|Lithuania]] on 8 June 2021.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://sefutbol.com/oficial-lista-convocados-encuentro-lituania |title=Oficial {{!}} Lista de convocados para el encuentro ante Lituania |trans-title=Official {{!}} Squad list for the match against Lithuania |work=[[Royal Spanish Football Federation]] |date=6 June 2021 |access-date=7 June 2021 |language=es |archive-date=8 October 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211008070336/https://sefutbol.com/oficial-lista-convocados-encuentro-lituania |url-status=dead }}</ref> Guillamón made his senior debut in the match and scored in the 3rd minute of their 4–0 win.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/international-football/espana-vs-lituania/cronica/2021/06/08/60bfd85222601dc65d8b458f.html|title=Spain youngsters impress in Lithuania rout|website=Marca|date=8 June 2021|accessdate=8 June 2021}}</ref>

Guillamón was chosen for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Azzoni |first1=Tales |title=University student Guillamón among Spain defenders in Qatar |url=https://www.seattletimes.com/sports/soccer/university-student-guillamon-among-spain-defenders-in-qatar/ |access-date=17 November 2022 |work=[[The Seattle Times]] |date=15 November 2022}}</ref> He took no part in Spain's four matches, and the side were eliminated in the last 16 by [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Torres |first1=David |title=Guillamón se vuelve para casa sin debutar: lo que cobra el Valencia por su Mundial |trans-title=Guillamón returns home without debuting: what Valencia make from his World Cup|url=https://www.eldesmarque.com/valencia/valencia-cf/380605-guillamon-se-vuelve-para-casa-sin-debutar-lo-que-cobra-el-valencia-por-su-mundial?ssp |access-date=6 December 2022 |publisher=El Desmarque |date=6 December 2022 |language=Spanish}}</ref>

==Personal life==
As of November 2022, Guillamón was combining his professional football career with a degree in [[biomedical engineering]] from the [[Technical University of Valencia]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Hidalgo |first1=Jorge |title=Hugo Guillamón, el universitario que irá con la selección española a Qatar|trans-title=Hugo Guillamón, the university student who will go with the Spanish national team to Qatar|url=https://www.marca.com/futbol/valencia/2022/11/11/636e822a22601d1d5f8b458c.html |access-date=17 November 2022 |work=Marca |date=11 November 2022 |language=Spanish}}</ref>
