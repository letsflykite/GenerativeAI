'''Granit Xhaka''' ({{IPA-sq|…°…æaÀànit Ààd íaka}}; born 27 September 1992) is a Swiss professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[Bundesliga]] club [[Bayer 04 Leverkusen|Bayer Leverkusen]] and [[Captain (association football)|captains]] the [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland national team]].

Xhaka began his career at hometown club [[FC Basel|Basel]], winning the [[Swiss Super League]] in each of his first two seasons. He then moved to [[Bundesliga]] team [[Borussia M√∂nchengladbach]] in 2012, developing a reputation as a technically gifted player and natural leader alongside criticism for his temperament.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/15118/10219172/granit-xhaka-profile-of-arsenals-first-summer-signing |title=Granit Xhaka: Profile of Arsenal's first summer signing |website=Sky Sports |date=26 May 2016}}</ref> He was made captain of Borussia M√∂nchengladbach in 2015 at the age of 22, leading the team to [[UEFA Champions' League]] qualification for a second successive season.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/blog/2016/may/26/granit-xhaka-arsenal-leader-borussia-monchengladbach |title=Granit Xhaka: a born leader who will finally bring some grit to Arsenal ‚Äì Marcus Christenson |first=Marcus |last=Christenson |date=26 May 2016 |newspaper=The Guardian}}</ref> He completed a high-profile transfer to [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] in May 2016 for a fee in the region of ¬£30&nbsp;million.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/15118/10291273/granit-xhaka-becomes-arsenals-third-most-expensive-transfer-at-around-30m |title=Granit Xhaka becomes Arsenal's third-most expensive transfer at around ¬£30m |website=Sky Sports}}</ref> He later became captain of the club, but was stripped of the captaincy less than six weeks later due to a controversial incident after being substituted. He did later receive vice-captaincy however. In July 2023, he transferred to Bayer Leverkusen after seven years at Arsenal.

Xhaka was part of the [[Switzerland national under-17 football team|Swiss team]] that won the [[2009 FIFA U-17 World Cup]]. He made his senior debut in 2011 and has won over 110 caps, representing the nation at the [[FIFA World Cup]] in [[2014 FIFA World Cup|2014]], [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022]] and the [[UEFA European Championship]] in [[UEFA Euro 2016|2016]] and [[UEFA Euro 2020|2020]].

==Club career==

Xhaka started his youth football with [[FC Concordia Basel|Concordia Basel]] and moved to [[FC Basel|Basel]] in 2002. He played for various Basel youth teams and in 2008 became a member of the U-21 squad.
