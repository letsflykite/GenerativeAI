'''Luka Sučić''' ({{IPA-sh|lûːka sûtʃitɕ|hr}};<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hjp.znanje.hr/index.php?show=search_by_id&id=e15vWBE%3D|title=Lȗka|quote=Lȗka|website=Hrvatski jezični portal|accessdate=30 November 2020|language=hr}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=sẉd<sup>1</sup>|url=http://hjp.znanje.hr/index.php?show=search_by_id&id=d1pjWxI%3D&keyword=sud|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|accessdate=30 November 2020|website=Hrvatski jezični portal|language=hr|quote=Sȕčić}}</ref> born 8 September 2002) is a Croatian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[Austrian Football Bundesliga|Austrian Bundesliga]] club [[FC Red Bull Salzburg|Red Bull Salzburg]] and the [[Croatia national football team|Croatia national team]].

==Career==

On 15 July 2020, Sučić signed a new contract with [[FC Red Bull Salzburg|Red Bull Salzburg]], until the summer of 2024.<ref>{{cite web |title=Sučić Gets New Contract |url=https://www.redbullsalzburg.at/en/fc-red-bull-salzburg/news/saison_2019_20/sucic-gets-new-contract.html |website=redbullsalzburg.at/ |publisher=FC Red Bull Salzburg |accessdate=15 July 2020 |date=15 July 2020}}</ref>

He made his [[2020–21 Austrian Football Bundesliga|Bundesliga]] debut for Salzburg on 13 September 2020 in a game against [[Wolfsberger AC|Wolfsberg]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=Soccerway|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2020/09/13/austria/bundesliga/wolfsberger-athletik-club/fc-red-bull-salzburg/3341874/|title=Wolfsberger v Red Bull game report|date=13 September 2020}}</ref> On 25 November 2020, he made his [[2020–21 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] debut, replacing [[Dominik Szoboszlai]] in 71st minute and providing [[Rasmus Kristensen]] with a pre-[[Assist (association football)|assist]] for [[Mërgim Berisha]]'s goal, as Salzburg lost 3–1 to [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=26 November 2020|title=Naš novi bik iz Salzburgova inkubatora: Luka Sučić ostavio je dobar dojam protiv Bayerna|url=https://telesport.telegram.hr/na-prvu/nas-novi-bik-iz-salzburgova-inkubatora-luka-sucic-ostavio-je-dobar-dojam-protiv-bayerna/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=30 November 2020|website=Telesport|language=hr}}</ref> On 10 February 2021, Sučić scored his debut Bundesliga goal in a 3–1 victory over [[FK Austria Wien|Austria Wien]], coming off the bench to replace Berisha.

By the beginning of the [[2021–22 FC Red Bull Salzburg season|2021–22 season]], Sučić already established himself in Salzburg's squad, most notably scoring an impressive goal against [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] in a friendly 2–1 victory on 4 August 2021.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Jurišić|first=Bernard|date=28 August 2021|title=Novi val dijaspore|url=https://telesport.telegram.hr/kolumne/juris/novi-val-dijaspore/|url-status=live|access-date=28 August 2021|website=Telesport|language=hr}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vecernji.hr/sport/salzburg-srusio-barcelonu-sucic-zabio-fenomenalan-gol-1513272|title=Salzburg srušio Barcelonu, Sučić zabio fenomenalan gol|author=[[Croatian News Agency|HINA]]|website=Večernji list|date=4 August 2021|access-date=28 August 2021|lang=hr}}</ref> On 14 September, he scored his first [[2021–22 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] goal in a 1–1 draw with [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=14 September 2021|title=Kakav početak Lige prvaka: Hrvati strijelci na čudnoj utakmici na kojoj su dosuđena četiri penala!|url=https://gol.dnevnik.hr/clanak/rubrika/nogomet/liga-prvaka-ivan-rakitic-i-luka-sucic-strijelci-na-utakmici-sevilla-red-bull-salzburg---666682.html|url-status=live|access-date=14 September 2021|website=Gol.hr|language=hr}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Sučić was born in [[Linz]] to [[Bosnian Croat]] parents Željko and Branislava from [[Bugojno]] who fled from the [[Bosnian War]]. Despite being born in Linz, Sučić does not hold [[Austrian nationality law|Austrian citizenship]]. He declared himself a fan of [[HNK Hajduk Split|Hajduk Split]] and [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]]; however, he named [[Luka Modrić]], a legend of those clubs' respective rivals [[GNK Dinamo Zagreb|Dinamo Zagreb]] and [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]], as his footballing [[role model]].<ref name="24sata1"/><ref>{{Cite web|last=Topić|first=Mihovil|date=6 October 2021|title=Veliki dečko|url=https://telesport.telegram.hr/price/veliki-decko/|url-status=live|access-date=9 October 2021|website=Telesport|language=hr}}</ref>
