'''Pau Francisco Torres''' (born 16 January 1997) is a Spanish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] for [[La Liga]] club [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]] and the [[Spain national football team|Spain national team]].

==Club career==
Born in [[Villarreal]], [[Province of Castellón]], [[Valencian Community]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ondacero.es/emisoras/comunidad-valenciana/castellon/deportes/pau-torres-cumple-100-partidos-villarreal_202111046183da8124761b000131b40b.html|title=Pau Torres cumple 100 partidos con el Villarreal|trans-title=Pau Torres celebrates 100 matches with Villarreal|publisher=[[Onda Cero]]|language=es|date=4 November 2021|access-date=3 January 2022}}</ref> Torres played youth football with [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]]. He made his league debut with [[Villarreal CF B|the reserves]] on 21 August 2016, starting in a 1–0 [[Segunda División B]] away loss against [[UE Cornellà|Cornellà]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.castelloninformacion.com/filial-villarreal-derrota-gol-descuento-cornella-puerto-ocasiones/|title=El Villarreal B se estrenó con derrota en Cornellà con un solitario gol encajado en el minuto 91 (1–0)|trans-title=Villarreal B had losing debut in Cornellà with lone goal against in the 91st minute (1–0)|publisher=Castellón Información|language=es|date=21 August 2016|access-date=18 December 2019}}</ref>

Torres scored his first senior goal on 8 October 2016, the first in a 2–2 home draw with [[CF Badalona|Badalona]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.castelloninformacion.com/filial-villarreal-empate-polemico-goles-anulados-badalona-descuento/|title=Al Villarreal B le anulan dos goles legales ante un Badalona que el empató en el minuto 94 (2–2)|trans-title=Villarreal B have two legal goals disallowed against a Badalona who drew in the 94th minute (2–2)|publisher=Castellón Información|language=es|date=9 October 2016|access-date=18 December 2019}}</ref> His maiden competitive appearance for the first team took place on 20 December, when he came on as a late [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] for [[Víctor Ruiz (footballer, born 1989)|Víctor Ruiz]] in the 1–1 home draw against [[CD Toledo|Toledo]] in [[2016–17 Copa del Rey|the round of 32]] of [[Copa del Rey]];<ref>{{cite news|url=https://as.com/futbol/2016/12/20/copa_del_rey/1482253090_726650.html|title=Pato evita la sorpresa del Toledo y Manolo, la goleada|trans-title=Pato avoids Toledo's surprise and Manolo, a rout|newspaper=[[Diario AS]]|first=Alfredo|last=Matilla|language=es|date=20 December 2016|access-date=27 October 2017}}</ref> by doing so, he became the first player born in Villarreal to debut for the club in 13 years.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.sport.es/es/noticias/laliga/pau-torres-primer-futbolista-nacido-vila-real-que-debuta-13-anos-5707549|title=Pau Torres, primer futbolista nacido en Vila-real que debuta en 13 años|trans-title=Pau Torres, first footballer born in Villarreal to debut in 13 years|newspaper=[[Sport (Spanish newspaper)|Sport]]|language=es|date=22 December 2016|access-date=27 October 2017}}</ref>

Torres played his first match in [[La Liga]] on 26 November 2017, replacing fellow youth graduate [[Manu Trigueros]] late into a 2–3 home loss to [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rtve.es/deportes/20171126/sevilla-remonta-dos-goles-se-lleva-victoria-villarreal/1638723.shtml|title=El Sevilla remonta dos goles y se lleva la victoria de Villarreal|trans-title=Sevilla come back from two goals and take win from Villarreal|publisher=[[RTVE]]|language=es|date=26 November 2017|access-date=18 December 2019}}</ref> He made his [[UEFA Europa League]] debut ten days later, starting and finishing the [[2017–18 UEFA Europa League|0–1 group stage defeat]] against [[Maccabi Tel Aviv F.C.|Maccabi Tel Aviv]] also at the [[Estadio de la Cerámica]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.lavanguardia.com/deportes/futbol/20171207/433479671309/villarreal-europa-league-maccabi-tel-aviv.html|title=El Villarreal cierra con derrota un partido intrascendente|trans-title=Villarreal complete formality of a match with loss|newspaper=[[La Vanguardia]]|language=es|date=7 December 2017|access-date=19 December 2019}}</ref>

On 6 August 2018, Torres was loaned to [[Málaga CF|Málaga]] for one year.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.laopiniondemalaga.es/malagacf/2018/08/08/pau-torres-oferta-malaga-cf/1025522.html|title=Pau Torres: "La oferta del Málaga CF era irrechazable"|trans-title=Pau Torres: "Málaga CF's offer could not be refused"|newspaper=La Opinión de Málaga|first=Manu|last=Puga|language=es|date=8 August 2018|access-date=9 August 2018}}</ref> He only missed four [[Segunda División]] games during his spell as the side reached the [[2019 Segunda División play-offs|promotion play-offs]] and, subsequently, was recalled by his parent club.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.laopiniondemalaga.es/malagacf/2019/06/05/pau-torres-volvera-villarreal/1093421.html|title=Pau Torres volverá al Villarreal|trans-title=Pau Torres will return to Villarreal|newspaper=La Opinión de Málaga|language=es|date=5 June 2019|access-date=3 October 2019}}</ref>

Torres subsequently cemented his place in Villarreal's starting XI, playing every minute in the league.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.lavanguardia.com/deportes/20191007/47853482340/pau-torres-entre-los-once-que-han-disputado-todos-los-minutos-en-liga.html|title=Pau Torres, entre los once que han disputado todos los minutos en Liga|trans-title=Pau Torres, amongst eleven who have played every minute in League|newspaper=La Vanguardia|language=es|date=7 October 2019|access-date=19 December 2019}}</ref> In October 2019, he was rewarded with a contract extension until 2024,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.villarrealcf.es/noticias/todas-las-noticias/item/25614-el-villarreal-cf-renueva-a-pau-hasta-2024|title=El Villarreal CF renueva a Pau hasta 2024|trans-title=Villarreal CF renew Pau until 2024|publisher=Villarreal CF|language=es|date=2 October 2019|access-date=3 October 2019}}</ref> and the same month scored his first goal in the [[Spanish football league system|Spanish top tier]], opening a 2–1 loss at [[CA Osasuna|Osasuna]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/spanish-football/cronica/2019/10/05/5d9904a422601db5518b4578.html|title=Villarreal are the latest to suffer at El Sadar|newspaper=Marca|first=Euan|last=McTear|date=5 October 2019|access-date=6 October 2019}}</ref>

==International career==
Torres got his first call up for the [[Spain national football team|Spain national team]] by [[Robert Moreno]] on 4 October 2019, for [[UEFA Euro 2020]] [[UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying|qualifiers]] against [[Norway national football team|Norway]] and [[Sweden national football team|Sweden]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/spanish-football/2019/10/04/5d971507e2704e76198b4571.html|title=Reguilon, Pau Torres and Gerard Moreno named in Spain squad for first time|newspaper=Marca|first1=José Félix|last1=Díaz|first2=Conor|last2=Clancy|date=4 October 2019|access-date=6 October 2019}}</ref> He did not debut until 15 November when he scored in a 7–0 rout of [[Malta national football team|Malta]] for the already qualified hosts, within a minute of replacing [[Sergio Ramos]]; [[Dani Olmo]] also scored on his first [[Cap (sport)|cap]] in that game, the first time that two Spaniards did so in exactly 30 years.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://en.as.com/en/2019/11/15/football/1573854279_669899.html|title=Dani Olmo and Pau Torres make history in Spain stroll|newspaper=Diario AS|date=15 November 2019|access-date=16 November 2019}}</ref>

Torres was included in [[Luis Enrique]]'s [[UEFA Euro 2020 squads#Spain|24-man squad]] for the finals.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/euro-2020-news-live-england-squad-b1852654.html|title=Euro 2020 news LIVE: Sergio Ramos left out of Spain squad plus latest before England announcement|newspaper=[[The Independent]]|first=Jamie|last=Braidwood|date=24 May 2021|access-date=24 May 2021|url-status=live|url-access=subscription|archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/archive/20220525/https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/euro-2020-news-live-england-squad-b1852654.html|archive-date=25 May 2022}}</ref> He was also selected for the [[Spain national under-23 football team|Olympic team]] that won a silver medal at the [[Football at the 2020 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|Summer Olympics in Tokyo]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2021/06/29/spain-olympics-squad-euro-2020-pedri-dani-olmo|title=Spain names six from Euro squad to Olympic roster|publisher=[[Sports Illustrated]]|date=29 June 2021|access-date=1 July 2021}}</ref>
