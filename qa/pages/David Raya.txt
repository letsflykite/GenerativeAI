'''David Raya Martín''' (born 15 September 1995) is a Spanish professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for {{English football updater|Brentfor}} club [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]] and the [[Spain national football team|Spain national team]].

Raya began his senior career in England with [[Blackburn Rovers F.C.|Blackburn Rovers]]. He made his professional breakthrough as part of the team that was promoted from [[EFL League One|League One]] in 2018. Raya transferred to [[EFL Championship|Championship]] club Brentford in 2019 and was a part of the team that was promoted to the Premier League in 2021. He made his international debut for Spain in 2022.

==Club career==
===Blackburn Rovers===
Born in [[Barcelona]], Raya began his career in his native [[Football in Spain|Spain]] and combined [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeping]] with playing as an [[outfield player]] in [[futsal]].<ref name="Marshall">{{Cite web |last=Marshall |first=Sam |title=The Long Read: David Raya |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2022/march/the-long-read-david-raya/ |access-date=14 March 2022 |website=www.brentfordfc.com |language=en-gb}}</ref> He later played youth football for [[UE Cornellà]], before moving to [[Football in England|England]] to join [[Blackburn Rovers F.C.|Blackburn Rovers]] on a scholarship in July 2012.<ref>{{Cite web |title=List of Players Registered as Scholars in Accordance with Rule C.3 Between 01/07/2012 and 31/07/2012 |url=http://www.thefa.com/~/media/2FC380172F88401482C3994AF130D59B.ashx |url-status=dead |access-date=7 July 2019 |publisher=The Football Association |page=49}}</ref><ref name="RoversProfile">{{Cite web |title=David Raya |url=https://www.rovers.co.uk/teams/first-team/goalkeeper/david-raya-martin/ |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190707175140/https://www.rovers.co.uk/teams/first-team/goalkeeper/david-raya-martin/ |archive-date=7 July 2019 |access-date=7 July 2019 |publisher=Blackburn Rovers F.C.}}</ref> Two years earlier, the transfer of Hugo Fernández to [[Ewood Park]] had led to an agreement between the two clubs for UE Cornellà players to join Blackburn Rovers for trials.<ref name="Marshall" /> He progressed through the club's [[Blackburn Rovers F.C. Under-23s and Academy|academy]] and signed a professional contract on 26 February 2014.<ref>{{Cite web |date=26 February 2014 |title=Raya pens pro deal |url=https://www.rovers.co.uk/news/2014/february/raya-pens-pro-deal/ |access-date=25 April 2015 |publisher=Blackburn Rovers F.C.}}</ref> Raya gained his first senior experience with a four-month spell on [[Loan (sports)|loan]] at [[Conference Premier]] club [[Southport F.C.|Southport]] during the first half of the [[2014–15 Football Conference|2014–15]] season and made 24 appearances.<ref name="SignsBrentford" /> After his return to Ewood Park, he made two [[2014–15 Blackburn Rovers F.C. season|late-season]] [[EFL Championship|Championship]] appearances and signed a new three-year contract in April 2015.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Hard work pays off for David |url=https://www.rovers.co.uk/news/2015/april/hard-work-pays-off-for-david/ |access-date=7 July 2019 |publisher=Blackburn Rovers F.C.}}</ref><ref name="Soccerbase1415" />

Despite making just 13 appearances during the [[2015–16 Blackburn Rovers F.C. season|2015–16]] and [[2016–17 Blackburn Rovers F.C. season|2016–17]] seasons,<ref name="Soccerbase1516" /><ref name="Soccerbase1617" /> Raya was Rovers' second-choice goalkeeper behind [[Jason Steele (footballer)|Jason Steele]] and was a frequent member of the matchday squad.<ref name="RoversProfile" /><ref name="Soccerway" /> Rovers' relegation to [[EFL League One|League One]] at the end of the 2016–17 season saw Raya take over as the club's first-choice goalkeeper.<ref name="FCHD">{{Fchd|id=BLACKBUR|name=Blackburn Rovers F.C.}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=David Raya pens long-term Rovers deal |url=https://www.rovers.co.uk/news/2018/february/another-new-contract-for-a-young-rover/ |access-date=7 July 2019 |publisher=Blackburn Rovers F.C.}}</ref> He made 47 appearances during the [[2017–18 Blackburn Rovers F.C. season|2017–18]] season and helped the club to automatic promotion straight back to the Championship.<ref name="FCHD" /><ref name="Soccerbase1718" /> He retained in his place during [[2018–19 Blackburn Rovers F.C. season|2018–19]] and made 46 appearances during a season of consolidation in the Championship.<ref name="FCHD" /><ref name="Soccerbase1819" /> Raya departed Rovers in July 2019,<ref name="SignsBrentford">{{Cite web |title=David Raya signs for Brentford |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2019/july/david-raya-signs/ |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220629043820/https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2019/july/david-raya-signs/ |archive-date=29 June 2022 |access-date=7 July 2019 |publisher=Brentford F.C.}}</ref> after making 108 appearances for the club.<ref>{{Soccerbase}}</ref>

===Brentford===
[[File:David_Raya,_Brentford_F.C._footballer,_July_2021.jpg|thumb|Raya playing for [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]] in 2021.|250x250px]]
On 6 July 2019, Raya signed for Championship club [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]] on a four-year contract for an undisclosed fee,<ref name="SignsBrentford" /> reported to be in the region of £3 million.<ref>{{cite news |title=Blackburn Rovers goalkeeper David Raya completes Brentford move |work=Lancashire Telegraph |location=Blackburn |url=https://www.lancashiretelegraph.co.uk/sport/football/rovers/news/17753757.rovers-goalkeeper-david-raya-completes-brentford-move/ |access-date=7 July 2019}}</ref> Raya's performances during the first half of the [[2019–20 Brentford F.C. season|2019–20]] season earned him a nomination for Goalkeeper of the Year at the 2020 London Football Awards and his 16 [[Clean sheet|clean sheets]] in league matches during the season saw him share the [[English Football League|EFL]] Golden Glove award with [[Bartosz Białkowski]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Bees up for London Football Awards prizes |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2020/february/london-football-award-nominations/ |access-date=9 February 2020 |publisher=Brentford F.C.}}</ref><ref name="GoldenGlove">{{cite news |title=David Raya presented with EFL Golden Glove prize |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2020/july/david-raya-golden-glove/ |access-date=28 July 2020 |publisher=Brentford F.C.}}</ref> Raya made 49 appearances during a season which ended with a 2–1 [[2020 EFL Championship play-off Final|2020 Championship play-off Final]] defeat to [[West London derby|West London rivals]] [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]].<ref name="Soccerbase1920" /><ref>{{Cite news |title=Fulham beat Brentford to reach Premier League |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/53603848 |access-date=29 May 2021}}</ref>

Raya began the [[2021–22 Brentford F.C. season|2021–22]] season as an ever-present in [[Premier League]] matches,<ref name="Soccerbase2122" /> before suffering a [[posterior cruciate ligament injury]] during a 2–1 defeat to [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] on 24 October 2021.<ref>{{cite news |title=David Raya sustains knee injury |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2021/october/david-raya-knee-injury/ |access-date=26 October 2021 |publisher=Brentford F.C.}}</ref> He returned to outdoor training on 10 January 2022.<ref>{{cite news |title=David Raya roars back in to training |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2022/january/david-raya-roars-back-in-to-training/ |access-date=12 January 2022 |publisher=Brentford F.C.}}</ref> After a [[Behind closed doors (sport)|behind closed doors]] [[Friendly match|friendly]] appearance on 1 February 2022,<ref>{{cite news |title=Bees beaten by Aston Villa in friendly |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2022/february/bees-beaten-by-aston-villa-in-friendly/ |access-date=1 February 2022 |publisher=Brentford F.C.}}</ref> Raya made his return to competitive match play with a start in a 4–1 [[FA Cup]] fourth round defeat to [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] four days later.<ref name="Soccerbase2122" /> He was an ever-present until the end of the campaign and finished a mid-table season with 25 appearances.<ref name="Soccerbase2122" /><ref>{{cite web |title=Tables – Premier League – England – Results, fixtures, tables and news |url=https://int.soccerway.com/national/england/premier-league/20212022/regular-season/r63396/tables/ |access-date=22 May 2022 |website=Soccerway}}</ref>

Raya continued as an ever-present in league matches during the [[2022–23 Brentford F.C. season|2022–23]] season and his performances during an unbeaten January 2023 (during which Brentford challenged the [[Premier League#International competitions|European places]]) saw him nominated for the [[Premier League Player of the Month]] award.<ref name="Soccerbase2223" /><ref name="RejectContract">{{cite news |title=Raya 'happy' despite rejecting new Brentford deals |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/64593562 |access-date=10 February 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Rashford voted EA SPORTS Player of the Month |url=http://www.premierleague.com/news/3042896 |access-date=10 February 2023 |website=Premier League}}</ref> That same month, Raya rejected a second offer of a new contract.<ref name="RejectContract" /> He was a member of the club's leadership group during a 2022–23 season in which he was an ever-present in league matches.<ref name="Harris">{{cite web |last=Harris |first=Jay |title=David Raya exclusive on his future, Champions League dream and dealing with errors |url=https://theathletic.com/4248008/2023/02/26/david-raya-exclusive-interview-brentford-contract/ |url-access=subscription |access-date=14 May 2023 |website=The Athletic}}</ref><ref name="Soccerbase2223" />

== International career ==
After failing to receive a call-up by [[Spain national football team|Spain]] at youth level,<ref>{{Cite web |last=Bosher |first=Luke |title=Manchester United's David de Gea left out of Spain squad with Brentford's David Raya included instead |url=https://theathletic.com/news/manchester-uniteds-david-de-gea-left-out-of-spain-squad-with-brentfords-david-raya-included-instead/3Rz0JAXzt9Be/ |url-access=subscription |access-date=18 March 2022 |website=The Athletic |language=en}}</ref> Raya won his maiden international call-up to the senior team for a pair of friendlies in March 2022.<ref name="2022WCSquad">{{Cite web |title=Raya in Spain squad |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/en/news/article/david-raya-in-spain-squad-2022-fifa-world-cup |access-date=11 November 2022 |website=www.brentfordfc.com |language=en-gb}}</ref> He made his debut with a start in a 2–1 win over [[Albania national football team|Albania]] on 26 March and remained an unused substitute in the second match.<ref name="2022WCSquad" /> Raya was an unused substitute during the entirety of Spain's victorious [[2022–23 UEFA Nations League A|2022–23 UEFA Nations League]] campaign.<ref name="Soccerway" /><ref name="UNLWin">{{Cite news |title=Spain win Nations League final on penalties |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/65944659 |access-date=18 June 2023}}</ref> Raya was named in Spain's [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]] squad,<ref name="2022WCSquad" /> but prior to the team's exit in the round of 16,<ref>{{Cite news |title=Hakimi hits winning penalty as Morocco stun Spain |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63789751 |access-date=6 December 2022}}</ref> his only match play during the period came with a second half substitute appearance in a pre-tournament friendly versus [[Jordan national football team|Jordan]].<ref name="NFT" />

== Style of play ==
A [[sweeper-keeper]], Raya "is renowned as a vocal keeper, as well as one who is adept with the ball at his feet".<ref name="Nash">{{Cite web |last=Nash |first=Matthew |date=20 August 2021 |title=David Raya predicts more Premier League scalps for Brentford after Arsenal upset |url=https://metro.co.uk/2021/08/20/brentfords-david-raya-predicts-more-premier-league-scalps-after-arsenal-win-15122709/ |access-date=23 August 2021 |website=Metro |language=en}}</ref> He "can play out from the back and is happy covering the space in behind", which allows a team "to play with a high line".<ref name="SignsBrentford" /> As a result of his and Brentford's style of play during the [[2020–21 EFL Championship|2020–21]] season, Raya attempted 300 more passes than any other goalkeeper in the Championship.<ref name="Nash" />

==Personal life==
Raya grew up in [[Pallejà]] and is a [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]] supporter.<ref name="Marshall" /><ref>{{Cite web |title=Get to Know: David Raya |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2019/july/david-raya-10-things/ |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220716122504/https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2019/july/david-raya-10-things/ |archive-date=16 July 2022 |access-date=7 July 2019 |publisher=Brentford F.C.}}</ref>
