'''Pervis Josué Estupiñán Tenorio''' ({{ipa-es|estupiˈɲan}}) (born 21 January 1998) is an [[Ecuador|Ecuadorian]] professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[left-back]] for {{English football updater|Brigh&HA}} club [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]] and the [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador national team]].

==Club career==
Born in [[Esmeraldas, Ecuador|Esmeraldas]], Estupiñán joined [[L.D.U. Quito|LDU Quito]]'s youth setup in 2011, aged 13. He was promoted to the first team ahead of the [[2015 Campeonato Ecuatoriano de Fútbol Serie A|2015 season]], and made his professional debut on 1 February of that year by starting in a 1–0 home win against [[C.D. El Nacional|El Nacional]].

=== Watford and following loans ===
An undisputed starter for the club, Estupiñán was sold to [[Premier League]] club [[Watford F.C.|Watford]] on 29 July 2016,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wdsport.co.uk/2016/07/watford-snare-trio-of-exciting-young-talent/|title=Watford snare trio of exciting young talent|publisher=WD Sport|date=29 July 2016|accessdate=18 March 2017}}</ref> being immediately loaned to [[Granada CF]] and assigned to the latter's [[Granada CF B|B-team]] in [[Segunda División B]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.granadacfweb.com/2016/07/29/pervis-estupinan-fichara-por-el-filial-rojiblanco/|title=Pervis Estupiñán fichará por el filial rojiblanco|trans-title=Pervis Estupiñán will sign for the reserve team ''rojiblanco''|publisher=Granada CF Web|language=es|date=29 July 2016|accessdate=18 March 2017}}</ref>

Estupiñán made his [[La Liga]] debut on 5 April 2017, starting in a 0–0 away draw against [[Deportivo de La Coruña]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marca.com/eventos/marcador/futbol/2016_17/la-liga/jornada_30/dep_gda/|title=Ochoa no firma la rendición|trans-title=Ochoa does not sign the surrender|publisher=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|language=es|date=5 April 2017|accessdate=9 August 2017}}</ref> On 17 July, he was loaned to [[Segunda División]] side [[UD Almería]] for one year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.udalmeriasad.com/noticia/el-almeria-logra-del-watford-ingles-la-cesion-del-ecuatoriano-pervis-estupinan|title=El Almería logra del Watford inglés la cesión del ecuatoriano Pervis Estupiñán|trans-title=Almería get from English Watford the loan of Ecuadorian Pervis Estupiñán|publisher=UD Almería|language=es|date=17 July 2017|accessdate=18 July 2017}}</ref>

On 9 August 2018, Estupiñán agreed to a one-year loan deal with [[RCD Mallorca]] in the second division.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rcdmallorca.es/contenidos/noticias/noticia/pervis-estupinan-ficha-por-el-rcd-mallorca|title=Pervis Estupiñán ficha por el RCD Mallorca|trans-title=Pervis Estupiñán signs for RCD Mallorca|publisher=RCD Mallorca|language=es|date=9 August 2018|access-date=12 August 2018}}</ref> The following 3 July, after [[2018–19 Segunda División|achieving promotion]], he signed a two-year loan deal with [[CA Osasuna]], also freshly promoted.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.osasuna.es/noticia/_/13096/03-07-2019-osasuna-se-refuerza-con-pervis-estupinan.html|title=Osasuna se refuerza con Pervis Estupiñán|trans-title=Osasuna bolster with Pervis Estupiñán|publisher=CA Osasuna|language=es|date=3 July 2019|accessdate=4 July 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190703102724/https://www.osasuna.es/noticia/_/13096/03-07-2019-osasuna-se-refuerza-con-pervis-estupinan.html|archive-date=3 July 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref>

=== Villarreal ===

On 16 September 2020, Estupiñán signed a seven-year contract with Spanish side [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]] for an initial fee of £15 million.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.villarrealcf.es/noticias/todas-las-noticias/item/28508-bienvenido-estupinan|title=¡BIENVENIDO, ESTUPIÑÁN!|date=16 September 2020|accessdate=16 September 2020|publisher=Villarreal CF|language=Spanish}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.watfordfc.com/news/official-estupinan-joins-villarreal|title=Official: Estupiñán Joins Villarreal}}</ref>

=== Brighton & Hove Albion ===

On 16 August 2022, Estupiñán signed a five-year contract with [[Premier League]] side [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]] for an undisclosed fee.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/2746649/pervis-estupinan-joins-from-villarreal|title=Pervis Estupinan joins from Villarreal|date=16 August 2022|access-date=16 August 2022|website=www.brightonandhovealbion.com|last=Hanson |first=Charlie}}</ref> He made his debut four days later, coming on and replacing [[Adam Lallana]] in the 63rd minute in the 2–0 away win over [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/62537746|title=West Ham 0-2 Brighton & Hove Albion: David Moyes 'concerned' by toothless start|date=21 August 2022|access-date=2 September 2022|website=BBC Sport|last=Mann |first=Mantej}}</ref> Estupiñán made his first start for the ''Seagulls'' on 27 August, helping Brighton to another win and clean sheet in the 1–0 home victory over [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Howarth |first=Matthew|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/62614441|title=Brighton & Hove Albion 1-0 Leeds United: Pascel Gross strikes as Seagulls set club record - BBC Sport|publisher=BBC Sport Sport|date=27 August 2022|access-date=2 September 2022}}</ref> Estupiñán assisted [[2022 FIFA World Cup|returning World Cup champion]] [[Alexis Mac Allister|Alexis Mac Allister's]] flick-in in the 5–1 away victory over [[EFL Championship]] side [[Middlesbrough F.C.|Middlesbrough]] in the [[2022–23 FA Cup #third round|FA Cup third round]] on 7 January 2023.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/3012703/five-star-albion-cruise-into-the-fourth-round|title=Five-star Albion cruise into the fourth round|website=Brighton & Hove Albion|date=7 January 2023|access-date=21 January 2023}}</ref> Two weeks later, on his 25th birthday, he made a brace of assists, setting up both [[Kaoru Mitoma|Kaoru Mitoma's]] opening goal and [[Evan Ferguson|Evan Ferguson's]] late equaliser in the 2–2 away draw at [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] in the league.<ref>{{cite news|last=Talbot |first=Bruce|url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/3031868/its-fergie-time-as-evans-late-leveller-lifts-albion|title=It's Fergie time as Evan's late leveller lifts Albion|publisher=Brighton & Hove Albion|date=21 January 2023|access-date=21 January 2023}}</ref> On 29 January, Estupiñán added to his three recent assists to make it four in as many games. Lobbing in a cross to the back post, finding Mitoma who brought the ball under control and fired ''Albion'' to a 2–1 victory over [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] to advance to the fifth round of the [[2022–23 FA Cup|FA Cup]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/3040813/its-magic-from-mitoma-as-albion-beat-liverpool-again|title=It's magic from Mitoma as Albion beat Liverpool again|publisher=Brighton & Hove Albion|date=29 January 2023|access-date=29 January 2023}}</ref> Two games later on 11 February, he added a further assist to his tally, with his searching delivery finding [[Solly March]] who fired in his effort to open the scoreline in the eventual 1–1 away draw against rivals Crystal Palace. Early on in the game he had a goal wrongly ruled out by [[John Brooks (referee)|John Brooks]] of [[video assistant referee|VAR]] after the official drew the offside lines against the wrong player. Brooks was later dropped for his next two matches.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Crystal Palace 1-1 Brighton & Hove Albion: Seagulls held to frustrating draw - BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/64520926|date=11 February 2023|access-date=11 February 2023|website=BBC Sport |language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Decision made as VAR official punished after Brighton blunder at Crystal Palace - including Liverpool v Everton and Arsenal v Man City suspension|url=https://www.sussexexpress.co.uk/sport/football/brighton-and-hove-albion/decision-made-as-var-official-punished-after-brighton-blunder-at-crystal-palace-including-liverpool-v-everton-and-arsenal-v-man-city-suspension-4024692|date=13 February 2023|access-date=18 February 2023|website=Sussex Express |language=en-GB}}</ref> Estupiñán went on to score his first goal for the ''Albion'' on 14 May, hitting in stoppage time, scoring the third in the 3–0 away win at [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]], whilst assisting [[Julio Enciso (footballer, born 2004)|Julio Enciso's]] opener.<ref>{{Cite web |title=It's three and easy as magnificent Albion move up to sixth
|url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/3345249/its-three-and-easy-as-magnificent-albion-move-up-to-sixth|date=14 May 2023|access-date=14 May 2023|website=Brighton & Hove Albion |language=en-GB}}</ref>

==International career==
Estupiñán made his debut for the [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador national team]] on 13 October 2019 in a friendly against Argentina that ended in 6–1 loss.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[ESPN]]|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/lineups?gameId=557810|title=Ecuador v Argentina game report|date=13 October 2019}}</ref>

On 14 November, Estupiñán was named in Ecuador's 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] alongside Brighton teammates [[Jeremy Sarmiento]] and [[Moisés Caicedo]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/mundial/nota/_/id/11228629/ecuador-lista-convocados-qatar-2022-mundial-copa-mundo |title=Ecuador, el último en entregar la lista de los 26 jugadores convocados para la Copa del Mundo de Qatar 2022 |trans-title=Ecuador, the last to deliver the list of the 26 players summoned for the Qatar World Cup 2022 |work=[[ESPN Deportes]] |date=14 November 2022 |access-date=15 November 2022 |language=es}}</ref>
