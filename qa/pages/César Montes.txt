'''César Jasib Montes Castro''' (born 24 February 1997) is a Mexican professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Centre-back|centre-back]] for [[Segunda División]] club [[RCD Espanyol|Espanyol]] and the [[Mexico national football team|Mexico national team]].

==Club career==
===Monterrey===
During a youth league playoff match with Pob. Miguel Alemán against [[C.F. Monterrey|Monterrey]], he caught the attention of the opposing team.<ref>{{cite web|title=ENTREVISTA: "Que nadie les diga que no lo pueden lograr": Cesar Jasib Montes|url=http://www.triunfodeportivo.com/vernoticiasanteriores.php?artid=5263&categoria=5|work=Triunfodeportivo.com|accessdate=20 September 2018}}</ref> He was eventually brought onto Monterrey's youth team and on 2 August 2015, Montes played his first match with Monterrey against Portuguese team [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]] on the inauguration of the [[Estadio BBVA Bancomer]]. Montes came in as a substitute for the second half, and at the 48th minute he scored the first ever-goal in the Estadio BBVA Bancomer. Monterrey defeated [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]] 3–0.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rayados goleó a Benfica en inauguración|url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/mexico/noticias/2015/08/03/rayados-goleo-a-benfica-en-inauguracion|work=Medio Tiempo|first=Sahid|last=Hernández Rosete|date=2 August 2015|accessdate=20 September 2018}}</ref>

Montes made his official debut for Monterrey on 29 July 2015, against [[Correcaminos UAT]] in an [[Apertura 2015 Copa MX]] group stage match, where Monterrey defeated Correcaminos 3–1.<ref>{{cite web|title=César Montes, inscrito en la historia de Rayados con 1er gol en nuevo estadio|url=http://espndeportes.espn.com/noticias/nota/_/id/2438080/cesar-montes-inscrito-en-la-historia-de-rayados-con-1er-gol-en-nuevo-estadio|work=ESPN Deportes|first=Alan|last=Rafalowski|date=3 August 2015|accessdate=20 September 2018}}</ref> At the end of the [[2015–16 Liga MX season#Torneo Clausura|2016 Clausura]], he was listed in the Best XI.<ref name="clausura">{{cite web|title=Lo Mejor del Clausura 2016 |url=http://www.ligabancomer.mx/cancha/detallenoticia/16543/revista-digital-liga-bancomer-mx-lo-mejor-del-clausura-2016 |language=Spanish|website=LigaMX.net |publisher=Liga Bancomer MX |accessdate=6 June 2016}}</ref>

At the end of December 2019, he would win the [[2019–20 Liga MX season#Torneo Apertura|2019 Apertura]] finals against [[Club América|América]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Monterrey defeat Club America on penalties to win Liga MX 2019 Apertura title|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/report?gameId=560779|work=ESPN|first=Tom|last=Marshall|date=30 December 2019}}</ref>

With Monterrey's victory of the [[2019–20 Copa MX]], they had obtained the [[Treble (association football)|continental treble]].<ref>{{cite web|title=¡TRI-PLE-TE! {{!}} Rayados consiguió triplete y ahora va por el campeonato del Guard1anes 2020|url=https://www.90min.com/es/posts/tri-ple-te-rayados-consiguio-triplete-y-ahora-va-por-el-campeonato-del-guard1anes-2020|work=90 Min|language=Spanish|date=5 November 2020}}</ref>

'''Clásico Regiomontano'''

[[Clásico Regiomontano]] matches against crosstown rivals [[Tigres UANL]] have proven to be significant for Montes, scoring three goals against them to date.<ref>{{cite web|title=César Montes, la pesadilla de Tigres|url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/2017/04/22/cesar-montes-la-pesadilla-de-tigres|work=Medio Tiempo|first=Jessika|last=Méndez|date=22 April 2017|accessdate=6 November 2018}}</ref> He would make his league debut on 19 September 2015 against Tigres UANL, contributing a 40-meter long ball assist to [[Rogelio Funes Mori]] to score the only goal for Monterrey where they lost Monterrey lost 3–1.<ref>{{cite web|title=Se lleva Tigres el Clásico regio|url=https://nnc.mx/articulo/Deportes/se-lleva-tigres-el-clasico-regio/1442740781|work=Nnc.mx|first=Fernando|last=Vanegas|date=20 September 2015|accessdate=20 September 2018}}</ref>  On 14 May 2016, he would score Monterrey's only goal of the second leg of the [[Clausura 2016 Liga MX championship stage|Clausura 2016 championship]] quarter-final against Tigres UANL, losing 2–1, but with an aggregate score of 4–3 in favor of Monterrey to allow them move onto the semi-final against [[Club América|América]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Cardiaco pase de Monterrey; elimina con polémica a Tigres|url=https://www.excelsior.com.mx/adrenalina/2016/05/14/1092617|work=Excelsior|date=15 May 2016|accessdate=20 September 2018}}</ref> On 29 October 2016, he would tie the game against Tigres UANL, 1–1.<ref>{{cite web|title=Tigres vs Rayados de Monterrey (1-1): resumen, resultado y goles|url=https://mexico.as.com/mexico/2016/10/29/futbol/1477753363_397506.html?omnaut=1|work=Mexico.as.com|date=30 October 2016|accessdate=20 September 2018}}</ref> On 21 April 2017, Montes scored the only goal in a 1–0 victory over Tigres UANL.<ref>{{cite web|title=César Montes, la pesadilla de Tigres|url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/2017/04/22/cesar-montes-la-pesadilla-de-tigres|work=Medio Tiempo|first=Jessika|last=Méndez|date=22 April 2017|accessdate=20 September 2018}}</ref>

===Espanyol===
On 27 December 2022, Montes moved abroad and joined [[La Liga]] side [[RCD Espanyol]], with a contract until 2028.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rcdespanyol.com/en/new/cesar-montes-joins-rcd-espanyol/14997|title=César Montes joins RCD Espanyol|publisher=RCD Espanyol|date=27 December 2022|accessdate=29 December 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
===Youth===
Montes was part of the roster that participated in the [[2018 Toulon Tournament]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Revelan convocatoria del Tri Sub-21 para el torneo Esperanzas de Toulon|url=http://www.espn.com.mx/futbol/mundial/nota/_/id/4333245/revelan-convocatoria-del-tri-sub-21-para-el-torneo-esperanzas-de-toulon|work=ESPN|date=22 May 2018|accessdate=19 September 2018}}</ref> He would go on to appear in all group stage matches including the semi-final, except for the final where Mexico would go to lose 1–2 against [[England national under-21 football team|England]] since he picked up an injury in the previous match against [[Turkey national under-21 football team|Turkey]].<ref>{{cite web|title=César Montes se perderá la Final de Toulon|url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/2018/06/06/cesar-montes-se-perdera-la-final-de-toulon|work=Medio Tiempo|date=6 June 2018|accessdate=19 September 2018}}</ref> He would go on to be included in the Best XI team of the tournament.<ref name="toulon">{{cite web|title=L'équipe type du Festival International Espoirs 2018 |url=https://festival-foot-espoirs.com/festival/detail_actualite.php?id=3391 |date=11 June 2018 |publisher=Toulon Tournament |language=French}}</ref>

[[File:Coréia do Sul x México - Futebol masculino - Olimpíada Rio 2016 (28866553346).jpg|thumb|upright=1.20|Montes playing against [[South Korea national under-23 football team|South Korea]] in the 2016 Summer Olympics]]
On 7 July 2016, Montes was named in Mexico's 21-man squad that would participate in the [[Football at the 2016 Summer Olympics|2016 Summer Olympics]] in [[Rio de Janeiro]], Brazil.<ref>{{cite web|title=Definida la convocatoria del Tri para Juegos Olímpicos |url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/2016/07/07/definida-la-convocatoria-del-tri-para-juegos-olimpicos |work=Medio Tiempo |language=Spanish |accessdate=7 July 2016}}</ref> He would appear in all of Mexico's group stage matches but Mexico would finish third in their group and were thus eliminated from the competition.

Ruled out for the [[2020 CONCACAF Men's Olympic Qualifying Championship]] as it was not a FIFA-sanctioned tournament, Montes was called up to participate in the [[2020 Summer Olympics]]. He won the [[bronze medal]] with the Olympic team.<ref>{{cite web|title=Revelan lista oficial de la Selección Sub-23 que acudirá a Tokio 2020|date=5 July 2021|url=https://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/selecciones/nota/_/id/8854207/mexico-sub23-convocatoria-jaime-lozano-tokio-2020}}</ref>

===Senior===
Montes was called up by the [[Mexico national football team|senior national team]] to participate in the [[2017 CONCACAF Gold Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Lista la convocatoria de México para la Copa Oro|url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/copa-oro/2017/06/29/59542cb0ca4741ab508b4609.html|work=Marca|date=28 June 2017|accessdate=30 October 2018}}</ref> He would make his debut on 13 July 2017 during a group stage match against [[Jamaica national football team|Jamaica]], coming in as a substitute for [[Rodolfo Pizarro]] at half-time, finishing the game at 0–0.<ref>{{cite web|title=Debuta César Montes con la Selección Mexicana|url=http://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/mundial/nota/_/id/3389954/debuta-cesar-montes-con-la-seleccion-mexicana|work=ESPN|date=13 July 2017|accessdate=30 October 2018}}</ref>

On 6 June 2019, Montes was called up by coach [[Gerardo Martino]] to participate in the [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup|CONCACAF Gold Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Selección Mexicana anuncia lista definitiva para Copa Oro 2019|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/copa-oro/seleccion-mexicana-anuncia-lista-definitiva-copa-oro-2019|date=6 June 2019|work=MedioTiempo.com}}</ref> He appeared as a substitute in the [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup Group A|group stage]] match against [[Cuba national football team|Cuba]] and as a starter in the final group stage match against [[Martinique national football team|Martinique]] as Mexico went on to win the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mexico Turns Tide, Wins Gold Cup Title Again vs. Wasteful USMNT|url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2019/07/07/mexico-usa-gold-cup-title-dos-santos-pizarro-pulisic-berhalter|publisher=[[Sports Illustrated]]|first=Brian|last=Straus|date=7 July 2019}}</ref>

In October 2022, Montes was named in Mexico's preliminary 31-man [[Mexico national football team|squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], and in November, he was ultimately included in the final 26-man roster.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lista de convocados de la Selección Mexicana al Mundial de Qatar 2022|url=https://www.tvazteca.com/aztecadeportes/lista-oficial-convocados-seleccion-mexicana-mundial-qatar-2022|work=Tvazteca.com|first=Omar|last=Moro|date=14 November 2022|language=Spanish}}</ref>

==Style of play==
A ball-playing defender that has shown maturity despite at a young age, Montes has a great range of passing, and has shown on a few occasions that he's able to pick out long-range passes, can execute a well-timed, and accurate, slide tackle, has shown he is capable of contributing goals in set-pieces, and is good in the air and able to deal with high balls into the box.<ref>{{cite web|title=Cesar Montes, Javier Eduardo Lopez and Alexis Vega's El Tri claims|url=http://www.espnfc.us/team/mexico/203/blog/post/2856406/cesar-montes-javier-eduardo-lopez-and-alexis-vega-el-tri-claims|work=ESPN|first=Cesar|last=Hernandez|date=23 April 2016|accessdate=8 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Scout Report: César Montes Monterrey's young defensive jewel|url=http://outsideoftheboot.com/2016/09/07/scout-report-cesar-montes/|work=Outsideoftheboot.com|first=Thomas|last=Harrison|date=7 September 2016|accessdate=20 September 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Lozano, Pineda, Montes, Pizarro and Aguirre are El Tri's ones to watch|url=http://www.espn.com.au/football/mexican-liga-mx/22/blog/post/3028049/hirving-lozano-orbelin-pineda-cesar-montes-rodolfo-pizarro-and-erick-aguirre-are-el-tris-ones-to-watch|work=ESPN|first=Tom|last=Marshall|date=30 December 2016|accessdate=11 October 2018}}</ref>
