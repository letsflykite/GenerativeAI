'''Ante Budimir''' ({{IPA-sh|ǎːnte bûdimiːr|hr}};<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hjp.znanje.hr/index.php?show=search_by_id&id=fVhlXw%3D%3D|title=Àntūn|quote=Ánte|website=Hrvatski jezični portal|access-date=19 March 2018|language=sh}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://hjp.znanje.hr/index.php?show=search_by_id&id=f15hXhk%3D|title=búditi|quote=Bȕdimīr|website=Hrvatski jezični portal|access-date=19 March 2018|language=sh}}</ref> born 22 July 1991) is a Croatian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[forward (association football)|forward]] for [[La Liga]] club [[CA Osasuna|Osasuna]] and the [[Croatia national football team|Croatia national team]].

==Club career==
===FC St. Pauli===
In August 2014, Budimir joined German club [[FC St. Pauli]] of the [[2. Bundesliga]] on a four-year deal until 2018.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.fcstpauli.com/profis/news/5787 | title = Ante Budimir wechselt zum FC St. Pauli | language = German | trans-title = Ante Budimir transfers to FC St. Pauli | publisher = [[FC St. Pauli]] | access-date = 9 September 2014 | date = 4 August 2014 }}</ref> St. Pauli had to pay a transfer fee believed to be around €900,000.<ref>{{cite news | url = http://www.abendblatt.de/sport/fussball/st-pauli/article131365722/Noethe-und-Budimir-sind-St-Paulis-Sturmduo-der-Hoffnung.html | title = Nöthe und Budimir sind St. Paulis Sturmduo der Hoffnung | language = German | trans-title = Nöthe and Budimir are St. Pauli's striker duo of hope | newspaper = [[Hamburger Abendblatt]] | access-date = 9 September 2014 | date = 19 August 2014 }}</ref> In an interview on Budimir's signing, former Croatian international [[Jurica Vranješ]] described him as "tall, strong in the air, and reliable in combinations" and compared his style to [[Dimitar Berbatov]].<ref>{{cite news | url = http://www.bild.de/sport/fussball/st-pauli/budimir-ist-ein-typ-wie-berbatov-37108166.bild.html | title = Kroaten-Star Vranjes lobt Paulis Neuen: "Budimir ist ein Typ wie Berbatov" | language = German | trans-title = Croatia-Star Vranjes praises Pauli's new arrival  | newspaper = [[Bild]] | access-date = 9 September 2014 | date = 5 August 2014 }}</ref> Budimir had a tough time at St. Pauli, scoring one goal in 20 appearances in the St. Pauli shirt.

===Crotone and Sampdoria===
On 1 September 2015, Budimir was loaned out to Italian club [[F.C. Crotone|Crotone]] for the remainder of the season.<ref>{{cite web|title=Leihe: Budimir wandert nach Kalabrien aus|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/intligen/intwettbewerbe/634188/artikel_leihe_budimir-wandert-nach-kalabrien-aus.html|work=[[Kicker (sports magazine)|kicker]]|access-date=10 September 2015|date=1 September 2015|language=German}}</ref> He made his Crotone debut on 7 September 2015, in a 4–0 loss to [[Cagliari Calcio]], coming on as a 71st-minute substitute for [[Pietro De Giorgio]]. In March 2016, Crotone exercised their €1 million buyout option on the player. Budimir ended the season as Crotone's top goalscorer with 16 goals in 40 [[Serie B]] appearances, as they were promoted as runners-up to Cagliari; this tally was fourth for goalscorers in the whole league season.<ref name=nou>{{cite news |last1=Morozzo |first1=Angelo |title=Ex Crotone, Budimir show: doppietta al Camp Nou|trans-title=Ex Crotone, Budimir show: brace at the Camp Nou |url=http://www.calabriasport24.net/ex-crotone-budimir-show-doppietta-al-camp-nou/ |access-date=2 February 2020 |publisher=Calabria Sport 24 |date=8 December 2019 |language=Italian}}</ref>

In June 2016, ahead of Crotone's debut [[Serie A]] season, [[Sampdoria]] of the same league activated Budimir's release clause believed to be in the region of €1.8 million, and the player signed a deal ending in mid-2020.<ref>{{cite news |title=Sampdoria snap up Budimir |url=https://www.football-italia.net/86345/sampdoria-snap-budimir |access-date=2 February 2020 |publisher=Football Italia |date=24 June 2016}}</ref> A year later, he was sent back to Crotone on a one-year loan with obligation to buy.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[U.C. Sampdoria]]|url=http://www.sampdoria.it/budimir-torna-al-crotone-titolo-temporaneo-con-obbligo-di-riscatto/|title=BUDIMIR TORNA AL CROTONE A TITOLO TEMPORANEO CON OBBLIGO DI RISCATTO|date=5 July 2017|language=Italian}}</ref> The obligation was fulfilled by now relegated Crotone at the end of the season, and he remained in the club on a permanent contract.

===Mallorca===
On 15 January 2019, Budimir moved on loan to Spanish club [[RCD Mallorca|Mallorca]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://m.tuttomercatoweb.com/serie-b/ufficiale-crotone-budimir-va-in-prestito-al-maiorca-1202302|access-date=15 January 2019|title=UFFICIALE: Crotone, Budimir va in prestito al Maiorca|language=Italian}}</ref> He scored his first goal for the [[Balearic Islands|Balearic]] club on 3 February as a [[Panenka (penalty kick)|Panenka penalty kick]] in a 2–0 home win over [[AD Alcorcón]], later being sent off.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Bryce |first1=Monro |title=Mallorca seventh after 2-0 win |url=https://www.majorcadailybulletin.com/sports/sports/2019/02/06/54535/mallorca-seventh-after-win.html |access-date=2 February 2020 |work=Majorca Daily Bulletin |date=6 February 2019}}</ref> On 27 June, after [[2018–19 Segunda División|achieving promotion]] to [[La Liga]] – he scored the opening goal as they overturned a 2–0 first-leg deficit to defeat [[Deportivo de La Coruña]] 3–2 on aggregate in the play-off final – he signed a permanent deal for a €2.2 million fee.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.marca.com/futbol/mallorca/2019/06/27/5d14dde6e2704e9ca48b4628.html|title=El Mallorca 'ficha' a Budimir|trans-title=Mallorca 'sign' Budimir|newspaper=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|language=es|date=27 June 2019|access-date=4 July 2019}}</ref>

During the [[2019–20 La Liga|2019–20]] season Budimir scored 13 goals,<ref name="osasuna"/> which also placed him as the 8th best 2019–20 La Liga top scorer.{{citation needed|date=October 2020}}

===Osasuna===
On 5 October 2020, Budimir was loaned to top tier side [[CA Osasuna]] for the [[2020–21 La Liga|2020–21 season]].<ref name="osasuna">{{cite web|url=https://www.osasuna.es/noticia/osasuna-logra-la-cesion-de-budimir|title=Osasuna logra la cesión de Budimir|trans-title=Osasuna get the loan of Budimir|publisher=CA Osasuna|language=es|date=5 October 2020|access-date=5 October 2020}}</ref>

On 7 June 2021, Osasuna announced the signing of Budimir on a permanent deal until June 2025.<ref name="osasuna signing">{{cite web|url=https://www.osasuna.es/en/news/osasuna-signs-budimir-until-2025|title=Osasuna ficha a Budimir, con el que firma una vinculación hasta 2025|trans-title=Osasuna signs Budimir until 2025|publisher=CA Osasuna|language=en|date=7 June 2021|access-date=8 June 2021}}</ref>

==International career==
On 27 August 2020, during pre-season training, Budimir was called up by the [[Croatia national football team|Croatia national team]] coach [[Zlatko Dalić]] for September [[2020–21 UEFA Nations League A#Group 3|Nations League]] clashes against [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]] and [[France national football team|France]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.diariodemallorca.es/rcd-mallorca/2020/08/27/budimir-sustituye-modric-rakitic-convocatoria/1534235.html |title=Budimir to replace Modrić and Rakitić| date=27 August 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.croatiaweek.com/luka-modric-and-ivan-rakitic-to-miss-croatias-opening-uefa-nations-league-matches/ |title=Luka Modrić and Ivan Rakitić to miss Croatia's opening UEFA Nations League matches |publisher=croatiaweek |date= 27 August 2020}}</ref> He made his national team debut on 7 October in a friendly 2–1 victory over [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]], providing [[Mario Pašalić]] with an assist for the winning goal.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Olivari|first=Davorin|date=7 October 2020|title=Hrvatska bez pola prve momčadi srušila Švicarsku! Zablistao 'Dalićev eksperiment', na kraju ušao i Modrić|url=https://sportske.jutarnji.hr/sn/nogomet/reprezentacija/hrvatska-bez-pola-prve-momcadi-srusila-svicarsku-zablistao-dalicev-eksperiment-na-kraju-usao-i-modric-15023796|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201021102941/https://sportske.jutarnji.hr/sn/nogomet/reprezentacija/hrvatska-bez-pola-prve-momcadi-srusila-svicarsku-zablistao-dalicev-eksperiment-na-kraju-usao-i-modric-15023796 |archive-date=21 October 2020 |access-date=|website=Sportske novosti|language=hr-hr}}</ref> He scored his debut goal on 11 November in a friendly 3–3 draw with [[Turkey national football team|Turkey]]. He notably handled the ball with his elbow during the build-up to the goal, which referee [[Slavko Vinčić]] failed to notice.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=11 November 2020|title=Ante Budimir: Mogao sam zabiti još koji gol|url=https://www.vecernji.hr/sport/ante-budimir-mogao-sam-zabiti-jos-koji-gol-1445402|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=11 November 2020|website=Večernji list|language=hr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Hina|date=11 November 2020|title=Budimir: Svjestan sam da je bio VAR, gola ne bi bilo|url=https://www.glasistre.hr/sport/budimir-svjestan-sam-da-je-bio-var-gola-ne-bi-bilo-679287|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=11 November 2020|website=Glas Istre|language=hr}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Budimir's family comes from the village of [[Ozimica]] in [[Bosnia and Herzegovina]], and he was born in [[Zenica]] as it was the nearest town with an adequate hospital. Fleeing the war, the family moved to [[Croatia]] when Budimir was 6 months old.<ref>[https://www.ara.cat/esports/rcd-espanyol/gent-dels-balcans-cami-triomfar-futbol_128_4363931.html Ante Budimir: “La gent dels Balcans sabem que el camí per triomfar en el futbol és llarg”] at ara.cat</ref>
