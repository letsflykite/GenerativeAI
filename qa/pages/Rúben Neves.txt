'''Rúben Diogo da Silva Neves''' (born 13 March 1997) is a Portuguese professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[defensive midfielder]] for [[Saudi Professional League]] club [[Al Hilal SFC|Al-Hilal]] and the [[Portugal national football team|Portugal national team]].

Neves started his career with [[FC Porto|Porto]] and made his first-team debut at the age of 17. He joined [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]] in 2017 for a reported transfer fee of £15.8 million. He  made 253 appearances and scored 30 goals for them, winning the [[EFL Championship]] in his [[2017–18 EFL Championship|first season]]. In 2023, he signed for Al-Hilal for a fee of £47 million.

Neves made more than 60 appearances for Portugal's national youth teams. He made his senior international debut for Portugal at the age of 18 in 2015, and was part of their squad at [[UEFA Euro 2020|Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]].

==Club career==
===Porto===
[[File:DK-Porto15 (4).jpg|thumb|upright|left|Neves playing for [[FC Porto|Porto]] in 2015]]

Born in [[Mozelos (Santa Maria da Feira)|Mozelos]], [[Aveiro District]], Neves grew up supporting [[FC Porto]] and joined the club's youth system at the age of eight.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2018/sep/21/ruben-neves-diogo-jota-wolves-porto-cricket-interview|title=Wolves' Rúben Neves and Diogo Jota: 'Porto has wine, food but here it's fine'|newspaper=The Guardian|location=London|first=Stuart|last=James|date=21 September 2018|access-date=5 November 2018}}</ref><ref name=PortuGoal>{{cite web|url=https://portugoal.net/on-the-rise/356-profile-of-porto-prodigy-ruben-neves|title=Profile of Porto prodigy Rúben Neves|website=PortuGOAL|date=24 December 2015|access-date=5 November 2018}}</ref> During his time with the academy, he also spent a season on [[Loan (association football)|loan]] at [[Padroense F.C.|Padroense]] which acted as the under-16 team;<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/newsid=2132818.html|title=The rapid rise of Rúben Neves|publisher=UEFA|last=Pimentel|first=José Nuno|access-date=20 August 2014|date=17 August 2014}}</ref> on his time in the youth structures, [[FC Porto B|reserve team]] manager [[Luís Castro (footballer, born 1961)|Luís Castro]] described him as a player with "extraordinary mental qualities, to go along with technical and tactical skills".<ref name=PortuGoal/> 

Ahead of the [[2014–15 FC Porto season|2014–15 season]], Neves was earmarked to bypass Porto's under-19s and play with the reserves instead. Following an injury to teammate [[Mikel Agu]], however, he was called by [[Julen Lopetegui]] to be part of the main squad's pre-season preparations.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.ojogo.pt/futebol/1a-liga/porto/noticias/interior/ruben-neves-tem-um-talento-mental-extraordinario-4070889.html|title=Rúben Neves tem um talento mental extraordinário|trans-title=Rúben Neves has an extraordinary mental talent|newspaper=O Jogo |location=Porto|language=pt|date=8 August 2014|access-date=21 August 2014}}</ref> On 15 August 2014, aged 17 years and five months, he made his [[Primeira Liga]] debut where he started and scored in a 2–0 home win against [[C.S. Marítimo|Marítimo]] – in doing so, he became the youngest player in the club's history to score a goal in the competition.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.maisfutebol.iol.pt/liga-fc-porto-maritimo-cronica/53ee72880cf2c1dfdf15bb68.html|title=FC Porto-Marítimo, 2–0 (crónica)|trans-title=FC Porto-Marítimo, 2–0 (report)|website=Maisfutebol|last=Almeida|first=Germano|language=pt|date=15 August 2014|access-date=18 August 2014}}</ref> He played his first match in the [[UEFA Champions League]] five days later in a 1–0 win over [[Lille OSC|Lille]], and broke another record, previously held by [[Cristiano Ronaldo]], by being the youngest Portuguese to appear in the tournament.<ref name=PortuGoal/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.maisfutebol.iol.pt/liga-campeoes-fc-porto-ruben-neves-cristiano-ronaldo-lille-play-off/53f4eee90cf2103fd4482b11.html|title=Rúben Neves o português mais novo de sempre a jogar na ''Champions''|trans-title=Rúben Neves the youngest Portuguese ever to play in the Champions|website=Maisfutebol|language=pt|date=20 August 2014|access-date=21 August 2014}}</ref>

On 10 December 2014, during a [[2014–15 UEFA Champions League|Champions League group stage]] fixture against [[FC Shakhtar Donetsk|Shakhtar Donetsk]], Neves suffered an injury to his right knee following a collision in midfield with [[Alex Teixeira]]. At the end of the game, the club explained that he suffered a sprain, with apparent damage to the internal lateral ligament.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.ojogo.pt/futebol/1a-liga/porto/noticias/interior/ruben-neves-com-lesao-no-joelho-direito-4288722.html|title=Rúben Neves com lesão no joelho direito|trans-title=Rúben Neves with right knee injury|newspaper=O Jogo |location=Porto |language=pt|date=10 December 2014|access-date=4 January 2015}}</ref> After spending nearly a month on the sidelines, he returned to the pitch in a 3–1 home win over [[C.F. União|União da Madeira]] in the [[Taça da Liga]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.record.xl.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-nos/fc-porto/detalhe/ruben-neves-regressa-aos-convocados-924835.html|title=Rúben Neves regressa aos convocados|trans-title=Rúben Neves returns to squad|newspaper=Record |location=Lisbon|language=pt|date=12 January 2015|access-date=23 March 2016}}</ref> Following his injury, his role in midfield was filled by [[Casemiro]] and he spent the majority of the remainder of the season behind the Brazilian in the pecking order.<ref name=PortuGoal/>

On 20 October 2015, aged 18 years and 221 days, Neves became the youngest player to start as [[Captain (association football)|captain]] in the Champions League, helping Porto to a 2–0 win against [[Maccabi Tel Aviv F.C.|Maccabi Tel Aviv]] in [[2015–16 UEFA Champions League|the group stage]] and surpassing previous record holder [[Rafael van der Vaart]], who held it since 16 September 2003.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/newsid=2296033.html|title=Porto's Rúben Neves, Iker Casillas make history|publisher=UEFA|date=20 October 2015|access-date=21 October 2015}}</ref> On 5 December, in a 2–1 win over [[F.C. Paços de Ferreira|Paços de Ferreira]] also at the [[Estádio do Dragão]], he broke another record by becoming the youngest player to make 50 appearances for the club, surpassing players like [[Fernando Gomes (Portuguese footballer)|Fernando Gomes]] and [[Jaime Magalhães]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.ojogo.pt/futebol/1a-liga/porto/noticias/interior/ruben-neves-e-o-mais-jovem-a-completar-50-jogos-4917762.html|title=Rúben Neves é o mais jovem a completar 50 jogos|trans-title=Rúben Neves is the youngest to complete 50 games|newspaper=O Jogo |location=Porto |language=pt|date=6 December 2015|access-date=6 December 2015}}</ref>

==International career==
===Youth===
[[File:Ruben Neves avec la selection.jpg|thumb|upright|Neves playing for [[Portugal national under-17 football team|Portugal U17]] in 2014]]

Neves represented [[Portugal national under-17 football team|Portugal]] at the [[2014 UEFA European Under-17 Championship]], helping the country reach the semi-finals as captain. For his performances, he was named among the top ten talents of the tournament by a selection of [[UEFA]] reporters.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/under17/news/newsid=2106764.html|title=Ten under-17 players to keep an eye on|publisher=UEFA|date=22 May 2014|access-date=18 August 2014}}</ref>

On 29 August 2014, still aged 17, Neves was called by coach [[Rui Jorge]] to be part of the [[Portugal national under-21 football team|under-21 team]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://desporto.sapo.pt/futebol/seleccao/euro_sub_21/artigo/2014/08/29/portugal-sub-21-sporting-domina-convocat-ria-ruben-neves-chamado|title=Sporting domina convocatória, Ruben Neves chamado|trans-title=Sporting dominate list, Ruben Neves called|website=SAPO Desporto |language=pt|date=29 August 2014|access-date=18 September 2014}}</ref> He scored his first goal on 14 October in a 5–4 home win against the [[Netherlands national under-21 football team|Netherlands]] in [[2015 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification|the play-off round]] of the [[2015 UEFA European Under-21 Championship]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/under21/season=2015/matches/round=2000413/match=2015238/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Portugal through after nine-goal thriller|publisher=UEFA|date=14 October 2014|access-date=24 February 2015}}</ref>

When the finals began in the Czech Republic, Neves broke a new record by becoming the youngest player to debut for the Portuguese under-21s in the tournament, aged 18 years and 3 months, playing five minutes to help beat [[England national under-21 football team|England]] 1–0 in the [[2015 UEFA European Under-21 Championship#Group B|group stage]] opener.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://desporto.sapo.pt/futebol/seleccao/portugal/artigo/2015/06/19/ruben-neves-quebra-recorde-mas-quer-mais-nao-posso-abrandar|title=Rúben Neves quebra recorde mas quer mais: "Não posso abrandar"|trans-title=Ruben Neves breaks record but wants more: "I can't slow down"|website=SAPO Desporto |language=pt|date=19 June 2015|access-date=3 July 2015}}</ref> It was his only appearance, as Portugal reached [[2015 UEFA European Under-21 Championship final|the final]], which they lost to [[Sweden national under-21 football team|Sweden]] in a [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shoot-out]].<ref name=UEFA>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/under21/season=2015/matches/round=2000411/match=2015383/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Spot-on Sweden beat Portugal to win U21 EURO|publisher=UEFA|last=Kell|first=Tom|date=30 June 2015|access-date=3 September 2015}}</ref>

===Senior===
Neves was first called up to [[Portugal national football team|the senior team]] on 10 November 2015, ahead of [[Friendly (association football)|friendlies]] against [[Russia national football team|Russia]] and [[Luxembourg national football team|Luxembourg]] as an injury replacement for João Moutinho.<ref>{{cite news |title=Rúben Neves substitui João Moutinho|trans-title=Rúben Neves replaced João Moutinho |url=https://www.dn.pt/desporto/seleccao/ruben-neves-substitui-joao-moutinho-4877555.html |access-date=25 June 2023 |work=Diário de Notícias |date=9 November 2015 |language=Portuguese}}</ref> He made his debut in the former match, featuring 17 minutes in the 1–0 loss in [[Krasnodar]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pt.uefa.com/uefaeuro/qualifiers/news/newsid=2304930.html|title=Rússia vence Portugal em Krasnodar|trans-title=Russia beat Portugal in Krasnodar|publisher=UEFA|language=pt|date=14 November 2015|access-date=17 November 2015}}</ref> before playing the entire 2–0 win against the latter at the [[Stade Josy Barthel]], sharing the midfield with Porto teammates [[Danilo Pereira]] and [[André André]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.maisfutebol.iol.pt/selecao/andre-andre/luxemburgo-portugal-2-0-cronica|title=Luxemburgo-Portugal, 0–2 (crónica)|trans-title=Luxembourg-Portugal, 0–2 (report)|website=Maisfutebol|language=pt|date=17 November 2015|access-date=18 November 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.ojogo.pt/internacional/selecao/noticias/interior/aproveitamos-o-meiocampo-do-fc-porto-4889964.html|title=Aproveitamos o meio-campo do FC Porto|trans-title=We took advantage of the FC Porto midfield|newspaper=O Jogo |location=Porto |language=pt|date=17 November 2015|access-date=18 November 2015}}</ref>

Neves was selected by manager [[Fernando Santos (footballer, born 1954)|Fernando Santos]] for a preliminary 35-man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/amp/news/revealed-every-world-cup-2018-squad-23-man-preliminary-lists/oa0atsduflsv1nsf6oqk576rb|title=Revealed: Every World Cup 2018 squad – 23-man & preliminary lists & when will they be announced?|website=Goal|date=17 May 2018|access-date=17 May 2018}}</ref> but did not make the final 23-man squad.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sbs.com.au/news/portugal-world-cup-squad|title=Nearly half Portugal's Euro squad to miss World Cup|website=SBS News |date=17 May 2018|access-date=17 May 2018}}</ref>

At the [[2019 UEFA Nations League Finals]] on home soil, Neves played in the 3–1 semi-final win over [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]], but was dropped from the starting eleven for the [[2019 UEFA Nations League final|final]] against the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]] for the more defensive Danilo.<ref>{{cite news |title=Danilo replaces Neves for Portugal in Nations League final |url=https://www.euronews.com/2019/06/09/danilo-replaces-neves-for-portugal-in-nations-league-final |access-date=16 August 2019 |publisher=Euronews |date=9 June 2019}}</ref> In that match at his former club ground the ''Dragão'', he came on in added time for [[William Carvalho]] as the Portuguese won 1–0.<ref>{{cite news |title=Portugal 1&ndash;0 The Netherlands |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2019/jun/09/portugal-v-netherlands-nations-league-final-live |access-date=16 August 2019 |newspaper=The Guardian |location=London |date=9 June 2019}}</ref>

Neves was one of four Wolves players included in the Portugal squad for [[UEFA Euro 2020]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Judah |first1=Nathan |title=Wolves quartet make Portugal Euro's squad |url=https://www.expressandstar.com/sport/football/wolverhampton-wanderers-fc/2021/05/20/wolves-quartet-make-portugal-euros-squad/ |newspaper=Express & Star |location=Wolverhampton |date=20 May 2021 |access-date=11 June 2022}}</ref> He made his sole appearance in the delayed competition on 23 June, as a substitute in a 2–2 group draw with [[France national football team|France]] in [[Budapest]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Fernandes |first1=Mariana |title=Tudo isto que vemos há tantos anos, senhoras e senhores, só tem um nome: história (a crónica do Portugal-França)|trans-title=Everything we saw so many years ago, ladies and gentlemen, has one name: history (Portugal-France report) |url=https://observador.pt/2021/06/23/tudo-isto-que-vemos-ha-tantos-anos-senhoras-e-senhores-so-tem-um-nome-historia-a-cronica-do-portugal-franca/ |access-date=11 June 2022 |newspaper=Observador |location=Lisbon |date=23 June 2021 |language=pt}}</ref>

Neves was chosen for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Dicken |first1=Alex |title=Julen Lopetegui must make four January signings to save Wolves from relegation |url=https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/sport/football/transfer-news/julen-lopetegui-wolves-january-transfers-25503442 |access-date=13 November 2022 |newspaper=Birmingham Mail |date=13 November 2022}}</ref>

==Style of play==
An article on [[UEFA]]'s website described Neves as a [[defensive midfielder]] who is not afraid to go forward and prompt attacks, showing great maturity combined with an accurate passing skill, great vision and an astute sense of positioning. His playing attributes also granted him comparisons to Portuguese international teammate João Moutinho for his fine first touch, similar desire to press his opponents and excellent distribution.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=por/news/newsid=2241045.html|title=UEFA.com's weekly wonderkid: Rúben Neves|publisher=UEFA|date=2 May 2015|access-date=5 May 2015}}</ref> Neves also drew praise in the media for his technical, tactical and mental qualities,<ref name=PortuGoal/> as well as his intelligence and long-range shooting ability.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.calciomercato.com/en/news/the-portuguese-star-who-broke-the-records-of-ronaldo-ready-to-jo-72883|title=The Portuguese star who broke the records of Ronaldo ready to join Juventus|publisher=Calcio Mercato|last=Laurenti|first=Mirko|date=23 November 2018|access-date=25 November 2018}}</ref>
