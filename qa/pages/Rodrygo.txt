'''Rodrygo Silva de Goes''' ({{IPA-pt|ʁoˈdɾiɡu ˈsiwvɐ dʒi ˈɡɔjs|br}};<ref>{{cite web |title=How to pronounce Rodrygo Goes |url=https://forvo.com/word/rodrygo_goes/ |publisher=Forvo |access-date=15 November 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190328203310/https://forvo.com/word/rodrygo_goes/ |archive-date=28 March 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> born 9 January 2001), simply known as '''Rodrygo''', is a Brazilian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Winger (association football)|winger]] or [[Forward (association football)|forward]] for [[La Liga]] club [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]] and the [[Brazil national football team|Brazil national team]].

He began his career with [[Santos FC|Santos]], where he played 80 games and scored 17 goals before a €45&nbsp;million transfer to Real Madrid in 2019. At international level, Rodrygo made his senior debut for Brazil in 2019, aged only 18.

==Club career==
===Santos===
[[File:RodrygoGoes.jpg|thumb|left|Rodrygo in 2018|262x262px]]
Born in [[Osasco]], [[São Paulo (state)|São Paulo]], Rodrygo joined [[Santos FC|Santos]]' youth setup in 2011 at the age of ten, initially assigned to the [[futsal]] team.<ref name="GE1"/> In March 2017, with the first team regulars in Peru for a [[Copa Libertadores]] match against [[Sporting Cristal]], he was called up to the first team by manager [[Dorival Júnior]] to complete training.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lance.com.br/santos/promessa-rodrygo-ganha-chance-aproxima-profissional.html|title=Na mira europeia, jogador de 16 anos faz treinos no profissional do Santos|trans-title=In the European sight, 16-year-old player does training at Santos' first team|publisher=[[Lance!]]|language=pt|date=10 March 2017|access-date=2 November 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190416101447/https://www.lance.com.br/santos/promessa-rodrygo-ganha-chance-aproxima-profissional.html|archive-date=16 April 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 21 July 2017, Rodrygo signed his first professional contract, after agreeing to a five-year deal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.santosfc.com.br/rodrygo-revelacao-da-base-assina-o-primeiro-contrato-profissional-com-o-santos-fc/|title=Rodrygo, revelação da base, assina o primeiro contrato profissional com o Santos FC|trans-title=Rodrygo, youth prospect, signs the first professional contract with Santos FC|publisher=Santos FC|language=pt|date=21 July 2017|access-date=2 November 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190327183806/https://www.santosfc.com.br/rodrygo-revelacao-da-base-assina-o-primeiro-contrato-profissional-com-o-santos-fc/|archive-date=27 March 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> On 1 November, he was promoted to the main squad by interim manager [[Elano]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://espn.uol.com.br/noticia/739868_elano-anuncia-promocao-de-rodrygo-e-yuri-alberto-ao-time-profissional-do-santos|title=Elano anuncia promoção de Rodrygo e Yuri Alberto ao time profissional do Santos|trans-title=Elano announces promotion of Rodrygo and Yuri Alberto to the first team of Santos|publisher=[[ESPN Brasil]]|language=pt|date=1 November 2017|access-date=2 November 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171117122544/http://espn.uol.com.br/noticia/739868_elano-anuncia-promocao-de-rodrygo-e-yuri-alberto-ao-time-profissional-do-santos|archive-date=17 November 2017|url-status=live}}</ref>

Rodrygo made his first team – and [[Campeonato Brasileiro Série A|Série A]] – debut on 4 November 2017, coming on as a late [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] for [[Bruno Henrique Pinto|Bruno Henrique]] in a 3–1 home win against [[Clube Atlético Mineiro|Atlético Mineiro]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://globoesporte.globo.com/sp/santos-e-regiao/futebol/brasileirao-serie-a/jogo/04-11-2017/santos-atletico-mg/|title=Santos sofre, mas vence Atlético-MG de Robinho e fica a três pontos do líder|trans-title=Santos suffer, but defeat Robinho's Atlético-MG and stay three points behind the leader|publisher=Globo Esporte|language=pt|date=4 November 2017|access-date=4 November 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190327161620/http://globoesporte.globo.com/sp/santos-e-regiao/futebol/brasileirao-serie-a/jogo/04-11-2017/santos-atletico-mg/|archive-date=27 March 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> The following 25 January he scored his first senior goal, netting a [[Last-minute goal|last-minute winner]] in a 2–1 [[Campeonato Paulista]] away win against [[Associação Atlética Ponte Preta|Ponte Preta]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://esporte.uol.com.br/futebol/campeonatos/paulista/ultimas-noticias/2018/01/25/sasha-e-nova-joia-do-santos-viram-o-jogo-e-dao-vitoria-contra-a-ponte.htm|title=Sasha e 'nova promessa' do Santos viram o jogo e dão vitória contra a Ponte|trans-title=Sasha and "new prospect" of Santos complete comeback and give victory against Ponte|publisher=[[Universo Online|UOL Esporte]]|language=pt|date=25 January 2018|access-date=26 January 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190416101441/https://esporte.uol.com.br/futebol/campeonatos/paulista/ultimas-noticias/2018/01/25/sasha-e-nova-joia-do-santos-viram-o-jogo-e-dao-vitoria-contra-a-ponte.htm|archive-date=16 April 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

Rodrygo made his [[Copa Libertadores]] debut on 1 March 2018, replacing [[Eduardo Sasha]] in a 2–0 away loss against [[Real Garcilaso]]; aged 17 years and 50 days, he became the youngest player of Santos to appear in the competition.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.gazetaesportiva.com/times/santos/rodrygo-e-o-mais-novo-jogar-libertadores-na-historia-do-santos/|title=Rodrygo é o mais novo a jogar Libertadores na história do Santos|trans-title=Rodrygo is the newest to play Libertadores in Santos' history|publisher=Gazeta Esportiva|language=pt|date=1 March 2018|access-date=1 March 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190416101443/https://www.gazetaesportiva.com/times/santos/rodrygo-e-o-mais-novo-jogar-libertadores-na-historia-do-santos/|archive-date=16 April 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> Fifteen days later he scored his first goal in the tournament, netting his team's second through an individual effort in a 3–1 win against [[Club Nacional de Football|Nacional]] at the [[Pacaembu Stadium]]; at the age of 17 years, two months and six days, he became the youngest Brazilian to score in the competition<ref>{{cite web|url=https://globoesporte.globo.com/sp/santos-e-regiao/futebol/times/santos/noticia/em-post-sobre-rodrygo-santos-exalta-sua-tradicao-em-revelar-jovens-talentos.ghtml|title=Em post sobre Rodrygo, Santos exalta sua tradição em revelar jovens talentos|trans-title=In post about Rodrygo, Santos lift up their tradition of uncover young talents|publisher=Globo Esporte|language=pt|date=16 March 2018|access-date=16 March 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191208144606/https://globoesporte.globo.com/sp/santos-e-regiao/futebol/times/santos/noticia/em-post-sobre-rodrygo-santos-exalta-sua-tradicao-em-revelar-jovens-talentos.ghtml|archive-date=8 December 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> before his record was broken by fellow Santos youth graduates [[Kaiky Fernandes|Kaiky]] and [[Ângelo Gabriel|Ângelo]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://globoesporte.globo.com/sp/santos-e-regiao/futebol/times/santos/noticia/noticias-santos-angelo-mais-novo-gol-libertadores.ghtml|title=Ângelo, do Santos, faz história e se torna jogador mais novo a fazer um gol na Libertadores|trans-title=Ângelo, from Santos, makes history and becomes the youngest to score a goal in the Libertadores|publisher=GloboEsporte.com|language=pt-BR|date=6 April 2021|accessdate=7 April 2021}}</ref>

Rodrygo scored his first goal in the main category of Brazilian football on 14 April 2018, netting the last in a 2–0 home win against [[Ceará Sporting Club|Ceará]].<ref>{{cite web |url= https://globoesporte.globo.com/sp/santos-e-regiao/futebol/brasileirao-serie-a/jogo/14-04-2018/santos-ceara.ghtml |title= Santos bate Ceará e volta a vencer em estreia de Brasileirão depois de 13 anos |trans-title= Santos defeat Ceará and is back to winning in debut after 13 years |publisher= Globo Esporte |language= pt |date= 14 April 2018 |access-date= 15 April 2018 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20180416013035/https://globoesporte.globo.com/sp/santos-e-regiao/futebol/brasileirao-serie-a/jogo/14-04-2018/santos-ceara.ghtml |archive-date= 16 April 2018 |url-status= live }}</ref> On 3 June, he scored a [[hat-trick]] and also [[Assist (association football)|assisted]] [[Gabriel Barbosa|Gabriel]]'s last goal in a 5–2 home routing of [[Esporte Clube Vitória|Vitória]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://esporte.uol.com.br/futebol/campeonatos/brasileiro/serie-a/ultimas-noticias/2018/06/03/santos-x-vitoria.htm|title=Com show de Rodrygo, Santos goleia o Vitória na Vila e dá fôlego a Jair|trans-title=With a show from Rodrygo, Santos thrash Vitória at the Vila and give air to Jair|publisher=UOL Esporte|language=pt|date=3 June 2018|access-date=4 June 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180612144046/https://esporte.uol.com.br/futebol/campeonatos/brasileiro/serie-a/ultimas-noticias/2018/06/03/santos-x-vitoria.htm|archive-date=12 June 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 26 July 2018, Rodrygo changed his jersey number from 43 to 9 (a number he already wore during the Libertadores).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.gazetaesportiva.com/times/santos/rodrygo-recebe-surpresa-e-e-oficialmente-o-novo-camisa-9-do-santos/|title=Rodrygo recebe surpresa e é, oficialmente, o novo camisa 9 do Santos|trans-title=Rodrygo receives surprise and is, officially, the new number 9 of Santos|publisher=Gazeta Esportiva|language=pt|date=26 July 2018|access-date=26 July 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191208144524/https://www.gazetaesportiva.com/times/santos/rodrygo-recebe-surpresa-e-e-oficialmente-o-novo-camisa-9-do-santos/|archive-date=8 December 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> For the 2019 campaign, he again switched numbers, now to jersey 11, previously worn by fellow youth graduate [[Neymar]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://esporte.uol.com.br/futebol/campeonatos/copa-sul-americana/ultimas-noticias/2019/02/12/lista-da-sul-americana-revela-que-rodrygo-abandona-camisa-9-e-assume-a-11.htm|title=Lista da Sul-Americana revela que Rodrygo abandona camisa 9 e assume a 11|trans-title=Sudamericana list reveals that Rodrygo leaves number 9 and takes 11|publisher=UOL Esporte|language=pt-BR|date=12 February 2019|access-date=19 March 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190717140953/https://esporte.uol.com.br/futebol/campeonatos/copa-sul-americana/ultimas-noticias/2019/02/12/lista-da-sul-americana-revela-que-rodrygo-abandona-camisa-9-e-assume-a-11.htm|archive-date=17 July 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 25 September 2019, Rodrygo made his first team debut and scored his first league goal against [[CA Osasuna|Osasuna]] within a mere minute.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/real-madrid/2019/09/25/5d8be1efca474112558b45a4.html|title=Rodrygo becomes Real Madrid's first 21st century scorer|work=Marca|location=Spain|date=25 September 2019|access-date=25 September 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191001200012/https://www.marca.com/en/football/real-madrid/2019/09/25/5d8be1efca474112558b45a4.html|archive-date=1 October 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> He scored his first hat-trick, and gave an assist for the club on 6 November, aged 18 years and 301 days old, against [[Galatasaray S.K. (football)|Galatasaray]] in a 6–0 win in the [[2019–20 UEFA Champions League]] season.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/rodrygo-makes-champions-league-history-with-quickfire-madrid/s41v7oj8qzl1pv50ot9f40bt|title=Rodrygo makes Champions League history with quickfire Real Madrid double|website=Goal.com|date=6 November 2019|access-date=6 November 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191106222217/https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/rodrygo-makes-champions-league-history-with-quickfire-madrid/s41v7oj8qzl1pv50ot9f40bt|archive-date=6 November 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> The second youngest ever to score a hat-trick in the competition, he is also the first player born in the 21st century to score in the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.as.com/en/2019/11/06/football/1573071750_168543.html|title=Rodrygo scores perfect hat-trick in Real Madrid UCL rout|website=as.com|date=6 November 2019|access-date=6 November 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191110132213/https://en.as.com/en/2019/11/06/football/1573071750_168543.html|archive-date=10 November 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> In his first season, he managed to make 19 appearances, while scoring two goals during the league season, as Real Madrid won the [[2019–20 La Liga]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/real-madrid/2020/07/17/5f10db2146163f3c2c8b45d7.html|title=Real Madrid win the longest LaLiga Santander season|date=17 July 2020|access-date=17 July 2020|website=Marca|location=Spain}}</ref> On 3 November 2020, Rodrygo scored the winning goal in a 3–2 victory over [[Inter Milan]] in the [[2020–21 UEFA Champions League]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/54784152 |title=Real Madrid 3–2 Inter Milan |website=BBC Sport |date=3 November 2020 }}</ref>

On 12 April 2022, after coming on as a substitute in the second leg of the [[2021–22 UEFA Champions League]] quarter-final tie against [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]], he scored with a volleyed finish to send the game into extra-time, in which [[Karim Benzema]] scored the winner from a header, allowing Real Madrid to progress into the semi-finals.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/61067661 |title=Real Madrid 3–2 Chelsea |website=BBC Sport |date=12 April 2022 }}</ref> On 30 April, Rodrygo helped Real clinch their [[2021-22 La Liga|35th La Liga title]] after scoring twice in a 4–0 win against [[RCD Espanyol|Espanyol]] at the [[Santiago Bernabéu Stadium|Bernabéu]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/61284264 |title=Real Madrid 4–0 Espanyol |website=BBC Sport |date=30 April 2022 }}</ref> On 4 May, while trailing 0–1 (3–5 on aggregate) in the second leg of the Champions League semi-final tie against [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]], he scored two goals between the 89th and 91st minutes to level the tie and send the game into extra-time. Benzema scored a penalty to win the game 3–1, allowing Real Madrid to progress into the [[2022 UEFA Champions League Final|final]] against [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] with an aggregate score of 6–5 and eventually win the tournament.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/may/04/real-madrid-manchester-city-champions-league-semi-final-second-leg-match-report |title=Real Madrid in final after Rodrygo and Karim Benzema stun Manchester City |website=The Guardian |date=4 May 2022 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2022/06/03/la-decimocuarta/amp|title=La Decimocuarta! |website=Real Madrid C.F. – Web Oficial|date=7 June 2022 }}</ref> Following the turnaround win against City and other recent last-minute winning contributions, Rodrygo’s impact on the team was praised despite his young age and he quickly turned into a cult hero at Madrid.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.planetfootball.com/trending/rodrygo-real-madrid-champions-league-man-city-goals-semi-final-assassin/|title=Rodrygo is the silent assassin who drips with Madrid's terrible purpose|website=Planet Football|date=7 June 2022 }}</ref>

On 18 April 2023, he scored a brace in a 2–0 away victory over Chelsea in the [[2022–23 UEFA Champions League]] quarter-final second leg, which secured his team's qualification to the semi-finals by winning 4–0 on aggregate.<ref>{{cite web |last=Wright |first=Nick |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/chelsea-vs-real-madrid/report/481694 |title=Chelsea 0-2 Real Madrid (agg 0-4): Rodrygo scores twice as Blues outclassed by holders in Champions League quarter-final |publisher=Sky Sports |date=18 April 2023 }}</ref> On 6 May, he scored two goals for Real Madrid in a 2–1 win over [[CA Osasuna|Osasuna]] in the [[2023 Copa del Rey final]], to be their 20th title in that competition.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/copa-rey/cronica/2023/05/07/6456ce98ca4741bb6c8b45d5.html |title=Rodrygo stars as Real Madrid win the Copa del Rey against spirited Osasuna |publisher=Marca |date=6 May 2023 }}</ref>

==International career==
On 30 March 2017, Rodrygo was called up to [[Brazil national under-17 football team|Brazil under-17s]] for the year's [[Montaigu Tournament]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://selecao.cbf.com.br/noticias/selecao-base-masculina/selecao-sub-17-convocada-para-torneio-de-montaigu|title=Seleção Sub-17 convocada para Torneio de Montaigu|trans-title=Under-17 national team called up for the Montaigu Tournament|publisher=[[Confederação Brasileira de Futebol|CBF]]|language=pt|date=30 March 2017|access-date=2 November 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180326141447/https://selecao.cbf.com.br/noticias/selecao-base-masculina/selecao-sub-17-convocada-para-torneio-de-montaigu#.WfqSpVtSzDc|archive-date=26 March 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> He made his debut in the championship by scoring his side's only goal in a 2–1 loss against [[Denmark national under-17 football team|Denmark]], and netted two more against [[Cameroon national under-17 football team|Cameroon]] and [[United States men's national under-17 soccer team|United States]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lance.com.br/santos/oito-gols-quatro-jogos-quarteto-peixe-destaca-selecao-sub.html|title=Oito gols em quatro jogos: quarteto do Peixe se destaca na Seleção sub-17|trans-title=Eight goals in four matches: ''Peixe's'' quartet is a spotlight at ''Seleção'' under-17|publisher=Lance!|language=pt|date=17 April 2017|access-date=2 November 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191208145501/https://www.lance.com.br/santos/oito-gols-quatro-jogos-quarteto-peixe-destaca-selecao-sub.html|archive-date=8 December 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 7 March 2018, Rodrygo and Santos teammate [[Yuri Alberto]] were called up to the [[Brazil national under-20 football team|under-20]]s,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://globoesporte.globo.com/futebol/selecao-brasileira/noticia/rodrygo-e-yuri-alberto-do-santos-substituem-joias-do-flamengo-na-selecao-sub-20.ghtml|title=Rodrygo e Yuri Alberto, do Santos, substituem joias do Flamengo na seleção sub-20|trans-title=Rodrygo and Yuri Alberto, from Santos, replace Flamengo's pearls in the national under-20 team|publisher=Globo Esporte|language=pt|date=7 March 2018|access-date=11 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191107044044/https://globoesporte.globo.com/futebol/selecao-brasileira/noticia/rodrygo-e-yuri-alberto-do-santos-substituem-joias-do-flamengo-na-selecao-sub-20.ghtml|archive-date=7 November 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> but both were cut from the squad six days later after a request from his club's president.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lance.com.br/santos/apos-ida-peres-rio-cbf-libera-rodrygo-yuri-alberto-selecao.html|title=Após ida de Peres ao Rio, CBF libera Rodrygo e Yuri Alberto da seleção|trans-title=After Peres' trip to Rio, CBF release Rodrygo and Yuri Alberto from the national team|publisher=Lance!|language=pt|date=13 March 2018|access-date=11 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191107044047/https://www.lance.com.br/santos/apos-ida-peres-rio-cbf-libera-rodrygo-yuri-alberto-selecao.html|archive-date=7 November 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

In November 2019, Rodrygo was called up for the first time to the [[Brazil national football team|Brazil senior team]], for the [[Superclásico de las Américas]] against rivals [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]] in [[Riyadh]], Saudi Arabia.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Munday |first1=Billy |title=Rodrygo gears up for his Brazil bow against Argentina |url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/real-madrid/2019/11/11/5dc9c70d268e3e4e3e8b4647.html |access-date=15 November 2019 |work=Marca|location=Spain |date=11 November 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191115234104/https://www.marca.com/en/football/real-madrid/2019/11/11/5dc9c70d268e3e4e3e8b4647.html |archive-date=15 November 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> In the [[2019 Superclásico de las Américas|1–0 loss]] on 15 November, he replaced [[Willian (footballer, born 1988)|Willian]] for the final 20 minutes.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Train |first1=Rob |title=Real Madrid: Rodrygo makes full debut for Brazil against Argentina |url=https://en.as.com/en/2019/11/15/football/1573844470_031298.html |access-date=15 November 2019 |work=Diario AS |date=15 November 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191129010722/https://en.as.com/en/2019/11/15/football/1573844470_031298.html |archive-date=29 November 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref>

On 7 November 2022, Rodrygo was named in the squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Seleção Brasileira está convocada para a Copa do Mundo FIFA Qatar 2022|url=https://www.cbf.com.br/selecao-brasileira/noticias/selecao-masculina/selecao-brasileira-esta-convocada-para-a-copa-do-mundo-fifa-qatar-2022|publisher=[[Brazilian Football Confederation|CBF]]|access-date=7 November 2022|date=7 November 2022|language=pt}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Rodrygo's father, [[Eric Batista de Goes|Eric]], is a former professional footballer. A [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|right back]], he played in several tiers of Brazilian football, the highest of which was [[Campeonato Brasileiro Série B|Série B]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://globoesporte.globo.com/ce/futebol/times/ceara/noticia/2013/02/filho-de-eric-se-destaca-na-base-do-santos-e-e-chamado-de-neymarzinho.html|title=Filho de Eric se destaca na base do Santos e é chamado de Neymarzinho|trans-title=Eric's son is a spotlight at Santos' youth setup and is called 'Little Neymar'|publisher=Globo Esporte|language=pt|date=27 February 2013|access-date=2 November 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191107044048/http://globoesporte.globo.com/ce/futebol/times/ceara/noticia/2013/02/filho-de-eric-se-destaca-na-base-do-santos-e-e-chamado-de-neymarzinho.html|archive-date=7 November 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>
