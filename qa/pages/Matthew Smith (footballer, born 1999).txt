'''Matthew Robert Smith''' (born 22 November 1999) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for {{English football updater|MiltonKD}} club [[Milton Keynes Dons F.C.|Milton Keynes Dons]] and the [[Wales national football team|Wales national team]].<ref name = "WF">{{Worldfootball.net|matt-smith_3}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Manchester City===
Having initially joined the academy of [[West Bromwich Albion F.C. Reserves and Academy|West Bromwich Albion]] at a young age, in 2013 Smith moved to the academy of [[Manchester City F.C. EDS and Academy|Manchester City]], where he progressed through several age groups.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.mancity.com/news/academy/academy-news/2016/september/all-about-matt-smith |title=All about...Matt Smith |publisher=[[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] |date=1 September 2016 |access-date=2 February 2022 }}</ref>

In July 2018 at the age of 18, Smith joined Dutch [[Eerste Divisie]] club [[FC Twente]] on a season-long loan,<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44714664 |title=Matt Smith: Manchester City's Wales midfielder joins FC Twente on loan |publisher=[[BBC]] |date=4 July 2018 |access-date=2 February 2022 }}</ref> eventually making 37 appearances and scoring 2 goals, playing a key role in the club's promotion to the [[Eredivisie]].<ref name="SmithMKDonsJoin">{{Cite web |url=https://www.mkdons.com/news/2022/january/matt-smith-joins-from-man-city/ |title=Matt Smith joins from Man City |publisher=[[Milton Keynes Dons F.C.|Milton Keynes Dons]] |date=31 January 2022 |access-date=2 January 2022 }}</ref> Over the next two seasons, he was again sent out on loan to [[EFL Championship]] clubs [[Queens Park Rangers F.C.|Queens Park Rangers]] and [[Charlton Athletic F.C.|Charlton Athletic]], as well as [[EFL League One]] club [[Doncaster Rovers F.C.|Doncaster Rovers]], making over 40 appearances for the latter.<ref name="SmithMKDonsJoin" /> Ahead of the [[2021–22 Hull City A.F.C. season|2021–22]] season, on 6 August 2021 Smith joined EFL Championship club [[Hull City A.F.C.|Hull City]] on another season-long loan.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.wearehullcity.co.uk/news/2021/august/matt-smith-joins-on-loan/ |title=Matt Smith Joins On Loan |publisher=[[Hull City A.F.C.|Hull City]] |date=6 August 2021 |access-date=2 February 2022 }}</ref>

===Milton Keynes Dons===
On 31 January 2022, following limited opportunities with Hull, Smith was recalled from his loan and immediately transferred to [[EFL League One|League One]] club [[Milton Keynes Dons F.C.|Milton Keynes Dons]] on a permanent deal for an undisclosed fee.<ref name="SmithMKDonsJoin" /> He made his debut for the club on 8 February 2022, in a 1–1 draw away to [[Fleetwood Town F.C.|Fleetwood Town]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/60210078 |title=Fleetwood Town 1-1 Milton Keynes Dons |publisher=[[BBC]] |date=8 February 2021 |access-date=10 February 2021 }}</ref>

==International career==
Born in England, Smith qualifies for the [[Wales national football team|Welsh national team]] through his grandfather who is from [[Gwent (preserved county)|Gwent]] in Wales.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/47713111|title=Matt Smith: Wales midfielder signs new Manchester City deal|website=BBC Sport|date=26 March 2019|accessdate=21 September 2019}}</ref> 

Smith has played for Wales at [[Wales national under-17 football team|U17]], [[Wales national under-19 football team|U19]], [[Wales national under-21 football team|U21]] and [[Wales national football team|senior]] level, often captaining the side at youth level. He received a first call-up to the senior squad on 20 May 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mcfcwatch.com/2018/05/20/manchester-city-academy-midfielder-called-up-by-ryan-giggs-for-wales-senior-squad/|title=Manchester City academy midfielder called up by Ryan Giggs for Wales senior squad – City Watch|publisher=|access-date=29 May 2018|archive-date=15 August 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180815044945/https://www.mcfcwatch.com/2018/05/20/manchester-city-academy-midfielder-called-up-by-ryan-giggs-for-wales-senior-squad/|url-status=dead}}</ref>

He made his senior debut for Wales in a friendly 0–0 tie with [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]], replacing [[Tom Lawrence]] after 81 minutes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.skysports.com/football/mexico-vs-wales/388746|title=Internationals (Sky Sports)|website=SkySports}}</ref> In May 2021 he was selected for the Wales squad for the delayed [[UEFA Euro 2020]] tournament.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57301006|title=Uncapped Colwill in Wales Euro 2020 squad|work=BBC Sport }}</ref> In November 2022 he was named in the Wales squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63545011 2022 Wales World Cup squad]</ref>
