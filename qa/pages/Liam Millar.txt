'''Liam Alan Millar''' (born September 27, 1999) is a Canadian professional [[Association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[Midfielder#Winger|winger]] for [[Swiss Super League]] club [[F.C. Basel|Basel]] and the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada national team]]. He featured in the youth ranks of Brampton Youth SC and [[North Mississauga SC]] before moving to England to join [[Fulham F.C. Academy|Fulham Academy]] aged 13. After three years with Fulham, he joined the [[Liverpool F.C. Reserves and Academy|Liverpool Academy]] in 2016.

==Early life==
Millar was born in [[Toronto]] and raised in [[Brampton]] and [[Oakville, Ontario|Oakville]].<ref name=CS/> He moved to England at the age of 13 for footballing purposes.<ref name=":2">{{Cite news |date=September 28, 2016 |title=Former NMSC Player Liam Millar Signs Pro Contract with Liverpool |language=en-CA |work=North Mississauga Soccer Club |url=http://nmsc.net/news/competitive/former-nmsc-player-liam-millar-signs-pro-contract-with-liverpool-2/ |access-date=March 12, 2018 |archive-date=March 15, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180315134927/http://nmsc.net/news/competitive/former-nmsc-player-liam-millar-signs-pro-contract-with-liverpool-2/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> He began playing soccer at age four with Brampton YSC.<ref>{{Canada Soccer player|id=6079}}</ref> Afterwards he played with [[North Mississauga SC]] and [[Burlington SC (League1 Ontario)|Burlington YSC]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://canadasoccer.com/news/canada-soccer-announce-squad-for-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022/|title=Canada Soccer announces squad for FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022|date=November 12, 2022|work=[[Canadian Soccer Association]]}}</ref>

His father played for [[Charlton Athletic F.C.|Charlton Athletic]] and is an [[electrician]] who works on television series, including ''[[Game of Thrones]]''.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Armstrong |first=Laura |date=November 17, 2018 |title=Millar at epicentre of Canadian soccer youthquake |language=en-CA |work=The Toronto Star |url=https://www.thestar.com/sports/soccer/2018/11/16/millar-at-epicentre-of-canadian-soccer-youthquake.html |access-date=December 8, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Alan Millar |url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0587649/ |access-date=December 8, 2018 |website=IMDb}}</ref>

==Club career==
=== Early career ===
Millar began his career with Brampton Youth SC at the age of four.<ref name=CS/> After two years he joined [[North Mississauga SC]] and remained at the club until his move to England. He joined [[Fulham F.C. Academy|Fulham]] at the age of 13 but felt he struggled to adapt to English football.<ref name=":4" /> On moving to England, Millar said "It took me a while to adjust to how English football is, it probably took me a good two-and-a-half years."<ref name=":4" />

=== Liverpool ===

In July 2016, he joined [[Premier League]] club [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] on a free transfer with striking options limited in the [[Liverpool F.C. Reserves and Academy|Academy]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news |last=Kelly |first=Andy |date=August 15, 2016 |title=Who is hat-trick hero Liam Millar? |work=Liverpool Echo |url=https://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/who-liverpool-u18s-hat-trick-11753309 |access-date=March 12, 2018}}</ref>

Millar scored four minutes into his Liverpool U18s career and marked his debut in the [[Professional Development League|U18 Premier League]] with a [[Hat-trick#Association football|hat-trick]] in a 4–0 victory against [[Blackburn Rovers F.C. Under-23s and Academy|Blackburn Rovers U18s]] in August 2016.<ref name=":3">{{Cite news |date=August 15, 2016 |title=A star in the making – 16-year-old Liam Millar scores hat-trick on U18s debut |language=en-US |work=The Empire of The Kop |url=http://www.empireofthekop.com/2016/08/15/a-star-in-the-making-16-year-old-liam-millar-scores-hat-trick-on-u18s-debut/ |access-date=March 14, 2018}}</ref> A month later, he signed a professional contract on his 17th birthday.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Hunter |first=Steve |date=September 27, 2016 |title=Academy striker Liam Millar signs professional contract |work=Liverpool FC |url=http://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/academy/237492-academy-striker-liam-millar-signs-professional-contract |access-date=March 12, 2018}}</ref> Injury ruled Millar out for three months of his inaugural season but a return in January saw him finish the season with six goals in 17 appearances in the U18 Premier League.<ref name=":4">{{Cite news |title=Meet the Academy: Liam Millar |work=Liverpool FC |url=http://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/academy/261596-meet-the-academy-liam-millar |access-date=March 14, 2018}}</ref>

He featured in every game of Liverpool's [[2017–18 UEFA Youth League]] campaign and scored twice in eight games.<ref>{{Cite web |last=uefa.com |title=UEFA Youth League - Liam Millar – UEFA.com |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefayouthleague/season=2018/clubs/player=250113266/index.html |access-date=March 14, 2018 |website=UEFA.com |language=en}}</ref> After 18 months under the guidance of [[Steven Gerrard]] in the Under-18s, Millar received his first call-up to the Under-23 squad in January 2018.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Pearce |first=James |date=March 12, 2018 |title=International call for Liverpool youngster |work=Liverpool Echo |url=https://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/canada-call-up-liverpool-youngster-14402569 |access-date=March 12, 2018}}</ref>

He made his senior Liverpool debut on February 4, 2020, starting in the [[FA Cup]] fourth-round replay against [[Shrewsbury Town F.C.|Shrewsbury Town]].<ref name="sw">{{Cite web |title=Liverpool vs. Shrewsbury Town - 4 February 2020 - Soccerway |url=https://uk.soccerway.com/matches/2020/02/04/england/fa-cup/liverpool-fc/shrewsbury-town-fc/3220481/ |website=uk.soccerway.com}}</ref> He was replaced by Joe Hardy in the 82nd minute. In mid-2020 Liverpool turned down multiple transfer offers for Millar, who received loan interest from clubs in the [[EFL Championship|Championship]] and [[Bundesliga]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Sam Carroll |date=July 15, 2020 |title=Liverpool international prospect attracting transfer interest from Bundesliga |url=https://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/liverpool-international-prospect-attracting-transfer-18597633 |website=Liverpool Echo}}</ref>

====Loans to Kilmarnock====
On January 31, 2019, Millar signed a long-term first-team contract with Liverpool<ref>{{Cite web |last=Williams |first=Sam |title=Liam Millar signs new LFC deal and joins Kilmarnock on loan |url=https://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/academy/336268-liam-millar-new-liverpool-contract-kilmarnock-loan |access-date=February 5, 2020 |publisher=Liverpool FC}}</ref> and joined Scottish Premiership side [[Kilmarnock F.C.|Kilmarnock]] on loan until the end of the 2018–19 season.<ref>{{Cite web |date=January 31, 2019 |title=Canadian international joins on loan from Liverpool |url=http://www.kilmarnockfc.co.uk/Article?id=8271 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190201013345/http://www.kilmarnockfc.co.uk/Article?id=8271 |archive-date=February 1, 2019 |access-date=January 31, 2019 |website=www.kilmarnockfc.co.uk}}</ref> He made his debut on February 1 in a 2–1 loss to [[Heart of Midlothian F.C.|Heart of Midlothian]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=February 1, 2019 |title=Kilmarnock 1-2 Hearts: Killie fall to home loss |url=http://www.kilmarnockfc.co.uk/Article?id=8291 |access-date=February 2, 2019 |archive-date=February 2, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190202095213/http://www.kilmarnockfc.co.uk/Article?id=8291 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He scored his first goal on March 11 against [[St Mirren F.C.|St Mirren]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=March 11, 2019 |title=St Mirren 0 Kilmarnock 1: Jubilant scenes in Paisley as Liam Millar grabs late winner |url=https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/sport/football/football-match-reports/st-mirren-0-kilmarnock-1-14120791}}</ref>

He returned on loan to Kilmarnock in August 2019.<ref>{{Cite web |date=August 6, 2019 |title=Kilmarnock: Liverpool forward Liam Millar returns to Kilmarnock on loan |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49251758 |access-date=August 6, 2019 |publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref>  This loan period was cut short in January 2020 when he returned to his parent club.<ref>{{Cite news |date=January 8, 2020 |title=Liverpool's Liam Millar returns from Kilmarnock as loan is cut short |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/51038544}}</ref>

====Loan to Charlton Athletic====

On January 5, 2021, Millar completed his move to [[Charlton Athletic F.C.|Charlton Athletic]] on loan until the end of the season.<ref>{{Cite news |date=January 5, 2021 |title=DONE DEAL: Canada international Liam Millar joins on loan from Liverpool |publisher=[[Charlton Athletic F.C.|Charlton Athletic]] |url=https://www.cafc.co.uk/news/view/5ff4326d9234d/done-deal-canada-international-liam-millar-joins-on-loan-from-liverpool |access-date=January 5, 2021}}</ref> He scored his first goal for Charlton on January 26 in a 1–0 league win over [[Milton Keynes Dons F.C.|MK Dons]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=January 26, 2021 |title=MK Dons 0-1 Charlton Athletic |publisher=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/55713920 |access-date=January 26, 2021}}</ref> Over the season he played 27 games for the club scoring two goals.

===Basel===
On July 8, 2021, Millar joined [[Swiss Super League]] club [[F.C. Basel|Basel]] on a deal until 2025.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Williams |first=Sam |date=July 8, 2021 |title=Liam Millar joins FC Basel in permanent transfer |url=https://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/academy/437415-liam-millar-joins-fc-basel-in-permanent-transfer |publisher=Liverpool FC |accessdate=July 8, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=July 8, 2021 |title=DER FCB VERPFLICHTET LIAM MILLAR |url=https://www.fcb.ch/aktuell/news/1-mannschaft/2021/07/liam-millar/}}</ref> The transfer fee was reported to be £1.3&nbsp;million.<ref>{{Cite web |date=July 8, 2021 |title=Liverpool forward Liam Millar completes £1.3m Basel move |url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/liverpool-forward-millar-completes-13m-basel-move/k7cvofz8cisv1u8dc1y3dpqri}}</ref> He made his official debut for the club on July 25, entering as a substitute for [[Valentin Stocker]] in an eventual opening day 2-0 victory over [[Grasshopper Club Zürich|Grasshoppers]].<ref>{{Cite tweet |number=1419327102415953927 |user=FC_Basel_en |title=2' - Next change for FCB. < Stocker > Millar A competitive debut for @liammillar11 |author=[[FC Basel 1893]] |date=July 25, 2021 |access-date=July 25, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=July 25, 2021 |title=2:0-SIEG BEI GC ZUM AUFTAKT IN DIE MEISTERSCHAFT |url=https://www.fcb.ch/aktuell/news/1-mannschaft/2021/07/gc-fcb/}}</ref> Millar scored his first goal for Basel on September 19 in a [[2021–22 Swiss Cup|Swiss Cup]] match against FC Rorschach-Goldach.<ref>{{Cite tweet |number=1439576978194063363 |user=FC_Basel_en |title=THE OPENER!! A first goal in Red/Blue for @liammillar11! |author=[[FC Basel 1893]] |date=September 19, 2021 |access-date=December 12, 2021}}</ref>

== International career ==

===Youth===

Millar was first involved in the international set-up at the age of 14 in the squad for the [[Montaigu Tournament|Tournoi Montaigu]].<ref name=CS/> After being invited to an U20 camp in Costa Rica in 2016, he made his debut in the [[CONCACAF Under-20 Championship]] in February 2017.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Canada Soccer |url=https://canadasoccer.com/?gid=1426&t=match |access-date=March 14, 2018 |website=canadasoccer.com}}</ref> He made three appearances as Canada finished third in Group A and failed to progress to the classification stage.<ref>{{Cite web |title=CONCACAF Under 20s – Men |url=http://concacaf.datafactory.la/en/concacafsub20/standings.html |access-date=March 14, 2018 |website=concacaf.datafactory.la}}</ref> In May 2018, Millar was named to Canada's under-21 squad for the [[2018 Toulon Tournament]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Alicia Rodriguez |date=May 19, 2018 |title=Canada national team roster for prestigious Toulon Tournament released |url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2018/05/19/canada-national-team-roster-prestigious-toulon-tournament-released |website=Major League Soccer}}</ref> Millar was named to the [[Canada men's national under-23 soccer team|Canadian U-23]] provisional roster for the [[2020 CONCACAF Men's Olympic Qualifying Championship]] on February 26, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web |date=February 26, 2020 |title=Canada Soccer announces Provisional List for Concacaf Men's Olympic Qualifying |url=https://www.canadasoccer.com/canada-soccer-announces-provisional-list-for-concacaf-men-s-olympic-qualifying-p162594-preview-1 |access-date=February 26, 2020 |publisher=Canada Soccer |archive-date=March 9, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200309191149/https://www.canadasoccer.com/canada-soccer-announces-provisional-list-for-concacaf-men-s-olympic-qualifying-p162594-preview-1 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

===Senior===

In March 2018, Millar was named in [[John Herdman]]'s first squad as national team manager.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Molinaro |first=John |date=March 12, 2018 |title=John Herdman names roster for 1st match in charge of men's team |language=en-CA |work=Sportsnet.ca |url=https://www.sportsnet.ca/soccer/john-herdman-names-roster-1st-match-charge-mens-team/ |access-date=March 12, 2018}}</ref> In that same camp, Millar made his senior debut and first start for Canada during a 1–0 win over [[New Zealand national football team|New Zealand]], impressing while doing so.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Sferra |first=German |date=March 24, 2018 |title=Canada 1, New Zealand 0 {{!}} International Friendly Recap |url=https://matchcenter.mlssoccer.com/matchcenter/2018-03-24-canada-vs-new-zealand/recap |access-date=March 24, 2018 |website=MLSSoccer.com |archive-date=February 28, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210228155617/https://matchcenter.mlssoccer.com/matchcenter/2018-03-24-canada-vs-new-zealand/recap |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=March 24, 2018 |title=Ricketts lifts Canada to victory over New Zealand in friendly |url=https://www.sportsnet.ca/soccer/ricketts-lifts-canada-victory-new-zealand-friendly/ |access-date=March 24, 2018 |website=Sportsnet}}</ref> In May 2019, Millar was named to the 23-man squad for the [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=May 30, 2019 |title=Together We Rise: Canada Soccer announces squad for the 2019 Concacaf Gold Cup |url=https://www.canadasoccer.com/together-we-rise-canada-soccer-announces-squad-for-the-2019-concacaf-gold-cup-p162198 |access-date=June 24, 2019 |archive-date=July 24, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200724182520/https://www.canadasoccer.com/together-we-rise-canada-soccer-announces-squad-for-the-2019-concacaf-gold-cup-p162198 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In November 2022, Millar was called-up to Canada's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canada-announces-26-man-squad-for-2022-fifa-world-cup-in-qatar|date=November 13, 2022|title=Canada announces 26-man squad for 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]|first=Charlie|last=O'Connor-Clarke}}</ref> He made one appearance at the tournament, in Canada's opening match against [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sportsnet.ca/soccer/article/canada-dominates-but-held-scoreless-in-loss-vs-belgium-to-open-world-cup/|date=November 23, 2022|title=Canada dominates, but held scoreless in loss vs. Belgium to open World Cup}}</ref>

In June 2023, Millar was named to the 23-man squad ahead of the [[2023 CONCACAF Gold Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canmnt-announces-final-23-man-squad-for-concacaf-gold-cup|date=June 19, 2023|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]|title=CanMNT announces final 23-man squad for Concacaf Gold Cup|first=Charlie|last=O'Connor-Clarke}}</ref> He scored his first goal for Canada during the tournament on July 4 against [[Cuba national football team|Cuba]], netting the final tally in an eventual 4–2 victory.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canpl.ca/article/match-analysis-canada-advance-to-gold-cup-quarter-finals-after-decisive-win-over-cuba|date=July 4, 2023|title=MATCH ANALYSIS: Canada advance to Gold Cup quarter-finals after decisive win over Cuba|first=Mitchell|last=Tierney|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]}}</ref>

== Style of play ==
Millar's preferred position is as a number nine, but he has been utilised in various roles with Liverpool due to his flexibility. He has been noted for his ability on the left flank.<ref>{{Cite news |date=October 4, 2017 |title=Who is Liverpool's young Canadian forward Millar? |language=en-GB |work=This Is Anfield |url=https://www.thisisanfield.com/2017/10/liam-millar-steven-gerrards-hidden-attacking-gem-liverpool/ |access-date=March 14, 2018}}</ref> In August 2016, manager [[Neil Critchley]] said: "He's a goalscorer. He can run down the side of defenders, he runs in behind, he gives them real problems with his movement and he's a good finisher off both feet. He's got a good brain, good awareness, very calm and can finish. He's a good player, a good all-round player."<ref name=":0" />
