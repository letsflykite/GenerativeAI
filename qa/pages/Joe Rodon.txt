'''Joseph Peter Rodon''' (born 22 October 1997) is a Welsh professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] for {{English football updater|TottenhH}} club [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]], and the [[Wales national football team|Wales national team]].

A youth product of [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]], Rodon made 54 senior appearances for the club before moving to Tottenham Hotspur in October 2020. In 2022, Rodon helped Wales qualify for the [[FIFA World Cup]] for the first time since 1958.<ref>{{cite web |title=Wales-Ukraine {{!}} European Qualifiers 2022 |url=https://www.uefa.com/european-qualifiers/match/2033893--wales-vs-ukraine/ |access-date=14 June 2022 |website=UEFA.com}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Swansea City===
Rodon grew up in [[Llangyfelach]], [[Swansea]], went to Llangyfelach Primary School, and was a [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]] season ticket holder before joining the club at the age of eight in 2005.<ref name="dedg">{{cite news|last1=Wathan|first1=Chris|title=The talented youngsters pushing for first XI places|url=https://www.walesonline.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/six-talented-swansea-city-youngsters-11246769|access-date=3 February 2018|website=WalesOnline|date=26 April 2016}}</ref> He signed his first professional contract in July 2015 and was named on the first-team bench for the first time in January 2016 for the [[FA Cup]] tie with [[Oxford United F.C.|Oxford United]].<ref name="dedg"/> He was then named on the first-team bench in the [[Premier League]] game at the [[Emirates Stadium]] against [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] in October 2017. Rodon played as captain of [[Swansea City A.F.C. Under-23s|Swansea's under-23 team]] in a 2–1 win over [[Cheltenham Town F.C.|Cheltenham Town]] in the [[EFL Trophy]] on 15 August 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.skysports.com/football/cheltenham-vs-swansea-city-u21/teams/380691|title=Cheltenham Town 1–2 Swansea City U21s|website=Sky Sports |access-date=15 August 2017}}</ref>

On 30 January 2018, he joined [[EFL League Two|League Two]] side Cheltenham Town on loan until the end of the [[2017–18 Cheltenham Town F.C. season|2017–18]] season.<ref>{{cite news|title=Joe Rodon: Cheltenham Town sign Swansea City defender on loan|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/42878118|access-date=3 February 2018|website=BBC Sport|date=30 January 2018}}</ref> Manager [[Gary Johnson (footballer, born 1955)|Gary Johnson]] said that "we have been keeping an eye on him for some time and he is undoubtedly a quality player".<ref>{{cite web |title=New Signing: Joe Rodon|url=https://www.ctfc.com/news/2018/january/signing-joe-rodon/|access-date=3 February 2018|publisher=Cheltenham Town F.C.|date=30 January 2018}}</ref>

Rodon returned to Swansea and was given his senior debut by manager [[Graham Potter]] ahead of the 2018–19 [[EFL Championship|Championship]] season. He became a regular starter at the club and made 21 appearances the following season under new coach [[Steve Cooper (football manager)|Steve Cooper]].

===Tottenham Hotspur===
Rodon signed for Premier League club [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] on 16 October 2020 for a fee of around £11 million on a five-year contract.<ref name="Rodon Leaves Swansea">{{cite web|url=https://www.swanseacity.com/news/joe-rodon-completes-move-tottenham-hotspur |title=Joe Rodon completes move to Tottenham Hotspur |publisher=Swansea City A.F.C. |access-date=16 October 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54577706 |title=Joe Rodon: Tottenham sign Wales centre-back from Swansea for £11m |website=BBC Sport |date=16 October 2020 |access-date=17 October 2020}}</ref> Rodon made his Premier League debut for Tottenham Hotspur on 26 October 2020 after coming on as a substitute for [[Son Heung-min]] in the 93rd minute in the 1–0 win away against [[Burnley F.C.|Burnley]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54594926 |title=Burnley 0–1 Tottenham Hotspur |first=Emlyn |last=Begley |work=BBC Sport |access-date=26 October 2020}}</ref> He made his first start for the club in the away match against [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] on 29 November as [[Toby Alderweireld]] was out injured, and helped keep a clean sheet in a 0–0 draw.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.walesonline.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/unseen-moment-tottenhams-jose-mourinho-19369621 |title=The unseen moment Tottenham's Jose Mourinho went 'crazy' at Joe Rodon vs Chelsea and his telling verdict|date=30 November 2020 |work=Wales Online |first=Mathew |last=Davies }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/joe-rodon-tottenham-debut-mistake-chelsea-b104373.html |title=Tottenham defender Joe Rodon vows to learn from Chelsea mistake after late let-off |first= Dan|last= Kilpatrick|date=29 November 2020|work=Evening Standard }}</ref>
====Rennes (loan)====
On 1 August 2022, it was announced that Rodon had joined French club [[Stade Rennais F.C.|Rennes]] on a year-long loan with an option to buy.<ref>{{cite web |title=Rodon joins Rennes on loan |url=https://www.tottenhamhotspur.com/news/2022/august/rodon-joins-rennes-on-loan/ |publisher=Tottenham Hotspur |access-date=1 August 2022 |date=1 August 2022 |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Joe Rodon est rouge et noir! |url=https://www.staderennais.com/joe-rodon-est-rouge-et-noir |publisher=Stade Rennais |access-date=1 August 2022 |date=1 August 2022 |language=French |url-status=live}}</ref>

==International career==
He was named in a preliminary senior [[Wales national football team|Wales]] squad in May 2018 for a friendly against [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]] but was left out of the final selection.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/44136238|title=Hal Robson-Kanu not included in Wales squad for Mexico friendly |website=BBC Sport |date=20 May 2018|access-date=21 May 2018}}</ref> He was called up for a competitive game for the first time for an September 2019 qualifier against [[Azerbaijan national football team|Azerbaijan]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49404349|title=Ashley Williams: Wales captain left out of squad for Euro 2020 qualifier|website=BBC Sport|date=20 August 2019|access-date=21 August 2019}}</ref>  Rodon made his debut against Azerbaijan, playing all 90 minutes in a 2–1 win.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49519722|title=Wales 2-1 Azerbaijan|work=BBC Sport }}</ref> In May 2021 he was selected for the Wales squad for the delayed [[UEFA Euro 2020]] tournament.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57301006|title=Uncapped Colwill in Wales Euro 2020 squad|work=BBC Sport }}</ref> In November 2022 he was named in the Wales squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63545011 2022 Wales World Cup squad]</ref>

==Style of play==
Rodon is a ball-playing [[centre-back]], and has earned comparisons to [[Fikayo Tomori]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Celtic linked with Swansea defender Joe Rodon|url=https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/celtic-linked-swansea-defender-joe-11143770|access-date=3 February 2018|newspaper=Daily Record|date=10 September 2017}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Rodon's father Keri played basketball for Wales.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.theguardian.com/football/2021/jun/24/wales-denmark-euro-2020-last-16-elis-james-joe-rodon-gareth-bale|title=Rodon, Bale and the dream of more famous Wales fridge magnet moments &#124; Elis James|date=24 June 2021|website=the Guardian}}</ref>

Rodon's grandfather [[Peter Rodon|Peter]], and his uncle [[Chris Rodon]] were also professional footballers.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://theathletic.com/2127522/2020/10/16/joe-rodon-tottenham-swansea/|title=The story of Tottenham's new centre-back Joe Rodon, told by his parents|first=Stuart|last=James|website=The Athletic}}</ref>
