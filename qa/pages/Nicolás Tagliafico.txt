'''Nicolás Alejandro Tagliafico''' (born 31 August 1992) is an Argentine professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[left back]] for [[Ligue 1]] club [[Olympique Lyonnais|Lyon]] and the [[Argentina national football team|Argentina national team]]. He was a member of the Argentina team that won the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

== Biography ==
Of [[Italians|Italian]] origins, his grandparents are from the city of [[Genoa]] on the paternal side and [[Calabria|Calabrian]] on the maternal side; he also has [[Italian citizenship]].<ref>[https://www.gazzetta.it/Calcio/Champions-League/Juventus/storie/09-04-2019/ajax-tagliafico-la-juventus-ronaldo-si-difende-squadra-dybala-messi-lautaro-higuain-icardi-zanetti/ Ajax, Tagliafico towards Juventus: "Ronaldo defends himself as a team", La Gazzetta dello Sport]</ref>

==Club career==
===Banfield===
Tagliafico made his professional debut for [[Club Atlético Banfield|Banfield]] on a 2−1 away victory against [[Club Atlético Tigre|Tigre]] for the fifth fixture of the [[2010–11 Argentine Primera División season#Torneo Clausura|2011 Clausura]], coming on as a substitute on the 72nd minute.<ref name=AS>{{cite web |url=http://www.argentinesoccer.com/player_apps.cfm?id=tgfc&Championship=cl11&Team=ban |title=Closing '11 statistics |publisher=Argentine Soccer |access-date=27 July 2011 }}</ref> Due to [[Marcelo Bustamante]]'s ban, he was a starter the following game, a 2−2 draw with [[Club Atlético Huracán|Huracán]].<ref name=AS/> In 2012, Tagliafico signed a one-year loan deal with Spanish club [[Real Murcia]].

===Independiente===
In 2015, he made the move to [[Club Atlético Independiente|Independiente]] for an undisclosed transfer fee and made his professional debut for the club during a 3−2 away win at [[Newell's Old Boys]]. Tagliafico would end up captaining the team that won the [[2017 Copa Sudamericana]].

===Ajax===
On 5 January 2018, Tagliafico made the move to [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]] for a fee of £4 million and made his professional debut for the club during a 2−0 home win against [[Feyenoord]] on 21 January 2018 in the [[De Klassieker|Klassieker]].
Since then he has played over 100 league games for the club and scored 7 league goals and became a starter for the team in 2019.

===Lyon===
On 23 July 2022, he joined [[Ligue 1]] club [[Olympique Lyonnais|Lyon]] on a three-year deal until June 2025, for €4.2 million<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.ol.fr/en/news/OL-welcomes-Argentine-defender-Nicolas-Tagliafico-through-2025 | title=OL.fr - Olympique Lyonnais }}</ref>

==International career==
Tagliafico has played in all the youth categories of the Argentina national team: under-15, [[Argentina national under-17 football team|under-17]] and [[Argentina national under-20 football team|under-20]]. Tagliafico impressed for the Argentina national under-20 football team at the [[2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup]] in [[Colombia]]; however, he missed Argentina's final kick in the quarter-final penalty [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|shoot-out]] against [[Portugal national under-20 football team|Portugal]], meaning Argentina exited that competition 5–4 on penalties ([[Cartagena, Colombia|Cartagena]], 13 August 2011).

In May 2018, he was named in Argentina's final 23 man [[Argentina national football team|squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018 World Cup]] in [[Russia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/world-cup/argentina-world-cup-squad-paulo-dybala-mauro-icardi-a8362096.html |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/archive/20220620/https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/world-cup/argentina-world-cup-squad-paulo-dybala-mauro-icardi-a8362096.html |archive-date=20 June 2022 |url-access=subscription |url-status=live|title=Argentina: Paulo Dybala completes star quartet as big name misses out|date=21 May 2018|website=independent.co.uk|publisher=The Independent|access-date=6 April 2020}}</ref> He also played the [[2019 Copa América]], and took with Argentina the third place, through a 2–1 victory over [[Chile national football team|Chile]] in the third-place match.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://copaamerica.com/es/noticias/Argentina-vencio-2-1-a-Chile-y-se-quedo-con-el-tercer-puesto/|title=Argentina venció 2-1 a Chile y se quedó con el tercer puesto|trans-title=Argentina beat Chile 2-1 and took third place|language=es|website=copaamerica.com|publisher=CONMEBOL|date=6 July 2019|access-date=6 April 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190708015400/https://copaamerica.com/es/noticias/argentina-vencio-2-1-a-chile-y-se-quedo-con-el-tercer-puesto/|archive-date=8 July 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In June 2021, he was included in [[Lionel Scaloni]]'s final Argentina 28-man squad for the [[2021 Copa América]].<ref>{{cite web |url = https://en.as.com/en/2021/06/14/football/1623671691_620875.html |title = Argentina National Team Squad 2021 |work = Diario AS |date = 15 June 2021 }}</ref>

He is the only foreign player to became champion at club level (Independiente, Sudamericana 2017) and at international level (Argentina, Copa América 2021) at the Maracaná stadium.

In November 2022, he was named in Argentina's final 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar by Scaloni. Tagliafico was part of the starting line-up in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup Final|final]] against [[France national football team|France]], where Argentina won the World Cup by a score of 4–2 on penalties.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/fifaplus/en/match-centre/match/17/255711/285077/400128145?country=DE&wtw-filter=ALL |title=Argentina 3–3 France (Argentina wins 4–2 on penalties) |publisher=FIFA |date=18 December 2022 }}</ref>

==Style of play==
He is noted for his "lung-busting stamina, excellent technique and continuous enterprise". As a result of his skills, as well as his playing position and club, and nationality, and striking visual resemblance Tagliafico has been compared to the [[F.C. Internazionale Milano|Internazionale]] and [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]] full-back [[Javier Zanetti]], formerly also of Banfield.<ref>{{cite web |title=Nicolás Tagliafico: the New Javier Zanetti? |url=http://mediolana.wordpress.com/2011/08/14/nicolas-tagliafico-the-new-javier-zanetti/ |publisher=Mediolana |date=14 August 2011 |access-date=3 January 2014 }}</ref>
