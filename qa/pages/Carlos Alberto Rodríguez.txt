'''Carlos Alberto''' "'''Charly'''" '''Rodríguez Gómez''' (born 3 January 1997) is a Mexican professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[Liga MX]] club [[Cruz Azul]] and the [[Mexico national football team|Mexico national team]].

==Club career==
Rodríguez originally hailed from [[C.F. Monterrey]]'s [[C.F. Monterrey Reserves and Academy|youth academy]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Carlos Rodríguez, la nueva promesa del Monterrey|url=https://mexico.as.com/mexico/2018/10/12/futbol/1539359923_264017.html|publisher=Mexico.as.com|date=12 October 2018|first=Alejandro|last= Martínez|language=Spanish}}</ref> He made his professional debut with Monterrey on 28 September 2016, in a [[2016–17 CONCACAF Champions League]] group stage match against [[Don Bosco FC|Don Bosco]], winning 3–0. Loaned out to [[Segunda División B|Spanish third division]] team [[CD Toledo|Toledo]], he made 30 league appearances along with scoring three goals.<ref>{{cite web|title=Carlos Rodríguez: Del descenso en Segunda B, a subirse al Tri|url=https://mexico.as.com/mexico/2019/02/12/futbol/1549943804_687627.html|publisher=Mexico.as.com|first=Mariano|last=Sánchez|date=11 February 2019|language=Spanish}}</ref>

Returning to Monterrey for the [[2018–19 Liga MX season|2018–19 season]], he made his [[Liga MX]] debut on 20 October 2018, coming on as a substitute in a 2–1 victory over [[Deportivo Toluca F.C.|Toluca]].<ref name="llega">{{cite web|title=Charly Rodríguez llega al Tri a cinco meses en Liga MX|url=https://www.publimetro.com.mx/mx/deportes/2019/02/11/rodriguez-tri-cinco-meses-liga.html|work=Publimetro.com|first=Israel|last=Salazar|date=11 February 2019|language=Spanish}}</ref> He would score his first goal with Monterrey on 15 February 2019, in a 3–2 victory over [[Monarcas Morelia]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Carlos Rodriguez, seleccionado nacional y primer gol con Monterrey en una semana|url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/liga-mx/2019/02/16/5c67856046163ffd258b4620.html|work=Marca Claro México|date=15 February 2019|language=Spanish}}</ref> He would prove to be a vital part of the club as they would win the [[2019 CONCACAF Champions League Finals]] against [[Clásico Regiomontano|town rivals]] [[Tigres UANL]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Rayados vanquish frustration of previous Final Regia loss with Concacaf Champions League victory|url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/rayados-vanquish-frustration-of-previous-final-regia-loss/g5fh6dfy2t9z1u9kvqq7l9dz3|work=Goal.com|first=Jon|last=Arnold|date=2 May 2019}}</ref> and be included in the competition's Team of the Tournament.<ref name=TotT>{{cite tweet |author=Scotiabank Concacaf Champions League|user=TheChampions |number=1124021294750732288 |title=Presenting the Scotiabank Concacaf Champions League 2019 Team of the Tournament!|date=2 May 2019 |access-date=2 May 2019}}</ref>

On 14 December, in the [[2019 FIFA Club World Cup]] quarter-final match against [[Al Sadd SC|Al Sadd]], he would score Monterrey's third goal in a 3–2 victory.<ref>{{cite web|title=Club World Cup: Monterrey edge Al-Sadd to set up semifinal vs. Liverpool|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/report?gameId=557730|first=Tom|last=Marshall|work=ESPN|date=14 December 2019}}</ref> At the end of the month, he disputed in both legs of the [[Apertura 2019 Liga MX championship stage|Apertura championship]] finals against [[Club América|América]]. He would score an own goal in the first leg but Monterrey eventually won the match with a 2–1 score.<ref>{{cite web|title=Epic golazo gives Monterrey leg up in Liga MX final vs. Club America|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/report?gameId=560780|work=ESPN|first=Tom|last=Marshall|date=26 December 2019}}</ref> After a 1–2 loss in the second leg, Monterrey would win the finals after defeating América in a [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shoot-out]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Monterrey defeat Club America on penalties to win Liga MX 2019 Apertura title|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/report?gameId=560779|work=ESPN|first=Tom|last=Marshall|date=30 December 2019}}</ref> He was eventually included in the Best XI of the Apertura 2019.<ref name="A2019">{{cite web|title=La Liga MX da a conocer el once ideal del Apertura 2019|url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/liga-mx/2020/01/04/5e0fcab746163fa2138b4589.html|work=Marca Claro México|date=3 January 2020|language=Spanish}}</ref>

With Monterrey's victory of the [[2019–20 Copa MX]], they had obtained the [[Treble (association football)|continental treble]].<ref>{{cite web|title=¡TRI-PLE-TE! {{!}} Rayados consiguió triplete y ahora va por el campeonato del Guard1anes 2020|url=https://www.90min.com/es/posts/tri-ple-te-rayados-consiguio-triplete-y-ahora-va-por-el-campeonato-del-guard1anes-2020|work=90 Min|language=Spanish|date=5 November 2020}}</ref>

==International career==
===Youth===
Rodríguez participated at the [[2020 CONCACAF Men's Olympic Qualifying Championship|2020 CONCACAF Olympic Qualifying Championship]], scoring one goal in all five matches, where Mexico won the competition. He was subsequently called up to participate in the [[2020 Summer Olympics]]. Rodríguez won the [[bronze medal]] with the Olympic team.<ref>{{cite web|title=Revelan lista oficial de la Selección Sub-23 que acudirá a Tokio 2020|date=5 July 2021|url=https://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/selecciones/nota/_/id/8854207/mexico-sub23-convocatoria-jaime-lozano-tokio-2020}}</ref>

===Senior===
Five months after his [[Liga MX]] debut, Rodríguez was included in [[Gerardo Martino]]'s first minicamp,<ref name="llega"/> and on 22 March 2019, he made his debut for the [[Mexico national football team|senior national team]] in a friendly against Chile, as a starter.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[ESPN]]|url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/match?gameId=532973|title=Mexico v Chile game report|date=22 March 2019}}</ref> Following his debut, Martino stated "Carlos Rodríguez plays like he plays, which looks like a 30-year-old."<ref>{{cite web|title=Rodriguez looks like player beyond his years in Mexico debut|url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/carlos-rodriguez-looks-like-player-beyond-his-years-in/1c32szf1iar7a1xsltl8k64eqf|publisher=Goal.com|first=Jon|last=Arnold|date=23 March 2019}}</ref>

In May 2019, Rodríguez was included in the preliminary roster for the [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Marco Fabián vuelve y no está Tecatito; anuncian lista previa de México para Copa Oro|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/seleccion-mayor/seleccion-mexicana-prelista-copa-oro-2019-tata-martino|work=MedioTiempo.com|first=Enrique|last=Martínez Villar|date=14 May 2019|language=Spanish}}</ref> and was included in the final list.<ref>{{cite web|title=Selección Mexicana anuncia lista definitiva para Copa Oro 2019|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/copa-oro/seleccion-mexicana-anuncia-lista-definitiva-copa-oro-2019|date=6 June 2019|work=MedioTiempo.com|language=Spanish}}</ref> He would appear in all matches of the tournament as Mexico went on to win the [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup Final|final]] against the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Mexico Turns Tide, Wins Gold Cup Title Again vs. Wasteful USMNT|url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2019/07/07/mexico-usa-gold-cup-title-dos-santos-pizarro-pulisic-berhalter|publisher=[[Sports Illustrated]]|first=Brian|last=Straus|date=7 July 2019}}</ref>

In October 2022, Rodríguez was named in Mexico's preliminary 31-man [[Mexico national football team|squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], and in November, he was ultimately included in the final 26-man roster.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lista de convocados de la Selección Mexicana al Mundial de Qatar 2022|url=https://www.tvazteca.com/aztecadeportes/lista-oficial-convocados-seleccion-mexicana-mundial-qatar-2022|work=Tvazteca.com|first=Omar|last=Moro|date=14 November 2022|language=Spanish}}</ref>

==Personal life==
On 6 October 2020, Rodríguez tested positive for [[COVID-19]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Carlos Rodríguez tests positive for Covid-19|url=https://en.as.com/en/2020/10/06/football/1602018444_379375.html|work=AS|first=Rodrigo|last=Serrano|date=6 October 2020}}</ref> Following a self-imposed quarantine, he resumed training with [[C.F. Monterrey|Monterrey]] about two weeks later.<ref>{{cite web|title=Charly Rodríguez está de regreso en Rayados tras superar covid-19|url=https://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/liga-mx/charly-rodriguez-regreso-rayados-superar-covid-19|work=Medio Tiempo|date=19 October 2020|language=Spanish}}</ref>
