'''Benjamin Sorba William Thomas''' (born 25 January 1999) is a professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[winger (association football)|winger]] for {{English football updater|HuddersT}} club [[Huddersfield Town A.F.C.|Huddersfield Town]] and the [[Wales national football team|Wales national team]].

==Club career==
===Boreham Wood===
A youth product of [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]], Thomas joined the youth academy of [[Boreham Wood F.C.|Boreham Wood]] at the age of 16. He began his senior career at Boreham Wood aged 17. He was an instrumental part of the under-18 side that reached the [[FA Youth Cup]] second round in [[2016–17 FA Youth Cup|2016–17]], which included a win over [[English Football League]] side [[Northampton Town F.C.|Northampton Town]].<ref name="Cheshunt"/> In January 2018, he spent a month on loan at [[Isthmian League]] North side [[Cheshunt F.C.|Cheshunt]].<ref name="Cheshunt">{{Cite web|url=https://nonleague.pitchero.com/transfers/ambers-bring-wood-youngster-loan/|title=Ambers Bring In Wood Youngster On Loan|website=Non League Pitchero|date=11 January 2018|accessdate=11 October 2021}}</ref>

===Huddersfield Town===
On 13 January 2021, Thomas signed a contract with [[Huddersfield Town A.F.C.|Huddersfield Town]] until the end of 2023–24 season with the club having the option of a further year's extension.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.htafc.com/news/2021/january/sorba-thomas-his-career-so-far/|title=Sorba Thomas: His Career So Far|website=www.htafc.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Transfer: Sorba Thomas Arrives At Town|url=https://www.htafc.com/news/2021/january/transfer-sorba-thomas-arrives-at-town/|access-date=28 August 2021|website=www.htafc.com|language=en-gb}}</ref> He made his professional debut for Huddersfield in a 3–2 [[EFL Championship]] loss to [[Wycombe Wanderers F.C.|Wycombe Wanderers]] on 13 February 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://uk.soccerway.com/matches/2021/02/13/england/championship/huddersfield-town-fc/wycombe-wanderers-fc/3345590/|title=Huddersfield Town vs. Wycombe Wanderers - 13 February 2021 - Soccerway|website=uk.soccerway.com}}</ref>

In the first Championship game of the 2021–22 season, a 1–1 draw with [[Derby County F.C.|Derby County]] on 7 August 2021, Thomas got his first assist, supplying a cross from a free kick which was headed in by [[Naby Sarr]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|title=Covid-hit Terriers fight back at Derby|language=en-GB|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58035735|access-date=10 September 2021}}</ref>  In the following game, a 5–1 home defeat to [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]], he overhit a backpass which ultimately resulted in Fulham's opening goal, but later provided the assist for Huddersfield's only goal from a corner kick, [[Matty Pearson]] scoring with a header.<ref name=":0" />

On 28 August 2021, he scored his first goal (as well as providing two assists) in a 4–0 home victory against [[Reading F.C.|Reading]]: he took possession of the ball at the halfway line, dribbled into the penalty area and placed a low shot past the goalkeeper.<ref>{{Citation|title=⚽️ Extended Highlights {{!}} Huddersfield Town 4-0 Reading|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GqjDDCFoZQ8|language=en|access-date=10 September 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|title=Thomas stars as Terriers thrash Reading|language=en-GB|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58289216|access-date=10 September 2021}}</ref>

On 10 September 2021, Thomas was awarded [[EFL Championship Player of the Month]] for August 2021.<ref name="Aug 21/22 POTM">{{Cite web|title=Sorba Thomas Named Sky Bet Championship Player Of The Month |url=https://www.htafc.com/news/2021/september/sorba-thomas-named-sky-bet-championship-player-of-the-month/|access-date=10 September 2021|website=www.htafc.com|language=en-gb}}</ref> On 19 May 2022, one week before the [[2022 EFL Championship play-off Final]], Thomas signed a new long-term contract with the club until 2026.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.htafc.com/news/2022/may/new-long-term-contract-for-sorba-thomas/|title=NEW LONG-TERM CONTRACT FOR SORBA THOMAS!|website=www.htafc.com|date=19 May 2022|accessdate=22 May 2022}}</ref>

On 25 January 2023, he joined fellow [[EFL Championship]] side [[Blackburn Rovers F.C.|Blackburn Rovers]] on loan for the remainder of the 2022–23 season.<ref>{{cite news |title=LOAN: SORBA THOMAS JOINS BLACKBURN ROVERS |url=https://www.htafc.com/news/2023/january/loan-sorba-thomas-joins-blackburn-rovers/ |access-date=26 January 2023 |publisher=Huddersfield Town AFC |date=25 January 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
In March 2020, Thomas was called up to the [[England national football C team|England C]] team for the first time for a fixture against [[Wales national semi-professional football team|Wales C]] team however this match was postponed due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.borehamwoodfootballclub.co.uk/uncategorized/england-c-come-calling-for-wood-trio/|title=ENGLAND C COME CALLING FOR WOOD TRIO|website=www.borehamwoodfootballclub.co.uk|date=2 March 2020|access-date=2 January 2022}}</ref>

On 28 September 2021, Thomas was called up into the senior [[Wales national football team|Wales]] squad for the first time for their [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)|2022 World Cup qualifiers]] against [[Czech Republic national football team|Czech Republic]] and [[Estonia national football team|Estonia]].<ref name="Wales">{{Cite news|title=Gareth Bale: Real Madrid forward misses Wales' October World Cup qualifiers with injury|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58708812|access-date=28 September 2021|language=en-gb}}</ref> He made his international debut for Wales on 8 October 2021 as a second half substitute in the 2–2 draw against Czech Republic,<ref>{{cite news|title=Thomas Wales debut - World Cup qualifying: Czech Republic 2-2 Wales|date=8 October 2021|work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/live/football/56117260 }}</ref> and completed his first full 90 minutes three days later in the match against Estonia. In November 2022 he was named in the Wales squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63545011 2022 Wales World Cup squad]</ref>

==Personal life==
Born in England, Thomas was born to a Sierra Leonean father and Welsh mother.<ref name="auto"/> He qualifies for [[Wales national football team|Wales]] through his mother who was born in [[Newport, Wales|Newport]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58258631|title=Sorba Thomas: Wales boss Page monitors Huddersfield winger|work=BBC Sport}}</ref>
