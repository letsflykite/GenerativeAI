'''Iké Dominique Ugbo''' (born 21 September 1998) is a professional [[Association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]] for {{English football updater|CardiffC}} club [[Cardiff City F.C.|Cardiff City]], on loan from [[Ligue 2]] club [[ES Troyes AC|Troyes]]. Born in England, he represents the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada national team]].

Born in England, Ugbo grew up in Canada and played youth soccer there before returning to England, where he played with the youth team of [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]]. He had loan spells at [[Barnsley F.C.|Barnsley]], [[Milton Keynes Dons F.C.|Milton Keynes Dons]], [[Scunthorpe United F.C.|Scunthorpe United]], [[Roda JC Kerkrade|Roda JC]] and [[Cercle Brugge K.S.V.|Cercle Brugge]], before signing for [[K.R.C. Genk|Genk]] in 2021.

He represented England at under-17 and under-20 youth levels, before making his senior international debut for [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada]] in 2021.

==Early life==
Ugbo was born in [[Lewisham]], Greater London,<ref name = "11v11">{{cite web|url=https://www.11v11.com/players/ik%C3%A9-ugbo-253790/|title=Profile|access-date=30 August 2018|website=11v11.com|publisher=AFS Enterprises}}</ref> to Nigerian parents.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://guardian.ng/sport/does-the-english-premiership-hold-the-key-to-nigerias-revival/|title=Does the English Premiership hold the key to Nigeria's revival?|date=18 April 2016|access-date=30 August 2018|author=Christian Okpara|publisher=Nigeria Guardian}}</ref> He moved to Canada with his family when he was four or five.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allnigeriasoccer.com/read_news.php?nid=37106|title='I Have Just Been There Ever Since' – Ike Ugbo Reveals How He Ended Up At Chelsea|date=19 August 2020|work=All Nigeria Soccer|first=Ifeanyi|last=Emmanuel}}</ref> In Canada, he played youth soccer with Brampton East SC and [[Woodbridge Strikers]],<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=https://canadasoccer.com/news/welcome-to-canmnt-ike-ugbo-joins-mens-national-team-program/|title=Welcome to #CANMNT: Iké Ugbo joins Men's National Team Program|date=4 November 2021|work=[[Canadian Soccer Association]]}}</ref> winning an international under-10 tournament at Disney in 2008 with the latter.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.yorkregion.com/news-story/1441969-strikers-score-big-to-win-disney-tournament-meet-mickey-mouse/|title=Strikers score big to win Disney tournament, meet Mickey Mouse|work=[[The Newmarket Era]]|date=13 August 2008}}</ref>

Upon his return to the UK, he joined the [[Chelsea F.C. Under-23s and Academy|Chelsea Academy]] at under-10 level.<ref name = "BP">{{cite news|url=http://www.bristolpost.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/ike-ugbo-chelsea-bristol-city--45690|title=Everything you need to know about Chelsea striker Ike Ugbo|date=5 May 2017|access-date=30 August 2018|newspaper=Bristol Post}}</ref> He won the [[UEFA Youth League]] with Chelsea in [[2014–15 UEFA Youth League|2014–15]] and [[2015–16 UEFA Youth League|2015–16]].<ref name="auto"/>

==Club career==
===Chelsea===
In 2015, he signed his first professional contract with [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]], but continued to play for the U17 through U23 sides.<ref name = "BP"/> On 17 July 2017, Ugbo signed a season-long loan deal with [[Barnsley F.C.|Barnsley]].<ref name = "BBC">{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40634252|title=Ike Ugbo: Barnsley sign Chelsea striker on a season-long loan deal|date=17 July 2017|access-date=19 July 2017|website=BBC Sport}}</ref> On 5 August 2017, Ugbo made his debut off the bench against [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]] which resulted in a 3–1 loss.<ref name = "2017-18"/> He scored his first professional goal on 26 August against [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allnigeriasoccer.com/read_news.php?nid=24384|title=Chelsea Loanee Ugbo Scores First Professional Goal For Barnsley|date=27 August 2017|work=All Nigeria Soccer|first=Ifeanyi|last=Emmanuel}}</ref> On 3 January 2018, Ugbo's loan spell with Barnsley was terminated, following a lack of game time.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chelseafc.com/news/latest-news/2018/01/two-return-from-loans.html|title=Two return from loans|date=3 January 2018|access-date=3 January 2018|publisher=Chelsea F.C.}}</ref>

The next day, Ugbo joined [[EFL League One|League One]] club [[Milton Keynes Dons F.C.|Milton Keynes Dons]] on loan for the remainder of the 2017–18 season.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/42570037|title=Ike Ugbo: Chelsea striker joins MK Dons on loan after Barnsley spell|date=4 January 2018|access-date=4 January 2018|website=BBC Sport}}</ref> On 6 January 2018, Ugbo made his debut for the club, featuring in a 1–0 third round [[FA Cup]] away victory over [[Queens Park Rangers F.C.|Queens Park Rangers]].<ref name = "2017-18"/>

He joined [[Scunthorpe United F.C.|Scunthorpe United]] on loan in August 2018, alongside [[Jak Alnwick]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45354844|title=Jak Alnwick and Ike Ugbo: Scunthorpe sign Rangers goalkeeper and Chelsea striker|date=30 August 2018|access-date=30 August 2018|website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

In August 2019, it was announced the striker would head to Holland to spend the season on loan at [[Roda JC Kerkrade|Roda JC]] in the Dutch second tier.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.chelseafc.com/en/teams/on-loan-players/ike-ugbo|title=Ike Ugbo &#124; Official Site &#124; Chelsea Football Club|website=ChelseaFC}}</ref>

On 18 August 2020, he went to Belgian club [[Cercle Brugge K.S.V.|Cercle Brugge]] on loan.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ugbo extends deal and heads to Belgium|url=https://www.chelseafc.com/en/news/2020/08/18/ugbo-extends-deal-and-heads-to-belgium|access-date=18 August 2020|publisher=Chelsea F.C.}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Aanvallende versterking: welkom Ike Ugbo!|url=https://www.cerclebrugge.be/nl/news/aanvallende-versterking-welkom-ike-ugbo|access-date=18 August 2020|publisher=Cercle Brugge K.S.V.|language=nl}}</ref>

===Genk===
In August 2021, he signed a permanent deal with Belgian club [[K.R.C. Genk|Genk]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.chelseafc.com/en/news/2021/08/25/ugbo-leaves-chelsea-for-genk|title=Chelsea Transfer News: Ike Ugo leaves Chelsea for Genk &#124; Official Site &#124; Chelsea Football Club|website=ChelseaFC}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.si.com/soccer/chelsea/transfer-news/official-ike-ugbo-completes-permanent-transfer-to-genk|title=Official: Ike Ugbo Completes Permanent Transfer to Genk|first=Nick|last=Emms|website=Sports Illustrated Chelsea FC News, Analysis and More}}</ref> He made his first competitive appearance on 29 August against [[R.S.C. Anderlecht|Anderlecht]] as a late substitute for [[Paul Onuachu]] and scored on his debut, netting the game-winner in a 1–0 victory.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://dev.ng-sportingnews.com/ca/soccer/news/ike-ugbo-scores-on-genk-debut-against-anderlecht/4whik5da7ira1ko3aafcmuxef|date=29 August 2021|first=Shina|last=Oludare|title=Ike Ugbo scores on Genk debut against Anderlecht|work=[[Sporting News]]}}</ref>

===Troyes===
In January 2022, Ugbo joined [[ES Troyes AC|Troyes]] of [[Ligue 1]] on loan.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.estac.fr/actualites/20976/ike-ugbo-rejoint-l-estac.html|title= Iké Ugbo rejoint L'ESTAC!|trans-title=Iké Ugbo joins ESTAC!|language=fr|date=31 January 2022|work=[[ES Troyes AC|Troyes]]}}</ref> He made his debut for Troyes on 13 February, playing the full 90 in a 5–1 defeat to [[Stade Brestois 29|Brest]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sportsnet.ca/soccer/article/canadians-abroad-roundup-corbeanu-remains-dominant-mk-dons/|date=15 February 2022|first=Peter|last=Galindo|work=[[Sportsnet]]|title=Canadians Abroad Roundup: Corbeanu remains dominant at MK Dons}}</ref> In Ugbo's third game for Troyes against [[Stade Rennais F.C.|Rennes]] on 20 February, he scored his first goal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.estac.fr/actualites/21065/rennes-estac-reactions-resume.html|title=RENNES-ESTAC : RÉACTIONS & RÉSUMÉ|date=21 February 2022}}</ref> In August 2022 Troyes announced they had reached a deal with Genk over the permanent transfer of Ugbo, signing him on a four-year deal until 2026.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.estac.fr/actualites/21589/ike-ugbo-de-retour-en-bleu-blanc.html|date=4 August 2022|title=IKÉ UGBO DE RETOUR EN BLEU & BLANC !}}</ref>

====Cardiff City (loan)====
On 4 July 2023, Ugbo joined [[EFL Championship|Championship]] club [[Cardiff City F.C.|Cardiff City]] on a season-long loan deal.<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.cardiffcityfc.co.uk/news/ike-ugbo-bluebird|title=Iké Ugbo is a Bluebird|website=www.cardiffcityfc.co.uk|date=4 July 2023|accessdate=4 July 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
Ugbo was eligible to represent England (through birth), Canada (through youth residency), and Nigeria (through his parents).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/canada-soccer-1.6237461|title=Former England youth international Ike Ugbo commits to Canada|first=Neil|last=Davidson|work=[[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation|CBC]]|date=4 November 2021}}</ref>

=== England ===
Ugbo has represented England at [[England national under-17 football team|under-17]] and [[England national under-20 football team|under-20 level]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefa.com/england/all-teams/players?p=356976|title=Profile|access-date=5 August 2017|publisher=The Football Association}}</ref> In June 2017, Ugbo represented England U20 at the [[2017 Toulon Tournament]], scoring the only goal in their 1–0 opening group stage match against [[Angola national under-20 football team|Angola]] and converted a penalty kick in the shootout of the final against [[Ivory Coast national under-20 football team|Ivory Coast]], helping his side win the title.<ref name = "BBC"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mkdons.com/news/2018/january/getting-to-know-you-ike-ugbo/|work=[[MK Dons]]|title=Getting to know you: Ike Ugbo|date=4 January 2018}}</ref>
