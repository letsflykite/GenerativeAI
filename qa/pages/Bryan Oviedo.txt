'''Bryan Josué Oviedo Jiménez''' (born 18 February 1990) is a Costa Rican professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[left-back]] or [[left midfielder]] for [[Major League Soccer]] club [[Real Salt Lake]] and the [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica national team]].

He was selected for the Costa Rica national team at the [[Copa América Centenario]] and the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup]]. Oviedo has previously represented Saprissa, [[FC Nordsjælland|Nordsjælland]], [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] and [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]].

==Club career==
===Copenhagen===
In early 2010 he signed for Danish club [[F.C. Copenhagen|Copenhagen]].

===Everton===
On 31 August 2012, Oviedo signed for English side [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] for an undisclosed fee.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/19423536 | title=Bryan Oviedo completes Everton move for £5m fee |publisher=BBC Sport | access-date=31 August 2012}}</ref> He made his debut as an 85th-minute substitute during a 3–0 away league win over [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]] in September.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/19615118 | title=Swansea 0–3 Everton |publisher=BBC Sport | access-date=22 September 2012}}</ref> Oviedo was very much a squad player during his first season with Everton as he made 15 league appearances, 14 of those as a substitute.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bryan Oviedo 2012/2013|url=https://int.soccerway.com/players/bryan-oviedo/42665//PlayerDetails.aspx?PlayerKey=179|work=Soccerbase|access-date=29 June 2013}} {{dead link|date=January 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> In 2012, in a starting 11 against [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]], Oviedo picked up his first assist for the club. He scored his first goal for Everton on 30 November 2013 in a 4–0 win over [[Stoke City F.C.|Stoke City]], also picking up an assist.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/25063878 | title=Everton 4–0 Stoke |publisher=BBC Sport | access-date=30 November 2013}}</ref> Four days later he scored against [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] in the 86th minute in a 1–0 win, leading Everton to their first win at [[Old Trafford]] in 21 years.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/live/match/300998/commentary | title=Manchester United 0–1 Everton | publisher=[[Sky Sports News]] | access-date=4 December 2013|date=4 December 2013}}</ref>

On 25 January 2014, Oviedo suffered a broken left leg 18 minutes into the [[Stevenage F.C.|Stevenage]] vs Everton [[FA Cup]] Fourth Round tie. He sustained the injury from a tackle on Stevenage midfielder [[Simon Heslop]], a clean break to both his [[tibia]] and [[fibula]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Richard Rae |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/jan/25/everton-bryan-oviedo-injury-stevenage |title=Everton's Bryan Oviedo suffers double break of left leg at Stevenage |work=[[The Guardian]] |date=25 January 2014}}</ref>

Immediately after the game, speaking to [[BT Sport]], [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] manager [[Roberto Martínez]] said "A real negative today is the unfortunate incident and we need to give Bryan as much support as we can now. He's had a terrific season and I've been very impressed with him. He's an important player in the group and he deserves a speedy recovery. The injury to Bryan affected everyone. Those type of injuries leave a sick taste."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/25898637 |title=Bryan Oviedo: Everton midfielder suffers double leg fracture |publisher=BBC |date=25 January 2014}}</ref>
Everton later confirmed that Oviedo would require surgery to heal a double fracture of his left leg.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.evertonfc.com/news/archive/2014/01/25/martinez-gives-oviedo-update |title=Martinez Gives Oviedo Update / News Archive / News / evertonfc.com – The Official Website of Everton Football Club |publisher=Everton F.C. |date=25 January 2014 |access-date=25 January 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140127115155/http://www.evertonfc.com/news/archive/2014/01/25/martinez-gives-oviedo-update |archive-date=27 January 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
In May 2014, Oviedo said he was unable to recover from his injury in time to be ready for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup|2014 World Cup]] with his national team.<ref>{{cite news|last=Valverde|first=Esteban|title=Bryan Oviedo no estará en el Mundial de Brasil 2014|url=http://www.aldia.cr/futbol-costa-rica/legionarios/Bryan_Oviedo-legionarios-Everton-Mundial_Brasil_2014_0_351564848.html|access-date=21 May 2014|newspaper=Al Día|date=10 May 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140521110726/http://www.aldia.cr/futbol-costa-rica/legionarios/Bryan_Oviedo-legionarios-Everton-Mundial_Brasil_2014_0_351564848.html|archive-date=21 May 2014}}</ref>

Oviedo returned from injury on 23 September in Everton's 3–0 defeat at Swansea City in the second round of the [[EFL Cup|League Cup]]; he started the match before being replaced after 58 minutes by [[James McCarthy (footballer)|James McCarthy]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Chowdhury|first1=Saj|title=Swansea 3–0 Everton|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/29216771|access-date=26 November 2014|publisher=BBC Sport|date=23 September 2014}}</ref>

===Sunderland===
On 30 January 2017, Oviedo and former Everton teammate [[Darron Gibson]] joined [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]] on permanent deals; with Oviedo penning a three-and-a-half-year deal, taking him up to the summer of 2020; while Gibson signed a one-a-half-year deal to reunite with former manager [[David Moyes]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Jiménez|first1=Daniel|title=Bryan Oviedo deja el Everton y firma con el Sunderland|url=http://www.nacion.com/deportes/legionarios/Bryan-Oviedo-Everton-firma-Sunderland_0_1612838744.html|access-date=30 January 2017|work=[[La Nación (San José)|La Nación]]|date=30 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.safc.com/news/team-news/2017/january/moyes-on-oviedo-and-gibson|title=Double deal: Moyes pleased with new recruits|last=Chamberlain|first=Oscar|date=30 January 2017|website=safc.com|publisher=Sunderland A.F.C.|access-date=30 January 2017}}</ref> On 4 February 2017, Oviedo made his Sunderland debut in a 4–0 win against [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]] at [[Selhurst Park]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.safc.com/news/team-news/2017/february/crystal-palace/team-news-v-palace-a|title=Oviedo and Januzaj start|date=4 February 2017}}</ref>

===Return to Copenhagen===
In July 2019 he re-signed for FC Copenhagen.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49152522|title=Bryan Oviedo: Sunderland's Costa Rica left-back leaves for FC Copenhagen|date=29 July 2019|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> After three seasons at Copenhagen, with only 33 appearances, Oviedo's contract with the Danish club came to an end in June 2022 and was not extended.<ref>[https://www.tipsbladet.dk/nyhed/superliga/faerdig-i-fck-han-har-faaet-et-meget-godt-tilbud Færdig i FCK: Han har fået et meget godt tilbud], tipsbladet.dk, 19 June 2022</ref>

===Real Salt Lake===
On 5 August 2022, Oviedo signed with [[Major League Soccer]] club [[Real Salt Lake]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.rsl.com/news/get-to-know-bryan-oviedo | title=Get to Know Bryan Oviedo &#124; Real Salt Lake }}</ref>

==International career==
In 2007, Oviedo represented Costa Rica at the [[2007 CONCACAF U17 Tournament|CONCACAF U17 Tournament]] (a precursor to the [[CONCACAF U17 Championship]]). Oviedo played in all three games, beating Trinidad and Tobago 2–0, drawing 0–0 with Canada and losing 2–1 to United States. Costa Rica finished in second place in Group B and qualifying for the [[2007 FIFA U-17 World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|title=2007 CONCACAF U17 Tournament Technical Report|url=http://www.concacaf.com/staticFiles/2d/44/0,,12813~148525,00.pdf|publisher=CONCACAF|access-date=31 August 2012|year=2007|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130126133509/http://www.concacaf.com/staticFiles/2d/44/0,,12813~148525,00.pdf|archive-date=26 January 2013}}</ref> Oviedo was not selected in the U17 World Cup squad.

Two years later Oviedo was named in the [[2009 CONCACAF U-20 Championship]] squad. Oviedo's Costa Rica beat Mexico 1–0 in their opening game, drew 0–0 with Trinidad and Tobago and beat Canada 2–1 in the final group stage game. Oviedo was not named in the team for the semi-final against Honduras but played in the final where Costa Rica beat USA 3–0.<ref>{{cite web|title=2009 CONCACAF U20 Championship Technical Report|url=http://www.concacaf.com/staticFiles/24/44/0,,12813~148516,00.pdf|publisher=CONCACAF.com|access-date=31 August 2012|year=2009|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120916122854/http://www.concacaf.com/staticFiles/24/44/0,,12813~148516,00.pdf|archive-date=16 September 2012}}</ref> Due to the successful campaign in the U-20 Championship, Costa Rica qualified for the [[2009 FIFA U-20 World Cup]]. Oviedo retained his place in the national under 20 team and was named in the squad for the U-20 World Cup.

The U20 World Cup did not get off to the best of starts. Oviedo featured in the team in a 5–0 loss to Brazil in Costa Rica's opening game in Group E. In the second game he helped Costa Rica defeat Australia 3–0 and was also named in the team that lost their final group game against Czech Republic 3–2. The three points were enough to qualify and Costa Rica defeated hosts Egypt 2–0 in front of 81,860 in the knock out stage. Costa Rica beat United Arab Emirates 2–1 after extra time in the quarter finals and lost to Brazil 1–0 at the semi-final stage. The Costa Ricans met Hungary in the third place playoff where they were defeated on penalties after a 1–1 draw.<ref>{{cite web|title=2009 FIFA U-20 World Cup Technical Report|url=https://www.fifa.com/mm/document/afdeveloping/technicaldevp/01/14/20/86/u20report_v2.pdf|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111220054010/http://www.fifa.com/mm/document/afdeveloping/technicaldevp/01/14/20/86/u20report%5fv2.pdf|url-status=dead|archive-date=20 December 2011|publisher=FIFA|year=2009}}</ref>

His debut match with the [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica national team]] was against Argentina on 26 January 2010.

Oviedo was named in the Costa Rica national team for the [[2011 CONCACAF Gold Cup]]. Costa Rica finished second in Group A after opening with a 5–0 win against Cuba, a 1–1 draw with El Salvador ensuring qualification before losing 4–1 to Mexico in the final group stage game. Costa Rica were knocked out of the competition following a penalty shootout after a 1–1 draw with Honduras.<ref>{{cite web|title=2011 CONCACAF Gold Cup Technical Report|url=http://www.concacaf.com/staticFiles/5b/8f/0,,12813~167771,00.pdf|publisher=CONCACAF.com|access-date=31 August 2012|year=2011|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130126132443/http://www.concacaf.com/staticFiles/5b/8f/0,,12813~167771,00.pdf|archive-date=26 January 2013}}</ref>

In May 2018 he was named in Costa Rica's 23-man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/costa-rica-names-23-man-world-cup-squad-featuring-keylor/1bw2iz8pkma5614bzushqyyo7g|title=Costa Rica national team World Cup squad: Oscar Ramirez names 23-man roster featuring Keylor Navas, six MLS players - Goal.com|website=www.goal.com}}</ref>
