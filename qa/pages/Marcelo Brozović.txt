==Club career==
===Early career===
Brozoviƒá was born in [[Zagreb]], and graduated from neighbouring [[NK Hrvatski Dragovoljac|Hrvatski Dragovoljac]]'s youth setup. He made his debut as a professional on 24 July 2010, starting and playing the full 90 minutes in a 1‚Äì4 loss against [[GNK Dinamo Zagreb|Dinamo Zagreb]]. Brozoviƒá scored his first goal on 18 March of the following year, the winner against [[NK Karlovac|Karlovac]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2011/03/18/croatia/1-hnl/nk-hrvatski-dragovoljac/nk-karlovac/941280/|title=Hrvatski Dragovoljac vs. Karlovac 1 ‚Äì 0|publisher=Soccerway|date=18 March 2011|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref>

===Lokomotiva===
In July 2011, he signed with [[NK Lokomotiva|Lokomotiva]], after Dragovoljac's relegation.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hrsport.net/mobile/analize/420920/nogomet-1-hnl/lokomotiva-cilja-na-ostanak/|title=Lokomotiva cilja na ostanak|trans-title=Lokomotiva aims to stay|language=hr|date=13 July 2011|access-date=29 June 2014}}</ref> He appeared in 27 league matches in [[2011‚Äì12 Prva HNL|his first campaign]], scoring four times as Lokomotiva achieved a comfortable mid-table position.

===Dinamo Zagreb===
In August 2012, Brozoviƒá signed a seven-year contract with Dinamo Zagreb, as a replacement for [[Hamburger SV]]-bound [[Milan Badelj]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.hrsport.net/vijesti/446038/nogomet-1-hnl/marcelo-brozovic-potpisao-sedmogodisnji-ugovor-s-dinamom/ |title=Marcelo Brozoviƒá potpisao sedmogodi≈°nji ugovor s Dinamom |trans-title=Marcelo Brozovic signed a seven-year contract with Dinamo |language=hr |last=Dubravac |first=Ante |date=31 August 2012 |work=Sportnet.hr |access-date=31 August 2012}}</ref> He was given squad number 77, and made his competitive debut on 14 September in the goalless draw against [[NK Osijek]] at home, appearing as a second-half substitute.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2012/09/14/croatia/1-hnl/nk-dinamo-zagreb/nk-osijek/1287286/|title=Dinamo Zagreb vs. Osijek 0 ‚Äì 0|publisher=Soccerway|date=14 September 2012|access-date=13 December 2016}}</ref> Four days later, Brozoviƒá made his [[UEFA Champions League]] debut by playing full-90 minutes in a 0‚Äì2 home defeat to [[F.C. Porto|Porto]] in the matchday 1 of [[2012‚Äì13 UEFA Champions League group stage#Group A|group stage]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2013/matches/round=2000347/match=2009496/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Porto extend Dinamo losing streak|publisher=UEFA.com|author=David Crossan|date=18 September 2012|access-date=13 December 2016}}</ref>

He had to wait until 14 April of the following year to score his first Dinamo Zagreb goal, as he netted the second of the 2‚Äì0 home win over [[NK Inter Zapre≈°iƒá|Inter Zapre≈°iƒá]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2013/04/14/croatia/1-hnl/nk-dinamo-zagreb/nk-inter-zapresic/1457546/|title=Dinamo Zagreb vs. Inter Zapre≈°iƒá 2 ‚Äì 0|publisher=Soccerway|date=14 April 2013|access-date=13 December 2016}}</ref> Brozoviƒá finished his first season with the club by making 30 appearances in all competitions, including 23 in league, scoring twice in the process, as Dinamo Zagreb won [[2012‚Äì13 Croatian First Football League|league]], reached second round of the [[2012‚Äì13 Croatian Football Cup]], and was eliminated in the group stage of the Champions League.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2013/matches/round=2000347/match=2009576/prematch/preview/|title=Jurƒçiƒá bids to stop Dinamo rot in Zagreb|publisher=UEFA.com|author=Elvir Islamoviƒá|date=3 December 2012|access-date=13 December 2016}}</ref>

===Inter Milan===
On 24 January 2015, Brozoviƒá signed with [[Inter Milan]] on a one-and-a-half year-loan deal, with a conditional obligation for Inter to buy.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/47427|title=Marcelo Brozovic is an Inter player|date=26 January 2015|access-date=27 December 2015|publisher=Inter Milan}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/47438|title=Marcelo Brozovic unveiling|publisher=Inter Milan|date=28 January 2015|access-date=28 January 2015}}</ref> He became the 900th player to wear the ''Nerazzurri'' shirt.<ref>{{cite web |title=Happy Birthday Marcelo! |url=https://static.inter.it/en/news/60425/happy-birthday-marcelo |website=Inter |access-date=26 August 2020 |date=16 November 2017}}</ref> Brozoviƒá took his preferred shirt number 77, last worn at Inter by [[Sulley Muntari]]. He made his official debut with the club on 1 February 2015, coming on as a substitute in the 3‚Äì1 away upset of Inter against [[U.S. Sassuolo Calcio|Sassuolo]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Simone Zaza and Nicola Sansone on target as Sassuolo beat Inter Milan |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/report?gameId=403631 |website=ESPN |access-date=26 August 2020 |date=1 February 2015}}</ref> He scored his first Inter goal in final day in a 4‚Äì3 win over [[Empoli F.C.|Empoli]], helping Inter to end the season with a win.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en-au/match/internazionale-vs-empoli/1836843/report|title=Inter 4-3 Empoli: Seven second-half goals see Nerazzurri finish with a flurry|publisher=Goal.com|date=1 June 2015|access-date=1 June 2015}}</ref> Brozoviƒá finished his first Inter season with 15 league appearances, 13 of them as a starter and one goal.

Brozoviƒá begun his first full-season at Inter by starting in the [[2015‚Äì16 Serie A]] opening match against [[Atalanta B.C.|Atalanta]] at home.<ref>{{cite web |title=Internazionale vs. Atalanta - Football Match Summary - August 24, 2015 |url=https://www.espn.in/football/match?gameId=432260 |website=ESPN |access-date=26 August 2020}}</ref> He scored his first league goal of the season on 23 November during the match against [[Frosinone Calcio|Frosinone]] at home, coming off the bench in 88th minute and netting the fourth goal of the match four minutes later, helping Inter to record its biggest win of the season.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.vavel.com/en/international-football/italy-calcio/570378-review-inter-4-frosinone-0.html|title=Inter Milan 4-0 Frosinone: Delight for Mancini as his team climb top|publisher=Vavel.com|author=Hern√°n Buso|date=23 November 2015|access-date=23 November 2015}}</ref> On 12 December, Brozoviƒá scored his second league goal, a screamer, and the last goal of the 4‚Äì0 away win against [[Udinese Calcio|Udinese]], celebrating the goal with his teammates with "EpicBrozo" signature in the process.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/35030506|title=Mauro Icardi scored twice as Inter Milan dispatched Udinese to extend their lead at the top of Serie A|publisher=BBC.com|date=13 December 2015|access-date=21 January 2016}}</ref>

Three days later, in the [[2015‚Äì16 Coppa Italia]]'s round of 16 match against [[Cagliari Calcio|Cagliari]], Brozoviƒá scored the same identical goal he scored against Udinese, helping Inter to progress in the quarter-final with a 3‚Äì0 win.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/match/internazionale-vs-cagliari/2140639/report|title=Inter 3-0 Cagliari: Mancini's men stroll to Coppa Italia win|publisher=Goal.com|date=15 December 2015|access-date=6 March 2016}}</ref> On 7 February of the following year, he assisted [[Jeison Murillo]]'s goal from a corner kick in the match against [[Hellas Verona F.C.|Hellas Verona]], which finished in a 3‚Äì3 away draw.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/02/07/italy/serie-a/hellas-verona-fc/fc-internazionale-milano/2120610/|title=Hellas Verona vs. Internazionale 3 ‚Äì 3|publisher=Soccerway|date=7 February 2016|access-date=6 March 2016}}</ref> Two week later, Brozoviƒá supplied his second assist to [[Mauro Icardi]] who scored the third goal of the 3‚Äì1 home win against [[U.C. On 2 March, in the returning leg of Coppa Italia's semi-final against [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]] at [[San Siro]], Brozoviƒá scored a brace, including one with penalty kick, to help Inter overturn the 3‚Äì0 defeat and equal the aggregate 3‚Äì3, which led the match into the penalty shootouts; he scored his penalty shootout attempt, but Inter lost 3‚Äì5 and eventually was eliminated from the competition.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/mar/02/internazionale-juventus-coppa-italia-match-report|title=Inter overturn 3-0 deficit but lose Coppa Italia shootout to Juventus|work=The Guardian|date=2 March 2016|access-date=6 March 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.football-italia.net/80709/coppa-juve-penalties-break-inter-hearts|title=Coppa: Juve penalties break Inter hearts|publisher=Football Italia|date=2 March 2016|access-date=6 March 2016}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:Brazil and Croatia match at the FIFA World Cup 2014-06-12 (36).jpg|thumb|left|Brozoviƒá during Brazil and Croatia match at the 2014 FIFA World Cup]]
Brozoviƒá appeared with [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]] in the under-18, [[Croatia national under-19 football team|under-19]], [[Croatia national under-20 football team|under-20]] and [[Croatia national under-21 football team|under-21]] categories. With the latter he appeared well during 2013, scoring seven goals in eight appearances.

On 31 May 2014, Brozoviƒá was included in [[Niko Kovaƒç]]'s final list for [[2014 FIFA World Cup]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/27401303|title=World Cup 2014: Lovren and Jelavic in Croatia squad|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|date=12 June 2014|access-date=29 June 2014}}</ref> and made his debut for the main squad on 7 June, starting in a 1‚Äì0 success against [[Australia national football team|Australia]] at the [[Est√°dio de Pitua√ßu]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/sport/football/australias-10-loss-to-croatia-was-a-step-in-the-right-direction-ahead-of-the-world-cup-kickoff/story-fnkjbhv0-1226946640529?nk=3d134797e589424cea6622cab13ddba3|title=Australia's 1‚Äì0 loss to Croatia was a step in the right direction ahead of the World Cup kick-off |publisher=[[Herald Sun]]|date=7 June 2014|access-date=29 June 2014}}</ref>
Brozoviƒá made his World Cup debut five days later, playing the last 26 minutes in a 1‚Äì3 loss against [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/25285029|title=Brazil 3‚Äì1 Croatia|work=BBC Sport |date=12 June 2014|access-date=29 June 2014}}</ref>

Since the 2014 World Cup, Brozoviƒá became a regular starter in Croatia's Euro 2016 qualifiers, playing alongside [[Luka Modriƒá]] and [[Ivan Rakitiƒá]] as part of a rotating midfield triangle for Croatia. He scored his first goal for Croatia against [[Azerbaijan national football team|Azerbaijan]], in a 6‚Äì0 win in [[Osijek]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro/season=2016/matches/round=2000446/match=2013880/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Croatia beat Azerbaijan for third straight win|publisher=UEFA.com|author=Elvir Islamoviƒá|location=Osijek|date=13 October 2014|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref> His second goal of the Euro 2016 qualifiers came in March 2015 against [[Norway national football team|Norway]], when he gave his team a 1‚Äì0 lead with a low curving shot from 20 meters into the bottom right corner.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.yahoo.com/croatia-thrashes-norway-5-1-euro-qualifier-191458414.html|title=Croatia thrashes Norway 5-1 in Euro qualifier|publisher=Yahoo|access-date=16 December 2015}}</ref>

In May 2016, Brozoviƒá was part of Croatia squad for the [[UEFA Euro 2016]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.croatiaweek.com/croatias-uefa-euro-2016-squad-named/|title=Croatia's UEFA Euro 2016 Squad Named|publisher=Croatia Week|date=16 May 2016|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref> He made his first [[UEFA European Championship]] appearance in Croatia's first match of [[UEFA Euro 2016 Group D|Group D]] versus [[Turkey national football team|Turkey]], playing full 90 minutes as the team won 1‚Äì0.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/36454287|title=Turkey 0-1 Croatia|publisher=BBC Sport|date=12 June 2016|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref> He later netted a brace for Croatia on 12 November 2016 in a [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification ‚Äì UEFA Group I|World Cup qualifier]] against [[Iceland national football team|Iceland]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://icelandreview.com/news/2016/11/14/croatia-beats-iceland-2-0|title=Croatia Beats Iceland 2-0|publisher=Iceland Review|author=Vala Hafstad|date=14 November 2016|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref>

In May 2018, he was named in Croatia's preliminary 32 man [[2018 FIFA World Cup squads#Croatia|squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Russia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.totalsportek.com/football/croatia-squad-world-cup/|title=Croatia World Cup 2018 Squad & Possible Starting Lineups|publisher=Total Sportek|author=Rauf Arshad|date=12 June 2018|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref> He made the final squad, as Croatia progressed all the way to the [[2018 FIFA World Cup Final|final]] versus [[France national football team|France]]. In May 2021, Brozoviƒá was called up for the postponed [[UEFA Euro 2020]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://hns-cff.hr/news/22677/izbornik-dalic-objavio-popis-kandidata-za-euro-2020/ |title=Izbornik Daliƒá objavio popis kandidata za EURO 2020! |trans-title=The selector Daliƒá published the list of candidates for Euro 2020! |work=[[Croatian Football Federation]] |date=17 May 2021 |access-date=17 May 2021 |language=hr}}</ref> In November 2022, he was selected in the 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://hns-cff.hr/en/news/24800/head-coach-dalic-presents-2022-fifa-world-cup-squad/ |title=Head coach Daliƒá presents 2022 FIFA World Cup squad! |publisher=Croatian Football Federation |date=9 November 2022 |access-date=9 November 2022}}</ref> Croatia managed to win the bronze medal in the latter, after a 2‚Äì1 win over [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]] in the third place play-off.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sportingnews.com/us/soccer/news/croatia-vs-morocco-world-cup-live-score-result-third-place/g05i5bcpg8yuty4242vegbzp |title=Croatia vs. Morocco final score, result: Orsic wonderstrike gives Modric World Cup farewell bronze |publisher=sportingnews.com |date=17 December 2022 |accessdate=13 April 2023}}</ref>

==Style of play==
He is noted for his passing, tackling, runs and stamina.<ref name="Brozo">{{cite web|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/football/transfer-news/marcelo-brozovic-everything-arsenal-fans-4657393|title=Marcelo Brozovic: Everything Arsenal fans need to know about the Croatian transfer target|publisher=Mirror.co.uk|author=Adam Durack|date=25 November 2014|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref> He is usually deployed as a [[Midfielder#Deep-lying playmaker (Strolling 10)|deep-lying playmaker]] or [[Midfielder#Box-to-box midfielder|box-to-box]] [[midfielder]], but is considered to be a versatile midfielder who can perform well anywhere in the midfield.<ref name="Brozo"/>He is considered as one of the best midfielders of his generation.
