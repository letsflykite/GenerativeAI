'''Marcelo Brozović''' ({{IPA-sh|martsělo brǒːzoʋitɕ|hr}};<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hjp.znanje.hr/index.php?show=search_by_id&id=fFpmUQ%3D%3D&keyword=Sv.|title=Àmbrōzije|quote=Brózović|website=Hrvatski jezični portal|access-date=17 March 2018|language=sh}}</ref> born 16 November 1992) is a Croatian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[defensive midfielder]] for [[Saudi Professional League]] club [[Al Nassr FC|Al Nassr]] and the [[Croatia national football team|Croatia national team]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=Inter's Brozovic joins Saudi side Al-Nassr |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/66051108 |access-date=2023-07-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=UEFA.com |title=Croatia-Spain {{!}} UEFA Nations League 2023 Final |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefanationsleague/match/2035584--croatia-vs-spain/ |access-date=2023-07-03 |website=UEFA.com |language=en}}</ref> Considered one of the best defensive midfielders in the world, he is known for his astute tactical awareness, robust physical presence, and exceptional passing ability.<ref>{{Cite web|date=6 August 2020|title=Top 10 Defensive Midfielders in the World Right Now|url=https://punditfeed.com/lists/best-defensive-midfielders-world/|access-date=15 August 2021|website=Pundit Feed|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=The 10 Best Defensive Midfielders In The World Right Now, Named And Ranked|url=https://www.sportbible.com/football/53021-best-defensive-midfielders-in-the-world-20210712|access-date=15 August 2021|website=www.sportbible.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=The 25 Best Defensive Midfielders in World Football - Ranked|url=https://www.90min.com/posts/the-25-best-defensive-midfielders-in-world-football-ranked|access-date=15 August 2021|website=90min.com|date=16 March 2020 |language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=1 September 2020|title=Marcelo Brozovic Analysis- Internationale Revival|url=https://crossbarhub.com/tactical-analysis/marcelo-brozovic-analysis-inter-revival/|access-date=15 August 2021|website=CrossBar}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Early career===
Brozović was born in [[Zagreb]], and graduated from neighbouring [[NK Hrvatski Dragovoljac|Hrvatski Dragovoljac]]'s youth setup. He made his debut as a professional on 24 July 2010, starting and playing the full 90 minutes in a 1–4 loss against [[GNK Dinamo Zagreb|Dinamo Zagreb]]. Brozović scored his first goal on 18 March of the following year, the winner against [[NK Karlovac|Karlovac]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2011/03/18/croatia/1-hnl/nk-hrvatski-dragovoljac/nk-karlovac/941280/|title=Hrvatski Dragovoljac vs. Karlovac 1 – 0|publisher=Soccerway|date=18 March 2011|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref>

===Lokomotiva===
In July 2011, he signed with [[NK Lokomotiva|Lokomotiva]], after Dragovoljac's relegation.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hrsport.net/mobile/analize/420920/nogomet-1-hnl/lokomotiva-cilja-na-ostanak/|title=Lokomotiva cilja na ostanak|trans-title=Lokomotiva aims to stay|language=hr|date=13 July 2011|access-date=29 June 2014}}</ref> He appeared in 27 league matches in [[2011–12 Prva HNL|his first campaign]], scoring four times as Lokomotiva achieved a comfortable mid-table position.

===Dinamo Zagreb===
In August 2012, Brozović signed a seven-year contract with Dinamo Zagreb, as a replacement for [[Hamburger SV]]-bound [[Milan Badelj]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.hrsport.net/vijesti/446038/nogomet-1-hnl/marcelo-brozovic-potpisao-sedmogodisnji-ugovor-s-dinamom/ |title=Marcelo Brozović potpisao sedmogodišnji ugovor s Dinamom |trans-title=Marcelo Brozovic signed a seven-year contract with Dinamo |language=hr |last=Dubravac |first=Ante |date=31 August 2012 |work=Sportnet.hr |access-date=31 August 2012}}</ref> He was given squad number 77, and made his competitive debut on 14 September in the goalless draw against [[NK Osijek]] at home, appearing as a second-half substitute.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2012/09/14/croatia/1-hnl/nk-dinamo-zagreb/nk-osijek/1287286/|title=Dinamo Zagreb vs. Osijek 0 – 0|publisher=Soccerway|date=14 September 2012|access-date=13 December 2016}}</ref> Four days later, Brozović made his [[UEFA Champions League]] debut by playing full-90 minutes in a 0–2 home defeat to [[F.C. Porto|Porto]] in the matchday 1 of [[2012–13 UEFA Champions League group stage#Group A|group stage]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2013/matches/round=2000347/match=2009496/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Porto extend Dinamo losing streak|publisher=UEFA.com|author=David Crossan|date=18 September 2012|access-date=13 December 2016}}</ref>

He had to wait until 14 April of the following year to score his first Dinamo Zagreb goal, as he netted the second of the 2–0 home win over [[NK Inter Zaprešić|Inter Zaprešić]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2013/04/14/croatia/1-hnl/nk-dinamo-zagreb/nk-inter-zapresic/1457546/|title=Dinamo Zagreb vs. Inter Zaprešić 2 – 0|publisher=Soccerway|date=14 April 2013|access-date=13 December 2016}}</ref> Brozović finished his first season with the club by making 30 appearances in all competitions, including 23 in league, scoring twice in the process, as Dinamo Zagreb won [[2012–13 Croatian First Football League|league]], reached second round of the [[2012–13 Croatian Football Cup]], and was eliminated in the group stage of the Champions League.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2013/matches/round=2000347/match=2009576/prematch/preview/|title=Jurčić bids to stop Dinamo rot in Zagreb|publisher=UEFA.com|author=Elvir Islamović|date=3 December 2012|access-date=13 December 2016}}</ref>

===Inter Milan===
On 24 January 2015, Brozović signed with [[Inter Milan]] on a one-and-a-half year-loan deal, with a conditional obligation for Inter to buy.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/47427|title=Marcelo Brozovic is an Inter player|date=26 January 2015|access-date=27 December 2015|publisher=Inter Milan}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/47438|title=Marcelo Brozovic unveiling|publisher=Inter Milan|date=28 January 2015|access-date=28 January 2015}}</ref> He became the 900th player to wear the ''Nerazzurri'' shirt.<ref>{{cite web |title=Happy Birthday Marcelo! |url=https://static.inter.it/en/news/60425/happy-birthday-marcelo |website=Inter |access-date=26 August 2020 |date=16 November 2017}}</ref> Brozović took his preferred shirt number 77, last worn at Inter by [[Sulley Muntari]]. He made his official debut with the club on 1 February 2015, coming on as a substitute in the 3–1 away upset of Inter against [[U.S. Sassuolo Calcio|Sassuolo]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Simone Zaza and Nicola Sansone on target as Sassuolo beat Inter Milan |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/report?gameId=403631 |website=ESPN |access-date=26 August 2020 |date=1 February 2015}}</ref> He scored his first Inter goal in final day in a 4–3 win over [[Empoli F.C.|Empoli]], helping Inter to end the season with a win.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en-au/match/internazionale-vs-empoli/1836843/report|title=Inter 4-3 Empoli: Seven second-half goals see Nerazzurri finish with a flurry|publisher=Goal.com|date=1 June 2015|access-date=1 June 2015}}</ref> Brozović finished his first Inter season with 15 league appearances, 13 of them as a starter and one goal.

Brozović begun his first full-season at Inter by starting in the [[2015–16 Serie A]] opening match against [[Atalanta B.C.|Atalanta]] at home.<ref>{{cite web |title=Internazionale vs. Atalanta - Football Match Summary - August 24, 2015 |url=https://www.espn.in/football/match?gameId=432260 |website=ESPN |access-date=26 August 2020}}</ref> He scored his first league goal of the season on 23 November during the match against [[Frosinone Calcio|Frosinone]] at home, coming off the bench in 88th minute and netting the fourth goal of the match four minutes later, helping Inter to record its biggest win of the season.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.vavel.com/en/international-football/italy-calcio/570378-review-inter-4-frosinone-0.html|title=Inter Milan 4-0 Frosinone: Delight for Mancini as his team climb top|publisher=Vavel.com|author=Hernán Buso|date=23 November 2015|access-date=23 November 2015}}</ref> On 12 December, Brozović scored his second league goal, a screamer, and the last goal of the 4–0 away win against [[Udinese Calcio|Udinese]], celebrating the goal with his teammates with "EpicBrozo" signature in the process.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/35030506|title=Mauro Icardi scored twice as Inter Milan dispatched Udinese to extend their lead at the top of Serie A|publisher=BBC.com|date=13 December 2015|access-date=21 January 2016}}</ref>

Three days later, in the [[2015–16 Coppa Italia]]'s round of 16 match against [[Cagliari Calcio|Cagliari]], Brozović scored the same identical goal he scored against Udinese, helping Inter to progress in the quarter-final with a 3–0 win.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/match/internazionale-vs-cagliari/2140639/report|title=Inter 3-0 Cagliari: Mancini's men stroll to Coppa Italia win|publisher=Goal.com|date=15 December 2015|access-date=6 March 2016}}</ref> On 7 February of the following year, he assisted [[Jeison Murillo]]'s goal from a corner kick in the match against [[Hellas Verona F.C.|Hellas Verona]], which finished in a 3–3 away draw.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/02/07/italy/serie-a/hellas-verona-fc/fc-internazionale-milano/2120610/|title=Hellas Verona vs. Internazionale 3 – 3|publisher=Soccerway|date=7 February 2016|access-date=6 March 2016}}</ref> Two week later, Brozović supplied his second assist to [[Mauro Icardi]] who scored the third goal of the 3–1 home win against [[U.C. Sampdoria|Sampdoria]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/sport/news/article.cfm?c_id=4&objectid=11593131|title=Watching Mourinho inspires Inter to 3-1 win over Sampdoria|publisher=Nzherald.co.nz|date=21 February 2016|access-date=6 March 2016}}</ref>

On 2 March, in the returning leg of Coppa Italia's semi-final against [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]] at [[San Siro]], Brozović scored a brace, including one with penalty kick, to help Inter overturn the 3–0 defeat and equal the aggregate 3–3, which led the match into the penalty shootouts; he scored his penalty shootout attempt, but Inter lost 3–5 and eventually was eliminated from the competition.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/mar/02/internazionale-juventus-coppa-italia-match-report|title=Inter overturn 3-0 deficit but lose Coppa Italia shootout to Juventus|work=The Guardian|date=2 March 2016|access-date=6 March 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.football-italia.net/80709/coppa-juve-penalties-break-inter-hearts|title=Coppa: Juve penalties break Inter hearts|publisher=Football Italia|date=2 March 2016|access-date=6 March 2016}}</ref>

Brozović started the new season by playing the last 20 minutes of the 2–0 defeat at [[A.C. Chievo Verona|Chievo Verona]], picking up a yellow card in the 79th minute.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/08/21/italy/serie-a/ac-chievo-verona/fc-internazionale-milano/2305754/|title=Chievo vs. Internazionale 2 – 0|publisher=Soccerway|date=21 August 2016|access-date=12 November 2016}}</ref> On 15 September, during the first match of [[2016–17 UEFA Europa League group stage#Group K|2016–17 UEFA Europa League group stage]] against [[Hapoel Be'er Sheva F.C.|Hapoel Be'er Sheva]], Brozović played as a starter before was substituted in the beginning of the second half; he refused to stay in the bench which created controversies at Inter.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/match/internazionale-vs-hapoel-beer-sheva/2348990/report|title=Inter 0-2 Hapoel Be'er Sheva: Woeful Nerazzurri whipped at home|publisher=Goal.com|date=15 September 2016|access-date=12 December 2016}}</ref> This led the coach [[Frank de Boer]] to omit him for the league match against Juventus for "unprofessional behaviour".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.football-italia.net/91613/unprofessional-brozovic-out-inter-juve|title='Unprofessional' Brozovic out of Inter-Juve|publisher=Football Italia|date=17 September 2016|access-date=12 November 2016}}</ref> Speaking about the issue, De Boer stated that Brozović “has to demonstrate his discipline”, adding that "he did something I couldn’t accept".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.football-italia.net/91740/de-boer-%E2%80%98brozovic-must-prove-himself%E2%80%99|title=De Boer: 'Brozovic must prove himself'|publisher=Football Italia|date=20 September 2016|access-date=12 December 2016}}</ref>

He was finally called back on 20 October for the league match against [[Cagliari Calcio|Cagliari]],<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.football-italia.net/92976/brozovic-back-inter-squad|title=Brozovic back in Inter squad|publisher=Football Italia|date=15 October 2016|access-date=12 December 2016}}</ref> finished in a 1–2 home defeat, remaining as an unused substitute.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/10/16/italy/serie-a/fc-internazionale-milano/cagliari-calcio/2305825/|title=Internazionale vs. Cagliari 1 – 2|publisher=Soccerway|date=16 October 2016|access-date=12 November 2016}}</ref> Four days later, he made his on-field debut in the matchday 3 of 2016–17 UEFA Europa League group stage against [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]], but was sent off in the 77th minute after receiving a second yellow card.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/football/match-reports/inter-milan-1-0-southampton-9091089|title=Inter Milan 1-0 Southampton: Candreva nets stunning winter for 10-man hosts - 5 things we learned|publisher=Mirror.co.uk|author=Adrian Kajumba|date=20 October 2016|access-date=12 December 2016}}</ref>

Brozović improved his game with the new coach [[Stefano Pioli]],<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.gazzetta.it/Calcio/Serie-A/Inter/11-12-2016/inter-brozovic-rinato-pioli-4-gol-5-gare-meglio-icardi-1701215291337.shtml?refresh_ce-cp|title=Inter, Brozovic rinato con Pioli: 4 gol in 5 gare, meglio di Icardi|publisher=Gazzetta.it|date=11 December 2016|access-date=12 December 2016}}</ref> scoring his first goal of the season on 24 November in the 3–2 away defeat to Hapoel Be'er Sheva, despite being 2–0 up in the first half, which confirmed the elimination of Inter from Europa League.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/news/newsid=2423836.html|title=Nine more through to round of 32, Inter Milan out|publisher=UEFA.com|date=24 November 2016|access-date=12 December 2016}}</ref> That was followed by his first league goal four days later, scoring inside two minutes with a right shoot from edge the box, helping Inter past [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]] 4–2 at San Siro.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/55899/inter-put-four-past-fiorentina|title=INTER PUT FOUR PAST FIORENTINA|publisher=Inter Milan|date=28 November 2016|access-date=12 December 2016}}</ref> On 7 December, Brozović extended his contract with Inter Milan until 2021, with the new deal including a significant wage rise.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.football-italia.net/95368/official-brozovic-renews-inter|title=Official: Brozovic renews with Inter|publisher=Football Italia|date=7 December 2016|access-date=12 December 2016}}</ref> Four days later, he scored a double helping Inter to beat Genoa 2–0.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/2016/12/12/nuovo-brozovic-e-la-difesa-a-tre-cosi-pioli-prova-a-sollevare-linter47.html|title=Nuovo Brozovic e la difesa a tre così Pioli prova a sollevare l'Inter|author=Andrea Sorrentino|newspaper=la Repubblica|date=12 December 2016|page=47|language=it}}</ref>

He scored his first goals of the [[2017–18 Inter Milan season|2017–18 season]] on 2 October in the 2–1 home at newly promoted side [[Benevento Calcio|Benevento]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://dailyfootballshow.com/talking-points-benevento-1-inter-milan-2-brozovic-double-inspires-the-nerazzurri-to-rough-win-131/|title=Talking Points: Benevento 1 Inter Milan 2 – Brozovic double inspires the Nerazzurri to rough win|publisher=Daily Football Show|date=2 October 2017|access-date=2 October 2017}}</ref> Later on 11 February of the following year, during the league match versus [[Bologna F.C. 1909|Bologna]], Brozović was replaced in 58th minute after a poor performance; while making his way out of the field, he was booed by some sections of the crowd, and he responded by sarcastically clapping back.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.90min.com/posts/5976852-inter-to-make-an-example-of-playmaker-marcelo-brozovic-for-sarcastic-clap-towards-fans|title=Inter to Make an Example of Playmaker Marcelo Brozovic for Sarcastic Clap Towards Fans|publisher=90min.com|author=Kavan Flavius|date=13 February 2018|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref> He was fined by the club for the incident,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.football-italia.net/117117/brozovic-be-fined-inter|title=Brozovic to be fined by Inter|publisher=Football Italia|date=13 February 2018|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref> and was left on bench for the following match versus [[Genoa C.F.C.|Genoa]], entering on the field only in the final minutes.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/43101306|title=Genoa 2-0 Inter Milan|publisher=BBC Sport|date=17 February 2018|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref> Later on throughout the season, Brozović returned to his best form, cementing his place in the starting and becoming a fan favourite again.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.fedenerazzurra.net/news/2018/5/29/agent-inter-fans-whistled-at-brozovic-and-now-he-is-their-idol-was-close-to-join-sevilla-in-january|title=Agent: "Inter fans whistled at Brozovic and now he is their idol. Was close to join Sevilla in January"|publisher=FedeNerazzurra.net|date=29 May 2018|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://sempreinter.com/2018/05/09/brozovic-has-changed-inter-become-unsellable/|title=Brozovic Has Changed Inter & Become Unsellable|publisher=Sempre Inter|author=Will Beckman|date=9 May 2018|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref> He made his 100th [[Serie A]] appearance on 12 May in the 1–2 upset loss to Sassuolo.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.calciomercato.com/en/news/serie-a-inter-sassuolo-confirmed-lineups-and-live-commentary-43623|title=Serie A: Inter-Sassuolo 1-2, as it happened...|publisher=Calcio Mercato|date=12 May 2018|access-date=24 June 2018}}</ref> In the final match of the season, against [[S.S. Lazio|Lazio]], his delivery in the first half resulted in a [[Danilo D'Ambrosio]] goal, while his cross from a corner kick in the second half was finished by a [[Matías Vecino]] header as Inter won 3–2 to secure a place in the [[UEFA Champions League]] group stage for the [[2018–19 UEFA Champions League|2018–19 season]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/8/main/2018/05/20/44938532/lazio-2-inter-3-spallettis-men-seal-champions-league|title=Lazio 2 Inter 3: Spalletti's men seal Champions League qualification with stunning comeback|publisher=Goal.com|author=Jamie Smith|date=20 May 2018|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref> Individually, he scored four goals and provided nine assists, eight of them in the second part of the season, in 31 league appearances.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/national/italy/serie-a/20172018/regular-season/r42011/|title=2017–18 Serie A statistics|publisher=Soccerway|access-date=22 June 2018}}</ref>

In the [[2018–19 Inter Milan season|2018–19 season]], Brozović made his first UEFA Champions League appearance for Inter on 18 September in the opening [[2018–19 UEFA Champions League group stage#Group B|Group B]] match in a 2–1 comeback win versus [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/match/internazionale-v-tottenham-hotspur/5p9k01y5c49x6lz4hobkvlvmy|title=Inter 2 Tottenham 1: Icardi, Vecino complete dramatic late turnaround|publisher=Goal.com|author=Harry West|date=18 September 2018|access-date=19 September 2018}}</ref> He netted his first goal of the campaign four days later, a 94th-minute winner over [[U.C. Sampdoria|Sampdoria]] in round 5 of Serie A.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/my/news/sampdoria-0-inter-1-brozovic-wins-it-late-after-var-takes-centre-stage|title=Sampdoria 0 Inter 1: Brozovic wins it late after VAR takes centre stage|publisher=FourFourTwo|date=22 September 2018|access-date=23 September 2018}}</ref>

On 26 August 2019, he scored Inter's first goal of the [[2019–20 Inter Milan season|season]] in a 4–0 victory over [[U.S. Lecce|Lecce]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/maybe-inters-brozovic-keen-to-maintain-focus-in-scudetto/1na3j2xaap9wx10c8tyqc3rau6|title='Maybe' - Inter's Brozovic keen to maintain focus in Scudetto challenge after stunning curler vs Lecce|work=Goal.com|access-date=9 February 2020|date=27 August 2019}}</ref> On 21 September he scored the opener in a 2–0 victory over [[A.C. Milan|Milan]] in ''[[Derby della Madonnina]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/49783140|title=AC Milan 0–2 Inter Milan|work=BBC Sport|access-date=9 February 2020|date=21 September 2019}}</ref> On 9 February 2020, Brozović [[Captain (association football)|captained]] Inter in another ''Derby della Madonnina'' and scored in the 4–2 victory.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/51428363|title=Inter Milan 4–2 AC Milan|work=BBC Sport|first=Shamoon|last=Hafez|date=9 February 2020}}</ref> On 17 August, in a [[2019–20 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]] [[2019–20 UEFA Europa League knockout phase#Semi-finals|semi-final]], he provided Danilo D'Ambrosio with an assist for the second goal in a 5–0 victory over [[FC Shakhtar Donetsk|Shakhtar Donetsk]] at [[Merkur Spiel-Arena]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hina|date=17 August 2020|title=Furiozni Inter u finalu Europske lige! Brozović asistirao, pa pokrenuo akciju za 3. gol u potopu Šahtara|url=https://sportske.jutarnji.hr/sn/nogomet/europska-liga/furiozni-inter-u-finalu-europske-lige-brozovic-asistirao-pa-pokrenuo-akciju-za-3-gol-u-potopu-sahtara-15014047|access-date=17 August 2020|website=Sportske novosti|language=hr-hr}}</ref> Four days later, he was named in the starting XI for the [[2020 UEFA Europa League Final|final]] against [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]], providing [[Diego Godín]] with an assist for Inter's second goal. Nevertheless, Sevilla won the trophy after a 3–2 victory over the ''Nerrazzuri''.<ref>{{Cite web|date=21 August 2020|title=Šokantan gol dotukao Brozovićev Inter! Lukaku zabio na pogrešnoj strani, Sevilla osvojila Europsku ligu|url=https://sportske.jutarnji.hr/sn/nogomet/europska-liga/sokantan-gol-dotukao-brozovicev-inter-lukaku-zabio-na-pogresnoj-strani-sevilla-osvojila-europsku-ligu-15014753|website=Sportske novosti|language=hr-hr}}</ref>

On 2 May 2021, four matchdays before the end of the season, Sassuolo drew 1–1 with Atalanta at home, meaning that Inter mathematically secured the [[2020–21 Serie A|Serie A]] title. It was Inter's first league title since [[2009–10 Serie A|2009–10 season]], ending Juventus' nine-season-long league-winning streak. The title was also Brozović's first trophy with the ''Nerrazzuri''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hina|date=2 May 2021|title=VIDEO: Inter je novi prvak Italije, pogledajte gol koji je Brozu, Perišiću i društvu donio veliko slavlje|url=https://sportske.jutarnji.hr/sn/nogomet/serie-a/video-inter-je-novi-prvak-italije-pogledajte-gol-koji-je-brozu-perisicu-i-drustvu-donio-veliko-slavlje-15069933|access-date=2 May 2021|website=Sportske novosti|language=hr-hr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2 May 2021|title=Inter je novi prvak Italije|url=https://www.index.hr/clanak.aspx?id=2273156|access-date=2 May 2021|website=Index.hr|language=hr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2 May 2021|title=Perišić i Brozović prvaci Italije! Konačno prekinuta velika vladavina Juventusa|url=https://gol.dnevnik.hr/clanak/rubrika/nogomet/perisic-i-brozovic-prvaci-italije-konacno-prekinuta-velika-vladavina-juventusa---650143.html|access-date=2 May 2021|website=Gol.hr|language=hr}}</ref> He finished the [[2020–21 Inter Milan season|season]] with two goals and seven assists over all competitions.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Antolić|first=Dražen|date=31 May 2021|title=Epic Brozo u svom stilu najavio turopoljsku premijeru Vatrenih: 'Ako osvojimo Euro, tetovirat ću se!'|url=https://sportske.jutarnji.hr/sn/nogomet/reprezentacija/epic-brozo-u-svom-stilu-najavio-turopoljsku-premijeru-vatrenih-ako-osvojimo-euro-tetovirat-cu-se-15077451|access-date=1 June 2021|website=Sportske novosti|language=hr-hr}}</ref>

On 27 August 2021, in a 3–1 [[2021–22 Serie A|league]] victory over Hellas Verona, Brozović made his 200th [[Serie A]] appearance, becoming the first Croatian to achieve that in the [[Three points for a win|three-points-for-a-win]] era.<ref>{{Cite web|date=30 August 2021|title=Inter 🏆🇮🇹 on Instagram|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CTM3S3hsrhZ |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/iarchive/s/instagram/CTM3S3hsrhZ |archive-date=26 December 2021 |url-access=registration|access-date=30 August 2021|website=[[Instagram]]|language=en}}{{cbignore}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:Brazil and Croatia match at the FIFA World Cup 2014-06-12 (36).jpg|thumb|left|Brozović during Brazil and Croatia match at the 2014 FIFA World Cup]]
Brozović appeared with [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]] in the under-18, [[Croatia national under-19 football team|under-19]], [[Croatia national under-20 football team|under-20]] and [[Croatia national under-21 football team|under-21]] categories. With the latter he appeared well during 2013, scoring seven goals in eight appearances.

On 31 May 2014, Brozović was included in [[Niko Kovač]]'s final list for [[2014 FIFA World Cup]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/27401303|title=World Cup 2014: Lovren and Jelavic in Croatia squad|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|date=12 June 2014|access-date=29 June 2014}}</ref> and made his debut for the main squad on 7 June, starting in a 1–0 success against [[Australia national football team|Australia]] at the [[Estádio de Pituaçu]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/sport/football/australias-10-loss-to-croatia-was-a-step-in-the-right-direction-ahead-of-the-world-cup-kickoff/story-fnkjbhv0-1226946640529?nk=3d134797e589424cea6622cab13ddba3|title=Australia's 1–0 loss to Croatia was a step in the right direction ahead of the World Cup kick-off |publisher=[[Herald Sun]]|date=7 June 2014|access-date=29 June 2014}}</ref>
Brozović made his World Cup debut five days later, playing the last 26 minutes in a 1–3 loss against [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/25285029|title=Brazil 3–1 Croatia|work=BBC Sport |date=12 June 2014|access-date=29 June 2014}}</ref>

Since the 2014 World Cup, Brozović became a regular starter in Croatia's Euro 2016 qualifiers, playing alongside [[Luka Modrić]] and [[Ivan Rakitić]] as part of a rotating midfield triangle for Croatia. He scored his first goal for Croatia against [[Azerbaijan national football team|Azerbaijan]], in a 6–0 win in [[Osijek]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro/season=2016/matches/round=2000446/match=2013880/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Croatia beat Azerbaijan for third straight win|publisher=UEFA.com|author=Elvir Islamović|location=Osijek|date=13 October 2014|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref> His second goal of the Euro 2016 qualifiers came in March 2015 against [[Norway national football team|Norway]], when he gave his team a 1–0 lead with a low curving shot from 20 meters into the bottom right corner.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.yahoo.com/croatia-thrashes-norway-5-1-euro-qualifier-191458414.html|title=Croatia thrashes Norway 5-1 in Euro qualifier|publisher=Yahoo|access-date=16 December 2015}}</ref>

In May 2016, Brozović was part of Croatia squad for the [[UEFA Euro 2016]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.croatiaweek.com/croatias-uefa-euro-2016-squad-named/|title=Croatia's UEFA Euro 2016 Squad Named|publisher=Croatia Week|date=16 May 2016|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref> He made his first [[UEFA European Championship]] appearance in Croatia's first match of [[UEFA Euro 2016 Group D|Group D]] versus [[Turkey national football team|Turkey]], playing full 90 minutes as the team won 1–0.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/36454287|title=Turkey 0-1 Croatia|publisher=BBC Sport|date=12 June 2016|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref> He later netted a brace for Croatia on 12 November 2016 in a [[2018 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group I|World Cup qualifier]] against [[Iceland national football team|Iceland]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://icelandreview.com/news/2016/11/14/croatia-beats-iceland-2-0|title=Croatia Beats Iceland 2-0|publisher=Iceland Review|author=Vala Hafstad|date=14 November 2016|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref>

In May 2018, he was named in Croatia's preliminary 32 man [[2018 FIFA World Cup squads#Croatia|squad]] for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Russia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.totalsportek.com/football/croatia-squad-world-cup/|title=Croatia World Cup 2018 Squad & Possible Starting Lineups|publisher=Total Sportek|author=Rauf Arshad|date=12 June 2018|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref> He made the final squad, as Croatia progressed all the way to the [[2018 FIFA World Cup Final|final]] versus [[France national football team|France]]. Brozović was a mainstay in the starting lineup, and he went the full 90 as Croatia lost 4–2.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44840504|title=World Cup 2018: France 4-2 Croatia - player ratings|date=15 July 2018|access-date=9 October 2019|language=en-GB}}</ref>

In May 2021, Brozović was called up for the postponed [[UEFA Euro 2020]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://hns-cff.hr/news/22677/izbornik-dalic-objavio-popis-kandidata-za-euro-2020/ |title=Izbornik Dalić objavio popis kandidata za EURO 2020! |trans-title=The selector Dalić published the list of candidates for Euro 2020! |work=[[Croatian Football Federation]] |date=17 May 2021 |access-date=17 May 2021 |language=hr}}</ref> In November 2022, he was selected in the 26-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://hns-cff.hr/en/news/24800/head-coach-dalic-presents-2022-fifa-world-cup-squad/ |title=Head coach Dalić presents 2022 FIFA World Cup squad! |publisher=Croatian Football Federation |date=9 November 2022 |access-date=9 November 2022}}</ref> Croatia managed to win the bronze medal in the latter, after a 2–1 win over [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]] in the third place play-off.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sportingnews.com/us/soccer/news/croatia-vs-morocco-world-cup-live-score-result-third-place/g05i5bcpg8yuty4242vegbzp |title=Croatia vs. Morocco final score, result: Orsic wonderstrike gives Modric World Cup farewell bronze |publisher=sportingnews.com |date=17 December 2022 |accessdate=13 April 2023}}</ref>

==Style of play==
He is noted for his passing, tackling, runs and stamina.<ref name="Brozo">{{cite web|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/football/transfer-news/marcelo-brozovic-everything-arsenal-fans-4657393|title=Marcelo Brozovic: Everything Arsenal fans need to know about the Croatian transfer target|publisher=Mirror.co.uk|author=Adam Durack|date=25 November 2014|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref> He is usually deployed as a [[Midfielder#Deep-lying playmaker (Strolling 10)|deep-lying playmaker]] or [[Midfielder#Box-to-box midfielder|box-to-box]] [[midfielder]], but is considered to be a versatile midfielder who can perform well anywhere in the midfield.<ref name="Brozo"/>He is considered as one of the best midfielders of his generation.

==Personal life==
Brozović married Silvija Lihtar in 2016; their daughter, Aurora, was born one year later.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-italia.net/113525/agent-why-brozovic-beast%E2%80%A6|title=Agent: Why Brozovic is a beast…|publisher=Football Italia|date=29 November 2017|access-date=22 June 2018}}</ref> In October 2019 they had their second child, a son called Rafael.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.calciomercato.com/en/news/inter-brozovic-is-a-dad-again-22688|title=Inter, Brozovic is a dad again!|publisher=Calciomercato|access-date=12 February 2020|date=6 October 2019}}</ref>

I.}}</ref> In June 2020, he paid for a [[hip surgery]] for a [[North Macedonia|Macedonian]] woman whom he had never met before.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hina|date=19 June 2020|title=Brozović oduševio regiju predivnom gestom: Ženi koju ne poznaje platio operaciju|url=https://gol.dnevnik.hr/clanak/rubrika/nogomet/fantastican-potez-brozovica-platio-operaciju-zeni-iz-sjeverne-makedonije---609893.html?amp-article|access-date=21 June 2020|website=Gol.hr|language=hr}}</ref>
