'''James Pantemis'''  ({{lang-el|Τζέιμς Παντέμης}};<ref name="ProtoThema ref">{{Cite web|url=https://www.protothema.gr/sports/article/1307589/tzeims-pademis-enas-ellinas-stous-831-paiktes-tou-moudial/|title=Τζέιμς Παντέμης: Ενας Ελληνας στους 831 παίκτες του Μουντιάλ|date=November 15, 2022|website=ProtoThema|language=Greek}}</ref> born February 21, 1997) is a Canadian professional [[association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[CF Montréal]] and the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada national team]].

==Club career==
===Early career===
Pantemis started playing football for [[AS Pierrefonds]] at the age of four and later played for [[Lakeshore SC]] before joining the [[Montreal Impact Academy|Montreal Impact]]'s academy system in 2014.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://canadasoccer.com/index.php?t=profile&pid=3515|title=Canada Soccer|website=canadasoccer.com|access-date=June 2, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=A closer look at the CanMNT U20s call-ups against England|url=http://www.rednationonline.ca/Articles2016/AcloserlookattheCanMNTU20sagainstEngland.aspx|website=rednationonline.ca|publisher=Red Nation|accessdate=August 23, 2017}}</ref>

===FC Montreal===
In 2016 Pantemis joined [[FC Montreal]], the reserve team of the [[CF Montréal|Montreal Impact]] in the [[United Soccer League]]. He made his professional debut on May 2, 2016, in a 1–0 defeat to [[Orlando City SC|Orlando City II]], and also featured in a 2–1 defeat to [[Bethlehem Steel FC|Bethlehem Steel]] on May 15, 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/players/james-pantemis/289038/|title=Canada – J. Pantemis – Profile with news, career statistics and history – Soccerway|website=uk.soccerway.com|access-date=June 2, 2016}}</ref> He played two games for FC Montreal after tearing his LCL in June 2016, and was still injured when the club ceased operations after the 2016 season, leaving him unattached for until he signed with the Impact.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.impactmontreal.com/en/post/2016/11/11/james-pantemis-remains-positive|title=James Pantemis remains positive|website=Montreal Impact|date=November 11, 2016}}</ref>

===Montreal Impact / CF Montréal===
After recovering from his LCL injury, Pantemis would train with [[Serie A]] club [[Bologna F.C. 1909|Bologna]] in September 2017,<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.impactmontreal.com/en/post/2017/09/06/james-pantemis-train-bologna-fc-1909-september |title= James Pantemis to train with Bologna FC 1909 in September |website= Montreal Impact |date= September 6, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.journaldemontreal.com/2017/09/06/le-gardien-james-pantemis-sentrainera-avec-le-fc-bologne |title= Pantemis en stage à Bologne |website= Journal de Montreal |date= September 6, 2017}}</ref> and would sign a first team contract with [[CF Montréal|Montreal Impact]] in November 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.impactmontreal.com/en/post/2017/11/13/james-pantemis-and-thomas-meilleur-gigu%C3%A8re-sign-mls-contracts-montreal-impact|title=James Pantemis and Thomas Meilleur-Giguère sign MLS contracts with Montreal Impact|website=Montreal Impact|date=November 13, 2017}}</ref> He made his competitive debut for the Impact on July 24, 2019, in a [[2019 Canadian Championship|Canadian Championship]] match against [[York 9 FC|York9]] with the Impact winning 1-0.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://matchcenter.mlssoccer.com/matchcenter/2019-07-24-montreal-impact-vs-york9/recap|title=Montreal Impact 1, York9 FC 0 - 2019 Canadian Championship Match Recap|first=Jonathan|last=Sigal|date=July 24, 2019|access-date=July 25, 2019|archive-date=September 23, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200923165752/https://matchcenter.mlssoccer.com/matchcenter/2019-07-24-montreal-impact-vs-york9/recap|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On March 6, 2020, Pantemis was sent on loan to [[Canadian Premier League]] side [[Valour FC]], reuniting with his former [[Canada men's national under-20 soccer team|Canada U20]] coach [[Rob Gale]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Valour FC acquire goalkeeper James Pantemis on loan |url=https://valourfc.canpl.ca/article/valour-fc-acquire-goalkeeper-james-pantemis-on-loan |publisher=Valour FC |accessdate=March 6, 2020 |date=March 6, 2020 }}{{Dead link|date=February 2023 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> He made his debut for Valour on August 16 against [[Cavalry FC]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canpl.ca/matchcentre/7t877d5i442n1ar3pg35nz02c/highlights|title=Canadian Premier League|date=August 16, 2020}}</ref>

After the [[2022 Major League Soccer season|2022 MLS season]], where Pantemis split starting duties with [[Sebastian Breza]], his option was initially declined by CF Montréal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.cfmontreal.com/news/cf-montreal-exercises-options-for-10-players|title=CF Montréal exercises options for 10 players|date=November 15, 2022|work=[[CF Montreal]]}}</ref> Pantemis then signed new one-year deal with the club, with an options for 2024 and 2025.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.cfmontreal.com/news/contract-extension-for-james-pantemis|date=December 16, 2022|title=Contract extension for James Pantemis}}</ref>

==International==
===Youth===
Pantemis was a part of Canada's U-20 program and made his start in a 2–1 victory over England.<ref name=":0" /> In May 2018, Pantemis was named to Canada's under-21 squad for the [[2018 Toulon Tournament]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2018/05/19/canada-national-team-roster-prestigious-toulon-tournament-released|title=Canada national team roster for prestigious Toulon Tournament released|author=Alicia Rodriguez|website=Major League Soccer|date=May 19, 2018}}</ref> In February 2020 Pantemis was named to the provisional roster for the [[Canada men's national under-23 soccer team|Canadian U-23]] team ahead of the [[2020 CONCACAF Men's Olympic Qualifying Championship]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Canada Soccer announces Provisional List for Concacaf Men's Olympic Qualifying |url=https://www.canadasoccer.com/canada-soccer-announces-provisional-list-for-concacaf-men-s-olympic-qualifying-p162594-preview-1 |publisher=Canada Soccer |accessdate=February 26, 2020 |date=February 26, 2020 |archive-date=March 9, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200309191149/https://www.canadasoccer.com/canada-soccer-announces-provisional-list-for-concacaf-men-s-olympic-qualifying-p162594-preview-1 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He was named to the final squad on March 10, 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canadasoccer.com/news/canada-soccer-announces-squad-for-concacaf-mens-olympic-qualifying/|title=CANADA SOCCER ANNOUNCES SQUAD FOR CONCACAF MEN'S OLYMPIC QUALIFYING|date=March 10, 2021}}</ref>

===Senior===
In June 2017, he was called up to the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canadian senior national team]] for a friendly against [[Curaçao national football team|Curaçao]], but did not appear. He was subsequently called up for a friendly against [[Jamaica national football team|Jamaica]] in September of that year.<ref name=":0" />

In November 2022, Pantemis was named to Canada's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canada-announces-26-man-squad-for-2022-fifa-world-cup-in-qatar|date=November 13, 2022|title=Canada announces 26-man squad for 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]|first=Charlie|last=O'Connor-Clarke}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Pantemis was born in Canada and is of Greek descent.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://worldfootballindex.com/2020/08/james-pantemis-montreal-impact-valour-fc-cananda/|title=James Pantemis On His Loan Spell In CPL, Developing With Montreal Impact & The 2026 World Cup|first=Thomas|last=Nef|date=August 26, 2020}}</ref> He holds a [[Greek passport]].<ref name="ProtoThema ref"/> His grandmother comes from [[Ano Doliana|Doliana]], a village in [[Arcadia (region)|Arcadia]], [[Greece]].
