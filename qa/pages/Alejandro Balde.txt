'''Alejandro Balde Martínez''' (born 18 October 2003) is a Spanish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|left-back]] for [[La Liga]] club [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] and the [[Spain national football team|Spain national team]].

==Club career==
Born in [[Barcelona]], [[Catalonia]], to a Bissau-Guinean father and a Dominican mother,<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.sport.es/en/news/barca/alejandro-balde-a-great-opportunity-for-the-la-masia-left-back-11895698 |title=Alejandro Balde: A great opportunity for the la Masia left back |website=sport.es |date=13 July 2021}}</ref> Balde joined [[FC Barcelona]] in 2011 at the age of eight after coming up through ranks at [[RCD Espanyol]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Rogé |first1=Albert |title=Profile: Electric Alejandro Balde has a lot to offer |url=https://www.sport.es/en/news/barca/profile-electric-alejandro-balde-has-a-lot-to-offer-8201674 |website=sport |date=12 November 2020}}</ref> In July 2021, he signed a contract renewal with Barcelona until 2024 with a release clause of 500 million euros.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/alejandro-balde-barcelona-teenage-left-back-500m-release/1ij5oor255tup1i0ls6yty4h47|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210813123056/https://www.goal.com/en/news/alejandro-balde-barcelona-teenage-left-back-500m-release/1ij5oor255tup1i0ls6yty4h47 |archive-date=13 August 2021 |title=Alejandro Balde: Barcelona's 'spectacular' teenage left-back with a €500m release clause |first1=Adrià |last1=Soldevila |date=11 August 2021 |website=Goal|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Balde impressed during pre-season in the summer 2021, and began the new campaign as [[Jordi Alba]]'s primary back-up at [[Camp Nou]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.sport.es/en/news/barca/barca-tie-down-alejandro-balde-their-jordi-alba-backup-11933006 |title=Barca tie down Alejandro Balde, their Jordi Alba backup |website=sport.es |date=22 July 2021}}</ref> He sat on the bench for the opening match against [[Real Sociedad]]<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.fotmob.com/livescores/3629093/lineup/barcelona-vs-real-sociedad?show=ongoing |title=Barcelona vs Real Sociedad lineup in LaLiga |website=fotmob}}</ref> and the next game against [[Athletic Bilbao]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.fotmob.com/livescores/3629105/lineup/athletic-club-vs-barcelona |title=Athletic Club vs Barcelona lineup in LaLiga |website=fotmob}}</ref>On 14 September 2021, he played his first official match for Barcelona's first team replacing Alba in the 74th minute of a 3–0 loss to [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] in the group stage of the [[2021–22 UEFA Champions League|UEFA Champions League]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fotmob.com/livescores/3714046/matchfacts/barcelona-vs-bayern-münchen |title=Champions League vs Barcelona}} </ref>

From the start of 2022–23 season, Balde was given a more prominent role by coach [[Xavi]], starting in more league matches than Jordi Alba and receiving more minutes in important matches.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.sport.es/en/news/barca/xavi-on-balde-an-18-year-old-playing-at-this-level-never-ceases-to-amaze-75566303 |title=Xavi on Balde: An 18-year-old playing at this level never ceases to amaze|website=sport.es |date=17 September 2022}}</ref>

==International career==
Balde received a surprised call up to the [[Spain national football team|Spain national team]] two days before the start of the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], as fellow left-back [[José Gayà]] had injured his ankle in training.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Marsden |first1=Sam |last2=Llorens |first2=Moises |title=World Cup 2022: Barcelona youngster Alejandro Balde replaces injured Jose Gaya in Spain squad |url=https://www.espn.co.uk/football/fifa-world-cup/story/4805677/alejandro-balde-replaces-jose-gaya-in-spain-world-cup-squad |access-date=18 November 2022 |publisher=[[ESPN]] |date=18 November 2022}}</ref> On 23 November 2022, he made his first international appearance in a 7–0 win against [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica]], substituting for [[Jordi Alba]] in the 64th minute.
