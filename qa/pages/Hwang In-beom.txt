'''Hwang In-beom''' ({{lang-ko|황인범}}; born 20 September 1996) is a South Korean professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[Super League Greece]] club [[Olympiacos F.C.|Olympiacos]] and the [[South Korea national football team|South Korea national team]].

==Club career==
Hwang signed with [[Daejeon Citizen]] in 2015. He scored his first goal in a league match against [[Pohang Steelers]] on 30 May and became the youngest scorer in Daejeon history.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://sports.chosun.com/news/ntype.htm?id=201506020100012170000538&servicedate=20150601 |script-title=ko:유스 출신' 황인범, 대전 시티즌 '최연소 득점' 기록세워 |publisher=Sports Chosun |date=1 June 2015|language=ko}}</ref> Hwang interested [[Hamburger SV]] after showing positive prospects in Daejeon and national team, but Daejeon sold Hwang to [[Vancouver Whitecaps FC]] which offered 
more transfer fees than Hamburg.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.taegukwarriors.com/hwang-in-beom-and-family-angry-over-derailed-transfer-to-hamburg-by-shady-agent/ |title=Hwang In-beom and family angry over derailed transfer to Hamburg by "shady" agent [UPDATE 2.15] |publisher=Taeguk Warriors |date=12 February 2019 |access-date=30 July 2022}}</ref>

On 30 January 2019, Hwang joined [[Major League Soccer]] side Vancouver Whitecaps as a Young [[Designated Player Rule|Designated Player]] on a two-year contract, with club options for the 2021 and 2022 seasons.<ref name="Hwang" >{{cite web|url=https://www.whitecapsfc.com/post/2019/01/30/whitecaps-fc-acquire-south-korean-international-hwang-in-beom|title=Whitecaps FC acquire South Korean international midfielder Hwang In-beom|publisher=Vancouver Whitecaps FC|date=30 January 2019}}</ref> He made his debut for the Whitecaps against [[Minnesota United]] on 2 March,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://dailyhive.com/vancouver/whitecaps-fc-mls-season-opener-2019|date=2 March 2019|title=9 players make Whitecaps debut in MLS season opener|first=Mark|last=Dailey}}</ref> and scored his first goal for the side in a 1–0 win over [[Los Angeles FC]] on 17 April.<ref>{{Cite web| title=Whitecaps top LAFC for 1st win of season| first=Gemma |last=Karstens-Smith| publisher=Canadian Broadcasting Corporation| date=18 April 2019| access-date=18 April 2019| url=https://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/mls/mls-vancouver-whitecaps-los-angeles-fc-recap-april-17-1.5103068}}</ref> In the 30th week of the [[2019 Major League Soccer season|2019 season]], Hwang was selected for the Team of the Week after providing three assists against [[LA Galaxy]],<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.whitecapsfc.com/news/hwang-named-mls-team-week-following-three-assist-performance |title=Hwang named to MLS Team of the Week following three-assist performance |publisher=Vancouver Whitecaps FC |date=1 October 2019 |access-date=30 July 2022}}</ref>

On 14 August 2020, Hwang transferred to Russian club [[FC Rubin Kazan|Rubin Kazan]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.rubin-kazan.ru/posts/hvan-in-bom-v-rubine |title=ХВАН ИН БОМ – В "РУБИНЕ" |publisher=Rubin Kazan |date=14 August 2020 |access-date=14 August 2020 |language=ru}}</ref> He was statistically evaluated as one of playmakers who was good at creating chances in the [[Russian Premier League]] until the first half of the 2020–21 season,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://t.me/markstats/341 |archive-url=https://archive.ph/7gMVH#selection-137.0-139.76 |title=Игроки Российской Премьер-Лиги, которые создают наибольшее количество голевых моментов для партнеров. |language=ru |author=Markstats |archive-date=22 December 2020 |date=24 November 2020}}</ref> but suffered [[SARS-CoV-2|coronavirus]] and heel injury afterward.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.hani.co.kr/arti/sports/soccer/969926.html |script-title=ko:벤투호 비상…권창훈·이동준·조현우·황인범 코로나19 양성 |publisher=The Hankyoreh |date=14 November 2020 |language=ko |access-date=30 July 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.chosun.com/sports/sports_photo/2021/03/09/SLN5BU72WSL4T4IA6QUNUPV5MU/ |script-title=ko:보호기는 풀었지만…아킬레스건 부상 황인범,4월께 복귀 전망 |publisher=The Chosun Ilbo |date=9 March 2021 |language=ko |access-date=30 July 2022}}</ref>

On 3 April 2022, Hwang's contract with Rubin was suspended until 30 June 2022 according to special FIFA regulations related to the [[2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine|Russian invasion of Ukraine]]. The regulations allow foreign players in Russia to suspend their contracts until the end of the 2021–22 season and sign with a club outside of Russia until that date.<ref>{{cite press release|publisher=[[FC Rubin Kazan]]|url=https://rubin-kazan.ru/posts/in-bom-hvan-priostanovil-kontrakt-s-rubinom|title=ИН БОМ ХВАН ПРИОСТАНОВИЛ КОНТРАКТ С "РУБИНОМ"|date=3 April 2022|language=ru}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[FIFA]]|url=https://www.fifa.com/about-fifa/organisation/fifa-council/media-releases/fifa-adopts-temporary-employment-and-registration-rules-to-address-several|title=FIFA adopts temporary employment and registration rules to address several issues in relation to war in Ukraine|date=7 March 2022}}</ref> Hwang joined [[FC Seoul]] on 5 April.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[FC Seoul]]|url=https://www.fcseoul.com/media/newsView?seq=3999&resultPart=News|title=FC서울, 국가대표 미드필더 황인범 영입|date=5 April 2022|language=ko}}</ref>

On 29 July 2022, Hwang joined [[Super League Greece]] club [[Olympiacos F.C.|Olympiacos]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=29 July 2022 |title=Στον Ολυμπιακό ο Ίνμπομ Χουάνγκ |url=https://www.olympiacos.org/2022/07/29/ston-olympiako-o-inmpom-xouangk/ |access-date=29 July 2022 |website=ΟΛΥΜΠΙΑΚΟΣ - Olympiacos.org |language=el}}</ref> On 18 August 2022, he scored, on his debut, the equaliser in a [[Europa League play-offs|Europa League playoff]] fixture away at [[Apollon Limassol FC|Apollon Limassol]], which finished 1–1.<ref>{{Cite web |last=UEFA.com |title=Apollon-Olympiacos {{!}} Stats {{!}} UEFA Europa League 2022/23 |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/match/2035499--apollon-vs-olympiacos/statistics/ |access-date=18 August 2022 |website=UEFA.com |language=en}}</ref> On 10 March 2023, he was named [[2022–23 Super League Greece#MVP Month|Super League Greece Player of the Month]] for February.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.slgr.gr/el/article/stoiximan-player-of-the-month-tou-phebrouariou-o-inmpom-khouangk/ |title=Stoiximan Player of the Month του Φεβρουαρίου o Ίνμπομ Χουάνγκ |publisher=Super League Greece |language=el |date=2023-03-10 |access-date=2023-03-30}}</ref>

==International career==
Hwang played a vital role in leading South Korea to the [[Football at the 2018 Asian Games – Men's tournament|2018 Asian Games]] title.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://english.yonhapnews.co.kr/news/2018/09/03/0200000000AEN20180903004400315.html|title=U-23 football team players return to heroes' welcome with Asiad gold|publisher=Yonhap News Agency |date=3 September 2018}}</ref> As a reward, he was discharged from [[Asan Mugunghwa FC|Asan Mugunghwa]] less than halfway through his military service and returned to [[Daejeon Citizen FC|Daejeon Citizen]].<ref name="beom" >{{cite web|url=https://sports.news.naver.com/kfootball/news/read.nhn?oid=144&aid=0000574062 |script-title=ko:눈물 펑펑 황인범 “생일에 조기 전역, 기쁘고 아쉬워…아산·대전 함께 승격하길” [인터뷰&] |trans-title=He was discharged from Asan Mugunghwa early. Because he won the gold medal at the 2018 Asian Games. |publisher=Sports Kyunghyang |language=ko-kr |date=20 September 2018}}</ref>

Hwang made his senior international debut in September 2018, and later that year, he scored his first senior international goal in a 2–2 friendly home draw against [[Panama national football team|Panama]] on 16 October.<ref name="MLS Profile"/>

In the [[2019 AFC Asian Cup]], Hwang showed impressive play and was selected for [[ESPN]]'s Team of the Tournament.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/afc-asian-cup/0/blog/post/3766085/qatars-record-breaking-almoez-ali-leads-asian-cup-team-of-the-tournament |title=Qatar's record-breaking Almoez Ali leads Asian Cup Team of the Tournament |publisher=ESPN |date=3 February 2019 |access-date=14 August 2020}}</ref> He was also named the Most Valuable Player of the [[2019 EAFF E-1 Football Championship|2019 EAFF Championship]] after leading South Korea to the title.<ref name="EAFF2019">{{cite news |url=https://eaff.com/competitions/eaff2019/index.html |title=EAFF E-1 Football Championship 2019 Final Korea Republic |publisher=EAFF |access-date=14 August 2020}}</ref>

Nicknamed the "Bento's crown prince", Hwang was one of South Korean players whom [[Paulo Bento]] trusted most, and he participated in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] as expected. He played all four of South Korea's World Cup matches until the round of 16.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.khan.co.kr/sports/worldcup/article/202212062210005 |script-title=ko:울어버린 ‘벤투 황태자’…“모든 게 감독님 덕분입니다” |lang=ko |publisher=Kyunghyang |date=2022-12-06 |access-date=2023-03-30}}</ref>

==Style of play==
J.J. Adams of ''The [[National Post]]'' noted that Hwang "can play [[Midfielder#Central midfielder|centrally]] or on the [[Midfielder#Winger|wing]], but thrives as an [[Midfielder#Attacking midfielder|attacking mid]] — a [[Midfielder#Box-to-box midfielder|box-to-box]] No. 8. He’s known for precision passing, aggressive tackling, possession composure and a willingness to take on defenders with the ball at his feet".<ref>{{cite news |url=https://nationalpost.com/sports/soccer/mls/vancouver-whitecaps/whitecaps-thrilled-to-be-signing-tantalizing-potential-so-hwang-on/wcm/8867f0df-a112-4e94-8b7b-a7a7d1aeb759 |title=Whitecaps thrilled to be signing tantalizing potential, so Hwang on |work=The National Post |last=Adams |first=J.J. |date=26 January 2019 |access-date=4 March 2020 }}</ref>
