'''Hassan Khalid Hassan Al-Haydos''' ({{lang-ar|حسن خالد حسن الهيدوس}}; born 11 December 1990) is a Qatari professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]] for and [[Captain (association football)|captains]] both [[Qatar Stars League]] club [[Al Sadd SC|Al Sadd]] and the [[Qatar national football team|Qatar national team]].

==Club career==
[[File:Hassan Al Haidous 2012 1.jpg|thumb|left|Al-Haydos playing for Al Sadd in a [[Qatar Stars League|Stars League]] match in 2012]]
Al-Haydos started playing football for [[Al-Sadd Sports Club|Al Sadd]] at the age of eight, advancing through the ranks until he reached the first team at age seventeen. He had support and guidance early on from [[Jafal Rashed Al-Kuwari]].<ref>[http://www.alarab.com.qa/details.php?docId=74297&issueNo=431&secId=20 حسن الهيدوس: نجاحات جفال لن تتوقف عند تركه اللعب] {{Webarchive|url=https://archive.today/20120717033804/http://www.alarab.com.qa/details.php?docId=74297&issueNo=431&secId=20 |date=17 July 2012 }} {{in lang|ar}}</ref>

He was one of the players to take a penalty kick in the penalty-shootout against [[Jeonbuk Hyundai Motors|Jeonbuk]] in the [[2011 AFC Champions League]], scoring after the ball deflected off the top post. The victory ensured Al Sadd would earn a place in the [[FIFA Club World Cup]] as the representative for Asia. In the third-place match between Al Sadd and [[Kashiwa Reysol]], he was again selected to take one of the penalty kicks after the match had ended 0–0. The penalty shoot-out was won by Al Sadd 5–3.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.qfa.com.qa/al-sadd-beat-kashiwa-reysol-for-third-place-in-club-world-cup--.aspx|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120913214829/http://www.qfa.com.qa/al-sadd-beat-kashiwa-reysol-for-third-place-in-club-world-cup--.aspx|url-status=dead|title=QFA &#124; Qatar Football Association - Al Sadd beat Kashiwa Reysol for third place in Club World Cup|archive-date=13 September 2012|access-date=28 February 2012}}</ref>

He was voted as the 'Best Qatari Player' in 2014 in a poll conducted by Doha Stadium, receiving 58 out of 104 votes from a panel of analysts, coaches and administrators.<ref name="alkass1">{{cite web|url=http://cdn.alkass.net/news_details.aspx?news_id=81901|title=استاد الدوحة: الهيدوس أفضل لاعب في 2014|publisher=alkass.net|date=5 January 2015|access-date=5 January 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150106030410/http://cdn.alkass.net/news_details.aspx?news_id=81901|archive-date=6 January 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==International career==
Al-Haydos made his debut for the [[Qatar national under-23 football team|Qatar Olympic team]] in 2007, coming off the bench to score a late goal against [[Japan national under-23 football team|Japan]] in the [[Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's Asian Qualifiers Preliminary Round 3|2008 Summer Olympic Qualifiers]] in order to help his team salvage a 2–1 win.<ref>[http://images.the-afc.com/Documents/competitions/fixtures/2339.pdf AFC - Qatar U-23 vs Japan U-23 - Match Summary]{{Dead link|date=July 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> He featured in two other matches in the competition, one against Saudi Arabia,<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://images.the-afc.com/Documents/competitions/fixtures/2341.pdf |title=AFC - Qatar U-23 vs Saudi Arabia U-23 - Match Summary |access-date=28 February 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120614220314/http://images.the-afc.com/Documents/competitions/fixtures/2341.pdf |archive-date=14 June 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and one against Vietnam, where he scored a goal;<ref>[http://images.the-afc.com/Documents/competitions/fixtures/2343.pdf AFC - Qatar U-23 vs Saudi Arabia U-23 - Match Summary]{{Dead link|date=July 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> both as second-half substitutes. He also played a large part in the [[Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's Asian Qualifiers|2012 Summer Olympic Qualifiers]] as the captain, scoring a goal against India in the preliminary stage.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.the-afc.com/en/component/joomleague/|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120913234000/http://www.the-afc.com/en/component/joomleague/?view=report&compID=407&matchId=3897|url-status=dead|title=Official Home of Asian Football|archive-date=13 September 2012|access-date=28 February 2012}}</ref> He played all the matches in the group stage, scoring a goal against Oman,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.the-afc.com/en/component/joomleague/?view=report&compID=407&matchId=4173|title=AFC - Qatar U-23 vs Oman U-23 - Match Summary}}</ref> and scoring against Saudi Arabia after being set up by [[Saleh Bader Al Yazidi|Saleh Badr]] to give Qatar a 2–1 win and a glance of hope for qualifying for the Olympics.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.the-afc.com/en/component/joomleague/?view=report&compID=407&matchId=4170|title=AFC - Qatar U-23 vs Saudi Arabia U-23 - Match Summary}}</ref> He won the [[Man of the Match]] award.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://cdn.alkass.net/news_details.aspx?news_id=32709|title=حسن الهيدوس : هدفنا التأهل الى اولمبياد لندن}}</ref>

Al-Haydos made his debut for the senior [[Qatar national football team]] in a [[2010 FIFA World Cup]] qualifying match against [[Bahrain national football team|Bahrain]] on 10 September 2008.<ref>{{FIFA player|284190|Hasan Al Haydos}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.allsoccerplayers.com/en/associations/cteam-153.html?PHPSESSID=ba5d4fa50c3836ee846cd4aa4571b680 |title=Qatar National Team |work=All Soccer Players |access-date=7 December 2008 }}{{dead link|date=October 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> He has since made [[List of men's footballers with 100 or more international caps|172 appearances]] and scored 36 goals for the national side, making him the most capped Qatari footballer ever and the fifth highest goalscorer.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Highest Cap Scorer|publisher=Living 2022|url=https://living2022.com/did-you-know/highest-cap-scorer/|date=26 July 2020|access-date=20 September 2021}}</ref>
