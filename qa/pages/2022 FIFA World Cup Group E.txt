Group E of the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] took place from 23 November to 1 December 2022.<ref name="match schedule">{{cite web |url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/464f16f856f5ed05/original/FIFA-World-Cup-Qatar-2022-Match-Schedule.pdf |title=FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022 – Match Schedule |website=FIFA.com |publisher=Fédération Internationale de Football Association |date=11 August 2022 |access-date=11 August 2022}}</ref> The group consisted of [[Spain national football team|Spain]], [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica]], [[Germany national football team|Germany]], and [[Japan national football team|Japan]]. The top two teams, Japan and Spain advanced to the [[2022 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Round of 16|round of 16]].<ref name="regulations">{{cite web |url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/2744a0a5e3ded185/original/FIFA-World-Cup-Qatar-2022-Regulations_EN.pdf |title=Regulations – FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022 |website=FIFA.com |publisher=Fédération Internationale de Football Association |date=15 December 2021 |access-date=30 March 2022}}</ref> Japan became the first Asian team to win a World Cup group since themselves in [[2002 FIFA World Cup Group H|Group H]] and [[South Korea national football team|South Korea]] in [[2002 FIFA World Cup Group D|Group D]], both in [[2002 FIFA World Cup|2002]], a tournament both countries co-hosted and the first country to defeat 2 former World Cup teams which became a big upset. Germany was eliminated from the group stage for the second consecutive tournament after going out as defending champions in [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]]. They became the second reigning world champions to be eliminated in the group stage of the two subsequent tournaments, following the [[2006 FIFA World Cup Final|2006 winners]] [[Italy national football team|Italy]].

===Germany vs Japan===
The two teams had faced each other twice, most recently in 2006 friendly, which finished in a 2–2 draw. 

In the 33rd minute, Germany was awarded a penalty when the Japanese goalkeeper fouled [[David Raum]]. [[İlkay Gündoğan]] scored the penalty with a shot down the middle of the net with the goalkeeper diving to the right.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63644792|title=Germany 1 Japan 2|date=23 November 2022|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=1 December 2022}}</ref>
In the 75th minute, it was 1–1 when [[Ritsu Dōan]] scored, finishing a rebound to the net after [[Manuel Neuer]] had saved a low shot from the left. Japan went in front eight minutes later when [[Takuma Asano]] received the ball down the right wing before running into the penalty area and shooting high to the net from the right of the six-yard box.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.the42.ie/japan-germany-5927859-Nov2022/|title=Japan come from behind in stunning win against Germany|date=23 November 2022|work=The 42|accessdate=1 December 2022}}</ref>
The result was the second consecutive time that Germany lost its opening World Cup match after [[2018 FIFA World Cup Group F#Germany vs Mexico|losing 1–0 to Mexico]] in [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]]. This match also marked the return of [[Mario Götze]] in a World Cup match since after having scored the decisive goal in the extra time in [[2014 FIFA World Cup final]] against [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]] (after sitting out the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] due to physical problems), in the 79th minute.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2022/nov/23/germany-v-japan-world-cup-2022-live|title=Germany 1 Japan 2:As it happened|date=23 November 2022|work=Guardian|accessdate=1 December 2022}}</ref>

===Spain vs Costa Rica===
The Spaniards had met the Costa Ricans three times, all of them being friendly matches, with the most recent on Spanish soil: 5–0 in 2017.<ref name="MundoDeportivo">{{cite news |last1=Muñoz |first1=Felip |title=Rival de España: Nueva Zelanda o Costa Rica, dos selecciones asequibles |url=https://www.mundodeportivo.com/futbol/mundial/20220401/1001778613/rival-espana-nueva-zelanda-costa-rica-dos-equipos-asequibles.html |access-date=1 April 2022 |work=[[Mundo Deportivo]] |date=1 April 2022 |language=es}}</ref>

Costa Rica tied their worst defeat ever, alongside another 7–0 defeat by [[Mexico men's national football team|Mexico]] in 1975. This was Spain's biggest win at the World Cup, surpassing the [[1998 FIFA World Cup Group D#Spain vs Bulgaria|6–1]] win against [[Bulgaria national football team|Bulgaria]] in 1998.

===Japan vs Costa Rica===
Japan had met Costa Rica in four friendlies, winning three and drawing one,<ref>{{cite news |title=Japan national football team: record v Costa Rica |url=https://www.11v11.com/teams/japan/tab/opposingTeams/opposition/Costa%20Rica/ |access-date=1 April 2022 |website=11v11.com}}</ref> with their most recent match being a 3–0 victory for the Japanese at [[Panasonic Stadium Suita]] on 11 September 2018.<ref>{{cite news |title=Japón golea 3-0 a Costa Rica en amistoso |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/futbol-japon-costarica-idLTAKCN1LR1H0-OUSLS |access-date=1 April 2022 |work=Reuters |date=11 September 2018 |language=es}}</ref>

===Spain vs Germany===
The teams had met four times in the World Cup, in Germany's [[1966 FIFA World Cup Group 2#West Germany vs Spain|2–1 group stage victory]] in [[1966 FIFA World Cup|1966]], [[1982 FIFA World Cup Group B#West Germany vs Spain|2–1 second group stage victory]] in [[1982 FIFA World Cup|1982]], a [[1994 FIFA World Cup Group C#Germany vs Spain|1–1 group stage draw]] in [[1994 FIFA World Cup|1994]], and [[2010 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Germany vs Spain|Spain's 1–0 semi-final win]] in [[2010 FIFA World Cup|2010]]. Their most recent meeting was in the [[2020-21 UEFA Nations League]], won 6-0 by Spain.

[[Dani Olmo]] shot a shot in the first half that [[Manuel Neuer]] turned onto the bar. In the 62nd minute substitute [[Álvaro Morata]] put Spain into the lead when he flicked [[Jordi Alba]]'s cross from the left into the net at the near post.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63692161|title=Spain 1 Germany 1|date=27 November 2022|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=1 December 2022}}</ref>
With seven minutes to go, another substitute [[Niclas Füllkrug]] equalized for Germany when he lashed the ball with his right foot high past Spanish goalkeeper [[Unai Simón]] from the right. [[Leroy Sane]] almost won it for Germany in the added time but the ball was eventually cleared from danger.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2022/nov/27/spain-v-germany-world-cup-2022-live|title=Spain 1 Germany 1:As it happened|date=27 November 2022|work=Guardian|accessdate=1 December 2022}}</ref>

===Japan vs Spain===
The two teams previously faced each other once in 2001, a friendly game in which Spain won 1–0.

In this match, Japan midfielder [[Ao Tanaka]] assisted [[Kaoru Mitoma]], who scored a controversial second goal after the ball appeared to go out of play. [[Video assistant referee|VAR]]  took several minutes to confirm that a sliver of the ball stayed in the field, making it a valid goal.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.givemesport.com/88090690-world-cup-japan-score-controversial-goal-vs-spain-as-ball-seemed-to-go-out|title=Fans are desperately trying to work out if the ball went out for Japan's shock goal vs Spain|date=1 December 2022|website=GiveMeSport}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/explained-why-japan-second-goal-spain-controversially-allowed-var/blt78d3cdd7144ece65|title=Explained: Why Japan's second goal against Spain was controversially allowed by VAR despite ball appearing to be out of play &#124; Goal.com|website=www.goal.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/football/2022/12/01/japan-vs-spain-world-cup-2022-live-score-pedri-group-e/|title=Japan vs Spain result: VAR gives Japan victory and puts Germany out of World Cup|first1=Sam|last1=Dean|first2=James|last2=Ducker|first3=Daniel|last3=Zeqiri|newspaper=The Telegraph |date=1 December 2022|via=www.telegraph.co.uk}}</ref> The decision proved critical in Japan's surprise topping of Group E and eliminating Germany from the tournament, with ''Die Mannschaft'' finishing third despite securing a two-goal victory over Costa Rica in the group's other game.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.independent.ie/sport/soccer/world-cup/world-cup-shock-as-germany-dumped-out-after-japan-stun-spain-in-dramatic-finale-42189474.html | title=World Cup shock as Germany dumped out after Japan stun Spain in dramatic finale }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63829718|title=World Cup 2022: Did it cross the line? ===Costa Rica vs Germany===
The Germans had played only one previous match against Costa Rica, which [[2006 FIFA World Cup Group A#Germany vs Costa Rica|served as the opening game]] for the [[2006 FIFA World Cup]] they hosted. Germany beat the Ticos 4–2.<ref>{{cite news |title=Germany 4-2 Costa Rica |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/world_cup_2006/4852498.stm |access-date=1 April 2022 |work=BBC Sport |publisher=British Broadcasting Corporation|date=9 June 2006}}</ref>

Germany won by the same scoreline, although they were still edged out by Spain due to a far worse goal differential (+1 to +6), with Germany being eliminated from the group stage for the second consecutive tournament after [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] (the same result as [[Italy national football team|Italy]] did in [[2014 FIFA World Cup Group D|2014]]).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63829138|title=World Cup 2022: 'Germany exit as a fallen giant of world football'|date=1 December 2022|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=2 December 2022}}</ref> Costa Rica left the tournament as the worst team in regards to goal differential.

==Discipline==
Fair play points would have been used as tiebreakers if the overall and head-to-head records of teams were tied. These were calculated based on yellow and red cards received in all group matches as follows:<ref name="regulations"/>
*first yellow card: −1 point;
*indirect red card (second yellow card): −3 points;
*direct red card: −4 points;
*yellow card and direct red card: −5 points;

Only one of the above deductions was applied to a player in a single match.
