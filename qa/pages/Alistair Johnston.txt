'''Alistair William Johnston''' (born October 8, 1998) is a Canadian professional [[association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|right-back]] for [[Scottish Premiership]] club [[Celtic F.C.|Celtic]] and the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada national team]].

Johnston previously played in [[Major League Soccer]] for [[Nashville SC]] and [[CF Montréal]], and in [[League1 Ontario]] for the [[Vaughan Azzurri]].

==Early life==
Johnston was born in [[Vancouver]] to a Canadian father and Northern Irish mother from [[Newtownards]]. His family moved to [[Montreal]] when he was four, where he began playing soccer with [[Lakeshore SC]]. The family again moved to [[Aurora, Ontario]] when he was seven.<ref>{{Canada Soccer player|id=3772}}</ref> There, he played youth soccer with [[Aurora FC (Canada)|Aurora FC]] and Richmond Hill SC.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.yorkregion.com/news-story/10504794-aurora-s-alistair-johnston-has-canada-s-back-on-fifa-world-cup-qualifying-trail/|title=Aurora's Alistair Johnston has Canada's back on FIFA World Cup qualifying trail|first=John|last=Cudmore|work=Aurora Banner|date=October 27, 2021}}</ref>  He made his debut in the Canadian youth program at an under-18 camp in 2015, when he was 16.<ref name=heads>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com/sports/soccer/2020/11/19/canadian-fullback-alistair-johnston-turning-heads-with-expansion-nashville-sc.html|title=Canadian fullback Alistair Johnston turning heads with expansion Nashville SC|first=Neil|last=Davidson|date=November 19, 2020|work=[[Toronto Star]]}}</ref> He played youth soccer with [[ANB Futbol]], and earned a tryout with French club [[Troyes AC]] while there.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.anbfutbol.com/news_and_buzz_june_30-2_2014.htm|work=[[ANB Futbol]]|title=Player Success|date=June 30, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://socceracademyalliancecanada.wordpress.com/tag/anb-futbol/page/2/|title=Seven ANB players travel to Europe|date=April 22, 2016|work=Soccer Academy Alliance Canada}}</ref> In 2015, he joined [[Vaughan SC]], helping them win the 2015 U-18/U-19 Disney Soccer Showcase, the 2015 OYSL U-18 League title,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://redstormsports.com/sports/mens-soccer/roster/alistair-johnston/1223|title=Alistair Johnson profile|work=[[St. John's Red Storm men's soccer|St. John's Red Storm]]}}</ref> and the 2016 and 2017 U21 [[Ontario Cup]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ontariosoccer.net/news_article/show/816045|title=Vaughan SC Win Fourth U-21 Men's Ontario Cup in a Row|date=July 23, 2017|work=[[Ontario Soccer Association]]}}</ref>

==College career==
Johnston attended [[St. John's University (New York City)|St. John's University]] for two years, before moving to [[Wake Forest University]] in 2018 in search of a bigger challenge both in soccer and academically. He switched positions from central midfield to right-back upon joining Wake Forest.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ctvnews.ca/sports/canadian-alistair-johnston-looks-to-take-next-soccer-step-in-mls-superdraft-1.4759567|title=Canadian Alistair Johnston looks to take next soccer step in MLS SuperDraft|first=Neil|last=Davidson|date=January 9, 2020|work=[[CTV News]]}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Early career===
While in college, Johnston returned to Canada to play with [[League1 Ontario]] side [[Vaughan Azzurri]] from 2015 to 2019 during the college offseason. He played in the [[2019 Canadian Championship]] for Vaughan Azzurri and was sent off in the second leg of the away goals defeat to the [[HFX Wanderers FC|HFX Wanderers]].

===Nashville SC===
On January 9, 2020, Johnston was selected 11th overall by [[Nashville SC]] in the [[2020 MLS SuperDraft]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.cbc.ca/amp/1.5421252 |title=Crop of Canadians taken early in MLS draft |publisher=[[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation|CBC]] |date=January 9, 2020|first=Neil|last=Davidson}}</ref> He officially signed with the club on February 25, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.nashvillesc.com/post/2020/02/25/nashville-sc-extends-offers-2020-mls-superdraft-selections |title=Nashville SC Extends Offers to 2020 MLS SuperDraft Selections &#124; Nashville SC |date=February 25, 2020}}</ref> Due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], which included Nashville withdrawing from the [[MLS is Back Tournament]] due to an outbreak amongst the team,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://torontoobserver.ca/2020/06/03/aurora-soccer-player-johnston-focuses-on-positive-mental-state-during-pandemic/|title=Aurora soccer player Johnston focuses on positive mental state during pandemic|first=Keegan|last=Lavigne|date=June 3, 2020|work=The Toronto Observer}}</ref> his debut was delayed until August 12 against [[FC Dallas]], coming on as a substitute.<ref name=heads /> He made his first start four days later, also against Dallas.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2020/08/17/nashville-sc-youngster-alistair-johnston-hailed-coach-gary-smith-after-impressive|title=Nashville SC youngster Alistair Johnston hailed by coach Gary Smith after impressive first MLS start|date=August 17, 2020|first=Jason|last=Le Miere|work=[[Major League Soccer]]}}</ref> He helped the club reach the [[MLS Cup Playoffs|MLS Playoffs]] in their debut season, making 18 appearances.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://fox17.com/sports/scoreboard/one-on-one-with-nashville-scs-alistair-johnston-on-2020-season-and-a-look-at-playoffs|title=One-on-one with Nashville SC's Alistair Johnston on 2020 season and a look at playoffs|first=Tatum|last=Everett|date=November 16, 2020|work=[[WZTV|WZTV NAshville - Fox 17]]}}</ref>

On September 22, 2021, Johnston scored his first MLS goal in a 5–1 victory over [[Inter Miami]].<ref>{{Cite web|title= Recap: Inter Miami CF 1, Nashville SC 5|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/recap-inter-miami-cf-1-nashville-sc-5|work=[[Major League Soccer]]|accessdate=September 22, 2021}}</ref> In December 2021, Nashville announced they were exercising Johnston's contract option, keeping him at the club through the [[2022 Major League Soccer season|2022 MLS season]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nashvillesc.com/news/nashville-soccer-club-announces-2021-roster-decisions#:~:text=Nashville%20has%20exercised%20the%20contract,their%20options%20exercised%20as%20well.|title=Nashville Soccer Club Announces 2021 Roster Decisions|date=December 10, 2021|work=[[Nashville SC]]}}</ref>

===CF Montréal===
On December 23, 2021, [[CF Montréal]] acquired Johnston from Nashville in exchange for a million dollars in allocation money, with Nashville also retaining a percentage of a transfer fee in a future sale.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tsn.ca/alistair-johnston-cf-montreal-nashville-sc-canada-canmnt-1.1738401|title=CF Montreal acquires CanMNT's Johnston|work=[[The Sports Network|TSN]]|date=December 23, 2021}}</ref> Montreal confirmed the deal on December 27 and announced Johnston had also signed a new contract taking him through 2023, with options for 2024 and 2025.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.cfmontreal.com/news/cf-montreal-acquires-canadian-international-alistair-johnston|date=December 27, 2021|title=CF Montréal acquires Canadian international Alistair Johnston|work=[[CF Montréal]]}}</ref> He made his debut for Montreal on February 23, as a substitute against [[Liga MX]] side [[Santos Laguna]] in the club's second leg of their [[2022 CONCACAF Champions League]] tie.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.cfmontreal.com/news/live-at-8pm-montreal-v-santos-laguna|title=CF Montréal 3-0 Santos Laguna|date=February 23, 2022|work=[[CF Montréal]]}}</ref> Johnston scored his first goal for Montreal on May 14, scoring the second goal in a 2-0 victory against [[Charlotte FC]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.cfmontreal.com/news/live-at-7pm-charlotte-fc-v-cf-montreal|title=Charlotte FC 0-2 CF Montréal|date=May 14, 2022|work=[[CF Montréal]]}}</ref>

===Celtic===
In December 2022, CF Montréal announced they had transferred Johnston to [[Scottish Premiership]] side [[Celtic F.C.|Celtic]], taking effect January 1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.cfmontreal.com/news/alistair-johnston-transferred-to-celtic-fc|title=Alistair Johnston transferred to Celtic FC|date=December 3, 2023|work=[[CF Montreal]]}}</ref> He signed a 5-year contract with the [[Glasgow]] club.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.celticfc.com/news/2022/december/03/celtic-delighted-to-sign-canadian-internationalist-alistair-johnston-on-five-year-deal/|title=Celtic delighted to sign Canadian internationalist Alistair Johnston on five-year deal|date=December 3, 2022|work=[[Celtic F.C.|Celtic]]}}</ref> Upon the announcement, Johnston stated at a press conference that former Celtic and current CF Montréal midfielder [[Victor Wanyama]] was a major influence on his decision to move to ''the Bhoys.''<ref>{{Cite web |last=mlssoccer |title=Agent Victor Wanyama: Celtic FC transfer easy sell for Alistair Johnston {{!}} MLSSoccer.com |url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/agent-victor-wanyama-celtic-fc-transfer-easy-sell-for-alistair-johnston |access-date=2023-01-08 |website=mlssoccer |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Citation |title=Alistair Johnston reveals Wanyama Celtic advice |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zc2FgIxvmp0 |language=en |access-date=2023-01-08}}</ref>

Johnston made his debut on January 2 in the [[Old Firm]], starting against [[Rangers F.C.|Rangers]] and playing the entire match in a 2–2 draw.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.celticfc.com/news/2023/january/02/kyogo-strikes-late-at-ibrox-as-honours-even-in-glasgow-derby-/|title=Kyogo strikes late at Ibrox as honours even in Glasgow derby|first=Ryan|last=Maher|date=January 2, 2022|work=[[Celtic F.C.|Celtic]]}}</ref> He then made his home debut a few days later, starting again in a 2–0 win against [[Kilmarnock F.C.|Kilmarnock]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Scots Prem (Sky Sports) |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/celtic-vs-kilmarnock/teams/465140 |access-date=2023-01-08 |website=Sky Sports |language=en}}</ref> Johnston ended his first season at [[Celtic Park]] a success, with the club securing a domestic treble, winning the [[2022–23 Scottish Premiership|Premiership]], the [[2023 Scottish League Cup final (February)|League Cup]] and the [[2023 Scottish Cup final|Scottish Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.celticfc.com/news/2023/june/03/eightsome-reel--celtic-make-history-with-another-treble-triumph/|date=June 3, 2023|title=Eightsome Reel… Celtic make history with another Treble triumph}}</ref>

==International career==
Johnston accepted an invitation to the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canadian senior national team]] camp in January 2021.<ref name=can2021>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/canada-soccer-ayo-akinola-national-team-camp-1.5853734|title=TFC's Ayo Akinola accepts invite to Canada national team camp|date=December 23, 2020|first=Neil|last=Davidson|work=[[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation|CBC]]}}</ref> He made his debut for Canada on March 25, 2021, as a 69th-minute substitute against [[Bermuda national football team|Bermuda]] in the first round of [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONCACAF)|2022 World Cup qualifying]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/canada-honduras-world-cup-qualifying-soccer-1.5964733|title=Larin, Davies team up to help Canada crush Bermuda in World Cup qualifier|accessdate=March 25, 2021}}</ref> and scored his first goal for Canada in the following match against the [[Cayman Islands national football team|Cayman Islands]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://northerntribune.ca/canada-world-cup-cayman-islands/|first=John|last=Jacques|title=Canada Coasts Past Cayman Islands In Historic Eleven-Goal Win|date=March 29, 2021}}</ref> In June 2021 Johnston was named to the 60-man preliminary squad for the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tsn.ca/canada-names-60-man-preliminary-roster-for-next-month-s-gold-cup-1.1656962|date=June 18, 2021|title=Davies, David highlight Canada's preliminary roster for Gold Cup}}</ref> and on July 1 he was named to the final squad.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.canadasoccer.com/news/canada-soccer-announces-2021-concacaf-gold-cup-roster/|title=Canada Soccer Announces 2021 Gold Cup Roster|date=July 1, 2021|work=[[Canadian Soccer Association]]}}</ref>

In November 2022, Johnston was called-up to Canada's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canada-announces-26-man-squad-for-2022-fifa-world-cup-in-qatar|date=November 13, 2022|title=Canada announces 26-man squad for 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]|first=Charlie|last=O'Connor-Clarke}}</ref> where he played in all three of Canada's matches. In June 2023 Johnston was named to Canada's 23-man squad for the [[2023 CONCACAF Nations League Finals]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Former CPLers Loturi, Zator called up by Canada for 2023 Concacaf Nations League Finals|date=June 7, 2023|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canada-canmnt-2023-concacaf-nations-league-final-roster|first=Mitchell|last=Tierney|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]}}</ref>

==Personal life==
His brother is soccer player [[Malcolm Johnston (soccer)|Malcolm Johnston]] who was selected in the first round of the [[2023 MLS SuperDraft]] by [[New York City F.C.|New York City]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://celtsarehere.com/proud-older-brother-moment-celtic-stars-brother-drafted-by-mls-club/|title= 'Proud Older Brother Moment' – Celtic Star's Brother Drafted by MLS Club|website=Celtsarehere.com|date= December 22, 2022}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url= https://www.cbc.ca/amp/1.6693147|title=Canada's Malcolm Johnston looks to follow brother Alistair ahead of MLS SuperDraft|website=cbc.ca}}</ref>
