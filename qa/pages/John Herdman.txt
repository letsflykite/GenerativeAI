'''John Herdman''' (born 19 July 1975) is an English professional [[Manager (association football)|football manager]] who is the head coach of the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada men's national team]].

==Coaching career==
=== Early career ===
Herdman began coaching football at a young age in England, while he was a student and part-time university lecturer at [[Northumbria University]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Davidson|first=Neil|title=With inspiration and motivation, John Herdman is changing the face of Canadian soccer|url=https://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/john-herdman-canada-men-soccer-1.5322990|url-status=live|publisher=CBC News}}</ref> He was a development coach for [[Sunderland A.F.C. Reserves and Academy|Sunderland's youth academy]] until 2001, when he moved to New Zealand.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Ebner |first1=Dave |title=John Herdman: The brain behind the brawn of Canada's World Cup dream |url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/sports/soccer/john-herdman-the-brain-behind-the-brawn-of-canadas-world-cup-dream/article24834240/ |access-date=5 September 2019 |publisher=The Globe & Mail}}</ref>

=== New Zealand women's ===
Herdman arrived in New Zealand and joined the national association football programme in 2003, initially acting as Coach Education Manager and later as the Director of Football Development. Herdman was head coach for the [[New Zealand women's national football team]] from 2006 to 2011. Working with the national women's teams, he led the U-20 squad to the World Championship in [[2006 FIFA U-20 Women's World Championship|2006]] and the later renamed U-20 World Cup in [[2010 FIFA U-20 Women's World Cup|2010]]. He also guided the senior squad to the [[FIFA Women's World Cup]]s in [[2007 FIFA Women's World Cup|2007]] and [[2011 FIFA Women's World Cup|2011]] as well as the [[Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Women's tournament|2008 Summer Olympics in Beijing]].<ref name="CBC Welcome">{{cite web |url=http://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/story/2011/09/01/sp-herdman.html | title= Herdman takes over Canadian women's soccer team |publisher=CBC/Radio-Canada |year=2011 |access-date=17 January 2012}}</ref><ref name="NZ Finish">{{cite web |url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/sport/football/5552775/Herdman-finishing-as-Football-Ferns-coach | title= Herdman finishing as Football Ferns coach |work=Stuff (company) |year=2011 |access-date=17 January 2012}}</ref><ref name="NZ to CA">{{cite web |url=http://www.womenssoccerunited.com/profiles/blogs/john-herdman-named-canada-s-women-s-national-team-head-coach |title=John Herdman named Canada's Women's National Team Head Coach |publisher=Women's Soccer United |year=2011 |access-date=17 January 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120703060540/http://www.womenssoccerunited.com/profiles/blogs/john-herdman-named-canada-s-women-s-national-team-head-coach |archive-date=3 July 2012 }}</ref>

During his time in New Zealand, Herdman played amateur football for [[NRFL Division 2]] club [[Hibiscus Coast AFC|Hibiscus Coast]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.friendsoffootballnz.com/2022/03/28/from-nrfl-to-world-cup-glory-the-amazing-story-of-john-herdman/|title = Former NRFL player becomes national hero as Canada qualifies for World Cup|date = 27 March 2022}}</ref>

=== Canada men's===
On 8 January 2018, Herdman was named head coach of the [[Canada men's national soccer team]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Davidson|first1=Neil|title=Decorated women's coach John Herdman takes over Canadian men's soccer program|url=http://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/john-herdman-takes-over-canadas-mens-program-1.4478560|access-date=8 January 2018|agency=[[The Canadian Press]]|website=[[CBC Sports]]|date=8 January 2018}}</ref> He "also becomes men's national director, with responsibility for all age groups from under-14s upward".<ref>{{cite news|title=John Herdman: Canada name Englishman as men's national team head coach|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/42617838|access-date=9 January 2018|website=[[BBC Sport]]|date=9 January 2018}}</ref>

In 2021, he guided the team up the [[FIFA World Rankings]] from 72nd to 40th, its highest position to date, to earn the team the honour of "Most Improved Side" of the year.<ref>{{cite news|title=Canadian men earn 'Most Improved Side' honours in end-of-year FIFA rankings|url=https://www.ctvnews.ca/sports/canadian-men-earn-most-improved-side-honours-in-end-of-year-fifa-rankings-1.5718020|first=Neil|last=Davidson|agency=[[The Canadian Press]]|website=[[CTV News]]|access-date=1 February 2022|date=23 December 2021}}</ref> On 10 February 2022, he improved Canada to 33rd in the FIFA World Rankings.<ref>{{cite news|title=Canadian men reach all-time high at No. 33 in FIFA rankings|url=https://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/canada-soccer-men-fifa-ranking-33-1.6346344|last=Davidson|first=Neil|agency=[[The Canadian Press]]|website=[[CBC Sports]]|access-date=8 April 2022|date=10 February 2022}}</ref>

On 27 March 2022, he led the team to qualify for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], its first World Cup in 36 years,<ref>{{cite news|title=Canadian men qualify for 1st World Cup since 1986 with shutout victory on home soil|url=https://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/soccer-world-cup-qualifying-canada-jamaica-march-26-1.6399334|first=Neil|last=Davidson|agency=[[The Canadian Press]]|website=[[CBC Cup.<ref>{{cite news|title=How Māori wisdom helped football coach John Herdman on the road to World Cup == Personal life ==
Herdman is originally from [[Consett]], [[County Durham]], England.<ref name=sweet/> Herdman is married to his childhood sweetheart, Claire, and has two children, Lilly and [[Jay Herdman|Jay]] who plays in the [[Vancouver Whitecaps]] development system, as well as the [[New Zealand national under-20 football team|New Zealand U20]] team.<ref name=sweet>{{cite web|last=Johnson|first=George|title=Sweet homecoming for women's soccer coach John Herdman|url=https://vancouversun.com/Sweet+homecoming+women+soccer+coach+John+Herdman/7008247/story.html|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180714051002/http://www.vancouversun.com/Sweet+homecoming+women+soccer+coach+John+Herdman/7008247/story.html|archive-date=14 July 2018|access-date=13 July 2018|date=29 July 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Taylor|first=Louise|date=17 June 2015|title=John Herdman tries to condition Canada into accepting women's football|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2015/jun/17/john-herdman-canada-womens-world-cup|access-date=13 July 2018|website=The Guardian}}</ref>
