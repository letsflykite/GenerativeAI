'''Derek Austin Cornelius''' (born 25 November 1997) is a Canadian professional [[Association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[Centre Back|centre-back]] for [[Allsvenskan]] club [[Malmö FF]] and the [[Canadian men's national soccer team|Canada national team]].

==Early life==
Cornelius was born in [[Ajax, Ontario]] to a Barbadian father and a Jamaican mother.<ref>{{Canada Soccer player|id=12673}}</ref> Cornelius started playing when he was selected as a younger player for the 1996 House League All-Star team.<ref name="experience">[http://www.derek-cornelius.com/experience-and-accomplishments Experience And Accomplishments] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160731232945/http://www.derek-cornelius.com/experience-and-accomplishments |date=31 July 2016 }} at derek-cornelius.com, Retrieved 5 November 2017</ref> He was then selected by the Ajax Thunder under-8 team at six years old.<ref name="experience"/> In the season after that, he was the Unionville Milliken Challenge Cup Champions Most Valuable Player.<ref name="experience"/> At the U11 level, he won the C.O.V.I. Championship and was proclaimed the Most Valuable Player.<ref name="experience"/>

In 2012, he played in the Oviedo Cup with [[PFC CSKA Moscow]] in Spain, then he played in the Milk Cup by CSKA Moscow in Northern Ireland where he led the team scoring four goals in the tournament. He ended that year by being invited to the CSKA Moscow Reserve Camp and by playing in the U21 Provincial and Men's Regional leagues, scoring 22 goals in 30 games.<ref name="experience"/>

In 2013, he was selected for the [[Canada men's national under-17 soccer team|Canada national under-17 team]] for camp in Florida and Costa Rica, and was later selected for the CONCACAF World Cup U17 Qualifier pre-camp in Florida. He was also selected for the Canada national under-16 Team for the Torneo delle Nazioni tournament in Italy. That same year he was invited to Hungary for trials with [[Nyíregyháza Spartacus FC]] and [[Győri ETO FC]] in March.<ref name="experience"/>

==Club career==
===Early career===
In January 2014, he moved to Germany. He began training with [[VfB Lübeck]] U19 team.<ref>[http://www.derek-cornelius.com/ Profile] at derek-cornelius.com, Retrieved 5 November 2017</ref> He spent two seasons with the main team,<ref name="zero">[http://www.thefinalball.com/player.php?id=472303 Derek Cornelius] at thefinalball.com</ref> but was unable to play due to [[FIFA]] rules for underaged players. He became eligible to play effective on 1 January 2016.<ref>[https://www.sportbuzzer.de/artikel/das-lange-tauziehen-um-derek-cornelius-er-will-doch-nur-spielen/ Das lange Tauziehen um Derek Cornelius: Er will doch nur spielen] at sportbuzzer.de</ref><ref>[https://www.dfb.de/datencenter/personen/derek-cornelius/spieler Derek Cornelius] at dfb.de</ref> With VfB Lübeck he won the [[Schleswig-Holstein Cup]] two years in a row and made an appearance in the [[2015–16 Regionalliga]] Nord.<ref name="soccerway">[https://int.soccerway.com/players/derek-cornelius/289024/ Derek Cornelius] at Soccerway</ref>

In the summer of 2016, he moved to [[VfR Neumünster]] playing with them the first half of the season.<ref name="zero"/>

===Javor Ivanjica===
During the winter break of the [[2016–17 Serbian SuperLiga|2016–17 season]], Cornelius was signed by [[Serbian SuperLiga]] side [[FK Javor Ivanjica]] after successful trials. He made his league debut on 29 April 2017, in an away game against [[FK Radnički Niš]].<ref name="soccerway"/> Javor head coach [[Srđan Vasiljević]] converted Cornelius from a [[forward (association football)|forward]] into a [[Defender (association football)#Centre-back|centre-back]].<ref name="Djuradj Vujcic interviews Derek Cornelius">{{cite news|last=Vujcic|first=Djuradj|authorlink=|url=http://rednationonline.ca/Articles2016/CanadianDerekCorneliusonprofessionaldebutin.aspx |title=Canadian Derek Cornelius on professional debut in Serbia |agency=[[RedNation Online]] |date=4 August 2017 |access-date=12 February 2019 |language=en}}</ref>

===Vancouver Whitecaps FC===
On 18 January 2019, Cornelius returned to his native Canada and signed with [[MLS]] side [[Vancouver Whitecaps FC]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.whitecapsfc.com/post/2019/01/18/whitecaps-fc-acquire-derek-cornelius|title=Whitecaps FC acquire Canadian international centre back Derek Cornelius from Serbian club FK Javor Ivanjica &#124; Vancouver Whitecaps FC|accessdate=10 March 2021}}</ref> He made his debut for the Whitecaps on 2 March in their [[2019 MLS season]] opener against [[Minnesota United]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://dailyhive.com/vancouver/whitecaps-fc-mls-season-opener-2019|date=2 March 2019|title=9 players make Whitecaps debut in MLS season opener|first=Mark|last=Dailey}}</ref> Cornelius scored his first goal for the club on 18 May against [[Sporting Kansas City]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.whitecapsfc.com/post/2019/05/18/goal-derek-cornelius-volleys-home-equalizer|title=GOAL: Derek Cornelius volleys home the equalizer|date=18 May 2019}}</ref>

In July 2021 the Whitecaps announced Cornelius had been loaned to [[Panetolikos F.C.|Panetolikos]] of the [[Super League Greece]] until December 2022.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.whitecapsfc.com/news/derek-cornelius-contract-extension-loan|date=12 July 2021|title=Whitecaps FC sign centre back Derek Cornelius to contract extension, loan him to Greek Super League side Panetolikos through 2022}}</ref>

===Malmö FF===
In December 2022 Vancouver announced Cornelius had been transferred to [[Allsvenskan]] side [[Malmö FF]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.whitecapsfc.com/news/whitecaps-fc-transfer-defender-derek-cornelius-to-swedish-side-malmo-ff|title=Whitecaps FC transfer defender Derek Cornelius to Swedish side Malmö FF|date=December 15, 2022}}</ref> He signed a contract through 2026 with the Scandinavian club.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mff.se/derek-cornelius-klar-for-malmo-ff/|title=DEREK CORNELIUS KLAR FÖR MALMÖ FF|date=December 15, 2022}}</ref> Cornelius made his competitive debut for his new club against [[Skövde AIK]] in the [[2022–23 Svenska Cupen|Svenska Cupen]] on February 19, 2023.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mff.se/intensiv-inledning-gav-premiarseger/|date=February 19, 2023|title=INTENSIV INLEDNING GAV PREMIÄRSEGER|work=[[Malmö FF]]}}</ref> He scored his first goal for Malmö on March 5 against [[Degerfors IF]], winning the match.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canadiansoccerdaily.com/2023/03/06/galindo-canadians-abroad-roundup-jonathan-david-putting-premier-league-teams-on-high-alert/|first=Peter|last=Galindo|title=Galindo: Canadians Abroad Roundup – Jonathan David putting Premier League teams on high alert|work=Canadian Soccer Daily|date=March 6, 2023}}</ref>

===Senior===
In March 2018, Cornelius received his first call up to the [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canadian senior side]] for a friendly against [[New Zealand national soccer team|New Zealand]] to be played on March 24.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2018/03/12/canada-call-10-mlsers-herdmans-first-camp-new-zealand-friendly|first=Charles|last=Boehm|date=12 March 2018|title=Canada call up 10 MLSers for Herdman's first camp, New Zealand friendly}}</ref> He would make his debut for Canada on  September 9, 2018, starting and playing the entire match in an 8–0 victory over the [[U.S. Virgin Islands national football team|U.S. Virgin Islands]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sportsnet.ca/soccer/canada-cruises-record-victory-u-s-virgin-islands/|date=9 September 2018|title=Canada cruises to record win in Concacaf Nations League opener}}</ref><ref>[https://www.national-football-teams.com/player/71878/Derek_Cornelius.html Derek Cornelius] at National-Football-Teams.com</ref> Cornelius was named to the 23-man squad for the [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup]] on May 30, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.canadasoccer.com/together-we-rise-canada-soccer-announces-squad-for-the-2019-concacaf-gold-cup-p162198|date=30 May 2019|title=Together We Rise: Canada Soccer announces squad for the 2019 Concacaf Gold Cup}}</ref>

In November 2022, Cornelius was named to Canada's squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canada-announces-26-man-squad-for-2022-fifa-world-cup-in-qatar|date=November 13, 2022|title=Canada announces 26-man squad for 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]|first=Charlie|last=O'Connor-Clarke}}</ref> In June 2023 Cornelius was named to the final 23-man squad for the [[2023 CONCACAF Nations League Finals]], but after suffering an injury with his club side [[Malmö FF]], he was withdrawn from the squad and replaced by [[Moïse Bombito]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Former CPLers Loturi, Zator called up by Canada for 2023 Concacaf Nations League Finals|date=June 7, 2023|url=https://canpl.ca/article/canada-canmnt-2023-concacaf-nations-league-final-roster|first=Mitchell|last=Tierney|work=[[Canadian Premier League]]}}</ref>
