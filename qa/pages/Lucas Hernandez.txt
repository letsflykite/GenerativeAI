the official Bundesliga website |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Sharma |first=Rik |title=Scouting Atletico Madrid's Rising Star Lucas Hernandez |url=https://bleacherreport.com/articles/2617928-scouting-atletico-madrids-rising-star-lucas-hernandez |access-date=2023-02-19 |website=Bleacher Report |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Teams |first=R. London |date=2022-11-19 |title=Bayern Munich defender Lucas Hernandez talks about being at the World Cup, recovering from his injury, and playing with his brother |url=https://www.bavarianfootballworks.com/2022/11/19/23467302/bayern-munich-lucas-hernandez-fifa-world-cup-theo-hernandez-ac-milan-didier-deschamps-barcelona |access-date=2023-02-19 |website=Bavarian Football Works |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-04-05 |title=SportMob – Top Facts About Lucas Hernandez, Europe’s Best Young Defender |url=https://sportmob.com/en/article/955456-top-facts-about-lucas-hernandez-europe-s-best-young-defender |access-date=2023-02-19 |website=SportMob |language=en}}</ref> He is the older brother of fellow professional footballer [[Théo Hernandez]], and their father [[Jean-François Hernandez]] was also a footballer. 

Hernandez started his career with [[Atlético Madrid]] where he made more than 100 competitive appearances, reaching the [[2016 UEFA Champions League Final]]. He also won the [[2018 UEFA Europa League Final|2018 Europa League]] and the [[2018 UEFA Super Cup|Super Cup]] in the same year. He signed for Bayern in 2019, winning the [[2019–20 Bundesliga|Bundesliga]], [[2019–20 DFB-Pokal|DFB-Pokal]], and [[2020 UEFA Champions League Final|Champions League]] in his first season as part of a [[Treble (association football)|treble]]. Hernandez joined Paris Saint-Germain in 2023.

A [[France national football team|French international]] since 2018, Hernandez was part of the winning squad at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]], and also participated at [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Club career==
===Atlético Madrid===
[[File:Loko-AM2018 tren (4).jpg|thumb|upright=0.8|Hernandez with Atlético Madrid]]

Born in [[Marseille]] while his father [[Jean-François Hernandez]] was playing for [[Olympique de Marseille]], Hernandez moved to Spain at age four (as a result, he would later speak French with a slight [[Spanish language|Spanish]] accent),<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/lucas-hernandez-france-spain-world-cup/1h2wrex3jxj4714gght6k39e2b |title=My home country is France – Lucas defends Spain snub |publisher=[[Goal (website)|Goal]] |last=Benson |first=Ryan |date=25 March 2018 |access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref> and joined [[Atlético Madrid]]'s youth setup in 2007 at age 11 from [[CF Rayo Majadahonda]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.mundodeportivo.com/20130614/atletico-madrid/lucas-hernandez-atletico-futbol-base_54375630708.html |title=Lucas Hernández: el 'jefazo' de la cantera|trans-title=Lucas Hernández: the 'big boss' of the defence |newspaper=[[Mundo Deportivo]] |language=es |date=14 June 2013 |access-date=6 December 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://majadahondamagazin.es/futbol-nuevo-fichaje-rodri-at-madrid-lucas-jugaron-juntos-majadahonda-94152 |title=Fútbol: el nuevo fichaje "Rodri" (At. Madrid) y Lucas jugaron juntos en Majadahonda|trans-title=Football: the new signing "Rodri" (At. Madrid) and Lucas played together at Majadahonda |publisher=Majadahonda Magazin |language=es |date=22 December 2017 |access-date=31 July 2018}}</ref> On 9 November 2013, while still a junior, he was called up by manager [[Diego Simeone]] for a [[La Liga]] match against [[Villarreal CF]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.vavel.com/es/futbol/atletico-de-madrid/301850-lucas-hernandez-es-un-sueno-poder-estar-en-la-convocatoria-del-primer-equipo.html |title=Lucas Hernández: "Es un sueño poder estar en la convocatoria del primer equipo"|trans-title=Lucas Hernández: "It is a dream to be in the first team's list |publisher=[[Vavel]] |last=Lechuga |first=Juan Ignacio |language=es |date=9 November 2013 |access-date=6 December 2014}}</ref> but remained unused in the 1–1 away draw on the following day.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.marca.com/eventos/marcador/futbol/2013_14/primera/jornada_13/vil_atm/ |title=El Atlético saca provecho de su manual de supervivencia|trans-title=Atlético take advantage of their survival guide |newspaper=Marca |last=Liceras |first=Ángel |language=es |date=10 November 2013 |access-date=6 December 2014}}</ref>

Hernandez made his senior debut on 26 April 2014, starting for [[Atlético Madrid B|the reserves]] in a 0–1 away loss against [[Real Sociedad B]] in the [[Segunda División B]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://clubatleticodemadrid.com/noticias/el-atletico-b-cayo-de-penalti-en-zubieta |title=El Atlético B cayó de penalti en Zubieta |trans-title=Atlético B fell from the penalty spot at Zubieta |publisher=Atlético Madrid |language=es |date=26 April 2014 |access-date=6 December 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141211075242/http://clubatleticodemadrid.com/noticias/el-atletico-b-cayo-de-penalti-en-zubieta |archive-date=11 December 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He appeared in three further matches during [[2013–14 Segunda División B|the season]], as they narrowly avoided relegation; he also signed a new deal with the club in June, running until 2018.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.marca.com/2014/10/11/futbol/equipos/atletico/1413024887.html |title=Lucas, primer 'fichaje'|trans-title=Lucas, first 'signing' |newspaper=Marca |last=Aznar |first=Luis |language=es |date=11 October 2014 |access-date=6 December 2014}}</ref>

Hernandez made his first-team debut on 3 December 2014, playing the full 90 minutes in a 3–0 away success over [[CE L'Hospitalet]] in [[2014–15 Copa del Rey|the campaign]]'s [[Copa del Rey]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.goal.com/en/match/lhospitalet-vs-atl%C3%A9tico-madrid/1958055/report |title=L'Hospitalet 0–3 Atletico Madrid: Griezmann and Gabi grind out the win |publisher=Goal |date=3 December 2014 |access-date=6 December 2014}}</ref> His maiden appearance in the [[Spanish football league system|Spanish top level]] took place on the 21st, when he came on as a late [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] for [[Guilherme Siqueira]] in a 4–1 away win against [[Athletic Bilbao]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.marca.com/eventos/marcador/futbol/2014_15/primera/jornada_16/ath_atm/ |title=Griezmann fue Diego Costa|trans-title=Griezmann was Diego Costa |newspaper=Marca |last=Malagón |first=Manuel |language=es |date=21 December 2014 |access-date=21 December 2014}}</ref>

====2015–16 season====
On 10 August 2015, Hernandez renewed his contract until 2019 and was definitively promoted to the main squad.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.clubatleticodemadrid.com/noticias/lucas-hernandez-renueva-hasta-el-2019 |title=Lucas Hernández renueva hasta el 2019 |trans-title=Lucas Hernández renews until 2019 |publisher=Atlético Madrid |language=es |date=10 August 2015 |access-date=10 August 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160305140145/http://www.clubatleticodemadrid.com/noticias/lucas-hernandez-renueva-hasta-el-2019 |archive-date=5 March 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref> His maiden appearance in the [[UEFA Champions League]] took place on 15 March 2016, as he replaced injured [[Diego Godín]] for [[Overtime (sports)|extra time]] of the [[2015–16 UEFA Champions League|round-of-16 tie]] against [[PSV Eindhoven]] (0–0 after 120 minutes, 8–7 [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shootout]] win);<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2016/matches/round=2000635/match=2015774/postmatch/report/index.html#atletico+madrid+overcome+psv+lengthy+shoot |title=Atlético Madrid overcome PSV in lengthy shoot-out |publisher=UEFA |date=15 March 2016 |access-date=15 March 2016}}</ref> he entered at the same point in [[2016 UEFA Champions League Final|the final]], a shootout loss to neighbours [[Real Madrid C.F.|Real Madrid]] in the [[San Siro]] on 28 May.<ref name=Final>{{cite web |url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2016/matches/round=2000638/match=2015789/postmatch/report/index.html#madrid+crowned+11th+time |title=Spot-on Real Madrid defeat Atlético in final again |publisher=UEFA |date=28 May 2016 |access-date=30 May 2016}}</ref>

====2016–17 season====
On 6 August 2016, Hernandez signed a contract extension with Atlético until 30 June 2020.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://en.atleticodemadrid.com/noticias/lucas-renews-until-2020 | title = Lucas Hernández renews until 2020 | publisher=Atlético Madrid | access-date = 5 April 2019}}</ref> He played a more important role for Atlético during the [[2016–17 Atlético Madrid season|2016-17 season]]. Hernandez played in 22 matches in all competitions during the season. He was in the starting lineup in 20 of these matches.

====2017–18 season====
During the [[2017–18 Atlético Madrid season|2017–18 season]], Hernandez was a key member of the team as he played in 44 matches in all competitions. Atlético reached the [[2018 UEFA Europa League Final]]. He started and played the whole match against French club [[Olympique de Marseille|Marseille]]. Atlético won the match 3-0 and Hernandez won his first international trophy.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2018/matches/round=2000895/match=2022218/index.html| title = Atlético win the 2018 Europa League title | publisher = UEFA | access-date = 5 April 2019}}</ref>

====2018–19 season====
Hernandez scored his first goal as a professional on 19 January 2019, in a 3–0 away defeat of [[SD Huesca]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.europapress.es/deportes/futbol-00162/noticia-atleti-repone-huesca-20190119203455.html |title=El Atleti se repone en Huesca|trans-title=Atleti pull it together in Huesca |publisher=[[Europa Press (news agency)|Europa Press]] |language=es |date=19 January 2019 |access-date=19 January 2019}}</ref>

On 27 March 2019, Atlético confirmed that Hernandez would leave the club in the summer of 2019. Atlético confirmed they offered Hernandez a contract extension, but he decided to sign for [[Bundesliga]] club [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]]. Atlético said that the German club activated Hernandez's release clause of €80 million.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://en.atleticodemadrid.com/noticias/agreement-with-bayern-munich-over-the-transfer-of-lucas-hernandez-for-the-amount-of-his-buyout-clause | title = Agreement with Bayern Munich over the transfer of Lucas Hernandez for the amount of his buyout clause | publisher=Atlético Madrid | access-date = 5 April 2019}}</ref> During the medical examination by Bayern Munich medical staff, a damage to the medial collateral ligament in Hernandez's right knee was found. The injury required a surgical repair. On 28 March 2019, Hernandez had a successful operation on his right knee. The operation ended Hernandez's season with Atlético.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2019/03/lucas-hernandez-erfolgreich-operiert | title = Lucas Hernández operation successful | publisher = FC Bayern Munich | access-date = 5 April 2019}}</ref>

Hernandez played in 14 La Liga matches and scored one goal. In all competitions, he played in 22 matches and scored one goal.

On 12 October 2019, Hernandez's former coach at Atlético, [[Diego Simeone]], said in an interview with ''[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]'' that the left back's departure to Bayern had hurt Atlético a great deal: "The exit that hurt us the most was Lucas, who was a boy from the academy ... [It hurt] More than [Antoine] [[Antoine Griezmann|Griezmann]]," who left Atlético at the same time for [[Barcelona FC|Barcelona]].<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.marca.com/en/football/spanish-football/2019/10/11/5d9fb602ca47419e528b45b4.html | title = Simeone: Lucas' departure was more painful than Griezmann's | date = 11 October 2019 | access-date = 13 October 2019 }}</ref>

===Bayern Munich===
On 27 March 2019, Bayern Munich announced that Hernandez would join the club on 1 July 2019 for a club record (and Bundesliga record) fee of €80&nbsp;million, signing a five-year contract lasting until 2024.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2019/03/fc-bayern-sign-lucas-hernandez-from-atletico-madrid |title=FC Bayern sign Lucas Hernandez |publisher=[[FC Bayern Munich]] |date=27 March 2019 |access-date=27 March 2019}}</ref><ref name="bayern">{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/47725208 |title=Lucas Hernandez: Bayern Munich sign Atletico Madrid's World Cup-winning defender |website=BBC Sport |date=27 March 2019 |access-date=27 March 2019}}</ref>

====2019–20 season====
He made his debut for Bayern on 10 August in a first round match against [[FC Energie Cottbus|Energie Cottbus]] in the [[DFB-Pokal]] as an 89th-minute substitute.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://fcbayern.com/en/matches/profis/dfb-pokal/2019-2020/fc-energie-cottbus-fc-bayern-muenchen-10-08-2019-matchreport | title = Energie Cottbus vs FC Bayern match report | publisher = FC Bayern Munich | access-date = 19 August 2019}}</ref> On 23 August 2020, along with his Bayern Munich teammates, Hernandez became a [[2019–20 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] winner after a 1–0 victory over [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] (PSG) in the final.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/53867676 | title = Paris St-Germain 0-1 Bayern Munich | website = BBC Sport | date = 23 August 2020 | access-date = 23 August 2020 | first = Phil | last = McNulty}}</ref>

====2020–21 season====
On 3 November 2020, Hernandez scored his first goal for Bayern Munich in a 6–2 away win over [[FC Red Bull Salzburg|Red Bull Salzburg]] in the [[2020–21 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/match/2029415--salzburg-vs-bayern/ |title=Salzburg 2–6 Bayern |website=UEFA |date=3 November 2020 }}</ref> In April 2021, Bayern were eliminated from the European competition in the quarter-finals by Paris Saint-Germain on away goal rule after a 3–3 draw on aggregate.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2021/apr/13/psg-v-bayern-munich-champions-league-quarter-final-second-leg-live |title=PSG 0-1 Bayern Munich (agg: 3-3): Champions League quarter-final, second leg – as it happened |website=The Guardian |date=13 April 2021 }}</ref>

====2021–22 season====
Hernandez missed three league matches at the start of the season due to a knee injury sustained during the postponed [[UEFA Euro 2020|Euro 2020]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/bayern-munich-s-lucas-hernandez-to-miss-start-of-2021-22-season-16520 |title=Bayern Munich's Lucas Hernandez to miss start of 2021/22 season |website=Bundesliga |date=4 July 2022 }}</ref> However, he was part of the team which reached the quarter-finals of the [[2021–22 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] before losing to Villarreal 2–1 on aggregate.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/61086842 |title=Bayern Munich 1–1 Villarreal |website=BBC Sport |date=12 April 2022 }}</ref>

====2022–23 season====
On 13 September 2022, he scored his first goal of the season from a header in a 2–0 win over Barcelona in the [[2022–23 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.eurosport.com/football/champions-league/2022-2023/bayern-munich-v-barcelona-follow-live_sto9142270/story.shtml |title=Bayern Munich 2-0 Barcelona: Lucas Hernandez and Leroy Sane goals enough to see off Barca in Champions League |website=Eurosport |date=14 September 2022 }}</ref> A month later, he sustained a cruciate ligament rupture in his right knee during the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|World Cup]], which sidelined him for the rest of the season.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2022/11/lucas-hernandez-successfully-operated-on |title=Lucas Hernández successfully operated on |website=Bayern Munich |date=24 November 2022 }}</ref>

=== Paris Saint-Germain ===
On 9 July 2023, Hernandez signed for [[Ligue 1]] club [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] on a five-year contract until 30 June 2028,<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-07-09 |title=Lucas Hernández signs for Paris Saint-Germain until 2028 |url=https://en.psg.fr/teams/first-team/content/lucas-hernandez-signs-for-paris-saint-germain-until-2028-2023-2024 |access-date=2023-07-09 |website=Paris Saint-Germain F.C. |language=en}}</ref> for a transfer fee reported to be in the region of €45 million.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Lucas Hernandez est un joueur du PSG (officiel) |url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Lucas-hernandez-est-un-joueur-du-psg-officiel/1407443 |access-date=2023-07-10 |website=L'Équipe |language=fr}}</ref>

==International career==
[[File:FRA-ARG (8).jpg|thumb|Hernandez playing for [[France national football team|France]] at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]]]]

Hernandez earned his first [[Cap (sport)|cap]] for France on 6 March 2012, playing the full 90 minutes for the [[France national under-16 football team|under-16 team]] in 1–1 [[Exhibition match|friendly]] draw against [[Italy national under-16 football team|Italy]] at [[Centro Tecnico Federale di Coverciano|Coverciano]]. In 2014, he also appeared with the [[France national under-18 football team|under-18]] and [[France national under-19 football team|under-19 sides]]. The following year, he was selected for [[2015 UEFA European Under-19 Championship]] Team of the Tournament for his performances.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/under19/season=2015/technical-report/uefa-technical-team/index.html|title=Under-19 - Technical report - Team of the Tournament|publisher=UEFA |date=20 July 2015 |access-date=2 April 2019}}</ref>

In March 2018, Hernandez was called up to [[France national football team|the senior team]] by manager [[Didier Deschamps]] for [[Exhibition game|friendlies]] with [[Colombia national football team|Colombia]] and [[Russia national football team|Russia]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://kwese.espn.com/football/france/story/3420956/wissam-ben-yedder-and-lucas-hernandez-handed-france-call |title=Wissam Ben Yedder and Lucas Hernandez handed France call-ups |publisher=[[Kwesé Sports]] |date=15 March 2018 |access-date=16 March 2018}}</ref> He made his debut against the former, replacing [[Lucas Digne]] for the last 14 minutes of the 2–3 loss in [[Paris]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.eleconomista.es/noticias-amp/9028415/Lucas-Hernandez-hizo-su-debut-con-Francia |title=Lucas Hernández hizo su debut con Francia|trans-title=Lucas Hernández made his debut with France |newspaper=[[El Economista (Spain)|El Economista]] |language=es |date=23 March 2018 |access-date=24 March 2018}}</ref>

Hernandez was selected for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.skysports.com/amp/football/news/12025/11376691/alexandre-lacazette-and-anthony-martial-on-standby-for-france-world-cup-squad-and-dimitri-payet-out |title=Alexandre Lacazette and Anthony Martial on standby for France World Cup squad and Dimitri Payet out |publisher=[[Sky Sports]] |last=Charles |first=Andy |date=17 May 2018 |access-date=17 May 2018}}</ref> He made his debut in [[FIFA World Cup|the competition]] on 16 June, playing the entire 2–1 group stage win against [[Australia national soccer team|Australia]];<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44422186 |title=France 2–1 Australia |website=[[BBC Sport]] |last=Sutcliffe |first=Steve |date=16 June 2018 |access-date=16 June 2018}}</ref> he featured in all of the matches for the eventual champions, playing for the full 90 minutes in six of France's seven matches on their way to winning the tournament.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.lepoint.fr/coupe-du-monde/coupe-du-monde-2018-lucas-hernandez-le-combattant-15-07-2018-2236291_2167.php |title=Coupe du monde 2018: Lucas Hernandez, le combattant|trans-title=2018 World Cup: Lucas Hernandez, the fighter |publisher=[[Le Point]] |last=Nabet |first=Sacha |language=fr |date=15 July 2018|access-date=15 July 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44754965 |title=France 4–2 Croatia |website=BBC Sport |last=McNulty |first=Phil |date=15 July 2018|access-date=15 July 2018}}</ref>

In October 2021, ahead of the [[2021 UEFA Nations League Finals|UEFA Nations League]] semi-finals against [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]], Lucas for the first time received a call-up to the national team from [[Didier Deschamps]] together with his brother [[Theo Hernandez|Theo]]; the two were later fielded in the 3–4–1–2 formation as a left center back and a left wingback, respectively, making it the first time they played together in a senior competitive game, which turned out to be a 3–2 comeback victory for France, with Theo scoring the winning goal in the 90th minute.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://as.com/futbol/2021/10/05/internacional/1633439250_629806.html | title = El sueño de los Hernández | language = es | website = as.com | date = 5 October 2021 | access-date = 23 December 2021 | first = Andrés | last = Onrubia}}</ref> Hernandez was selected for France's squad ahead of the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] but suffered a ruptured anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) 13 minutes into the first group match against Australia and was subsequently ruled out for the remainder of the tournament.<ref>{{Cite news |date=23 November 2022 |title=France's Hernandez out of World Cup with knee injury |language=en |work=Reuters |url=https://www.reuters.com/lifestyle/sports/frances-hernandez-sustains-pretty-serious-knee-injury-2022-11-22/ |access-date=23 November 2022}}</ref> His younger brother [[Theo Hernandez]] substituted into the match for him after the injury and played out the remainder of France's World Cup 2022 run in his stead.<ref>{{Cite news |last=BBC |date=23 November 2022 |title=World Cup 2022: Lucas Hernandez out of France squad |language=en |work=BBC |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63726480 |access-date=4 December 2022}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Hernandez's father, [[Jean-François Hernandez|Jean-François]], was also a footballer who played as a centre back. Of Spanish descent,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.ladepeche.fr/article/2018/03/21/2764068-lucas-hernandez-l-espagnol-a-l-accent-francais.html |title=Lucas Hernandez, l'Espagnol à l'accent français|trans-title=Lucas Hernandez, the Spaniard with the French accent |newspaper=[[La Dépêche du Midi]] |last=Barrère |first=Sébastien |language=fr |date=21 March 2018 |access-date=1 April 2018}}</ref> he also played for Atlético Madrid;<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.abc.es/atletico-madrid/noticias/20141205/abci-lucas-hernandez-atletico-201412051718.html |title=Lucas, el futuro jefe de la defensa|trans-title=Lucas, the future boss of the defence |newspaper=[[ABC (newspaper)|ABC]] |language=es |date=5 December 2014 |access-date=6 December 2014}}</ref> Hernandez's younger brother, [[Theo Hernandez|Theo]], was also raised at the club, and played in the same position.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.mundodeportivo.com/futbol/atletico-madrid/20160206/301951417690/atletico-atletico-de-madrid-atleti-lucas-hernandez-theo-hernandez.html |title=Theo y Lucas, dos hermanos del Atlético sobre el campo 60 años después|trans-title=Theo and Lucas, two brothers from Atlético on the field 60 years later |newspaper=Mundo Deportivo |last=G. Fuente |first=Chema |language=es |date=6 February 2016 |access-date=21 July 2016}}</ref> In an interview with ''Marca'' in October 2018, he stated that his family had not heard from their father for 12 or 13 years.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/spanish-football/2018/10/17/5bc70d0fca4741a6108b45e5.html |title=Lucas Hernandez speaks about his father: We've not heard from him for 12 years |newspaper=Marca |date=17 October 2018 |access-date=17 October 2018}}</ref> In 2022, French newspaper ''[[L'Équipe]]'' found that Jean-François – who went missing in 2004 – was living in Thailand, and had allegedly been legally blocked by his ex-partner from seeing their children.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/2022/11/12/636f792ce2704e9c7f8b4590.html| title=Father of Lucas and Theo Hernandez 'turns up' 18 years later: He's waiting for his children to call him |newspaper=Marca|date=12 November 2022|access-date=12 November 2022}}</ref>

On 3 February 2017, Hernandez was arrested on suspicion of assaulting his girlfriend,<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/38856326 |title=Atletico Madrid defender Lucas Hernandez arrested |website=BBC Sport |date=3 February 2017|access-date=3 February 2017}}</ref> who was also summoned to appear in court.<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.sport.es/es/noticias/laliga/asi-fue-pelea-entre-lucas-hernandez-y-su-pareja-5791140 |title=Así fue la pelea entre Lucas Hernández y su pareja|trans-title=This is how the fight between Lucas Hernández and his partner went down |newspaper=[[Sport (Spanish newspaper)|Sport]] |language=es |date=7 February 2017|access-date=9 February 2017}}</ref> Both Hernandez and his girlfriend, Amelia Llorente, were sentenced to 31 days of community service, while Hernandez was also served with a restraining order preventing him from being within 500 metres of Llorente.<ref name="ESPN">{{cite web | url = https://www.espn.co.uk/football/bayern-munich-gerbayern_munich/story/4506755/bayern-munichs-lucas-hernandez-avoids-jail-time-for-disobeying-restraining-order | title = Bayern Munich's Lucas Hernandez avoids jail time for disobeying restraining order | website = ESPN | date = 27 October 2021 | access-date = 23 December 2021 | first = Adriana | last = Garcia}}</ref><ref name = "Sky_Sports">{{cite web | url = https://www.skysports.com/amp/football/news/11890/12433428/lucas-hernandez-bayern-munich-and-france-defender-appeals-six-month-jail-sentence-for-breaking-restraining-order | title = Lucas Hernandez: Bayern Munich and France defender appeals six-month jail sentence for breaking restraining order | website = Sky Sports | date = 14 October 2021 | access-date = 23 December 2021}}</ref>

Hernandez was arrested again in June of the same year for violating the restraining order after he and Llorente landed together in Madrid, returning from their honeymoon. For this violation, on 13 October 2021, Hernandez was sentenced to six-months in prison by a Spanish court; Hernandez successfully appealed his sentence and was instead given a four year suspended sentence and ordered to pay a €96,000 fine.<ref name="ESPN" /><ref name="Sky_Sports" /><ref>{{Cite news|date = 13 October 2021 |title=Lucas Hernandez: France defender gets six-month jail term|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-58897147|access-date=  13 October 2021}}</ref>
