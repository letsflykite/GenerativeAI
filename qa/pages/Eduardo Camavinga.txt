'''Eduardo Celmi Camavinga''' (born 10 November 2002) is a professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[La Liga]] club [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]]. He can also be deployed as a [[left-back]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Cheval |first1=Bastien |title=Eduardo Camavinga on his left-back role: “It’s a different way of defending.” |url=https://onefootball.com/id/berita/eduardo-camavinga-on-his-left-back-role-its-a-different-way-of-defending-37030263 |website=OneFootball |date=23 March 2023 |access-date=5 April 2023 |archive-date=5 April 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230405195253/https://onefootball.com/id/berita/eduardo-camavinga-on-his-left-back-role-its-a-different-way-of-defending-37030263 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Didier Deschamps confirms Eduardo Camavinga as a left-back for France |website=Get French Football News |url=https://www.getfootballnewsfrance.com/2023/didier-deschamps-confirms-eduardo-camavinga-as-a-left-back-for-france/ |date=16 March 2023 |access-date=14 April 2023 |archive-date=14 April 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230414083247/https://www.getfootballnewsfrance.com/2023/didier-deschamps-confirms-eduardo-camavinga-as-a-left-back-for-france/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Born in Angola, he plays for the [[France national football team|France national team]].

==Early life==
Camavinga was born in a [[refugee camp]] in [[Cabinda (city)|Cabinda]], Angola in 2002, to  [[Democratic Republic of the Congo|Congolese]] parents.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/de/tournaments/mens/worldcup/qatar2022/news/camavinga-seiner-zeit-voraus |title=Camavinga: Seiner Zeit voraus |publisher=FIFA |language=de |date=9 September 2020 |access-date=29 November 2022 |archive-date=29 November 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221129133343/https://www.fifa.com/de/tournaments/mens/worldcup/qatar2022/news/camavinga-seiner-zeit-voraus |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://actu.fr/bretagne/fougeres_35115/football-eduardo-camavinga-pepite-fougeres_20433586.html|title=VIDÉO. Football : Eduardo Camavinga, la pépite de Fougères qui vient de signer pro au Stade Rennais|website=actu.fr|access-date=23 July 2019|archive-date=8 July 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200708060939/https://actu.fr/bretagne/fougeres_35115/football-eduardo-camavinga-pepite-fougeres_20433586.html|url-status=live}}</ref> He has five siblings. His family moved to France when he was two.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2023/feb/20/eduardo-camavinga-real-madrid-champions-league|title=Eduardo Camavinga: ‘People think Madrid are dead, but Madrid are never, never dead’|date=20 February 2023|work=Guardian|accessdate=21 February 2023|archive-date=20 February 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230220231420/https://www.theguardian.com/football/2023/feb/20/eduardo-camavinga-real-madrid-champions-league|url-status=live}}</ref>
They eventually settled in [[Fougères]], where he grew up. He practiced [[judo]] for a time before giving it up to focus solely on football. In 2013, the Camavingas' house was burnt down, destroying most of the family's property. Camavinga stated that this was a source of motivation that pushed him to pursue a career in football and help his family.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/french-ligue-1/story/3950530/eduardo-camavinga-is-only-16-but-his-form-for-rennes-has-top-european-scouts-circling|title=Eduardo Camavinga is only 16 but his form for Rennes has top European scouts circling|publisher=ESPN|date=25 September 2019|access-date=4 April 2020|archive-date=1 September 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210901101247/https://www.espn.com/soccer/french-ligue-1/story/3950530/eduardo-camavinga-is-only-16-but-his-form-for-rennes-has-top-european-scouts-circling|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 7 July 2020, he obtained the ''[[Baccalauréat]]'' ES (Economic and Social) [[Baccalauréat#Passing and honours|without mention]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Rennes : Eduardo Camavinga a eu son bac|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Rennes-eduardo-camavinga-a-eu-son-bac/1150514|access-date=29 August 2020|website=L'Équipe|language=fr|archive-date=17 November 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201117022617/https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Rennes-eduardo-camavinga-a-eu-son-bac/1150514|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Rennes : Eduardo Camavinga nouveau bachelier|url=https://sport.francetvinfo.fr/football/ligue-1/rennes-eduardo-camavinga-nouveau-bachelier|access-date=29 August 2020|website=Francetvsport|language=fr|archive-date=15 November 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201115021232/https://sport.francetvinfo.fr/football/ligue-1/rennes-eduardo-camavinga-nouveau-bachelier|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Rennes===
Camavinga joined [[Stade Rennais F.C.|Rennes]]' youth system when he was 11. He signed his first professional contract on 14 December 2018, at the age of 16 years and one month, becoming the youngest ever professional for the club at that time.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.staderennais.com/premier-contrat-professionnel-pour-eduardo-camavinga|title=Premier contract Professionnel pour Eduardo Camavinga|language=fr|date=14 December 2018|website=staderennais.com|publisher=Stade Rennais F.C.|access-date=4 April 2020|archive-date=19 December 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191219064841/https://www.staderennais.com/premier-contrat-professionnel-pour-eduardo-camavinga|url-status=live}}</ref> He made his professional debut for Rennes in a 3–3 [[Ligue 1]] tie with [[Angers SCO|Angers]] on 6 April 2019,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2019/04/06/france/ligue-1/angers-sporting-club-de-louest/stade-rennais-fc/2792517/|title=Angers SCO vs. Rennes – 6 April 2019|publisher=Soccerway|date=6 April 2019|access-date=4 April 2020|archive-date=18 November 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201118013951/https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2019/04/06/france/ligue-1/angers-sporting-club-de-louest/stade-rennais-fc/2792517/|url-status=live}}</ref> becoming the youngest player to ever play for Rennes' first team,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://bleacherreport.com/articles/2861180-rennes-sensation-eduardo-camavinga-is-a-new-kind-of-16-year-old-footballer|title=Rennes Sensation Eduardo Camavinga Is a New Kind of 16-Year-Old Footballer|website=bleacherreport.com|publisher=Bleacher Report|date=7 November 2019|access-date=4 April 2020|archive-date=22 January 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210122132046/https://bleacherreport.com/articles/2861180-rennes-sensation-eduardo-camavinga-is-a-new-kind-of-16-year-old-footballer|url-status=live}}</ref> at 16 years and six months old.

On 18 August 2019, Camavinga recorded an assist and was awarded the man of the match award in a 2–1 win against [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=19 August 2019|title=Eduardo Camavinga: Angola youngster reacts after helping Rennes stun PSG|url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/eduardo-camavinga-angola-youngster-reacts-after-helping/g7jidorlz4kk1vqi77uk9r801|access-date=18 August 2020|website=Goal (website)|archive-date=23 November 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201123225633/https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/eduardo-camavinga-angola-youngster-reacts-after-helping/g7jidorlz4kk1vqi77uk9r801|url-status=live}}</ref> Hence, he became the youngest player to make an assist in Ligue 1, aged 16 years and 281 days.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.guinnessworldrecords.com/world-records/587424-youngest-player-to-make-an-assist-in-ligue-1 |title=Youngest player to make an assist in Ligue 1 |publisher=Guinness World Records }}</ref> He then won the [[UNFP Player of the Month]] award that month, to be the youngest player to achieve this feat.<ref name="auto1">{{cite web |url=https://www.goal.com/en-kw/lists/17-fun-facts-about-eduardo-camavinga/blta4a327b53e7f543e#cs1d079b032e6ef6a3 |title=17 fun facts about Eduardo Camavinga |publisher=Goal.com |date=24 April 2023 }}</ref> He scored his first goal for Rennes in a 1–0 win against [[Olympique Lyonnais|Lyon]] on 15 December 2019, in the 89th minute of the game.<ref>{{Cite web|date=15 December 2019|title=Rennes Beat Lyon|url=https://bleacherreport.com/game/olympique-lyonnais-vs-stade-rennais-fc-2019-12-14|website=Bleacher Report|access-date=19 August 2020|archive-date=31 August 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210831031035/https://bleacherreport.com/game/olympique-lyonnais-vs-stade-rennais-fc-2019-12-14|url-status=live}}</ref>

In the [[2020–21 UEFA Champions League]], he featured in four matches for Rennes against [[FC Krasnodar|Krasnodar]], [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] and [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://theathletic.com/2531232/2021/04/25/eduardo-camavinga-youve-heard-the-name-heres-why-europes-top-clubs-want-him/ |title=Eduardo Camavinga: You've heard the name, here's why Europe's top clubs want him |website=The Athletic |date=25 April 2021 |access-date=30 August 2021 |archive-date=30 August 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210830234516/https://theathletic.com/2531232/2021/04/25/eduardo-camavinga-youve-heard-the-name-heres-why-europes-top-clubs-want-him/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

===Real Madrid===
On 31 August 2021, [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]] announced the signing of Camavinga until 30 June 2027.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Official Announcement: Camavinga {{!}} Real Madrid CF |url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2021/08/31/official-announcement-camavinga |access-date=2023-06-22 |website=Real Madrid C.F. - Web Oficial |language=en}}</ref> He made his debut in a 5–2 win over [[RC Celta de Vigo|Celta Vigo]] on 12 September 2021, in which he scored a goal shortly after coming off the bench.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-09-12 |title=Real Madrid teenage star Camavinga scores six minutes into debut {{!}} Goal.com |url=https://www.goal.com/en-in/news/real-madrid-teenage-star-camavinga-scores-six-minutes-into-debut/b7u5e7cs01551q4oj2l8bysiw |access-date=2023-06-22 |website=www.goal.com |language=}}</ref> Three days later, on 15 September 2021, he made his Champions League debut for Real Madrid, coming on as a substitute for [[Luka Modrić]] in the 80th minute and assisting [[Rodrygo]] for the winner against [[Inter Milan]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Real Madrid: Eduardo Camavinga's assist for the winner against Inter shows wisdom beyond his years |url=https://therealchamps.com/2021/09/16/real-madrid-eduardo-camavinga-assist-inter/ |access-date=16 September 2021 |website=therealchamps.com |date=16 September 2021 |publisher=therealchamps.com |archive-date=17 September 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210917180251/https://therealchamps.com/2021/09/16/real-madrid-eduardo-camavinga-assist-inter/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Camavinga made a substitute appearance in the 85th minute of the [[2022 UEFA Champions League Final]], helping Real Madrid win 1–0 over [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] to earn the [[2021–22 UEFA Champions League]] title with his team.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://uk.sports.yahoo.com/news/camavinga-labels-real-madrids-champions-144516550.html|title=Camavinga labels Real Madrid's Champions League triumph a 'dream come true'|website=uk.sports.yahoo.com|access-date=2 June 2022|archive-date=2 June 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220602180244/https://uk.sports.yahoo.com/news/camavinga-labels-real-madrids-champions-144516550.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 9 May 2023, he provided an assist to [[Vinícius Júnior]] to take the lead in a 1–1 draw against [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] in the [[2022–23 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] semi-final first leg at [[Santiago Bernabéu Stadium]], to be the youngest in the competition's semi-finals to do so, aged 20 years and 180 days.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://onefootball.com/en/news/real-madrids-eduardo-camavinga-after-1-1-draw-against-manchester-city-its-not-necessarily-a-bad-result-its-50-50-37435420 |title=Real Madrid's Eduardo Camavinga after 1-1 draw against Manchester City: “It's not necessarily a bad result… It's 50-50.” |publisher=OneFootball |date=9 May 2023 }}</ref>

==International career==
On 5 November 2019, Camavinga obtained French citizenship.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Rennes-eduardo-camavinga-a-ete-naturalise-francais/1076897|title=Rennes : Eduardo Camavinga a été naturalisé is Ecuadorian français|language=fr|website=[[L'Équipe]]|date=5 November 2019|access-date=5 November 2019|archive-date=29 October 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201029204712/https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Rennes-eduardo-camavinga-a-ete-naturalise-francais/1076897|url-status=live}}</ref> Six days later, he was selected to represent [[France national under-21 football team|France's under-21 team]] for games against Georgia and Switzerland, after [[Matteo Guendouzi]] was called up to the senior team.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Eduardo-camavinga-rennes-officiellement-convoque-en-equipe-de-france-espoirs/1079298|title=Eduardo Camavinga (Rennes) officiellement convoqué en équipe de France Espoirs|language=fr|website=[[L'Équipe]]|date=11 November 2019|access-date=11 November 2019|archive-date=16 October 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201016224146/https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Eduardo-camavinga-rennes-officiellement-convoque-en-equipe-de-france-espoirs/1079298|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 27 August 2020, Camavinga was called up to play on [[France national football team|France's senior team]] after [[Paul Pogba]] was sidelined by a positive [[Coronavirus disease 2019|COVID-19]] test. He became, in the process, the youngest player to be called up to the French senior team since [[René Gérard (footballer)|René Gérard]] in 1932, who was only 17 years, nine months and 17 days old.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.francefootball.fr/news/Eduardo-camavinga-devient-le-plus-jeune-joueur-appele-en-equipe-de-france-depuis-l-apres-guerre/1165253|title=Eduardo Camavinga devient le plus jeune joueur appelé en équipe de France depuis l'après-guerre...|language=fr|website=[[France Football]]|date=27 August 2020|access-date=28 August 2020|archive-date=20 April 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210420204817/https://www.francefootball.fr/news/Eduardo-camavinga-devient-le-plus-jeune-joueur-appele-en-equipe-de-france-depuis-l-apres-guerre/1165253|url-status=live}}</ref> On 8 September, he debuted in a 4–2 win against [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]] in the [[2020–21 UEFA Nations League A#Group 3|UEFA Nations League]], replacing [[N'Golo Kanté]] midway through the second half.<ref>{{Cite news|title=France 4–2 Croatia|language=en-GB|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54002970|access-date=8 September 2020|archive-date=9 September 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200909171609/https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54002970|url-status=live}}</ref> In doing so he became the youngest player to play for French national team since [[Maurice Gastiger]] in 1914 at 17 years, nine months, and 29 days old.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Eduardo Camavinga plus jeune international français depuis 1945|language=fr|work=[[L'Équipe]]|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Eduardo-camavinga-plus-jeune-international-francais-depuis-1945/1169600|access-date=9 September 2020|archive-date=25 October 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201025200353/https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Eduardo-camavinga-plus-jeune-international-francais-depuis-1945/1169600|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 7 October 2020, Camavinga made his first start for France in a 7–1 win against [[Ukraine national football team|Ukraine]] where he scored his first international goal, opening the scoring with an overhead kick. This made him the youngest goalscorer for France since [[Maurice Gastiger]] in 1914.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Eduardo Camavinga plus jeune buteur des Bleus depuis 1914|language=fr|work=[[L'Équipe]]|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Eduardo-camavinga-plus-jeune-buteur-des-bleus-depuis-1914/1181112|access-date=8 October 2020|archive-date=8 October 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201008054203/https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Eduardo-camavinga-plus-jeune-buteur-des-bleus-depuis-1914/1181112|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 25 June 2021, Camavinga was named in [[Sylvain Ripoll]]'s 18-man [[France national football team|France squad]] for the [[2020 Summer Olympics|Summer Olympics in 2021]].<ref>{{Cite news|date=25 June 2021|title=THE LIST FOR THE TOKYO GAMES|language=fr|work=[[French Football Federation]]|url=https://www.fff.fr/article/3089-la-liste-pour-les-jeux-de-tokyo.html|access-date=25 June 2021|archive-date=29 June 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210629181432/https://www.fff.fr/article/3089-la-liste-pour-les-jeux-de-tokyo.html|url-status=live}}</ref> However he was later removed from the squad after his club, Rennes, objected.<ref>{{cite news |last1=De Cosemo |first1=Harry |title=Tokyo 2020: Olympic men's football players to watch |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/olympics/57833180 |access-date=22 July 2021 |work=BBC Sport |date=21 July 2021 |archive-date=26 July 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210726025545/https://www.bbc.com/sport/olympics/57833180 |url-status=live }}</ref>

In November 2022, he was named in the French squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite web |last=Orsini |first=Vincent |url=https://www.fff.fr/article/8465-la-liste-des-vingt-cinq-pour-le-mondial.html |title=La liste des vingt-cinq pour le Mondial |trans-title=The list of twenty-five for the World Cup |publisher=[[French Football Federation]] |date=9 November 2022 |access-date=12 November 2022 |language=fr |archive-date=9 November 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221109210257/https://www.fff.fr/article/8465-la-liste-des-vingt-cinq-pour-le-mondial.html |url-status=live }}</ref>
