'''Keysher Fuller Spence''' (born 12 July 1994) is a Costa Rican professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (Association football)#Full-back|right-back]] for [[Liga FPD]] Club Sport [[C.S. Herediano|Herediano]] and the [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica national team]].<ref name=sw/><ref>{{NFT player|73502}}</ref>

Fuller made his debut for the senior [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica national team]] at [[Avaya Stadium]] on 2 February 2019 against the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://us.soccerway.com/matches/2019/02/02/world/friendlies/united-states-of-america/costa-rica/2937422/|title=United States vs. Costa Rica - 2 February 2019 - Soccerway|website=us.soccerway.com}}</ref>

==Club career==
From [[Limón]], Fuller was in the Deportivo Saprissa youth academy. Fuller made his debut in the Second Division reserve team in January 2012. In 2016 he went to [[C.S. Uruguay de Coronado]] and a season later to [[Municipal Grecia]] where he become one a first team regular during the 2017-18 season.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.diarioextra.com/Noticia/detalle/334436/grecia-se-carga--de-veteranos|title=Greece is loaded with veterans|website=diorioextra.com}}</ref> His form for Municipal Grecia led to [[C.S. Herediano]] acquiring his services in March 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://everardoherrera.com/index.php/futbol-costa-rica/76-futbol-nacional/39838-herediano-ataca-el-mercado-y-ficha-a-keysher-fuller-de-grecia|title=Herediano attacks the market and signs Keysher Fuller from Greece|website=Evaradoherrera.com}}</ref> In his first season with Herediano he won the [[CONCACAF League]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nacion.com/puro-deporte/futbol-nacional/herediano-sufre-en-honduras-pero-es-campeon-de/N5WHU25BFJG2PLZ32EJNOGKF34/story/|title=Herediano suffers in Honduras, but is the Concacaf League champion|website=nacion.com}}</ref>

==International career==
Born in Costa Rica, Fuller is of Jamaican descent.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rsqNSuRu2rQ|title=How many players of Jamaican Heritage will be at the World Cup?|via=www.youtube.com}}</ref> He made his senior debut for [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica]] in a friendly against the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]] on February 2, 2019. The next month on March 27 he scored his first international goal in a 1-0 win over [[Jamaica national football team|Jamaica]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://everardoherrera.com/index.php/futbol-costa-rica-seleccion-nacional/67-seleccion/51845-una-costa-rica-sin-mucha-mejoria-vence-1-0-a-jamaica|title= Una Costa Rica sin mucha mejoría vence 1-0 a Jamaica|website=Everadoherrera.com}}</ref> Fuller also represented Costa Rica at the [[2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://soccer.nbcsports.com/2021/07/18/2021-gold-cup-squads-for-every-team/|date=July 17, 2021|title=2021 Gold Cup squads for every team|first=Andy|last=Edwards}}</ref>

In November 2022, Fuller was named to the Costa Rican squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/fifaplus/en/articles/costa-rica-squad-list-fifa-world-cup-qatar-2022|title=Suarez names Costa Rica squad for Qatar 2022}}</ref> In Costa Rica's second group stage match on November 27, he scored the only goal of the game in a 1-0 victory over [[Japan national football team|Japan]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63692163|title=World Cup 2022: Japan 0-1 Costa Rica - Keysher Fuller earns shock win|work=BBC Sport }}</ref>
