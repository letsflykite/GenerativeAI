'''Jeremie Agyekum Frimpong''' (born 10 December 2000) is a Dutch professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|right-back]] for [[Bundesliga]] club [[Bayer 04 Leverkusen|Bayer Leverkusen]] and the [[Netherlands national under-21 football team|Netherlands U21 national team]].

EDS and Academy|Manchester City]] at the age of nine<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/sport/football/transfer-news/man-city-transfers-celtic-frimpong-16857176|title=Man City insert transfer clauses into Celtic deal for young defender Jeremie Frimpong|first=Simon|last=Bajkowski|date=3 September 2019|access-date=26 September 2019|publisher=Manchester Evening News}}</ref> and progressed through the club's youth ranks, with regular appearances in [[Premier League 2]] and the [[UEFA Youth League]].<ref>{{UEFA player|250113276}}</ref>

=== Bayer Leverkusen ===

==International career==
Born in the Netherlands, Frimpong is of Ghanaian descent.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ghanasoccernet.com/ghana-set-to-miss-out-on-whiz-kid-jeremie-frimpong-after-netherlands-call-up|title=Ghana set to miss out on whiz-kid Jeremie Frimpong after Netherlands call-up|date=13 November 2019|website=GhanaSoccernet}}</ref><ref name="scotsman">{{cite news |last=Halliday |first=Stephen |title=Jeremie Frimpong aiming to follow in Liverpool star Virgil van Dijk's footsteps at Celtic and in Netherlands national team |url=https://www.scotsman.com/sport/football/scottish-premiership/latest-premiership-news/jeremie-frimpong-aiming-follow-liverpool-star-virgil-van-dijks-footsteps-celtic-and-netherlands-national-team-1404486 |access-date=15 December 2020 |work=The Scotsman |date=23 October 2019}}</ref> His family moved to England when he was seven years old. As such, he is eligible to play for the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]], [[Ghana national football team|Ghana]] and [[England national football team|England]].<ref name="scotsman" />

Frimpong has represented the Netherlands at [[Netherlands national under-19 football team|under-19 youth international level]], and made his international debut against [[Armenia national under-19 football team|Armenia U19]] in November 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.onsoranje.nl/teams/185189/speler/25677|title=Jeremy Frimpong|access-date=26 September 2019|publisher=OnsOranje}}</ref> In November 2019, he made his under-20 debut.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.onsoranje.nl/teams/206598/speler/25677|title=Jeremy Frimpong|access-date=23 November 2019|publisher=OnsOranje}}</ref>

In November 2022, Frimpong was included in the final selection of the Netherlands National Football Team for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite tweet|number=1583413124987658240|user=OnsOranje|title=ğŸ¦ ğğ«ğ¨ğ¯ğ¢ğ¬ğ¢ğ¨ğ§ğšğ¥ ğ’ğªğ®ğšğ: This is the longlist of 39 players where our final World Cup 2022 squad will be selected from! ğŸ“‹ |date=21 October 2022}}</ref> Even though, according to an article on the [[Nederlandse Omroep Stichting|NOS]] website, he does not speak a lot of Dutch, although he understands match talks and football language.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nos.nl/artikel/2451924-dag-van-de-wk-selectie-hardste-klappen-bij-oranje-vallen-voorin|title=Dag van de WK-selectie: hardste klappen bij Oranje vallen voorin|date=11 November 2022}}</ref> He did not appear in the tournament as the Netherlands were knocked out by [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]] in the quarter-finals.
