'''Nicolas Julio Nkoulou Ndoubena''' (born 27 March 1990), known as '''Nicolas Nkoulou''', is a Cameroonian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]] for [[Super League Greece]] club [[Aris Thessaloniki F.C.|Aris]].

Nkoulou began his career in the French league with [[AS Monaco FC|Monaco]], where he spent three years before signing for [[Olympique de Marseille|Marseille]] in 2011. With Marseille, he won his first senior trophy, the [[Coupe de la Ligue]] in the [[2011–12 Coupe de la Ligue|2011–12]] season. In 2016, he left Marseille for [[Olympique Lyonnais|Lyon]], but after a disappointing year he left to join Italian club [[Torino F.C.|Torino]], initially on loan but later on a permanent basis. Though initially impressing, he fell out of favour, and left the club in 2021, before signing for English club [[Watford F.C.|Watford]]

Internationally, Nkoulou played 75 times for Cameroon between 2008 and 2017, and decided to retire shortly after winning the [[2017 Africa Cup of Nations|2017]] [[Africa Cup of Nations]], scoring the equalising goal in the [[2017 Africa Cup of Nations Final|final]]. He represented Cameroon in two [[FIFA World Cup|World Cups]] and three Africa Cup of Nations. In September 2022, he was called up to the national team for the first time after 5 years.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://fecafoot-officiel.com/2022/09/13/periode-fifa-de-septembre-2022-qui-sont-les-26-choisis-par-rigobert-song/|title=PERIODE FIFA DE SEPTEMBRE 2022: QUI SONT LES 26 CHOISIS PAR RIGOBERT SONG?|publisher=[[Cameroonian Football Federation]]|date=13 September 2022|language=fr}}</ref>

==Club career==

===Monaco===
Nkoulou began his career at [[Kadji Sports Academy]] in [[Douala]], the same academy that produced the likes of [[Samuel Eto'o]], [[Idriss Carlos Kameni|Carlos Kameni]], and [[Eric Djemba-Djemba]]. In late 2007, it was announced that he was acquired by the [[principality]]-based side [[AS Monaco FC|AS Monaco]]. He agreed to a three-year professional contract with the club.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.camfoot.com/Nicolas-NKoulou-le-Lion-de-l-AS.html|title=Nicolas NKoulou, le Lion de l'AS Monaco|publisher=CamFoot.com|language=fr|date=21 July 2008|access-date=27 September 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080723153822/http://www.camfoot.com/Nicolas-NKoulou-le-Lion-de-l-AS.html|archive-date=23 July 2008|url-status=dead}}</ref> He spent the latter portion of the 2007–08 season playing with Monaco's [[Championnat de France Amateurs|CFA]] squad, before joining the squad for preseason in July 2008, where he was handed the number 3 kit.<ref>{{cite news|title=Un jour à Monaco avec Nicolas Nkoulou|url=http://www.camfoot.com/les-lions-en-club/un-jour-a-monaco-avec-nicolas,9858.html|publisher=Cam Foot|language=fr|date=23 October 2008|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref>

Nkoulou made two appearances during the pre-season starting their opening friendly match against fellow Ligue 1 side [[Toulouse FC|Toulouse]] and also starting against Croatian side [[NK Zagreb]]. He made his professional debut on 13 September 2008 in a 2–0 victory over [[FC Lorient|Lorient]] starting the match before being substituted in the 65th minute.<ref>{{cite news|title=Nicolas Nkoulou, la force tranquille|url=http://www.camfoot.com/les-lions-en-club/nicolas-nkoulou-la-force,9750.htmll|publisher=Cam Foot|language=fr|date=23 September 2008|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref> Though he made 24 appearances in the 2008–09 season,

The 2009–10 season saw Nkoulou to play in defensive midfielder for the most of the season under the new management of [[Guy Lacombe]] and newly signing, [[Sébastien Puygrenier]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Nkoulou: "Un cauchemar à effacer"|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Nkoulou-un-cauchemar-a-effacer/54019|publisher=L’Equipe|language=fr|date=19 August 2009|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref> As the 2009–10 season progressed, Nkoulou signed a new contract with the club, keeping him until 2012<ref>{{cite news|title=Foot – L1 – Monaco : Nkoulou jusqu'en 2012|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Nkoulou-jusqu-en-2012/94345|publisher=L’Equipe|language=fr|date=8 March 2010|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref> and was then reverted to central defence.<ref>{{cite news|title=Foot – L1 – Monaco : Costa et Alonso absents|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Costa-et-alonso-absents/96475|publisher=L’Equipe|language=fr|date=19 March 2010|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Foot – L1 – Monaco : Retours de Nkoulou et Nenê|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Retours-de-nkoulou-et-nene/106054|publisher=L’Equipe|language=fr|date=7 May 2010|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title= Foot – L1 - 34e j. In the 2010–11 season, Nkoulou found himself in the transfer speculation and ended up staying at the club despite fallen out with Lacombe.<ref>{{cite news|title=Quel avenir pour les jeunes pépites de Monaco ?|url=http://www.footmercato.net/ligue1/transferts/quel-avenir-pour-les-jeunes-pepites-de-monaco_49806|publisher=Foot Mercato|language=fr|date=22 May 2010|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref> Nkoulou continued remain in the first team, playing in the defensive midfielder position for the most of the season and central defence. Despite suffering from injuries<ref>{{cite news|title=Hécatombe en défense|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Hecatombe-en-defense/166208|publisher=L’Equipe|language=fr|date=21 December 2010|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref> and suspension,<ref>{{cite news|title=Foot – L1 – Monaco Diarra déjà dans le bain|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Diarra-deja-dans-le-bain/174783|publisher=L’Equipe|language=fr|date=29 January 2011|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref> Nkoulou finished the 2010–11 season, making 30 appearances. However, following their relegation to [[Ligue 2]], Nkoulou was among key players to be expected to leave the club.<ref>{{cite news|title=AS Monaco : l'appel de l'étranger pour une jeune pépite ?|url=http://www.topmercato.com/32091,1/monaco---l-appel-de-l-etranger-pour-une-jeune-pepite.html|publisher=Top Mercato|date=3 June 2011|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref>

His performances with Monaco have led to him being scouted by several prominent European clubs. English club [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]],<ref>{{cite news|title=Arsenal tracking Monaco pair Nimini, N'Koulou|url=http://www.tribalfootball.com/?q=content/arsenal-tracking-monaco-pair-nimini-nkoulou|publisher=Tribal Football|date=28 January 2011|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref> Spanish sides [[Sevilla F.C.|Sevilla]] and [[Atlético Madrid]]<ref>{{cite news|title=Atletico Madrid, Sevilla following Monaco defender Nicolas N'Koulou|url=http://www.tribalfootball.com/articles/atletico-madrid-sevilla-following-monaco-defender-nicolas-nkoulou-203241|publisher=Tribal Football|date=30 September 2008|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref> and French champions [[Olympique Lyonnais]] have all declared their interests in the Cameroonian defender.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://fr.sports.yahoo.com/29092008/47/l1-monaco-n-koulou-se-fait-remarquer.html|title=L1 / MONACO :N'Koulou se fait remarquer|publisher=Yahoo! He has yet to score a goal.

===Lyon===
After spending five years at Marseille, with his contract at the club expired, Nkoulou moved to [[Choc des Olympiques|rivals]] [[Olympique Lyonnais|Lyon]] on a four–year contract on 29 June 2016; coincidentally was the day he joined Marseille.<ref>{{cite news|title=Mercato : l'ancien de l'OM Nicolas Nkoulou s'est engagé avec l'Olympique Lyonnais|url=http://www.francefootball.fr/news/Mercato-l-ancien-de-l-om-nicolas-nkoulou-s-est-engage-avec-l-olympique-lyonnais/700775|publisher=France Football|language=fr|date=29 June 2016|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref> Upon joining the club, Nkoulou stated he joined Lyon to achieve greater things and was given number 3 shirt, which he quoted: ‘’“The No. 3? It is a wink to those close to me, because I am the youngest of three children.”’’<ref>{{cite news|title=Nicolas Nkoulou excited about transfer to Lyon from Marseille|url=http://www.espnfc.com/story/2904458/nicolas-nkoulou-excited-about-transfer-to-lyon-from-marseille|publisher=ESPN|date=29 June 2016|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref>

Nkoulou played his first official match for Lyon at the [[2016 Trophée des Champions|Trophée des Champions]], making his first start, in a 4–1 loss against Paris Saint–Germain.<ref>{{cite news|title=Paris Saint-Germain cruise past Lyon to win Trophee des Champions|url=http://www.espnfc.us/report?gameId=458112|publisher=ESPN|date=6 August 2016|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref> A week later, he made his league debut for the club, in the opening game of the season, in a 3–0 win over [[AS Nancy|Nancy]].<ref>{{cite news|title=À Nancy, Lyon aligne le même onze que contre Paris|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/A-nancy-lyon-aligne-le-meme-onze-que-contre-paris/716866|publisher=L’Equipe|language=fr|date=14 August 2016|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref> In his first match against Marseille for the first time, which saw them draw  0–0 on 18 September 2016, Nkoulou received boos from Marseille supporters during the match every time he touched the ball.<ref>{{cite news|title=OL : Nkoulou a jugé les sifflets des fans phocéens "un peu ===Torino===
On 5 August 2017, Italian club [[Torino F.C.|Torino]] announced they had signed Nkoulou on a year-long [[Loan (sports)|loan]] with an option to purchase.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://torinofc.it/news/05/08/2017/nkoulou-al-toro_15129|title=N'Koulou al Toro|work=TORINO FC 1906 SITO UFFICIALE|access-date=6 April 2018|language=it}}</ref> He made his debut on 11 August 2017, in the [[Coppa Italia]] against [[Trapani Calcio|Trapani]], won by Torino 7–1. His debut in [[Serie A]] took place instead on 20 August, against [[Bologna F.C. 1909|Bologna]] (1–1). He scored his first goal for the club on 2 December, during a 1–1 draw against [[Atalanta B.C.|Atalanta]]. At the end of his first season with the club, he was voted by the fans as the best player of Torino in the 2017–18 season.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.toronews.net/toro/toro-news-award-20172018-nkoulou-campione-ljajic-conquista-il-podio/|title = Toro News Award 2017/2018: Nkoulou campione, Ljajic conquista il podio}}</ref> His contract was redeemed by Torino for €3.5 million.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.olweb.fr/fr/article/mercato-options-d-achat-levees-pour-nicolas-nkoulou-et-sergi-darder-35489.html|title = Mercato : Options d'achat levées pour Nicolas Nkoulou et Sergi Darder}}</ref>

On 2 September 2018, he scored his first goal of the 2018–19 season, and the match-winner with a header against [[S.P.A.L.|SPAL]] (1–0).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.gazzetta.it/Calcio/Serie-A/02-09-2018/serie-a-torino-spal-1-0-decisivo-nkoulou-esordio-zaza-290683128488.shtml|title = Serie A, Torino-Spal 1-0: Decisivo Nkoulou, esordio per Zaza}}</ref> In August 2019, Nkoulou asked to not be selected for Torino's Serie A fixture against [[U.S. Sassuolo Calcio|Sassuolo]],<ref>{{cite news |last=Said |first=Nick |url=https://africanfootball.com/news/771843/Nkoulou-asks-not-to-play-at-Torino-while-future-is-assessed |title=Nkoulou asks not to play at Torino while future is assessed |work=African Football |date=26 August 2019 |access-date=13 October 2021}}</ref> and their [[UEFA Europa League]] [[2019–20 UEFA Europa League qualifying phase and play-off round#Play-off round|play-off]] against [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]], with a desire to transfer to [[AS Roma]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Husband |first=Ben |url=https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/wolves-torino-europa-league-fixture-16816300 |title=Torino have been dealt this blow ahead of Wolves clash |work=[[Birmingham Mail]] |date=26 August 2019 |access-date=13 October 2021}}</ref> In September, with no transfer taking place, he wrote a letter saying he had decided to "leave the difficult moment of the summer behind me", and apologised to Torino fans, staff, and his teammates.<ref>{{cite news |last=Chefor |first=Nji Nelson |url=https://www.kick442.com/%F0%9F%87%A8%F0%9F%87%B2%F0%9F%87%AE%F0%9F%87%B9after-transfer-controversy-nkoulou-writes-to-torino/ |title=After Transfer Controversy, Nkoulou writes to Torino |work=Kick442 |date=13 September 2019 |access-date=13 October 2021}}</ref>

At the end of the 2020–21 season, Nkoulou parted ways with [[Torino F.C.|Torino]] upon the expiry of his contract.

===Watford===
After spending time as a [[free agent]], on 7 October 2021, [[Premier League]] club [[Watford F.C.|Watford]] announced the signing of Nkoulou on a deal until the end of the season.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.watfordfc.com/news/official-watford-sign-nkoulou|title = Official: Watford sign Nkoulou}}</ref>

Following relegation at the end of the [[2021–22 Watford F.C. season|2021–22 season]], Nkoulou was released by the club.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.watfordfc.com/news/news-first-team-departures-confirmed|title=News: First-Team Departures Confirmed|website=www.watfordfc.com|date=26 May 2022|accessdate=26 May 2022}}</ref>

=== Aris ===
On 23 August 2022, Nkoulou joined [[Super League Greece]] club [[Aris Thessaloniki F.C.|Aris]] on a free transfer on a two-year contract.<ref>{{Cite web |date=23 August 2022 |title=Ο Nicolas Nkoulou στον ΑΡΗ! - Ανακοινώσεις - News |url=https://www.arisfc.com.gr/index.php/gr/enimerosi/item/5-anakoinoseis/3127-o-nicolas-nkoulou-ston-ari |access-date=24 August 2022 |website=Aris FC}}</ref>

==International career==
Nkoulou was included in the [[Cameroon national football team|Cameroon U-23]] squad that participated in the [[2008 Summer Olympics]]. He helped the squad reach [[Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|the knockout round]], where they were eliminated by [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]].

[[File:Brasil camaroes 2.jpg|thumb|250px|Nkoulou observes on the background as his compatriot [[Landry N'Guémo]] battles [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]] midfielder [[Paulinho (footballer, born July 1988)|Paulinho]] for the ball during the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]] on 23 June.]]
Nkoulou was called up by the [[Cameroon national football team|national team]] for the first time on 4 November 2008<ref>{{cite news|title=Pierre Wome Nlend remplacé par Nicolas Nkoulou|url=http://www.camfoot.com/actualites/pierre-wome-nlend-remplace-par,9909.html|publisher=Cam Foot|language=fr|date=4 November 2008|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref> He would make his international debut in the senior squad on 19 November 2008 in a friendly against [[South Africa national football team|South Africa]], which they lost 3–2.<ref>{{cite news|title=South Africa 3 Cameroon 2: Match Report|url=http://www.camfoot.com/actualites/south-africa-cameroon-3-2-match,9953.html|publisher=Cam Foot|language=fr|date=19 November 2008|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref>

Nkoulou selected for the [[2010 African Cup of Nations]] by Cameroon National coach [[Paul Le Guen]]<ref>{{cite news|title=Cameroon omit defender Sebastien Bassong from squad|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/africa/8427094.stm|publisher=BBC Sport|date=23 December 2009|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref> and went on to play four times in the cup competitions. Later in 2010, N’Koulou was called up again for the [[2010 FIFA World Cup]]<ref>{{cite news|title= Foot - CM - CAM : Eto'o et les stars sont là|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Eto-o-et-les-stars-sont-la/110556|publisher=L’Equipe|language=fr|date=30 May 2010|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref> and made his World Cup debut, making his first start and playing 90 minutes, in a 1–0 loss against [[Japan national football team|Japan]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Japan 1 – 0 Cameroon|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/world_cup_2010/matches/match_10/default.stm|publisher=BBC Sport|date=14 June 2010|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref> N’Koulou played all three matches in the World Cup, as Cameroon were eliminated in the group stage.

After captain Samuel Eto'o and vice–captain [[Eyong Enoh]] were suspended for five and two matches respectively by Cameroon Football Federation for their action of boycotting a friendly match against [[Algeria national football team|Algeria]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www2.lequipe.fr/redirect-v6/homes/Football/breves2011/20111216_205545_eto-o-suspendu-15-matches.html|title= Foot – CAM Eto'o suspendu 15 matches !|publisher=L’Equipe|language=fr|date=16 December 2011|access-date=27 September 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120214002146/http://www2.lequipe.fr/redirect-v6/homes/Football/breves2011/20111216_205545_eto-o-suspendu-15-matches.html|archive-date=14 February 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> Nkoulou was appointed as captain of Cameroon on a temporary basis and captained his first national match, in a 1–0 win over [[Guinea-Bissau national football team|Guinea-Bissau]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.om.net/fr/Saison/101005/Actualites/59286/Capitaine_N_Koulou_commence_par_un_succes|title=Capitaine N'Koulou commence par un succès|publisher=Olympique de Marseille|language=fr|date=29 February 2012|access-date=27 September 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120302215512/http://www.om.net/fr/Saison/101005/Actualites/59286/Capitaine_N_Koulou_commence_par_un_succes|archive-date=2 March 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Following Eto’o refused to return to the national squad in late-August 2012, The Cameroon Football Federation stripped him of his captaincy in response and Nkoulou was appointed as a new captain of Cameroon.<ref>{{cite news|title=Cameroon coach to strip Samuel Eto'o of captaincy|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/18832588|publisher=BBC Sport|date=12 September 2012|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Capitanat maintenu à Nkoulou|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Capitanat-maintenu-a-nkoulou/311380|publisher=L’Equipe|language=fr|date=6 September 2012|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref> In the [[2014 FIFA World Cup|FIFA World Cup]], Nkoulou was among 23 players to be included for the squad.<ref>{{cite news|title=23 Lions indomptables|url=http://sport24.lefigaro.fr/football/coupe-du-monde/2014-bresil/actualites/23-lions-indomptables-697497|publisher=Le Figaro|language=fr|date=2 June 2014|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref> Nkoulou played all three matches and retained his captaincy, as Cameroon struggled in the World Cup after losing three matches that saw them eliminated in the World Cup. The following year, Nkoulou was called up into the squad by the national team for the [[2015 Africa Cup of Nations|African Cup of Nations]].<ref>{{cite news|title=OM : Nkoulou et Ayew quittent Marseille pour la CAN 2015|url=http://www.topmercato.com/98358,1/om---nkoulou-et-ayew-quittent-marseille-pour-la-can-2015.html|publisher=Top Mercato|language=fr|date=5 January 2015|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref>

Two years later on 26 March 2016, Nkoulou scored his first international goal, in a 2–2 draw against South Africa, the team he made his national debut eight years ago.<ref>{{cite news|title=Cameroon 2-2 South Africa: Kekana scores goal of the year contender|url=http://www.goal.com/en-us/match/cameroon-vs-south-africa/2038138/report|publisher=Goal.com|date=26 March 2016|access-date=27 September 2016}}</ref>

His equalizing goal against Egypt in the [[2017 Africa Cup of Nations Final]] is the first goal to be conceded by Egypt in the Africa Cup of Nations finals since [[Mengistu Worku]]'s goal in the [[1962 African Cup of Nations]] final.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.elyomnew.com/news/sports/2017/02/06/63296|title=5 records broken at the CAN final|date=6 February 2017|access-date=2 June 2017|language=ar|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170208195402/http://www.elyomnew.com/news/sports/2017/02/06/63296|archive-date=8 February 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>
