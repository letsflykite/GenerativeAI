'''Czesław Michniewicz''' ({{IPA-pl|ˈt͡ʂɛswaf mʲixˈɲɛvʲit͡ʂ}}; born 12 February 1970) is a Polish [[association football|football]] [[Manager (association football)|manager]] and former player who manages [[Saudi Professional League]] side [[Abha Club|Abha]]. He managed the [[Poland national football team|Poland national team]] in 2022.

==Managerial career==
===Early years===
In September 2003, Michniewicz was appointed as the manager of [[Lech Poznań]]. He led the side to the 2003–04 [[Polish Cup]] and [[Polish SuperCup|Polish Super Cup]] titles. He left the club in June 2006. In October 2006, he was appointed manager of [[Ekstraklasa]] side [[Zagłębie Lubin]], leading the club to the [[2006–07 Ekstraklasa|2006–07]] league title. In October 2007, he was relieved of his duties. From 8 July 2008 to 12 April 2009 he managed [[Arka Gdynia]] in the Ekstraklasa. From 15 November 2010 until 30 June 2011, he managed [[Widzew Łódź]].<ref>[http://ekstraklasa.wp.pl/kat,53716,title,Michniewicz-nowym-trenerem-Widzewa,wid,12852590,wiadomosc.html?ticaid=1b40b Michniewicz nowym trenerem Widzewa] 15 November 2010, wp.pl</ref> On 22 July 2011, he was appointed as the manager of [[Jagiellonia Białystok]], but left the position on 22 December 2011 by mutual consent. On 28 March 2012, Michniewicz was named as the new coach of [[Polonia Warsaw]], which he led until 8 May 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/worldfootball/clubfootball/news/newsid=1607412.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120406061549/http://www.fifa.com/worldfootball/clubfootball/news/newsid=1607412.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=6 April 2012|title=Polonia replace Zielinski with Michniewicz|publisher=FIFA.com|date=28 March 2012}}</ref>
[[File:Czesław Michniewicz Podbeskidzie.JPG|thumb|Michniewicz managing [[Podbeskidzie Bielsko-Biała]] in 2013]]
From 22 March to 22 October 2013, he managed [[Podbeskidzie Bielsko-Biała]]. After a year and a half pause, he was hired as the coach of [[Pogoń Szczecin]]. Michniewicz's side finished the league at sixth place in the [[2015–16 Ekstraklasa|2015–16 season]]. Despite the best result in years, his contract was not renewed and he left the club on 30 June 2016. From 1 July 2016 to 22 March 2017, he was in charge of [[Bruk-Bet Termalica Nieciecza]] in Ekstraklasa.

===Poland national under-21 football team===
On 7 July 2017, he was appointed as the coach of the [[Poland national under-21 football team|Poland under-21 national team]]. After defeating [[Portugal national under-21 football team|Portugal]] in the play-offs, Poland under his leadership qualified for the first time since 1994 for the final tournament of the [[2019 UEFA European Under-21 Championship]]. On 15 October 2020, he was replaced by [[Maciej Stolarczyk]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.90minut.pl/news/307/news3074766-Maciej-Stolarczyk-trenerem-reprezentacji-mlodziezowej.html |title=Maciej Stolarczyk trenerem reprezentacji młodzieżowej  |publisher=90minut.pl |language=pl |date= 15 October 2020 |access-date= 15 October 2020}}</ref>

===Legia Warsaw===
On 21 September 2020, he was appointed as the coach of [[Legia Warsaw]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.90minut.pl/news/306/news3068521-Czeslaw-Michniewicz-trenerem-Legii.html |title=Czesław Michniewicz trenerem Legii  |publisher=90minut.pl |language=pl |date= 21 September 2020 |access-date= 21 September 2020}}</ref> In his debut Legia won 2–0 over [[FC Drita]] at the [[2020–21 UEFA Europa League]] qualifiers. On 18 October 2020, he made his [[Ekstraklasa]] debut, as Legia won 2–1 over [[Zagłębie Lubin]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.90minut.pl/news/307/news3075620-Legia-odrabia-straty.html |title=Legia odrabia straty |publisher=90minut.pl |language=pl |date= 18 October 2020 |access-date= 19 October 2020}}</ref> On 11 December 2020, he was named [[2020–21 Ekstraklasa#Coach of the Month|Ekstraklasa's Coach of the Month]] after Legia's successful run in November.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://ekstraklasa.org/aktualnosci/trener-listopada-2020-czeslaw-michniewicz-15209 |title=Trener Listopada 2020: Czesław Michniewicz |publisher=Ekstraklasa |date=11 December 2020 |access-date=11 December 2020 |language=pl |archive-date=11 December 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201211101313/https://ekstraklasa.org/aktualnosci/trener-listopada-2020-czeslaw-michniewicz-15209 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He was awarded the same distinction for March 2021, as his team completed the campaign in the above-mentioned month without any defeat.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://ekstraklasa.org/aktualnosci/trener-marca-2021-czeslaw-michniewicz-15499 |title=Trener Marca 2021: Czesław Michniewicz |publisher=Ekstraklasa |date=8 April 2021 |access-date=9 April 2021 |language=pl |archive-date=8 April 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210408091432/https://ekstraklasa.org/aktualnosci/trener-marca-2021-czeslaw-michniewicz-15499 |url-status=dead }}</ref> On 28 April 2021, Michniewicz won [[2020–21 Ekstraklasa|his second Polish Championship]] after 0–0 draw between [[Jagiellonia Białystok]] and [[Raków Częstochowa]] (as the latter team had already lost mathematical chances of catching up with Legia), with three games to spare.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.90minut.pl/news/311/news3115531-Legia-mistrzem-Polski.html |title=Legia mistrzem Polski |publisher=90minut.pl |date=28 April 2021 |access-date=6 May 2021}}</ref>

On 26 August 2021, Legia led by Michniewicz defeated [[SK Slavia Prague]], and for the first time in five years qualified for the group stages of a European cup.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://polskieradio24.pl/art4147_2795690|language=pl|title=Legia Warszawa w fazie grupowej Ligi Europy. Slavia Praga odprawiona na Łazienkowskiej|website=PolskieRadio24.pl}}</ref> They would face [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]], [[S.S.C. Napoli|Napoli]] and [[FC Spartak Moscow|Spartak Moscow]] in the [[2021–22 UEFA Europa League|UEFA Europa League]] group stage.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://polskieradio.pl/art7790_2796434|title=Europa League draw: Legia Warsaw to play Napoli, Leicester, Spartak|website=PolskieRadio.pl}}</ref>

Although Legia led their [[2021–22 UEFA Europa League#Group C|group]] after wins against [[FC Spartak Moscow|Spartak Moscow]] and [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.polsatsport.pl/wiadomosc/2021-10-20/liga-europy-szalony-mecz-w-grupie-legii-warszawa-leicester-wygralo-w-moskwie/|title=Liga Europy: Szalony mecz w grupie Legii Warszawa. Leicester wygrało w Moskwie|language=pl|website=www.polsatsport.pl}}</ref> they struggled in the league. After four consecutive defeats, managing nine points in ten fixtures and sitting 15th in the table,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rdc.pl/informacje/kolejna-porazka-legii-w-ekstraklasie-wojskowi-ulegli-piastowi-14/|title=Kolejna porażka Legii w lidze. "Wojskowi" ulegli Piastowi 1:4|language=pl|website=www.rdc.pl}}</ref> Michniewicz was dismissed on 25 October 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://legia.com/pilka-nozna/komunikat-klubu-w-sprawie-zmian-w-sztabie-szkoleniowym/14566|title=Komunikat klubu w sprawie zmian w sztabie szkoleniowym|publisher=[[Legia Warsaw]]|date=25 October 2021|language=pl}}</ref>

===Poland national team===
On 31 January 2022, Michniewicz was named as the new manager of [[Poland national football team|Poland]], signing a deal until 31 December 2022 with an extension option,<ref>{{cite press release|publisher=[[Polish Press Agency]]|url=https://www.pap.pl/aktualnosci/news,1066556,czeslaw-michniewicz-selekcjonerem-reprezentacji-polski.html|title=Czesław Michniewicz selekcjonerem reprezentacji Polski|date=31 January 2022|access-date=31 January 2022|language=pl}}</ref> following the previous manager [[Paulo Sousa]]'s decision to join [[Clube de Regatas do Flamengo|Flamengo]] three months before Poland's first [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA second round|2022 FIFA World Cup qualification play-offs]] game.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20211229-portugal-s-paulo-sousa-leaves-poland-to-be-flamengo-coach|title=Portugal's Paulo Sousa leaves Poland to be Flamengo coach|publisher=France 24|date=29 December 2021|access-date=2 February 2021}}</ref> He was scheduled to make his debut against [[Russia national football team|Russia]] in [[Moscow]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sport.tvp.pl/58263192/czeslaw-michniewicz-jestesmy-delikatnym-faworytem-w-starciu-z-rosja|title=Reprezentacja Polski. Czesław Michniewicz: jesteśmy delikatnym faworytem w starciu z Rosją|publisher=TVP Sport|date=1 February 2022|access-date=2 February 2022|language=pl}}</ref> prior to the disqualification of Russia.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Fifa and Uefa suspend all Russian teams |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/athletics/60560567 |access-date=3 March 2022}}</ref> On 24 March, Poland played their first match under Michniewicz, a 1–1 draw in a friendly against [[Scotland national football team|Scotland]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tvp.info/59240474/szkocja-polska-11-wynik-lepszy-niz-gra-slaby-mecz-polakow|title=Wynik lepszy niż gra. Polacy zawiedli w debiucie Michniewicza|publisher=TVP Info|date=24 March 2022|access-date=25 March 2022|language=pl}}</ref>

On 29 March 2022, Michniewicz led Poland to a 2–0 victory over [[Sweden national football team|Sweden]] in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|World Cup]] qualification play-off final.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sport.tvp.pl/59325764/reprezentacja-polski-wygrala-ze-szwecja-i-pojedzie-na-mistrzostwa-swiata-2022-w-katarze|title=Mistrzostwa świata 2022. Reprezentacja Polski pokonała Szwecję i pojedzie na mundial w Katarze|trans-title=The 2022 World Cup. The Polish national team defeated Sweden and will play at the World Cup in Qatar|publisher=TVP Info|date=29 March 2022|access-date=31 March 2022|language=pl}}</ref> On 30 November 2022, Poland advanced to the knockout stage of the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] for the first time since [[FIFA World Cup 1986|1986]] under the lead of Michniewicz.<ref>{{cite web |author=Phil Hay |url=https://theathletic.com/live-blogs/poland-argentina-world-cup-2022-live-score-updates-result/U6axwGQihIZA/ |title=How Argentina advanced out of Group C in victory over Poland: Live World Cup updates |website=The Athletic |date=30 November 2022 }}</ref>

On 22 December 2022, following criticism of the national team's defensive tactics and style of play under Michniewicz by the media,<ref>{{cite web |title=Czesław Michniewicz odpiera krytykę. "Zasłużyliśmy na nagrodę" |url=https://sportowefakty.wp.pl/pilka-nozna/1034002/czeslaw-michniewicz-odpiera-krytyke-zasluzylismy-na-nagrode |website=sportowefakty.wp.pl |language=pl |date=3 December 2022}}</ref> a turmoil regarding bonuses for national team players and staff,<ref>{{cite web |title=Podwójna rola Jakuba Kwiatkowskiego. Nowe fakty ws. "afery premiowej" |url=https://www.sport.pl/pilka/7,65037,29285984,podwojna-rola-jakuba-kwiatkowskiego-nowe-fakty-ws-afery-premiowej.html |website=sport.pl |language=pl |date=21 December 2022}}</ref> Michniewicz blocking several journalists on Twitter before deleting his account,<ref>{{cite web |title=Reprezentacja Polski. Czesław Michniewicz zniknął z Twittera. "To konto nie istnieje" |url=https://gol24.pl/reprezentacja-polski-czeslaw-michniewicz-zniknal-z-twittera-to-konto-nie-istnieje/ar/c2-17134991 |website=gol24.pl |language=pl |date=16 December 2022}}</ref> and reportedly shouldering part of the blame for the media fallout on team manager Jakub Kwiatkowski,<ref>{{cite web |title=Reprezentacja Polski. Media. Konflikt na linii Czesław Michniewicz – Jakub Kwiatkowski? Kolejne szokujące informacje |url=https://sport.tvp.pl/65149075/reprezentacja-polski-konflikt-na-linii-czeslaw-michniewicz-jakub-kwiatkowski-kolejne-szokujace-informacje |website=sport.tvp.pl |language=pl |date=17 December 2022}}</ref> it was announced Michniewicz's contract would not be extended and he was to leave his post on 31 December 2022.<ref>{{cite web |title=Komunikat Polskiego Związku Piłki Nożnej 22.12.2022 |url=https://pzpn.pl/reprezentacje/reprezentacja-a/aktualnosci/2022-12-22/komunikat-polskiego-zwiazku-pilki-noznej-22-12-2022 |publisher=[[Polski Związek Piłki Nożnej]] |language=pl |date=22 December 2022}}</ref>

===Abha===
On 12 June 2023, Michniewicz was announced as the new manager of Saudi club [[Abha Club|Abha]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Czesław Michniewicz nowym trenerem saudyjskiego Abha Club |url=https://eurosport.tvn24.pl/pilka-nozna/saudyjska-premier-league/2022-2023/czeslaw-michniewicz-nowym-trenerem-saudyjskiego-abha-club_sto9657817/story.shtml |website=eurosport.tvn24.pl |language=pl |date=12 June 2023}}</ref>

==Personal life==
[[File:Czeslaw Michniewicz 2010.jpg|thumb|Michniewicz in 2010]]
Michniewicz was born in [[Byarozawka]], then in the [[Byelorussian Soviet Socialist Republic|Byelorussian SSR]], [[Soviet Union]] (present-day [[Belarus]]), while his mother was visiting her family; but grew up in [[Biskupiec, Nowe Miasto County|Biskupiec, Poland]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://michniewicz.com.pl/en/about-me/about-me/46 |title=About me » About me.. |last=Michniewicz |first=Czesław |website=Official Website |publisher=Sportwin |location=[[Poznań]] |access-date=22 June 2019 }}</ref> On 20 June 1998, he married Grażyna Rzewuska, with whom he has two sons: Mateusz (born 22 January 2001) and Jakub (born 13 August 2003).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://michniewicz.com.pl/en/about-me/family/47 |title=About me » Family |last=Michniewicz |first=Czesław |website=Official Website |publisher=Sportwin |location=[[Poznań]] |access-date=22 June 2019}}</ref>
