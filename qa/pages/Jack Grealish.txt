'''Jack Peter Grealish''' (born 10 September 1995) is an English professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Winger (association football)|winger]] or [[attacking midfielder]] for {{English football updater|AstonVil}} club [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] and the [[England national football team|England national team]]. Regarded as one of the best wingers in the world,<ref>{{cite news |url= https://thetrivelaeffect.com/ranking-the-10-best-wingers-in-world-football-for-2023/ |title= Ranking the 10 best wingers in world football for 2023 |newspaper=The Trivela Effect |access-date=13 June 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url= https://interestingfootball.com/top-20-best-wingers-in-the-world/ |title= Top 20 best wingers in the world 2023 |newspaper=Interesting Football |access-date=13 June 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/ranked-the-10-best-left-wingers-in-the-world |title= Ranked! The 10 best left-wingers in the world right now |newspaper=FourFourTwo |access-date=13 June 2023}}</ref> Grealish is known for his dribbling and playmaking ability as well as his robust defensive capabilities.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.theguardian.com/football/blog/2023/apr/13/jack-grealish-superstar-cog-ruthless-machine-manchester-city |title= Jack Grealish has worked out how to be a superstar cog in a ruthless machine |newspaper=The Guardian |access-date=13 June 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.coachesvoice.com/cv/jack-grealish-aston-villa/ |title= Jack Grealish: Premier League Player Watch |newspaper=Coaches’ Voice|access-date=13 June 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.mancity.com/news/mens/jack-grealish-2022-2023-season-stats-so-far-63815527 |title= Jack Grealish’s influence on City’s 2022/23 season so far in numbers |work=Manchester City|access-date=13 June 2023}}</ref> Grealish is often considered to be one of the most popular and marketable athletes in England.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://theathletic.com/4287363/2023/03/11/jack-grealish-britain-most-marketable-footballer/# |title= How Jack Grealish became the most marketable British footballer since David Beckham |newspaper=The Athletic |access-date=13 June 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/jack-grealish-england-germany-euro-2020-b1875435.html |title= Why do fans love Jack Grealish? England’s creative spark is a symbol of possibility |newspaper=The Independent |access-date=13 June 2023}}</ref><ref name=“GazzaAndGrealish”>{{cite news |url= https://apnews.com/article/jack-grealish-man-city-fa-cup-beckham-e7b04b8978e9bdb1ca0746f012549696 |title= A mix of Beckham and Gazza, Man City’s Grealish emerging as the face of English soccer |website=AP News|access-date=16 June 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.theguardian.com/football/2023/jun/13/jack-grealish-is-having-the-best-week-of-his-life-manchester-city-treble |title= The hedonist’s hedonist: why Jack Grealish is having the best week of his life |website=The Guardian|access-date=17 June 2022 }}</ref>  

Grealish joined [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]] at the age of six, and made his debut for the club in May 2014, following a [[Loan (association football)|loan]] at [[Notts County F.C.|Notts County]], becoming club [[Captain (association football)|captain]] in 2019. In 2021, Grealish was signed by Manchester City in a transfer deal worth £100 million, making him [[List of most expensive association football transfers|the most expensive English player ever]]. In his first season with the club, he won a [[2021–22 Premier League|Premier League title]], before securing a [[Treble (association football)|continental treble]] in his second campaign.

Born in England and having Irish ancestry, Grealish was eligible to represent either England or the Republic of Ireland internationally. He was capped by the Republic of Ireland up to [[Republic of Ireland national under-21 football team|under-21 level]] before confirming his decision to play for England in April 2016. Grealish played for the [[England national under-21 football team|England under-21s]] for the first time in May 2016, winning the [[2016 Toulon Tournament]]. He represented the senior side at [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]].

==Early life==
Grealish was born in [[Birmingham]], West Midlands<ref>{{Hugman|26152|access-date=31 May 2018}}</ref> and raised in nearby [[Solihull]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/football/teams/aston-villa/11550090/Aston-Villas-Jack-Grealish-could-spark-international-tug-of-war-between-Ireland-and-England.html |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/archive/20220112/https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/football/teams/aston-villa/11550090/Aston-Villas-Jack-Grealish-could-spark-international-tug-of-war-between-Ireland-and-England.html |archive-date=12 January 2022 |url-access=subscription |url-status=live |title=Aston Villa's Jack Grealish could spark international tug-of-war between Ireland and England |first1=John |last1=Percy |first2=Jeremy |last2=Wilson |first3=Luke |last3=Edwards |newspaper=The Daily Telegraph |location=London |date=20 April 2015 |access-date=31 May 2018}}{{cbignore}}</ref> He attended Our Lady of Compassion Roman Catholic Primary School and [[St Peter's Catholic School, Solihull|St Peter's Roman Catholic Secondary School]] in Solihull.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/aston-villa-playing-callum-reilly-8138398 |title=Aston Villa: Playing with Callum Reilly made me competitive, says Jack Grealish |first=Gregg |last=Evans |newspaper=Birmingham Mail |date=20 November 2014 |access-date=31 May 2018}}</ref>

Grealish is of Irish descent, through his maternal grandfather from [[County Dublin]], his paternal grandfather from [[Gort]], County Galway, and his paternal grandmother from [[Sneem]], County Kerry.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.irishtimes.com/sport/soccer/international/jack-grealish-poised-to-commit-to-ireland-senior-squad-1.1966879 |title=Jack Grealish poised to commit to Ireland senior squad |first=John |last=Fallon |newspaper=The Irish Times |location=Dublin |date=17 October 2014 |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref><ref name=realdeal/><ref name=fatherinterview>{{cite web |url=https://talksport.com/football/264949/exclusive-jack-grealish-faces-tough-decision-about-england-future-claims-father/ |title=Exclusive&nbsp;– Jack Grealish faces tough decision about England future, claims father |first=Nick |last=Rostron-Pike |publisher=Talksport |date=21 April 2015 |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref> Influenced by his Irish heritage, Grealish played [[Gaelic football]] for [[John Mitchel's Hurling Club|John Mitchel's Hurling and Camogie Club]] of [[Warwickshire GAA]] between the ages of 10 and 14. He competed against former [[Aston Villa W.F.C.|Aston Villa]] and current [[Manchester United W.F.C.|Manchester United]] defender [[Aoife Mannion]], a school classmate of his, at Gaelic football.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.avfc.co.uk/page/NewsDetail/0,,10265~2848775,00.html |title=Mannion: Mixed Gaelic football games toughened me up |first=Paul |last=Brown |publisher=Aston Villa F.C. |date=13 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150531021226/http://www.avfc.co.uk/page/NewsDetail/0,,10265~2848775,00.html |archive-date=31 May 2015}}</ref>

Grealish's younger brother, Keelan, died of [[sudden infant death syndrome]] in April 2000 at the age of nine months.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Kendrick |first1=Mat |title=The family tragedy that inspires Aston Villa's Birmingham City derby hero Jack Grealish |url=https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/aston-villa-england-grealish-brother-13226229 |access-date=11 March 2019 |newspaper=Birmingham Mail |date=8 April 2018}}</ref>

====2014–2015: Development and maturation====
[[File:Jack Grealish aug 2014.jpg|thumb|upright|Grealish playing for [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]] in 2014]]

Following his loan with Notts County, Grealish returned to Aston Villa and made his club debut on 7 May, coming on as an 88th-minute substitute for [[Ryan Bertrand]] in a 4–0 away defeat to [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] in the Premier League.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/26396253 |title=Manchester City 4–0 Aston Villa |first=David |last=Ornstein |website=BBC Sport |date=7 May 2014 |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref> With his contract due to expire in the summer of 2015, he was offered a new four-year deal by the club in September 2014.<ref name=contract14/> On 14 October, Grealish signed a new four-year contract with Aston Villa.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/29619527 |title=Jack Grealish: Aston Villa midfielder signs new contract |website=BBC Sport |date=14 October 2014 |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref> Grealish made his first start in an [[2014–15 FA Cup|FA Cup]] third round tie on 4 January 2015 against [[Blackpool F.C.|Blackpool]] at Villa Park, which his team won 1–0. He played 75 minutes before being substituted for [[Andreas Weimann]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/30614386 |title=Aston Villa 1–0 Blackpool |first=Steve |last=Canavan |website=BBC Sport |date=4 January 2015 |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref> On 7 March, in the sixth round, a 2–0 home win over [[West Bromwich Albion F.C.|West Bromwich Albion]], he replaced [[Charles N'Zogbia]] after 74 minutes, and was sent off for a second booking for [[Diving (association football)|diving]] in added time.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/31669806 |title=Aston Villa 2–0 West Bromwich Albion |first=Phil |last=McNulty |website=BBC Sport |date=7 March 2015 |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref> On 7 April, Grealish started his first match for Aston Villa in the Premier League, a 3–3 home draw against [[Queens Park Rangers F.C.|Queens Park Rangers]] in which his performance was highly praised.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/32126419 |title=Aston Villa 3–3 Queens Park Rangers |first=Jonathan |last=Jurejko |website=BBC Sport |date=7 April 2015 |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref> In the FA Cup semi-final against [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] at [[Wembley Stadium]], Grealish played a part in both of Villa's goals including assisting [[Fabian Delph]]'s winner, as they came from behind to advance to the final.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/32273487 |title=Aston Villa 2–1 Liverpool |first=Phil |last=McNulty |website=BBC Sport |date=19 April 2015 |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref> On 30 May, Grealish played the entirety of the [[2015 FA Cup final]] at Wembley Stadium, as Villa lost 4–0 to [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]].<ref name="2015facupfinal">{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/32856754 |title=Arsenal 4–0 Aston Villa |first=Phil |last=McNulty |website=BBC Sport |date=30 May 2015 |access-date=31 May 2018}}</ref>

In April 2015, Grealish was warned by Aston Villa [[Manager (association football)|manager]] [[Tim Sherwood]] after ''[[The Sun (United Kingdom)|The Sun]]'' published images showing him allegedly inhaling [[nitrous oxide]] for recreational purposes. Sherwood said that "We can't condone that behaviour. He is now in a responsible position as a professional footballer, he's got to make sure it won't happen again".<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/32436515 |title=Jack Grealish warned by Aston Villa boss Tim Sherwood |website=BBC Sport |date=23 April 2015 |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref> Sherwood later said in May 2015 that Grealish was learning from his friendship with his midfield partner, the experienced [[Joe Cole]]. Sherwood considered Cole a role model for Grealish when concealing his private life, as he "didn't read too much about Joe being on the front pages".<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/32558982 |title=Tim Sherwood: Jack Grealish can learn from Joe Cole at Aston Villa |website=BBC Sport |date=1 May 2015 |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref> Grealish scored his first goal for Villa on 13 September 2015, a 20-yard shot to open the scoring away to [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]]; however, his team lost 3–2.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/34166753 |title=Leicester City 3–2 Aston Villa |first=Andy |last=Cryer |website=BBC Sport |date=13 September 2015 |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref> In November, he chose to stay in [[North West England]] and go clubbing after Villa's 4–0 loss to [[Everton F.C.|Everton]]. New manager [[Rémi Garde]] punished him for this decision by making Grealish train with the under-21 team, and stated that "You have to behave as a professional and it was not the case this time for Jack".<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/34930220 |title=Jack Grealish: Aston Villa boss Remi Garde drops midfielder |website=BBC Sport |date=26 November 2015 |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref> He returned to full training on 8 December.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/35038482 |title=Jack Grealish: Aston Villa player returns to first-team training |website=BBC Sport |date=8 December 2015 |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref>

====2016–2019: Injury and regained form====
On 7 January 2016, [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]] head coach [[Steve Evans (footballer, born 1962)|Steve Evans]] said that Villa had rejected an enquiry to take Grealish on loan.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.leedsunited.com/news/team-news/423/evans-provides-latest-transfer-news |title=Evans provides latest transfer news |publisher=Leeds United F.C. |date=7 January 2016 |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref> Villa finished the season in last place, ending their status as Premier League ever-presents. Grealish played 16 matches, all defeats, breaking a record for worst season previously held by Sunderland's [[Sean Thornton]], who lost in all 11 of his appearances in [[2002–03 FA Premier League|2002–03]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/1862/premier-league/2016/05/18/23687422/aston-villas-jack-grealish-sets-unwanted-premier-league |title=Aston Villa's Jack Grealish sets unwanted Premier League record |first=Chris |last=Davie |website=Goal.com |publisher=Perform Group |date=18 May 2016 |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref> In September, Villa opened an internal disciplinary investigation after reports that Grealish was at a party at a Birmingham hotel which had to be shut down by the police in the early morning. In response, owner [[Tony Xia]] wrote on Twitter that Grealish had to focus on and off the pitch, and associate with the right people.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/37438851 |title=Jack Grealish: Aston Villa investigate midfielder after reports of all-night party |website=BBC Sport |date=22 September 2016 |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref> In October, he was suspended for three matches after accepting a charge of violent conduct following a stamp on [[Conor Coady]] in Villa's 1–1 draw with [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.espnfc.com/aston-villa/story/2975354/aston-villa-midfielder-jack-grealish-banned-for-three-games-over-stamp |title=Aston Villa midfielder Jack Grealish banned for three games over stamp |website=ESPN FC |agency=PA Sport |date=18 October 2016 |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref> On 29 July 2017, during a post-season friendly against [[Watford F.C.|Watford]], while challenging for a ball in the air near the Watford box, he collided with Watford midfielder [[Tom Cleverley]]. The collision caused him to be rushed to the [[Heartlands Hospital]] in [[Birmingham]] where he went through surgery.<ref>{{Cite news |date=29 July 2017 |title=Aston Villa's Jack Grealish taken to hospital after Watford draw |website=Sky Sports |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11677/10967017/aston-villas-jack-grealish-taken-to-hospital-after-watford-draw |access-date=27 April 2023}}</ref> It was later revealed that he had been elbowed by Cleverley, causing his kidney to be ruptured, and leaving him sidelined for several months as he recovered.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Kendrick |first=Mat |date=30 July 2017 |title=Aston Villa's Jack Grealish has an operation - extent of his injury revealed |newspaper=Birmingham Mail |url=https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/aston-villas-jack-grealish-operation-13408313 |access-date=27 April 2023}}</ref> In an interview with the ''[[Daily Mirror]]'' in May 2018, he revealed that the injury had been life-threatening.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Moxley |first=Neil |date=20 May 2018 |title='The surgeon told me, Jack, you could die': Aston Villa's Grealish on freak injury last summer that nearly killed him |newspaper=Daily Mirror |url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/football/news/the-surgeon-told-me-jack-12563789 |access-date=27 April 2023}}</ref>

On 10 March 2019, Grealish was assaulted by a pitch invader during the [[Second City Derby|derby match]] away to [[Birmingham City F.C.|Birmingham City]]. Later in the second half, he scored to give Villa a 1–0 victory.<ref>{{cite news |first=Ged |last=Scott |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/47431193 |title=Birmingham City 0–1 Aston Villa |website=BBC Sport |date=10 March 2019}}</ref> A 27-year-old man from [[Rubery]] was arrested. He appeared on 11 March at [[Birmingham Magistrates' Court]] charged with encroachment on to the pitch and assault.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/47515993 |title=Jack Grealish attacked by spectator in Birmingham v Aston Villa game |date=11 March 2019 |website=BBC Sport |access-date=11 March 2019}}</ref> He pleaded guilty to the offences and was sent to prison for 14 weeks.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-england-birmingham-47523268 |title=Fan jailed for Grealish pitch attack |date=11 March 2019 |website=BBC News |access-date=11 March 2019}}</ref> Grealish captained the team from March onwards, a period which saw them amass a club-record 10 league wins in a row. ====2020–2021: Club talisman and individual success====
In March 2020, the Premier League was suspended midway through Aston Villa's return season, due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=Premier League, Football League and WSL suspended until April |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2020/mar/13/premier-league-and-football-league-suspended-until-4-april-coronavirus |newspaper=The Guardian |location=London |last=MacInnes |first=Paul |date=13 March 2020 |access-date=8 August 2020}}</ref> During that enforced break, it was revealed that Grealish had violated government guidance to stay home. A technically gifted player, Grealish plays as a [[Winger (association football)|winger]] (normally on the left flank, a position which allows him to cut inside onto his stronger right foot) or [[attacking midfielder]], and has been noted for his ability to run and [[Dribbling#Association football|dribble]] past defenders.<ref name=open/><ref name="emerging">{{cite web ===Allegations of diving===
Despite his ability and deft work on the ball, Grealish has on numerous occasions been criticised for going down too easily and allegedly [[Diving (association football)|diving]] to win penalties and free kicks.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Winterburn |first1=Sarah |title=Jack Grealish, diving and the utter failure of the law |url=https://www.football365.com/news/jack-grealish-diving-utter-failure-law |website=Football365 |date=3 December 2020 |publisher=Planet Sport Publishing Ltd |access-date=6 June 2021}}</ref> In August 2019, Grealish was booked for a dive against [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]], which led to an equalising goal from Villa being disallowed.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Rosser |first1=Jack |title=Jack Grealish still reeling from Crystal Palace 'diving' controversy as Aston Villa denied goal |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/jack-grealish-aston-villa-crystal-palace-diving-controversy-a4241561.html |newspaper=Evening Standard |date=19 September 2019 |location=London |access-date=6 June 2021}}</ref> Former [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] defender [[Steve Nicol]] said in December 2020, "Jack Grealish throws himself on the ground 50 times a game. I was actually looking at the clock today. The first time he went down was just under a minute on the clock. The next one was on three minutes! [It is] Most embarrassing. He threw himself on the ground where the dugouts were. Just embarrassing. He is a brilliant player. He really spoils it for me when I'm watching."<ref>{{cite web |last1=Blake |first1=John |title=Steve Nicol slams Aston Villa's Jack Grealish for diving |url=https://sportslens.com/steve-nicol-slams-aston-villas-jack-grealish-for-diving/323936/|website=SportsLens |date=29 December 2020 |access-date=6 June 2021}}</ref> Moreover, former [[Birmingham City F.C.|Birmingham City]] and England defender [[Matthew Upson]] has criticised Grealish, saying: "We could see it here and we are a fair distance away. Jack Grealish waited for the contact and literally just dived on the floor... I can see Grealish having a cheeky little chuckle to himself and happy with the free-kick he has just won."<ref>{{cite web |last1=Owen |first1=Brian |title=Matt Upson's reaction to Jack Grealish 'dive' and VAR offside |url=https://www.theargus.co.uk/sport/18910312.matt-upsons-reaction-jack-grealish-dive-var-offside/ |newspaper=The Argus |location=Brighton |access-date=6 June 2021}}</ref> However, Grealish has defended himself, saying that the fact he is the most fouled player in the league suggests defenders make more mistakes trying to stop him, thus giving away more penalties and free kicks.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Byrom |first1=David |title=Jack Grealish calls out Leeds United fans as he responds to Aston Villa diving accusations |url=https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/jack-grealish-diver-leeds-foul-19446604 |newspaper=Birmingham Mail |date=12 December 2020 |access-date=6 June 2021}}</ref> This statement was supported by the statistics, with Grealish officially being the most fouled player in the 2020–21 Premier League season.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Mann-Bryans |first1=Mark |title=Jack Grealish prepared for rough treatment as he aims for successful Euros |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/jack-grealish-premier-league-austria-england-villa-b1859383.html |newspaper=The Independent |location=London |date=3 June 2021 |access-date=12 October 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In March 2020, Grealish was found to have violated government guidance to stay at home in relation to [[COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom|COVID-19 regulations]] and was fined by Aston Villa.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.avfc.co.uk/News/2020/03/30/club-statement-jack-grealish |title=Club statement |publisher=Aston Villa F.C. |date=30 March 2020 |access-date=27 August 2020}}</ref> Grealish was banned from driving for nine months in the UK and fined £82,499 after pleading guilty to two counts of careless driving in March and October 2020, one in which he was filmed colliding with several parked cars during a turn in the road in his [[Range Rover]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/premier-league/aston-villa/jack-grealish-driving-ban-fine-news-b1774208.html|title=Jack Grealish banned from driving for nine months and fined after pleading guilty to careless driving |newspaper=The Independent |date=15 December 2020 |access-date=15 December 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-birmingham-55315924 |title= Jack Grealish: Aston Villa captain banned from driving |newspaper=BBC |date=15 December 2020 |access-date=13 June 2023}}</ref>

Grealish has known his girlfriend, Sasha Rebecca Attwood, since meeting her at secondary school aged 16.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.birminghamworld.uk/news/people/sasha-attwood-message-jack-grealish-champions-league-4179589 |title= 'Beyond proud': Sasha Attwood pens heartwarming message to Jack Grealish after Champions League win |newspaper=[[BirminghamWorld|Birmingham World]] |date=12 June 2023 |access-date=13 June 2023}}</ref> His great-great-grandfather, [[Billy Garraty]], was also a footballer, who earned one England cap and won the [[1905 FA Cup final]] with [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/aston-villa-v-arsenal-1905-9067929 |title=Aston Villa v Arsenal: This 1905 FA Cup winner is related to a current Villa star&nbsp;– find out who |first=Mat |last=Kendrick |newspaper=Birmingham Mail |date=26 May 2015 |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://englandstats.com/players.php?pid=380 |title=Player info: William Garraty |website=Englandstats.com |publisher=Davey Naylor |access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref> Grealish has a sister, Hollie, who has [[cerebral palsy]]. He describes her as his “best friend”.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.joe.co.uk/sport/jack-grealish-says-little-sister-with-cerebral-palsy-is-his-best-friend-368035# |title= Jack Grealish says little sister with cerebral palsy is his ‘best friend’ |website=JOE |access-date=16 June 2023}}</ref>

Grealish is known to have a close relationship with many of his Manchester City teammates,<ref>{{cite web |url= https://talksport.com/football/1459925/jack-grealish-bernardo-silva-transfer-man-city/ |title= Jack Grealish spotted begging Bernardo Silva to stay at Man City during treble celebrations |website=TalkSport |access-date=16 June 2023}}</ref> especially Norwegian [[Erling Haaland]]. On his relationship with Haaland, Grealish commented that he got on "really well with him," having “the same type of banter”.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.sportbible.com/football/jack-grealish-man-city-erling-haaland-lifestyle-766461-20230505 |title= Jack Grealish gives refreshingly honest insight into his lifestyle choices compared to Erling Haaland |website=SportBible |access-date=16 June 2023}}</ref> Grealish has named [[Elvis Presley]] and [[Queen (band)|Queen]] as two of his favourites musical artists of all time.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.goal.com/en-gb/lists/11-fun-facts-about-jack-grealish/blt868fd39c9dc7c021#cse65243368c27fcaa |title= 11 fun facts about Jack Grealish |website=Goal |access-date=2 July 2023}}</ref>
