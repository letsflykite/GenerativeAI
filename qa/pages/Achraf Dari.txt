'''Achraf Dari''' ({{lang-ar|أشرف داري}}; born 6 May 1999) is a Moroccan professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[defender (association football)|defender]] for {{French football updater|Brest}} club [[Stade Brestois 29|Brest]] and the [[Morocco national football team|Morocco national team]].<ref>{{FootballDatabase.eu|id=322832-achraf-dari}}</ref> He started his professional career playing for [[Wydad AC]].

Dari represented Morocco at various youth levels, before making his international debut in 2021. He was chosen in Morocco's squads for the [[FIFA Arab Cup]] in [[2021 FIFA Arab Cup|2021]].

== Club career ==

=== Wydad ===
Dari began his professional football career with [[Wydad AC]]. On 31 May 2022, Dari won his first international trophy with Wydad after defeating [[Al Ahly SC|Al Ahly]] 2–0 in [[2022 CAF Champions League Final]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Al Ahly v Wydad Casablanca Match Report, 30/05/2022, CAF Champions League {{!}} Goal.com |url=https://www.goal.com/en-bh/match/al-ahly-v-wydad-casablanca/report/45a4r4i0hov91381npsybcqvo |access-date=28 September 2022 |website=www.goal.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |title=El Moutaraji wins Champions League for Wydad |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/africa/61637713 |access-date=28 September 2022}}</ref> On 30 June 2022, Dari was nominated for the [[CAF Awards|CAF inter club player of the year awards]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Football |first=CAF-Confedération Africaine du |title=First list of nominees for CAF Awards 2022 revealed |url=https://www.cafonline.com/press-release/news/first-list-of-nominees-for-caf-awards-2022-revealed |access-date=28 September 2022 |website=CAFOnline.com |language=en}}</ref>

=== Brest ===
On 30 July 2022, Dari signed for [[Ligue 1]] club [[Stade Brestois 29|Brest]] on a four-year contract.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Cassé |first=Josué |date=30 July 2022 |title=Achraf Dari s'est engagé à Brest |url=https://www.footmercato.net/a8829189414029947955-achraf-dari-sest-engage-a-brest |access-date=30 July 2022 |website={{Interlanguage link|Foot Mercato|lt=Foot Mercato|fr|Foot Mercato}} |language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=New faces: All of Ligue 1 Uber Eats' new arrivals |url=https://www.ligue1.com/Articles/NEWS/2022/08/17/sanchez-schmeichel-ramsey-new-signings-transfers-new-arrivals |access-date=29 August 2022 |website=Ligue1 COM |language=en}}</ref> The transfer fee paid to [[Wydad AC]] was reported to be in the region of €2.7 million.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Cassoly |first=Marius |date=30 July 2022 |title=Brest s'offre l'international marocain Achraf Dari (officiel) |url=https://www.dailymercato.com/news/brest-soffre-linternational-marocain-achraf-dari-officiel |access-date=30 July 2022 |website=Dailymercato |language=fr}}</ref> On 7 August 2022, Dari made his debut against [[RC Lens]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=A.KITABRI |title=Ligue1 : Début prometteur d'Achraf Dari |url=https://www.lopinion.ma/Ligue1-Debut-prometteur-d-Achraf-Dari_a30471.html |access-date=29 August 2022 |website=L'Opinion Maroc - Actualité et Infos au Maroc et dans le monde. |language=fr}}</ref> On 21 August, Dari scored his first professional goal for the club in a 3–1 victory against [[Angers SCO]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Theate the matchwinner as Rennes beat Ajaccio |url=https://www.ligue1.com/Articles/MATCH%20REPORTS/2022/08/21/theate-rennes-ajaccio-clermont-brest-auxerre |access-date=29 August 2022 |website=Ligue1 COM |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Hakimi scores record-breaking goal as PSG thrash Lille in Ligue 1 {{!}} Goal.com |url=https://www.goal.com/en-ug/news/hakimi-scores-record-breaking-goal-as-psg-thrash-lille-in/bltba4c7b33a153d85e |access-date=29 August 2022 |website=www.goal.com |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Round 3 review: PSG run riot as Lyon, Marseille stay in touc |url=https://www.ligue1.com/Articles/NEWS/2022/08/22/round-3-review-psg-run-riot-as-lyon-marseille-stay-in-touch |access-date=29 August 2022 |website=Ligue1 COM |language=en}}</ref>

== International career ==
Dari made his debut for the [[Morocco A' national football team]] in a 1–0 win over [[Saudi Arabia national football team|Saudi Arabia]] at the [[2021 FIFA Arab Cup]].<ref>{{NFT player|84893|Achraf Dari|access-date=30 July 2022}}</ref> On 12 June 2022, [[Vahid Halilhodžić]] summoned Dari to the [[Morocco national football team|Moroccan national football team]].<ref>{{Cite news |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |title=Morocco: Halilhodzic summons Achraf Dari |work=Moroccolatestnews |url=https://moroccolatestnews.com/morocco-halilhodzic-summons-achraf-dari/}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=13 June 2022 |title=Vahid Halilhodzic invites Wydad's Achraf Dari to join national team in CAN 2023 second qualifier game |url=https://en.hespress.com/43872-vahid-halilhodzic-invites-wydads-achraf-dari-to-join-national-team-in-can-2023-second-qualifier-game.html |access-date=28 September 2022 |website=HESPRESS English - Morocco News |language=en-US}}</ref> He made his debut in a 2–0 victory against [[Liberia national football team|Liberia]] in the [[2023 Africa Cup of Nations qualification]] that took place in the [[Stade Mohammed V]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=Echabouch |first=Elmahdi |title=Morocco Wins 2-0 Against Liberia, Edges Closer to AFCON 2023 |work=Morocco World News |url=https://www.moroccoworldnews.com/2022/06/349678/morocco-wins-2-0-against-liberia-edges-closer-to-afcon-2023}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Kokoi |first=Anthony |title=2023 AFCON Q: Liberia Fall to Morocco 2-0 |url=https://www.liberianobserver.com/2023-afcon-q-liberia-fall-morocco-2-0 |access-date=28 September 2022 |website=www.liberianobserver.com |language=en}}</ref>

In September 2022, Dari was called up to join the Morocco national team to take part in [[Friendlies]] matches against [[Chile national football team|Chile]] and [[Paraguay national football team|Paraguay]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=12 September 2022 |title=Équipe du Maroc |url=https://www.facebook.com/ENMAROCofficiel/photos/ms.c.eJw1x8kJACAQBMGMZNyx98g~;MUGwfkVia6srTkiLdyUede1~;QhSeC~_IbCXI~-.bps.a.123756449548237/565330108724200/ |access-date=12 September 2022}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=12 September 2022 |title=Hakim Ziyech makes return to World Cup team under Regragui's preselection |url=https://en.hespress.com/49144-hakim-ziyech-makes-return-to-word-cup-team-under-regraguis-selection.html |access-date=24 September 2022 |website=HESPRESS English - Morocco News |language=en-US}}</ref>

On 10 November 2022, he was named in Morocco's 23-man squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Qatar]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Morocco World Cup 2022 squad: Who's in and who's out? {{!}} Goal.com |url=https://www.goal.com/en-bh/lists/morocco-2022-world-cup-squad-hakimi-ziyech-mazraoui-qatar/blta36257fc4feeb66a |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=www.goal.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=10 November 2022 |title=Moroccan coach unveils list of 26 Atlas Lions in 2022 World Cup |url=https://en.hespress.com/52697-moroccan-coach-unveils-list-of-26-atlas-lions-in-2022-world-cup.html |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=HESPRESS English - Morocco News |language=en-US}}</ref> On 17 December, he scored his first international goal in the World Cup third-place playoff against [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]], which ended in a 2–1 defeat.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-12-17 |title=Croatia 2-1 Morocco: World Cup 2022 third-place playoff – as it happened |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2022/dec/17/croatia-morocco-world-cup-2022-third-place-playoff-live-score-updates |access-date=2022-12-17 |website=the Guardian |language=en}}</ref>
