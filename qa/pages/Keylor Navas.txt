'''Keylor Antonio Navas Gamboa''' (born 15 December 1986) is a  Costa Rican professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[Ligue 1]] club [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] and [[Captain (association football)|captains]] the [[Costa Rica national football team|Costa Rica national team]].

Navas started his youth career at [[Deportivo Saprissa|Saprissa]] in his home country, where he won six [[Liga FPD]] titles and the [[CONCACAF Champions League]]. He subsequently moved to Spanish club [[Albacete Balompié|Albacete]] in 2010, and then to [[Levante UD|Levante]] in [[La Liga]] the following year, where he won the LFP Award for Best Goalkeeper in his final season with the latter club. Navas later joined [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]] in 2014 for €10 million, where he won a total of twelve titles,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/football/squad/keylor-navas|title=Keylor Navas|publisher=Real Madrid CF|access-date=20 April 2019}}</ref> including one [[La Liga]] and three consecutive [[UEFA Champions League]] titles as the first choice goalkeeper.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/futbol/plantilla/keylor-navas#playerinfo|title=Keylor Navas: Player Info|publisher=realmadrid.com|access-date=6 February 2019}}</ref> In 2019, he was signed by French club Paris Saint-Germain.<ref>{{cite web |title=Madrid and PSG switch goalkeepers Navas and Areola |url=https://www.foxsports.com/stories/soccer/madrid-and-psg-switch-goalkeepers-navas-and-areola |access-date=18 March 2022 |website=Fox Sports}}</ref>

Navas has made over 100 appearances for Costa Rica since making his debut in 2008. He has represented the country at two [[CONCACAF Gold Cup]]s and the [[2014 FIFA World Cup|2014]], [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]], and [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022]] editions of the [[FIFA World Cup]]. He was a member of the Costa Rica national team that reached the semi-finals of the [[2009 CONCACAF Gold Cup]], in which he was named best goalkeeper. His impressive performances also helped the team reach the quarter-finals of the World Cup for the very first time in 2014.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/12040/9373172/world-cup-costa-rica-hero-hails-nations-efforts-as-holland-end-journey|work=[[Sky Sports]]|title=World Cup: Costa Rica hero hails nation's efforts as Holland end journey|date=6 July 2014|access-date=6 July 2014}}</ref>

Navas was awarded [[CONCACAF Awards|CONCACAF Men's Goalkeeper of the Year]] for three consecutive years between [[2016 CONCACAF Awards|2016]] and [[2018 CONCACAF Awards|2018]]. He was also named the best male [[CONCACAF]] Player of the Decade by the [[International Federation of Football History & Statistics|IFFHS]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.iffhs.com/posts/949|title=IFFHS BEST MAN PLAYER - CONCACAF - OF THE DECADE 2011-2020|publisher=IFFHS|access-date=27 March 2021}}</ref> His performances in the [[2017–18 UEFA Champions League|2017–18 season]] earned him the [[UEFA Club Football Awards|2017–18 UEFA Club Football Award]] for [[Best European Goalkeeper|best UEFA goalkeeper]],<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/newsid=2567659.html|title=Keylor Navas: Champions League Goalkeeper of the Season|publisher=UEFA|access-date=20 April 2019}}</ref> and also saw him named in the [[2017–18 UEFA Champions League#Squad of the season|UEFA Champions League Squad of the Season]] of 2018.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/newsid=2560100.html|title=UEFA Champions League Squad of the Season|publisher=UEFA|access-date=20 April 2019}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Saprissa===
Born in [[Pérez Zeledón (canton)|San Isidro de El General]], Navas made his professional debut with [[Deportivo Saprissa]] on 6 November 2005, in a [[Costa Rican Primera División|Primera league]] match against [[Asociación Deportiva Carmelita]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nacion.com/ln_ee/futbol/campeonato2005-2006/noviembre/07/sap_car.html |title=Juego apenas para el gasto |trans-title=I play only to make ends meet |publisher=La Nación |language=es |date=7 November 2005 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070813131145/http://www.nacion.com/ln_ee/futbol/campeonato2005-2006/noviembre/07/sap_car.html |archive-date=13 August 2007 }}</ref> He was the first-choice keeper in his two final seasons with the club,<ref>{{Cite web |title=¿En qué temporadas fue Keylor Navas titular en el Saprissa? |url=https://futbolcentroamerica.com/costarica/En-que-temporadas-fue-Keylor-Navas-titular-en-el-Saprissa-20220413-0005.html |access-date=13 April 2022 |website=Fútbol Centroamérica |language=es-ES}}</ref> winning six [[Liga FPD|national championships]] and the [[2005 CONCACAF Champions' Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/football/squad/keylor-navas#playerinfo|title=Keylor Navas: Player Info|publisher=realmadrid.com|access-date=6 February 2019}}</ref> Navas was also part of the Saprissa team that finished in 3rd place at the [[2005 FIFA Club World Championship]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fifa.com/clubworldcup/archive/japan2005/clubs/club=1896632/matches.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151025132222/http://www.fifa.com/clubworldcup/archive/japan2005/clubs/club=1896632/matches.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=25 October 2015|title=Fifa Club World Championship Toyota Cup Japan 2005 - Clubs - Saprissa|publisher=[[FIFA]]|access-date=6 February 2019}}</ref>

===Albacete===
In July 2010, Navas signed with [[Albacete Balompié]] of the [[Segunda División]] in [[Spain]] exactly 20 years after countryman [[Luis Gabelo Conejo|Luis Conejo]], who occupied the same position.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.albacetebalompie.es/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=447:navas-qvengo-con-mucho-compromisoq&catid=43:ultimas-noticias&Itemid=117|title=Navas: "Vengo con mucho compromiso"|trans-title=Navas: "I come full of commitment"|publisher=Albacete's official website|language=es|date=9 July 2010|access-date=27 October 2013}}</ref> He played in 36 games out of 42 during [[2010–11 Segunda División|his first season]], but his team suffered relegation after finishing in last position.

====2015–16====
[[File:Keylor Navas en el Real Madrid.JPG|thumb|right|Navas with Real Madrid in 2016]]
With the departure of Casillas from Real Madrid, Navas inherited his number 1 shirt for the [[2015–16 La Liga]] season.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/11/transfer-zone/2015/07/19/13720702/navas-takes-casillas-no1-jersey-at-madrid|title=Navas takes Casillas' No.1 jersey at Madrid|date=19 July 2015|access-date=20 July 2015|publisher=Goal.com}}</ref> Madrid agreed to transfer Navas to [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] in part-exchange for [[David de Gea]] on 31 August 2015, but the deal collapsed because the documents were not submitted to [[FIFA]] before the Spanish transfer deadline.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/34117354 |title=David De Gea: Real Madrid 'did everything' to sign Man Utd keeper |work=BBC Sport |publisher=BBC |date=1 September 2015 |access-date=1 September 2015}}</ref>

Madrid thrashed [[Real Betis]] in their home opener by a score of 5–0 with a fine performance from Navas making several saves as well as stopping a penalty.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/08/5-0-spectacular-display-of-firepower-in-the-bernabeus-la-liga-opener|title=5–0: Spectacular display of firepower in the Bernabéu's La Liga opener|publisher=realmadrid.com|date=30 August 2015|access-date=7 July 2018}}</ref> He was a regular starter when the team won the [[2015–16 UEFA Champions League]]. Navas earned the dual distinction of becoming the first Costa Rican and Central American to ever play in, and win, the men's Champions League final.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://ticotimes.net/2016/05/28/keylor-navas-makes-history-champions-league|title=Keylor Navas makes history as Real Madrid wins Champions League title|publisher=The Tico Times|date=28 May 2016|access-date=19 July 2018}}</ref> He set a new record for Real Madrid in the [[UEFA Champions League]] by not conceding a single goal in his first 8 appearances; 6 of these were in the 2015–16 season. He also usurped [[Edwin van der Sar]] to reach the second spot in clean sheets behind [[Jens Lehmann]] in the all-time list. He was the best goal keeper in the 2015–16 UEFA Champions League season conceding only 3 goals in 12 appearances, keeping 9 clean sheets.<ref name="2015–16 CL">{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2016/matches/round=2000638/match=2015789/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Spot-on Real Madrid defeat Atlético in final again|date=28 May 2016|work=uefa.com}}</ref>

====2016–17====
He was the starting goalkeeper when Madrid won the [[2016–17 La Liga]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.laliga.es/noticias/real-madrid-campeon-de-laliga-santander-2016-17|title=El Real Madrid, campeón de LaLiga Santander 2016/17|date=21 May 2017|work=laliga.es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/39926301|title=Real Madrid win La Liga title with victory at Malaga|date=21 May 2017|work=bbc.com}}</ref> and the [[2016–17 UEFA Champions League]].<ref name=Cardiff>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2017/matches/round=2000787/match=2019641/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Majestic Real Madrid win Champions League in Cardiff|date=3 June 2017|work=uefa.com}}</ref> On 29 January 2017 after the 3–0 win at home to Real Sociedad in La Liga, Navas became the first player from Costa Rica to reach 100 La Liga appearances.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/optajose/status/825804208523923458|title=100 – Keylor Navas is the first player from Costa Rica to reach 100 appearances in La Liga history. Tico.|publisher=OptaJose|date=29 January 2017|access-date=13 July 2018}}</ref> His performances during the double trophy winning season led him to win the Player of the Year for the second time at the [[2017 CONCACAF Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2017/12/keylor-navas-concacaf-player-of-the-year|title=Keylor Navas, Concacaf player of the year|publisher=Real Madrid|date=18 December 2017|access-date=7 July 2018}}</ref>

====2017–18====
Navas made his 100th appearance for Madrid during the [[2017 Supercopa de España|2–0]] second leg home win over [[FC Barcelona |Barcelona]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2017/08/keylor-navas-100-games-for-real-madrid|title=Keylor Navas, 100 games for Real Madrid|publisher=Real Madrid|date=16 August 2017|access-date=14 July 2018}}</ref> During the [[2017–18 UEFA Champions League]], he started eleven games, as Madrid won their third consecutive and 13th overall Champions League title beating [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] [[2018 UEFA Champions League Final|3–1]] in the final.<ref name=Kiev>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2018/matches/round=2000885/match=2021711/postmatch/report/index.html|title=Madrid beat Liverpool to complete hat-trick|date=26 May 2018|work=uefa.com}}</ref> The win against Liverpool saw Navas claim his 100th victory for Madrid in only 141 appearances.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://realmadrid.com/en/news/2018/05/keylor-navas-claims-100th-win-in-a-real-madrid-shirt|title=Keylor Navas claims 100th win in a Real Madrid shirt|publisher=Real Madrid|date=26 May 2018|access-date=13 July 2018}}</ref> Navas produced some fine performances in the Champions League including a superb display against [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] in their [[2017–18 UEFA Champions League knockout phase|semi-final]] second leg clash which helped Real Madrid qualify to the final. He made 8 saves during the game, his highest tally in a Champions League knock-out game.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/OptaJose/status/991418962419376129/photo/1|title=8 – Keylor Navas made eight saves against Bayern Munich, his highest tally in a Champions League knock-out game. Hero.|publisher=OptaJose|date=1 May 2018|access-date=19 July 2018}}</ref> Bayern Munich manager [[Jupp Heynckes]] praised Navas after the game as the reason for Real Madrid's qualification.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://marca.com/en/football/real-madrid/2018/05/01/5ae8dffe468aeb13348b4663.html|title=Heynckes: Real Madrid can thank Keylor for their qualification|publisher=Marca|date=1 May 2018|access-date=19 July 2018}}</ref>

====2018–19====
Navas’ fifth season at Real Madrid began with the much-anticipated arrival of [[Thibaut Courtois]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marca.com/en/football/real-madrid/2018/08/08/5b686c67ca474125568b4766.html|title=Official: Real Madrid sign Courtois|publisher=Marca|date=8 August 2018|access-date=5 September 2018}}</ref> to compete for the number 1 goalkeeping spot. ==International career==
===Youth career and early senior career===
Navas was part of the Costa Rica national team that played in the [[2003 FIFA U-17 World Championship]] held in [[Finland]]. He was called up for the [[Costa Rica national football team|senior national team]] for the first time in August 2006, to play a [[Exhibition game|friendly]] tournament in Europe against [[Austria national football team|Austria]] and [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]]. He was a member of the team that won a bronze medal at the [[Football at the 2006 Central American and Caribbean Games|2006 Central American and Caribbean Games]]. Navas gained his debut senior [[Cap (sport)|cap]] on 11 October 2008, in a 4–1 away victory over [[Suriname national football team|Suriname]] in [[2010 FIFA World Cup qualification – CONCACAF third round|the third round of qualification]] for the [[2010 FIFA World Cup]], which qualified the team into the next stage.<ref>{{cite news|title=Con el primer boleto|trans-title=With the first ticket|url=http://www.espn.cl/futbol/cronica?id=244641|access-date=23 August 2015|publisher=ESPN|date=11 October 2008|language=es}}</ref> Four days later at the [[Estadio Ricardo Saprissa Aymá|Estadio Ricardo Saprissa]], he kept his first international clean sheet in a 2–0 victory over [[Haiti national football team|Haiti]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Sigue enrachado|trans-title=Carry on being lucky|url=http://espndeportes.espn.go.com/futbol/cronica?id=244645|access-date=23 August 2015|publisher=ESPN|date=15 October 2008|language=es}}</ref> The team eventually reached the [[2010 FIFA World Cup qualification (inter-confederation play-offs)|intercontinental play-offs]] for the tournament, losing narrowly to [[Uruguay national football team|Uruguay]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Uruguay 1 Costa Rica 1, agg 2–1: match report|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/football/world-cup/6603766/Uruguay-1-Costa-Rica-1-agg-2-1-match-report.html |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/archive/20220112/https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/football/world-cup/6603766/Uruguay-1-Costa-Rica-1-agg-2-1-match-report.html |archive-date=12 January 2022 |url-access=subscription |url-status=live|access-date=23 August 2015|publisher=The Daily Telegraph|date=19 November 2009}}{{cbignore}}</ref> Navas was also a member of the Costa Rica squad that finished runners-up in the [[2009 UNCAF Nations Cup]].<ref name="2009 UNCAF Nations Cup"/>

Navas appeared with ''Los Ticos'' in two [[CONCACAF Gold Cup]] competitions, being named the best goalkeeper in the [[2009 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2009 edition]] as he helped his country reach the semi-finals.<ref name=best/> He returned to help them to the quarter-finals [[2011 CONCACAF Gold Cup|two years later]], in what is to date his last appearance at a Gold Cup, as he would miss the tournament for the following ten years, a period that encompasses five editions.<ref>{{cite news |title=Keylor Navas cumple diez años sin jugar en una Copa Oro |url=https://www.espn.co.cr/futbol/costa-rica/nota/_/id/8831593/keylor-navas-cumple-diez-anos-sin-jugar-copa-oro |access-date=29 November 2022 |work=ESPN.com.mx |date=26 June 2021 |language=es}}</ref> He also missed the [[Copa América Centenario]] in similar fashion.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Welle (www.dw.com) |first1=Deutsche |title=Confirman baja de Keylor Navas para la Copa América {{!}} DW {{!}} 31.05.2016 |url=https://www.dw.com/es/confirman-baja-de-keylor-navas-para-la-copa-am%C3%A9rica/a-19294796 |access-date=29 November 2022 |work=DW.COM |date=31 May 2016 |language=es-ES}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Often rated by several pundits as one of the best goalkeepers in the world,<ref name="iffhs1">{{cite news|url=https://iffhs.de/the-worlds-best-goalkeeper-2017-gianluigi-buffon-makes-it-five/|title=THE WORLD'S BEST GOALKEEPER 2017 : GIANLUIGI BUFFON MAKES IT FIVE !|publisher=IFFHS|access-date=20 April 2019}}</ref><ref name="iffhs2">{{cite news|url=https://iffhs.de/iffhs-awards-2018-the-worlds-best-goalkeeper-thibaut-courtois-still-crowned/|title=IFFHS AWARDS – THE WORLD'S BEST GOALKEEPER : THIBAUT COURTOIS STILL CROWNED !|publisher=IFFHS|access-date=20 April 2019}}</ref> and considered by some in the sport as the best in the history of CONCACAF,<ref name="diez">{{cite news|url=https://www.diez.hn/blogs/1077115-500/keylor_navas-concacaf-mejor-portero-historia|title=Keylor Navas es el mejor portero de la historia de CONCACAF|publisher=www.diez.hn|access-date=20 April 2019}}</ref> Navas is an energetic keeper, who is primarily known for his agility, speed, and athleticism in goal, as well as his quick reflexes and excellent shot-stopping ability, which enables him to compensate for his relative lack of height. He has also drawn praise in the media for his decisive performances in important matches, and his penchant for producing difficult and crucial saves for his team in key moments.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/real-madrid/2016/05/27/574863ae22601d850a8b45dc.html |title=Navas' agility vs Oblak's reliability |publisher=Marca |last1=Ortego |first1=Enrique |last2=Winterburn |first2=Chris |date=27 May 2016 |access-date=11 March 2020 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://bleacherreport.com/articles/2802528-the-evolution-of-the-goalkeeper#slide1 |title=The Evolution of the Goalkeeper: What Makes the Perfect Modern-Day No. 1? |publisher=Bleacher Report |last1=Tighe |first1=Sam |date=5 November 2018 |access-date=11 March 2020 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.thescore.com/chlg/news/1289056 |title=Perfect player series: Building a flawless goalkeeper |publisher=The Score |last1=Nesci |first1=Gianluca |date=24 July 2017 |access-date=11 March 2020 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://bleacherreport.com/articles/1955860-scouting-report-is-levantes-keylor-navas-the-best-goalkeeper-in-europe |title=Scouting Report: Is Levante's Keylor Navas the Best Goalkeeper in Europe? |publisher=Bleacher Report |last1=Jiang |first1=Allan |date=11 February 2014 |access-date=11 March 2020 }}</ref> In 2017, former goalkeeper [[Manuel Almunia]] praised Navas, describing him as "the prototype model" goalkeeper, also adding: "He's spectacular between the posts, he's brave, skillful and very agile. He maintains his concentration and he's also intelligent... For me he deserves all the respect he gets for what he's done."<ref name="Kepa is good with his feet">{{cite web |url=https://en.as.com/en/2017/12/13/football/1513169565_572541.html |title=Manuel Almunia: "Kepa is good with his feet, like Luis Arconada" |publisher=AS.com |last1=Ruiz |first1=Marco |date=13 December 2017 |access-date=6 May 2020 }}</ref>

In 2020, Navas cited compatriot [[Lester Morgan]] as his goalkeeping idol.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Gómez |first1=Keish |title=Keylor Navas recuerda nostálgico a su ídolo de la infancia, Lester Morgan |url=https://www.espn.co.cr/futbol/costa-rica/nota/_/id/6897559/keylor-navas-lester-morgan-idolo-infancia-futbol-costa-rica |access-date=30 April 2020 |work=ESPN Costa Rica |date=29 April 2020 |language=es}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Keylor Navas was raised in a poor family in [[San Isidro de El General]], a town in the southeast of [[San José Province|San José province]]. He is a devout [[Christianity|Christian]]. He spoke about his faith saying, "God for me comes first. Before every game I kneel, I open my arms and pray ... My favourite passage of the Bible is [[Epistle to the Galatians|Galatians]] 1:10 which says: "If I were still trying to please man, I would not be a servant of Christ."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.efe.com/efe/english/entertainment/real-madrid-goalie-keylor-navas-weds-in-costa-rica/50000264-2645262|title=Real Madrid goalie Keylor Navas weds in Costa Rica}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/jun/28/world-cup-2014-costa-rica-greece-keylor-navas-goalkeeper|title=World Cup 2014: Costa Rica's Keylor Navas is the man Greece must beat|last=Lowe|first=Sid|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=28 June 2014|access-date=29 June 2014}}</ref> On 3 December 2014,  Navas announced that he had obtained Spanish citizenship.<ref>{{cite web|title=Keylor Navas now has Spanish citizenship|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2014/12/keylor-navas-now-has-spanish-citizenship|publisher=Real Madrid|access-date=3 December 2014|date=3 December 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Keylor Navas obtains Spanish nationality|url=http://www.marca.com/en/2014/12/03/en/football/real_madrid/1417633827.html|access-date=3 December 2014|work=Marca|date=3 December 2014}}</ref>

A documentary film, ''Hombre de Fe'' (Man of Faith), based on the life of Navas was released on 28 December 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://qcostarica.com/hombre-de-fe-the-story-of-keylor-navas/|title=Hombre De Fé, The Story Of Keylor Navas|trans-title=Man of Faith, The Story of Keylor Navas|publisher=QCostaRica|language=es|date=28 December 2017|access-date=7 July 2018}}</ref>

His paternal half-brother, Eder Navas, is also a footballer who plays for Saprissa.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Gómez |first1=Keish |title=Keylor revela que su hermano Eder Navas formará parte del Saprissa |url=https://www.espn.co.cr/futbol/costa-rica/nota/_/id/9779438/keylor-navas-emotivo-mensaje-bienvenida-eder-saprissa |access-date=11 April 2022 |work=ESPN.com.mx |date=13 January 2022 |language=es}}</ref>

In 2022, Navas obtained his academic degree as a master in [[Sport management|Sport Management]] from [[Johan Cruyff Institute]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=archysport |date=9 October 2022 |title=Keylor Navas is already preparing for life after being a professional footballer |url=https://www.archysport.com/2022/10/keylor-navas-is-already-preparing-for-life-after-being-a-professional-footballer/ |access-date=11 October 2022 |website=Archysport |language=en-US}}</ref>
