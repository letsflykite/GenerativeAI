'''Jonas Omlin''' (born 10 January 1994) is a Swiss professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for {{German football updater|BoMönche}} club [[Borussia Mönchengladbach]] and the [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland national team]].<ref name="SW">{{soccerway|jonas-omlin/349933|access-date=26 March 2017}}</ref>

==Club career==
Omlin played for [[SC Kriens|Kriens]] in the 1. Liga Promotion. He moved on to [[FC Luzern|Luzern]], but was loaned out to [[FC Le Mont|Le Mont-sur-Lausanne]] for the 2015/16 season. He returned to Luzern for two seasons.

[[FC Basel]] announced on 22 June 2018 that they had signed goalkeeper Omlin from Luzern.<ref name="fcb 2018-06-12">{{cite web | first =  | last =  | author-link = FC Basel 1893 | year = 2018 | url = https://www.fcb.ch/de-CH/News/2018/06/12/Der-FC-Basel-1893-verpflichtet-Jonas-Omlin | title = Der FC Basel 1893 verpflichtet Jonas Omlin | format =  | publisher = FC Basel 1893 | access-date = 12 June 2018 | archive-date = 12 June 2018 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20180612210518/https://www.fcb.ch/de-CH/News/2018/06/12/Der-FC-Basel-1893-verpflichtet-Jonas-Omlin | url-status = dead }}</ref> After five test games Omlin played his domestic league debut for his new club in the home game at the [[St. Jakob-Park]] on 21 July 2018 as Basel were defeated 2–1 by [[FC St. Gallen|St. Gallen]].<ref name="fcb-achiv-fcb-sg-2018/19">{{cite web | last = Verein "Basler Fussballarchiv” | year = | url = https://www.fcb-archiv.ch/saison/spiele?command=detail&id=31311 | title = FC Basel - FC St. Gallen 1:2 (0:0) | format = | publisher = Verein "Basler Fussballarchiv” | access-date = 21 July 2018}}</ref>

Under trainer [[Marcel Koller]] Basel won the [[2018–19 Swiss Cup|Swiss Cup in the 2018–19 season]]. In the first round Basel beat FC Montlingen 3–0, in the second round [[FC Echallens|Echallens Région]] 7–2 and in the round of 16 [[FC Winterthur|Winterthur]] 1–0. In the quarter-finals [[FC Sion|Sion]] were defeated 4–2 after extra time and in the semi-finals [[FC Zürich|Zürich]] were defeated 3–1. All these games were played away from home. The final was held on 19 May 2019 in the [[Stade de Suisse|Stade de Suisse Wankdorf Bern]] against [[FC Thun|Thun]]. Striker [[Albian Ajeti]] scored the first goal, [[Fabian Frei]] the second for Basel, then [[Dejan Sorgić]] netted a goal for Thun, but the end result was 2–1 for Basel.<ref name="fcb-achiv-fcb-thun-cupfinal-2018–19">{{cite web | first = | last = | year = | url = https://www.fcb-archiv.ch/saison/spiele?command=detail&id=31396 | title = FC Basel - FC Thun 2:1 (1:0) | format = | publisher = Verein "Basler Fussballarchiv” | access-date = 16 November 2019}}</ref> Omlin played in four cup games. On 12 August 2020, the club announced that Omlin had transferred to [[Montpellier HSC|Montpellier]] as of straight away.<ref name="fcb-homepage-2020-08-12-Jonas Omlin">{{cite web | last = FC Basel 1893 | year = 2020 | url = https://www.fcb.ch/de-CH/News/2020/08/12/Jonas-Omlin-wechselt-per-sofort-zum-Montpellier-HS | title = Jonas Omlin wechselt per sofort zum Montpellier HS | format = | publisher = FC Basel homepage| access-date = 12 August 2020}}</ref> In his two seasons with the club Omlin played a total of 92 games for Basel. 59 of these games were in the [[Swiss Super League|Nationalliga A]], five in the [[Swiss Cup]], six in the [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]], nine in the [[UEFA Europa League]] and 13 were friendly games.<ref name="fcb-achiv-Jonas Omlin">{{cite web | last = Verein "Basler Fussballarchiv” | year = | url = https://www.fcb-archiv.ch/spieler?command=detail&id=8663 | title = Jonas Omlin - FCB-Statistik | format = | publisher = Verein "Basler Fussballarchiv” | access-date = 16 November 2019}}</ref>

On 12 August 2020, Omlin signed for [[Montpellier HSC|Montpellier]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Jonas Omlin nouveau gardien du MHSC|url=https://www.mhscfoot.com/articles/2020-2021/equipe-pro/jonas-omlin-nouveau-gardien-du-mhsc|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=15 August 2020|publisher=Montpellier HSC|language=fr}}</ref>

On 19 January 2023, Omlin signed for [[Bundesliga]] club [[Borussia Mönchengladbach]] on a four-and-a-half year deal.<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.borussia.de/en/news/news/news-detailpage/2023-01-19-borussia-sign-jonas-omlin-from-montpellier|title=BORUSSIA SIGN JONAS OMLIN FROM MONTPELLIER|website=www.borussia.de/en|date=19 January 2023|accessdate=19 January 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
In May 2019, he was named to the national team for the [[2018–19 UEFA Nations League]] final matches but did not appear in the tournament.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/48191915|title=Pickford the hero in England shootout win|work=BBC Sport }}</ref>

He made his [[Switzerland national football team|national team]] debut on 7 October 2020 in a friendly against [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[ESPN]]|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/match?gameId=573691|title=Switzerland v Croatia game report|date=7 October 2020}}</ref>

===Euro 2020===
Originally called up to the Swiss squad for the [[Euro 2020]], Omlin suffered a minor injury to his right ankle during the warm-up of the opening game. The [[Swiss Football Association]] called up [[Gregor Kobel]] to replace his position.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[Record (newspaper)|Record]]|url=https://www.record.pt/internacional/competicoes-de-selecoes/europeu/euro-2020/suica/detalhe/gregor-kobel-substitui-lesiona-jonas-omlin-na-suica|date=13 June 2021|title=Gregor Kobel substitui lesionado Jonas Omlin na Suíça}}</ref>
