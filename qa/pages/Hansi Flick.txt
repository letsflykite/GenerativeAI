'''Hans-Dieter''' "'''Hansi'''" '''Flick''' (born 24 February 1965) is a German professional [[Association football|football]] [[Manager (association football)|manager]] and former [[Football player|player]] who is the current head coach of the [[Germany national football team|Germany national team]]. From August 2006 to July 2014, he was the assistant coach of Germany under manager [[Joachim Löw]]. In August 2020, Flick won the [[2019–20 UEFA Champions League|UEFA Champions League]] as the manager of [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]], completing the club's second [[Treble (association football)|continental treble]]. In 2021, he also led the side to a [[2020 FIFA Club World Cup|FIFA Club World Cup]] and another [[Bundesliga]] title.<ref name="Germany head coach">{{cite news |url=https://www.dfb.de/news/detail/hansi-flick-wird-neuer-bundestrainer-226926/ |title=Hansi Flick wird neuer Bundestrainer |trans-title=Hansi Flick is the new national coach |work=[[German Football Association]] |date=25 May 2021 |access-date=25 May 2021 |language=de}}</ref> Alongside [[Pep Guardiola]], he is one of the only two managers to achieve a [[Sextuple (football)|sextuple]] with their team. He took charge of the Germany national team in 2021, replacing Löw.

==Playing career==
As a player, he was a midfielder who played 104 matches for [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] and scored five goals between 1985 and 1990,<ref name=":1">{{cite web |url=https://www.kicker.de/hans-dieter-flick-10757/spieler/1-bundesliga/2010-11/1-fc-koeln-16 |title=Flick, Hans-Dieter |website=kicker.de |publisher=[[Kicker (sports magazine)|kicker]] |location=Nuremberg |language=de |access-date=26 May 2011}}</ref> where he won four [[Bundesliga]] titles as well as one [[DFB-Pokal]] title, and played in the [[1987 European Cup Final]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/hansi-flick-10-things-on-bayern-munich-s-record-breaking-title-winning-coach-11663 |title=Hansi Flick: 10 things on Bayern Munich's record-breaking, treble-winning coach |website=Bundesliga |date=August 2020 }}</ref> He later played 44 matches for [[1. FC Köln|Köln]] before retiring from professional football in 1993 due to injuries. His last spell as a footballer was with [[Victoria Bammental]] from 1994 until 2000.{{citation needed|date=June 2021}}

He never played for the [[Germany national football team]], but he made two appearances for the [[Germany national youth football team|Germany under-18 team]], in the [[1983 UEFA European Under-18 Championship#Group A|group stage of the 1983 UEFA European Under-18 Championship]] on 15 and 17 May 1983, in a 1–0 win over [[Sweden national football team|Sweden]] and in a 3–1 win over [[Bulgaria national football team|Bulgaria]], respectively.{{citation needed|date=June 2021}}

==Managerial career==
Flick's managerial career began in 1996 as a [[Player-coach|player-manager]] of Viktoria Bammental, which was playing in the [[Oberliga Baden-Württemberg]] at that time. At the end of the 1998–99 season, the club was relegated to the [[Verbandsliga Baden]], but Flick remained their coach for one more season.{{citation needed|date=June 2021}}

===1999 Hoffenheim===
In July 2000, he became a manager of the Oberliga Baden-Württemberg side [[TSG 1899 Hoffenheim|1899 Hoffenheim]], winning the league and gaining promotion to the [[Regionalliga Süd (1994–2012)|Regionalliga Süd]] in his first season at the club. After four unsuccessful attempts to reach the [[2. Bundesliga]], he was released from duties on 19 November 2005.<ref name="DFB">{{cite web |url=https://www.dfb.de/news/detail/flick-als-co-trainer-der-dfb-auswahl-vorgestellt-koepke-verlaengert-bis-2008-8418/ |title=Flick als Co-Trainer der DFB-Auswahl vorgestellt – Köpke verlängert bis 2008 |date=24 August 2006 |website=dfb.de |publisher=[[German Football Association|DFB]] |location=Frankfurt |language=de |trans-title=Flick introduced as assistant coach of the DFB national team – Köpke extends until 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191106210740/https://www.dfb.de/news/detail/flick-als-co-trainer-der-dfb-auswahl-vorgestellt-koepke-verlaengert-bis-2008-8418/ |archive-date=6 November 2019 |url-status=live |access-date=6 November 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.spiegel.de/sport/fussball/retortenclub-dfb-genehmigt-hoffenheimer-fussballfusion-a-387770.html |title=Retortenclub: DFB genehmigt Hoffenheimer Fußballfusion |date=30 November 2005 |newspaper=[[Der Spiegel]] |location=Hamburg |language=de |trans-title=Retort Club: DFB approved Hoffenheim football fusion |access-date=5 November 2019}}</ref>

===Red Bull Salzburg (assistant)===
Flick then worked briefly as an assistant of [[Giovanni Trapattoni]] and [[Lothar Matthäus]] and sporting coordinator at [[FC Red Bull Salzburg|Red Bull Salzburg]].<ref name="DFB"/> Flick stated that his work under Trapattoni, one of the world's most renowned managers, taught him many things, especially on tactics and in developing relations with players, but also said that he disagreed with Trapattoni's defence-first approach.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.rund-magazin.de/index.php?mact=News,cntnt01,print,0&cntnt01articleid=430&cntnt01showtemplate=false&cntnt01returnid=76 |title=Interview Hansi Flick |website=Rund-Magazin.de |language=de |date=January 2014 | access-date = 20 November 2019}}</ref>

===Germany (assistant)===
[[File:Löw-Flick.jpg|thumb|Flick (right) with [[Joachim Löw]] in 2006]]

He was named the assistant coach for Germany on 23 August 2006. Although not listed as an officially recognized manager by the [[German Football Association|DFB]], due to the sending off of [[Joachim Löw]] in the previous game, Flick was technically the German manager for the [[UEFA Euro 2008]] quarter final against [[Portugal national football team|Portugal]] on 19 June 2008, which ended in a 3–2 win for Germany. After finishing second at the UEFA Euro 2008 and third at the [[2010 FIFA World Cup]], he reached the semi-finals at the [[UEFA Euro 2012]] and won the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]] as assistant coach of Germany. He became sporting director at the German Football Association after the 2014 World Cup until 16 January 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.dfb.de/news/detail/dfb-stimmt-wunsch-von-flick-nach-vertragsaufloesung-zu-160600/ |title=DFB stimmt Wunsch von Flick nach Vertragsauflösung zu |date=16 January 2017 |website=dfb.de |publisher=[[German Football Association|DFB]] |location=Frankfurt |language=de |trans-title=DFB agrees to Flick's request for contract termination |access-date=16 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.football.co.uk/germany/hansdieter-flick-steps-down-as-german-football-association/8241896/#GoOAcB4kwPI2HR5H.97 |title=Hans-Dieter Flick steps down as German Football Association sporting director |website=football.co.uk |date=16 January 2017 | access-date = 7 February 2017}}</ref>

===Bayern Munich===
On 1 July 2019, he joined Bayern Munich as an assistant coach, under the management of [[Niko Kovač]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2019/07/interview-with-hansi-flick |title=Hansi Flick interviewed: 'A top job and a club that's very close to my heart' |date=7 July 2019 |website=fcbayern.com |publisher=[[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] |access-date=19 August 2019}}</ref> When Kovač left Bayern by mutual consent on 3 November 2019, Flick was promoted to the interim manager position.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2019/11/fc-bayern-relieve-head-coach-niko-kovac-of-his-duties |title=FC Bayern and head coach Niko Kovac part company |date=3 November 2019 |website=fcbayern.com |publisher=[[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] |access-date=3 November 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.kicker.de/761680/artikel/bayern_muenchen_trennt_sich_von_trainer_kovac |title=Bayern München trennt sich von Trainer Kovac |date=3 November 2019 |website=kicker.de |publisher=[[Kicker (sports magazine)|kicker]] |location=Nuremberg |language=de |trans-title=Bayern Munich separates from coach Kovac |access-date=3 November 2019}}</ref> In his first match in charge, Bayern defeated [[Olympiacos F.C.|Olympiacos]] 2–0 in the [[2019–20 UEFA Champions League group stage#Group B|UEFA Champions League group stage]] on 6 November 2019.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/bayern-munich-olympiacos-champions-league-live-line-ups-stats-lewandowski-7935 |title=Robert Lewandowski on target again as Bayern Munich down Olympiacos to reach UEFA Champions League last 16 |website=bundesliga.com |date=6 November 2019 | access-date=6 November 2019}}</ref> After a satisfying spell as interim coach, Bayern announced on 22 December 2019 that Flick would remain manager until the end of season.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2019/12/hansi-flick-to-remain-fc-bayern-head-coach-until-end-of-season |title=Flick to remain FC Bayern head coach until end of season |date=22 December 2019 |website=fcbayern.com |publisher=[[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] |access-date=26 December 2019}}</ref>

In April 2020, Bayern Munich gave Flick a new contract lasting until 2023.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/soccer/2020/04/03/bayern-gives-coach-hansi-flick-permanent-deal-through-2023/111519252/ |title=Bayern gives coach Hansi Flick permanent deal through 2023 |website=USA Today |date=3 April 2020}}</ref>

During the [[2019–20 FC Bayern Munich season|2019–20 season]], Flick successfully guided Bayern to win the [[2019–20 Bundesliga|Bundesliga]], [[2019–20 DFB-Pokal|DFB-Pokal]] and [[2019–20 UEFA Champions League|UEFA Champions League]], thus completing the continental [[Treble (association football)|treble]] for the second time in the club's history.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/53867676 |title=Bayern beat PSG to win Champions League |website=BBC Sport |date=23 August 2020}}</ref> He was subsequently named [[German Football Manager of the Year]] by sports magazine ''[[Kicker (sports magazine)|kicker]]'',<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |date=30 August 2020 |title=Flick löst Klopp als Trainer des Jahres ab |url=https://www.kicker.de/783112/artikel/flick_loest_klopp_als_trainer_des_jahres_ab |website=kicker.de |language=de}}</ref> and also won the [[UEFA Men's Coach of the Year Award]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Men's Coach of the Year: Flick, Klopp or Nagelsmann |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/0261-107135de7c40-67dde23c4c0f-1000--men-s-coach-of-the-year/ |publisher=UEFA |date=23 September 2020 |access-date=23 September 2020}}</ref> The following season, he led Bayern to win the [[2020 UEFA Super Cup]] against [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]].<ref name="USC">{{cite news |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefasupercup/match/2029141/postmatch/report/ |title=Bayern win Super Cup: Javi Martínez heads extra-time winner against Sevilla |publisher=UEFA |date=24 September 2020 |access-date=24 September 2020}}</ref> He also led Bayern to win its first ever sextuple after winning [[2020 FIFA Club World Cup|Club World Cup]] in February 2021 by defeating Mexican team [[Tigres UANL|Tigres]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Bayern Munich win Club World Cup for sixth trophy in a year|url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2021/2/11/bayern-munich-win-club-world-cup-for-sixth-trophy-of-season|access-date=11 February 2021|publisher=Al Jazeera|language=en}}</ref>

On 17 April 2021, Flick announced that he had told the club he wanted to leave at the end of the season. He voiced his desire to coach the [[Germany national football team|Germany national team]], given his previous job as an assistant to present German team manager, [[Joachim Löw]]. Flick left Bayern with one of the greatest winning records in modern football history. During his tenure, Bayern lost just seven games and won seven trophies ([[Bundesliga]] twice, [[DFB-Pokal]], [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]], [[DFL-Supercup]], [[UEFA Super Cup|UEFA Supercup]], [[FIFA Club World Cup|Club World Cup]]). Bayern went undefeated in the [[2019–20 UEFA Champions League|2019–20 Champions League]], the first team in European/Champions League history to lift the trophy with a 100 percent win record, and won 23 matches in a row across all competitions between 16 February 2020 and 18 September 2020, a record in German professional football. Flick also coached Bayern to a [[Treble (association football)|treble]], the second treble in Bayern's history. Flick held one of the highest win rates in football history, winning 83% of his games and helped Bayern average 3.0 goals per game across all competitions.<ref name="sportsmax.tv">{{Cite web | title=Flick leaving Bayern: A record win rate and a trophy every 14 games – the key numbers behind departing coach's tenure|url=https://www.sportsmax.tv/index.php/football/international/item/80730-flick-leaving-bayern-a-record-win-rate-and-a-trophy-every-14-games-the-key-numbers-behind-departing-coach-s-tenure | date = 17 May 2021 | access-date = 23 May 2021 | website = sportsmax.tv}}</ref> In October 2020, Flick won [[UEFA Men's Coach of the Year Award|Europe's Coach of the Year]], an award for the best coach in football in the [[Big Five (association football)|major football leagues of Europe]].<ref name="sportsmax.tv"/>

===Germany===
[[File:2022-07-30 Fußball, Männer, DFL-Supercup, RB Leipzig - FC Bayern München 1DX 3165 by Stepro.jpg|thumb|Flick in 2022]]
On 25 May 2021, the [[German Football Association]] announced that Flick signed a three-year contract from 1 August 2021 to serve as the manager of the [[Germany national football team|Germany national team]], and he replaced his former boss [[Joachim Löw]] after [[UEFA Euro 2020]]. On 2 September 2021, Flick won his first match 2–0 against [[Liechtenstein national football team|Liechtenstein]] in a [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)|2022 FIFA World Cup qualification]] match.<ref name="Germany head coach"/> On 4 June 2022, Germany drew 1–1 with [[Italy national football team|Italy]] in the [[2022–23 UEFA Nations League A]]; hence, he became the third coach to stay undefeated in his first ten matches, after [[Sepp Herberger]] and [[Josef Derwall]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.eurosport.com/football/uefa-nations-league/2022-2023/italy-1-1-germany-joshua-kimmich-cancels-out-lorenzo-pellegrini-goal-as-hosts-held-in-nations-league_sto8971849/story.shtml |title=Italy 1-1 Germany: Experimental Azzurri side held by Hansi Flick's unbeaten Germany in Nations League opener |website=Eurosport |date=4 June 2022 }}</ref> This run would end on 23 September, as Germany lost 1–0 to Hungary in the same competition.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Hungary beat Germany to near Nations League finals |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63012525 |access-date=25 September 2022}}</ref>

In the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], Germany was eliminated in the group stage for the second time in a row, as they finished third in their group, despite winning their final match 2–4 against Costa Rica.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.dw.com/en/germany-eliminated-from-second-consecutive-world-cup-in-group-stages/a-63956794 |title=Germany out of World Cup at group stage |website=DW |date=1 December 2022 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.thenationalnews.com/fifa-world-cup-2022/2022/12/01/germany-crash-out-of-world-cup-despite-victory-over-costa-rica/ |title=Germany crash out of World Cup despite victory over Costa Rica |website=The National News |date=1 December 2022 }}</ref> Flick received criticism for his substitutions, particularly against Japan in their tournament opener, which they lost 1–2.<ref>{{Cite web |last=zippy86 |date=2022-11-24 |title=Hansi Flick defends his Germany substitutions in loss to Japan |url=https://www.bavarianfootballworks.com/2022/11/24/23474779/germany-japan-fifa-world-cup-loss-substitutions-hansi-flick-thomas-muller-musiala-fullkrug-hofmann |access-date=2023-01-18 |website=Bavarian Football Works |language=en}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Flick is married to Silke Flick. As of 2020, they have been married for over 30 years. They have two children and two grandchildren.<ref>{{cite web |title=Hansi Flick: Privatleben und Karriere – alle Infos zum Bayern-Trainer |url=https://www.heidelberg24.de/sport/tsg-1899-hoffenheim/hansi-flick-fc-bayern-kinder-tochter-bammental-wohnort-tsg-hoffenheim-karriere-fussball-13599122.amp.html |access-date=23 August 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Hansi Flick: 5 things on Bayern Munich's new coach |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/hansi-flick-5-things-on-bayern-munich-assistant-coach-germany-niko-kovac-5473 |access-date=24 August 2020}}</ref>
