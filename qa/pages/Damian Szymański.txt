'''Damian Dawid Szymański''' (born 16 June 1995) is a Polish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[Super League Greece|Greek Super League]] club [[AEK Athens F.C.|AEK Athens]] and the [[Poland national football team|Poland national team]].

==Club career==

===Early club career===

Szymański started his career with [[GKS Bełchatów]].<ref>[https://weszlo.com/2018/02/22/talent-poparty-ciezka-praca-przede-wszystkim-ciezka-praca/ Talent poparty ciężką pracą? Przede wszystkim ciężka praca] weszlo.com</ref>

===Akhmat Grozny===
On 12 January 2019, [[Wisła Płock]] announced his transfer to the [[Russian Premier League]] club [[FC Akhmat Grozny]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[Wisła Płock]]|url=http://wisla-plock.pl/pl/kolejny-wielki-transfer-szymanski-w-achmacie-grozny11-3114|title=Kolejny wielki transfer! Szymański w Achmacie Grozny|trans-title=Another big transfer! Szymański in Akhmat Grozny|date=12 January 2019|accessdate=13 January 2019|language=Russian}}</ref> Three days later Akhmat confirmed that the club signed a 4.5-year contract with Szymański.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=FC Akhmat Grozny|url=http://fc-akhmat.ru/page/10230|title=Магомед Даудов: "Дамиан Шиманьски – первый новичок нашего клуба в 2019 году"|trans-title=Magomed Daudov: Damian Szymański is our first new player in 2019|language=Russian|date=15 January 2019}}</ref>

===AEK Athens===
On 13 January 2020, Szymański travelled to [[Athens]] to pass his medicals and complete a move to [[AEK Athens F.C.|AEK Athens]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sport24.gr/football/omades/Aek/aek-efere-simanski.5669133.html |title=ΑΕΚ: Έφερε Σιμάνσκι! |publisher=www.sport24.gr |date=13 January 2020}}</ref> The Polish midfielder will join the Greek club on an initial six-month loan, and AEK will maintain the right to buy him for a fee of €1,500,000.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sport24.gr/football/omades/Aek/aek-simanski-me-1-5-ekat-eyrw.5669172.html |title=ΑΕΚ: Σιμάνσκι με 1,5 εκατ. ευρώ |publisher=www.sport24.gr |date=13 January 2020}}</ref> One day later, the deal was made official and Szymański was presented by his new club.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sport24.gr/football/omades/Aek/aek-anakoinwse-ton-simanski.5669583.html |title=ΑΕΚ: Ανακοίνωσε τον Σιμάνσκι |publisher=www.sport24.gr |date=14 January 2020}}</ref>

On 30 June 2020, Szymański penned a four-year deal with AEK after his transfer from Akhmat Grozny was made permanent.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.aekfc.gr/newsdetails/paiktis-tis-aek-o-ntamian-simanski-124633.htm?lang=en&path=-1597697767 |title=AEK FC signed Damian Szymanski on a four-year deal! |publisher=www.aekfc.gr |date=30 June 2020}}</ref> The Greek club paid €1,300,000 for his services, while Akhmat will keep a resale rate of 15%.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sport24.gr/football/omades/Aek/aek-anakoinwse-ton-simanski-mexri-to-2024.5726905.html |title=ΑΕΚ: Ανακοίνωσε τον Σιμάνσκι μέχρι το 2024 |publisher=www.sport24.gr |date=30 June 2020}}</ref> Five days later, Szymanski scored his first goal for the club, sealing an emphatic 4–1 away win against [[Aris Thessaloniki F.C.|Aris Thessaloniki]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sport24.gr/football/ellada/SuperLeague/arhs-aek-1-4-foyl-gia-sentoni-h-enwsh.5728997.html |title=Άρης - ΑΕΚ 1-4: Φουλ για σεντόνι η Ένωση |publisher=www.sport24.gr |date=5 July 2020}}</ref>

On 20 January 2021, he scored helping to a 2–0 home win against [[Apollon Smyrnis F.C.|Apollon Smyrnis]] for the first leg of the [[2020–21 Greek Football Cup|Greek Cup round of 16]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sport24.gr/football/aek-apollon-2-0-petaxe-pros-ta-proimitelika.9117442.html |title=ΑΕΚ - Απόλλων 2-0: "Πέταξε" προς τα προημιτελικά |publisher=www.sport24.gr |date=20 January 2021}}</ref> On 15 February 2021, he sealed a dramatic 4–2 away win against [[Athlitiki Enosi Larissa F.C.|AEL]], after a low cross from [[Levi García]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sport24.gr/football/ael-aek-2-4-o-apo-michanis-athanasiadis-tis-edose-to-triponto-se-mats-thriler.9144422.html |title=ΑΕΛ - ΑΕΚ 2-4: Ο από μηχανής Αθανασιάδης της έδωσε το τρίποντο σε ματς-θρίλερ |publisher=www.sport24.gr |date=15 February 2021}}</ref> The following week, Szymański scored a brace in a 2–2 home draw against [[Asteras Tripolis F.C.|Asteras Tripolis]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sport24.gr/football/aek-asteras-2-2-o-simanski-tin-esose-i-atychia-tin-stamatise.9149484.html |title=ΑΕΚ - Αστέρας 2-2: Ο Σιμάνσκι την έσωσε, η ατυχία την σταμάτησε |publisher=www.sport24.gr |date=20 February 2021}}</ref>
On 5 December 2021, he was the only scorer in a vital 1–0 home win against rivals [[Panathinaikos F.C.|Panathinaikos]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.gazzetta.gr/football/superleague/2067917/aek-panathinaikos-1-0-vids |title=ΑΕΚ - Παναθηναϊκός 1-0 |publisher=www.gazzetta.gr |date=5 December 2021}}</ref>

At the start of the [[2022–23 AEK Athens F.C. season|2022–23 season]], the appointment of [[Matías Almeyda]] as the club's new coach helped Damian to revive his career, eventually receiving a call-up for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]]. The Argentine coach managed to harness his athleticism and passion, making him a significant member of the team, thus attracting interest from various clubs including [[Standard Liège]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sport24.gr/football/aek-i-poliorkia-gia-toys-paiktes-tis-enosis-molis-archise.9918487.html |title=ΑΕΚ: Η πολιορκία για τους παίκτες της Ένωσης μόλις άρχισε |publisher=www.sport24.gr |date=27 January 2023}}</ref>

On 20 April 2023, Damian signed a new contract, running until the summer of 2027. <ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sport24.gr/football/aek-ananeose-eos-to-2027-o-simanski.10013908.html |title=ΑΕΚ: Ανανέωσε έως το 2027 ο Σιμάνσκι |publisher=www.sport24.gr |date=20 April 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
Szymański made his [[Poland national football team|Poland national team]] debut on 7 September 2018 in a [[2018–19 UEFA Nations League A]] game against [[Italy national football team|Italy]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[UEFA]]|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefanationsleague/season=2019/matches/round=2000959/match=2024019/index.html|title=Italy v Poland game report|date=7 September 2018}}</ref> He made his first start for Poland in the return game against Italy on 14 October.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[UEFA]]|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefanationsleague/season=2019/matches/round=2000959/match=2024023/index.html|title=Poland v Italy game report|date=14 October 2018}}</ref> He scored his first goal, an equaliser in the injury time of the second half, on 8 September 2021 in a [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group I|2022 FIFA World Cup qualification]] match against England held at the [[Stadion Narodowy|National Stadium]] in Warsaw, which ended in a 1–1 draw.<ref>{{cite news| url = https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/58492134 |title=Poland 1-1 England: Painful final minutes for England but they edge closer to Qatar 2022 |work=BBC Sport | access-date = 9 September 2021}}</ref>
