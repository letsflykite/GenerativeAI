'''Andreas Bødtker Christensen ''' (born 10 April 1996) is a Danish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[centre-back]] for [[La Liga]] club [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] and the [[Denmark national football team|Denmark national team]].

Christensen began his career at [[IF Skjold Birkerød|Skjold Birkerød]] and later joined [[Brøndby IF|Brøndby]]. He joined Chelsea at the age of 15 in February 2012, making his professional debut in October 2014. After a two-year loan to German club [[Borussia Mönchengladbach]], he returned to Chelsea in 2017, winning the [[2017–18 FA Cup|FA Cup]], [[2018–19 UEFA Europa League|UEFA Europa League]] and [[2020–21 UEFA Champions League|UEFA Champions League]] with the club. In 2022, he was signed by Barcelona, where he won [[2022–23 La Liga|La Liga]] in his debut season.

Christensen made his full international debut for Denmark in June 2015, and represented the nation at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]], [[UEFA Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]].

==Club career==
===Chelsea===
====Early career====
[[File:Andreas Christensen.jpg|thumb|upright|Christensen playing for [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] in 2013]]

Christensen was born in [[Lillerød]], Allerød Municipality.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/andreas-christensen-chelseas-thriving-loanee-whos-attracting-bayern-and-barca |title=Andreas Christensen: Chelsea's thriving loanee who's attracting Bayern and Barça |first=Kristan |last=Heneage |website=FourFourTwo |date=21 April 2016 |access-date=26 August 2018}}</ref> The son of former [[Brøndby IF|Brøndby]] goalkeeper [[Sten Christensen]], he began his career with [[IF Skjold Birkerød|Skjold Birkerød]] and later joined Brøndby. He spent eight years there, attracting the interest of Europe's elite clubs including [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]], [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]], [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]], and [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]]. On 7 February 2012, Christensen signed for Chelsea on a free transfer, near the end of [[André Villas-Boas]]' tenure as the club's manager. Upon joining the London side, Christensen said: "I have chosen Chelsea because they play the kind of football I like.”<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/2896/premier-league/2012/02/07/2892141/chelsea-complete-signing-of-15-year-old-brondby-defender-andreas- |title=Chelsea complete signing of 15-year-old Brondby defender Andreas Christensen on a free transfer |date=7 February 2012 |website=Goal |access-date=7 February 2012}}</ref>

Christensen was first included in a Chelsea senior squad for their last game of the [[2012–13 Chelsea F.C. season|2012–13 season]] on 19 May 2013, but did not feature in the match, which ended a 2–1 home win over [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] in [[Rafael Benítez]]'s last match as manager.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Phillips |first1=Owen |title=Chelsea 2–1 Everton |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/22499152|access-date=28 October 2014 |website=BBC Sport |date=15 May 2013}}</ref> In the pre-season tour of the United States before the [[2013–14 Chelsea F.C. season|2013–14 season]], he was part of the senior team squad and signed a professional contract thereafter.<ref name=chelsea>{{cite web |url=http://www.chelseafc.com/teams/first-team/andreas-christensen.html |title=Christensen Chelsea Profile |date=7 February 2012 |publisher=Chelsea F.C. |access-date=7 February 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140815081559/http://www.chelseafc.com/teams/first-team/andreas-christensen.html |archive-date=15 August 2014}}</ref>

====2014–15 season====
He made his professional debut on 28 October 2014, playing the full 90 minutes at right-back as Chelsea won 2–1 away to [[Shrewsbury Town F.C.|Shrewsbury Town]] in the fourth round of the [[EFL Cup|League Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Chowdhury |first1=Saj |title=Shrewsbury 1–2 Chelsea |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/29699744|access-date=28 October 2014 |website=BBC Sport |date=28 October 2014}}</ref> Christensen did not play again until 24 January 2015, when he was utilised in the same position for Chelsea's [[Chelsea F.C. 2–4 Bradford City A.F.C. (2015)|4–2 home defeat]] to [[EFL League One|League One]] team [[Bradford City A.F.C.|Bradford City]] in the [[FA Cup]] fourth round.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Emons |first1=Michael |title=Chelsea 2–4 Bradford |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/30860394|access-date=24 January 2015 |website=BBC Sport |date=24 January 2015}}</ref>

Although Christensen played no further part in the campaign, Chelsea won the League Cup with a 2–0 victory over [[Chelsea F.C.–Tottenham Hotspur F.C. rivalry|rival]] club [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] in [[2015 Football League Cup Final|the final]]. When asked who was [[man of the match]] for the final, manager [[José Mourinho]] said, "Man of the match was Andreas Christensen who played well against Shrewsbury. There are more than [[John Terry]] (the official Man of the match for the final) because we are a team. I am proud of the guys."<ref>{{cite news |title=Matic's kit, phone calls, but no party: Chelsea's cup celebrations |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/31687889|access-date=2 March 2015 |website=BBC Sport |date=1 March 2015}}</ref>

On 13 April 2015, Christensen played for Chelsea U19 in the final of the 2014–15 [[UEFA Youth League]] against [[FC Shakhtar Donetsk|Shakhtar Donetsk]] in Switzerland and although he scored an own goal to cancel out [[Izzy Brown]]'s opener in the first-half, Chelsea still ended with a 3–2 victory.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.uefa.com/uefayouthleague/season=2015/matches/round=2000561/match=2016409/postmatch/report/index.html#brown+inspires+chelsea+youth+league+glory |title=Brown inspires Chelsea to Youth League glory |date=13 April 2015}}</ref> He made his [[Premier League]] debut against [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]] on 24 May, replacing [[Mikel John Obi]] with 12 minutes remaining in a 3–1 home victory.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Winton |first1=Richard |title=Chelsea 3–1 Sunderland |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/32770811|access-date=24 May 2015 |website=BBC Sport |date=24 May 2015}}</ref> Although Christensen only made one league appearances out of the whole season, Mourinho stated that he would receive a replica medal from his club for his contributions this season.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.espn.co.uk/football/chelsea/story/2450893/jose-mourinho-every-chelsea-player-to-get-prem-medal |title=Every Chelsea player to get a Premier League medal says Jose Mourinho |publisher=ESPN |date=15 May 2015 |access-date=26 August 2018}}</ref>

====2015–2017: Loan to Borussia Mönchengladbach====
On 10 July 2015, Christensen joined [[Borussia Mönchengladbach]] on a two-year [[loan (sports)|loan]] deal from Chelsea.<ref>{{cite web |title=German loan for Christensen |url=https://www.chelseafc.com/news/latest-news/2015/07/german-loan-for-christensen.html |publisher=Chelsea F.C. |date=10 July 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.borussia.de/english/season/news/borussia-news/news-detailansicht.html?tx_ttnews%5Btt_news%5D=14343&cHash=fdce92ddca7a52a6d25fe4c765c9add5 |title=Borussia bring in Chelsea's Andreas Christensen on loan |publisher=Borussia Mönchengladbach |date=10 July 2015|access-date=10 July 2015}}</ref> He made his debut on 10 August against [[FC St. Pauli]] in the first round of the [[2015–16 DFB-Pokal|DFB-Pokal]], winning 4–1.<ref>{{citation |title=St. Pauli vs. Borussia M'gladbach 1–4 |url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2015/08/10/germany/dfb-pokal/fc-st-pauli-von-1910-ev/borussia-monchengladbach/2038932/ |website=Soccerway |date=10 August 2015}}</ref> Five days later, Christensen made his [[Bundesliga]] debut in a 4–0 loss at [[Borussia Dortmund]].<ref>{{citation |title=Borussia Dortmund vs. Borussia M'gladbach 4–0 |url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2015/08/15/germany/bundesliga/bv-borussia-09-dortmund/borussia-monchengladbach/2054192/ |website=Soccerway |date=15 August 2015}}</ref> He scored his first professional goals on 5 February 2016, a brace in a 5–1 win over [[SV Werder Bremen|Werder Bremen]] at [[Borussia-Park]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Mönchengladbach delight at dismantling Bremen |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/blmd20n-borussia-moenchengladbach-5-1-sv-werder-bremen-post-match-reaction.jsp|access-date=29 December 2017 |publisher=Bundesliga |date=5 February 2016}}</ref>

After an impressive debut season, Christensen was voted the team's Player of the Season, ahead of [[Granit Xhaka]], the team [[captain (association football)|captain]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://metro.co.uk/2016/06/13/chelsea-prodigy-andreas-christensen-wins-borussia-monchegladbach-player-of-the-year-ahead-of-arsenals-granit-xhaka-5942365/ |title=Chelsea prodigy Andreas Christensen wins Borussia Monchegladbach player of the year ahead of Arsenal's Granit Xhaka |newspaper=Metro |date=13 June 2016|access-date=16 June 2016}}</ref> After the success of his first season, Mönchengladbach made multiple attempts to make his loan permanent during the [[List of English football transfers summer 2016|2016 summer transfer window]], although it was reported that Chelsea rejected the £14.25&nbsp;million bid from the German club.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/11668/10506225/chelsea-reject-borussia-monchengladbach-bid-for-andreas-christensen-sky-sources |title=Chelsea reject Borussia Monchengladbach bid for Andreas Christensen – Sky sources |website=Sky Sports |date=19 July 2016}}</ref>

In the [[2016–17 UEFA Europa League]], Christensen scored the final goal of a 4–2 win away to [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]] in the second leg of the last 32 to assure a 4–3 aggregate win.<ref>{{cite news |title=Fiorentina 2–4 Borussia Mönchengladbach |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/39068922|access-date=29 December 2017 |website=BBC Sport |date=23 February 2017}}</ref> He was on target again in the next round against neighbours [[FC Schalke 04|Schalke]], who won on away goals after a 3–3 aggregate draw.<ref>{{cite news |title=Borussia Monchengladbach 2 Schalke 2 (3–3 agg, Schalke win on away goals): Bentaleb penalty sends visitors through |url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/news/borussia-monchengladbach-2-schalke-2-3-3-agg-schalke-win-away-goals-bentaleb-penalty-sends|access-date=29 December 2017 |website=FourFourTwo |date=16 March 2017}}</ref>

====2017–2022: Return to Chelsea====
[[File:ACvsQarabag17.png|thumb|upright|Christensen playing for Chelsea in 2017]]

On 12 August 2017, Christensen made his first appearance for Chelsea following his two-year loan spell in Germany, coming off the bench following captain [[Gary Cahill]] being sent off, in Chelsea's 3–2 opening day home defeat against [[Burnley F.C.|Burnley]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40835426 |title=Chelsea 2–3 Burnley |website=BBC Sport |date=12 August 2017}}</ref> Eight days later, he made his first Premier League start in a 2–1 victory over Tottenham Hotspur at [[Wembley Stadium]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/40914668 |title=Tottenham Hotspur 1–2 Chelsea |website=BBC Sport |date=20 August 2017}}</ref> On 9 January 2018, Christensen signed a new four-and-half-year deal with Chelsea running until 2022, after establishing himself in the first team.<ref>{{cite web |title=Christensen commits |url=http://www.chelseafc.com/news/latest-news/2018/01/christensen-commits-.html |publisher=Chelsea F.C. |date=9 January 2018 |access-date=9 January 2018}}</ref> Christensen made 40 appearances for the Blues in 2017–18, including three in the victorious [[FA Cup]] campaign. However, a back injury meant he was not involved in the final.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Revealed: Why Andreas Christensen was not in Chelsea's squad for FA Cup victory over Man United |url=https://www.football.london/chelsea-fc/players/christensen-chelsea-fa-cup-final-14680790 |website=football.london |date=20 May 2018 |access-date=21 May 2018}}</ref> Prior to the season's finish, he was awarded the club's [[Chelsea Young Player of the Year|Young Player of the Year]] award.<ref>{{cite web |title=Christensen: Great to play on the big stage |url=http://www.chelseafc.com/news/latest-news/2018/05/christensen--great-to-play-on-the-big-stage.html |publisher=Chelsea F.C. |date=15 May 2018 |access-date=21 May 2018}}</ref>

New manager [[Maurizio Sarri]] preferred to pair [[David Luiz]] and [[Antonio Rüdiger]] in central defence, and by February 2019 Christensen had made 15 appearances, only two of which in the league. However, he ruled out wanting to leave the club.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Burton |first1=Chris |title=Christensen had 'doubts' but never considered leaving Chelsea |url=https://www.goal.com/en-gh/news/christensen-had-doubts-but-never-considered-leaving-chelsea/1onure75w5lpv1lfg1tlqzis4y |access-date=26 February 2019 |website=Goal |date=18 February 2019}}</ref>

On 29 May 2021, Christensen replaced the injured [[Thiago Silva]] in the 39th minute as he won his first-ever [[UEFA Champions League]] after Chelsea won 1–0 against [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] in the [[2021 UEFA Champions League Final]] in [[Porto]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/0269-12650cedca55-37515f69768e-1000--report-chelsea-claim-second-title/ |title=Man. City 0–1 Chelsea: Havertz gives Blues second Champions League triumph |publisher=UEFA |date=29 May 2021 |accessdate=29 May 2021}}</ref> On 20 October 2021, Christensen scored his first goal for Chelsea in a 4–0 win against [[Malmö FF]] in the group stage of the [[2021–22 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.si.com/soccer/chelsea/news/andreas-christensen-scoring-first-chelsea-goal-was-a-proud-moment#:~:text=He%20was%20there%20in%20the,senior%20goal%20for%20the%20club. |title='Proud Moment' – Andreas Christensen Reflects On Scoring First Chelsea Goal |website=Sports Illustrated |date=22 October 2021 |accessdate=23 November 2021}}</ref>

=== Barcelona ===
On 4 July 2022, Christensen signed for [[La Liga]] club [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] in a [[Free transfer (association football)|free transfer]] on a four-year contract with a buyout clause of €500&nbsp;million.<ref>{{Cite web |date=4 July 2022 |title=Christensen, second signing for FC Barcelona |url=https://www.fcbarcelona.com/en/football/first-team/news/2666783/christensen-second-signing-for-fc-barcelona |access-date=4 July 2022 |publisher=FC Barcelona}}</ref> On 13 August, he made his debut in 0–0 draw against [[Rayo Vallecano]] in the league.<ref>{{Cite web |date=13 August 2022 |title=Barcelona frustrated by Rayo Vallecano as Lewandowski & Raphinha's La Liga debuts spoiled |url=https://www.goal.com/en-in/news/barcelona-frustrated-rayo-vallecano-opening-night/bltcd5e7b01eea1ab09 |access-date=16 August 2022}}</ref> He began playing more consistently as the season progressed, and eventually formed a defensive partnership alongside [[Ronald Araújo]], helping Barcelona keep the most clean sheets (16) in all of Europe's top 5 leagues.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Marsden |first1=Sam |title=Araujo key as Barcelona nab 16th shutout, extend LaLiga lead over Real Madrid |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/barcelona-espbarcelona/story/4876613/barcelona-nab-16th-clean-sheet-grip-laliga-title-over-real-madrid |access-date=26 February 2023 |work=ESPN |date=12 February 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Record-breaking defensive stats |url=https://www.fcbarcelona.com/en/football/first-team/news/3063142/record-breaking-defensive-stats |website=FC Barcelona |access-date=26 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230216025929/https://www.fcbarcelona.com/en/football/first-team/news/3063142/record-breaking-defensive-stats |archive-date=16 February 2023 |date=13 February 2023}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Christensen is a [[centre-back]] and is known for his calmness in possession and precise passing, as well as his aerial dominance. In 2018, Chelsea manager [[Antonio Conte]] said the Dane had displayed "great maturity" adding that he has "a lot of trust in Christensen. This player is a player for the present for Chelsea, and for Chelsea's future for 10 or 14 years. He can also become the captain of this team in the future".<ref>{{cite news |title=Chelsea boss Antonio Conte backs Andreas Christensen to become club captain |first=Vaishali |last=Bhardwaj |date=23 February 2018|access-date=24 February 2018 |newspaper=Evening Standard |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/antonio-conte-backs-andreas-christensen-to-become-chelsea-captain-a3774486.html}}</ref> In appreciation of his playing talents, fans have also labelled him as the 'Danish Maldini',<ref>{{cite news |title=How Christensen drew Guardiola's interest and rose to become Chelsea's Danish Maldini |first=Nizaar |last=Kinsella |date=28 May 2021|access-date=28 May 2021 |newspaper=Goal |url=https://www.goal.com/en-in/amp/news/how-christensen-drew-guardiolas-interest-and-rose-to-become/ejjukyvj4pq81l3x70dbisb6t.html}}</ref> drawing comparisons with the Italian defender [[Paolo Maldini]].{{citation needed|date=December 2022}}
