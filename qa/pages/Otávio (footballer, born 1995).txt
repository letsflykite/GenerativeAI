'''Otávio Edmilson da Silva Monteiro''' (born 9 February 1995), commonly known as '''Otávio''' or '''Otavinho''',<ref name=Dunga>{{cite web|url=http://www.espn.com.br/noticia/563178_bronca-de-dunga-transformou-gordinho-em-joia-de-r-200-milhoes|title=Bronca de Dunga transformou 'gordinho' em joia de R$ 200 milhões|trans-title=Dunga earful turned 'fatty' into R$ 200 million gem|publisher=[[ESPN (Brazil)|ESPN Brasil]]|first1=Francisco|last1=De Laurentiis|first2=Vladimir|last2=Bianchini|language=pt|date=9 December 2015|access-date=27 November 2018}}</ref> is a Portuguese professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as an [[attacking midfielder]] or a [[winger (association football)|winger]] for [[Primeira Liga]] club [[FC Porto|Porto]] and the [[Portugal national football team|Portugal national team]].

He came through [[Sport Club Internacional|Internacional]]'s, before signing with Portuguese [[Primeira Liga]] side [[FC Porto|Porto]] in 2014 as a free agent. He was immediately sent two-year loan deal to [[Vitória S.C.|Vitória de Guimarães]], before returning in 2016. After returning he became a starter, and won three [[Primeira Liga]] titles, two [[Taça de Portugal]] and two [[Supertaça Cândido de Oliveira|Supertaças Cândido de Oliveira]], while being part of the squad that won two [[Double (association football)|domestic doubles]] in 2020 and 2022, making over 200 official appearances for them.

Born and raised in Brazil, Otávio represented his native country internationally at the [[Brazil national under-20 football team|under-20]] level in 2014, but switched allegiance at the senior international level to Portugal, making his senior international debut in 2021.

==Club career==
===Internacional===
Born in [[João Pessoa, Paraíba]], Otávio finished his development at [[Sport Club Internacional|Internacional]] after joining the [[Porto Alegre]] club's academy at the age of 15. He made his [[Campeonato Brasileiro Série A|Série A]] debut only two years later, coming on as a second-half [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] in the 0–0 home draw against [[Santos FC|Santos]] on 15 July.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.leiaja.com/esportes/2012/07/15/cheios-de-desfalques-inter-e-santos-ficam-no-0-0/|title=Cheios de desfalques, Inter e Santos ficam no 0 a 0|trans-title=With many absences, Inter and Santos leave it at 0 to 0|publisher=Leia Já|language=pt|date=15 July 2012|access-date=27 November 2018}}</ref>

Otávio's first league goal arrived on 8 June 2013, when he started and helped to a 2–2 away draw to [[Cruzeiro Esporte Clube|Cruzeiro]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://oglobo.globo.com/esportes/com-um-menos-cruzeiro-empata-com-inter-em-sete-lagoas-8631606|title=Com um a menos, Cruzeiro empata com o Inter em Sete Lagoas|trans-title=With one less, Cruzeiro draw with Inter at Sete Lagoas|newspaper=[[O Globo]]|language=pt|date=8 June 2013|access-date=27 November 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181128075718/https://oglobo.globo.com/esportes/com-um-menos-cruzeiro-empata-com-inter-em-sete-lagoas-8631606|archive-date=28 November 2018}}</ref> He added a further five during [[2013 Campeonato Brasileiro Série A|the season]], in a 13th-place finish.

Otávio credited manager [[Dunga]] for helping him overcome his weight problems. During his spell at the ''[[Estádio Beira-Rio]]'', he netted seven times in 62 competitive matches.<ref name=Dunga/>

===Porto===
On 1 September 2014, even though it was originally thought the transfer had cost [[Euro|€]]5 million, Otávio joined [[FC Porto]] as a [[free agent]] after Internacional ceded his federative rights, and signed a five-year contract with a €50 million buyout clause.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://maisfutebol.iol.pt/transferencias/01-09-2014/oficial-otavio-no-fc-porto-por-cinco-anos|title=OFICIAL: Otávio no FC Porto por cinco anos|trans-title=OFFICIAL: Otávio to FC Porto for five years|publisher=Mais Futebol|language=pt|date=1 September 2014|access-date=27 November 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.record.pt/futebol/football-leaks/detalhe/otavio-chegou-gratuitamente-ao-fc-porto|title=Otávio chegou gratuitamente ao FC Porto|trans-title=Otávio arrived at FC Porto for free|newspaper=[[Record (newspaper)|Record]]|first=António|last=Varela|language=pt|date=21 February 2016|access-date=27 November 2018}}</ref> He was initially assigned to [[FC Porto B|the reserve team]], who competed in the [[Segunda Liga]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://desporto.sapo.pt/futebol/primeira-liga/artigos/empresario-diz-que-otavio-se-distingue-pela-inteligencia|title=Empresário diz que Otávio se distingue pela "inteligência"|trans-title=Agent says Otávio stands out for "intelligence"|publisher=SAPO|language=pt|date=10 October 2014|access-date=27 November 2018}}</ref>

In the last day of the 2015 January [[transfer window]], Otávio, [[Ivo Rodrigues (footballer)|Ivo Rodrigues]] and [[Leocísio Sami]] were loaned to [[Vitória S.C.|Vitória de Guimarães]] in the [[Primeira Liga]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://maisfutebol.iol.pt/transferencias/liga/oficial-ivo-rodrigues-sami-e-otavio-no-vitoria-guimaraes|title=Oficial: Ivo Rodrigues, Sami e Otávio no Vitória Guimarães|trans-title=Official: Ivo Rodrigues, Sami and Otávio to Vitória Guimarães|publisher=Mais Futebol|language=pt|date=2 February 2015|access-date=27 November 2018}}</ref> He made his debut in the competition on 8 February by playing 30 minutes in a 0–1 home loss against [[C.F. Os Belenenses|Belenenses]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://maisfutebol.iol.pt/vitoria-guimaraes-belenenses-vitoria-guimaraes-belenenses-liga-topnews/54d7ad8d0cf2a422a976d5c0.html|title=V. Guimarães-Belenenses, 0–1 (crónica)|trans-title=V. Guimarães-Belenenses, 0–1 (match report)|publisher=Mais Futebol|first=Bruno José|last=Ferreira|language=pt|date=8 February 2015|access-date=27 November 2018}}</ref> and scored his first goal in the last matchday of [[2014–15 Primeira Liga|the campaign]] to help the visitors beat [[Associação Académica de Coimbra – O.A.F.|Académica de Coimbra]] 4–2.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.record.xl.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-nos/detalhe/academica-v-guimaraes-2-4-futebol-de-ataque-foi-prenda-no-adeus-950475.html|title=Académica-V. The deal was extended for the entire [[2015–16 Primeira Liga|2015–16]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vavel.com/pt/noticias/2015/07/17/515070-fc-porto-empresta-lica-ao-vitoria-de-guimaraes.html|title=FC Porto empresta Licá e Otávio ao Vitória de Guimarães|trans-title=FC Porto loan Licá and Otávio to Vitória de Guimarães|publisher=[[Vavel]]|first=Tiago|last=Pratas|language=pt|date=17 July 2015|access-date=28 November 2018}}</ref> and his performances while on loan earned him a contract extension with Porto subsequently.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.jn.pt/desporto/otavio-prolonga-contrato-com-o-f-c-porto-ate-2021-5477386.html|title=Otávio prolonga contrato com o F. C. Porto até 2021|trans-title=Otávio extends contract with F. C. Porto until 2021|newspaper=[[Jornal de Notícias]]|first=Nuno A.|last=Amaral|language=pt|date=3 November 2016|access-date=29 September 2020}}</ref> He made his competitive debut for the latter on 12 August 2016, starting in the 3–1 away defeat of [[Rio Ave F.C.|Rio Ave]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://tribunaexpresso.pt/futebol-nacional/2016-08-12-FC-Porto-comeca-a-caminhada-possivel-para-o-impossivel|title=FC Porto começa a caminhada possível para o impossível|trans-title=FC Porto start possible journey towards impossible|newspaper=[[Expresso (newspaper)|Expresso]]|first=Mariana|last=Cabral|language=pt|date=12 August 2016|access-date=28 November 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://tvi24.iol.pt/liga/12-08-2016/rio-ave-fc-porto-1-3-destaques|title=Rio Ave-FC Porto, 1–3 (destaques)|trans-title=Rio Ave-FC Porto, 1–3 (highlights)|publisher=[[CNN Portugal|TVI 24]]|first=Vítor Hugo|last=Alvarenga|language=pt|date=12 August 2016|access-date=28 November 2018}}</ref>

Otávio won the Portuguese League at the end of [[2017–18 Primeira Liga|his second season]], contributing two goals from 15 appearances.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.dn.pt/lusa/interior/fc-porto-campeao-os-numeros-do-fc-porto-na-edicao-201718-da-i-liga-9312412.html|title=FC Porto campeão: Os números do FC Porto na edição 2017/18 da I Liga|trans-title=FC Porto champions: The numbers of FC Porto in the I League's 2017/18 edition|newspaper=[[Diário de Notícias]]|language=pt|date=5 May 2018|access-date=28 November 2018}}</ref> He was sidelined for several weeks during the tournament, due to injury problems.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-nos/fc-porto/detalhe/otavio-admite-culpa|title=Otávio admite 'culpa'|trans-title=Otávio admits "guilt"|newspaper=Record|language=pt|date=4 November 2017|access-date=28 November 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://desporto.sapo.pt/futebol/primeira-liga/artigos/lesao-de-otavio-so-o-permite-regressar-em-2018|title=Lesão de Otávio só o permite regressar em 2018|trans-title=Otávio injury only allows his comeback in 2018|publisher=SAPO|language=pt|date=8 December 2017|access-date=28 November 2018}}</ref>

During the [[2019–20 Primeira Liga|2019–20 campaign]], Otávio featured in 48 games and scored two goals, helping the team conquer [[Double (association football)|the double]].<ref name=2020end>{{cite news|url=https://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-nos/fc-porto/detalhe/otavio-foi-considerado-o-melhor-de-julho|title=Otávio foi considerado o melhor de julho|trans-title=Otávio considered best in July|newspaper=Record|first=Rui|last=Sousa|language=pt|date=15 August 2020|access-date=29 September 2020}}</ref> As a result, he was included in the league's Best XI.<ref name=Best>{{cite news|url=https://www.jn.pt/desporto/amp/do-melhor-jogador-ao-golo-do-ano-confira-os-vencedores-do-kick-off-da-liga-12562865.html|title=Do melhor jogador ao golo do ano. ==International career==
===Brazil===
Shortly after making his Internacional debut, Otávio was selected to the [[Brazil national under-20 football team|Brazil under-20 side]] by [[Alexandre Gallo]].<ref name=Dunga/>

===Portugal===
Otávio became a [[Portuguese nationality law|Portuguese citizen]] in March 2021.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.ojogo.pt/futebol/1a-liga/porto/noticias/otavio-escolhe-a-dobradinha-e-assume-fico-mais-tranquilo-por-ser-portugues-13492112.html|title=Otávio escolhe a dobradinha e assume: "Fico mais tranquilo por ser português"|trans-title=Otávio chooses double and acknowledges: "I'm more at ease now that I am Portuguese"|newspaper=[[O Jogo]]|first=Ana Luísa|last=Magalhães|language=pt|date=23 March 2021|access-date=27 March 2021}}</ref> On 26 August of that year, he was called up to the [[Portugal national football team|Portugal national team]] by [[Fernando Santos (footballer, born 1954)|Fernando Santos]] for [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)|qualifiers]] against the [[Republic of Ireland national football team|Republic of Ireland]] and [[Azerbaijan national football team|Azerbaijan]] and a [[Exhibition game|friendly]] with [[Qatar national football team|Qatar]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://observador.pt/2021/08/26/ficou-feliz-e-tranquilo-por-ser-portugues-agora-otavio-foi-chamado-a-selecao-nacional-portuguesa/|title=Ficou "feliz" e "tranquilo" por ser português. Agora Otávio foi chamado à seleção nacional portuguesa|trans-title=He was "happy" and "at ease" for being Portuguese. Now Otávio has been called to the Portugal national team|newspaper=[[Observador]]|first=Rui|last=Salvador|language=pt|date=26 August 2021|access-date=26 August 2021}}</ref> He made his debut against the latter, scoring in the 3–1 win at the [[Nagyerdei Stadion]] in [[Debrecen]], Hungary.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.jn.pt/desporto/selecao-nacional-triunfa-perante-um-catar-demasiado-tenro-14090184.html|title=Seleção nacional triunfa perante um Catar demasiado "tenro"|trans-title=National team triumph against too-"soft" Qatar|newspaper=Jornal de Notícias|first=Bernardo|last=Monteiro|language=pt|date=4 September 2021|access-date=4 September 2021}}</ref>

On 24 March 2022, Otávio scored his second goal, opening an eventual 3–1 home victory over [[Turkey national football team|Turkey]] in the semi-finals of the [[2022 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA second round|World Cup qualification play-offs]] and later providing an [[Assist (association football)|assist]] for [[Diogo Jota]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://maisfutebol.iol.pt/mundial-2022/selecao/mundial-2022-portugal-turquia-3-1-cronica|title=Mundial 2022: Portugal-Turquia, 3–1 (crónica)|trans-title=2022 World Cup: Portugal-Turkey, 3–1 (report)|publisher=Mais Futebol|first=Vítor|last=Maia|language=pt|date=24 March 2022|access-date=25 March 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://maisfutebol.iol.pt/selecao/portugal/otavio-portugal-unido-e-muito-forte-e-ficou-aqui-a-prova|title=Otávio: "Portugal unido é muito forte e ficou aqui a prova"|trans-title=Otávio: "Portugal are very strong when united and here's proof"|publisher=Mais Futebol|language=pt|date=24 March 2022|access-date=25 March 2022}}</ref>

In October, he was named in Portugal's preliminary 55-man [[Portugal national football team|squad]] for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sportskeeda.com/football/rumor-portugal-reportedly-announce-provisional-55-man-squad-2022-fifa-world-cup-liverpool-star-receives-surprise-call|title=Portugal reportedly name provisional 55-man squad for 2022 FIFA World Cup; Liverpool star receives surprise call up|publisher=Sportskeeda|first=Dakir Mohammed|last=Thanveer|date=25 October 2022|access-date=9 November 2022}}</ref> being included in the final 26-man squad for the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://desporto.sapo.pt/futebol/mundial/artigos/os-26-convocados-de-portugal-para-o-mundial2022|title=Os 26 convocados de Portugal: Gonçalo Ramos, António Silva, Otávio e Matheus Nunes chamados|trans-title=Portugal select 26: Gonçalo Ramos, António Silva, Otávio and Matheus Nunes called|publisher=SAPO|language=pt|date=10 November 2022|access-date=10 November 2022}}</ref>
