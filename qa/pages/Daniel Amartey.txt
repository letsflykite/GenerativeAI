'''Daniel Amartey''' (born 21 December 1994) is a Ghanaian professional [[Association football|footballer]] who last played as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]] or [[midfielder]] for [[EFL Championship]] club [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] and the [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national team]].

A graduate of [[International Allies F.C.|International Allies]]' [[youth academy]], Amartey played for [[Djurgårdens IF Fotboll|Djurgården]] and [[F.C. Copenhagen|Copenhagen]] before joining Leicester City in 2016.

==Club career==
===Djurgårdens IF===
Amartey started out playing for second-tier club [[International Allies F.C.|International Allies]] in Ghana where he was spotted as a sixteen-year-old by [[Magnus Pehrsson]] who was on a scouting trip in [[Africa]] during his preparation to take over as manager of [[Djurgårdens IF Fotboll|Djurgårdens IF]]. When Pehrsson became manager he secured the transfer rights for Amartey from the day he turned 18.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://dif.se/2011/08/26/mp-om-de-nya-ghananerna/ |title=MP om de nya ghananerna |date=26 August 2011 |publisher=Djurgårdens IF |language=sv |access-date=1 April 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110910135527/http://dif.se/2011/08/26/mp-om-de-nya-ghananerna/ |archive-date=10 September 2011}}</ref> To help prepare Amartey for his future permanent move to [[Sweden]] in 2013, the club brought him over for shorter periods of time in both 2011 and 2012 where he got to play for the club's U21 team.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://dif.se/2011/08/27/afrikanska-loften-pa-kaknas/ |title=Afrikanska löften på Kaknäs |date=27 August 2011 |publisher=Djurgårdens IF |language=sv |access-date=1 April 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130129133434/http://dif.se/2011/08/27/afrikanska-loften-pa-kaknas/ |archive-date=29 January 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://dif.se/2012/07/11/daniel-amartey-ansluter-i-augusti/ |title=Daniel Amartey ansluter i augusti |date=11 July 2012 |publisher=Djurgårdens IF |language=sv |access-date=1 April 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130127164406/http://dif.se/2012/07/11/daniel-amartey-ansluter-i-augusti/ |archive-date=27 January 2013}}</ref>

Amartey made his [[Svenska Cupen]] debut on 3 March 2013 against [[Umeå FC]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://dif.se/2013/03/03/ertons-hattrick-sankte-umea/ |title=Ertons hattrick sänkte Umeå |date=3 March 2013 |publisher=Djurgårdens IF |language=sv |access-date=12 March 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130308015912/http://dif.se/2013/03/03/ertons-hattrick-sankte-umea/ |archive-date=8 March 2013}}</ref> He then made his league debut in the [[2013 Allsvenskan]] opening game away against [[Helsingborgs IF]] on 31 March.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://svenskfotboll.se/allsvenskan/match/?scr=result&fmid=1995655&html5=1 |title=Allsvenskan, herrar Matchinformation: Helsingborgs IF – Djurgården |date=31 March 2013 |publisher=SvFF |language=sv |access-date=1 April 2013 |archive-date=2 February 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140202215504/http://svenskfotboll.se/allsvenskan/match/?scr=result&fmid=1995655&html5=1 |url-status=live }}</ref> Amartey was praised by the media for his strong start to the 2013 season, and foreign clubs like [[FC Schalke 04]] and [[1. ===Copenhagen===
In July 2014, Amartey moved to [[F.C. Copenhagen]] for a fee of €2.5&nbsp;million plus add-ons, and made his [[Danish Superliga|Superliga]]-debut on 20 July 2014, in a match against [[Silkeborg IF]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.espn.in/football/match?gameId=398600 |title=Silkeborg IF vs. FC Copenhagen - Football Match Summary - July 20, 2014 |website=ESPN |access-date=19 February 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190220002811/http://www.espn.in/football/match?gameId=398600 |archive-date=20 February 2019 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

===Leicester City===
[[File:Daniel Amartey 2021.jpg|thumb|upright|Amartey playing for [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] in 2021]]

On 22 January 2016, Amartey joined [[Premier League]] side [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] on a four-and-a-half-year contract for a fee around the region of up to £6&nbsp;million.<ref>{{cite news |title=Leicester City Sign Ghana Star Daniel Amartey |url=http://www.lcfc.com/news/article/leicester-city-sign-ghana-star-daniel-amartey-2914554.aspx |publisher=Leicester City F.C. |date=22 January 2016 |access-date=22 January 2016 |archive-date=17 November 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201117194859/https://www.lcfc.com/news/432705 |url-status=live }}</ref> In his first season in his new country, Amartey featured five times as his team Leicester City won the Premier League title. He made his debut for the club on 27 February 2016, in 1–0 home league win over [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]].<ref name="Leicester City 1-0 Norwich City">{{cite news |title=Leicester City 1–0 Norwich City |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/35619679 |website=[[BBC Sport]] |date=27 February 2016 |access-date=30 January 2018 |archive-date=15 July 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180715134605/https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/35619679 |url-status=live }}</ref>

In the 2016–17 season, Amartey became a first team regular following the departure of [[N'Golo Kanté]].<ref name="Leicester City's Daniel Amartey filling the Kanté void will take time">{{cite news |title=Leicester City's Daniel Amartey filling the Kante void will take time |url=http://www.leicestermercury.co.uk/leicester-city-s-daniel-amartey-filling-the-kante-void-will-take-time/story-29763963-detail/story.html |work=Leicester Mercury |date=29 September 2016 |access-date=5 January 2017 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160930171817/http://www.leicestermercury.co.uk/leicester-city-s-daniel-amartey-filling-the-kante-void-will-take-time/story-29763963-detail/story.html |archive-date=30 September 2016 }}</ref> partnering teammate [[Danny Drinkwater]] in the central midfield role.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sportsbeatfeed.co.uk/2016/09/amartey-learning-drinkwater-leicester/|title=Amartey learning from Drinkwater at Leicester|date=17 September 2016|access-date=24 September 2020|archive-date=29 November 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221129184421/https://sportsbeatfeed.co.uk/2016/09/amartey-learning-drinkwater-leicester/|url-status=live}}</ref> While his workrate roughly equalled Kanté's, he has been unable to match his tackles and interceptions.<ref>{{cite news |title=Leicester City: What has changed at the Premier League champions? |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/38879315 |website=BBC Sport |date=9 February 2017 |access-date=12 February 2017 |archive-date=24 August 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180824041306/https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/38879315 |url-status=live }}</ref> On 14 September 2016, Amartey started in his [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] debut for Leicester in a 3–0 away win over [[Club Brugge KV|Club Brugge]] in the [[2016–17 UEFA Champions League group stage#Group G|group stage]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Jennings|first=Patrick|title=Club Brugge 0-3 Leicester City|website=[[BBC Sport]]|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/37331303|date=14 September 2016|access-date=27 February 2021|archive-date=8 November 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201108133228/https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/37331303|url-status=live}}</ref> Amartey scored his first goal for Leicester in an 88th-minute equaliser away at [[Stoke City F.C.|Stoke City]] on 17 December 2016, salvaging a 2–2 draw.<ref name="Stoke City 2-2 Leicester City">{{cite news |title=Stoke City 2–2 Leicester City |website=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/38272819 |date=17 December 2016 |access-date=30 January 2018 |archive-date=5 August 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180805032822/https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/38272819 |url-status=live }}</ref> On 8 February 2017, just over 12 hours after returning to Leicester from international duty, Amartey played 120 minutes (including [[Overtime (sports)|AET]]) in a 3–1 [[FA Cup]] fourth-round replay win over [[Derby County F.C.|Derby County]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Leicester City 3–1 Derby County Leicester |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/38822191 |access-date=8 February 2017 |website=BBC Sport |date=8 February 2017 |archive-date=8 February 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170208125006/http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/38822191 |url-status=live }}</ref>

In October 2018, Amartey broke his ankle in a game against [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]], ruling him out for the rest of the [[2018-19 Leicester City F.C. season|2018–19 season]].<ref>{{Cite news|url = https://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-soccer-england-lei-whu-amartey/leicester-manager-puel-concerned-about-injured-amartey-idUKKCN1N10TH|title = Leicester manager Puel concerned about injured Amartey|newspaper = Reuters|date = 27 October 2018|access-date = 30 April 2019|archive-date = 30 April 2019|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20190430092238/https://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-soccer-england-lei-whu-amartey/leicester-manager-puel-concerned-about-injured-amartey-idUKKCN1N10TH|url-status = live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ghanaweb.com/GhanaHomePage/SportsArchive/Daniel-Amartey-making-promising-recovery-from-horror-injury-739260#|title=Daniel Amartey making 'promising recovery' from horror injury|date=17 April 2019}}</ref>

On 15 May 2021, following Leicester City's [[2021 FA Cup Final|FA Cup final]] victory over [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]], Amartey was filmed picking up a Chelsea [[Pennant (sports)|pennant]] in the Leicester dressing room and throwing it over his shoulder to the floor in a video that went viral. He received criticism and backlash from many social media users, who deemed the gesture disrespectful.<ref>{{cite web|date=16 May 2021|title=Football fans turn on Daniel Amartey for 'disrespecting' Chelsea pennant [VIDEO]|url=https://citisportsonline.com/2021/05/16/football-fans-turn-on-daniel-amartey-for-disrespecting-chelsea-pennant-video/|access-date=17 May 2021|website=Citi Sports Online|archive-date=16 May 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210516110632/https://citisportsonline.com/2021/05/16/football-fans-turn-on-daniel-amartey-for-disrespecting-chelsea-pennant-video/|url-status=live}}</ref> Leicester City later issued an apology to Chelsea, who accepted.<ref>{{cite web|title=Leicester issue Chelsea apology over Daniel Amartey FA Cup actions - MyJoyOnline.com|url=https://www.myjoyonline.com/leicester-issue-chelsea-apology-over-daniel-amartey-fa-cup-actions/|date=17 May 2021|access-date=17 May 2021|website=MyJoyOnline.com|archive-date=17 May 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210517140508/https://www.myjoyonline.com/leicester-issue-chelsea-apology-over-daniel-amartey-fa-cup-actions/|url-status=live}}</ref>

Amartey and Leicester started the [[2021–22 Leicester City F.C. season|2021–22 season]] with the [[2021 FA Community Shield]] against [[Manchester City]]. Amartey played the full match as Iheanacho scored the winning goal, an 89th-minute penalty against his former club.<ref name=":1">{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/58036460 |title=Leicester City 1–0 Manchester City |first=Shamoon |last=Hafez |work=BBC Sport |date=7 August 2021 |access-date=7 August 2021 |archive-date=21 October 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211021081912/https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/58036460 |url-status=live }}</ref>

On 5 June 2023, following the club's relegation from the [[Premier League]], it was announced that Amartey and six other first team players would be leaving the club upon the expiration of their contracts at the end of the month.<ref>{{cite news |title=Seven Players To Depart LCFC |url=https://www.lcfc.com/news/3529945/seven-players-to-depart-lcfc |access-date=24 June 2023 |work=Leicester City Football Club |date=5 June 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
In May 2012 Amartey made his debut for the [[Ghana national under-20 football team]] in a game against [[Nigeria national under-20 football team|Nigeria]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ghanasoccernet.com/ghana-u-20-outscores-nigeria-in-five-goal-thriller/ |title=Ghana U20 get their revenge against Nigeria U-20 in five-goal thriller |date=25 May 2012 |publisher=Ghana Soccernet |access-date=1 April 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140408213541/http://www.ghanasoccernet.com/ghana-u-20-outscores-nigeria-in-five-goal-thriller/ |archive-date=8 April 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He was also selected to play in the [[2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup]] but Djurgården wanted to keep him in Sweden since the tournament clashed with the [[2013 Allsvenskan]] season.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.expressen.se/sport/fotboll/allsvenskan/talangen-kunde-spelat-vm---stoppades-av-dif/ |title=Talangen kunde spelat VM – stoppades av Dif |date=25 June 2013 |work=[[Expressen]] |language=sv |access-date=26 June 2013 |archive-date=4 May 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160504153537/http://www.expressen.se/sport/fotboll/allsvenskan/talangen-kunde-spelat-vm---stoppades-av-dif/ |url-status=live }}</ref> In January 2015, Amartey played in all the group matches for [[Ghana]] at the 2015 Africa Cup of Nations, where the Black Stars finished as runners-up.

He played six times at the [[2017 Africa Cup of Nations]], lasting 90 minutes in every match to help Ghana finish fourth in the tournament.<ref name="Leicester City's Amartey in AFCON team of tournament - at centre-half"/> His performances saw him named in the CAF Team of the tournament.<ref name="Leicester City's Amartey in AFCON team of tournament - at centre-half">{{cite news |title=Leicester City's Amartey in AFCON team of tournament – at centre-half |url=http://www.leicestermercury.co.uk/leicester-city-s-amartey-in-afcon-team-of-tournament-at-centre-half/story-30114584-detail/story.html#k0HrfcHm2FCRzssh.99 |work=Leicester Mercury |author=MJClayton |date=6 February 2017 |access-date=9 February 2017}}{{dead link|date=January 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Amartey featured in the [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations|2021 Africa Cup]] of Nations in [[Cameroon]] whom the [[Ghana national football team|Ghana national team]] was eliminated in the early stage of the competition.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Africa Cup of Nations (Sky Sports)|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/ghana-vs-comoros/teams/457221|access-date=9 February 2022|website=Sky Sports|language=en|archive-date=9 February 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220209105345/https://www.skysports.com/football/ghana-vs-comoros/teams/457221|url-status=live}}</ref>
