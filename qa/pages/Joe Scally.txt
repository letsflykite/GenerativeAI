'''Joseph Michael Scally''' (born December 31, 2002) is an American professional [[Association football|soccer]] player who plays as a [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|right-back]] for {{German football updater|BoMönche}} club [[Borussia Mönchengladbach]] and the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States national team]].

==Club career==
===New York City===
Scally signed his first professional contract with [[New York City FC|New York City]] on March 21, 2018, at the age of 15,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2018/03/21/nycfc-sign-15-year-old-fullback-joe-scally-mls-homegrown-contract|title=NYCFC Signs 15 Year Old Fullback Joe Scally MLS Homegrown Contract|date=March 21, 2018|publisher=MLS Soccer}}</ref> and in doing so became the second youngest professional soccer player in the United States, after [[Freddy Adu]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nypost.com/2018/03/21/meet-the-15-year-old-with-big-plans-in-nycfcs-future/|title=Meet the 15-year-old with big plans in NYCFC's future|date=March 21, 2018|publisher=New York Post}}</ref> He made his professional debut for New York City in a 4–0 [[U.S. Open Cup]] loss to [[New York Red Bulls]] on June 6, coming on as a late substitute.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nycfc.com/post/2018/06/06/match-recap-red-bulls-4-0-nycfc|title=Match Recap Red Bulls 4-0 NYCFC|date=June 6, 2018|publisher=NYCFC.com}}</ref>

===Borussia Mönchengladbach===
On November 13, 2019, it was announced that Scally would join [[Bundesliga]] side [[Borussia Mönchengladbach]] at the end of the [[2020 Major League Soccer season|2020 MLS season]].<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.nycfc.com/post/2019/11/13/nycfc-homegrown-joe-scally-join-borussia-monchengladbach-2021|title=NYCFC Homegrown Joe Scally to Join Borussia Monchengladbach in 2021 &#124; New York City FC|access-date=November 13, 2019|archive-date=November 13, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191113161430/https://www.nycfc.com/post/2019/11/13/nycfc-homegrown-joe-scally-join-borussia-monchengladbach-2021|url-status=dead}}</ref> On October 2, 2021, he scored his first Bundesliga goal against [[VfL Wolfsburg]] in a 3–1 victory.

==International career==
Scally was a youth international for the United States, representing the [[United States men's national under-15 soccer team|United States under-15]]s<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2017/08/16/23/11/20170816-recap-u15bnt-advances-to-concacaf-u15-semifinals|title=U-15 BNT Advances to CONCACAF U-15 Championship Semifinals|website=www.ussoccer.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2017/08/17/23/22/20170817-recap-u15bnt-advances-to-concacaf-championship|title=U-15 BNT Defeats Panama 3-0 to Advance to First CONCACAF Championship|website=www.ussoccer.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2017/12/04/00/06/20171203-recap-u17mnt-usa-vs-brazil-nike-international-friendlies|title=USA Draws Brazil 1-1 to Close Out 2017 Nike International Friendlies|website=www.ussoccer.com}}</ref> and the [[United States men's national under-17 soccer team|United States under-17]]s despite being three years younger than his teammates.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2017/11/30/02/08/20171129-recap-u17mnt-usa-vs-england-nike-international-friendlies|title=U-17 MNT Opens 2017 Nike Friendlies with Hard-Fought 1-0 Loss to England|website=www.ussoccer.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2017/12/02/02/12/20171201-recap-u17mnt-usa-vs-netherlands-nike-international-friendlies|title=U-17 MNT Falls to Netherlands 5-1 at 2017 Nike International Friendlies|website=www.ussoccer.com}}</ref>

In October 2019, he was named to the squad for the [[2019 FIFA U-17 World Cup]] in Brazil.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2019/10/head-coach-raphael-wicky-names-usa-roster-for-2019-fifa-u17-world-cup-in-brazil|title=Head Coach Raphael Wicky Names USA Roster for 2019 FIFA U-17 World Cup in Brazil|website=www.ussoccer.com|language=en|access-date=October 10, 2019}}</ref>

In November 2021, he was named to the squad for the senior [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]] team ahead of their home [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] qualifiers against [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]] and [[Jamaica national football team|Jamaica]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2021/11/berhalter-calls-25-players-to-cincinnati-as-2022-world-cup-qualifying-campaign-nears-halfway-point|title=Berhalter Calls 25 Players to Cincinnati as 2022 World Cup Qualifying Campaign Nears Halfway Point|website=www.ussoccer.com}}</ref> On June 1, 2022, he made his senior debut for the United States in a 3–0 win in a friendly match against [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/joe-scally-and-malik-tillman-make-usmnt-debuts-in-morocco-friendly-20166 |title=Joe Scally and Malik Tillman make USMNT debuts in Morocco friendly |website=Bundesliga |date=June 1, 2022 }}</ref> In November 2022, Scally was named to the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] squad, but did not appear with the national team.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Gusoff |first1=Carolyn |title=Long Island's Joe Scally set to take the pitch with U.S. National Team at World Cup |url=https://www.cbsnews.com/newyork/news/long-islands-joe-scally-set-to-take-the-pitch-with-u-s-national-team-at-world-cup/ |website=[[WCBS-TV]] |publisher=[[CBS]] |access-date=December 19, 2022 |date=November 16, 2022}}</ref>
