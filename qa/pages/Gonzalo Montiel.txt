'''Gonzalo Ariel Montiel''' (born 1 January 1997) is an Argentine professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as [[right-back]] for [[La Liga]] club [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]] and the [[Argentina national football team|Argentina national team]].

He began his professional career with [[Club Atlético River Plate|River Plate]] in 2016, playing 139 games and winning honours including the [[Argentine Primera División]] in [[2021 Argentine Primera División|2021]] and the [[Copa Libertadores]] in [[2018 Copa Libertadores|2018]]. In 2021, he signed for [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]] for €11 million.

Montiel made his international debut for [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]] in 2019. He was a part of the squads that won the [[2021 Copa América]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup]], scoring the winning penalty in the [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|shoot-out]] of the latter [[2022 FIFA World Cup final|final]].

== Club career ==

=== River Plate ===
Born in [[González Catán]], [[Buenos Aires Province]], Montiel was a product of the [[Club Atlético River Plate]] academy. In October 2015, manager [[Marcelo Gallardo]] called the 18-year-old up for the first time for an [[Argentine Primera División]] game at [[Defensa y Justicia]]; he was unused in the 1–0 loss on 15 October.<ref>{{cite news |title=Gallardo quiere confiar en una defensa joven ante Defensa y Justicia|trans-title=Gallardo wants to trust a young defender against Defensa y Justicia|url=https://www.lanacion.com.ar/deportes/futbol/gallardo-quiere-confiar-en-una-defensa-joven-ante-defensa-y-justicia-nid1835873/ |access-date=20 December 2022 |work=[[La Nación]] |date=12 October 2015 |language=Spanish}}</ref> He made his debut on 30 April 2016 in his one appearance of [[2016 Argentine Primera División|that season]], as a half-time substitute for [[Pablo Carreras]] in a goalless draw at home to [[Club Atlético Vélez Sarsfield]]; Gallardo had made 11 changes to his team with an eye to a [[Copa Libertadores]] second leg against [[C.S.D. Independiente del Valle]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Montiel: cinco años de evolución|trans-title=Montiel: five years of evolution |url=https://www.ole.com.ar/river-plate/montiel-debut-anos-river-evolucion_0_ugz4eanw3.html |access-date=20 December 2022 |work=[[Olé (sports newspaper)|Olé]] |date=2 May 2021 |language=Spanish}}</ref>

Montiel played four league matches the 2016–17 season. On 30 October, he was sent off in a 2–2 draw at [[Arsenal de Sarandí]].<ref>{{cite news |title=River igualó en Sarandí|trans-title=River matched in Sarandí|url=https://www.eltribuno.com/jujuy/nota/2016-10-31-1-30-0-river-igualo-en-sarandi |access-date=20 December 2022 |work=El Tribuno |date=31 October 2016 |language=Spanish}}</ref> He lifted the [[Copa Argentina]] trophy after playing all the matches from the last 16 to the [[2017 Copa Argentina Final|final]], in which his team beat [[Atlético Tucumán]].<ref name=copa17>{{cite news |last1=Balbi Vignolo |first1=Juan Patricio |title=Una gran sonrisa para terminar el año: River derrotó a Atlético Tucumán y volvió a conquistar la Copa Argentina|trans-title=A big smile to end the year: River defeated Atlético Tucumán and conquered the Copa Argentina again |url=https://www.lanacion.com.ar/deportes/futbol/river-atletico-tucuman-copa-argentina-el-equipo-de-gallardo-busca-defender-el-titulo-frente-al-decano-nid2089914/ |access-date=20 December 2022 |work=La Nación |date=10 December 2017 |language=Spanish}}</ref> He played in the [[2017 Copa Libertadores]], scoring a goal and assisting three, playing every minute from the quarter-finals to the semi-finals, where River Plate were defeated by [[Club Atlético Lanús]]; his first career goal came on 1 November in a 4–2 loss at Lanús.<ref>{{cite news |title=Lanús logró una remontada colosal y se metió en la final de la Libertadores|trans-title=Lanús achieved a colossal comeback and put themselves in the Libertadores final |url=https://www.eldia.com/nota/2017-11-1-4-41-36-lanus-logro-una-remontada-colosal-y-se-metio-en-la-final-de-la-libertadores-deportes |access-date=20 December 2022 |work=[[El Día (La Plata)|El Día]] |date=1 November 2017 |language=Spanish}}</ref>

In the 2017–18 season, Montiel played 35 matches, fifteen of them coming in the league. He made 14 appearances he made as the team lifted the Copa Libertadores at the [[Santiago Bernabéu Stadium]] against [[Superclásico]] rivals [[Boca Juniors]].<ref name=lib>{{cite news |last1=Ouzia |first1=Malik |title=River Plate win 2018 Copa Libertadores: Stunning Juan Quintero goal downs Boca Juniors at Santiago Bernabeu |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/river-plate-win-2018-copa-libertadores-stunning-juan-quintero-goal-downs-boca-juniors-at-santiago-bernabeu-a4012606.html |access-date=20 December 2022 |work=[[Evening Standard]] |date=9 December 2018}}</ref> Playing five matches in the Copa Argentina, the team were eliminated on penalties in the semi-finals against [[Club de Gimnasia y Esgrima La Plata]]. The team secured a double that season as they beat Boca  in the [[Supercopa Argentina]].

The following season, Montiel played 12 matches in the league without being substituted. For the second season in a row, Montiel reached the Copa Libertadores final after twelve matches; however, River Plate would be defeated by [[CR Flamengo]]. He won the Copa Argentina once more as River beat Central Córdoba - he played every minute in the cup run. After winning last season's Copa Libertadores, River contested the [[FIFA Club World Cup]] where they finished third. An injury prevented him from playing in the Super Cup. After recovering, he returned to contest the Recopa as River overcame a 1–0 first-leg defeat to win the trophy with a 3–0 win against Club Athletico Paranaense.<ref name="SW"/>

On 20 February 2021, Montiel scored his first league goal for River, a [[Penalty kick (association football)|penalty]] in a 3–0 win against [[Rosario Central]] in the first game at the [[Estadio Monumental (Buenos Aires)|Estadio Monumental]] after a six-month renovation.<ref>{{cite news |title=En su regreso al Estadio Monumental, River jugó un gran partido y goleó a Rosario Central|trans-title=On their return to the Estadio Monumental, River played a great game and thrashed Rosario Central |url=https://www.infobae.com/deportes/2021/02/20/en-su-vuelta-al-monumental-river-buscara-levantar-ante-rosario-central-hora-tv-y-formaciones/ |access-date=20 December 2022 |publisher=Infobae |date=20 February 2021 |language=Spanish}}</ref>

=== Sevilla ===
On 13 August 2021, [[La Liga]] club [[Sevilla FC]] signed Montiel on a five-year deal for €11 million. Manager [[Julen Lopetegui]] signed him to compete with club icon [[Jesús Navas]] at right back after the exit of [[Aleix Vidal]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Oficial: Gonzalo Montiel ficha por el Sevilla|trans-title=Official: Gonzalo Montiel signs for Sevilla |url=https://www.marca.com/futbol/sevilla/2021/08/13/61169c1aca47418e3d8b45e6.html |access-date=20 December 2022 |work=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]] |date=13 August 2021 |language=Spanish}}</ref> He made his debut on 14 September in a [[UEFA Champions League]] group stage 1–1 home draw against [[FC Red Bull Salzburg]], replacing Navas for the last three minutes.<ref>{{cite news |title=Reparto de puntos en el empate a uno entre Sevilla y Red Bull Salzburgo|trans-title=Points shared in the one-one draw between Sevilla and Red Bull Salzburg |url=https://www.infobae.com/america/agencias/2021/09/13/sevilla-recibira-a-red-bull-salzburgo-por-la-fecha-1-del-grupo-g/ |access-date=20 December 2022 |publisher=Infobae |date=13 September 2021 |language=Spanish}}</ref> Eight days later, he made his league bow by starting against [[Valencia CF]] at the [[Ramón Sánchez Pizjuán Stadium]], scoring in the 15th minute of a 3–1 win before making way for Navas before the hour.<ref>{{cite news |title=Con goles del Papu Gómez y Montiel, el Sevilla derrotó al Valencia y sueña con dar pelea en la Liga de España |trans-title=With goals from Papu Gómez and Montiel, Sevilla defeated Valencia and dream of fighting for the Spanish League|url=https://www.infobae.com/america/deportes/espn/2021/09/22/con-goles-del-papu-gomez-y-montiel-el-sevilla-derroto-al-valencia-y-suena-con-dar-pelea-en-la-liga-de-espana/ |access-date=20 December 2022 |publisher=Infobae |date=22 September 2021 |language=Spanish}}</ref>

Montiel was [[Ejection (sports)|sent off]] on 6 November 2022 in a 1–1 [[Seville derby]] draw at [[Real Betis]] for a 38th-minute foul on [[Álex Moreno (footballer)|Álex Moreno]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Lowe |first1=Sid |title=Red cards, a rocket and a ruckus: Seville derby ends in glorious chaos |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/nov/07/red-cards-a-rocket-and-a-ruckus-seville-derby-ends-in-glorious-chaos |access-date=20 December 2022 |work=[[The Guardian]] |date=7 November 2022}}</ref> On 31 May 2023, he scored the winning penalty in the [[2023 UEFA Europa League final|Europa League final]] against [[A.S. Roma|Roma]] in a 4–1 victory in the penalty shootouts after a 1–1 draw, which secured their seventh title in the competition.<ref name="auto1"/>

==International career==
In March 2019, [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]] manager [[Lionel Scaloni]] called up Montiel as one of ten domestic-based players for [[Exhibition game|friendlies]] against [[Venezuela national football team|Venezuela]] and [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Los diez convocados por Scaloni de la Superliga|trans-title=The ten Superliga players called up by Scaloni |url=https://ar.marca.com/claro/futbol/seleccion-argentina/2019/03/07/5c81419d22601dde6a8b45a6.html |access-date=20 December 2022 |work=Marca |date=7 March 2019 |language=Spanish}}</ref> He made his debut on 22 March against Venezuela at the [[Metropolitano Stadium]] in [[Madrid]], playing the entirety of a 3–1 loss.<ref>{{cite news |last1=McTear |first1=Euan |title=More Wanda Metropolitano misery for Argentina on Messi's return |url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/international-football/cronica/2019/03/22/5c9560c6ca4741d7198b4615.html |access-date=20 December 2022 |work=Marca |date=22 March 2019}}</ref>

On 11 June 2021, Montiel was one of the 28-man squad announced by Argentina for the [[2021 Copa América]] tournament.<ref>{{cite news |title=Argentina national team squad Copa America 2021: selected players, absences... |url=https://en.as.com/en/2021/07/10/soccer/1625909611_909521.html |access-date=20 December 2022 |work=[[Diario AS]] |date=10 July 2021}}</ref> He played four of the seven matches played by the  team in the tournament including the [[2021 Copa América Final|final]] victory, which he had to complete with a bleeding ankle as a result of a tackle from [[Fred (footballer, born 1993)|Fred]] in the first half of the game.<ref name=copa21>{{cite web|last1=Armen Graham|first1=Bryan|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2021/jul/10/argentina-v-brazil-copa-america-final-live |title=Argentina 1-0 Brazil: Copa América final – as it happened |website=The Guardian |date=10 July 2021 }}</ref>

In November 2022, Montiel was named in the final squad for the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in Qatar.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Lea |first1=Greg |title=Argentina World Cup 2022 squad: Lionel Scaloni's full team |url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/argentina-world-cup-2022-squad-players-line-up-qatar |access-date=20 December 2022 |work=[[FourFourTwo]] |date=18 December 2022}}</ref> On 18 December, he conceded a penalty late in extra time after a handball which [[Kylian Mbappé]] scored, but Montiel then scored the tournament-winning penalty kick for Argentina in a 4–2 victory against [[France national football team|France]] to win the [[2022 FIFA World Cup Final|final]], when the game went to penalties after a 3–3 draw.<ref name=wc22>{{cite web |last=Burton |first=Chris |url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/watch-montiel-wins-shootout-messi-argentina-world-cup-final-france/bltadcb447f18994d35 |title=WATCH: Montiel wins shootout for Messi & Argentina after thrilling World Cup final clash with France |website=[[Goal.com]] |date=18 December 2022 }}</ref>
