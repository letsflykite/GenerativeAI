'''Leon Christoph Goretzka''' (born 6 February 1995) is a German professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for {{German football updater|BaMunich}} club [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] and the [[Germany national football team|Germany national team]]. Due to his defensive prowess and passing range, Goretzka is widely considered one of the world's best box-to-box midfielders. <ref>{{Cite web |last=CCyler |date=15 October 2022 |title=BFW Film Room: How Leon Goretzka evolved into Bayern Munich’s most important midfielder |url=https://www.bavarianfootballworks.com/2022/10/15/23404078/bayern-munich-match-analysis-bfw-film-room-leon-goretzka-joshua-kimmich-leroy-sane-marcel-sabitzer |access-date=19 February 2023 |website=Bavarian Football Works |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Leon Goretzka: 10 things on the Bayern Munich and Germany midfield magician |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/leon-goretzka-10-things-on-schalke-midfielder-bayern-barcelona-liverpool-interest-467549.jsp |access-date=19 February 2023 |website=bundesliga.com - the official Bundesliga website |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=10 December 2018 |title=Leon Goretzka enjoys his new Bayern Munich midfield role |url=https://bayernstrikes.com/2018/12/10/leon-goretzka-enjoying-new-midfield-role-bayern-munich/ |access-date=19 February 2023 |website=Bayern Strikes |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Leon Goretzka: The All-Action Pressing Machine |url=https://breakingthelines.com/player-analysis/leon-goretzka-the-all-action-pressing-machine/ |access-date=19 February 2023 |website=Breaking The Lines |language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=25 August 2020 |title=Why throwback midfielder Leon Goretzka could be Hansi Flick's greatest masterstroke |url=https://www.twenty3.sport/why-throwback-midfielder-goretzka-could-be-flicks-greatest-masterstroke/ |access-date=19 February 2023 |website=Twenty3 |language=en-GB}}</ref>

Starting off his career with [[VfL Bochum]] in 2012, he went on play more than 100 Bundesliga games for [[FC Schalke 04|Schalke 04]] before signing with Bayern Munich in 2018. A full international since 2014, he has won more than 40 [[Cap (sport)|caps]] for the [[Germany national football team|Germany national team]] and represented his country at the [[2017 FIFA Confederations Cup]], the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]], and [[UEFA Euro 2020]]. He was also a part of the [[Germany Olympic football team|Germany Olympic team]] that finished second at the [[Football at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|2016 Summer Olympics]].

==Club career==
===VfL Bochum===
In 1999, Goretzka started his career with Werner SV 06 Bochum. He stayed for two years with the WSV before making the move to [[VfL Bochum]] in 2001.<ref name="2012–13 season">{{cite web|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/2bundesliga/vereine/2-bundesliga/2012-13/vfl-bochum-8/66754/spieler_goretzka-leon.html|language=de|title=Leon Goretzka|publisher=[[Kicker (sports magazine)|kicker]]|access-date=4 August 2012}}</ref>
	
On 30 July 2012, Goretzka was awarded the [[Fritz Walter Medal#2012|2012 under-17 Fritz Walter Medal in gold]].<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://www.dfb.de/index.php?id=511739&tx_dfbnews_pi1&#91;showUid&#93;=35370 |title=Fritz-Walter-Medaille für Goretzka, Ginter, Rüdiger und Lotzen|trans-title=Fritz-Walter-Medal for Goretzka, Ginter, Rüdiger and Lotzen|language=de|publisher=[[German Football Association]]|date=30 July 2012|access-date=1 March 2012}}</ref> On 4 August 2012, he made his professional debut for Bochum in the [[2. Bundesliga]] against [[Dynamo Dresden]] in the [[rewirpowerSTADION]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/2bundesliga/spieltag/2-bundesliga/2012-13/1/1441351/spielbericht_vfl-bochum-8_dynamo-dresden-65.html |title=Trio infernale: Iashvili, Goretzka und Freier|trans-title=Trio infernale: Iashvili, Goretzka and Freier|publisher=[[Kicker (sports magazine)|kicker]]|access-date=1 March 2013 | date = 4 August 2012}}</ref>

Goretzka had an impressive [[2012–13 VfL Bochum season|2012–13 season]] at Bochum and was their standout performer as Bochum narrowly avoided relegation from the 2. Bundesliga. During the season, Goretzka was linked to several big clubs around Europe including [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]], [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]], [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] and [[Real Madrid]]. [[Matthias Sammer]], the then sporting director of Bayern Munich, reportedly met with Goretzka to try to convince him of joining Bayern in the summer of 2013.<ref>{{cite web | url =http://global.espn.com/football/news/story/_/id/1211620/bayern-munich-lead-the-chase-for-bochum's-leon-goretzka | title = Sammer meets with Goretzka | website = [[ESPN]] | date = 2 November 2012 | access-date = 18 May 2018}}</ref>

===Schalke 04===
On 30 June 2013, [[FC Schalke 04|Schalke 04]] confirmed the transfer of Goretzka from Bochum. He signed a five-year contract until 30 June 2018. The transfer fee was reported to be €3.250 million.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.spiegel.de/sport/fussball/goretzka-wechsel-von-bochum-nach-schalke-offiziell-a-908644.html | title = Schalke und Bochum einigen sich im Fall Goretzka | language = de | website = Spiegel Online | date = 30 June 2013 | access-date = 18 May 2018}}</ref>

====2013–14 season====
[[File:Leon Goretzka.jpg|thumb|right|upright=0.8|Goretzka training for [[FC Schalke 04|Schalke 04]] in 2013]]

During the [[2013–14 FC Schalke 04 season|2013–14 season]], Goretzka had a promising first season, scoring five goals in 32 appearances in all competitions. He became a regular starter in the second half of the season and helped Schalke finish in third place in the Bundesliga behind champions Bayern Munich and [[Borussia Dortmund]].<ref name="2013–14 season">{{cite web|title=Leon Goretzka|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/2bundesliga/vereine/2013-14/66754/vereinsspieler_goretzka-leon.html|website=kicker.de|publisher=kicker|access-date=11 January 2017|language=de}}</ref> At the end of the season, Goretzka was called up for the 30-man preliminary squad of the [[Germany national football team|Germany national team]] for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]] in Brazil. He made his debut in a friendly match against [[Poland national football team|Poland]] prior to the World Cup.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.sport.de/news/ne2503804/goretzka-neuanfang-in-der-nationalelf/ | title = Goretzka: "Neuanfang" in der Nationalelf | language = de | website = sport.de | date = 14 November 2016 | access-date = 30 June 2020}}</ref>

====2014–15 season====
During the [[2014–15 FC Schalke 04 season|2014–15 season]], Goretzka was limited to only 11 matches in all competitions<ref name="Leon Goretzka » Club matches">{{cite web|title=Leon Goretzka » Club matches|url=http://www.worldfootball.net/player_summary/leon-goretzka/2/|publisher=World Football|access-date=11 January 2017}}</ref> due to a thigh injury. He returned from the injury on Matchday 24 against [[TSG 1899 Hoffenheim]]. Goretzka played in only 10 Bundesliga matches during the season. Schalke had a disappointing season and finished in sixth place in the Bundesliga.<ref name="GoretzkaConFedCup">{{cite web | url = https://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/schalke-s-leon-goretzka-comes-of-age-in-russia-confederations-cup-454008.jsp | title = Schalke's Leon Goretzka comes of age in Russia | website = bundesliga.com | date = 2 July 2017 | access-date = 18 May 2018}}</ref>

====2015–16 season====
[[File:Goretzka Schalke 2015.jpg|thumb|right|upright=0.8|Goretzka training for Schalke 04 in 2015]]

During the [[2015–16 FC Schalke 04 season|2015–16 season]], Goretzka scored two goals in 34 appearances in all competitions.<ref name="2015–16 season">{{cite web|title=Leon Goretzka|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/2bundesliga/vereine/2015-16/66754/vereinsspieler_goretzka-leon.html|website=kicker.de|publisher=kicker|access-date=11 January 2017|language=de}}</ref> He was back to full fitness at the start of the season, but had several injuries throughout the season. Goretzka was also diagnosed with an [[inflammatory bowel disease]] during the season and said "I was diagnosed with a chronic bowel inflammation, which had been having a negative impact on my ability to recover." Goretzka continued: "So I completely changed what I eat, cutting out gluten, cow's milk, pork and nuts. As a result, I have fewer issues with my health and I can recover from a game much quicker.<ref name = "GoretzkaConFedCup"/>

====2016–17 season====
During the [[2016–17 FC Schalke 04 season|2016–17 season]], Goretzka played in a career-high 41 matches in all competitions, scoring eight goals.<ref name="2016–17 season">{{cite web |title=Leon Goretzka |url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/2bundesliga/vereine/2016-17/66754/vereinsspieler_goretzka-leon.html |website=kicker.de |publisher=kicker |access-date=21 November 2018 |language=de}}</ref> This was regarded as Goretzka's best season to date as he flourished in a more attacking role. On 20 April, Goretzka received a concussion and suffered a double fracture of his jaw while playing against [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]] in the [[Europa League]]. He continued and played almost the entire match until he was substituted in the 84th minute. Schalke finished in a disappointing 10th place in the Bundesliga and did not qualify for European places.<ref name = "GoretzkaConFedCup"/>

====2017–18 season====
During the [[2017–18 FC Schalke 04 season|2017–18 season]], Goretzka played in 29 matches in all competitions and scored four goals.<ref name="2017–18 season">{{cite web |title=Leon Goretzka |url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/2bundesliga/vereine/2017-18/66754/vereinsspieler_goretzka-leon.html |website=kicker.de |publisher=kicker |access-date=21 November 2018 |language=de}}</ref> This was his first season without European football since his arrival at Schalke in 2013. He had problems with stress reaction in bones in his lower leg, which kept him out of action for over two months. Goretzka helped Schalke finish second behind champions Bayern Munich to qualify for the [[UEFA Champions League]] for the first time since the 2014–15 season. On 25 November 2017, he played in his 100th Bundesliga game in a match against Schalke's bitter rivals, Borussia Dortmund. On 19 January 2018, Goretzka announced that he would leave Schalke in the summer of 2018 and join rivals Bayern Munich. His decision did not go down well with the Schalke fans and board. Schalke's supervisory board chairman, [[Clemens Tönnies]], expressed his first reaction regarding Goretzka's decision on a football talk show. Tönnies said: "My first reaction was, that you shouldn’t wear the jersey of Schalke anymore." Tönnies said that Goretzka could be forced to sit in the stands should his decision have a negative impact on the team.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://sportbild.bild.de/bundesliga/vereine/schalke/toennies-goretzka-trikot-nicht-mehr-tragen-tribuene-fc-bayern-wechsel-54546282.sport.html | title = Tönnies: "Du solltest das Trikot von Schalke nicht mehr tragen" | language = de | website = SportBild | date = 21 January 2018 | access-date = 18 May 2018}}</ref>

====2019–20 season====
On 26 November 2019, Goretzka scored his first Champions League goal during a 6–0 away win at [[Red Star Belgrade]].<ref>{{cite web | url = https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2019/11/maiden-cl-goal-for-goretzka | title = Goretzka's maiden goal sets Bayern on their way | publisher = fcbayern.com | access-date = 27 November 2019}}</ref> He played vital part of [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern's]] treble win under [[Hansi Flick]]. He played most of the games, including the whole [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] campaign, making a midfield duo with [[Joshua Kimmich]]. After [[Benjamin Pavard]] got injured and Kimmich was asked to play as a [[right-back]], Goretzka played in a [[Box-to-box|box-to-box midfielder]] role next to [[Thiago Alcântara|Thiago]], including in the [[2020 Champions League Final|Champions League final]].{{cn|date=February 2022}}

====2020–21 season====
On 24 September 2020, Goretzka scored a goal in a 2–1 win over [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]] after extra time in the [[2020 UEFA Super Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefasupercup/match/2029141--bayern-vs-sevilla/ |title=Bayern 2–1 Sevilla |website=UEFA |date=24 September 2020 }}</ref> In April 2021, he missed the second leg against [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] in the [[2020–21 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] quarter-finals due to muscle problems.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.besoccer.com/new/goretzka-has-little-chance-of-playing-second-leg-in-paris-978388 |title=Goretzka has little chance of playing second leg in Paris |website=besoccer.com |date=April 2021 }}</ref> However, he ended the season winning his third consecutive Bundesliga title.<ref name="auto1"/>

==== 2021–22 season ====
Ahead of the season, Goretzka was assigned the number 8 shirt left vacant by [[Javi Martínez]]. On 17 August 2021, Goretzka conquered the [[2021 DFL-Supercup|2021 DFL-Super Cup]] with Bayern, playing the full 90 minutes. On 16 September, Bayern announced that Goretzka had signed a new contract, keeping him at the club until 2026.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Leon Goretzka extends contract|url=https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2021/09/leon-goretzka-extends-bayern-contract-until-2026|website=FC Bayern Munich|date=16 September 2021}}</ref>

====2022–23 season====
On 12 October 2022, he scored a brace in a 4–2 away win over [[FC Viktoria Plzeň|Viktoria Plzeň]], in which Bayern secured their qualification to the [[2022–23 UEFA Champions League knockout phase|Champions League knockout phase]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.focus.de/sport/fussball/fc-bayern-noten-zum-fc-bayern-gegen-viktoria-pilsen-doppelpacker-goretzka-bester-bayer-sane-in-wm-form_id_164179456.html |title=FC Bayern in der EinzelkritikDoppelpacker Goretzka überragt, Sané in brillanter Form |website=focus.de |language=de |date=12 October 2022 }}</ref>

===Senior===
On 8 May 2014, Goretzka was included in the 30-men preliminary squad of the Germany national team for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]] by Germany's manager, [[Joachim Löw]].<ref name="9_Leon Goretzka">{{cite news|title=Löw überrascht beim vorläufigen WM-Kader|trans-title=Löw surprises with the preliminary World Cup squad|url=http://www.dw.de/l%C3%B6w-%C3%BCberrascht-beim-vorl%C3%A4ufigen-wm-kader/a-17619351| first = Calle | last = Kops|work=dw.de|publisher=[[Deutsche Welle]]|date=8 May 2014|access-date=2 September 2014|language=de}}</ref> On 13 May 2014, he made his debut in a 0–0 draw against [[Poland national football team|Poland]].<ref name="10_Leon Goretzka">{{cite news|title=Germany vs Poland: More useful to Löw than you might think|url=http://www.dw.de/germany-vs-poland-more-useful-to-l%C3%B6w-than-you-might-think/a-17630809| first = Mark | last = Hallam |publisher=[[Deutsche Welle]]|date=12 May 2014|access-date=2 September 2014}}</ref> After Germany's match against Poland, in which Goretzka had suffered a muscle injury, he was removed from the team's preparatory training camp and final squad for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup|2014 World Cup]] in Brazil.<ref name="11_Leon Goretzka">{{cite news|title= Löw streicht vier und holt Kramer |trans-title=Löw eliminates four and gets Kramer |url=http://www.spox.com/de/sport/fussball/wm/wm2014/1405/News/jogi-lowe-streicht-marcell-jansen-max-meyer-leon-goretzka-andre-hahn-aus-wm-kader-christoph-kramer-neu-dabei.html |publisher=Spox.com |date=14 May 2014|access-date=2 September 2014|language=de}}</ref>

In May 2017, Goretzka was named in Germany's squad for the [[2017 FIFA Confederations Cup|Confederations Cup]] in Russia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en-za/news/4563/main/2017/05/17/35592432/kroos-muller-and-ozil-among-stars-left-out-of-germanys|title=Kroos, Muller and Ozil among stars left out of Germany's Confederations Cup squad|website=Goal|last=Myson|first=Chris|date=17 May 2017|access-date=17 June 2017}}</ref>
In the nation's opening match for the tournament on 20 June, he scored his first goal for Germany in a 3–2 [[2017 FIFA Confederations Cup Group B|Group B]] win over [[Australia national soccer team|Australia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2017/jun/19/australia-germany-conferations-cup-match-report|title=Socceroos face uphill task after being edged out by Germany|date=19 June 2017|work=The Guardian|access-date=20 June 2017}}</ref> Goretzka scored two goals against [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]] in the semi-finals. Goretzka finished joint top scorer with [[Timo Werner]] and [[Lars Stindl]] in the competition with three goals. The Germany national team won the competition, beating [[Chile national football team|Chile]] in the [[2017 FIFA Confederations Cup Final|final]] in [[Saint Petersburg]].<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.fifa.com/confederationscup/news/y=2017/m=7/news=awards-list-dominated-by-victorious-germans-2900157.html | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20170703001253/http://www.fifa.com/confederationscup/news/y=2017/m=7/news=awards-list-dominated-by-victorious-germans-2900157.html | url-status = dead | archive-date = 3 July 2017 | title = Awards list dominated by victorious Germans | website = FIFA.com | date = 2 July 2017 | access-date = 18 May 2018}}</ref>

On 4 June 2018, Goretzka was included in Germany's final 23-man squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dw.com/en/germany-announce-final-world-cup-squad-with-manuel-neuer-without-leroy-san%C3%A9/a-44064011|title=Germany announce final World Cup squad, with Manuel Neuer, without Leroy Sané|publisher=Deutsche Welle|access-date=4 June 2018}}</ref> On 27 June, he made his [[FIFA World Cup|World Cup]] debut in the last match of the group stage in a 2–0 defeat to [[South Korea national football team|South Korea]] as his side got knocked out from the World Cup in the first round for the first time since [[1938 FIFA World Cup|1938]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dw.com/en/world-cup-2018-germany-exit-at-group-stage-after-shock-south-korea-loss/a-44418816|title=World Cup 2018: Germany exit at group stage after shock South Korea loss|publisher=Deutsche Welle|access-date=28 June 2018}}</ref> On 19 May 2021, he was selected to the squad for the [[UEFA Euro 2020]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.kicker.de/em-kader-offiziell-loew-beruft-mueller-hummels-und-volland-804642/artikel|title=EM-Kader offiziell: Löw beruft Müller, Hummels und Volland|date=19 May 2021|website=kicker.de|language=de|access-date=19 May 2021}}</ref> He scored a goal in the final group fixture, a 2–2 draw against Hungary which saved Germany from elimination.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://footballexpress.co.uk/euro-2020-germany-2-2-hungary-post-match-analysis/ |title=Euro 2020: Germany 2-2 Hungary Post-Match Analysis | date = 24 June 2021 | access-date = 24 June 2021 | website = FootballExpress.co.uk | first =  Adithya | last = Namboothiri}}</ref>

Leon Goretzka"/> [[Peter Neururer]], Goretzka's head coach at Bochum, said that he had "never seen an eighteen year old footballer which had a potential as Goretzka", and titled him as the "talent of the century".<ref name="1_Leon Goretzka">{{cite news|title=Schalke verpflichtet Bochums Supertalent Goretzka|trans-title=Schalke signed Bochum Super Talent Goretzka teammates.<ref name="5_Leon Goretzka">{{cite news |url=http://nastytackle.com/leon-goretzka-germanys-next-big-talent |title=Leon Goretzka – Germany's Next Big Talent |website=Nasty Tackle |date=21 ==Personal life==
Goretzka was born in [[Bochum]], [[North Rhine-Westphalia]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.worldfootball.net/player_summary/leon-goretzka/ |title=Leon Goretzka: Profile |website=worldfootball.net |publisher=HEIM:SPIEL |access-date=28 June 2020}}</ref> He completed his ''[[Abitur]]'' and graduated from the {{Interlanguage link|Alice-Salomon-Berufskolleg|de}} (''Alice-Salomon-Vocational School'') in Bochum.<ref name="Goretzka has the matriculation examination filed">{{cite web | url = http://www.reviersport.de/279308---schalke-goretzka-hat-reifepruefung-abgelegt.html | title = RevierSport: Goretzka hat die Reifeprüfung abgelegt |trans-title=Reviersport: Goretzka took the matriculation examination| work = reviersport.de | publisher = Reviersport |date = 3 July 2014 | access-date = 2 September 2014 | language = de}}</ref> His father, Konrad, is an [[Automotive engineering|automotive engineer]] and [[Electrical engineering|electrical engineer]] for [[Opel]].<ref name="Opel">{{cite web | url = https://www.bloomberg.com/news/2012-03-01/gm-peugeot-pact-makes-opel-workers-rivals-for-survival-at-two-plants-cars.html | title = GM-Peugeot Pact Makes Opel Workers Rivals for Survival at Two Plants: Cars | first1 = Alex | last1 = Webb | first2 = Steve | last2 = Rothwell | work = bloomberg.com | publisher = [[Bloomberg L.P.]] | date = 1 May 2012 | access-date = 2 September 2014 }}</ref>  His mother, Katharina, was presbyter in the Protestant parish of Altenbochum-[[Laer]]<ref>{{Cite web |title=AltBOWeltladen : Evangelischer Kirchenkreis Bochum |url=https://www.kirchenkreis-bochum.de/meldungen/archiv/archiv-2018/22-11-2018-archiv-11-2018/27-11-2018-altboweltladen |access-date=1 May 2022 |website=www.kirchenkreis-bochum.de |language=de}}</ref> and worked as a business manager in many institutions in Bochum.

Goretzka launched an online initiative, "We Kick Corona", with his Bayern Munich teammate [[Joshua Kimmich]], to help charitable, social or medical institutions during the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.dfb.de/en/news/detail/we-kick-corona-goretzka-and-kimmich-donate-1-million-euros-214271/?no_cache=1 |title="We Kick Corona": Goretzka and Kimmich donate 1 million euros |website=dfb.de |date=20 March 2020 |access-date=1 October 2020 |archive-date=15 October 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201015121755/https://www.dfb.de/en/news/detail/we-kick-corona-goretzka-and-kimmich-donate-1-million-euros-214271/?no_cache=1 |url-status=live }}</ref>

Goretzka has been an activist against [[Crime in Germany|hate crimes]], visiting the [[Dachau concentration camp|Dachau]] [[Holocaust]] memorial, and meeting [[Holocaust survivors|Holocaust survivor]] [[Margot Friedländer]]. He is a critic of the [[Far-right politics|far-right]] political party [[Alternative for Germany]], calling them a "disgrace for Germany" and saying "when a party is supported and led by [[Holocaust denial|Holocaust revisionists]] and [[COVID-19 misinformation|Corona deniers]], then they are unmasking themselves".<ref>{{cite web |url=https://web.de/magazine/regio/bayern/goretzka-prominenz-fussball-faschisten-35855370 |title=Goretzka will Prominenz nutzen: Kein Fußball den Faschisten |website=web.de |language=German |date=28 May 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bavarianfootballworks.com/2020/12/20/22191760/bayern-munich-leon-goreztka-rot-gegen-rassismus-holocaust-activism-germany-we-kick-corona-covid |title=Leon Goretzka speaks out against Germany's far right |website=Bavarian Football Works |language=English |date=20 December 2020}}</ref>

Beside football, Goretzka has also primarily invested in a marketing company in 2014, in August 2020 Goretzka started his business in real estate, LivinCG inmobilien GMBH,<ref>{{Cite web |title=Livin CG Immobilien GmbH, Herne, Germany |url=https://www.northdata.com/Livin+CG+Immobilien+GmbH,+Herne/Amtsgericht+Bochum+HRB+18752 |access-date=1 May 2022 |website=www.northdata.com |language=en}}</ref> which is managed by his girlfriend.
