'''Érick Gabriel Gutiérrez Galaviz''' (born 15 June 1995) is a Mexican professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[Liga MX]] club [[C.D. Guadalajara|Guadalajara]] and the [[Mexico national football team|Mexico national team]].

Gutiérrez began both his youth and professional career at [[C.F. Pachuca|Pachuca]], winning the [[2015–16 Liga MX season#Torneo Clausura|2016 Clausura]] title and the [[2016–17 CONCACAF Champions League]] in his five years with the club. During the 2015–2016 season, he was named [[Captain (association football)|captain]] of the team at the age of 20. In August 2018, he joined PSV Eindhoven.

Gutiérrez has participated for various national youth teams, including the U-20 team and the U-23 side that played the [[Football at the 2016 Summer Olympics|2016 Summer Olympics]]. At the [[Mexico national football team|senior]] level,  Gutiérrez has represented Mexico at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]] and [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022]] [[FIFA World Cup]], and won the [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup|2019 edition]] of the [[CONCACAF Gold Cup]].

==Club career==
===Pachuca===
Gutiérrez joined [[Liga MX]] club [[C.F. Pachuca|Pachuca]] at the age of 12.<ref name="espnfc">{{cite news|last1=Marshall|first1=Tom|title=Pachuca's Erick Gutierrez proving to be a young Mexican standout|url=http://www.espnfc.com/club/pachuca/234/blog/post/2451227/erick-gutierrez-of-pachuca-a-young-mexican-standout|accessdate=10 August 2016|work=ESPNFC|date=15 May 2015}}</ref> In his early career he started as a right-back, then moved to midfield. He made his debut on 26 October 2013 as a starter in a 0–0 draw at home against [[Cruz Azul]].<ref name="ligamxprofile">{{cite web|title=Erick Gabriel Gutiérrez Galaviz|url=http://www.ligabancomer.mx/cancha/jugador/80679/erick-gabriel-gutierrez-galaviz|website=Liga MX|accessdate=10 August 2016|language=Spanish}}</ref><ref name="espnfc"/><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/ficha.php?id_partido=47502 |title=Erick Gutierrez Debut |access-date=14 April 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304225827/http://www.mediotiempo.com/ficha.php?id_partido=47502 |archive-date=4 March 2016 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> He made ten more appearances including 4 more starts that [[2013–14 Liga MX season|season]]. He was made a permanent starter at the beginning of the [[2014–15 Liga MX season]] under the management of [[Diego Alonso]]. Gutiérrez scored his first Liga MX goal on 7 February 2015 in a 1–1 home draw against [[Deportivo Toluca F.C.|Toluca]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/ficha.php?id_partido=59941 |title=First Goal |access-date=14 April 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160305000940/http://www.mediotiempo.com/ficha.php?id_partido=59941 |archive-date=5 March 2016 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> In April 2016, at the age of 20, Gutiérrez was assigned as Pachuca's captain after only 70 league matches.<ref>{{cite news |title=Erick Gutiérrez capitán de Pachuca con 20 años |url=http://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/mexico/nota/_/id/2637328/erick-gutierrez-capitan-de-pachuca-con-20-anos |accessdate=30 August 2018 |work=ESPN Deportes |date=3 April 2016 |language=Spanish}}</ref> At the end of the [[2015–16 Liga MX season#Torneo Clausura|Clausura 2016]], he was named on the tournament's Best XI.<ref name="record">{{cite web|title=Liga MX da a conocer el once ideal del Clausura 2016|url=http://www.record.com.mx/futbol-futbol-nacional-liga-mx/liga-mx-da-conocer-el-once-ideal-del-clausura-2016|work=Récord|date=31 May 2016|accessdate=12 October 2018}}</ref>

In his time at the club, Gutiérrez amassed 144 league appearances.<ref name="psv">{{cite news |last1=Marshall |first1=Tom |title=PSV Eindhoven sign Pachuca, Mexico midfielder Erick Gutierrez |url=http://www.espn.com/soccer/soccer-transfers/story/3616952/psv-eindhoven-sign-pachuca-mexico-midfielder-erick-gutierrez |accessdate=30 August 2018 |work=ESPN FC |date=30 August 2018}}</ref>

===PSV===
On 29 August 2018, Gutiérrez joined Dutch club [[PSV Eindhoven]] on a five-year contract, joining up with international teammate [[Hirving Lozano]].<ref name="psv"/> He was handed jersey number 25.<ref>{{cite web|title=¿Qué número usará Erick Gutiérrez en el PSV?|url=https://www.goal.com/es-mx/noticias/que-numero-usara-erick-gutierrez-en-el-psv/18lkmj69watn81ayec3t4ohnpr|work=Goal.com|language=Spanish|date=31 August 2018}}</ref> On 15 September, he made his debut with PSV against [[ADO Den Haag|ADO]]; within five minutes of being subbed in, Gutiérrez assisted in Lozano's goal as well as scoring himself in their 7–0 away win.<ref>{{cite web|title=Move over, Canelo! Lozano and Gutierrez get Mexican Independence Day party rolling early at PSV|url=http://www.goal.com/en-us/news/move-over-canelo-lozano-and-gutierrez-get-mexican/xkhfj0c505fc19y7vtmqldyz3|work=Goal.com|first=Jon|last=Arnold|date=15 September 2018|accessdate=24 September 2018}}</ref> Three days later, he made his [[2018–19 UEFA Champions League|UEFA Champions League]] debut against [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]], coming on in the 82nd minute for [[Pablo Rosario]] in PSV's 4–0 defeat.<ref>{{cite web|title=Barcelona 4-0 PSV Eindhoven|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/45566374 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=18 September 2018}}</ref> On 30 October, he would score his first [[2018–19 KNVB Cup|KNVB Cup]] goal during the second round match against [[RKC Waalwijk]], scoring in the first minute of the game but PSV would go on losing 3–2.<ref>{{cite web|title=PSV, con Gutiérrez y Lozano, queda fuera de la Copa de Holanda|url=http://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/reporte?juegoId=527365|work=ESPN|language=Spanish|date=30 October 2018|accessdate=15 November 2018}}</ref>

The following [[2019–20 Eredivisie|season]], he was handed the number 15 jersey.<ref>{{cite web|title=Erick Gutiérrez cambia de número en el PSV|url=https://www.tudn.com/futbol/dutch-eredivisie/erick-gutierrez-cambia-de-numero-en-el-psv|work=Tudn.com|date=17 July 2019}}</ref> In September, he picked up a hand injury while training with the national team and returned in a match against [[PEC Zwolle]], coming on as a substitute and scoring the fourth goal of the 4–0 victory.<ref>{{cite web|title=Gutierrez scores goal in return to action for PSV, Govea scores in Belgium in quiet week for Mexicans in Europe|url=https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/gutierrez-scores-goal-in-psv-return-govea-scores-in-belgium/hmxqa0lwvsm116uy1risers4e|first=Jon|last=Arnold|publisher=Goal.com|date=30 September 2019}}</ref>

During the [[2020–21 Eredivisie|2020–21]] season, Gutiérrez suffered from two different injuries, only playing 150 minutes during the whole Eredivisie season. In the subsequent [[2021–22 Eredivisie|season]], he became more important for PSV, playing more games and scoring a goal in the KNVB Cup Final against Ajax.<ref>{{Cite web |title=VIDEO: PSV Eindhoven come from behind to win Dutch Cup final |url=https://www.besoccer.com/new/video-psv-eindhoven-come-from-behind-to-win-dutch-cup-final-1129053 |access-date=11 August 2022 |website=www.besoccer.com |language=en}}</ref>

On 11 August 2022, it was announced that Gutiérrez had signed a contract extension with PSV that runs until 2025. This was just one day after he scored in the team's 4–3 aggregate victory over [[AS Monaco FC|AS Monaco]] in their [[2022–23 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round|Champions League Qualification]] game.<ref>{{Cite web |date=11 August 2022 |title=Érick Gutiérrez signs contract extension until 2025 |url=https://www.psv.nl/english-psv/news/article/erick-gutierrez-signs-contract-extension-until-2025.htm |access-date=11 August 2022 |website=www.psv.nl |language=en-US}}</ref>

===Guadalajara===
On 2 July 2023, Gutiérrez returned to Mexico and joined [[C.D. Guadalajara|Guadalajara]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.chivasdecorazon.com.mx/es/noticias/36474/Erick-Gutirrez-es-nuevo-jugador-de-Chivas|title=Erick Gutiérrez es nuevo jugador de Chivas|publisher=Club Deportivo Guadalajara|date=2 July 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
===Youth===
Gutiérrez was called up by the [[Mexico national under-20 football team|under-20]] team managed by [[Sergio Almaguer]] for the [[2015 CONCACAF U-20 Championship]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Almaguer define a sus guerreros para el Mundial Sub 20|url=https://www.excelsior.com.mx/adrenalina/2015/05/08/1023097|work=Excelsior|date=8 May 2015|accessdate=12 October 2018}}</ref> He was named captain of the team despite only playing only in four matches out of the six as Mexico went on to win the tournament and earned a berth to the [[2015 FIFA U-20 World Cup]] in New Zealand.<ref>{{cite web|title=En penales, México se proclama campeón de Premundial sub 20|url=https://www.excelsior.com.mx/adrenalina/2015/01/25/1004548|work=Excelsior|date=25 January 2015|accessdate=12 October 2018}}</ref> He would also be listed on the tournament's Best XI.<ref name="TSG">{{cite web|title=TSG announces CU20 Best XI, Awards|url=http://www.concacaf.com/article/tsg-announces-cu20-best-xi-awards|publisher=CONCACAF|date=25 January 2015|accessdate=3 July 2016}}</ref>

Selected to participate at the 2015 FIFA U-20 World Cup, he was Mexico's captain during the tournament as the team failed to pass the group stage.<ref>{{cite news|title=Mexico out after Serbia defeat, Uruguay through after drawing of lots|url=http://www.espnfc.com/u-20-world-cup/story/2480846/u20-mexico-out-after-serbia-defeat-uruguay-through-on-lots|accessdate=10 August 2016|work=ESPNFC|date=6 June 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=FIFA U-20 World Cup New Zealand 2015 – Matches – Serbia-Mexico|url=https://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/matches/round=271602/match=300269553/index.html#lineups|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150528074212/http://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/matches/round=271602/match=300269553/index.html#lineups|url-status=dead|archive-date=28 May 2015|website=FIFA|accessdate=10 August 2016}}</ref>

[[File:Coréia do Sul x México - Futebol masculino - Olimpíada Rio 2016 (28899234915).jpg|thumb|upright=1.20|Gutiérrez playing against [[South Korea national under-23 football team|South Korea]] in the [[Football at the 2016 Summer Olympics|2016 Summer Olympics]]]]
On 18 September 2015, Gutiérrez was selected by coach [[Raúl Gutiérrez]] to play in the [[2015 CONCACAF Men's Olympic Qualifying Championship]].<ref>{{cite web|title=MEXICO NAMES SQUAD FOR OLYMPIC QUALIFYING CHAMPIONSHIP|url=http://www.mexicanfut.com/2015/09/mexico-names-squad-for-olympic-qualifying-championship/|website=Mexicanfut.com|first=Rafael|last=Diaz|date=18 September 2015|accessdate=10 October 2018}}</ref>

He was also picked to represent Mexico at the [[Football at the 2016 Summer Olympics|2016 Summer Olympics]] in [[Rio de Janeiro]], Brazil.<ref>{{cite web|title=Definida la convocatoria del Tri para Juegos Olímpicos |url=http://www.mediotiempo.com/futbol/2016/07/07/definida-la-convocatoria-del-tri-para-juegos-olimpicos |work=Medio Tiempo |language=Spanish |accessdate=7 July 2016}}</ref>  On 7 August, he scored four goals as Mexico bounced back to win 5–1 after [[Fiji Olympic football team|Fiji]] took the lead in the first half of the second group stage match.<ref>{{cite web|title=Gutierrez's four-goal tally helps Mexico storm back |url=http://www.espnfc.com/mens-olympic-tournament/71/blog/post/2924823/five-second-half-goals-spark-shaky-mexico-to-olympic-victory-over-fiji}}</ref>

He was named in Mexico's squad for a friendly against [[Senegal national football team|Senegal]] in February 2016.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.espnfc.co.uk/team/mexico/203/blog/post/2804058/pachuca-youngsters-take-center-stage-for-mexico-vs-senegal|title=Pachuca youngsters take center stage for Mexico vs. Senegal|publisher=espnfc.co.uk |date=9 February 2016 |accessdate=3 July 2016}}</ref> He would eventually make his debut with the senior national team on 11 October 2016, in a 1–0 friendly victory against [[Panama national football team|Panama]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Debutaron Érick Gutiérrez Luis Robles con el Tri|url=http://espndeportes.espn.com/futbol/mundial/nota/_/id/2816206/debutaron-erick-gutierrez-luis-robles-con-el-tri|work=ESPN|date=12 October 2016|accessdate=4 October 2018}}</ref>

On 28 June 2017, Gutiérrez was called up to participate in the [[2017 CONCACAF Gold Cup]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Lista la convocatoria de México para la Copa Oro|url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/copa-oro/2017/06/29/59542cb0ca4741ab508b4609.html|work=Marca|date=28 June 2017|accessdate=10 October 2018}}</ref> managing to appear in all group stage games including the semi-final where Mexico lost 1–0 against [[Jamaica national football team|Jamaica]].

In May 2018, Gutiérrez was named in Mexico's preliminary squad for the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Guardado, Reyes make Mexico's 23-man World Cup squad|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/guardado-reyes-make-mexicos-23-man-world-cup-squad/11do86vgjx7h31u6uvo4toc30o|work=Goal|first=Jon|last=Arnold|date=4 June 2018|accessdate=1 October 2018}}</ref> He originally did not make the final 23,<ref>{{cite web|title=Mexico's 2018 World Cup roster: Who joins Chicharito & Guardado in final 23-man squad? |url=http://www.goal.com/en-us/news/mexicos-2018-world-cup-roster-who-chicharito-guardado-final/9k4np6x1pri31g4t8jt72jvun|work=Goal|first=Jon|last=Arnold|date=4 June 2018|accessdate=4 October 2018}}</ref> but due to an injury to [[Diego Reyes]], he was selected as a replacement.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://miseleccion.mx/diego-reyes-causa-baja-la-seleccion-nacional-mexico/ |title=Diego Reyes causa baja de la Selección Nacional de México |trans-title=Diego Reyes is out of the Mexico national team |website=miseleccion.mx |publisher=[[Mexican Football Federation]] |date=13 June 2018 |access-date=13 June 2018 |language=Spanish}}</ref>

He was included in [[Gerardo Martino]]'s preliminary roster for the [[2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Tata Martino Announces Mexico Preliminary Gold Cup Squad; Vela, Chicharito, Herrera, Corona left out|url=https://www.fmfstateofmind.com/2019/5/14/18534851/mexico-el-tri-concacaf-gold-cup-copa-oro-squad-final-list-preliminary-corona-vela-chicharito-herrera|work=Fmfstateofmind.com|first=Antonio|last=Tinajero|date=14 May 2019}}</ref> and was subsequently included in the final roster for the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mexico unveil final Gold Cup roster;
