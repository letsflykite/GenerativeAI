'''Unai Simón Mendibil''' (born 11 June 1997) is a Spanish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] for [[La Liga]] club [[Athletic Bilbao]] and the [[Spain national football team|Spain national team]].

==Club career==
Simón was born in [[Vitoria-Gasteiz]], [[Álava]], [[Basque Country (autonomous community)|Basque Country]], and raised in the nearby village of [[Murgia (Álava)|Murgia]];<ref name="Pueblo">{{cite web|url=https://elpais.com/deportes/eurocopa-futbol/2021-07-04/el-pueblo-de-mikel-landa-ya-es-el-pueblo-de-unai-simon.html?outputType=amp|title=El pueblo de Mikel Landa ya es el pueblo de Unai Simón|trans-title=Mikel Landa's village is now Unai Simón's village |publisher=[[El País]] |language=es|date=4 July 2021|access-date=9 January 2022}}</ref> he joined [[Athletic Bilbao]]'s youth system in 2011, from [[CD Aurrerá de Vitoria]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lacanteradelezama.com/entrevista-a-unai-simon/|title=Entrevista a Unai Simon|trans-title=Interview to Unai Simon|website=La Cantera de Lezama|language=es|date=11 July 2015|access-date=18 July 2017}}</ref> He made his debut as a senior with [[CD Basconia|the farm team]] in the [[2014–15 Tercera División|2014–15 season]], in the [[Tercera División]].<ref name="ACprofile">{{cite web|url=https://www.athletic-club.eus/en/players/unai-simon-mendibil|title=Unai Simón|publisher=Athletic Bilbao|access-date=25 October 2021}}</ref> 

On 8 June 2016, Simón was promoted to [[Bilbao Athletic|the reserves]], [[2015–16 Segunda División|recently relegated]] to [[Segunda División B]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.athletic-club.eus/en/news/16822/leaving-basconia.html|title=Leaving Basconia|publisher=Athletic Bilbao|date=8 June 2016|access-date=18 July 2017}}</ref> He immediately became a starter for the side, contributing with 29 appearances during [[2016–17 Segunda División B|the campaign]].<ref name="ACprofile"/>

On 2 June 2017, Simón was called up to the main squad for the pre-season by new first team manager [[José Ángel Ziganda]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.athletic-club.eus/en/news/17960/preseason-with-the-premier-team.html|title=Preseason with the premier team|publisher=Athletic Bilbao|date=2 June 2017|access-date=18 July 2017}}</ref> but continued to appear exclusively for the Bs. On 13 July 2018, he signed a contract extension until 2023,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.athletic-club.eus/en/news/19190/renewal-of-unai-simon.html|title=Renewal of Unai Simón|publisher=Athletic Bilbao|date=13 July 2018|access-date=20 August 2018}}</ref> and was loaned to [[Segunda División]] side [[Elche CF]] two weeks later.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.athletic-club.eus/en/news/19230/unai-simon-on-loan-to-elche-cf.html|title=Unai Simón on-loan to Elche CF|publisher=Athletic Bilbao|date=27 July 2018|access-date=20 August 2018}}</ref>

On 15 August 2018, after [[Kepa Arrizabalaga]]'s departure from the club and [[Iago Herrerín]]'s injury, Simón was recalled by Athletic.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.athletic-club.eus/en/news/19288/recovery-of-unai-simon.html|title=Recovery of Unai Simón|publisher=Athletic Bilbao|date=15 August 2018|access-date=20 August 2018}}</ref> He initially appeared to be behind the older [[Álex Remiro]] in the queue for selection,<ref name=Kepa>{{cite news|url=https://athletic.elcorreo.com/salida-kepa-facilitara-20180808223557-nt.html|title=La salida de Kepa facilita la continuidad de Remiro|trans-title=Kepa's departure makes it easier for Remiro to continue|newspaper=[[El Correo]]|first=Javier|last=Ortiz de Lazcano|language=es|date=8 August 2018|access-date=11 February 2019}}</ref> but made his professional – and [[La Liga]] – debut five days later, starting in a 2–1 home win against [[CD Leganés]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mundodeportivo.com/futbol/athletic-bilbao/20180821/451393160369/athletic-unai-simon-remiro-porteria-san-mames.html|title=Simón debuta con un paradón providencial|trans-title=Simón debuts with a providential save|publisher=[[Mundo Deportivo]]|language=es|last1=Zubieta|first1=Jon|date=21 August 2018|access-date=21 August 2018 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marca.com/en/football/spanish-football/2018/08/21/5b7b3ce422601d0f4f8b4646.html|title=Muniain gives Berizzo his first win as Athletic Club coach|publisher=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|last1=García|first1=Óscar|date=21 August 2018|access-date=21 August 2018}}</ref> In just his third league appearance for Athletic Bilbao, Simón played in a 1–1 draw with [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]] where he made several key saves and was named player of the match.<ref>{{cite web|title=Isco goal salvages point for Real Madrid away to Athletic Bilbao|url=https://www.eurosport.com/football/liga/2018-2019/isco-goal-salvages-point-for-real-madrid-away-to-athletic-bilbao_sto6932432/story.shtml|date=15 September 2018|access-date=19 September 2018|first=Graham|last=Ruthven|publisher=[[Eurosport]]}}</ref> However, when Herrerín regained full fitness in October 2018 he was made the starting goalkeeper, with Simón's only appearances over the subsequent four months coming in the [[Copa del Rey]].<ref name="ACprofile"/>

For the [[2019–20 Athletic Bilbao season|2019–20 season]], Simón was given the #1 squad number and began the campaign as first choice, keeping a clean sheet in an opening day win over [[FC Barcelona]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/49371762|title=Athletic Bilbao 1–0 Barcelona|publisher=BBC Sport|date=17 August 2019|access-date=26 August 2019}}</ref> He continued his strong form throughout the season, missing two fixtures with illness and completing 33 matches in the league, conceding 29 goals<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bdfutbol.com/en/p/j22117.html?cat=1a&temp=2019-20&equip=6&rival=-&jug=-|title=Matches Unai Simón (filters: 2019-20 season, La Liga, Athletic Club) |website=BDFutbol|access-date=17 July 2020 }}</ref> to finish third in the [[Ricardo Zamora Trophy|Zamora Trophy]] rankings.<ref>[https://www.marca.com/futbol/primera-division/zamora.html Zamora de LaLiga Santander 2019 - 2020], Marca (in Spanish). Retrieved 4 September 2020</ref> On the 37th, penultimate, matchday, he was sent off during the first half against Leganés for a 'last man' challenge outside the penalty area, with the resultant suspension ending his season a game early; with ten men, Athletic Bilbao lost 2–0 to end their slim hopes of qualifying for the [[UEFA Europa League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cdleganes.com/en/new/leganes-wins-against-athletic-at-san-mames-and-is-still-alive-0-2|title=Leganés wins against Athletic at San Mamés and is still alive (0-2)|publisher=CD Leganés|date=16 July 2020 |access-date=17 July 2020}}</ref>

In August 2020, he signed a new contract with the club running to the summer of 2025, with no buyout clause.<ref>[https://www.athletic-club.eus/en/news/2020/08/20/unai-simon-renews-contract-until-2025 Unai Simón renews contract until 2025], Athletic Club website, 20 August 2020</ref>

==International career==
Simón was selected for [[Spain national under-19 football team|Spain]] at [[Spain national football team|under-19]] and [[Spain national under-21 football team|under-21]] levels. He was a member of the squad that won the [[2015 UEFA European Under-19 Championship]], albeit as the backup goalkeeper to [[Antonio Sivera]] during the tournament.<ref name=sub19>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bidebietairratia.com/noticias/basauri/2015/07/22/el-portero-del-basconia-unai-simon-gana-el-europeo-sub-19-con-espana/|title=El portero del Basconia Unai Simón gana el Europeo sub'19 con España|trans-title=Basconia goalkeeper Unai Simón wins the U19 European Championship with Spain|last=Villapun|first=Jon|date=22 July 2015|website=Bidebieta|language=es|access-date=16 April 2020}}</ref> In the same year he was in the [[Basque Country national football team|Basque Country under-18 team]] that won the {{ill|Spanish regional championship|es|Campeonato de España de selecciones autonómicas de fútbol}}.<ref name="Pueblo"/>

On 1 September 2017, Simón debuted with the under-21 team against [[Italy national under-21 football team|Italy]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.marca.com/futbol/seleccion/2017/09/01/59a9b49546163fb7378b4583.html|title=Selección de España: España da un repaso a Italia|trans-title=Spanish national team: Spain reviews Italy|date=1 September 2017|website=Marca.com|language=es|access-date=16 April 2020}}</ref> He was selected for the [[2019 UEFA European Under-21 Championship]], playing the first game of the group stage as Spain once more finished as champions (Sivera was again first-choice).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.marca.com/futbol/europeo-sub-21/cronica/2019/06/30/5d18fcec268e3ea1378b457e.html|title=La Quinta de Oro|trans-title=The Golden Fifth|date=30 June 2019|website=Marca.com|language=es|access-date=16 April 2020}}</ref><ref name=u21bbc/>

Simón received his first call up to [[Spain national football team|the senior side]] on 20 August 2020, for the initial two matches of the [[2020–21 UEFA Nations League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sefutbol.com/oficial-lista-convocados-seleccion-espanola-partidos-uefa-nations-league-alemania-y-ucrania|title=OFICIAL {{!}} Lista de convocados de la Selección española para los partidos de la UEFA Nations League ante Alemania y Ucrania|trans-title=OFFICIAL {{!}} List of called-up players by the Spain national team for the UEFA Nations League matches against Germany and Ukraine|publisher=[[Royal Spanish Football Federation|SEFutbol]]|language=es|date=20 August 2020|access-date=20 August 2020}}</ref> Despite having made an error which led to a goal at club level a few days earlier, he was selected for his full debut on 11 November 2020, playing the entirety of a friendly against [[Netherlands national football team|the Netherlands]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://athletic.elcorreo.com/unai-simon-debuta-seleccion-20201111195911-nt.html|title=Luis Enrique echa un capote a Simón|trans-title=Luis Enrique throws a cape on Simón|publisher=El Correo |language=es|date=12 November 2020|access-date=12 November 2020}}</ref>

On 24 May 2021, Simón was included in [[Luis Enrique]]'s [[UEFA Euro 2020 squads#Spain|24-man squad]] for [[UEFA Euro 2020]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Braidwood |first1=Jamie |title=Euro 2020 news LIVE: Sergio Ramos left out of Spain squad plus latest before England announcement |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/euro-2020-news-live-england-squad-b1852654.html |access-date=24 May 2021 |work=The Independent |date=24 May 2021}}</ref> He started all of the team's matches, and caused an [[own goal]] in the 5–3 last-16 win over [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]] when he was unable to control a long backwards pass by [[Pedri]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=del Río |first1=José |title=Unai Simon's horrific mistake for Croatia's opener |url=https://www.marca.com/en/football/uefa-euro/2021/06/28/60da04b1268e3ebe3b8b459b.html |access-date=7 July 2021 |work=Marca |date=28 June 2021}}</ref> Luis Enrique praised his mentality for having recovered from that mistake.<ref>{{cite news |title=Unai Simon and Pedri team up for shocking Spain own goal - but keeper gains 'redemption' |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/57643907 |access-date=7 July 2021 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=28 June 2021}}</ref> He then managed to save two penalties as Spain won the shootout against [[Switzerland national football team|Switzerland]] in the quarter-finals after a 1–1 draw,<ref name="Pueblo"/> for which he was also awarded the Star of the Match.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro-2020/news/026b-12a94a0fe9f1-c2503bc98c72-1000--spain-pip-swiss-on-penalties/ |title=Switzerland 1-1 Spain (pens: 1-3): La Roja pip Swiss on penalties to reach semi-finals |website=UEFA |date=2 July 2021 }}</ref> The semi-final against [[Italy national football team|Italy]] ended with the same score, and Simón saved the first attempt in the shootout by [[Manuel Locatelli]], but the Italians prevailed.<ref>{{cite news |title=Italy hold nerve to beat Spain on penalties and reach final |url=https://www.thehindu.com/sport/football/euro-2020-italy-hold-nerve-to-beat-spain-on-penalties-and-reach-final/article35182298.ece |access-date=7 July 2021 |work=The Hindu |date=6 July 2021}}</ref> Simón was also called up for the [[Spain national under-23 football team|Spanish Olympic team]] for the [[Football at the 2020 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|delayed 2020 tournament]] in Japan.<ref>{{cite news |title=Pedri, Garcia & Torres among Spain's Euro 2020 players named in Tokyo Olympics squad |url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/pedri-garcia-torres-among-spains-euro-2020-players-named-in/1c8y6858hfpia1xji4f8ej3pqi |access-date=7 July 2021 |publisher=Goal |date=29 June 2021}}</ref> With Simón as the starting goalkeeper they reached the final, but lost to [[Brazil national under-23 football team|Brazil]].<ref name="Oly2021">{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/amp/football/news/15234/12375555/tokyo-2020-olympics-brazil-retain-olympic-football-gold-with-malcom-extra-time-winner|title=Tokyo 2020 Olympics: Brazil retain Olympic football gold with Malcom extra-time winner|publisher=Sky Sports |date = 8 August 2021|access-date=25 October 2021}}</ref>

He was the starter at the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]] in which Spain progressed from their group with a win, a draw and a defeat, then lost 3–0 on penalties to [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]] in the Round of 16 after a goalless draw – Simón saved from [[Badr Benoun]], but three team-mates all failed with their efforts.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63789751|title=World Cup 2022: Morocco 0–0 Spain (3–0 pens) Achraf Hakimi chips winning penalty to reach quarters |first=Shamoon|last=Hafez|date=6 December 2022|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=6 December 2022}}</ref> They had better fortune in the [[2023 UEFA Nations League final]] six months later, where another 0–0, against [[Croatia national football team|Croatia]], was followed by a 5–4 win in the shootout to claim the title, Simón blocking attempts from [[Lovro Majer]] and [[Bruno Petković]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2023/jun/18/croatia-v-spain-nations-league-final-live|title= Croatia 0 Spain 0 (4–5 on pens): As it happened|date=18 June 2023|work=Guardian|accessdate=20 June 2023}}</ref>
