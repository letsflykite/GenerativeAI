'''Christian Thers Nørgaard''' (born 10 March 1994) is a Danish professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[defensive midfielder]] for {{English football updater|Brentfor}} club [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]] and the [[Denmark national football team|Denmark national team]].

Between abortive spells with [[Hamburger SV]] and [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]], Nørgaard came to prominence in his homeland with [[Brøndby IF|Brøndby]]. He transferred to Brentford in 2019 and in 2021 was part of the squad which won promotion to the [[English football league system|top-flight of English football]] for the second time in the club's history. Nørgaard was part of the Denmark squads at [[UEFA Euro 2020|Euro 2020]] and the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]].

==Club career==
===Early years===
[[File:Christian Nørgaard 2 -HSV.jpg|thumb|Nørgaard playing for [[Hamburger SV II]] in 2012.|upright]]
A [[defensive midfielder]], Nørgaard began his career in his native Denmark with BK Heimdal and Espergærde IF, before entering the [[youth system]] at [[Lyngby BK|Lyngby]] in 2008.<ref name="Andersen">{{Cite web |last1=Andersen |first1=Mads |last2=Nielsen |first2=Jonathan Roth |last3=Mousluh |first3=Mohammed |title=Christian Nørgaard, stortalentet fra Espergærde |url=https://helsingordagblad.dk/artikel/christian-n%C3%B8rgaard-stortalentet-fra-esperg%C3%A6rde |url-status=dead |access-date=28 May 2019 |website=Helsingør Dagblad |language=da-DK}}</ref> He progressed to win three calls into the first team squad during the [[2011–12 Danish Superliga|2011–12]] season and made his only senior appearance for the club as a substitute in a 2–2 [[Danish Superliga|Superliga]] draw with [[HB Køge]] on 20 November 2011.<ref name="Soccerway" /> Two months later, Nørgaard spurned interest from [[A.C. Milan]] and [[TSG 1899 Hoffenheim]] to transfer to [[Bundesliga]] club [[Hamburger SV]] for a €400,000 fee.<ref name="Kristensen">{{Cite web |last=Hanghøj Kristensen |first=Søren |date=25 January 2012 |title=Lyngby-talent solgt to HSV |url=http://www.tipsbladet.dk/nyhed/bundesliga/lyngby-talent-solgt-til-hsv |access-date=25 January 2012 |publisher=www.tipsbladet.dk |language=Danish}}</ref><ref>{{Cite AV media |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8Tx1L_UAjnU |title="Football is about highs and lows, you can never complete it" {{!}} My Journey {{!}} Christian Nørgaard |date=16 March 2022 |type=Interview |time=6:45}}</ref> He signed a {{frac|3|1|2}}-year contract,<ref name="Kristensen" /> but was unable to settle at the club and failed to make any first team appearances before his departure in August 2013.<ref name="Andersen" /> He gained his first regular experience of senior football with 22 appearances for the [[Hamburger SV II|reserve team]].<ref name="Soccerway" />

===Fiorentina===
On 19 July 2018, Nørgaard moved to Italy to sign for [[Serie A]] club [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]] for a fee of approximately €3.5 million.<ref>{{Cite web |date=19 July 2018 |title=Brøndby sælger Christian Nørgaard til Fiorentina |url=https://www.dr.dk/sporten/fodbold/superliga/broendby-saelger-christian-noergaard-til-fiorentina |access-date=20 July 2018 |website=DR |publisher=DR |language=da-DK}}</ref> He signed a four-year contract,<ref>{{Cite web |title=Medie: Christian Nørgaard er på vej væk fra Fiorentina |url=https://www.tipsbladet.dk/nyhed/serie/medie-christian-noergaard-er-paa-vej-vaek-fra-fiorentina |access-date=28 May 2019 |website=Tipsbladet.dk |language=Danish}}</ref> but made just six appearances before departing the club in May 2019.<ref name="Soccerbase1819" /><ref name="SignsBrentford">{{Cite web |date=28 May 2019 |title=Christian Nørgaard signs from Fiorentina |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2019/may/christian-norgaard-joins-brentford-from-fiorentina/ |access-date=28 May 2019 |publisher=Brentford F.C.}}</ref>

==International career==
Nørgaard won 73 caps and scored five goals for Denmark between [[Denmark national under-16 football team|U16]] and [[Denmark national under-21 football team|U21]] level.<ref name="DBU">{{DBU player|id=6776}}</ref> He was a part of the Danes' [[2011 FIFA U-17 World Cup|2011 U17 World Cup]] and [[2015 UEFA European Under-21 Championship|2015]] and [[2017 UEFA European Under-21 Championship|2017 European U21 Championship]] squads.<ref name="SignsBrentford" />

Nørgaard an unused substitute for the [[Denmark national football team|senior team]] during four [[2018–19 UEFA Nations League B#Group 4|2018–19 Nations League group stage]] and [[UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying Group D|Euro 2020 qualifying]] matches in 2018 and 2019.<ref name="Soccerway" /> On 8 September 2020, Nørgaard made his debut for the senior team with a start in a 0–0 [[2020–21 UEFA Nations League A#Group 2|Nations League]] draw with [[England national football team|England]] and his performance was recognised with the [[Danish Football Association|DBU]]'s [[man of the match]] award.<ref name="MOTM">{{Cite web |title=Christian Norgaard impresses on Danish international debut |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/news/2020/september/international-recap-20200909/ |access-date=9 September 2020 |publisher=Brentford F.C.}}</ref> After winning three further caps during the 2020–21 season,<ref name="DBU" /> Nørgaard was named in the Denmark squad for [[UEFA Euro 2020|Euro 2020]] and appeared as a substitute in five of the six matches of the Danes' run to the semi-final.<ref name="2022WCSquad">{{Cite web |title=Norgaard added to Denmark squad |url=https://www.brentfordfc.com/en/news/article/christian-norgaard-added-to-denmark-world-cup-squad |access-date=14 November 2022 |website=www.brentfordfc.com |language=en-gb}}</ref> Nørgaard was named in Denmark's [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]] squad and made one substitute appearance prior to the team's group stage exit.<ref name="2022WCSquad" /><ref name="DBU" />

== Style of play ==
Nørgaard has been described as "technically good and looks to pass the ball forward. Importantly, he is also good at intercepting passes and has a strong defensive mindset".<ref name="SignsBrentford" /> He "can play in a variety of different positions in the midfield, but has given his best performances as a deep midfield player".<ref name="SignsBrentford" />

== Personal life ==
Nørgaard was born in [[Copenhagen]] and grew up in [[Espergærde]] from the age of five.<ref name="Carey" /> He is married with a son.<ref name="Fisher" />
